[[Crossocheilus]]

CATEGORIES: Cyprinidae, Fish of Asia

Crossocheilus is a genus of fish in the family Cyprinidae, the carps and minnows. It is distributed in Asia,Froese, R. and D. Pauly. (Eds.) Crossocheilus species list. FishBase. 2011. including India, China, Thailand, Indonesia, and Malaysia.Rui-Fang, S., et al. (2000). A review of the Chinese species of Crossocheilus, with description of a new species (Ostariophysi: Cyprinidae). The Raffles Bulletin of Zoology 48(2) 215-21.
A fish of this genus has a large rostral cap on its snout with a fringe of tassel-like fissures covering the upper jaw, a mouth in an inferior position on the snout, and one or two pairs of barbels.
These fish occur in several types of habitat, often fast-flowing rivers with rocky bottoms.Kottelat, M. and T. H. Hui. (2011). Crossocheilus elegans, a new species of fish from northern Borneo (Teleostei: Cyprinidae). The Raffles Bulletin of Zoology 59(2), 195-99.
