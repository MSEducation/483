[[King County Administration Building]]

CATEGORIES: Government buildings completed in 1971, Government buildings in Seattle, Washington, County government buildings in Washington (state)

The nine-story King County Administration Building Seattle, the seat of King County, Washington, USA. It lies between Jefferson and James streets and between Fourth and Fifth avenues. It features a unique hexagaonal honeycomb theme in its walls and windows. It was built in 1971.King County document[tpl]dead link|date=April 2012[/tpl]

==Agencies==

The King County Administration Building is located at 500 Fourth Ave. Parking is available in parking garages on all surrounding streets. Bus information is available from Metro.

==Aesthetics==

According to the Seattle Times, "County and city officials agree on at least one thing: the dubious aesthetics of the Administration Building, a squat, fortresslike building with diagonal lines". Seattle Deputy Mayor Tim Ceis stated "I think everybody acknowledges it may be the ugliest building in downtown Seattle."[tpl]citation needed|date=June 2012[/tpl] 
It was also voted the ugliest government building in the country in the '70s, shortly after it was built.Keith Ervin and Bob Young, Sims wants tower to replace King County's "ugly" building, Seattle Times, October 18, 2006.
The old Seattle Municipal Building and Seattle Public Library were also known as relatively ugly buildings.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2007[/tpl]
