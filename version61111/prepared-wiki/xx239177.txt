[[Meelis Kubo]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 births, Living people, Magicians, Estonian television personalities

Meelis Kubo (born 23 November 1982) is an Estonian magician.Rum, http://www.yliopilasleht.ee/1435 Üliõpilasleht, 2006-12-04
In autumn 2007 Kubo, in cooperation with Muusikamaailm Ltd, performed the full-length magic show Miraakel in 13 towns around Estonia.Teesalu, http://www.postimees.ee/050907/esileht/meelelahutus/hip/281028.php Postimees, 2007-09-05
Meelis Kubo is one of the founders and board members of the Society of Estonian Magicians. The society was founded on 30 April 2008.Eelmaa, http://www.sirp.ee/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=8520&catid=2&Itemid=5 Sirp, 2009-04-07
In the same year Kubo, Charlekas, Kevin-Chris, Simeoni Sundja and Tom Pintson brought on stages the magic show Tabalukk (Padlock).Raud, http://www.epl.ee/artikkel/443988 Eesti Päevaleht, 2008-10-06Sander, http://paber.ekspress.ee/viewdoc/66F21F9E13848262C22574E90051D85E Eesti Ekspress, 2008-10-24
In February 2009, Meelis Kubo and Ande released a book of magic for children Lembitu maagiline maailm (The Magical World of Lembit).Post, http://www.arileht.ee/kultuur/462689?lk_id=1111&sisu=27 Äripäev, 2009-03-20
In the same year during the winter/spring season Kubo and Charlekas performed on Estonian national channel's (ETV) morning program "Terevisioon" every week.
In November 2009, Meelis Kubo and Charlekas came out with a shocking magic show Räpane sessioon (Dirty Session) where they played notorious surgeons doctor Cubo and professor Charlekas.Sander, Meelis Kubo ja Karl Eelmaa teevad õudset mustkunsti Eesti Ekspress, 2009-11-13
In 2010, Meelis Kubo won the first prize in Estonian close-up magic competition.Kairi Prints, Mustkunsti meister selgunud Eesti Ekspress, 2010-06-07
