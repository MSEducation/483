[[Vladimir Alganov]]

CATEGORIES: 1952 births, Living people, KGB officers, SVR officers, Russian businesspeople, Lukoil, Energy in Poland, Poland–Russia relations, Lithuania–Russia relations

Vladimir Alganov ([tpl]lang-ru|Владимир Петрович Алганов[/tpl]; born 1952 in Sankt Petersburg) is a Russian spy. He was Soviet KGB officer in Warsaw, Poland in the 1980s and Russian SVR officer in the same city in the 1990s.
In 1996, Poland's Prime Minister Józef Oleksy resigned because of his links to Alganov.[tpl]cite news | title = Polish PM forced to resign over links with KGB man | publisher = The Independent | url = http://www.independent.co.uk/news/world/polish-pm-forced-to-resign-over-links-with-kgb-man-1325658.html | date=25 January 1996 | accessdate = 2009-10-17 | location=London[/tpl]
Alganov was deported from Poland in 1997.
In 2005 Lithuanian authorities said that Alganov had been issued a long-term Lithuanian visa in 2002 and Alganov had met managers of the Ignalina Nuclear Power Plant.[tpl]cite web | title = Chronicle of Russian Espionage in the Baltic Countries | publisher = Axis Globe | url = http://www.axisglobe.com/article.asp?article=49 | date=2005-04-20 | accessdate = 2009-10-17[/tpl][tpl]cite web | title = Baltic News (2005/01) | publisher = Baltics Worldwide | url = http://www.balticsworldwide.com/baltic-news-200501/ | date=January 2005 | accessdate = 2009-10-17[/tpl]
