[[Michelia doltsopa]]

CATEGORIES: Michelia, Flora of Asia, Flora of the Himalayas, Trees of Asia, Trees of India, Garden plants of Asia, Ornamental trees

Michelia doltsopa,  closely related to the magnolia and known as Sweet Michelia, is a large shrub or tree native to the eastern Himalayan region and the Meghalaya subtropical forests in India.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=Q1_fAywb_bkC&lpg=PA394&dq=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&pg=PA394#v=onepage&q=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&f=false | title=Landscape Plants for Eastern North America: Exclusive of Florida and the Immediate Gulf Coast | publisher=John Wiley & Sons | author=Flint, Harrison L. | year=1997 | pages=394 | isbn=9780471599197[/tpl] The wood is fragrant.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=gQADAAAAYAAJ&dq=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&pg=PA291#v=onepage&q=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&f=false | title=Arboretum Et Fruticetum Britannicum | publisher=Harvard University | author=Loudon, John Claudius | year=1838 | pages=291[/tpl]

==Description==

The plant varies in form from bushy to narrow and upright, can grow to a height of [tpl]convert|30|m|ft[/tpl] tall. The tree flowers in spring and produces heavily scented white flowers. It has long leathery and glossy dark-green leaves, [tpl]convert|6|-|17|cm|in[/tpl] in length, that provide a point of interest all year long. The wood is a rich brown.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=xdZ6AA4rFcQC&lpg=PA3&dq=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&pg=PA10#v=onepage&q=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&f=false | title=A Painter's Year in the Forests of Bhutan | publisher=University of Alberta | author=Hellum, A. K. | year=2001 | pages=10 | isbn=9780888643230[/tpl]
The "Silver Cloud" variety grows to 15 feet and flowers earlier in its lifespan.

==Cultivation==

Michelia doltsopa is used as a featured ornamental tree and street tree, or pruned as a hedge.  It enjoys a sheltered position in full or part sun, and appreciates well drained soil.[tpl]cite journal | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=0I9BAQAAIAAJ&q=%22michelia+doltsopa%22&dq=%22michelia+doltsopa%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=0BKwUq37AYu7lQXMqICwBw&ved=0CFwQ6AEwCTgU | title=California Horticultural Journal | author=Pacific Horticultural Foundation | journal=California Horticultural Journal | year=1960 | volume=21-22[/tpl] It can tolerate acidic soil.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=MMMjW6AuzHAC&lpg=PA218&dq=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&pg=PA453#v=onepage&q=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&f=false | title=The Complete Burke's Backyard: The Ultimate Book of Fact Sheets | publisher=Murdoch Books | author=Burke, Don | year=2005 | pages=453 | isbn=9781740457392[/tpl]

==Global distribution and use==

In Nepal, the wood of the Michelia doltsopa is used for house building. It is also used for house building in Bhutan, where in the 1980s it suffered from over-harvesting.
The Michelia doltsopa is useful in a Shifting cultivation system, which relies on species with "good nitrogen fixation in soil".[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=fGolqwLoLTcC&lpg=PA260&dq=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&pg=PA260#v=onepage&q=%22michelia%20doltsopa%22&f=false | title=Ecology and Environment | publisher=Rastogi Publications | author=Sharma, P.
