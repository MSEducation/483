[[Geoffrey Pidcock]]

CATEGORIES: 1897 births, 1976 deaths, Royal Flying Corps officers, British World War I flying aces, People from Eastbourne, Royal Air Force air marshals, Recipients of the Croix de Guerre (France)

Air Vice-Marshal Geoffrey Arthur Henzell Pidcock (6 November 1897 - February 1976) began his career as a World War I flying ace credited with six aerial victories.The Aerodrome Retrieved 18 February 2010. He remained in the Royal Air Force after World War I and rose in rank.
Pidcock was born at Eastbourne, Sussex, and educated at St Cyprian's School.St Cyprian's Chronicle 1919 During World War I he joined the Royal Flying Corps and served in No. 60 Squadron RFC, No. 1 Squadron RFC, No. 44 Squadron RFC and No. 73 Squadron RAF. He reached the rank of captain and scored six aerial victories in 1917 and 1918. He was awarded the French Croix de Guerre at that time.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/paget.php Retrieved 18 February 2010.
Post World War I, he served with 1 Squadron again, in India. He then flew Sopwith Snipes in Iraq from 1921 to 1923.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 304 [/tpl]
