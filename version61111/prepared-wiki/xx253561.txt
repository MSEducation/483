[[Tomasz Imieliński]]

CATEGORIES: 1954 births, Living people, Polish computer scientists, Rutgers University faculty, Gdańsk University of Technology alumni, Polish emigrants to the United States, People from Toruń

Tomasz Imieliński (born July 11, 1954 in Toruń, Poland) is a Polish-American computer scientist. He is currently professor at Rutgers University in New Jersey, United States. He served as chairman of the computer science department at Rutgers from 1996 to 2003. In 2000, he co-founded Connotate Technologies [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.connotate.com/ |title=Web Data. Simplified. | Web Data Collection, Scraping & Monitoring Solutions |publisher=Connotate.com |date= |accessdate=2014-02-07[/tpl] – a web data extraction company based in New Brunswick, NJ. He also served as Executive Vice President at Ask.com.[tpl]fact|date=February 2014[/tpl]
His joint paper with Agrawal and SwamiR. Agrawal; T. Imieliński; A. Swami: ''Mining Association Rules Between Sets of Items in Large Databases", SIGMOD Conference 1993: 207-216 started  the Association rule mining research area[tpl]fact|date=February 2014[/tpl] and is one of the most cited publications in computer science,[tpl]cite web|url=http://citeseerx.ist.psu.edu/stats/articles;jsessionid=BBEADE76D40826608564B55170EE1842 |title=CiteSeerX — Statistics - Most Cited Articles in Computer Science |publisher=Citeseerx.ist.psu.edu |date= |accessdate=2014-02-07[/tpl] with over 12,000 citations according to Google Scholar. This paper received the 2003 - 10 year Test of Time ACM SIGMOD award.
Imieliński has also been one of the pioneers of mobile computing and for his joint paper with Badri NathT. Imieliński; B.R. Badrinath: Querying in highly mobile distributed environments, VLDB Conference 1992: 41-52 he received the Ten Year VLDB Best Paper award in 2002.[tpl]fact|date=February 2014[/tpl]
Imieliński graduated with B.E/M.E degree in Electrical Engineering from Politechnika Gdańska and received a doctorate in Computer Science  from Polish Academy of Sciences, in Poland.
