Post Grad is a 2009  romantic comedy film directed by Vicky Jenson and starring Alexis Bledel, about a recent college graduate who moves back in with her family while she figures out what she wants to do next.
Originally under the working titles of Ticket to Ride and then The Post-Grad Survival Guide,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cinematical.com/tag/Vicky+Jenson/|title=Alexis Bledel Gets Not Only a Lover, but Mr. Mom and Carol Burnett Too!|date=December 8, 2007|publisher=Cinematical|accessdate=2009-05-31[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.canmag.com/nw/9586-alexis-bledel-ticket-ride|title=Alexis Bledel Has a Ticket to Ride|last=Parsons|first=Ryan|date=November 12, 2007|publisher=CanMag|accessdate=2009-05-31[/tpl] the film was released on August 21, 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.film-releases.com/movies/film-information/movie-7218|title=The Post Grad Survival Guide |publisher=Film Releases |accessdate=2009-05-31[/tpl]

==Plot==

Ryden Malby graduates from college in the middle of the late-2000s recession and is forced to move back in with her parents, because her dream job has been given to her arrogant college nemesis Jessica Bard. Ryden and her best friend Adam, who has had a longtime crush on her, must find a job for Ryden before she loses hope for her future dream as an editor of a big publishing company. But will sudden feelings for her longtime friend interfere with her dreams?

==Reception==

Post Grad was panned by film critics and filmgoers alike. The film stands with a 7% "rotten" rating by 93 critics at Rotten Tomatoes; the consensus states: "A lightweight, unambitious comedy, Post Grad features fine actors that can do little with its middling, uninspiring script."[tpl]rotten-tomatoes|post_grad|Post Grad[/tpl] It scores 35 at Metacritic, based on 25 reviews, which also represents "generally unfavorable reviews".[tpl]metacritic film|post-grad|Post Grad[/tpl]
Roger Ebert, however, awarded it three out of four stars, stating, "If you're cynical or jaded, it might not get past you. But here is the first movie in a long time that had me actually admitting I wouldn't mind seeing a sequel."Ebert, Roger."Post Grad" (review), ''Chicago Sun-Times, August 19, 2009

==Box office==

During opening weekend the film opened at #11, grossing $2,651,996.
