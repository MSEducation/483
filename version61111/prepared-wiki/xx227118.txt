[[Memory Chapel]]

CATEGORIES: Churches in Los Angeles, California, Chapels in the United States, Wilmington, Los Angeles, Religious buildings completed in 1870, 19th-century church buildings, Los Angeles Historic-Cultural Monuments, Carpenter Gothic churches in California

Memory Chapel is a Los Angeles Historic-Cultural Monument (HCM #155) located in the Wilmington section of Los Angeles, California, near the Port of Los Angeles.  Built in 1870 in the Carpenter Gothic Victorian architecture style, it is the oldest Protestant church in the Harbor area.  Originally located at "F" Street and Marine Avenue, it was moved to its present location in 1939.[tpl]cite web|title=Wilmington's Monuments Landmarks and  Historic Places|publisher=Wilmington Historical Society|url=http://www.lafn.org/community/whs/Historic__Landmarks.html[/tpl] 
The chapel is used now by a Philippine church, with Sunday services in the Tagalog language. The original pews dating to 1870 are still in use at the chapel.[tpl]cite news|title=Wilmington|publisher=LA Okay.com|url=http://www.laokay.com/Wilmington.htm[/tpl] English services are held in the adjacent Calvary Presbyterian Church -- which replaced the Memory Chapel.[tpl]cite web|title=No. 155 - Memory Chapel|publisher=Big Orange Landmarks|date=2008-06-19|url=http://bigorangelandmarks.blogspot.com/2008/06/no-155-memory-chapel.html[/tpl]
