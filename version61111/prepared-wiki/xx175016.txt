[[Gmina Poronin]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Lesser Poland Voivodeship, Tatra County

Gmina Poronin is a rural gmina (administrative district) in Tatra County, Lesser Poland Voivodeship, in southern Poland. Its seat is the village of Poronin, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|7|km|mi|0[/tpl] north-east of Zakopane and [tpl]convert|80|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south of the regional capital Kraków.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|83.55|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 10,706.

==Villages==

Gmina Poronin contains the villages and settlements of Bustryk, Małe Ciche, Murzasichle, Nowe Bystre, Suche and Ząb.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Poronin is bordered by the town of Zakopane and by the gminas of Biały Dunajec, Bukowina Tatrzańska, Czarny Dunajec and Kościelisko.
