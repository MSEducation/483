[[Nikolai Yakovlevich Kuznetsov]]

CATEGORIES: 1873 births, 1948 deaths, Lepidopterists, Russian entomologists, Russian physiologists, People from Saint Petersburg, Saint Petersburg State University alumni, Recipients of the Order of the Red Banner of Labour, Russian paleontologists

Nikolai Jakovlevice Kusnezov, also spelled Kusnetzov, Kusnetsov and Kuznetsov [tpl]cite web|title=Николай Яковлевич Кузнецов|url=http://jugan2.narod.ru/kuznja.html|publisher=Галерея лепидоптерологов России|accessdate=25 October 2011[/tpl]  ([tpl]lang-rus|Николай Яковлевич Кузнецов[/tpl]) (May 23, 1873, in St. Petersburg – April 8, 1948 Leningrad) was a Russian entomologist, paleoentomologist and physiologist.
He graduated from the University of St. Petersburg in 1895. Then he was appointed demonstrator in animal physiology at the University and later became a lecturer, and a professor. He also lectured on entomology and insect physiology at the Institute of Applied, Zoology, but, his main interest was centred at the Zoological Institute of the Academy of Sciences, where he was in charge of Lepidoptera for more than forty years, he was specialized in the Pieridae family   and also studied fossil insects in amber.[tpl]cite journal | title=Obituary | journal=Nature |date=July 1949 | volume=164 | pages=94–95 | doi=10.1038/164094b0[/tpl] He was colleague and friend of Grigory Grumm-Grzhimaylo.
