[[Shire of Aramac]]

CATEGORIES: Former local government areas of Queensland, 1879 establishments in Australia, 2008 disestablishments in Australia

The Shire of Aramac was a local government area located in central Queensland about [tpl]convert|1100|km|mi|0[/tpl] north west of the state capital, Brisbane, between the towns of Barcaldine and Winton. It covered an area of [tpl]convert|23364.1|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and existed as a local government entity from 1879 until 2008, when it amalgamated with the Shires of Barcaldine and Jericho to form the Barcaldine Region.

==History==

The Aramac Division was created on 11 November 1879 as one of 74 divisions around Queensland under the Divisional Boards Act 1879 with a population of 841.[tpl]Gazette QLD|title=Proclamation Division|volume=25|page=1005|date=11 November 1879[/tpl]
On 9 May 1900, part of the Aramac Division was excised to create the new Longreach Division under the Divisional Boards Act 1887.[tpl]cite QSA Agency|11204|Longreach Divisional Board|21 September 2013[/tpl]
On 27 December 1902, part of the Aramac Division was excised to create the Ilfracombe Division under the Divisional Boards Act 1887.[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article52979957 |title=WESTERN DIVISIONAL BOARDS. |newspaper=Morning Bulletin (Rockhampton, Qld. : 1878 - 1954) |location=Rockhampton, Qld. |date=2 January 1903 |accessdate=21 September 2013 |page=5 |publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl]
With the passage of the Local Authorities Act 1902, Aramac Division became the Shire of Aramac on 31 March 1903.[tpl]cite QSA Agency|296|Aramac Shire Council|21 September 2013[/tpl]
On 15 March 2008, under the Local Government (Reform Implementation) Act 2007 passed by the Parliament of Queensland on 10 August 2007, the Shire of Aramac merged with the Shires of Barcaldine and Jericho to form the Barcaldine Region.[tpl]cite QSA Agency|11031|Barcaldine Regional Council|21 September 2013[/tpl]
The Shire operated a railway from Aramac to its junction with the railway from Rockhampton to Longreach at BarcaldineThe Aramac Tramway Preston, R & Tonkies, R. Australian Railway Historical Society Bulletin, August, 1957 pp118-123 from 1911 to 1975.
