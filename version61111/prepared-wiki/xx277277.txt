[[Ella Naper]]

CATEGORIES: Alumni of Camberwell College of Arts, British artists' models, British jewellery designers, English artists, Newlyn School of Artists, 1886 births, 1972 deaths

Ella Louise Naper, (9 February 1886 â€“ 1972) was an artist, jeweller and potter. She was born in Charlton, one of the nine children of Alfred Champion, a fireman, and Mary Ann Champion. She attended the Camberwell School of Arts and Crafts from 1904-06. There, under the jeweller, Frederick James Partridge she learned a wide range of techniques for working in metal, wood and enamell. In 1906 she went to Branscombe, Devon where Partridge rented some cottages for his students. There she met the architect and painter Charles Naper whom she married in 1910.[tpl]cite book|author=Caroline Fox|publisher=Newlyn Orion|year=1985|title=Painting in Newlyn 1900-1930|ISBN=[/tpl] The couple spent two years in Looe, Cornwall before making their permanent home at Trewoofe in Lamorna.[tpl]cite web|author=|year=|title=Ella Naper|publisher=cornwell artists index|accessdate=31 July 2013|url=http://cornwallartists.org/cornwall-artists/ella-naper[/tpl]
Ella Naper worked from home producing decorative enamal and horn work jewellary. Naper sold much of her work through events such as 'Arts and Crafts Exhibition', 'Woman's Art Exhibition', Liberty's in London and, after 1924, Newlyn Art Gallery craft exhibitions. During the First World War, Ella Naper and Laura Knight collaborated on the design of several pieces of painted jewellery and enamel plaques,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.damelauraknight.com/biography.html |title=The Official Dame Laura Knight Website: Biography |work= |accessdate=15 January 2011[/tpl] including Two Dancers (1912).[tpl]cite web|author=|year=|title=Two Dancers|publisher=BBC/Public Catalogue Foundation|accessdate=2 August 2013|url=http://ichef.bbci.co.uk/arts/yourpaintings/images/paintings/phg/624x544/cw_phg_pezph_2000_42_624x544.jpg[/tpl] In 1915, Naper and Knight exhibited  several of these pieces in a joint exhibition, with Lamorna Birch, held at the London Fine Art Society.[tpl]cite book|author=Rosie Broadley|publisher= National Portrait Gallery,London|year=2013|title=Laura Knight Portraits|ISBN=978-1-85514-463-7[/tpl] Naper received commissions for mayorial chains and war memorials, including one in Exeter Cathedral and also designed the memorial to the artist Benjamin Leader in St Buryan's Church. After 1919 Naper, together with Kate Westrup and Emily Westrup, ran the Lamorna Pottery, which continued in production until 1935.
Ella Naper was the subject of several paintings by other artists living in Lamorna, including Charles Naper, Ruth Simpson and Harold Knight. She is one of the models in Harold Harvey's painting The Critics (1922) and was also painted by, and produced some work with, Gluck.[tpl]cite web|author=|year=|title=Gluck|publisher=cornwell artists index|accessdate=5 September 2013|url=http://cornwallartists.org/cornwall-artists/gluck[/tpl] Naper features in several works by Laura Knight, including Spring (1916-1920) and is the model Knight is seen painting in Self Portrait (1913).[tpl]cite news|author=Tessa Hadley|date=6 July 2013|title= Laura Knight:The unashamed illustrator|work=The Guardian|accessdate=31 July 2013|url=http://www.theguardian.com/artanddesign/2013/jul/06/laura-knight-unashamed-illustrator?INTCMP=SRCH[/tpl]
