[[Ashbury railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Disused railway stations in Devon, Former London and South Western Railway stations, Railway stations opened in 1879, Railway stations closed in 1966, Beeching closures in England

Ashbury railway station was located on the Okehampton to Bude Line 3Â¾ miles east of Halwill Junction, and served the hamlet of Ashbury and the village of Northlew in the English county of Devon.

==History==

Opened by the London and South Western Railway, the station was absorbed by the Southern Railway during the Grouping of 1923, In 1948 with  nationalisation the station then passed on to the Southern Region of British Railways and later Western Region of British Railways. The station was subsequently closed by the British Railways Board.
