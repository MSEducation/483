Sir Smith Child, 1st Baronet (5 March 1808 – 27 March 1896) was a Conservative Party politician in the United Kingdom.
He was born at Newfield Hall, Tunstall, Staffordshire the son of John George Child and the grandson of Admiral Smith Child (1730–1813).Sir Smith Child  In 1835 he married Sarah, daughter and heiress of Richard Clarke Hill of Stallington Hall, Stone, Staffordshire.[tpl]cite book|title=Burke's Peerage, Baronetage and Knightage, 1949|publisher=Burke's Peerage Ltd|page=403[/tpl]  They had issue, beside one daughter, two sons:
He was Member of Parliament (MP) for Staffordshire North from 1851 to 1859, and for Staffordshire West from 1868 to 1874. He was appointed High Sheriff of Staffordshire for 1865.[tpl]cite web | url= http://www.thepotteries.org/people/child_smith.htm| title=Sir Smith Child| accessdate= 8 February 2011[/tpl]
He was made a baronet on 7 December 1868, of Newfield and of Stallington in the county of Staffordshire, and of Dunlosset, Islay, the county of Argyll.London Gazette Issue 23445 published on the 27 November 1868. Page 1 of 128
Tunstall clock tower was erected in his honour in 1893. Following his death in 1896, aged eighty-eight, his title passed to his eldest grandson Smith Hill Child.
