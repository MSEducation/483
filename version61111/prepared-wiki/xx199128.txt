[[Bob Thoms]]

CATEGORIES: 1826 births, 1903 deaths, English cricket umpires, English cricketers

Robert Arthur "Bob" Thoms (19 May 1826â€“10 June 1903) was a first-class cricket umpire who stood in two Test Matches, the first two  played in England, in 1880 and 1882.  His long umpiring career in first-class matches lasted from 1863 to 1900. When he died, The Times said that he was "the most famous umpire the game has known".Wisden Cricketers' Almanack, 1904 edition, obituary notice.
He had appeared as a player in three first-class matches in the period 1850-1851: one for an Under 36 XI, one for Marylebone Cricket Club and Metropolitan Clubs and one for the All England Eleven.
He was born in Marylebone, London and died in the vicinity of Regent's Park, London.
