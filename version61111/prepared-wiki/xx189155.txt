[[Symphytum officinale]]

CATEGORIES: Symphytum, Demulcents, Plants described in 1753

Symphytum officinale is a perennial flowering plant of the genus Symphytum in the family Boraginaceae. Along with several other species of Symphytum, it is commonly known as comfrey. To differentiate it from other comfreys, this species may be known as common comfrey,The potential of Russian comfrey (Symphytum officinale) as an animal feedstuff in Uganda Quaker comfrey, and cultivated comfrey. Other common names include boneset,GRIN Species Profile knitbone, consound, and slippery-root. It is native to Europe and it is known elsewhere, including North America, as an introduced species and sometimes a weed.

==Uses==

Symphytum officinale roots have been used in the traditional Austrian medicine internally (as tea or tincture) or externally (as ointment, compresses,or alcoholic digestion) for treatment of disorders of the locomotor system and gastrointestinal tract.
