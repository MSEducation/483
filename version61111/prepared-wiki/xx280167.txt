[[Maurice Burton (cyclist)]]

CATEGORIES: 1955 births, Living people, People from London, English cyclists

Maurice Burton (born 29 October 1955)[tpl]Cycling archives|2838[/tpl]Telegraph gives his birth date as 29 January 1995: [tpl]cite web| url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/othersports/cycling/7950461/Germain-Burton-QandA.html| title=Germain Burton: Q&A| author=John MacLeary| publisher=The Telegraph| date=12 September 2010|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] is an English cycle shop owner and former racing cyclist from Catford, London.
Born in London to an English mother and a Jamaican father, Maurice Burton was the first black British champion in cycling. His first taste of success came when he won the Junior Sprint national title in 1973. He won the amateur scratch title the following year, raced over a 20 km distance although he was booed as he crossed the line. He went on to represent Britain at the 1974 Commonwealth Games, but was not selected for the Olympic squad in 1976.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.pezcyclingnews.com/page/interviews/?id=85159#.UgTXRYU5uHk| title=PEZ Profiles: Maurice Burton| author=Edmond Hood| publisher=PEZ Cycling News| date=11 November 2005| accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
Burton became frustrated by the racism pervasive in Britain at the time, he moved to Belgium in 1977, basing himself in Ghent.  He was described as the first coloured professional cyclist.[tpl]cite web| url=http://sixday.org.uk/html/1923-80_riders.html| title=1923–1980 Riders| publisher=SixDay.org.uk| accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] 
Burton rode 56 professional Six Day events, and retired from competitive cycling after a serious racing accident at the Buenos Aires Six Day in 1984. In 1987, he took over De Ver Cycles, a thriving bike shop in Streatham, South London.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.cyclorama.net/viewArticle.php?id=340| title=Profile: Maurice Burton| publisher=Bike Culture| accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
His son Germain Burton is also a racing cyclist who has represented Britain on an international level as a junior.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.britishcycling.org.uk/zuvvi/media/bc_files/gb_team/Germain_Burton_Biog.pdf| title=Germain Burton Bio| publisher=British Cycling| date=24 July 2013|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
