[[17th Wisconsin Volunteer Infantry Regiment]]

CATEGORIES: Wisconsin Civil War regiments, Irish-American culture in Wisconsin, Irish regiments of the United States Army

The 17th Regiment Wisconsin Volunteer Infantry was an infantry regiment that served in the Union Army during the American Civil War. It was organized in Madison, Wisconsin and mustered into federal service on March 15, 1862. The 17th Wisconsin fought in the Battle of Corinth, Grant's Central Mississippi Campaign, Battle of Port Gibson, Battle of Champion's Hill, Siege of Vicksburg, Atlanta Campaign, Carolinas Campaign, and the Battle of Bentonville.[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2012[/tpl] 
The regiment was mustered out on July 14, 1865 at Louisville, Kentucky.[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2012[/tpl]

==Casualties==

The 17th Wisconsin suffered 41 enlisted men killed in action or who later died of their wounds, plus another 228 enlisted men who died of disease, for a total of 269 fatalities.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.civilwararchive.com/Unreghst/unwiinf1.htm#17th|title=Union Regimental Histories: Wisconsin[/tpl][tpl]Clarify|date=August 2012[/tpl]
