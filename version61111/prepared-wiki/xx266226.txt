[[Rod DeHaven]]

CATEGORIES: 1966 births, Living people, American runners, American marathon runners, Athletes (track and field) at the 2000 Summer Olympics

Rod DeHaven (born September 21, 1966 in Palo Alto, California) is the current Head Men's and Women's Cross Country Coach at South Dakota State University as of 2011.[tpl]cite news |title=Adjusting on the Run |author=Spencer Chase  |url=http://www.sdsucollegian.com/2011/09/21/adjusting-on-the-run/ |newspaper=SDSU Collegian |date=September 21, 2011 |accessdate=September 26, 2011[/tpl]  SDSU is his alma mater where he holds several school records in track.South Dakota State Track & Field School Records, SDSU Athletics at GoJacks.com, retrieved February 14, 2012.
Before returning to SDSU, Rod spent time as a professional runner.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]  His most notable achievement is winning the 2000 Olympic Trials marathon and being the only athlete to represent the USA at the marathon at the 2000 Summer Olympics in Sydney.[tpl]cite news |title=DeHaven traveled long, unusual path to the winner's circle |author=Lori Shontz |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=hrNRAAAAIBAJ&sjid=gm8DAAAAIBAJ&pg=6936,6018374 |newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette |date=May 8, 2000 |accessdate=September 26, 2011[/tpl] DeHaven is also the course record holder for the Naples Half Marathon, with a 1:03:12 time set in 2000.[tpl]cite web|title=2000 Half Marathon Special Report|url=http://web.naplesnews.com/special/ndnhm/00/|accessdate=May 8, 2013[/tpl]
