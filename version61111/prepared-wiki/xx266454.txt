[[John Horsley Palmer]]

CATEGORIES: 1779 births, 1858 deaths, Governors of the Bank of England, People educated at Charterhouse School, Burials at Kensal Green Cemetery, Deputy Governors of the Bank of England

John Horsley Palmer (7 July 1779 - 7 February 1858) was an English banker and Governor of the Bank of England.
Palmer was the fourth son and seventh child of William Palmer  of Nazeing, Essex, and his wife Mary Horsley, daughter of John Horsley rector of Thorley, Hertfordshire, and sister of Dr. Samuel Horsley, bishop of St Asaph. His father was a London merchant.[tpl]cite DNB|wstitle=Palmer, George|volume=43[/tpl] He was educated at Charterhouse School where in 1794 he took part in the first school cricket match against Westminster School.
Palmer became a Director of the Bank of England in 1811, remaining until 1857. In 1820, he purchased Hurlingham House in Fulham.The Hurlingham Club He served as Deputy Governor of the bank from 1828 to 1830, and Governor from 1830 to 1833.J K Horsefield The Opinions of Horsley Palmer  Economica Vol 16 No 62 He extended the property at Hurlingham by six acres, and let it to the brother of the Duke of Wellington. He was a member of Political Economy Club and published several pamphlets including The Causes and Consequences of the Pressure Upon the Money-market .
Palmer died aged 78 and was buried in Catacomb B at Kensal Green Cemetery.The catacombs at Kensall Green Cemetery
Palmer married Elizabeth Belli, daughter of John Belli and Elizabeth Stuart Cockerell, on 16 November 1810.The peerage.com Her portrait was painted by Thomas Lawrence.Sothebys Portrait of Elizabeth, Mrs Horsley
