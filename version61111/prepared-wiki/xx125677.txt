[[Tom Ashbrook]]

CATEGORIES: 1956 births, Living people, American radio journalists, American radio reporters and correspondents, Nieman Fellows, NPR personalities, People from Bloomington, Illinois, People from Newton, Massachusetts, Yale University alumni

Tom Ashbrook is an American journalist and radio broadcaster.  He hosts the public radio call-in program On Point.

==Early life and education==

Born in 1956 on a farm in Bloomington, Illinois, he studied American history at Yale University and Gandhi's independence movement at Andhra University in India.http://www.onpointradio.org/about-on-point/tom-ashbrook

==Early career==

Ashbrook worked in Alaska as a surveyor and dynamiter of oil fields to help pay for college. After leaving for Asia, he produced English-dubbed kung fu films in Hong Kong[tpl]cite web|last=Soroff|first=Jonathan|title=Soroff On: Tom Ashbrook|url=http://www.improper.com/features/tom-ashbrook/|publisher=The Improper Bostonian|accessdate=17 November 2012[/tpl]  where he spent a decade as a foreign correspondent in India, Hong Kong, and Tokyo.  His first newspaper job was at the South China Morning Post.  During the late 1980s Ashbrook became foreign editor for The Boston Globe.
As a correspondent Ashbrook covered the "refugee exodus from Vietnam and the post-Mao opening of China, and has covered turmoil and shifting cultural and economic trends in the United States and around the world, from Somalia and Rwanda to Russia and the Balkans."[tpl]cite web|title=On Point Website|url=http://onpoint.wbur.org/about-on-point/tom-ashbrook[/tpl] 
Ashbrook was a Nieman Fellow at Harvard University during which time he decided to leave the Globe in 1996 and create an Internet startup with college friend Rolly Rouse.  This company eventually became homeportfolio.com.  Ashbrook wrote a book called The Leap: A Memoir of Love and Madness in the Internet Gold Rush about his experience in Internet entrepreneurship.[ref]

==On Point==

Ashbrook joined public radio following the September 11, 2001 Attacks, when he was enlisted by NPR and WBUR-Boston for special coverage.  He currently hosts the National Public Radio show On Point.
On Point is a two-hour radio program, airing five days per week, Monday through Friday.
Ashbrook oversees a staff of 10 who assist with research and show preparation - typically compiling information relating to the topics to be discussed on the following day's program for Ashbrook to take and read.[tpl]cite web|last=SOROFF|first=JONATHAN|title=Soroff On: Tom Ashbrook|url=http://www.improper.com/features/tom-ashbrook/|publisher=The Improper Bostonian[/tpl]
Senior On Point news analyst and former editor of The Atlantic Monthly, Jack Beatty is a regular guest and contributor on the program.

==Personal life==

Tom Ashbrook has a wife, a daughter, and two sons.  He lives in Newton, Massachusetts.
