[[Lenn Hannon]]

He held office from 1974 to 2001, switching from the Democratic to the Republican party in 1980.[tpl]Oregon Encyclopedia|hannon_lenn_1943_2010_[/tpl] He represented Oregon's 26th district, in southern Jackson County.[tpl]cite news|last=Mann|first=Damian|title=Lenn Hannon: 'He told it to you straight' |url=http://www.mailtribune.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20100403/NEWS/4030307|accessdate=18 April 2011|newspaper=Mail Tribune|date=3 April 2010[/tpl] His political career began when he defeated longtime Republican Lynn Newbry in 1974 by just 37 votes. He earned the nickname "Landslide Lenn" for squeaking by this and other votes.[tpl]cite news |title=A man of his word |author=Staff|url=http://www.mailtribune.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20100409/OPINION/4090313|newspaper=Mail Tribune|date=9 April 2010 |accessdate=27 October 2012[/tpl]
In late 2003, after retiring from the Senate, Hannon was appointed to the Oregon state parole board by Gov. Ted Kulongoski.
Hannon was born in Roseburg, Oregon and served in the U.S Army from 1961 to 1967. He attained the rank of Sergeant serving in the Oregon National Guard.[tpl]cite book |title=Who's Who In American Politics|publisher=R. R.
