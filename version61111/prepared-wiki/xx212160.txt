[[Jerry A. Moore, Jr.]]

CATEGORIES: 1918 births, Living people, Washington, D.C. City Council members

Jerry Alexander Moore, Jr. (born June 12, 1918), is a retired Baptist minister and former politician in Washington, D.C. He served as pastor of the Nineteenth Street Baptist Church for 50 years. In 1974, Moore was elected as a Republican to be an at-large member of the Council of the District of Columbia in the first election after home rule was established; he held that position for 10 years.
