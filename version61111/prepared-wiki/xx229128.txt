[[Tara Hunt]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Canadian technology writers, University of Calgary alumni, People from Saskatoon, Canadian women writers, Canadian bloggers, Writers from Montreal, 1973 births

Tara Hunt (born in Saskatoon, Saskatchewan, Canada) is an author, speaker and startup founder. She has been called a "pioneer in online marketing and one of the most respected authorities on online communities".[tpl]cite news|work=Omaha World-Herald|title=Omaha karaoke fans invited to tap their inner whuffie|url=http://www.omaha.com/article/20090721/LIVING/707219910/0/AP0408|first=Josefina|last=Loza|date=July 21, 2009|accessdate= January 11, 2010[/tpl]

==Biography==

Hunt obtained a degree in Communications and Cultural Studies at the University of Calgary. In 2002, she founded a small marketing brand called "Rogue Strategies." She moved, along with her business, to Toronto, Ontario before moving to San Francisco in 2005.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theglobeandmail.com/servlet/story/RTGAM.20080910.wcoworking10/BNStory/specialTravel/home|title=A place to lay your laptop|last=Azpiri|first=Jon|date=September 10, 2008|work=Toronto Globe and Mail|accessdate= November 4, 2008[/tpl] She co-founded Citizen Agency in San Francisco,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.randomhouse.com/author/84607/tara-hunt?sort=best_13wk_3month |title=Tara Hunt |deadurl=no |accessdate=February 4, 2013[/tpl] a now-defunct[tpl]cite web|title=Citizen Agency is on hiatus until further notice|url=http://citizenagency.com/blog/2010/06/19/citizen-agency-is-on-hiatus-until-further-notice/[/tpl] community-marketing consulting firm.
Hunt was hired by the San Francisco based visual search engine Riya to lead their marketing efforts.[tpl]cite web|url=http://be.sys-con.com/node/198788|title=Web 2.0 Company Riya Uploads 1 Million Photos In 2 Days After Launch|author=Web 2.0 News Desk|date=March 27, 2006|work=SYS-CON Publications|accessdate=November 4, 2008[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.seattlepi.com/business/246392_geeks29.html|title=Latest in tech trends: 24-hour brain raves|last=Cook|first=John|date=October 29, 2005|work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|accessdate= November 4, 2008[/tpl] In June 2006, Hunt coined a movement of "post-cluetrain" marketing called Pinko Marketing.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2006/11/05/BUG78M5OUA1.DTL|title=The people who populate Web 2.0|last=Fost|first=Dan|date=November 5, 2006|work=San Francisco Chronicle|page=F5|accessdate= November 4, 2008[/tpl] Pinko Marketing picked up where The Cluetrain Manifesto left off, changing the focus from company to consumer marketing to consumer-to-consumer marketing.
She was named one of the most influential women in tech by Fast Company in 2009, as she is an early leader of "user generated conferences" such as BarCamp[tpl]cite web|title=Women in Tech: The Evangelists 9 thought leaders who are changing our ideas about technology.|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/magazine/132/the-most-influential-women-in-technology-the-evangelists.html[/tpl] and is considered to be one of the founders of the co-working movement.[tpl]cite web|title=Leadership Lessons From Community Maven Tara Hunt: When Not To Listen To The Crowd|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/1810627/leadership-lessons-from-community-maven-tara-hunt-when-not-to-listen-to-the-crowd[/tpl]
Hunt was one of the lead bloggers on the popular Canadian online portal One Degree.[tpl]cite web|title=Tara Hunt|url=http://www.onedegree.ca/tara_hunt/|publisher=One Degree|accessdate=26 June 2011[/tpl]
She wrote "The Whuffie Factor", published in April 2009.[tpl]cite web|title=The Power of Social Networking|url=http://www.randomhouse.com/book/84596/the-whuffie-factor-by-tara-hunt[/tpl] The title of the book refers to the reputation-based currency of Cory Doctorow's science-fiction novel, 'Down and Out in the Magic Kingdom'. Along with the humorous metaphor implied by the title, the book gives practical guidance for online and social media marketing.[tpl]cite news|title=Saturday Reader: 'Whuffie Factor' shows how social networking is good for business |work=Arizona Daily Star|url=http://azstarnet.com/business/article_5dea893d-0c4d-57d0-a4c4-aaedb04178a8.html|first= Richard|last=Pachter|date=May 9, 2009| accessdate= January 11, 2010[/tpl] The Whuffie Factor has been translated into French and Portuguese. In May 2010, it was reprinted in paperback in May 2010 with the title The Power of Social Networking: Using the Whuffie Factor to Build Your Business .
Hunt speaks at a variety of conferences and events such as SXSW Interactive (2008, 2009, 2011),[tpl]cite web|title=Panel: Shopping as a Revolutionary Act?|url=http://schedule.sxsw.com/events/event_IAP5414|publisher=SXSW|accessdate=26 June 2011[/tpl] the MESH conference (2006) and TEDxConcordia (2011).[tpl]cite web|last=Hunt|first=Tara|title=The Unclear Path|url=http://tedxconcordia.com/talks/tara-hunt/|publisher=TEDxConcordia[/tpl]
In 2010, along with Co-Founders Jerome Paradis and Cassandra Girard, Hunt launched Buyosphere, formerly known as Shwowp, a social site which lets users organize and share buying trends with others.[tpl]cite news|last=Tsotsis|first=Alexia|title=Shwowp Wants To Change The Way You Shop|url=http://techcrunch.com/2010/09/28/shwowp/|accessdate=26 June 2011|newspaper=TechCrunch|date=Sep 28, 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=O'Dell|first=Jolie|title=In a World Without Tracking & Cookies, Can Online Commerce Succeed?|url=http://mashable.com/2011/05/10/buyosphere/|accessdate=26 June 2011|newspaper=Mashable|date=10 May 2011[/tpl]
Tara is currently Social Digital Leader at MSLGROUP (Toronto)
