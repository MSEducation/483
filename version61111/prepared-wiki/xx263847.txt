[[Ponyo, Lahe]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in Naga Self-Administered Zone, Lahe Township

 
 Ponyo is a village in Lahe Township, Naga Self-Administered Zone, in the Sagaing Region of northwestern Burma. It is located on the border with India and lies at the headwaters of the Namteik Kha river.[tpl]cite map|title=Google Maps|publisher=Google|accessdate=2 January 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite map|title=Bing Maps|publisher= Microsoft and Harris Corporation Earthstar Geographics LLC|accessdate=2 January 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-68273454.html|title=Human Sacrifice and Slavery in the "Unadministered" Areas of Upper Burma During the Colonial Era.|author=Menas, Gordon P.|publisher=Journal of Social Issues in Southeast Asia |date=October 1, 2000|accessdate=2 January 2010[/tpl]
The village is inhabited by peoples known as the Ponyo Nagars[tpl]Disambiguation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl], which have been dominant in other villages in the area.[tpl]cite book|last=Barua|first=Surendra Nath|title=Tribes of Indo-Burma border: a socio-cultural history of the inhabitants of the Patkai Range|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=OAZuAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=2 January 2011|year=1991|publisher=Mittal Publications|isbn=978-81-7099-308-7[/tpl]
Human sacrifice and headhunting has been documented in Ponyo and the headchief of the village has been quoted in the 1920s as saying "we take the heads of human beings because they are the only fit morsels for the Nat".
