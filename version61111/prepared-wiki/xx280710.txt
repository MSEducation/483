[[Polacca Airport]]

CATEGORIES: Airports in Arizona, Native American airports, Transportation in Navajo County, Arizona, Buildings and structures in Navajo County, Arizona

Polacca Airport 
 is a public use airport in Navajo County, Arizona, United States. The airport is owned by the Hopi Tribe. It is located three nautical miles (6 km) southwest of the central business district of Polacca, an unincorporated community on the Hopi Reservation.
This airport is included in the National Plan of Integrated Airport Systems for 2011–2015, which categorized it as a general aviation facility.

==Facilities and aircraft==

Polacca Airport covers an area of 50 acres (20 ha) at an elevation of 5,573 feet (1,699 m) above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 4/22 with an asphalt surface measuring 4,200 by 50 feet (1,280 x 15 m).
For the 12-month period ending April 17, 2010, the airport had 200 general aviation aircraft operations, an average of 16 per month.
