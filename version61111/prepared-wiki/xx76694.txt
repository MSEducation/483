Piper Lisa Perabo (Pear-a-bow; born October 31, 1976)[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tvguide.com/celebrities/piper-perabo/170483 |title=Piper Perabo News, Piper Perabo Bio and Photos |publisher=TVGuide.com |accessdate=April 30, 2012[/tpl] is an American stage, film and television actress. Since her breakthrough role in Coyote Ugly (2000), she has appeared in films such as Lost and Delirious (2001), Cheaper by the Dozen (2003), Cheaper by the Dozen 2 (2005), The Prestige (2006), Beverly Hills Chihuahua (2008), and Looper (2012). Since 2010, she has played Annie Walker on the USA Network series Covert Affairs, for which she has been nominated for a Golden Globe Award for Best Actress.

Perabo was first noticed a year before she graduated from university. She was in New York City, visiting her then-boyfriend, and accompanied him to an audition. Casting director Denise Fitzgerald spotted her and asked her to read for a part. She was not cast, but when Fitzgerald found out that she didn't have any representation, she made phone calls on Perabo's behalf and found her an agent.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/04/04/piper-perabo-covert-affairs-women-in-hollywood_n_3014977.html |title=Piper Perabo, 'Covert Affairs' Star, On The Biggest Misconception About Women In Hollywood |publisher=The Huffington Post |date=4 April 2013 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

After graduating, Perabo moved to New York, where she worked as a waitress.[tpl]cite web|url=http://piper-perabo.com/press/2010/11/gq-july-2000/ |title=Piper Pours a Pint |publisher=GQ |date=July 2000 |accessdate=6 April 2013 |author=Elizabeth Gilbert[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://blastmagazine.com/the-magazine/entertainment/movies/the-blast-interview-piper-perabo/ |title=The Blast Interview: Piper Perabo |publisher=Blast Magazine |date=1 March 2010 |accessdate=6 April 2013 |author=Melissa Kory[/tpl] She also studied acting at the La MaMa Experimental Theatre Club and had roles in various plays.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.broadway.com/buzz/6640/piper-perabo/ |title=Piper Perabo |publisher=Broadway.com |date=19 June 2008 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl] She was cast in her first feature film just a month after moving to the city, Marc Levin's comedy Whiteboyz. In 2000, she was cast in The Adventures of Rocky and Bullwinkle as FBI agent Karen Sympathy. Her next film role was in Coyote Ugly as Violet "Jersey" Sanford, for which she won an MTV Movie Award for Best Music Moment for "One Way or Another".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mtv.com/ontv/movieawards/2001/ |title=MTV Movie Awards |work=Mtv |date=June 2, 2001 |accessdate=April 30, 2012[/tpl]
After the success of Coyote Ugly, Perabo made a conscious decision to move away from blockbusters and focus on independent films.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.darkhorizons.com/features/892 |title=Exclusive Interview: Piper Perabo for "Slap Her, She's French" |publisher=DarkHorizons.com |date=29 August 2001 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl] In 2001, she starred in an independent Canadian drama called Lost and Delirious, playing a boarding school student who falls in love with a female classmate. The film was met with mixed reviews, but her performance was widely praised,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.murphysmoviereviews.net/Archive/lostdelirious.html |title=Lost and Delirious |publisher=murphysmoviereviews |date=21 December 2001 |accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl] with Loren King of the Chicago Tribune calling it her "breakout performance".[tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/2001-07-13/entertainment/0107130416_1_lea-pool-delirious-piper-perabo |title=`Lost' a welcome break from summer's light fare |publisher=Chicago Tribune |date=13 July 2001 |accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl] Entertainment Weekly[tpl]'s[/tpl] Owen Gleiberman called her "an actress of glittering ferocity" and her performance "a geyser of emotion".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,255489,00.html |title=Lost and Delirious (2001) |publisher=Entertainment Weekly |date=3 August 2001 |accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl] Jim Lane of the Sacramento News & Review said that "Perabo is a revelation, wild and fiery {–} it's a breakthrough performance, astonishing in its fervency",[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.newsreview.com/sacramento/lost-delirious/content?oid=8293 |title=Lost and Delirious |publisher=Sacramento News & Review |date=23 August 2001 |accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl] and Roger Ebert praised her performance for its sincerity and "wonderful abandon and conviction".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rogerebert.com/reviews/lost-and-delirious-2001 |title=LOST AND DELIRIOUS |publisher=RogerEbert.com |date=13 July 2001 |accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl]
The next year she starred as a French exchange student in the independent comedy Slap Her... She's French, which was shelved in North America for two years, then released under the new title She Gets What She Wants. The film was released under its original title in Europe. She had a role as the eldest Baker child, Nora, in Cheaper by the Dozen (2003), a role she reprised in the film's 2005 sequel. Her other films include The I Inside (2003), Perfect Opposites (2004), George and the Dragon (2004), The Cave (2005), Imagine Me & You (2005), Edison (2005), The Prestige (2006), and Beverly Hills Chihuahua (2008). She appeared as a nutritionist on the Fox show House, in an episode entitled "Resignation".
In 2008, Perabo made her Off-Broadway debut in the Neil LaBute play Reasons to Be Pretty.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.style.com/vogue/mensvogue/2009_April_Piper_Perabo/ |title=Staging Around |last=Antrim |first= Taylor |publisher=style.com |accessdate=July 3, 2009[/tpl] As the play's run was coming to an end, she read the script for the USA Network television spy series Covert Affairs: "I was reading movie scripts and I wasn't finding anything that was really speaking to me and my agent suggested that I read Covert Affairs script. And I hadn't thought about doing television, but when I read it, it kind of changed everything for me. She's such a powerful character, she's so smart, the action is so intense, and I really thought it would be fun to do."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.movieweb.com/news/piper-perabo-takes-us-inside-the-spy-world-of-covert-affairs |title=Piper Perabo Takes Us Inside the Spy World of Covert Affairs |author=Brian Gallagher |publisher=Movieweb.com |date=12 July 2010 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://ramblingsofatvwhore.raelee.com/2010/06/25/ca-piper-perabo-interview/index.html |title=In Between Scenes with Covert Affairs’ Piper Perabo |publisher=RTVW |author=Emma Brown |date=25 June 2010 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl] The following year, she was officially cast as CIA agent Annie Walker, the lead character in Covert Affairs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2009/07/02/development-update-thursday-july-2/8184/|title=Development Update: Thursday, July 2|date=July 2, 2009|work=The Futon Critic|accessdate=May 8, 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.usanetwork.com/series/covertaffairs/ |title=Covert Affairs |publisher=USA Network |accessdate=August 13, 2010[/tpl]
In August 2010, Perabo injured her knee filming an episode of Covert Affairs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://uk.eonline.com/news/piper_perabos_not_covert_knee_injury/196292|title=Piper Perabo's Not So Covert Knee Injury|date=August 19, 2010|author=Natalie Finn|publisher=E!Online|accessdate=April 13, 2012[/tpl] After the finale of the first season it was announced that the second season would begin in summer of 2011. For her work on Covert Affairs, she received a nomination for the 2010 Golden Globe Award for Best Actress – Television Series Drama.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goldenglobes.org/blog/2010/12/the-68th-annual-golden-globe-awards-nominations/ |title=The 68th Annual Golden Globe Awards NOMINATIONS | OFFICIAL WEBSITE of the HFPA and the GOLDEN GLOBE AWARDS |publisher=Goldenglobes.org |date=December 14, 2010 |accessdate=April 30, 2012[/tpl] Perabo broke her leg the day before shooting finished for the second season finale of Covert Affairs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cinemablend.com/pop/Actress-Piper-Perabo-Breaks-Leg-TV-Show-Stunt-29551.html |title=Actress Piper Perabo Breaks Leg In TV Show Stunt |publisher=Cinemablend.com |date=21 January 2011 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl]
In September 2012, Perabo had a supporting role in the time travel thriller Looper. In December 2012, she was cast as a recurring character on the sitcom Go On. Her character, Simone, is a former member of the support group that Matthew Perry's character is a member of, returning to the group and becoming a love interest of his. Perabo was originally scheduled to appear in three episodes, but her arc was increased to four.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/live-feed/covert-affairs-piper-perabo-go-on-396920 |title='Covert Affairs' Star Piper Perabo Books Arc on NBC's 'Go On' |publisher=Hollywood Reporter |date=3 December 2012 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl] Perabo next has a starring role in the thriller Endangered, scheduled for release in 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cinemablend.com/new/Piper-Perabo-Joins-Thomas-Jane-Billy-Bob-Thornton-Red-Machine-29162.html |title=Piper Perabo Joins Thomas Jane And Billy Bob Thornton In Red Machine |publisher=Cinemablend |date=1 February 2012 |accessdate=6 April 2013[/tpl]

==Personal life==
