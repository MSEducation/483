[[Warren Cooper]]

Warren Ernest Cooper, CNZM, JP (born 21 February 1933), is a former New Zealand politician. He was a National Party MP from 1975 to 1996, holding cabinet positions including Minister of Foreign Affairs and Minister of Defence.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10435251&pnum=0 |title=Jim Bolger - regrets and legacies |last=Trevett|first=Claire|date=21 April 2007|publisher=New Zealand Herald|accessdate=2009-07-04[/tpl] Cooper also twice served as Mayor of Queenstown, from 1968 to 1975 and 1995 to 2001.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.teara.govt.nz/Places/Otago/Otago/13/en |title=Government and politics - Otago Association and Otago province|last=McKinnon|first=Malcolm |publisher=Te Ara - the Encyclopedia of New Zealand|accessdate=2009-07-04[/tpl]

==Early life==

Cooper was born in Dunedin in 1933. He received his education at Musselborough School and King's High School. He later moved to Queenstown. He worked as a retailer, a painting, decorating and signwriting contractor, and a motel manager, and also became involved in local politics.[tpl]sfn|Gustafson|1986|p=305[/tpl]

==Member of Parliament==

He was first elected to Parliament in the 1975 elections as MP for Otago Central,[tpl]sfn|Wilson|1985|p=190[/tpl] defeating the newly elected Ian Quigley of the Labour Party.[tpl]sfn|Wilson|1985|p=228[/tpl] In the [tpl]NZ election link|1978[/tpl], he successfully contested the replacement electorate [tpl]NZ electorate link|Otago[/tpl].[tpl]sfn|Wilson|1985|p=190[/tpl]
Just after the 1978 election, his ministerial career started. He was Minister of Tourism (1978–1981), Minister of Regional Development (1978–1981), Postmaster-General (1980–1981), and Minister of Broadcasting (1981).[tpl]sfn|Wilson|1985|p=96[/tpl] When Brian Talboys retired from Parliament in 1981, Cooper was appointed to replace him as Minister of Foreign Affairs; he held this position until the government of Robert Muldoon was defeated in 1984.[tpl]sfn|Wilson|1985|p=96[/tpl] Later, in the government of Jim Bolger, Cooper served as Minister of Defence. Cooper remained in Parliament until the 1996 election, when he stepped aside in favour of Gavan Herlihy.
In the 1997 New Year Honours, Cooper was made a Companion of the New Zealand Order of Merit, for public services.New Year Honours List 1997. Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet. Retrieved 4 January 2013.

==Mayor==

Cooper was Mayor of Queenstown Borough Council from 1968 to 1975, and Mayor of Queenstown Lakes District Council from 1995 to 2001.[tpl]cite web|title=Past Mayors of the QLDC |url= http://www.qldc.govt.nz/past_mayors_of_the_qldc |publisher=Queenstown Lakes District Council|accessdate=15 November 2010[/tpl] He was involved in a public argument with actor Sam Neill in 2000, over development in Queenstown.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10127454 |title=Former mayor and Sam Neill make up |date=25 May 2005 |publisher=New Zealand Herald|accessdate=4 July 2009[/tpl]
