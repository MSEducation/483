[[Thinking about the immortality of the crab]]

CATEGORIES: Spanish idioms

thumb|WikiWorldThinking about the immortality of the crab ([tpl]lang-es|Pensar en la inmortalidad del cangrejo[/tpl]) is a Spanish idiom about daydreaming. The phrase is usually a humorous way of saying that one was not sitting idly, but engaged constructively in contemplation or letting one's mind wander.[tpl]cite web |url= http://forum.wordreference.com/showthread.php?t=245616  |title=La inmortalidad del cangrejo |publisher= WordReference.com Language Forums|accessdate=12 November 2013[/tpl]
The phrase is usually used to express that an individual was daydreaming, "When I have nothing to do I think about the immortality of the crab" ([tpl]lang|es|Cuando no tengo nada que hacer pienso en la inmortalidad del cangrejo[/tpl]). It is also used to wake someone from a daydream; "are you thinking about the immortality of the crab?" ("[tpl]lang|es|¿Estás pensando en la inmortalidad del cangrejo?[/tpl]")
