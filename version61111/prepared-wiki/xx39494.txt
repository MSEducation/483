[[Stinking badges]]

CATEGORIES: English phrases, Quotations from film and television, Badges

"Badges? We don't need no stinkin' badges!" is a well-known and widely (mis)quoted line from cinematic history.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.blockbuster.com/browse/catalog/personDetails/4404 |title=Alfonso Bedoya Movies |accessdate=2010-04-27 |author=Hal Erickson |year=2010 |work= |publisher=Blockbuster[/tpl] In 2005, it was chosen as #36 on the American Film Institute list, AFI's 100 Years...100 Movie Quotes.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.usatoday.com/life/movies/news/2005-06-21-top-movie-quotes_x.htm |title=AFI's 100 Years...100 Movie Quotes |accessdate=2010-04-27 |author= |year=2010 |work= |publisher=USA Today[/tpl] It comes from a line of dialogue from a 1927 novel, The Treasure of the Sierra Madre, and first appeared in film 21 years later in a movie of the same name. The line was parodied in a 1967 episode of the Monkees TV show called "It's a Nice Place to Visit" and in the Mel Brooks film Blazing Saddles in 1974 and was cited in many movies after that.
