[[Weston-under-Redcastle]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Shropshire, Civil parishes in Shropshire

Weston-under-Redcastle is a small village and civil parish in Shropshire, England. It lies 6.2 miles via road east of Wem within the North Shropshire District Counicil.
At one end of the village is the main entrance to Hawkstone Park hotel and golf courses, and at the other end is a wood.[tpl]cite web|title=North Shropshire Tourism|url=http://www.northshropshire.co.uk/weston-under-redcastle/|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl] 
Weston was mentioned in the Domesday Book, and part of the 1989 BBC adaptation of Prince Caspian was filmed in part of the village. It is situated under the south west edge of the Hawkstone Ridge.[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2012[/tpl]

==The village==

Weston-under-Redcastle’s Church the Chapel of Saint Luke dates back to 1791 in the Gothic style but with a Georgian tower.  Weston has a Village Hall, and Maynard’s Farm Shop mentioned in Rick Stein Food Heroes of Britain.[tpl]cite web|title=Maynardsfarm|url=http://www.maynardsfarm.co.uk/cookies_failed.asp|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl] Two bus stops lie opposite and adjacent to the church providing links to Wem and Bings Heath. 
Weston is home to the 18th century Hawkstone Park Hotel which has 2 golf courses, 2 restaurants, bars and award winning Hawkstone Park Follies.[tpl]cite web|title=Hawkstone Park Follies|url=http://www.booking.com/hotel/gb/hawkstone-park.html?aid=311076;label=hotel-33883-gb-QH6O_lEQkESNMEHhQBOGTQS4548720840;ws=&gclid=CLalg86O4K8CFU4lfAodJ1EWBQ|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl] Weston is also home to The Citadel guesthouse which dates back to the 1820s as well as the Windmill Cottage Guesthouse.[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2012[/tpl]

==History==

===Doomsday book===

Weston-under-Redcastle was pronounced as Westune in the 1086 Doomsday Book, it was included in the Hundred of Hodnet within the county of Shropshire. The county had a population of 21 households which was considered quite large for the time with total tax value of 3 geld units. In 1066 the value to the lord was £3 whilst in 1086 was £2. The village contained 3 villages, 9 smallholders, 8 slaves and 1 rider. Weston had 8 plough lands, 2 lords plough teams and one men’s plough team. In 1066 the lord was Edric the wild whilst in 1086 it was Ranulf Peverel.[tpl]cite web|title=domesdaymap Weston-Under-Redcastle|url=http://domesdaymap.co.uk/place/SJ5628/weston-under-redcastle/|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl] The fifteen Shropshire hundreds mentioned in the Domesday Survey were entirely rearranged in the 1100-1135 reign of King Henry I, with the Hundred of Hodnet being merged before 1203 into the Hundred of Bradford. When Bradford was divisioned, Weston was in North Bradford.

===Hawkstone Park===

Hawkstone park consists of 100 acres (40 ha) of follies and landscaped parkland grounds and rocky outcrops, based around the authentic Norman castle of Red Castle.The history of Hawkstone Park stretches back almost 800 years.In 1227 Henry de Audley, Sheriff of Shropshire and Staffordshire, built a sandstone castle on a natural outcrop of rock that was flanked on all sides by wide valleys.Hawkstone house was established by Richard Hill of Hawkstone (1655-1727) also known as the 'Great Hill'.[tpl]cite web|title=Hawkstone Park|url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawkstone_Park|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl]-->

====King Arthur====

In one of the caves of Hawkstone Park it is rumoured that it was the burial ground of King Arthur.[tpl]cite web|title=BBC Shropshire|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/local/shropshire/hi/people_and_places/newsid_8199000/8199441.stm|accessdate=2 May 2012[/tpl]

==Transport==

The village is situated closely to the east of the A49 linking it with nearby Shrewsbury. The nearest railway station is Wem taking a usual two hourly service southbound to Shrewsbury and northbound to Crewe. There are two bus stops in Weston connecting the village with Shrewsbury. The nearest airports are situated in Liverpool and Manchester.[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2012[/tpl]
