[[Verdens Gang]]

CATEGORIES: Newspapers published in Norway, Media in Oslo, Publications established in 1945, 1945 establishments in Norway

Verdens Gang (lit.: "The way of the world" or "The course of the world" or even "The gait of the world"), generally known under the abbreviation VG, is a Norwegian tabloid newspaper. The editor in chief is Torry Pedersen.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.vg.no/nyheter/innenriks/artikkel.php?artid=10021209 |language=Norwegian |title=Bernt Olufsen går av som VG-redaktør |publisher=VG |date=17 January 2011 |accessdate=3 November 2011[/tpl]

VG was established by members of the resistance movement shortly after the country was liberated from German occupation in 1945. Christian A. R. Christensen served as its first editor in chief and the first issue was published on 23 June 1945.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mediabase1.uib.no/krigslex/v/v1.html#verdens-gang |title=Verdens Gang |accessdate=2008-08-30 |work= |publisher=NorgesLexi.com |date= |language=Norwegian [/tpl]
VG is owned by the media conglomerate Schibsted, which also owns Norway’s largest newspaper, Aftenposten, as well as newspapers in Sweden and Estonia and shares in some of Norway’s larger regional newspapers.
Verdens Gang is not affiliated with any political party.

==VG Nett==

VG Nett is VG's news site online, operated through its wholly owned subsidiary VG Multimedia. VG Nett made a net operating profit of 40 percent in 2006, making it an unusually successful online media operation.Pfanner, Eric.
