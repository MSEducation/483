[[Owen Rand Kenan]]

CATEGORIES: 1804 births, 1887 deaths, Members of the North Carolina General Assembly

Owen Rand Kenan, (March 4, 1804 – March 3, 1887) was a North Carolina politician.http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/kempa-kenan.html He was born in Kenansville, North Carolina in Duplin County, and served in the state legislature from 1834 to 1838. He also represented the state during the Civil War in the First Confederate Congress from 1862 to 1864.Samuel A. She, Biographical History of North Carolina 3:253 He grew up in Kenansville and took over his father Thomas S Kenan’s Plantation Liberty Hall, which became a hot spot for gala affairs.http://www.ncmarkers.com/Results.aspx?k=Search&ct=btn He married Sarah Rebecca Graham, the daughter of a physician.Liberty Hall official archives Owen Rand Kenan was a successful and prosperous planter; he and Sarah had four children, three sons that served in the Civil War and a daughter Annie.1850 Census records, Duplin County, NC His wife Sarah died in 1871 and Owen died in March 1887; both he and Sarah's grave stones are located at the present day Liberty Hall Restoration. At Owen's death his unmarried daughter Annie Kenan became Mistress of Liberty Hall.
