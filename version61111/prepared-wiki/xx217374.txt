[[Wilsthorpe, Lincolnshire]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Lincolnshire, South Kesteven, Former civil parishes in Lincolnshire

Wilsthorpe is a village in the district of South Kesteven in the county of Lincolnshire, England. It is situated approximately [tpl]convert|6|mi|km|0[/tpl] north-east from Stamford and approximately [tpl]convert|4|mi|km|0[/tpl] south from Bourne.
Originally a Chapelry in Greatford parish,[tpl]cite web|title=Wilsthorpe|url=http://www.visionofbritain.org.uk/place/place_page.jsp?p_id=14085|work=Vision of Britain|publisher=University of Portsmouth|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] Wilsthorpe was created a civil parish in 1866 and lasted until 1931 when it was abolished to create the civil parish of Braceborough and Wilsthorpe.[tpl]cite web|title=Wilsthorpe civil parish|url=http://www.visionofbritain.org.uk/relationships.jsp?u_id=10463762&c_id=10001043|work=Vision of Britain|publisher=University of Portsmouth|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] 
Wilsthorpe is mentioned in Domesday Book of 1086 when it was listed as having 20 households, [tpl]convert|40|acre|km2|1[/tpl] of meadow, [tpl]convert|12|acre|km2|2[/tpl]  of woodland, and two mills.[tpl]cite web|title=Wilsthorpe|url=http://www.domesdaymap.co.uk/place/TF0913/wilsthorpe/|work=Domesday Map|publisher=Anna Powell-Smith/University of Hull|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] 
A possible Roman villa has been located as cropmarks to the south-east of the village,[tpl]cite web|title=Wilsthorpe Roman Villa|url=http://www.pastscape.org.uk/hob.aspx?hob_id=348304&sort=4&search=all&criteria=wilsthorpe%20lincolnshire&rational=q&recordsperpage=10|work=Pastscape|publisher=English Heritage|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] and King Street[tpl]where?|date=July 2012[/tpl] is a Roman road.[tpl]cite web|title=King Street Roman Road, Wilsthorpe|url=http://www.lincstothepast.com/Roman-coins-and-possible-Roman-station--Braceborough-and-Wilsthorpe/225594.record?pt=S|work=Lincs to the Past|publisher=Lincolnshire Archives|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] 
The church is a Grade II* listed building dedicated to Saint Faith. Built in 1715, it was restored and altered by James Fowler of Louth in 1869. In the sanctuary is a late-13th-century effigy of a knight in chain mail;[tpl]cite web|title=St Faiths, Wilsthorpe|url=http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1062675|work=National Heritage List for England|publisher=English Heritage|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] perhaps a Wake family member. Hereward the Wake was an Anglo-Saxon who led resistance to the Norman Conquest, and was born in or near Bourne.[tpl]cite web|title=Braceborough and Wilsthorpe|url=http://parishes.lincolnshire.gov.uk/BraceboroughandWilsthorpe/|work=Lincolnshire Parishes|publisher=Lincolnshire County Council|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] 
To the west of the village is the former railway station of Braceborough Spa Halt which was on the Essendine and Bourne Railway line. It opened in 1860 and closed in 1951. The old station house is now a private house.[tpl]cite web|title=Braceborough Spa Halt|url=http://www.pastscape.org.uk/hob.aspx?hob_id=506986&sort=4&search=all&criteria=wilsthorpe%20lincolnshire&rational=q&recordsperpage=60|work=Pastscape|publisher=English Heritage|accessdate=12 August 2011[/tpl] 
Nearby is the pumping station house from the old Peterborough Waterworks with its [tpl]convert|52|ft|m|1[/tpl] deep artesian well drilled during the late 19th century when it provided a million gallons of water each day to supply the cathedral city [tpl]convert|14|mi|km[/tpl] away.[tpl]citation needed|date=August 2011[/tpl]
