[[Kaizen]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese business terms, Lean manufacturing

[tpl]Nihongo|Kaizen|改善[/tpl], Japanese for "improvement" or "change for the best", refers to philosophy or practices that focus upon continuous improvement of processes in manufacturing, engineering, business management or any process. It has been applied in healthcare,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2010/07/11/business/11seattle.html|title=Factory Efficiency Comes to the Hospital|first =Julie |last = Weed | work = The New York Times | date=July 10, 2010[/tpl] psychotherapy,[tpl]cite web | year = 1992 | work = Psychiatric Bulletin | publisher = Royal College of Psychiatrists | pages = 334–336 | url=http://pb.rcpsych.org/cgi/reprint/16/6/334.pdf | title= Audit in psychotherapy: the concept of Kaizen | author=M. M. Feldman [/tpl] life-coaching, government, banking, and other industries. When used in the business sense and applied to the workplace, kaizen refers to activities that continually improve all functions, and involves all employees from the CEO to the assembly line workers. It also applies to processes, such as purchasing and logistics, that cross organizational boundaries into the supply chain.[ref]
