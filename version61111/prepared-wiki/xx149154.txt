[[St Barnabas' Church, Erdington]]

CATEGORIES: Church of England churches in Birmingham, West Midlands, Grade II listed churches, Grade II listed buildings in the West Midlands, Religious buildings completed in 1823, 19th-century Church of England church buildings, Anglican congregations established in the 19th century, Erdington, Commissioners' churches, Thomas Rickman buildings

St. Barnabas' Church is the parish church of Erdington, in the north of Birmingham, England.

==Background==

It is located on the High Street, in the main shopping centre area of Erdington, and is a Grade II listed building.[tpl]IoEentry|217292|Listing on Images of England[/tpl] The vicar is the Reverend Freda Evans, inducted on June 19, 2008.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.erdington-team-ministry.org.uk/stb/st_barnabas_main_page.htm|title=Erdington Team Ministry|date=2008-06-19|accessdate=2008-07-23[/tpl]

==Fire==

On the morning of 4 October 2007, the building was severely damaged by fire. West Midlands Fire Service were called to a fire at the church at 6am. Approximately 75 firefighters and 15 engines attended the fire.Erdington Team Ministry: Saint Barnabas The roof and all but one of the stained glass windows were completely destroyed, though the clock and bell tower and walls remain intact.BBC News story about fire The Rt Revd. David Urquhart, Bishop of Birmingham, stated he was determined to rebuild the church.Birmingham Mail on-line news and video footage It is believed that the fire was caused by arsonists."Arson suspected as blaze wrecks church".Birmingham Post. 5 October 2007

===Tower Bells===

St Barnabas has a ring of eight bells.

===Rebuild===

Rebuilding of the church started in January 2011 and completed in 2012. The building work was undertaken by Linfords who also performed the cleanup of the fire damage in late 2007 and early 2008.The building was re-dedicated in December 2012 by the Bishop of Birmingham.

==Churchyard==

The churchyard contains scattered war graves of 66 service personnel, 29 of World War I and 37 of World War II.  A Screen Wall memorial lists those buried in graves in the old ground which could not be individually marked.http://www.cwgc.org/find-a-cemetery/cemetery/ERDINGTON%20(ST.%20BARNABAS)%20CHURCHYARD CWGC Cemetery Report.
