[[Forget Me Not Farm]]

CATEGORIES: BBC children's television programmes, Television series with live action and animation

Forget Me Not Farm (also known as Forget-Me-Not Farm) is a BBC children's television programme which ran on BBC Two's children's BBC time slot in the United Kingdom during the 1990s.[tpl]cite web|title=Forget-Me-Not Farm|url=http://ftvdb.bfi.org.uk/sift/title/447298|work=Film & TV Database|publisher=British Film Institute|accessdate=1 April 2011[/tpl]

==Show storyline==

Set on Forget-Me-Not farm, the show included the characters Dandelion and Burdock the crows, Trundle the tractor, Portly the pig, Mrs. Mouse, and Scarecrow.[tpl]cite web|last=Amatt|first=Mike|title=forget-me-not farm - BBC TV 1990 - 2002|url=http://www.auos53.dsl.pipex.com/forgetmenotfarm.html|accessdate=1 April 2011[/tpl]  Each of the thirteen episodes concentrated on an aspect of the farming process.[tpl]cite web|last=Leach|first=Adam|title=Forget Me Not Farm Series|url=http://bbc.ask-adders.com/series/Forget-Me-Not-Farm/|work=Ask Adders BBC Listings|accessdate=1 April 2011[/tpl]  In December 2010 the show was rebroadcast on BBC Entertainment.
Each episode of the show centres around a particular theme or phenomenon. Episode themes include: food, machinery, doctors and vets, prize day (when the crows and the farmer visit the Royal Lancashire Agricultural Show and the scarecrow is sad because he has to stay at the farm), growing, milk, market, littler, maintenance, signs, rain, frost, and snow.
