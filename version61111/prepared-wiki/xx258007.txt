[[Ellen S. Pressman]]

CATEGORIES: American television producers, American television directors, Emmy Award winners, Women television directors, Living people, Place of birth missing (living people), Year of birth missing (living people)

Ellen S. Pressman is an American television director and producer.
She began her career as an associate producer on the series Hill Street Blues in 1983. Followed by a number of other television producing credits in L.A. Law for which she won her an Emmy Award as a part of the production team[tpl]cite news|title= L.A. Law' Wins Emmy as Best Drama Series |publisher= LA Times|date=1987-09-21|url= http://articles.latimes.com/1987-09-21/news/mn-5978_1_drama-series|accessdate=2010-12-31 | first=Lee | last=Margulies[/tpl] and Thirtysomething, where she made her directorial debut. 
Her other directorial credits include Sirens, Missing Persons, My So-Called Life, Party of Five, Arliss, Buffy the Vampire Slayer, Hyperion Bay, Charmed, Felcity, Time of Your Life,Ellen S. Pressman Filmography at Fandango.com Pasadena, American Dreams, Huff, Jack & Bobby, Everwood and Windfall, her last directorial credit.
