[[Hazuri Bagh Baradari]]

CATEGORIES: Architecture of Lahore, Buildings and structures in Lahore, Walled City of Lahore, Sikh architecture, Buildings and structures completed in 1818

The Hazuri Bagh Baradari ([tpl]lang-ur|[tpl]Nastaliq|حضوری باغ بارہ دری[/tpl][/tpl]) is a baradari of white marble located in the Hazuri Bagh of Lahore, Pakistan. It was built by Maharaja Ranjit Singh, Sikh ruler in 1818. Elegant carved marble pillars support delicate cusped arches. The central area, where Maharaja Ranjit Singh held court, has a mirrored ceiling. The pavilion consisted of two storeys until it was damaged by lightning in 1932.
