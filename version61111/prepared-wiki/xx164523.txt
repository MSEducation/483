Michael John Arnone (born September 10, 1932) is an American Republican Party politician, who served in the New Jersey General Assembly from 1989 to 2004, where he represented the 12th legislative district. 
Arnone and fellow-Republican running mate Clare Farragher were defeated in the 2003 elections by Democrats Michael J. Panter and Robert Lewis Morgan.Kinney, David. ==Legislative career and biography==

Arnone served in the Assembly on the Housing & Local Government Committee.Assemblyman Arnone's Legislative Website, New Jersey Legislature, from the Internet Archive, dated January 6, 2004. Accessed August 1, 2007.
Arnone was the Mayor of Red Bank, New Jersey from 1978to 1990, was the Red Bank Fire and Police Commissioner from 1970-1973 served on the Red Bank Council from 1969–1973 and as the borough's Zoning Board Chair in 1969. Arnone served in the United States Army from 1959–61, attaining the rank of Captain.
Arnone attended Seton Hall University and received his D.D.S. from the Temple University School of Dentistry.
