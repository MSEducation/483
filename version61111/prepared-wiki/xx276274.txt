[[Gaarriye]]

CATEGORIES: 1949 births, 2012 deaths, Somalian poets

Gaarriye (born Maxamed Xaashi Dhamac; 1949[tpl]Cite book | editors=B. W. Andrzejewski, S. Pilaszewicz, W. Tyloch | title=Literatures in African Languages: Theoretical Issues and Sample Surveys | page=394 | year=2009 | origyear=1985 | isbn=9780521256469 | publisher=Cambridge UP | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=gfH2A6AhZ7wC&lpg=PA394&dq=Gaarriye%201949&pg=PA393#v=snippet&q=Gaarriye&f=false | accessdate=1 October 2012[/tpl] – 30 September 2012) was a poet from Somalia.[tpl]cite web|last=Orwin|first=Martin|title=Maxamed Xaashi Dhamac 'Gaarriye'|url=http://www.poetrytranslation.org/poets/Maxamed_Xaashi_Dhamac_%27Gaarriye%27|publisher=Poetry Translation Centre|accessdate=17 September 2012[/tpl]  
Gaarriye was born in Hargeisa.  He was a member of the Somali National Movement.Didier Morin, Littérature et politique en Somalie, Centre d'étude d'Afrique noire, Institut d'études politiques de Bordeaux, 1997, p.32 He composed one of the best known Somali poems on the theme of reconciliation, "Hagarlaawe" (The Charitable).Rebuilding Somaliland:issues and possibilities, Red Sea Press, 2005, p. 141
Gaarriye died at a hospital in Norway on 30 September 2012.[tpl]cite web|title=The Great Somali Poet, Mohamed Hashi Dama (Gaarriye), Passes away in Norway|url=http://jubbalandnews.com/?p=8773|work=JubbalandNews|publisher=Jubbalandnews.com|accessdate=1 October 2012|date=1 October 2012[/tpl]
