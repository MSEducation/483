David Nii Addy (born 21 February 1990 in Prampram) is a Ghanaian professional footballer, who plays for Primeira Liga club Vitória Guimarães.

==Club career==

===Randers===

In Summer 2008, Addy made his first international move to Europe, from a Ghanaian Club – International Allies FC being signed by Danish SAS Ligaen club Randers FC.
At Randers FC,Addy made his debut on 19 October against Vejle gaining 7 minutes.Spiel: Randers FC vs Vejle – Danish SAS-Ligaen – ESPN Soccernet
In the following season, Addy was part of the Randers FC squad that went to compete in the UEFA Europa League.He also made his debut in the UEFA Europa League, in the first qualifying round against Linfield FC from Northern Ireland on 2 July 2009.Spiel: Randers FC vs Linfield FC – Europa League – Randers FC Official Website

===Porto===

On 1 February 2010 the Ghanaian U-20 champion joined Portuguese top club FC Porto from SAS Ligaen side Randers FC for €800,000, signing a three ½ year contract.Randers FC sælger David Batty til den portugisiske storklub FC PortoDavid Addy på plads i FC Porto – dr.dk/Sporten/Fodbold/Superliga
The young Ghanaian U-20 made his debut for FC Porto on 14 April 2010 in a 4–0 Victory against Rio Ave FC.

===Académica de Coimbra===

In July 2010, he joined Académica de Coimbra on a season-long loan from Portuguese giants FC Porto.

===Panetolikos===

On 31 August 2011, he joined a newly promoted Greek Superleague side Panetolikos F.C. on a season-long loan from FC Porto.

===Vitoria S.C===

On 30 September 2012, Addy  signed a two year deal for Vitoria S.C..http://www.soccerfame.com/transfer/15151/david-addy In May 2013 Addy helped Vitoria S.C. win the 2012/13 Taca de Portugalhttp://www.ghanasoccernet.com/david-addy-wins-portuguese-cup-with-vitoria-guimaraes-after-ghana-snub/ and qualified for the 2013/14 UEFA Europa League.

==International career==

===Ghana U-20===

Addy earned his first Black Satellites call-up after an impressive performance with the Local Black Stars in 2008, making his debut in January 2008 in a match against Angola.
In 2009 Addy was part of the squad that won the 2009 African Youth Championship. His success continued in October 2009 as he also took part in the 2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup held in Egypt which the team went on to win, making them the first African Nation to have ever won the 2009 FIFA U-20 World Cup.

==Personal life==

In summer 2010,Addy got engaged to German-Ghanaian Economics graduate Dyen Gabriel. The couple first met in 2008 through a mutual friend. In June 2013, Addy married his fiancée Dyen Gabriel in a private ceremony in Accra.http://ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-defender-david-addy-marries-girlfriend-dyen-gabriel/
