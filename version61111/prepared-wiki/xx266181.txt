[[Marcus Wight]]

CATEGORIES: 1969 births, Living people, People from Kensington, Sportspeople from London, Alumni of the University of Exeter, Alumni of the University of Cambridge, English cricketers, Cambridge University cricketers, Gloucestershire cricketers

Robert Marcus Wight (born 12 September 1969) is a former English cricketer. Wight was a right-handed batsman who bowled right-arm off break. He was born in Kensington, London and later undertook further education at Exeter University, before attending Cambridge University.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/all_teams.html|title=Teams Marcus Wight played for|publisher=ESPNcricinfo|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
While studying at Cambridge, Wight made his first-class debut for Cambridge University Cricket Club against Leicestershire in 1992. He made eight further first-class appearances for the university in that season, the last of which came against Oxford University at Lord's.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/First-Class_Matches.html|title=First-Class Matches played by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] In his nine first-class appearances for the university, he scored 366 runs at an average of 28.15, with a high score of 62 not out.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/f_Batting_by_Team.html|title=First-class Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] This score, which was one of two fifties he made for the university, came against Oxford University.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/56/56077.html|title=First-Class Matches played by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] With the ball, he took 18 wickets at a bowling average of 37.61, with best figures of 3/65.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/f_Bowling_by_Team.html|title=First-class Bowling For Each Team by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] He also made a single first-class appearance for a combined Oxford and Cambridge Universities team against the touring Pakistanis in that season.
Following his studies, he joined Gloucestershire for the 1993 season, making his debut for the county against Northamptonshire in the 1993 County Championship. He made eight further first-class appearances for the county, the last of which came against the touring New Zealanders. In his nine first-class appearances for Gloucestershire, he scored a total of 205 runs at an average of 17.08, with a high score of 54. This score, which was his only first-class half century for Gloucestershire, came against Leicestershire in 1993.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/57/57457.html|title=Leicestershire v Gloucestershire, 1993 County Championship|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] In his debut season with Gloucestershire, he also made his List A debut against Kent in the AXA Equity & Law League. Wight made eleven further List A appearances, the last of which came against Hampshire in the 1994 AXA Equity & Law League.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/List_A_Matches.html|title=List A Matches played by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] In his twelve List A appearances, he scored 51 runs at an average of 7.28, with a high score of 18.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/a_Batting_by_Team.html|title=List A Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] With the ball, he took 9 wickets at an average of 31.00, with best figures of 2/28.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/11/11424/a_Bowling_by_Team.html|title=List A Bowling For Each Team by Marcus Wight|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] He left Gloucestershire at the end of the 1994 season.
