Ivan Simon Cary Elwes ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|ɛ|l|w|ɪ|s[/tpl]; born 26 October 1962),[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.upi.com/Odd_News/2008/10/26/The_almanac/UPI-34121225006200|title=The Almanac|publisher=UPI|date=26 October 2008|accessdate=29 July 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|title=World Almanac|work=Kent County Daily Times|date=26 October 2010|quote=Today's Birthdays: Cary Elwes[/tpl] known professionally as Cary Elwes, is an English actor and voice actor.
He acted in off-Broadway plays during college and moved to the United States in the early 1980s. He is known for his roles as Westley in The Princess Bride, Arthur Holmwood in Francis Ford Coppola's Bram Stoker's Dracula, Robin Hood in Robin Hood: Men in Tights, Garrett in Quest for Camelot, and Dr. Lawrence Gordon in Saw and Saw 3D: The Final Chapter. 
He appeared in box office hits such as Days of Thunder, Hot Shots!, Twister, Liar, Liar and New Year's Eve.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/people/chart/?id=caryelwes.htm|title=Cary Elwes Movie Box Office Results|work=Box Office Mojo|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl] He has had recurring roles in television series such as  The X-Files playing Brad Follmer and Psych playing Pierre Despereaux.

==Early life and education==

Elwes was born in Westminster, London, the third and youngest son of portrait-painter Dominick Elwes[tpl]cite journal|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20105004,00.html|title=A Hero to the Hilt|last=Cerio|first=Gregory|volume=43|work=People|date=6 February 1995|accessdate=26 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/61z6f7VsK|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no|issue=5[/tpl] and interior designer and socialite Tessa Kennedy, who is of Serbian, Croatian, Anglo-Irish, and Scottish ancestry. His paternal grandfather was painter Simon Elwes,[tpl]cite journal|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-3979412.html|first=Jae-Ha|last=Kim|title=British actor takes turn as American in 'Glory'|page=25|work=Chicago Sun-Times|date=12 January 1990[/tpl] whose father was the diplomat and tenor Gervase Elwes (1866–1921).[tpl]cite book|title=Loose Canon: A Portrait of Brian Brindley|url=http://books.google.com/?id=At3vy0eh2LUC&pg=PA134&dq=Gervase+Elwes+Simon#v=onepage&q=Gervase%20Elwes%20Simon&f=false|last=Thompson|first=Damian|authorlink=Damian Thompson|page=134|publisher=Continuum|isbn=0-8264-7418-7|date=22 June 2004[/tpl] His brothers are Damian Elwes, an artist, and Cassian Elwes, a producer and agent. He was the stepson of Elliott Kastner, an American film producer. One of Elwes' ancestors is John Elwes, who is alleged in some sources to have been the inspiration for Ebenezer Scrooge in A Christmas Carol (1843)[tpl]cite web|url=http://thecia.com.au/reviews/c/images/christmas-carol-production-notes.doc|title=Disney's A Christmas Carol Production Notes|page=6|format=152KB .DOC file; HTML version|publisher=Cinematic Intelligence Agency|date=21 October 2009|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5tLrhTKfT|archivedate=9 October 2010|deadurl=no[/tpl] (Elwes played five roles in the 2009 film adaptation of the novel).
He was brought up as a Roman Catholic and was an altar boy at Westminster Cathedral, although he did not attend denominational schools, unlike most of the men on his father's side of the family, including his own father.[tpl]cite web|last=Guthrie|first=Marisa|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/entertainment/2005/12/04/2005-12-04_the_spirit_moved_him.html|title=The spirit moved him|work=Daily News|quote=Elwes, who grew up a Catholic...|date=4 December 2005|accessdate=13 September 2010|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/61zzNYuVF|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] Elwes attended Harrow School in London and then the London Academy of Music and Dramatic Art.[tpl]cite journal|title=Dr Gordon is back in Saw – Cary Elwes's character from the first Saw film is back by popular demand|work=The Straits Times|date=5 January 2011[/tpl] He discussed some of this background in an interview while he was filming the 2005 CBS television film, Pope John Paul II, in which Elwes played the young priest Karol Wojtyła, many years before he was elected pontiff in 1978.
His parents divorced when he was four years old, and in 1975 when Elwes was thirteen his father committed suicide.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/property/4810656/Inside-story-Stewarts-Grove.html|title=Inside story: Stewart's Grove|author=Roger Wilkes|date=9 September 2000|work=The Telegraph|accessdate=23 April 2012[/tpl] In 1981, he returned to the United States to study acting at Sarah Lawrence College in Bronxville, New York.[tpl]cite journal|title=The 'Robin' Who Has Sherwood Forest Chuckling|last=Tam Jr|first=Henry|work=San Francisco Chronicle|page=C6|date=13 August 1993[/tpl] While living in New York, Elwes studied acting at both the Actors Studio and the Lee Strasberg Theatre and Film Institute.
Elwes worked as a production assistant on the films Octopussy and Superman, where he worked for a week assigned to Marlon Brando. When Elwes introduced himself, Brando told him he was lying and that his (Elwes') name was actually Rocky.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Q2yqE_QYanU|title=Mark & Brian w/Cary Elwes on Marlon Brando|publisher=YouTube|date=8 May 2008|accessdate=23 June 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

===Film===

Elwes made his acting debut in 1984 with Marek Kanievska's film Another Country. He played James Harcourt, a young and sentimental homosexual student from an English boarding school. He went on to play Guilford Dudley in the British film Lady Jane, co-starring Helena Bonham Carter. He was cast as a stable boy turned swashbuckler Westley in Rob Reiner's fantasy-comedy The Princess Bride, based on the novel of the same name by William Goldman. It was a modest box office success,[tpl]cite journal|title='The Princess Bride' Turns 20|journal=The Palm Beach Post|page=6J|last=Gray Streeter|first=Leslie|date=9 December 2007|quote=was a modest hit. But it became a raging cult classic after being released on video and shown on cable.[/tpl] but received critical acclaim, earning a score of 96% on the review aggregation website Rotten Tomatoes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/princess_bride|title=Prince Bride (1987)|work=Rotten Tomatoes|accessdate=September 2011 27[/tpl] Since being released on home video and television it has become a cult classic. In an interview around the film's DVD release in 2001, Elwes said, "The studio didn't know how to sell it[tpl]spaced ndash[/tpl]as an adventure, fantasy, comedy or love story, it had to rely on word of mouth". He also acknowledged the film's cult following saying, "Many people tell me they have it in their video collection, it's a family film but also a cult film in a way, being passed down to other generations".[tpl]cite journal|title='Bride' basks in glow of video|journal=Houston Chronicle|last=Westbrook|first=Bruce|date=6 September 2001[/tpl]
He continued working steadily, varying between dramatic roles, as in The Bride (1985) with Sting and Jennifer Beals, to the Academy Award-winning Glory (1989), and comedic roles, as in Hot Shots! (1991). In 1993, he starred as Robin Hood in Mel Brooks's comedy, Robin Hood: Men in Tights. Elwes also appeared in such films as Francis Coppola's adaptation of Bram Stoker's Dracula, The Crush, Rudyard Kipling's The Jungle Book, Twister, Liar Liar, Cradle Will Rock, The Cat's Meow and Kiss the Girls.[tpl]citation needed|date=March 2014[/tpl]

He made an uncredited appearance as Sam Green, the man who introduced Andy Warhol to Edie Sedgwick, in the 2006 film Factory Girl. In 2007, he appeared in Garry Marshall's Georgia Rule with Jane Fonda. ===Television===

In 1996, Elwes made his first television appearance as David Lookner on the sitcom Seinfeld. In 1998, he played astronaut Michael Collins in the Golden Globe Award-winning HBO miniseries From the Earth To the Moon. In 1999, he guest starred as Dr. John York in an episode of the television series The Outer Limits. He earned two Golden Satellite Award nominations for his performances in the television films The Pentagon Wars and Uprising. Elwes had a recurring role in the final season (from 2001 to 2002) of The X-Files as FBI Assistant Director Brad Follmer. In 2004, he played serial killer Ted Bundy in the A&E Network television film The Riverman, which was based on the book The Riverman: Ted Bundy and I Hunt for the Green River Killer written by Robert D. Keppel. In 2005, Elwes played the young Pope John Paul II in the CBS television film Pope John Paul II.
In 2007, he made a guest appearance on the Law & Order: Special Victims Unit episode "Dependent" as a Mafia lawyer. In 2009, he played the role of Pierre Despereaux, an international art thief, in the fourth season premiere of Psych.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dreadcentral.com/news/35164/first-two-teaser-clips-psych-9|title=First Two Teaser Clips Psych: 9|work=DreadCentral|publisher=CraveOnline|date=2 January 2010|accessdate=26 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/620BPhle9|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] In 2010, he returned to Psych, reprising his role in the second half of the fifth season, and again in the show's sixth season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tvline.com/2011/07/psych-spoiler-video-season-6-james-roday-dule-hill|title=Psych First Look: The Season 6 Scene Everyone Will Be Talking About! Plus, Musical Update!|last=Slezak|first=Michael|work=TVLine |date=21 July 2011|accessdate=27 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/620HTJJfL|archivedate=27 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] In March 2011, Elwes was selected to appear as Henry Detmer in the pilot episode of NBC's Wonder Woman.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.deadline.com/2011/03/cary-elwes-joins-nbcs-wonder-woman/|title=Cary Elwes Joins NBC's 'Wonder Woman'|last=Andreeva|first=Nellie|work=Deadline.com|publisher=PMC|date=4 March 2011|accessdate=26 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/620B3NYSM|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] However, the show was never picked up for a series.[tpl]cite web|url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2011/05/12/nbc-rejects-wonder-woman/|title=NBC rejects 'Wonder Woman'|last= Rice|first=Lynette|work=Entertainment Weekly|date=12 May 2011|accessdate=26 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/620B7uApH|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] Elwes recently completed work on the USA Network pilot, Horizon, produced by Gale Anne Hurd.

===Voiceovers===

Elwes' voice-over work includes the narrator in James Patterson's audio book The Jester,[tpl]cite book|title=The Jester|last=Paterson|first=James|authorlink=James Patterson|last2=Gross|first2=Andrew|authorlink2=Andrew Gross|isbn=1-58621-535-3 |publisher=Hachette Audio| edition=Abridged|date=1 March 2003[/tpl] as well as characters in film and television animations such as Quest for Camelot, Pinky and The Brain, Batman Beyond, and the English versions of the Studio Ghibli films Porco Rosso, Whisper of the Heart and The Cat Returns. For the 2004 video game The Bard's Tale, he served as scriptwriter, improviser, and voice actor of the main character The Bard. In 2009, he appeared in Robert Zemeckis's motion capture adaption of Charles Dickens' A Christmas Carol. In 2011 he also appeared in Steven Spielberg's motion capture adaptation of Belgian artist Hergé's popular comic strip The Adventures of Tintin. That same year Elwes performed the English voice over for Indian film, Delhi Safari.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bollywoodhungama.com/news/2011/05/30/15833/index.html|title=Delhi Safari to feature voiceover by Vanessa Williams and Jason Alexander|work=Bollywood Hungama|date=30 May 2011|accessdate=26 September 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/620CPeGXA|archivedate=26 September 2011|deadurl=no[/tpl] The following year Elwes portrayed the part of Gremlin Gus in Disney's video game, Epic Mickey 2: The Power of Two.
