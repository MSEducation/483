==Career==

Perry is the former co-Director of the Washington, D.C., London, and Beirut-based Conflicts Forum,[tpl]cite web|url=http://conflictsforum.org/ |title=Conflicts Forum |publisher=Conflicts Forum |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] which specializes in engaging with Islamist movements in the Levant in dialogue with the West. Perry served as co-Director for over five years.  A detailed five-part series on this experience was published by the Asia Times in March and in July 2006.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.atimes.com/atimes/others/howtolose.html |title=Asia Times Online :: Islamism Facsism Terrorism |publisher=Atimes.com |date=September 11, 2001 |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] Perry served as an unofficial advisor to PLO Chairman and Palestinian President Yasser Arafat from 1989 to 2004.Jonathan Neumann, "Our Defenders at the CIA," Jewish Ideas Daily, Jan. 18, 2012.
Perry has appeared on numerous national and international televised forums. He is a frequent guest commentator and expert on Al-Jazeera television, has appeared regularly on CNN’s The International Hour and on Special Assignment. Perry’s work on the CIA’s program to destabilize the Saddam Hussein regime, originally published by Regardies magazine, was the basis for an award winning  BBC Panorama production of “The Intelligence War Against Iraq”. His son, Cal Perry, was a CNN Mideast correspondent and is currently al-Jazeera's Jerusalem correspondent.
Perry’s books have met with critical acclaim from Kirkus Reviews, The Washington Post, The New York Review of Books, The New York Times, The New Yorker, and many other publications. He has served as editor of Washington D.C.’s City Paper,[tpl]cite web|last=Shott |first=Chris |url=http://www.washingtoncitypaper.com/ |title=D.C. Arts, News, Food and Living |publisher=Washington City Paper |date=October 15, 2011 |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] and the Veteran, the largest circulation newspaper for veterans.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vva.org/veteran.html |title=The VVA Veteran |publisher=Vva.org |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl]  Perry was also Washington correspondent for The Palestine Report,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.palestinereport.ps/ |title=Palestine Report – Palestinian news in English |publisher=Palestinereport.ps |date=September 29, 2005 |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] and is currently a senior fellow at the Jerusalem Media and Communications Center.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jmcc.org/ |title=Jerusalem Media and Communications Centre – Breaking News & Opinion on the Israeli-Palestinian Conflict |publisher=JMCC.org |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl]
Perry was a senior foreign policy analyst for Vietnam Veterans of America Foundation (VVAF).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.veteransforamerica.org/ |title=Message From Bobby Muller |publisher=Veterans For America |date=February 19, 2010 |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] VVAF, a leading international humanitarian organization, co-founded the International Campaign to Ban Landmines, the 1997 Nobel Peace Prize recipient.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.icbl.org/ |title=International Campaign to Ban Landmines |publisher=ICBL |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] Perry served as the political director for the VVAF’s Campaign for a Landmine Free World.
As of 2011, he was at work on his ninth book, MacArthur At War, to be published by Basic Books in 2012.

==Awards==

Perry is the recipient of both the 1995 National Jewish Book Award for his second book, A Fire In Zion,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.harpers.org/archive/2007/06/hbc-90000294 |title=Six Questions for Mark Perry on the Conflict in Palestine—By Ken Silverstein (Harper's Magazine) |publisher=Harpers.org |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl] as well as the University of California’s prestigious “Censored Award”.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.projectcensored.org/ |title=Project Censored |publisher=Project Censored |accessdate=October 21, 2011[/tpl]
