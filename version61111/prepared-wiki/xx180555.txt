[[Lee Zeldin]]

Lee M. Zeldin (born January 30, 1980) is a Republican member of the New York State Senate, representing the 3rd district.

==Early life, education, and military service==

Zeldin was raised in Shirley, New York, and graduated from William Floyd High School in 1998. He received a B.A. (cum laude) in political science from the State University of New York at Albany in 2001 and earned his Juris Doctorate from Albany Law School in May 2003.
Upon graduation from law school, he received an Army ROTC commission as a Second Lieutenant, assigned to the Military Intelligence Corps of the United States Army. He became a member of the New York State Bar in January 2004 at the age of 23. In 2006, Zeldin was deployed to Iraq with an infantry battalion of fellow paratroopers from the Army's elite 82nd Airborne Division. In 2007, he transitioned from active duty to the Army Reserves where he currently serves with the rank of Major. In 2008, Zeldin started a law practice in Smithtown, New York. He is a member of the Veterans of Foreign Wars, the American Legion, and the Jewish War Veterans.

==New York Senate==

===Elections===

Zeldin ran for the New York Senate's 3rd district to challenge Democratic incumbent Brian X. Foley.  Zeldin defeated Foley 57%-43%.http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=682898 In 2012, he won re-election to a second term, defeating Democrat Francis Genco 56%-44%.http://www.ourcampaigns.com/RaceDetail.html?RaceID=773927

===Tenure===

In the State Senate, Zeldin was successful in leading the effort to repeal the MTA Payroll Tax for 80 percent of employers, eliminate the Salt Water Fishing License Fee and create the PFC Joseph Dwyer Program for Veterans with Post Traumatic Stress Disorder (PTSD) and Traumatic Brain Injury (TBI). Zeldin also wrote the law that protects veterans and their families from protests at military burials.
