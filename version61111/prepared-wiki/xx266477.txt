[[Lex loci protectionis]]

CATEGORIES: Conflict of laws, Latin legal terms

Lex loci protectionis (Latin: "the law of the place where the protection is claimed") is a choice of law rule applied to cases concerning the infringement of intellectual property (IP) rights, such as copyrights or patents. 
It stipulates that the law applied to such cases is the law of the locus protectionis, that is, the law of the country in which legal protection for the intellectual property is claimed.[tpl]cite book|last=Rosen|first=Jan|title=Individualism and Collectiveness in Intellectual Property Law|year=2012|publisher=Edward Elgar Publishing|isbn=9780857938978|page=57|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=EuCx7saXmjwC&pg=PA57[/tpl]  Consequently, the law of the country where the intellectual property was created or registered is not applied.
