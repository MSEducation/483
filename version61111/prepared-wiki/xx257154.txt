[[Rango Bapuji Gupte]]

CATEGORIES: Indian independence activists from Maharashtra

Rango Bāpuji Gupte ([tpl]lang-mr|रंगो बापूजी गुप्ते[/tpl]) (??? – Missing July 5, 1857) was an Indian diplomat, freedom fighter, and a revolutionary.
The rulers at Satara were one of last independent branches of the Maratha Empire. After the British dissolved the state in 1839, ruler Pratapsinh sent Rango Bapuji Gupte to England to defend the case in front of the British Parliament. He stayed there for 14 years without much success.[ref]
</ref>
After returning to India, he became "one of the masterminds behind the 1857 revolt", known as the Indian Rebellion of 1857.[ref]
