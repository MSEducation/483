[[Minuscule 541]]

CATEGORIES: Greek New Testament minuscules, 15th-century biblical manuscripts, University of Michigan

Minuscule 541 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), 554 (in the Scrivener's numbering), ε 400 (in Soden's numbering),[tpl]cite book|last=Gregory|first=Caspar René|authorlink=Caspar René Gregory|title=Die griechischen Handschriften des Neuen Testament|url=http://www.archive.org/stream/diegriechischen00greggoog#page/n77/mode/2up|year=1908|publisher=J. C. Hinrichs'sche Buchhandlung|location=Leipzig|page=67[/tpl] is a Greek minuscule manuscript of the New Testament, on a parchment. Palaeographically it has been assigned to the 15th century.[ref]
</ref> 
The manuscript was adapted for liturgical use. It is incomplete.

==Description==

The codex contains some parts of the Gospel of Matthew and Gospel of Mark, on 49 parchment leaves (size [tpl]×|21|14.2[/tpl]), with numerous lacunae. The text is written in one column per page, 21 lines per page. It is neatly written, but has survived in bad condition. The iota adscript does not occur.[ref]
