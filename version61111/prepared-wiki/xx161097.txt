David Johnson Foster (June 27, 1857 â€“ March 21, 1912) was an American lawyer and politician. He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Biography==

Foster was born in Barnet, Vermont. He attended the public schools in Barnet and graduated from St. Johnsbury Academy in 1876 and 
Dartmouth College in Hanover, New Hampshire in 1880.
He studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1883. He began the practice of law in Burlington, Vermont. Foster served as Chittenden County State's Attorney from 1886 until 1890.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=127676||title =DAVID J FOSTER|publisher= Our Campaigns|accessdate= December 21,  2012[/tpl] He served as a member of the Vermont State Senate from 1892 until 1894.[tpl]cite web|url= http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/foster.html||title =Foster, David Johnson (1857-1912)|publisher= The Political Graveyard|accessdate= December 21, 2012[/tpl] Foster was the first president of the Young Men's Republican Club of Vermont, which was organized in 1894.[tpl]cite book|last=Forbes|first=Charles S.|title=The Vermonter, Volumes 4-5|year=1898|publisher=Charles S. He served as chairman of the board of railroad commissioners from 1898 until 1900,[tpl]cite book|last=Vermont. General Assembly|title=Journal of the Senate of Vermont|year=1899|publisher=Vermont. Foster was elected as a Republican candidate to the Fifty-seventh and to the five succeeding Congresses, serving from March 4, 1901 until his death in Washington, D.C. on March 21, 1912.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/david_foster/404249||title =Rep. David Foster|publisher= Govtrack.us|accessdate= December 21,  2012[/tpl] He served as chairman of the Committee on Expenditures in the Department of Commerce and Labor during the Fifty-ninth, Sixtieth and Sixty-first Congresses. He served as the chairman on the Committee on Foreign Affairs in the Sixty-first Congress.
Foster was interred in Lakeview Cemetery in Burlington, Vermont.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=22661||title =  David Johnson Foster|publisher= Find A Grave|accessdate= December 21, 2012[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Foster married Mabel M. Allen Fosterin 1883. They had three children together, Mabel Foster, Mathilde Foster and Mildred Foster.[tpl]cite book|last=Vermont Historical Society|title=Vermont history|year=1918|publisher=Vermont Historical Society|page=122|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fvEVAAAAIAAJ&pg=PA122&lpg=PA122&dq=david+johnson+foster++Barnet,+Vermont&source=bl&ots=uytdZcO8Wb&sig=nRX-V9hQ_Yy4b-O8Y8MSvQaA-tc&hl=en&sa=X&ei=ofrVUKTAMuKU2QWAx4H4Bg&ved=0CGQQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=david%20johnson%20foster%20%20Barnet%2C%20Vermont&f=false[/tpl]
