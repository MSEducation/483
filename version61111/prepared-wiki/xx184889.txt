[[Nicolas Coutelot]]

CATEGORIES: French male tennis players, People from Hauts-de-Seine, Sportspeople from Strasbourg, 1977 births, Living people

Nicolas Coutelot (born 9 February 1977 in Strasbourg, France) is a retired professional male tennis player from France who reached a career-high singles ranking of World No. 87 in May 2002. He also failed a drugs test in 2004 and was suspended for two months.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.itftennis.com/antidoping/news/newsarticle.asp?articleid=15923|title=Decision in the case of Nicolas Coutelot|publisher=itf.com|date=10 August 2004[/tpl]

==1996–2004==

At the 2001 French Open Coutelot caused a major upset when he knocked out former World No. 1 Marcelo Rios in straight sets in the second round.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.sportvox.fr/article.php3?id_article=18948|publisher=SportVox|title=Arrêt sur images: Roland-Garros 2001|date=2 December 2008|author=Morglen|language=French[/tpl] But he lost a five set battle to Wayne Arthurs in the next round. In 2002 at the French Open Coutelot caused controversy when he claimed that Juan Carlos Ferrero had fained injury, after he lost their second round encounter in the final set. However, Ferrero really was injured as he took a tumble whilst practicing against Tommy Robredo.[tpl]cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2002/may/31/sports/sp-french31|title=Coutelot accuses Ferrero of faking|publisher=LA Times|date=31 May 2002[/tpl] At the US Open Coutelot made his best appearance by reaching the first round before losing in straight sets to defending champion and World No. 1 Lleyton Hewitt.http://airwolf.lmtonline.com/sports/archive/082802/pageb1.pdf
Coutelot again reached the third round of the French Open when he bundled out David Nalbandian in five sets, despite being two sets to love up, before winning the final set.http://www.breakingnews.ie/print/hewitt-forced-to-dig-deep-again-100790.html
His career-high singles ranking was World No. 87.

==Drugs Suspension==

Coutelot was suspended for two months in 2004 after he tested positive for cannabis when attempting to qualify for the Movistar Open.
