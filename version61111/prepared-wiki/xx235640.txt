[[Hélène Binet]]

CATEGORIES: Swiss photographers, 1959 births, Living people, People from Ticino, Swiss people of French descent, Swiss emigrants to the United Kingdom, Women photographers, Architectural photographers, Fellows of the Royal Institute of British Architects

Hélène Binet (born 1959) is a Swiss-French architectural photographer based in London, who is also one of the leading architectural photographers in the world.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/culture/2002/apr/15/artsfeatures|title=The dream life of buildings|last=Glancey|first=Jonathan|date=15 April 2002|work=The Guardian|quote= ..Hélène Binet is one of the world's finest architectural photographers..|accessdate=9 May 2011[/tpl] She is  most known for her work with architects Daniel Libeskind, Peter Zumthor and Zaha Hadid, and has published books on works of several architects.

==Biopgraphy==

Hélène Binet was born in 1959 in Sorengo, Switzerland to Swiss and French parents.[tpl]cite web |title=About Helene |url=http://www.helenebinet.com/about.html |date= |publisher=Website |accessdate=May 22, 2-13[/tpl] She studied photography at the Istituto Europeo di Design in Rome, where she grew up. She worked as a photographer at the Grand Théâtre de Genève, an opera house in Geneva, Switzerland, where she photographed various performances for two years, before turning to architectural photography, encouraged by architect, Daniel Libeskind, who believed, " she exposes architecture’s achievements, strength, pathos and fragility." [tpl]cite web |title=Ten questions for photographer Hélène Binet |url=http://uk.phaidon.com/agenda/photography/articles/2012/december/06/ten-questions-for-photographer-helene-binet/ |quote=..one of the world’s leading architectural photographers. ..|date=December 6, 2012 |publisher=Phaidon |accessdate=May 22, 2013[/tpl]
She has worked with Raoul Bunschoten, Caruso St John, David Chipperfield, Tony Fretton, Zaha Hadid, Zvi Hecker, John Hejduk, Coop Himmelb(l)au, Daniel Libeskind, Peter Cook  Sauerbruch Hutton, Peter Zumthor, Josef Paul Kleihues and many others, besides publishing books on buildings by Le Corbusier, Alvar Aalto, Sigurd Lewerentz, and Dimitris Pikionis .[tpl]cite web |title=Hélène Binet is a master at capturing the world's most beautiful buildings|author=James Cartwright|url=http://www.itsnicethat.com/articles/helene-binet |date=15 January 2013 |publisher=Its Nice That |accessdate=May 22, 2013[/tpl]
She was awarded the Redaksjonell Fotografi from Visuelt, 1997, Wissenschaft fur Leben Scholarship, 2004 from the Olympus Europa Stiftung, Forum AID Award 2006, and Honorary Fellowship of Royal Institute of British Architects, 2008 [tpl]cite web |title=RIBA Honorary Fellowships 2008:  Hélène Binet |url=http://www.architecture.com/Awards/RIBAHonoraryFellowships/Archive/HonoraryFellowships2008.aspx#.UZxOtqI3CHg |date= |publisher= |accessdate=[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Binet lives on Osea Island in the River Blackwater Estuary in Essex. [tpl]citation needed|date=May 2013[/tpl] She is married to Raoul Bunschoten, and the couple lives in London with their two children.
