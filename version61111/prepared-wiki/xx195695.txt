[[Old Medical College]]

CATEGORIES: School buildings completed in 1834, National Historic Landmarks in Augusta, Georgia, Historic American Buildings Survey in Georgia (U.S. state), Georgia Health Sciences University, Visitor attractions in Augusta, Georgia

Old Medical College Building, also known as Augusta Garden Center, was the original Medical Academy of Georgia (now Georgia Regents University) building, founded in 1828. It became vacant when Richmond Academy moved to new quarters.    
It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1996.[tpl]Cite document|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Historic Landmark Nomination: Old Medical College / Medical College of Georgia|32 KB[/tpl]|date=September 23, 1994 |author=Todd Savitt and Anne Floyd |publisher=National Park Service|postscript=[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying  photos, exterior and interior, from 1973 and 1995|32 KB[/tpl]
