[[P. D. Kode]]

CATEGORIES: Indian judges, Living people, 1953 births

Pramod Dattaram Kode (born 13 February 1953) usually referred to as PD Kode, is Additional Judge of Bombay High Court. Prior to that, he was the designated judge of the TADA court that dealt with the 1993 Bombay Bombings case. The case is the longest criminal case in India's history. Judge Kode allowed some of the accused to go on the Haj pilgrimage. He took no leave from March 1996 until June 2007. He is noted for not missing court sessions when he broke his arm after slipping in his bathroom, nor when his parents died.[tpl]cite news|title=What makes Kode Hero No 1|url=http://www.dnaindia.com/mumbai/report_what-makes-kode-hero-no-1_1113184|accessdate=28 August 2012|newspaper=DNA|date=Aug 2, 2007|author=Anshika Misra[/tpl] Kode is a Hindi film enthusiast and is fond of computer games.[tpl]cite news|title=Meet Judge Kode: Movie buff, gamer, numerologist|url=http://content.ibnlive.in.com/article/01-Aug-2007india/meet-judge-kode-movie-buff-gamer-numerologist-46042-3.html|accessdate=28 August 2012|newspaper=IBN Live|date=Aug 1, 2007|author=Toral Varia|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6AFT4EGVS|archivedate=28 August 2012[/tpl]
He was elevated to the Bombay High Court as an Additional Judge on 10 February 2009.[tpl]cite web|title=Profile - Justice PD Kode at Bombay HC|url=http://bombayhighcourt.nic.in/libweb/judges/KodePD.html|publisher=Bombay High Court|accessdate=28 August 2012|quote="Justice Pramod Dattaram Kode, B.Sc.(Hons.), LL.B. - Born on February 13, 1953. He worked as City Civil & Additional Sessions Judge at City Civil Sessions Court, Bombay from April 15, 1991, Designated Judge under TADA for Greater Bombay from October 11, 1993, Presiding Officer, Designated Court under TADA for Bombay Blast Cases, Greater Bombay at Arthur Road Prison from March 29, 1996.  He was appointed as an Additional Judge of the Bombay High Court on February 10, 2009."[/tpl]
