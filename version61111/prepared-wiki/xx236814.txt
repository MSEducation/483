[[Park Hyo-Ji]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, South Korean taekwondo practitioners, 1988 births

Park Hyo-Ji (Hangul: 박효지, born October 23, 1988 in Incheon) is a South Korean female taekwondo practitioner.

==Career==

In 2007, she was selected as a member of the South Korean national taekwondo team and competed in the 2007 World Taekwondo Championships, but lost to Charlotte Craig of the United States in Round of 32.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.taekwondotimes.com/index.php?PAGE_ID=301|title=2008 U.S. Olympic Taekwondo Storylines|publisher=Taekwondo Times|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]
In July 2009, Park won the gold medal in finweight (-47 kg) at the Summer Universiade in Belgrade, Serbia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ncta-usa.com/2009SWUGSummary.html|title=2009 Summer World University Games Summary|publisher=National Collegiate Taekwondo Association|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]  On October 17, she won the gold medal in finweight (-46 kg) at the 2009 World Taekwondo Championships in Copenhagen, Denmark.

==Doping test controversy==

In December 2009, Park refused to take the random doping test requested by the WTF, at the South Korean national championships in Gimje. As a result, the WTF imposed a two years' suspension on Park for missing the drug test.
