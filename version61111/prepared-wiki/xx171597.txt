[[Tonina fluviatilis]]

CATEGORIES: Aquatic plants, Eriocaulaceae

Tonina fluviatilis is a  plant found in South American, primarily in the Guianas (Guyana, Suriname, French Guiana) where it is widespread. It is the sole species in the monotypic genus Tonina.
One use of the plant is for medicinal purposes. The plant is ground up and mixed with a bark to make a liquid in which to bathe nursing infants. It is thought to give the infant strength.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mnh.si.edu/biodiversity/bdg/medicinal/MedPlantsGui2.pdf|title=Medicinal Plants of the Guianas|publisher=|accessdate=2007-08-03[/tpl]
It is also used as an aquarium plant.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.albanyaquarium.com/menu_aquaticplants.htm|title=Albany Aquarium Plant list|publisher=Albany Aquarium|accessdate=2007-08-03|archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20070629095923/http://www.albanyaquarium.com/menu_aquaticplants.htm |archivedate = 2007-06-29[/tpl] The shape of its leaf is pleasing and its color makes a good contrast with other plants when it is planted in small groups. However, it is difficult to grow as an aquarium plant.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.aquaticcommunity.com/tr/Toninafluviatilis.php|title=Tonina fluviatilis|publisher=|accessdate=2007-08-03[/tpl]
