[[Richard Stengel]]

CATEGORIES: 1955 births, Living people, Alumni of Christ Church, Oxford, American magazine editors, American Rhodes Scholars, News editors, Princeton Tigers men's basketball players, Time (magazine) people

Richard Allan "Rick" Stengel (born May 1955http://adage.com/article/media/time-names-16th-managing-editor-richard-stengel/109256/) is an American editor, journalist and author. He is famed for being Time magazine's 16th managing editor.[tpl]cite news |title=Richard Stengel Is Chosen to Be Top Editor at Time |author=Katharine Q. Seelye |newspaper=New York Times |date=18 May 2006 |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/05/18/business/media/18time.html?_r=1 |accessdate=3 November 2010[/tpl] While best known for his work for Time, he has written a number of books[tpl]cite news |title=Journalist Paints a South African Reality |author=Anne Marie Welsh |newspaper=San Diego Union-Tribune |date=20 May 1990[/tpl] including a collaboration with Nelson Mandela on Mandela's autobiography.[tpl]cite news |title=Ghost Writer Hired To Speed Way Of Mandela Story |author=David Beresford |newspaper=The Guardian (London)|date=13 November 1992[/tpl] Prior to taking up his role as managing editor of Time in 2006, Stengel was the president and chief executive officer of the National Constitution Center.[tpl]cite news |title=Paper: Time magazine editor to be new National Constitution Center president  |author=Associated Press |newspaper=Associated Press |date=2 February 2004[/tpl]
On September 12, 2013 Stengel announced that he would be stepping down from his role as Time's managing editor  in order to serve as Under Secretary of State for Public Diplomacy and Public Affairs for President Barack Obama's cabinet.http://www.politico.com/story/2013/09/richard-stengel-leaving-time-state-department-96732.html?hp=f1

==Early life and education==

Stengel was born and brought up in New York. He attended Princeton University and played on the Princeton Tigers basketball team as part of the 1975 National Invitation Tournament.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.timewarner.com/corp/newsroom/pr/0,20812,1194937,00.html |title=Richard Stengel Named Managing Editor of Time |date=17 May 2006 |work=Timewarner.com |accessdate=3 November 2010 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20060524022158/http://www.timewarner.com/corp/newsroom/pr/0,20812,1194937,00.html |archivedate=May 24, 2006[/tpl] He graduated magna cum laude in 1977. After college, he won a Rhodes Scholarship and  studied English and history at Christ Church, Oxford.

==Career==

===Early career===

Stengel joined Time in 1981 and contributed to the magazine through the early and mid-1980s, including articles on South Africa, which he also covered for Rolling Stone magazine. He became a senior writer and essayist for Time, covering both the 1988 and 1996 presidential campaigns.
While working for Time, Stengel also wrote for The New Yorker, The New Republic, Spy, and the New York Times and appeared on television as a commentator, even contributing to Indecision '92, the 1992 Comedy Central coverage of the Democratic Convention in New York.[tpl]cite news |title=Unconventional Wisdom; Comedy Central on Air This Week With 'Indecision '92' |author=Craig Winneker |newspaper=Roll Call |date=13 July 1992[/tpl] Using his experiences as a journalist as a basis, in 1998 Stengel taught a course at Princeton on "Politics and the Press". He was one of the original on-air contributors for MSNBC.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.richardstengel.com/bio.html |title=Author's Bio |author=Richard Stengel |date= |work=richardstengel.com |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20050213172915/richardstengel.com/bio.html |archivedate=13 February 2005 |accessdate=24 November 2010[/tpl]
Stengel left Time in 1999, to become a senior advisor and chief speechwriter for Bill Bradley who ran unsuccessfully for the Democratic nomination for the 2000 presidential election.

===Time.com===

Stengel returned to Time in 2000 and took on the role of managing editor of Time.com. As announced by Time Inc. in May 2000, Stengel replaced Richard Duncan in the role and took on the responsibilities of overseeing news coverage and editorial content.[tpl]cite news |title=Time Inc. Leafs Through Top Exex |author=K.D. Shirkani |newspaper=Daily Variety |date=11 May 2000 |url= |accessdate=[/tpl] He later held several other roles at Time, including a period as national editor of the magazine.

===National Constitution Center===

Stengel left his role as national editor of Time in February 2004[tpl]cite news |title=Time National Editor Quits for Museum Job |author=Keith J. Kelly |newspaper=The New York Post |date=3 February 2004[/tpl] to become the president and CEO of the National Constitution Center, a museum and education center in Philadelphia on 1 March 2004. He succeeded Joe Torsella (who resigned to seek a seat in the U.S. House from the 13th district) in the position, with the role of raising the center's profile, adding to its endowment, and increasing the number of visitors. At the Constitution Center, Stengel was responsible for starting the Peter Jennings Institute, offering constitutional training for journalists;[tpl]cite web |url=http://constitutioncenter.org/jennings/bios/stengel.html |title=Richard Stengel |work=ConstitutionCenter.org |publisher= |accessdate=1 December 2010[/tpl] Constitution High, a charter school for students interested in history and government; summer teacher institutes; and brought the Liberty Medal to the organization.[tpl]cite news |title=Liberty Medal nears its independence |author=Joseph A. Slobodzian |newspaper=The Philadelphia Inquirer |date=12 May 2006[/tpl]

==Managing editor of Time==

In 2006, Stengel once again returned to Time, this time as managing editor of the magazine. The appointment was announced on 17 May 2006 by the Editor in Chief of Time Inc., John Huey, and he officially entered the role on 15 June 2006 as the 16th managing editor of the magazine, which was in its 83rd year at the time. In his role as managing editor, Stengel oversees Time magazine, which is one of the largest magazines worldwide, and Time.com, as well as Time Books, and Time for Kids.[tpl]cite news |title=Time's Managing Editor Stengel to Deliver Bullion Lecture on April 21 |author= |newspaper=Targeted News Service |date=9 April 2008[/tpl]
His first major initiative was to change the magazine's newsstand date to Friday, starting in early 2007.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iwantmedia.com/people/people61.html |title=Richard Stengel: 'All the Rules Are Being Remade' |author=Patrick Phillips |date=28 August 2006 |work=I Want Media.com |accessdate=3 November 2010[/tpl] Following this, Stengel implemented an ambitious graphic redesign[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2007/03/12/business/media/12time.html |title=With Redesign of Time, Sentences Run Forward |author=Katharine Q. Seelye |date=12 March 2007 |work=The New York Times |publisher= |accessdate=5 December 2010[/tpl] and changes in the magazine's content, stating that he wanted the magazine to be more selective and to represent "knowledge" rather than "undigested information." He increased reporting on war and politics, giving Time a more focused editorial profile. In his first year as managing editor, Stengel selected "You" as Times "Person of the Year", which was the subject of much media coverage and debate.[tpl]cite web |url=http://nymag.com/news/features/28976/ |title=The Time of Their Lives |author=Joe Hagan |date=4 March 2007 |work= |publisher=New York Magazine |accessdate=3 November 2010[/tpl] In 2010, Time chose another social media-oriented "Person of the Year", Facebook founder Mark Zuckerberg.[tpl]cite news |title=Person of the Year 2010: Mark Zuckerberg |author=Lev Grossman |url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2036683_2037183,00.html |periodical=Time |date=15 December 2010 |accessdate=15 December 2010[/tpl]
In 2008, Stengel approved the changing of Time's emblematic red border for only the second time since its adoption. The border was changed to green for a special issue focused on the environment.[tpl]cite news |title=Why We're Going Green |author=Richard Stengel |magazine=Time |date=17 April 2008 |url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,1731899,00.html |accessdate=[/tpl] The cover, which included an altered version of Joe Rosenthal's iconic Raising the Flag on Iwo Jima photograph—substituting a tree for the American flag—was criticized by some veterans groups. ==National Service Movement==

In September 2007, Stengel wrote a Time cover story called "The Case For National Service" in which he argued that Americans needed to redouble their efforts to get involved in community service and volunteerism, and that the presidential candidates needed to make the issue a top priority in the 2008 presidential campaign.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.time.com/time/specials/2007/article/0,28804,1657256_1657317,00.html  |title=The Case For National Service |author=Richard Stengel |date=30 August 2007 |work=Time.com |publisher=Time Inc. |accessdate=5 November 2010[/tpl] Through this essay, he became involved with national service groups Be the Change, City Year, Civic Enterprises, and others to form ServiceNation, a coalition of more than 100 organizations dedicated to promoting national service and volunteerism.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bethechangeinc.org/pages/the-vision/ |title=Vision |work=Be The Change Inc.org |publisher=ServiceNation |accessdate=5 November 2010[/tpl]
ServiceNation announced that it had secured both U.S. Presidential candidates to participate in Presidential Forum on National Service at Columbia University on 11 September 2008.[tpl]cite news |title=John McCain, Barack Obama slated for 9/11 public service forum |author=Adam Lisberg |newspaper=NY Daily News |date=21 August 2008 |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/news/politics/2008/08/21/2008-08-21_john_mccain_barack_obama_slated_for_911_.html |accessdate=5 November 2010[/tpl] Stengel served as co-moderator of the forum, along with PBS journalist Judy Woodruff, and both Senators Barack Obama and John McCain answered questions in front of a live audience at Columbia University about their plans for national service.[tpl]cite news |title=The Spirit of Public Service |newspaper=New York Times |date=12 September 2008 |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/09/13/opinion/13sat3.html?_r=1&oref=slogin |accessdate=5 November 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mediabistro.com/tvnewser/politics/cable_nets_to_carry_presidential_forum_94146.asp?c=rss |title=Cable Nets to Carry Presidential Forum |author=Chris Ariens |date=10 September 2008 |work=MediaBistro |publisher=WebMediaBrands |accessdate=5 November 2010[/tpl]

==Books==

Stengel has authored several books including January Sun: One Day, Three Lives, A South African Town, a non-fiction work about the lives of three men in rural South Africa, published in 1990 and You're Too Kind: A Brief History of Flattery a popular history of flattery, published in 2000.[tpl]cite news |title=Book Review: "You're Too Kind: A Brief History Of Flattery" |author=Jonathan Yardley |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=11 June 2000[/tpl] Mandela's Way: Fifteen Lessons on Life, Love and Courage was released in March 2010 and is based on Stengel's personal interactions with Nelson Mandela.[tpl]cite web |url=http://washingtonscene.thehill.com/party-events-pictures/archive/3235-book-party-for-time-editor-richard-stengel |title=Book party for Time Editor Richard Stengel |author=Christina Wilkie |date=March 2010 |work=The Hill |publisher=Capitol Hill Publishing |accessdate=3 November 2010[/tpl] The book has drawn praise from President Bill Clinton, Deepak Chopra, and Harvard's Henry Louis Gates, Jr. In 2012, Stengel edited and wrote the lead essay for the book The Constitution: The Essential User’s Guide, which explored the relevance of the U.S. Constitution in modern day events.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dcmilitary.com/article/20121108/NEWS12/711089973/0/SEARCH |author= Youssef Aboul-Enein |title=The Constitution: The Essential User’s Guide |date=8 November 2012 |work=DCMilitary.com |accessdate=22 May 2013[/tpl]
The book that Stengel is best known for is his collaboration with Nelson Mandela on Mandela's autobiography, Long Walk to Freedom. In 1992 he signed a ghostwriting deal with publishers Little, Brown to work on the book, having first been cleared by the African National Congress as a suitable author. The book was published in 1995, and was praised by the Financial Times, which stated: "Their collaboration produced surely one of the great autobiographies of the 20th century".[tpl]cite news |title=Mandela's magic |author=Alec Russell |newspaper=Financial Times |date=24 July 2010 |url=http://www.ft.com/cms/s/2/9813073e-95e8-11df-bbb4-00144feab49a.html#axzz16yAi0H00 |accessdate=2 December 2010[/tpl] Stengel later served as co-producer of the 1996 documentary film Mandela, which was nominated for an Academy Award.

==Personal life==

Stengel is married to Mary Pfaff, a native of South Africa. They have two sons. The couple met while Stengel was in South Africa working on Nelson Mandela's autobiography, and Mandela was godfather to their oldest son, Gabriel.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jose-antonio-vargas/mandelas-way-richard-sten_b_518140.html |title=Richard Stengel On Mandela: Neither 'Terrorist' Nor Mother Teresa |author=Jose Antonio Vargas |date=30 March 2010 |work=The Huffington Post |accessdate=3 November 2010[/tpl]
