[[Josh Baker]]

Joshua Stevenson Baker (born December 25, 1986[tpl]cite web|title=Josh Baker|url=http://www.bluehens.com/sportsinfo/football/roster09-baker.html|publisher=Northwest Missouri State University Athletics|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6A6qpynw6|archivedate=22 August 2012|accessdate=22 August 2012[/tpl]) is an American football fullback and tight end for the Tampa Bay Buccaneers of the National Football League (NFL). He played college football at Northwest Missouri State.  He played high school football at Western Branch High School.

==College career==

Baker began his collegiate career at the University of Delaware in 2005. Although among the team's top scout team players, Baker did not participate in a formal match-up that year.[tpl]cite web|title=Josh Baker|url=http://www.bluehens.com/sportsinfo/football/roster09-baker.html|publisher=University of Delaware Athletics|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6A6pKFknX|archivedate=22 August 2012|accessdate=22 August 2012[/tpl] In 2006, he was named the Blue Hens' third string tight end appearing in ten games in a back-up role primarily on running plays and special teams. Baker recorded one catch for no yards and had a 14 yard kickoff return. In 2007, Baker was named the second string tight end and ranked sixth on the team in receiving, totaling 20 catches for 344 yards and 2 touchdowns. In 2008, Baker was suspended from the team for a year after violating the school's three strike policy which included infractions such as underage drinking and having a lit candle in his dorm room.[tpl]cite news|title=Josh Baker's 'long, crazy journey' to Jets|url=http://www.metro.us/newyork/sports/article/1145679--josh-baker-s-long-crazy-journey-to-jets|work=Metro New York|author=Dyer, Kristian|date=15 June 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6A6pmv9ky|archivedate=22 August 2012|accessdate=22 August 2012[/tpl]
Baker went to live with his father in Waco, Texas where he attended Collin County Community College and worked at an OfficeMax earning $9.00 an hour, which went toward his community college fees. He also worked with a professional trainer to stay in shape.[tpl]cite web|title=Long shots: Undrafted and undeterred|url=http://espn.go.com/new-york/nfl/story/_/id/6899483/new-york-jets-undrafted-rookies-try-beat-odds-make-roster|publisher=ESPN New York|author=Cimini, Rich|date=29 August 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/62HUvLodt|archivedate=8 October 2011|accessdate=22 August 2012[/tpl] Delaware retained Baker's athletic scholarship and he returned for his redshirt senior season in 2009. Baker tore multiple knee ligaments in the first quarter of the Blue Hens' first game of the year, ending his season.
Baker was denied a medical redshirt and transferred to Division II Northwest Missouri State. Baker had 66 catches for 838 yards and a touchdown, and 3 rushing touchdowns for Missouri in 2010 and was named to the Associated Press' Little All-America Team.[tpl]cite web|title=Three Bearcats earn AP All-America honors|url=http://www.nwmissouri.edu/sports/football/2010/pressreleases/10fb1217a.htm|publisher=Northwest Missouri State University Athletics|date=17 December 2010|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6A6qRb63w|archivedate=22 August 2012|accessdate=22 August 2012[/tpl]
