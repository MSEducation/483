[[Llanrhaeadr-ym-Mochnant]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Powys

Llanrhaeadr-ym-Mochnant is a village in Powys, Wales. Until 1974, Llanrhaeadr ym Mochnant was split into two civil parishes, the northern parish being in historic Denbighshire and the southern parish in historic Montgomeryshire. The divide continued between 1974 and 1996, with the former Denbighshire parish being placed in Clwyd and former Montgomeryshire parish in Powys. However, in 1996 both parts of Llanrhaeadr ym Mochnant were united within the county of Powys.
In the 2001 Census it had a population of 1,470,UK Census – 2001. Neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk. Retrieved on 2013-02-05. of whom 55% were Welsh-speaking.UK Census – 2001. Neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk. Retrieved on 2013-02-05.

==Parish church==

The Norman church of St Dogfan is a grade II* listed building, restored between 1879 and 1882. The parish is best known for being former parish of William Morgan, then a vicar who first translated the Old Testament into Welsh in the 1580s and later rose to become a Bishop at Llandaff Cathedral and St. Asaph.William-Morgan. Encyclopaedia Britannica. Retrieved on 2013-02-05.

==Local attractions==

Attractions near the village include the Pistyll Rhaeadr waterfall, known for its natural arch.
The Berwyn Mountains and Lake Vyrnwy are nearby.

==Public transport==

Bus services are operated by family-run firm Tanat Valley Coaches, which is based on the edge of the village. Route D76 links the village with Llanfyllin, Meifod and Welshpool, whilst routes D78/D79 connect the village with Llangynog, Llangedwyn, Penybontfawr and Oswestry.
