[[Llangammarch Wells]]

CATEGORIES: Spa towns in Wales, Villages in Powys, Post towns in the LD postcode area

Llangammarch Wells or simply Llangammarch ([tpl]lang-cy|Llangamarch[/tpl]) is a village in the parish of Llangammarch in Powys, within the historic boundaries of Brecknockshire, mid Wales, lying on the River Irfon. 
It is the smallest of the four spa villages of mid-Wales, alongside  Llandrindod Wells, Builth Wells and Llanwrtyd Wells. The spa was focused on a barium well, which is now closed, but the village is still popular with anglers. 
The old village is centred around the parish church of St Cadmarch, which is a grade II* listed building.
 [tpl]cite web| url =http://www.britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/wa-84374-church-of-saint-cadmarch-llangamarch| title= Church of Saint Cadmarch, Llangamarch| publisher= British Listed Buildings|accessdate = 15 December 2013[/tpl] 
It benefits from a railway station on the Heart of Wales Line. It lies on Route 43 of the National Cycle Network.
