[[Maroon Bells–Snowmass Wilderness]]

CATEGORIES: Wilderness Areas of Colorado, Protected areas of Pitkin County, Colorado, Protected areas of Gunnison County, Colorado, Protected areas established in 1980, Gunnison National Forest, White River National Forest

The Maroon Bells–Snowmass Wilderness is a U.S. Wilderness Area located in the Elk Mountains of central Colorado.  The [tpl]convert|181535|acre|km2|adj=on[/tpl] wilderness was established in 1980 in the Gunnison and White River national forests. Within its boundaries are [tpl]convert|100|mi|km[/tpl] of trails, 6 of Colorado's fourteeners and 9 passes over [tpl]convert|12000|ft|m[/tpl]. The wilderness is named after the two peaks known as the Maroon Bells as well as Snowmass Mountain.[tpl]cite web |title=Maroon Bells-Snowmass Wilderness |publisher=Wilderness.net |url=http://www.wilderness.net/index.cfm?fuse=NWPS&sec=wildView&WID=344 |accessdate=August 11, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web |title=Maroon Bells-Snowmass Wilderness Area |publisher=Colorado Wilderness |url=http://www.coloradowilderness.com/wildpages/maroon.html |accessdate=August 11, 2012[/tpl]
