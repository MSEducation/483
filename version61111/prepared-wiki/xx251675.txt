[[St. Mark's Lutheran Church (Guilderland, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Lutheran churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Italianate architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1872, 19th-century Lutheran church buildings, Churches in Albany County, New York, Former churches in New York

St. Mark's Lutheran Church is a historic Lutheran church on Main Street in Guilderland, Albany County, New York. It was built in 1872 in a vernacular Italianate style.  It has curved arched windows and a bracketed cornice. It features a square bell tower with pedimented cornice and steeple.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=217|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: St. Mark's Lutheran Church|date=June 1979|accessdate=2010-10-13 |author=Town of Guilderland|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] St. Mark's served the Lutherans until 1973, when the town leased it as a community center. 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.
