[[Russia–Seychelles relations]]

CATEGORIES: Russia–Seychelles relations, Africa–Russia relations, Bilateral relations of Russia, Bilateral relations of Seychelles

Russia–Seychelles relations ([tpl]lang-ru|Российско-сейшельские отношения[/tpl]) is the bilateral relations of Russia and Seychelles. Diplomatic relations between Seychelles and the Soviet Union were established on 30 June 1976, a day after the island nation gained its independence from the United Kingdom.[tpl]cite book|last=Ginsburgs|first=George|title=A calendar of Soviet treaties, 1974-1980|publisher=BRILL|year=1987|pages=163|chapter=1976|isbn=90-247-3628-5|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=pSyu2DzyfNoC&pg=PA163|accessdate=2009-05-27[/tpl] Russia has an embassy in Victoria.[tpl]ru icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mid.ru/zu_r.nsf/e0f3cd1a55ff248dc32571e7003f460b/21216b84f02e86d4c32565e8003604cc?OpenDocument|title=Посольство в Виктории|publisher=Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Russia|accessdate=2009-05-27[/tpl] Seychelles is represented in Russia through its embassy in Paris (France) and two honorary consulates (in Saint Petersburg and Yekaterinburg).
In February 2009, Alexander Vladimirov, Russia's Ambassador to Seychelles paid a final visit to President James Michel and Vice-President Joseph Belmont at the State House.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nation.sc/index.php?art=14919|title=Seychelles-Russia relations now ‘at their best’|date=11 February 2009|publisher=Seychelles Nation|accessdate=2009-05-27[/tpl]

==Soviet-era relations==

On 15 February 1980, the Soviet Union and Seychelles signed the Agreement on merchant navigation in Victoria.[tpl]cite web|url=http://untreaty.un.org/unts/60001_120000/10/32/00019573.pdf|title=Agreement on merchant navigation. Signed at Victoria on 15 February 1980|date=15 February 1980|publisher=Government of the Soviet Union and Government of the Seychelles|accessdate=2009-05-26[/tpl] The government of Seychellois President France-Albert René supported the Soviet invasion of Afghanistan.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.seychellesweekly.com/7-7-06/page%202.html|title=Catholic church says SPPF not telling the truth about past relationship|date=7 July 2006|publisher=Seychelles Weekly|accessdate=2009-05-26[/tpl]
In 1987 The Sunday Times, quoting unnamed US intelligence officers, reported that the Soviet Union had landed 50 naval infantry troops in the Seychelles after making landfall on the Ivan Rogov in October 1986; a month after a foiled assassination attempt on René.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=zDcSAAAAIBAJ&sjid=m-8DAAAAIBAJ&pg=4562,6577815|title=Soviet base reported in Seychelles|location=London|date=9 November 1987|publisher=The Spokesman Review|accessdate=2009-05-26[/tpl]

==Economic relations==

Bilateral trade between Russia and the Seychelles in 2008 reached a total of US$6.23 million. Russia exported some US$4.54 million worth of goods and services to the Seychelles, including mineral oils, machinery and equipment. The Seychelles exports to Russia totalled US$1.69 million made up of fish, seafood and spices.
In 1999 an agreement on co-operation in the field of tourism was concluded between the two nations, and in 2008 some 6,400 Russian tourists visited the Seychelles.[tpl]ru icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mid.ru/ns-rafr.nsf/89414576079db559432569d8002421fc/b66fce875f0dc71943256a2b0032fdea?OpenDocument|title=Российско-сейшельские отношения|date=16 February 2009|publisher=Ministry of Foreign Affairs of Russia|accessdate=2009-05-26[/tpl] Burgeoning Russian tourism to the Seychelles led the Seychellois national airline Air Seychelles to launch a weekly flight to Moscow's Vnukovo International Airport in March 2009, with the expectation of the addition of another weekly flight once its presence in the Russian market has been consolidated.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.seychelles.travel/en/news/display-item.php?newsid=199|title=Air Seychelles officially launches flight linking Seychelles-Moscow|date=24 March 2009|publisher=Seychelles.travel|accessdate=2009-05-26[/tpl]
