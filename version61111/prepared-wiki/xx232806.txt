[[2000 Kipawa earthquake]]

CATEGORIES: 2000 in Canada, Earthquakes in Quebec, Earthquakes in Ontario, 2000 earthquakes

The 2000 Kipawa earthquake struck Quebec and Ontario, Canada with a moment magnitude of 5.2 at 6:22 a.m. on Saturday, January 1.Natural Resources Canada: The 01 January 2000 Kipawa Earthquake (mn=5.2) It occurred in the Western Quebec Seismic Zone.
The main shock epicenter was located in Lake Kipawa about [tpl]convert|10|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] north of Témiscaming in southwestern Quebec and [tpl]convert|70|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] northeast of North Bay, Ontario.Natural Resources Canada: 2000-01-01: M=5.2 near Témiscaming, Quebec The shaking associated with this earthquake was strongest within [tpl]convert|50|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] of the epicenter. It was felt in Témiscaming, North Bay and as far away as Toronto, making it one of the most significant earthquakes in Canada in 2000.Natural Resources Canada: Earthquakes Reports for 2000 The earthquake was triggered by major thrust faults associated with the Ottawa-Bonnechere Graben. A total of 17 aftershocks were recorded.
Minor damage was reported during this earthquake, including fallen light objects, a damaged ventilation pipe and fractures in plaster.
The earthquake's epicenter was very close to the epicenter of the 1935 Timiskaming earthquake and lies in a group of 76 located earthquakes since 1935.
