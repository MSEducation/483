==Description==

It can grow 10–20 ft or more with a spread of 1/3 to 2/3 the height. It is a very slender tree with few branches as well as leaves. The petiolar-rachis is characteristically long and functions as a cladophyll. it has a white or yellow-colored peeling off bark. The leaves have 5-6 leaflets in the end. It may drop leaves in autumn and winter. The flowers are like catkins, rod or bottle-brush-like, white or light yellow in color. The pods are multichambered, and 3-4 in long specimens.[tpl]cite web|last=Moore|first=Tony|title=Acacia willardia, Palo blanco, Fabaceae Family|url=http://ag.arizona.edu/pima/gardening/aridplants/Acacia_willardiana.html|work=Government of Arizona[/tpl] The flowers occur in pale yellow spikes.[tpl]cite book|last=Jaeger|first=E. ==Distribution and habitat==

The plant is found in the western Sonora desert and northwestern Sinaloa (Mexico), its native place, in rocky bajdas, slopes and arroyos from 0 to 2,000 feet elevation.[tpl]cite web|title=Acacia willardiana Rose|url=http://www.ars-grin.gov/cgi-bin/npgs/html/taxon.pl?1035|work=United States Department of Agriculture|publisher=Germplasm Resources Information Network[/tpl]
