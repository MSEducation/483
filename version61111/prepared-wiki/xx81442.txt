[[Marie-Josée Croze]]

CATEGORIES: 1970 births, Actresses from Montreal, Canadian film actresses, Canadian television actresses, French Quebecers, Genie Award winners for Best Actress, Genie Award winners for Best Supporting Actress, Living people, 20th-century Canadian actresses, 21st-century Canadian actresses

Marie-Josée Croze ([tpl]IPA-fr|maʁi ʒoze kʁoz[/tpl]; born February 23, 1970) is a Canadian actress. She also holds French citizenship, which she obtained in December 2012.

==Career==

Croze was born in Montreal, QC. She won the award for Best Actress at the 2003 Cannes Film Festival for her performance in The Barbarian Invasions.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.festival-cannes.com/en/archives/ficheFilm/id/4071956/year/2003.html |title=Festival de Cannes: The Barbarian Invasions |accessdate=2009-11-07|work=festival-cannes.com[/tpl] She was cast by prominent Hollywood director Steven Spielberg for his film Munich which was released in December 2005. She also made a major appearance as a speech therapist in Julian Schnabel's 2007 film adaptation of Jean-Dominique Bauby's memoir The Diving Bell and the Butterfly.
In November 2012 she was selected as a member of the main competition jury at the 2012 International Film Festival of Marrakech.
