[[Urukh River]]

CATEGORIES: Rivers of North Ossetia-Alania, Rivers of Kabardino-Balkaria

Urukh River, known as Kharves[tpl]Citation needed|date=September 2009[/tpl] in its upper reaches ([tpl]lang-ru|Урух[/tpl], [tpl]lang-os|Æрæф[/tpl], Æræf, Digor: Ирæф, Iræf), is a river in North Ossetia–Alania and Kabardino-Balkaria (Russia), a left tributary of the Terek River. The length of the Urukh is 104 km, the area of its drainage basin is 1280 km². The Urukh originates in the glaciers of the Greater Caucasus. The river freezes up in December and remains icebound until March.
