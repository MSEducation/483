[[J.W. Cappelens Forlag]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct companies of Norway, Publishing companies of Norway, Companies based in Oslo, Companies established in 1829, Companies disestablished in 2007, Publishing companies established in the 1820s

J.W. Cappelens Forlag, usually referred to as Cappelen, was one of the oldest publishing houses of Norway. Cappelen Damm AS  (Store norske leksikon)
J.W. Cappelens Forlag was founded in 1829 by Jørgen Wright Cappelen, of the distinguished Cappelen family. Torger Baardseth was the director of Cappelen  between 1904 and 1943. JW Cappelens Publishing was taken over by the Swedish media group Bonnier in 1987.  Bonnier AB(Store norske leksikon)In 2007 it merged with N.W. Damm & Søn, another old Norwegian publishing house, to become Cappelen Damm. Its last CEO was Sindre Guldvog.
NW Damm & Son was founded in 1843 by Niels Wilhelm Damm. N.W.Damm(Store norske leksikon)It was taken over by the Danish media group Egmont in 1984. Egmont Gruppen (Store norske leksikon) It previously merged with Hjemmets publishing in 2001.Hjemmets Bokforlag (Store norske leksikon)
