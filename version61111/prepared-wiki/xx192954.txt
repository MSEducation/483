[[Hop-o'-Nose Knitting Mill]]

CATEGORIES: Industrial buildings completed in 1881, Industrial buildings and structures on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Buildings and structures in Greene County, New York, Grinding mills in the United States

The Hop-o'-Nose Knitting Mill was a structure representative of the industrial history of Catskill, New York in the nineteenth century.  The mill was built by Wolfe Bros., carpenters, and Mull & Fromer, Masons and Builders, in 1881[tpl]Cite journal|title = Forty Years Ago:  Items from The Recorder, Dec. 16, 1881| journal = The Recorder| volume = 130| issue = 19| pages = | publisher = | location = Catskill, N.Y.| date = Dec 16, 1921| url = http://fultonhistory.com/Newspapers%207/Catskill%20NY%20Recorder/Catskill%20NY%20Recorder%201921%20Grayscale/Catskill%20NY%20Recorder%201921%20Grayscale%20-%200408.pdf| accessdate = 2010-11-20[/tpl] at 130 West Main Street.  West Main Street is located on the western side of Catskill Creek, while East Main Street is on the eastern side of the creek.  The mill was named to the National Register of Historic Places in 1995 as Hop-O-Nose Knitting Mill.  It has since been demolished, with the exception of the tower, which remains overlooking a lot full of brick demolition debris.[tpl]cite web|last=Bonafide|first=John A.|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination:Hop-O-Nose Knitting Mill|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=2619|date=April 1995|accessdate=2008-04-29[/tpl] and Accompanying 5 photos, exterior and interior, from 1995
