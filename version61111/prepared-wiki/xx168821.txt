[[Chalee Tennison]]

CATEGORIES: 1969 births, American country singers, American female singers, Living people, Asylum Records artists, DreamWorks Records artists

Chalee Tennison (born April 11, 1969) is an American country music artist from the state of Texas. She has released three major-label studio albums (two on Asylum Records and one on Dreamworks Records) in addition to charting six singles on the Billboard Hot Country Singles & Tracks (now Hot Country Songs) charts. Her highest charting singles, "Just Because She Lives There" and "Go Back", both reached No.Â 36 on that chart.

==Biography==

Prior to her music career, Tennison worked as a prison guard in the Texas Department of Criminal Justice, in addition to performing in various Texas bands.[tpl]cite web |url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p371146/biography|pure_url=yes[/tpl] |title=allmusic ((( Chalee Tennison > Biography ))) |accessdate=2007-08-02 |last=Ankeny |first=Jason |work= Allmusic[/tpl] She moved to Nashville, Tennessee and worked as a demo singer, eventually signing to Asylum Records in 1999. She recorded two albums on Asylum, both albums producing minor chart entries. In 2002, she moved to DreamWorks Records and recorded her Parading in the Rain album on that label.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cmt.com/artists/news/1452265/20020212/tennison_chalee.jhtml |title=Chalee Tennison to DreamWorks |accessdate=2007-08-02 |work=CMT.com[/tpl] Although she has not recorded any albums since, she made an appearance on Leland Martin's 2006 single "Same Old Song and Dance" from his self-titled album.
