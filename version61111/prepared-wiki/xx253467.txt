David G. Greenfield is an American attorney and a member of the New York City Council representing the 44th council district, which comprises the neighborhoods of Bensonhurst, Borough Park, and Midwood in Brooklyn, New York.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theyeshivaworld.com/article.php?p=44474|title=Inside Politics: Ask Greenfield!|publisher=The Yeshiva World|accessdate=2010-01-10[/tpl] Greenfield was elected by his Brooklyn colleagues to co-chair the Brooklyn delegation and serve as their representative on the Budget Negotiating Team of the New York City Council. [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cityandstateny.com/brooklyn-leadership-rumble-slated-wednesday|title=Brooklyn Leadership Rumble|publisher=City & State|accessdate=2012-09-18[/tpl] Greenfield is ranked as the 51st most powerful New Yorker in City & State's most recent Power 100 list, ranking him one slot behind billionaire political activist George Soros. [tpl]cite web|url=http://media.cityandstateny.com/power-lists/power-100.html#.UxvJPEtX_Ww|title=City & State - Power 100|publisher=City & State|accessdate=2014-03-08[/tpl]
Greenfield is the founding director and counsel of TEACH NYS [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theyeshivaworld.com/news/General+News/44332/Simcha+Felder+To+Leave+Council+&+Join+Comptroller+-+Race+Between+Greenfield+&+Dear+for+Council.html|title=Simcha Felder To Leave Council & Join Comptroller - Race Between Greenfield & Dear for Council|publisher=The Yeshiva World|accessdate=2010-01-10[/tpl] and prior to his election served as the executive vice president of the Sephardic Community Federation.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2009/07/24/nyregion/24rabbi.html|work=The New York Times|title=Syrian Sephardic Communities Shaken by Charges Against a Leading Rabbi|first=Paul|last=Vitello|date=2009-07-24|accessdate=2010-05-01[/tpl] Greenfield is a long-time advocate for increased government funding for public and private (including religious) schools.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/03/nyregion/as-mayor-continues-to-insist-on-teacher-layoffs-observers-ask-why.html|title=As Mayor Holds Firm on Teacher Layoffs, Some See Reasons Beyond Money|publisher=NYTimes.com|date=2011-06-02|accessdate=2011-07-04|first=Fernanda|last=Santos[/tpl] 
As Director and Counsel of TEACH NYS, Greenfield organized state-wide advocacy campaigns that resulted in private and public school parents receiving tax breaks and private schools receiving more government assistance.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nysun.com/news/ny-tuition-crisis-puts-many-risk|title=N.Y. 'Tuition Crisis' Puts Many at Risk|publisher=Nysun.com|accessdate=2010-01-10[/tpl] Greenfield served as deputy director of finance in Senator Joseph Lieberman's 2004 presidential campaign. Prior to that, he had a stint as chief of staff to Assemblyman Dov Hikind.[tpl]cite web|url=http://matzav.com/city-council-candidate-david-greenfield-receives-key-endorsements-from-state-senators|title=City Council Candidate David Greenfield Receives Key Endorsements From State Senators|publisher=Matzav.com|accessdate=2010-01-10[/tpl]

==Religious Beliefs==

Greenfield is Orthodox Jewish and prays in Landau's Synagogue in the Midwood section of Brooklyn.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emunahmagazine.com/catching-up-with-rising-political-star-david-greenfield|title=Emunah Magazine Exclusive Interview With David Greenfield|publisher=EmunahMagazine.com|accessdate=2010-02-25[/tpl] Due to his religious beliefs, Greenfield was the only member of the City Council to vote against an August 2010 bill that required the City Clerks office to post on its website, and hand out at its office, information on where exactly in the U.S. and the world same sex couples are able to get married.Gotham Gazette report on Greenfield's vote against same sex marriage info from city clerks

==Political Views==

Greenfield is a political moderate who has made passing quality of life legislation and promoting common sense solutions to government problems his signature issues. Greenfield authored a law that banned the Department of Sanitation from placing hard-to-remove stickers on vehicles that were parked on the wrong-side of the street. [tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2012/01/19/nyregion/new-york-city-council-votes-to-ban-alternate-side-parking-sticker.html|work=New York Times|title=Forgot to Move Car? City Council Votes to End a Sticker of Shame|date=2012-01-18|first=Christine|last=Haughney[/tpl] Greenfield also co-authored a law that requires the Department of Education to notify parents and teachers about potentially harmful PCBs in classrooms. [tpl]cite news|url=http://council.nyc.gov/html/releases/191211stated.shtml|work=New York City Council|date=2011-12-19[/tpl] Greenfield also introduced a law that would stop New York City from towing cars for unpaid parking tickets and instead boot the car. This would save automobile owners the hassle of spending a day dealing with government bureaucracy trying to retrieve their cars. [tpl]cite news|url=http://www.theyeshivaworld.com/article.php?p=114423|work=Yeshiva World News|date=2012-01-11[/tpl] Greenfield's proposed legislation was adopted by the New York City Council Department of Finance as a pilot program in June of 2012. [tpl]cite news|url=http://www.brooklyneagle.com/articles/brooklyn-pilots-citywide-parking-enforcement-reboot|work=Brooklyn Eagle|date=2012-06-14[/tpl]

==Electoral history==

Greenfield, a first time candidate, won after defeating Joe Lazar. He sits on the New York City Council, representing the 44th Council District. After polls were closed on March 23, 2010, Greenfield had 57% of the vote while Lazar had 37%.Yeshive World results of Greenfield v Lazar race
