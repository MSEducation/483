[[T. J. Maher]]

CATEGORIES: 1922 births, 2002 deaths, Independent politicians in Ireland, Irish farmers, Politicians from County Tipperary, MEPs for the Republic of Ireland 1989–94, MEPs for the Republic of Ireland 1984–89, MEPs for the Republic of Ireland 1979–84, Independent Ireland MEPs

Thomas Joseph Maher (29 April 1922 – 19 April 2002) was an Irish politician and farmers' leader. 
He was born on a small farm near Cashel, County Tipperary in 1922. He was educated locally and became a member of Macra na Feirme.[tpl]cite web|url=http://archives.tcm.ie/thekingdom/2002/05/02/story4459.asp|title=T.J. Maher fought the good fight for farmers|accessdate=29 April 2008|publisher=The Kingdom[/tpl] He was a founder member of National Farmers' Association (later the Irish Farmers' Association) in 1955. He became the President of the IFA in 1967. In 1977 he became president of the Irish Co-Operative Organisation Society (ICOS). 
He was first elected to the European Parliament at the 1979 European election for the Munster constituency as an independent and was re-elected at the 1984 and 1989 elections.[tpl]cite web|url=http://electionsireland.org/candidate.cfm?ID=3246|title=T J Maher|work=ElectionsIreland.org|accessdate=10 April 2009[/tpl] He stood unsuccessfully as an Independent candidate at the 1981 general election in the Tipperary South constituency. He retired from politics at the 1994 European election. He was awarded an honorary doctorate by the University of Limerick in 1997.
