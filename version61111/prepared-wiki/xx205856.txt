[[VoloMedia]]

CATEGORIES: Internet advertising, Internet advertising services and affiliate networks, Companies based in Sunnyvale, California

VoloMedia is a Sunnyvale, California based  advertising network that has developed technology to enable advertisements to be inserted into downloadable audio and video, such as podcasts.  Large media publishers such as Public Radio International, MSNBC and Fox News[tpl]cite news |url= http://sanjose.bizjournals.com/sanjose/stories/2008/03/03/story13.html|title= Silicon Valley Business Journal: Re-branded VoloMedia focuses on online - and offline - ads | first=Joseph C. | last=Panettieri |date=2008-03-02[/tpl] utilize their technologies in order to provide free downloadable programming.  The company is funded by venture capital firms Mayfield Fund, Sutter Hill Ventures and Worldview Technology Partners.
VoloMedia is a founding member of the Association for Downloadable Media.

==History==

VoloMedia was founded in 2005 as Podbridge by Murgesh Navar and Andrey Yruski. The company launched its downloadable audio advertisement network in February 2006.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.mediaweek.com/mw/news/interactive/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1002034052|title= MediaWeek: Podbridge Starts Podcast Ad Net  [/tpl]  In May of the same year, the company announced support for downloadable video as well.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.volomedia.com/home/news/PodbridgeVideoFinal.pdf|title= Release: Podbridge advertising network to include video content  [/tpl] The company changed its name to VoloMedia in November 2007.[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.bizjournals.com/sanjose/stories/2007/11/26/daily40.html|title= Silicon Valley Business Journal: Podbridge is now VoloMedia | date=2007-11-28[/tpl]
In July 2006, the company's advertisement insertion and rotation technologies were awarded a U.S. patent.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.clickz.com/showPage.html?page=3623001|title= ClickZ: Podbridge Snares Patent for Ads in Time-Shifted Content[/tpl]
