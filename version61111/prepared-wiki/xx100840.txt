[[Steve Finnan]]

Stephen John "Steve" Finnan (born 24 April 1976)[tpl]cite book |editor1-last=Hugman |editor1-first=Barry J. |title=The PFA Footballers' Who's Who 2005/2006 |publisher=Queen Anne Press |year=2005 |page=141 |isbn=978-1-85291-662-6[/tpl] is a retired Irish international footballer who played as a right back.
He is the only player to have played in the World Cup, UEFA Champions League, UEFA Cup, Intertoto Cup, all four levels of the English league football and the Football Conference.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.sportspundit.com/soccer/players/2252-steve-finnan |title=Steve Finnan – News, Stats and Info About the Football Player |publisher=Sportspundit.com |date=24 April 1976 |accessdate=23 October 2011[/tpl] Among the honours won during his career was the 2005 UEFA Champions League Final for Liverpool. Finnan is known for his attacking role and ability to cross the ball.[tpl]cite web|title=Player Profile|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport3/worldcup2002/hi/team_pages/rep_of_ireland/squad/newsid_1944000/1944103.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=22 January 2013[/tpl]
He played 52 internationals for the Republic of Ireland from his debut in 2000, scoring twice. He played for them at the 2002 FIFA World Cup.

==Club career==

===Early career===

Finnan was born in the Janesboro area of Limerick, and moved to Chelmsford, England, at a young age.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.limerickleader.ie/news/blue-in-the-face-with-brian-mcdonnell-1-2183293 |title=Blue in the face |first=Brian |last=McDonnell |work=Limerick Leader |date=27 February 2008 |accessdate=3 February 2013[/tpl] He began his career playing in Wimbledon's youth system,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.liverpoolfc.tv/news/drilldown/N158919080221-1444.htm|title=What's in a name: Steve Finnan|publisher=Liverpool F.C.|date=21 February 2008|accessdate=21 February 2008 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080225175352/http://www.liverpoolfc.tv/news/drilldown/N158919080221-1444.htm  |archivedate = 25 February 2008[/tpl] but he joined non-League club Welling United in 1993 after being released at the age of 16. Finnan turned professional when he signed with Birmingham City in 1995, who paid a fee of £100,000 to Welling United to acquire his services.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=2516 |title=Finnan Birmingham City fee|publisher=Soccerbase |accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] He scored his first professional goal against Watford.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/latest-news/liverpool-v-watford-stat-attack |title=Liverpool v Watford: Stat Attack|publisher=Liverpool F.C.|accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl]

===Notts County===

Finnan made a loan switch to Notts County in March 1996, making his debut in a 2–1 victory over Walsall and scoring his first goal a month later in a 4–2 victory over Bristol Rovers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://homepage.ntlworld.com/carousel/ncfc9596.html |title=Notts County FC season 1995/96|accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://homepage.ntlworld.com/carousel/ncfc/1995-96.html |title=1995-96 Notts County FC Match Line-Up's & Details|publisher=Up the Maggies|accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] He appeared in all of Notts' game for the remainder of the season, helping the team finish fourth in the Second Division and qualify for the promotion play-offs.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng96.html |title=English Football League 1995–1996|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] In the first leg of the semi-finals held at the Alexandra Stadium he scored against Crewe Alexandra, with the match ending 2–2. The return leg at home saw Notts win 1–0, winning 3–2 on aggregate. In the play-offs final against Bradford City at Wembley Stadium, Notts County lost 2–0. He returned to Birmingham following the conclusion of the loan deal.
During the following season, 1996–97, Notts County signed Finnan for a fee £300,000 on 30 October 1996.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=2516 |title=Finnan Notts County fee|publisher=Soccerbase |accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] He played his first match as a permanent player for Notts County after being introduced as a substitute against Shrewsbury Town. The season was not a successful one for Finnan as Notts County finished 24th in the table and were relegated to the Third Division.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng97.html |title=English Football League 1996–1997|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl]   	
In the 1997–98 season, his first full season at the club, Finnan established himself as a key member of the team and was an ever present, appearing in 51 matches,
 helping the club win the Third Division title with record points and time.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nottscountyfc.co.uk/page/HistoryDetail/0,,10426~1028224,00.html|archiveurl=http://archive.is/gxTj|archivedate=15 June 2012|title=Notts County history|publisher=Notts County F.C.|date=9 May 2012|accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] The other notable record Finnan established with Notts County during this season was a streak of 10 consecutive victories; a club record and one which still stands today.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.nottinghampost.com/Magpies-make-history-thrilling-win-Imps/story-12239574-detail/story.html |title=Magpies make history|accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl]

===Fulham===

After strong showings for Notts County in the 1997–98 season, Fulham manager Kevin Keegan paid £600,000 for his services in November 1998.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=2516 |title=Fulham fee|publisher=Soccerbase|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] He made his Fulham debut against Chesterfield on 21 November 1998 and scored his first goal for Fulham against Blackpool on 20 March 1999. While at Fulham, he also became a favourite among the fans.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/club/messageboard.aspx?mode=thread&TopicID=23757&page=1 |title=100 PWSTC |date=19 September 2007|publisher=Fulham F.C.|accessdate=16 July 2009[/tpl] His first season with the club was a success, with Fulham winning the Second Division championship and being promoted to the First Division.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng99.html |title=English Football League 1998–1999|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl]   	
Finnan finished ninth with Fulham the following season under new manager Paul Bracewell, despite promising early season form and an unbeaten run lasting over two months.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng00.html |title=English Football League 1999–2000|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] Bracewell was replaced with Jean Tigana in May 2000, and under him in the 2000–01 season, Fulham won the First Division championship, winning promotion to the Premier League, with Finnan appearing in all but one of the league matches.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng01.html |title=English Football League 2000–2001|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/2329189/Premier-League-facts-might-help-Fulham-think.html |title=Bracewell replaced by Tigana|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|accessdate=31 July 2009 | location=London | first=Graham | last=Taylor | date=22 December 2007[/tpl]   	      	
In his debut season in the Premier League, he helped Fulham qualify for the UEFA Intertoto Cup, was voted into the 2001–02 Professional Footballers' Association team of the year and was also voted as the Fulham player of the year.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/Profile/493 |title=Fulham notes |publisher=Liverpool F.C. |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Player of the Season|url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/news/2002/may/02/player-of-the-season|publisher=Fulham Official Website|date=2 May 2002|accessdate=10 April 2014[/tpl] Fulham then went on to win the Intertoto Cup, giving Finnan his first taste of European football.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesu/uic02.html |title=Intertoto Cup 2002|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl] Next season saw Jean Tigana replaced by Chris Coleman, Finnan's fourth manager in five seasons.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/2399833/Fulham-sack-Tigana.html |title=Fulham sack Tigana|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|accessdate=31 July 2009 | location=London | date=17 April 2003[/tpl] By April 2003 with few games to go, Fulham were close to the relegation zone but picked up 10 points out of a possible 15 to stay in the top-flight, finishing in 14th position.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng03.html |title=English Football League 2002–2003|publisher=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl]   	
In the summer of 2003 Finnan found himself courted by many of England's top clubs,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/latest-news/exclusive-steve-finnan-interview |title=Exclusive Steve Finnan interview |first=Mark |last=Platt |publisher=Liverpool F.C |date=31 July 2003 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] and eventually Fulham agreed to sell Finnan to Liverpool for a fee of £3.5m.

===Liverpool===

Finnan made his Liverpool debut against Chelsea on 17 August 2003. His first season was disrupted by injury,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/history/past-players/steve-finnan |title=Past players: Steve Finnan |publisher=Liverpool F.C |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] but in 2004–05, he established himself as the first-choice right back and a firm fan favourite.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.com/news/latest-news/100-pwstk-no-55-steve-finnan |title=100 PWSTK – No.55: Steve Finnan |first=Mark |last=Platt |date=19 July 2006 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] The season was a highly successful one for Finnan. He scored his first and only goal for Liverpool against West Bromwich Albion, played in the League Cup final,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2005/feb/28/match.liverpool |title=Mourinho the scene stealer claims his first English prize |first=Kevin |last=McCarra |newspaper=The Guardian |date=28 February 2005 |accessdate=12 December 2013 |location=London[/tpl] and was in the starting eleven as Liverpool won the Champions League Final, though a thigh injury meant he was substituted at half time.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/europe/4573159.stm |title=AC Milan 3–3 Liverpool (aet) |publisher=BBC Sport |date=25 May 2005 |accessdate=30 September 2010[/tpl] Finnan's goal against WBA meant that he had scored in each of the five highest divisions of English football, a record he shares with Jimmy Willis.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.liverpoolecho.co.uk/sport/football/football-news/goal-king-jimmy-willis-league-3498776 |title=Goal king Jimmy Willis in a league of his own! |first=David |last=Prentice |newspaper=Liverpool Echo |date=8 November 2007 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl]
In the 2005–06 season, Liverpool broke their record for number of points in a Premier League season, finishing on 82 points with Finnan being ever present.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/SeasonArchive/Game/4968 |title=2005–2006 Premiership |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] At the end of the season he picked up another medal with Liverpool, winning the FA Cup.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/SeasonArchive/Game/4990 |title=2006 FA Cup final |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl]
Finnan played for Liverpool as first choice right back for the 2006–07 season despite heavy competition from newly arrived team-mate Álvaro Arbeloa.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/SeasonArchive/Appearances/116 |title=Appearances total |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] He started in the 2007 UEFA Champions League Final and was subbed off after 88 minutes, Liverpool went on to lose the game 2–1.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/SeasonArchive/Game/5048 |title=2007 Champions league final |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] In July 2007, Finnan agreed a new two-year contract to remain with Liverpool.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/l/liverpool/6911440.stm |title=Finnan signs new Liverpool deal |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 August 2007 |accessdate=30 September 2010[/tpl]
The following season, Liverpool's success continued as they finished third in the League and reached the semi-finals of the Champions League. Finnan took his total appearances for Liverpool past the 200 mark and also made it into the top 100 capped Liverpool players of all time.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Stats/PlayerAppearances |title=Top 100 appearances |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/Profile/493 |title=Past 200 appearances |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] Finnan featured in 35 games, but lost his starting place, manager Rafa Benítez often preferring Arbeloa to start.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/Profile/493 |title=2007–2008 notes |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/GamesPerSeason/493-117 |title=Finnan 2007–2008 appearances |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lfchistory.net/Players/Player/GamesPerSeason/1177-117 |title=Arbeloa 2007–2008 appearances |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] In the summer of 2008, after the arrival of fullback Philipp Degen at the club from Borussia Dortmund, Finnan was linked with a move away from Anfield.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,12876_4007410,00.html|title=Finnan in Liverpool 'limbo' |accessdate=31 July 2009 | work=Sky Sports[/tpl] He was offered by Liverpool as a makeweight in a deal to sign Gareth Barry from Aston Villa; the player accepted the move but the clubs were unable to agree a fee.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.guillembalague.com/interview_desp.php?id=24&titulo=Steve%20Finnan%20on%20his%20move%20from%20Liverpool%20to%20Espanyol |archiveurl=http://archive.is/U3LeE |archivedate=4 October 2013 |title=Finnan on his move from Liverpool to Espanyol |publisher= guillembalague.com|date=9 September 2008 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] He made a guest appearance in Jamie Carragher's testimonial match in September 2010.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.liverpoolfc.tv/news/latest-news/carra-on-target-in-derby-win |title=Carra on target in derby win |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl]

===Espanyol===

On transfer deadline day, 1 September 2008, Finnan signed a two-year deal with La Liga club Espanyol for an undisclosed fee.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,12875_4082310,00.html |title=Finnan seals Spanish switch |publisher=Sky Sports |date=1 September 2008 |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl] Injuries limited his first team appearances,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.hindu.com/tss/tss3209/stories/20090228501801500.htm |title=Frustrated by injuries |first=Tim |last=Clement |magazine=Sportstar Weekly |agency=PA Sport |publisher=The Hindu |date=28 February 2009 |accessdate=1 October 2010 |location=Chennai, India[/tpl] and rumours of a return to the Premier League in the January 2009 transfer window with Arsenal or Tottenham Hotspur were dismissed by Espanyol's sports director Paco Herrera and by coach Mané, who considered him an important member of the team.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11842/4686533 |title=Espanyol rule out Finnan exit |publisher=Sky Sports |first=Francisco |last=Acedo |date=23 December 2008 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] By mid-January, Espanyol were "surprised" that a move to Hull City did not proceed, apparently for medical reasons.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11661_4808094,00.html |title=Finnan 'surprise' for Espanyol |publisher=Sky Sports |first=Francisco |last=Acedo |date=17 January 2009 |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl] In July 2009, Espanyol and Finnan agreed to mutually terminate the remainder of his contract.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rcdespanyol.com/ingles/index.php?modulo=detalleNoticia&idnoticia=400&idseccion=28&Amenu=detalleNoticia |title=Finnan leaves Espanyol |publisher=RCD Espanyol |date=27 July 2009|accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl]

===Portsmouth===

Finnan signed a one-year contract with Premier League side Portsmouth on 31 July 2009.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/portsmouth/8177923.stm |title=Portsmouth sign defender Finnan |publisher=BBC Sport |date=31 July 2009 |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl] He made his debut for the club on 3 October in a 1–0 victory against Wolverhampton Wanderers at Molineux, and played regularly in the second half of the season,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=2516&seasonid=139 |title=Games played by Steve Finnan in 2009/2010 |work=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl] but with the club in administration and relegated to the Championship, he was not offered a new deal.[tpl]cite news |url=http://espnfc.com/news/story?id=788824&sec=england&cc=5739 |title=Pompey offer key players new contracts |date=19 May 2010 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] His last appearance for the club was the 2010 FA Cup Final against Chelsea.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/fa_cup/8680493.stm |title=Chelsea 1–0 Portsmouth |publisher=BBC Sport |date=15 May 2010 |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl]

==International career==

A Republic of Ireland Under-21 international, Finnan stepped up to the senior side in 2000 against Greece. He cemented his place in the Irish starting XI during the 2002 World Cup qualification matches, supplying the cross for Jason McAteer to score the only goal in a crucial 1–0 home win against the Netherlands in September 2001.
Finnan appeared in all three of Ireland's group E games in the 2002 FIFA World Cup against Cameroon, Germany and Saudi Arabia. Ireland progressed to the second round of the tournament for only the third time in their history where they faced Spain. The match ended 1–1 in extra time and went to penalties. Finnan took a penalty and scored, but Ireland eventually lost 3–2.
Injury disrupted Finnan's participation in the unsuccessful attempt to reach the Euro 2004 finals. He was a regular choice in the 2006 World Cup qualification games but Ireland failed to qualify for the finals.
He was again a regular selection in the 2008 Euro qualification games but Ireland did not qualify for the finals. He then announced his retirement from international football on 22 January 2008 after making 50 appearances for the Republic of Ireland.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rte.ie/sport/soccer/international/2008/0122/227147-finnans/ |title=Finnan confirms international retirement |publisher=RTÉ Sport |date=22 January 2008 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] Tempted by the prospect of working with the newly appointed Ireland manager Giovanni Trappatoni,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.fifa.com/world-match-centre/news/newsid/789/383/index.html |title=Finnan considers international U-turn |agency=Agence France Presse |publisher=FIFA |date=4 June 2008 |accessdate=12 December 2013[/tpl] Finnan came out of retirement in August 2008 to be included in the squad to face Norway in an international friendly.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/republic-of-ireland/2551856/Republic-of-Ireland-coach-Giovanni-Trapattoni-calls-for-Georgia-venue-change.html |title=Republic of Ireland coach Giovanni Trapattoni calls for Georgia venue change |date=13 August 2008 |accessdate=1 October 2010 |location=London |work=The Daily Telegraph[/tpl]
