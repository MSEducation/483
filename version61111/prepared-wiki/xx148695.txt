CATEGORIES: Submissions for Best Foreign Language Film Academy Award (by year)

The Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences has invited the film industries of various countries to submit their best film for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film every year since the award was created in 1956.[tpl]cite web | title = History of the Academy Awards - Page 2| publisher = Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences | url = http://www.oscars.org/aboutacademyawards/history02.html | accessdate = 2008-07-08 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080622191328/http://www.oscars.org/aboutacademyawards/history02.html  |archivedate = 2008-06-22[/tpl] The award is handed out annually by the Academy to a feature-length motion picture produced outside the United States that contains primarily non-English dialogue.[tpl]cite web | title = Special Rules for the Best Foreign Language Film Award|publisher = Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences | url = http://www.academyaward.com/77academyawards/rules/rule14.html | accessdate = 2008-07-08[/tpl] The Foreign Language Film Award Committee oversees the process and reviews all the submitted films.
For the 77th Academy Awards, which were held on February 27, 2005, the Academy invited 89 countries to submit films for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2004/04.07.01a.html|title=Academy Invites 89 Countries to Submit Their Best Pictures for Foreign Language Film Oscar|publisher=Academy of Motion Picture Arts and Sciences|date=2004-07-01|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] Fifty-one countries submitted films to the Academy, including Malaysia, which submitted a film for the first time.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.asiaarts.ucla.edu/article.asp?parentid=18902|title=Asia Pacific Arts: Oscars in Full|publisher=University of California, Los Angeles|author=Hu, Brian|date=2004-12-21|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl] The submissions from Colombia, Hong Kong and Ukraine were rejected before the formal review process, but Colombia submitted another film as a replacement.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.indiewire.com/biz/biz_041103world.html|title=Foreign Oscar Quandary: Academy Nixes "Maria," Colombia Adds "El Rey," and Other Stories from the Foreign-Lingo Category|publisher=indieWire|author=Kaufman, Anthony|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.latimes.com/entertainment/news/la-et-goldstein23oct23,0,6184894.story|title=THE BIG PICTURE: Oscars' foreign policy problem|publisher=Los Angeles Times|author=Goldstein, Patrick|date=2007-10-23|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl] The Academy released a list of the five nominees for the award on January 25, 2005.[tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.yahoo.com/feature/osc_03nominees.html|title=2005 Oscars 77th Academy Awards Nominees|publisher=Yahoo! Movies|date=2005-01-25|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl] The winner of the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film was Spain's The Sea Inside, which was directed by Alejandro Amenabar.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/7042801/|title=‘Sea Inside’ wins for best foreign film|publisher=MSNBC|date=2005-02-27|accessdate=2008-07-08[/tpl]
