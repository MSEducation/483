[[Adin B. Capron]]

Adin Ballou Capron (January 9, 1841 – March 17, 1911) was an American miller and politician from the U.S. state of Rhode Island. He served in the American Civil War and was a member of the United States House of Representatives.

==Early life and military career==

Born in Mendon, Massachusetts,[tpl]cite book|last=Mendon (Mass.)|title=Vital Records of Mendon, Massachusetts, to the Year 1850|date=1920|publisher=Wright & Potter printing Company|page=40|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=YMUMAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA40&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Mendon,+Massachusetts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=mpM0U4HeLqjf2QWzpIFg&ved=0CDIQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Mendon%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl] Capron attended Woonsocket High School and Westbrook Seminary, near Portland, Maine.[tpl]cite book|last=Neale|first=Walter|title=Autobiographies and portraits of the President, cabinet, Supreme court, and Fifty-fifth Congress, Volume 1|date=1899|publisher=The Neale company|page=1|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=1WgfAQAAMAAJ&pg=PT380&lpg=PT380&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Westbrook+Seminary&source=bl&ots=NbHNLJ_T9H&sig=1xP-chyMjJPKp_3ktobqs9CAmQo&hl=en&sa=X&ei=9ZQ0U5e3GtTI2wWyloGgDg&ved=0CEwQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Westbrook%20Seminary&f=false[/tpl] He settled in Stillwater, Rhode Island, and engaged in milling and dealing in grain. He enlisted as a sergeant in the 2nd Rhode Island Regiment of the  Rhode Island Volunteer Infantry in May 1861. He was promoted to the rank of Sergeant Major on July 11, 1861, and commissioned lieutenant in September 1861.[tpl]cite web|url=  http://wc.rootsweb.ancestry.com/cgi-bin/igm.cgi?op=GET&db=tcwlmo&id=I65513 |title=The Wright Family Genealogy|publisher = Ancestry.com|accessdate= March 27, 2014[/tpl]
He served in the Signal Corps until the close of the Civil War, having been commissioned first lieutenant on March 3, 1863, and subsequently promoted to the rank of captain and major by brevet.[tpl]cite book|last=Herringshaw|first=Thomas William|title=Herringshaw's National Library of American Biography: Contains Thirty-five Thousand Biographies of the Acknowledged Leaders of Life and Thought of the United States; Illustrated with Three Thousand Vignette Portraits|date=1909|publisher=American Publishers' Association|page=552|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=urzTAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA552&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Mendon,+Massachusetts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=mpM0U4HeLqjf2QWzpIFg&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Mendon%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl]

==Political career==

From 1887-1892, Capron served as member of the Rhode Island House of Representatives, and was speaker of the State House in 1891 and 1892.[tpl]cite book|last=United States. Government Printing Office|title=Congressional serial set|date=1918|publisher=U.S. G.P.O.|page=530|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=aMlTAAAAIAAJ&pg=PA530&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Mendon,+Massachusetts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=mpM0U4HeLqjf2QWzpIFg&ved=0CDgQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Mendon%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl] He was an unsuccessful candidate for election in 1892 to the Fifty-third Congress.[tpl]cite book|last=Neale|first=Walter|title=Autobiographies and portraits of the President, cabinet, Supreme court, and Fifty-fifth Congress, Volume 1|date=1899|publisher=The Neale company|page=1|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=1WgfAQAAMAAJ&pg=PT380&lpg=PT380&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Westbrook+Seminary&source=bl&ots=NbHNLJ_T9H&sig=1xP-chyMjJPKp_3ktobqs9CAmQo&hl=en&sa=X&ei=9ZQ0U5e3GtTI2wWyloGgDg&ved=0CEwQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Westbrook%20Seminary&f=false[/tpl] 
Capron was elected as a Republican candidate to the 55th United States Congress and to the six succeeding Congresses, serving in Congress from March 4, 1897 to March 3, 1911.[tpl]cite book|last=Herringshaw|first=Thomas William|title=Herringshaw's National Library of American Biography: Contains Thirty-five Thousand Biographies of the Acknowledged Leaders of Life and Thought of the United States; Illustrated with Three Thousand Vignette Portraits|date=1909|publisher=American Publishers' Association|page=552|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=urzTAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA552&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron+Mendon,+Massachusetts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=mpM0U4HeLqjf2QWzpIFg&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron%20Mendon%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl] He was not a candidate for renomination in 1910.  
After leaving Congress, he resumed his former business activities in Stillwater, where he died March 17, 1911.  He was interred in Swan Point Cemetery in Providence, Rhode Island.[tpl]cite book|last=Spencer|first=Thomas E.|title=Where They're Buried: A Directory Containing More Than Twenty Thousand Names of Notable Persons Buried in American Cemeteries, with Listings of Many Prominent People who Were Cremated|date=1998|publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com|page=296|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=eLWao2lIGTEC&pg=PA296&dq=Adin+Ballou+Capron&hl=en&sa=X&ei=uJI0U4a_OqWC2wXSl4G4BA&ved=0CFgQ6AEwCA#v=onepage&q=Adin%20Ballou%20Capron&f=false[/tpl]
