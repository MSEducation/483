[[Pendhā]]

CATEGORIES: Indian alcoholic beverages, History of Maharashtra, Sorghum, Indigofereae

Pendhā (or pindia) was an intoxicating beverage made by the Pindari community of mercenaries in central India in the 18th and 19th centuries, and possibly the etymological origin or the group's name.[tpl]cite book|last=Luard|first=Charles Eckford|title=The Central India State Gazetteer Series|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=W_8bAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA397|accessdate=2 August 2013|year=1907|publisher=Thacker, Spink|page=397[/tpl] The drink was said to have been made by fermenting sorghum (jowār), or possible Indigofera linifolia (pandhi, pandheri pati).[tpl]cite book|last1=Bakshi|first1=Shiri Ram|last2=Ralhan|first2=S.R. Bakshi And O.P.|title=Madhya Pradesh Through the Ages|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=pZPmLzgkF0UC&pg=PA171|accessdate=2 August 2013|year=2008|publisher=Sarup & Sons|isbn=978-81-7625-806-7|page=171[/tpl]
