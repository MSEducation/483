[[James Bayley (politician)]]

CATEGORIES: Nationalist Party of Australia politicians, Members of the Australian House of Representatives for Oxley, Members of the Australian House of Representatives, Members of the Queensland Legislative Assembly, 1882 births, 1968 deaths

James Garfield Bayley (26 March 1882 â€“ 14 January 1968) was an Australian politician. Born in Franklin, Tasmania, he moved to Queensland as a child. He was educated at Brisbane Grammar School and at Stanford University in the United States. He returned to Australia as a teacher and eventually a school principal. In 1917, he was elected to the Australian House of Representatives as the Nationalist member for Oxley. He held the seat until his defeat in 1931. In 1933, he was elected to the Legislative Assembly of Queensland as the member for Wynnum, representing the Country and Progressive National Party. He held Wynnum until 1935.
