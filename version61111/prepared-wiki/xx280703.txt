[[Rubus invisus]]

CATEGORIES: Rubus

Rubus invisus is a species of dewberry, known as  Upland dewberry. Like other dewberries, it is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, related to the blackberry. It is found in the Eastern United States.

==Description==

Leaves are large and very coarsely toothed.[tpl]cite book|last=Bailey|first=Liberty Hyde|title=Sketch of the evolution of our native fruits|year=1911|publisher=Macmillan Co|location=London|pages=346-347|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=NrBBAQAAIAAJ&pg=PA346&dq=Rubus+invisus&hl=en&sa=X&ei=yBoFUsHiB_ij4AOM8YHoAw&ved=0CEoQ6AEwBQ#v=onepage&q&f=false[/tpl] Flowers and fruit form on unusually long stems. Canes are short, and form dense mats up to [tpl]convert|1.5|ft|cm[/tpl] thick.

==Distribution and habitat==

Rubus invisus has been found in Indiana, Kentucky, Massachusetts, Maryland, New Hampshire, New Hampshire, New York, Ohio, Pennsylvania, Tennessee, Virginia, Vermont, West Virginia.  It typically inhabits areas of rocky soil and partial to full shade.
