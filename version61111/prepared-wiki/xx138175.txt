Metro Agrícola Oriental ([tpl]Lang-es|Estación Agrícola Oriental[/tpl]) is a metro station on Line A of the Mexico City Metro system.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.metro.df.gob.mx/red/estacion.html?id=123|title=Agrícola Oriental|language=Spanish|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|last=Archambault|first=Richard|url=http://mexicometro.org/metro/linea/agricola-oriental/|title=Agrícola Oriental »  Mexico City Metro System|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl]
It opened on 12 August 1991 as did the other stations along the rest of Line A.[tpl]Cite web|last=Janberg|first=Nicolas|url=http://en.structurae.de/structures/data/index.cfm?id=s0057327|title=Agrícola Oriental Metro Station (1991) - Structurae|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl]
The logo for the station represents two spikes of wheat.
It is an at grade station in the median of Calzada General Ignacio Zaragoza near the intersection of Calle 1 in Colonia Agrícola Oriental of Iztacalco borough.
It lies to the east of the Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.autodromohermanosrodriguez.com.mx/|title=Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl]
