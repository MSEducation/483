Philip "Phil" Eastwood (born 6 April 1978 in Blackburn) is an English former professional footballer who played in the Football League as a striker for Burnley.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=9821 |title=Phil Eastwood |work=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl]

==Career==

Eastwood was born in Whalley and attended Pleckgate High School in Blackburn.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/news/1550228.exclarets_striker_phil_has_a_new_target/ |title=Ex-Clarets' striker Phil has a new target |newspaper=Lancashire Telegraph |first=Steve |last=Tinniswood |date=17 July 2007 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] As a schoolboy, he was with Blackburn Rovers before joining Burnley as a YTS trainee when he left school.[tpl]cite news |url=http://archive.thisislancashire.co.uk/2001/1/6/699141.html |title=The Saturday Interview, with Phil Eastwood |newspaper=Lancashire Evening Telegraph |date=6 January 2001 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] While at Burnley he won a Pontins League Second Division winners' medal with the reserve team,
[tpl]cite news |url=http://archive.lancashireeveningtelegraph.co.uk/1998/6/9/799414.html |title=Make or break for Eastwood |newspaper=Lancashire Evening Telegraph |date=9 June 1998 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] had loan spells at non-league sides Leek Town, Telford United and Kettering Town, and made 16 first-team appearances, scoring once.
Released at the end of the 1998–99 season, he went on to play for Conference clubs Morecambe and Southport, then spent four seasons with Stalybridge Celtic. He scored 63 goals from 153 appearances in all competitions,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.stalybridgeceltic.co.uk/player.php?code=EASTP |title=Phil Eastwood |publisher=Stalybridge Celtic F.C |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] and spent time on loan at Hyde United[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.hydeunited.com/playerstats.php?p=36 |title=Player Statistics: Phil Eastwood |publisher=Hyde United F.C |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] and Ashton United,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.freewebs.com/aufc/de.htm |title=Eames – Eyres |work=Ashton United (Hurst FC) Player Database |publisher=www.ashtonutd.com |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] before signing for Rossendale United in 2006.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.nonleaguedaily.com/news/index.php?newsmode=FULL&nid=35597 |title=Triple Coup For Rossendale United boss |work=NonLeague Daily |date=15 June 2006 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] Over his three seasons with the club, he averaged 20 goals a season, was their leading scorer, and was player-manager from November 2008 to the end of the 2008–09 season, when he left the post by mutual consent after Rossendale finished bottom of the Northern Premier League Division One North.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rossendalefreepress.co.uk/sport/football/s/1081036_the_jobs_philed |title=The job's Phil-ed |newspaper=Rossendale Free Press |date=21 November 2008 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://www.rossendalefreepress.co.uk/sport/football/s/1112934_eastwood_and_united_part |title=Eastwood and United part |newspaper=Rossendale Free Press |date=1 May 2009 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] He then joined Bamber Bridge as a player.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bamberbridgefc.co.uk/news/090610.htm |title=Greenwood Makes His First Signings |publisher=Bamber Bridge F.C |date=10 June 2009 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
In January 2009, Eastwood joined Preston North End as technical youth development officer, to work with the youngest players in the club's centre of excellence.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.prestoncitizen.co.uk/sport/prestonnorthend/4063546.Rossendale_United_boss_handed_Preston_North_End_role/ |title=Rossendale United boss handed Preston North End role |newspaper=Preston and Leyland Citizen |date=21 January 2009 |accessdate=25 October 2009[/tpl]
