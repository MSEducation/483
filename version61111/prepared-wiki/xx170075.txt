[[Transcription factor II B]]

CATEGORIES: Molecular genetics, Proteins, Gene expression, Transcription factors

Transcription factor IIB (TFIIB) is one of several general transcription factors that make up the RNA polymerase II preinitiation complex.[tpl]cite book | author = Lewin, Benjamin | title = Genes VIII | publisher = Pearson Prentice Hall | location = Upper Saddle River, NJ | year = 2004 | pages = p636–637 | isbn = 0-13-144946-X | oclc = | doi = [/tpl] It is encoded by the [tpl]gene|TFIIB[/tpl] gene.[tpl]cite journal | author = Ha I, Lane WS, Reinberg D | title = Cloning of a human gene encoding the general transcription initiation factor IIB | journal = Nature | volume = 352 | issue = 6337 | pages = 689–95 | year = 1991 | pmid = 1876184 | doi = 10.1038/352689a0 | issn = [/tpl][tpl]cite journal | author = Heng HH, Xiao H, Shi XM, Greenblatt J, Tsui LC | title = Genes encoding general initiation factors for RNA polymerase II transcription are dispersed in the human genome | journal = Hum. Mol. Genet. | volume = 3 | issue = 1 | pages = 61–4 | year = 1994 | pmid = 8162052 | doi = 10.1093/hmg/3.1.61 | issn = [/tpl]
Transcription factor IIB localizes to the nucleus where it forms a complex (the DAB complex) with transcription factors IID and IIA. The protein serves as a bridge between transcription factor IID, which initially recognizes the promoter sequence, and RNA polymerase II.[tpl]cite web | title = Entrez Gene: GTF2B general transcription factor IIB| url = http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/sites/entrez?Db=gene&Cmd=ShowDetailView&TermToSearch=2959| accessdate = [/tpl] It is involved in the selection of the transcription start site; mutations in TFIIB cause a shift in the transcription start site.[tpl]cite journal | author = Lee TI, Young RA | title = Transcription of eukaryotic protein-coding genes | journal = Annu. Rev. Genet. | volume = 34 | issue = | pages = 77–137 | year = 2000 | pmid = 11092823 | doi = 10.1146/annurev.genet.34.1.77[/tpl]

==Interactions==

Transcription factor IIB makes protein-protein interactions with the TATA-binding protein (TBP) subunit of transcription factor IID,[tpl]cite journal  | author=Tang H, Sun X, Reinberg D, Ebright RH |title=Protein-protein interactions in eukaryotic transcription initiation: structure of the preinitiation complex |journal=Proc. Natl. Acad. Sci. U.S.A. |volume=93 |issue= 3 |pages= 1119–24 |year= 1996 |pmid= 8577725 |doi=10.1073/pnas.93.3.1119  | pmc=40041  [/tpl][tpl]cite journal |last=Bushnell |first=David A |authorlink= |coauthors=Westover KD, Davis RE, Kornberg RD |date=Feb 2004 |title=Structural basis of transcription: an RNA polymerase II-TFIIB cocrystal at 4.5 Angstroms |journal=Science |volume=303 |issue=5660 |pages=983–8 |publisher= |location = United States| issn = | pmid = 14963322 |doi = 10.1126/science.1090838 [/tpl] and the RPB1 subunit of RNA polymerase II.
Transcription Factor IIB makes sequence-specific protein-DNA interactions with the B recognition element (BRE), a promoter element flanking the TATA element.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Genes and Development|author=Lagrange T, Kapanidis AN, Tang H, Reinberg D, Ebright RH|year=1998|volume=12|issue=1|pages=34–44|title=New core promoter element in RNA polymerase II-dependent transcription: sequence-specific DNA binding by transcription factor IIB|pmid=9420329|doi=10.1101/gad.12.1.34|pmc=316406[/tpl][tpl]cite journal| journal=Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences of the USA| title=The structural basis for the oriented assembly of a TBP/TFB/promoter complex |year=1999 |author=Littlefield O, Korkhin Y, Sigler PB |volume=96 |issue=24 |pages=13668–13673 |pmid=10570130| doi=10.1073/pnas.96.24.13668| pmc=24122[/tpl]  The role of TFIIB in the vast number of human promoters lacking TATA and BRE elements remains unclear.
Recent study shows that TFIIB links transcription inhibition with the p53-dependent DNA damage response.[tpl]cite web|title=TFIIB dephosphorylation links transcription inhibition with the p53-dependent DNA damage response|url=http://www.pnas.org/content/early/2012/10/30/1207483109.abstract|accessdate=2 November 2012|doi=10.1073/pnas.1207483109[/tpl]
