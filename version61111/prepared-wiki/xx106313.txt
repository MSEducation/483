[[London Scottish Bank]]

CATEGORIES: Bank failures, Defunct banks of the United Kingdom, Banks disestablished in 2008, Companies that have entered administration in the United Kingdom

London Scottish Bank PLC was a British bank based in Manchester, specialising in subprime lending.

==About==

London Scottish Bank was a specialist financial services company that had been established for over a century. They had a nationwide network of high street branches.
The Group employed over 2,000 staff and operated in a range of markets, specialising in the provision of subprime and low income borrower loans. These services included consumer and commercial lending, secured and unsecured broking, debt collection and factoring.
In February 2008, the company announced they were stopping all lending.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/rbssFinancialServicesAndRealEstateNews/idUSL2614598220080226|title=UPDATE 2-London Scottish to stop lending, restructure|last=Wills|first=Chris|date=2008-02-26|publisher=Reuters|accessdate=7 January 2010[/tpl] In November 2008 the company entered administration.[tpl]cite news|url=http://business.timesonline.co.uk/tol/business/industry_sectors/banking_and_finance/article5265804.ece|title=London Scottish Bank Collapses|last=Pagnamenta |first=Robin|date=2008-12-01|publisher=The Times|accessdate=7 January 2010[/tpl]
Robinson Way, the Debt Collection arm of London Scottish Bank was subject to a management buy out in August 2009.
