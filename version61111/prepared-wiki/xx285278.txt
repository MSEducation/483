[[Flora Spencer-Longhurst]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, English television actresses, English stage actresses, 21st-century English actresses, 1985 births

Flora Spencer-Longhurst is an English stage and television actress.  She is best known for her role in the CBBC series Leonardo and the ITV series Unforgiven.

==Career==

Flora Spencer-Longhurst entered the National Youth Music Theatre at age eleven and had studied at Manchester University.  She made her television debut in 2005 in the BBC television series Dalziel and Pascoe which featured actor Warren Clarke. Her professional stage debut was in 2007 at the Young Vic Theatre in a critically acclaimed production of The Member of the Wedding where she played the part of Frankie Addams.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.britishtheatreguide.info/reviews/memberwedding-rev|title=Theatre review: The Member of the Wedding|publisher=britishtheatreguide.info[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.londontheatre.co.uk/peterbrown/reviews/memberofthewedding07.htm|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20130815130413/http://londontheatre.co.uk/peterbrown/reviews/memberofthewedding07.htm|archivedate=August 15, 2013|title=London Theatre Guide Review of The Member of the Wedding|publisher=londontheatre.co.uk|accessdate=December 15, 2013|deadurl=yes[/tpl]  Among her other stage credits was the role of Rosalie in the Lillian Helman play The Children's Hour at the The Royal Exchange Theatre. She had played the character Cecily in a musical adaptation of Oscar Wilde's The Importance of Being Earnest[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-reviews/8962117/The-Importance-of-Being-Earnest-Riverside-Studios-review.html|title=The Importance of Being Earnest Review|publisher=telegraph.co.uk[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.earnestthemusical.co.uk/cast-creative/|title=Cast and Creative|publisher=earnestthemusical.co.uk[/tpl] and had also performed the role of Belle in a stage version of A Christmas Carol at the King's Head Theatre. Her most recent stage role was that of Reza in the West End production of the musical Once.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lastminutetheatretickets.com/blog/index.php/43074/interview-with-flora-spencer-longhurst-from-the-cast-of-once/|title=Interview with Flora Spencer-Longhurst |publisher=lastminutetheatretickets.com|accessdate=December 23, 2013[/tpl]
Her most recent and probably best known television part was the dual role of Lisa/Tomaso in the CBBC series Leonardo where she played the part of a girl disguised as an apprentice male artist. Other roles in television have included Losing It with Martin Clunes and the 2006 pilot episode of Inspector Lewis. As Spencer-Longhurst has done more stage and theatre work, she has not had extensive film and television exposure.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2011/03_march/29/leonardo5.shtml|title=Press Office|publisher=bbc.co.uk[/tpl] Her motion picture debut Walking with the Enemy had won her a Best Supporting Actress award at the Fort Lauderdale International Film Festival and was released in April 2014.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fliff.com/|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20131126151249/http://www.fliff.com/|archivedate=November 26, 2013|title=FLIFF announces winners|publisher=Fort Lauderdale International Film Festival|accessdate=December 15, 2013|deadurl=no[/tpl]  Her other television work has had her working alongside actors such as Kenneth Branagh (Wallander), Peter Davison (Unforgiven), and Ben Kingsley (Walking with the Enemy).
