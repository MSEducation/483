Broderick Wright (born 24 July 1987) is a professional rugby league player for the Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks in the National Rugby League (NRL) competition. He plays  in the second-row.
After graduating from Baulkham Hills High School in 2005, Wright made his debut against the North Queensland Cowboys in round 20 of the 2008 NRL season. He was 18th man for Parramatta against Melbourne in the 2009 Grand Final as an injury stand-by for Nathan Cayless. Wright signed with the Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks for the 2010 NRL season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.news.com.au/dailytelegraph/sport/nrl/player/0,26796,110922-107,00.html|title=Broderick Wright|publisher=Daily Telegraph|date=2008-10-06|accessdate=2008-10-06[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=August 2010|bot=RjwilmsiBot[/tpl]
Wright was the first ever and so far only Parramatta Eels player to start his junior career playing the game for the Junior Eels, at a young age, and work his way up through the system into the First Grade side, playing for Parramatta junior teams Junior Eels, Harold Matts, SG Ball, and the Toyota Cup squad.
