[[Robert Siegel]]

CATEGORIES: 1947 births, Living people, American Jews, American radio journalists, NPR personalities, Columbia University alumni, Stuyvesant High School alumni, American broadcast news analysts, American reporters and correspondents

Robert Siegel is an American radio journalist best known as host of the National Public Radio evening news broadcast All Things Considered.

==Career==

Siegel's first professional broadcasting job was at WGLI in Babylon, New York, where he "did morning newscasts and a show that was part phone-ins, part Top Forty, all under the pseudonym Bob Charles."[tpl]cite journal |url=http://www.college.columbia.edu/cct/may_jun08/features1 |title=Three Moments with Robert Siegel '68 |first=John E |last=Mulligan |magazine=Columbia College Today |date=May–June 2008 |volume=35 |number=5[/tpl]  After a year at Columbia University Graduate School of Journalism, Siegel left academia for good and worked for WRVR in New York from 1971 to 1976.
Siegel was hired as a newscaster for NPR in Washington, D.C., in 1976, and he has held various news and production jobs at NPR since then.  Since 1987, he has been a host of All Things Considered. He took a short break in 1992 to host Talk of the Nation, NPR's call-in talk show.
In 2009, Siegel appeared in director James Kerwin's scifi noir film Yesterday Was a Lie.
On Tuesday, Nov. 16, 2010, Siegel was presented with Chancellor Award for Excellence in Journalism by the Journalism School of Columbia University.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.journalism.columbia.edu/cs/ContentServer/jrn/1165270069774/page/1212609905162/JRNSimplePage2.htm |website=The Journalism School of Columbia University |department=Journalism Awards |title=The Chancellor Award |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20101023043859/http://www.journalism.columbia.edu/cs/ContentServer/jrn/1165270069774/page/1212609905162/JRNSimplePage2.htm |archivedate=2010-10-23[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Siegel grew up in Stuyvesant Town—Peter Cooper Village, in New York City.[tpl]cite AV media |url=http://www.wnyc.org/audio/newsmag/m3u/atc/2013/apr/03/ |title=All Things Considered |date=3 April 2013 |time= |format=m3u |medium=Radio |publisher=National Public Radio |website=WNYC[/tpl] He is the son of Joseph, a commercial education teacher, and Edith, a secretary at Stuyvesant High School.  His maternal grandfather claimed to descend from rabbinical scholar Mordechai Yoffe [tpl]cite journal |url=http://momentmag.com/moment/issues/2012/10/GreatDNAExperiment.html |title=The Great DNA Experiment |magazine=Moment Magazine |first=Nadine |last=Epstein |date=20 September 2012 |department=Culture, Science[/tpl] and Siegel has identified on-air as Jewish. After graduating in 1964 from Stuyvesant,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=2101185 |title=Robert Siegel, NPR Biography |website=National Public Radio |accessdate=2007-11-01[/tpl] Siegel studied at Columbia University, graduating in 1968. During this time he was an anchor for the reporting of the 1968 Columbia demonstrations at the college radio station, WKCR-FM.
Siegel is married to Jane Siegel, who works for the United States Department of Commerce; they are the parents of two daughters, Erica Anne Siegel[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/01/08/fashion/weddings/08SIEG.html |newspaper=The New York Times |title=Erica Siegel and Mark Narron |date=8 January 2006[/tpl] and musician Leah Siegel.[tpl]cite web |author=All Songs Considered |url=http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=15960593 |title=Antibalas, The Besnard Lakes, Let's Go Sailing |website=National Public Radio |date=12 April 2007 |accessdate=2011-06-16[/tpl]
