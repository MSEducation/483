[[Wendell Pierce]]

CATEGORIES: 1966 births, Living people, American male film actors, American male television actors, Juilliard School alumni, Male actors from New Orleans, Louisiana, African-American male actors, 20th-century American male actors, 21st-century American male actors

Wendell Edward Pierce (born December 8, 1966) is an American actor, best known for his work in HBO dramas, including his portrayals of Detective Bunk Moreland in The Wire,  trombonist Antoine Batiste in Treme and "Michael Davenport" in Waiting to Exhale.

==Early life & education==

Pierce was born in New Orleans, Louisiana.  He was one of three sons born to a teacher and a decorated World War II veteran who worked as a maintenance engineer.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.metacritic.com/person/wendell-pierce?filter-options=movies|title=Wendell Pierce Biography|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]  His father's segregated Army unit helped U.S. Marines win the Battle of Saipan in 1944.  Pierce said of his father's experience:[tpl]Quote|text=“When the country wasn’t loving my father, my father was loving his country.  It’s the ultimate act of patriotism.”[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jane-levere/seen-and-heard-in-new-yor_b_574036.html |title=Seen and Heard in New York: Wendell Pierce, Cory Booker, Alan Rickman|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl][/tpl]
Pierce was raised in the black middle-class community of Pontchartrain Park.  It was the first African-American post-war suburb.  His father, along with many black veterans, bought into the neighborhood after returning home from the war.  The neighborhood was wiped out during Hurricane Katrina in 2005, including Pierce's family home, which was flooded with 14 feet of water.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.whereyat.com/neworleans/article-1925-wendell-pierces-new-.html|title=Pierce's New Orleans|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://m.hollywoodreporter.com/news/treme-wendell-pierce-supermarket-302181|title=Why "Treme" Star Wendell Pierce is Getting into the Supermarket Business|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]
After first attending Benjamin Franklin High School, Pierce graduated from the New Orleans Centre for Creative Arts (along with Wynton Marsalis, Branford Marsalis, Harry Connick Jr., and Lolis Eric Elie) where he was a Presidential Scholar of the Arts.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.katc.com/Global/story.asp?S=7812455|title=Actor wants to revive Pontchartrain Park|accessdate=2008-08-27|author=Associated Press|publisher=KATC Channel 3|year=2008|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5aORISSV6|archivedate=2008-08-27[/tpl] As a young actor, Pierce appeared in The Winter’s Tale at the Tulane Shakespeare Festival. He produced and hosted Think About It, a youth themed talk show, for the local NBC affiliate station, and also hosted a weekly jazz show on WYLD-FM RADIO called "Extensions from Congo Square."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.friendsofneworleans.org/uploads/FONOAspenEventNOLA%20Leaders.pdf|title=Wendell Pierce Award Winning Actor and President of the Pontchartrain Park Community Development Corp.|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]
He then attended the Juilliard School's Drama Division (Group 14: 1981–1985) where he graduated with a Bachelor of Fine Arts.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2007-2008/0709/index.php|title=Alumni News|publisher=The Juilliard School|date=September 2007|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20111111121334/http://juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2007-2008/0709/index.php|archivedate=2011-11-11[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/person/56769/Wendell-Pierce/biography|title=Wendell Pierce|work=All Movie Guide|publisher=The New York Times|accessdate=April 5, 2012[/tpl]

==Career==

Pierce has been in over 30 films, appeared in nearly 50 television shows and has performed in dozens of stage productions. He worked on the HBO dramas The Wire and Treme. He appeared in The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn – Part 2 as "J. Jenks". [tpl]cn|date=April 2014[/tpl]
For his role in Treme, Pierce learned to play the trombone, though relies on “sound double”, Stafford Agee, of the Rebirth Brass Band. Agee actually records the music, while Pierce shadows the sound.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.whereyat.com/neworleans/article-1925-wendell-pierces-new-.html|title=Pierce's New Orleans|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]
Pierce was nominated for an Independent Spirit Award for Best Male Lead for his portrayal of "Joe" in Four, playing a married, closeted gay man who steps out on his family with a young white man he met online. The film was released on September 13, 2013, around the same time that The Michael J. Fox Show debuted on NBC, in which Pierce played Michael J. Fox's character's boss until the show's cancellation some five months later.[tpl]cite web|url=http://online.wsj.com/article/PR-CO-20130716-908756.html|title=Controversial Award-Winning Film|accessdate=2013-07-22[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2013/06/21/nbc-announces-fall-premiere-dates-for-new-season-628112/20130621nbc01/|title=NBC Announces Fall Premiere Dates for New Season|date=June 21, 2013|work=The Futon Critic|accessdate=July 3, 2013[/tpl]

===Stage===

Pierce has been in numerous stage productions.  He was lauded for his performance as Holt Fay in Queenie at the John F. Kennedy Center.  He has performed  on Broadway in staged productions of The Piano Lesson, Serious Money and The Boys of Winter.  He has performed off Broadway in  The Cherry Orchard (for which he was nominated for a VIV Award for Lead Actor, Waiting for Godot (which was set on a New Orleans rooftop post-Hurricane Katrina, and Broke-ology, performed at Lincoln Center for the Performing Arts.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.lct.org/showBio.htm?id=188&creditId=1311 |title=Cast: Wendell Pierce |accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]
Other performances include Cymbeline (at The Public Theater),  The Good Times Are Killing Me, Two Gentlemen of Verona, Tis Pity She's a Whore and Ms. Ever's Boys performed at the ACT Theatre.
Pierce is also a theater producer, having produced the Broadway show, Clybourne Park.  The show was nominated for four Tony Awards.  It went on to win the Tony Award for Best Play in 2012.
[tpl]cite web |url=http://scenelouisiana.com/new-orleans-natives-bring-home-tony-award/2012/06/ |title=New Orleans Natives Bring Home Tony Award |accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]

===Radio===

In 2009, Pierce became the host of the nationally syndicated, Peabody Award-winning radio program, Jazz at Lincoln Center, which features live recordings from Jazz at Lincoln Center's House of Swing.

==Business & Philanthropy==

Pierce considers himself a "true capitalist" and a "classic entrepreneur."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.whereyat.com/neworleans/article-1925-wendell-pierces-new-.html|title=Pierce's New Orleans|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://m.hollywoodreporter.com/news/treme-wendell-pierce-supermarket-302181|title=Why "Treme" Star Wendell Pierce is Getting into the Supermarket Business|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]  In 2013, Fast Company named Pierce one of the "100 Most Creative People in Business."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fastcompany.com/3009230/most-creative-people-2013/98-wendell-pierce|title=100 Most Creative People in Business 2013 Business|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]

===Pontchartrain Park Community Development Corp.===

Pierce started the non-profit, Pontchartrain Park Community Development Corp., to build new affordable solar and geothermal homes in the area for families displaced by Hurricane Katrina.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.poolspanews.com/hurricanes/exploring-the-big-easy---wendell-pierce--hometown-hero.aspx | title = Exploring the Big Easy: Wendell Pierce, Hometown Hero | publisher = Pool & Spa News | accessdate = July 27, 2012[/tpl]

===Sterling Farms===

Inspired in part by Michelle Obama’s initiative to bring more supermarkets to “food deserts” – where residents lack easy access to fresh produce – Pierce, along with 2 partners (childhood friend, Troy Henry, and James Hatchett), started a chain of grocery stores, Sterling Farms, in the Ninth Ward of New Orleans in 2012.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/8723313/Tremes-Wendell-Pierce-interview.html |title=Treme's Wendell Pierce |accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]  Sterling Farms also has a convenience store division called "Sterling Express."  The stores are named after Sterling Henry, his business partner's father who ran a pharmacy for about 40 years at the Lower 9th Ward.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.npr.org/2012/03/20/149002193/wendell-pierce-on-the-wire-treme-and-food | title = Wendell Pierce on "The Wire," "Treme" and Food | publisher = NPR | accessdate = July 27, 2013[/tpl]  Sterling Farms opened its first store, a 30,000-square-foot supermarket, in Marreo, LA.

==Personal life==

Pierce is "tri-coastal," splitting his time between L.A., New York and New Orleans.[tpl]cite web | url = http://scenelouisiana.com/wendell-pierce-new-orleans-favorite-son/2012/08/ | title = New Orleans' Favorite Son | publisher = Scene Magazine | accessdate = July 27, 2013[/tpl]  New Orleans locals nicknamed him "Saint Wendell."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/8723313/Tremes-Wendell-Pierce-interview.html |title=Treme's Wendell Pierce |accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]  His favorite sports team is the New Orleans Saints.
Pierce is politically active.   He attended the 2012 Democratic National Convention, was one of President Barack Obama's top campaign fundraisers in 2012 (along with Harvey Weinstein and Eva Longoria), and once escorted Gwen Ifil to a White House State Dinner.[tpl]cite web |url=http://m.hollywoodreporter.com/news/treme-wendell-pierce-supermarket-302181|title=Why "Treme" Star Wendell Pierce is Getting into the Supermarket Business|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.politico.com/gallery/2012/09/celebrities-spotted-at-the-democratic-national-convention/000417-005557.html| work= Politico| title=Celebrities spotted at the Democratic National Convention | date=September 7, 2012 | first=Vivyan | last=Tran[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://bigstory.ap.org/article/obama-campaign-releases-list-top-fundraisers|title=Obama Campaign Releases List of Top Campaign Fundraisers|accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl]
When first cast in The Wire, Pierce and his cast mates doubted the show would be a hit: [tpl]Quote|text=''"I remember the first time we all sat around and watched the pilot. We all turned to each other and said, ‘Man, I don’t think this s--- is going anywhere.'"[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/tvandradio/8723313/Tremes-Wendell-Pierce-interview.html |title=Treme's Wendell Pierce |accessdate=2013-07-27[/tpl][/tpl]
