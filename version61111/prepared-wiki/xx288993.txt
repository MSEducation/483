==Company History==

Animoto was founded in 2006 by Jason Hsiao, Brad Jefferson, Steve Clifton and Tom Clifton because of the poor video quality found on the internet.Animoto: The No-Infrastructure Startup | Fast Company | Business + Innovation Animoto’s patented Cinematic Artificial Intelligence technology allows users to turn photos, video clips and music into video slideshows.[tpl]cite web|last=Summers|first=Nick|title=Reporter for The Next Web|url=http://thenextweb.com/insider/2013/01/14/animoto-celebrates-its-fifth-birthday-with-six-million-users-and-new-i-love-ny-video-style/|publisher=The Next Web|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] Animoto’s founders include former producers of ABC, MTV, VH1, Comedy Central and the Documentary Group.[tpl]cite web|last=Wilson|first=Sara|title=The Animoto Revolution: How this band of hipsters is taking photo sharing into the 21st century|url=http://www.entrepreneur.com/article/199000|publisher=Entrepreneur.com|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]
Animoto launched a Facebook application during the 2008 SXSW Interactive Festival. The application experienced viral growth in April 2008. According to the New York Times, by mid-April, Animoto's Facebook application had nearly 750,000 people sign up in three days. At the peak, almost 25,000 people tried Animoto in a single hour.[tpl]cite news|last=Fitzgerald|first=Michael|title=Cloud Computing: So You Don’t Have to Stand Still|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/05/25/technology/25proto.html?_r=0|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The New York Times|date=May 5, 2008[/tpl] Animoto decided to use Amazon's cloud computing servers in order to meet the growing number of subscribers.Animoto: When scalability becomes a matter of prosperity or death - The Next Web
In June 2009, the Animoto launched an iPhone app, allowing users to create video using pictures on their mobile phone.[tpl]cite web|last=Chang|first=Alexandra|title=Animoto's new mobile app promises easy video slideshow creation|url=http://www.macworld.com/article/1164385/animoto_mobile_app_promises_easy_video_slideshow_creation.html|publisher=MacWorld|accessdate=14 August 2013[/tpl]
By January 2013, the company had reached 6 million users and received its patent for Cinemtatic Artificial Intelligence technology.

==Investors==

Investors of Animoto include Amazon.com, Soft Tech, Bruce Livingstone (founder of iStockphoto and Stocksy United), Madrona Venture Group, and Spectrum Equity Investors.[tpl]cite news|last=Geron|first=Tomio|title=Online Video Creator Animoto Nabs $25 Million|url=http://www.forbes.com/sites/tomiogeron/2011/06/29/online-video-creator-animoto-nabs-25-million/|accessdate=14 August 2013|newspaper=Forbes Magazine|date=May 29, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Arrington|first=Michael|title=Animoto In It To Win It, Takes $25 Million In Third Venture Round|url=http://techcrunch.com/2011/06/29/animoto-in-it-to-win-it-takes-25-million-in-third-venture-round/|accessdate=14 August 2013|newspaper=Tech Crunch|date=June 29, 2011[/tpl] In 2009, Animoto received $4.4 million in series B funding from Madrona Venture Group, Amazon, SoftTech VC, Bruce Livingstone.Animoto Raises $4.4M, Will Add Video Soon — Tech News and Analysis By 2011, the company raised $25 million in series C funding led by Spectrum Equity Investors, Madrona Venture Group and Amazon.

==Awards==

Animoto gained considerable  attention from the press because of the company's acceptance speech at the Webby Awards. The Huffington Post ranked Animoto's acceptance speech in their "Best 5-Word Webby Speeches" of the Webby Awards Ceremony.[tpl]cite news|last=Hibbard|first=Laura|title=The Best 5-Word Webby Speeches|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/06/07/best-webby-speeches-videos_n_872562.html#s288473title=18_Animotos_Five|accessdate=14 August 2013|newspaper=Huffington Post|date=June 7, 2013[/tpl]  Animoto's CFO, Nathan Meagher, wore a wig and disrobed to leopard skin tights in front of the audience and Webby Awards host, Seth Meyers. Meagher than screamed, "Thank you, New York... WOOOOO," because of the 5 word maximum speech limit at the awards ceremony.
