[[Franco Scepi]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing (living people), Living people, Italian artists

Franco Scepi is an Italian artist and film director. He directed the art film Can Cannes.[tpl]cite book |title=Dizionario del cinema italiano |last=Chiti |first=Roberto |coauthors=Poppi, Roberto |year=2000 |publisher=Gremese Editore |isbn=88-7742-423-0 |pages=107 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=3ZSEf-WwH7UC&pg=PA107&dq=%22Franco+Scepi%22&num=100&client=firefox-a&sig=iofD2PiNHjCqlh76b1q5si8yf3Y |accessdate=2008-06-23[/tpl]
In 1999, Mikhail Gorbachev and the Nobel Peace Laureates added their signatures to Scepi’s image Man for Peace,[tpl]cite book |title=Cronica ONU |last= |first= |year=2003 |publisher=United Nations Publications |isbn=970-661-225-4 |pages=76 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=WvI903cH1agC&pg=PA76&dq=%22Franco+Scepi%22&num=100&client=firefox-a&sig=vWE_yfoPSOdysuWelnD-ncpKD9I |accessdate=2008-06-23[/tpl] created in 1977 for the poster of the film Man of Marble by Andrzej Wajda, inspired by Karol Wojtyła and defined as the symbol that foresaw the fall of the Berlin Wall. With the support of the MAGI Museum, several copies of the monument have been acquired by towns and art galleries.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2008[/tpl]
