[[United Congregational Church of Irondequoit]]

CATEGORIES: Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1910, Churches in Rochester, New York

United Congregational Church of Irondequoit, also known as Irondequoit United Church of Christ, is a historic Congregational church complex located at Rochester in Monroe County, New York.  The complex consists of three interconnected buildings: the Colonial Revival style church (1926), Woman's Christian Temperance Union hall (1910), and church school.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=101396|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: United Congregational Church of Irondequoit|date=February 2002|accessdate=2009-10-01 |author=Robert T. Englert|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=9876|title=Accompanying 11 photos[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2002.
