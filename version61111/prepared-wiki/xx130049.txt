Adriano Basso (born 18 April 1975) is a Brazilian footballer who plays as a goalkeeper.
Basso began his playing career in his native Brazil before entering English league football with Bristol City for whom he remained for five years. After being released in 2010, he signed a short term deal with Wolverhampton Wanderers the following year.

==Career==

Basso, a devout Christian,[tpl]cite web|date=12 March 2008|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/7289241.stm|title='Hand of God' helps City stay top|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl] previously played for Ponte Preta and Atlético Paranaense in his homeland before moving to England. He had an unsuccessful three-month trial with Arsenal before joining non-league sides St Albans City and Woking.[tpl]cite web|date=23 May 2008|url=http://www.mirror.co.uk/sport/football/2008/05/23/bride-boom-89520-20426347/|title=Championship Play-off Final preview: Bristol City vs Hull|publisher=The Mirror[/tpl]
He eventually signed for League One side Bristol City shortly after the 2005–06 campaign had begun, making his first team debut against Swindon Town on 11 November 2005.[tpl]cite web|date=11 November 2005|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/4409546.stm|title=Swindon 2–1 Bristol City|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl] He quickly established himself as the club's first choice goalkeeper and was part of the team that won promotion to the Championship in the following season, also being crowned BBC West's Footballer of the Year.[tpl]cite web|date=3 December 2007|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/tv_and_radio/sports_personality_of_the_year/7113369.stm|title=City boss Johnson lands top award|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl]
The 2007–08 season saw Basso came within one game of reaching the Premier League, but the Robins lost the Championship Play-off Final to Hull City. 
He remained the club's number one for the following campaign that saw a mid-table finish, but in July 2009 it was reported that he had rejected a new contract and subsequently handed in a transfer request.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bcfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10327~1729070,00.html|title=Adriano submits transfer request|publisher=Bristol City|date=21 July 2009[/tpl] Despite rumours of moves to other clubs in the close season, Basso remained at the club for the majority of the 2009–10 season, but made only four appearances, as relations soured between him and the club.[tpl]cite web|date=3 August 2009|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/8182513.stm|title=Basso not in our plans – Johnson|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl][tpl]cite web|date=30 November 2009|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/8386722.stm|title=Bristol City banish keeper Basso|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl] On 5 March 2010, his contract was terminated, ending his five-year stay at the club.[tpl]cite web|date=5 March 2010|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/8552667.stm|title=Bristol City terminate contract of keeper Adriano Basso|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl]
Basso remained a free agent until 31 January 2011, when he joined Premier League side Wolverhampton Wanderers on a short-term contract until the end of the season, after having trained with the club for two weeks.[tpl]cite web|date=31 January 2011|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/bristol_city/9382820.stm|title=Bristol City sign Wolves striker Andy Keogh on loan|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl] Following the end of his contract at Wolves, Basso was a free agent for a short while before moving to Hull City on a one-year contract, with the option of a second in the club's favour, after a successful trial. He made his debut on 9 August 2011 at the KC Stadium in a 0–2 defeat by Macclesfield Town in the League Cup.[ref]
