[[Anuj Dhar]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing (living people), Living people, Indian journalists, Indian writers

Anuj Dhar is an Indian author and former journalist.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.historytoday.com/hugh-purcell/subhas-chandra-bose-afterlife-india%E2%80%99s-fascist-leader | title=Subhas Chandra Bose: The Afterlife of India’s Fascist Leader | publisher=History Today, Volume: 60 Issue: 11 2010 | accessdate=7 November 2013 | author=Hugh Purcell[/tpl] Dhar has published several books on the  death of Subhas Chandra Bose which occurred on 18 August 1945, when a Japanese plane carrying him crashed in Japanese-occupied Taiwan. Dhar claims in his books that there was no air crash and that Bose actually died in the 1980s after living as hermit monk in Faizabad.[tpl]cite web|last=Kirpal|first=Raman|title=Why Subhas Chandra Bose's death is India's 'biggest cover-up'|publisher=First Post India|date= 12 July 2012|url=http://www.firstpost.com/india/why-subhas-chandra-boses-death-is-indias-biggest-cover-up-375766.html[/tpl] Dhar is also the founder-trustee of New Delhi based not for profit organisation Mission Netaji.
Dhar and Mission Netaji investigate the claims that Bose lived in the Uttar Pradesh state of India as a hermit till 1985. [tpl]cite web | url=http://www.rediff.com/news/2006/mar/17spec.htm | title=Netaji did not die in aircrash, says web site | publisher=Rediff.com | date=18 March 2006 | accessdate=7 November 2013[/tpl] 
In 2005, the Taiwan government provided emails to Dhar that it has no records of a plane crash during the period of August 14 to October 25, 1945, at the old Matsuyama Airport (now Taipei Domestic Airport).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.outlookindia.com/pti_news.asp?id=277465 |title=No crash at Taipei that killed Netaji: Taiwan govt |publisher=Outlook India |place=Kolkata |date=3 February 2005 |accessdate=7 November 2013[/tpl]
