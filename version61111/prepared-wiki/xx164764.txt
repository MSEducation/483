[[Thaddeus Parker]]

CATEGORIES: United States Navy sailors, Recipients of the Navy Cross, Recipients of the Silver Star, Recipients of the Purple Heart medal, American military personnel killed in World War II, 1923 births, 1943 deaths, People from Florida

Thaddeus Parker was born on 13 November 1923 in Cross City, Florida.

==Navy career==

Parker enlisted in the United States Navy on 27 June 1941. Upon completion of basic training, Parker was assigned to the U.S. Naval Hospital, Norfolk, Virginia, as a Hospitalman Apprentice on 30 August. On 31 October 1941, he was stationed at the Marine Corps Base, Quantico, Virginia, where he served as a hospital corpsman in the U.S. Naval Hospital, and, after 29 March 1942, in the First Raider Battalion, Fleet Marine Force.

==Namesake==

USS Thaddeus Parker (DE-369) was named in his honor. She was laid down on 23 May 1944 at Orange, Texas, by Consolidated Steel Corp.; launched on 26 August 1944; sponsored by Miss Iva Lee Parker; and commissioned on 25 October 1944, Lt. Comdr. Duane R. Stoneleigh, USNR, in command.
