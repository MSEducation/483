[[Vahase]]

CATEGORIES: Uninhabited islands of Estonia, Kaarma Parish, Estonian islands in the Baltic

Vahase is a [tpl]convert|65.52|ha|acre|abbr=on[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Vahase saar|url=http://loodus.keskkonnainfo.ee/webeelis/infoleht.aspx?obj=saar&id=1597638934|publisher=EELIS â€“ Estonian Nature Infosystem|accessdate=24 May 2011|language=Estonian[/tpl] Estonian islet in the Gulf of Riga. It's located about [tpl]convert|200|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] west of the island of Abruka. Administratively Vahase belongs to the Abruka village in Kaarma Parish, Saare County.
Vahase arose from the sea about thousand years ago. The island has a tiny forest with junipers, oaks, and pines.
Because of shallow water Vahase is accessible from Abruka by foot. There's only one farmstead on the island.
Writer Albert Uustulnd has described Vahase in his novel Tuulte tallermaa.
