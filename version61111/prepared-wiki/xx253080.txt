</ref> He often collaborated with original film director [[Gil Junger]] on various ABC Family projects, where he regularly quizzed Junger for information about the movie.  These discussions with Junger, coupled with Covington's desire to create a "reimagined"<ref name=NYT/> TV version of the movie, led to the creation of a [[Television pilot|pilot]] episode of ''[[10 Things I Hate About You (TV series)|10 Things I Hate About You]]'', which Junger directed.  On July 7, 2009, the series premiere brought in 1.60 million viewer, a record for a 30-minute comedy debut on the ABC Family network.<ref name="tvbythenumbers.com">[tpl]cite web|title=ABC Family's "10 Things I Hate About You" Delivers Record Performance in Series Debut|url=http://tvbythenumbers.com/2009/07/08/abc-familys-10-things-i-hate-about-you-delivers-record-performance-in-series-debut/22516|work=TVbytheNumbers|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5iQGqnQwi|archivedate=2009-07-20|last=Gorman|first=Bill|date=July 8, 2009|deadurl=no|accessdate=2009-07-11[/tpl]</ref> The series was canceled after one twenty-episode season.
