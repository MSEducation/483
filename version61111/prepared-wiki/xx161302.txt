[[George Tisdale Hodges]]

George Tisdale Hodges (July 4, 1789 â€“ August 9, 1860) was an American politician. He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Early life==

Hodges was born in Clarendon, Vermont and attended the common schools. He was involved in the banking industry in Rutland, Vermont, and served as president of the Bank of Rutland for over twenty-five years.

==Career==

Hodges served as a member of the Vermont House of Representatives from 1827 to 1829, 1839 and 1840.[tpl]cite web|url= http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=H000673|title=HODGES, George Tisdale, (1789 - 1860)|publisher= Biographical Directory of the United States Congress|accessdate= October 10, 2012[/tpl] He served in the Vermont State Senate from 1845 to 1847 and was President pro tempore in 1846 and 1847.[tpl]cite web|url= http://politicalgraveyard.com/geo/VT/RU-buried.html|title=George Tisdale Hodges (1789-1860) |publisher= The Political Graveyard|accessdate= October 10, 2012[/tpl]
He was a Whig Presidential Elector for Vermont in 1848.  He became a Republican when that party was founded, and in 1856 was elected to the Thirty-fourth Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of James Meacham. He served from December 1, 1856 to March 3, 1857.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/george_hodges/405527|title=  Rep. George Hodges|publisher= govtrack.us |accessdate= October 10, 2012[/tpl] He was not a candidate for renomination in 1856.

==Death==

Hodges died on August 9, 1860 in Rutland. He is interred at Evergreen Cemetery in Rutland.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=7180778|title= George Tisdale Hodges|publisher= Find A Grave |accessdate= October 10, 2012[/tpl]
