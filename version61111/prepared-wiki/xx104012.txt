[[Jayaram Padikkal]]

CATEGORIES: Indian police chiefs, Indian police officers, People from Kerala, Living people

R. Jayaram Padikkal was a police official in India. He had served as the Director General of Police (DGP), the highest rank in the force in the period 1993-94.[tpl]cite web|title=Our Chiefs till Now|url=http://www.keralapolicehistory.com/chief.html|publisher=keralapolicehistory.com|accessdate=2 June 2012[/tpl]

==Early Years==

Jayaram Padikkal I.P.S. was born in Peruvemba village Palghat in Kerala as the son of an Indian Army officer and had his education in Pune. He was said to be a good hockey player and a voracious reader, especially history, crime and philosophy and later joined the Indian Police Service.
In 1982 he was made the Deputy Inspector General of Police (DIG), for the state of Kerala and thus became the youngest DIG in the country.http://pages.rediff.com/jayaram-padikkal/965655#!info[tpl]cite book|author=Kerala (India). Vigilance Dept|title=Administration report|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Z6C2AAAAIAAJ|accessdate=27 May 2012|year=1984|publisher=Govt. of Kerala[/tpl] He was one of the few Indian Police officers to have received training at the Scotland Yard. He was a recipient of President's Police Medal.

==Controversy==

When he was Deputy Inspector General (Crime Branch) of Kerala police,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2005/10/11/stories/2005101112940300.htm|title='Police station attack was a conspiracy'|date=October 11, 2005|work=The Hindu|accessdate=2 June 2012[/tpl] he was accused of complicity in the torture and death of a student named Rajan at the hands of the police during the period of The Emergency.
