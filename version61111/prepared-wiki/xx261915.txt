==Career==

Schwartz graduated from Palisades Charter High School.[tpl]cite news|url=http://top40-charts.com/news.php?nid=42720|title=New Artist Lucy Schwartz Writes & Performs Two Songs For 'The Women' Original Motion Picture Soundtrack|date=August 9, 2008|publisher=Top40-Charts.com|accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl] She attended Occidental College, but dropped out after one semester to pursue her career in music.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.motherjones.com/mixed-media/2013/05/lucy-schwartz-arrested-development-song-boomerang|title=Meet Lucy Schwartz, The Artist Behind The Awesome Song at The End of Arrested Development|last=Suebsaeng|first=Asawin|date=May 31, 2013|work=Mother Jones|accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]
In 2010, Schwartz released her sophomore album, Life in Letters.
In 2011, she had a duet with Aqualung on The Twilight Saga: Breaking Dawn and released the EP, "Keep Me". In December, Act As If released a Christmas single, "Sleighbells & Reindeer", featuring Schwartz with Brian Fennell of Barcelona.
Schwartz has toured with Lilith Fair,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.verbicidemagazine.com/2010/12/02/interview-lucy-schwartz|title=Interview: Lucy Schwartz|last= Schofner |first=Heather|date=December 2, 2010|work=Verbicide Magazine|accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl] alongside Sarah McLachlan and Sheryl Crow, and opened for The Weepies, Brandi Carlile, The Civil Wars, Agnes Obel, Toad the Wet Sprocket, A Fine Frenzy, Joshua Radin, and The Belle Brigade.
Her songs have been featured in over a dozen television programs, including Grey's Anatomy and Army Wives. Schwartz wrote the international theme song for NBC's show, Parenthood, and she has written original songs for numerous films, including Twilight, Shrek Forever After, Mother and Child, Post Grad, Monte Carlo, Adam, and the Meg Ryan comedy, The Women.
Her single, "Boomerang", appeared in the closing credits of the fourth season finale of the television series Arrested Development, for which her father, David Schwartz, composed the soundtrack. She had previously performed the vocal recordings "Mr. F" and "For British Eyes Only" in a faux-English accent for the series.
Schwartz performed a minor role in the second season premiere of House of Lies, singing The Carpenters's song, "Close to You".[tpl]cite web|url=http://lucyschwartzmusic.com/2013/01/08/lucy-schwartz-acts-sings-on-house-of-lies|title=Lucy Schwartz Acts and Sings on House of Lies|date=January 8, 2013[/tpl]
"Black Roses," a song written by Schwartz, is sung by the character of Scarlett O'Connor (portrayed by Clare Bowen) in the TV show Nashville.
She has worked with CHOC Children's Hospital, Rock 'n' Roll Camp for Girls, Songs of Love for Japan, and Symphony of Hope: The Haiti Project.
