CATEGORIES: 1989 births, Living people, People from Gosport, English footballers, Portsmouth F.C. players, Dagenham & Redbridge F.C. players, Notts County F.C. players, Swindon Town F.C. players, A.F.C. Bournemouth players, Premier League players, The Football League players

Matthew Thomas "Matt" Ritchie (born 10 September 1989) is an English football player, who plays as an inverted right winger for Bournemouth.

==Career==

===Portsmouth===

Ritchie has been involved in Portsmouth's academy since he turned 13. Before-hand he was playing football locally in Gosport when he started at age six.[tpl]cite news|title=Ritchie in dream land|url=http://www.portsmouth.co.uk/sport/pompey/ritchie-in-dream-land-1-1299296|publisher=Portsmouth News|date=13 August 2008|accessdate=12 February 2013[/tpl] After signing his first professional contract with the club,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.portsmouthfc.co.uk/index.php?cms_ref=news&qs_article_id=1315 |title=Portsmouth Football Club :: News |publisher=Portsmouthfc.co.uk |date=1 July 2008 |accessdate=2 November 2008[/tpl] and a positive 2008–09 pre-season campaign, including a hat-trick in a 4–1 victory over hometown club Gosport Borough,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11095_3967462,00.html |title=Sky Sports | Football | News | Pompey see off Gosport |publisher=Skysports.com |date= |accessdate=2 November 2008[/tpl] After the match, Ritchie says scoring a hat-trick was unreal.
Ritchie was taken on trial alongside fellow academy graduate Joel Ward by Bournemouth with a view to a loan move to help give the youngsters important first-team experience in league football. Ritchie was however kept at Portsmouth following a number of injuries to first-team midfielders. He was called up to Portsmouth's UEFA Cup squad as a home-grown talent and presented with the squad number 36.
However, due his parent club Portsmouth's mounting injury list, he was recalled in March and was named on the bench for their 2–0 Premier League loss at the hands of Tottenham Hotspur after making four appearances for Swindon in his first loan. On 14 April, Ritchie made his debut for Portsmouth in the Premier League game at Wigan Athletic. He played 79 minutes but was then substituted for Frédéric Piquionne. Afrer the match, Ritchie admits making his Portsmouth debut was a dream come true.[tpl]cite news|title=Ritchie: It was a dream come true|url=http://www.portsmouth.co.uk/sport/pompey/ritchie-it-was-a-dream-come-true-1-1249529|publisher=Portsmouth News|date=15 February 2010|accessdate=12 February 2013[/tpl] At the end of the season, with Portsmouth relegation to the Premier League, Ritchie signed a new two-year deal.[tpl]cite news|title=Ritchie set for Pompey stay|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/6221148/|publisher=Sky Sports|date=22 June 2010|accessdate=12 February 2013[/tpl]
For the 2010–11 season, Ritchie was handed the number 5 shirt and was selected in Portsmouth's starting XI for their first match of the season at Coventry City.Coventry   2 – 0   Portsmouth  However, Ritchie soon found himself out of the Portsmouth line-up and, with the club having disbanded its reserve team, manager Steve Cotterill agreed to loan out the player for fitness reasons.Cotterill: Ritchie has a Blues future

===Loan to Dagenham & Redbridge===

Speaking after the match against Barnet, Still had to say: "He's got a terrific leap. He showed that with his goal. He's come in and done very well. The Nicest kid in the world. Come down from a Premier League club. No big time about him. Harry Redknapp and Paul Hart speak ever so well of him. It's a pleasure to have him. He's got a smile on his face in training and loves being here." 
Still has also made it clear he wishes to extend the loan deal of Ritchie until later on in the season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.teamtalk.com/football/story/0,16368,1782_4344933,00.html |title=Daggers boss keen on Ritchie stay|publisher=Teamtalk.com |date= |accessdate=2 November 2008|archiveurl=https://web.archive.org/web/20081021174750/http://www.teamtalk.com/football/story/0,16368,1782_4344933,00.html|archivedate=21 October 2008[/tpl][tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010[/tpl] Dagenham announced on 30 December that Ritchie would remain with the club until the end of the current season.[tpl]cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/d/dagenham_and_redbridge/7804737.stm | work=BBC News | title=Daggers extend Ritchie loan spell | date=30 December 2008 | accessdate=12 May 2010[/tpl] His impressive performances have seen him rewarded with a new two-year deal at Portsmouth,[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11674_5262599,00.html | work=Sky Sports | title=Pompey offer Ritchie new deal | date=1 May 2009[/tpl] and their young player of the Season Award.[tpl]cite news|title=Hat-trick of honours for Johnson|url=http://www.portsmouth.co.uk/sport/pompey/hat-trick-of-honours-for-johnson-1-1232220|publisher=Portsmouth News|date=20 May 2009|accessdate=12 February 2013[/tpl]
