[[Empire Hotel, Bath]]

CATEGORIES: Hotel buildings completed in 1901, Grade II listed buildings in Bath, Somerset, Buildings and structures in Bath, Somerset, Hotels in Somerset, Grade II listed hotels

The Empire Hotel in Bath, Somerset, England was built in 1901 and has been designated as a Grade II listed building.[tpl]cite web|title=Empire Hotel|url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/Details/Default.aspx?id=448647|work=Images of England|publisher=English Heritage|accessdate=31 December 2010[/tpl] It is situated on Orange Grove close to both Bath Abbey and Pulteney Bridge.
It was designed by the Bath City Architect Major Charles Edward Davis for the hotelier Alfred Holland and built from Bath Stone, on the site of the Athenaeum. It was described by Sir Nikolaus Pevsner as a ‘monstrosity and an unbelievable piece of pompous architecture’.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ssb.plymouth.ac.uk/labplus/science/Bath/EmpireHotel.htm | title=Empire Hotel |work=Bath Virtual Fieldtrip | publisher=Plymouth University | accessdate=26 November 2012[/tpl]
The building occupies a large L shaped block. It is six storeys high plus the octagonal corner tower. The front of the building onto Orange Grove has eight bays and the side overlooking the River Avon has nine bays.
The architecture of the roof shows the three classes of people, Castle on the corner for Upper Class, A House for the Middle classes and a cottage for the lower classes.
During World War II it was used by the Royal Navy as a postal sorting offices and remained in their possession until the 1990s.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.bath-heritage.co.uk/empire.html|title=Empire Hotel | publisher=Bath Heritage |accessdate=26 November 2012[/tpl] It was then was refurbished and became apartments and a restaurant.[tpl]cite web | url=http://madisonoakley.wordpress.com/2011/02/21/the-empire-hotel-in-bath/ | title=The Empire Hotel in Bath | publisher=Madison Oakley |accessdate=26 November 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://retirementhousing.co.uk/pages/search/scheme.asp?dev=3096 | title=The Empire | publisher=Retirment Housing | accessdate=26 November 2012[/tpl]
