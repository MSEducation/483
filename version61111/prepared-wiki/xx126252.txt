[[Newell Hall (Gainesville, Florida)]]

CATEGORIES: National Register of Historic Places in Alachua County, Florida, Buildings at the University of Florida, William Augustus Edwards buildings

Newell Hall is a historic site in Gainesville, Florida, United States. It is located in the northeastern section of the University of Florida. On June 27, 1979, it was added to the U.S. National Register of Historic Places.

==Namesake==

Newell Hall is named for Wilmon Newell, the Provost of Agriculture at the University of Florida from 1921 until his death in October 1943.
