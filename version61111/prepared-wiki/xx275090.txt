[[Charlie Honaker]]

CATEGORIES: 1899 births, 1974 deaths, People from Greenup County, Kentucky, Players of American football from Kentucky, Ohio State Buckeyes football players, Cleveland Bulldogs players

Charles Franklin "Honey" Honaker (October 11, 1899 â€“ April 21, 1974) was a professional American football player, who played in 5 games, in the early National Football League for the Cleveland Bulldogs in 1924. He was a member of the Bulldogs 1924 NFL Championship team. Prior to his professional career, he played at the college level for Virginia Military Institute and the Ohio State Buckeyes, where he lettered for the team in 1921, 1922 and 1923. In 1921, he was given the nickname "Honey" by the Buckeyes fans. According to an October 7, 1922 game program between Ohio State and Ohio Wesleyan University, Honaker's hometown was Huntington, West Virginia.
