[[Pope John Paul II Park Reservation]]

CATEGORIES: State parks of Massachusetts, Massachusetts natural resources, Parks in Suffolk County, Massachusetts, Pope John Paul II

Pope John Paul II Park Reservation, also known as Pope Park, is a [tpl]convert|66|acre|adj=on[/tpl] Massachusetts state park bordering the Neponset River in the Dorchester section of Boston. The park has been reclaimed from the former site of a landfill and the Neponset Drive-In as part of the Lower Neponset River Master Plan and the development of the Neponset River Reservation.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mass.gov/eea/agencies/dcr/massparks/region-boston/neponset-river-reservation.html |title=Neponset River Reservation |work=MassParks |publisher=Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs, Department of Conservation and Recreation |accessdate=September 9, 2013[/tpl] The park is managed by the Department of Conservation and Recreation (DCR).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mass.gov/eea/agencies/dcr/massparks/region-boston/pope-john-paul-ii-park-reservation.html |title=Pope John Paul II Park Reservation |work=MassParks |publisher=Executive Office of Energy and Environmental Affairs, Department of Conservation and Recreation |accessdate=September 9, 2013[/tpl]
DCR has restored a salt marsh area and planted native trees and shrubs. The area now attracts an increased variety of birds to the habitat, including snowy egrets and great blue herons.
