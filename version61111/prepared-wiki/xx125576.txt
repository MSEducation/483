[[Portunus trituberculatus]]

CATEGORIES: Portunoidea, Edible crustaceans, Commercial crustaceans, Indo-Pacific crustaceans, Animals described in 1876

[tpl]Italic title[/tpl]

Portunus trituberculatus, the gazami crab, Japanese blue crab or horse crab, is the most widely fished species of crab in the world. It is found off the coasts of East Asia and is closely related to Portunus pelagicus.

==Fishery==

P. trituberculatus is the world's most heavily fished crab species, with over 300,000Â tonnes being caught annually, 98% of it off the coast of China.[tpl]cite web|title=FAO fisheries global information system|url=http://www.fao.org/figis/servlet/FiRefServlet?ds=species&fid=2630|accessdate=August 2, 2006[/tpl]

==Distribution==

P. trituberculatus is found off the coasts of Japan, Korea, China and Taiwan.[tpl]cite web |url=http://species-identification.org/species.php?species_group=crabs_of_japan&menuentry=soorten&id=1106&tab=beschrijving |title=Portunus trituberculatus |work=Crabs of Japan |publisher=Marine Species Identification Portal |accessdate=March 20, 2011[/tpl]

==Description==

The carapace may reach [tpl]convert|15|cm[/tpl] wide, and [tpl]convert|7|cm|abbr=on[/tpl] from front to back. P. trituberculatus may be distinguished from the closely related (and also widely fished) P. pelagicus by the number of broad teeth on the front of the carapace (3 in P. trituberculatus, 4 in P. pelagicus) and on the inner margin of the merus (4 in P. trituberculatus, 3 in P. pelagicus).

==Taxonomy==

P. trituberculatus was first described by Edward J. Miers in 1876, under the name Neptunus trituberculatus.[tpl]cite web |author=Peter Davie |year=2010 |title=Portunus (Portunus) trituberculatus (Miers, 1876) |publisher=World Register of Marine Species |url=http://www.marinespecies.org/aphia.php?p=taxdetails&id=208796 |accessdate=May 6, 2011[/tpl]
