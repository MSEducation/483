[[Trams in Düsseldorf]]

CATEGORIES: Tram transport in Germany, Transport in Düsseldorf

The Düsseldorf tramway network ([tpl]lang-de|Straßenbahnnetz Düsseldorf[/tpl]) is a network of tramways serving Düsseldorf, the capital city of the federal state of North Rhine-Westphalia, Germany.  In combination with the Düsseldorf Stadtbahn and Rhine-Ruhr S-Bahn, it forms the backbone of the public transport system in Düsseldorf.
The tramway network is currently operated by Rheinbahn AG.  [tpl]As of|2012[/tpl], its eleven tram lines ran over [tpl]convert|78.0|km|mi[/tpl] of route, serving 178 stops.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rheinbahn.de/unternehmen/Zahlen%20%20Berichte/Rheinbahn_in_Zahlen.pdf |title=Zahlenübersicht Stand 31. Dezember 2012 |publisher=Rheinbahn |language=German |trans_title=Figures Summary as of December 31, 2012 |page=7 |type=pdf |date=December 31, 2012 |accessdate=2013-10-19[/tpl]

==History==

In 1876, the first horse-drawn tram line opened in Düsseldorf, joining Castle Square with the Bergisch- Märkischen station and the concert hall.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rheinbahn.de/unternehmen/geschichte/1914_1920/Seiten/1920.aspx |title=1920: Das Ende der Düsseldorfer Straßenbahn |publisher=Rheinbahn |language=German |trans_title=1920: The end of the Düsseldorf tram company |date= |accessdate=2013-10-19[/tpl] In 1896, the first electric tram ran in Düsseldorf, and the full conversion of the system to electricity continued through 1900.

==Lines==

[tpl]As of|2012[/tpl], eleven tram lines running on [tpl]convert|78.0|km|mi[/tpl] of route, and serving 178 stops, operate in the city of Düsseldorf and in portions of the urban areas of Neuss and Ratingen:
A 3.4-kilometre extension of the Düsseldorf Stadtbahn, called the Wehrhahn-Linie, is currently under construction, and projected to open in Fall 2015. When this extension goes in to service, a realignment of the tram lines in Düsseldorf is planned, including the replacement of three tram lines (703, 712 and 713) by new Stadtbahn service.

==Current fleet==

The current tram fleet is a mix of older trams from Duewag, and new trams from Siemens.
