[[Wenche Olsen]]

CATEGORIES: Labour Party (Norway) politicians, People from Halden, Living people, 1965 births, Members of the Parliament of Norway

Wenche Olsen (born in 1965 in Halden) is a Norwegian politician and member of the Storting who represents the Norwegian Labour Party. Olsen was nominated as the Labour Party's fourth candidate for Østfold at the Norwegian parliamentary election, 2009,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tv2nyhetene.no/valg09/velgerguiden/fylke/ostfold/ap/wenche-olsen/|title=Velgerguiden: Wenche Olsen|publisher=TV2 Nyhetene|language=Norwegian|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] and won a seat after a strong performance in that county.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nrk.no/nyheter/distrikt/ostfold/valg_2009_ostfold/1.6775767|title=Dette er de nye fjesene|date=15 September 2009|publisher=NRK Østfold|language=Norwegian|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] Her issues in the campaign were on healthcare, in particular she supported the construction of a new hospital in Halden.[tpl]cite web|url=http://ostfold.arbeiderparti.no/Valg-2009/Nytt-sykehus-og-arbeid|title=Nytt sykehus og arbeid|publisher=Østfold Labour Party|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=November 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
Prior to her election into national level politics, Olsen was elected to the municipality council in Halden between 1999 and 2003, she had already been a deputy member from 1995. From 2007 until 2009, she was deputy mayor of Halden.Stortinget biography (see external link) She has also been the leader of the Halden division of the LO trade union confederation.
Apart from politics, Olsen started her career as a cleaner at Halden sykehus in 1982, and she has since advanced to head of the cleaning and textile department at Sykehuset Østfold from 1999. She held that position up until her election to parliament and is now on leave from that job.
