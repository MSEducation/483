Gavin Victor Holligan (born 30 June 1980 in Lambeth) is an English former footballer and musician.

==Football career==

Holligan turned down a university scholarship to study music in order to play football.[tpl]cite book|last=Belton|first=Brian|title=The Black Hammers|year=2000|publisher=Pennant Books|location=London|isbn=0-9550394-5-2|pages=222–223[/tpl] He started his footballing career with Walton and Hersham in August 1997 and at one stage had 18 of 20 Premier League teams representatives watching him play. Having impressed in non-league football he made a £150,000 move from Conference side Kingstonian to the Premiership with West Ham United in November 1998. However he failed to break into the first team, making just one appearance in the 2-2 draw with Liverpool at Anfield in February 1999 coming on as a late substitute for Joe Cole.[tpl]cite news|last=Bramwell|first=Neil|title=Football: Foe has friends in high places|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football-foe-has-friends-in-high-places-1072431.html|accessdate=23 August 2011|newspaper=www.theindependent.co.uk|date=22 February 1999[/tpl] Subsequent loan spells with Leyton Orient and Exeter City and a return to Kingstonian failed to reignite his career and he was released by West Ham in 2001. After a trial he had impressed manager Lawrie Sanchez and was signed by  Wycombe Wanderers and played there for three seasons[tpl]cite web|last=James|first=Stuart|title=HOLLIGAN ACCEPTS WYCOMBE OFFER|url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11766_2215426,00.html|publisher=www.skysports.com|accessdate=23 August 2011[/tpl] until he was released by new Wycombe manager Tony Adams. 
He also had spells with Crawley Town, Hornchurch, and Havant & Waterlooville.

==Music career==

In 2006 a severe thigh injury ended Holligan's football career and he moved into the music industry. He is an autodidact pianist, session musician, singer-songwriter, producer and entrepreneur.[tpl]cite web|title=Striker-Turned-Singer: Gavin Holligan Interview|url=http://www.soulculture.co.uk/features/interviews/striker-turned-singer-gavin-holligan-interview/|publisher=www.soulculture.co.uk|accessdate=23 August 2011[/tpl]
