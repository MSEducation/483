[[Chōzubachi]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese tea ceremony

A [tpl]Nihongo|chōzubachi|手水鉢[/tpl] is a vessel originally designed to keep water for rinsing one’s mouth and cleansing one’s body before entering the sacred precincts of a Shinto shrine or a Buddhist temple in Japan.  
A derivative vessel is the [tpl]Nihongo|tsukubai|つくばい[/tpl], an important feature of the Japanese tea ceremony found within the yard surrounding the tea house.
