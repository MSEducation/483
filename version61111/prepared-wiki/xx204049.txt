[[Acrolepia autumnitella]]

CATEGORIES: Acrolepiidae, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Estonia, Insects of Sweden, Insects of Romania, Insects of Poland, Insects of Latvia, Insects of Finland, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Hungary, Insects of Bulgaria, Insects of Italy, Insects of Ukraine

Acrolepia autumnitella is a species of moth of the family Acrolepiidae. It is found in most parts of Europe. The wingspan ranges from 11 to 13Â mm.
The larvae can be found on the leaves of bittersweet (Solanum dulcamara) or occasionally deadly nightshade (Atropa belladonna). The pupa is formed in a cocoon created from silk. The two generations in a usual year are in July and  October.
