[[Norddalsfjorden]]

CATEGORIES: Fjords of Møre og Romsdal, Norddal, Stranda

Norddalsfjorden is a branch off of the main Storfjorden in Møre og Romsdal county, Norway.  The fjord is located in Norddal MunicipalityCaplex: Cappelens ettbinds leksikon. Oslo: Cappelen, 1990. and a small part is also in Stranda Municipality.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.yr.no/place/Norway/M%C3%B8re_og_Romsdal/Norddal/Norddalsfjorden/|title=Norddalsfjorden|publisher=yr.no|language=Norwegian|accessdate=2010-10-11[/tpl] Helland, Anund (1911): Norges land og folk. Topografisk-statistisk beskrevet : topografisk-statistisk beskrivelse over Romsdals amt. (2 volumes). Kristiania: Aschehoug. The fjord is [tpl]convert|16|km[/tpl] long, when you include the Tafjorden arm that stretches further east, it is [tpl]convert|24|km[/tpl] in total. In the Middle Ages, the combined Norddalsfjorden and Tafjorden were probably called «Todarfjorden» (Tafjorden).Kleiva, Ivar (1975): Grunn og gror. Norddal bygdebøker, III. Valldal: Norddal kommune. Norwegian County Road 63 and Norwegian County Road 650 runs along the fjord and includes a ferry crossing between Eidsdal and Linge.Fjord1 ferries currently (2013) operated by fjord1, accessed August 22, 2013. In the 1960s villages Stranda-Liabygda-Eidsdal-Valldal-Norddal-Fjørå-Tafjord were still connected by a web of ferry crossings.Torvik, Arne Inge: Om samferdsel i Møre og Romsdal. Molde: MRF, 2000.
The fjord's mouth sits to the west between the village of Liabygda on the northern shore and mount Skrednakken on the southern shore. Further into the fjord, the villages of Eidsdal and Norddal lie along the southern shore, and their respective rivers .  Eidsdal is connected to Linge on the northern shore by a ferry. A few kilometers east of Linge lies the village Sylte at the mouth of river Valldøla which runs through the large Valldalen valley.Helland, Anund (1911): Norges land og folk. Topografisk-statistisk beskrevet : topografisk-statistisk beskrivelse over Romsdals amt. (2 volumes). Kristiania: Aschehoug. Further east lies the tiny village of Fjøra, at the mouth of Tafjorden.  The village of Tafjord lies at the end of the Tafjorden arm.
