Tri-Angle is the debut studio album by South Korean pop group TVXQ, released on October 11, 2004 by S.M. Entertainment.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mnet.interest.me/album/23468?otype=3|title=Tri-Angle|publisher=Mnet Music|language=Korean|accessdate=2013-01-09[/tpl] It sold 242,540 copies and became the eighth most successful album of the year in South Korea.[tpl]cite web|title=2004 Year End Sales|url=http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_2004.htm|publisher=Music Industry Association of Korea |accessdate= 2009-01-31|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080801080645/http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_2004.htm  |archivedate = 2008-08-01|language=Korean[/tpl] The first single "Hug" debuted with 169,532 copies sold in 2004, peaking at number four on the national monthly chart.[tpl]cite web|title= April 2004 Korean Chart|url=http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_200404.htm|publisher=Music Industry Association of Korea |accessdate= 2009-01-31 |archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20080803162353/http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_200404.htm  |archivedate = 2008-08-03|language=Korean[/tpl] As of 2014, it sold 242,890 copies. In November 2004, the Japanese version of "Hug" was released in Japan by Rhythm Zone, ultimately selling 4,710 copies.
The second single, "The Way U Are" debuted at number two on the national chart and sold 214,069 copies.[tpl]cite web|title= July 2004 Korean Chart|url=http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_200406.htm|work=Music Industry Association of Korea |accessdate= 2009-01-31|archiveurl = https://web.archive.org/web/20100113194826/http://www.miak.or.kr/stat/kpop_200406.htm |archivedate = 2010-01-13|deadurl=yes[/tpl] By the end of the year, the single sold 300,226 copies.
According to a statement by SM Entertainment mid-July 2012, the album currently has sold over 310,000 copies in South Korea alone.[tpl]cite web|url=http://star.mk.co.kr/new/view.php?mc=ST&no=447887&year=2012|title=2세대 아이돌, 음반 판매량 순위 공개 ‘SM-동방신기 올킬’|date=2012-07-19|accessdate=2013-01-03|publisher=Star Today|language=Korean[/tpl]
"Tri-Angle", the lead single off the album, uses a sample from the Symphony No. 40 in G minor by Mozart. The single also features the vocals of BoA and rock band the TRAX.
