George Denison Faber, 1st Baron Wittenham CB DL (14 December 1852 – 1 February 1931) was a Conservative Party politician in the United Kingdom.

==Background==

Faber was the second surviving son of Charles Wilson Faber and the nephew of Lord Grimthorpe.York History yorkhistory.org.uk George Denison Faber He was the brother of Edmund Faber, 1st Baron Faber, and of Mrs Edward Kennard (1850–1936), a novelist.

==Political career==

Faber was Member of Parliament (MP) for York from 1900 to January 1910,leighrayment.com House of Commons: Yardley to Youghal when he lost his seat, and for Clapham from 1910 to 1918.leighrayment.com House of Commons: Chichester to Clitheroe

On 29 June 1918, he was elevated to the peerage as Baron Wittenham, of Wallingford in the County of Berkshire.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=30793 |date=12 July 1918 |startpage=8199 [/tpl]

==Personal life==

Lord Wittenham married Hilda Georgina, daughter of Sir Frederick Graham, 3rd Baronet, of Netherby in Cumberland, in 1895. The marriage was childless. He died on 1 February 1931, aged 78, when the barony became extinct.
