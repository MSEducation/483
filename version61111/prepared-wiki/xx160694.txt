==Premier League==

The Egyptian Premier League ([tpl]lang-ar|الدوري المصري الممتاز[/tpl]) (League A) has nineteen clubs, Al masry of portsaid will not play in 2012-013 premier league season due to the port said disaster . ==National football team's achievements==

The Egypt national football team ([tpl]lang-ar|منتخب مصر لكرة القدم[/tpl]), also known under the nickname of The Pharaohs, is, as their name states, the national team of Egypt and is administered by the Egyptian Football Association. The team was founded in 1921.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.fifa.com/associations/association=egy/countryInfo.html |title=Country info |publisher=FIFA World Cup™ |date= |accessdate=2013-03-28[/tpl] The team has won multiple cups over the years. They won the African Cup of Nations 7 times. Egypt won the inaugural Cup in 1957, 1959, 1986, 1998, 2006, 2008  and 2010.[tpl]cite web|author=Aaron Ross |url=http://www.thenational.ae/sport/football/the-man-at-the-epicentre-of-egyptian-football |title=The man at the epicentre of Egyptian football - The National |publisher=Thenational.ae |date=2012-08-18 |accessdate=2012-08-22[/tpl] Making them record holders of most African cup wins (for winning 7 times) and most wins in a row (for winning 3 times in a row). This makes them one of the best teams in Africa. Their highest FIFA ranking was in July 2010 when they ranked 9th in the world, making it their greatest achievement.name="fifa"/> They were the first from an African country to participate in the World Cup when they played in 1934, losing to Hungary 4-2. Although they lost, they are still considered one of the strongest teams in Africa.

==Stadiums==

Considering football is the main sport in Egypt, it has a total of 27 soccer stadiums spread around the country.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.worldstadiums.com/middle_east/countries/egypt.shtml |title=Stadiums in Egypt |publisher=Bugarri |date=2013-02-07 |accessdate=2013-03-28[/tpl] The main stadium used to be Cairo International Stadium but after the new stadium was built, Borg El Arab Stadium in Alexandria has become the home stadium for the Egyptian National Team. This stadium carries a capacity of 86,000 big enough to fit all the fans that come out to watch the Egyptian Premier League along with all the other games the Egyptian teams play.[tpl]cite web|author=Fifa |url=http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/destination/stadiums/stadium=5020891/index.html |title=Stadiums in Egypt |publisher=FIFA World Cup™ |date=2013-01-03 |accessdate=2013-04-04[/tpl]
