[[Ali Ahmeti]]

Ali Ahmeti ([tpl]lang-mk|Али Ахмети[/tpl]) (born January 4, 1959 in the village of Zajas, SR Macedonia, SFR Yugoslavia) is the political leader of the Democratic Union for Integration ([tpl]lang-sq|Bashkimi Demokratik për Integrim[/tpl]), a governing (as of 2008) political party in Republic of Macedonia. Ali Ahmeti is also known as the political leader of the former Albanian National Liberation Army in the Macedonian Conflict in 2001.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.balkaninsight.com/en/article/ali%20ahmeti |title=Ali Ahmeti: Rebel Turned Politician |publisher=BalkanInsight |date=October 28, 2010[/tpl]

==Biography==

From 1979 to 1983 Ahmeti studied Philosophy at the University of Pristina in Kosovo, graduating in 1983. Between 1981 and 1983, he also was one of the student leaders in the 1981 protests in Kosovo. For these activities, Ahmeti was arrested and imprisoned for one year by the Serbian and Yugoslav authorities[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2009[/tpl].  
During the years 1984/1986 he was involved with reconsolidation of the student movement ( and general popular movement ) in Kosovo.[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2009[/tpl] In 1986, Ahmeti gained political asylum in Switzerland, where he lived until 2001, and was working like a coordinator of different groups.[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2009[/tpl] 
During 1988/89 he was one of the leaders of the student and miners protests against the Milosevic government. During 1989/90, he was one of the main organizers of protests of the Albanian diaspora in Europe. 
Ahmeti, gained his recent political support from the National Movement for the Liberation of Kosovo. In the year 1986, he was elected as a member of the Main Council, with a specific duty, interconnecting Kosovo with Europe. In the year 1988, he was elected member of the leadership of the National Movement of Kosovo. He was re-elected in this position in 1993, with the special duty in the military sector.
During the year 1996, Ahmeti was one of the main founders of the Kosovo Liberation Army, in 1998, when the war started, he was elected member of the main headquarters of the Kosovo Liberation Army.
In 2001, Ahmeti was elected Supreme Commander and political representative of the National Liberation Army (NLA). 
After the signing of the Ohrid Agreement in August 2001, and the disarmament of the NLA in September, Ahmeti was engaged in the political process of the implementation of this Agreement.[tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2001/aug/20/news/mn-36296 |title=Rebel Leader No Longer Persona Non Grata |publisher=Los Angeles Times |date=August 20, 2001[/tpl] In this light, he initiated and was named as a leader of the Coordination Council which unified all Albanian political parties in Macedonia, and the former structures of the NLA.
In June 2002, Ahmeti founded a new political party called the Democratic Union for Integration ([tpl]lang-sq|Bashkimi Demokratik për Integrim[/tpl]). In September 2002, DUI won the elections among Albanian parties in the Republic and Ali Ahmeti was elected as a deputy in the Macedonian Parliament. DUI entered in coalition with the winning party from the Macedonian block, SDSM (Social Democratic Union of Macedonia).[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bdi.mk/en/biografia.php |title=Ali Ahmeti: Biography |publisher=DEMOCRATIC UNION FOR INTEGRATION[/tpl]
In 2008, DUI entered in coalition with VMRO-DPMNE.
