[[Benoît and the Mandelbrots (Band)]]

CATEGORIES: German experimental musical groups, Computer music, Live coding

Benoît and the Mandelbrots, named after French American mathematician Benoît Mandelbrot, is a Computer Music band formed in 2009 in Karlsruhe, Germany.  They are known for their live coded performances,[tpl]cite news|last=Dupelius|first=Friedemann|title=Show us your Screens|url=http://www.dissonance.ch/de/hauptartikel/326/abstract/en/|accessdate=10 July 2012|newspaper=Dissonance|date=March 2012[/tpl] the Digital Arts practice of improvising with programming languages.
The band consists of Juan A. Romero, Holger Ballweg, Patrick Borgeat and Matthias Schneiderbanger,[tpl]cite web|title=Benoît and the Mandelbrots (official site)|url=http://www.the-mandelbrots.de/|accessdate=1 July 2012[/tpl] who met while studying at the Institute for Musicology and Music Informatics at the University of Music Karlsruhe in Germany.  They perform live using the SuperCollider programming language, writing code to improvise music in a range of electronic music genres, from techno to noise.[tpl]cite news|last=Blank|first=Julia|title=Sehen, was man tut. Das Laptop-Ensemble 'Benoit and the Mandelbrots' im Porträt|url=http://www.nmz.de/artikel/sehen-was-man-tut|accessdate=1 July 2012|newspaper=Neue musikzeitung|date=November 2011[/tpl]
As an improvising group focused solely on live performance, Benoit and the Mandelbrots have not officially released music recordings. However, they have performed at major venues on the international stage,[tpl] cite web|title=Benoit and the Mandelbrots in conversation|last=Cotton|first=Ross|date=January 2012|url=http://networkmusicfestival.org/2012/benoit-and-the-mandelbrots-in-conversation/ [/tpl] and were recognised with an honorary mention at the highly prestigious Prix Ars Electronica awards in 2012,[tpl]cite web|title=Ars Electronica Honorary Mentions: Benoît and the Mandelbrots|url=http://prix2012.aec.at/prixwinner/4962/|accessdate=1 July 2012[/tpl] and had a live recording published by the esteemed Computer Music Journal in 2011.
