[[Jury Prize (Cannes Film Festival)]]

CATEGORIES: Lists of films by award, Cannes Film Festival

The Jury Prize ([tpl]lang-fr|Prix du Jury[/tpl]) is an award presented at the Cannes Film Festival. It is chosen by the jury from the 'official section' of movies at the festival.Cannes awards summary at the Internet Movie Database It is considered the third most prestigious prize at the film festival, after the Palme d'Or and the Grand Prix.
From 1951 until 1966 the name Special Jury Prize was used for the second most important award of the festival. In 1967, the second place was renamed Grand Prize of the Jury and the Special Jury Prize disappeared. Two years later, in 1969, the Jury Prize was created separately from the Grand Prize, and both have been awarded yearly since. 
The Special Jury Prize reappeared twice in the 1990s, and one International Jury Prize was awarded in 1946.
