[[Cherwell Council election, 1998]]

CATEGORIES: English local elections, 1998, Cherwell Council elections

The 1998 Cherwell Council election took place on 7 May 1998 to elect members of  Cherwell District Council in Oxfordshire, England. One third of the council was up for election and the council stayed under no overall control.[tpl]cite news | title = Local Elections results | publisher = The Times | page = 46 | date = 1998-05-09 | accessdate = 2009-10-19[/tpl]
17 seats were contested in the election with the three main political parties standing in most of the seats along with 2 independent candidates.[tpl]cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/1998/4/8/86266.html | title = Election is a family affair | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | date = 1998-04-08 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl] The results saw the Labour party lose 2 seats but remain the largest party on the council, while the Conservatives gained 2 seats.[tpl]cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/1998/5/8/85924.html | title = Lord mayor beats Greens | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | date = 1998-05-08 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl]
