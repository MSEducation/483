[[Tikchik River]]

CATEGORIES: Landforms of Dillingham Census Area, Alaska, Rivers of Alaska

The Tikchik River is a stream, [tpl]convert|45|mi|km[/tpl] long, in the U.S. state of Alaska. Beginning at Nishlik Lake in the Kuskokwim Mountains, it flows southeast into Tikchik Lake, [tpl]convert|65|mi|km[/tpl] north of Dillingham. Tikchik Lake empties into the Nuyakuk River, a tributary of the Nushagak River, which flows to Nushagak Bay, an arm of Bristol Bay.[tpl]cite book|title=Alaska Atlas & Gazetteer|publisher=DeLorme|location=Yarmouth, Maine|pages=48, 56â€“57, 131|edition=7th|year=2010|isbn=978-0-89933-289-5[/tpl] 
Water from Upnuk Lake flows about [tpl]convert|10|mi|km[/tpl] to join the river downstream of Nishlik Lake.[tpl]cite web|title=Tikchik River Trail|publisher=Alaska Department of Natural Resources|url=http://dnr.alaska.gov/parks/aktrails/ats/sw/tikchik.htm|year=2007|accessdate=November 22, 2013[/tpl] Both lakes and the river lie within Wood-Tikchik State Park, at [tpl]convert|1.6|e6acre[/tpl] the largest state park in the United States.[tpl]cite web|title=Wood-Tikchik State Park|url=http://dnr.alaska.gov/parks/units/woodtik.htm|publisher=Alaska Department of Natural Resources|accessdate=November 22, 2013[/tpl]
Alaska Fishing says the river "makes an exciting float...with some potentially good fishing...".[tpl]cite book|last=Limeres|first=Rene|coauthor=Pedersen, Gunnar, et al.|title=Alaska Fishing: The Ultimate Angler's Guide|edition=3rd|publisher=Publishers Design Group|location=Roseville, California|year=2005|page=238|isbn=1-929170-11-4[/tpl]  Boating dangers include overhanging vegetation and bears, which feed on salmon. The main game fish frequenting the Tikchik are Arctic grayling, char, and red salmon, as well as lake trout in the lakes.
