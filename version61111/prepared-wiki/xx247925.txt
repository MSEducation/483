[[Ernest Elton]]

CATEGORIES: 1893 births, 1958 deaths, Royal Flying Corps soldiers, British World War I flying aces, Recipients of the Distinguished Conduct Medal, Recipients of the Military Medal, Recipients of the Silver Medal of Military Valor

Flight Sergeant Ernest John Elton  was the highest scoring sergeant flying ace for British aviation during World War I. He was credited with 17 aerial victories.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/elton.php Retrieved 8 January 2010.
While an Air Mechanic with No. 6 Squadron RFC, Elton worked with Lanoe Hawker in June 1915 in developing a machine gun mount for the Bristol Scout.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/elton.php Retrieved 8 January 2010.
In February 1918, Elton piloted a Bristol F.2 Fighter to his first victory, when he destroyed two Albatros D.Vs near Lens.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/elton.php Retrieved 8 January 2010. He and his gunner were unhurt when shot down during this engagement.http://books.google.com/books?id=rL8So-qQDRYC&pg=PA72&lpg=PA72&dq=%22ernest+john+elton%22&source=bl&ots=A5SV-8Aet5&sig=JkHcNX7fMwyHV1KmfPVpgBN10E4&hl=en&ei=8HtHS--kEI-gsgOx-JX2Dw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5&ved=0CBMQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=%22ernest%20john%20elton%22&f=false Retrieved on 8 January 2010. In the next 31 days, Elton would be credited with destroying twelve more enemy aircraft and driving two down out of control.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/elton.php Retrieved 8 January 2010. He scored ten of his wins himself, and his gunner in the rear seat scored six times.http://books.google.com/books?id=rL8So-qQDRYC&pg=PA72&lpg=PA72&dq=%22ernest+john+elton%22&source=bl&ots=A5SV-8Aet5&sig=JkHcNX7fMwyHV1KmfPVpgBN10E4&hl=en&ei=8HtHS--kEI-gsgOx-JX2Dw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5&ved=0CBMQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=%22ernest%20john%20elton%22&f=false Retrieved on 8 January 2010. His string culminated in a triple victory on 29 March 1918, when he destroyed three German two-seater reconnaissance planes in ten minutes. They were the only two-seaters he ever destroyed; the remainder of his wins were over enemy fighters, principally Albatros D.Vs.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/elton.php Retrieved 8 January 2010.
