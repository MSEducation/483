[[Herfast]]

CATEGORIES: Lord Chancellors of England, Bishops of Elmham, Bishops of Thetford, 11th-century Roman Catholic bishops, Anglo-Normans, Normans, 1085 deaths, Year of birth unknown

Herfast (or Arfast) (died 1085) was a medieval Lord Chancellor of England, Bishop of Elmham and Bishop of Thetford.

==Life==

Born in Normandy, he joined William the Conqueror during the Norman Conquest of England, and was appointed head of the royal writing office after the Battle of Hastings. He was Lord Chancellor of England from 1068 to 1070.[tpl]cite book |author1=Fryde, E. B. |author2=Greenway, D. E. |author3=Porter, S. |author4=Roy, I. |title=Handbook of British Chronology |edition=3rd |publisher=Cambridge University Press |location=Cambridge |year=1986 |isbn=0-521-56350-X |page=83[/tpl] After serving as Lord Chancellor, he was consecrated Bishop of Elmham in 1070, but about 27 May 1072 he moved the see to Thetford, thus becoming Bishop of Thetford.Fryde, ibid., pp. 217 and 243. He had a conflict with the Bury St. Edmunds Abbey, and when the case was brought forward, the Archbishop of Canterbury Lanfranc ruled against Herfast. He signed the Accord of Winchester in 1072 and died in 1084.[tpl]cite book |last=Greenway |first=Diana E. |year=1971 |title=Fasti Ecclesiae Anglicanae 1066-1300 |url=http://www.british-history.ac.uk/report.aspx?compid=33866 |volume=volume 2: Monastic cathedrals (northern and southern provinces) |pages=55â€“58[/tpl] He was a married bishop, and his sons held land in Norfolk at the time of Domesday Book in 1086.[tpl]cite book |author=Williams, Ann |title=The English and the Norman Conquest |publisher=Boydell Press |location=Ipswich |year=2000 |isbn=0-85115-708-4 |page=46[/tpl]
