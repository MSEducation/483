[[Yukon–Koyukuk Census Area, Alaska]]

CATEGORIES: Yukon–Koyukuk Census Area, Alaska, Alaska census areas, Counties of the United States with Native American majority populations

Yukon-Koyukuk Census Area is a census area located in the state of Alaska, United States. It has the largest area of any county or county-equivalent in the United States. As of the 2010 census, the population was 5,588.  It is part of the unorganized borough and therefore has no borough seat.  Its largest communities are the cities of Galena, in the west, and Fort Yukon, in the northeast.

==Geography==

The census area has a total area of [tpl]convert|147,843|sqmi|km2[/tpl], of which, [tpl]convert|145,900|sqmi|km2[/tpl] is land and [tpl]convert|1,943|sqmi|km2[/tpl] –1.31%– is water. The area is roughly the same size as the U.S. state of Montana or the country of Germany. Its population density, at [tpl]convert|[tpl]#expr: (6551/145900) round 4[/tpl]|PD/sqmi|/sqkm[/tpl], is the lowest in the United States.

===Adjacent boroughs and census areas===

Also shares eastern border with the Yukon Territory, Canada.

==Demographics==

As of the census[tpl]GR|2[/tpl] of 2000, there were 6,551 people, 2,309 households, and 1,480 families residing in the census area. It is the least densely populated county-equivalent of all 3,141 county-equivalents of the United States. There were 3,917 housing units at an average density of [tpl]convert|[tpl]#expr: (3917/145900) round 3[/tpl]|/sqmi|/sqkm[/tpl].
