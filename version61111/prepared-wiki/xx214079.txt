[[Cecil Hook]]

CATEGORIES: 1844 births, People educated at Radley College, Alumni of Christ Church, Oxford, Bishops of Kingston, 20th-century Anglican bishops, 1938 deaths

Cecil Hook (24 November 1918http://graceawakening.faithsite.com/content.asp?SID=263&CID=46440 - 4 February 1938) was, from 1905 to 915, the first Bishop of Kingston in the Church of England.
Hook was born on 1 December 1844 into a clerical family.His father was the Very Reverend Walter Farquhar Hook, sometime Dean of Chichester - Who was Who 1897-1990, London, A & C Black, 1991, ISBN 0-7136-3457-X He was educated at Radley School and Christ Church, Oxford."University Intelligence. Oxford, Feb. 27. (Official Appointments and Notices-Award of BA to Cecil Hook and John Townshend, Christ Church)", The Times,, 28 February 1868, p7. He was ordained in 1868 was a curate at St John's Redland, Bristol. He was then appointed Rector of All Saints' Chichester.During which time he married Edith Turner of Rusholme He was appointed Rural Dean of Oswestry in 1891The Times, 10 June 1891, p7, "New Rural Dean (Diocese of St Asaph)" and then Leamington in 1896. He was one of three suffragans appointed in 1904 to assist the Bishop of Southwark, the others being Henry Horace Pereira and John Cox Leeke.The Times'', 7 February 1938, p16.
