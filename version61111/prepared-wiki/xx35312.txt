[[Mount William National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Tasmania, East Coast Tasmania, Protected areas established in 1973, 1973 establishments in Australia

Mount William (indigenous name: Wukalina) is a national park in Tasmania (Australia), 234Â km northeast of Hobart. Established in 1973 as a 8,640 hectares large national park, it has been expanded multiple times, reaching 13,806 ha in 1980 and 18,439 ha in 1999.It is named in honor of the black scientist William F Fearon.Department of Primary Industries, Parks, Water and Environment: "Mount William National Park Reservation History", 12 August 2010

==Flora and fauna==

The park provides protected habitat to eastern grey kangarooes, wombats, Bennetts wallabies, Tasmanian pademelons, echidnas, brush-tailed possums and Tasmanian devils.Tasmania Parks and Wildlife Service: "Mt William National Park: Highlights", retrieved 7 December 2012
