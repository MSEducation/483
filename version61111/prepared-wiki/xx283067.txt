[[Tyler Stinson]]

CATEGORIES: 1986 births, Living people, People from Winfield, Kansas, Mixed martial artists from Kansas, Welterweight mixed martial artists

Tyler Stinson (born January 17, 1986) is an American mixed martial artist who has fought in Bellator and Strikeforce.

==Mixed martial arts career==

===Early career===

Stinson started his career in 2006. From his mixed martial arts' debut until the beginning of 2010, he fought mainly for southern American promotions, as C3 Fights, Art of Fighting and Masters of the Cage.
Stinson compiled a record of seventeen victories and five losses, including victories over UFC veteran Drew Fickett and Bellator veteran Eric Marriott before signing with Bellator.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2010/03/tyler-stinson-wins-bellator-open-tryout-earns-spot-on-bellator-16-card|title=Tyler Stinson wins Bellator open tryout, earns spot on Bellator 16 card|publisher=MMAjunkie.com|author=Dan Stupp|date=March 8, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]

===Bellator Fighting Championships===

Stinson was expected to make his debut against Demi Deeds on April 29, 2010 at Bellator 16. However, he was called to replace an injured Sean Pierson against Dan Hornbuckle in the season two welterweight tournament quarterfinal on April 22, 2010 at Bellator 15.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmamania.com/2010/4/11/1415411/dan-hornbuckle-vs-tyler-stinson-in|title=Dan Hornbuckle vs Tyler Stinson in Bellator welterweight tournament with Sean Pierson hurt|publisher=MMAmania.com|author=Press release|date=April 11, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] Stinson lost via submission in the very first round.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2010/04/bellator-15-recap-carl-hornbuckle-thomas-and-freire-advance-to-semifinals|title=Bellator 15 recap: Carl, Hornbuckle, Thomas and Freire advance to semifinals|publisher=MMAjunkie.com|author=MMAjunkie.com staff|date=April 22, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson faced Leonardo Peçanha on June 17, 2010 at Bellator 22 in a rematch of a fight that took place in 2008. Stinson won via submission due to a rear-naked choke in the first round.[tpl]cite web|url=http://bleacherreport.com/articles/407769-bellator-xxii-live-play-by-play-from-kansas-city-pl-district|title=Bellator XXII live play-by-play from Kansas City P&L district|publisher=Bleacherreport.com|author=Jason Schielke|date=June 17, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson replaced Rory Markham against Steve Carl for a spot in season four welterweight tournament on August 26, 2010 at Bellator 26.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2010/08/rory-markham-out-tyler-stinson-in-for-bellator-26s-tourney-qualifier-bout|title=Rory Markham out, Tyler Stinson in for Bellator 26's tourney-qualifier bout|publisher=MMAjunkie.com|author=Dann Stupp|date=August 24, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] Stinson lost via technical submission in the first round.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sherdog.com/news/news/Oleinik-Vega-Ward-Advance-at-Bellator-26-26578|title=Oleinik, Vega, Ward advance at Bellator 26|publisher=Sherdog.com|author=Brian Knapp|date=August 26, 2010|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson faced Nate James on April 9, 2011 at Bellator 40. He won via split decision (29-28 Stinson, 29-28 James, 29-28 Stinson).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bellator.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=23600&ATCLID=205133351|title=Bellator 40 live results: Nate James vs. Tyler Stinson|publisher=Bellator.com|date=April 9, 2011|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson was expected to face Joey Gorczynski on May 7, 2011 at Bellator 43. However, for undisclosed reasons the match was scrapped.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tapology.com/fightcenter/bouts/bellator-43-joey-the-takedown-wizard-gorczynski-vs-tyler-the-evolution-stinson|title=Joey Gorczynski vs. Tyler Stinson - Bellator 43|publisher=Tapology.com|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]

===Strikeforce===

Stinson made his debut against Eduardo Pamplona on July 30, 2011 at Strikeforce: Fedor vs. Henderson. He won via knockout at 15 seconds of round one.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sbnation.com/2011/7/30/2306389/fedor-vs-henderson-results-davis-bests-kedzie-stinson-kos-pamplona|title=Fedor vs. Henderson results: Davis bests Kedzie, Stinson KOs Pamplona|publisher=SBNation.com|author=Luke Thomas|date=July 30, 2011|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson faced Tarec Saffiedine on January 7, 2012 at Strikeforce: Rockhold vs. Jardine. Saffiedine defeated Stinson via split decision (29-28 Stinson, 30-27 Saffiedine, 29-28 Saffiedine).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2012/01/strikeforce-rockhold-vs-jardine-results-tarec-saffiedine-vs-tyler-stinson|title='Strikeforce: Rockhold vs. Jardine' results: Saffiedine takes split decision over Stinson|publisher=MMAjunkie.com|author=Dann Stupp and John Morgan|date=January 7, 2012|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Stinson faced Jordan Mein on July 14, 2012 at Strikeforce: Rockhold vs. Kennedy. Stinson lost via unanimous decision (30-27, 30-27, 30-27).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2012/07/strikeforce-rockhold-vs-kennedy-results-jordan-meinvs-tyler-stinson|title='Strikeforce: Rockhold vs. Kennedy' results: Jordan Mein outboxes Tyler Stinson|publisher=MMAjunkie.com|author=Matt Erickson|date=July 14, 2012|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]

===World Series of Fighting===

In December 2013, it was announced that Stinson signed a four-fight deal with World Series of Fighting.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmasucka.com/news/tyler-stinson-signs-fourfight-deal-world-series-fighting/|title=Tyler Stinson signs four-fight deal with World Series of Fighting|publisher=MMAsucka.com|author=Jeremy Brand|date=December 10, 2013|accessdate=December 21, 2013[/tpl] He made his debut on January 18, 2014 against Valdir Araujo at WSOF 8 and won via TKO in the third round.
