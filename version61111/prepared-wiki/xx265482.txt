[[Waterlink Way]]

CATEGORIES: Cycleways in London, National Cycle Routes

Waterlink Way is a long distance cyclepath and walking route in South East London. It extends from Cator Park in New Beckenham to the Cutty Sark ship in Greenwich, a total of eight miles.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lewisham.gov.uk/myservices/sport/get-active/walking/Pages/Waterlink-Way.aspx |title=Lewisham Council - Waterlink Way |publisher=Lewisham.gov.uk |date=2011-07-11 |accessdate=2013-07-11[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bromley.gov.uk/info/200123/cycling/430/cycle_routes/6 |title=The Waterlink Way cycle route | Cycle routes | London Borough of Bromley |publisher=Bromley.gov.uk |date=2011-03-24 |accessdate=2013-07-11[/tpl]
The Waterlink Way is an established part of the National Cycle Network (Route 21). It connects a number of parks and green spaces in South East London – including Ladywell Fields and Brookmill Park – while following the Pool and Ravensbourne rivers.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sydenhamsociety.com/2011/01/waterlink-way-car-free-cycling-heaven-2/ |title=Waterlink Way – car-free cycling heaven! |publisher=The Sydenham Society |date= |accessdate=2013-07-11[/tpl] The route was designed to be accessible, with a large number of railway stations including Kent House, Lower Sydenham, Ladywell, Lewisham and Greenwich en route.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sustrans.org.uk/sustrans-near-you/london/easy-rides-in-london/waterlink-way |title=Waterlink Way - Map |publisher=Sustrans |date=2013-04-08 |accessdate=2013-07-11[/tpl]
In Cator Park, the cyclepath links with the Capital Ring and Green Chain Walk long distance footpaths.
