==Career==

She was born in Nagercoil to Madhavan Nair and Sathyabhama Amma, she left them early in life to study dance with her aunt Saraswathi Amma in Chennai, alongside her first cousins the Travancore Sisters, Lalitha, Padmini and Ragini (their mothers were sisters). She learned dance with the sisters and mastered dance forms like Keralanadanam, Kathakali and Bharathanatyam.http://www.weblokam.com/cinema/profiles/0610/06/1061006018_3.htm[tpl]Dead link|date=March 2013[/tpl] She went for an all-India tour with the sisters. Sukumari was noticed by a director when she went to the sets of a film with Padmini, and she debuted in the lead role in the Tamil film Or iravu. She has acted in various Malayalam films of the black and white age, like Chettathi, Kusruthikkuttan, Kunjali Marakkar, Thacholi Othenan, Yakshi and Karinizhal. She later became popular acting in various films of Priyadarshan like Poochakkoru Mookkuthi, Oodarathuammava Aalariyam, Boeing Boeing and Vandanam. She was also noticed in various films directed by Balachandra Menon like Manicheppu Thurannappol and Karyam Nissaram. In later years she played a major role in Nizhalkkuthu (2002) directed by Adoor Gopalakrishnan.http://www.weblokam.com/news/keralam/0302/02/1030202054_1.htm[tpl]Dead link|date=March 2013[/tpl] She had played many notable mother roles. She has acted in films in Malayalam, Tamil, Kannada, Telugu and Hindi, and used her own voice in all languages without using dubbing artists.She has acted in many dramas also .She has acted in television serials like Swapnam,Thulabharam,Sreeguruvaayoorappan,Malakhamar,Aakashadhoothu,Velankanni Mathavu and many serials of Sreekumaran Thampi etc.She has acted in some advertisements and albums also.She is an accomplished dancer also.She has participated in many stage programmes,public functions,talk shows etc.She has judged popular comedy reality show COMEDY FESTIVAL telecasting in Mazhavil Manorama Channel.

==Personal life==

Sukumari married director A. Bhimsingh in 1959. He died in 1978. The couple has a son, Suresh.Malayala Manoram Online Edition,27 March 2013

==Death==

On 26 March 2013, Sukumari died from a cardiac arrest at a hospital in Chennai where she was receiving treatments for burns she had sustained  while lighting a traditional lamp at her residence in February.[tpl]cite web|title=Actor Sukumari succumbs to burns|url=http://www.thehindu.com/features/cinema/actor-sukumari-succumbs-to-burns/article4551759.ece|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Sukumari, veteran Malayalam actress, dies|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/india/Sukumari-veteran-Malayalam-actress-dies/articleshow/19222718.cms|publisher=Times of India|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Gopi|first=Vinod|title=Ammavilikannanju: Sukumari Inni Orma|accessdate=27 March 2013|newspaper=Malayala Manorama|date=27 March 2013[/tpl]

===Response===

Following her death, several South Indian personalities offered their condolences. Tamil Nadu Chief Minister Jayalalithaa, who has co-starred with Sukumari, said in a condolence message that Sukumari had left a unique imprint on South Indian cinema and theatre with her talents. Kerala's Chief Minister, Oommen Chandy called Sukumari a "phenomenon in Malayalam cinema." Actor and Minister for cinema K.B. Ganesh Kumar said actors in Malayalam film industry had lost "the loving presence of a mother in the sets." Leader of the Opposition V.S. Achuthanandan called Sukumari’s death a "huge loss for Indian cinema." Kerala Pradesh Congress Committee president Ramesh Chennithala said Sukumari would be remembered for "the diversity of characters that she gave life to." Communist Party of India State secretary Pannian Ravindran said Sukumari was "an unforgettable acting genius." Union Minister of State for Food and Consumer Affairs K.V. Thomas described Sukumari as an actor who conquered the film scene with her unique acting style.[tpl]cite web|title=Grief over Sukumari’s death|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/kerala/grief-over-sukumaris-death/article4551457.ece|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl]   South Indian Film Artistes Association president R. Sarathkumar remembered the actor as being an inspiration to others. Actor and renowned television producer Raadhika Sarathkumar recalled Sukumari's many dance performances.  Actor and president of Association of Malayalam Movie Artists Innocent said at a condolence meeting at Kochi that her death was an irreparable loss to Malayalam film industry. South Indian film star Mohanlal likened her death to the loss of "a mother, sister and a good friend." Speaker G. Karthikeyan, also expressed grief at the death of actor Sukumari.[tpl]cite web|title=Actor and Padma Shri awardee Sukumari passes away|url=http://www.deccanchronicle.com/130326/news-current-affairs/article/actor-and-padma-shri-awardee-sukumari-passes-away|publisher=Deccan Herald|accessdate=26 March 2013[/tpl] Director Shaji Kailas deemed her loss an "irreparable one for the film industry."[tpl]cite web|title=Actress Sukumari's funeral to be held in Chennai today|url=http://m.ibnlive.com/news/veteran-malayalam-actress-sukukumari-passes-away/381427-71-210.html|publisher=IBN Live|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl]
