[[Paul Laurence Dunbar House]]

CATEGORIES: National Historic Landmarks in Ohio, Historic American Buildings Survey in Ohio, Houses completed in 1906, Historic house museums in Ohio, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Ohio, Museums in Dayton, Ohio, Ohio Historical Society, Biographical museums in Ohio, History of Dayton, Ohio, Literary museums in the United States, Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park

The Paul Laurence Dunbar House was the 1904-1906 home of poet Paul Laurence Dunbar in Dayton, Ohio. It is a historic house museum owned by the state of Ohio and operated by Dayton History on behalf of the Ohio Historical Society. It is located at 219 Paul Laurence Dunbar Street (formerly called North Summit Street) in Dayton.

==History==

Dunbar had a long association with Dayton. He was born there of former slaves in 1872[tpl]Cite book |last=Kraft |first=Stephanie |date=1979 |title=No Castles on Main Street: American Authors and Their Homes |location= |publisher=Rand McNally |page=178 |isbn=0-528-81828-7[/tpl] and his first poems were printed in the Dayton Herald in 1888. His first book, Oak and Ivy (1892) was published in Dayton by the United Brethren Publishing House as well.[tpl]Cite book |last=Brasch |first=Walter M. |date=1981 |title=Black English and the Mass Media |location= |publisher=University of Massachusetts Press |isbn=0-87023-335-1[/tpl][tpl]rp|125[/tpl]
After serving as an elevator operator in Dayton and meeting some success for his writings, Dunbar took a job in Washington, D.C., at the Library of Congress. 
Dunbar purchased the home for his mother, Matilda Dunbar, in June of 1904. The house is an eight-room brick structure on a quiet street.[tpl]Cite book |last=Betts |first=Glynne Robinson |date=1981 |title=Writers in Residence: American Authors At Home |location= |publisher=Viking Press |page=120 |isbn=0-670-79108-3[/tpl] When Dunbar separated from his wife Alice Ruth Moore in 1902, he moved in as well.
By the time Dunbar moved into the house, he was very sick with tuberculosis. He also suffered from alcoholism.[tpl]rp|133[/tpl] It was in this home that Dunbar died in 1906.[tpl]Cite book |last=Alexander |first=Eleanor C. |date=2001 |title=Lyrics of Sunshine and Shadow: The Tragic Courtship and Marriage of Paul Laurence Dunbar and Alice Ruth Moore |location=New York |publisher=New York University Press |page=192 |isbn=0-8147-0696-7[/tpl] His death that February was signified by a black wreath which hung on the front door of the home.[tpl]Cite book |last=McKissack |first=Pat |date=1984 |title=Paul Laurence Dunbar: A Poet to Remember |location= |publisher=Scholastic Library Publications |page=118 |isbn=0-516-43209-5[/tpl]
After Dunbar's death, his mother Matilda lived here for the remainder of her life and kept his books, manuscripts, and study as he left them.[tpl]Cite book |last=Curtis |first=Nancy C. |date=1996 |title=Black Heritage Sites: An African American Odyssey and Finder's Guide |location= |publisher=ALA Editions |page=507 |isbn=0-8389-0643-5[/tpl] In 1932, it was reported that Matilda held an open house every year on June 27, the anniversary of her son's birth.[tpl]Cite book |last=Trubek |first=Anne |date=2011 |title=A Skeptic's Guide to Writers' Houses |location= |publisher=University of Pennsylvania Press |page=115 |isbn=978-0-8122-4292-8[/tpl] She died in 1936.

==Modern history==

The home was purchased by the state of Ohio in 1936; that same year it was dedicated as the first state memorial to an African-American. It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1962 and became part of Dayton Aviation Heritage National Historical Park in 1992.[tpl]Cite journal |title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Register of Historic Places Inventory-Nomination: the Paul Laurence Dunbar House|32 KB[/tpl] |date=May 12, 1975 |last=Mendinghall |first=Joseph S. |publisher=National Park Service[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying 5 photos, exterior and interior, from 1975 and undated|32Â KB[/tpl] It is a component of the National Aviation Heritage Area.[tpl]cite web |title=Home of the Wright Brothers |url=http://www.aviationheritagearea.org/ |publisher=National Aviation Heritage Area |accessdate=16 March 2012[/tpl]
A visitors center includes displays memorabilia relating to Dunbar's life and work.[tpl]Cite book |last=Cangor |first=George |date=1997 |title=Old Roads of the Midwest |location= |publisher=University of Michigan Press |page=109 |isbn=978-0-472-08288-9[/tpl] The visitors center is housed in a neighboring building. The property includes an old barn.
