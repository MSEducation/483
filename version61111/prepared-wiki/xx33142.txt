</ref> There are a few late dates of around 8000 BP and one of 7000 BP<ref name=Turvey>[tpl]cite book|last=Turvey|first=Sam|title=Holocene extinctions|year=2009|publisher=[[Oxford University Press]]|isbn=0-19-953509-4|page=24|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=mbU-F42JU1AC&pg=PA24[/tpl]</ref> for ''Megatherium'' remains, but the most recent date viewed as credible is about 10,000 BP.<ref name = "Fiedal">
</ref> The use of bioclimatic envelope modeling indicates that the area of suitable habitat for ''Megatherium'' had shrunk and become fragmented by the mid-Holocene. While this alone would not likely have caused its extinction, it has been cited as a possible contributing factor.<ref>[tpl]Cite journal|author=Lima-Ribeiro, Matheus Souza, et al. |title=Potential Suitable Areas of Giant Ground Sloths Dropped Before its Extinction in South America: the Evidences from Bioclimatic Envelope Modeling.|journal= Natureza & Conservação |volume=10|date = December 2012|pages= 145–151|url=http://dx.doi.org/10.4322/natcon.2012.022[/tpl]</ref>
