[[Football at the 1906 Intercalated Games]]

CATEGORIES: Football at the Summer Olympics, 1906 Intercalated Games events, 1906 in association football, International association football competitions hosted by Greece, Ottoman Thessalonica, Ottoman İzmir/Smyrna, Athletic history of Thessalonica

At the 1906 Summer Olympics, called "Intercalated Games", in Athens, an unofficial football event was contested. Only four teams competed, three of them clubs from Greece and the Ottoman Empire. Some medal tables give the gold to Denmark and the silver and bronze to Turkey.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/summer/1906/FTB/ |title=Football: 1906 Intercalated Games Results |accessdate=2014-01-28 |work=sports-reference.com[/tpl]
The Athens team withdrew from the final at half time to preserve their dignity, and were then invited to play off in a match to decide second place[tpl]Citation needed|date=May 2010[/tpl], but declined and were promptly ejected from the tournament.
Smyrna and Thessaloniki, both then cities in the Ottoman Empire, played off instead. The Thessaloniki team composition was Greek from the group of "Friends of the Arts" (Omilos Philomuson, later Iraklis Thessaloniki F.C.).Søren Elbech, Karel Stokkermans 1906 Summer Olympics at Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation The Smyrna team consisted of English, French and Armenian players.
The goalscorers of this tournament are unknown.
