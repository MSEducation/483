[[Chiltern-Mount Pilot National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Victoria (Australia), Protected areas established in 1997, 1997 establishments in Australia, Important Bird Areas of Victoria (Australia)

Chiltern-Mount Pilot is a national park in Victoria, Australia, 237 kilometres northeast of Melbourne. It is close to the town of Chiltern.

==Environment==

The park is notable for its diverse orchids, Box-Ironbark forests and birdlife.  It is part of the Warby-Chiltern Box-Ironbark Region Important Bird Area, identified as such by BirdLife International because of its importance for the conservation of Box-Ironbark forest ecosystems and several species of threatened woodland birds dependent on them.BirdLife International. (2011). Important Bird Areas factsheet: Warby-Chiltern Box-Ironbark Region. Downloaded from http://www.birdlife.org on 2011-11-20.
