[[Defending the Undefendable]]

CATEGORIES: Anarcho-capitalist books, 1976 books

Defending the Undefendable is a book by Walter Block originally published in 1976.Walter Block, Defending the Defendable book reprint, Ludwig von Mises Institute, 2008 version. Marcus Epstein describes the book as defending "pimps, drug dealers, blackmailers, corrupt policemen, and loan sharks as 'economic heroes'."Defending the Truly Undefendable It has been translated into ten foreign languages.Walter Block faculty page, Loyola University New Orleans, accessed July 31, 2013.
Cable news pundit John Stossel said of it, "Defending the Undefendable... opened my eyes to the beauties of libertarianism. It explains that so much of what is assumed to be evil â€“ is not."American Spectator, December, 2006, p. 37. In 2011, writing that economics "illuminates what common sense overlooks", Stossel called the book "eye-opening" and detailed its contents.John Stossel, Almost Everything We're Taught Is Wrong, Using economics to explode fallacies, Reason, August 25, 2011.
