[[Patrick O'Brien (musician)]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American musicians, American people of Irish descent, People from New York, American lutenists

Patrick O'Brien is an American guitarist and lutenist[tpl]cite news|last=Henahan|first=Donal|title=Lute: Patrick O'Brien Plays|url=http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F70F10FD355513728DDDAD0894DF405B888BF1D3|accessdate=10 February 2011|newspaper=New York Times|date=4 July 1978[/tpl]  born in New York. He is a recording artist, but is best known as a pedagogue in the field of Early plucked instruments in America,  and an expert in musicians' hand anatomy.  He has worked with musicians on many instruments, reworking their technique around repetitive stress injuries and breakdowns of coordination.

==Biography==

Patrick O'Brien was born in Brooklyn, New York in 1947.[tpl]citation needed|date=January 2013[/tpl] He is an admirer and was a student of many American folk and jazz musicians.[tpl]citation needed|date=January 2013[/tpl]
O'Brien has taught early, classical, and other guitars, lute, archlute, theorbo and early harp in his native New York City for over fifty years. He is active as a continuo lutenist, guitarist, citternist and theorbist, and has performed throughout Europe and North America with the Harp Consort, Baltimore Consort, Schola Antiqua, New York City Opera; and at the Caramoor, Spoleto and Boston Early Music festivals.  He has served on the faculties of the Mannes College of Music, the State and City Universities of New York and the annual Lute Society of America's Seminar. He is currently plucked instrument teacher for Juilliard Baroque.http://www.juilliard.edu/degrees-programs/music/faculty/detail.php?FacultyId=9858&School=College&Division=Music
His students include Jozef van Wissem, Andrew Rutherford, Mara Galassi,http://polyhymnion.org/mus/galassi/bio.html and Roman Turovskyhttp://www.nyfolklore.org/pubs/voic37-3-4/dialogues.html inter alia.

==Discography==

Patrick O'Brien has made several recordings with the Harp Consort, The King's Noyse and Paul O'Dette.
