[[Debbie Freeman]]

CATEGORIES: 1962 births, Living people, Australian female tennis players, Australian Open champions, Sportswomen from Sydney, Tennis people from New South Wales, Wimbledon champions, Wimbledon junior champions, Grand Slam (tennis) champions in girls' singles

Debbie Freeman (born 5 June 1962) is a former Australian tennis player who won Junior Wimbledon in 1980. 
In 1978 Freeman won the Australian Open Girl's Doubles title with Kathy Mantle.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.aoonline.org/junior/pages/2008AOJCMediaGuide.pdf|format=pdf|title=Australian Junior Summer Circuit|publisher=Tennis Australia|year=2008|accessdate=1 April 2010[/tpl]
In 1980 Freeman became the first Australian to win Girls' Singles title at Wimbledon.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1300&dat=19811130&id=yTMRAAAAIBAJ&sjid=wZQDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4626,7870380|title=Top grooming for future stars|publisher=The Age|date=30 November 1981|accessdate=1 April 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1301&dat=19800708&id=1oARAAAAIBAJ&sjid=6-YDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4484,2525288|title=Winner was last minute choice|publisher=Sydney Morning Herald|date=8 July 1980|last=Nicklin|first=Lenore|accessdate=1 April 2010[/tpl] At the moment she is the only Australian to win the Junior Girls Wimbledon title.  Freeman received a telegram from the Prime Minister of Australia, Malcolm Fraser, congratulating her on her win.
Freeman however failed to capitalise on her Junior titles, losing first round matches at the US Open, Wimbledon and the French Open in the following year. She retired from professional tennis soon after.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.theage.com.au/breaking-news-sport/high-hopes-for-tennis-australia-recruit-20100101-lli1.html|title=High hopes for Tennis Australia recruit|publisher=The Age|date=1 January 2010|last=Walton|first=Darren|accessdate=1 April 2010[/tpl]
