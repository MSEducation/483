==Life==

Robin Harrison was born in 1900 and was educated at Haileybury and Merton College, Oxford. He became a master at Westminster School and returned to Merton in 1930. In 1932 he married Margaret, eldest daughter of Sir David Ross.[tpl]cite news|title=Marriages|url=http://find.galegroup.com/ttda/infomark.do?&source=gale&prodId=TTDA&userGroupName=oxford&tabID=T003&docPage=article&searchType=BasicSearchForm&docId=CS286336912&type=multipage&contentSet=LTO&version=1.0|accessdate=10 May 2013|newspaper=The Times|date=16 December 1932|page=17[/tpl] He was a nephew of Sir Francis Younghusband and a cousin of Dame Eileen Louise Younghusband. At the start of the Second World War he entered government service in the Ministry of Food, where he became Deputy Director of Public Relations and Private Secretary to the minister Lord Woolton. He was awarded an OBE in 1943[tpl]cite news|title=The Birthday Honours|url=http://find.galegroup.com/ttda/infomark.do?&source=gale&prodId=TTDA&userGroupName=oxford&tabID=T003&docPage=article&searchType=BasicSearchForm&docId=CS134692036&type=multipage&contentSet=LTO&version=1.0|accessdate=10 May 2013|newspaper=The Times|date=4 June 1943|page=8[/tpl] and made a CBE in 1950.[tpl]cite news|title=The Birthday Honours|url=http://find.galegroup.com/ttda/infomark.do?&source=gale&prodId=TTDA&userGroupName=oxford&tabID=T003&docPage=article&searchType=BasicSearchForm&docId=CS50547400&type=multipage&contentSet=LTO&version=1.0|accessdate=10 May 2013|newspaper=The Times|date=8 June 1950|page=3[/tpl] That year he returned to Merton to take up his old job as Fellow and Tutor in Ancient History.[tpl]cite news|title=University News|url=http://find.galegroup.com/ttda/infomark.do?&source=gale&prodId=TTDA&userGroupName=oxford&tabID=T003&docPage=article&searchType=BasicSearchForm&docId=CS136923748&type=multipage&contentSet=LTO&version=1.0|accessdate=10 May 2013|newspaper=The Times|date=4 March 1950|page=8[/tpl] He served for a time as Domestic Bursar and was elected Warden in 1963.[tpl]cite news|title=New Warden Of Merton|url=http://find.galegroup.com/ttda/infomark.do?&source=gale&prodId=TTDA&userGroupName=oxford&tabID=T003&docPage=article&searchType=BasicSearchForm&docId=CS203514719&type=multipage&contentSet=LTO&version=1.0|accessdate=10 May 2013|newspaper=The Times|date=31 October 1962|page=12[/tpl] He was involved in university planning and helped in the foundation of two new colleges, Wolfson and St. Cross. He was made an honorary fellow of both.Postmaster, 1969
