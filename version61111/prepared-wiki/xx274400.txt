Pilliarchetty Bhakthavatsalam Naidu Rajeevalochana (15 August 1935 – 5 March 2013), better known as Rajasulochana ([tpl]lang-te|రాజసులోచన[/tpl]) was a South Indian classical dancer and film actress.[tpl]cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2013-03-05/chennai/37469133_1_song-and-dance-film-historian-film-club|title=Veteran actress Rajasulochana passes away in Chennai|last=Soman|first=Sandhya |date=5 March 2013|publisher=The Times of India|accessdate=5 March 2013[/tpl] She has acted in more than 300 films in Telugu, Tamil, Kannada, Malayalam and Hindi language films.Memories linger, Randor Guy at The Hindu, 2 September, 2011.

==Early life==

She was born on 15 August 1935 in Bezawada (now Vijayawada), in the Andhra Pradesh. Her father Bhakthavatsalam Naidu worked in Indian Railways and was transferred to Madras as PA to the General Manager of M&SM Railway. At school her name was recorded in error as Rajasulochana.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.thehindu.com/features/cinema/the-queen-of-the-screen/article4478600.ece | location=Chennai, India | work=The Hindu | first=Randor | last=Guy | title=The queen of the screen | date=5 March 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

She learned Indian classical dance from Lalithamma, K. N. Dhandayuthapani Pillai, Acharyulu and Vempati Chinna Satyam, Krishnakumar, Vishnu Vysarkar, and Kalamandalam Madhavan.
The Kannada stage and screen maestro H. L. N. Simha gave her an acting opportunity in "Gunasagari" (Kannada, 1953), produced by Gubbi Veeranna. Subsequently she acted in about 274 films in all South Indian languages. She acted with all the leading stars of South Indian cinema such as M. G. Ramachandran, Sivaji Ganesan, N. T. Rama Rao, Akkineni Nageswara Rao, Rajkumar, S. S. Rajendran, A. P. Nagarajan and M. N. Nambiar.

===Pushpanjali Nritya Kala Kendram===

She founded the dance school "Pushpanjali Nritya Kala Kendram" in 1961 in Chennai. It has trained many students in Indian classical dance forms and celebrated its Silver Jubilee in 1986. She gave many dance performances in India and abroad and got critical acclaim for the dance dramas.

==Personal life==

She married Actor Director C. S. Rao and had twin daughters and a son. One daughter is a talented dancer and lives in Chennai. Her other twin daughter and son live in the US.
