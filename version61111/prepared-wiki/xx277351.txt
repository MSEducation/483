[[Respect (UEFA campaign)]]

CATEGORIES: UEFA, Public awareness campaigns

Respect is a social responsibility programme launched in 2008 by the Union of European Football Associations UEFA. Its main objective is to work towards unity and respect across gender, race, religion and ability.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.uefa.com/uefa/socialresponsibility/respect/news/newsid=1807947.html |title= EURO Respect campaign launched |publisher= UEFA |date= 6 June 2012 | language = |accessdate= 8 August[/tpl] 
The Respect project at UEFA EURO 2012 tournament had four main stands: fight against racism, increasing and improving access for fans with disabilities, promoting health through physical activity and improving intercultural dialogue between fans. Ambassadors for the campaign were the Italian referee Pierluigi Collina and a Dutch footballer Clarence Seedorf. The campaign was mainly visible through TV spot which was broadcast at half-time during matches, as well as on screens at the stadiums. Part of the programme was in co-operation with the Football Against Racism in Europe network.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uefa.com/uefa/mediaservices/mediareleases/newsid=1806141.html |title= RESPECT campaign launched at UEFA EURO 2012 |publisher= UEFA Media Release |date= 6 June 2012 | language = |accessdate= 8 August[/tpl]
