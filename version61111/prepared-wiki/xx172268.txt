[[Lawrence Jacobs]]

CATEGORIES: American lawyers, American businesspeople, News Corporation people, Brooklyn Law School alumni, Living people, 1955 births

Lawrence "Lon" A. Jacobs (born May 4, 1955) is the former Chief Legal Officer, Senior Executive Vice President and Group General Counsel of News Corporation.[tpl]cite web|title=News Corporation Executives|url=http://www.newscorp.com/management/newscor.html|publisher=News Corporation|accessdate=11 December 2010[/tpl] In April 2013, he began his tenure as the General Counsel of Empire State Development, however Jacobs resigned in November in order to join Time Inc., where he currently serves as their General Counsel.

==Biography==

===Early Life and Education===

Jacobs grew up in Bucks County, Pennsylvania.[tpl]cite news|last=Harper|first=Timothy|title=Rupert Murdoch’s Right-Hand Man|url=http://www.superlawyers.com/new-york-metro/article/Rupert-Murdochs-Right-Hand-Man/007ebd91-1e78-4fea-90e1-5dfa7c03ea5d.html|accessdate=23 June 2011|newspaper=Super Lawyers|date=December 2008[/tpl] 
He graduated from Temple University summa cum laude in 1978 and Brooklyn Law School cum laude in 1981.[tpl]cite web|title=Group General Counsel Arthur M. Siskind To Retire At Year-End And Be Succeeded By Lawrence A. Jacobs|url=http://www.newscorp.com/news/news_228.html|publisher=News Corporation|accessdate=17 June 2011[/tpl] Jacobs is an Adjunct Professor at his alma mater Brooklyn Law School.[tpl]cite web|title=Adjunct|url=http://www.brooklaw.edu/Faculty/Directory/Adjunct.aspx|work=Faculty Directory|publisher=Brooklyn Law School|accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl]  His curriculum as of the Spring 2012 semester is a Media Law and Policy Seminar.[tpl]cite web|title=Curriculum|url=http://www.brooklaw.edu/academics/curriculum/coursedescriptions/course.aspx?id=28|work=Media Law and Policy Seminar|publisher=Brooklyn Law School|accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl]
Jacobs has been a member of the Bar of the State of New York since 1982.[tpl]cite web|title=Lawrence A. Jacobs Profile|url=http://people.forbes.com/profile/lawrence-a-jacobs/56505|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=17 June 2011[/tpl]

===News Corporation===

Jacobs joined News Corp. in 1996 as deputy to Arthur Siskind, whose position he took over in 2004 when Siskind stepped down and announced his retirement.[tpl]cite news|title=Movers and shakers: Eugenie Turton's gateway to the Wates board|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/finance/2898672/Movers-and-shakers-Eugenie-Turtons-gateway-to-the-Wates-board.html|accessdate=11 December 2010|newspaper=The Daily Telegraph|date=4 November 2004[/tpl] 
Most notably, Jacobs was responsible for the acquisitions of MySpace, Dow Jones and DirectTV during his tenure as General Counsel.[tpl]cite news|last=Lieberman|first=David|title=Surprise At News Corp: General Counsel Lawrence Jacobs Leaves|url=http://www.deadline.com/2011/06/surprise-at-news-corp-general-counsel-lawrence-jacobs-leaves/|accessdate=2 December 2011|newspaper=Deadline.com|date=8 June 2011[/tpl]
His salary in 2007 was $4.1 million.[tpl]cite news|last=Steffens|first=Miriam|title=News Corp executives pocket $112m|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/business/news-corp-executives-pocket-112m/2007/09/07/1188783495861.html|accessdate=11 December 2010|newspaper=The Age|date=8 September 2007[/tpl]

====Resignation====

On June 8, 2011, Jacobs announced that he would be stepping down from his position as General Counsel at News Corp. in order to "pursue new opportunities." 
Jacobs' resignation came when he approached the company Chief Operating Officer Chase Carey to inform him that News Corp. was in breach of his contract; chairman & CEO Rupert Murdoch was using former New York City Schools Chancellor, and newly appointed News Corp. executive, Joel Klein, who joined the company in November 2010, for legal counsel instead of himself.[tpl]cite news|last=Sherman|first=Gabriel|title=How Elisabeth Murdoch is Trying to Finally Get Her Due|url=http://nymag.com/news/media/elisabeth-murdoch-2011-11|accessdate=30 November 2011|newspaper=New York Magazine|date=4 November 2011[/tpl] He was released from his multimillion-dollar contract with News Corp. with four years remaining on it.
There are numerous reports regarding the cause for Jacobs' sudden departure, though it is rumored that Jacobs left due to being the top legal officer during the time of the News International phone hacking scandal,[tpl]cite news|last=Peters|first=Jeremy W.|title=General Counsel of News Corp. Resigns in Wake of Settlement|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2011/06/09/business/media/09newscorp.html|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=The New York Times|date=8 June 2011[/tpl] as well as friction with Klein.[tpl]cite news|last=Flint|first=Joe|title=News Corp. general counsel is leaving after 15 years|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2011/jun/09/business/la-fi-ct-newscorp-20110609|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=9 June 2011[/tpl]  
Despite such speculation as to Jacobs' reasoning for leaving, it is not known that Jacobs had any involvement in or knowledge of the News of the World phone hacking.[tpl]cite news|last=Pilkington|first=Ed|title=Murdoch under pressure to contain phone hacking scandal within the UK|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/jul/07/murdoch-phone-hacking-scandal-us|accessdate=31 July 2011|newspaper=The Guardian|date=7 July 2011[/tpl]

===Empire State Development===

Jacobs currently serves on the board of multiple organizations. On April 1, 2013, he officially began his position as General Counsel of Empire State Development, a public authority of the state of New York that finances and operates several ambitious state projects.[tpl]cite web|title=Jacobs' Twitter Page|url=https://twitter.com/lonjacobs|work=Twitter|accessdate=25 April 2013[/tpl] This was Jacobs first time as a Chief Legal Officer following his departure from News Corporation. Despite having joined the company in April, Jacobs left only seven months into his tenure in order to join Time Inc.[tpl]cite web|last=Kelly|first=Keith J.|title=Time shocker: Pearlstine’s back, Nelson’s out|url=http://nypost.com/2013/11/01/time-shocker-pearlstines-back-nelsons-out/|work=New York Post|accessdate=April 15, 2014[/tpl]

===Time Inc.===

On November 1, 2013, Jacobs returned to the private sector, joining Time Inc. as it began its transformation from a publishing house into a media corporation, all while being spun off from its parent company, Time Warner.[tpl]cite news|last=Burton|first=Lucy|title=Legal changes at TIME as News Corp veteran steps in prior to spinoff from Time Warner|url=http://www.thelawyer.com/in-house/legal-changes-at-time-as-news-corp-veteran-steps-in-prior-to-spinoff-from-time-warner/3011832.article|accessdate=30 December 2013|newspaper=The Lawyer|date=1 November 2013[/tpl] Jacobs is playing an important role in the company's transition into a public corporation.[tpl]cite news|title=Time Inc. Builds Senior Management Team and Reorganizes Editorial Operations|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2013/10/31/ny-time-idUSnBw315984a+100+BSW20131031|accessdate=31 December 2013|newspaper=Reuters|date=31 October 2013[/tpl]

===Personal life===

Jacobs is a Democrat. He was a supporter of Hillary Clinton's 2008 presidential campaign.
He lives with his wife Hannah and their two daughters on the Upper West Side of New York City. Jacobs youngest daughter is intellectually disabled.

====Philanthropy====

Jacobs is involved in many philanthropic efforts including those that grant educational opportunities for people from underprivileged backgrounds. Because of this involvement, he has been associated with numerous organizations.  
In March 2008, Jacobs was honored for his work with the JCRC and was awarded the Corporate Leader Award.  He was presented with the award by his colleague, Fox News President and CEO Roger Ailes.[tpl]cite news|last=Boxer|first=Tim|title=JCRC - Doctor’s Advice To A Wife Can Be Lethal To Her Hubby|url=http://15minutesmagazine.com/archives/issue_88/pow_0806-05.htm|accessdate=15 January 2012|newspaper=15 Minutes Magazine|year=2008[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Leon|first=Masha|title=CUNY Chancellor Matthew Goldstein Honored by JCRC|url=http://www.forward.com/articles/13144/|accessdate=15 January 2012|newspaper=Jewish Daily Forward|date=11 April 2008[/tpl]
Jacobs was the honoree at the Cooke Center for Learning and Development on April 27, 2009, where he received the Cooke's Founders Award.  He spoke out against discrimination directed towards people with disabilities. Jacobs has been a fervent supporter of disability awareness.  He is against the misuse of the word "retard" because of its offensive connotation towards people with special needs. Jacobs was honored while he was Chairman of the Cooke Center Board of Directors.  
In May 2011, Jacobs, along with New York Giants quarterback Eli Manning and George Hornig, was honored by College Bound Initiative, run by Ann Rubenstein Tisch, for their 10th anniversary ceremony.  Jacobs was recognized for his work with organizations in order to further his mission to offer a higher education to people of all income levels.[tpl]cite news|title=College Bound Initiative Celebrates 10 Years|url=http://www.myfoxny.com/dpp/news/college-bound-initiative-celebrates-10-years-20110506|accessdate=15 January 2012|newspaper=MyFox New York|date=6 May 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Delevingne|first=Lawrence|title=Hedge funders boost New York City non-profit CollegeBound Initiative|url=http://philanthropyis.com/?p=1909|accessdate=15 January 2012|newspaper=Philanthropy Is|date=May 2011[/tpl]
In November 2011, Jacobs was honored with his wife Hannah, who is a disability advocate, and Woody Allen by Resources for Children with Special Needs.  Mr. Jacobs was acknowledged for his work with Special Olympics for creating a PSA for the Spread the Word to End the Word campaign.[tpl]cite web|title=Thank you from RCSN--2011 Fall Gala|url=http://resourcesnyc.org/thank-you-rcsn-2011-fall-gala|publisher=Resources for Children with Special Needs Inc.|accessdate=15 January 2012[/tpl]
