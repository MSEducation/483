==Career==

After his film debut, Krush Groove, Underwood's 1985 appearance on The Cosby Show landed him a short stint on the ABC soap opera One Life to Live as Bobby Blue, which eventually led to his performance on the TV series L.A. Law, where he appeared from 1987 to 1994. In 1996 he was featured in the July issue of Playgirl.
Underwood broke into film with roles in Just Cause (1995), Set It Off (1996) and Deep Impact (1998). He also had a supporting role as a geneticist in Gattaca. In 2000, he played the lead role in the short-lived television series City of Angels. In 2003, he guest starred in four episodes on the HBO series Sex and the City playing Cynthia Nixon's love interest. In 2004, he played the role of Roger De Souza opposite Heather Locklear in NBC's LAX.[tpl]cite web | title=Yahoo Movies | work=Blair Underwood Biography | url=http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800086024/bio | accessdate=January 7, 2007[/tpl] He gained acclaim as the sexy grade school teacher in the CBS sitcom The New Adventures of Old Christine opposite Julia Louis-Dreyfus for two years. In 2007, he guest starred in an episode of the NBC series Law & Order: Special Victims Unit.[tpl]cite web | title=MovieWeb | work=NBC's Law & Order: Special Victims Unit January Guest Star Appearances | url=http://www.movieweb.com/tv/news/91/16591.php | accessdate=January 7, 2007[/tpl] He played the character Alex in the first season of the HBO series In Treatment, for which he was nominated for best supporting actor at the 2009 Golden Globes.http://www.upi.com/Entertainment_News/2008/12/11/TVs_Treatment_up_for_5_Golden_Globes/UPI-34601229009900
Underwood has received three NAACP Image Awards, for his film work in Rules of Engagement, and his television work in L.A. Law, City of Angels, Murder in Mississippi and Mama Flora's Family.
Underwood was voted one of People 's "50 Most Beautiful People" in 2000, and one of TV Guide 's "Most Influential Faces of the 90s".[tpl]cite web | title=imdb.com | work=Biography for Blair Underwood | url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0005516/bio | accessdate=January 7, 2007[/tpl] In 2007, Underwood co-authored the novel Casanegra: A Tennyson Hardwick Novel with husband-and-wife team Steven Barnes and Tananarive Due.
In 2010–2011, Underwood portrayed United States President Elias Martinez in the NBC drama series The Event, which premiered in Fall 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thefutoncritic.com/news/2010/05/07/nbc-picks-up-three-new-series-for-2010-11-season-with-the-event-outsourced-and-love-bites/20100507nbc01 |title=NBC Picks Up Three New Series for 2010–11 Season with 'The Event', 'Outsourced' and 'Love Bites'|date=May 7, 2010|work=The Futon Critic|accessdate=May 16, 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://livefeed.hollywoodreporter.com/2010/05/nbc-fall-schedule-2010-11.html|title=NBC's fall schedule, upfront revealed|date=May 16, 2010|last=Hibberd|first=James|work=The Live Feed|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=May 16, 2010[/tpl]
Underwood portrayed the role of Saint Mark in The Truth & Life Dramatized audio New Testament Bible, a 22-hour, celebrity-voiced, fully dramatized audio New Testament, based on the RSV-CE translation.
In 2012, he played the lead role of Stanley in the Broadway revival of A Streetcar Named Desire.[tpl]cite web|last=Hetrick|first=Adam|title=A Streetcar Named Desire, With Blair Underwood and Nicole Ari Parker, Extends Broadway Run|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/165961-A-Streetcar-Named-Desire-With-Blair-Underwood-and-Nicole-Ari-Parker-Extends-Broadway-Run|accessdate=26 August 2013[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Underwood is a part of several charitable organizations. He won the 1993 Humanitarian Award for his work with the Los Angeles chapter of the Muscular Dystrophy Association. In 2003, along with Ashley Judd, he served as the spokesperson for YouthAIDS.  In addition, he is involved with the AIDS Healthcare Foundation's Blair Underwood Clinic in Washington, DC.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.aidshealth.org/archives/news/aids-healthcare-foundations/|title=AIDS Healthcare Foundation|work=aidshealth.org[/tpl]
Underwood also appeared in a 2004 public service announcement for The Fulfillment Fund.[tpl]cite web | title=blairunderwood.com | work=Difference | url=http://www.blairunderwood.com/main.html | accessdate=January 7, 2007[/tpl]  He is a Trustee for the Robey Theatre Company in Los Angeles, a non-profit theatre group founded by Danny Glover, focusing on plays about the Black experience.
He supported President Barack Obama's candidacy and spoke at campaign rallies for Obama. Underwood got to know Obama while researching his L.A. Law role at Harvard Law School, while Obama was president of the Harvard Law Review.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2007/01/28/us/politics/28obama.html?pagewanted=all |title=In Law School, Obama Found Political Voice |accessdate=December 28, 2008 |last=Kantor |first=Jodi |authorlink=Jodi Kantor|date=January 28, 2007|work=The New York Times[/tpl]
Underwood's DNA test showed that he is a descendant of the Babungo people of Cameroon.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.africanancestry.com/testimonials/index.html |title=Testimonials:A welcome surprise that my people are from Nigeria & Ibo people|first=Blair |last=Underwood |publisher=Africanancestry.com |accessdate=November 23, 2008[/tpl]  Additional DNA testing and genealogical research revealed that his African ancestry comes from the Bamum, Brong and lgbo ethnic groups of Western Africa.Preview Blair Underwood. nbc.com The DNA test also connected Blair to a distant cousin in Babungo, Cameroon.
On September 17, 1994, he married Desiree DaCosta, with whom he has three children, Paris, Brielle, and Blake.
