[[Talmadge Hill]]

CATEGORIES: Columbia University alumni, People from Baltimore, Maryland, People from East Orange, New Jersey, American basketball coaches, Morgan State Bears football players, Morgan State Bears football coaches, Morgan State Bears men's basketball coaches

Talmadge L. "Marse" Hill  was the head basketball coach and an assistant football coach at Morgan State College, from the 1930s to the 1960s; a time when African American colleges were segregated from playing in the NCAA.

==Early life==

Hill is originally from East Orange, New Jersey.[tpl]cite book|last=Patterson|first=Ted|title=Football In Baltimore|publisher=Johns Hopkins University Press|location=Baltimore, Maryland|year=2000|pages=304|isbn=0-8018-6424-0|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=cZeye8iTWyMC&pg=PA48&lpg=PA48&dq=marse+hill&source=web&ots=9gDYMMb2YO&sig=UE_DAYWQNcCYuEHIuEnSrzm25Co&hl=en&sa=X&oi=book_result&resnum=4&ct=result#PPA48,M1|accessdate=2008-07-26[/tpl] He graduated from Morgan State College in 1928. Morgan eventually was awarded the touchdown and won the game. ==Coaching career==

Early in his coaching career at Morgan, Hill was the assistant to Edward P. Hurt in football, basketball and track.  He took over head coaching responsibilities for the basketball team in 1948 and served as the head coach until 1960. Hill was described as a conservative coach and one who did not like "fancy" ball handlers playing for him.
