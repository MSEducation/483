[tpl]Nihongo|Alessandro Santos|三都主 アレサンドロ|Santosu Aresandoro[/tpl], formerly Alessandro dos Santos (born 20 July 1977), and often known as Alex, is a Brazilian-Japanese football player.

==Career==

He was born in Maringá in Paraná, and moved to Japan in 1994 at the age of sixteen. He enrolled in Meitoku Gijuku High School in Kōchi and played football for the school club. After graduating from the school, he joined the J. League Division 1 team Shimizu S-Pulse in 1997. In 1999, he received the J. League Player of the Year.[tpl]cite web | url =http://www.japantimes.co.jp/text/sj20001130a1.html| title =Alex: Dreadlocks in deadlock at S-Pulse | date = 30 November 2000 | accessdate = 26 December 2012 | publisher = The Japan Times[/tpl]
In 2001, he obtained Japanese citizenship. He made his first appearance for Japan on March 21, 2002 against Ukraine, and he was part of Philippe Troussier's selection for the 2002 FIFA World Cup. He was the second foreign-born person to play for Japan in the World Cup finals after Wagner Lopes, who played in the 1998 World Cup and also the third naturalized citizen to play for Japan after Lopes and another Brazilian-born Japanese citizen, Ruy Ramos.
In August 2002, Alex agreed to join English Premier League club Charlton Athletic. But he was denied a work permit by the Home Office because he had not made the minimum number of national team appearances required for players from outside the European Union and returned to Shimizu for the remainder of the season.Charlton miss out on Alex, BBC, 28 August 2002 In January 2004, he left Shimizu to join the Urawa Red Diamonds.
Since Zico took over as the national team manager, Alex was a constant on the left side of the Japanese lineup, as a fullback in 4-4-2 formation or midfielder in 3-5-2 formation, and was selected to Japan's 2006 World Cup squad in May 2006, providing an assistance for Keiji Tamada in a group stage match against his former country Brazil.
In January 2007, Alex was loaned out to Red Bull Salzburg.Japan's Alex to join Miyamoto at Salzburg, December 21, 2006 He went back to Urawa in January 2008. He received a serious injury in a test match and had only one appearance in this season. League Division 2 side Tochigi SC for the 2013 season. He made 25 appearances there, scoring twice. In January 2014, he joined fellow J. League Division 2 team F.C. Gifu.
