[[Miriam Tlali]]

CATEGORIES: 1933 births, Living people, South African women novelists

Miriam Tlali (born 1933, Johannesburg) is a South African novelist.  She was the first black woman in South Africa to publish a novel., Muriel at Metropolitan, in 1979. She was also one of the first to write about Soweto.
Tlali was born in Doornfontein, Johannesburg, and attended St Cyprian's Anglican School and then Madibane High School. She studied at the University of the Witwatersrand until it was closed to Blacks during the apartheid era; she later went to the University of Roma, Lesotho. She left there because of lack of funds, and became an office clerk.Hans M. Zell, Carol Bundy & Virginia Coulon, A New Reader's Guide to African Literature, Heinemann Educational Books, 1983, p. 499.
Tlali's first book, Muriel at Metropolitan (1979; originally Between Two Worlds), is a semiautobiographical work and its "viewpoint is a new one in South African literature".Mary Dyer, Reality, vol. 7, no. 6, January 1976, p. 15. She later wrote other books such as Amandla (1980), Mihloti (1984), and Footprints in the Quag (1989)."Tlali, Miriam"; The Columbia Electronic Encyclopedia, 6th ed., 2007.
