[[Jet Aircraft Museum]]

CATEGORIES: Aviation history of Canada, Aerospace museums in Ontario, Military and war museums in Canada, Museums in London, Ontario

The Jet Aircraft Museum is a charitable foundation aviation museum specializing in Canadian Forces jet aircraft. The museum is located at the London International Airport, Ontario, Canada. [tpl]cite web|url = http://www.jetaircraftmuseum.ca/AboutUs/WhoWeAre.aspx|title = Who We Are|accessdate = 15 October 2010|last = Jet Aircraft Museum|authorlink = |year = 2010[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|url = http://www.chathamdailynews.ca/ArticleDisplay.aspx?e=1539648|title = Jet Aircraft Museum takes off in London|accessdate = 2009-10-15|last = Boughner|first = Bob, Chatham Daily News|authorlink = |date=May 2009[/tpl]
The museum officially opened on 12 September 2009.

==Mission==

The museum states its mission as:
[tpl]cquote|The Jet Aircraft Museum (JAM) will acquire, preserve, maintain, display and fly jet aircraft of the Canadian Forces from the DeHavilland Vampire to present day and future aircraft. [tpl]cite web|url = http://www.jetaircraftmuseum.ca/AboutUs/Mission.aspx|title = Mission|accessdate = 15 October 2010|last = Jet Aircraft Museum|authorlink = |year = 2010[/tpl] [/tpl]
The museum has indicated its intention "JAM will strive to maintain four or more of each type as flying aircraft with a flight of four  reflecting authentic Canadian Forces paint schemes." The museum has listed the CF-100 Canuck, Canadair Sabre, F2H-3 Banshee, CF-101 Voodoo, CF-104 Starfighter, CF-5 Freedom Fighter and CT-114 Tutor as being targets for intended acquisition.
