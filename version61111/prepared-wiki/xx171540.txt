[[Spitfire Ace]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 video games, Atari 8-bit family games, Commodore 64 games, DOS games, Sid Meier games, Video games set in France, World War II flight simulation video games

Spitfire Ace is a combat flight simulator video game created and published by then-newly created MicroProse for several 1980s home computer systems. It was one of the first video games designed and programmed by Sid Meier. It was developed for Atari 400/800 (1982), Commodore 64 (1984) and DOS (as a booter, 1984). The Atari version was written by Meier and the game was ported to DOS by R. Donald Awalt.Spitfire Ace PC-Booter credits  The Commodore 64 version was ported by Dale Gray and Ron Verovsky.[tpl]moby game|id=/c64/spitfire-ace/credits|name=Spitfire Ace Commodore 64 credits[/tpl]

==Gameplay==

The game puts the player in the pilot's seat during World War II.  The player defends London during The Blitz while flying the Supermarine Spitfire.MobyGames description The game offers 15 different scenarios that include France, Malta and D-Day.
