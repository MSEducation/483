Ryeisha Berrain (born November 25, 1990), known by her stage name Rye Rye, is an American rapper, dancer and actress. Signed to N.E.E.T. Recordings, she released Go! Pop! Bang! on May 15, 2012. In early 2009, MSNBC listed Rye Rye as one of the "5Top: Top of the pops! Up-and coming young singers."[tpl]cite web|author=Sclafani, Tony|url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/30307286/ns/today-entertainment/t/top-pops-up-and-coming-young-singers/#.T66n-K6OhNs|work=MSNBC|title= Top of the pops! Up-and-coming young singers|date=April 20, 2009|accessdate=May 12, 2012[/tpl] In 2011, Rye Rye was named one of Billboard 's "21 Under 21".[tpl]cite web|author=Depland, Michael|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/467255/21-under-21-rye-rye-2011|work=Billboard|title=21 Under 21: Rye Rye (2011)|date=September 27, 2011|accessdate=7 April 2012[/tpl] Rye Rye made her acting debut in the 2012 film 21 Jump Street.

==Career==

===Early career===

Rye Rye began performing at the age of 16.[tpl]Cite news|first=Paul |last=MacInnes |title=Music Weekly: The Rascals interview and Miles Kane acoustic|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/audio/2008/may/01/music.weekly.podcast |work=The Guardian |date=2008-05-01 |accessdate=2008-09-14 [/tpl] She wanted to join the music industry because she felt there was potential there, adding "I love performing, singing, dancing, all of that and I felt that given the opportunity I would destroy it."[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.femalefirst.co.uk/music/interviews/Rye+Rye-49810.html|title=Rye Rye Interview|date=26 March 2008|accessdate=6 April 2012|work=Female First[/tpl]
She met the musician Blaqstarr, a friend of her sister's, and worked with him on her early material after leaving a rap verse on his answering machine. On this process she notes "I used to like to write poetry and stories. One day I was home, bored, and started to compose songs."[tpl]cite news|author=MC|title=Faces to Watch|work=Billboard|date=24 January 2009|accessdate=October 9, 2010|pages=23–25[/tpl]
Her song "Shake It To The Ground,"  produced in collaboration with Blaqstarr, was a club hit in 2006, leading to local speculation that Rye Rye could be a fresh, new female success within hip hop music.[tpl]cite web|author=Shipley, Al|title=Ladies First|url=http://www2.citypaper.com/special/story.asp?id=13862|work=Baltimore City Paper|date=18 July 2007|accessdate=9 October 2010[/tpl] A meeting between Rye Rye, M.I.A. and Diplo in a studio in 2006/2007 was arranged by Blaqstarr after the song caught M.I.A.'s attention. Blaqstarr had told Rye Rye somebody wanted to meet her, but refused to say more. Although upon meeting them Rye Rye did not know who they were, M.I.A. told the rapper that she had been looking for her.[tpl]cite web|author=Chapman, Alex|work=URB|title=Interview: Rye Rye (MP3)|url=http://www.urb.com/2010/10/05/interview-rye-rye/|date=5 October 2010|accessdate=9 October 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news |first=Shaheem |last=Reid |title=T-Pain Proves His Rap Skills On Pr33 Ringz; Andre 3000 Wants You To Say He's Wack: Mixtape Monday|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1594197/20080905/story.jhtml |work=MTV |date=8 September 2008 |accessdate=12 September 2008 [/tpl][tpl]Cite news|first=Elizabeth |last=Aguirre |title=Rap artist Rye Rye shows us how she gets it poppin' in Baltimore |url=http://www.grooveeffect.com/art-music/060608-rap-artist-rye-rye-shows-us-ho.php |work=Groove Effect |date=2008-06-06 |accessdate=2008-06-30 [/tpl] At the time, Rye Rye was in her junior year at Dr. Samuel L. Banks High School.[tpl]cite web|author=Kilpatrick, Kate|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2009/07/30/AR2009073004474_2.html|work=Washington Post|title=Rye Rye is the Baltimore Club kid who had to grow up fast|date=2 August 2009|accessdate=9 October 2010[/tpl] She toured with M.I.A. during the KALA Tour in fall and winter of 2007, which included opening for her during the CMJ Music Marathon and dates in the United Kingdom in 2007, the People Vs. Money Tour in early 2008 and briefly with Afrikan Boy.[tpl]cite web|author=Swash, Rosie|work=The Guardian|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2007/dec/04/3|title=Finally, Mary J Blige is Just Fine|date=4 December 2007|accessdate=9 October 2010[/tpl] They both appeared in M.I.A.'s 2007 music video for the song "Paper Planes."

Rye Rye is the first artist signed to M.I.A.'s record label N.E.E.T., imprint of Interscope Records.[tpl]Cite web|author=Martin, Andrew|url=http://www.prefixmag.com/reviews/rye-rye/go-pop-bang/39953/|title=Go! Pop! Bang! was set to come out in March 2009, but its release was delayed. In the interim, she recorded guest verses for other songs. ==Artistry==

===Style and inspiration===

The LA Times described her music as a "hardcore club sound."[tpl]cite web|author=Martens, Todd|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/music_blog/2008/11/mia-slumdog.html|work=LA Times|title=Pop & Hiss goes to the movies: 'Slumdog Millionaire' M.I.A.'s latest cultural mix|date=24 November 2008|accessdate=9 October 2010[/tpl] Rye Rye collaborated on the album with executive producer, co-writer and stylist M.I.A., whom Rye Rye described as her fashion icon. Rolling Stone described Go! Pop! Bang! as "the hip-hop album most likely to set the club on fire" while Paper stated the album "will take over car stereos one impossibly ear-catching kinetic party jam at a time." Bust described her debut album as "Baltimore grime at its best." She told Rap-Up TV that she was also a fan of clothing designer Jeremy Scott.[tpl]cite web|work=Rap-Up TV|title=Rye Rye Interview|url=http://www.rap-up.com/2010/05/28/rap-up-tv-rye-rye-reacts-to-mias-comments-shares-love-for-chris-brown/|accessdate=October 5, 2010[/tpl]
Speaking of the ease at which writing her lyrics comes to her, Rye Rye notes that listening to her songs' beats repeatedly in the studio helps her to focus.[tpl]cite web|author=Cornish, Melanie|url=http://www.femalefirst.co.uk/music/interviews/Rye+Rye-49810.html|work=Female First|title=Rye Rye Interview|date=March 26, 2008|accessdate=March 30, 2008[/tpl] Rye Rye has spoken of her intention to work with Chris Brown, Lil Wayne, Rihanna and Jay-Z as well as a Disney Channel artist to reach out to child audiences. She says, "Of course I want to work with artists that can dance so in saying that I have to say that Ciara is someone I really want to work with, as that girl have moves. Not forgetting Ms. Missy Elliott of course as I think her videos are crazy."
