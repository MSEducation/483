</ref> These representations alluded to the story of the preaching fox found in popular Medieval literature such as [[Reynard cycle|''The History of Reynard the Fox'' and ''The Shifts of Raynardine'' (the son of Raynard)]]. The fox lured the geese closer and closer with its words until it was able to snatch a victim to devour. The moral of this story was that foolish people are seduced by false doctrines.
