[[James Coughlan]]

James Coughlan (born 6 December 1980 in Cork, Ireland) is an Irish rugby union player for Munster in the Pro 12 and Heineken Cup. He can play across the Back-Row, but primarily plays at Number 8.

==Early rugby career==

Born 6 December 1980 in Cork, Ireland, Coughlan started playing rugby with Old Christians at the age of 10. He continued playing when he went to CBC, and was selected for Irish Schools. He went to UCC, and played for Irish Universities. He joined Dolphin in 2001 and has played 85 times for them in AIB League/Cup, scoring 32 tries, a club record. Coughlan also played on the Irish Sevens team in 2008 that qualified for the 2009 Rugby Sevens World Cup, which was held in Dubai.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/515.php|title=Carney & Coughlan in Ireland Sevens Squad|date=23 February 2009|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl]

==Munster==

Coughlan made his Munster debut against Cardiff Blues in the Magners League in September 2006,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/294.php|title=Munster make Blues work for their win|date=1 September 2006|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl] and his performances in his first season earned him a full contract. He made one appearance for Munster during their victorious 2007–08 Heineken Cup campaign.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/302.php|title=Llanelli Scarlets 16–29 Munster|date=8 December 2007|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl] Coughlan was one of the stars for Munster in their 16–18 defeat by New Zealand at the opening of Thomond Park in November 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/328.php|title=Oh So Close|date=20 November 2008|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl] Denis Leamy's injuries propelled Coughlan into the Munster starting XV in the 2009/2010 season, and since then he has become a mainstay in the team. Coughlan captained Munster during their historic 15–6 win over Australia in November 2010,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/8287.php|title=Class of 2010 Excel|date=17 November 2010|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=2 October 2011[/tpl] and he won the Munster Player of the Year award for 2011,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/9028.php|title=James Coughlan Scoops Player of the Year|date=6 May 2011|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl] beating fellow nominees Keith Earls, Ronan O'Gara and Doug Howlett. He started at Number 8 for Munster as they beat old rivals Leinster 19–9 to win the 2011 Magners League Grand Final.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/9094.php|title=Munster Crowned Magners League Champions|date=28 May 2011|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=3 October 2011[/tpl] Coughlan was ruled out of the remainder of the 2011–12 season after a fractured bone in his hand, sustained in Munster's Heineken Cup quarter-final defeat against Ulster on 8 April 2012, required surgery.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10361.php|title=Surgery For Coughlan|date=11 April 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 April 2012[/tpl] In January 2013, Coughlan agreed a contract extension with Munster.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/11336.php|title=Nine Contract Extensions Agreed|date=3 January 2013|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=4 January 2013[/tpl] Coughlan scored a try and won the Man-of-the-Match award in Munster's 2013–14 Heineken Cup Round 6 38-6 win against Edinburgh on 19 January 2014, a win that secured a home quarter-final for Munster.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/12703.php|title=Bonus-Point Munster Move Into Home Quarters|date=19 January 2014|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=19 January 2014[/tpl] Coughlan was nominated for the IRUPA Unsung Hero 2014 award on 23 April 2014.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/18389.php|title=Munster Nominees For 2014 IRUPA Awards|date=23 April 2014|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=24 April 2014[/tpl] Coughlan was also nominated for the Munster Rugby Senior Player of the Year 2014 award on 1 May 2014.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/18449.php#.U2KHAE1OWM8|title=Players Nominated For Munster Rugby Awards|date=1 May 2014|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=1 May 2014[/tpl]

==Ireland==

Coughlan was called into the senior Ireland squad for the 2012 Six Nations Championship to replace the injured Leo Cullen but did not play.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10027.php|title=James Coughlan Joins Ireland Senior Squad|date=24 January 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=24 January 2012[/tpl] He was also called up to the Ireland squad for the uncapped warm-up against the Barbarians on 29 May 2012 in Gloucester.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.independent.ie/sport/rugby/ireland-call-for-reds-duo-3118570.html|title=Ireland call for reds duo|date=25 May 2012|work=irishindependent.ie|accessdate=26 May 2012[/tpl] He came on in the 60th minute for Chris Henry.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/26597.php|title=Late Penalty Denies Ireland XV in Gloucester|date=29 May 2012|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=30 June 2012[/tpl]
He was called into Ireland's training squad for the 2013 Six Nations Championship on 21 January 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/28155.php|title=Seven Players Added To Training Squad|date=21 January 2013|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=21 January 2013[/tpl] Coughlan captained Ireland Wolfhounds in their friendly against England Saxons on 25 January 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/28193.php|title=Wolfhounds Lose Grip on First Half Lead|date=25 January 2013|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=26 January 2013[/tpl] He was added to Ireland's squad for their game against Scotland on 17 February 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/11583.php|title=Coughlan Gets National Call Up|date=17 February 2013|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=19 February 2013[/tpl] On 23 October 2013, Coughlan was named in the Ireland squad for the 2013 Autumn Tests.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/29927.php|title=Ireland Squad Announced For GUINNESS Series|date=23 October 2013|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl]
