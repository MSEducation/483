[[Charles Shaar Murray]]

CATEGORIES: English guitarists, English music critics, 1951 births, People educated at Reading School, Living people

Charles Shaar Murray (born Charles Maximillian Murray on 27 June 1951) is an English music journalist and broadcaster. He has worked on the New Musical Express and many other magazines and newspapers, and has been interviewed for numerous television documentaries and reports on music.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rocksbackpages.com/Library/Writer/charles-shaar-murray/|title=Charles Shaar Murray at rock's backpages library||accessdate=26 May 2013[/tpl] 

==Biography==

Murray grew up in Reading, Berkshire[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2001/aug/02/pressandpublishing.g2|title=I was an Oz schoolkid|date=2 August 2001|publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=7 February 2011[/tpl] where he attended Reading School and learnt to play the harmonica and guitar. His first experience in journalism came aged 18 in 1970 when he was asked to contribute to the satirical magazine Oz. In particular, he contributed to the notorious Schoolkids OZ issue, and was involved in the consequent obscenity trial. 
He then wrote for IT (International Times), before decamping to the New Musical Express in 1972[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/film/2000/oct/20/culture.features|title=A tale of two rock critics|date=20 October 2000|publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=7 February 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/music/1836411.stm|title=NME: Still rocking at 50|date=24 February 2002|publisher=BBC|accessdate=7 February 2011[/tpl] for which he wrote until around 1986. Subsequently he worked for a number of publications including Q magazine, Mojo, MacUser, New Statesman, Prospect, The Guardian, The Observer, The Daily Telegraph, Vogue, and The Independent. He currently writes a monthly column about his lifelong love affair with guitars in Guitarist.
