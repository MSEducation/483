[[What Doesn't Kill You]]

CATEGORIES: 2008 films, American films, English-language films, 2000s crime drama films, Directorial debut films, Heist films, Films shot in Massachusetts, Films set in Boston, Massachusetts

What Doesn't Kill You is a 2008 American crime drama based on the true life story of the film's director Brian Goodman,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.comingsoon.net/news/movienews.php?id=50984|title=Exclusive: Brian Goodman Faces What Doesn't Kill You|date=2008-12-10|work=comingsoon.net|accessdate=2009-10-23[/tpl] detailing his own exploits involved with South Boston's Irish Mob.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bostonherald.com/entertainment/movies/dvd/view.bg?articleid=1168036|title=What Doesn't Kill You Blurb by Dave Wedge|date=2009-04-26|work=BostonHerald.com|accessdate=2009-10-23[/tpl] Starring Ethan Hawke and Mark Ruffalo, it premiered at the 2008 Toronto International Film Festival,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/index.asp?layout=festivals&jump=story&id=2652&articleid=VR1117991493|title=Toronto Film Festival lineup 2008|date=2008-08-19|work=Variety|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl] and was released on a very small scale in December 2008 due to the collapse of its distributor Yari Film Group.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117997332.html?categoryid=13&cs=1|title=Bob Yari crashes into Chapter 11|date=2008-12-12|work=Variety|accessdate=2009-10-19 | first=Michael | last=Fleming[/tpl]

==Synopsis==

Hawke and Ruffalo play childhood friends Paulie and Brian, who are forced to survive on the tough streets of South Boston through a life of petty thievery. They join a local gang of criminals, but Brian finds it hard to reconcile his work and friendship with Paulie and his relationship with his wife (Amanda Peet) and sons.
