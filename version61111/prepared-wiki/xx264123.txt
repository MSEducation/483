The Pratt & Whitney XH-3130 (sometimes called the XL-3130[tpl]Citation needed|date=January 2011[/tpl]) was an H-block aircraft engine project developed for the United States Navy in the late 1930s.Gunston 2006, p. 163. The design was later enlarged as the XH-3730), but the project was canceled in 1940 in favor of Pratt & Whitney developing the R-4360 Wasp Major air-cooled radial engine.Connors, p. 147.
