[[Farmers and Merchants Union Bank (Columbus, Wisconsin)]]

CATEGORIES: National Historic Landmarks in Wisconsin, Commercial buildings completed in 1919, Buildings and structures in Columbia County, Wisconsin, Louis Sullivan buildings, Bank buildings on the National Register of Historic Places in Wisconsin, Columbus, Wisconsin

The Farmers and Merchants Union Bank in Columbus, Wisconsin, also known as Farmers' and Merchants' Union Bank, is the last of eight "jewel box" banks designed by Louis Sullivan, and the next to last of those being constructed.HistoryImages of Farmers and Merchants Union Bank by Louis Sullivan
It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1976.[tpl]Cite document|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Register of Historic Places Inventory-Nomination: Farmers' and Merchants' Union Bank|243 KB[/tpl]|date=July, 1975 |author=Carolyn Pitts |publisher=National Park Service|postscript=[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying 3 photos, exterior and interior, from 1972 and undated.|1.04 MB[/tpl]
It is located at the northwest corner of James Street and Broadway in Columbus.

==Movie set==

The building was used as the location of a bank robbery in the 2009 Johnny Depp film Public Enemies, which was filmed in March 2008.
