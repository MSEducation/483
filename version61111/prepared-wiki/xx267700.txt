[[14th Parliament of British Columbia]]

CATEGORIES: Political history of British Columbia, Terms of British Columbia Parliaments, 1917 establishments in Canada, 1920 disestablishments in Canada, 20th century in British Columbia

The 14th Legislative Assembly of British Columbia sat from 1917 to 1920. The members were elected in the British Columbia general election held in September 1916.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.elections.bc.ca/docs/rpt/1871-1986_ElectoralHistoryofBC.pdf |title=Electoral History of British Columbia, 1871-1986 |publisher=Elections BC |accessdate=2011-07-27| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20110613200446/http://www.elections.bc.ca/docs/rpt/1871-1986_ElectoralHistoryofBC.pdf| archivedate= 13 June 2011 [/tpl] The British Columbia Liberal Party, led by Harlan Carey Brewster, formed the government. Following Brewster's death in March 1918, John Oliver became Premier.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.llbc.leg.bc.ca/public/reference/premiers.pdf |title=Premiers of British Columbia 1871- |publisher=BC Legislature |accessdate=2011-09-23[/tpl]
John Walter Weart served as speaker until the start of the 1918 session, when John Keen succeeded him as speaker.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.llbc.leg.bc.ca/public/reference/speakers.pdf |title=Speakers of the Legislative Assembly of British Columbia 1872- |publisher=BC Legislature |accessdate=2011-09-23[/tpl]
