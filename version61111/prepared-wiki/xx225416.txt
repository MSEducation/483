[[Cheesefoot Head]]

CATEGORIES: Sites of Special Scientific Interest in Hampshire, Sites of Special Scientific Interest notified in 1951, Geography of Hampshire, Visitor attractions in Hampshire

Cheesefoot Head is a large natural amphitheatre (also known as Matterley Bowl) and beauty spot just outside Winchester, England. It is situated on the A272 road (South Downs Way), and is rated a Site of Special Scientific Interest.[tpl]cite paper|title=SSSI Citation â€” Cheesefoot Head |publisher=Natural England |url=http://www.english-nature.org.uk/citation/citation_photo/1004528.pdf |format=PDF |accessdate=2008-10-25[/tpl] There are three bowl barrows on the site.http://www.themodernantiquarian.com/site/11485/cheesefoot_head_barrows.html
During the Second World War boxing events were held here for the entertainment of American troops stationed locally, and prior to D-Day, General Eisenhower addressed those troops.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.hampshirecam.co.uk/dday/dday.html |title=Hampshire Cam UK - The Photographic Rambles of a Retired Photographer |publisher=www.hampshirecam.co.uk |accessdate=2009-10-21 [/tpl]
Cheesefoot Head is regularly the site of crop circles, though at least one year there was an outline of a footballer. http://www.cropcircleconnector.com/2008/cheesefoot/cheesefoot2008a.html
The now defunct Homelands music festival was held in Matterley Bowl annually. In addition, The Glade music festival was held here in 2009.
