[[Kelly C. Crabb]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American lawyers, Brigham Young University alumni, Columbia University alumni, Year of birth missing (living people)

Kelly C. Crabb is an American lawyer who specializes in media and entertainment issues.[tpl]cite web|title=Kelly C. Crabb Joins Corporate Group in Los Angeles|url=http://www.mofo.com/press/xpqPressDetailMoFo.aspx?xpST=PressDetail&news=2141|work=Morrison Foster|accessdate=30 October 2013|date=28 April 1999[/tpl] He served as the chief legal representative for the Beijing Olympics Organizing Committee in Los Angeles.[tpl]cite web|title=Two New Sheppard Mullin Partners Expand Firm's Entertainment Practice Capabilities In Los Angeles|url=http://www.sheppardmullin.com/press-releases-276.html|work=Sheppard Mullin|accessdate=30 October 2013|date=1 February 2010[/tpl] Crabb is fluent in Japanese and received a bachelor's degree and then a Master of Public Policy degree from Brigham Young University and a law degree from Columbia University.  Crabb currently serves as a counselor in the Pasadena California State Presidency.
Crabb served as the legal counsel for the company that made the movie The Swan Princess.[tpl]cite web|title=Kelly C. Crabb|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/person/704525/Kelly-C-Crabb|work=The New York Times|accessdate=30 October 2013[/tpl] Crabb also negotiated the media rights related to the Elizabeth Smart kidnapping.  He has also worked as a lawyer for the Mountain West Conference working on how to increase the number of carriers of its TV channel.
Crabb's book The Movie Business was published by Simon & Schuster, and was enthusiastically praised by film producers Ralph Winter, (X-Men and X-Men II), Mace Neufeld, and Gerald R. Molen.
