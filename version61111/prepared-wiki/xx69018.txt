</ref> a lock to secure the bike during deliveries is essential. Simple chain and padlocks are often used, with the locked chain worn around the waist like a belt while riding. [[U-lock]]s are also popular.
Messengers typically carry basic tools, weather-proof clothing and a street map.[tpl]cite web | first = 'Buffalo' Bill | last = Chidley and others | title = How to be a messenger and not get stitched up, nicked or run over | publisher = London Bicycle Messenger Association | url = http://www.londonmessengers.org/FAQ | accessdate = 2010-12-29 | archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080629084442/http://www.londonmessengers.org/FAQ | archivedate = 2008-06-29[/tpl]

==Communications==

Messengers communicate and are dispatched to assignments via hand-held communication devices including two-way radios, cell phones, and personal digital assistants.
