[[Peter Lykke-Seest]]

CATEGORIES: 1868 births, 1948 deaths, Writers from Oslo, Norwegian poets, Norwegian novelists, Norwegian dramatists and playwrights, Norwegian film directors

Peter Lykke-Seest (1868 – 1948) was a Norwegian poet, novelist, playwright, non-fiction writer, script writer and film director.
He was born in Christiania as a son of wholesaler Jens Peter Seest and Caroline Christiane Frederikke Thy.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|year=|title=Peter Lykke-Seest |encyclopedia=Norsk biografisk leksikon|first=Gunnar |last=Iversen |authorlink= |editor=Helle, Knut|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/.nbl_biografi/Peter_Lykke-Seest/utdypning|language=Norwegian|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
He made his literary debut in 1896 with the poetry collection Hvide nætter, and published the novel Under Paddehatten in 1898. His most popular film was Historien om en gut from 1919. He published a book on the trial against Vidkun Quisling in 1945.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|title=Peter Lykke-Seest |encyclopedia=Store norske leksikon|editor=Henriksen, Petter|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/Peter_Lykke-Seest |language=Norwegian|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
