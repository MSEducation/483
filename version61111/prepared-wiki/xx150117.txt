Bartosz Ślusarski ([tpl]IPA-pol|ˈbartɔʂ ɕluˈsarski[/tpl]; born 11 December 1981 in Szamocin) is a Polish footballer who is currently playing for Lech Poznań.

==Career==

Ślusarski joined Dyskobolia Grodzisk Wielkopolski in early 2004 as a replacement for Grzegorz Rasiak who had refused to sign a new contract.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dyskobolia.org/index.php?id=slusarski |title=Bartosz Slusarski |accessdate=24 August 2007 |author=Maestro |publisher=Dyskobolia.org |language=Polish | archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20070829173727/http://www.dyskobolia.org/index.php?id=slusarski| archivedate= 29 August 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl] He spent the 2006–07 season on loan at UD Leiria in the Portuguese top flight, helping them qualify for the Intertoto Cup and winning their Player of the Year award.[tpl]cite news |author=Chris Lepkowski |title=Mowbray casts an eye over Polish striker |url=http://icbirmingham.icnetwork.co.uk/0200sport/globalsport/tm_headline=mowbray-casts-an-eye-over-polish-striker&method=full&objectid=19666651&siteid=50002-name_page.html   |publisher=Birmingham Mail |date=21 August 2007 |accessdate=24 August 2007 [/tpl] On 24 August 2007, he signed a two-year deal with West Bromwich Albion; the Baggies paid Dyskobolia £680,000 for Ślusarski's services.[tpl]cite news |title=West Brom bag striker Slusarski |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/west_bromwich_albion/6962741.stm |publisher=BBC Sport |date=24 August 2007 |accessdate=24 August 2007 [/tpl] Ślusarski made his only Albion appearance on 20 October 2007, coming on as a late substitute in a 3–2 defeat at Colchester United.
On 20 November 2007, Ślusarski signed on-loan with Blackpool until January 2008.[ref]
