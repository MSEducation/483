[[Nasal palatal approximant]]

CATEGORIES: Nasal consonants, Palatal consonants

The nasal palatal approximant is a type of consonantal sound used in some oral languages. The symbol in the International Phonetic Alphabet  that represents this sound is [tpl]angle bracket|[tpl]IPA|j̃[/tpl][/tpl], that is, a j with a tilde. The equivalent X-SAMPA symbol is j~, and in the Americanist phonetic notation it is [tpl]angle bracket|[tpl]IPA|ỹ[/tpl][/tpl].
The nasal palatal approximant is sometimes called a nasal yod; [tpl]IPA|j̃[/tpl] and [tpl]IPA|w̃[/tpl] may be called nasal glides. 
Transcriptions using [tpl]angbr|[tpl]IPA|j̃[/tpl][/tpl] may actually intend a non-syllabic nasal vowel, [tpl]IPA|ĩ̯[/tpl] (that is, the second element of a diphthong), as in Portuguese, or be ambiguous between the two.

==Occurrence==

[tpl]IPA|j̃[/tpl], written ny, is a common realization of [tpl]IPA|/j/[/tpl] before nasal vowels in many languages of West Africa which do not have a phonemic distinction between voiced nasal and oral stops, such as Ewe and Bini.
