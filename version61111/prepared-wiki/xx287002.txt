[[Alafia scandens]]

CATEGORIES: Alafia, Plants described in 1903, Plants used in traditional African medicine, Flora of Senegal, Flora of the Gambia, Flora of Guinea-Bissau, Flora of Guinea, Flora of Sierra Leone, Flora of Ivory Coast, Flora of Ghana, Flora of Burkina Faso, Flora of Togo, Flora of Benin, Flora of Nigeria, Flora of Cameroon, Flora of Gabon, Flora of the Republic of the Congo, Flora of the Democratic Republic of the Congo

Alafia scandens grows as a liana up to [tpl]convert|20|m[/tpl] long, with a stem diameter of up to [tpl]convert|2.5|cm[/tpl]. Its fragrant flowers feature a white corolla, dark red at the throat. Fruit is dark brown with paired cylindrical follicles, each up to [tpl]convert|45|cm[/tpl] in diameter. Habitat is forest and savanna, from sea-level to [tpl]convert|1000|m[/tpl] altitude. Local medicinal uses include as a treatment for rheumatism.  The plant has been used as arrow poison.[tpl]cite book | title = Medicinal Plants | publisher = PROTA | year= 2008 | pages = 52 | isbn = 978-9-05782-204-9[/tpl] A. scandens is native to Senegal, The Gambia, Guinea-Bissau, Guinea, Sierra Leone, Ivory Coast, Burkina Faso, Ghana, Togo, Benin, Nigeria, Cameroon, Republic of Congo and Democratic Republic of Congo.Search for "Alafia scandens", [tpl]Citation |title=World Checklist of Selected Plant Families |publisher=Royal Botanic Gardens, Kew |url=http://apps.kew.org/wcsp/ |accessdate=12 August 2013 [/tpl]
