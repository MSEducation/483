[[Mr. Simple (song)]]

CATEGORIES: Super Junior songs, Korean-language songs, 2011 singles, S.M. Entertainment singles, Songs written by Yoo Young-jin, Singles certified gold by the Recording Industry Association of Japan, Songs with lyrics by Gorō Matsui, Avex Trax singles, 2011 songs

"Mr. Simple" is a song by South Korean boy band Super Junior, released as their tenth Korean and lead single off their fifth studio album of the same name digitally on August 2, 2011 via SM Entertainment in South Korea. The song topped the charts hours after its release.
The Japanese version of the song was released as their second Japanese single on December 7, 2011 under Avex Trax.[tpl]cite web|author=by Kingpolo |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2011/10/super-junior-unveils-teaser-video-for-japanese-version-of-mr-simple |title=Super Junior unveils teaser video for Japanese version of “Mr. Simple” |publisher=Allkpop.com |date=2011-10-07 |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl] A b-side track of the single entitled "Snow White" was revealed via the group's official Japanese website on November 30, 2011. The single debuted at #2 in the Oricon Weekly Charts, selling 89,000 copies on its first week of release, outselling their previous Japanese single Bijin (Bonamana) which sold 59,000 on its first week and 67,000 on total sales, making it Super Junior's highest-selling Japanese release yet.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/w/2011-12-19/ |title=CDシングル 週間ランキング-ORICON STYLE ランキング |publisher=Oricon.co.jp |date=2011-12-19 |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl] The song was used as a commercial tie-up for Japanese satellite communication holding company SKY Perfect JSAT Group.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oricon.co.jp/prof/artist/381232/products/music/941422/1/ |title=Ｍｒ．Ｓｉｍｐｌｅ ＳＵＰＥＲ ＪＵＮＩＯＲのプロフィールならオリコン芸能人事典-ORICON STYLE |publisher=Oricon.co.jp |date= |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl] It will also became an ending theme song of TBS's Tsubo Musume for January 2012.[tpl]cite web|author=By Vitalsign |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2011/12/super-juniors-mr-simple-to-be-used-as-the-ending-theme-song-for-japans-tsubo-musume |title=Super Junior’s “Mr. Simple” to be used as the ending theme song for Japan’s ‘Tsubo Musume’ |publisher=Allkpop.com |date=2011-12-28 |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl]
Mr. Simple became the 85th best-selling single of 2011 in Japan according to the Oricon chartshttp://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/y/2011/more/5/ and is certified Gold by the RIAJ for 100,000 units shipped to Japanese music stores.http://www.riaj.or.jp/data/others/gold/201112.html

==Background==

The song was released along with the rest of the album's tracks on August 2, 2011 and has topped online charts hours after its release. The song was written by Yoo Young-jin, which was also the writer of their previous lead singles "Twins (Knock Out)", "Don't Don", "Sorry, Sorry" and "Bonamana". ==Charts and sales==

The single debuted at number three in the Oricon Daily Singles Chart on December 6, 2011, selling 17,000 copies.http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/d/2011-12-06/ It plunged down to number six for three days and later peaked at number one by December 10, 2011, the day in which the group's Super Show 4 Osaka leg commenced, selling 36,000 copies, beating AKB48's Ue kara Mariko which sold 23,000 copies, at number two.http://www.oricon.co.jp/rank/js/d/2011-12-10/ The latter marks Super Junior's first time to have a release to peak at number one since debut.[tpl]cite web|author=by leesa86 |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2011/12/shinee-and-super-junior-climb-to-the-top-of-the-oricon-charts |title=SHINee and Super Junior climb to the top of the Oricon Charts |publisher=Allkpop.com |date=2011-12-08 |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl] By the end of 2011, the single was ranked as the 85th best-selling single of 2011 in Japan according to the Oricon charts.
