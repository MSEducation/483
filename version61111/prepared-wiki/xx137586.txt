[[Berkeley Zen Center]]

CATEGORIES: Zen centers, Buddhist temples in California, Culture of Berkeley, California, Religious organizations established in 1967, Places of worship in Alameda County, California

Berkeley Zen Center (BZC), temple name [tpl]nihongo|Shogakuji|正覚寺|Shōgaku-ji[/tpl], is a Sōtō Zen Buddhist practice center located in Berkeley, California led by Sojun Mel Weitsman. An informal affiliate to the San Francisco Zen Center (SFZC),Ford, 129 BZC was originally founded in 1967 by Weitsman and Shunryu Suzuki as a satellite group for the SFZC.Dimidjian, 137 Despite founding the center, Weitsman was not installed as abbot there until 1985, one year after receiving Dharma transmission from Hoitsu Suzuki.Gach, 230 Weitsman's Dharma heir, Alan Senauke, lives on site with his wife Laurie Senauke (as of 1999) and also works for the Buddhist Peace Fellowship.Prebish, 108 Another former teacher at BZC was Maylie Scott, who died in 2001.Queen, 266 In 1969 Zenkei Blanche Hartman began sitting zazen at BZC, receiving Dharma transmission from Weitsman in 1988.http://www.zencircle.org/speakers.html In 1979 the center relocated to its current location on Russell Street—and today houses a small group of residents who live on site.http://www.berkeleyzencenter.org/aboutBZC.shtml
