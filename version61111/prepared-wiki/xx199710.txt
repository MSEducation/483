</ref> It is a 0.3-1.3 m tall [[perennial plant|perennial]] [[herbaceous]] plant, native to [[fen]]s and damp grassy meadows in [[Siberia]], [[Central Europe|Central]] and [[Eastern Europe]]. Once fairly common, it has disappeared from many places in [[Europe]], owing to drainage of [[wetlands]] and competition from other plants invading its natural habitats; when growing in shade plants do not flower or set seed very well and seed germination is greatly reduced.<ref>http://www.ib-pan.krakow.pl/pubs-pdf/Biuletyn%20Ogr.%20Bot./2003/bo12_11-22.pdf</ref> This species is sometimes grown in gardens for its large leaves and tall spike like arrangement of yellow daisy like flowers.
