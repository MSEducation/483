[tpl]Nihongo|Hitomi Katayama|片山 瞳|Hitomi Katayama|born September 22, 1980 in Fukuoka[/tpl] is a Japanese actress.

==Career==

Katayama started her career as a fashion model. In 2007, Katayama was chosen to star in Osamu Minorikawa’s film, Life Can Be So Wonderful.[tpl]cite web|title=『世界はときどき美しい』片山瞳さんインタビュー|url=http://www.fjmovie.com/main/interview/2007/03_sekaiha.html|work=fjmovie.com|accessdate=13 August 2013|language=Japanese[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=片山瞳インタビュー|url=http://auctions.yahoo.co.jp/html/entget/200703/sekaihatokidoki/interview1a.html|publisher=Yahoo|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] In 2008, she co-starred in the TV drama, Ashita no Kita Yoshio.[tpl]cite web|title=Katayama Hitomi|url=http://kotobank.jp/word/%E7%89%87%E5%B1%B1%E7%9E%B3|publisher=Talent Databank|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] In 2012, Katayama starred as the heroine in Koji Wakamatsu’s Petrel Hotel Blue (which had a special showing at the Busan Film Festival), and in 2013, again co-starred in Wakamatsu's film The Millenial Rapture (featured at the Venice Film Festival). 
Upcoming film works include as a leading role And Then I Knew 'Twas Wind by Yoshihiro Hanno, a co-starring role in Shin Yotsuya Kaidan by Takashi Miike, and a co-starring role in Tokyo Tribe by Sion Sono.[tpl]cn|date=February 2014[/tpl]
In 2014, Katayama moved to Los Angeles to start a new career.[tpl]cn|date=February 2014[/tpl]
