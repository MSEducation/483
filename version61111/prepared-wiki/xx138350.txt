[[Steffen Basho-Junghans]]

CATEGORIES: German musicians, Fingerstyle guitarists, Avant-garde guitarists, Living people, 1953 births, People from Thuringia

Steffen Basho-Junghans (born Steffen Junghans in 1953)[tpl]cite web|last=Wilmoth|first=Charlie|title=Steffen Basho-Junghans biography|url=http://allmusic.com/artist/steffen-basho-junghans-p230621/biography|publisher=allmusic.com|accessdate=13 January 2011[/tpl] is a German guitarist and composer. He was born in North Thuringia in the former German Democratic Republic. At the age of 17 he taught himself to play acoustic guitar and in 1978 formed the folk band Wacholder. In 1979, influenced by the American guitarist Leo Kottke,[tpl]cite web|last=Jacobson|first=Nils |title=Song of the Earth review|url=http://www.allaboutjazz.com/php/article.php?id=6585|publisher=allaboutjazz.com|accessdate=13 January 2011[/tpl] he began performing solo instrumental music on 6- and 12-string guitar. Throughout the 1980s he became influenced by artists on John Fahey's Takoma Records label. During this period he discovered the music of Robbie Basho, and consequently became interested in Japanese haiku poet Matsuo Bash≈ç, who had influenced Robbie Basho. Steffen Junghans incorporated the name 'Basho' into his own,[tpl]cite web|last=Tilland|first=William|title=Rivers and Bridges - Steffen Basho-Junghans review|url=http://allmusic.com/album/rivers-and-bridges-r625259/review|publisher=allmusic.com|accessdate=13 January 2011[/tpl] as a mark of respect. Throughout the 1980s he lectured on acoustic guitar history and performed numerous concerts. 
Steffen Basho-Junghans has released recordings on the German label Blue Moment Arts; the American labels Sublingual, Strange Attractors and Locust; the Italian label Sillyboy; the Australian label Preservation; and Kning Disk in Sweden, appears on several compilations. His music, unorthodox explorations between traditions and experimentation, is influenced by North American Indian, Asian, New music and European classical music.
