[[St. John's Evangelical Lutheran Church (Livingston, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Religious buildings completed in 1901, 20th-century Lutheran church buildings, Lutheran churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Churches in Columbia County, New York

St. John's Evangelical Lutheran Church is a historic Evangelical Lutheran church at 923 NY 19 in Livingston, Columbia County, New York.  It was built in 1901-1902 and is a brick building with a medium pitched gable roof, deep bracketed cornice, and semi-engaged tower in the center bay of the front facade in the Late Gothic Revival style church.  It is a one story building constructed of red pressed brick and wood frame, a high basement level, a high pitched gable roof, and two priminent towers of different heights.  The front gable features a three part pointed arch window.
