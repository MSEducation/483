[[Herve]]

CATEGORIES: Municipalities of Liège (province)

Herve is a Walloon municipality of Belgium in Province of Liège. On January 1, 2006 Herve had a total population of 16,772. The total area is [tpl]convert|56.84|km²|2|abbr=out[/tpl] which gives a population density of 295 inhabitants per km².
It is famed for its cheese, the Herve.

==Architecture==

==Municipal merger==

On January 1, 1977, the municipality of Herve was merged with Battice, Bolland, Chaineux, Charneux, Grand-Rechain, Julémont and Xhendelesse under the name of "City of Herve" ("Ville de Herve" in French).
Herve is currently constituted of 11 villages: Battice, Bolland, Bruyères, Chaineux, Charneux, Grand-Rechain, Herve, José, Julémont, Manaihant, Xhendelesse.
There are a number of smaller villages in the Herve region, such as Hacboister.
