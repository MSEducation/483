[[Mutsu-Sawabe Station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Aomori Prefecture, Gonō Line, 1936 establishments in Japan

[tpl]nihongo| Mutsu-Sawabe Station|陸奥沢辺駅|Mutsu-Sawabe-eki[/tpl] is a railway station on the JR East Gonō Line located in the town of Fukaura, Aomori Prefecture, Japan. It is 53.6 rail kilometers from the southern terminus of the Gonō Line at Higashi-Noshiro Station.

==History==

Mutsu-Sawabe Station was opened on July 30, 1932 as a station on the Japanese Government Railways (JGR). With the privatization of the Japanese National Railways (successor of JGR) on April 1, 1987, it came under the operational control of JR East.

==Lines==

==Station layout==

Mutsu-Sawabe Station has  a single side platform serving bidirectional traffic. The station is unattended, and is managed from Fukaura Station. The station building is of identical design to that of [tpl]ja-stalink| Ōmagoshi [/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Higashi-Hachimori[/tpl].
