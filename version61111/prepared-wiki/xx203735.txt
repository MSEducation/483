[[Robert F. Sargent]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing, Year of death missing, War photographers, American photographers, United States Coast Guard personnel, Operation Overlord people

Robert F. Sargent was a Chief Photographer's Mate in the United States Coast Guard.  He is best known for Into the Jaws of Death, a photograph he took of troops of Company E, 16th Infantry, 1st Infantry Division landing on Omaha Beach from a Coast Guard landing craft (from the U.S. Coast Guard-manned [tpl]USS|Samuel Chase|APA-26|6[/tpl]) on D-Day.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= August 27, 1999|url = http://www.nytimes.com/1999/08/27/arts/photography-review-an-american-century-through-the-government-s-lens.html|title = PHOTOGRAPHY REVIEW; An American Century, Through the Government's Lens|work=New York Times| accessdate = June 25, 2011 | last=Goldberg| first= Vicki|quote=[/tpl][tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= 12 September 2010 |url = http://www.famouspictures.org/greatest-generation-d-day-landing/|title = Greatest Generation D-day landing|publisher = Famous Pictures| accessdate = May 25, 2013 | last=Lucas| first= Dean|quote=[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Price|first=Scott T.|url=http://www.uscg.mil/history/articles/h_normandy.asp|title=U.S. Coast Guard at Normandy|publisher=U.S. Coast Guard Historian's Office|accessdate=January 30, 2012[/tpl]
