Jason Dewande Bell (born April 1, 1978)[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uclabruins.com/sports/m-footbl/mtt/bell_jason00.html|title=UCLA Athletics: Jason Bell|year=2000|publisher=UCLABruins.com|accessdate=2010-11-04[/tpl] is a former American football cornerback in the National Football League.  He played college football at UCLA.

==Early life==

Bell was born on April 1, 1978 in Long Beach, California to parents Cortland and Geraldine Bell. He attended Millikan High School and majored in history at UCLA.
Jason Bell played football at UCLA where he was a 3-year starter at corner. UCLA won the PAC 10 championship in 1998, when he led the team in passes defensed.
Bell played 7 seasons in the NFL. After joining the Dallas Cowboys in 2001, Bell was traded to the Houston Texans in 2002 where he played 4 seasons. In 2006 Bell went to the New York Giants. After a back injury, Bell was on the Giants injured reserved list in 2007, and retired in 2008 after career-ending back surgery.
After his retirement from the NFL in 2008, Bell attended the Harvard Executive Business program and transitioned into private wealth management.  He joined Masters Private Client Group,http://masterspcg.com where he is currently a partner, specializing in professional athletes and entertainers and their unique financial needs.  He has been noted as one of the “Top 36 Football Players on Wall Street” by Business Insider.http://www.businessinsider.com/best-football-players-on-wall-street-2013-6?op=1
