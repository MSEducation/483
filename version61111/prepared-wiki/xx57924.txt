</ref> The route remained intact until plans for a bypass around Haddonfield arose in 1929.<ref name="1929law">[tpl]cite book|title=ROUTE NO. 41. Moorestown to Fairview. Beginning at a point in Route No. 38 in the vicinity of Moorestown and passing in the vicinity of Haddonfield and connecting with Route No. 47 at Fairview|publisher=New Jersey State Legislature|year=1929|chapter=Chapter 125|page=119[/tpl]</ref> By the [[1953 New Jersey state highway renumbering|second state highway renumbering in 1953]], the bypass around Haddonfield had only been partially built, so instead, the [[New Jersey State Highway Department]] realigned Route 41 onto the Kings Highway, leaving the former route unnumbered.<ref name="nj1953">[tpl]citation|title=1953 renumbering|url=http://en.wikisource.org/wiki/1953_New_Jersey_state_highway_renumbering|publisher=New Jersey Department of Highways|accessdate=July 31, 2009[/tpl]</ref> The route remained unnumbered until the 1960s, when it was given the designation of Route 154.<ref name="chevron">[tpl]cite map|publisher=[[Chevron Oil Company]]|title= Map of New Jersey |year=1969|cartography=[[H.M. Gousha]][/tpl]</ref> 
In 1938, the State Highway Department tore down the Ellisburg General Store, a market located at the intersection of then-Route 41 and Route 40 (now Route 70) to construct a new traffic circle at the junction.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cherryhill-nj.com/about/default.asp|title=About Us|year=2009|publisher=Cherry Hill, New Jersey|accessdate=October 15, 2009[/tpl] This circle, known as the Ellisburg Circle, was removed in 1992, while plans arose in the late 1980s to remove the congested[tpl]cite news|title=Rough Road To Improving Route 70|date=July 8, 1984|publisher=The Philadelphia Inquirer|location=Philadelphia, Pennsylvania[/tpl] circle, hosting the junctions of Route 154, 41 and 70. The impetus to remove the structure and replace it with a four-way intersection[tpl]cite news|title=Cherry Hill Awaits Details of Route 70 Project|date=November 25, 1984|publisher=The Philadelphia Inquirer|location=Philadelphia, Pennsylvania[/tpl] was produced in 1987, with a cost of $8 million (1987 USD) to rebuild.[tpl]cite news|title=Route 70 Awaits The Green Light For The Future|date=April 29, 1987|publisher=The Philadelphia Inquirer|location=Philadelphia, Pennsylvania[/tpl]
