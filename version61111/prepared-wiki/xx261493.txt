[[Eternal Light Peace Memorial]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures completed in 1938, Ceremonial flames, American Civil War military monuments and memorials, Limestone buildings, Paul Philippe Cret buildings, Towers in Pennsylvania, Gettysburg Battlefield, Peace monuments and memorials

The Eternal Light Peace Memorial is a 1938 Gettysburg Battlefield monument commemorating the 1913 Gettysburg reunion for the fiftieth anniversary of the 1863 Battle of Gettysburg.  The natural gas flame in a one ton bronze urn is atop a tower on a stone pedestrian terrace with views from the terraced hill summit over about [tpl]Convert|400|sqmi|km2|abbr=on[/tpl], and the flame is visible from [tpl]Convert|20|mi|km|abbr=on[/tpl] away.

==History==

In 1887, "the Philadelphia Brigade, Col. Cowan and others" advocated a "grand monument to American Heroism on this battlefield",[tpl]Cite news |date=October 4, 1887 |title=Town and Country |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=ilQmAAAAIBAJ&sjid=JgAGAAAAIBAJ&pg=1646,6385126&dq=round-top-park+1887&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |newspaper=Gettysburg Compiler |accessdate=2011-07-07[/tpl]col. 4 and President William McKinley spoke to Cowan about North/South peace in 1900.New York Times, March 24, 1900, p. 3.  The "first tentative programme" of October 1910 for the 1913 Gettysburg reunion planned a "Peace Jubilee" to be held on "National Day" with an oration by President Woodrow Wilson and the cornerstone placement for the "Great Peace Memorial" at noon.[tpl]Cite report |last=Beitler |first=Lewis Eugene (editor and compiler) |date=December 31, 1913 |title=Fiftieth Anniversary of the Battle of Gettysburg: Report of the Pennsylvania Commission |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=swkTAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA167 |format=Google Books |location=Harrisburg, Pennsylvania |publisher=Wm. Stanley Bay (state printer) |pages=173 |accessdate=2011-02-04 |quote[/tpl] (Col Andrew Cowan speech, July 3, 1913)[tpl]Rp|173[/tpl]  However, after being "presented, January 11th, 1912, to the Joint Committee of the Congress for the Celebration of the Fiftieth Anniversary of the Battle of Gettysburg", funding was "found, in March 1912, impossible of accomplishment in the 62nd Congress".[tpl]Rp|166[/tpl] Instead of the laying of a cornerstone, on July 3 during the New York Veterans' Celebration in the 1913 Great Tent,[tpl]Rp|153[/tpl] Colonel Andrew Cowan gave a speech advocating the memorial, and "steps to accomplish such purpose were immediately taken … which resulted in the Gettysburg Peace Memorial Association being formed. … That Association's Bill was, on December 20th, 1913, presented to Congress…creating the Gettysburg Memorial Commission."[tpl]Rp|167[/tpl]
The original plan was for a $250,000 "monument of peace" at The Angle,[tpl]Cite news |date=July 3, 1913 |title=Veteran's Joyful Over Camp's End |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=S0kiAAAAIBAJ&sjid=rnQFAAAAIBAJ&pg=2009,237140&dq=the-angle+gettysburg&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |location=New London, Connecticut |newspaper=The Day |accessdate=2011-10-19[/tpl] but despite 1914 "Peace Memorial Bill" presentations to the US House of Representatives[tpl]Cite news |title=The Peace Memorial Bill: Speech of Colonel Andrew Cowan of Louisville |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=kdElAAAAIBAJ&sjid=yfQFAAAAIBAJ&pg=7214,4563599&dq=andrew-cowan+gettysburg&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |newspaper=Gettysburg Compiler |accessdate=2011-10-19 |quote=[/tpl] that compared the planned memorial with Christ the Redeemer of the Andes and the Lincoln Memorial, federal funding remained "postponed".  In August 1936, the memorial's commission issued 10,000 four page circulars to publicize the plan, and Virginia in 1936 was the first to appropriate funds.[tpl]Cite web |date= |title=Eternal Peace Light Memorial |url=http://www.hmdb.org/marker.asp?marker=15447 |publisher=National Park Service |accessdate=2011-02-02[/tpl]  In 1937 the Pennsylvania legislature began planning a peace memorial on Big Round Top,[tpl]cite news |date=Jan 20, 1937 |title=Senator Rice Sponsors Bill for Gettysburg Peace Memorial |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=CigzAAAAIBAJ&sjid=bQAGAAAAIBAJ&pg=6811,2285355&dq=1937+big-round-top&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |newspaper=Gettysburg Compiler |accessdate=2010-03-20[/tpl] and the state's "Peace Memorial Bill" was signed on February 24, 1937, to appropriate $5,000 for the state's "Gettysburg Peace Memorial fund".[tpl]Cite news |title=February 27, 1937 |title=Signs Bill for Light Memorial |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=ZoklAAAAIBAJ&sjid=ufIFAAAAIBAJ&pg=5568,3308147&dq=peace-memorial-bill&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |newspaper=The Star and Sentinel |accessdate=2011-02-02[/tpl]  The peace memorial committee selected from the 6 designs by August 1937 and on December 10, 1937, Lee Lawrie was announced as the sculptor for the structure "overlooking Big Round Top and Little Round Top".[tpl]Cite news |date=December 16, 1937 |title=3,000 C. W. Vets to attend Reunion |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=e889AAAAIBAJ&sjid=ZTcMAAAAIBAJ&pg=7512,2289228&dq=lee-lawrie+gettysburg&hl=en |newspaper=New Oxford Item |accessdate=2011-10-17 |quote=[/tpl]  With additional funding by New York, Indiana, Tennessee, Illinois, and Wisconsin; the $60,000 monument was instead completed northwest of Gettysburg, Pennsylvania.  Groundbreaking was on February 14, and the last foot of piping for the flame's gas supply was placed on May 31.[tpl]Cite news |date=May 31, 1938 |title=Roosevelt to Dedicate Gettysburg Memorial |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=d7IhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=65sFAAAAIBAJ&pg=4020,6488061&dq=woodring+gettysburg&hl=en |newspaper=Reading Eagle |accessdate=2011-02-10 |quote=[/tpl]

===Dedication===

Attendance for the memorial's dedication at the 1938 Gettysburg reunion was 250,000; a further 100,000 attempted to attend but failed to arrive due to congested roads. President Franklin D. Roosevelt arrived at a temporary platform on his special train via the Reading Railroad from the North after leaving Springwood at Hyde Park NY that morning. The U.S. 3d Cavalry Division escorted the President's motorcade to the memorial on Oak Hill,[tpl]Cite news |date=July 4, 1938 |title=Philosophy of Lincoln used by Roosevelt |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=v4NSAAAAIBAJ&sjid=Pw0EAAAAIBAJ&pg=7013,636571&dq=1938+roosevelt+gettysburg&hl=en |newspaper=Milwaukee Sentinel |accessdate=2011-02-08[/tpl] and Roosevelt's open car arrived with a 21 gun salute.
Roosevelt used "a new Mobile sound system unit" to address the audiencehttp://news.google.com/newspapers?id=B7QhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=6psFAAAAIBAJ&pg=3982,4979085&dq=1938+roosevelt+gettysburg&hl=en including the veterans on specially-constructed grandstands under a canopy.[tpl]Cite news |date=July 4, 1938 |title=Flame Burning on Monument at Gettysburg |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=k9tBAAAAIBAJ&sjid=3akMAAAAIBAJ&pg=3879,598708&dq=1938+roosevelt+gettysburg&hl=en |newspaper=The Telegraph Herald |accessdate=2011-02-08[/tpl] As his nine minute speech ended at sunset, the Peace Memorial covered by a 50 foot flag was unveiled by George N. Lockwood and Confederate A. G. Harris (both age 91) with two regular army attendants a photocell automatically lit the flame[tpl]cite news| url=http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F60815FE3F5D12738DDDAC0894DF405B888FF1D3 | work=The New York Times | title=Eternal' Light Shut Off in Day | date=July 5, 1938[/tpl] Grand Army of the Republic chaplain Martin V. Stone ended the ceremony with a benediction prayer, and on the way to his car Roosevelt spoke with the oldest attending veteran, William Barnes of the US Colored Troops, age 112. A Sixth Field Artillery battery near Oak Hill fired a 21 gun salute as the President departed at 7 p.m. (his train to Washington used the Western Maryland Railroad.
The flame was reduced to a pilot light during World War II (from December 25, 1941) and just prior to the 1946 Paris peace conference, President Truman commented about the inscribed motto, Peace Eternal in a Nation United: "That is what we want, but let's change that word (nation) to world and we'll have something."http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=RXglAAAAIBAJ&sjid=BfMFAAAAIBAJ&pg=4015,1093790&dq=gettysburg+peace-memorial&hl=en  The deteriorated Alabama limestone in the lower section that had been approved for use by the Bureau of Standardshttp://news.google.com/newspapers?id=FShUAAAAIBAJ&sjid=NDoNAAAAIBAJ&pg=6799,5579197&dq=gettysburg+peace-memorial&hl=en was replaced with gray granite in June 1941,http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=MIslAAAAIBAJ&sjid=wPIFAAAAIBAJ&pg=2544,3900338&dq=gettysburg+peace-memorial&hl=en and repairs were also made in 1950.  A 1962 protest against nuclear arms and testing was held at the memorial, and the flame was extinguished in 1974 for the oil crisis[tpl]Cite news |title=[Oil Crisis Claims Eternal Flame |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=EaRJAAAAIBAJ&sjid=V4QMAAAAIBAJ&pg=738,4690630&dq=gettysburg+peace-memorial&hl=en |format=Google News Archive |newspaper=Youngstown Vindicator |accessdate=2011-02-02[/tpl] after the 93rd United States Congress prohibited such flames (except for the John F. Kennedy Eternal Flame), and the extinguished gas flame was replaced by an electrical light in 1976. A Gettysburg Peace Celebration Committee had been formed by June 1988, and the gas flame was restored at their Fiftieth Anniversary Rededication on July 3.[tpl]Cite news |last=Black |first=Marshall D. |date=July 5, 1988 |title=Children carry flags of peace to Eternal Light Peace Memorial |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=DBgmAAAAIBAJ&sjid=H_0FAAAAIBAJ&pg=4380,460341&dq=gettysburg+peace-memorial&hl=en |accessdate=2011-10-17 |newspaper=Gettysburg Times Archives |publisher=Times and News Publishing Company[/tpl]
