[[Matthew Ianniello]]

CATEGORIES: 1920 births, 2012 deaths, American mobsters of Italian descent, People from New York City, Genovese crime family, American military personnel of World War II, People convicted of racketeering

Matthew Joseph "Matty the Horse" Ianniello (June 18, 1920 – August 15, 2012) was a New York mobster with the Genovese crime family who was once the acting boss of the Genovese Crime Family. During the 1960s and 70's, Ianniello controlled the lucrative adult entertainment business that were then centered in the Times Square section of Manhattan.

==Young years==

Matthew Ianniello was born in 1920 in Little Italy, Manhattan.[tpl]cite book|last=Collins|first=R. Thomas|title=Newswalker: A Story for Sweeney|year=2002|publisher=RavensYard Publishing, Ltd|isbn=978-1-928928-03-4|pages=120|url=http://books.google.com/books?ei=1LJ3T4W2OKLi0QGryry8DQ&id=5earR_pTmWcC&dq=intitle%3ANewswalker&q=Matty#v=snippet&q=Matty&f=false[/tpl] He grew up in the Italian neighborhoods of New York.
Ianniello was married to Beatrice May and the couple had four children.
Ianiello allegedly got his nickname “Matty The Horse” in a youth baseball game. Ianiello was a gifted player with a hard swing. During one game, the opposing pitcher threw a hard pitch into the face of the batter. A fight erupted in which Ianiello knocked down the pitcher, who was older and taller than him. After this episode, someone remarked about Ianiello: “That boy is as strong as a horse.”
In 1940, Ianniello started working for his uncle as a waiter in a restaurant in the Brooklyn dockyards. In 1942 or 1943, Ianniello enlisted in the U.S. Army and served in World War II. In 1945 Ianiello returned home as a decorated war veteran, having received a purple heart and a bronze star. He went back to work at his uncle's restaurant and by 1949 became partners with his uncle in a second restaurant, Matty's Towncrest Restaurant.
In 1951, Ianniello was arrested on charges of possessing heroin, but the charges were dropped.
In 1960, Ianniello became partners with Edward L. DeCurtis, a longtime associate, in running private afterhours drinking clubs for gay men.  Ianniello would eventually own a string of clubs and nightclubs for both gay and straight men, including the Gilded Grape and the Hay Market.[tpl]cite news|last=Vitello|first=Paul|title=Matthew Ianniello, 92, Former Mafia Boss|url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9807EFDD103AF937A1575BC0A9649D8B63|accessdate=23 September 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=August 24, 2012[/tpl]

==Genovese family==

In the 1960s, Ianniello joined the Genovese crime family, then run by imprisoned boss Vito Genovese. Ianniello's sponsor was mobster and future acting boss Frank Tieri.
Ianniello eventually controlled Almagamated Transit Union, bus drivers Local 1181, giving him the power to extort payments from school bus companies in New York as well as the union driver.[tpl]cite book|last=Jacobs|first=James B.|title=Breaking the devil's pact the battle to free the Teamsters from the mob|publisher=New York University Press|location=New York|isbn=0-8147-4366-8|pages=13|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Po9o6mB0Ir8C&pg=PA13&dq=%22Matthew+Ianniello%22&hl=en&sa=X&ei=jK93T4CaFMnf0QG67Nm_DQ&ved=0CF4Q6AEwBjgK#v=onepage&q=%22Matthew%20Ianniello%22&f=false|coauthors=Cooperman, Kerry T.[/tpl]
On February 2, 1965, Ianniello was indicted on contempt of grand jury charges for refusal to testify. However, the charges were dismissed in 1966.[tpl]cite news|last=Gage|first=Nicholas|title=Study Shows Courts Lenient With Mafiosi|url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F00F17FE3B5B127A93C7AB1782D85F468785F9|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New YOrk Times|date=September 25, 1972[/tpl]
At the beginning of the 1970s Ianniello was promoted to caporegime. By now, Ianniello controlled over 80 restaurants and sex-oriented clubs in New York, including most of those located in the Times Square area of Manhattan.Collins (2002), 126. Officially he still had a respectable job with the union.
In 1972, Colombo crime family rebel Joey Gallo was murdered at Umberto's Clam House, a restaurant in Little Italy, Manhattan that was secretly owned by Ianniello.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2013[/tpl] On April 7, 1972, early in morning, Gallo, who was celebrating his birthday with family and friends, arrived at Umberto's for a late night snack.[tpl]cite news|last=Skillings|first=Pamela |url=http://manhattan.about.com/od/historyandlandmarks/ss/Photo-Tour-Of-Sites-Of-Famous-New-York-Deaths_9.htm  |work=About.com|title=Manhattan, New York: Photo Tour of Sites of Famous New York Deaths|page=9 of 11|accessdate=September 8, 2013[/tpl] When he arrived, Gallo greeted Ianniello. A Colombo associate sitting at the bar saw Gallo and immediately left to notify his superiors. 
Soon afterward, several armed Colombo associates stormed into the restaurant and shot and killed Gallo.[tpl]cite news|last=Gage|first=Nicholas|title=Story of Joe Gallo's Murder|url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F20910FC3F5F117B93C1A9178ED85F468785F9|accessdate=3 November 2011|newspaper=New York Times|date=May 3, 1972[/tpl]  Ianniello was in the kitchen at the time and missed the entire attack. Ianniello later claimed no prior knowledge of the attack[tpl]cite news|journal=The New York Times|date=August 23, 2012|author=Paul Vitello|title=Matthew Ianniello, the Mafia Boss Known as ‘Matty the Horse,’ Dies at 92| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2012/08/23/nyregion/mafia-boss-matty-the-horse-ianniello-dies-at-92.html?_r=0[/tpl] and was not charged in relation to it.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2013[/tpl]
The Nevada Daily Mail reports Matthew was at the cash register that night but "the proprietor dove into the kitchen and lay on the tile floor with his hands over his eyes as soon as Sonny Pinto and two out-of-town torpedoes known only as Cisco and Benny came in the side door blasting. The next thing he knew, Pete "The Greek" Diopoulis, a Gallo bodyguard, was pushing a gun in his face and pulling the trigger but only clicks came out because it had been emptied trying to save Joey."[tpl]cite news|journal=The Nevada Daily Mail| url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=W6AfAAAAIBAJ&sjid=t9UEAAAAIBAJ&pg=1531%2C3294861| title=In These Eateries, Pasta Was Served With Bullets|date=May 5, 1985|page=3A|author=Hugh A. Mulligan[/tpl]
In 1986, Judge Weinfeld sentenced Matthew Ianniello to six years in prison on a racketeering charge that involved skimming over $2 million from bars and restaurants (including Umberto's Clam House, the Peppermint Lounge, and a topless bar called the Mardi Gras, all in Manhattan), secretly owned by Matthew; his business partner Benjamin Cohen of North Hills, L.I.; and seven associates.[tpl]cite news|journal=The New York Times|title=Ianniello Is Sentenced In Racketeering Trial|date= February 16, 1986| url=http://www.nytimes.com/1986/02/16/nyregion/ianniello-is-sentenced-in-racketeering-trial.html?scp=44&sq=Matthew%20Ianniello&st=cse[/tpl]

==Indictments==

On February 28, 1985, Ianniello was indicted in federal court in New York on charges of racketeering charges involving the operation of several restaurants, bars and carting companies.[tpl]cite news|last=Lubasch|first=Arnold H|title=REPUTED CRIME BOSSES ARRAIGNED|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1985/03/01/nyregion/reputed-crime-bosses-arraigned.html|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=March 1, 1985[/tpl] Using a wiretap on Ianniello's office, agents assembled proof that he was skimming profits from several establishments that he secretly owned. On December 30, 1985, Ianniello was convicted on numerous counts.[tpl]cite news|last=Lubasch|first=Arnold H|title=9 OF 10 FOUND GUILTY IN SKIMMING TRIAL|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1985/12/31/nyregion/9-of-10-found-guilty-in-skimming-trial.html?scp=52&sq=Matthew%20Ianniello&st=cse|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=December 31, 1985[/tpl]  On February 16, 1986, Ianniello was sentenced to six years in federal prison on the 1985 charges.
On May 13, 1986, Ianniello was acquitted on all charges in the 1986 indictment on racketeering in the garbage industry.[tpl]cite news|last=Saxon|first=Wolfgang|title=SIX DEFENDANTS ARE ACQUITTED IN A MOB TRIAL|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1986/05/14/nyregion/six-defendants-are-acquitted-in-a-mob-trial.html|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=May 14, 1986[/tpl]  On May 17, 1986, Ianiello was indicted in federal court in New York on new charges of labor racketeering, construction bid-rigging, extortion, gambling and murder conspiracies.[tpl]cite news|title=U.S. Attorney Reports Indictment of Ianniello|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1986/05/16/nyregion/us-attorney-reports-indictment-of-ianniello.html|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=May 16, 1986[/tpl]
On May 18, 1988, Ianniello was indicted again in Newark, New Jersey on racketeering charges involving the 1984 Genovese takeover of a gravel company in Edgewater, New Jersey.[tpl]cite news|last=Uhlig|first=Mark A|title=8 Reputed Members Of Genovese Family Indicted by U.S. Jury|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1988/05/19/nyregion/8-reputed-members-of-genovese-family-indicted-by-us-jury.html|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=Neew York Times|date=May 19, 1988[/tpl] On October 13, 1988, Ianniello was sentenced to 13 years in federal prison after being convicted of the 1986 bid rigging racketeering charges.[tpl]cite news|title=Salerno, Now Serving 100 Years, Gets 70 More in Bid-Rigging Case|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1988/10/14/nyregion/salerno-now-serving-100-years-gets-70-more-in-bid-rigging-case.html?scp=64&sq=Matthew%20Ianniello&st=cse|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=October 14, 1988[/tpl]

==Acting Boss==

In 1995, Ianniello was released from prison. When Genovese boss Vincent Gigante went to prison, Ianniello became acting boss. By 1998, Ianiello was deeply involved in Almagated Transit Union Local 1181, a bus drivers union."Two Accused of Extorting Bus Companies" By Paul von Zielbauer New York Times June 3, 2009 Through the union, Ianiello forced a medical center to pay $100,000 to renew their lease and then make regular cash payments in order to keep it.[tpl]cite news|last=Hays|first=Tom|title=Authorities crack down on New York's Genovese crime family, with 20 arrests|url=http://www.nctimes.com/news/national/authorities-crack-down-on-new-york-s-genovese-crime-family/article_83fed787-19ce-53f6-84e0-fd1cf98b2482.html|accessdate=1 April 2012|newspaper=North County Times|date=July 29, 2005[/tpl]  Between 2001 and 2005, protection fees on Connecticut garbage businesses earned Ianniello more than $800,000.
On July 27, 2005, Ianniello was indicted on racketeering charges in New York involving extortion and loansharking. Agents arresting Ianniello at his home reported that he was watching the film The Godfather III.[tpl]cite news|last=Rashbaum|first=William K|title=Metro Briefing [tpl]![/tpl] New York: Manhattan: Reputed Mob Family Boss Indicted|url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=940DE1DD113FF93AA15754C0A9639C8B63&scp=40&sq=Matthew%20Ianniello&st=cse|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=July 29, 2005[/tpl]"Reputed Genovese family members indicted" Marissa Muller CNN July 28, 2005; On June 10, 2006, Ianiello was indicted in federal court in New Haven on charges of racketeering involving trash hauling in Southwestern Connecticut.[tpl]cite news|title=Mob Figures Are Charged With Controlling Trash Hauling in Connecticut|url=http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F60A13FD39550C738DDDAF0894DE404482&scp=14&sq=Matthew%20Ianniello&st=cse|accessdate=31 March 2012|newspaper=New York Times|date=June 10, 2006[/tpl] In 2006, Ianniello pleaded guilty to the New York racketeering charges and received an 18 month prison sentence. The same year, he pleaded guilty in Connecticut to two racketeering charges for extorting the trash hauling industry and was sentenced to two years in federal prison to run concurrent with the 18 month New York sentence.McShane 2007 Ianniello's attorney had asked for leniency, saying Ianniello had cancer and was in general poor health.

==Later life==

On April 3, 2009, Ianniello was released from the Federal Medical Center (FMC) for prisoners in Butner, North Carolina.Bureau of Prisons Inmate Locator On August 15, 2012, Ianniello died at his Long Island home in Old Westbury, New York of health problems related to heart ailments and other illnesses, including prostate cancer.
