[[Liviu Comes]]

CATEGORIES: Romanian composers, 1918 births, 2004 deaths

Liviu Comes (December 13, 1918, Şerel, Hunedoara County, Romania — September 28, 2004) was a Romanian composer and musicologist.
He studied music at the Municipal Music Conservatory in Târgu Mureş (1927–1937), later at the Cluj Music Conservatory (1946–1950). He also studied medicine at the Cluj-Sibiu University (1937–1943).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.operanationala.ro/personalitati/pers_detalii.php?id_pers=2895&prenume_pers=Liviu&nume_pers=COMES|title=Liviu COMES|language=Romanian|accessdate=December 27, 2013[/tpl]
Between 1950 and 1969 he was a professor of harmony, counterpoint and musical forms at the Music Conservatory in Cluj. Between 1965 and 1970 he was chancellor of the same Conservatory.
From 1969 to 1981 he was professor of counterpoint and fugue at the Bucharest Music Conservatory. There he was a department head from 1979 to 1981.
Between 1977 and 1990 he was secretary of the didactic and children's section at the Composers' Union of Romania.
He was awarded several prizes, including the "Romanian Academy's Prize" (1974) and "The Romanian Composers' Union Prize" (1972, 1976, 1981, 1985, 1987, 1991, 1998).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.compendium.ro/pers_detalii.php?id_pers=2895&prenume_pers=Liviu&nume_pers=COMES|title=Liviu COMES|language=Romanian|accessdate=December 27, 2013[/tpl]
There is a composition contest named in his honor.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.amgd.ro/creatie-si-cercetare/concurs-de-compozi-ie-liviu-comes/474/|title=Concurs de compozitie Liviu Comes|language=Romanian|accessdate=December 27, 2013[/tpl]
In 1994, he was made an honorary citizen of Cluj-Napoca.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.primariaclujnapoca.ro/cetateni-de-onoare.html|title=Cetăţeni de Onoare ai Municipiului Cluj-Napoca|language=Romanian|accessdate=December 29, 2013[/tpl]
