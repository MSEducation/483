[[Scorpaeniformes]]

CATEGORIES: Scorpaeniformes, Acanthopterygii, Fish orders, Articles which contain graphical timelines

The Scorpaeniformes are a diverse order of ray-finned fish, but have also been called the Scleroparei. It is one of the five largest orders of bony fishes by number of species with over 1,320.Umich.edu http://www.biokids.umich.edu/critters/Scorpaeniformes
They are known as "mail-cheeked" fishes due to their distinguishing characteristic, the suborbital stay: a backwards extension of the third circumorbital bone (part of the lateral head/cheek skeleton, below the eye socket) across the cheek to the preoperculum, to which it is connected in most species.[tpl]cite book |editor=Paxton, J.R. & Eschmeyer, W.N.|author= Eschmeyer, William N.|year=1998|title=Encyclopedia of Fishes|publisher= Academic Press|location=San Diego|pages= 175|isbn= 0-12-547665-5[/tpl]
Scorpaeniform fishes are carnivorous, mostly feeding on crustaceans and on smaller fish. Most species live on the sea bottom in relatively shallow waters, although species are known from deep water, from the midwater, and even from fresh water. They typically have spiny heads, and rounded pectoral and caudal fins. Most species are less than [tpl]convert|30|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] in length, but the full size range of the order varies from the velvetfishes, which can be just [tpl]convert|2|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] long as adults, to the lingcod, which can reach [tpl]convert|150|cm|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] in length.
