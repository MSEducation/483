[[5-Methoxysalicylic acid]]

CATEGORIES: Natural phenols, Salicylic acids

5-Methoxysalicylic acid is a chemical compound. It is a component of castoreum, the exudate from the castor sacs of the mature beaver.[tpl]Cite journal|doi=10.1007/BF00994195|title=Pheromonal activity of single castoreum constituents in beaver,Castor canadensis|year=1991|last1=Müller-Schwarze|first1=D.|last2=Houlihan|first2=Peter W.|journal=Journal of Chemical Ecology|volume=17|issue=4|pages=715–734|pmid=24258917[/tpl]
A mixture of 5-methoxysalicylic acid and spermine can be used as a matrix for oligonucleotides analysis in MALDI mass spectrometry.[tpl]Cite journal|pmid=11322192|year=2001|last1=Distler|first1=AM|last2=Allison|first2=J|title=5-Methoxysalicylic acid and spermine: A new matrix for the matrix-assisted laser desorption/ionization mass spectrometry analysis of oligonucleotides|volume=12|issue=4|pages=456–62|doi=10.1016/S1044-0305(01)00212-4|journal=Journal of the American Society for Mass Spectrometry[/tpl]
It is an isomer of vanillic acid.
