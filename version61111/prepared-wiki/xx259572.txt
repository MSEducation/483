[[William McCappin]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, Alumni of Trinity College, Dublin, Anglican archdeacons in Ireland, 20th-century Anglican bishops, Bishops of Connor, 1991 deaths, Irish military chaplains, World War II chaplains

William John McCappin was an Irish clergyman in the second half of the 20th century. Born in  1919 and educated at Trinity College, Dublin,
he was ordained in 1943 and began his career as a curate at St Mark, Armagh. He was a Chaplain to the Forces  from 1944 to 1947 and then Curate in charge of Ardoyne until 1951.Crockford's Clerical Directory 1975-76 London: Oxford University Press, 1976 ISBN 0-19-200008-X He held incumbencies at St Patrick, JordanstownChurch web site and then St Bartolomew, Belfast before becoming   Archdeacon of Connor in 1969 and Connor in 1981.Fryde, E. B.; Greenway, D. E.; Porter, S.; Roy, I. (1996). Handbook of British Chronology (Third Edition, revised ed.). Cambridge: CUP. ISBN 0-521-56350-X.  He retired in 1987 and died on 3 July 1992.
