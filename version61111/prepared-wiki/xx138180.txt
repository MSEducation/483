Gregory Michael (born May 30, 1981) is an American actor who most recently starred as "Grant," a fraternity brother, on the ABC Family television series Greek.

==Biography==

Michael was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. He is the only son of Gregory and Rosaria, and has one older sister, Christina. He attended the Christian Brothers' La Salle College High School, in Wyndmoor, Pennsylvania. Michael went on to college to pursue his studies in theatre and acting at Pennsylvania State University. Michael currently resides in Los Angeles, California.[tpl]cite web | title=Myspace.com | work=Gregory Michael Official Myspace | url=http://www.myspace.com/gregory_michael | accessdate=April 10, 2008[/tpl]

==Acting career==

At a young age, Michael was active in community theater, portraying lead characters in the musicals Blood Brothers, The Music Man, and Damn Yankees, which were performed at the Walnut Street Theatre in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania. In high school, he captured national championship awards in his Dramatic and Humorous Interpretation of literature, in competing with the National Forensics League, under the coaching of the late Brother Rene Sterner FSC.[tpl]cite web | title=nflonline.org | work=NFL Coach | url=http://www.nflonline.org/Main/BroReneSterner | accessdate=May 19, 2009[/tpl]
Michael's first professional work was for Walt Disney Entertainment, at Walt Disney World in Orlando, Florida as a character performer.[tpl]cite web | title=SoapOperaDigest.com | work=Interview with Gregory Michael | url=http://www.soapoperadigest.com/features/as-the-world-turns/interviews/Michael/ | accessdate=May 19, 2009[/tpl] The Walt Disney World Christmas Day Parade was one of Michael's first appearances on television.[tpl]cite web | title=IMDb.com | work=Biography | url=http://imdb.com/name/nm1640764/bio | accessdate=May 19, 2009[/tpl] Michael's notable television roles include; "Clark Watson" on the CBS soap opera As The World Turns, "Kevin Archer", in the supernatural gothic soap opera Dante's Cove, and "Grant Ellis" on ABC Family's Greek.
