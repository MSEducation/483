[[Agnes Claypole Moody]]

CATEGORIES: 1870 births, 1954 deaths, American zoologists, Cornell University alumni

Agnes Mary Claypole Moody (1870 - 1954) was an American zoologist and professor of natural science.

==Background==

Agnes was born in Bristol, England to Jane (Trotter) and Edward Walker Claypole. She had a twin sister, Edith Jane Claypole (1870-1915), who was also a biologist.[tpl]cite web|title=Agnes Mary Claypole Moody (1870-1954) Smithsonian Biography|url=http://siarchives.si.edu/collections/siris_arc_306379|publisher=Smithsonian Institution Archives|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]  Agnes attended Buchtel College, and in 1894 she attended Cornell University for her master's degree. She attained her Ph. D in Chicago University in 1896.[tpl]cite book|title=The Biographical Dictionary of Women in Science: Pioneering Lives ..., Volume 1|publisher=Taylor & Francis US|isbn=9780203801451|page=266|url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=QmfyK0QtsRAC&pg=PA266&lpg=PA266&dq=agnes+claypole+moody&source=bl&ots=BSwtXMXlqF&sig=uUFViT_Z81KVb2D_fpBxlMsVagk&hl=en&sa=X&ei=wFkDUrDHC8vPkgWWKA&ved=0CGAQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=agnes%20claypole%20moody&f=false|editor=Joyce Harvey, Marilyn Ogilvie|accessdate=8 August 2013|date=2000-07-27[/tpl]

==Research==

For her thesis, Agnes studied the digestive tract of eels.[tpl]cite web|title=About the Division of Biology|url=http://biology.caltech.edu/about/history/Prehistory.html|work=California Institute of Technology|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] Agnes worked in various positions, including: Instructor in Zoology, and Professor of Natural Science and Curator (1903-4).

==Significance==

Agnes is the first woman appointed to a teaching position in the Medical Department of Cornell University.[tpl]cite journal|title=Obituary.|journal=The British Medical Journal|date=October 29, 1898|page=1375|url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC2434529/pdf/brmedj08688-0079b.pdf|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]
