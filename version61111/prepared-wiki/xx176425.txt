[[Saginaw and Clare County Railroad]]

CATEGORIES: Railway companies established in 1877, Railway companies disestablished in 1889, Defunct Michigan railroads, Predecessors of the Pere Marquette Railway

The Saginaw and Clare County Railroad was a wholly owned subsidiary of the Flint and Pere Marquette Railroad (F&PM). It was chartered on September 4, 1877, to construct a branch line to Lake George, Michigan.Michigan Railroad Commission (1877), xx. On September 30, 1880, the company completed a [tpl]convert|15.5|mi|km|adj=on[/tpl] branch line from Harrison Junction (between Farwell and Clare along the main line) to Harrison. The line did not serve Lake George.Ivey (1919), 223; Michigan Railroad Commission (1881), 159. In 1883 this line was extended north to Meredith (in Franklin Township), for a total length of [tpl]convert|29.91|mi|km[/tpl].Michigan Railroad Commission (1884), 187. The Saginaw & Clare County was consolidated with the F&PM on January 30, 1889.Michigan Railroad Commission (1890),  356.
