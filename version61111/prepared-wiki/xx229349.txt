[[Roshni Chopra]]

CATEGORIES: 1980 births, Indian television actresses, Indian stage actresses, Indian film actresses, Actresses in Hindi cinema, Indian female models, Indian television presenters, Living people, Actresses from Mumbai, People from Delhi, 21st-century Indian actresses

Roshni Chopra is an Indian actress, television presenter and the winner of NDTV Imagine's reality show, Dil Jeetegi Desi Girl.[tpl]cite web|title=Roshni Chopra bags Desi Girl title|url=http://www.hindustantimes.com/Roshni-Chopra-bags-Desi-Girl-title/Article1-567433.aspx|publisher=Hindustan Times|accessdate=18 October 2010[/tpl]
She is known for playing the role of Pia, the younger sister of protagonist Bani Walia in Kasamh Se on Zee TV.[tpl]cite web|title=Desi Girl' Roshni Chopra now eyes Hollywood|url=http://in.movies.yahoo.com/news-detail/93046/Desi-Girl-Roshni-Chopra-eyes-Hollywood.html|publisher=IANS|accessdate=2010-10-18[/tpl] An alumnus of the Our Own English High School, she was also the presenter of the pre and post-match cricket show on Doordarshan known as Fourth Umpire. Chopra was also one of the three hosts of the Indian variant of Got Talent series known as, India's Got Talent in 2009.
Chopra starred in Vikram Bhatt's film, Phhir, which released in  August 12, 2011. She has hosted[tpl]when|date=October 2010[/tpl] the show Comedy Circus Teen Ka Tadka on Sony TV, and is currently the anchor of 'Heroes - Moments and Memories' on Star Sports. She also made an appearance in Maut Ka Khel, an episode of the horror TV series, Aahat on Sony TV.[tpl]when|date=October 2010[/tpl]She is currently working in Comedy Nights With Kapil on Colours.
She was seen in the show Pyaar Mein Twist which aired on Star Plus from 29 January 2011.[tpl]cite web|title=Star Plus gets ready with two weekend shows|url=http://www.tellychakkar.com/news/star-plus-gets-ready-two-weekend-shows|accessdate=2011-01-25[/tpl]
Chopra gave birth to her first child, a son Jaiveer on November 5, 2012. Her younger sister, Deeya Chopra is also an actress.
