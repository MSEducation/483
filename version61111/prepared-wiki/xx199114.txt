[[Alma Hanlon]]

CATEGORIES: 1890 births, 1977 deaths, Actresses from New Jersey, Actresses from New York, American silent film actresses, 20th-century American actresses

Alma Hanlon  (April 30, 1890 – October 26, 1977) was an American silent film actress. Hanlon's film career was short, lasting only four years. She appeared in twenty-three films. Her first film role was as Dorothy Dare in The Fixer (1915) and her last was in The Profiteer (1919). 
She was born the youngest daughter of George Hanlon in New Jersey and died in Monterey, California. Her first husband was former correspondent and theatrical press agent Walter J. Kingsley, from 1905[tpl]cite news |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=9400EFD6113BE631A25754C2A9679D946497D6CF |title=SHOWER OF ROSES ON BRIDE.; Miss Alma Hanlon Becomes Mrs. Kingsley in a Rain of Blossoms. |work=New York Times |date=November 27, 1905 |page=9[/tpl] until their divorce in 1917, with whom she had one child, Dorothy Kingsley (1909–1997). In 1918 she married  director Louis Myll (1871-1939), when she had been living at Bayside, Queens for the last two years;[tpl]cite news |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=9C05E5D61531E433A2575BC2A96F9C946996D6CF |title=Alma Hanlon, Actress, Marries Again |work=New York Times |date=September 28, 1918 |page=11[/tpl] she later moved with her daughter to the affluent suburb of Grosse Pointe, Michigan.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1997/10/03/arts/dorothy-kingsley-87-writer-of-1950-s-mgm-screenplays.html?scp=1&sq=Alma+Hanlon&st=nyt |title=Dorothy Kingsley, 87, Writer Of 1950's MGM Screenplays |first=MEL |last=GUSSOW |date=October 3, 1997 |work=New York Times[/tpl]
