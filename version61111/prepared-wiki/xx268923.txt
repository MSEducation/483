[[Here and Now (Nickelback album)]]

CATEGORIES: Nickelback albums, 2011 albums, Roadrunner Records albums, English-language albums, Universal Music Canada albums, Albums produced by Joey Moi

Here and Now is the seventh studio album by Canadian rock band Nickelback. The album was released on November 21, 2011.[tpl]cite web|title=Nickelback To Release ‘Here and Now’ helloIn November|url=http://rockaccess.net/2011/09/08/nickelback-to-release-here-and-now-in-november/|publisher=Rock Access|accessdate=October 11, 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/62LNy86Iy|archivedate=October 10, 2011|date=September 8, 2011[/tpl] It is the follow-up to their multi-platinum selling Dark Horse in 2008. On September 26, the band officially released two singles, "When We Stand Together" and "Bottoms Up". Both songs were made available for download on September 27, 2011. The first track of the record, "This Means War", was released on November 10, 2011 as the third single.[tpl]cite web|title=Nickelback – This Means War|url=http://musicremedy.com/n/nickelback/songs/this-means-war-56096.html|publisher=Music Remedy|accessdate=November 13, 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/63AumFblb|archivedate=November 13, 2011[/tpl] A music video is shot for the single. The artwork for the album is the Gastown Steam Clock in Vancouver and the clock is set at 11:21, the date the album was released.[tpl]cite web|title=Nickelback sticks to format with Here and Now|url=http://www.vancouversun.com/entertainment/Nickelback+sticks+format+with+Here/5738041/story.html|publisher=Vancouver Sun|accessdate=1 December 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/63atDQNJj|archivedate=30 November 2011|date=19 November 2011[/tpl] The album debuted at number two on the US Billboard 200 selling approximately 227,000 copies in its first week, just 0.18% below the number one spot, Michael Bublé's Christmas.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/464922/michael-buble-bests-nickelback-rihanna-on-billboard-200 |title=Michael Buble Bests Nickelback, Rihanna on Billboard 200 |publisher=Billboard.com |date=2009-09-14 |accessdate=2011-12-31[/tpl] Nickelback toured in support of the album on their Here and Now Tour. As of October 2013, the album has sold two million copies worldwide.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/page/News?news_id=122127|title=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.co.uk/page/News?news_id=122127|title=NICKELBACK // THE BEST OF NICKELBACK VOLUME 1 TO BE RELEASED THIS NOVEMBER|publisher=Roadrunner Records|date=October 3, 2013|accessdate=October 28, 2013|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6KiYduTxS|archivedate=October 28, 2013[/tpl]

==Reception==

===Critical response===

The album has received mixed reviews from professional critics. According to Metacritic, the album has received an average review score of 51/100, based on 9 reviews.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/music/here-and-now-2010/critic-reviews |title=Critic Reviews for Here and Now at Metacritic |publisher=Metacritic.com |date= |accessdate=2011-12-31[/tpl] Consequence of Sound awarded the album two stars, out of a possible 5, stating that "Here and Now might prove to be a step above the last effort, but likewise a step high enough to hang its creators on a barn rafter."[tpl]cite web|url=http://consequenceofsound.net/2011/11/album-review-nickelback-here-and-now/ |title=Album Review: Nickelback – Here And Now « Consequence of Sound |publisher=Consequenceofsound.net |date=2011-11-22 |accessdate=2011-12-31[/tpl] Conversely, Canadian music and entertainment website Rockstar Weekly awarded the album a perfect 5 stars, calling it "far and away, the best Canadian album of 2011."[tpl]cite web|last=Savoie |first=Dan |url=http://www.rockstarweekly.com/cd-review-nickelback-here-and-now.html/ |title=CD Review: Nickelback - Here And Now |publisher=RockStar Weekly |date= |accessdate=2011-12-31[/tpl]

===Commercial performance===

The album has topped both the HMV Canada CD sales chart and the US iTunes Album chart in its first week of sales. The album debuted at  number 10 in the UK charts. On December 16, 2011, the album was certified gold by the British Phonographic Industry (BPI) for shipments of 100,000 copies in the UK.[tpl]Certification Cite Ref|region=United Kingdom|artist=Nickelback[/tpl]
