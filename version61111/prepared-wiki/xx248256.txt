"Glam" is a song by American recording artist Christina Aguilera, taken from her sixth studio album Bionic (2010). The song was written by Aguilera, Claude Kelly and C. "Tricky" Stewart, with production was handled by Stewart. It is an electropop song with hip hop elements talks about getting glam and sexy before a night out. According to Aguilera and Kelly, "Glam" was a throwback to Madonna's "Vogue" (1990). 
At first, the track was planned to be the lead single from Bionic, however "Not Myself Tonight" was released instead. "Glam" received mixed reviews from music critics, with some praised it as one of the best uptempo tracks on the album, while others named it dull and criticized that the song was not as good as "Vogue". Upon the release of Bionic, "Glam" peaked at number two on the South Korean International Download Chart and number 42 on the main South Korean Download Chart.

==Critical reception==

"Glam" received mixed reviews from music critics. Allison Stewart from The Washington Post named it "a great moment on the album".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2010/06/04/AR2010060402181.html |title=Christina Aguilera's new album 'Bionic' is a mish-mash of Gaga and blah |first=Allison |last=Stewart |work=The Washington Post |publisher=The Washington Post Company |date=June 7, 2010 |accessdate=June 7, 2010[/tpl] Mesfin Fekadu from Boston.com website agreed, calling it "one of the disc's best uptempo tracks".[tpl]cite news|last=Fekadu|first=Mesfin|title=Review: Aguilera has an identity crisis on new CD|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2010/06/07/review_aguilera_has_an_identity_crisis_on_new_cd/?rss_id=Boston+Globe+--+CD+reviews|accessdate=May 15, 2012|publisher=Boston.com|date=June 7, 2010[/tpl] USA Today editor Elysa Gardner gave the song a positive review, writing that "Aguilera chants deadpan before letting loose a creamy belt that evokes an earthier version of Whitney Houston in her prime".[tpl]cite news|last=Gardner|first=Elysa|title='Bionic' reveals the inner workings of Christina Aguilera|url=http://www.usatoday.com/life/music/reviews/2010-06-08-christina08_ST_N.htm|accessdate=May 18, 2012|newspaper=USA Today|publisher=Gannett Company|date=June 8, 2010[/tpl] UK newspaper The Scotsman wrote that the song "is her blatant bid to produce a Vogue for our times", while Eric Henderson from Slant Magazine labelled "Glam" as a "top-heavy cocktail that mixes one part 'Vogue' with two parts 'Technologic'".[tpl]cite web|url=http://slantmagazine.com/music/review/christina-aguilera-bionic/2139 |title=Christina Aguilera: Bionic | Music Review |publisher=Slant Magazine |date=June 2, 2010|accessdate=June 6, 2010[/tpl] 
Brad Steirn from MuuMuse named it "dud",[tpl]cite news|last=Stern|first=Bradley|title=CHRISTINA AGUILERA: BIONIC (ALBUM REVIEW)|url=http://www.muumuse.com/2010/06/christina-aguilera-bionic-album-review.html/|accessdate=May 17, 2012|publisher=MuuMuse|date=June 7, 2012[/tpl] while Michael Cragg from musicOMH called it "dated".[tpl]cite news|last=Cragg|first=Michael|title=Christina Aguilera - Bionic | album reviews|url=http://www.musicomh.com/albums/christina-aguilera-2_0610.htm|accessdate=May 15, 2012|publisher=musicOMH|date=June 7, 2010[/tpl] Omar Kholeif of PopMatters wrote a negative review, stating "Christina isn’t afraid to shake, thrust, and hustle, as she professes on 'Glam', an obvious homage to 'Vogue' that isn’t nearly as catchy or sultry as it thinks it is."[tpl]cite web|url =http://www.popmatters.com/pm/review/126933-christina-aguilera-bionic|title =Christina Aguilera: Bionic|last=Kholeif|first=Omar|publisher=PopMatters|accessdate =June 22, 2010[/tpl] Jordan Richardson from Blogcritics agreed, writing that "The track is a frustrating and uncomfortable ode to Madonna’s 'Vogue,' but Aguilera fails in recreating any of the slinkiness of the track she’s trying to emulate".[tpl]cite news|last=Richardson|first=Jordan|title=Music Review: Christina Aguilera - Bionic - Page 2|url=http://blogcritics.org/music/article/music-review-christina-aguilera-bionic/page-2/|accessdate=May 15, 2012|publisher=Blogcritics|date=June 25, 2010[/tpl]

==Chart performance==

Upon the release of the album Bionic, "Glam" debuted at number 2 on the Gaon International Downloads Chart on the chart issue June 13, 2010, becoming the best-charting song from the album on the chart.[tpl]cite web|title=Gaon International Download Chart - Issue date: June 13, 2010 - June 20, 2010|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/digital_chart/download.php?nationGbn=E&current_week=25&current_year=2010&chart_Time=week|publisher=Gaon Chart|language=Korean|accessdate=May 15, 2012[/tpl] "Glam" also peaked at number 42 on the Gaon Singles Chart in the week of June 20-27, 2010.[tpl]cite web|title=Search: Gaon Chart - Issue date: June 14, 2010 - June 20, 2010|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/digital_chart/download.php?nationGbn=T&current_week=26&current_year=2010&chart_Time=week|publisher=Gaon Chart|language=Korean|accessdate=May 18, 2012[/tpl] "Glam" was the 35th best-selling international single on the Gaon Chart in 2010, with 354,259 digital copies sold.[tpl]cite web|url=http://gaonchart.co.kr/digital_chart/download.php?nationGbn=E&current_year=2010&chart_Time=year|title=Gaon Chart: Year-End 2010|publisher=Gaon Chart|accessdate=February 9, 2014[/tpl]
