[[1916 Summer Olympics]]

CATEGORIES: 1916 Summer Olympics, Cancelled Olympic Games, Olympic Games in Germany, Events cancelled due to World War I, 1916 in multi-sport events, Sport in Berlin, 20th century in Berlin, 1916 in Germany, Summer Olympic Games

The 1916 Summer Olympics, officially known as the Games of the VI Olympiad, were scheduled to be held in Berlin, Germany, but were eventually cancelled due to the outbreak of World War I. Berlin was selected as the host city during the 14th IOC Session in Stockholm on July 4, 1912,Bill Mallon and Jeroen Heijmans, Historical Dictionary of the Olympic Movement (Scarecrow Press, 2011) xiv defeating bids from Alexandria, Amsterdam, Brussels, Budapest and Cleveland.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gamesbids.com/english/archives/past.shtml |title=Past Olympic host city election results |publisher=GamesBids |accessdate=17 March 2011 |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5xFvf0ufx |archivedate=17 March 2011[/tpl] After the 1916 Games were cancelled, Berlin would eventually host the 1936 Summer Olympics.

==History==

Work on the stadium, the Deutsches Stadion ("German Stadium"), began in 1912 at what was the Grunewald Race Course. It was planned to seat over 18,000 spectators.[tpl]cite news |coauthors= |title=Berlin Stadium Is Begun. At the outbreak of World War I in 1914, organization continued as no one expected that the war would continue for several years. Eventually, though, the Games were cancelled.[tpl]cite book |author=Pelle, Kimberly D.; Findling, John E. |editor= |others= |title=Historical dictionary of the modern Olympic movement |edition= |publisher=Greenwood Press |location=Westport, Conn |year=1996 |origyear= |pages= 47â€“53|quote= |isbn=0-313-28477-6 |oclc= |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=InQ_9QaRTlMC&pg=PA52&dq=1916+Summer+Olympics+berlin&ei=Xgu_SJqJDpKQzQTyyqGKDg&sig=ACfU3U0zyPFfvQ-DHSQKUD46oNB3ygeznQ [/tpl]  
A winter sports week with speed skating, figure skating, ice hockey and nordic skiing was planned; the concept of this week eventually gave rise to the Winter Olympic Games. The central venue was to have been the Deutsches Stadion. Berlin returned to Olympic bidding in 1931, when it beat Barcelona, Spain for the right to host the 1936 Summer Olympics, the last Olympics before the outbreak of WWII.[tpl]cite book |author=Guttmann, Allen |editor= |others= |title=The Olympics, a history of the modern games |edition= |publisher=University of Illinois Press |location=Urbana |year=2002 |origyear= |pages= 53|quote= |isbn=0-252-07046-1 |oclc= |url= http://books.google.com/books?id=TbLmQQG-2bQC&pg=PA53&vq=1931&dq=1931+Summer+Olympics+berlin+barcelona&source=gbs_search_s&sig=ACfU3U2E0IXGK2lc0RdxUps0oscnR1fJsA[/tpl]
