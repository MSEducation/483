[[1st Mechanised Brigade (France)]]

CATEGORIES: Brigades of France, Military units and formations established in 1999, 1999 establishments in France

The 1st Mechanised Brigade ([tpl]lang-fr|1[tpl]sup|re[/tpl] Brigade Mécanisée, 1[tpl]sup|re[/tpl] BM[/tpl]) is a mechanised unit of the French Army. The brigade is heir to the honours and traditions of the 1st Armoured Division, created during World War II.

==History==

The 1st Armoured Division was dissolved with the reorganisation of the French Army in 1999, and the dissolution of the French Forces in Germany. The division’s honours and traditions are carried on with the formation of the 1st Mechanised Brigade in Châlons-en-Champagne. The brigade took part in the Operation Trident in Kosovo in 1999.

==Organization==

The brigade consists of around 5700 personnel in 6 regiments and 3 brigade units.[tpl]cite web|title=1[tpl]sup|re[/tpl] brigade mécanisée|url=http://www.defense.gouv.fr/terre/presentation/organisation-des-forces/brigades/1re-brigade-mecanisee|publisher=French government|accessdate=17 February 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/65bpbST8z|archivedate=2012-02-20|deadurl=no[/tpl]
