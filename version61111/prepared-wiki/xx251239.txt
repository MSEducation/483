[[Bill Allison (baseball)]]

CATEGORIES: Brooklyn Eckfords (NABBP) players, Brooklyn Eckfords players, Burials at Cypress Hills National Cemetery, 19th-century baseball players, 1850 births, 1887 deaths

William Andrew "Bill" Allison (1850 – January 25, 1887), was an American professional baseball player. In the  National Association he was a substitute infielder for the 1872 Brooklyn Eckfords He was the younger brother of Eckfords teammate Andy Allison.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.retrosheet.org/boxesetc/A/Pallib102.htm|title=Bill Allison|work=retrosheet.org|publisher=Retrosheet, Inc|accessdate=13 January 2010[/tpl]  
"Billy" Allison previously played for the Eckfords in the second of their four professional seasons, 1870. While the team won 2, tied 1, and lost 12 pro matches, he was one of the second basemen.
[ref]Ryczek, William J. When Johnny Came Sliding Home: The Post-Civil War Baseball Boom, 1865–1870. Jefferson NC: McFarland & Co. 1998. Page 261.
 For the 1870 Eckfords, Marshall Wright does not list Billy Allison as a five-game player. Bill Ryczek lists him second of two second basemen.[/ref]

After his baseball career Bill Allison served one term as a Brooklyn city alderman, then was appointed an appraiser in the Custom House, a position which he held when he died of heart disease on January 25, 1887 in Brooklyn.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thedeadballera.com/Obits/Obits_A/Allison.Bill.Obit.html|title=The Obit For Bill Allison|date=January 26, 1887|work=thedeadballera.com|publisher=The Brooklyn Daily Eagle|accessdate=January 13, 2010[/tpl] He is interred at Cypress Hills Cemetery, Brooklyn.
