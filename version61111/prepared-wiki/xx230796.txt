[[Malaysia–Sweden relations]]

CATEGORIES: Bilateral relations of Malaysia, Bilateral relations of Sweden

Diplomatic relations between Sweden and Malaysia were established in 1958.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.scandasia.com/viewNews.php?coun_code=se&news_id=4370 |title=National Day Of Sweden Celebrations In Malaysia |accessdate=2009-06-05 |quote= |publisher=Scandasia.com [/tpl] Sweden has an embassy in Kuala Lumpur, and Malaysia has an embassy in Stockholm. As of 2009, 90 Swedish companies are present in Malaysia and about 450 Swedish citizens live in Malaysia. In 2011, 44,138 Swedish nationals visited Malaysia. As of September 2012, there are no direct flights to Stockholm from Kuala Lumpur.

==Economic ties==

Between January and July 2012, bilateral trade stood at 2 billion MYR, increased from the trade at 1.9 billion MYR between January and June 2011[tpl]cite news|title=Direct Flights Can Boost Malaysia-Sweden Trade Relations, Says Envoy|url=http://www.bernama.com/bernama/v6/newsgeneral.php?id=695026|accessdate=17 September 2012|newspaper=Bernama|date=16 September 2012[/tpl] In September 2012, Malaysian Ambassador to Sweden, Datuk Badruddin Ab Rahman, said about 120 Swedish companies operate in Malaysia, with an investment of more than 10 million MYR, however there are no Malaysian companies operating in Sweden. The Malaysian embassy in Sweden makes programmes to attract Swedish businesses to Malaysia such as a briefing on investment opportunities in Malaysia. 
In 2006, Swedish exports to Malaysia totaled approximately 1.6 billion MYR and the imports from Malaysia approximately 0.7 billion MYR. Swedish exports to Malaysia consist of telecommunication equipment, motor vehicles, chemical products, power generating machinery and equipment, machines, paper, as well as iron ore and steel. Swedish imports from Malaysia electronics and electrical components, machinery and apparatus, textiles, palm oil and raw rubber.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.swedenabroad.com/Page39260.aspx |title=Trade with Malaysia |accessdate=2009-06-11 |quote= |publisher=Sweden [/tpl][tpl]cite news |first= |last= |authorlink= |coauthors= |title=Malaysia, Sweden trade on the rise. |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=1nAWAAAAIBAJ&sjid=xh4EAAAAIBAJ&pg=6837,2902835&dq=malaysia+sweden%27s+trade |quote= |work=New Straits Times |date=7 September 1995 |accessdate=2009-06-05 [/tpl]
