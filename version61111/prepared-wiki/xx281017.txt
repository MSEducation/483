[[Redland, Texas]]

CATEGORIES: Census-designated places in Angelina County, Texas, Census-designated places in Texas

Redland is an unincorporated town and census-designated place (CDP) in Angelina County, Texas, United States. The population was 1,047 at the 2010 census.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/1600000US4861124| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Redland CDP, Texas| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=August 9, 2013[/tpl]

==Geography==

Redland is located in northern Angelina County [tpl]convert|6|mi|0[/tpl] north of the center of Lufkin, the county seat. U.S. Route 59, a four-lane highway, runs through the CDP, leading south to Lufkin and north [tpl]convert|14|mi[/tpl] to Nacogdoches.
According to the United States Census Bureau, the CDP has a total area of [tpl]convert|10.7|sqkm|disp=flip[/tpl], of which [tpl]convert|0.07|sqkm|disp=flip|2[/tpl], or 0.62%, is water.
