[[Brian A. Skiff]]

CATEGORIES: American astronomers, Discoverers of asteroids, Living people

Brian A. Skiff is an American astronomer noted for discovering a number of comets including the periodic comets 114P/Wiseman-Skiff (with Jennifer Wiseman) and 140P/Bowell-Skiff (with Edward Bowell).  He has discovered a number of asteroids including the Trojan asteroid [tpl]mpl|(15398) 1997 UZ|23[/tpl].
Working on the LONEOS project he rediscovered the long lost asteroid 69230 Hermes in October 2003 and the Apohele asteroid [tpl]mpl|2004 JG|6[/tpl] in May 2004.
The asteroid 2554 Skiff was named in his honour.  Skiff has worked as an Astronomer at Lowell Observatory since 1976.Brian Skiff at Lowell Observatory
Skiff is also a volleyball player with the Mars Hill summer volleyball squad.
