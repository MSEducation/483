[[Jack Conway (footballer)]]

CATEGORIES: Geelong Football Club players, 1867 births, Year of death unknown

John Joseph "Jack" Conway (26 March 1867 – 22 October 1949) was an Australian rules footballer for the Geelong Football Club in the Victorian Football League (VFL). He was Geelong's first captain in the VFL, captaining the club for its first three seasons in the new competition before retiring at the end of the 1899 season.Geelong Football Club Honour Roll He is also notable for being in the inaugural team for Geelong in the VFL, and is credited with being the first player selected for a Geelong team.[tpl]cite web | first = | last = | title = Every Player: A–C | url = http://www.gfc.com.au/Default.aspx?tabid=8903 | publisher = Geelong Football Club | date =  | accessdate = 29 April 2009 [/tpl]

==Personal life==

Jack married Mary Catherine "Polly" Doloughty and had 5 children from 1899 to 1915.
