Johann Georg Veit Engelhardt (November 12, 1791 â€“ September 13, 1855), a German theologian.

==Life==

He was born at Neustadt-on-the-Aisch.
He and was educated at Erlangen, where he afterwards taught in the gymnasium (1817), and became professor of theology in the university (1821).[tpl]sfn|Chisholm|1911[/tpl] During the years 1845, 1847 and 1848 was the representative of his university in the diet at Munich.[tpl]Cite Americana|Engelhardt, Johann Georg Veit[/tpl]

==Works==

His two great works were a Handbuch der Kirchengeschichte in 4 volumes (1833-1834), and a Dogmengeschichte in 2 volumes, (1839).[tpl]sfn|Chisholm|1911[/tpl] Other works included a translation of the writings ascribed to Dionysius the Areopagite and Richard von St. Victor und Johannes Ruysbroek (1838). He died at Erlangen on September 13, 1855.[tpl]sfn|Chisholm|1911[/tpl]
