[[Jonathan Glickman]]

CATEGORIES: 1969 births, American film producers, American Jews, Living people, People from Wichita, Kansas, People from Detroit, Michigan, University of Michigan alumni, American film studio executives, Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer executives

Jonathan Glickman (born May 18, 1969) is an American film producer and the President of Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer Pictures Film Division.

==Personal life and education==

Glickman is the son of Rhoda Yura and Dan Glickman, the former Kansas Congressman, Secretary of Agriculture, and president of the MPAA.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117907297.html | work=Variety | title=Valenti passes the torch | first1=Cathy | last1=Dunkley | first2=Susan | last2=Crabtree | date=July 1, 2004[/tpl][tpl]cite news | url = http://www.nytimes.com/2004/07/02/business/an-old-washington-hand-to-succeed-valenti-in-hollywood.html?pagewanted=2 | work=The New York Times | title=An Old Washington Hand To Succeed Valenti in Hollywood | first1=Sharon | last1=Waxman | date=July 2, 2004[/tpl] He was born in Detroit, Michigan and raised in Wichita, Kansas and Washington, D.C.
While he was a child, his mother introduced him to directors Martin Scorsese and Woody Allen.http://www.thecrimson.com/article/2004/3/10/producer-talks-politics-fighting-to-find/ Glickman graduated from Georgetown Day School in Washington, D.C. in 1987,http://www.mightyhoppers.org/georgetowndays/spring2001.pdf and from the University of Michigan in 1991.http://deepblue.lib.umich.edu/html/2027.42/61998/entertain.html He worked briefly for HBO before enrolling in the University of Southern California's School of Cinematic Arts in 1992, although he quit in 1993.

==Career==

Glickman began working in Hollywood when he convinced producer Joe Roth to give him an internship at Caravan Pictures after meeting him in an elevator at USC in 1993.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2002/07/29/business/media-triumph-of-the-producing-class.html?pagewanted=all | work=The New York Times | title=MEDIA; Triumph of the Producing Class | first=Michael | last=Cieply | date=July 29, 2002[/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117931420.html | work=Variety | title=Starkie network helps H'wood run | first=Nicole | last=LaPorte | date=October 23, 2005[/tpl] By 1997, Glickman was president of production at Caravan.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1116674347.html | work=Variety | title=Glickman new prexy at Caravan | first=Dan | last=Cox | date=September 18, 1997[/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/1998/08/23/magazine/the-blockbuster-script-factory.html?pagewanted=2 | work=The New York Times | title=The Blockbuster Script Factory | first=Jaime | last=Wolf | date=August 23, 1998[/tpl]http://media.www.themsj.com/media/storage/paper207/news/2003/04/21/Features/Ems-Club.Networks.In.L.a-421661.shtml By 2002, he was president of production at Spyglass Entertainment.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/search/article_display.jsp?vnu_content_id=1600676 | deadurl=yes[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=January 2012|bot=RjwilmsiBot[/tpl] In 2003, he became president and partner at Spyglass.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117894582.html | work=Variety | title=Spyglass adds new partner, prez | first=Cathy | last=Dunkley | date=October 26, 2003[/tpl]
Glickman was invited to join the Academy of Motion Picture Arts & Sciences in 2007.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117934309.html | work=Variety | title=Rush Hour 3 Movie Review | first=Robert | last=Koehler | date=August 2, 2007[/tpl]
On February 2, 2011, Glickman was named president of MGM's film division. His role at MGM will include the oversight of development and production for all feature films, as well as helping to identify co-financing opportunities with other studios.
As producer, Glickman's two great hits at the box office have been Rush Hour 2 which grossed $347,325,802 at the world wide box office, and The Tourist which grossed $278,346,189 at the worldwide box office, both off an estimated 100million dollar budget.
