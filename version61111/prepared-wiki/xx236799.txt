[[Kayla Williams (gymnast)]]

CATEGORIES: 1993 births, Living people, Alabama Crimson Tide gymnasts, American female artistic gymnasts, Medalists at the World Artistic Gymnastics Championships, People from Huntington, West Virginia, People from Nitro, West Virginia, Level 10 gymnasts

Kayla Williams (born May 8, 1993) is an American artistic gymnast. She is the 2009 Vault World Champion and 2009 Vault National Champion.

==Senior career==

Williams began 2009 as a Level 10 gymnast, which is below elite level in USA Gymnastics. In May 2009, she won the Junior Olympic National Championships in the all-around, vault, and floor exercise and won the silver medal on balance beam, losing to fellow gymnast Kailie Ponto. She qualified to elite level at a meet in June.[tpl]cite news | first = Tommy R. | last= Atkinson | url = http://wvgazette.com/Sports/200908090106 | title = Spot in nationals fulfills dream for Huntington gymnast | date = August 9, 2009 | accessdate = October 17, 2009 | publisher = Charleston Gazette[/tpl]
After qualifying for the U.S. Classic, she won the vault and floor exercise at that event and placed fifth in the all-around and on balance beam. This qualified her to the 2009 U.S. Artistic Gymnastics Championships.[tpl]cite news | first = Tommy R. | last = Atkinson | url = http://www.sundaygazettemail.com/Sports/200910050965 | title = Nitro native heads to world stage | date = October 5, 2009 | accessdate = October 17, 2009 | publisher = Charleston Gazette[/tpl] Williams was the first West Virginian since Mary Lou Retton in 1984 to compete at the U.S. Championships. At the U.S. Championships,  she won the National title on vault and made the U.S. National Team as a senior elite. Two months later, Williams was named to the team for the 2009 World Artistic Gymnastics Championships.
At the 2009 World Championships, Williams qualified first on vault. On the night of the vault event final, she competed a handspring laidout Rudi and a Yurchenko double full for a combined score of 15.087 and won the gold medal. She is the first American gymnast to win the World vault title.[tpl]cite news | url = http://sports.espn.go.com/oly/gymnastics/news/story?id=4569108 | title = Williams vaults to gold at worlds | date = October 17, 2009 | accessdate = October 17, 2011 | publisher = ESPN[/tpl]

==Collegiate career==

She retired from elite gymnastics in July 2010, but competes in Level 10 and in the NCAA. In May 2010, she committed to compete for the University of Alabama gymnastics team.[tpl]cite news | first = Grant | last= Traylor | url = http://www.herald-dispatch.com/sports/x631788013/Williams-going-to-Alabama | title = Williams going to Alabama | date = May 29, 2010 | accessdate = June 1, 2010 | publisher = The Herald-Dispatch[/tpl]
As a freshman in 2012, Williams helped lead Alabama to their second consecutive National Championship.[tpl]cite news | first = Tommy | last= Atkinson | url =http://www.wvgazette.com/Sports/201207220127 | title = Olympic dreams swapped for NCAA title | date = July 22, 2012 | accessdate = August 2, 2012 | publisher = The Charleston Gazette[/tpl]
