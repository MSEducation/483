On 7 August 2013, members of the youth wing of the Congress Party attacked the Pakistan embassy in New Delhi, India.[tpl]cite news|last=Front Desk|title=Pak HC in New Delhi attacked by Congress youth wing|url=http://pakobserver.net/detailnews.asp?id=214949|accessdate=9 August 2013|newspaper=Pakistan Observer|date=8 August 2013[/tpl]  The attack was motivated when the news of death of five Indian Army soldiers began to air in the Indian news channels.[tpl]cite news|last=Azam Khan / Aditi Phadnis|title=Flaring tempers: Pakistan protests mob attack on its mission in India|url=http://tribune.com.pk/story/588171/flaring-tempers-pakistan-protests-mob-attack-on-its-mission-in-india/|accessdate=9 August 2013|newspaper=Express Tribune|date=7 August 2013[/tpl]
Protests flared by the members of the youth wing of Congress Party against the alleged killing of five Indian soldiers by Pakistan Army at the Line of Control (LoC) tried to enter the Pakistan High Commission.[tpl]cite news|last=Jitender Singh|title=Indians attack Pakistan Embassy in New Delhi|url=http://www.thenewstribe.com/2013/08/07/indians-attack-pakistan-embassy-in-new-delhi/|accessdate=9 August 2013|newspaper=News Tribe|date=8 August 2013[/tpl]  The protesters turned violent as they pushed back the police barricades and entered into a scuffle with the policemen. Police resorted to using water cannons to disperse the protestors.[tpl]cite news|last=Staff|title=Congress Youth Wing attacks Pakistan High Commission in New Delhi|url=http://www.geo.tv/article-112779-Congress-Youth-Wing-attack-Pakistani-High-Commission-in-New-Delhi|accessdate=9 August 2013|newspaper=GEO NEws|date=8 August 2013[/tpl]  Around 175 were detained, but later let off. Similar protests were also organized in other major urban centres of India, including Mumbai and Hyderabad.
