[[Leonard Glasser]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American film directors, American animators, American screenwriters, Year of birth missing (living people)

Leonard Glasser is a screenwriter, cartoonist and animator.[tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/person/167214/Leonard-Glasser/filmography|title=Leonard Glasser|work= The New York Times[/tpl][tpl]cite web | title=André François, RIP | work=Cartoon Brew | url=http://www.cartoonbrew.com/archives/2005_04.html | accessdate = 2005-06-06 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20050415033206/http://www.cartoonbrew.com/archives/2005_04.html  |archivedate = 2005-04-15[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cartoonbrew.com/animators/the-fine-art-by-legendary-director-len-glasser.html|work=Cartoonbrew.com|date=December 2008|title=The Fine Art[/tpl]
Glasser designed Ernie Pintoff's The Interview and The Old Man And The Flower. He cited André François and Saul Steinberg as inspirations.
He was the screenwriter for Mojave Moon and Out Cold and the animator for the 1971 film The Telephone Book. 
Glasser attended the Philadelphia Museum School of Art 1953-1956. His production company was called "Stars and Stripes Forever Productions."
