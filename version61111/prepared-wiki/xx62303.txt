CATEGORIES: 1945 births, 2006 deaths, Kyungbock High School alumni, Seoul National University alumni, South Korean health workers, South Korean physicians, University of Hawaii alumni, World Health Organization officials, People in public health, Mugunghwa Medals of the Order of Civil Merit (South Korea)

Lee Jong-wook (1945–2006) was the director-general of the World Health Organization for three years. Born on 12 April 1945 in Seoul, Korea, Lee obtained a medical degree at Seoul National University, then obtained an MA at the University of Hawaii in public health. He joined the WHO in 1983, working on a variety of projects including the Global Programme for Vaccines and Immunizations and Stop Tuberculosis. He began his term as director-general in 2003. He was the first Korean to lead an international agency. He died on 22 May 2006, while in office, in Geneva, Switzerland, following surgery for a blood clot in the brain (a subdural hematoma).NYT W.H.O.
