Edward Bishop Elliott graduated from Trinity College, Cambridge in 1816.[tpl]acad|id=ELT811EB|name=Elliott, Edward Bishop[/tpl] He was given the vicarage of Tuxford, Nottinghamshire in 1824 and later was made prebendary of Heytesbury, Wiltshire. In 1849 he became incumbent of St Mark's Church, Kemptown, Brighton. Elliott was evangelical, premillennial and an ardent supporter of missions. Thoroughly equipped as a scholar, he spent a lifetime in the study of prophecy.Le Roy Froom, The Prophetic Faith of Our Fathers, Vol 3. 716
Edward's most notable work is found in the eschatological study, Horae Apocalypticae (Hours of the Apocalypse).
