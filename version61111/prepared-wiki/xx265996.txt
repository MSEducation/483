[[Tibisay Lucena Ramírez]]

CATEGORIES: Electoral branch of the Government of Venezuela, Living people, Central University of Venezuela alumni

Tibisay Lucena is the president of the National Electoral Council or CNE, one of the five independent branches of government of the Bolivarian Republic of Venezuela. She was first elected to this post in 2006, and was reelected in 2009 for the 2009-2013 period.[tpl]cite web |title= Consejo Nacional Electoral de Venezuela| url=http://www.cne.gob.ve/web/la_institucion/autoridades.php |accessdate=September 22, 2011[/tpl]

==Early Life and Education==

Tibisay Lucena was born in Caracas. She studied cello and performed with Venezuela's National Youth Orchestra and Simon Bolivar Symphony Orchestra (1980–87). Lucena earned a B.A. in sociology from the Central University of Venezuela and worked as a research professor at the Institute of Advanced Studies of Administration. She then pursued graduate studies in the United States, earning an M.A. and Ph.D. in sociology from the New School for Social Research.
