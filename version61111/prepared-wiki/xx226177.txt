[[St. Paul's Evangelical Lutheran Church (Liberty, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Religious buildings completed in 1908, 20th-century Lutheran church buildings, Lutheran churches in New York, Lutheran congregations established in the 20th century, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Churches in Sullivan County, New York

St. Paul's Evangelical Lutheran Church is a historic Evangelical Lutheran church in Liberty, Sullivan County, New York.  It was built in 1908 and is a modest Late Gothic Revival style building.  It is built of iron spot brick with limestone and terra cotta trim.  It features a gable roofed nave with and engaged side entrance tower.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=9134|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: St. John's Episcopal Church and Rectory |date=April 1997|accessdate=2010-06-26 |author=Kathleen LaFrank|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=9156|title=Accompanying three photos[/tpl] 
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1997.
