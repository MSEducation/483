[[Jim Snyder (shortstop)]]

CATEGORIES: Major League Baseball shortstops, Brooklyn Eckfords (NABBP) players, Brooklyn Eckfords players, 19th-century baseball players, 1847 births, 1922 deaths, Baseball players from New York

James C. A. Snyder  (1847–1922), was an American professional baseball player. In the National Association he was the regular shortstop of the 1872 Brooklyn Eckfords.
Snyder previously played for the Eckfords in the second of their four professional seasons, 1870. While the team won 2, tied 1, and lost 12 pro matches, he was a catcher and shortstop. Overall he appeared in 19 games on record, two fewer than the team leaders, and he was a weak batter in the company of his teammates.
