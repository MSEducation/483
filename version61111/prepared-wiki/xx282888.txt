[[List of Cobra the Animation episodes]]

CATEGORIES: Lists of anime episodes

The original video animation (OVA) and anime series Cobra the Animation is based on the manga series of the same name written by Buichi Terasawa. All series were produced by Guild Project and animated by Magic Bus. The first OVA, Cobra the Animation: The Psychogun, was directed by Buichi Terasawa, while Time Drive was directed by Kenichi Maejima.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_ova1.html | title=COBRA THE ANIMATION 「コブラ　ザ・サイコガン」 | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_ova2.html | title=COBRA THE ANIMATION 「コブラ　タイムドライブ」 | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl] The anime subtitled Rokunin no Yūshi was directed by Keizō Shimizu.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.bs11.jp/anime/835/ | title=アニメ COBRA THE ANIMATION | publisher=Nippon BS Broadcasting | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl]
The both OVAs were released direct-to-DVD by Happinet: The Psychogun was released between August 29, 2008 and February 27, 2009, while Time Drive was released between April 24, 2009 and June 26, 2009. Rokunin no Yūshi was broadcast by BS 11 between January 2, 2010 and March 27, 2010. The OVA were later released in a DVD box set on February 19, 2010 by Happinet.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_ova_bdbox.html | title=COBRA THE ANIMATION 「コブラOVAシリーズ Blu-ray BOX」 | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl] The anime series episodes were later released in seven DVD and Blu-ray compilations between April 23, 2010 and October 2, 2010 by Happinet.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_tv.html | title=COBRA THE ANIMATION TVシリーズvol.1 | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_tv7.html | title=COBRA THE ANIMATION TVシリーズvol.7 | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl]
On December 18, 2009, Crunchyroll and Happinet announced that Crunchyroll would begin to stream the first OVA series on that day.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/press-release/2009-12-18/crunchyroll-and-happinet-announce-release-of-extreme-sci-fi-action-space-opera-epic-series-cobra-the-animation | title=Crunchyroll and Happinet Announce Release of Extreme Sci-Fi Action Space Opera Epic Series Cobra The Animation | date=December 12, 2008 | publisher=Anime News Network | accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] The last episode was streamed on January 8, 2010.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/cobra-the-animation/episode-4-cobra-the-animation-the-psychogun-ova-538392?skip_wall=1 | title=Cobra the Animation: The Psychogun OVA Episode 4 – Cobra the Animation: The Psychogun OVA | publisher=Crunchyroll | accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] The two episodes of Time Drive were uploaded on January 1, 2008.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/cobra-the-animation/episode-1-538356?skip_wall=1 | title=Cobra the Animation: Time Drive Episode 1 | publisher=Crunchyroll | accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/cobra-the-animation/episode-2-538358?skip_wall=1 | title=Cobra the Animation: Time Drive Episode 2 | publisher=Crunchyroll | accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] The anime series begin to be streamed on January 2, 2010, and the last episode was available on March 27, 2010 for premium users, and on April 3, 2010 for free users.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.crunchyroll.com/cobra-the-animation/episode-13-distant-memories-539914?skip_wall=1 | title=Cobra the Animation Episode 13 – Distant Memories | publisher=Crunchyroll | accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
Each series used different pieces of theme music but all of them used a single opening theme and a single ending theme. The opening theme from The Psychogun is [tpl]nihongo|"Kizudarake no Yume"|傷だらけの夢[/tpl] by Yoko Takahashi and it ending theme is "Wanderer" by Shigeru Matsuzaki.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.buichi.com/top/anime/anime_ova_cd.html | title= OVAシリーズ主題歌・サントラCD | publisher=Buichi.com | accessdate=August 10, 2013 | language=Japanese[/tpl] The second OVA used "Time Drive" by Sasja Antheunis as opening theme and [tpl]nihongo|"Kimi ga bi Waraunara"|君が微笑うなら[/tpl] by Shigeru Matsuzaki as ending theme. "Cobra the Space Pirate" by Sasja Antheunis and [tpl]nihongo|"Kimi no Uta"|君の歌[/tpl] were used respectively as opening theme and ending theme for Rokunin no Yūshi.
