[[Lily Tuck]]

Lily Tuck (born Oct. 10, 1938) is an American novelist and short story writer whose novel The News from Paraguay won the 2004 National Book Award for Fiction.[ref]

Her novel Siam was nominated for the 2000 PEN/Faulkner Award for Fiction.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.nationalbook.org/nba2004_ltuck_winner.htm|title=2004 National Book Award Winner: Fiction: Lily Tuck |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=National Book Foundation[/tpl] She has published four other novels, a collection of short stories, and a biography of Italian novelist Elsa Morante (see "Works" below).
An American citizen born in Paris, Tuck now divides her time between New York City and Maine; she has also lived in Thailand and (during her childhood) Uruguay and Peru.[tpl]cite news |first=Larry |last=Rohter |title='Paraguay' author finally goes there, finding an uproar |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2005/02/17/international/americas/17paraguay.html?pagewanted=1&_r=1 |publisher=New York Times |date=2005-02-17 |accessdate=2008-08-19 [/tpl] Tuck has stated that "living in other countries has given me a different perspective as a writer. It has heightened my sense of dislocation and rootlessness. ... I think this feeling is reflected in my characters, most of them women whose lives are changed by either a physical displacement or a loss of some kind".[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.bookbrowse.com/author_interviews/full/index.cfm?author_number=1079|title=An Interview with Lily Tuck|accessdate=2008-08-19|publisher=Book Browse[/tpl]
