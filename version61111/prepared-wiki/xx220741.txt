[[Cnaemidophorus rhododactyla]]

CATEGORIES: Platyptilini, Moths of India, Moths of Japan, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Estonia, Insects of Sweden, Insects of Romania, Insects of Poland, Insects of Latvia, Insects of Finland, Insects of Albania, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Insects of Hungary, Insects of Greece, Insects of Bulgaria, Insects of Turkey, Insects of Italy

Cnaemidophorus rhododactyla is a moth of the family Pterophoroidea. It is found in the Northern Hemisphere, except for Greenland, South-East Asia and most of North Africa.
The wingspan is 18–26 mm.
The larvae feed on various roses, including Rosa rugosa.
