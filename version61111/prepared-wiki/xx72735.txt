[[Janaab' Pakal]]

CATEGORIES: 612 deaths, Rulers of Palenque, 7th-century monarchs in North America, Year of birth unknown

Janaabʻ Pakal or Pakal I (died 9 March, 612CE) was a king of the Maya city-state of Palenque. Pakal means "shield". 

==Family==

Pakal’s dynastic position is not entirely certain,[tpl]cite web|url=http://research.famsi.org/whos_who/people.php?mathewsnumber=PAL%20021|title=Who's who in the classic Maya world|first=Peter|last=Matthews|publisher=Foundation for the Advancement of Mesoamerican Studies, Inc|date=2005-10-18|accessdate=2007-04-15[/tpl] though he may have been the father of Aj Ne' Yohl Mat. It seems that he never ascended to the high-kingship in his own right.Travel Cancun: Cozumel, Playa del Carmen, Tulum, Xcaret, Mexican Riviera
He was the father of Lady Sak K'uk', one of the rare queens regnant of Maya history. His wife was Yohl Ik'nal.Ajen Yohl MatChronicle of the Maya Kings and Queens by Simon Martin and Nikolai Grube 
He should not be confused with his grandson, K'inich Janaabʻ Pakal,The Ark of Millions of Years: Volume Three, by E. J. Clark and Alexander Agnew who during a long reign of some 68 years was responsible for the construction or extension of some of Palenque’s most notable surviving inscriptions and monumental architecture.
