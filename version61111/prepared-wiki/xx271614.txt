[[Sammy Watkins (American football)]]

CATEGORIES: 1993 births, Living people, American football wide receivers, Clemson Tigers football players, People from Fort Myers, Florida

Sammy Watkins (born June 14, 1993) is an American football wide receiver. He played college football at Clemson.

==High school career==

Watkins attended South Fort Myers High School in Fort Myers, Florida, where he became the all-time leading receiver in the history of Lee County with 133 catches for 2,997 yards and 36 touchdowns during his career. In his junior and senior years, he was a first-team all-state selection. As a sophomore, he had 33 receptions for 537 yards and 10 scores. In his junior year, he added 46 catches for 1,192 yards and 14 touchdowns. As a senior, Watkins had eight 100-yard receiving games on the season, accumulating totals of 54 catches for 1,268 yards and 12 touchdowns. He led an undefeated South Fort Myers team into the 3A state semifinals, where they lost 44–28 to a Duke Johnson-led Miami Norland team.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.naplesnews.com/news/2010/dec/10/prep-football-uncharacteristic-mistakes-doom-south/ |title=Uncharacteristic mistakes doom South Fort Myers in state semis |newspaper=Naples News |date=December 10, 2010 [/tpl]  He played in the 2011 U.S. Army All-American Bowl.
Watkins was a highly touted recruit before the 2011 college football season.[tpl]cite web |title=National Signing Day - ACC: Scott is 1st to send signed letter to Miami |url=http://www.brownsvilleherald.com/sports/acc-122372-signing-miami.html |work=The Brownsville Herald |date=February 2, 2011 |first=Tim |last=Reynolds |publisher=Associated Press |accessdate=November 15, 2011[/tpl] Regarded as a five-star recruit by Rivals.com, he was rated the third-best wide receiver prospect in the nation, behind only George Farmer and Trey Metoyer.http://sports.yahoo.com/ncaa/football/recruiting/rankings/rank-2652 He was ranked fifth best by Scout.com.[tpl]cite web |title=Sammy Watkins |url=http://usc.scout.com/a.z?s=15&p=8&c=1&nid=4224994 |publisher=Scout.com |accessdate=November 15, 2011[/tpl] Recruited by dozens of schools, Watkins chose Clemson over offers from Florida, Florida State, Miami (FL), Michigan, and Tennessee.

===Track and field===

Watkins is also a standout track athlete. He ran for the South Fort Myers High School track and field team. As a junior, he finished fifth in the 100 meters and sixth in the 200 meters at the state meet. As a senior, he won the AA state title in the 200 meters, with a time of 21.11, which is still his personal best, and he finished second in the 100 meters, with a time of 10.45.http://www.athletic.net/TrackAndField/Athlete.aspx?AID=807008
Watkins also competed in the 60 meter dash, recording a personal best of 6.91 seconds in 2011.

==Personal life==

In May 2012, Watkins was arrested by Clemson campus police for possession of marijuana. He was also found to have one pill each of Vyvanse and Adderall and charged with possession of a controlled substance.http://www.naplesnews.com/news/2012/may/04/college-football-fort-myers-clemson-sammy-watkins/ As a result, Watkins was suspended for the first two games of the 2012−2013 season.http://www.usatoday.com/sports/college/football/story/2012-08-03/Clemson-Sammy-Watkins-suspended-two-games/56764730/1 Watkins completed pre-trial intervention and had the charge expunged from his record.http://www.news-press.com/article/20120531/CRIME/120531010/
His older brother, Jaylen Watkins, was a defensive back for the Florida Gators football team from 2010−13.
