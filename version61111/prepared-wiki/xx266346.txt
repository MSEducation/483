[[Puros Trankazos]]

CATEGORIES: 2011 compilation albums, Fonovisa Records compilation albums, Spanish-language albums, Spanish-language compilation albums

Puros Trankazos (Huge Hits) is a compilation album released by Fonovisa Records on July 16, 2011. The album includes tracks recorded by several artist from the Regional Mexican genre, such as Julian Álvarez y su Norteño Banda, Voz de Mando, Vagón Chicano, Enigma Norteño, Larry Hernández, Los Horóscopos de Durango, Chuy Lizárraga and his Banda Tierra Sinaloense, Violento, Banda Sinaloense MS de Sergio Lizárraga, El Chapo, Fidel Rueda and Alfredo Olivas. 
Upon release, the album peaked at number-one in the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart for three non-consecutive weeks. Puros Trankazos also peaked at the top of the Regional Mexican Albums chart. The first track, "Olvídame", performed by Julión Álvarez y su Norteño Banda and available exclusively to this release, reached the top five of the Latin Songs chart in the United States. A second volume to the album was released in November 2011 titled Mas Trankazos.

==Reception and commercial performance==

The week of its release, Puros Trankazos debuted at number two on the Billboard Top Latin Albums chart, behind Prince Royce's debut album. Four weeks later, the album reached the top of the chart, where it spent three non-consecutive weeks.[tpl]cite web |title=Latin Albums – Week of September 3, 2011 |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |work=Billboard |url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2011-09-03/latin-albums|date=September 3, 2011 |accessdate=February 12, 2013 [/tpl] 
On the Regional Mexican Albums chart it was at the top during seven consecutive weeks.[tpl]cite web |title=Latin Albums – Week of September 3, 2011 |publisher=Prometheus Global Media |work=Billboard |url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2011-09-03/regional-mexican-albums|date=September 17, 2011 |accessdate=February 12, 2013 [/tpl] The sales of the album are aided by success of the single "Olvídame" by Julión Álvarez y Su Norteño Banda, a banda-ballad, that became a number-one hit in the Regional Mexican Songs chart and reached number three in the Latin Songs chart.[tpl]cite web |title=Olvidame – Julión Alvarez y Su Norteño Banda |publisher=Poder Grupero |url=http://www.podergrupero.com/olvidame-julion-alvarez-y-su-norteno-banda/ |language=Spanish |date=March 8, 2011 |accessdate=September 20, 2011 [/tpl]
