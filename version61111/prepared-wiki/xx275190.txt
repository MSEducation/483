[[Gravesham Council election, 2011]]

CATEGORIES: English local elections, 2011, Gravesham Council elections

The 2011 Gravesham Council election took place on 5 May 2011 to elect members of Gravesham Borough Council in Kent, England. The whole council was up for election and the Labour party gained overall control of the council from the Conservative party.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/election2011/council/html/29ug.stm|title=England council elections|date=10 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]

==Background==

Before the election the Conservatives had run the council since the 2007 election when they won 26 seats, compared to 16 for Labour.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/vote2007/councils/html/29ug.stm|title=Gravesham|date=4 May 2007|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl] However Labour gained a seat back at a by-election in June 2009 in Painters Ash ward.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.kentonline.co.uk/gravesend_messenger/news/2009/june/5/by-election_victory_for_labour.aspx|title=Labour win narrow victory in by-election|date=5 June 2009|work=Gravesend Messenger|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl]
During the campaign the national Labour leader Ed Miliband came to Gravesham twice, while the Conservative Prime Minister David Cameron met party activists,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-13323577|title=Labour's big push in the South East reaps mixed results|last=Stewart|first=Louise|date=7 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl] along with visits by other senior figures from the two parties.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.gravesendreporter.co.uk/news/voters_go_to_the_polls_in_gravesham_dartford_and_sevenoaks_1_884572|title=Voters go to the polls in Gravesham Dartford and Sevenoaks|last=Hitchens|first=Nick|date=5 May 2011|work=Gravesend Reporter|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl] Apart from the Conservative and Labour parties there were also candidates from the United Kingdom Independence Party in 5 wards and the Liberal Democrats in 3 wards.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.newsshopper.co.uk/news/elections/local_elections/gravesham/9011264.Will_voters_keep_Gravesham_Council_Conservative_run_/|title=Gravesham Council elections could see Labour claw back rule from the Tories|last=Maden|first=Sophie|date=5 May 2011|work=News Shopper|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl]

==Election result==

Labour gained 8 seats to take control of the council from the Conservatives.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-kent-13282384|title=Labour gains control of Gravesham from Conservatives|date=6 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl] Labour made gains in Central, Painters Ash, Singlewell and Westcourt wards to hold 25 seats, compared to 19 for the Conservatives.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.gravesendreporter.co.uk/news/labour_win_gravesham_but_conservatives_hold_dartford_in_local_elections_1_885811|title=Labour win Gravesham but Conservatives hold Dartford in local elections|date=6 May 2011|work=Gravesend Reporter|accessdate=27 September 2011[/tpl]
Following the election Ed Miliband came back to Gravesham and said "Our victory here is both a sign of our progress and a symbol of our task ahead." Both the local Conservative and Labour leaders on the council, Mike Snelling and John Burden, put the results down to the cuts being made by the national Conservative led government.
