[[Anthony Gatto]]

CATEGORIES: 1973 births, American circus performers, Living people, Jugglers

Anthony Gatto (real name Anthony Commarota) is an American juggler who holds several juggling world records. He began performing in Las Vegas at the age of ten.
Born in Manhattan April 14, 1973Gatto, From Vaudeville Acro-Cat to the King of Juggling, Produced by Alan Plotkin and growing up in Maryland, he was a juggling prodigy: world-class by the age of nine.Biographical Information He made his first television appearance at the age of 8 on the program That's Incredible! Gatto's early exposure to juggling, and much of his initial act, can be credited to his father Nick Gatto, a longtime performer.Gatto, Anthony. "History of Gatto Ent?" 5 May 2008. Juggling Talk. 
In 1998 Gatto met his future wife Danielle, who would go on to become his assistant during his performances. A professional dancer, Danielle added circus-style skills to her résumé to accompany her husband on circus tours. Danielle and Anthony married in 1999 and they have two children.
In 2000 he became the first and so far only juggler to win the prestigious Golden Clown award at the 24th International Circus Festival of Monte-Carlo.[tpl]cite web |title=List of previous award winners |publisher=International Circus Festival of Monte-Carlo |url=http://www.montecarlofestivals.com/siteweb/palmares_precedentes.html |accessdate=October 16, 2009[/tpl] Between 2007 and 2009 Gatto's performance was a part of Cirque du Soleil's show Koozå. From 2010 to 2012 he was part of Cirque du Soleil's show La Nouba.
In 2014 it was discovered that Anthony Gatto may have retired from performing in Cirque Du Soleil in order to run his concrete resurfacing business "Big Top Concrete Resurfacing LLC" under his birth name of Anthony Commarota.http://grantland.com/features/anthony-gatto-juggling-cirque-du-soleil-jason-fagone/ The business was founded by him in August 2012 and is located in Orlando, Florida.
