[[Bob Katz]]

Bob Katz is an audio mastering engineer and author of a popular book on audio mastering.[tpl]cite book |title=Mastering Audio: The Art and the Science |date=2007 |author=Bob Katz |ISBN=0240808371[/tpl] Katz has mastered three Grammy Award-winning albums and one nominated album. He has received acclaim from audiophilesStereophile: 1992 Records To Die For[tpl]Failed verification|date=April 2013[/tpl] and his book on mastering has received acclaim,Elsevier[tpl]dead link|date=April 2013[/tpl] with some considering it the "definitive work on mastering".[tpl]cite web|title=Bob Katz Mastering Audio|url=http://www.soundonsound.com/sos/oct03/articles/bobkatz.htm|publisher=Sound On Sound|accessdate=14 December 2012[/tpl] He has developed proprietary systems K-Stereo and K-Surround. These processes are designed to "recover lost or amplify hidden ambience, space and imaging, and generate stereo from mono signals without adding artificial reverberation."[tpl]cite magazine |url=http://mixonline.com/news/headline/bobkatz-uspatent-kstereoksurround-102406/ |title=Mastering Engineer Bob Katz Awarded U.S. Patent |date=2006-10-24 |publisher=Mix (magazine) |accessdate=2013-04-18[/tpl]
Katz taught at the Institute of Audio Research from 1978 to 1979. In 1988, Katz joined Chesky Records and began recording jazz and classical artists there, as well as producing oversampled commercial recordings. In 1990, he founded an audio mastering company called Digital Domain Mastering, where he continues to work.
