[[Java (town)]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in Dzau district, Cities and towns in Shida Kartli

Java or Dzau ([tpl]lang-ka|ჯავა[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-os|Дзау[/tpl], Dzaw; [tpl]lang-ru|Джава[/tpl]) is a town of approximately 1,500 people in Georgia (in South Ossetia[tpl]South Ossetia-note[/tpl]). According to Georgia's current official administrative division, Java is a main town of Java district in the north of Shida Kartli region. According to the South Ossetian side Dzau is an administrative center of Dzau district. The town is situated on the southern slopes of the Greater Caucasus, within the Greater Liakhvi Gorge, [tpl]convert|1040|m|0|abbr=on[/tpl] above sea level.   
Java is the second largest urban settlement in South Ossetia, after Tskhinvali. It is located outside the Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe-defined boundaries of the Georgian-Ossetian conflict zone – an area within a 15-km radius of Tskhinvali.GEORGIA: AVOIDING WAR IN SOUTH OSSETIA, p. 4. European Security and Defence Assembly Assembly of WEU. 4 December 2008
The town played a major role in the 2008 South Ossetia war, with most of the South Ossetian military forces being located thereThe Russian Air Force didn't perform well during the conflict in South Ossetia at the time of the Georgian offensive. During the Battle of Tskhinvali, the government of South Ossetia relocated to Java. 
Georgia had accused the Russian military of building a large military base in Java before the war. These concerns were brought by the President of Georgia, Mikheil Saakashvili, to the attention of the UN General Assembly on September 26, 2007.Saakashvili Attacks Russia in UN Speech. Civil Georgia.
