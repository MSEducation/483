[[Fides ecclesiastica]]

CATEGORIES: Catholic theology and doctrine, Latin religious phrases

Fides ecclesiastica is a classification of those Roman Catholic dogmas which are Church teachings, definitively decided on by the Magisterium, but not (yet) as being Divine revelations properly speaking.[tpl]cite book|last=Dulles|first=Avery|title=The Survival of Dogma|year=1973|publisher=Image Books|location=Colorado Springs|isbn=0385089570|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=rDkqAAAAYAAj[/tpl]  They are considered infallible and irrevocable because, although they are not "truths of faith" (De Fide), they are nevertheless "closely related to them".[tpl]cite journal|last=John Paul II|title=The Holy Spirit Assists the Roman Pontiff|journal=General Audience|date=March 24, 1993|url=http://www.vatican.va/holy_father/john_paul_ii/audiences/alpha/data/aud19930324en.html[/tpl]
It is one level below De fide and one level above Sententia fidei proxima which are generally accepted as divine revelation but not defined as such by the Magisterium.
