[[São Tomé and Príncipe legislative election, 2010]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in São Tomé and Príncipe, 2010 elections in Africa, 2010 in São Tomé and Príncipe

A legislative election was held in São Tomé and Príncipe on 1 August 2010. The President of São Tomé and Príncipe, Fradique de Menezes, announced on 17 March 2010 that legislative election would be held on 1 August 2010.[tpl]cite web|author=Kris Haamer |url=http://saotomeblog.com/2010/03/18/santomean-parliamentary-elections-2010/ |title=Updated: Elections in Sao Tome & Principe Announced |publisher=Saotomeblog.com |date=2010-03-18 |accessdate=2010-08-21[/tpl]

==Results==

According to preliminary results from 2 August 2010, the opposition Independent Democratic Action won a plurality of seats (26) of the 55 up for grabs. Prime Minister Joaquim Rafael Branco's MLSTP-PSD came second with 21 seats. Third was PCD, a member of the ruling coalition, with 7 seats. President Fradique de Menezes's MDFM-PL got only one seat. Turnout was reportedly very high at almost 88%.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rnw.nl/africa/article/opposition-adi-wins-sao-tome-election-no-absolute-majority|title=Map  Opposition ADI wins Sao Tome election, no absolute majority|date=2 August 2010|publisher=AFP/Radio Netherlands Worldwide|accessdate=2 August 2010[/tpl]
