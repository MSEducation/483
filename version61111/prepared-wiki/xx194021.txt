[[Ram Island (Tasmania)]]

CATEGORIES: Furneaux Group, Important Bird Areas of Tasmania

Ram Island is a small mudstone and dolerite island, with an area of 1 ha, in south-eastern Australia.  It is part of Tasmania’s Vansittart Island Group, lying in eastern Bass Strait between Flinders and Cape Barren Islands in the Furneaux Group.  It is privately owned.  

==Wildlife==

BirdLife International identifies Ram Island as part of the Franklin Sound Islands Important Bird Area because it holds over 1% of the world populations of six bird species.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.birdata.com.au/iba.vm |title=IBA: Franklin Sound Islands |accessdate=2011-06-22 |work= Birdata |first= |last= |publisher=Birds Australia |date= [/tpl]

==Flora and fauna==

Recorded breeding seabird, waterbird and wader species are Little Penguin, Pacific Gull, Sooty Oystercatcher, White-faced Storm-Petrel, Black-faced Cormorant, Caspian Tern and Cape Barren Goose.Brothers, Nigel; Pemberton, David; Pryor, Helen; & Halley, Vanessa.
