[[Rødekro]]

CATEGORIES: Former municipalities of Denmark, Cities and towns in Region of Southern Denmark

Rødekro is a town with a population of 6,127 (1 January 2013)BEF44: Population 1st January, by urban areas database from Statistics Denmark and a former municipality (Danish, kommune) in Aabenraa municipality in Region of Southern Denmark on the Jutland peninsula in south Denmark.  The former Rødekro municipality covered an area of 202 km², and had a total population of 11,695 (2005).  Its last mayor was Tove Larsen, a member of the Social Democrats (Socialdemokraterne) political party.

On January 1, 2007 Rødekro municipality ceased to exist as the result of Kommunalreformen ("The Municipality Reform" of 2007).  It was merged with Bov, Lundtoft, Tinglev,  and Aabenraa municipalities to form the new Aabenraa municipality.   This created a municipality with an area of 951 km² and a total population of 60,151 (2005).  The new municipality  belongs to Region of Southern Denmark ("South Denmark Region").
