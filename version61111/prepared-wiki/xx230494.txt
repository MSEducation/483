Virgile Boumelaha (born 6 August 1983 in Mulhouse) is a French association footballer of Algerian descent,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dzfoot.com/article-297.php |title=Interview de Boumelaha : "Sans hésitation je choisirai le bled" | Football algérien |publisher=Dzfoot.com |date= |accessdate=21 August 2010[/tpl] he plays for FC Grenchen.

==Career==

Boumelaha began his career with FC Saint-Louis, he moved then to FC Basel and later to FC Sochaux-Montbéliard in 2002 but made two appearance for the club before joined to VfB Stuttgart in 2004. He failed to break into the first team there, also,  signed for HJK Helsinki where he played 1 gam over the course of the 2005/06 season. He then moved to Switzerland with SR Delémont in January 2006 where play 19 games. After just two seasons with Delémont, he made his way to FC Gossau[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.search.ch/sport/fussball/2008-08-29/foot-virgile-boumelaha-engage-par-gossau.fr.html |title=FOOT: Virgile Boumelaha engagé par Gossau |publisher=News.search.ch |date=29 August 2008 |accessdate=21 August 2010[/tpl] of the Challenge League, who he played 6 months between 18 December 2008 who left the team,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.toggenburgertagblatt.ch/sport/fussball/Gossau-verlaengert-auslaufende-Vertraege-nicht;art609,1235905 |title=www.toggenburgertagblatt.ch – Gossau verlängert auslaufende Verträge nicht |publisher=Toggenburgertagblatt.ch |date= |accessdate=21 August 2010[/tpl] he signed than in February 2009 with FC Grenchen.[tpl]cite web|url=http://regiofussball.ch/content/view/1057/417/ |title=Fussball in der Region Luzern - Zentralschweiz - Transfernews Spieler und Trainer |publisher=REGIOfussball.ch |date=23 September 2009 |accessdate=21 August 2010[/tpl]
