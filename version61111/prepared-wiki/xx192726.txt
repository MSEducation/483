[[John Sherman Birthplace]]

CATEGORIES: National Historic Landmarks in Ohio, Houses completed in 1823, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Ohio, Museums in Fairfield County, Ohio, Historic house museums in Ohio, Biographical museums in Ohio, Houses in Fairfield County, Ohio

Sherman Birthplace, also known as Sherman House Museum is a site in Lancaster, Ohio significant for its association with births and lives of U.S. senator John Sherman and one of his older brother's William T. Sherman, a Union general in the American Civil War.
It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1964.[tpl]Cite document|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Register of Historic Places Inventory-Nomination: |32 KB[/tpl]|date=January, 1978 |author=Joseph S. Mendinghall, Cecil McKithan and William Birdsell |publisher=National Park Service|postscript=[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying three photos, exterior, from 1975|32 KB[/tpl]
The house is located at 137 E. Main St. in Lancaster, and is operated by the Fairfield Heritage Association.
