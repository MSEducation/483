[[Patsy Rodenburg]]

CATEGORIES: 1953 births, Living people, Academics of the Guildhall School of Music and Drama, Alumni of the Central School of Speech and Drama, Officers of the Order of the British Empire, LGBT entertainers from England

Patsy Rodenburg, OBE (born 1953) is a British voice coach, author, and theatre director. She is the Head of Voice at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London, the Director of Voice at Michael Howard Studios in New York City, and has also worked with the Royal Shakespeare Company and Royal National Theatre.[tpl]cite news|author=Tran, Mark|title=A working life: The voice coach|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/money/2011/apr/02/working-life-voice-coach-patsy-rodenburg|work=The Guardian|date=2 April 2011|accessdate=26 July 2011[/tpl]

==Career==

Rodenburg trained in Voice Studies at the Central School of Speech and Drama, and initially worked as an actress before moving into teaching.[tpl]cite news|author=Gussow, Mel|title=Henry Higgins Is Real (And Female); Patsy Rodenburg Teaches British Stars How to Speak|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2001/01/08/theater/henry-higgins-real-female-patsy-rodenburg-teaches-british-stars-speak.html|work=The New York Times|date=8 January 2001|accessdate=27 July 2011[/tpl] She has been Head of Voice at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London since 1981 and the Director of Voice at Michael Howard Studios in New York since 1982. She worked with the Royal Shakespeare Company for nine years from 1981, and with the Royal National Theatre from 1990, where she founded their Voice Department. She has worked globally with the Moscow Art Theatre, Complicite, Cheek by Jowl, and Comedie-Francaise. She has taught thousands of actors, including Daniel Craig, Orlando Bloom, Joseph Fiennes, Ewan McGregor and Fay Ripley to name a few.
She has authored several books on the subject of voice coaching, including "Speaking Shakespeare," "The Actor Speaks," "The Right to Speak," "The Need for Words," and "Second Circle" (released in the UK as "Presence").[tpl]cite web|title=Ms Patsy Rodenburg, OBE|url=http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/search/results/8424/Patsy%20RODENBURG.aspx|work=Debrett's People of Today|accessdate=27 July 2011[/tpl]
In 2005, Rodenburg was appointed Order of the British Empire in the Queen's Birthday Honours.[tpl]cite news|title=Queen honours Sylvia Young, voice coach Rodenburg and VLV’s Hay|url=http://www.thestage.co.uk/news/newsstory.php/8223/queen-honours-sylvia-young-voice-coach|work=The Stage|date=13 June 2005|accessdate=27 July 2011[/tpl]
In 2006, Rodenburg directed a production of King Lear at the Electric Lodge theatre in Los Angeles. The Los Angeles Times called the production "emotionally potent," and Variety’s critic claimed, "The brilliant cast brings this play to vibrant, painful life."[tpl]cite news|author=Wolf, Matt|title=‘We’ll Find Out Together’: A Voice Coach Tries Her Hand at Directing|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/09/04/theater/04rode.html?ei=5088&en=287f07cb1c203e88&ex=1315022400&adxnnl=1&partner=rssnyt&emc=rss&adxnnlx=1305379673-8lgW3Zq0tZlWUwTvq+AUzw|work=The New York Times|date=4 September 2006|accessdate=26 July 2011[/tpl]
In 2010, Rodenburg was listed at number 15 of The Times newspaper's "The Luvvie Power List 2010," a list of theatre’s 50 most influential people, saying Rodenburg "has worked with some of the world's most powerful actors, among them Judi Dench and Ian McKellen."[tpl]Cite news|title=The luvvie power list 2010: theatre’s 50 most influential people|newspaper=The Times|date=15 December 2010|url=http://www.thetimes.co.uk/tto/arts/stage/theatre/article2844309.ece[/tpl]
The Board of Directors at VASTA, the Voice And Speech Trainers Association, honored Rodenburg with their Lifetime Distinguished Membership. They cite it as an honor reserved for individuals "who have made outstanding contributions to the field of Voice & Speech."Voice and Speech Trainers Association, Lifetime Distinguished Members

==Desert Island Discs==

In March 2012 Rodenburg was Kirsty Young's guest on BBC Radio 4's Desert Island Discs. Her choices of music were: Mahler Symphony No.5; Sandy Denny - Who Knows Where the Time Goes?; Beethoven Symphony No. 7; Johann Sebastian Bach — Concerto in E Major for Violin;  Daniel Evans — Finishing the Hat by Stephen Sondheim; Jean Sibelius - Violin Concerto; Kathleen Ferrier — The Keel Row; Johann Sebastian Bach - Prelude to Cello Suite No.1 in G.  Her choice of book was an anthology of poetry and her luxury a supply of "builder's tea".BBC Radio 4, Desert Island Discs, - Patsy Rodenburg

==Personal life==

Rodenburg was married to a teacher at the Royal Academy of Speech and Drama, but his alcoholism led to the breakdown of the relationship. She now lives with her son Michael, born 2003, and her partner the ballet dancer and choreographer Antonia Franceschi.
