[[Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Burgundy (French region), TER Bourgogne, Railway stations opened in 1870

Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve is a French train station located at Junot Avenue in Dijon.  It is in the Côte-d'Or department, within France's Burgundy region. Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve is the secondary station for the city of Dijon, with the primary station being Gare de Dijon-Ville. TER (Transport express régional) trains take six minutes to go from one station to the other, crossing the city. ==Location==

Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve is located at kilometre post 321.935 on the "Dijon-Ville – Is-sur-Tille Line". This situates it between the Dijon-Ville and the Ruffey-lès-Echirey stations. Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve is at an altitude of [tpl]convert|243|m|ft[/tpl], whereas the altitude of Dijon's main train station, Dijon-ville, is [tpl]convert|247.48|m|ft[/tpl].[tpl]Cite web |url=http://wikiplm.railsdautrefois.fr/wikiPLM/index.php?title=Fichier:ProfLg_Dijon_Is_001_RF.jpg |title=Survey of the Dijon à Is-sur-Tille Line |publisher=Chemins de fer de Paris à Lyon et à la Méditerranée[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl] The Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve passenger station is located next to its freight station, which extends north toward the Toison d'Or neighborhood of Dijon.

==History==

The French railway company Chemins de fer de Paris à Lyon et à la Méditerranée (PLM) was awarded a concession in 1863 to build a rail line from Dijon to Langres, and from there to France's border with Belgium. The large existing PLM train station was judged as not suitable for the new line and a site to the east of the city was chosen instead. Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve opened in 1870, after the beginning of the Franco-Prussian War.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cndp.fr/crdp-dijon/IMG/pdf_clemenceau.pdf |title=Un quartier: Paysage et fonction; Enquête dans le quartier Clémenceau – Boudronnée |publisher=Institut pour une meilleure connaissance de l'histoire urbaine et des villes |location=Dijon, France |author= |accessdate=3 November 2013[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://initiativesdijon.over-blog.com/article-quel-avenir-pour-la-place-de-la-republique-52014953.html |title=Quel avenir pour la Place de la République ? |date=10 June 2010 |author=François-Xavier Dugourd |location=Dijon, France |publisher=Initiatives Dijon |accessdate=3 November 2013[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl]
The new station was located adjacent to the existing station of the Departmental Railways of the Côte-d'Or, a secondary (not part of France's national railway network) narrow gauge railway servicing the following lines: Meuilley — Dijon,  Dijon — Champlitte, and  Dijon — Chatillon-sur-Seine. The last of these secondary lines would eventually close in 1948.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.trains-fr.org/facs/lig21.htm |title=Les Chemins de fer secondaires de France – Département de la Côte-d'Or |year=2008 |editor=Fédération des amis des chemins de fer secondaires (FACS) |accessdate=29 May 2011[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl][tpl]Cite web |url=http://plm1950.msts.free.fr/rail/TacotSaulieu.html |title=Lignes de la Côte-d'Or |author=Gilbert Gillet |year=2008 |editor=Témoin du chemin de fer de France dans la seconde partie du XXe siècle |accessdate=29 May 2011[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl]
For the LGV Rhin-Rhône Project, which had its first branch open on 11 December 2011, the Dijon government will expand and modernize Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.grand-dijon.fr/regards-sur/territoire/le-reseau-metropolitain-rhin-rhone-10579.jsp |title=Le Réseau métropolitain Rhin-Rhône |accessdate= 13 July 2012[/tpl] [tpl]Fr[/tpl] In a related development, the train and bus station in the town center underwent a reorganization in 2008.

==Passenger and freight service==

Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve is an SNCF railway station building without a ticket window. It is equipped with a TER automated ticketing system and also has nearby parking for bicycles and other vehicles. The train platforms are situated on a concrete walkway, with stairways providing access. The station is served by TER Bourgogne trains on the line from the Dijon-Ville station to Is-sur-Tille. A train on the Intercités Dijon — Reims line and some trains to Culmont-Chalindrey and Troyes also service the station. ==TGV project==

Due to the decision to increase the western branch of the LGV Rhin-Rhône to pass through Dijon, and the need for a new TGV station on this line, the current Gare de Dijon-Porte-Neuve will become Dijon's new TGV station, with an expected re-opening in 2025.[ref]
 [tpl]Fr[/tpl][/ref] Improvements to Dijon-Porte-Neuve have already included the creation of a tram station and a link to Gare de Dijon-Ville, using the Dijon Tramway, which began operation in September 2012.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.railwaygazette.com/news/single-view/view/dijon-tram-network-opens-six-months-early.html| title=Dijon tram network opens six months early| work=Railway Gazette| date=3 September 2012[/tpl]
