[[Champlain Valley Transportation Museum]]

CATEGORIES: Museums in Clinton County, New York, Museums established in 2000, Transportation museums in New York, Automobile museums in New York, 2000 establishments in New York

The Champlain Valley Transportation Museum in Plattsburgh, New York, United States, originally founded in 2000 to be a museum dedicated to the history of Lozier Motors, has grown in scope to cover all the transportation in the region. It includes artifacts and displays on the history of land and water based transportation in the region,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.adirondackdailyenterprise.com/page/content.detail/id/284.html?nav=32|title=Lake City of Plattsburgh|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2008-01-13|work=Adirondack Daily Enterprise[/tpl] especially on Lake Champlain and the St. Lawrence Seaway.
The museum was the idea of Anthony Vaccaro, a Plattsburgh physician, who owned and restored a 1915 Lozier.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.cvtmuseum.com/aboutus.html|title=About Us|accessdate=2008-05-21|publisher=Champlain Valley Transportation Museum[/tpl] He saw a museum dedicated to Lozier Motors as a way of promoting Plattsburgh's rich history. Despite the museum's expanded scope, the Lozier remains its centerpiece.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PPRB&p_theme=pprb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=106D69B2C6BC1D50&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=A New Time Machine|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2004-11-27|author=Jeff Meyers|work=Press Republican[/tpl] The museum's Type 82 Lozier is the only known Type 82 in existence.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.apnmag.com/winter_2007/galantyCVTM.php|title=Champlain Valley Transportation Museum|accessdate=2008-05-21|year=2007|author=Max Galanty|work=All Points North[/tpl]
The museum officially opened on the site of the former Plattsburgh Air Force Base in 2004 with the help of a corps of volunteers.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PPRB&p_theme=pprb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=1016C217E1CDEC70&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Road Flattening out for Local Transportation Museum|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2003-04-01|author=Jeff Meyers|work=Press Republican[/tpl] Its permanent collection includes two Lozier Automobiles. Also housed at the museum are the Bill Gates Diner (a cafe housed in a trolley car),[tpl]Cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PPRB&p_theme=pprb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10D78A850C54A978&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Historic Diner/Trolley Car Now a Part of Transportation Museum|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2005-10-15|author=Jeff Meyers|work=Press Republican[/tpl] a 1924 REO Speed Wagon used by Plattsburgh Motor Services' founder Walter Church,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PPRB&p_theme=pprb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10BBD7895C27A828&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Delivery Complete|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2005-07-18|author=Jeff Meyers|work=Press Republican[/tpl] a 1929 Model A Ford and a 1967 Jaguar.
Following a 2006 grant that helped establish the museum,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.wnbz.com/October%202006/102306.htm|title=Funding Secured for TL Multi-Use  Trail|accessdate=2008-05-21|publisher=WNBZ|date=2006-10-23[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] in 2007, the museum received a $1 million grant from the New York State Department of Transportation to assist in the 
upgrade of the museum's infrastructure. The museum is required to raise $200,000 in order to receive the grant and in January 2008 it began a capital campaign to achieve this goal.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.pressrepublican.com/0503_museums/local_story_014223116.html|title=Transportation Museum Looks to Expand|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=2008-01-14|author=Jeff Meyers|work=Press Republican[/tpl]
