[[Rolling Hills, Alberta]]

CATEGORIES: Hamlets in Alberta, Designated places in Alberta, Newell County, Alberta

Rolling Hills is a hamlet in southern Alberta, Canada within the County of Newell.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://municipalaffairs.gov.ab.ca/documents/msb/2010-ruralmun.pdf |title= Specialized and Rural Municipalities and Their Communities |author=Alberta Municipal Affairs |authorlink=Alberta Municipal Affairs |accessdate=2010-06-05 |date=2010-04-01[/tpl]  Rolling Hills was founded in 1939 by the Prairie Farm Rehabilitation Administration (PFRA) with settlers from dried out regions of Saskatchewan and Alberta.

==Demographics==

As a designated place in the 2011 Census, Rolling Hills had a population of 205 living in 84 of its 100 total dwellings, a -16.7% change from its 2006 population of 246. With a land area of [tpl]convert|0.69|km2|sqmi|abbr=on[/tpl], it had a population density of [tpl]Pop density|205|0.69|km2|sqmi[/tpl] in 2011.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www12.statcan.gc.ca/census-recensement/2011/dp-pd/hlt-fst/pd-pl/Table-Tableau.cfm?LANG=Eng&T=1302&PR=48&S=51&O=A&RPP=25 | title=Population and dwelling counts, for Canada, provinces and territories, and designated places, 2011 and 2006 censuses (Alberta) | publisher=Statistics Canada | date=2012-02-08 | accessdate=2012-04-07[/tpl]

==Economy==

Rolling Hillsâ€™ primary industry is agriculture. Specialty crops grown in the area include dry edible beans, sugar beets, potatoes, seed canola and seed alfalfa.  Cattle have always been a major part of the agricultural landscape in the Rolling Hills area. 

==Education==

One school, serving kindergarten through grade 9, is located in Rolling Hills.  After grade 9, students attend high school in Brooks, which is approximately [tpl]convert|50|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] northwest of Rolling Hills. 

==Amenities==

Rolling Hills has a nine-hole golf course, a community hall with an attached curling rink, an ice hockey rink, an exercise establishment[tpl]Clarify|June 2010|date=June 2010[/tpl] and a park with camping facilities.
