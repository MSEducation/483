[[Wo Ai Ni Mommy]]

CATEGORIES: Documentary films about Asian Americans, American documentary films, P.O.V. (TV series) films, Documentary films about adoption

Wo Ai Ni Mommy ([tpl]lang-en|I Love You Mommy[/tpl]) is a 2010 American television documentary film directed by Stephanie Wang-Breal,[tpl]cite news|title=No breakouts at this year's Boston Jewish Film Festival|url=http://articles.boston.com/2010-10-31/ae/29300207_1_michal-bat-adam-previous-festivals-anita/4|work=Boston.com|publisher=boston.com|accessdate=24 September 2011|deadurl=yes[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=February 2012|bot=RjwilmsiBot[/tpl] and distributed by P.O.V.. The documentary follows the story of a then 8-year-old Chinese girl, Fang Sui Yong,[tpl]cite web|last=Milano|first=Valerie|title=Transracial and Transnational Adoption|url=http://www.hollywoodtoday.net/2010/08/31/pov-the-trail-to-self-discovery/|work=POV â€“ The Trail to Self Discovery|publisher=Hollywood Today|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] who was adopted by a Jewish Long Island family. The film had its world premiere at the San Francisco International Asian American Film Festival in 2010.
Given a new name of "Faith" by her new adopter mother, Donna Sadowsky, the film documents Faith's life just before her adoption in China, and follows her journey to America for a period of 18 months. Faith lived with her new adopted father, Jeff, as well as the Sadowsky's two biological sons and another Chinese adoptee.

==Awards==

Wo Ai Ni Mommy won the Best Documentary Feature at the San Francisco International Asian American Film Festival,[tpl]cite news|title=Asian American festival awards|url=http://articles.sfgate.com/2010-04-02/movies/20832191_1_festival-awards-special-jury-award-cannes-film-festival|work=SF Gate|publisher=SF Gate|accessdate=24 September 2011|date=27 June 2011[/tpl] and the 'Sterling Award for Best U.S. Feature' at the Silverdocs.[tpl]cite web|last=Renninger|first=Bryce J.|title=Silverdocs Juries Reward "Mommy" and "5 Elephants"|url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/silverdocs_juries_reward_mommy_and_5_elephants/|work=Silverdocs|publisher=indieWIRE|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] The film is nominated for an Emmy for Outstanding Informational Programming-Long Form, which will be awarded on September 26, 2011.
