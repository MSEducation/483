[[The Public (play)]]

CATEGORIES: Plays by Federico García Lorca, 1930 plays, Metafictional works, LGBT-related plays

The Public (El público), also known as The Audience, is a play by the twentieth-century Spanish dramatist Federico García Lorca.[tpl]Citation|last=Martínez Nadal|first=Rafael|author-link=|title=Lorca's The Public: A Study of his Unfinished Play (El público) and of Love and Death in the Work of Federico García Lorca|place=London|publisher=Calder & Boyars|year=1974|isbn=0-7145-2752-1|pages=70–82[/tpl] It was written between 1929 and 1930, but remained unpublished until 1978 and did not receive its first professional theatrical production until 1986.[tpl]Citation|last=García Lorca|first=Federico|author-link=Federico García Lorca|last2=Edwards|first2=Gwynne|title=Plays: Three|place=London|publisher=Methuen|year=1994|isbn=0-413-65240-8|pages=xxiv|chapter=Introduction[/tpl] The world premiere of the play was directed by Victoria Espinosa on 15 February 1978 at the University of Puerto Rico.[tpl]Citation|last=Rodríguez Pagán|first=Juan Antonio|title=El otro lado de El público de Lorca|place=London|publisher=Isla Negra Editores (San Juan, P.R)|year=1999|isbn=1-881715-47-7|pages=82–83|chapter=2, En las comedias imposibles está mi verdadero teatro...[/tpl]

==Production History==

The Public was first presented at the University of Puerto Rico on 15 February 1978, where it was directed by Victoria Espinosa. A translated version by Henry Livings (in consultation with the director/designer, Ultz) was first performed on 3 October 1988 at the Theatre Royal Stratford East.
