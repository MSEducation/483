[[Kangaroo Lake State Natural Area]]

CATEGORIES: Protected areas established in 2002, Protected areas of Door County, Wisconsin, State Natural Areas of Wisconsin, 2002 establishments in Wisconsin

Kangaroo Lake State Natural Area is a nature sanctuary in Door County, Wisconsin, United States, located on and around Kangaroo Lake.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dnr.state.wi.us/ORG/LAND/ER/sna/sna335.htm|title=Kangaroo Lake State Natural Area|accessdate=2007-08-03|date=2007|publisher=Wisconsin Department of Natural Resources[/tpl] The land was designated a State Natural Area in 2002. Home to the endangered Hine's Emerald Dragonfly, it has the largest breeding population of the dragonfly in the world.[tpl]cite web | title=Hines Dragonfly | publisher=Hinesdragonfly.org | url=http://www.hinesdragonfly.org/ | accessdate=2007-08-03[/tpl] The area also provides recreational activities for the public.

==Species in the State Natural Area==

Many species of plants, mammals, birds, and fish inhabit Kangaroo Lake State Natural Area.
