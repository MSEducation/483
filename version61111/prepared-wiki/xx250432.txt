[[William Duthie Morgan]]

CATEGORIES: 1891 births, 1977 deaths, Knights Grand Cross of the Order of the Bath, Companions of the Distinguished Service Order, Recipients of the Military Cross, British Army generals of World War II, Royal Artillery officers

General Sir William Duthie Morgan GCB DSO MC (1891–1977) was a British Army General during World War II.

==Military career==

Morgan was commissioned into the Royal Artillery in 1913.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=28683|supp=|startpage=497|date=21 January 1913|accessdate=9 January 2010[/tpl] He served in World War I winning the DSO at the Battle of Le Cateau in 1914 and later the MCLiddell Hart Centre for Military Archives and was mentioned in despatches four times. After the war his postings included active service in Waziristan and a period as a staff officer (GSO3) in the War Office in London. In 1929 he was appointed military attaché at the British Embassy in Budapest in Hungary where he remained until 1931. In 1933 when he was posted as a major to 19th Field Brigade RA in Bordon and in 1934 be became Chief Instructor at the Royal Military Academy, Woolwich.
In World War II Morgan (nicknamed Monkey) initially commanded 10th Field Regiment Royal Artillery with the British Expeditionary Force and then became the senior staff officer (GSO1) with 1st Division in France. Back in the UK he was appointed to the rank of temporary brigadier to be Brigadier General Staff (BGS) of 1st Corps. Having had his permanent rank advanced to full colonel in May 1941(with seniority back dated to 1939),[tpl]London Gazette|issue=35157|supp=y |startpage=2648|date=6 May 1941|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] he was appointed acting major-general and named Commander[tpl]London Gazette|issue=35192|supp=y |startpage=3440|date=13 June 1941|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] of 55th Division in that June. In October 1941 he was injured and was forced to relinquish this appointment and revert to the rank of colonel on full pay.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=35337|supp=y |startpage=6427|date=4 November 1941|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl]
Returned to fitness, in September 1942 Morgan was appointed an acting lieutenant-general[tpl]London Gazette|issue=35740|supp=y |startpage=4431|date=9 October 1942|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] to be Chief of the General Staff for Home Forces. When British land forces were reorganised in July 1943 to create 21st Army Group for the planned invasion of northwest Europe, Morgan became Chief of Staff of the new army group.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.unithistories.com/default.asp |title=World War II unit histories and officers|accessdate=18 August 2010 |last=Houterman |first=Hans| coauthors=Koppes, Jeroen[/tpl]  His rank was upgraded to temporary lieutenant-general in September 1943[tpl]London Gazette|issue=36186|supp=y |startpage=4295|date=24 September 1943|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] and he was appointed Companion of the Order of the Bath (CB) in the 1944 New Year's honours list.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=36309 |supp=y |startpage=4 |date=31 December 1943|accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] In February 1944 he was made General Officer Commanding-in-Chief for Southern Command. While still appointed a temporary lieutenant-general, Morgan's permanent rank was advanced to major-general in May 1944.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=36519|supp=y |startpage=2273 |date=16 May 1944 |accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] In March 1945Jackson, p. 196. he became Chief of Staff to the Supreme Allied Commander Mediterranean, Field Marshal Harold Alexander In May 1945, he accepted the surrender of all Axis Forces in Italy. In September 1945 he was appointed Deputy Supreme Allied Commander for the Mediterranean Theatre and then in October succeeded Alexander as Supreme Allied Commander. Also in October he was made Knight Commander of the Order of the Bath.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=37310 |supp=y |startpage=5097 |date=16 October 1945 |accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] The Morgan Line, which at one time demarcated the boundary between Italy and Yugoslavia, was named after him.White's Political Dictionary, 1947 In August 1946 Morgan's rank of lieutenant-general was made permanent (with seniority backdated to the end of 1944)[tpl]London Gazette|issue=37701 |supp=y |startpage=4295 |date=23 August 1946 |accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] and in November 1946 was promoted to general.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=37796 |supp=y |startpage=5769|date=22 November 1946 |accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl]
In 1947 Morgan was made Commander of British Army Staff in Washington, D.C. and Army member of the Joint Staff Mission to the United States. In this capacity Morgan was offered access to the atomic bomb by General Dwight Eisenhower as an incentive to persuade Britain to give up its own programme.Bomb offer exposed 6 December 1992 His knighthood was advanced to Knight Grand Cross of the Order of the Bath in the 1949 New Year's honours list[tpl]London Gazette|issue=38493 |supp=y |startpage=2 |date=31 December 1948 |accessdate=18 August 2010[/tpl] and he retired from the army in June 1950.
