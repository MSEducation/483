[[Ernő Rubik]]

CATEGORIES: 1944 births, Living people, People from Budapest, Hungarian architects, Hungarian inventors, Recreational mathematicians, Puzzle designers, Rubik's Cube, Toy inventors

Ernő Rubik ([tpl]IPA-hu|ˈrubik ˈɛrnøː|lang[/tpl]; born July 13, 1944) is a Hungarian inventor, architect and professor of architecture. He is best known for the invention of mechanical puzzles including Rubik's Cube (1974), Rubik's Magic, Rubik's Magic: Master Edition, Rubik's Snake and Rubik's 360.

==Life and career==

Ernő Rubik was born in Budapest, Hungary, July 13, 1944, during World War II, and has lived all his life in Hungary. His father, Ernő Rubik, was a flight engineer at the Esztergom aircraft factory, and his mother, Magdolna Szántó, was a poet.[tpl]cite book|last=|first=|authorlink=|coauthors=|title=International Who's Who 2000|publisher=Europa|year=1999|location=|pages=1342|month=|url=|isbn=1-85743-050-6[/tpl] He graduated from the Technical University, Budapest (Műszaki Egyetem) Faculty of Architecture in 1967 and began postgraduate studies in sculpting and interior architecture. From 1971 to 1975 he worked as an architect, then became a professor at the Budapest College of Applied Arts (Iparművészeti Főiskola). It was during his time there[tpl]contradiction-inline | reason = dates are not consistent: if he joined the college during or after 1975 then he could not have been there in 1974|date=May 2013[/tpl] that he built designs for a three-dimensional puzzle and completed the first prototype of the Rubik's Cube in 1974, applying for a patent on the puzzle in 1975. In an interview with CNN, Rubik stated that he was "searching to find a good task for my students." The little cube that changed the world Retrieved 2013-04-29.
In the early 1980s, he became editor of a game and puzzle journal called ..És játék (...And games), then became self-employed in 1983, founding the Rubik Stúdió, where he designed furniture and games. In 1987 he became professor with full tenure; in 1990 he became the president of the Hungarian Engineering Academy (Magyar Mérnöki Akadémia). At the Academy, he created the International Rubik Foundation to support especially talented young engineers and industrial designers.
At present he is mainly working on video game development and architectural topics and is still leading Rubik Stúdió.
He is known to be an introvert, barely accessible and hard to contact or to get hold of for autographs.[tpl]citation needed|date=November 2013[/tpl] He typically does not attend speedcubing events.
