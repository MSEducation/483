[[Pleioblastus]]

CATEGORIES: Flora of China, Flora of Japan, Bamboo genera

Pleioblastus is a genus of monopodial bamboos,[tpl]cite book | last = Ohrnberger | first = D. | title = The Bamboos of the World | publisher = Elsevier Science | year = 1999 | pages = 596 | isbn = 978-0-444-50020-5[/tpl] native to China and Japan.[tpl]cite book|title=RHS A-Z encyclopedia of garden plants|year=2008|publisher=Dorling Kindersley|location=United Kingdom|isbn=1405332964|pages=1136[/tpl] Genetic research indicates that this genus may properly be part of the genus Arundinaria.[tpl]Cite journal | doi = 10.1007/BF02856844 | last = Zhuge | first = Qiang | coauthors = Yu-long Ding, Chen Xu, Hui-yu Zou, Min-ren Huang, and Ming-xiu Wang | title = A preliminary analysis of phylogenetic relationships of Arundinaria and related genera based on nucleotide sequences of nrDNA (ITS region) and cpDNA (trnL-F intergenic spacer) | journal = Journal of Forestry Research | publisher = Springer | volume = 16 | issue = 1 | pages = 5â€“8 | year = 2005 | accessdate = 2009-01-02 | postscript = [/tpl] 
The plant spreads by vigorous underground rhizomes which run along just beneath the soil surface, producing plantlets at the nodes.  These can be used to propagate new plants, but if not removed they can become invasive.
The species P. variegatus[tpl]cite web|title=RHS Plant Selector - Pleioblastus variegatus|url=http://apps.rhs.org.uk/plantselector/plant?plantid=1497|accessdate=28 May 2013[/tpl] (green and cream stripes), and P. viridistriatus[tpl]cite web|title=RHS Plant Selector - Pleioblastus viridistriatus|url=http://apps.rhs.org.uk/plantselector/plant?plantid=1493|accessdate=28 May 2013[/tpl] (green and yellow stripes) have gained the Royal Horticultural Society's Award of Garden Merit.[tpl]cite book|title=RHS A-Z encyclopedia of garden plants|year=2008|publisher=Dorling Kindersley|location=United Kingdom|isbn=1405332964|pages=1136[/tpl]
