Lieutenant General Sir Roderick Alexander 'Roddy' Cordy-Simpson [tpl]post-nominals|KBE|CBE|DL[/tpl] (born 1944) is a retired British Army general.

==Career==

Educated at Radley College,Radley College Foundation Report 2004 Roddy Cordy-Simpson was commissioned into the 13th/18th Hussars in February 1966.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=43898|supp=yes|startpage=1755|date=15 February 1966|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl] He was deployed to Bosnia as Chief of StaffSrebrenica air evacuation halted by Serb shelling Prescott Courier, 25 March 1993 of the United Nations Protection Force in 1992Select Committee on Committee House of Commons, 3 February 1999 and was appointed General Officer Commanding 1st (UK) Armoured Division in 1994.Whitaker's Almanack 1994 He was deployed to Bosnia again in December 1996Media attacked over Kosovo campaign BBC News, 9 July 1999 as Deputy Force Commander Operations for the Stabilisation Force (SFOR).NATO in Bosnia
In March 2001 he was appointed Lieutenant of the Tower of London,[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=56136|supp=|startpage=2634|date=2 March 2001|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl] leaving that role in March 2004.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=57221|supp=|startpage=2671|date=2 March 2004|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl] Having settled at Bishopstrow in Wiltshire, he was appointed a Deputy Lieutenant of Wiltshire in 2004.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=57445|supp=|startpage=13359|date=22 October 2004|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl] He was awarded the CB in June 1993[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=53333|supp=yes|startpage=33|date=12 June 1993|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl] and the KBE in the New Year Honours List 1998.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=54993|supp=yes|startpage=5|date=31 December 1997|accessdate=26 September 2009[/tpl]

==Family==

He is married to Virginia and has two children, Zoe and Angus.
