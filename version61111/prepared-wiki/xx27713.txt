[[Ahikam]]

CATEGORIES: Hebrew Bible people

Ahikam, in Hebrew אחיקם, "My brother has risen". One of the five whom Josiah sent to consult the prophetess Huldah in connection with the discovery
of the book of the law.[tpl]bibleref|2Kings|22:12-14[/tpl]; [tpl]bibleref|2Chronicles|34:20[/tpl] He was
the son of Shaphan, the royal secretary, and the father of Gedaliah, governor of Judea after the destruction of Jerusalem by the Babylonians.[tpl]bibleref|2Kings|25:22[/tpl]; [tpl]bibleref|Jeremiah|40:5-16[/tpl]; [tpl]bibleref|Jeremiah|43:6[/tpl]) 
On one occasion, in a move described by Jonathan Magonet as taking the prophet into 'protective custody',Magonet, Jonathan (1992) Bible Lives (London, SCM), 107 he protected Jeremiah against the fury of Jehoiakim.([tpl]bibleref|Jeremiah|26:24[/tpl])Ahicam - Catholic Encyclopedia article It was in the chamber of another son (Gemariah) of Shaphan that Baruch read Jeremiah's scroll in the hearing of all the people.
