[[Jen Bekman Gallery]]

CATEGORIES: Contemporary art galleries in the United States, Art galleries in Manhattan, Art galleries established in 2003, 2003 establishments in New York, SoHo

Jen Bekman Gallery is a former art gallery located at 6 Spring Street in New York City.http://www.jenbekman.com/ It was established by Jen Bekman in March 2003 on Spring Street west of Bowery, and closed in August 2013. Bekman's goals were to help emerging artists become more appreciated, and to encourage a broader swath of people to feel comfortable buying art.Scelfo, Julie. "Easing the Pain of Collecting". New York Times. February 28, 2008.
Jen Bekman Gallery exhibited the work of artists in the mediums of photography, works on paper, paintings and mixed media.
Jen Bekman Projects have included 20×20020×200 website (editioned prints at affordable prices) (2007-2013), Hey, Hot Shot!Hey, Hot Shot! website (an international competition for emerging photographers) (2004-2012), jen@joe (a revolving exhibition of photographs at Joe, affordably priced and available for purchase online only) (2003-2006), and PersonismPersonism, Jen Bekman's blog (a personal blog about design, photography, and current events) (2009-2010).
In 2006, Bekman was named an Innovator of the Year by American Photo.  They wrote, "She's developing a new generation of photo artists and consumers."2006 Innovators: Galleries - - PopPhotoJanuary/February 2007

==20×200==

20×200 was an e-commerce site by Jen Bekman Projects that sold original artist editions at affordable prices that operated from 2007 to 2013. The company received $800,000 in Series A funding in October 2009, with an additional $2 million secured in August 2010.[tpl]cite news|url=http://techcrunch.com/2009/10/21/online-art-store-20x200-scores-800000-from-true-ventures-and-angels/|title=Online Art Store 20×200 Scores $800,000 From True Ventures And Angels|publisher=TechCrunch|date=2009-10-21|first=Leena|last=Rao[/tpl]
When the business first launched, the smallest size was reprinted in the largest batch — an edition of 200 — and sold at the lowest price — $20. Hence the name 20×200 (pronounced: "twenty by two-hundred").20×200: Our StoryDana, Aaron. "Jen Bekman on Art". GOOD Magazine. April 5, 2007.Walker, Alyssa. "Jen Bekman, founder of 20×200". Fast Company. April 12, 2010.Wilhide, Jenny. "Printastic: why prints are more popular than installations". London Evening Standard. April 19, 2010.
According to Bekman, the company brought in $2.5 million in revenue in 2010, including $100,000 worth of work by artist William Wegman in a single day in February.[tpl]cite news|url=http://money.cnn.com/2011/04/26/technology/art_startups/index.htm|title=Wealthy investors add art startups to their collections|publisher=CNNMoney|date=2011-04-26|accessdate=2013-02-13|first=Laurie|last=Segall[/tpl]Pollock, Lindsay. "Jen Bekman Sells 100k in Wegmans in a single day". Art Market Views. March 2, 2010.
In January 2013, 20x200 went offline and operations were suspended.[tpl]cite news|url=http://blogs.artinfo.com/artintheair/2013/02/01/update-20x200-still-offline-confirms-some-staff-departures-in-email/|title=Update: 20×200 Still Offline, Confirms Some Staff Departures in Email|publisher=ARTINFO|date=2013-02-01|accessdate=2013-02-13|first=Shane|last=Ferro[/tpl] The board of 20×200 including lead investor Tony Conrad resigned and the company's staff were dismissed. The company reportedly left hundreds of unfulfilled orders and owes vendors tens of thousands of dollars.[tpl]cite web|last=Halperin|first=Julia|title=Can 20×200 Be Saved?
