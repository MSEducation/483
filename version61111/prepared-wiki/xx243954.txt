[[Edward Young Clarke]]

CATEGORIES: Ku Klux Klan members, People convicted of violating the Mann Act, People from Louisiana, Businesspeople in advertising, Year of death missing, Year of birth missing, American businesspeople convicted of crimes

Edward Young Clarke was an advertising executive from Louisiana and the Imperial Wizard pro tempore of the Ku Klux Klan for several years following its revival after 1915; he resigned from leadership in 1922. He devised the "kluxing" system of payments to the hierarchy within the Klan.[tpl]cite journal |last= |first= |editor=Lyman Abbott, Ernest Hamlin Abbott, and Hamilton Wright Mabie |coauthors= |title=The Outlook, Volume 127  |year=1921 |publisher= |quote= | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=J3XPAAAAMAAJ&q=kleagle+zarephath+young&dq=kleagle+zarephath+young |isbn= [/tpl] Along with Elizabeth Tyler, he helped to turn the initially anemic second Ku Klux Klan into a mass-membership organization with a broader social agenda.

==Biography==

Edward Young Clarke was born in Louisiana. He became an advertising executive.
In the early 20th century, Clarke joined the Ku Klux Klan, which appealed to people as one of several fraternal organizations.
In 1922, Clarke stepped down from his position at the Klan, which he had held for several years.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |authorlink= |coauthors= |title=Clarke Quits Post As Head Of Ku Klux.
