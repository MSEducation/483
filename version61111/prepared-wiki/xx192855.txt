[[Pete Earley]]

CATEGORIES: American journalists, American political writers, Living people

Pete Earley is an American journalist and writer of non-fiction books and novels.

==Career==

A former Washington Post reporter, he is the author of books about the Aldrich Ames and John Walker espionage cases. His book Circumstantial Evidence: Death, Life, and Justice in a Southern Town. won an Edgar Award from the Mystery Writers of America for Best Fact Crime Book in 1996.[tpl]cite web|author=Pete Earley |url=http://us.macmillan.com/author/peteearley |title=Pete Earley | Authors | Macmillan |publisher=Us.macmillan.com |date=2009-12-04 |accessdate=2012-02-14[/tpl] This book helped free an innocent man from the death row in Alabama. His book about John Walker spy ring, Family of Spies, was a New York Times bestseller and was made into a CBS miniseries starring Powers Boothe and Lesley Ann Warren.  In 2007, he was a finalist for a Pulitzer Prize for his book Crazy.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.pulitzer.org/finalists/2007 | title=2007 finalists | publisher=pulitzer.org | accessdate=1 September 2009[/tpl]
