[[Gulaga National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of New South Wales, South Coast (New South Wales), Protected areas established in 2001, 2001 establishments in Australia, Important Bird Areas of New South Wales

Gulaga is a national park on the south coast of New South Wales, Australia.  The park was created in 2001.  It is 46.73 km² in area.[tpl]cite web | year = 2006 | url = http://www.nationalparks.nsw.gov.au/parks.nsf/parkContent/N0165?OpenDocument&ParkKey=N0165&Type=xo | title = Gulaga National Park | publisher = National Parks and Wildlife Service (New South Wales) | accessdate = 2006-05-22[/tpl]  The park forms part of the Ulladulla to Merimbula Important Bird Area, identified as such by BirdLife International because of its importance for Swift Parrots.BirdLife International. (2012). Important Bird Areas factsheet: Ulladulla to Merimbula. Downloaded from http://www.birdlife.org on 2012-01-02.
On 6 May 2006 the freehold titles to Gulaga and Biamanga National Parks were handed back to the Yuin people by the New South Wales Government.  Freehold title of Gulaga National Park will be held in trust for the Aboriginal owners by Merrimans and Wagonga Local Aboriginal Lands Councils, while that of Biamanga will be held in trust by Merrimans and Bega Local Aboriginal Lands Councils.[tpl]cite news  | first=Laurelle  | last=Pacey  | url= http://narooma.yourguide.com.au/detail.asp?class=news&subclass=local&story_id=479434&category=General%20News&m=5&y=2006 | title= Handing back heartbeat of Yuin nation  | work=  | publisher= Narooma News  | pages=  | page=  | date= 11 May 2006  | accessdate= 2006-05-22[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
