[[Abanyom language]]

CATEGORIES: Languages of Nigeria, Ekoid languages

Abanyom, or Bakor, is a language of the Ekoid subfamily of Niger–Congo.   It is spoken by the Abanyom people in the Cross River State region of Nigeria, numbering about 12,500 people in 1986.[ref]Gordon, Raymond G., Jr. (ed.), 2005. Ethnologue: Languages of the World, Fifteenth edition. Dallas, Tex.: SIL International. Online version: http://www.ethnologue.com/.
[/ref] A member of the Southern Bantoid group, Abanyom is fairly closely related to the Bantu languages. It is tonal and has a typical Niger–Congo noun class system.
