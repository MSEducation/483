[[O2 Academy Leeds]]

CATEGORIES: Grade II listed buildings in West Yorkshire, Listed buildings in Leeds, Music in Leeds, Music venues in West Yorkshire, Visitor attractions in Leeds, O2 (United Kingdom)

The O2 Academy Leeds (formerly known as the Leeds Academy)[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.leeds-academy.co.uk/ |title=Leeds Academy |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=Leeds Academy Official Site [/tpl] is a music venue situated in Leeds intended as a larger music venue for the city.  It is run by the Academy Music Group and follows in-line with their other music venues around the UK. The Academy was nominated for the TPi Awards 2010 for the country's favourite venue.http://www.academy-music-group.co.uk/news/article.aspx?aid=e2ffb044-facb-45f1-a181-f693d3a258f5

==Building history==

The venue for the Leeds Academy on Cookridge Street in the city was originally opened in 1885 by Prince Albert[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.clubzone.com/c/Leeds/Nightclub/Creation.html |title=Creation Leeds (Nightclub) |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=ClubZone [/tpl] and is a grade II listed Gothic building.  [tpl]cite web|url = http://www.britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/en-466117-the-town-and-country-club-|title= The Town and Country Club, Leeds|publisher= British Listed Buildings|accessdate = 17 February 2014[/tpl] [tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nme.com/news/various-artists/38580 |title=Leeds Academy set to open this autumn |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=NME |date=2008-08-01 [/tpl]  Known originally as the Coliseum it hosted many different events during the early 20th century such as political meetings and circus shows.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/music-gigs/Leeds-Town-and-Country-Club.3500528.jp |title=Leeds Town and Country Club back |accessdate=2008-08-19 |work=Paul Robinson |publisher=Yorkshire Evening Post |date=2007-11-19 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080531144805/http://www.yorkshireeveningpost.co.uk/music-gigs/Leeds-Town-and-Country-Club.3500528.jp  |archivedate = 2008-05-31[/tpl] Between the 1930s and 1990s the building accommodated a cinema, television studio and bingo hall.
The venue opened in 1992 as the Town and Country Club and between 1992 and 2000 staged gigs by artists such as Blur, David Bowie, Tangerine Dream, Robbie Williams, The Stereophonics,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nme.com/reviews/stereophonics/943 |title=Stereophonics - Leeds Town & Country Club |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=NME |date=1999-03-30 [/tpl] East 17 and The Stone Roses.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.angelfire.com/band2/bffh/boots.html |title=Bootleg Trade List |accessdate=2008-08-19 |date=2005-11-08 [/tpl]  The venue however closed in 2000.
In 2001, after a £7 million re-development of the venue by First Leisure Corporation, it opened as Creation Nightclub, but closed after six years in February 2007. The licence was acquired by AMG from Luminar and plans put in place for the new Leeds Academy in November 2007.

==The venue==

The venue itself has a large room accommodating 2,300 guests, including a balcony which can hold 500 people.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.academy-music-group.co.uk/venue/venue.aspx?id=561 |title=O2 Academy Leeds |accessdate=2012-11-25 |publisher=AMG [/tpl]  There is also a basement room for gigs by up and coming bands which will accommodate 400 guests. The Kaiser Chiefs opened the venue with a gig on 8 October 2008[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nme.com/news/kaiser-chiefs/39050 |title=Kaiser Chiefs to launch Leeds Academy |accessdate=2008-08-19 |publisher=NME |date=2008-08-19 [/tpl] Some other well known musicians which have played at The Academy have included: Cher Lloyd, All Time Low, Foals, Wolfmother, Last Shadow Puppets, Ellie Goulding, The Pigeon Detectives, MGMT, Paolo Nutini, The Proclaimers, Madness (band), The Fratellis, Beady Eye, The Pogues, Deftones, Band of Horses, Crystal Castles and Hot Chip.
On 6 November 2008, it was announced that Telefónica Europe (owners of the O2 Network in the UK) had become the new sponsor of all Academy venues, in a deal with music promotor Live Nation. The deal, which lasts for five years, sees all venues rebranded "The O2 Academy", in line with Telefónica's purchase of the Millennium Dome (now The O2).[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2008/nov/06/marketingandpr-mediabusiness|title=O2 to sponsor Academy music venues|publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=6 December 2008 | location=London | first=Mark | last=Sweney | date=2008-11-06[/tpl]
