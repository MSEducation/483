[tpl]nihongo|A Certain Magical Index|とある魔術の禁書目録|Toaru Majutsu no Indekkusu[/tpl] is a Japanese animated television series based on the light novel series of the same name written by Kazuma Kamachi and illustrated by Kiyotaka Haimura. The plot of the episodes follows the life of Tōma Kamijō, a high school student with a unique power in his right hand, who resides in a scientifically advanced city, and his interactions with other psychic and magic users after he meets Index, a young nun whose mind has been implanted with the 103,000 magical texts of the Index Librorum Prohibitorum.
The anime is a production of J.C.Staff directed by Hiroshi Nishikiori, series composition by Masanao Akahoshi, music by Maiko Iuchi, character design by Yuichi Tanaka and art and sound direction by Yasunori Ebina respectively. The anime adaptation was confirmed on June 3, 2008 in the sixteenth volume of Toaru Majutsu no Index,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2008-06-03/to-aru-majutsu-no-index-light-novels-gets-tv-anime|title=Toaru Majutsu no Index Light Novels Get TV Anime|publisher=Anime News Network|date=2008-06-03|accessdate=2008-12-14[/tpl] and the official website began to stream promotional videos on July 7, 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2008-07-07/kemeko-dx-to-aru-majutsu-no-index-promos-streamed|title=Kemeko-DX, Toaru Majutsu no Index Promos Streamed|publisher=Anime News Network|date=2008-07-07|accessdate=2008-12-14[/tpl] The episodes began airing on October 4, 2008 on Mainichi Broadcasting System, Chiba TV, and TV Kanagawa. They were also broadcast at later dates on Chubu-Nippon Broadcasting and TV Saitama. Eight DVD compilations, each containing three episodes and a mini Toaru Kagaku no Railgun novel written by Kazuma Kamachi and art by Motoi Fuyukawa, were released between January 23, 2009 and August 21, 2009 by Geneon Entertainment.[tpl]cite web|url=http://project-index.net/item/dvd_bd.php|title=Item - DVD|language=Japanese|publisher=Square-Enix|accessdate=2008-12-14[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B001HZEYRC/ |title=とある魔術の禁書目録 第7巻 (初回限定版) DVD|publisher=Amazon.com|language=Japanese|accessdate=2008-12-14[/tpl]
Four pieces of theme music are used for the episodes: two opening and two ending themes. The first opening theme is "PSI-Missing" by Mami Kawada, used from episodes 1 to 16 (corrected in the DVD version to episode 14). From episode 17 (episode 15 in the DVD version) onwards, a second opening theme, "Masterpiece" by Mami Kawada, was used. The first ending theme is [tpl]nihongo|"Rimless ~Fuchinashi no Sekai~"|Rimless 〜フチナシノセカイ〜|Rimless ~Rimless World~[/tpl] by Iku. From episode 20 onwards, a second ending theme called [tpl]nihongo|"Chikaigoto ~Sukoshi Dake Mō Ichido~"|誓い言 〜スコシだけもう一度〜|Oath ~Just a Little Once More~[/tpl], also by Iku, was used.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jcstaff.co.jp/sho-sai/index-shokai/index-music.htm|title=Index - Music|publisher=J.C.Staff|language=Japanese|accessdate=2008-12-14[/tpl]
A second season, titled Toaru Majutsu no Index II, aired in Japan between October 8, 2010 and April 1, 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2010-06-07/toaru-majutsu-no-index-2nd-season-green-lit|title=Toaru Majutsu no Index[tpl]'[/tpl]s 2nd Season Green-Lit|publisher=Anime News Network|date=June 7, 2010|accessdate=June 7, 2010[/tpl] The opening theme music of Index II, which aired from episode 1 to 16, is "No Buts!" by Mami Kawada and was released on November 3, 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hmv.co.jp/news/article/1008040058|title=『とある…禁書目録』OP主題歌!|trans_title=Toaru...Index OP Theme Song!|publisher=HMV|accessdate=August 16, 2010|language=Japanese[/tpl] The first ending theme music of Index II which aired from episode 1 to 13 is "Magic∞world" by Maon Kurosaki and the single was released on November 24, 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.project-index.net/news/hp0001/index00040000.html|title=OP＆ED　リリース決定！ -とある魔術の禁書目録Ⅱ＜インデックスⅡ＞ 公式サイト-|accessdate=September 2, 2010|language=Japanese[/tpl] The second ending theme music which aired from episode 14 is [tpl]Nihongo|"Memories Last"|メモリーズ・ラスト|Memorīzu Rasuto[/tpl] by Maon Kurosaki and will be released on March 2, 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.amazon.co.jp/dp/B004GBLG9S/|title=メモリーズ･ラスト|trans_title=Memories Last|publisher=Amazon.co.jp|accessdate=December 17, 2010|language=Japanese[/tpl] The second opening theme starting from episode 17 onwards is "See Visions" by Mami Kawada, released on February 16, 2011.

==Bonus episodes==

Bonus segments entitled Toaru Majutsu no Index-tan, which is a parody of a similar segment from the Shakugan no Shana series, are included with certain volume releases, featuring various outtake moments from the series, as well as a chibified Index. These segments are not included in the North American releases.

==Blu-ray and DVD releases==

The first Blu-ray and DVD compilation for the series was released on January 23, 2009, with one scheduled for each month until all episodes were collected into eight volumes. Each volume contains 3 episodes. A DVD Box Set of the first season of Index was released on December 22, 2010 containing the first twelve episodes of the season, with a second Box Set scheduled to be released on March 9, 2011 containing the rest of the season. The first Blu-ray and DVD compilation of the second season of Index was released on January 26, 2011, and followed the same model as the first season's Blu-ray and DVD release schedule.
