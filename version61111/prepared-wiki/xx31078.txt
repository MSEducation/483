[[Geneva County, Alabama]]

CATEGORIES: Alabama counties, Geneva County, Alabama, 1868 establishments in the United States, Dothan, Alabama metropolitan area

Geneva County is a county of the U.S. state of Alabama. As of the 2010 census, the population was 26,790.[tpl]cite web|last=United States Census Bureau|title=2010 Census Data|url=http://2010.census.gov/2010census/data/|publisher=United States Census Bureau|accessdate=19 July 2012[/tpl]  Its county seat is Geneva. Geneva County is a prohibition or dry county. 
The county was named after its county seat, which in turn was named after Geneva, New York which was named after Geneva, Switzerland, by Walter H. Yonge, an early town resident and Swiss native.[tpl]cite web |publisher=Alabama Department of Archives and History |title=Geneva County |url=http://www.archives.state.al.us/counties/geneva.html |date=June 4, 2009 |accessdate=Aug 1, 2009[/tpl]
Geneva County is part of the Dothan, Alabama, Metropolitan Statistical Area.
