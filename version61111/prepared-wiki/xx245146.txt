The Hours is a 2002 drama film directed by Stephen Daldry and written by David Hare. The screenplay is based on the eponymous novel by Michael Cunningham, which follows three generations of women whose lives are affected by Virginia Woolf's 1925 novel, Mrs Dalloway.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/culture/2011/feb/07/michael-cunningham-life-writing|title=Michael Cunningham: A life in writing|last=Brockes|first=Emma|date=February 5, 2011|work=The Guardian|publisher=Guardian Media Group|accessdate=February 13, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/books/2003/feb/02/features.review1|title=A jump into the uncertain possibilities of life|last=Thorpe|first=Vanessa|date=2 February 2003|work=The Observer|publisher=Guardian Media Group|accessdate=21 May 2011[/tpl] The film's world premi√®re was on December 18, 2002 at the Mann's National Theatre in California, before a limited release in the United States on December 27, 2002.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.life.com/image/1693881|title=Moore/Streep/Kidman At World Premiere Of The Hours|date=December 18, 2002|work=Life|publisher=Time Warner|accessdate=February 14, 2011[/tpl] It went into wide release on February 14, 2003. The Hours earned over $108 million in its combined total gross at the box office.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boxofficemojo.com/movies/?page=main&id=hours.htm|title=The Hours|work=Box Office Mojo|publisher=Amazon.com|accessdate=February 14, 2011[/tpl] 
The Hours garnered various awards and nominations following its release, with nominations ranging from recognition of the film itself to Hare's screenplay, Philip Glass' score and the cast's acting performances, particularly those of Nicole Kidman and Julianne Moore. The film received nine Academy Award nominations; the ceremony saw Kidman win the Best Actress award. At the 56th British Academy Film Awards, The Hours won two awards from eleven nominations. The Deutscher Filmpreis awarded The Hours Best Foreign Film, while the GLAAD Media Awards named it Outstanding Wide Release Film. The Hours received seven nominations at the 60th Golden Globe Awards, winning the Best Drama Film and Best Actress awards. The Las Vegas Film Critics Society Awards saw John C. Reilly and Kidman win Best Supporting Actor and Best Actress respectively. Moore earned a Best Actress award from the Los Angeles Film Critics Association, while Meryl Streep was given the award for Best Performance by an Actress in a Leading Role from Outfest, a LGBT-oriented film festival.
The film was nominated for a total of eight awards from the Satellite and 9th Screen Actors Guild Award ceremonies, but failed to win any. The Hours won three Vancouver Film Critics Circle Awards, including Best Supporting Actress for Toni Collette. Collette also won the Boston Society of Film Critics Award for Best Supporting Actress. Hare and Cunningham were given the 2002 USC Scripter Award for Best Screenplay. The Writers Guild of America named Hare the winner of the Best Adapted Screenplay award, while the London Film Critics' Circle named him British Screenwriter of the Year.
