[[Ohio State Route 757]]

CATEGORIES: State highways in Ohio, Transportation in Perry County, Ohio

State Route 757 (SR 757) is a northâ€“south state highway located in the central portion of the U.S. state of Ohio.  The highway runs from its southern terminus at SR 13 in Somerset to its northern terminus at a T-intersection with SR 204 in Glenford.

==Route description==

SR 757 runs exclusively within northern Perry County.  The highway is not included as a portion of the National Highway System.[tpl]cite map | title=National Highway System: Ohio | date=December 2003 | publisher=Federal Highway Administration | url=http://www.fhwa.dot.gov/planning/nhs/maps/oh/oh_Ohio.pdf | format=PDF|accessdate=2010-10-07[/tpl]

==History==

This state route came into existence in 1937, starting from SR 13 approximately [tpl]convert|1.25|mi|km[/tpl] northwest of Somerset, and utilizing Black Horse Road (County Road 27) up to where it meets the current SR 757, then following the current alignment of the highway from that point to its current northern terminus at SR 204 in Glenford.[tpl]cite map|title=Ohio State Map|year=1936|publisher=Ohio Department of Transportation[/tpl][tpl]cite map|title=Ohio State Map|year=1937|publisher=Ohio Department of Transportation[/tpl]  By 1957, the southern portion of SR 757 was re-aligned onto the routing that it follows today from the Black Horse Road intersection southeasterly to its intersection with SR 13 in Somerset.[tpl]cite map|title=Ohio State Map|year=1955|publisher=Ohio Department of Transportation[/tpl][tpl]cite map|title=Ohio State Map|year=1957|publisher=Ohio Department of Transportation[/tpl]
