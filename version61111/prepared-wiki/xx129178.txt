Adam David Lallana (born 10 May 1988) is an English footballer who plays as a winger for Premier League club Southampton and the England national football team. Born in St Albans, Lallana  began his career with Southampton as a youth player in 2000, signing professional terms in 2006, and has remained with the club since, becoming captain in 2012.

==Early life==

Lallana was born in St Albans but moved to the Iford area of Bournemouth when he was five years old.[tpl]cite web | title=Bank on Adam| work= Bournemouth Echo| url=http://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/3966295.BANK_ON_ADAM/|date= 11 December 2008 | accessdate=16 December 2008[/tpl] As a child, Lallana supported Everton, like his father.

==Club career==

Lallana was a member of the Bournemouth Centre of Excellence, where his rich potential was eventually spotted by a talent scout from Southampton, with Lallana snapped up by the Premier League club, joining the Saints Academy as a 12-year old in 2000. Southampton paid £3,000 compensation to Bournemouth with further payments of £5,000 and £10,000 when he signed scholarship and professional contracts respectively. 
Lallana was involved in Southampton's FA Youth Cup squads of 2004–05 and 2005–06, when they reached the final and semi-final respectively.
Lallana joined the first team squad in July 2006, making his debut on 23 August 2006, in a 5–2 victory over Yeovil Town, in the League Cup.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/league_cup/5266330.stm|title=Southampton 5–2 Yeovil |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 August 2006 |accessdate=4 January 2014[/tpl] On 31 October 2006 he signed a new contract to keep him at Southampton until December 2009.[tpl]cite web | title=Lallana signs new deal| work= www.saintsfc.co.uk| url=http://www.saintsfc.co.uk/news/?page_id=7672|date= 31 October 2006 | accessdate=9 October 2007| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20070929091417/http://www.saintsfc.co.uk/news/?page_id=7672| archivedate= 29 September 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl] On 9 October 2007 he joined Bournemouth on loan for one month.[tpl]cite web | title=Lallana on loan| work= www.saintsfc.co.uk| url=http://www.saintsfc.co.uk/news/?page_id=9101|date= 9 October 2007| accessdate=9 October 2007[/tpl] On 28 April 2008, Lallana scored his first professional goal for Southampton against West Bromwich Albion in a crucial Championship clash, with a well placed finish in the bottom left corner.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_1/7356066.stm |title=West Brom 1–1 Southampton |publisher=BBC Sport |date=28 April 2008 |accessdate=4 September 2009[/tpl]
He became a regular in the first team at the start of the 2008–09 season. On 29 August 2008, he signed a new three-year deal with the Saints.
He finished the 2009–10 season with a total of twenty goals in all competitions, netting his 20th on the final day of the season against Southend United. This made him the first Southampton midfielder to score twenty goals in a season since Matthew Le Tissier's 30 in the 1994–95 season.
On 7 January 2011, Lallana signed a new contract with Southampton which expires in the summer of 2015.[tpl]cite web |title =Midfielder Signs Long Term Deal |url=http://www.saintsfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10280~2261409,00.html|publisher=Southampton F.C.| accessdate=8 January 2011|date=7 January 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Leitch |first=Adam |title=Lallana signs new Saints deal |url=http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/sport/8778862.Lallana_signs_new_Saints_deal/|publisher=Southern Daily Echo|accessdate=8 January 2011 |date=7 January 2011[/tpl] He was named in the League One Team of the Year for the 2010–11 season after scoring 11 goals that season.
He then scored on the opening day of the 2011–12 season, a 3–1 victory over Leeds United at St Mary's Stadium which broke a 12-year hoodoo of Southampton not winning on the opening day and secured a record-breaking seventh consecutive league win. He then scored twice in a 5–2 victory at Ipswich Town. His 150th start for the club came in a 1–1 draw at south coast rivals Portsmouth. His tenth goal of the season came in a 4–0 victory over Derby County. He then grabbed a brace in a 2–0 victory over Barnsley. He was nominated for the Player of the Year award but lost out to teammate Rickie Lambert. Along with Lambert and Kelvin Davis, he was one of three Southampton players named in the Championship Team of the Year for the 2011–12 season. He scored on the final day of the campaign to finish with 13 goals, 11 of which came in the league.
He made his first Premier League appearance on the opening day of the 2012–13 season, setting up a goal and wearing the captain's armband in a 3–2 defeat against 2011-12 champions Manchester City."Man City 3–2 Southampton" BBC Sport. 19 August 2012. Retrieved 25 November 2012. He scored his first Premier League goal in a 4–1 defeat away to West Ham United."West Ham 4–1 Southampton" BBC Sport. 20 October 2012. Retrieved 25 November 2012. His second goal of the season came on 25 November, as he opened the scoring in a 2–0 home win against Newcastle United."Southampton 2–0 Newcastle" BBC Sport. 25 November 2012. The 50th goal of his career came in a 2–0 victory at Reading.
On 11 April 2013, Lallana signed a new five-year contract with Southampton.[tpl]cite web |title =Southampton: Adam Lallana signs a new five-year contract |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/22112980|publisher=BBC Sport| accessdate=12 April 2013|date=11 April 2013[/tpl]
In Southampton's 4–1 Premier League victory over Hull City on 9 November 2013, Lallana started with the ball 30 yards from goal and dribbled his way past five players into the left-hand side of the 18-yard box, before slotting it past Hull goalkeeper Steve Harper.[tpl]citeweb|url = http://www.theguardian.com/football/2013/nov/09/southampton-hull-city-premier-league|title=Adam Lallana's brilliant goal helps Southampton crush feeble Hull City|date=9 November 2013|work=Guardian |accessdate=30 April 2014[/tpl]
On 18 April 2014, he was named as one of the six players on the shortlist for the PFA Player of the Year award.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.com/sport/0/football/27076251|title=PFA award: Liverpool's Suarez, Gerrard & Sturridge on shortlist|date=18 April 2014|accessdate=19 April 2014|work=BBC[/tpl] On 27 April 2014, he was named alongside teammate Luke Shaw in the PFA Team of the Year.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11668/9286998/pfa-unveils-team-of-the-year|title=PFA unveils Team of the Year|date=27 April 2014|accessdate=28 April 2014|work=BSkyB[/tpl]

==International career==

Lallana has played for England Under-18s, scoring on his debut against Slovenia, 18 months after undergoing an operation to correct an irregular heartbeat. He has also played once each for
England Under-19s and England Under-21s. On 10 September 2012, Lallana was called up to the senior England squad for the first time for the 2014 World Cup qualifying match against Ukraine although he was not selected for the match itself.[tpl]cite news |title=Shock England call for trio |url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/12098/8069628/Shock-England-call-for-trio |work=Sky Sports |publisher=Skysports.com |date=10 September 2012 |accessdate=10 September 2012 [/tpl]
On 7 November 2013, along with Southampton team mates Rickie Lambert and fellow uncapped Saint Jay Rodriguez, he was called up to the England team.[tpl]cite news |title=England squad: Southampton striker Jay Rodriguez called up |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24849964|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=7 November 2013|date=7 November 2013[/tpl] Lallana won his first cap for England on 15 November in a friendly match against Chile at Wembley Stadium (which they lost 2–0) [tpl]cite web|last=McNulty|first=Phil|title=England 0 – 2 Chile|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24862578|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=17 November 2013|date=15 November 2013[/tpl] to generally favourable reviews.[tpl]cite web |last=Burt |first=Jason |title=England v Chile: Adam Lallana flies flag high for Southampton |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/teams/england/10453486/England-v-Chile-Adam-Lallana-flies-flag-high-for-Southampton.html |publisher=Daily Telegraph|accessdate=17 November 2013|date=15 November 2013[/tpl] He also started in England's defeat against Germany.[tpl]cite web|title= England 0 – 1 Germany|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24907635|publisher=BBC Sport|date=19 November 2013| accessdate= 30 March 2014[/tpl] He gained his third cap in a 1–0 friendly win against Denmark, coming on as a substitute for Jack Wilshere where he set up the winning goal for Daniel Sturridge.
