[[Kevin J. Schreiber]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, People from York, Pennsylvania, York College of Pennsylvania alumni, Pennsylvania State University alumni, Pennsylvania Democrats, Members of the Pennsylvania House of Representatives

Kevin Schreiber is a member of the Pennsylvania House of Representatives, representing the 95th district. 

==Personal life and education==

Schreiber graduated from St. Piucx High School in 1998 and earned a B.A. in Public Relations from York College of Pennsylvania in 2002. He earned an MPA from Penn State in 2012.[tpl]cite web|title=Rep. Kevin Schreiber|url=http://www.legis.state.pa.us/cfdocs/legis/home/member_information/House_bio.cfm?id=1680|publisher=Pennsylvania House of Representatives|accessdate=8 September 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

Schreiber has served in the House since 2013, when he won a special election to succeed Eugene DePasquale, who resigned his seat to become Pennsylvania Auditor General.[tpl]cite news|last=Mahon|first=Ed|title=Democrat Kevin Schreiber wins 95th state House seat|url=http://www.ydr.com/politics/ci_23295469/democrat-kevin-schreiber-wins-95th-state-house-seat|accessdate=8 September 2013|newspaper=York Daily Record|date=22 May 2013[/tpl] He sits on the Children & Youth, Local Government, and Urban Affairs committees.[tpl]cite web|title=Rep. Kevin Schreiber|url=http://www.legis.state.pa.us/cfdocs/legis/home/member_information/House_bio.cfm?id=1680|publisher=Pennsylvania House of Representatives|accessdate=8 September 2013[/tpl] Schreiber describes education and keeping small communities competitive as his top priorities.[tpl]cite news|last=Gross|first=Greg|title=Schreiber says education would be among his priorities if he wins state House seat|url=http://www.yorkdispatch.com/ci_22282114/york-city-economic-development-director-hopes-replace-depasquale|accessdate=8 September 2013|newspaper=The York Dispatch|date=25 April 2013[/tpl]
Schreiber previously served as the director of economic and community development for the York municipal government.[tpl]cite news|last=Mahon|first=Ed|title=Democrat Kevin Schreiber wins 95th state House seat|url=http://www.ydr.com/politics/ci_23295469/democrat-kevin-schreiber-wins-95th-state-house-seat|accessdate=8 September 2013|newspaper=York Daily Record|date=22 May 2013[/tpl]

==Controversy==

In the 2013 special election, criticism arose when it was learned that some of Schrieber's campaign volunteers had made anti-gay comments about his openly gay Republican opponent, Brian Tate.
