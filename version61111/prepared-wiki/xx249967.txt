[[German Reformed Sanctity Church Parsonage]]

CATEGORIES: Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1767, Houses in Columbia County, New York, Clergy houses in the United States

German Reformed Sanctity Church Parsonage is a historic church parsonage on Maple Avenue in Germantown, Columbia County, New York.  The parsonage is a rectangular building composed of two square sections with gable roofs. The earliest section was built about 1746 and the second in about 1767.  It is built of fieldstone.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1411|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: German Reformed Sanctity Church Parsonage|date=July 1975|accessdate=2010-06-26 |author=James Goche and Lynn A. Beebe|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1412|title=Accompanying three photos[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1976.
