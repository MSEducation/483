[tpl]Nihongo|Eiichi Ohtaki|大瀧 詠一|Ōtaki Eiichi[/tpl] (July 28, 1948 – December 30, 2013) was a Japanese musician, singer-songwriter and record producer. He first became known as a member of the influential rock band Happy End, but was better known for his solo work.

==Biography==

Ohtaki was born in Esashi District, what is now part of Ōshū. His 1981 album A Long Vacation is particularly well-known and highly acclaimed. It was the first Japanese album to be released on CD, was named "Best Album" of the year at the 23rd Japan Record Awards, certified double platinum by the RIAJ and has been re-released in 20th anniversary and 30th anniversary editions. In 2007, it was named the 7th greatest Japanese rock album of all time by Rolling Stone Japan; the list was topped by Happy End's Kazemachi Roman.[tpl]cite web |title=Finally! "The 100 Greatest Japanese Rock Albums of All Time" Listed |work=Exclaim! |url=http://exclaim.ca/News/finally_100_greatest_japanese_rock_albums_of_all_time |accessdate=2012-08-18[/tpl]
After choking on an apple and collapsing in his Tokyo home at 5 p.m. on December 30, 2013, Ohtaki was rushed to hospital but died shortly afterwards.[tpl]cite web|title=大滝詠一さん急死　リンゴのどに詰まらせ(tr: Otaki Eiichi's Sudden Death, Chokes on Apple)|url=http://www.nikkansports.com/entertainment/news/f-et-tp0-20131231-1238243.html|work=Nikkan Sports[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=ミュージシャンの大滝詠一さんが死去 (tr: Otaki Eiichi's musicians death)|url=http://www3.nhk.or.jp/news/html/20131231/k10014215111000.html|work=NHK News (in Japanese)[/tpl]http://exclaim.ca/News/happy_ends_eiichi_ohtaki_dies_at_65 His official cause of death was a dissecting aneurysm.[tpl]cite news|title=Singer-songwriter Eiichi Ohtaki dies after collapsing at home|url=http://www.japantoday.com/category/entertainment/view/singer-songwriter-eiichi-ohtaki-dies-after-collapsing-at-home|accessdate=3 January 2014|newspaper=Japan Today|date=December 31, 2013|month=December|year=2013[/tpl]
