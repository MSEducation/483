[[Martin Freedman]]

CATEGORIES: Judges in Manitoba, University of Manitoba alumni, Living people, Year of birth missing (living people)

Martin H. Freedman, Q.C., was appointed a judge of the Manitoba Court of Appeal on July 16, 2002 and retired from the court in 2012.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mhs.mb.ca/docs/people/manitobajudges.shtml |title=Judges of Manitoba |author= |date= |work= |publisher=Manitoba Historical Society |accessdate=6 February 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite press release |title=Russel Metals Inc.: Martin H. Freedman Appointed to Manitoba Court Of Appeal. |url=http://goliath.ecnext.com/coms2/gi_0199-2026810/Russel-Metals-Inc-Martin-H.html |publisher=Business Wire |date=22 July 2002 |accessdate=2011-02-06[/tpl]  He replaced Madam Justice Bonnie M. Helper, who resigned.
Mr. Justice Freedman received a Bachelor of Laws from the University of Manitoba in 1963 and won the Gold Medal in Law from both the University of Manitoba and the Law Society of Manitoba.[tpl]cite news |title=A lawyer questions the corporate elite |author=Anthea Finlay |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=AkQ_AAAAIBAJ&sjid=6lEMAAAAIBAJ&pg=4587,1334983 |newspaper=Windsor Star |date=4 June 1976 |accessdate=6 February 2011[/tpl]  At the time of his appointment, he was a partner with the Winnipeg law firm of Aikins MacAulay & Thorvaldson. He had served as the firm's managing partner from 1991 until 2001.  Throughout his career, he acquired a vast expertise in corporate-commercial law and arbitration-mediation matters.  In recent years, Mr. Justice Freedman had concentrated his practice on arbitration, especially in labour-related disputes in different jurisdictions throughout Canada.
Over the course of his legal career, Mr. Justice Freedman was very active in a wide range of professional organizations, including having served as President (1978–1979) and Bencher (1971–1979) of the Law Society of Manitoba and as a Director of the Federation of Law Societies of Canada (1981–1983).  For 20 years, he was a lecturer at the Faculty of Law at the University of Manitoba and a frequent presenter at numerous professional and continuing legal education seminars.  Mr. Justice Freedman was appointed Queen's Counsel in 1975.
His late father, Samuel Freedman, also served as a judge of the Manitoba Court of Appeal (1960–1983).
