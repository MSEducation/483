[[Leader Sewing Machine]]

CATEGORIES: Sewing machine brands

The Leader Sewing Machine Company produced sewing machines from 1870 to 1899 or there about.[tpl]cite web|title=Sewing machines|url=http://www.sil.si.edu/DigitalCollections/Trade-Literature/Sewing-Machines/CF/single-record.cfm?AuthorizedCompany=Leader%20Sewing%20Machine%20Company|work=Historical trade literature in the Smithsonian Collection|publisher=Leader Sewing Machine Company|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] 
Later overlockers were made under that brand though they may have been made at a different company to the first one.  At least some of the overlockers including a 4 thread model (DC-647) were shipped from China.
The base of operations shows as 479 to 497 Case Avenue, Cleaveland, Ohio [tpl]cite web|last=Best|first=David|title=A-Z of American Sewing Machine Manufacturers|url=http://www.sewmuse.co.uk/american%20sewing%20machine%20manufacturers.htm#l|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] and 1115 Olive Street, St Louis, Montana USA on some 1884 and other Victorian trade cards and 1885 envelopes traded on eBay.
The A. G. Mason Manufacturing Company manufactured a sewing machine designated the "Leader" amongst others in their line up of Florence, Crown and New Century, there is no known connection to the Leader Sewing Machine Company.[tpl]cite web|title=Leader sewing machine made by the A. G. Mason Manufacturing Company|url=http://www.thetreadlersvillage.com/LeaderSewingMachine-A.G.Mason.html|publisher=The Treadlers Village|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl]
