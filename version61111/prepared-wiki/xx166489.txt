[[SS Papoose]]

CATEGORIES: Tankers of the United States, Ships built in Los Angeles, California, 1921 ships, Tankers of the Netherlands, World War II tankers of the United States, Ships sunk by German submarines in World War II, Shipwrecks of the Carolina coast, World War II shipwrecks in the Atlantic Ocean, Oil tankers, Maritime incidents in 1926, Maritime incidents in 1942, Ships sunk in collisions

SS ''Papoose was an oil tanker built in 1921 by the Southwestern Shipbuilding & Drydock Company[tpl]cite web | url=http://shipbuildinghistory.com/history/shipyards/2large/inactive/bethsanpedro.htm | title=Southwestern Shipbuilding & Drydock Company | work=shipbuildinghistory.com | date=27 April 2011 | accessdate=11 December 2012 | author=Colton, Tim[/tpl] in San Pedro, California as 'SS Silvanus'[tpl]cite web |url=http://uwex.us/papoose.html |title=The Wreck of the Papoose |author=Barnette, Michael C. |year=2007 |publisher=Association of Underwater Explorers |accessdate=2008-09-06 |authorlink=Michael C. Barnette [/tpl] for the Nederlandsch-Indische Tankstoomboot Maatschappij ("Dutch-Indies Steam Tanker Company").[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.helderline.nl/tanker/855/silvanus/ | title=Silvanus | work=helderline.nl | accessdate=11 December 2012[/tpl] In 1926 Silvanus collided with the tanker [tpl]SS|Thomas H. Wheeler||2[/tpl] in the Mississippi River, resulting in the death of 26 seamen. The Silvanus'' was declared a total loss and rebuilt in Beaumont, Texas.
It started operating as the Papoose for the Petroleum Navigation Company in Houston, Texas in March 1927. In March 1942, it was attacked by German U-boat [tpl]GS|U-124|1940|2[/tpl] off the coast of Cape Lookout, North Carolina. It drifted for several days and eventually sank in [tpl]convert|200|ft|m[/tpl] of water off Oregon Inlet, North Carolina.
