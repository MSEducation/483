[[Cauquenes Province]]

CATEGORIES: Provinces of Maule Region, Provinces of Chile

Cauquenes Province ([tpl]lang-es|Provincia de Cauquenes[/tpl]) is one of four provinces of the central Chilean region of Maule (VII). The provincial capital is the city of Cauquenes.

==Geography and demography==

The provincial capital, Cauquenes, lies  approximately [tpl]convert|370|km|0|abbr=on[/tpl] southwest of Santiago. According to the 2002 census by the National Statistics Institute (INE), the province spans an area of [tpl]convert|3027.2|sqkm|0|abbr=on[/tpl] and had a population of 57,088 inhabitants (28,356 men and 28,732 women), giving it a population density of [tpl]convert|18.9|PD/sqkm|0|abbr=on[/tpl]. Of these, 38,660 (67.7%) lived in urban areas and 18,428 (32.3%) in rural areas. Between the 1992 and 2002 censuses, the population grew by 3.3% (1,846 persons).

==Administration==

As a province, Cachapoal is a second-level administrative division of Chile, governed by a provincial governor who is appointed by the president.

===Communes===

The province is composed of three communes, each governed by a municipality consisting of an alcalde and municipal council: Cauquenes, Chanco and Pelluhue.
