[[Sydney Pope]]

Air Commodore Sydney Leo Gregory ("Poppy") Pope, [tpl]post-nominals|country=GBR-cats|CBE|MC|DFC|AFC[/tpl] (born Ireland 27 March 1898 - died 5 November 1980) was a World War I flying ace credited with six aerial victories.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/ireland/pope.php Retrieved 18 February 2010. He made the Royal Air Force his career, retiring in 1946 after serving in Bomber Command's  B5 Group[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 306 [/tpl]

==World War I==

Pope joined the Inns of Court Army Officer Training Corps as a lance corporal in 1916 and was commissioned a second lieutenant in May 1916.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/ireland/pope.php Retrieved 18 February 2010. He was assigned to 60 Squadron in Bloody April 1917, where he originally flew a Nieuport fighter. Pope scored twice with a Nieuport, on 8 and 20 June 1917. He then upgraded to a Royal Aircraft Factory SE.5 and destroyed an Albatros D.III on 16 September. Switching to a slightly improved SE.5a, he destroyed two reconnaissance planes on 8 November and a D.III on the 11th. One week later, he was wounded and forced to land near Saint Julien[tpl]disambiguation needed|date=November 2012[/tpl]. He ended the war with a Military Cross.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 306 [/tpl]

==Postwar service==

He served in Iraq and Egypt. In 1932, he commanded 54 Squadron. He was awarded an Air Force Cross. By 1939, he was in command of RAF Debden as a Wing Commander. He ended his career as an Air Commodore.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 306 [/tpl]
