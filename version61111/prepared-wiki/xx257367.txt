[[Thoma Orollogaj]]

CATEGORIES: Albanian politicians, People from Korçë, 1947 deaths, 1888 births, Balli Kombëtar

Thoma Orollogaj (1888–1947) was an Albanian jurist, politician and resistance fighter of World War II.

==Life==

Born in 1888 in Korçë, Ottoman Empire (modern southern Albania) he studied law in Athens and Paris.[tpl]cite book|last=Neuwirth|first=Hubert|title=Widerstand und Kollaboration in Albanien 1939-1944|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Qa97yONE4IwC&pg=PA274|accessdate=30 December 2010|date=2008-09-09|publisher=Otto Harrassowitz Verlag|language=German|isbn=978-3-447-05783-7|page=174[/tpl] After finishing his studies he returned to Korçë, where he worked as a lawyer and supported Kostaq Kota's efforts for independence of the area from French rule.[tpl]cite book|last=Schmidt-Neke|first=Michael|title=Enstehung und Ausbau der Königsdiktatur in Albanien, 1912-1939|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=vpIFsca3lKYC&pg=PA348|accessdate=30 December 2010|year=1987|publisher=Oldenbourg Wissenschaftsverlag|language=German|isbn=978-3-486-54321-6|page=348[/tpl] In 1927 he became a member of the law reform committee of Albania and a professor of law at the school of law of Tiranë. 
In 1931 he became the president of the civil code reforms committee, while a year later he was elected deputy of Korçë in the Albanian parliament. During Kostaq Kota's second premiership he became minister of justice. After Albania's occupation by Italy during WWII he joined Balli Kombëtar and was elected a member of its central committee. In 1943 he was a representative of Balli Kombëtar at the Mukje Agreement. In 1944 he became for the second time minister of justice of Albania in the first government after the German defeat. After the consolidation of power by the Party of Labour of Albania, he was accused as a collaborationist and executed in September 1947.
