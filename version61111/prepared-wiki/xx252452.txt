Vilayattasseri Mullambalath Balachandran (25 November 1924 – 9 January 2010), known as Vimcy, was a sports writer from Kerala. He is considered to be the pioneer of sports journalism in Kerala.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.mathrubhumi.com/story.php?id=76672|title=കുലഗുരുവിന് പ്രണാമം |date=9 Jan 2010|publisher=Mathrubhumi|language=Malayalam|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] He won numerous awards during his journalistic career spanning more than half a century.

==Biography==

Balachandran was born on 25 November 1924 at Thamarassery in Kozhikode district to Dr. Narayanan Nair and Narayani. Unable to finish his intermediate education at Palakkad Victoria College due to ill health, he joined the Kozhikode newspaper Dinaprabha in 1949 as a journalist. He used to write articles in Sanjayan and Viswaroopam under the pen-name of Thampi.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.manoramaonline.com/cgi-bin/MMOnline.dll/portal/ep/malayalamContentView.do?articleType=Malayalam+News&contentId=6549801&contentType=EDITORIAL&BV_ID=@@@|title=വിംസീ അന്തരിച്ചു|publisher=Malayala Manorama|language=Malayalam|date=10 January 2010|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] In 1950, he became the assistant editor at Mathrubhumi. After his retirement from Mathrubhumi in 1984, he became associate editor for Calicut Times. He continued to write sports columns for Mathrubhumi, Calicut Times and Madhyamam. His autobiography titled Valkkashnam was published in 2008.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.mathrubhumi.com/php/featureDetails.php?general_links_id=8&feature_category_id=1261&general_ns_dt=10-01-10&general_archive_display=yes&Farc=|title=കളിയെ ഉപാസിച്ച ജീവിതം (Editorial)|publisher=Mathrubhumi|language=Malayalam|date=10 January 2010|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] Vimcy died on 9 January 2010 at Bilathikulam in Kozhikode.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/national/kerala/article78001.ece|title=Veteran sports scribe Vimcy passes away|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.expressbuzz.com/edition/story.aspx?Title=Vimci+passes+away&artid=eFxac58Ntdc=|title=Vimci passes away|publisher=The New Indian Express|accessdate=19 January 2010[/tpl]

===Family===

Balachandran was married to Amminiyamma and had three children – Unnikrishnan, Vijayakrishnan, and Meenakshi Sadanandan.

==Sports journalism==

At a time when Malayalam newspapers dedicated little space to sports news, Vimcy started an entire sports page with Mathrubhumi.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.metrovaartha.com/2010/01/09103955/VIMCY.html|title=വിംസി അന്തരിച്ചു|publisher=Metro Vartha|language=Malayalam|date=9 January 2010|accessdate=24 January 2010[/tpl] It was under him that sports writing became a separate branch of journalism in Kerala. Vimcy was also behind the weekly sports feature page Sports Special in Mathrubhumi. He used to write extensively about Football.
Vimcy won numerous awards including one from Kerala State Sports council for lifelong contributions to the field of sports journalism.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.madhyamamonline.com/story/%E0%B4%95%E0%B4%B3%E0%B4%BF%E0%B4%AF%E0%B5%86%E0%B4%B4%E0%B5%81%E0%B4%A4%E0%B5%8D%E0%B4%A4%E0%B5%81%E0%B4%95%E0%B4%BE%E0%B4%B0%E0%B4%A8%E0%B5%8D%E2%80%8D-%E0%B4%B5%E0%B4%BF%E0%B4%82%E0%B4%B8%E0%B4%BF-%E0%B4%85%E0%B4%A8%E0%B5%8D%E0%B4%A4%E0%B4%B0%E0%B4%BF%E0%B4%9A%E0%B5%8D%E0%B4%9A%E0%B5%81|title=കളിയെഴുത്തുകാരന്‍ വിംസി അന്തരിച്ചു|date=January 9, 2010|publisher=Madhyamam|language=Malayalam|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] Other awards included M.P. Paily award, Neelambaran Memorial award (from Kesari Memorial Committee), T. Aboobacker award and Press Academy Silver Jubilee award.
