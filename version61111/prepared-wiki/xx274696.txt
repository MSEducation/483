[[Haskell House (New Windsor, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Historic American Buildings Survey in New York, Houses completed in 1724, Buildings and structures in Orange County, New York

John Haskell House, also known as The Hermitage, was a historic home located near New Windsor in Orange County, New York.  It was built about 1724, and was a 2 1/2-story log dwelling with a rear ell.  It had a gable roof and large interior chimney at each end.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6565|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: John Haskell House|date=undated|accessdate=2009-10-31 |author=Steven S. Levy|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6572|title=Accompanying three photos[/tpl] 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1973.  The building was  demolished sometime after 2009.
