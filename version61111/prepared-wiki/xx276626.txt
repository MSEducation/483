[[Mickey Rottner]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, 2011 deaths, Basketball players from Illinois, Chicago Stags players, Guards (basketball), Jewish American sportspeople, Jewish basketball players, Loyola Ramblers men's basketball players, Sheboygan Red Skins players, Sportspeople from Chicago, Illinois, Undrafted National Basketball Association players

Marvin "Mickey" Rottner (March 23, 1919 – September 21, 2011) was an American basketball player.  He played three seasons of professional basketball in the years leading up to the formation of the National Basketball Association.
Rottner, a 5'10" guard from Tuley High School in Chicago, played collegiately at Loyola University in Chicago.  He played for the Ramblers from 1939 to 1942 and was later named to the school's athletics hall of fame.Loyola Mourns The Loss Of Mickey Rottner, accessed September 26, 2011
Following his college career, Rottner played professionally, first in the National Basketball League (NBL) for the Sheboygan Red Skins in 1945–46.  He averaged four points per game for the Red Skins as they made it all the way to the NBL finals, where they lost to the Rochester Royals.'Mickey' Rottner, former Red Skins player, dies, accessed September 26, 2011  Rottner then moved to the Basketball Association of America (BAA) to play for the Chicago Stags for two seasons.  He averaged 5.4 points and 1.4 assists for the Stags in 100 games.Mickey Rottner basketballreference.com profile, accessed September 26, 2011
Rottner died September 21, 2011.
