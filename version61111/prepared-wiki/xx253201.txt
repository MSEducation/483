[[Sulcus subtarsalis]]

CATEGORIES: Human eye anatomy

Sulcus subtarsalis is a groove in the innersurface of eyelid near the eyelid margin and which is also parallel to it.[tpl]cite web|title=Conjuctiva|url=http://www.eophtha.com/eophtha/anatomyofconjunctiva.html|publisher=Online Resources of Ophthalmology|accessdate=19 June 2013[/tpl] This separates marginal conjunctiva from tarsal conjunctiva. It provides space for lodging of foreign bodies. Arlt's line is a linear line present in the sulcus subtarsalis in Chlamydia trachomatis infection.[tpl]cite book|last=Khurana|first=A. K.|title=Comprehensive Ophthalmology|year=2008|publisher=Anshan Publishers|location=Kent, UK|isbn=978-1905740789|page=65|edition=4[/tpl]
