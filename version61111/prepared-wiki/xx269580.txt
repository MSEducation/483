[[Bertha Bay-Sa Pan]]

CATEGORIES: American film directors, American people of Taiwanese descent, Living people, American people of Chinese descent

Bertha Bay-Sa Pan is an American Director, Writer and Producer. Born in New Jersey and raised in Taiwan, Pan was educated at Boston University (B.A. in Film & Art) and the Columbia University Graduate Film School in 1997 [tpl]cite web|author=By David Edelstein |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2005/03/20/movies/20edel.html |title=The New York Times > Movies > The Clash of China's Generations, Set to a Hip-Hop Beat |publisher=Nytimes.com |date= |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl] receiving a Masters of Fine Arts degree in Directing, while working as a Sales Executive in Film Distribution. Pan’s graduate thesis short film at Columbia University, entitled "Face," garnered various awards from Film Festivals worldwide, including the Director's Guild Award for Best Asian American Student Filmmaker and the Polo Ralph Lauren Award for Best Screenplay.[tpl]cite web|author=September 22, 2010 |url=http://columbiafocus.org/site/profiles/bbsp/ |title=Bertha Bay-Sa Pan (Writer/Director/Producer) » FOCUS - Filmmakers of Color United in Spirit |publisher=Columbiafocus.org |date=2010-09-22 |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl]

==Films==

Pan’s first feature film was titled Face (like her award-winning graduate thesis short film) – which stars Kristy Wu, Bai Ling, Kieu Chinh, Ken Leung, Treach, Will Yun Lee and Golden Globe Award and Emmy Award nominee Tina Chen, Premiered at the 2002 Sundance Film Festival in the Dramatic Competition, co-written by Oscar nominee Oren Moverman and featured a score by Leonard Nelson Hubbard of The Roots. “Face” obtained the CRITICS AWARD for Best Director at the CineVegas Film Festival,The Audience Award at the GenArt Film Festival, and the Grand Jury Award for Best Director at the Urbanworld Film Festival. Pan was nominated for the prestigious Open Palm at the GOTHAM AWARDS, and awarded the PREMIO SPECIALE PRIZE at the International Women’s Film Festival in Turin, Italy. “Face” was released theatrically in 2005, and received glowing reviews from major publications including The New York Times (as a New York Times Critics Pick [tpl]cite web|last=Stevens |first=Dana |url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/261741/Face/overview |title=Face - Trailer - Cast - Showtimes - NYTimes.com |publisher=Movies.nytimes.com |date=2010-11-01 |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl]), Entertainment Weekly, The Hollywood Reporter, TV Guide, The San Francisco Chronicle, The Star Ledger, The Chicago Tribune, and the BBC, and is currently available on DVD and cable TV.[tpl]cite web|last=Stevens |first=Dana |url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/261741/Face/overview |title=Face - Trailer - Cast - Showtimes - NYTimes.com |publisher=Movies.nytimes.com |date=2010-11-01 |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl]
Pan's Second Feature, Almost Perfect stars Kelly Hu, Edison Chen, Ivan Shaw, Tina Chen, Christina Chang, and Tony Award winner Roger Rees. It screened at various Festivals including The San Francisco International Asian American Film Festival, The Los Angeles Asian Pacific Film Festival (as Centerpiece Film), Hawaii International Film Festival (as American Immigrant Filmmaker in Profile), Friars Club Comedy Film Festival, and the San Diego Asian Film Festival (as Opening Night Film), amongst others.,[tpl]cite web|last=Tofu |first=Stinky |url=http://taiwaneseamerican.org/ta/2011/04/24/interview-with-bertha-bay-sa-pan-director-of-%E2%80%9Calmost-perfect%E2%80%9D/ |title=Interview with Bertha Bay-Sa Pan, Director of "Almost Perfect" |publisher=Taiwaneseamerican.org |date= |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl] Pan also received the Philadelphia Asian American Film Festival's HBO EMERGING FILMMAKER AWARD. The film had a limited U.S. theatrical release in 2012,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt1249158/awards |title=Almost Perfect |publisher=IMDB[/tpl] garnering positive reviews from Variety, San Francisco Examiner, The Honolulu Pulse, etc.[tpl]cite web|url=http://richgirlproductions.com/?p=2327 |title=Almost Perfect-Bertha Bay-Sa Pan |publisher=richgirlproductions.com |date=2012-09-26[/tpl]

==Short Films and Music Videos==

Pan’s graduate thesis short film, which she did at Columbia University was entitled Face like her first feature film, and was awarded a number of accolades, including the Director's Guild Award for Best Asian American Student Filmmaker and the Polo Ralph Lauren Award for Best Screenplay.[tpl]cite web|author=September 22, 2010 |url=http://columbiafocus.org/site/profiles/bbsp/ |title=Bertha Bay-Sa Pan (Writer/Director/Producer) » FOCUS - Filmmakers of Color United in Spirit |publisher=Columbiafocus.org |date=2010-09-22 |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl]
Pan has also directed a short film entitled "Sluggers", and produced feature films such as Fighting Fish.
