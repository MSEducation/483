[[Clausidium vancouverense]]

CATEGORIES: Poecilostomatoida, Crustaceans of the eastern Pacific Ocean, Animals described in 1912

[tpl]Italic title[/tpl]

Clausidium vancouverense, the red copepod, is a symbiont of the ghost shrimp Neotrypaea californiensis.[tpl]cite journal |author=A. D. Humes |year=1949 |title=A new copepod (Cyclopoida: Clausidiidae) parasitic on mud shrimps in Louisiana |journal=Transactions of the American Microscopical Society |volume=68 |pages=93–103 |jstor=3223256 |pmid=18153316[/tpl] It is one of six species in the genus Clausidium and is found with its host in the Pacific Ocean from Alaska to Baja California.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mbayaq.org/efc/living_species/default.asp?hOri=0&hab=3&inhab=456 |publisher=Monterey Bay Aquarium |title=Online Field Guide |accessdate=May 23, 2008[/tpl]

==Description==

Females range in length from [tpl]convert|1.086|-|1.222|sigfig=2|mm[/tpl]. The female is larger than the male, and a female sometimes carries a male attached to her dorsal thorax.[tpl]cite journal |author=Charles Branch Wilson |year=1935 |title=Parasitic copepods from the Pacific coast |journal=American Midland Naturalist |volume=16 |pages=776–797 |jstor=2420107[/tpl]
Several features separate this copepod from other species in its genus. These include the blade-like seta on the first leg and the small lateral hairs on the setae of the fifth leg on females. In males, the structure of the maxilliped is unique.
