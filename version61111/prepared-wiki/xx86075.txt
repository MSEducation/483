[[Robin Hull]]

CATEGORIES: 1974 births, Living people, Finnish snooker players, Finnish people of English descent

Robin Hull (born 16 August 1974 in Finland) is a Finnish professional snooker player. For some time he was the sole Nordic player on the game's main tour. Hull was born and raised in Finland, to a Finnish mother and English father."Snooker: Contenders wait on O'Sullivan's shoulder" (The Independent)

==Career==

He qualified for the World Championship in 2002, knocking out Steve Davis in the final qualifying round. In the first round proper, he lost 6–10 to Graeme Dott. He has twice reached ranking event quarter-finals, the 2003 Welsh Open"Snooker: Hull conquers Higgins" (The Independent) and the 2006 Malta Cup.
Hull is a solid [tpl]Cuegloss|Break|break[/tpl]-builder and has compiled 115 competitive [tpl]Cuegloss|Century|centuries[/tpl] during his career: as of 23 October 2011, this is the 3rd highest amount for a player who has never featured in the elite top 16, behind Andy Hicks (134) and Dominic Dale (117). However, he once missed the final [tpl]Cuegloss|Black ball|black[/tpl] during a 147 attempt.European Billiards & Snooker Association Player Profile: Robin Hull[tpl]dead link|date=August 2010[/tpl]
A potentially-fatal viral infection kept him out of much of the 2003/2004 season. He was forced to pull out of qualifying for the 2007 World Championship, due to an irregular heartbeat thought to be linked to his past illness.World Snooker News: Hull withdraws from Prestatyn qualifiers  After a similar problem in 2008, he decided to retire from professional competition.BBC Sport: Ailing Hull quits snooker circuit
Hull returned to competition in 2011, taking part in the Q School tournaments as he attempted to qualify for the 2011/2012 main tour. He qualified for the main tour as one of the semi-finalists from the first Q School event.[tpl]cite web|title=Hull Back On Pro Tour|url=http://www.worldsnooker.com/page/NewsArticles/0,,13165~2361110,00.html|work=worldsnooker.com|publisher=World Professional Billiards and Snooker Association[/tpl] He only played in a handful of events during the season with his best run coming in qualifying for the UK Championship in November where he beat Lucky Vatnani and Yu Delu, before losing to Peter Lines 4–6.[tpl]cite web | url=http://snooker.org/res/index.asp?player=549&season=2011 | title=Robin Hull 2011/2012 | publisher=Snooker.org | accessdate=24 May 2012[/tpl] Hull did not enter another tournament after this and finished the season ranked world number 84, outside of the top 64 who retain their places for the 2012/2013 season and therefore did not retain his spot on the main tour.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.worldsnooker.com/staticFiles/1d/b0/0,,13165~176157,00.pdf | title=Official World Ranking List for the 2012/2013 Season | accessdate=24 May 2012[/tpl] In the 2012/2013 season Hull entered qualifying for the World Championship as an amateur, where he lost in the first round of preliminary qualifying 2–5 to Paul Wykes, despite making a 137 break during the match.[tpl]cite web | url=http://snooker.org/res/index.asp?season=2012&player=549 | title=Robin Hull 2012/2013 | publisher=Snooker.org | accessdate=5 April 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://livescores.worldsnookerdata.com/Matches/Result/13299?matchId=171209 | title=Betfair World Championship Pre-Qualifiers | publisher=World Snooker | accessdate=5 April 2013[/tpl]
Hull regained his main tour place for the 2013/2014 season by winning the EBSA European Snooker Championships in Zielona Góra, Poland, beating Welshman Gareth Allen 7–2 in the final, finishing the match with two consecutive centuries.[tpl]cite web|title=European Snooker Championships Men – Zielona Góra/Poland 2013 – knockout results|url=http://esnooker.pl/turnieje/2013/me/en/show_drabinka.php?id_t=10|publisher=European Billiards and Snooker Association|accessdate=6 June 2013[/tpl] He qualified for the 2013 International Championship by beating Liu Chuang 6–2, although he had to withdraw from the venue stages in China, and came close to beat the reigning world champion Ronnie O'Sullivan in the last 64 of the minor-ranking Kay Suzanne Memorial Cup, losing 3–4 after leading 3–1. However, Hull failed to win any other match at the rest of the tournaments, and due to financial reasons skipped most of the second part of the season until the World Championship, where he delivered his best performance in years. He scored an impressive 10–3 win against Tony Drago in the first round, followed by a 10–6 defeat of Tian Pengfei in round 2, and a 10–4 win from 0–3 behind against Ian Burns in round 3, to set up a round 4 match against Peter Ebdon, which Hull won 10–8 to reach the Crucible for the second time in his career. He played Ronnie O'Sullivan in the last 32 and lost 10–4, despite making a century in one of the frames.
