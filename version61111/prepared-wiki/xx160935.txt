[[Benning M. Bean]]

CATEGORIES: 1782 births, 1866 deaths, People from Moultonborough, New Hampshire, People from Carroll County, New Hampshire, New Hampshire Jacksonians, Members of the United States House of Representatives from New Hampshire, Members of the New Hampshire House of Representatives, New Hampshire State Senators, Members of the New Hampshire Executive Council, Presidents of the New Hampshire Senate

Benning Moulton Bean (January 9, 1782 – February 6, 1866) was an American farmer and politician. He served as a United States Representative from New Hampshire, as a member of the New Hampshire Senate, and as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives.

Born in Moultonborough, New Hampshire, Bean was the son of Moody Bean.[tpl]cite book|last=Metcalf|first=Henry Harrison and McClintock, John Norris|title=The Granite Monthly: A New Hampshire Magazine Devoted to History, Biography, Literature, and State Progress, Volume 5|year=1882|publisher=H.H. ==Political career==

He served as Selectman for Moultonborough from 1811–1829 and from 1832–1838.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.moultonboroughhistory.org/MHS%20Articles%20&%20Tidbits/Articles/MHS%206th%20Grade%20Essays/The%20History%20of%20Moultonborough%201600%20-%201930.pdf|title= Bean Cemetery|publisher= Moultonborough Historical Society|accessdate= January 21, 2014[/tpl] He was Justice of the Peace in 1816,[tpl]cite web|url= http://archive.org/stream/newhampshireann_1828farm/newhampshireann_1828farm_djvu.txt|title= The New-Hampshire annual register|publisher=The New-Hampshire annual register, and United States calendar|accessdate= January 21, 2014[/tpl] and trustee of Sandwich Academy in 1824.[tpl]cite book|last=Parker|first=Benjamin Franklin|title=History of Wolfeborough (New Hampshire)|year=1901|publisher=Press of Caustic & Claflin|page=406|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=tObqwKRR5yMC&pg=PA405&dq=benning+m+bean+trustee+of+Sandwich+Academy&hl=en&sa=X&ei=YVHfUpC0EtPjsASdqILIDw&ved=0CC8Q6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=benning%20m%20bean%20trustee%20of%20Sandwich%20Academy&f=false[/tpl] A member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives from 1815–1823, Bean also served in the New Hampshire Senate from 1824–1826.[tpl]cite book|last=United States. Government Printing Office|title=Congressional serial set|year=1918|publisher=United States. In 1829, he was member of the Governor's council.[tpl]cite book|last=Merrill|first=Georgia Drew|title=History of Carroll County, New Hampshire|year=1889|publisher=W.A. Bean served in the New Hampshire Senate again in 1831 and 1832, and was president of the State Senate in 1832.[tpl]cite web|title=Benning Moulton Bean|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=nQE2AQAAMAAJ&pg=PA419&lpg=PA419&dq=Benning+Moulton+Bean&source=bl&ots=ZGuTe0F-Mk&sig=A6rsrIP9uUI0jYaIvfFpcjWN6rE&hl=en&sa=X&ei=-SHcUvLvDcK-sQStmIGgDQ&ved=0CEMQ6AEwBg#v=onepage&q=Benning%20Moulton%20Bean&f=false|publisher=History of Carroll County, New Hampshire|accessdate=19 January 2014[/tpl] He was elected as a Jacksonian candidate to the Twenty-third and Twenty-fourth Congresses, serving in Congress March 4, 1833 – March 3, 1837. During his time in Congress, Bean was an advocate for temperance and helped form the Congressional Temperance Society with George N. Briggs in 1833. He served as president of the society.[tpl]cite book|last=Merrill|first=Georgia Drew|title=History of Carroll County, New Hampshire|year=1889|publisher=W.A. ==Death==

Bean died in Moultonborough, Carroll County, New Hampshire on February 6, 1866 (age 84 years, 28 days). He is interred at Bean Cemetery in Moultonborough.[tpl]cite book|last=Spencer|first=Thomas E.|title=Where They're Buried: A Directory Containing More Than Twenty Thousand Names of Notable Persons Buried in American Cemeteries, with Listings of Many Prominent People who Were Cremated|year=1998|publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com|page=221|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=eLWao2lIGTEC&pg=PA221&dq=Benning+Moulton+Bean+interred+Bean+Cemetery&hl=en&sa=X&ei=7FbdUsWXMOe0sATrr4HQCA&ved=0CEkQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Benning%20Moulton%20Bean%20interred%20Bean%20Cemetery&f=false[/tpl] 

==Personal life==

On May 31, 1812, Bean married Eliza Ramsey. They had one daughter who died in childhood. After Eliza's death, he married Lydia Adams on October 30, 1817. They had six sons and four daughters together, including John Q. A. Bean, Benjamin F. Bean, A. A. Bean, George L. Bean, William E. Bean and Hannah J. Bean.[tpl]cite book|last=Metcalf|first=Henry Harrison and McClintock, John Norris|title=The Granite Monthly: A New Hampshire Magazine Devoted to History, Biography, Literature, and State Progress, Volume 5|year=1882|publisher=H.H.
