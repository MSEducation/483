[[Coming Street Cemetery]]

CATEGORIES: Cemeteries on the National Register of Historic Places in South Carolina, Jewish cemeteries in South Carolina, Geography of Charleston, South Carolina, Cemeteries in South Carolina, 1762 establishments in the Thirteen Colonies, National Register of Historic Places in Charleston, South Carolina

The Coming Street Cemetery is located at 189 Coming Street, in Charleston, South Carolina.[tpl]Cite web | last = Ziff | first = Stephen | coauthors = | title = Coming Street Cemetery| work = National Register of Historic Places - Nomination and Inventory | date = December 6, 1995 | url = http://www.nationalregister.sc.gov/charleston/S10817710163/S10817710163.pdf | format = pdf | accessdate = 11 June 2012[/tpl][tpl]Cite web | title = Coming Street Cemetery, Charleston County (189 Coming St., Charleston) | work = National Register Properties in South Carolina | publisher = South Carolina Department of Archives and History | url = http://www.nationalregister.sc.gov/charleston/S10817710163/index.htm | accessdate = 11 June 2012[/tpl] This Jewish cemetery, one of the oldest in the United States was founded in 1762 and is the oldest Jewish burial ground in the South. Burials in the Coming Street Cemetery are now restricted to the few vacancies in the adjacent family plots. The cemetery was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1996.
The Coming Street cemetery is a private burial ground owned by Kahal Kadosh Beth Elohim Synagogue and is not open to the public.
