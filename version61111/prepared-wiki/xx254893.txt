[[Carmelita Tropicana]]

CATEGORIES: American actresses, American people of Cuban descent, Lesbian actresses, Lesbian writers, LGBT writers from Cuba, Living people, Obie Award recipients, American writers of Cuban descent, LGBT writers from the United States

Alina Troyano, better known as Carmelita Tropicana, is a Cuban-American stage and film actress who lives and works in New York City. In 1999, she received an Obie Award for Sustained Excellence in Performance. She is the sister of the independent film director Ela Troyano, who directed Carmelita Tropicana: Your Kunst Is Your Waffen.[tpl]cite news|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/434320/Carmelita-Tropicana-Your-Kunst-Is-Your-Waffen/overview |title=Carmelita Tropicana: Your Kunst Is Your Waffen (1996) |work= The New York Times|accessdate=5 September 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9C0CE5DA1F39F93AA25754C0A960958260 |title=FILM REVIEW; A Lesbian Perspective |work= The New York Times|date=19 July 1996|first=Stephen|last=Holden|accessdate=5 September 2012[/tpl] Both form part of the alternative arts scene in the East Village and Lower East Side.
