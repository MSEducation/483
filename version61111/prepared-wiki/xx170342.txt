[[1950 Giro d'Italia]]

CATEGORIES: Giro d'Italia, 1950 in cycle racing, 1950 in Italian sport, 1950 in road cycling

The 1950 Giro d'Italia was the 33rd edition of the Giro d'Italia, one of cycling's Grand Tours. The Giro started off in Milan on 24 May with a [tpl]convert|225|km|1|abbr=on[/tpl] individual time trial and concluded in Salsomaggiore Terme with a [tpl]convert|230|km|1|abbr=on[/tpl] relatively flat mass-start stage on 13 June. Fifteen teams entered the race, which was won by Swiss Hugo Koblet of the Guerra team. Second and third respectively were Italians Gino Bartali and Alfredo Martini.[tpl]cite news|url=http://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/1950/06/14/pagina-1/666377/pdf.html#|title=El suizo Koblet|language=Spanish|date=14 June 1950|page=1|publisher=El Mundo Deportivo|accessdate=27 May 2012|trans_title=The Swiss Koblet|location=Rome, Italy|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6Hldnd6qZ|archivedate=2013-06-30|deadurl=no[/tpl]

==Teams==

A total of 15 teams were invited to participate in the 1950 Giro d'Italia. Each team sent a squad of seven riders, so the Giro began with a peloton of 105 cyclists. Out of the 105 riders that started this edition of the Giro d'Italia, a total of 75 riders made it to the finish in Rome.
