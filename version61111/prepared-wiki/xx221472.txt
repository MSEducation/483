[[Arsène Millocheau]]

CATEGORIES: 1867 births, 1948 deaths, People from Eure-et-Loir, French male cyclists

Arsène Millocheau was a French cyclist of the early 1900s. He was born in Champseru in 1867.
He participated in the 1903 Tour de France, the first Tour, and came last, finishing behind the winner Maurice Garin by 64 hours, 57 minutes and 8 seconds, winning the Lanterne rouge ([tpl]lang-fr|red lantern[/tpl]).
In 1921, 54 years old, Millocheau competed in the 1200 km Paris–Brest–Paris.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lagrandeboucle.com/article.php3?id_article=1347|title=Millocheau pour l'honneur|publisher=La Grand Boucle|language=French|accessdate=21 July 2010[/tpl]
He died in Paris in 1948.
