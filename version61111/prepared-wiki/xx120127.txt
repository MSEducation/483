Logan Bailly (born 27 December 1985 in Liège) is a Belgian footballer who plays as a goalkeeper for Belgian club OH Leuven.

==Career==

He arrived in Borussia Mönchengladbach in 2009 from Genk, where he had played since 2002. For the duration of the 2003–04 season, Bailly was on loan at Beringen-Heusden-Zolder, along with ten other Genk players.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.uefa.com/memberassociations/news/newsid=74999.html | publisher = UEFA.com | title = Heusden-Zolder sign Genk eleven | date = 16 June 2003 | accessdate = 26 December 2010[/tpl]
Bailly replaced Jan Moons as Genk's first-choice goalkeeper at the beginning of the 2006–07 season; he played 90 minutes in every single Belgian League match for the club that season. He kept 14 clean sheets and received no bookings.[tpl]cite web | url = http://football.stats.com/belg/players.asp?player=42345 | title = Belgian League stats | publisher = football.stats.com | accessdate = 22 March 2010[/tpl] His good performance contributed to Genk's surprising second-place finish. He was transferred to Borussia Mönchengladbach during the winter of season 2008–09 and immediately adapted to the Bundesliga. He was elected best player of gameday 20 in the league.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.bundesliga.de/de/liga/news/2008/index.php?f=0000119725.php  | title = Bailly erobert die Herzen der Fans im Sturm  | trans_title = Bailly takes the supporters' hearts by storm | accessdate = 22 March 2010 | date = 18 February 2009 | language = German | publisher = bundesliga.de[/tpl] During the 2011–12 season he was initially loaned out to Swiss side Neuchâtel Xamax, for whom he never played a single match. In the second part of the season, he was loaned back to Genk. After the season he returned to Mönchengladbach where his contract was not renewed, allowing him to sign as a free agent player for Oud-Heverlee Leuven. At OHL, he was rejoined with coach Ronny Van Geneugden, who was his coach during his period as a youth player at Genk. After a series of good performances, Bailly was rewarded on 2 December with a one year extension, giving him a contract until the end of the 2013–14 season.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.sporza.be/permalink/1.1495563  | title = Bailly verlengt zijn contract bij OHL | trans_title = Bailly extends contract with OHL | accessdate = 3 December 2012 | date = 2 December 2012 | language = Dutch | publisher = sporza.be[/tpl]

==International career==

Bailly is a member of Belgium national team.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.uefa.com/competitions/euro/news/kind=1/newsid=516837.html | publisher = UEFA.com | title = Vandereycken names fresh faces[/tpl][tpl]Dead link|date=March 2010[/tpl] He was named in a provisional 30-man squad for the UEFA Under-21 Championship in 2007.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.uefa.com/under21/news/newsid=537673.html | publisher = UEFA.com | title = Belgium hope for better Kompany | date = 12 May 2007 | accessdate = 26 December 2010[/tpl] He also represented Belgium at the 2008 Olympic Games.
