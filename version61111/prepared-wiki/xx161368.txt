[[Henry Fisk Janes]]

Henry Fisk Janes (October 10, 1792 - June 6, 1879) was an American lawyer and politician. He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Biography==

Janes was born in  Brimfield, Massachusetts and moved with his parents to  Calais, Vermont where he pursued academic studies.  He served in the War of 1812 as an officer in Captain Gideon Wheelock's company of Vermont Militia, and participated in the Battle of Plattsburgh.[tpl]cite book|last=Lewis|first=Theodore Graham|title=History of Waterbury, Vermont, 1763-1915|year=1915|publisher=Record print|pages=64|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Qr5Byevb88oC&pg=PA64&lpg=PA64&dq=henry++janes+Brimfield,+Massachusetts&source=bl&ots=P_F2cdbgNc&sig=flB0qu8JUbKdYiOWBwg3QnEm-ts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=NlW6UO_uN8TLrQG5gIHwBA&ved=0CEsQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=henry%20%20janes%20Brimfield%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl]
He studied law in  Montpelier, Vermont and was admitted to the bar. He began the practice of law in  Waterbury, Vermont in 1817. He was Postmaster from 1820 until 1830.[tpl]cite book|last=Lewis|first=Theodore Graham|title=History of Waterbury, Vermont, 1763-1915|year=1915|publisher=Record print|pages=64|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Qr5Byevb88oC&pg=PA64&lpg=PA64&dq=henry++janes+Brimfield,+Massachusetts&source=bl&ots=P_F2cdbgNc&sig=flB0qu8JUbKdYiOWBwg3QnEm-ts&hl=en&sa=X&ei=NlW6UO_uN8TLrQG5gIHwBA&ved=0CEsQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=henry%20%20janes%20Brimfield%2C%20Massachusetts&f=false[/tpl] He served as a member of the Governor's Council from 1830 until 1834.
Janes was elected as an Anti-Masonic candidate to the Twenty-third Congress to fill the vacancy caused by the death of Benjamin F. Deming and was reelected to the Twenty-fourth Congress, serving from December 2, 1834 until March 3, 1837. He was an unsuccessful Anti-Masonic candidate for reelection in 1836.
He was the Vermont State Treasurer from 1838 until 1841,[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=13507000|title=Henry Fisk Janes|publisher= Find A Grave|accessdate= November 26, 2012[/tpl] and served as a member of the state council of censors in 1848. Janes was a town representative in 1854, 1861, and 1862. He served as member of the Vermont House of Representatives in 1855.

==Personal life==

In 1827 Janes married Fanny Butler, the daughter of Governor Ezra Butler.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=49505867|title=Fanny Butler Janes|publisher= Find A Grave|accessdate= November 26, 2012[/tpl] Their daughter Helen Maria was born in 1828,[tpl]cite book|last=Janes|first=Frederic|title=The Janes Family: A Genealogy and Brief History of the Descendants of William Janes the Emigrant Ancestor of 1637, with an Extended Notice of Bishop Edmund S. Janes, D. D., and Other Biographical Sketches|year=1868|publisher=J. H. Dingman|pages=199|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=egN0uNYkLioC&pg=PA199&lpg=PA199&dq=henry++janes+Calais,+Vermont&source=bl&ots=IE5NKLJU1X&sig=M7R9SGlKV46_segZyROO94hSCBU&hl=en&sa=X&ei=5FW6UJKkPMWFqgG3yYC4DA&ved=0CGYQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=henry%20%20janes%20Calais%2C%20Vermont&f=false[/tpl] and their son Henry Janes was born in 1832 and died in 1915.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=14502426|title=Henry Janes|publisher= Find A Grave|accessdate= November 26, 2012[/tpl]  Henry Janes was a physician during the American Civil War, and attained the rank of Brigadier General as Surgeon General of the Vermont National Guard.

==Death==

Janes died on June 6, 1879 in Waterbury, Vermont, and is interred in Hope Cemetery in Waterbury.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=13507000|title=Henry Fisk Janes|publisher= Find A Grave|accessdate= November 26, 2012[/tpl]
