[[Badagas]]

CATEGORIES: Scheduled Tribes of India, Tamil society, Dravidian peoples, Social groups of Tamil Nadu

The Badagas are an indigenous people inhabiting the Nilgiri Hills of Tamil Nadu, southern India. Their language is Badaga. They are the largest indigenous social group in Nilgiris.[tpl]Cite news | first =D. |last =Radhakrishnan | title =Festival of Badagas begins in the Nilgiris|newspaper=The Hindu |  date =9 January 2012 | url = http://www.thehindu.com/news/cities/Coimbatore/festival-of-badagas-begins-in-the-nilgiris/article2787873.ece|accessdate=6 February 2013[/tpl]

==History==

Badaga people migrated from the area of old Mysore state more than three centuries ago.[tpl]Cite journal|last=Thurston |first=E. |title=Badagas and Irulas of Nilgiris |url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=Ca6wmDzhMDoC |accessdate=6 February 2013 |journal=Bulletin Madras Government Museum|volume=2|issue=1 |year=1909 |publisher= Madras Government Museum|location=Madras, India|pages=2–7 [/tpl] They speak a language which is very similar to the Old Kannada language. They also claimed their ancestry to a village named Badaganahalli, near Mysore. Six distinct groups were identified in Badagas, which are Udaya or Wodeyar,Thoreya, Adhikari, Haruva, Badaga and Kanaka.[tpl]Cite journal|last=Thurston |first=E. |title=Badagas and Irulas of Nilgiris |url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=Ca6wmDzhMDoC |accessdate=6 February 2013 |journal=Bulletin Madras Government Museum|volume=2|issue=1 |year=1909 |publisher= Madras Government Museum|location=Madras, India|pages=2–7 [/tpl] 
In the 1930s, [IGNDP. (Indo German Nilgiri Development project) founded the Nilgiris Cooperative Marketing Society (NCMS), to help achieve better prices for Badagas farm products.[tpl]Cite news|author=Special correspondent|date=5 December 2012|title=Badaga leader’s birth anniversary celebrated|newspaper=The Hindu|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-tamilnadu/badaga-leaders-birth-anniversary-celebrated/article4165972.ece|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20130508175927/http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-tamilnadu/badaga-leaders-birth-anniversary-celebrated/article4165972.ece|archivedate=8 May 2013|deadurl=no[/tpl] The NCMS was in response to chicanery by lowland middlemen who would reduce prices by playing off one farmer against another.Hari Gowder was the first Badagas to be elected to the Madras Legislative Council.[tpl]Cite book|last=Hockings|first=Paul|year=1980|title=Ancient Hindu Refugees: Badaga Social History 1550-1975|location=The Hague, Netherlands|publisher=Mouton|page=178|isbn=978-90-279-7798-4[/tpl]

==Culture==

"Thundu" (a white piece of cloth) forms integral part of attire of Badaga women and the same is presented to dignitaries visiting the villages, as a gesture of good will . Badugas wil marry within their community and also they follow different tradition function during marriage session. They celebrate karthigai deepam and their important festive is hathai habba. ==Religion==

Badagas worship several Hindu deities, including Shiva. But their main deity is "Hethai" and they celebrate "Hethai Habba" in a grand fashion which spreads over a month during December-January every year, and the festival is celebrated all over the district.
