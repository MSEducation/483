[[James Woodhouse, 4th Baron Terrington]]

CATEGORIES: 1915 births, 1998 deaths, Barons in the Peerage of the United Kingdom, Royal Norfolk Regiment officers, King's Royal Rifle Corps officers, British Army personnel of World War II, People educated at Winchester College, Graduates of the Royal Military College, Sandhurst

Major James Allen David Woodhouse, 4th Baron Terrington (born 30 December 1915, died 6 May 1998[tpl]cite web|url=http://thepeerage.com/p8453.htm#i84521|title=James Allen David Woodhouse, 4th Baron Terrington - Person Page 8453|work=The Peerage|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]) was a Baron in the Peerage of the United Kingdom and Deputy Chairman of Committees in the House of Lords. He was a member of the London Stock Exchange and a director of S J Carr and County (Gunmakers) Ltd. He served in the Royal Norfolk Regiment and Queen's Westminster Rifles and was wounded in World War II.
Woodhouse was the son of Horace Martin Woodhouse, 3rd Baron Terrington and Valerie Phillips, and was educated at Winchester College and then at Royal Military College, Sandhurst. He married Suzanne Irwin, daughter of Colonel Thomas Strutt Irwin, on 7 November 1942. They had three daughters.
On his death in 1998, having no male heirs, was succeeded by his brother, Montague Woodhouse, 5th Baron Terrington.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/news/2001/feb/20/guardianobituaries2 | location=London | work=The Guardian | first=Richard | last=Clogg | title=Obituary: Monty Woodhouse | date=20 February 2001[/tpl] James is depicted in three glass-plate negative photographs held by the National Portrait Gallery, two of which show him as a child.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.npg.org.uk/collections/search/person/mp62071/james-allen-david-woodhouse-4th-baron-terrington|title=National Portrait Gallery - Person  - (James Allen) David Woodhouse, 4th Baron Terrington|publisher=National Portrait Gallery|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]
