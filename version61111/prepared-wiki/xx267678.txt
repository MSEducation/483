[[The Humph Trust]]

CATEGORIES: Scholarships in the United Kingdom, 2010 establishments in the United Kingdom, Jazz music education, Organizations established in 2010, Music charities based in the United Kingdom

The Humph Trust was set up in the name of British jazz musician Humphrey Lyttelton, to raise and distribute tailored funding that will support young jazz musicians in building their careers.
Started by Humph's son, Stephen Lyttelton, The Humph Trust was launched on 25 April 2010 with A Celebration Concert at the HMV Apollo, London.[tpl]cite web|url=http://londonjazz.blogspot.com/2010/03/humphrey-lyttelton-celebration-concert.html |title=LondonJazz: Humphrey Lyttelton Celebration Concert |publisher=Londonjazz.blogspot.com |date=2010-03-04 |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl] The Concert featured the current Humphrey Lyttelton Band joined by former members Wally Fawkes (clarinet), Eddie Taylor (drums), Karen Sharp (saxophone), John Barnes (tenor saxophone), John Picard (trombone) and Eddie Harvey (piano).[tpl]cite web|author=Jasper Rees |url=http://www.theartsdesk.com/comedy/antidote-charity-fundraisers |title=The antidote to charity fundraisers | Comedy reviews, news & interviews |publisher=The Arts Desk |date=2010-03-25 |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl]
Guest singers and musicians included: Elkie Brooks, Jools Holland, Stacey Kent, Jim Tomlinson, Charlie Watts, Acker Bilk, Kenny Ball, Tina May, Louise Parker, Annie Whitehead, Sue Richardson, Ben Waters and Axel Zwingelberger.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.musbook.com/rss/1208/Review-Humphrey-Lyttelton-Celebration.html |title=User Imported Blog: Review: Humphrey Lyttelton Celebration |publisher=MusBook.com |date=2010-04-25 |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl]
The show also featured comedy from Jack Dee, Barry Cryer, Tim Brooke-Taylor, Graeme Garden, Rob Brydon, Jeremy Hardy, Sandi Toksvig, Tony Hawks, Andy Hamilton and Colin Sell from the BBC panel show I'm Sorry I Haven't a Clue.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.comedy.co.uk/forums/thread/16870/ |title=Humph Tribute - British Comedy Forums |publisher=Comedy.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl]
A recording of the show was broadcast by BBC Radio 4 on Boxing Day 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00tj5qp |title=BBC Radio 4 - Humph Celebration Concert |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |date=2010-12-25 |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl]
The Humph Award (originally The Humphrey Lyttelton Royal Academy of Music Jazz Award) was set up in conjunction with the Royal Academy of Music and is awarded annually to an outstanding student from the Academy's Jazz Department. Trumpeter Tom Walsh was the inaugural recipient of The Humph Award and opened The Celebration Concert with his own quartet, playing A Song For My Father by Horace Silver.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jazzwisemagazine.com/component/content/11383?task=view |title=Jazz breaking news: All Star Turn Out for Humphrey Lyttelton Royal Academy of Music Jazz Award At Hammersmith Apollo |publisher=Jazzwisemagazine.com |date=2010-04-26 |accessdate=2012-10-04[/tpl]
