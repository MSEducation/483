[[Situationist Antinational]]

CATEGORIES: Magazines established in 1974, Magazines disestablished in 1974, American political magazines, Defunct magazines of the United States

The Situationist Antinational was an American magazine formed in 1974, two years after the disbanding of the Situationist International. Only one issue of the magazine was published. It included a contribution by the American Situationist Jon Horelick, entitled "Beyond The Crisis Of Abstraction And The Abstract Break With That Crisis: The SI"[tpl]cite web | title = Beyond The Crisis Of Abstraction And The Abstract Break With That Crisis: The SI  | author = Jon Horelick | url = http://www.infopool.org.uk/7403.html | publisher = infopool.org | date =  | accessdate = 2008-10-13[/tpl] and a Draft Manifesto of Antinational Situationist, signed by Jørgen Nash, Heimrad Prem, and Helmut Sturm, amongst others.[tpl]cite web | title = Draft Manifesto of Antinational Situationist  | author = Jens Jørgen Thorsen | url = http://www.infopool.org.uk/7402.html | publisher = infopool.org | year = 1974 | accessdate = 2008-10-13[/tpl] All of the contributors were associated with the Second Situationist International.
