Emmanuel "Ema" Boateng (born 17 January 1994) is a Ghananian footballer who plays for Helsingborgs IF as a forward.[tpl]cite web|title=Ema Boateng|url=http://svenskfotboll.se/allsvenskan/person/?playerid=7955|publisher=Svenskfotboll|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

Boateng grew up in Ghana.[tpl]cite web |url=http://espn.go.com/blog/high-school/player-of-the-year/post/_/id/7741/boateng-nets-national-player-of-the-year |title=Boateng nets National Player of the Year |date=17 May 2012|publisher=ESPN |accessdate=28 August 2013[/tpl] At the age of twelve he was picked to join the Right to Dream Academy where he combined studies with football training. When he turned 15 the academy gave him a scholarship to attend Cate School in the United States.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.independent.com/news/2013/jun/21/meet-soccer-star-ema-boateng/ |title=Meet Soccer Star Ema Boateng |date=21 June 2013|publisher=Santa Barbara Independent |accessdate=28 August 2013[/tpl] There he combined his studies with playing for the local clubs Santa Barbara Soccer Club and South Coast Strikers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://hd.se/sport/2013/07/20/boateng-gillar-sitt-nya-gang/ |title=Boateng gillar sitt nya g√§ng |date=20 July 2013|publisher=Helsingborgs Dagblad |language=Swedish |accessdate=28 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2012 he received the annual Gatorade Player of the Year award for his performance on the pitch. He then skipped the final year of high school to start college at the University of California, Santa Barbara where he played for the school team UC Santa Barbara Gauchos.
During the summer of 2013, he played wiith the USL Premier Development League (mostly U-23) side Ventura County Fusion. After receiving an invitation to come and train with them from Swedish side Helsingborgs IF in the summer of 2013 Boateng paid for his own airplane ticket across the Atlantic Ocean to be able to visit the Allsvenskan club. He made a big impression and soon thereafter they signed him to a 3.5 year contract.

==Personal life==

Outside of football Boateng's ambition is to study to become a doctor. His story was featured in "The Beautiful Game", a 2012 documentary about football in Africa. He has also appeared at age 15 in a story on the international news network CNN.[tpl]cite web |url=http://edition.cnn.com/2010/WORLD/africa/04/15/football.academy.ghana/ |title=African football academy creates future stars |date=15 April 2010|publisher=CNN |accessdate=28 August 2013[/tpl]
