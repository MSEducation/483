[[Nasal labialized velar approximant]]

CATEGORIES: Nasal consonants, Velar consonants

The nasal labio-velar approximant is a type of consonantal sound used in some oral languages. The symbol in the International Phonetic Alphabet  that represents this sound is [tpl]angle bracket|[tpl]IPA|w̃[/tpl][/tpl], that is, a w with a tilde. The equivalent X-SAMPA symbol is w~.
The nasal approximants [tpl]IPA|ȷ̃[/tpl] and [tpl]IPA|w̃[/tpl] may also be called nasal glides. In some languages like Portuguese, they form a second element of nasal diphthongs.
Transcriptions using [tpl]angbr|[tpl]IPA|w̃[/tpl][/tpl] may actually intend a non-syllabic vowel, [tpl]IPA|ũ̯[/tpl], or, in some dialects of Brazilian Portuguese, a non-syllabic nasalized near-close near-back rounded vowel [tpl]IPA|ʊ̯̃[/tpl] .
