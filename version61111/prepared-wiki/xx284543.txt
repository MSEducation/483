Alex Haddow (born 8 January 1982) is an English footballer who plays as a left sided defender or midfielder. He played in the Football League for Reading and Carlise United before moving into non-league football following a serious knee injury.

==Club career==

Born in Fleet, Haddow began his career in the Academy at Reading.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.readingfc.co.uk/players/Haddow.html |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20010212073248/http://www.readingfc.co.uk/players/Haddow.html |archivedate=12 February 2001 |title=Alex Haddow profile |publisher=Reading F.C |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] He made his debut on 24 August 1999 against Peterborough United in the League Cup, making a total of three appearances during the 1999–2000 season. He joined Barnet on a one-month loan in July 2000 but did not make any appearances and returned to Reading,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.readingfc.co.uk/news.html |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20000816151625/http://www.readingfc.co.uk/news.html |archivedate=16 August 2000 |title=Latest news |publisher=Reading F.C |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] though he featured in just one game over the course of the season, a 4–0 win at home to Brentford.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players/player.sd?player_id=17401 |title=Alex Haddow career statistics |work=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] He was released by Reading in May 2001 and joined Carlisle United three months later. After five appearances for the club he damaged his cruiciate ligament and was ruled out for the rest of the season. He left Carlisle at the end of the season and trialled with Canadian side Vancouver Whitecaps before moving into non-league football.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.uslsoccer.com/home/54646.html |title=Vancouver downs Abbotsford in preseason opener |publisher=USL Soccer |accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.angelfire.com/sports/bumble/Profiles/alex__haddow.htm |title=Alex Haddow |publisher=Player Archive |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]
After short spells at Hartley Wintney and Aldershot Town he joined Isthmian League side Slough Town in August 2003.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nonleaguedaily.com/news/index.php?newsmode=FULL&nid=8329 |title=Looking good for Wallingford as Dons go down |publisher=Non League Daily |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nonleaguedaily.com/news/index.php?&newsmode=FULL&nid=12094 |title=Haddow back at the Rec |publisher=Non League Daily |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sloughtownfc.net/profile/110 |title=Alex Haddow profile |publisher=Slough Town F.C |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] In two years with club Haddow accumulated 72 appearances in all competitions, scoring twice. He also won the FA Cup Player of the Round award for his performance in Slough's 2–1 first-round win over Walsall in November 2004.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/fa_cup/4034397.stm |title=Haddow gets FA Cup player award |publisher=BBC Sport |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] In February 2005 he joined divisional rivals Eastleigh on a two-year contract[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/archive/2005/02/16/5555291.Hitman_Haddow_is_heading_to_Eastleigh/?ref=arc |title=Hitman Haddow is heading to Eastleigh |newspaper=Southern Daily Echo |location=Southampton |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] though he did not remain at the club long, joining Salisbury City on loan in September before making the move permanent two months later.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/archive/2005/09/15/5637628.FOOTBALL__Taylor_and_Warren_are_released__as_Eastleigh_ring_the_changes/ |title=Taylor and Warren are released as Eastleigh ring the changes |newspaper=Southern Daily Echo |location=Southampton |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nonleaguedaily.com/news/index.php?&newsmode=FULL&nid=30745 |title=Haddow makes City move permanent |publisher=Non League Daily |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] He remained with the side until the end of the 2006–07 season, scoring nine goals in more than 70 appearances, but was not offered a new contract upon Salisbury's promotion to the Conference Premier.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.dailyecho.co.uk/archive/2007/05/29/1430149.Declan_and_Alex_on_the_move/ |title=Declan and Alex on the move |newspaper=Southern Daily Echo |location=Southampton |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
He briefly turned out for Hampton & Richmond Borough before joining Bognor Regis Town in late 2007.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.richmondandtwickenhamtimes.co.uk/sport/1614598.Double_whammy_for_10_man_Hampton/ |last=Rance |first=Les |date=13 August 2007 |title=Double whammy for 10-man Hampton |newspaper=Richmond & Twickenham Times |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nonleaguedaily.com/news/index.php?newsmode=FULL&nid=45737 |title=Boro go top alone as Rooks lose unbeaten tag |publisher=Non League Daily |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] He remained with the club until the end of the season before financial problems at Bognor saw him move to Horsham where he played until 2010.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/sport/2330826.Horsham_set_to_sign_Bognor_midfielder/ |last=Donovan |first=Mike |date=10 June 2008 |title=Horsham set to sign Bognor midfielder |newspaper=The Argus |location=Brighton |accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]
