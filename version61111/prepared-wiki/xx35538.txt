[[Eucla National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1979

Eucla National Park is a national park in Western Australia (Australia), [tpl]convert|1238|km|mi|0[/tpl] east of Perth.
The southern edge of the park borders a section of the Great Australian Bight. Other notable features of the park include Wilson Bluff and Delisser sandhills.
The area is composed of mallee scrub and heath vegetaton, typical of the southern coast.
Wildflowers such as Cockie's Tongue (Templetonia retusa), with its distinctive red, pink or yellow flowers are common throughout the park. A rare plant species of senecio that is native to the limestone cliff area is known to exist in the park.[tpl]cite AHD|9809|Eucla National Park|date=2010|accessdate=27 November 2010[/tpl]
Access to the area is via the Eyre Highway found on the northern border of the park. No facilities are available for visitors in the park and no sealed roads exist withn the park, only 4WD tracks.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.planetware.com/eucla/eucla-national-park-aus-wa-euclanp.htm |title=Planetware Travel - Eucla National Park|year=2008|accessdate=15 May 2010[/tpl]
No camping is permitted within the park, the nearest camping facilities are at Eucla and the Border Village.
Historical ruins such as the Eucla Telegraph station and the original Eucla township can be found at the western end of the park. Both are partially buried by encroaching sand dunes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dec.wa.gov.au/component/option,com_docman/task,doc_download/gid,4229/Itemid,1355/|title=Department of Environment and Conservation - Cape Arid and Eucla National Parks |year=2007|accessdate=15 May 2010[/tpl]
