[[EMC Corporation]]

EMC Corporation (stylized as EMC²) is an American multinational corporation headquartered in Hopkinton, Massachusetts, United States.Reuters Profile: EMC CORPORATION (EMC)[tpl]cite web|title=About EMC Corporation|url=http://www.emc.com/about/index.htm[/tpl] EMC offers data storage, information security, virtualization, analytics, cloud computing and other products and services that enable businesses to store, manage, protect, and analyze data. EMC's target markets include large companies and small- and medium-sized businesses across various vertical markets.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.hoovers.com/company/EMC_Corporation/rfcsri-1.html |title=EMC Corporation Company Information |publisher=Hoover's |year=2012 |accessdate=February 1, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Dignan|first=Larry|title=EMC targets mid-market, plans to undercut NetApp|url=http://www.zdnet.com/blog/btl/emc-targets-mid-market-plans-to-undercut-netapp/43672|accessdate=26 September 2013|newspaper=ZDNet|date=17 January 2011[/tpl]  The stock was added to the New York Stock Exchange on April 6, 1986,EMC Corporation Announces Two-for-One Stock Split;Annual Meeting of Stockholders, Live Video Web, May 3, 2000, D&B AllBusiness and is also listed on the S&P 500 index.

==History==

thumb|left|Richard Egan, co-founder of EMC Corporation EMC, founded in 1979 by Richard Egan and Roger Marino (the E and M in EMC),"What does EMC stand for?"[tpl]dead link|date=April 2014[/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=April 2014[/tpl] introduced its first 64-kilobyte memory boards for the Prime Computer in 1981[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.forbes.com/forbes/2001/1126/062.html|publisher=Forbes|title=What's Eating EMC?|accessdate=2009-07-01|date=November 26, 2001|first=Daniel|last=Lyons[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.forbes.com/forbes/2001/1126/062s01.html|publisher=Forbes|title=Crony Capitalism|accessdate=2009-10-23|date=November 26, 2001|first=Daniel|last=Lyons[/tpl] and continued with the development of memory boards for other computer types. In the mid-1980s the company expanded beyond memory to other computer data storage types and networked storage platforms. EMC began shipping its flagship product, the Symmetrix, in 1990. Symmetrix was the main reason for EMC's rapid growth in the 1990s, both in size and value, from a company valued in the hundreds of millions of dollars to a multi-billion company.EMC Company Web site, July 19, 2000 "EMC Reports 43% Growth in Storage Revenue, First $2 Billion Quarter".
Michael Ruettgers joined EMC in 1988 and served as CEO from 1992 until January 2001.[tpl]cite news|title=Michael Ruettgers|url=http://www.forbes.com/profile/michael-ruettgers/|work=Bio|publisher=Forbes|accessdate=26 September 2013[/tpl] Under Ruettgers’ leadership, EMC revenues grew from $120 million to nearly $9 billion 10 years later, and the company shifted its focus from memory boards to storage systems.[tpl]cite web|title=High-Tech Veteran Michael Ruettgers Joins Gigamon’s Board of Directors|url=http://www.gigamon.com/high-tech-veteran-michael-ruettgers-joins-gigamon-s-board-of-directors|work=Press Release|publisher=Gigamon|accessdate=26 September 2013[/tpl]  Ruettgers was named one of BusinessWeek’s "World's Top 25 Executives;" one of the "Best Chief Executive Officers in America" by Worth Magazine; and one of Network World’s "25 Most Powerful People in Networking."
In 2001, Joe Tucci was named Chief Executive Officer of EMC.[tpl]cite web|title=Joseph M. Tucci|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=135162&ticker=EMC|work=Executive Profile|publisher=Bloomberg Businessweek|accessdate=26 September 2013[/tpl] He has been credited with transforming EMC’s business model from a focus on high-end storage platforms to a portfolio of platforms, software, and services that enable organizations to deliver IT as a service through cloud computing. Under Tucci’s leadership, EMC grew to $21.7 billion in revenues in 2012.[tpl]cite web|title=EMC Corp.|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/investing/stock/emc/financials|work=Annual Financials|publisher=MarketWatch|accessdate=26 September 2013[/tpl]
In 2009, EMC and Cisco, with investments from VMware and Intel, formed a joint venture called VCE to develop products and services for the converged infrastructure and cloud computing markets.[tpl]cite news|last=Burke|first=Steven|title=Cisco Partner Summit Features Revved Up VCE Effort|url=http://www.crn.in/news/data%20center/2013/06/04/cisco-partner-summit-features-revved-up-vce-effort|accessdate=26 September 2013|newspaper=CRN|date=4 June 2011[/tpl]
EMC announced its 450,000 square foot Center of Excellence (COE) in Durham, North Carolina in 2011.[tpl]cite news|last=Miller|first=Rich|title=EMC Opens New Cloud Data Center in NC|url=http://www.datacenterknowledge.com/archives/2011/09/15/emc-opens-new-cloud-data-center-in-nc/|accessdate=26 September 2013|newspaper=Data Center Knowledge|date=15 September 2011[/tpl] The COE houses a virtualized data center and includes 130,000 square feet of global research and development labs. The company also has COEs in India, China, Egypt, Israel, Ireland, and Russia, which perform engineering, research and development, customer, and translation services for EMC business units.
In 2013, EMC launched a new company named Pivotal.[tpl]cite news|last=Kovar|first=Joseph F.|title=EMC, VMware To Launch Pivotal Initiative As Separate $300M Firm|url=http://www.crn.com/news/cloud/240150744/emc-vmware-to-launch-pivotal-initiative-as-separate-300m-firm.htm?itc=xbodysb|accessdate=26 September 2013|newspaper=CRN|date=13 March 2013[/tpl] Led by Paul Maritz, the organization combines technologies from VMware and EMC and is focused on delivering Platform-as-a-Service offerings.

==Products and services==

In addition to those of the majority-owned VMware and Pivotal companies, EMC offers products and services designed to allow IT departments to move to a cloud computing model and to analyze Big Data.

==Big Data projects==

In 2012, EMC sponsored The Human Face of Big Data,[tpl]cite web|title=The Human Face of Big Data|url=http://thehumanfaceofbigdata.com/|work=Web site|publisher=The Human Face of Big Data|accessdate=27 September 2013[/tpl] a globally crowd-sourced media project focusing on the ability to collect, analyze, triangulate and visualize vast amounts of data in real time. The Human Face of Big Data, produced by Rick Smolan and Jennifer Erwitt, includes “a number of fascinating stories…(that) represent some of the most innovative applications of data that are shaping our future.”[tpl]cite news|last=Simon|first=Phil|title=The Human Face of Big Data: An Interview with Rick Smolan|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/phil-simon/the-human-face-of-big-dat_b_3094579.html|work=Blog|publisher=Huffington Post|accessdate=27 September 2013|date=16 April 2013[/tpl]
EMC sponsors The Digital Universe, a study by IDC “measuring and characterizing all the digital data created, replicated, and consumed worldwide in a single year.”[tpl]cite web|title=The Digital Universe in 2020|url=http://www.emc.com/leadership/digital-universe/index.htm|work=Web site|publisher=EMC|accessdate=27 September 2013[/tpl] The Digital Universe 2012 report anticipates that
  “during the next eight years, the amount of digital data produced will exceed 40 zettabytes, which is the equivalent of 5,200 GB of data for every man, woman and child on Earth. To hit that figure, all data is expected to double every two years through 2020.”[tpl]cite news|last=Mearian|first=Lucas|title=By 2020, there will be 5,200 GB of data for every person on Earth|url=http://www.computerworld.com/s/article/9234563/By_2020_there_will_be_5_200_GB_of_data_for_every_person_on_Earth?taxonomyId=19&pageNumber=1|accessdate=27 September 2013|newspaper=Computerworld|date=11 December 2012[/tpl]

==Collaboration with colleges and universities==

EMC’s Academic Alliance program, a collaboration with colleges and universities worldwide, offers curriculum-based education on technology topics such as virtualization, cloud computing, big data analytics, and information storage and management.[tpl]cite web|title=EMC Academic Alliance|url=http://www.likester.com/pages/ItemDetails.aspx?ItemID=122057804471174|work=Web site|publisher=Likester|accessdate=27 September 2013[/tpl] The global program is geared towards arming students with the knowledge necessary to differentiate themselves in the job market and improve their competitive advantage.[tpl]cite web|title=EMC|url=http://www.stemconnector.org/EMC|work=Web site directory|publisher=STEMconnector|accessdate=27 September 2013[/tpl]
