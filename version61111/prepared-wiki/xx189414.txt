[[Charles Rivkin]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American television producers, The Jim Henson Company, Harvard Business School alumni, Yale University alumni, American media executives, Ambassadors of the United States to France, American Jews, 1962 births, Ambassadors of the United States to Monaco

Charles H. Rivkin currently serves as the Assistant Secretary of State for Economic and Business Affairs.  He was confirmed by the U.S. Senate on February 11, 2014.http://politics.nytimes.com/congress/votes/113/senate/2/29
Prior to his appointment, Rivkin served for more than four years as the United States Ambassador to France and Monaco.  He was the youngest Ambassador in nearly 60 years to serve as the senior U.S. representative in France, and his confirmation as Assistant Secretary marks the first time a non-career bilateral ambassador has led the Economic and Business Affairs Bureau.

==Early life and education==

Rivkin earned a B.A. from Yale University in 1984 where he graduated with distinction in political science and international relations.  At Yale he also sang with the famed Whiffenpoofs, America's oldest collegiate a cappella singing group.  He then earned an M.B.A. from Harvard University in 1988.
He speaks fluent French and spent years as a youth studying, traveling and working in France, including studying in Rennes with School Year Abroad and working as an intern at Renault. Business interests regularly brought him to Paris or Cannes prior to his appointment as ambassador.[tpl]cite web |url=http://france.usembassy.gov/ambassador.html|title=US Embassy France |date=2011-09-12[/tpl]

==Business career==

Prior to entering government service, Rivkin worked in the media sector for over 20 years, serving as president and CEO of award winning entertainment companies such as The Jim Henson Company, home of the world-renowned "Muppets".  He also worked at Wildbrain where he won a BAFTA Award as Executive Producer of the hit TV series Yo Gabba Gabba.http://www.c21media.net/resources/detail.asp?area=79&article=46212  Rivkin helped engineer the sale of The Jim Henson Company to EM.TV in 2000 for nearly $1 billion."Henson tab near $1 bil", Variety, 24 February 2000 His contribution to the television landscape has influenced generations of viewers and helped shape American popular culture.

==Political activities==

Rivkin served as an at-large California delegate for Senator John Kerry at the 2004 Democratic National Convention and for Barack Obama at the 2008 Democratic National Convention. Rivkin was the California finance co-chair for Obama's 2008 presidential campaign."New U.S. Envoy Takes Up Post" by Brian Knowlton, The New York Times, Aug. 16, 2009. Retrieved 8/16/09.

==Ambassador to France==

As Ambassador to France, Rivkin led America's first and one of its largest diplomatic missions, which has six constituent posts throughout France and represents as many as 50 federal agencies and departments. Ambassador Rivkin's service in France came at one of the bilateral relationship's strongest moments in recent history.http://www.euronews.net/2010/01/28/friendship-on-the-menu-and-french-us-dinner/"Interview: «Cooperation Has Reached an Unprecedented Level»", ''Les Echos, 26 March 2013.  To honor the legacy of Franco-American friendship and to commemorate the 68th anniversary of the Allied invasion of mainland Europe during World War II, Ambassador Rivkin took part in a mass parachute jump over the coast of Normandy on June 3, 2012.[tpl]cite web|title=US ambassador marks D-Day with Normandy parachute jump|url=http://worldnews.msnbc.msn.com/_news/2012/06/04/12043638-us-ambassador-marks-d-day-with-normandy-parachute-jump?lite|publisher=MSNBC[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=U.S. Ambassador Charles Rivkin marks D-Day with a parachute jump|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=6ueMWvcYb_w|publisher=YouTube|accessdate=July 1, 2012|format=Video[/tpl] During his 12,000-foot jump, Rivkin was accompanied by members of the U.S. Army Golden Knights parachute team.[tpl]cite news|title=U.S. ambassador to France joins paratroopers for 12,000ft skydive over Normandy to celebrate D-Day anniversary|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-2154642/U-S-ambassador-France-joins-paratroopers-12-000ft-skydive-celebrate-D-Day-anniversary.html|newspaper=The Daily Mail|date=June 4, 2012[/tpl] An estimated crowd of 25,000 watched Rivkin land in a field near Sainte-Mère-Église amidst heavy winds, as he became the first U.S. Ambassador to France to jump from a plane in honor of the troops who fought on D-Day.[tpl]cite news|title=US ambassador in D-Day jump|url=http://www.connexionfrance.com/US-ambassador-D-Day-parachute-freefall-Normandy-13805-view-article.html|newspaper=The Connexion|date=June 4, 2012[/tpl] In support of the US Navy, Rivkin became the first U.S. Ambassador to take off and land on a Navy aircraft carrier in an F-18 Super Hornet when he participated in a training exercise with naval aviators on the USS Eisenhower in March, 2013.http://www.lemeilleurdemarseille.fr/au-revoir/18722-marseille-likes-ike
According to the Department of State Office of Inspector General’s report in May 2012, Ambassador Rivkin placed new emphasis on support for U.S. exports of goods and services into France.[tpl]cite web|title=Office of Inspector General's Report on Embassy Paris, May 2012|url=http://oig.state.gov/documents/organization/191912.pdf[/tpl] The report called Rivkin a "dynamic and visionary noncareer Ambassador," and credited him with expanding the U.S. Embassy’s public diplomacy activities, particularly through his use of social media and his appearances on French national television. Ambassador Rivkin introduced social media to Embassy Paris, establishing its first ever Facebook[tpl]cite web|title=Embassy Paris Facebook page|url=http://www.facebook.com/usdos.france[/tpl]  and Twitter accounts.[tpl]cite web|title=Embassy Paris Twitter account|url=https://twitter.com/#!/USEmbassyFrance[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Ambassador Charles H. Rivkin's Twitter account|url=https://twitter.com/#!/AmbRivkin[/tpl]
As ambassador, Rivkin made youth outreach one of his key priorities and connected the embassy to the next generation of leaders throughout France, including in disadvantaged communities often referred to as the banlieues."How the U.S. Engages with French 'Banlieues'", Le Parisien, 26 January 2013.  Ambassador Rivkin organized a series of seminars for French youth, inviting them to meet with prominent American government officials, actors and musicians. Beginning with actor Samuel L. Jackson’s April 2010 visit with students in Bondy, an economically depressed Parisian suburb, Ambassador Rivkin set up seminars with Attorney General Eric Holder and Secretary of Health and Human Services Kathleen Sebelius, as well as Woody Allen, Jodie Foster, will.i.am of the Black Eyed Peas, Robert Zemeckis, Allen Stone, Tony Bennett, Herbie Hancock, Maya Angelou, Toni Morrison and many others.[tpl]cite news|last=Sayare|first=Scott|title=Feeling Slighted by France, and Respected by the U.S.|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2010/09/23/world/europe/23france.html|newspaper=The New York Times|date=Sep 22, 2010[/tpl] "Much of the embassy's outreach is meant to dispel 'mistruths' about the United States," Ambassador Rivkin said in an interview, adding, "It's easier to hate something you don't understand." In January 2012, Ambassador Rivkin broadened his outreach efforts by creating the Washburne Award for Innovation in Diversity, recognizing one French and one American company for their best practices in fostering diversity in hiring practices."Un plan coordonné en faveur de l'innovation | Nos amis américains | Prix Washburne", Le Monde, 30 May 2013.
On July 16, 2013, President Francois Hollande awarded Ambassador Rivkin the rank of Commander in the Légion d'honneur at the Elysée Palace.  Rivkin is the first U.S. Ambassador in half a century to receive the decoration from a sitting French president.[tpl]cite web |url=http://france.usembassy.gov/event20130716.html|title=US Embassy France Internet page|date=2013-07-26[/tpl]
