[[Cady Noland]]

CATEGORIES: 1956 births, Living people, American conceptual artists, American contemporary artists, Postmodern artists, Artists from New York, Sarah Lawrence College alumni, American women sculptors, American installation artists

Cady Noland (born 1956 in Washington, DC.) is a postmodern conceptual sculptor and an internationally exhibited installation artist, whose work deals with the failed promise of the American Dream and the divide between fame and anonymity, among other themes.JOURNAL OF CONTEMPORARY ART, conversation, Cady Noland and Michèle Cone Retrieved January 11, 2010http://www.askart.com/AskART/artists/biography.aspx?searchtype=BIO&artist=101165 Retrieved January 10, 2010  Her work has been exhibited in museums and expositions including the Whitney Biennial in 1991 and Documenta 9 in Kassel, Germany.http://oneartworld.com/index.php?pg=awards&aw=Documenta+9,+1992 retrieved January 10, 2010 She attended Sarah Lawrence College and is the daughter of the Color Field painter Kenneth Noland (1924-2010).

==Exhibition history==

Her work has been exhibited in museums and expositions including the Whitney Biennial in 1991 and Documenta 9 in Kassel, Germany.http://oneartworld.com/index.php?pg=awards&aw=Documenta+9,+1992 retrieved January 10, 2010
Solo exhibitions of Noland’s work have been organized by the Paula Cooper Gallery in New York (1994), Museum Boijmans Van Beuningen in Rotterdam (1995), and Wadsworth Atheneum in Hartford, Connecticut (1996).

==Market history==

Noland holds the record for the highest price ever paid for an artwork by a living woman ($6.6m), for her 1989 work Oozewald sold at Sothebys.[tpl]cite web|title=The price of being female: Post-war artists at auction|url=http://www.economist.com/blogs/prospero/2012/05/post-war-artists-auction|work=Prospero blog|publisher=The Economist|accessdate=May 25, 2012|date=May 25, 2012[/tpl]  In the fall of 2012 the same auction house, Sothebys removed her piece "Cowboys Milking"(1990) from a contemporary sale after the artist "disavowed" the work.
