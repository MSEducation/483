[[Tyldesley Little Theatre]]

CATEGORIES: Theatres in Greater Manchester, Buildings and structures completed in 1905

Tyldesley Little Theatre is a small theatre in Lemon Street, Tyldesley, Greater Manchester, England and is the home of the amateur dramatic society that had its first production at the theatre in 1921. The theatre has been described as 'one of the few remaining authentic back street theatres'.[tpl]citation| url=http://www.tyldesley.towntalk.co.uk/about/ | title=Town Talk |publisher = Towntalk|accessdate=2011-01-06 [/tpl]  
The theatre building started life as "Church House" built by subscription in 1905 and managed by a committee from St George's Church. It originally had gymnasium facilities[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.peterjtyldesley.com/bongs/lunn/pages/149.html |title=The Church House, 1905 (cont.)|publisher= Peter Tyldesley |accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl] and was used by the local community, including, from 1921, the amateur dramatic society.[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.tlt.org.uk/|title=About Tyldesley Little Theatre|publisher= Tyldesley Little Theatre|accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl] In 1959 the building had fallen into disrepair and was given to the amateur dramatic society for its sole use.
The theatre's stage has a proscenium arch and seating for 150 people in either the ground-floor stalls or first-floor balcony. The company produces a variety of plays and an annual traditional family pantomime. The society is a member of the Greater Manchester Drama Federation (GMDF),[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.gmdf.org/|title=Greater Manchester Drama Federation|publisher= Greater Manchester Drama Federation|accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl] the Bolton Amateur Theatre Society (BATS),[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.b-a-t-s.net/|title=Bolton Amateur Theatre Societies|publisher= Bolton Amateur Theatre Societies|accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl] and is a registered charity run by volunteers. 
In 1957 the theatre produced the premiere of the comedy Sky's the Limit written by Leigh playwright, Arthur Helsby. The play was presented in September 2007 to celebrate the play's 50th anniversary[tpl]citation| url=http://www.theboltonnews.co.uk/archive/2007/04/11/Old+Pals%3A+Keeping+in+touch+around+the+world+%28old_pals%29/1321164.IAN_TAYLOR_c_o_Tyldesley_Little_Theatre__Lemon_StreetM29_8HT/ | title=Tyldesley Little Theatre, Lemon Street|publisher = Bolton News Archive|accessdate=2011-01-06 [/tpl] The company welcomed Arnold Helsby's daughter, Wendy, who was visiting the UK from the USA to the production.
Tyldesley Little Theatre has twice received assistance from waste company Viridor which distributes grants from landfill tax credits. In 2009, a Viridor grant paid for roof repairs and upgrades to technical equipment, and in 2010 grant aid worth Â£42,000 was used to create a basement refreshment/rehearsal area, improve the heating system and create storage space.[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.theboltonnews.co.uk/news/8236405./|title=Grant boost for Little Theatre|work= The Bolton News|accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl]
In 2010 Tyldesley Little Theatre won three awards out of seven nominations at the Greater Manchester Drama Federation (GMDF) awards[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.leighjournal.co.uk/news/8258790.Tyldesley_company_scoops_awards/|title=Tyldesley Little Theatre hat-trick at Manchester drama dinner|work= Leigh Journal|accessdate=2011-01-06[/tpl] and in July 2011 won five awards out of nine nominations at the GMDF award ceremony.[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.leighjournal.co.uk/news/9149601.Theatre_group_play_on_success/|title=Tyldesley Little Theatre GMDF Success|work= Leigh Journal|accessdate=2011-08-03[/tpl]
