[[Tasmanian Wilderness]]

The Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area is a World Heritage Site in Tasmania, Australia.[tpl]cite web|last=World Heritage Centre|title=World Heritage List|url=http://whc.unesco.org/en/list|publisher=UNESCO|accessdate=May 2, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Parks and Wildlife Service Tasmania|title=Tasmanian Wilderness World Heritage Area|url=http://www.parks.tas.gov.au/index.aspx?base=391|publisher=Tasmania Online|accessdate=May 2, 2012[/tpl]
The area is one of the largest conservation areas in Australia, covering 15,800 km², or almost 20% of Tasmania after extensions in 1989 and 2013.[tpl]Citation | author1=Australia. The Tasmanian Wilderness, a network of parks and reserves with steep gorges, underwent severe glaciation. Human remains dating back more than 20,000 years have been found in limestone caves in the area.[tpl]cite web|last=United Nations Education, Scientific and Culture Organization|title=Tasmanian Wilderness|url=http://whc.unesco.org/en/list/181|publisher=UNESCO|accessdate=May 2, 2012[/tpl]
