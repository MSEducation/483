[[Richard Long (journalist)]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, New Zealand journalists, New Zealand columnists, Officers of the New Zealand Order of Merit, 1940s births

Richard Arthur Long, ONZM (c. born 1940) is a New Zealand journalist, and a former editor of The Dominion newspaper. He worked in the parliamentary press gallery for over 20 years, which included writing a series questioning the Think Big economic strategy of the government. The articles led Prime Minister Robert Muldoon to bar all Dominion reporters from his press conferences.[tpl]cite news |title=Richard Long: Glory days in the gallery |first=Kate |last=Newton |url= http://www.stuff.co.nz/national/new-years-honours/4504127/Richard-Long-Glory-days-in-the-gallery |newspaper=The Dominion Post|date=31 December 2010|accessdate=31 December 2010[/tpl]
After the merger of The Dominion with The Evening Post in 2002, Long left the newspaper to become chief of staff for New Zealand National Party leaders Bill English and Don Brash.
[tpl]As of|2011[/tpl] he writes a political column "The Long View" for The Dominion Post.
Long was made an Officer of the New Zealand Order of Merit in the 2011 New Year Honours, for services to journalism.[tpl]cite web|title=New Year Honours 2011|url=http://www.dpmc.govt.nz/honours/lists/list.asp?id=49|work=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet|date=31 December 2010|accessdate=31 December 2010[/tpl]
