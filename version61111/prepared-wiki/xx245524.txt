[[Samruk-Kazyna]]

CATEGORIES: Companies based in Astana, Companies of Kazakhstan, Economy of Kazakhstan

Samruk-Kazyna ([tpl]lang-kk|Самұрық-Қазына[/tpl], [tpl]lang-ru|Самрук-Казына[/tpl]), officially known as the National Welfare Fund "Samruk-Kazyna", is a sovereign wealth fund and joint stock company in Kazakhstan which owns, either in whole or in part, many important companies in the country, including the national rail and postal service, the state oil and gas company KazMunayGas, the state uranium company Kazatomprom, Air Astana, and numerous financial groups. The state is the sole shareholder of the fund.[tpl]dead link|date=February 2014[/tpl] It was created in October 2008 with the merger of two funds, "Samruk" and "Kazyna", by decree of the president of Kazakhstan, Nursultan Nazarbayev. Timur Askarovich Kulibayev is the Chief Executive Officer and Chairman of the Management Board. Samruk-Kazyna controls $78 billion in assets, or nearly 56% of GDP. Kazakhstan’s government expects assets held by its sovereign wealth fund to reach $100 billion by 2015.[tpl]cite news|title=Kazakhstan Expects Oil Fund to Reach $100 Billion by 2015|url=http://www.satrapia.com/news/article/kazakhstan-expects-oil-fund-to-reach-100-billion-by-2015/|accessdate=30 August 2012|newspaper=The Gazette of Central Asia|date=28 Aug 2012|publisher=Satrapia[/tpl] 
In 2013 Aftenposten quoted Denis Jivaga (a human rights activist and lawyer) saying that there is an "oil fund in Kazakhstan, but nobody knows how the income is spent".[tpl]cite web|author=Ingrid Fredriksen, Marie Havnen |url=http://www.aftenposten.no/nyheter/iriks/politikk/Observatorer-fra-tidligere-Sovjet-jakter-pa-valg-juks-7305886.html#.Ui7pCY3IaIg |title=Observatører fra tidligere Sovjet jakter på valg-juks |publisher=Aftenposten.no |date=2013-09-10 |accessdate=2014-02-02[/tpl]
