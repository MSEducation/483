[[Boydton Historic District]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, Historic districts in Virginia, Federal architecture in Virginia, Greek Revival architecture in Virginia, Buildings and structures in Mecklenburg County, Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Mecklenburg County, Virginia

The Boydton Historic District is a national historic district located at Boydton, Mecklenburg County, Virginia. It encompasses 199 contributing buildings, 6 contributing sites, 6 contributing structure, and 2 contributing objects in the central business district and surrounding residential areas of the town of Boydton.  Notable buildings include the municipal building (1905), the old jail (1870), the Beales, Bedinger, and
Gregory, Inc. car dealership building (1918), Washington Tavern, Williams and Goode Bank (1908), Boydton Department Store (1935), Mecklenburg County Building Department (1949), Southside Regional Library (1939), Presbyterian Meeting House (1819), Saint James Episcopal Church (1840-1841), Boydton Baptist Church, Trinity Episcopal Church (1890s), "Cedar Crest" (1825), and "On the Hill" (1920).  Located in the district and separately listed are the Boyd's Tavern and Mecklenburg County Courthouse.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Mecklenburg/173-5001_Boydton_Historic_District_2002_Final_Nomination.pdf |title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Boydton Historic District|author=Edna Johnston & Kathryn Gettinqs Smith| date=September 2001|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying two photos and Accompanying map
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2002.
