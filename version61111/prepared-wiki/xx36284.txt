Bertrand Blier (born 14 March 1939 in Boulogne-Billancourt) is a French screenwriter and film director. His 1978 film Get Out Your Handkerchiefs won the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film at the 51st Academy Awards.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/awards/academyawards/legacy/ceremony/51st-winners.html |title=The 51st Academy Awards (1979) Nominees and Winners |accessdate=2013-06-07 |work=oscars.org[/tpl]
He is the son of famous French actor Bernard Blier. His 1996 film Mon Homme was entered into the 46th Berlin International Film Festival.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.berlinale.de/en/archiv/jahresarchive/1996/02_programm_1996/02_Programm_1996.html |title=Berlinale: 1996 Programme |accessdate=2012-01-01 |work=berlinale.de[/tpl] His 2005 film How Much Do You Love Me? was entered into the 28th Moscow International Film Festival where he won the Silver George for Best Director.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.moscowfilmfestival.ru/miff34/eng/archives/?year=2006 |title=28th Moscow International Film Festival (2006) |accessdate=2013-04-21 |work=MIFF[/tpl]
A defence of Blier's work up until 2000 was written by Sue Harris, Queen Mary College, London and published in 2001 By Manchester University Press.Bertrand Blier - In the series French Film Directors. ISBN 0-7190-5297-1
