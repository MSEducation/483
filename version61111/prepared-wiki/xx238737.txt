Andrew Joseph Jordan (born 14 December 1979 in Manchester) is an English-born Scottish former professional footballer and Scotland under-21 international,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/blogs/gavinstrachan/2009/04/remembering_my_international_h.html |title=Remembering my international heyday |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 April 2009 |accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] who made 21 appearances in The Football League between 1997 and 2005. He is the son of former Scotland international Joe Jordan and the brother of Tom Jordan.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/football/article-510469/You-Havant-easy-son--Jordan-Snr-knows-life-timer.html |title=You Havant had it easy, son – Jordan snr knows all about life as part-timer |publisher=Daily Mail |date=25 January 2008 |accessdate=18 October 2009 | location=London | first=Matt | last=Lawton[/tpl]

==Career==

Jordan began his career as a trainee at Bristol City where he made eleven league appearances.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/bristolcity/bristolcity.html |title=Bristol City: 1946/47 – 2008/09 |publisher=Neil Brown |accessdate=18 October 2009| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20091029232848/http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/bristolcity/bristolcity.html| archivedate= 29 October 2009 | deadurl= no[/tpl] In October 2000 he joined Cardiff City for a fee of £30,000, making his debut in a 4–0 victory over York City on 4 November 2000. However after a handful of first team appearances, during which he scored two own goals,[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/fa_cup/1028250.stm |title=Cardiff 5–1 Bristol Rovers |publisher=BBC Sport |date=19 November 2000 |accessdate=14 November 2009[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_3/1095197.stm |title=Cardiff 6–1 Exeter |publisher=BBC Sport |date=1 January 2001 |accessdate=14 November 2009[/tpl] Jordan was struck by injury which severely restricted his remaining time at Ninian Park as he remained sidelined for two years and was allowed to leave the club in May 2003.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/cardiff_city/2940882.stm |title=Cardiff release Fortune-West |publisher=BBC Sport |date=28 May 2003 |accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl]
After talks with Cardiff's South Wales rivals Swansea City,[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/swansea_city/2996610.stm |title=Swans make double swoop |publisher=BBC Sport |date=17 May 2003 |accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] Jordan joined Hartlepool United in July 2003. He made his debut on the opening day of the 2003–04 season in a 4–3 win over Peterborough United on 8 August 2003.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/3113687.stm |title=Peterborough 3–4 Hartlepool |publisher=BBC Sport |date=9 August 2003 |accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] Having made just six first-team appearances for the side, Jordan was again struck by injury and subsequently retired from professional football.
