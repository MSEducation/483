[[Hart State Park]]

CATEGORIES: Protected areas of Hart County, Georgia, Reservoirs in Georgia (U.S. state), State parks of Georgia (U.S. state)

The Hart State Outdoor Recreation Area, also known as Hart State Park and Hart Park, is a community [tpl]Convert|147|acre|ha[/tpl] natural park located at 330 of Hart Park Road, in Hartwell, Georgia, in northeast Georgia.  The park is named after the American Revolutionary War heroine Nancy Hart, who lived in the Georgia frontier, and it was her devotion to freedom that has helped make her name commonplace in the Georgia upcountry today.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.sas.usace.army.mil/lakes/hartwell/hart.html |title=Nancy Hart-Revolutionary War Heroine |publisher=Army Corps of Engineers [/tpl]
This recreation area is used by the communiry for swimming, boating, water skiing and water sports in general, and fishing on Lake Hartwell, and for hiking and biking on a [tpl]Convert|1.5|mi|km[/tpl] trail.  Hart Park also offers a playground for children and a self-registration campground, which is opened seasonally from March 15 until September 15.
