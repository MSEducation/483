[[KRKI]]

CATEGORIES: Radio stations in South Dakota, ESPN Radio stations, Radio stations established in 2000

KRKI (99.5 FM, "99-5 The Range") is a radio station licensed to serve Keystone, South Dakota. The station serves Rapid City, South Dakota, with an on-channel broadcast booster licensed as KRKI-FM1. The station is owned by Michael Radio Group, LLC, although a sale to Bad Lands Broadcasting Company was approved in January 2006 but remains unconsummated as of March 2010. KRKI airs a Classic Country format.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fcc.gov/fcc-bin/fmq?call=KRKI |title=KRKI Facility Record |work=United States Federal Communications Commission, audio division[/tpl]

==History==

The station signed on in 2000 as KVAM (changing its call letters to KRKI later that year) with a rhythmic format as Hip Hop Radio serving Newcastle, Wyoming. In 2007, the station upgraded to 100,000 watts and moved to Rapid City, South Dakota, with a format flip to the Real Country satellite network as US 99.5. On January 5, 2009, KRKI switched to a 24-hour simulcast of ESPN Radio becoming the first full-time sports talk station in the Rapid City market.[tpl]cite news |work=Rapid City Journal |url=http://www.rapidcityjournal.com/articles/2009/01/08/news/top/doc49659433a099e553501745.txt |title=Rapid City station makes switch to carry ESPN Talk Radio |date=January 8, 2009[/tpl]
On April 1, 2012, KRKI changed affiliations from ESPN Radio to Fox Sports Radio, while KTOQ switched to ESPN Radio the same day. [tpl]cite news |work=Rapid City Journal |url=http://rapidcityjournal.com/sports/area-scene-ktalk-now-an-espn-affiliate/article_a5f8dbde-7d2d-11e1-9a27-0019bb2963f4.html |title=Area Scene: KTALK now an ESPN affiliate |date=April 2, 2012[/tpl]
Effective October 8, 2012, it has now branded itself "99-5 The Range" and adopted the Classic Country format. [tpl]cite news |work=Radio & Television Business Report |url=http://rbr.com/michael-and-silberberg-swap-fms-in-sd-wy-border-area/ |title=Michael and Silberberg swap FMs in SD-WY border area |date=October 8, 2012[/tpl]
On November 29, 2012, KRKI's city of license was changed from Newcastle, Wyoming to Keystone, South Dakota.
