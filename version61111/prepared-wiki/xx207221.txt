[[Church of St Martin, Marple]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Stockport, Grade II* listed churches, Grade II* listed buildings in Greater Manchester, 1870s architecture, Church of England churches in Greater Manchester

The Church of St Martin is a 19th-century church in Marple, Greater Manchester, England ([tpl]gbmapping|SJ963894[/tpl]). 
It was designed by J. D. Sedding for Maria Anne Hudson (1819-1906) who lived in nearby Brabyns Hall and was built between 1869–70. The north chapel and aisle were both added later by H. Wilson in 1895–96 and 1909 respectively. The stained glass in the windows in the chancel are by the Morris company with designs by Edward Burne-Jones, Dante Gabrielle Rossetti, Ford Maddox Brown and William Morris. The organ from 1870 is by Henry Willis.
On 11 October 1985, it was designated a Grade II* listed building.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/Details/Default.aspx?id=441062 |title=Church of St Martin, Marple |accessdate=2008-07-26 |publisher=Images of England[/tpl]
