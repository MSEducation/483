[[Pyotr Novikov]]

CATEGORIES: 1901 births, 1975 deaths, Russian mathematicians, Soviet mathematicians, 20th-century mathematicians, Moscow State University alumni, Logicians, Group theorists, Full Members of the USSR Academy of Sciences

Pyotr Sergeyevich Novikov ([tpl]lang-ru|Пётр Серге́евич Но́виков[/tpl]; 15 August 1901, Moscow, Russian Empire – 9 January 1975, Moscow, Soviet Union) was a Soviet mathematician.
Novikov is known for his work on combinatorial problems in group theory: the word problem for groups, and Burnside's problem. For proving the undecidability of the word problem in groups he was awarded the Lenin Prize in 1957.S. I. Adian, Mathematical logic, the theory of algorithms and the theory of sets, AMS Bookstore, 1977, ISBN 0-8218-3033-3, p. 26.
In 1953 he became a corresponding member of the USSR Academy of Sciences and in 1960 he was elected a full member.
He was married to the mathematician Lyudmila Keldysh (1904–1976). The mathematician Sergei Novikov is his son. Sergei Adian and Albert Muchnik were among his students.
