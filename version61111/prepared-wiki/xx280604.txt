[[James L. Manley]]

CATEGORIES: Articles created via the Article Wizard, American biologists, Living people, Columbia University faculty, American scientists, 1949 births

James Manley, PhD, is a JC Levi Professor at Columbia University, where his laboratory researches gene expression in animal cells.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.columbia.edu/cu/biology/faculty-data/james-manley/faculty.html|title=Dr. James Manley|publisher=columbia.edu|accessdate=2014-04-18[/tpl] From 1996 to 2006, he was the recipient of the NIH MERIT Award for his work in mRNA splicing. In 2006, he was elected as a fellow to the American Academy of Arts and Sciences, and in 2011, he was elected to the National Academy of Sciences.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nasonline.org/member-directory/members/20024806.html|title=James Manley|publisher=nasonline.org|accessdate=2014-04-18[/tpl]

==Career==

After completing his BS at Columbia University in 1971, James conducted research at Cold Spring Harbor Laboratory, leading to his thesis "Properties of in vivo and in vitro synthesized abnormal polypeptides encoded by the lacZ gene of E. coli". He did postdoctoral research at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology before being hired to the Columbia University Department of Biological Sciences at an Assistant Professor in 1980.[tpl]cite web|url=http://stemcell.ny.gov/james-l-manley|title=James L.  Manley | NYSTEM|publisher=stemcell.ny.gov|accessdate=2014-04-18[/tpl]  He was named a Julian Clarence Professor of Life Sciences in 1995, the same year in which he was elected Chair of the department, a position he held until 2001.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.columbia.edu/cu/biology/faculty/manley/Manley%20CV%20.pdf|title=CURRICULUM VITAE|author=Anthony J Gomez|date=25 February 2009|accessdate=18 April 2014[/tpl]
