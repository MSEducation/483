[[Lithuania national under-17 football team]]

CATEGORIES: European national under-17 association football teams, Football in Lithuania

The Lithuania national under-17 football team or Lithuania U-17 represents Lithuania in association football at the under-17 youth level, and is controlled by the Lithuanian Football Federation.
The team is for Lithuanian players aged 17 or under at the start of a two-year European Under-17 Football Championship cycle, so players appearing for the team can actually be up to 19 years of age. It is considered a feeder team of the Lithuania under-19 and under-21 teams.

==Competition history==

Prior to Lithuania's independence in 1991 Lithuanian players were eligible for selection to the Soviet Union U-16 team. Following the dissolution of the Soviet Union, the Lithuanian Football Federation was admitted to UEFA as a full member in 1992, and the Lithuania U-16 team played their first competitive matches in the first phase of the qualifying tournament for the 1994 European U-16 Championship. The team's competitive debut came on 30 August 1993 against Wales U-16 and they finished their first qualifying campaign as 3rd out of 3 teams, behind Wales and Iceland.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/ec-u16-94.html|title=European U-16 Championship 1994|last=Garin|first=Erik|date=7 December 2003|work=Rec.Sport.Soccer Statistics Foundation|accessdate=30 December 2010[/tpl]
Although the team continued to participate in every under-16 and under-17 European Championship qualifying cycle since 1994, the team never qualified for any of the tournaments.
