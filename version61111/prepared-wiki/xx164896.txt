[[Gulf State Park]]

CATEGORIES: State parks of Alabama, Protected areas of Baldwin County, Alabama

Gulf State Park is a [tpl]convert|6150|acre|adj=on[/tpl] state park in Gulf Shores, Alabama in southern Baldwin County. The park property mostly covers the land behind the Gulf Shores beach community, between Highway 59 and SH 161, but the west end extends further south to a wide beach area along the Gulf of Mexico (see photo right).[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.alapark.com/GulfState/ |title=Gulf State Park |work=Alabama State Parks |publisher=Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural Resources  |accessdate=2012-11-06[/tpl] There are three spring-fed, fresh-water lakes within Gulf State Park: Lake Shelby ([tpl]convert|750|acres|ha|disp=or[/tpl]), Middle Lake and Little Lake.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.alapark.com/GulfState/Trails/ |title=Gulf State Park (Trails)|work=Alabama State Parks |publisher=Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural Resources |accessdate=2012-11-06[/tpl]

==History==

Gulf State Park has weathered several hurricanes but was largely devastated by Hurricane Ivan in 2004. Major projects undertaken in the wake of the storm saw the construction of the longest fishing pier in the Gulf of Mexico ([tpl]convert|1,540|ft|m|disp=or[/tpl]),[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.alapark.com/GulfState/Gulf%20State%20Park%20Pier/ |title=Gulf State Park (Pier) |work=Alabama State Parks |publisher=Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural Resources  |accessdate=2013-02-02[/tpl] a 5,000-square-foot swimming pool and splash pad, an interactive nature center, a beach pavilion, and complete renovation of the campground.

==Activities and amenities==

The park includes [tpl]convert|2.5|mi[/tpl] of white sand beaches along the Gulf, a modern campground for both RVs and tents, rental cottages, and nature trails, plus both fresh and saltwater fishing and swimming. There are 496 campsites within the park's [tpl]convert|29.9|km2|sqmi|disp=flip[/tpl]; all have electrical, water, and sewage hook-ups.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.alapark.com/GulfState/Camping/ |title=Gulf State Park (Camping) |work=Alabama State Parks |publisher=Alabama Department of Conservation and Natural Resources |accessdate=2013-02-02[/tpl]  The park's 18-hole championship golf course is open seven days a week and includes a proshop and snack bar.
Security is provided around the clock by Park Enforcement Rangers.
