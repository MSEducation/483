Abhra Mondal ([tpl]lang-bn|অভ্র মন্ডল[/tpl]) is an Indian footballer, who is currently playing at East Bengal FC in I-League.[tpl]cite web|title=INDIAN PLAYER TRANSFER GOSSIP-RUMOR- 2013-14: Part 21 | url= http://www.kolkatafootball.com/transfer_gossip_india/index.html#2 |publisher=www.kolkatafootball.com[/tpl] He plays as a Goalkeeper. Abhra Mondal helped East Bengal FC win the Federation Cup in 2009 after three tremendous saves in the penalty shoot-out.http://goal.com/en-india/news/1773/federation-cup/2010/01/04/1724371/federation-cup-it-was-the-best-day-of-my-career-east-bengal

==Career==

===East Bengal===

Mondal signed for East Bengal FC in 2005 and ever since Mondal has mostly been used in the Federation Cup matches.

===Loan to Pune===

On 24 November 2011 Mondal was officially loaned out by East Bengal to fellow I-League club Pune F.C. for the 2011-12 I-League season.[tpl]cite news |title= Pune FC sign ’keeper Abhra ‘on-loan’|url=http://punefc.com/?news=pune-fc-sign-’keeper-abhra-‘on-loan’ |work=Pune F.C. |publisher=Pune F.C. |date=24 November 2011 |accessdate=8 January 2011[/tpl] He then made his debut for Pune against Air India FC in the I-League and helped Pune to the 2-0 victory.http://www.goal.com/en-india/match/70696/pune-fc-vs-air-india/report
In his short stint, till he suffered a shin bone injury in a match against Lajong FC, he ensured that his side remained unbeaten in all the matches he played in I-League.

===Pune F.C===

On 11 July 2012, he signed a full contract with the club. This came after an impressive 11-match unbeaten streak and turned out to be hailed as the biggest protagonist in the club's record and longest unbeaten run ever in the I-League.http://punefc.com/?news=pune-fc-squad-revamped-and-geared-up-for-2012-13-season

===East Bengal===

After a spending two years at Pune, Mondal returned to East Bengal for the 2013-14 season.
