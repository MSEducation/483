[[Permian Investment Partners]]

CATEGORIES: Hedge fund firms in New York City

Permian Investment Partners is a hedge fund founded in 2008 by partners Alex Duran and Scott Hendrickson with offices currently located at 295 Madison Avenue, New York NY.[tpl]cite web|title=SEC Form ADV|url=http://www.adviserinfo.sec.gov/IAPD/Firm/156893[/tpl] The fund employs a long/short equity strategy with a focus on management change and management quality, predominantly in Western Europe.[tpl]cite web|title=Emerging Manager Monthly|url=http://www.emergingmanagermonthly.com/2008/07/02/permian-looks-east-to-western-europe-for-investment-opportunities/[/tpl] The firm's name is derived from the Permian geological period.[tpl]cite web|title=HFM Week Online|url=http://www.hfmweek.com/comment/the-short/will-wainewright/whats-in-a-name-permian-investment-partners[/tpl]
The firm was the recipient of the 2012 Europe-Focused Global Emerging Manager award presented by S&P Capital IQ.[tpl]cite web|title=S&P Capital IQ|url=http://www.emergingmanagerawards.com[/tpl] In November 2011, Permian was among several large shareholders to publicly oppose Sun Pharmaceuticals' offer to acquire Taro Pharmaceuticals.[tpl]cite web|title=PR Newswire|url=http://www.prnewswire.com/news-releases/permian-investment-partners-opposes-sun-pharmaceuticals-offer-to-acquire-taro-pharmaceutical-industries-at-2450-per-share-133844908.html[/tpl]
