Payne Harry Ratner (October 3, 1896 â€“ December 27, 1974) was the 28th Governor of Kansas from 1939 to 1943. 

==Biography==

Born in Casey, Illinois, Ratner graduated from Blackwell High School in Oklahoma. During World War I he served as an ensign in the U.S. Navy. He earned a law degree at Washington University in St. Louis in 1920.[tpl]cite web|title=Payne Ratner|url=http://www.nga.org/cms/home/governors/past-governors-bios/page_kansas/col2-content/main-content-list/title_ratner_payne.html|publisher=National Governors Association|accessdate=3 October 2012[/tpl] He married Cliffe Dodd on August 21, 1920[tpl]cite web|title=Payne Ratner|url=http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/ransome-rattliff.html|publisher=The Political Graveyard|accessdate=3 October 2012[/tpl]  and they had three children, Jurie, Teno, and Darb.

==Career==

Ratner practiced law in Sibley, Iowa, and then in Parsons, Kansas. In Parsons, he was the Labette County Attorney from 1923 to 1927. He was elected as a Republican to the Kansas Senate in 1929 and also served as State Senator from 1937 to 1939.[tpl]cite web|title=Payne Ratner|url=http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/ransome-rattliff.html|publisher=The Political Graveyard|accessdate=3 October 2012[/tpl] 
Winning the 1938 Republican gubernatorial nomination and the election, Ratner was sworn in as Governor of Kansas on January 9, 1939. He was reelected in 1940. During his tenure, a department of labor was established, a department of revenue and taxation was organized, the highway commission was given authority over the vehicle department, a teacher's pension plan was implemented, a small permanent building fund for schools was authorized, and the state fire marshal's office, the hotel commission, and the inspector of restaurants were re-established.[tpl]cite web|title=Payne Ratner|url=http://www.nga.org/cms/home/governors/past-governors-bios/page_kansas/col2-content/main-content-list/title_ratner_payne.html|publisher=National Governors Association|accessdate=3 October 2012[/tpl] He left office on January 11, 1943 and retired from politics. In 1962 Ratner was indicted on unethical legal activities; the case was dismissed; and he was cleared of all charges.

==Death==

Ratner died in 1974 in Wichita, Kansas. He is interred at the Wichita Park Cemetery and Mausoleum, Wichita, Sedgwick County, Kansas USA.[tpl]cite web|title=Payne Ratner|url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=6635568|publisher=Find A Grave|accessdate=3 October 2012[/tpl]
