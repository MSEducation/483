[[Badgingarra National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1973, Wheatbelt (Western Australia)

Badgingarra National Park is a national park in Western Australia (Australia), 190Â km north of Perth off the Brand Highway adjacent to the town of Badgingarra, Western Australia.
The Park is [tpl]convert|13108|ha[/tpl] in area and features high breakaway country overlooking low undulating sandplains. The park is renowned for its incredible diversity of endemic wildflowers.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dec.wa.gov.au/component/option,com_hotproperty/task,view/id,66/Itemid,1584/|publisher=Government of Western Australia|title=Park Finder Badgingarra National Park[/tpl]
Mullering Brook passes through the park creating a swampy area.
The area is mostly composed of low scrub with plant species such as mottlecah, smokebush, Banksia, Verticordia, kangaroo paw and the rare Badgingarra mallee are found throughout the area.
The area is threatened by the spread of dieback.
Some of the spectacular wildflowers that can be found within the park include rare species such as Hakea flabellifolia, Strangea cynanchicarpa and Eucalyptus pendens.[tpl]cite AHD|10200|Badgingarra National Park|date=2010|accessdate=14 November 2010[/tpl]
Many animals such as western grey kangaroos, emus, bustards and wedgetail eagles also inhabit the area.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.about-australia.com/travel-guides/western-australia/australias-coral-coast/attractions/natural/badgingarra-national-park/ |title=About Australia - Badgingarra Valley National Park|year=2008|accessdate=30 April 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100326154030/http://www.about-australia.com/travel-guides/western-australia/australias-coral-coast/attractions/natural/badgingarra-national-park/| archivedate= 26 March 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl]
