[[Kondut, Western Australia]]

CATEGORIES: Wheatbelt (Western Australia), Grain receival points of Western Australia

Kondut is a small town in Wheatbelt region of Western Australia.
The first European to visit and chart the area was the surveyor C Crossland in 1884. He named a nearby well Conduit Well.
The town was originally a railway siding that was constructed in 1913 as part of the Dowerin to Mullewa line.
The townsite was later gazetted in 1917.[tpl]LandInfo WA|c|K|7 October 2008[/tpl]
The surrounding areas produce wheat and other cereal crops. The town is a receival site for Cooperative Bulk Handling.[tpl]cite web|url=https://www.cbh.com.au/media/120302/cbh%20receival%20sites%20-%20contact%20details.pdf|title=CBH receival sites|year=2011|accessdate=1 April 2013[/tpl]
