The economy of California is large enough to be comparable to that of the largest of countries. [tpl]As of|2012[/tpl], the gross state product (GSP) is about $2.003 trillion, the largest in the United States.[tpl]cite web|title=GDP by State|url=http://www.bea.gov/newsreleases/regional/gdp_state/gsp_newsrelease.htm|publisher=U.S. Bureau of Economic Analysis|accessdate=January 22, 2014[/tpl] California is responsible for 12.9 percent of the United States' approximate $15.5 trillion gross domestic product (GDP). California's GDP is larger than that of all but 8 countries in dollar terms (the United States, China, Japan, Germany, France, Brazil, the United Kingdom, and Italy). California's GDP is larger than the GDPs of Russia, India, Canada, Australia, and Spain; in terms of Purchasing Power Parity,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.businessweek.com/ap/financialnews/D9JS1MLO0.htm |title=Calif. retains economy that would be 8th largest|publisher=Businessweek.com |date=December 2, 2010 |accessdate=September 2, 2012[/tpl] it is larger than all but 9 countries (the United States, China, India, Japan, Germany, Russia, Brazil, France, the United Kingdom, Italy), larger than Mexico, South Korea, Spain, Canada, and Turkey.
In terms of jobs, the five largest sectors in California are trade, transportation, and utilities; government; professional and business services; education and health services; and leisure and hospitality. In terms of output, the five largest sectors are financial services, followed by trade, transportation, and utilities; education and health services; government; and manufacturing.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lao.ca.gov/reports/2011/calfacts/calfacts_010511.aspx |title=2011 CalFacts |publisher=Lao.ca.gov |accessdate=April 22, 2011[/tpl]  [tpl]as of|2013|11[/tpl], California has the 6th highest unemployment rate in the nation at 8.5%.[ref]
</ref>
California's economy is very dependent on trade and international related commerce accounts for approximately one-quarter of the state's economy. In 2008, California exported $144 billion worth of goods, up from $134 billion in 2007 and $127 billion in 2006.[ref]
</ref>
Computers and electronic products are California's top export, accounting for 42 percent of all the state's exports in 2008.
Agriculture is an important sector in California's economy. Farming-related sales more than quadrupled over the past three decades, from $7.3 billion in 1974 to nearly $31 billion in 2004.[ref]
</ref> This increase has occurred despite a 15 percent decline in acreage devoted to farming during the period, and water supply suffering from chronic instability. Factors contributing to the growth in sales-per-acre include more intensive use of active farmlands and technological improvements in crop production.<ref name="Cal Facts 2006 State Economy"/> In 2008, California's 81,500 farms and ranches generated $36.2 billion products revenue.<ref name="California Agricultural Production Statistics 2009â€“2010">
[/ref] In 2011, that number grew to $43.5 billion products revenue.
