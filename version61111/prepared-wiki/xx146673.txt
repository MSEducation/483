[[Azra Erhat]]

CATEGORIES: 1915 births, 1982 deaths, People from Istanbul, Turkish writers, Place of death missing, Turkish translators, Translators from Greek, Translators to Turkish

Azra Erhat (b. 6 June 1915, Istanbul – d. 6 September 1982, Istanbul) was a Turkish author, archaeologist, academician and translator.

==Biography==

Together with Cevat Şakir Kabaağaçlı (alias Halikarnas Balıkçısı, "The Fisherman of Halicarnassus") and Sabahattin Eyüboğlu, she is also in the origin of the literary and touristic term, "the Blue Cruise" ([tpl]lang-tr|Mavi Yolculuk[/tpl]). She is notable of translating Homer's Iliad and Odyssey, in collaboration with A. Kadir, which are admitted to be the references in the Turkish language for these works.
