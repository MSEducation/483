[[Elmer L. Meyers Junior/Senior High School]]

CATEGORIES: 1930 establishments in Pennsylvania, Public middle schools in Pennsylvania, Public high schools in Pennsylvania, Schools in Luzerne County, Pennsylvania, Educational institutions established in 1930

Elmer L. Meyers Junior/Senior High School (commonly known throughout the area as 'E. L. Meyers' or 'Meyers') is an urban, public school located on 341 Carey Avenue, in Wilkes-Barre, Pennsylvania.  It is one of three public high schools in the Wilkes-Barre Area School District.  Meyers is both a junior and senior public high school, offering education to approximately 949 students (2010) in grades 7–12. The student to teacher ratio is approximately 14 students per teacher.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.publicschoolreview.com/school_ov/school_id/71373|title=Public School Profile|accessdate=2009-02-05[/tpl] Meyers' sports teams are called "Mohawks." Meyers and G.A.R. High Schools have one of the longest standing sports rivalries in the Wyoming Valley.

==Speech and Debate==

E. L. Meyers High School is known across the country for its speech and debate team. Founded in 1997, Meyers hosts the Martin Luther King Open Speech and Debate Tournament every January. The team was tied for a national championship with four other schools in 2009 at the National Catholic Forensic League championships held in Albany, NY.[tpl]cite news|title=Meyers shares national debate title|url=http://www.timesleader.com/news/hottopics/goodnews/Meyers_shares_national_debate_title_05-28-2009.html|date=June 24, 2009|accessdate=2009-06-24|archivedate=2009-06-24|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5hmFOmYx8|deadurl=no[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ncfl.org/gnt/gntpast/assets/NCFL2009FinalResults-Consolidated.pdf|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20100926112357/http://ncfl.org/gnt/gntpast/assets/NCFL2009FinalResults-Consolidated.pdf|archivedate=2010-09-26 |title=NCFL 2009 Consolidated Final Results |format=PDF |accessdate=2010-10-19[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.meyersspeechanddebate.com/ |title=E.L.Meyers Speech and Debate |publisher=Meyersspeechanddebate.com |accessdate=2011-06-08[/tpl]

==Fight song==

The director of marching band obtained official copies of and permission to perform the Florida State Seminoles' War Chant.[tpl]cite news|title=Seminoles Flatten Syracuse|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1991/10/06/sports/college-football-seminoles-flatten-syracuse.html|first=Malcolm|last=Moran|date=October 6, 1991|work=New York Times:New York Edition|page=8.1|accessdate=2009-06-11[/tpl]  This song is used as a fight song for the school–especially during sporting events–and is often accompanied by the waving of a tomahawk or rigid arm (to signify the war axe).
