[[Saleh Al-Fawzan]]

CATEGORIES: 1933 births, Living people, Saudi Arabian Muslims, Muslim writers, Sunni Muslim scholars of Islam, Hanbalis

Saleh Al-Fawzan (Arabic: صالح بن فوزان الفوزان), born 1933,[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl] is an Islamic scholar and a member of the highest religious committees in Saudi Arabia.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
His surname is also rendered Fozan or Fawzaan. He is also known as Saleh Ibn Fawzan Ibn Abdullah, Saleh Ibn Fawzan al-Fawzan, Saalih ibn Fawzaan ibn 'Abdullaah al-Fawzaan, Ibn al-Fowzaan and Ibn al-Fawzaan.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]

==Biography==

Fawzan is from the family of Fawzan from the people/tribe of ash-Shamaasiyyah.
Fawzan's father died when he was of a young age. He was subsequently brought up by his family and learned the Qur'ân, the basics of reading and writing by the Imam of the Masjid of their town.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]

==Education==

Fawzan studied in the state school in ash-Qamariyah when it opened 1948. [tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
In 1950 he completed his studies at the Faysaliyyah school in Buraydah and subsequently was appointed a teacher at the school.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
When it opened in 1952, Fawzan joined the Educational Institute in Buraydah and subsequently graduated from there in 1956.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
Fawzan was a student of Imam Muhammad ibn Saud Islamic University in Riyadh, initially studying at the faculty of Sharia and graduating in 1960; he later on earned his master's and doctorate from the same university, focusing on Fiqh.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]

===Career===

After the completion of his doctorate, he was a teacher at the faculty of Shari'ah at his alma mater before being transferred into the department of the fundamentals of religion, or Department for Higher Studies within the Faculty of the Principles of the Religion (usool ad-deen).[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl] He was later the head of Saudi Arabia's Supreme Court of Justice.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
He is currently a member of the Permanent Committee for Islamic Research and Fataawa http://www.fatwa-online.com/scholarsbiographies/15thcentury/permanentcommittee.htm, Saudi Arabia's highest religious body, a member of the Council of Religious Edicts and Research.
He is also the Imam, khateeb and teacher, of Prince Mitaeb Mosque (Prince Mut'ib Ibn 'Abdul-'Azeez Masjid), in al-Malzar in Riyadh.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
Besides his main role on the Permanent Committee, he is a member of the Council of Senior Scholars, and member of the Fiqh Committee in Makkah (part of ar-Raabitah), and a member of the Committee for Supervision of the Callers (du'aat) in Hajj.[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
He also contributes to answering questions on the radio program "Noorun 'alad-Darb" (Light along the Path).[tpl]fact|date=March 2014[/tpl]
