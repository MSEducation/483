Musa Shannon (born 1 August 1975) is a Liberian football administrator and former striker.
Shannon played professionally in the United States, Portugal and China; he also competed at international level.

==Early and personal life==

Shannon was born in the United States, where his Liberian parents were attending Syracuse University. Shannon was raised in the Liberian capital of Monrovia, before returning to the United States as a fifteen-year-old in 1990 following the escalation of the First Liberian Civil War.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.liberiansoccer.com/Musa_Shannon_Heads_FCAK-Lib20071234567.htm|title=Musa Shannon Heads FCAK-Lib|accessdate=1 January 2011|author=Juju Johnson|publisher=LiberianSoccer.com[/tpl]

===National team===

Shannon also represented Liberia at international level, scoring 1 goal in 12 appearances between 2000 and 2001.

===Administration career===

Shannon was named as President of FCAK-Liberia in 2008.
In 2010, Shannon was elected to the position of Vice-President of the Liberia Football Association.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/89/africa/2010/12/09/2253524/exclusive-lfa-vice-president-musa-shannon-admits-liberian|title=EXCLUSIVE: LFA Vice-President Musa Shannon Admits Liberian Football Is Under Construction|accessdate=1 January 2011|date=9 December 2010|author=K.N.S Mensah|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl]
