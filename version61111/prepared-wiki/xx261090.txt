[[Carmenta theobromae]]

CATEGORIES: Sesiidae

The Cocoa Fruit Borer (Carmenta theobromae) is a moth of the Sesiidae family.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nic.funet.fi/pub/sci/bio/life/insecta/lepidoptera/ditrysia/sesioidea/sesiidae/sesiinae/carmenta/index.html |title=Carmenta at funet.fi |publisher=Nic.funet.fi |date= |accessdate=2011-12-16[/tpl] It was described by Busck in 1910, and is known from Colombia and Venezuela.
The larva of the species feed on Theobroma cacao. They perforate the pods of their host plant and are considered a serious pest.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bioone.org/doi/abs/10.1653/024.092.0222 |title=Pupal Development, Longevity and Behavior of Carmenta theobromae (Lepidoptera: Sesiidae) |publisher=Bioone.org |date=2009-07-01 |accessdate=2011-12-16[/tpl]
