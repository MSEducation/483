[[Tell me]]

CATEGORIES: Politics of Hong Kong, 2005 in Hong Kong

right|200pxTell me is the title of an advertisement calling for universal suffrage in Hong Kong.  It ran once as a full page ad on the front page of most Hong Kong newspapers on 28 October 2005, and inspired many other people and groups in Hong Kong to run advertisements supporting democracy.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.hku.hk/ppaa/articles/article5.htm|title=幻滅了的政改方案|publisher=Politics and Public Administration Association sss hkusu, The University of Hong Kong|year=2006|accessdate=22 September 2007|language=Traditional Chinese[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]

It was written in white text on a dark background. It also included a picture of an hourglass.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.atchinese.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=10001&Itemid=66|title=香港民主運動轉由議會外主導|work=Asia Times|date=14 November 2005|accessdate=22 September 2007|language=Traditional Chinese[/tpl] About HK$200,000 ($25,600) was spent in placing the ads. In 2007, two more ads were placed costing about HK$100,000.  The old man quoted in the ad worked in the property industry and is now retired. Legislator James To assisted him in designing and placing the advertisements.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.rfa.org/cantonese/zhuanti/jujiaoxianggang/2005/11/16/hongkong_reform/?simple=1|title=聚焦香港﹕香港政改討論的對抗升溫--姬勵思|publisher=Radio Free Asia|date=16 November 2005|accessdate=22 September 2007|language=Traditional Chinese[/tpl]
