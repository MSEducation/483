[[Lake Kickapoo]]

CATEGORIES: Protected areas of Archer County, Texas, Reservoirs in Texas, 1947 establishments in Texas, Protected areas established in 1947

Lake Kickapoo is a reservoir in Archer County, Texas. Created in 1947. It has a surface area of [tpl]convert|6,200|acre|km2[/tpl]. Named after the Kickapoo tribe native to the area.
One of the nine Air Force Space Surveillance System (formerly NAVSPASUR) sites is located at Lake Kickapoo ([tpl]coord|33|32.764|N|98|45.763|W|type:landmark_region:US[/tpl]). It is the master transmitter and the most powerful continuous wave (CW) station in the world, at 768Â kW radiated power.
