[[Naomi Radcliffe]]

CATEGORIES: English soap opera actresses, Living people, People from Oldham, Alumni of the Royal Welsh College of Music & Drama, 1971 births

Naomi Radcliffe (born 1971) is an English actress. She was born in Oldham, Lancashire to Albert Radcliffe—a Canon of Manchester Cathedral—and Petrina, a teacher.[tpl]cite news|first= Jame|last= Oddy|title= Naomi Radcliffe: How I found life after death|work= Daily Mirror|date= 2002-04-27[/tpl] She was educated at Blue Coats School in Oldham and the Royal Welsh College of Music and Drama, having not been accepted on her first attempt.[tpl]cite web|title= Born and Bred: Naomi Radcliffe|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/drama/born_and_bred_jean.shtml |work= bbc.co.uk (at the Internet Archive)|date= 2002-10-30|accessdate= 2008-01-02 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20021030015304/http://www.bbc.co.uk/drama/born_and_bred_jean.shtml |archivedate = 2002-10-30[/tpl]
Her first professional stage appearance was in Be My Baby in 1997. Early television appearances include major roles in Kay Mellor's Band of Gold and Russell T Davies's The Grand, before she took the role of Alison Wakefield in the Granada Television soap opera Coronation Street. She remained on the soap for two years between 1998 and 2000. When her contract was not renewed, the character was written out in a scene that saw her commit suicide by jumping in front of a lorry. The scene, watched by 17 million viewers, won Radcliffe the British Soap Award for Best Exit.[tpl]cite news|last= Staff writer|title= EastEnders triumph at UK soap awards|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/1353527.stm|work= BBC News Online|date= 2001-05-23|accessdate= 2008-01-02[/tpl] From 2002 to 2005 she played Jean Bradshaw in the BBC drama Born and Bred. In 2007 she played Bev in the BBC Three situation comedy The Visit.
