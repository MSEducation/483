[[Skopska Crna Gora]]

CATEGORIES: Mountains of Kosovo, Landforms of the Republic of Macedonia, Kosovo–Republic of Macedonia border, International mountains of Europe

Skopska Crna Gora (meaning "Black Mountain of Skopje"; Macedonian and [tpl]lang-sr|Скопска Црна Гора[/tpl], [tpl]IPA-sh|skôpskaː tsr̩̂ːnaː ɡǒra|pron[/tpl]), also historically known as Kara-dagh (Turkish for "black mountain", [tpl]lang-sq|Mali i Zi or Malet e Karadakut[/tpl]) during Ottoman times, is a mountain range on the border between Kosovo[tpl]ref label|status|a|[/tpl] and the Republic of Macedonia, between the cities of Kačanik (in southern Kosovo) and Skopje (in Macedonia). The highest peaks are Ramno (1,651 m), Sokolovec and Dušanovec. The population speak the Skopska Crna Gora dialect.
