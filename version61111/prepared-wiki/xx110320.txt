==History==

The station went on the air as KRSS. On 1991-04-29, the station changed its call sign to the current KSBN.[tpl]cite web|url=http://licensing.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/call_hist.pl?Facility_id=64668&Callsign=KSBN |title=KSBN Call Sign History |work=United States Federal Communications Commission, audio division [/tpl]
"Mothballed Local Radio Station Born Again." Spokesman-Review. Spokane, Washington. 14 September 1985. Page 7.
The station's current format, all business, was established by TCC Broadcasting in 1990 after the station went off the air for a short time.  Owner/Manager Tom Cock sold the station in 1995 to KSBN Radio, Inc.
