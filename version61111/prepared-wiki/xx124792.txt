[[Cass, New Zealand]]

CATEGORIES: Selwyn District, Populated places in New Zealand

Cass is a locality in the Selwyn District of the Canterbury region in New Zealand's South Island.[tpl]LINZ|17880|Cass|3 November 2007[/tpl]
It is named for Thomas Cass, an important pioneer surveyor in the area.Peter Bromley Maling, "Thomas Cass", 1966 Encyclopaedia of New Zealand.  State Highway 73 passes near the town, and the Midland Line between Christchurch and the West Coast was opened to the town in 1910, with the full line opened in 1923 when the Otira Tunnel was completed.  Since November 1987, the world-famous TranzAlpine passenger train has run through the town.
It is one of the few places in the world with a population of one resident. there are only 5 batches there today[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/travel/news/article.cfm?c_id=7&objectid=10630916 |title=Cass: Colourful  character right  on track |author=Eagles, Jim |date=10 March 2010 |work=The New Zealand Herald |accessdate=23 September 2011[/tpl]
For some time from 1910, while the Midland Line was under construction (and in particular, the tunnel at Arthur's Pass), Cass was the railhead coming from the east coast. At that time, Cass had a population of around 800.[tpl]cite web |title=Cass |url= http://www.greatalpinehighway.co.nz/region_page.php?type=page&page_id=58 |publisher=The Great Alpine Highway |accessdate=7 April 2013[/tpl] The Cass railway station was painted in 1936 by Rita Angus; it is "one of New Zealand's best-loved works of art".[tpl]cite web |last=Atkinson |first=Neill |title=Railways - Stations and refreshment rooms |url= http://www.teara.govt.nz/en/artwork/21411/rita-anguss-cass |publisher=Te Ara - the Encyclopedia of New Zealand |accessdate=7 April 2013 |date=4 February 2013[/tpl]
