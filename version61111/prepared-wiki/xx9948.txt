[[Peninsula]]

CATEGORIES: Peninsulas, Coastal geography

A peninsula ([tpl]lang-la|paeninsula[/tpl] from paene "almost" and insula "island"; also called a byland or biland) is a piece of land that is bordered by water on three sides but connected to mainland.[ref]
</ref> The surrounding water is usually understood to belong to a single, contiguous body,<ref name = "AH_Dictionary">
</ref><ref name = "Cambr_Dictionary">
</ref> but is not always explicitly defined as such.<ref name = "Merriam_Webster">
</ref>
A peninsula can also be a  headland (head), cape, island promontory, bill, point, or spit.Peninsula. – Britannica Student Encyclopedia. 2007. Encyclopædia Britannica, Retrieved 2007-07-19. Note that a point is generally considered a tapering piece of land projecting into a body of water that is less prominent than a cape.http://travelingluck.com/Africa/Sierra%20Leone/Northern/_2409328_Fourah+Point.html#local_map In English, the plural of peninsula is peninsulas or, less commonly, peninsulae.
