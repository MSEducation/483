[[Vietnamese parliamentary election, 2011]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in Vietnam, 2011 elections in Asia, 2011 in Vietnam

A parliamentary election was held in Vietnam on 22 May 2011.[tpl]cite news |first= |url=http://www.thanhniennews.com/2010/Pages/20110516100659.aspx |title=Truong Sa residents cast early votes in legislative elections |work=Thanh Nien |date=May 20, 2011 |accessdate= [/tpl] Since Vietnam is a single-party state, the ruling Communist Party of Vietnam is guaranteed to win.[tpl]cite news |first= |url=http://www.google.com/hostednews/afp/article/ALeqM5if2Z8EcyMNAQO70JpKCuTR8SGcTw |title=One-party Vietnam votes in national election |author=Ian Timberlake |publisher=AFP |date=May 22, 2011 |accessdate=May 22, 2011 [/tpl]
According to the Ministry of Home Affairs, there were 827 candidates. 31.4% were women, 14.3% were not members of the Communist Party, 16.1% were members of ethnic minorities and 22.1% were candidates running for reelection.http://english.people.com.cn/90001/90777/90851/7387493.html
500 MPs were elected. 333 are first-time MPs, four are self-nominated. Almost all of them have at least a bachelor's degree; 15.6% are from ethnic minorities, 24.4% are women, and 8.4% are not members of the Communist Party. Turnout was 99.51%,http://english.people.com.cn/90001/90777/90851/7400242.html with the northern mountain provinces of Lai Châu, Hà Giang, Hòa Bình and Lạng Sơn reporting a turnout of 99.99%http://english.vovnews.vn/Home/General-Secretary-calls-for-election-analysis/20115/127060.vov
