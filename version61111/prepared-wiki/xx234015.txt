[[Harry Topping]]

CATEGORIES: 1913 births, People from Kearsley, 2001 deaths, English footballers, Manchester City F.C. players, Exeter City F.C. players, New Brighton F.C. players, Bristol Rovers F.C. players, Stockport County F.C. wartime guest players, English football managers, Feyenoord managers, PSV Eindhoven managers, English expatriate football managers

Harry Topping (21 September 1913 – 2001) was an English football player (who played at full back) and coach.

==Career==

===Playing career===

Topping was born in Kearsley, Lancashire[tpl]cite web|last=Joyce|first=Michael|title=Harry Topping|url=http://sports.groups.yahoo.com/group/soccerdatauk/message/83|publisher=SoccerData UK|accessdate=27 August 2010|date=18 January 2007[/tpl] and spent time with several Football League clubs, including Manchester City and Exeter City, where he made one league appearance, before a short spell at  New Brighton.[tpl]cite book |last=Joyce |first=Michael |title=Football League Players' Records 1888 to 1939 |publisher=SoccerData (Tony Brown) |location=Nottingham |year=2004 |page=261 |isbn=978-1-899468-67-6[/tpl]
During World War II he played for Stockport County, where he partnered his namesake Henry Westby Topping in several wartime matches in 1941–42. He played for Bristol Rovers in the FA Cup campaign of 1945–46.[tpl]cite web|first=|title=EURO 2008 Connections: Holland|url=http://www.bristolrovers.co.uk/page/LatestNews/0,,10328~1325830,00.html|publisher=Bristol Rovers FC|accessdate=27 August 2010|date=10 June 2008[/tpl]

===Coaching career===

As a football coach, Topping managed two of the Netherlands' biggest clubs in the early 1950s — Feyenoord between 1950 and 1951, and PSV between 1951 and 1952.
He was coach to Norwich City in their run to the 1958–59 FA Cup semi-final.[tpl]cite web|title=Your Memories of 1958-59|url=http://www.canaries.co.uk/page/cuprun/0,,10355~1510356,00.html|publisher=Norwich City FC|accessdate=27 August 2010|date=15 January 2009[/tpl]  After leaving Norwich he coached Torquay United.[tpl]cite web|title=Torbay AC celebrates its 40th anniversary|url=http://www.torbayaac.co.uk/Pages/club_history.htm|publisher=Torbay Athletic Club|accessdate=27 August 2010[/tpl]
