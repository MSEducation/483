[[SS Empire Beaumont]]

CATEGORIES: 1942 ships, Tees-built ships, Empire ships, Ministry of War Transport ships, Steamships of the United Kingdom, Ships sunk by aircraft, Maritime incidents in 1942, World War II shipwrecks in the Arctic Ocean

Empire Beaumont was a 7,044 GRT cargo ship which was built by Furness Shipbuilding Ltd, Haverton Hill-on-Tees in 1942. She was owned by the Ministry of War Transport (MoWT) and managed by W Runciman & Co Ltd. Empire Beaumont had a short career, being sunk on 13 September 1942 while a member of Convoy PQ 18.

==Description==

Empire Beaumont was built by Furness Shipbuilding Co Ltd, Haverton Hill-on-Tees.[tpl]cite book | first = and Sawyer, L A| last = Mitchell, W H | year = 1995| month = | title = The Empire Ships| pages = | publisher = Lloyd's of London Press Ltd| location = London, New York, Hamburg, Hong Kong|isbn=1-85044-275-4[/tpl] She was yard number 345 and was laid down on 15 August 1941[tpl]csr|register=MSI|id=1164854|accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl]  Empire Beatrice was launched on 31 March 1942 and completed in June that year. She had a GRT of 7,044. Her port of registry was Middlesbrough. Empire Beaumont was managed by W Runciman & Co Ltd for the MoWT.

==Career==

Empire Beaumont was a member of a number of convoys during the Second World War.
onvoy UR 32 sailed from Loch Ewe on 11 July 1942 and was destined for Reykjavík, Iceland. Arrival was on or about 16 July.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.warsailors.com/convoys/ur32.html |title=CONVOY UR 32 |publisher=Warsailors |accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl]
Convoy PQ 18 sailed from Loch Ewe on 2 September 1942 and arrived at Archangelsk on 21 September.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.convoyweb.org.uk/pq/index.html?pq.php?convoy=18!~pqmain |title=Convoy PQ.18 |publisher=Convoyweb |accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl] At 15:15 on 13 September, Empire Beaumont was attacked by Heinkel He 111 aircraft of KG26 and torpedoed.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.halcyon-class.co.uk/Arctic/pq18.htm |title=Convoy PQ18 |publisher=Halcyon Class Ships |accessdate=20 September 2009[/tpl] Empire Beaumont was still afloat at 16:45 although it was believed that she had sunk by 18:30. Thirty-three survivors were rescued by [tpl]HMS|Sharpshooter|J68|6[/tpl]. The majority of them were transferred to [tpl]HMS|Scylla||98|6[/tpl] and five were landed at Archangelsk for transfer to [tpl]SS|Empire Bard[/tpl]. Those lost on Empire Beaumont are commemorated at the Tower Hill Memorial, London.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.benjidog.co.uk/Tower%20Hill/Ship%20Index%20A-F.html |title=Ship Index A-F |publisher=Brian Watson |accessdate=20 May 2011[/tpl]

==Official Numbers and Code Letters==

Official numbers were a forerunner to IMO Numbers. Empire Beaumont had the UK Official Number 164854 and used the Code Letters BDWD.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.plimsollshipdata.org/pdffile.php?name=42b0271.pdf |title=LLOYD'S REGISTER, NAVIRES A VAPEUR ET A MOTEURS |accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl]

==Propulsion==

Empire Beaumont was propelled by a triple expansion steam engine which had cylinders of 24½ inches (62 cm), [tpl]convert|39|in|cm[/tpl], and [tpl]convert|70|in|cm[/tpl] diameter by [tpl]convert|48|in|cm[/tpl] stroke. It was built by Richardsons, Westgarth & Co Ltd, Hartlepool.
