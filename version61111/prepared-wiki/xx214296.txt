[[Trimeresurus tibetanus]]

CATEGORIES: Animals described in 1982, Endemic fauna of Tibet, Reptiles of China, Trimeresurus

Trimeresurus tibetanus is a venomous pit viper species found only in Tibet. No subspecies are currently recognized.[tpl]ITIS|id=634934|taxon=Trimeresurus tibetanus|accessdate=28 July 2008[/tpl]

==Description==

Scalation includes 21 (19 or 20) rows of dorsal scales at midbody, 147-152/145-159 ventral scales in males/females, 46-54/40-48 subcaudal scales in males/females, and 7-9 supralabial scales.

==Geographic range==

Found in Tibet (Tibet Autonomous Region, China). The type locality given is "Xizang Province, Nielamou District, Quekesumou, altitude [tpl]convert|3200|m[/tpl]". In their English translation of Huang's publication, David and Tong (1997) list the type locality as "Tibet", but give "Naylam, Chokesumo" in the summary.
