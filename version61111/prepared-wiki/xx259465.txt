[[Kain Massin]]

CATEGORIES: Australian novelists, Australian short story writers, Living people, Year of birth missing (living people)

Kain Massin is an Australian writer of speculative fiction.

==Biography==

Massin is based in Adelaide, South Australia where he is a high school maths and science teacher.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.horrorscope.com.au/2008/04/news-kain-massin-wins-2008-abc-fiction.html | title = News: Kain Massin wins 2008 ABC Fiction Award  | publisher = HorrorScope | date = 2008-04-02 | accessdate = 2010-12-31 |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5vO9tsuSc |archivedate=2010-12-31 |deadurl=no[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url = https://www.allenandunwin.com/default.aspx?page=94&book=9780733324307 | title = God for the Killing | publisher = Allen & Unwin | date = | accessdate = 2010-12-31 |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5vOA4bbdX |archivedate=2010-12-31 |deadurl=no[/tpl] He is also a member of the Blackwood Writers Group. Massin's first work was published in 1998 with his short story "Escape from Stalingrad" which was featured in fourth edition of Harbinger. "Escape from Stalingrad" was nominated for the 1999 Aurealis Award for best horror short story but lost to Sean Williams and Simon Brown's "Atrax".[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.locusmag.com/SFAwards/Db/Aurealis2000.html | title = The Locus Index to SF Awards: 2000 Aurealis Awards | publisher = Locus Online | date = | accessdate = 2010-12-31 |archiveurl= |archivedate= |deadurl=no[/tpl] In 2008 Massin's first novel was published by ABC Books, entitled God for the Killing, after he won the 2008 ABC Fiction Award which has a A$10,000 prize and a publication deal for the novel.
