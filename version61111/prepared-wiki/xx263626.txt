[[South Ossetian presidential election, 2011]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in South Ossetia, 2011 elections in Europe, 2011 in Georgia (country), 2011 in South Ossetia

Presidential elections were held in South Ossetia on 13 November 2011.http://www.mherrera.org/elections.htm A referendum was held on the same day.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.presidentrso.ru/edicts/detail.php?ID=3914|title=Decree to postpone a vote on the referendum in the Republic of South Ossetia|date=12 August 2011|publisher=Official website of the President of South Ossetia|language=Russian|accessdate=26 August 2011[/tpl] A run-off was held on 27 November, but the result were invalidated by the Supreme Court of South Ossetia. ==Candidates==

Incumbent president Eduard Kokoity was constitutionally barred from serving a third term in office. Attempts were made to call a referendum to change the constitution, but this was blocked by the Supreme Court. Another attempt to change the constitution by a two-thirds majority in parliament was blocked by parliamentary chairman and Communist Party of South Ossetia leader Stanislav Kochiev.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rferl.org/content/south_ossetia_eduard_kokoity_third_term/24237175.html|title=South Ossetian Supreme Court Nixes Proposed Referendum On Third Presidential Term|last=Fuller|first=Liz|date=16 June 2011|publisher=Radio Free Europe|accessdate=10 July 2011[/tpl] 
Kokoity himself stated he had no intention in seeking a third term,[tpl]cite news|url=http://osinform.ru/29479-eduard-kokoyty-ya-ne-nameren-ballotirovatsya-na-tretiy-srok.html|title=Eduard Kokoity: I do not intend to run for a third term|date=4 May 2011|publisher=OSInform / IA "Res"|language=Russian|accessdate=10 July 2011|location=Tskhinvali[/tpl] and called on everybody to refrain from initiatives to allow him to serve a third term. Kokoity made it clear that he would not disappear from the South Ossetian political scene.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.presidentrso.ru/main_notice/detail.php?ID=3773|title=Message from the President of the Republic of South Ossetia, Eduard Kokoity|date=16 June|publisher=Official website of the president of South Ossetia|language=Russian|accessdate=11 July 2011[/tpl]

==Election day==

Polling stations were open from 8:00 until 20:00. There were 86 such stations, located all over the country, as well as in Moscow and Sukhumi. Special polling stations were opened for South Ossetian citizens living in North Ossetia–Alania. In South Ossetia, a turnout of 50% + 1 vote is required for an election to be valid. This threshold was reached around 16:00.

==Results==

===First round===

The two most successful participants were Bibilov, with 25.44% of votes cast, and Dzhioyeva, with a 25.37% share of the vote. They went through to a runoff on 27 November, which was needed as no candidate had achieved a majority in the first round.

===Second round===

Preliminary results released on 27 November show Dzhioyeva in the lead, with 8955 votes to Bibilov's 6205.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cik.ruo.su/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=248:2011-11-27-21-24-17&catid=43:-2011&Itemid=66|title=По предварительным данным, на выборах в Южной Осетии лидирует Алла Джиоева|date=27 November 2011|publisher=South Ossetian Electoral Commission|language=Russian|accessdate=27 November 2011[/tpl] The final results confirmed this.[tpl]cite web|url=http://osinform.ru/32497-alla-dzhioeva-obschestvo-vyneslo-prigovor-epohe.html|title=Алла Джиоева: Общество вынесло приговор эпохе|date=29 November 2011|publisher=OSInform|language=Russian|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl] However, the Unity Party filed a complaint at the Supreme Court of South Ossetia, alleging Dzhioyeva's campaign used illegal means to secure the victory. The Supreme Court subsequently declared the election invalid.[tpl]cite web|url=http://osinform.ru/32516-srochno-verhovnyy-sud-yuzhnoy-osetii-priznal-vybory-nedeystvitelnymi.html|title=Срочно!
