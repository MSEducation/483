[[Freethinkers' Party (Greece)]]

CATEGORIES: Political parties established in 1922, Monarchist parties in Greece, Nationalist parties in Greece, Conservative parties in Greece, Defunct political parties in Greece, 1922 establishments in Greece

The Freethinkers' Party ([tpl]Lang-el|Kόμμα των Ελευθεροφρόνων[/tpl]) was a Greek Nationalist and Monarchist  party founded led by Ioannis Metaxas who was the Prime Minister and dictator of Greece from 1936 to 1941.Peter Davies, Derek Lynch. The Routledge companion to fascism and the far right. London, England, UK; New York, New York, USA: Routledge, 2002. Pp. 276. It was formally founded in November 1922 after the adoption of the party's manifesto that was unveiled on October 13, 1922.Marina Petrakis. The Metaxas Myth: Dictatorship and Propaganda in Greece. London, England, UK; New York, New York, USA: I. B. Taurus, 2006. Pp. 18. Metaxas had the party and all other parties dissolved following the establishment of the 4th of August Regime, in which he ruled as an official independent.Jürgen Fischer. Balkan strongmen: dictators and authoritarian rulers of South Eastern Europe. London, England, UK: Purdue University Press, 2007. Pp. 181.
The first programmatic declaration of the party was published in the daily Nea Imera on 13 October 1922.
