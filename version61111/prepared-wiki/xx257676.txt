[[John Rogerson (1676–1741)]]

CATEGORIES: 1676 births, 1741 deaths, Attorneys-General for Ireland, Irish judges, Irish MPs 1713–14, Members of the Parliament of Ireland (pre-1801), Solicitors-General for Ireland, Members of the Privy Council of Ireland

John Rogerson (born 1676, died 1741)[tpl]Rayment-hc-ie|date=March 2012[/tpl] was an Irish politician, lawyer, and judge who became Solicitor-General Attorney-General for Ireland and Lord Chief Justice of the King's Bench in Ireland.  He was Member of Parliament for Granard and Dublin City. He was appointed as Solicitor-General in 1714.[tpl]cite book |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=rqeSXWqKAD8C&lpg=RA1-PA116&dq=William%20Whitshed&pg=RA1-PA199#v=onepage&q=rogerson&f=false |title=The Judges in Ireland, 1221-1921 |publisher=The Lawbook Exchange, Ltd.|year=2005|author=F. Elrington Ball|accessdate=2010-12-30[/tpl] He became Attorney-General for Ireland in 1720 and Lord Chief Justice of the King's Bench in 1727. He was the son of Sir John Rogerson, Member for Clogher and Dublin City, who gave his name to Sir John Rogersons' Quay.
