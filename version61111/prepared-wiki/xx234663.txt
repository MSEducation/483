[[5th Kresowa Infantry Division (Poland)]]

CATEGORIES: Infantry divisions of Poland, Military units and formations established in 1943, Infantry divisions of Poland in World War II, Military units and formations disestablished in 1946, Military articles needing translation from Polish Wikipedia

The Polish 5th Kresowa Infantry Division ([tpl]lang-pl|5 Kresowa Dywizja Piechoty[/tpl]) was a Polish Armed Forces in the West unit fighting during World War II in the Italian Front. It was formed in March 1943. It was part of the Władysław Anders' 2nd Polish Corps, affiliated with the Western Allies.
It was commanded by Zygmunt Bohusz-Szyszko (1943) and Nikodem Sulik (from 1943 to 1946).
Composition:
The division was named after the Kresy region (eastern borderlands of the Second Polish Republic).
