[[Gnome's hat hydroid]]

CATEGORIES: Hydrozoa

The gnome's hat hydroid, Candelabrum capensis is a species of sessile hydroid cnidarian. It is a member of the family Candelabridae. Millard, N.A.H. 1975. Monograph on the Hydroida of Southern Africa. Ann. S. Afr. Mus. 68:1-513

==Description==

The gnome's hat hydroid grows to about 3cm tall. It is a small conical hydroid with 30-40 adhesive knobs at its base. The cone is made up of 400-600 densely packed warty tentacles which contain the stinging cells. Its colour is variable, from a purple-brown to magenta  or pale pink.

==Distribution==

This species is found around the southern African coast from Luderitz in Namibia to East London from the subtidal to at least 27m underwater.

==Ecology==

These animals are usually found among red seaweed and brown seagrass, singly or in small groups.
