[[Asian Boyz]]

CATEGORIES: Asian-American gangs, Modern street gangs, Gangs in Los Angeles, California, Gangs in New York, Crips sets, Gangs in Massachusetts

The Asian Boyz, or ABZ, are an Asian American street gang founded by Filipino American Marvin "Shy Boy" Mercado in the 1990s in Southern California.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2012/08/16/pierre-mercado-convicted_n_1791574.html |title=Pierre Mercado, Asian Boyz LA Gang Member, Convicted Of Four Murders |work=Huffington Post |date=16 August 2012 |accessdate=16 December 2012[/tpl] The Asian Boyz gang members are mainly males of Vietnamese, Cambodian or Laotian descent,.
The gang are allied with the Crips. They are active in 14 states, in 28 different cities in the U.S.

==History==

The Asian Boyz was formed in Long Beach due to Asian immigrants being harassed by the East Side Longos, a Mexican American gang that originated from Anaheim Street.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,155910,00.html |title=From Killing Fields to Mean Streets|work=Time Magazine |date=June 24, 2001 |accessdate=March 20, 2014[/tpl]
On the East Coast, their gang colors are forest green, black and yellow. Asian Boyz also have a tattoo of a dragon head with crystal globes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.newstimes.com/news/article/Police-eye-gang-in-killing-240488.php |title=Police eye gang in killing |publisher=NewsTimes |date=27 January 2005|accessdate=16 December 2012[/tpl] On the West Coast, they use blue, similar to the Crips.[tpl]cite book|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=pM_3zER88l4C&pg=PA34&lpg=PA34&dq=asian+boyz+rival+latin&source=bl&ots=wbMDA5XZ11&sig=QmWyhLIj5iYOdteMAb4N6UKZpOM&hl=en&sa=X&ei=4nRSUIbIIpG88wSG34GgBw&ved=0CCwQ6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=asian%20boyz%20rival&f=false |title=Frequently Asked Questions About Gangs and Urban Violence|author=Ann Byers|page=34 |publisher=The Rosen Publishing Group |date=15 January 2011 |accessdate=16 December 2012[/tpl]
The Asian Boyz gang was established in Long Beach. There are additional factions in states on the east coast. However, the ABZ are spread across the United States, mainly on the east coast of the U.S.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pressherald.com/news/24-with-gang-links-arrested-in-maine-cities-during-sweep-_2010-08-21.html |title=24 with gang links arrested in Maine cities during sweep |work=The Portland Press Herald / Maine Sunday Telegram |date=21 August 2010 |accessdate=16 December 2012[/tpl] According to the FBI's National Gang Threat Assessment in 2011, the Asian Boyz has members in California, Massachusetts, Missouri, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, and Utah.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fbi.gov/stats-services/publications/2011-national-gang-threat-assessment |title=2011 National Gang Threat Assessment â€“ Emerging Trends|date=2011 |accessdate=[/tpl]

==Notable crimes==

In August 1997, the leader of the Asian Boyz Van Nuys set, Sothi Menh, was arrested in Phnom Penh, Cambodia, and brought back to the United States after fleeing the country in January that year. He was wanted for committing five gang-related murders in the San Fernando Valley in 1995.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/GANG+SUSPECT+RETURNED%3B+MAN+SOUGHT+IN+ASIAN+BOYS+CASE+IN+CUSTODY-a083875296 |title=Gang Suspect Returned; Man Sought In Asian Boys Case In Custody |publisher=Thefreelibrary.com |date=2 August 1997 |accessdate=16 March 2011[/tpl] In September 1998, seven of the Asian Boyz were charged with seven murders, 18 attempted murders and five instances of conspiracy to commit murder in Van Nuys, Los Angeles, in 1995.[tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1998/sep/20/news/mn-24877 |title=Asian Boyz Face Group Trial in Spate of Killings|author=Evelyn Larrubia |work=Los Angeles Times|date=20 September 1998 |accessdate=16 March 2011[/tpl]
On August 12, 2006, a fight broke out between Bloods and Asian Boyz gang members at a house in Lowell, Massachusetts, where a birthday party was being held. Asian Boyz members left the party and allegedly started throwing bottles and other objects. Billeoum Phan, 14, began firing at the Asian Boyz members. One of the shots hit Asian Boyz member Samnang Oth, killing him. Phan was convicted of manslaughter and sentenced to incarceration until he turns 21. He is required to serve a 5-years probation after his release.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lowellsun.com/news/ci_11118747?source=rss |title=Lowell murder trial set to begin this week|author=|work=Lowell Sun|date=December 2, 2008 |accessdate=December 4, 2013[/tpl]
Three members of the gang were charged with beating a 15-year-old boy named Sang Vu to death during a gang initiation in Utica, New York, in January 2007. Richie Nguyen was sentenced to 5 to 15 years of prison for manslaughter.[tpl]cite web|last=Crossett |first=Nate |url=http://www.wktv.com/news/local/6168221.html?m=y|title=Nguyen Pleads Guilty to Manslaughter|publisher=WKTV |year=2007|accessdate=August 15, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Crossett |first=Nate |url=http://www.wktv.com/news/local/6223611.html |title=Third Asian Boyz Gang Member Pleads Guilty |publisher=Wktv.com |date=1 March 2007 |accessdate=16 March 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last= |first= |url=http://www.wktv.com/news/local/6973837.html|title=Nguyen Sentenced 5 - 15 Years|publisher=WKTV |date=April 11, 2007 |accessdate=August 15, 2013[/tpl] Samnang Chou was sentenced to 10 years of prison for second-degree assault.[tpl]cite web|last= |first= |url=http://www.wktv.com/news/local/6484032.html|title=Utica Man Sentenced to 10 Years|publisher=WKTV |date=March 14, 2007 |accessdate=August 15, 2013[/tpl]
In March 2008, four men abducted 24-year-old Vutha Au from Santa Rosa and drove him to a beach near Jenner, California where they fatally shot him. Quentin Russell, who was 24 years old at the time of the shooting, was the shooter, and brothers Sarith Prak and David Prak, and Preston Khaoone, were charged in connection with the murder and abduction of Au. All four defendants were convicted[tpl]cite web|last=Payne |first=Paul |url=http://www.pressdemocrat.com/article/20120628/ARTICLES/120629478/0/FRONTPAGE?p=1&tc=pg|title=Jury convicts 4 gang members in Jenner beach slaying|work=The Press Democrat |date=28 June 2012 |accessdate=29 June 2012[/tpl] and sentenced to life without parole on July 27, 2012."Asian Boyz Gang Members Sentenced to Life without the Possibility of Parole Plus 25 Years to Life for Blind Beach Murder", Press release, 27 July 2012, by the Office of the District Attorney, Sonoma County. Retrieved August 13, 2013
In the 1990s, Pierre Mercado, the brother of Marvin Mercado, was responsible for four murders in an attempt to intimidate other gangs. He fled to the Philippines and remained there for 11 years until he was brought back to the United States in 2012. In 2013, Mercado was sentenced to 218 years to life in prison.[tpl]cite web|last= Marcellino |first=Elizabeth |url=http://www.dailynews.com/general-news/20120824/former-asian-boyz-gang-leader-gets-218-years|title=Former Asian Boyz gang leader gets 218 years|publisher=Los Angeles Daily News |date=May 14, 2013|accessdate=August 15, 2013[/tpl]
