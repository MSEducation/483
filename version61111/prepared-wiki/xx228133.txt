David Andrew Coles (born 15 June 1964) is a goalkeeping coach for Al Jazira. He was the goalkeeping coach at Southampton F.C. from 1997 for 8 years which included their run to the F.A. cup final. He then worked as the goalkeeping coach for their South Coast rivals Portsmouth F.C. before moving to West Ham United in July 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.whufc.com/articles/20100702/groves-and-coles-appointed_2236884_2082283 |title=Groves and Coles appointed | News | Latest News | News | West Ham United |publisher=Whufc.com |date= |accessdate=2010-07-02[/tpl] He parted company with the club on 24 June 2011, following the arrival of Sam Allardyce before joining Al Jazira. On 21st November 2013 he rejoined Portsmouth as a goalkeeping coach.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.portsmouth.co.uk/sport/pompey/coach-makes-surprise-pompey-return-1-5696022 |title=Coach makes surprise Pompey return |publisher=The News |date=2013-11-21 |accessdate=2013-11-21[/tpl]
In his playing days, Coles was a goalkeeper in the lower leagues, mainly at Aldershot. He also played briefly in Finland 1988 and at non-league Gloucester City and Cobham.[tpl]cite web|url=http://hartleywintney.football4ever.co.uk/assets/programme/hwfc_prog_dec6_2011-cobham-web.pdf |title=Matchday Programme |publisher=Hartney Witney FC |date= 2011-12-06 |accessdate=2012-10-18[/tpl]
