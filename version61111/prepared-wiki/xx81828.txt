====Canada====

In Canada, adoption by same-sex couples is legal in every province and territory.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.adoptiveparents.ca/nun_issues.shtml |title=Adopting in Nunavut: Important Adoption Issues |publisher=Adoptiveparents.ca |date= |accessdate=16 March 2013[/tpl]

====Mexico====

In Mexico City, the Legislative Assembly of the Federal District passed legislation on 21 December 2009 enabling same-sex couples to adopt children.[tpl]cite news | author=Tracy Wilkinson | publisher=Los Angeles Times | title=Mexico City moves to legalize same-sex marriage | date=22 December 2009 | url=http://www.latimes.com/news/nation-and-world/la-fg-mexico-gays22-2009dec22,0,250742.story | accessdate=30 December 2009[/tpl] Eight days later, Head of Government ("Mayor") Marcelo Ebrard signed the bill into law, which officially took effect on 4 March 2010.[tpl]cite news | author=Tracy Wilkinson | publisher=Los Angeles Times | title=Gay marriage closer to reality in Mexico | date=29 December 2009 | url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/laplaza/2009/12/gay-marriage-closer-to-reality-in-mexico.html | accessdate=30 December 2009[/tpl]
On 24 November 2011, the Coahuila Supreme Court struck down the state's law barring same-sex couples from adopting, urging the state's legislature to amend the adoption law as soon as possible.[tpl]sp icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web | author=José Reyes | publisher=Vanguardia | title=Adopción gay será posible en Coahuila | date=28 November 2011 | url=http://www.vanguardia.com.mx/adopciongayseraposibleencoahuila-1158988.html | accessdate=14 February 2012[/tpl] On 12 February 2014, the state's congress overwhelmingly approved the measure more than two years following the supreme court decision.

====United States====

According to a report by the Williams Institute, in 2007 there were 270,000 children in the United States who lived with same-sex couples. Of these, one-quarter, or 65,000, were adopted.Romero, Adam, Census Snapshot, Williams Institute, December 2007
In the U.S., states may restrict adoption by sexual orientation or marital status. Furthermore, since adoptions are mostly handled by local courts in the United States, some judges and clerks accept or deny petitions to adopt on criteria that vary from other judges and clerks in the same state,Human Rights Campaign, State Adoption Laws. Retrieved 2007-09-27. leading to a confused or ambiguous legal status for same-sex parents in some states.
