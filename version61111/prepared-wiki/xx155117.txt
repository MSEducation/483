[[Tarong North Power Station]]

CATEGORIES: Coal-fired power stations in Queensland, Wide Bay-Burnett, Power stations in Queensland

Tarong North Power Station is a 443 megawatt coal fired power station on the same site as Tarong Power Station in the South Burnett. The Queensland Government commissioned the construction of the power station in November 1999.Power station runs on time[tpl]dead link|date=January 2014[/tpl] by Wal Baker. 12 February 2002. Retrieved on 31 May 2007.  Construction work began in 2000.  The power station was initially owned by a 50/50 joint venture between Tarong Energy and TM Energy.Tarong North Power Station. Retrieved on 3 January 2014. Full ownership of the power station by Tarong Energy was obtained in November 2009.
The plant was opened in 2003 and is based on an energy efficient supercritical design.  The Steam Generator was supplied by IHI and the steam turbine was manufactured by Toshiba.Power Technology Project Page for Tarong North
Particulate emissions at the power station are reduced by bag filter technology.
