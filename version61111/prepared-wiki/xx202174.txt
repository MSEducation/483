[[List of Arizona state symbols]]

CATEGORIES: Arizona-related lists, Lists of United States state symbols by state, Symbols of Arizona

The following is a list of symbols of the U.S. state of Arizona. The majority of the items in the list are officially recognized after a law passed by the state legislature. Most of the symbols were adopted in the 20th century. The first symbol was the motto, which was made official in 1864 for the Arizona Territory. The newest adopted symbol of Arizona is the 
Colt Single Action Army in 2011.[tpl]cite news|title=Arizona governor makes Colt revolver official state gun|author=David Schwartz|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/04/29/us-arizona-colt-idUSTRE73S05920110429|newspaper=Reuters|date=28 April 2011|accessdate=29 April 2011[/tpl] Arizona became the second state to adopt a "state firearm" after Utah adopted the Browning M1911.[tpl]cite news |title=Arizona governor makes Colt revolver official state gun |url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/04/29/us-arizona-colt-idUSTRE73S05920110429?feedType=RSS |publisher=Reuters |date= April 28, 2011 |accessdate=May 1, 2011[/tpl] Fifteen of the state symbols are on display on the Arizona Capitol Museum.[tpl]Ref label|Symbols|A|A[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.azlibrary.gov/braille/talking_book_news/2007/tbn12-07/symbols.cfm|title= ARIZONA'S STATE SYMBOLS |accessdate= 2008-06-12 |publisher=Talking Book News[/tpl]
