[[Regional railroad]]

CATEGORIES: Regional railroads in the United States

In the United States, a regional railroad is a railroad company that is not Class I, but still has a substantial amount of traffic or trackage (and is thus not a short line). The Association of American Railroads (AAR) has defined the lower bound as [tpl]convert|350|mi|km[/tpl] of track or $40 million in annual operating revenue.Association of American Railroads, Overview of America's Freight Railroads, May 2008 (The Class I threshold is $250 million, adjusted for inflation since 1991.Surface Transportation Board, FAQs, accessed October 2008)
