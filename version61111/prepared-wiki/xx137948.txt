[[Metro Apatlaco]]

CATEGORIES: Mexico City metro stations, Railway stations opened in 1994, Nahuatl words and phrases

Metro Apatlaco is a station along Line 8 of the metro of Mexico City.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.metro.df.gob.mx/red/estacion.html?id=108|title=Apatlaco|language=Spanish|publisher=Sistema de Transporte Colectivo|accessdate=31 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|last=Archambault|first=Richard|url=http://mexicometro.org/metro/line8/apatlaco/|title=Apatlaco »  Mexico City Metro System|accessdate=31 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.mexicocity.gob.mx/detalle.php?id_pat=5945|title=ESTACIÓN APATLACO|language=Spanish|accessdate=31 July 2011[/tpl]
The station's logo is a house with hot water and steam inside of it. Apatlaco is a Nahuatl word that means "place of medicinal baths".  The station was opened on 20 July 1994.[tpl]Cite web|last=Monroy|first=Marco|editor-last=Schwandl|editor-first=Robert|url=http://www.urbanrail.net/am/mexi/mex-history.htm|title=Opening Dates for Mexico City's Subway|accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl]
Ridership at the station dipped during a swine flu panic in the spring of 2009.[tpl]Cite news|last=Gerardo Mejía|first=José|url=http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/nacion/167551.html|title=Compras de pánico en supermercados|work=El Universal|language=Spanish|date=27 April 2009|accessdate=31 July 2011[/tpl]
