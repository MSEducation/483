[[1999 FIFA Women's World Cup]]

CATEGORIES: 1999 FIFA Women's World Cup, 1999 in women's football, FIFA Women's World Cup tournaments, International women's soccer competitions hosted by the United States, Sports in Portland, Oregon

The 1999 FIFA Women's World Cup, the third edition of the FIFA Women's World Cup, was held in the United States and won by the host team.[tpl]cite web|author=By JERE LONGMANPublished: June 22, 1999 |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1999/06/22/sports/women-s-world-cup-bigger-crowds-watching-better-play.html |title=WOMEN'S WORLD CUP; Bigger Crowds Watching Better Play - New York Times |publisher=Nytimes.com |date=1999-06-22 |accessdate=2012-10-30[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1999/05/20/sports/soccer-1999-women-s-world-cup-beautiful-game-takes-flight.html?pagewanted=all|title=SOCCER; 1999 Women's World Cup: Beautiful Game Takes Flight|publisher=Nytimes.com|accessdate=2012-08-03[/tpl] The final between the U.S. and China, held on July 10 at the Rose Bowl in Pasadena, California was the most-attended women's sports event in history with an official attendance of 90,185.[tpl]cite news|author=By JERE LONGMANPublished: May 20, 1999 |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1999/05/20/sports/soccer-1999-women-s-world-cup-beautiful-game-takes-flight.html?pagewanted=all&src=pm |title=SOCCER; 1999 Women's World Cup: Beautiful Game Takes Flight - New York Times |publisher=Nytimes.com |date=1999-05-20 |accessdate=2012-08-03[/tpl] The final was scoreless after extra time and won by the U.S. in a penalty shootout[tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2008/jan/30/sports/sp-daniels30 |title=1999 U.s. Women's Soccer Team - Los Angeles Times |publisher=Articles.latimes.com |date=2012-07-13 |accessdate=2012-08-02[/tpl]
