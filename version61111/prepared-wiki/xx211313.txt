[[Emanuel Nascimento]]

Emmanuel Fortes Nascimento (born August 13, 1970 in Minas Gerais) is a former international freestyle swimmer from Brazil, who participated in two consecutive Summer Olympics for his native country, starting in 1988. His best result was the 6th place in the Men's 4×100-metre freestyle in 1992.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/fo/emanuel-fortes-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl] 
At the 1988 Summer Olympics, in Seoul, he finished 10th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, 12th in the 4×100-metre freestyle, 18th in the 4×100-metre medley, 36th in the 200-metre butterfly, and 37th in the 100-metre freestyle.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/fo/emanuel-fortes-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
He was the South American record holder of the 100-metre freestyle, between 1990 and 1991. [tpl]cite web|title = EVOLUTION OF THE SOUTH AMERICAN RECORD OF 100 FREE-MALE|work=Best Swimming|year = February 16, 2008|url = http://www.bestswimming.com.br/conteudo.php?i=7972|accessdate =May 5, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl]
At the 1991 World Aquatics Championships, Nascimento finished 24th in the 100-metre freestyle, and 26th in the 200-metre freestyle. [tpl]cite web|title = Results at 1991 Perth|work=USA Swimming|year = 2013|url = http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/4015d620-c19d-4fd6-af8a-f727a163d31a/91_wchamps%5B1%5D.pdf|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
He was at the 1991 Pan American Games, in Havana. He won gold in the 4×100-metre freestyle, and silver in the 4×200-metre freestyle.  [tpl]cite web | title = Brazil medals at 1991 Pan | work = UOL | year = 2007 | url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/historia/1991/nopodio.jhtm | accessdate = April 20, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl] He also finished 6th in the 100-metre freestyle, and 7th in the 100-metre butterfly. [tpl]cite web|title = Results at 1991 Pan Am Games in Havana|work=USA Swimming|year = 2013|url = http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/97d438f7-816e-454f-ac9a-ce177a1d3bd6/91_panam%5B1%5D.pdf|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
At the 1992 Summer Olympics, in Barcelona, he finished 6th in the 4×100-metre freestyle, 7th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, and 25th in the 100-metre freestyle.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/fo/emanuel-fortes-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl] 
After closing the swimming career, he became commercial director of Monytel, a company that operates in the field of communications.Monytel
