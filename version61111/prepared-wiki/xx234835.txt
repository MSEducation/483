[[Gian Tommaso Scarascia Mugnozza]]

CATEGORIES: 1925 births, University of Bari alumni, Tuscia University faculty, Italian agronomists, Italian geneticists, Living people

Gian Tommaso Scarascia Mugnozza (born May 27, 1925, in Rome, Italy) is an Italian agronomist and geneticist 
After graduating in agriculture at the University of Bari in 1946, he started teaching there and became full professor in 1968. He was director of the ENEA Casaccia laboratory in Cesano, Rome.
Other positions include; Dean of the Faculty of Agriculture at the University of Bari, chairman of the Agriculture and Forestry Commission of the Italian National Research Council, and rector at Tuscia University at Viterbo. He is presently president of the Accademia Nazionale delle Scienze, and is a fellow of several Italian and foreign academies.
His scientific research focused on wheat breeding and adaptation to arid and semi-arid climates. He has coordinated national and international research programs in agriculture and plant breeding.
