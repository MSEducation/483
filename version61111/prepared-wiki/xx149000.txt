[[South Lakeland Council election, 2002]]

CATEGORIES: Council elections in South Lakeland, English local elections, 2002

The 2002 South Lakeland Council election took place on 2 May 2002 to elect members of South Lakeland District Council in Cumbria, England. One third of the council was up for election and the council stayed under no overall control.[tpl]cite web| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/hi/english/static/vote2002/local_elections/129.stm | title = South Lakeland | accessdate = 2009-11-10 | publisher = BBC News Online[/tpl]

==Background==

Before the election the Liberal Democrats were the largest group on the council, but no party had a majority.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2002/04/19/The+Lake+District+Archive/6931147.Housing_becomes_political_hotcall/|title=Election call|date=26 April 2002|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=10 August 2011[/tpl] 3 councillors stood down at the election, Liberal Democrat John Sudholme of Kendal Castle ward, Labour's Jim Blamire of Kendal Underley and independent Philip Ball of Kendal Oxenholme.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2002/04/12/The+Lake+District+Archive/6931303.Stalwarts_back_out_of__elections/|title=Stalwarts back out of elections|date=12 April 2002|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=10 August 2011[/tpl]
Issues in the election included plans to move council housing from the direct control of the council, with Labour opposing the move and both the Conservatives and Liberal Democrats supporting it. Other issues included improving the council finances, increasing recycling, parking and social exclusion.

==Election result==

The results saw little change in the party balance on the council,[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/1965683.stm|title=BNP shock for North|date=3 May 2002|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=9 August 2011[/tpl] with the only net change being the Conservatives going up by 1 seat to 19 councillors, at the expense of the independents, who dropped to 3 seats.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2002/05/03/The+Lake+District+Archive/6930837.Election_results_no__surprise/|title=Election results no surprise|date=3 May 2002|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=10 August 2011[/tpl] Close results came in Kendal Nether, where Labour gained a seat from the Liberal Democrats by 7 votes after a recount, and Kendal Highgate, where the Liberal Democrats took a seat from Labour by 13 votes.
