[[Okazu]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese cuisine

[tpl]Nihongo|O-kazu|おかず or お数; お菜; 御菜[/tpl] is a Japanese word meaning a side dish to accompany rice; subsidiary articles of diet.Kenkyusha's New Japanese-English Dictionary, ISBN 4-7674-2015-6 They are typically made from fish (tempura), meat, vegetable, or tofu and designed to add flavor to the rice. In modern Japanese cuisine, o-kazu can accompany noodles in place of rice.
