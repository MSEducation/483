[[Red Rooster]]

CATEGORIES: Companies established in 1972, Fast-food chains of Australia, Fast-food poultry restaurants, Companies based in Perth, Western Australia, Fast-food franchises, Privately held companies of Australia

Red Rooster is an Australian fast food chain and one of the largest of the Australian owned and operated fast food restaurants. Its products include roasted chicken and related products, including burgers, wraps, salads and desserts.

==History==

The Kailis family opened the first Red Rooster store in the Perth suburb of Kelmscott, Western Australia in 1972. Myer Emporium (later part of Coles Myer) purchased the business in July 1981.[tpl]cite news|title=Myer Purchases Red Rooster Fast-food Chain|url=http://news.google.com.au/newspapers?id=--NYAAAAIBAJ&sjid=VecDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4632,2987079&hl=en|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=10 July 1981|page=17[/tpl] In 1992 Red Rooster bought the Big Rooster chain to expand into the eastern states, and renamed the stores "Red Rooster".[tpl]cite news|last=Beyer|first=Mark|title=Red Rooster/Chicken Treat in $180m private equity deal|url=http://www.wabusinessnews.com.au/en-story/1/51087/Red-Rooster-Chicken-Treat-in-180m-private-equity-deal|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=Western Australian Business News|date=17 April 2007[/tpl] In May 2002, Red Rooster was purchased by Western Australian company Australian Fast Foods Pty Limited,[tpl]cite web | author= | date=30 April 2002 | title=Australian Fast Foods acquisition of Amalgamated Food & Poultry Pty Ltd | work=Australian Competition and Consumer Commission | url=http://www.accc.gov.au/content/index.phtml/itemId/476499 | accessdate=18 July 2006[/tpl] which owned the competing Chicken Treat fast food chain. [tpl]As of|2012[/tpl] there were over 360 Red Rooster stores, in all states and territories of Australia except Tasmania.[tpl]cite web|title=Store Locator|url=http://www.redrooster.com.au/Store-Locator/|work=Red Rooster|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl]
In April 2007, Red Rooster and Chicken Treat were sold for A$180 million to a consortium formed by the management and the venture capital arm of Westpac known as Quadrant Capital.[tpl]cite news|last=Carson|first=Vanda|title=Consortium buys Red Rooster|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/business/consortium-buys-red-rooster/2007/04/16/1176696758572.html|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald|date=17 April 2007[/tpl]
In April 2009, the Red Rooster chain in New Zealand closed its stores.[tpl]cite web|title=Liquidators' First Report Red Rooster Franchising (NZ) No.3 Limited (In Liquidation)|url=http://www.mmh.co.nz/documents/reports/redrooster3first.pdf|work=Meltzer Mason Heath|accessdate=16 June 2011|date=24 September 2009[/tpl]
In 2010, Red Rooster changed company-owned stores to franchises.[tpl]cite news|last=Sonti|first=Chalpat|title=Red Rooster result affected by franchise conversions|url=http://www.watoday.com.au/wa-news/red-rooster-result-affected-by-franchise-conversions-20101116-17vpz.html|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=WAtoday|date=17 November 2010[/tpl]
In June 2011, Quadrant Capital sold Quick Service Restaurants, which owns the Red Rooster chain, for A$450 million to Archer Capital.[tpl]cite news|last=Ooi|first=Teresa|title=Archer buys up $450m worth of quick chicken|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/business/industry-sectors/archer-buys-up-450m-worth-of-quick-chicken/story-e6frg9h6-1226074537350|accessdate=16 June 2011|newspaper=The Australian|date=14 June 2011[/tpl]
Red Rooster employs over 7,500 people.[tpl]cite web|title=About Us|url=http://www.redrooster.com.au/Corporate/Our-Story/|work=Red Rooster|accessdate=14 June 2013[/tpl]

==Marketing and promotions==

Over the years, Red Rooster has utilised various slogans and campaigns but they mainly focus on the name "Red Rooster" or at least the word "Red".
In 2010, Red Rooster ceased advertising kids' meals under an agreement made with The Australian Association of National Advertisers as a matter of social responsibility.[tpl]dead link|date=September 2012[/tpl]
In 2010, Red Rooster was a sponsor of the factory-backed Holden V8 Supercars Series team.
In 2011, Red Rooster changed to promoting its restaurants as healthy, fresh and quick.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.redrooster.com.au/ |title=Red Rooster |publisher=Red Rooster |date=2009-10-06 |accessdate=2012-09-14[/tpl]
