[[Fazel Ahmed Manawi]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Afghan judges, Afghan Tajik people

Fazel Ahmed Manawi was Deputy Chief Justice in the Supreme Court of Afghanistan. In January 2004, he publicly stated that he was opposed to women singing on state television."Woman singer angers Afghan judges", BBC News, 14 January 2004 In April 2004, the Associated Press reporter Todd Pitman reported that Manawi had said that any Muslim caught drinking in the Irish Pub in Kabul would be punished.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2012[/tpl] On June 21, 2003, he announced that the Afghan Supreme Court would try Sayed Madawi, the editor of Aftab,[tpl]ambig|date=December 2012[/tpl] and his deputy Ali Payam Sestani for "libelling Islam".[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2012[/tpl]  Manawi is chairman of the IEC.[tpl]ambig|date=December 2012[/tpl]
