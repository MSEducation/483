[[The Mothman Prophecies]]

CATEGORIES: 1975 books, Books about extraterrestrial life, Paranormal, Novels set in West Virginia

The Mothman Prophecies is a 1975 book by John Keel.Keel, J. 1975. The Mothman Prophecies. New York: Saturday Review Press and E. P. Dutton. 269 pp. ISBN 0-8415-0355-9.

==Synopsis==

The book relates Keel's account of his investigation into alleged sightings of a large, winged creature called Mothman in the vicinity of Point Pleasant, West Virginia, during 1966 and 1967.North, E. L. 1979. "Mysteries in the Mountains and Skies of West Virginia", Chapter 19, pp. 190-196 in: Redcoats, Redskins, and Red-Eyed Monsters. South Brunswick and New York: A. S. Barnes and Company. 278 pp. ISBN 0-498-02085-1.  It combines these investigations with his own theories about UFOs and various supernatural phenomena, ultimately connecting them to the December 15, 1967, collapse of the Silver Bridge across the Ohio River (which had already been known to have been caused by stress corrosion cracking in an eyebar in a suspension chain as early as 1971[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.ca/newspapers?id=cJ1eAAAAIBAJ&sjid=MVMNAAAAIBAJ&pg=4537,3056163|newspaper=The Portsmouth Times|date=28 May 1976|page=12|title=Claims In Disaster Denied[/tpl]).

==Film==

The book was the inspiration for the film of the same name released in 2002, starring Richard Gere.
