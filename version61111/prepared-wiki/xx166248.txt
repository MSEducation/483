[[Joe Soboleski]]

CATEGORIES: 1926 births, Living people, People from Grand Rapids, Michigan, American football offensive guards, Michigan Wolverines football players, Chicago Hornets players, Washington Redskins players, Detroit Lions players, Dallas Texans (NFL) players, New York Yanks players

Joseph Robert Soboleski, Jr. (born August 22, 1926) is a former American football guard.  
Soboleski began his football career playing for Catholic Central High School in Grand Rapids, Michigan.  After graduating from high school, Soboleski spent 18 months in the United States Navy, including eight months in the Pacific Theater during World War II.  He played college football at the University of Michigan as a defensive lineman from 1945 to 1948.[tpl]cite news|author=Jan Brodt|title=Soboleski Comes From School Of Rough And Ready Guards|newspaper=Michigan Daily|date=December 10, 1947|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=mwNgAAAAIBAJ&sjid=oR4NAAAAIBAJ&pg=3049,3958605&dq=soboleski&hl=en[/tpl]   He was a starting defensive lineman on Michigan's undefeated 1947 and 1948 national championship teams.  At the conclusion of his college career, Soboleski was selected to play in the 1949 College All-Star Game in Chicago.[tpl]cite news|title=Three Added to All-Star Eleven|date=July 3, 1949|newspaper=The New York Times|url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F10E14FD3B59157A93C1A9178CD85F4D8485F9[/tpl]
