[[Ernst G. W. Keyl]]

CATEGORIES: 1804 births, 1872 deaths, American Lutheran clergy, University of Leipzig alumni, German emigrants to the United States

Ernst Gerhard Wilhelm Keyl (born in Leipzig, Germany, 22 May 1804; died in Monroe, Michigan, 4 August 1872) was a German-born Lutheran clergyman who worked in the United States. He was a founder and the first president of the Missouri Synod's Eastern District.

==Biography==

He graduated from Leipzig in 1829, and, after holding Lutheran pastorates, emigrated to the United States in 1839, and officiated successively in Missouri, Milwaukee, Baltimore, and Willshire, Ohio. From 1854 to 1869, he was president of the Eastern District of the Missouri Synod, of which he was a founder.

==Works==

His biography was published by J. F. Kostering (St. Louis, 1882).
