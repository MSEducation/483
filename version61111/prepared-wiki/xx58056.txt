[[Sune Sik]]

CATEGORIES: Swedish princes

Sune Sik, (born c. 1154), was allegedly a Swedish prince. According to Olaus Petri, he would have been a younger son of King Sverker I of Sweden and father of Ingrid Ylva.
In surviving contemporary documents one Sune Sik can be found who lived much later. That Sune Sik made a donation to Vreta Abbey as late as in 1297.[tpl]cite book|author=Harrison, Dick|title=Jarlens sekel|year=2002|pages=248|ISBN=91-7324-999-8|quote=... en viss Sune Sik när denne år 1297 stadfäste en donation till Vreta kloster.[/tpl] He might have ordered a restoration of a chapel in which he eventually was interred, and later Cistercian tradition may then have turned him into a prince.[tpl]cite journal|url=http://fornvannen.se/pdf/1920talet/1921_022.pdf|author=Natanael Beckman|title=Kungagravar och medeltidshistoria.|journal=Fornvännen|year=1921|issue=16|pages=46|accessdate=2009-07-17|quote=Jag har nämnt ovan, att cisterciensernas tradition tilldelat Sverker en son, Sune Sik, som icke rimligtvis kan vara historisk i denna egenskap. Jag har också antytt, att han antagligen fått sin prinsvärdighet genom missförstånd och vore att identifiera med en donator till klostret, som möter i ett diplom av 1297[/tpl] This has caused some historians to view Olaus Petri's account of him as unreliable.
