[[William Henry Beaumont de Horsey]]

CATEGORIES: 1915 deaths, Grenadier Guards officers, British Army personnel of the Crimean War, People educated at Eton College, Year of birth missing

William Henry Beaumont de Horsey (died 6 May 1915LIEUT. GEN. DE HORSEY DEAD – An Officer of Famous Light Brigade in Charge at Balaklava. – View Article – NYTimes.com As an officer in the Foot Guards, de Horsey did not ride in the Charge of the Light Brigade, but his future brother-in-law Lord Cardigan commanded the action.) was a British soldier.
He was the son of Spencer Horsey de Horsey and brother of Algernon Frederick Rous de Horsey and Adeline Louisa Marie de Horsey. He was educated at Eton College.Adeline, Countess of Cardigan and Lancastre, My Recollections, London 1909, p. 36 (online here https://archive.org/details/myrecollections00carduoft)
At the age of sixteen he joined the Army, and through the influence of the Duke of Wellington he was commissioned as Ensign and Lieutenant in the Grenadier Guards on 22 November 1844.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/20412/pages/4561 |title=Viewing Page 4561 of Issue 20412 |work=London Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl] His promotion to Lieutenant and Captain was purchased on 22 March 1850.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/21079/pages/871 |title=Viewing Page 871 of Issue 21079 |work=London Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl] He served in the Crimean War, and was given army rank as Major by brevet dated 12 December 1854.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/21699/pages/1576 |title=Viewing Page 1576 of Issue 21699 |work=London Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl] On 13 March 1857 was promoted to Captain and Lieutenant-Colonel, again by purchase,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/21977/pages/980 |title=Viewing Page 980 of Issue 21977 |work=London Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl] and on 2 March 1858 he was authorised to accept the Order of the Medjidie, fifth class, conferred upon him by Sultan Abdülmecid I of Turkey.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/22107/pages/1258 |title=Viewing Page 1258 of Issue 22107 |work=London Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl]

Gazette |accessdate=21 January 2012[/tpl] and on 9 April 1870 was appointed to the regimental rank of Major.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/23605/pages/2097 |title=Viewing
