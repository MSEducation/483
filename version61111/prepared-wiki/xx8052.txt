[[Mary of Rome]]

CATEGORIES: New Testament people, 1st-century women, Epistle to the Romans, Women in the Bible

The Apostle Paul's Epistle to the Romans (16:6) mentions a Mary. She is said to have treated Paul with special kindness, and to have "laboured much among" the early Christian community.
Although it has been conjectured that she is the same person as the Mary mother of John Mark [tpl]fact|date=April 2013[/tpl], this is generally considered to be unproven [tpl]fact|date=April 2013[/tpl]. Most traditions hold that there is nothing more known about her.  Who was Who in the Bible, ISBN 0 7852 4240 6, p255
