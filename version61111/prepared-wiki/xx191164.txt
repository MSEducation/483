Byron Bernard "Bam" Hardmon (born January 7, 1981) is a former American college and professional football player who was a linebacker.  Hardmon played college football for the University of Florida.  He signed out of college with the Miami Dolphins, but spent his rookie season on injured reserve.  Hardmon subsequently played professional football for the Hamburg Sea Devils in NFL Europa, and in pre-season games with the Seattle Seahawks and Tampa Bay Buccaneers.

==Early years==

Hardmon was born in Jacksonville, Florida in 1981. Hardmon earned the nickname "Bam Bam" in his youth and it was later shortened to "Bam".  He attended Edward H. White High School in Jacksonville, and played high school football for the Ed White Commanders.  Hardmon was an all-state and prep All-American who was selected to play in the Georgia-Florida High School All-Star Game at the Georgia Dome in June 1999.[tpl]cite news|author=Andy Staples|title=Future rivals team up for Florida-Georgia prep all-star game|publisher=University Wire|date=June 18, 1999|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P1-21960509.html[/tpl]

==College career==

==Professional career==

He was signed by the Miami Dolphins in April 2003, but he spent his rookie season on "injured reserve" status.[tpl]cite news|title=Former Gator Hardmon Signs With Tampa Bay|newspaper=Gainesville Sun|date=April 6, 2005|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=2twnAAAAIBAJ&sjid=QewDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3391,1324559&dq=hardmon&hl=en[/tpl]  In the spring of 2004, Hardmon signed with the Hamburg Sea Devils in the NFL Europa.  After playing well in his first season of professional football with Hamburg, he was signed by the Seattle Seahawks in June 2004.  Hardmon was released by the Seahawks on September 6, 2004.  In 2005, Hardmon signed with the Tampa Bay Buccaneers.  In 2007, Hardmon was one of the leaders of the Hamburg team that defeated the Frankfurt Galaxy 37-28 in the World Bowl.[tpl]cite news|title=Die World- Bowl-Sieger sind abgetaucht|newspaper=Hamburger Abendblatt|date=June 20, 2008|url=http://www.abendblatt.de/sport/article544437/Die-World-Bowl-Sieger-sind-abgetaucht.html[/tpl]  He was also named Defensive Player of the Week in Week 5 of the 2007 NFL Europa season, after he led Hamburg to a vcitory over the Amsterdam Admirals with a 34-yard interception return for a touchdown, four tackles, a sack, a quarterback hurry and two pass defenses.[tpl]cite web|title=NFL Europa Players of Week 5 |publisher=GridironFans.com (from NFLEurope.com)|url=http://gridironfans.com/forums/general-football-discussion/25545-nfl-europa-thread-6.html[/tpl]
