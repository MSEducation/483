[[Scream Thy Last Scream]]

CATEGORIES: Pink Floyd songs, Songs written by Syd Barrett, Unreleased songs, Psychedelic rock songs

"Scream Thy Last Scream" is an unreleased song by Pink Floyd, written by frontman Syd Barrett and was scheduled to be the band's next single.[tpl]cite book|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=JW46US9TcOMC&pg=PA187#v=onepage&q&f=false|last=Chapman|first=Rob|title=Syd Barrett: A Very Irregular Head|year=2010|publisher=Faber|location=London|isbn=978-0-571-23855-2|edition=Paperback|accessdate=5 October 2012|page=187[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Syd Barrett & Pink Floyd: Dark Globe|year=2010|publisher=Plexus|location=London|isbn=0859654311|edition=Rev.|accessdate=28 October 2012|page=262[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Manning|first=Toby|title=The Rough Guide to Pink Floyd|year=2006|publisher=Rough Guides|location=London|isbn=1-84353-575-0|page=41|edition=1st[/tpl] 

==Recording==

Only two takes are complete. It was recorded in the same sessions as "Set the Controls for the Heart of the Sun", on 7–8 August 1967.[tpl]cite web|last=Jones|first=Malcolm|title=The Making of The Madcap Laughs|year=2003|publisher=Brain Damage|edition=21st Anniversary|accessdate=15 August 2012|page=23[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Chapman|first=Rob|title=Syd Barrett: A Very Irregular Head|year=2010|publisher=Faber|location=London|isbn=978-0-571-23855-2|edition=Paperback|accessdate=5 October 2012|page=186[/tpl]

==Music and lyrics==

The song features: several changes in tempo, a sped-up double-tracked vocal part by Barrett, while Mason simultaneously sings the normal part[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Syd Barrett & Pink Floyd: Dark Globe|year=2010|publisher=Plexus|location=London|isbn=0859654311|edition=Rev.|accessdate=28 October 2012|page=264[/tpl] (one of the rare moments he ever sang on a Floyd record),Mason, Nick. Inside Out: A Personal History of Pink Floyd (Weidenfeld & Nicolson, 2004). ISBN 978-0-297-84387-0. a range of bells,[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Lost in the Woods: Syd Barrett and the Pink Floyd|year=1998|publisher=Boxtree|location=London|isbn=0-7522-2328-3|accessdate=7 December 2012|page=178[/tpl] crowd noises, an instrumental section that continually increases in speed featuring wah-wah guitar solos and keyboards, and surreal lyrics.[tpl]cite book|last=Chapman|first=Rob|title=Syd Barrett: A Very Irregular Head|year=2010|publisher=Faber|location=London|isbn=978-0-571-23855-2|edition=Paperback|accessdate=5 October 2012|pages=186–187[/tpl] Barrett is only clearly audible on one line in the song, "she'll be scrubbing bubbles on all fours".

==Aborted release and other versions==

The two tracks were arranged to be released as a single on 8 September, before it was vetoed by the band's record company, EMI.[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Lost in the Woods: Syd Barrett and the Pink Floyd|year=1998|publisher=Boxtree|location=London|isbn=0-7522-2328-3|accessdate=7 December 2012|page=180[/tpl] The song was again scheduled for release, this time with "Vegetable Man" as the B-side, but cancelled and both tracks remain unreleased.[tpl]cite web|last=Jones|first=Malcolm|title=The Making of The Madcap Laughs|year=2003|publisher=Brain Damage|edition=21st Anniversary|accessdate=16 September 2012|page=23[/tpl]
A promotional video was made for the track, directed by poet Spike Hawkins. A final studio cut of "Scream Thy Last Scream" was completed along with a somewhat different BBC radio version (which features both Barrett and Mason on lead vocals)[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Syd Barrett & Pink Floyd: Dark Globe|year=2010|publisher=Plexus|location=London|isbn=0859654311|edition=Rev.|accessdate=28 October 2012|page=306[/tpl] and even some live recordings.http://pinkfloydhyperbase.dk/unreleased/index.html#SCREAM THY LAST SCREAM All are widely available on bootleg recordings.[tpl]cite book|last=Schaffner|first=Nicholas|title=Saucerful of Secrets: The Pink Floyd Odyssey|year=2005|publisher=Helter Skelter|location=London|isbn=1-905139-09-8|edition=New|accessdate=31 October 2012|page=112[/tpl] A late-Barrett era rendition was recorded live in Rotterdam in November 1967, at the Hippy-Happy Fair.[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Syd Barrett & Pink Floyd: Dark Globe|year=2010|publisher=Plexus|location=London|isbn=0-85965-431-1|edition=Rev.|page=299[/tpl] Producer Malcolm Jones (who produced Barrett's The Madcap Laughs solo album) had remixed "Vegetable Man" and "Scream Thy Last Scream",[tpl]cite book|last=Palacios|first=Julian|title=Syd Barrett & Pink Floyd: Dark Globe|year=2010|publisher=Plexus|location=London|isbn=0-85965-431-1|edition=Rev.|accessdate=18 July 2012|page=419[/tpl] for inclusion on the Barrett rarities album Opel (1988), however the band blocked its inclusion.[tpl]cite book|last=Manning|first=Toby|title=The Rough Guide to Pink Floyd|year=2006|publisher=Rough Guides|location=London|isbn=1-84353-575-0|page=186|edition=1st|accessdate=28 July 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Schaffner|first=Nicholas|title=Saucerful of Secrets: The Pink Floyd Odyssey|year=2005|publisher=Helter Skelter|location=London|isbn=1-905139-09-8|edition=New|accessdate=1 November 2012|page=134[/tpl]
