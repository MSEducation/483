[[John Treacy Egan]]

CATEGORIES: 1962 births, Living people, American male stage actors, Rye Country Day School alumni, State University of New York at Purchase alumni

John Treacy Egan (born July 10, 1962) is an American actor and singer.

==Early life==

Egan attended the Rye Country Day School and graduated from SUNY Purchase .

==Career==

Egan first appeared off-Broadway in When Pigs Fly at the Howard Fairbanks Theater in 1996.[tpl]cite news| url=http://theater.nytimes.com/mem/theater/treview.html?res=9D0DEFD81531F936A2575BC0A960958260 | work=The New York Times[/tpl]  In 1997 Egan made his Broadway debut in Jekyll & Hyde, playing the roles of Groom and Mike.[tpl]cite web|author=The Broadway League |url=http://www.ibdb.com/person.php?id=75131 |title=The official source for Broadway Information |publisher=IBDB |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl]  He is also in the film version Broadway Television Networks/Atlantic Recordings.http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0279887/  In 2001 Egan was in the Off-Broadway show Bat Boy: The Musical.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/celebritybuzz/whoswho/biography/1086 |title=JOHN TREACY EGAN Theatre Credits – Who's Who Bio |work=Playbill  |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl]  Also in 2001 Egan appeared as Hortensio in Kiss Me, Kate at the Shubert Theatre in Los Angeles.[tpl]cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2001/aug/27/entertainment/ca-38813?pg=3 |title=Not Just Another Op'nin' – Los Angeles Times |publisher=Articles.latimes.com |date=August 27, 2001 |accessdate=October 18, 2011 |first=Michael |last=Phillips[/tpl]  Egan then starred in the musical The Producers.  He played the role of Franz Liebkind from May 2002 to April 2003, and then took over the role of Roger De Bris from April to October.  He returned to the show a year later, playing Max Bialystock for one week in December 2004. On November 1, 2005, he officially took over the role of Max,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.newyorktheatreguide.com/news/oct05/producers4oct05.htm |title=The Producers: John Treacy Eagan to play Max Bialystock from the 25 Oct & Jai Rodriguez, Carmen Ghia from the 1 Nov 2005 |publisher=Newyorktheatreguide.com |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl] and played it through the end of the Broadway run in 2007 (except for a break of several months in 2006, in which sitcom star Tony Danza assumed the role). He is currently the only actor to have played three principal roles.  Egan was also in the 2005 film The Producers with Nathan Lane and Matthew Broderick, as First Nighter.  Egan appeared with Josh Groban in the Actors' Fund Benefit Concert of Chess on September 22, 2003, at the New Amsterdam Theater.  In 2007 Egan created the role of Chef Louis in The Little Mermaid.  As of October 2009 Egan is back on Broadway, playing a parent and reporter in the Roundabout Theatre Company's production of Bye Bye Birdie. Currently on Broadway in the Tony Nominated musical, Sister Act.
John released his first solo CD Count The Stars in April 2008.  His second solo CD, On Christmas Morning, was released in October 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/BIRDIE_Star_John_Treacy_Egans_Holiday_Recording_ON_CHRISTMAS_MORNING_Now_Available_20091202 |title='BIRDIE' Star John Treacy Egan's Holiday Recording 'ON CHRISTMAS MORNING' Now Available |publisher=Broadwayworld.com |date=December 2, 2009 |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl]
Egan will soon be seen in the movie Last Night with Eva Mendes and Keira Knightley.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.broadwayworld.com/article/BIRDIE_Star_John_Treacy_Egans_Holiday_Recording_ON_CHRISTMAS_MORNING_Now_Available_20091031 |title='BIRDIE' Star John Treacy Egan's Holiday Recording 'ON CHRISTMAS MORNING' Now Available |publisher=Broadwayworld.com |date=October 31, 2009 |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl]  He has appeared on 30 Rock, Law & Order, As the World Turns, and Late Night with Conan O'Brien.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.christycat.com/JohnEgan/Biography.html |title=Biography |publisher=Christycat.com |accessdate=October 18, 2011[/tpl]
Egan can be heard on the cast albums of When Pigs Fly, Jekyll & Hyde, Bat Boy, The Producers (film), and ''The Little Mermaid.
