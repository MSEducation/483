[[R. S. Pelpola]]

CATEGORIES: 1898 births, 1971 deaths, Sinhalese people, High Commissioners of Sri Lanka to Malaysia

R. S. Pelpola (1898 - 19 July 1971) was the Speaker of the Parliament of Ceylon and a member of Parliament in the cabinet of S. W. R. D. Bandaranaike.[tpl]cite web|last=Attygalle|first=Randima|title=I will never call it a day|url=http://www.nation.lk/2007/12/02/eyefea3.htm|publisher=Nation.lk|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]  He resigned from his position of speaker in 1964[tpl]cite journal |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |year=1973 |title=Mr. Speaker in Sri Lanka |journal=Journal of Constitutional and Parliamentary Studies |volume=7 |issue=2-4 |pages=87 |publisher=Institute of Constitutional and Parliamentary Studies |doi= |url= |accessdate=7 August 2013[/tpl] Later in his life he was appointed Sri Lankan High Commissioner to Malaysia. Pelpola died in Kuala Lumpur in 1971.[tpl]cite web|title=Envoy's body is flown home|url=http://newspapers.nl.sg/Digitised/Article/straitstimes19710721.2.34.aspx|publisher=The Straits Times|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl] He had three children.
