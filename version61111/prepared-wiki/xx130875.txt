==Biography==

Born in Waterloo, Iowa, to a Chinese immigrant father from Canton, China who had come to the United States to start a Chinese restaurant,[tpl]cite news |title=Waterloo to Washington: The Long Journey of Coral Wong Pietsch |author=LTC Randy Pullen |newspaper=American Forces Press Service |date=April 24, 2001 |url=http://www.defenselink.mil/news/newsarticle.aspx?id=44948 |accessdate=15OCT09[/tpl] and a Czech American mother, she grew up feeling different than her peers during the height of the Cold War, often being mocked for the Asian half of her ethnicity.[tpl]cite news |title=Pioneer speaks at Fort Gordon  |author=Jeremy Craig |newspaper=Augusta Chronicle |date=May 20, 2005 |url=http://chronicle.augusta.com/stories/052105/met_4205747.shtml |accessdate=October 15, 2009[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]  Initially earning a bachelor's degree in theatre, and later a master's degree in drama, she went on to attend The Catholic University of America to attend law school.  There she would meet her future husband, an army officer who was also attending to become a lawyer.
Commissioned into the Judge Advocate General Corps in 1974, she was assigned to Eighth Army in Korea then to Fort Shafter, Hawaii, completing her active duty requirement, and transferring to the Army Reserves.  After active duty, she settled down and began to reside in Hawaii with her husband and became a civilian attorney for U.S. Army Pacific.  While a reservist she had been deployed to Johnston Atoll, Japan, the Philippines, Washington D.C., and Iraq.  She was a chair commissioner of the Hawaii Civil Rights Commission, with her term expiring while deployed."Hawaii Civil Rights Commission - About the Commissioners", URL accessed 08/23/06
On November 1, 2011, President Obama nominated her to the United States Court of Appeals for Veterans Claims as his replacement for Judge William P. Greene, who had reached the end of his 15-year term.The White House, Office of the Press Secretary, Presidential Nominations and Withdrawal Sent to the Senate, 1 November 2011. She was confirmed by the Senate as the newest judge of the Court of Appeals on May 24, 2012.
