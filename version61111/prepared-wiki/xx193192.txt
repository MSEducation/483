[[Ben Woods]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 births, Living people, English rugby union players, Leicester Tigers players, Rugby union flankers, People educated at Queen Elizabeth Grammar School, Wakefield, Alumni of Durham University, Cambridge University R.U.F.C. players, Newcastle Falcons rugby players

Benjamin Woods (born 9 June 1982 in Barnsley, Yorkshire, England) is a retired former rugby union player who played for Newcastle Falcons and Leicester Tigers as  an openside flanker.
Woods started his professional career at Newcastle Falcons in 2003 but suffered a badly broken leg at the end of his first season and was sidelined for 18 months. On his return to the game he quickly made his mark on the Falcons first team and earned a call up to play for the England Saxons in the 2006 Churchill Cup.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/international/5039582.stm|title=England Saxons 7–13 Scotland A|publisher=BBC|date=2006-06-03|accessdate=2009-08-01[/tpl] 
He was also selected for 2007 Churchill Cup.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/6662037.stm|title=England Saxons 51–3 USA |publisher=BBC|date=2007-05-18|accessdate=2009-08-01[/tpl] Woods played in the Saxons side that defeated Ireland A on 1 February 2008 at Welford Road.[tpl]cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/english/7217616.stm|title=Varndell shines in Saxons victory|publisher=BBC|date=2008-02-02|accessdate=2008-02-05[/tpl]
Woods left the Falcons and moved to Leicester Tigers in 2008 along with Toby Flood.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/main.jhtml?xml=/sport/2008/05/05/srnewc105.xml|title=Defiant Newcastle dent Leicester's hopes|publisher=Telegraph | location=London | first=Rob | last=Wildman | date=2008-05-05 | accessdate=2010-04-26[/tpl] 
Injury to Lewis Moody allowed Woods to make his mark at Leicester and he played in 23 games for them in his first season, culminating in a try scoring appearance in the 2009 Heineken Cup Final.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/8057916.stm|title=Leicester 16–19 Leinster|publisher=BBC|date=2009-05-23|accessdate=2009-08-01[/tpl]
Woods started in all three games of the 2009 Churchill Cup.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/8112050.stm|title=Ireland A 49–22 England Saxons|publisher=BBC|date=2009-06-21|accessdate=2009-08-01[/tpl]
He captained Tigers for the first time in the vital LV= Cup win at Harlequins in 2011/12.

==Retirement==

On September 5, 2012, Woods announced via Twitter that he would be retiring from rugby due to a wrist injury.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.leicestertigers.com/news/14895.php|title=Woods Retires|publisher=Leicester Tigers|date=2012-09-05|accessdate=2012-09-05[/tpl]
