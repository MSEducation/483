[[Back in the Crowd]]

CATEGORIES: 2011 singles, Tom Waits songs, Songs written by Tom Waits

"Back in the Crowd" is a song by American rock musician Tom Waits. It is the second single and seventh track from Waits' seventeenth studio album, Bad as Me.[tpl]cite web | url=http://exclaim.ca/MusicVideo/ClickHear/tom_waits-back_in_crowd | title=Tom Waits - "Back in the Crowd" | publisher=Exclaim! | date=September 27, 2013 | accessdate=August 11, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://thequietus.com/articles/07069-tom-waits-new-single-back-in-crowd | title=New Tom Waits Single Available | publisher=The Quietus | date=September 27, 2013 | accessdate=August 11, 2013[/tpl] Written and produced by Waits and his wife Kathleen Brennan, the song was released on September 27, 2011 as a digital download via iTunes and Spotify.

==Music==

"Back in the Crowd" is a guitar-driven Latin-influenced midtempo ballad.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2011/09/28/tom-waits-back-in-the-crowd-bad-as-me_n_985076.html | title=Tom Waits' 'Back In The Crowd,' Second Track Off 'Bad As Me' | publisher=The Huffington Post | date=September 28, 2011 | accessdate=August 11, 2013 | author=Mallika, Rao[/tpl] It features guitar work from Los Lobos guitarist and vocalist David Hidalgo and longtime collaborator Marc Ribot.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.stereogum.com/825121/tom-waits-back-in-the-crowd/mp3s/ | title=Tom Waits – "Back In The Crowd" | publisher=Stereogum | date=Sep 27, 2011 | accessdate=August 11, 2013 | author=Breihan, Tom[/tpl]
Tom Waits described the song as "an old fashioned jukebox tune for a slow dance with your girl (or guy)."[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/tom-waits-releases-new-single | title=Tom Waits Releases New Single | publisher=Clash | date=September 27, 2011 | accessdate=August 11, 2013 | author=Murray, Robin[/tpl]

==Critical reception==

Upon its release, the song received mainly positive reviews. the Crowd | publisher=Allmusic | accessdate=August 11, 2013 | author=Jurek, Thom[/tpl] Filter magazine wrote: "the song is obviously short and oftentimes tinged with bittersweetness", also adding that "Waits' deep croon evokes the sadness found in the song's mellow melody and lyrics."[tpl]cite web | url=http://filtermagazine.com/index.php/news/entry/look_tom_waits_is_back_in_the_crowd | title=LOOK:
