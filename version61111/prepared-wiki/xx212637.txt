[[Yoshivia]]

CATEGORIES: Moshavim, Religious Israeli communities, Populated places established in 1950, Populated places in Southern District (Israel)

Yoshivia ([tpl]lang-he-n|יוֹשִׁבְיָה[/tpl]) is a religious moshav in southern Israel. Located near Netivot, it falls under the jurisdiction of Sdot Negev Regional Council. In 2006 it had a population of 298.
The village was established in 1950 by immigrants from Algeria on land which had previously belonged to the abandoned Arab village of al-Muharraqa. It was named after Yuval Ben Yoshivia, a member of the Tribe of Simeon which lived in the area and mentioned in 1 Chronicles 4:35;
 and Joel, and Jehu the son of Joshibiah, the son of Seraiah, the son of Asiel;
