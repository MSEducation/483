[[Abbi Glines]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American women novelists, 21st-century American novelists, Year of birth missing (living people), Place of birth missing (living people)

Abbi Glines is an American New York Times,http://www.nytimes.com/best-sellers-books/2013-03-31/combined-print-and-e-book-fiction/list.html?date=2013-03-31&category=combined-print-and-e-book-fiction&pagewanted=print USA Today,http://books.usatoday.com/book/abbi-glines-forever-too-far/l50314?csp=bestsellingbooks4 and Wall Street Journal http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424127887323628004578456722639384656.html bestselling novelist. Her new-adult fiction title Fallen Too Far was self-published on eBook and paperback in 2012.http://www.barnesandnoble.com/w/fallen-too-far-abbi-glines/1113981837?ean=9780988301313 She is also the author of the initially self-published young adult romance bestseller series The Vincent Boys.http://www.nytimes.com/2012/12/22/books/young-adult-authors-add-steaminess-to-their-tales.html?_r=0
Glines is currently working on a spin offhttp://www.goodreads.com/series/102659-untitled-too-far-spinoff of her widely successful Too Far series as well as the 4th book in the series which will tell the original story of Fallen Too Far in the lead male character's point of view.http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/18211536-fallen-too-far-in-rush-s-pov Both are tentatively scheduled to be released in 2014.
