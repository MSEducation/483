[[Cherwell Council election, 2000]]

CATEGORIES: English local elections, 2000, Cherwell Council elections

The 2000 Cherwell Council election took place on 4 May 2000 to elect members of  Cherwell District Council in Oxfordshire, England. One third of the council was up for election and the Conservative party gained overall control of the council from no overall control.[tpl]cite news| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/hi/english/static/uk_politics/vote2000/locals/25.stm | title = Cherwell | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | publisher = BBC News Online[/tpl]
The results saw the Conservatives gain 7 seats, 6 of them from the Labour party, to win a majority on the council for the first time since 1995.[tpl]cite news| url = http://www.guardian.co.uk/politics/2000/may/05/election2000.uk6 | title = Tories back on the map after heartland wins | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | date = 2000-05-05 | publisher = guardian.co.uk | location=London | first=Nicholas | last=Watt[/tpl] Conservative gains came mainly in the Banbury and Bicester areas, with the biggest name Labour councillor to lose in the election being the former mayor, John Hanna.[tpl]cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2000/5/5/78295.html | title = Elections 2000: Cherwell | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | date = 2000-05-05 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl] One of the Conservative winners in the election was Mary Young, who won in South East Kidlington ward to become a councillor for the first time at the age of 81.[tpl]cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2000/5/9/78216.html | title = New councillor is 81 years young | accessdate = 2009-10-19 | date = 2000-05-09 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl] Turnout in the election varied from a high of 53% in Ruscote to a low of 11% in Neithrop.
