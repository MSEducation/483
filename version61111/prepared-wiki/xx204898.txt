[[Judith Berman]]

CATEGORIES: 21st-century American novelists, American fantasy writers, American science fiction writers, American women novelists, 1958 births, Living people

Judith Berman (born 1958[tpl]cite LAF|id=n2005-23181[/tpl]) is an American science fiction and fantasy writer. She is a linguistic anthropologist by training and has published articles on Native American myth and translations, in particular those of the Pacific Northwest. She also has a form of synesthesia.Interview at Strange Horizons 
Her science fiction and fantasy has occasionally drawn on her anthropological background. Her novel "Bear Daughter" made use of this in part and was nominated for the Crawford Award. In 2008 her story "Awakening" was nominated for a Nebula Award for Best Novella.Locus Index to SF Awards She also received a SFRA Pioneer Award for the essay "Science Fiction Without the Future."SFRA
