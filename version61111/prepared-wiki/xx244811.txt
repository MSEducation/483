[[Archie Golembeski]]

CATEGORIES: 1900 births, 1976 deaths, American basketball coaches, Holy Cross Crusaders football players, Providence Steam Roller players, Providence Steam Roller coaches, Providence Friars football coaches, Providence Friars men's basketball coaches, College men's basketball head coaches in the United States, Players of American football from Kentucky

Anthony Edward Golembeski (May 25, 1900 – March 9, 1976) was an American professional football player and coach in the National Football League for the Providence Steam Roller. He also played at the college level and became an All-East football star while attending Holy Cross. In 1926–27 he coached the Providence College basketball program to its first officially recognized NCAA win in the school history.He served as the head football coach at Providence from 1925 to 1933 compiled a record of 27–34–12.[tpl]cite book |title=Providence College Fighting Friars 1921–1941 |publisher=Providence College Special Collections |location=Providence, Rhode Island |url=http://library.providence.edu/spcol/pcfootball/flip/media/media_21_41.pdf |format=PDF |accessdate=November 6, 2011[/tpl]
