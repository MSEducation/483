[[George Barker Hall]]

CATEGORIES: 1819 births, 1858 deaths, Members of the Legislative Assembly of the Province of Canada

George Barker Hall (1819 â€“ February 1858[tpl]cite book |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=orMNAAAAQAAJ&pg=PA71 |title=A sketch of the early settlement and subsequent progress of the town of Peterborough and of each township in the county of Peterborough |date=1867 |last=Poole |first=Thomas W |page=71[/tpl]) was a lawyer, judge and political figure in Canada West. He represented South Northumberland in the Legislative Assembly of the Province of Canada from 1844 to 1847 as a Conservative.
He was born in Brooklyn, New York, later settling in Peterborough, Upper Canada. Hall owned a flour mill in Peterborough.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.trentu.ca/admin/library/archives/75-1018.htm |title=George Barker Hall indenture |publisher=Trent University |accessdate=2011-01-01[/tpl] In 1847, he was named a judge for the Colborne District.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.peterboroughmuseumandarchives.ca/law.htm |title=City Archives, Peterborough |publisher=Peterborough Museum |accessdate=2011-01-01[/tpl] Hall served briefly as an ensign in the local militia around the time of the Upper Canada Rebellion. He died suddenly at Beavermead, his residence near Peterborough, at the age of 39. John A. Macdonald was executor for Hall's estate.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.peterboroughexaminer.com/ArticleDisplay.aspx?archive=true&e=686300 |publisher=Peterborough Examiner |last=Jones |first=Elwood |title=Did John A.
