Gary Orlando Forbes (born February 25, 1985) is a Panamanian professional basketball player. The 6 ft 7 in, [tpl]convert|220|lb|kg|abbr=on[/tpl] small forward played college basketball initially at the University of Virginia, then transferred to the University of Massachusetts following his sophomore season at Virginia.

==High school==

Forbes and his mother moved to Brooklyn, New York when he was a child.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.boston.com/sports/colleges/mens_basketball/articles/2007/12/22/umasss_forbes_a_reluctant_leader/ | work=The Boston Globe | first=Marty | last=Dobrow | title=UMass's Forbes a reluctant leader | date=December 22, 2007[/tpl]  He went on to attend and play basketball at Benjamin Banneker Academy in Brooklyn.  He scored 1,512 points and grabbed 766 rebounds during his high school career, and established career records for points, rebounds and blocked shots.  In local awards, he was selected the Newsday Player of the Year in Brooklyn as a senior, nominated for City Player of the Year, and earned first-team All-City and All-Tri-State honors.  He was also a McDonald's All-American finalist.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/mtt/forbes_gary00.html |title=Gary Forbes Bio - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
As a junior, Forbes averaged 27 points, 11 rebounds and six assists a game.  As a senior, he averaged 31 points, 13 rebounds, three assists and three blocked shots a game.  That year, he scored 30 points and grabbed 10 rebounds in Benjamin Banneker's 75-69 loss to Lincoln High School in the Public School Athletic League "A" Championship game at Madison Square Garden.

==College==

Forbes was ranked as the #4 shooting guard, and the #20 overall prospect on ESPN's Top 100 for the incoming class of 2003.[tpl]cite web|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/recruiting/s/030703topshootingguards.html |title=ESPN.com: RECRUITING - Top Shooting Guards |publisher=Sports.espn.go.com |date=2003-05-20 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://espn.go.com/recruiting/s/020703top100.html |title=ESPN.com: RECRUITING - Recruiting: Top 100 |publisher=Espn.go.com |date=2003-05-15 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  He committed to play at the University of Virginia, after also receiving scholarship offers from Georgia Tech and Mississippi State.[tpl]cite web|url=http://rivals.yahoo.com/louisville/basketball/recruiting/player-Gary-Forbes-4667 |title=Gary Forbes - Yahoo! Sports |publisher=Rivals.yahoo.com |date=2011-04-20 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Coach Pete Gillen's group of newcomers was ranked #17 on the list of top recruiting classes on Rivals.com.[tpl]cite web|url=http://rivalshoops.rivals.com/content.asp?SID=910&CID=188535 |title=Rivals.com Basketball Recruiting - Class of 2003 - Top Recruiting Classes |publisher=Rivalshoops.rivals.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
As a freshman in 2003-04, Forbes played in all of Virginia's 31 games, starting in 17.  He averaged 7.6 points and 4.0 rebounds per game.  He finished second on the team in blocked shots (28) and steals (30).  Tallied 22 points (season high) and 9 rebounds against Minnesota in the ACC – Big Ten Challenge.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://virginiasports.cstv.com/sports/m-baskbl/recaps/120303aaa.html |title=Cavaliers Improve To 4-0 With ACC-Big Ten Challenge Victory - VIRGINIASPORTS.COM - The University of Virginia Official Athletic Site |publisher=Virginiasports.cstv.com |date=2003-12-03 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  As a sophomore in 2004-05, Forbes played in 29 games, started 4, and averaged 9.4 points and 4.1 rebounds per game.  Scored 23 (season high), on 8-11 shooting, in a loss at #4 North Carolina.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://virginiasports.cstv.com/sports/m-baskbl/recaps/021605aaa.html |title=Virginia Falls To No. 4 North Carolina, 85-61 - VIRGINIASPORTS.COM - The University of Virginia Official Athletic Site |publisher=Virginiasports.cstv.com |date=2005-02-16 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  After starting the game at Duke, Forbes tallied a double-double with 17 points and 10 rebounds.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.fansonly.com/schools/va/sports/m-baskbl/recaps/011605aaa.html |title=Virginia Falls To No. 5 Duke, 80-66 - VIRGINIASPORTS.COM - The University of Virginia Official Athletic Site |publisher=Fansonly.com |date=2005-01-16 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
Gillen resigned from Virginia after the end of the 2004-05 season, and Forbes decided to transfer out of the school.  He transferred to the University of Massachusetts, and due to NCAA transfer rules, he was forced to sit out the 2005-06 season.
In the 2006-07 season, his first with the Minutemen, Forbes started in 23 of the team's 33 games.  Playing behind established seniors Rashaun Freeman and Stephane Lasme, Forbes still averaged 13.0 points and 5.8 rebounds per game.  He scored a career high 31 points and grabbed 14 rebounds in a game at Temple.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/recaps/021107aaa.html |title=Forbes Goes For 31, But Minutemen Fall To Temple, 98-89 - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Forbes scored 20 or more points on three other occasions and snared 16 rebounds in a game versus Richmond.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/recaps/020307aaa.html |title=Forbes & Lowe Push UMass Past Richmond, 72-56 - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Helped the Minutemen win their first Atlantic 10 title since the 1995-96 season.  UMass also advanced to the second round of the NIT, their first post-season play since the 1999-2000 season.
Coming into his senior year in 2007-08, Forbes was named to Preseason Atlantic 10 Third Team.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/102507aaa.html |title=Gary Forbes Named Preseason Third Team All-Conference - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date=2007-10-25 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  By the end of the regular season, he was averaging 20.3 points per game, tied for the league lead in scoring with Temple's Dionte Christmas.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/031008aaa.html |title=Forbes Claims A-10 Player Of Year; Harris Named A-10 Most Improved Player - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date=2008-03-10 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Forbes scored 25 or more points on six occasions, including a 29 point high at Northern Iowa.  The A-10 honored him as the league's Player of the Year, and also placed him on the all-conference First Team.  Forbes helped UMass make a deep run in the NIT, advancing past Syracuse, two-time defending NCAA champion Florida, and on to the final before falling to Ohio State.  He was named to the NIT's All-Tournament team.
Forbes scored a total of 1,639 points over his college career (698 at Virginia, 1,128 at UMass).

==2007 FIBA Americas Championship==

In the summer of 2007, Gary played for Panamanian National Team in the FIBA Americas Championship in Las Vegas. In four games with the team he averaged 17.8 points and 4.5 rebounds a game. He scored a game high 29 points against Argentina.
He was also named to the Panama team for the 2011 FIBA Americas Championship.

==Professional career==

After UMass, Forbes participated in the NBA Pre-Draft Camp in Orlando in the summer of 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/news/draft_prospects_081104.html |title=NBA Development League: 2008 NBA D-League Draft: Top Prospects |publisher=Nba.com |date=2008-11-05 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  However, he was not selected in the 2008 NBA Draft.  The Washington Wizards invited him to participate in their summer mini-camp, and he played in all 5 games, but he only averaged 3.8 points and 0.6 rebounds per game.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/wizards/news/wizsummercamp_080707.html |title=The Official Site Of The Washington Wizards |publisher=Nba.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
In August 2008, Forbes had signed with Italian club Basket Napoli, but the team soon dissolved.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://thehoop.blogspot.com/2008/09/forbes-and-dickens-are-leaving-napoli.html |title=The Hoop: Forbes and Dickens are leaving Napoli |publisher=Thehoop.blogspot.com |date=2008-09-24 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
Forbes was selected as the #4 overall pick in the NBA Development League's 2008 draft, by the Sioux Falls Skyforce.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://umassathletics.cstv.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/110708aaa.html |title=Forbes Selected Fourth Overall By Sioux Falls In NBA D-League Draft - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.cstv.com |date=2008-11-07 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  In the D-League's 2008-09 season, he played 22 games for Sioux Falls before being traded to the Tulsa 66ers, where he played in 30 games.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/tulsa/66ers_Acquire_Hill_and_Forbes.html |title=NBA Development League: 66ers Acquire Steven Hill and Gary Forbes |publisher=Nba.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Overall that season, Forbes averaged 17.4 points and 5.3 rebounds per game.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/playerfile/career.jsp?player=gary_forbes |title=NBA Development League: Gary Forbes Playerfile |publisher=Nba.com |date=1985-02-25 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  He also participated in the league's All-Star weekend, finishing third in the Three Point Shootout.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/news/dream_factory_090213.html |title=NBA Development League: James White Soars To NBA D-League Slam Dunk Championship |publisher=Nba.com |date=2009-02-13 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
Forbes then played several short stints with international clubs.
In July 2010, Forbes returned to the US for another shot at the NBA.  He was invited to play with the Houston Rockets on their summer league team in Las Vegas.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/news/summer_league_rosters_2010.html |title=NBA Development League: Former NBA D-League Players In Las Vegas Summer League |publisher=Nba.com |date=2010-07-09 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  Forbes played in 4 of the team's 5 games, averaging 3.8 points and 3.0 rebounds per game.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/summerleague2010/players/gary_forbes/index.html |title=2010 NBA Summer League |publisher=NBA.com |date=1985-02-25 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
On September 18, 2010, the Denver Nuggets invited Forbes to training camp.[tpl]dead link|date=October 2013[/tpl]  Playing in 8 games, Forbes averaged 19.0 minutes, 10.1 points, and 2.9 rebounds per game.  He survived pre-season cuts and made the season roster.[tpl]cite news| url=http://blogs.denverpost.com/nuggets/2010/10/25/gary-forbes-cashes-in-on-opportunity-with-nuggets/ | work=Denver Post|title=Gary Forbes Cashes In On Opportunity With Nuggets|accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.umassathletics.com/sports/m-baskbl/spec-rel/102510aae.html |title=Gary Forbes Makes Denver Nuggets Roster - University of Massachusetts Official Athletic Site |publisher=Umassathletics.com |date=2010-10-25 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]
On December 10, 2011 Forbes signed an offer sheet with the Toronto Raptors.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nba.com/raptors/news/20111210/21917/raptors-sign-forbes-offer-sheet |title=Raptors Sign Forbes To Offer Sheet |work=NBA.com |date=2011-12-10 |accessdate=2011-12-11[/tpl] On December 13, 2011, Forbes was officially signed by the Raptors.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rotoworld.com/content/playerpages/playerbreakingnews.asp?sport=NBA&id=1785&line=164332&spln=1 |title=Raptors Sign Forbes |work=Rotoworld.com|date=2011-12-13 |accessdate=2011-12-13[/tpl]
On July 11, 2012, Forbes was traded to the Houston Rockets along with a future first round pick for Kyle Lowry.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nba.com/rockets/news/rockets-net-first-rounder-and-forbes-raptors |title=Rockets Net First Rounder and Forbes From Raptors |work=NBA.com |date=2012-07-11 |accessdate=2012-07-11[/tpl] He was waived by Houston on October 29, 2012.Rockets waive Shaun Livingston, Jon Brockman, Gary Forbes, Lazar Hayward, JaJuan Johnson
In December 2012, Forbes joined the Zhejiang Lions club in the Chinese Basketball Association. He replaced Al Thornton on the team.[tpl]cite web|last=Pastuszek|first=Jon|title=Forbes for Thornton in Guangsha one of several mid-season import moves|url=http://www.niubball.com/2012/12/forbes-for-thornton-in-guangsha-one-of-several-mid-season-import-moves/|publisher=NiuBBall.com|accessdate=14 December 2012|date=14 December 2012[/tpl]
In September 2013, Forbes signed with the Brooklyn Nets.Brooklyn Nets Announce Training Camp Roster However, he was waived on October 17.Brooklyn Nets Waive Gary Forbes
On November 1, 2013, he was acquired by the Springfield Armor.[tpl]cite web|title=Returning Players Highlight Initial Training Camp Invites|url=http://www.nba.com/dleague/springfield/returning_players_for_2013201_2013_11_01.html|work=NBA.com|date=November 1, 2013|accessdate=November 1, 2013[/tpl] On January 3, 2014, he was waived by the Armor due to a season-ending injury.

==Personal life==

Forbes has type 1 diabetes, which has a history in his family. He was diagnosed with the disease at the age of 19, initially keeping the diagnosis secret out of fear that it would affect his career prospects.[tpl]cite news|last=Neithercott|first=Tracey|title=NBA Player Gary Forbes Scores on His Dreams|url=http://forecast.diabetes.org/magazine/features/nba-player-gary-forbes-scores-his-dreams|accessdate=18 June 2012|newspaper=Diabetes Forecast|year=2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Lopez |first=Aaron J. |url=http://www.nba.com/nuggets/features/forbes_diabetes_11_14_2010.html |title=Forbes no longer reluctant role model for those with diabetes | THE OFFICIAL SITE OF THE DENVER NUGGETS |publisher=Nba.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl]  He has five older siblings.
