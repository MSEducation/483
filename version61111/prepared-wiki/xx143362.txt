[[Charybdis (genus)]]

CATEGORIES: Portunoidea

Charybdis is a genus of swimming crabs in the family Portunidae; "Charybdis" is Greek for whirlpool.

==Species==

The genus Charybdis contains the following species:[tpl]cite web |author=P. Davie & M. Türkay |year=2009 |title=Charybdis De Haan, 1833 |publisher=World Register of Marine Species |url=http://www.marinespecies.org/aphia.php?p=taxdetails&id=106923 |accessdate=March 30, 2010[/tpl]

===Charybdis affinis===

Charybdis affinis has a hexagonal, concave carapace with a yellowish-grey colour. This crab is found in the Indian Ocean and in the West Pacific.[tpl]Cite journal|author = K. H. ===Charybdis hellerii===

Charybdis hellerii is characterised by a hexagonal, concave carapace with a mottled brownish-grey colour. This crab originates from the Indo-West Pacific, from the Red Sea to New Caledonia. However this crab has now also successively invaded the Western Atlantic (Florida to Brazil)[tpl]Cite journal |author = J. F. Dineen, P. F. Clark, A. H. Hines, S. A. Reed & H. P. ===Charybdis japonica===

Charybdis japonica has a hexagonal, concave carapace around [tpl]convert|12|cm[/tpl] wide, the whole animal being pale green to olive green in colour. It occurs naturally in the waters around Japan, Korea and Malaysia, but has become an invasive species in New Zealand.

===Charybdis natator===

Charybdis natator is characterised by a brownish upper surface with some white spots among the wafts or bright red granules. On its under surface it is bluish, mottled with white and pale red.[tpl]cite web |url = http://www.poppe-images.com/images/search_results.php?category=crabs&species=Charybdis%20natator |title = Marine Iconography of the Philippines Archipelago[/tpl] This crab is not a major target for commercial fishing.[tpl]cite web |url = http://personal.cityu.edu.hk/~bhworm/Marine%20seafood/seafood/Species/sp8i.htm |title = Hong Kong City University[/tpl]
