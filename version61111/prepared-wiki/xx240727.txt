[[Sir Robert Bateson, 1st Baronet]]

Sir Robert Bateson, 1st Baronet DL (13 March 1782 – 21 April 1863)[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.leighrayment.com/baronetage/baronetsB2.htm | title = Leigh Rayment - Baronetage | accessdate = 19 October 2009 [/tpl] was an Irish baronet, landowner and Conservative politician.
He was the only son of Thomas Bateson and his wife Elizabeth, youngest daughter of George Lloyd.[tpl]cite book | last = Walford | first = Edward | title = The County Families of the United Kingdom | year = 1860 | publisher = Robert Hardwicke | location = London | pages = 37 [/tpl] 
On 18 December 1818, he was created a Baronet, of Belvoir Park, in the County of Down.[tpl] LondonGazette | issue = 17404 | startpage = 1767 | date = 3 October 1818 | accessdate = 19 October 2009 [/tpl] Bateson entered the British House of Commons in 1830, sitting for Londonderry until 1842, when he was succeeded in the constituency by his eldest son Robert.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.leighrayment.com/commons/Lcommons4.htm | title = Leigh Rayment - British House of Commons, Londonderry | accessdate = 19 October 2009 [/tpl] He was a magistrate in County Down and represented it as a Deputy Lieutenant.[tpl]cite book | last = Dod | first = Robert P. | title = The Peerage, Baronetage and Knightage of Great Britain and Ireland | year = 1860 | publisher = Whitaker and Co. | location = London | pages = 107 [/tpl]
He married Catherine, the youngest daughter of Samuel Dickinson on 27 April 1811, and had by her two daughters and four sons.[tpl]cite web | url = http://thepeerage.com/p12959.htm#i129583 | title = ThePeerage - Sir Robert Bateson, 1st Bt | accessdate = 19 October 2009 [/tpl] In 1863, Bateson died aged 83 at his seat Belvoir Park and his son Robert having predeceased him at Jerusalem him twenty years before,[tpl]cite book | last = Sylvanus | first = Urban | title = The Gentleman's Magazine | location = London | publisher = John Henry and James Parker | year = 1863 | pages = 804–805 | volume = part I [/tpl] he was succeeded in the baronetcy by his second son Thomas, later raised to the Peerage of the United Kingdom as Baron Deramore. Bateson's youngest son George was per a special remainder heir to his brother's barony.[tpl]cite book | last = Cokayne | first = George Edward | editor = Vicary Gibbs and H.
