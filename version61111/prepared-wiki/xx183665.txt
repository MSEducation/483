[[October 22 Group]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct organizations designated as terrorist in Italy, Terrorism in Italy, Years of Lead (Italy), Communist organizations

The October 22 Group (Italian: Gruppo XXII Ottobre) was an Italian terrorist grouping, instituted on 22 October 1969.

==Leadership and attacks==

The group was led by Mario Rossi, who received a life sentence in 1973 for killing a messenger in September 1971.[tpl]cite news|title=Maoist group leader jailed for life in Italy|publisher=The Times|date=19 April 1973[/tpl] The group unsuccessfully attempted to bomb the US consulate in Genoa on 12 April 1970, which was the first recorded attack of the group. The last officially recorded attack of the group was on 26 April 1971 when two members tried to rob a housing organization in Genoa and accidentally killed an employee.

==Disband and other related events==

The group was disbanded in 1971.[tpl]cite web|title=October 22 Circle|url=http://www.stanford.edu/group/mappingmilitants/cgi-bin/groups/view/230|publisher=Standford|accessdate=22 December 2012[/tpl] The Red Brigades in 1978 demanded the release of three of the October 22 members from prison in exchange for the release of Italian prime minister Aldo Moro, who the Red Brigades had captured and later shot.[tpl]cite news|title=Terrorists in Italy Offer to Exchange Moro for 13 in Jail|publisher=The New York Times|date=25 April 1978[/tpl]
