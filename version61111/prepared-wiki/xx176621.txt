[[Hokuetsu]]

CATEGORIES: Named passenger trains of Japan, East Japan Railway Company, Railway services introduced in 1970

The [tpl]nihongo|Hokuetsu|北越[/tpl] is a limited express train service in Japan operated by East Japan Railway Company (JR East), which runs between [tpl]ja-stalink|Kanazawa[/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Niigata[/tpl].[tpl]cite book | title = JR新幹線＆特急列車ファイル |trans_title= JR Shinkansen & Limited Express Train File| publisher = Kōtsū Shimbun | year = 2008 | location = Japan | page = | isbn = 978-4-330-00608-6[/tpl]

==Rolling stock==

Hokuetsu services are formed of 6-car 485 series or refurbished 485-3000 series EMUs.

==History==

The Hokuetsu was first introduced from 1 March 1970 as a limited express operating between [tpl]ja-stalink|Osaka[/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Niigata[/tpl]. From 2 October 1978, the operating route was shortened to Kanazawa to Niigata.[tpl]cite book | title = 列車名鑑1995 |trans_title= Train Name Directory 1995| publisher = Railway Journal| date = August 1995 | location = Japan| page = | isbn = [/tpl]
