[[Governors Village (Chapel Hill, NC)]]

CATEGORIES: Articles created via the Article Wizard, Chapel Hill-Carrboro, North Carolina, Geography of Orange County, North Carolina

The Governors Village is a 450+ acre New Urbanism neighborhood located in Chatham County in Chapel Hill, North Carolina.  Governors Village consists of four  residential home neighborhoods (Governors Village, Governors Park, Governors Forest, Governors Lake), 160 townhomes (Governors Village and Governors Lake Townes), 242 apartments (Camden Governors Village), and nearly 250,000 sf of commercial space.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.domicilenc.com/neighborhoods.html |title=Governors Club Chapel Hill,Briar Chapel,Preserve Chapel Hill |publisher=Domicilenc.com |date= |accessdate=2011-10-17[/tpl] Domicile Realty is located in Governors Village and handles on site sales within the community.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.domicilenc.com/index.html |title=Chapel Hill Real Estate | Domicile Realty | Listings | Homes |publisher=Domicilenc.com |date= |accessdate=2011-10-17[/tpl]
Governors Village is located directly across the street from the main entrance to Governors Club, a 1,600 acre gated community with a 27 hole Jack Nicklaus Signature Golf Course that has over 1,200 single family home sites.[tpl]cite web|url=http://governorsvillage.org/ |title=six neighborhoods – one community |publisher=Governors Village |date= |accessdate=2011-10-17[/tpl]

==Business District==

The original Governors Village Shopping Center opened in 2000 with the opening of Food Lion.  That shopping center now includes a dry cleaners, ABC store, nail salon, and a Subway.  Shortly thereafter, RBC Bank (which is now PNC Bank) opened at the main entrance to Governors Village.  A 55,000 sf two story class A office building was also built about the same time.  That building, The Bold Building, is now under new ownership and attracting new companies to the neighborhood.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boldnc.com |title=Bold Building |publisher=boldnc.com |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl]  The Bold Building has class A office suites available from 1,000 sf to 16,000 sf.
In 2005, a second shopping center opened in Governors Village featuring Bean & Barrel.  The Bean is a coffee shop, bar and grill.  The Bean serves as the neighborhood center.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.beanandbarrel.com/ |title=Bean and Barrel |publisher=http://www.beanandbarrel.com/ |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl]  In addition, the shopping center now includes Village Vet, China Chef, Tarantini Italian restaurant, and Atkins Dentistry.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kbatkinsdentistry.com/ |title=Dentistry | Dental  | Medical | Business |publisher=http://www.kbatkinsdentistry.com/ |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.falconbridgeanimal.com/village/about_us.php |title=Veterinary | Animal | Medical | Business |publisher=http://www.falconbridgeanimal.com/vvhabout.html |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tarantinirestaurant.com/ |title=Dining | Food | Restaurant | Business |publisher=http://www.tarantinirestaurant.com/ |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl]  In 2012, The Commons @ Governors Village, another commercial retail center, opened in Governors Village.  The Commons features business like Domicile Realty, Build Ex (a local builder that has built many homes in Governors Village and Governors Club), Igloo Organic Frozen Yogurt and Governors Pharmacy (an independent pharmacy).  The Commons will have additional medical/retail space in future phases.[tpl]cite web|url=http://villagenc.com/index.html |title=Governors Village |publisher=villagenc.com |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl]  The next building in the Commons is expected to be completed in the winter of 2012.

==Recreation==

The community has numerous parks, sidewalks and walking trails including a small community lake.  In addition, the community is located just a few miles from Jordan Lake, a large recreation lake).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ncparks.gov/Visit/parks/jord/main.php |title=Jordan Lake Recreation | N.C. ==Awards==

Based on its community and volunteer activities Governors Village was named “Community of the Year” for 2009 by the North Carolina Chapter of the Community Associations Institute (Mid-sized Mixed-Use division).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cai-nc.org/link/linkshow.asp?link_id=20295 |title=NC Chapter of Community Associations Institute | Community Associations Institute | Awards | Homes |publisher=http://www.cai-nc.org |date= |accessdate=2011-11-03[/tpl]
