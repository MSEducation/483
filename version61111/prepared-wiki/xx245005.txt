[[Fader (The Temper Trap song)]]

CATEGORIES: 2009 songs, The Temper Trap songs, Song recordings produced by Jim Abbiss, 2010 singles

"Fader" is the third single from the Australian indie rock[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.clashmusic.com/news/the-temper-trap-ponder-album-no-2|title= The Temper Trap Ponder Album No. 2|work=Clash|accessdate=12 March 2011[/tpl] band The Temper Trap from their debut album Conditions.
The single was first released in Australia in December 2009, where it peaked at No. 85, marking the single highest and currently only placement in the Charts. It was also voted in at No. 21 in the 2009 Triple J Hottest 100.
The single was released digitally in the UK on 4 January 2010 where it peaked at number 76 on the UK Singles Chart later that week. 
The song was featured in the 2010 Australian film Tomorrow, When the War Began and in the 2011 films The Roommate and Chalet Girl.[tpl]cite web|title=Chalet Girl Soundtrack on Spotify|url=http://chaletgirlfilm.wordpress.com/2011/03/21/chalet-girl-soundtrack-on-spotify/|accessdate=15 September 2011|date=21 March 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://chaletgirl-movie.com/ |title=Chalet Girl â€“ In Cinemas March 16th |publisher=Chaletgirl-movie.com |accessdate=15 February 2012[/tpl] The song was also featured in season 1 episode 6 of the hit US TV series The Vampire Diaries[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cwtv.com/thecw/the-vampire-diaries-music |title=The Vampire Diaries | Mystic Grill Music Page: Presented by AT&T |publisher=Cwtv.com |accessdate=15 February 2012[/tpl] and in the video games MLB 11: The Show and Test Drive Unlimited 2.

==Chart performance==

Despite being an Australian act, "Fader", the third single from Conditions only managed to reach a current peak of No. 47 in the Australian Singles Chart.Top 50 Singles Chart Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA). Retrieved 14 November 2010.
After the success of "Sweet Disposition" in the UK and Ireland, "Fader" has received increasing amount of radio coverage through December 2009 and January 2010, leading up to the single's release at the beginning of January 2010. With the album already released, the song has begun to receive pre-release downloads.
Right across many Australian radio stations, Fader was added back to the playlist as a re-release on October 2010.
Fader reached a new peak of No. 54 on 8 November 2010.
On 10 January 2010, "Fader" entered the UK Singles Chart at its peak of #76.
