Arnold Barry Latman (born May 21, 1936, in Los Angeles, California) is a former professional baseball pitcher.

==Career==

He played all or part of 11 seasons in the majors, from [tpl]by|1957[/tpl] until [tpl]by|1967[/tpl], for the Chicago White Sox, Cleveland Indians, Los Angeles/California Angels, and Houston Astros.
He was an All Star in both 1961 and 1962.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/l/latmaba01.shtml |title=Barry Latman Statistics and History |publisher=Baseball-Reference.com |date= |accessdate=February 12, 2011[/tpl]  In 1961 he was 4th in the AL with a .722 winning percentage, as he went 13â€“5 for the Indians.
Through 2010, he was 6th all-time in career strikeouts (directly behind Jason Marquis), and 8th in games (344; directly behind Sandy Koufax) and wins (59; directly behind Erskine Mayer) among Jewish major league baseball players.[tpl]cite web|title=Career Pitching Leaders |url=http://www.jewishmajorleaguers.org/|work=Career Leaders|publisher=Jewish Major Leaguers|accessdate=February 10, 2011[/tpl]

==Current life==

Latman resides in Puerto Vallarta, Mexico, with his wife Patti Latman.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.internetpv.com/blog/page/218/ |title=Puerto Vallarta News |publisher=Internetpv.com |date= |accessdate=February 12, 2011[/tpl] He has three grandchildren:  Michael, Clarissa, and Lindsey Latman.[tpl]citation needed|date=January 2011[/tpl]
