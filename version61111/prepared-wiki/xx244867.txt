[[Leland Curtis]]

CATEGORIES: Explorers of Antarctica, 1897 births, 1989 deaths, Sierra Club, American environmentalists, American ski mountaineers, 20th-century American painters, Artists of the American West

Leland S. Curtis (7 August 1897 – 17 March 1989) was an American artist, mountaineer, skier, environmentalist and Antarctic explorer.

==Life==

Curtis was born in Denver, Colorado, and lived in Seattle, Washington as a child. He moved to Los Angeles in 1914 and attended the Manual Arts High School, where he studied under artist and screenwriter Rob Wagner.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hardygalleries.com/Default.asp?Page=34|title=Leland Curtis (1897–1989)|publisher=Josh Hardy Galleries, LLC|accessdate=7 January 2010|location=Carmel, CA[/tpl] He was the official artist for the United States Antarctic Expeditions of 1939 to 1940, 1955 to 1956 and also 1957. The 1939–1940 expedition was called the United States Antarctic Service Expedition and was led by Rear Admiral Richard Evelyn Byrd. The 1955–1956 expedition was called Operation Deepfreeze I, and was also led by Byrd, who died shortly after the expedition ended. Curtis was sponsored by the National Science Foundation for the 1957 expedition, which was called Operation Deepfreeze III, led by Admiral George J. Dufek. This was the first U.S. expedition to reach the South Pole.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.antarctic-circle.org/fox.htm|title=Antarctic Image Chronology|last=Fox|first=William L.|date=21 November 2009|publisher=The Antarctic Circle|accessdate=7 January 2010|location=Jaffrey, NH[/tpl]  These Operation Deepfreeze expeditions were part of the International Geophysical Year.
Curtis was a longtime member of the Sierra Club, and was a founding member of its Ski Mountaineers Section on 26 September 1935.[tpl]cite web|url=http://angeles.sierraclub.org/skimt/text/brnton45.htm|title=Encyclimpedia Brintonica|last=Brinton|first=Bob|date=4 October and 1 November 1945|work=Mugelnoos|publisher=Angeles Chapter – Sierra Club|accessdate=7 January 2010|location=Los Angeles, CA[/tpl] He served on the national Sierra Club board of directors from 1943 to 1946.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sierraclub.org/history/officers/directors.pdf|title=Roster of Sierra Club Directors|publisher=Sierra Club|accessdate=23 October 2009[/tpl]  He illustrated many Sierra Club publications over the years.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thehighsierra.org/sierra_club_cup.htm|title=The Sierra Club Cup|last=Stallings|first=Blanche|coauthors=illustration by Leland Curtis|year=1940|work=originally in the Sierra Club Bulletin|publisher=The High Sierra.org|accessdate=7 January 2010|location=San Franscisco, CA[/tpl]
Most of his paintings were of Antarctica, the High Sierra, the Grand Tetons where he owned a cabin in Moose, Wyoming, and the deserts of the southwestern United States.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hardygalleries.com/Default.asp?Page=34|title=Leland Curtis (1897–1989)|publisher=Josh Hardy Galleries, LLC|accessdate=7 January 2010|location=Carmel, CA[/tpl]  He won dozens of gold medals in Southern California art competitions.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.calart.com/Data/Artists/Leland_Curtis.asp|title=Biography: Leland Curtis (1897–1989)|last=Hughes|first=Edan|publisher=CalArt: California & American Art|accessdate=7 January 2010[/tpl] There is an active market for his paintings.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.askart.com/askart/artist.aspx?artist=3310|title=Leland S. Curtis (1997–1989)|publisher=Ask ART: The Artists's Bluebook|accessdate=7 January 2010[/tpl]
He died in Carson City, Nevada.
