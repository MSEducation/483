[[Atlantic Canada's 100 Greatest Books]]

CATEGORIES: Canadian non-fiction books, Books about books, 2009 books, Atlantic Canada, Top book lists

Atlantic Canada's 100 Greatest Books is a non-fiction book by Trevor J. Adams and Stephen Patrick Clare, published by Nimbus Publishing. The first book of its type, it ranks and reviews the top 100 books written by Atlantic Canadian writers, published in the region, or focusing on the region. The 100 books chosen were selected from a list of over 2,000 titles voted on by over 700 authors, editors and critics.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thecoast.ca/ArtAttack/archives/2009/11/04/atlantic-canadas-100-greatest-books-launches-tomorrow|title=Atlantic Canada’s 100 Greatest Books launches tomorrow |last=Flinn|first=Sue Carter|date=Nov 4, 2009|work=The Coast|accessdate=2009-11-11[/tpl] The book includes reviews of each of the ranked titles, top-ten lists on a variety of literary subjects, and lists from guest contributors such as the premiers of the Maritime Provinces and novelists such as David Adams Richards and Ami MacKay.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.quillandquire.com/blog/index.php/2009/10/22/the-10-best-atlantic-canadian-books-of-all-time/|title=The 10 best Atlantic Canadian books of all time|last=Woods|first=Stuart|date=October 22, 2009 |work=Quill & Quire|accessdate=2009-10-26| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20091026014456/http://www.quillandquire.com/blog/index.php/2009/10/22/the-10-best-atlantic-canadian-books-of-all-time/| archivedate= 26 October 2009 | deadurl= no[/tpl]
The book lists Alistair MacLeod’s No Great Mischief as the greatest Atlantic Canadian book of all time.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.capebretonpost.com/index.cfm?sid=297391&sc=145|title=No Great Mischief the greatest: Alistair MacLeod’s novel gets top spot in new book, Atlantic Canada’s 100 Greatest Books|last=GRANT |first=LAURA JEAN |date=2009-10-25  |work=The Cape Breton Post|accessdate=2009-10-26[/tpl]
Anne of Green Gables by Lucy Maud Montgomery was second.
Four authors — Richards, Raymond Fraser, Wayne Johnston and Thomas Raddall — each had five books make the list.
Born and raised in Digby County, Nova Scotia, Trevor J. Adams currently lives in Halifax, where he works as editor of Halifax Magazine.http://www.halifaxmag.ca/blog_halifax_magazine.asp Stephen Patrick Clare is a freelance writer and professional musician, originally from Montreal and currently residing in Halifax.
