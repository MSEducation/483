[[Bob Harvey (musician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1933 births, Living people, American bass guitarists, Place of birth missing (living people)

Bob Harvey (born 1933) and the former bassist of the band, Jefferson Airplane in 1965. He was replaced by Jack Casady.
He co-founded the bluegrass group the Slippery Rock String Band.
He was in Holy Mackerel, with Paul Williams who recorded the song "Wildflowers," and in San Francisco Blue with Brian Fowler.http://www.artistdirect.com/artist/bio/bob-harvey/1033640
They changed their name to Georgia Blue.http://www.coltrecords.com/BobHarvey/

==Family==

Harvey grew up in San Francisco and lives today in Georgia.[tpl]cn|date=January 2014[/tpl] He is married to Charlene Harvey[tpl]cn|date=January 2014[/tpl] and was previously married to Elizabeth Atwood.[tpl]cn|date=January 2014[/tpl] He has three children.
