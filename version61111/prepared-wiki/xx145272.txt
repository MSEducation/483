[[Brekstad]]

CATEGORIES: Cities and towns in Norway, Populated places in Sør-Trøndelag, Ørland

Brekstad is the administrative centre of the municipality of Ørland in Sør-Trøndelag county, Norway. It is located on the Trondheimsfjord at the entrance to the Stjørnfjord.  It is about [tpl]convert|5|km[/tpl] south of Uthaug and about [tpl]convert|7|km[/tpl] west of Austrått and Ottersbo.
The [tpl]convert|1.76|km2|acre|adj=on[/tpl] city has a population (2009) of 1,888.  This gives the village a population density of [tpl]convert|1073|PD/km2[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=Urban settlements. Population and area, by municipality.|url=http://www.ssb.no/beftett_en/tab-2009-06-16-01-en.html|date=1 January 2009|authorlink=Statistics Norway|author=Statistisk sentralbyrå[/tpl]  Brekstad received city status on 8 October 2005, thus becoming the 95th city in Norway.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.snl.no/Brekstad|title=Brekstad|author=Store norske leksikon|authorlink=Store norske leksikon|language=Norwegian|accessdate=2011-01-13[/tpl]
Brekstad is the seat of Fosen District Court, Brekstad road maintenance department, and NAV Fosen work.  The major employers are Ørland Main Air Station / Ørland Airport, Mascot Høie linen factory, Coop Fosen, and Tine Midt-Norge.  Ørland Church and Ørland cultural center are both located in the city.  
Brekstad has connections with the Hurtigruten boat to Trondheim, Hitra, Frøya, Storfosna, and Kristiansund.  The Brekstad–Valset Ferry has regular service across the Trondheimsfjord from Brekstad to Valset in Agdenes. There are also road connections to Bjugn.

==History==

Brekstad post office was established in 1883.[tpl]cite book|editor-first=Jon|editor-last=Bakken|title=Posten 350 år: historisk utvikling i kommunene Ørland, Bjugn, Rissa|location=Trondheim|year=1997|language=Norwegian[/tpl]  Brekstad is also the site of Skjeggehaugen, from the Old Norse word haugr meaning mound. Skjeggehaugen is an ancient burial mound which measures about [tpl]convert|50|to|60|m[/tpl] across and from [tpl]convert|6|to|7|m[/tpl] in height. It is located about [tpl]convert|60|m[/tpl] northeast of Ørland Church.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.megalithic.co.uk/article.php?sid=24337|title=Skjeggehaugen church|publisher=The Megalithic Portal|accessdate=2011-01-13[/tpl]
