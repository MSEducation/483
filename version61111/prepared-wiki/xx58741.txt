[[Jiu River]]

CATEGORIES: Commons category with local link same as on Wikidata, Rivers of Romania, Rivers of the Jiu subbasin, Rivers of Hunedoara County, Rivers of Gorj County, Rivers of Dolj County, Tributaries of the Danube

 
Jiu ([tpl]IPA-ro|ʒiw[/tpl], [tpl]lang-la|Rabon[/tpl]) is a river of southern Romania. It is formed near Petroşani by the junction of headwaters Jiul de Vest and Jiul de Est. 
It flows southward through the Romanian counties Hunedoara, Gorj and Dolj before flowing into the Danube a few kilometers upstream from the Bulgarian city of Oryahovo, 339 kilometers from its sources. It has a basin of 10,080 km².2004 Romanian Statistical Yearbook - Geography, Meteorology and Environment [tpl]ro icon[/tpl]
The upper Jiu Valley, around Petroșani and Lupeni, is Romania’s principal coal mining region.

==History==

Historically, the Jiu River was also called Rhabon by the Dacian people.

==Towns and cities==

The following towns are situated along the river Jiu, from source to mouth: Petroşani (Jiul de Est), Lupeni (Jiul de Vest), Bumbești-Jiu, Târgu Jiu, Turceni,  Filiaşi, Craiova.
