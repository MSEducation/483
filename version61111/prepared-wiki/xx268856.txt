[[Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception (Lake Charles, Louisiana)]]

CATEGORIES: Religious organizations established in 1869, Religious buildings completed in 1913, Roman Catholic cathedrals in the United States, Roman Catholic churches in Louisiana, Italianate architecture in Louisiana, Buildings and structures in Lake Charles, Louisiana, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in Louisiana, Churches in Calcasieu Parish, Louisiana

The Cathedral of the Immaculate Conception is a Catholic cathedral located in Lake Charles, Louisiana, United States.  It is the seat of the  Diocese of Lake Charles, and it is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.  Immaculate Conception Parish was established in 1869.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.gcatholic.org/churches/northamerica/2047.htm|title=Immaculate Conception Cathedral |publisher= GCatholic |accessdate=2011-09-22|last=|first= [/tpl]  The church was designed by the architectural firm of Favrot & Livaudais and it was built in 1913.  On January 29, 1980 Blessed John Paul II established the Diocese of Lake Charles[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/dlake.html|title= Diocese of Lake Charles |publisher= Catholic-Hierarchy|accessdate=2011-09-23|last=|first=[/tpl]   and Immaculate Conception became the cathedral for the new diocese.
