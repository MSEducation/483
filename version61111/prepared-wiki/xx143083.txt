[[Gust Zarnas]]

CATEGORIES: 1913 births, 2000 deaths, American football offensive guards, Brooklyn Dodgers (NFL) players, Chicago Bears players, College Football Hall of Fame inductees, American people of Greek descent, Greek players of American football, Green Bay Packers players, Ohio State Buckeyes football players

Gustave Constantine Zarnas (December 16, 1913 – December 26, 2000) was a college football All-American and professional football player. 
Born in Ikaria, Greece, Zarnas came to America with his parents. He played the guard position at the Ohio State University from 1935 to 1937 and received All-American recognition his last year as a Buckeye. At [tpl]height|ft=5|in=10[/tpl], 220 lbs., Zarnas played professionally in the NFL for the Chicago Bears and Green Bay Packers from 1938 to 1940, and enlisted into the United States Navy becoming a lieutenant during World War II. He was inducted into the College Football Hall of Fame in 1975.
