==History==

===Background and early ventures (1969-1988)===

The company's background can be traced to 1969, when airline and casino tycoon Kirk Kerkorian bought a controlling stake in the Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer film studio.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM/UA under Kerkorian meant 20 years of change|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=March 8, 1990|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1990-03-08/business/fi-2987_1_mgm-grand|accessdate=2013-08-17[/tpl] In 1970 and 1971, Kerkorian struggled with debt from his acquisitions of MGM and Western Airlines, and was forced to sell a majority of his casino company, International Leisure, to Hilton Hotels at a steep discount.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM's tale of woe and wizardry|newspaper=Washington Post|author=Leroy F. Aarons|date=December 9, 1973|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/148369919/13FF52702C85D0FAA7F/48[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Hilton to buy 6% more of International Leisure|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|author=Paul E. Steiger|date=March 12, 1971|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/156673172/13FF536131B1188AE82/22[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl] When the Las Vegas Hilton, the casino he had built, subsequently became the most successful hotel in Las Vegas, Kerkorian was inspired to lead the studio into the gambling industry. It opened the original MGM Grand Hotel and Casino (now Bally's Las Vegas) in 1973.[tpl]cite news|title=Flat opening for a Grand hotel|newspaper=Washington Post|author=Leroy F. Aarons|date=December 7, 1973|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/148348965/13FF52702C85D0FAA7F/47[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl] The MGM Grand Reno followed in 1978.[tpl]cite news|title=High rolling in Reno|newspaper=Washington Post|date=May 6, 1978|author=Lou Cannon|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/147025911/13FF5436AD52694EC7C/203[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl]
By 1979, the two hotel-casinos accounted for most of MGM's income, and the company announced a plan to split itself in two.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM may split, become movie-TV, casino firms|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|author=Al Delugach|date=November 15, 1979|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/162726928/13FF56ABA0C63D513D4/232[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl] The next year, the film studio was spun off as a new company, while the original company, renamed as MGM Grand Hotels Inc., retained the two hotel-casinos.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM Film offer made, withdrawn|newspaper=New York Times|author=Pamela G. Hollie|date=June 3, 1980|url=http://search.proquest.com/docview/423947977/13FF56ABA0C63D513D4/269[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=ProQuest[/tpl] Kerkorian held a 47 percent stake in both companies.
In 1985, Kerkorian began seeking a buyer for MGM Grand Hotels, to allow him to concentrate on running United Artists and on developing new properties under the MGM Grand name.[tpl]cite news|title=Bally-MGM Grand deal announced|newspaper=New York Times|author=Richard W. Stevenson|date=November 18, 1985|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=NYTF000020050505dhbi00v26&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl] A deal was reached for Bally Manufacturing to buy the company; the deal closed in April 1986,[tpl]cite news|title=Bally completes purchase of MGM Grand Hotels|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|date=April 28, 1986|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=j000000020011119di4s00bj6&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl] and the two casinos were renamed under the Bally's brand.[tpl]cite news|title=Bally Plays Nevada Game Before Rolling Dice Again|newspaper=Crain's Chicago Business|author=Lewis Lazare|date=May 18, 1987|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=cchi000020011118dj5i00037&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl] The terms of the sale allowed Kerkorian to retain rights to the MGM Grand name, and plans were announced to offer the stockholders of MGM Grand Hotels shares in a new company that would hold the naming rights.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM Grand holders will be offered stock in new company|newspaper=Dow Jones News Service|date=December 30, 1985|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=dj00000020011125dhcu01ahc&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl]
The company now known as MGM Resorts International was formed in 1986 as Grand Name Co.[tpl]cite report|title=Form 10-K|publisher=MGM Grand Inc.|url=http://edgar.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/789570/0000898430-94-000218.txt|date=March 29, 1994|page=1[/tpl] as a subsidiary of Kerkorian's Tracinda Corporation.[tpl]cite news|title=Tracinda names new president, unveils MGM unit|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|date=July 29, 1987|author=Roy J. Harris, Jr.|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=j000000020011118dj7t00j9p&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl] It was renamed the following year as MGM Grand, Inc.
The company's first venture was MGM Grand Air, a luxury airline offering service between New York and Los Angeles, which launched in September 1987.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM Grand Air takes a flier in luxury service|date=September 7, 1987|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|first=Denise|last=Gellene|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1987-09-07/business/fi-4177_1_new-airline[/tpl] The company also made an offer to take over financially struggling Pan American World Airways, but it was rejected by Pan Am's board in November 1987 for being too conditional.[tpl]cite news|title=Kerkorian drops bid for Pan American, but Braniff continues to show interest|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|date=November 20, 1987|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=j000000020011118djbk00rff&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl]
In August 1987, MGM Grand bid $152 million for the bankrupt Dunes Hotel in Las Vegas, but was beat out by Japanese billionaire Masao Nangaku.[tpl]cite news|title=Japanese bid wins Las Vegas casino|newspaper=New York Times|agency=AP|date=August 5, 1987|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1987/08/05/business/company-news-japanese-bid-wins-las-vegas-casino.html|accessdate=2012-11-17[/tpl] Instead, the company acquired the Desert Inn and Sands casinos in February 1988 from Summa Corporation for $167 million.[tpl]cite news|title=Tracinda unit completes buy|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|date=February 3, 1988|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=j000000020011117dk2300323&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl] The Sands was promptly sold to Sheldon Adelson's Interface Group for $110 million in April 1989.[tpl]cite news|title=MGM Grand's sale of Sands|newspaper=Wall Street Journal|date=May 1, 1989|url=http://global.factiva.com/redir/default.aspx?P=sa&an=j000000020011116dl5100bl7&cat=a&ep=ASE[/tpl] [tpl]subscription required|via=Factiva[/tpl]
