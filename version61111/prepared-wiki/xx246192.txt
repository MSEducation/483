[[Marcell Dareus]]

CATEGORIES: 1990 births, Living people, Sportspeople from Birmingham, Alabama, Players of American football from Alabama, American football defensive ends, American football defensive tackles, Alabama Crimson Tide football players, Buffalo Bills players

Marcell Dareus (born March 13, 1990) is an American football defensive tackle for the Buffalo Bills of the National Football League. He played college football at the University of Alabama, playing defensive end in the Crimson Tide's 3-4 defense. He was seventh in the Southeastern Conference in quarterback sacks in the 2009 season,[tpl]cite web|title=2009 Southeastern Conference Year Summary [tpl]![/tpl] College Football at Sports-Reference.com|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/cfb/conferences/sec/2009.html|accessdate=29 August 2013[/tpl] and was also named Defensive MVP of the 2010 BCS National Championship Game.

==Early years==

Dareus attended Huffman High School in Birmingham, Alabama, where an ASWA All-State honorable mention defensive lineman. He totaled 117 tackles and 20 sacks as a senior, and also returned a fumble for a touchdown. He was teammates with Andre Smith. Dareus was listed on the Atlanta Journal-Constitution's Super Southern 100, as well as the Mobile Press-Register Elite 18.
Considered a three-star recruit by Rivals.com, Dareus was listed as the #28 defensive tackle prospect in the class of 2008.[tpl]Cite web |url=https://sports.yahoo.com/ncaa/football/recruiting/rankings/rank-1835 |title=Defensive tackles 2008 |work=Rivals.com |date=January 17, 2008 [/tpl] He was also ranked the No. 17 overall player on the Alabama Top 35 list.[tpl]Cite web |url=https://sports.yahoo.com/ncaa/football/recruiting/rankings/rank-1881 |title=Alabama Top 35 2008 |work=Rivals.com |date=January 22, 2008 [/tpl] Dareus chose Alabama over scholarship offers from Auburn, Tennessee, North Carolina, among others.

==College career==

In his true freshman season at Alabama, Dareus played in eight games, making his debut in the Crimson Tide's season opener against Clemson.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rolltide.com/sports/m-footbl/stats/2008-2009/teamcume.html#TEAM.DEF |title=2008–09 Cumulative Season Statistics: Overall Defensive Statistics |author=University of Alabama Department of Intercollegiate Athletics |publisher=RollTide.com |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl] He registered four tackles while recording three quarterback hurries. Dareus saw playing time at nose tackle in third-down-and-long situations, replacing the pure run-stuffing Terrence Cody.
As a sophomore, Dareus was a big contributor all season at defensive end for Alabama. He played in 14 games and made four starts while serving as the Tide's top pass rusher. He totaled 33 total tackles, 9.0 tackles for loss (−49), seven quarterback hurries, an interception and two pass breakups.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rolltide.com/sports/m-footbl/stats/2009-2010/teamcume.html#TEAM.DEF |title=2009–10 Cumulative Season Statistics: Overall Defensive Statistics |author=University of Alabama Department of Intercollegiate Athletics |publisher=RollTide.com |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl] He finished eighth in the Southeastern Conference and ranked tied for 90th nationally with 6.5 sacks (−44) or .46 per game. For his performance, including a 28-yard interception return for a touchdown, Dareus was named defensive MVP of the 2010 BCS National Championship Game.[tpl]cite news |title=Alabama sidesteps Texas' charge to emerge with BCS title |url=http://www.usatoday.com/sports/college/football/2010-01-07-bcs-title-game_N.htm |first=Kelly |last=Whiteside |newspaper=USA Today |date=January 8, 2010 |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl]
In July 2010, Dareus was investigated by the University of Alabama in conjunction with the NCAA into whether Dareus paid his own expenses when attending a party in Miami, Florida hosted by a sports agent, and whether the circumstances constituted a NCAA violation.[tpl]cite news |title=Marcell Dareus suspended two games |url=http://www.tuscaloosanews.com/article/20100903/NEWS/100909925 |first=Chase |last=Goodbread |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |date=September 3, 2010 |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl] In early September, the NCAA suspended him for the first two games of the season in addition to requiring him to pay back the $1,787.17 received in impermissible benefits to the charity of his choice. After the university decided to not appeal the ruling, he made his 2010 debut against Duke in week three.[tpl]cite news |title=Dareus suspension won't be appealed |url=http://www.tuscaloosanews.com/article/20100906/news/100909769 |first=Chase |last=Goodbread |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |date=September 6, 2010 |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl] For the season, he totaled 34 total tackles, 11.0 tackles for loss (−41), 4.5 quarterback sacks (−26), ten quarterback hurries and four pass breakups.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rolltide.com/sports/m-footbl/stats/2010-2011/teamcume.html#TEAM.DEF |title=2010–11 Cumulative Season Statistics: Overall Defensive Statistics |author=University of Alabama Department of Intercollegiate Athletics |publisher=RollTide.com |accessdate=January 8, 2011[/tpl] In the week following the Capital One Bowl victory, Dareus declared his eligibility for the 2011 NFL Draft on January 7.[tpl]cite news |title=Ingram, Dareus, Jones going pro, Hightower returning |url=http://www.tidesports.com/article/20110107/NEWS/110109699/1011?Title=Ingram-Dareus-Jones-going-pro-Hightower-returning |first=Brian |last=Reynolds |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |date=January 7, 2011 |accessdate=January 7, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Tide's Marcell Dareus, Mark Ingram, Julio Jones announce they are going pro |url=http://www.al.com/sports/index.ssf/2011/01/tides_marcell_dareus_mark_ingr.html |first=Don |last=Kausler, Jr. |newspaper=The Birmingham News |date=January 7, 2011 |accessdate=January 7, 2011[/tpl] At the time of the announcement, he was projected as a first round pick.

==Professional career==

===2011  NFL Draft===

Dareus was selected third overall by the Buffalo Bills in 2011. He was the highest selected defensive lineman in Crimson Tide history, and the highest selected Alabama defensive player since Cornelius Bennett in 1987.

===Buffalo Bills===

Dareus was selected with the 3rd overall pick in the 2011 NFL Draft by the Buffalo Bills on April 28, 2011.[tpl]cite news |title=Four UA players go in first round |url=http://alabama.rivals.com/content.asp?CID=1216817 |first=Chase |last=Goodbread |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |date=April 28, 2011 |accessdate=April 29, 2011[/tpl] On April 29, the day after the first round, the Bills announced that Dareus would wear jersey number 99. On July 29, 2011, Dareus signed a 4 year deal with the Bills worth $20.4 million.[tpl]cite news |title=Bills' First-Rounder Dareus Gets $20.4 million Deal|url=http://blogs.buffalonews.com/billboard/2011/07/bills-first-round-pick-dareus-gets-204-million-deal.html|first=Mark |last=Gaughan |newspaper=The Buffalo News|date=July 29, 2011 |accessdate=July 30, 2011[/tpl] Dareus started all sixteen games for the Bills, registering 5.5 sacks, the most for a Bills rookie since the now-retired Aaron Schobel, despite suffering injuries to his shoulder and hand during the season.http://www.buffalonews.com/sports/columns/jerry-sullivan/article688731.ece
In 2011 Dareus became a member of School of the Legends (SOTL), an official partner of the NFLPA.[tpl]cite web |author=Anthony Stalter |title=Kevin Carter talks NFL lockout, Steve Spurrier and SchoolOfTheLegends.com |publisher=The Scores Report |date=2011-06-06 |url=http://www.scoresreport.com/2011/06/06/kevin-carter-talks-nfl-lockout-steve-spurrier-and-schoolofthelegends-com/[/tpl]
In 2012, Dareus was excused from the team after his younger brother was shot and killed.https://sports.yahoo.com/news/bills-dt-dareus-excused-brother-164215061--nfl.html
He was named to his first Pro Bowl in 2013 as an injury replacement for Justin Smith of the 49ers.
