[[Alexander City micropolitan area]]

CATEGORIES: Alexander City micropolitan area, Geography of Tallapoosa County, Alabama, Geography of Coosa County, Alabama, Micropolitan areas of Alabama

The Alexander City Micropolitan Statistical Area, as defined by the United States Census Bureau, is an area consisting of two counties in Alabama, anchored by the city of Alexander City. As of the 2010 census, the μSA had a population of 53,155.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.census.gov/popest/metro/tables/2009/CBSA-EST2009-01.csv | title = Table 1. Annual Estimates of the Population of Metropolitan and Micropolitan Statistical Areas: April 1, 2000 to July 1, 2009 (CBSA-EST2009-01) | format = CSV | work = 2009 Population Estimates | publisher = United States Census Bureau, Population Division | date = 2010-03-23 | accessdate = 2010-03-24[/tpl]
The Alexander City Micropolitan Statistical Area is part of the Montgomery–Alexander City Combined Statistical Area.

==Demographics==

As of the census[tpl]GR|2[/tpl] of 2000, there were 53,677 people, 21,338 households, and 15,217 families residing within the μSA. The racial makeup of the μSA was 71.31% White, 27.37% African American, 0.28% Native American, 0.15% Asian, 0.01% Pacific Islander, 0.27% from other races, and 0.62% from two or more races. Hispanic or Latino of any race were 0.75% of the population.
The median income for a household in the μSA was $30,309, and the median income for a family was $37,115. Males had a median income of $26,974 versus $19,088 for females. The per capita income for the μSA was $15,892.
