[[Johann Jakob Quandt]]

CATEGORIES: 1686 births, 1772 deaths, German librarians, German Lutheran theologians, Translators of the Bible into Lithuanian, University of Königsberg alumni, University of Königsberg faculty, People from Königsberg, People from the Duchy of Prussia

Johann Jakob Quandt ([tpl]lang-lt|Jonas Jokūbas Kvantas[/tpl]; 27 March 1686 in Königsberg – 17 January 1772 in Königsberg) was a German orthodox Lutheran theologian. He opposed Pietism, but sympathized with Wolffianism.[tpl]cite book| title=Kant: a biography |first=Manfred |last=Kuehn |page=70 | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=V-v7ye60jSUC&pg=PA70 |publisher=Cambridge University Press |year=2001 |isbn=978-0-521-49704-6[/tpl] He is known for sponsoring the first complete translation of the Bible into Lithuanian, the Quandt Bible of 1735.[tpl]cite book| title=The Oxford companion to the Bible |first=Bruce Manning |last=Metzger |coauthors=Michael David Coogan |publisher=Oxford University Press |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Y2KGVuym5OUC&pg=PA771 |year=1993 |isbn= 978-0-19-504645-8 |page=771 [/tpl] He was also a librarian of the Königsberg Public Library.
