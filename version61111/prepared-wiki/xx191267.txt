[[Kisan Kanya (1937 film)]]

CATEGORIES: Cinecolor films, Hindi-language films, 1937 films, Films based on Indian novels

{{Blacklisted-links|1=

Kisan Kanya was a 1937 Hindi feature film which was directed by Moti B. Gidvani and produced by Ardeshir Irani of Imperial Pictures. It is largely remembered by the Indian public on account of it being India's first indigenously made colour film.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.thecolorsofindia.com/interesting-facts/cinema/first-color-film-made-in-india.html|title=First Color Film Made in India|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=colorsofindia.com[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2004/may/27cinema.htm|title=Know more about Hindi cinema|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=rediff.com[/tpl][tpl]cite book | title=Bollywood: A Guidebook to Popular Hindi Cinema |url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=DIVlw5AzQTgC&pg=PT220&lpg=PT220&dq=kisan+kanya+color+film+story&source=web&ots=M1G9HE5EDT&sig=Y6BMrc4JzN8FwfqFdAoZDu8jjRM&hl=en| last=Ganti| first=Tejaswini| year=2004| pages=208| publisher=Routledge| ISBN=0415288533[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.culturopedia.com/Cinema/earlyera.html|title=The Early Films (1930s-1960s)|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=culturopedia.com[/tpl]

==Production==

Film pioneer Ardeshir Irani, who had produced notable films as Nala Damayanti (1920) which was India's first international co-production[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.punjabilok.com/misc/movies/first_indian_cinema.htm|title=The firsts in Indian cinema |accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=culturopedia.com|author=Subir Ghosh[/tpl] (with Italy) and India's first talkie Alam Ara (1931)[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.indianetzone.com/9/ardeshir_irani.htm|title=Ardeshir Irani, Indian film maker|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=indianetzone.com[/tpl] conceived the idea of producing a color film. The result of his efforts was the color film Kisan Kanya made with the Cinecolor process[tpl]cite book | title=Encyclopedia of Hindi cinema| url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=8y8vN9A14nkC&pg=PT76&lpg=PT76&dq=Kisan+Kanya+film+cinecolor&source=web&ots=rvPx1hxmb8&sig=LiIkMiY2Ks1-pNU7fb5iV2u4l9A&hl=en| last=Encyclopædia Britannica| coauthors=Gulzar, Govind Nihalani, Saibal Chatterjee| year=2003| pages=52| publisher=Encyclopædia Britannica (India) Pvt Ltd| ISBN=8179910660[/tpl] whose process rights Irani had obtained from an American company.
V. Shantaram had earlier produced a Marathi film Sairandhri (1933) which had scenes in color.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.culturopedia.com/first_in_india_misc.html|title=First in India|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=culturopedia.com[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/mp/2002/05/02/stories/2002050200380100.htm|title=A navrang of Shantaram's films |accessdate=2008-04-29[/tpl] However, the film was processed and printed in Germany Kisan Kanya was, therefore, India's first indigenously made color film.

==Plot==

Kisan Kanya was based on a novel by Saadat Hasan Manto and focussed on the plight of poor farmers. The story revolved on the life of a poor peasant Ram (Nisar) who was being ill-treated by his landlord Ghani.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0214858/plotsummary|title=Plot summary from the IMDB entry for Kisan Kanya|accessdate=2008-04-29|publisher=IMDB[/tpl] Eventually, Ghani is murdered and Ram becomes the prime suspect in the eyes of the public. The film performed moderately at the box-office.
