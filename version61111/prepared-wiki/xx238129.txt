[[Sour Sweet]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 novels, 1960s in fiction, Novels set in London

Sour Sweet is a novel by Timothy Mo first published in 1982. 
Written as a 'sour sweet' comedy the story follows the tribulations of a Hong Kong Chinese immigrant and his initially reluctant wife as they attempt to make a home for themselves in 1960s[tpl]cite book |title=The Cambridge history of twentieth-century English literature |last=Marcus |first=Laura |coauthors=Peter Nicholls |year=2004 |publisher=Cambridge University Press |page=744 [/tpl] London.[tpl]cite book |title=Defining new idioms and alternative forms of expression |chapter=Acculturation and family structure: Mo's Sour Sweet, Kureishi's The Buddha of Suburbia, Ishiguro's A Pale View of Hills|last=Mergenthal |first=Silvia |editor=Eckhard Breitinger |year=1996 |publisher=Rodopi |page=119 [/tpl] It was awarded the Hawthornden Prize for 1982. 

==Film adaptation==

The novel was adaptated for the screen in 1988. Mike Newell directed. Novelist Ian McEwan wrote the script.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0096146/|title=Soursweet[/tpl]
