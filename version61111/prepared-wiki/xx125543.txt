[[Moviewatch (Channel 4 programme)]]

CATEGORIES: 1993 British television programme debuts, 1997 British television programme endings, Film criticism television series, Channel 4 television programmes

Moviewatch was a film review television programme broadcast on Channel 4 in the United Kingdom.[tpl]cite web|author=Johanna Thomas-Corr |url=http://www.standard.co.uk/lifestyle/johnny-vaughan-delves-deep-into-his-trials-and-tribulations-6571383.html |title=Johnny Vaughan delves deep into his trials and tribulations - Life & Style - London Evening Standard |publisher=Standard.co.uk |date=2011-02-25 |accessdate=2013-10-17[/tpl][tpl]cite news|author=Ben Summerskill |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2003/feb/09/broadcasting.comment |title=The Observer Profile: Johnny Vaughan | Media | The Observer |publisher=Guardian |date= 10 February 2003|accessdate=2013-10-17 |location=London[/tpl] It ran from 1993 to 1997.
The programme was hosted by Johnny Vaughan. It was broadcast weekly. Each week four members of the public would watch four forthcoming films. They would then discuss each film in turn, along with Johnny.
The guest reviewers would mark each film out of ten. The film with the highest rating at the end of the show was declared the Moviewatch Film of the Week. During the show's run, only one film achieved a perfect 10/10 from all four reviewers - Spike Lee's Malcolm X.
