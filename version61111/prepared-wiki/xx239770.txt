[[Joe Kissock]]

CATEGORIES: 1893 births, 1959 deaths, People from Saltcoats, New Zealand association footballers, New Zealand international footballers, Association football defenders, The Football League players, Bury F.C. players, Bristol Rovers F.C. players, Peebles Rovers F.C. players

Joseph Gartshore Kissock (5 June 1893–1 September 1959)[tpl]Cite book|last1=Byrne |first1=Stephen |last2=Jay |first2=Mike |title=Bristol Rovers Football Club - The Definitive History 1883-2003 |year=2003 |publisher=Tempus |location=Stroud |isbn=0-7524-2717-2 |page=500[/tpl] is a former association football player who represented New Zealand at international level.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.ultimatenzsoccer.com/NZRepSoccer/id1708.htm|title=A-International Appearances - Overall|author=|publisher=The Ultimate New Zealand Soccer Website|date=|accessdate=18 Oct 2009[/tpl]
He played for Vale of Clyde in his native Scotland, before moving to England in 1919 to join Bury. He spent a year with Bristol Rovers during the 1921–22 season, and after a brief spell back in Scotland with Peebles Rovers, he moved to the United States in 1922. He stayed in America for a year, and moved to New Zealand in 1923.[tpl]Cite book|last1=Jay |first1=Mike |last2=Byrne |first2=Stephen |title=Pirates in Profile: A Who's Who of Bristol Rovers Players |year=1994 |publisher=Potten, Baber & Murray |location=Bristol |isbn=0-9524835-0-5 |pages=144–145[/tpl] Here, he played for Porirua team Hospital.[tpl]HiltonNZAssoc[/tpl] p. 131
Kissock made his full All Whites debut in a 1-2 loss to Australia on 9 June 1923 and played two further official internationals that month, beating Australia 3-2 and 4-1. These were to be his only official internationals for New Zealand.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.ultimatenzsoccer.com/NZRepSoccer/id1559.htm|title=A-International Lineups|author=|publisher=The Ultimate New Zealand Soccer Website|date=|accessdate=18 Oct 2009[/tpl]
