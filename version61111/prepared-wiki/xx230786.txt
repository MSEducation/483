[[Malaysia–Switzerland relations]]

CATEGORIES: Bilateral relations of Malaysia, Bilateral relations of Switzerland, Malaysia–Switzerland relations

Malaysia–Switzerland relations refers to bilateral foreign relations between the two countries, Malaysia and Switzerland. Malaysia has an embassy in Bern,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kln.gov.my/web/che_berne/home|title=Official Website of Embassy of Malaysia, Bern|publisher=Ministry of Foreign Affairs, Malaysia|accessdate=7 February 2014[/tpl] and Switzerland has an embassy in Kuala Lumpur.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.eda.admin.ch/eda/en/home/reps/asia/vmys/embkua.html|title=Embassy Kuala Lumpur|publisher=Federal Department of Foreign Affairs|accessdate=7 February 2014[/tpl]

==History==

In 19th century, Swiss trading companies has set up operations in Malaysia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.eda.admin.ch/eda/en/home/reps/asia/vmys/bilmal.html|title=Bilateral relations between Switzerland and Malaysia|publisher=Federal Department of Foreign Affairs|accessdate=7 February 2014[/tpl] While the diplomatic relations has been established since 1963.

==Economic relations==

Switzerland maintains good contacts in the educational field and supports projects of the International Tropical Timber Organization (ITTO) for the sustainable management of tropical forests. Many Swiss companies has operating in Malaysia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.m2mevolution.com/news/2012/02/10/6112723.htm|title=MALAYSIA,SWITZERLAND : Swiss Semiconductor firm chooses Penang for its new manufacturing unit|publisher=Machine Evolution|date=10 February 2012|accessdate=7 February 2014[/tpl] Since 2011, there are currently 792 Swiss nationals who reside in Malaysia. Several agreements, such as avoidance on tax were also been signed.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hasil.gov.my/pdf/pdfam/Switzerland.pdf|title=AGREEMENT BETWEEN THE GOVERNMENT OF MALAYSIA AND THE SWISS FEDERAL COUNCIL FOR THE AVOIDANCE OF DOUBLE TAXATION WITH RESPECT TO TAXES ON INCOME|publisher=Inland Revenue Board of Malaysia|date=1 January 1974|accessdate=7 February 2014|format=PDF[/tpl] Many Swiss foods brand such as chocolates and yogurts has reach Malaysian markets and popular on the country.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.moodiereport.com/document.php?c_id=37&doc_id=21158|title=Toblerone brings a piece of Switzerland to Malaysia|publisher=The Moodie Report|date=6 July 2009|accessdate=7 February 2014|author=Melody Ng[/tpl]
