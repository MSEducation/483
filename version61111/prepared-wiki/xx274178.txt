[[Alby Naismith]]

CATEGORIES: 1917 births, Australian rules footballers from Victoria (Australia), Hawthorn Football Club players, Oakleigh Football Club players, Year of death missing

Albert "Alby" Naismith (born 13 November 1917) was an Australian rules footballer who played with Hawthorn in the Victorian Football League (VFL).
Naismith was a forward, used in the pockets and at centre half-forward.[tpl]cite book|last=Holmesby, Russell & Main, Jim|title=The Encyclopedia Of AFL Footballers| publisher=BAS Publishing|year=2007|isbn=978-1-920910-78-5[/tpl] An Alphington recruit, he was the leading goal-kicker for Hawthorn on two occasions, with 30 goals in 1938 and 25 goals in 1940.AFL Tables: Albert Naismith
He had a brother, Herb Naismith, who played for Collingwood. They were both sons of Wally Naismith, a premiership player at Fitzroy.
Naismith later played for Oakleigh, as a full-forward.The Argus,"A. Naismith Deadly At Oakleigh", 24 April 1950, p. 20
