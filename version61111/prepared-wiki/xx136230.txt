[[Georgia State Route 339]]

CATEGORIES: State highways in Georgia (U.S. state), Transportation in Towns County, Georgia

State Route 339 (SR 339) is a [tpl]convert|3.5|mi|km|adj=mid|-long[/tpl] state highway completely within Towns County near Young Harris, Georgia, in the North Georgia mountains, just south of the North Carolina state line. It is locally known as Crooked Creek Road.

==Route description==

SR 339 begins at an intersection with SR 66 just north of SR 66's crossing of Brasstown Creek. This intersection is approximately [tpl]convert|4000|ft|m[/tpl] south of the North Carolina state line. It travels in an east-northeast direction until it meets its eastern terminus, and intersection with SR 17/SR 515 approximately [tpl]convert|1000|ft|m[/tpl] south of the state line. The appropriately named road includes a number of sharp curves and turns along its length.

==History==

SR 339 first became part of the state highway system in 1961, and has seen little change since then, except for shoulder widening.[tpl]cn|date=October 2012[/tpl]
