[[Rothschild]]

CATEGORIES: Rothschild family, Jewish surnames, Yiddish-language surnames

Rothschild (German Rotschild Schild: [tpl]IPA-de|ˈʁoːt.ʃɪlt|[/tpl]), meaning "red shield", is a surname derived from a house distinguished with a red shield (Middle High German rot (or roth)= "red" + schild = "shield"), the earliest recorded example dating from the 13th century.[tpl]cite web|url=http://surnames.behindthename.com/name/rothschild|title=Rothschild|publisher=Behind the Name[/tpl] In English-speaking countries it is often pronounced as "Roth's child".
The German surname "Rothschild" is not related to the Scottish/Irish surname of "Rothchilds" from the United Kingdom.

==People==

Most notable people with this surname are descendants of Mayer Amschel Rothschild who formed a European financial dynasty and, in modern history, perhaps the wealthiest family by the scale of their private fortune.
