Jennifer Beals (born December 19, 1963) is an American actress and a former teen model. She played the role of Alexandra "Alex" Owens in the 1983 film Flashdance, and appeared as Bette Porter on the Showtime drama series The L Word.  She earned an NAACP Image Award and a Golden Globe Award nomination for the former. She has appeared in more than 50 films.

==Early life==

Beals was born on the South Side of Chicago,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.suntimes.com/entertainment/television/2830126,SHO-Sunday-ride24.article |title='Ride-along' creator, cast case life on street with cops|publisher= suntimes.com |accessdate=2010-10-24|date=2010-10-24|author= Mike Thomas[/tpl] the daughter of Jeanne (née Anderson), an elementary school teacher, and Alfred Beals, who owned grocery stores.Jennifer Beals Biography (1963–)"Article on Beals" March 1990, Ebony She is bi-racial;[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.windycitymediagroup.com/ARTICLE.php?AID=2342 |title= Television: The L Word's Jennifer Beals Flashdance Star in Town for HRC Gala|publisher= Windy City Times|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2003-05-21|author= Gregg Shapiro[/tpl] her father was African American, and her mother is Irish American.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.afterellen.com/archive/ellen/People/jenniferbeals.html |title= Jennifer Beals Tackles Issues of Race, Sexuality on The L Word |publisher= AfterEllen|accessdate=2011-02-27|author= Sarah Warn|date=December 2003[/tpl] She has two brothers, Bobby and Gregory.Jennifer Beals Biography | TVGuide.com Her father died when Beals was nine years old,[tpl]cite web |url=http://readingeagle.com/article.aspx?id=286772 |title=Jennifer Beals relies on her masculine side for new series |publisher=readingeagle.com |accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2011-02-31|author= Nancy Mills[/tpl] and her mother married Edward Cohen in 1981."With Some Fancy Footwork—not All Her Own—yale Freshman Jennifer Beals Gets Top Marks for Flashdance" May 16, 1983, People Magazine Beals has said her biracial heritage had some effect on her, as she "always lived sort of on the outside", with an idea "of being the other in society". She got her first job at age 13 at an ice cream store, using her height at the time (she is now nearly [tpl]height|ft=5|in=9[/tpl]), to convince her boss she was 16.
Beals was inspired to become an actress by two events: doing a high school production of Fiddler on the Roof and seeing Balm in Gilead with Joan Allen while volunteer-ushering at the Steppenwolf Theatre.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/arts/television/jennifer-beals-bringing-it-all-back-home/article1915762/ |title=Jennifer Beals: Bringing it all back home|publisher=theglobeandmail.com |accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2011-02-22|author= Andrew Ryan[/tpl]
Beals graduated from the progressive Francis W. Parker School. She also was chosen to attend the elite Goodman Theatre Young People's Drama Workshop. Beals attended Yale University, receiving a B.A. in American literature in 1987;[tpl]cite web |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20040215172036/http://www.yaleherald.com/article.php?Article=2799|archivedate= 2004-02-15 |title= TV show may increase lesbian awareness at 'Gay Ivy' |publisher= Yale Herald|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2004-01-23|author= Mitchell Hoffman|url=http://www.yaleherald.com/article.php?Article=2799[/tpl] she deferred a term so she could film Flashdance.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=PO0gAAAAIBAJ&sjid=_XEFAAAAIBAJ&pg=1230,4525226&dq=jennifer-beals+rockwell&hl=en |title= Actress-Yale Grad returns to films|publisher= The Day (New London)|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 1987-06-19|author= Bob Thomas[/tpl] While at Yale, Beals was a resident of Morse College.

==Career==

===Film===

Beals had a minor role in the 1980 film My Bodyguard,[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=vz4dAAAAIBAJ&sjid=lKYEAAAAIBAJ&pg=3014,6989762&dq=jennifer-beals+rockwell&hl=en |title= In Step With: Jennifer Beals|publisher= Anchorage Daily News|accessdate=2011-02-27|author= James Brady|date= 1987-11-29[/tpl] then came to fame with her starring part in Flashdance. The third-highest grossing U.S. film of 1983, Flashdance is the story of 18-year-old Alex, a welder by day and exotic dancer by night, whose dream is to be accepted someday at an illustrious school of dance. Beals was cast for this key role while still a student at Yale. She was nominated for a Golden Globe and the film received an Academy Award for Best Song. Many of Beals' elaborate dance moves were actually performed by stunt double Marine Jahan.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20057935,00.html |title=Maniac on the Floor|publisher= Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=2011-02-27|author=Mandi Bierly|date= 2007-10-01 [/tpl]
After she filmed Flashdance, Beals resumed her studies, making only one film during that time: playing the titular character The Bride with singer-actor Sting, a gothic horror film loosely based on the 1935 classic Bride of Frankenstein,[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.google.ca/newspapers?id=df9kAAAAIBAJ&sjid=W4gNAAAAIBAJ&pg=2340,179118&dq=jennifer-beals&hl=en |title= Flashdancer Jennifer Beals off to meet Frankenstein|publisher= Edmonton Journal|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 1984-06-11[/tpl] shot during her summer break.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=DPEvAAAAIBAJ&sjid=-PwDAAAAIBAJ&pg=6756,3130490&dq=jennifer+beals&hl=en |title= Jennifer Beals dons blue dress|publisher= Lakeland Ledger|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 1995-10-03|author= James Ryan[/tpl] She also appeared in the "Cinderella" episode of Faerie Tale Theatre. Beals was asked by Joel Schumacher to do St. Elmo's Fire but turned it down, preferring to stay at Yale.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.google.ca/newspapers?id=z65JAAAAIBAJ&sjid=xA4NAAAAIBAJ&pg=3098,1906787&dq=jennifer-beals&hl=en |title= Four women rebuild their lives and dreams on "2000 Malibu Road"|publisher=Bangor Daily News|accessdate= 2011-02-27|date= 1992-08-21|author= Wendy Wallace[/tpl]
Starring opposite Nicolas Cage, the actress portrays a lusty and thirsty vampire in 1989's Vampire's Kiss.
In 1995, Beals and Denzel Washington co-starred in Devil in a Blue Dress, a period film based on a Walter Mosley novel featuring L.A. private detective, Easy Rawlins. Beals plays a biracial woman passing for white. That same year she appeared with Tim Roth in two segments of the four-story anthology Four Rooms, one of which was directed by her then-husband, Alexandre Rockwell.
Rockwell had previously directed her in the 1992 independent film In the Soup, which was a Grand Prize winner at the Sundance Film Festival.[tpl]cite web |url=http://articles.latimes.com/1992-08-23/news/tv-6965_1_jennifer-beals |title=Jennifer Beals Slow Dances To Hollywood|publisher= Los Angeles Times|accessdate=2011-02-27|author= Susan King|date= 1992-08-23[/tpl] In 2003, she played one of the sequestered jury members in the film adaptation of Runaway Jury.
She had a leading role in 2006's The Grudge 2, sequel to the hit horror film of two years earlier. In 2010, Beals reunited with Denzel Washington in the post-Apocalyptic action drama, The Book of Eli, where she played a blind woman who is the mother of Mila Kunis' character and a servant of Gary Oldman's.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.artistdirect.com/nad/news/article/0,,4941449,00.html |title=Jennifer Beals Joins Eli|publisher=www.artistdirect.com |accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2009-02-10[/tpl]

===Television===

In 1992, she appeared in 2000 Malibu Road as attorney Perry Quinn. It was her first ongoing television series; she said she had been leery as she previously had not "found a character I wanted to live with for several years".[tpl]cite web |url= http://news.google.ca/newspapers?id=R8BKAAAAIBAJ&sjid=vukMAAAAIBAJ&pg=4563,5996860&dq=jennifer-beals&hl=en|title= Character lured Beals to TV role |publisher= The Daily Gazette|accessdate=2011-02-27|author= Jerry Buck|date= 1992-08-23[/tpl]
In 2004, Beals made a brief cameo in the final episode of Frasier. In 2007, she appeared in the small TV drama My Name Is Sarah, in which she plays Sarah Winston, a sober woman who joins Alcoholics Anonymous to conduct research for her book but finds herself falling in love with a recovering alcoholic and - as a result - having to deal with her original deception in joining the group.[tpl]cite web|title=My Name is Sarah|url=http://www.mylifetime.com/movies/my-name-is-sarah|publisher=MyLifetime.com|accessdate=10 May 2013[/tpl]
Beals starred in Showtime Network's The L Word, wherein she played Bette Porter, an Ivy League-educated lesbian. At Beals' request, Bette was made biracial, enabling Pam Grier's Kit Porter character to become Bette's half-sister.[tpl]cite web |url=http://msnbc.msn.com/id/6975797/site/todayshow/ns/today-entertainment/ |title=Jennifer Beals returns with ‘The L Word'|publisher= MSNBC|accessdate=2011-02-27|date= 2005-02-15 [/tpl] Beals' initial research for the part focused more on the woman's profession as an art museum director than on her life as a lesbian;[tpl]Cite episode|accessdate= 2011-02-27|series= Live with Regis and Kelly|serieslink= Live with Regis and Kelly|airdate= 2004-03-31|title=Live with Regis and Kelly[/tpl] "I was much more obsessed by the work that Bette did, because she was so obsessed by the work that she did."[tpl]Cite episode|series= Fresh Air|serieslink= Fresh Air|network = National Public Radio|airdate = 2004-04-06|title=Fresh Air[/tpl] The series ran for six seasons and ended in March 2009.
She also appears alongside Tim Roth in Lie to Me, as Cal Lightman's ex-wife, Zoe Landau.
Beals is the female lead in Fox's TV drama The Chicago Code. Her character Teresa Colvin is Chicago's first female police superintendent.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/jennifer-beals-lands-role-fox-21943 |title=Jennifer Beals lands role in Fox drama|publisher= The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate= 2011-02-27|author= Nellie Andreeva|date= 2010-10-14[/tpl] The series was canceled after its first season."Chicago Code Canceled: Fox Scraps Crime Drama After One Season" May 11, 2011, Huffington Post
Beals turned down an offer to appear on Dancing with the Stars, saying: "I am not a dancer. They asked me and I said 'no.' You could back up a truck to my door filled with cash and I wouldn't do it."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20466926,00.html |title=Dancing with the Stars Season 12 Cast - Jennifer Beals Says No |publisher= People|accessdate= 2011-02-27|date= 2011-02-17|author= Monica Rizzo[/tpl]
In 2013, Beals signed on for the main role of the ABC drama pilot Westside produced by McG and developed by Ilene Chaiken.[tpl]cite web|url=http://insidetv.ew.com/2013/02/12/abc-drama-pilot-stages-l-word-reunion/|title=ABC drama pilot stages 'L Word' reunion|author=Lynette Rice|date=February 12, 2013|work=Entertainment Weekly|accessdate=2013-02-23[/tpl]
On March 10, 2014, it was announced that Beals would star as Dr. Kathryn Russo in “Proof,” a TNT supernatural medical drama about a hard-nosed surgeon struggling with the loss of her teenage son who begins to investigate that there may be life after death. The series will be produced by Kyra Sedgwick.
