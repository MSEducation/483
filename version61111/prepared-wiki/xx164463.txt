Sir Mark Ellis Powell Jones (born 5 February 1951)[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.debretts.com/people/biographies/browse/j/7145/Mark%20Ellis%20Powell+JONES.aspx |title=Sir Mark Jones |work=Debrett's People of Today |accessdate=2010-12-05[/tpl] is a British art historian, numismatist and museum director. He is the current Master of Saint Cross College, Oxford. Previously, from 2001 to 2011, he was director of the Victoria and Albert Museum.Saint Cross College, Oxford: New Master elected. 17 November 2010

==Personal==

Jones was born to writer and historian, Ann Paludan. He read politics, philosophy and economics at Worcester College, Oxford, before taking an MA degree in art history at the Courtauld Institute of Art.

==Career==

Jones spent 18 years in the Department of Coins and Medals at the British Museum, where he curated the acclaimed exhibition FAKE? The Art of Deception. In 1992 he was appointed director of the National Museums of Scotland, and here he gained a high reputation. He oversaw the creation in 1998 of the Museum of Scotland, which went on to win 22 prizes for its displays and a Stirling Prize nomination for its building. 
In May 2001 Jones became director of the Victoria and Albert Museum. On his first day as director, he announced the scrapping of entry charges to the museum.[tpl]cite news | author= | title= Museums set dates for free entry| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/1306644.stm | work=BBC News | date=1 May 2001 | accessdate=11 December 2011[/tpl] Under his directorship a number of renewal projects have been completed, including the Medieval and Renaissance galleries which opened in 2009.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.vam.ac.uk/page/c/completed-futureplan-projects/ |title=Victoria and Albert Museum: Completed FuturePlan projects |accessdate=7 December 2011[/tpl]
