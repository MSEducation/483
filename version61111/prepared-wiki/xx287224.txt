[[Raj Mathai]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1971 births, American television journalists, People from Mumbai, People from Palo Alto, California

Raj Mathai is an American television journalist. He is a four-time Emmy Award winner at NBC owned KNTV in the San Francisco Bay Area where he anchors the nightly newscasts.[tpl]cite news | last = | first = | coauthors = | title =Raj Mathai Is A Four Time Emmy Winner Who Has Traveled The World For NBC Bay Area | newspaper =NBC Bay Area (KNTV) | location = | pages = | language = | publisher = | date = | url =http://www.nbcbayarea.com/on-air/about-us/Raj_Mathai.html | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl]

He was on the air for seven consecutive hours and was the first journalist to interview a survivor of the crash.[tpl]cite video | people =Benjamin Levy, Jon Schuppe, & Raj Mathai | title =Flight 214 Survivor Describes Normal Flight That Went Suddenly Wrong | medium =Television News | publisher =NBC | location =San Francisco, California | date =31 July 2013 | url =http://www.nbcbayarea.com/news/local/Flight--214499761.html [/tpl]  
Prior to his News career, Mathai was a sportscaster for NBC Bay Area (1998-2010). In 1995 he was attributed with being the first Indian sportscaster for a network affiliate in the United States when he began sportscasting for KYMA. He worked for NBC affiliates KNSD in San Diego, California, KYMA in Yuma, Arizona, and KSEE in Fresno, California before arriving at KNTV in 1998.[tpl]cite news | last = | first = | coauthors = | title =Meet Raj Mathai, Bay Area NBC’s Spots Director | newspaper =Blog Talk Radio| location = | pages = | language = | publisher = | date =14 May 2010 | url =http://www.blogtalkradio.com/saja/2010/05/14/meet-raj-mathai-bay-area-nbcs-sports-director | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl] He has reported on-location from the Olympics from London, Vancouver, Turin, Athens, and Salt Lake City. He was also an Olympic torch bearer in 1996, 2002, and 2008. One of his most notable roles was that of co-host on Sports Sunday along with Jerry Rice, Nnamdi Asomugha, Jeff Garcia and Ken Norton, Jr.[tpl]cite news | last =Ryan | first =John | coauthors = | title =Amping Up Coverage | newspaper =San Jose Mercury News| location = | pages = | language = | publisher =HighBeam | date =7 September 2006 | url =http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-151062864.html | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl][tpl]cite news | last =Staff Report | first = | coauthors = | title =Airwaves: Mathai An Ideal Host For Postgame Show | newspaper =The San Francisco Examiner| location = | pages = | language = | publisher = | date =11 October 2009 | url =http://www.sfexaminer.com/sanfrancisco/airwaves-mathai-an-ideal-host-for-postgame-show/Content?oid=2141759 | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl] He was formerly part of the NBC broadcast team for the San Francisco Giants along with Jon Miller and Mike Krukow.

==Awards==

Mathai is a four-time Emmy Award winner, including regional Emmys for Outstanding Cultural Program in 2012 for his coverage of the London Olympics and Outstanding On-Camera Talent in 2011.[tpl]cite web | last = | first = | authorlink = | coauthors = | title =NBC Bay Area Leads The Winners At The 42nd Annual Northern CA Emmy Awards | work = | publisher =NBC Universal | date =19 June 2013 | url =http://www.nbcuni.com/corporate/newsroom/nbc-bay-area-leads-the-winners-at-the-42nd-annual-northern-ca-emmy-awards/ | format = | doi = | accessdate =7 August 2013[/tpl] He also won a regional Emmy for Outstanding Sports Program in 1998 and 2002.

==Personal==

Mathai was born in Trivandrum,Kerala, India. He lived in Mumbai  until age 7 when he moved Palo Alto, California.[tpl]cite news | last =Cowan | first =Marlene | coauthors = | title =Raj Mathai: LAHS Grad Shares Secrets To TV Success In LA Rotary Club Talk | newspaper =Los Angeles Town Crier | location = | pages = | language = | publisher = | date =24 April 2013 | url =http://www.losaltosonline.com/news/sections/community/44532-J46079 | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl] He comes from a family of journalists and writers including his father TJ Mathew who ran magazines in India in the 1970s and his uncle T. J. S. George who founded Asiaweek.[tpl]cite news | last =Vivek | first = | coauthors = | title =Raj Matha – USA’s First Indian Sportscaster Talks About…Well Sports & Some More! | newspaper =Indian Entertainment | location = | pages = | language = | publisher = | date =29 November 2009 | url =http://www.indianentertainment.info/2009/11/29/raj-mathai-%E2%80%93-usa%E2%80%99s-first-indian-sportscaster-talks-about-%E2%80%A6well-sports-some-more.html | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl]
Mathai is the cousin of best-selling authors Abraham Verghese and Jeet Thayil.  He is also the nephew of Thomas Kailath, noted Stanford Professor.
Mathai attended Los Altos High School and San Diego State University where he graduated with a degree in journalism and political science in 1993. Prior to his career as a broadcaster, Mathai was a public relations assistant with the San Diego Chargers.[tpl]cite news | last =Del Grande | first =Dave | coauthors = | title =Mathai Getting Ready For Giant Change | newspaper =The Oakland Tribune | location = | pages = | language = | publisher =HighBeam | date =6 November 2007 | url =http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-10193436.html | accessdate =6 August 2013 [/tpl] He was also  part of the Super Bowl staff for the National Football League in 1993 and 1994.
