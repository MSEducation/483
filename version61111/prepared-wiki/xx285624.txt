[[Guy Olivier Faure]]

CATEGORIES: 1943 births, Living people, University of Paris faculty, French sociologists, French political scientists

Guy Olivier Faure (born in 1943 in France), professor of International Negotiation.

==Academic  work==

Faure is a professor and researcher on “International Negotiation”,[tpl]cite news|title=What failed negotiations teach us|url=http://www.bostonglobe.com/ideas/2012/12/09/what-failed-negotiations-teach/i3FwKpWFfJ5bN0X33gEqxH/story.html|accessdate=2013-08-12|newspaper=Boston Globe|date=2012-12-09[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|title=Entretien avec  Guy-Olivier Faure|journal=Négociations|year=2011/2012|issue=16|pages=117–133|url=http://www.cairn.info/resume.php?ID_ARTICLE=NEG_016_0117|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] “Conflict Resolution”, and “Strategic Thinking and Action”, which are subjects he has introduced and taught at the Sorbonne University, Paris. He is a member of the editorial board of the three major international journals dealing with negotiation theory and practice: Group Decision and Negotiation (New York),Group Decision and Negotiation,[tpl]cite web|title=Group Decision and Negotiation|url=http://www.springer.com/business+%26+management/operations+research/journal/10726?detailsPage=editorialBoard|publisher=Springer|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] International Negotiation (Washington)[tpl]cite web|title=International Negotiation|url=http://www.negotiations.org/journal.html|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] and Negotiation Journal (Harvard). He is a member of the editorial boards of two major scientific book series: International Negotiation (Brill Academic Publisher);[tpl]cite book|title=International Negotiation|publisher=Brill Academic Publisher|url=http://www.brill.com/international-negotiation#EDIBOA_1[/tpl] Advances in Group Decision and Negotiation (Springer).
He is a member of the steering committee of PIN/Clingendael,[tpl]cite web|title=PIN/Clingendael Steering Committee Members|url=http://www.pin-negotiation.org/index.php?page=80&item=89|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] The Hague, a program on international negotiation processes that links together 5000 people involved in the domain. He has worked on peace issues, especially in French-German cooperation programs within the framework of the international treaty of friendship and cooperation signed in 1963 between these two countries. He has also contributed to field actions on peace making and reconciliation with NGOs in the Middle East.
His major research interests are in diplomatic negotiations, focusing on strategies and intercultural issues. For over 20 years he has been involved with Chinese business issues at the CEIBS (China- Europe International Business School, Shanghai).[tpl]cite web|title=China Europe International Business School|url=http://www.ceibs.edu/faculty/cv/3162.shtml|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] He has published research on multilateral negotiations, problem resolution and problem transformation. He is also concerned with developing interdisciplinary approaches in such domains as terrorism,[tpl]cite web|title=Interview with Professor Guy Olivier Faure: Asymmetric Warfare: Negotiating with Terrorists, CERIS (Centre Européen de Recherches Internationales et Strategiques)|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=CmrwVdKmP7o|accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] and engages in consulting and training activities with governments and international organizations. Among them: The United Nations; UNESCO; the European Union; the World Trade Organization (WTO)''.
He is referenced in the Diplomat’s Dictionary published by the National Defence University Press, Washington, 1994.[tpl]cite book|last=Freeman|first=Charles W. Jr|title=The Diplomat's Dictionary|year=1994|publisher=National Defence University Press|location=Washington[/tpl] He has authored, co-authored and edited 16 books and over 100 articles. His works have been published in twelve different languages.
