[[Michael Anaba]]

CATEGORIES: 1993 births, Living people, Ghanaian footballers, 2013 African U-20 Championship players, 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup players, Asante Kotoko F.C. players, Elche CF players

Michael Anaba is a Ghanaian professional footballer and currently plays for Elche CF in the Spanish La Liga.[tpl]cite web|title=EXCLUSIVE: Kotoko agree to sell Michael Anaba to Elche|url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/exclusive-kotoko-agree-to-sell-michael-anaba-to-elche|publisher=ghanasoccernet.com|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] He plays as a Midfielder. Prior to his move outside Ghana, he played for Asante Kotoko in the Glo Premier League where he won the Premiership in the 2012–2013 season.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.goal.com/en-gh/people/ghana/91983/michael-anaba/national | title=Michael Anaba | publisher=goal.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] He won silver and bronze medals with the Ghana U-20 team during the 2013 African U-20 Championship in Algeria and 2013 FIFA U-20 World Cup respectively.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/live-watch-africa-u20-final-ghana-0-0-egypt/ | title=Black Satellites miss out on CAF U20 trophy after penalties defeat to Egypt | publisher=www.ghanasoccernet.com | accessdate=19 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Ghana Sub 20 vs Iraq Sub 20 Report|url=http://www.goal.com/en-gh/match/116587/ghana-sub-20-vs-iraq-sub-20/report?|publisher=www.goal.com|accessdate=19 August 2013[/tpl]

==Club career==

Michael Anaba started his professional football career with Windy Professionals, a division two football team based in Winneba.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ghanabusinessnews.com/2013/08/13/kotoko-midfielder-anaba-seals-3-year-deal-with-spanish-la-liga/ | title=Michael Anaba seals 3-year deal with Spanish side, Elche CF | publisher=ghanabusiness.com | accessdate=19 August 2013[/tpl] He was signed from the team by Asante Kotoko in 2011 and in 2013 won the Glo Premier League with them.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/breaking-news-asante-kotoko-defend-ghana-premier-league-title-with-amidaus-win/ | title=Asante Kotoko retain Ghana Premier League title with Amidaus win | publisher=ghanasoccernet.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://allsports.com.gh/2013/05/19/glo-pl-preview-kotoko-to-play-in-sunyani/ | title=Michael Anaba | publisher=allsports.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] In August 2013, Elche CF purchased him from Kotoko to play in the Spanish second division, this was after he had been successful in a two-week trial at the club.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.modernghana.com/sports/481702/2/exclusive-kotoko-agree-to-sell.html | title=Kotoko Agree To Sell Michael Anaba | publisher=modernghana.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]

==International Championships==

===CAF U-20 Championship, Algeria 2013===

Anaba was a member of the Ghana Under-20 national team during the qualification rounds for the 2013 African U-20 Championship. In 2013, coach Sellas Tetteh called him up for the Ghana Under-20 national team for the 2013 tournament in Algeria.[tpl]cite web|title=Ghana names final squad for U-20 finals|url=http://www.ghanafa.org/pages/blacksatellites/201303/8200.php|publisher=GhanaFA.org|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl] He was made vice captain to Lawrence Lartey prior to the start of the African tournament.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.goal.com/en-gh/news/4389/ghana/2013/03/10/3812325/kotokos-michael-anaba-named-deputy-skipper-for-black | title=Kotoko's Michael Anaba named deputy skipper for Black Satellites | publisher=goal.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]

===FIFA U-20 Championship, Turkey 2013===

Anaba was a member of the Ghana Under-20 national team that took part in the 2013 FIFA U-20 tournament in Turkey.[tpl]cite web | url=http://allsports.com.gh/2013/06/14/revealed-ghanas-final-21-man-squad-for-u-20-world-cup/ | title=Revealed: Ghana’s final 21-man squad for U-20 World Cup | publisher=allsports.com.gh | accessdate=8 July 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ghanasoccernet.com/ghana-fa-confirms-u20-squad-numbers-for-fifa-world-cup-in-turkey/ | title=Ghana FA confirms U20 squad numbers for FIFA World Cup in Turkey | publisher=ghanasoccernet.com | accessdate=8 July 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.mtnfootball.com/africa/ghana/news/2013/apr/24-ghana-u20-coach-releases-preliminary-world-cup-squad.html | title=Ghana U20 coach releases preliminary World Cup squad | publisher=mtnfootball.com | accessdate=8 July 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://int.soccerway.com/teams/ghana/ghana-under-20/ | title=Ghana Under 20 | publisher=soccerway.com | accessdate=8 July 2013[/tpl] During the tournament he failed to land a permanent place in the team and was used mainly as a substitute. He however, started the semi finals of the tournament in which he captained the Ghanaian team against France. Ghana lost that match 2:1 to the French team. The Ghanaian team ended the tournament with a third place after beating Iraq.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.fifa.com/u20worldcup/matches/round=259227/match=300232840/summary.html | title=Three A's see Ghana finish third | publisher=fifa.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ghanamma.com/full-time-ghana-3-0-iraq-fifa-u20-world-cup-third-place/ | title=Full Time: Ghana 3–0 Iraq Fifa U20 World Cup Third Place | publisher=ghanamma.com | accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]
