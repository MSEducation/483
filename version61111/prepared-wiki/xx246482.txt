[[CNBLUE]]

CATEGORIES: CNBLUE

CNBLUE ([tpl]lang-ko|씨엔블루[/tpl], Japanese: シーエヌブルー), is a South Korean rock band formed in Seoul in 2009. The band consists of Jung Yong-hwa (lead vocals, rhythm guitar), Lee Jong-hyun (lead guitar, vocals), Lee Jung-shin (bass guitar, vocals) and Kang Min-hyuk (drums, vocals). CN is an abbreviation of 'Code Name' while BLUE is an abbreviation of the members’ individual images. 'Burning' represents Lee Jong-hyun, 'Lovely' represents Kang Min-hyuk, 'Untouchable' represents Lee Jung Shin, and 'Emotional' represents Jung Yong-hwa.[tpl]cite web|title=Soompi’s Interview with FTISLAND & CNBLUE (Part 2)|url=http://www.soompi.com/2012/03/11/exclusive-soompis-interview-with-ftisland-cnblue-part-2/|publisher=Soompi|accessdate=January 21, 2013|date=March 11, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=CNBLUE attends press conference prior to fan meet in Singapore|url=http://www.allkpop.com/2010/08/cn-blue-attends-press-conference-prior-to-fan-meet-in-singapore|publisher=allkpop|accessdate=January 21, 2013|date=August 21, 2010[/tpl]
The band debuted on August 19, 2009 in Japan with their first mini album, Now or Never. They released four teasers for their 1st Korean mini album Bluetory, starring one member per each of the videos in the sequence of Jonghyun, Minhyuk, Jungshin and lastly, Yonghwa on January 6, 8, 11 and 13, 2010 respectively. On January 14, 2010, CNBLUE debuted their first mini-album in Korea with the lead single "I'm a Loner" ([tpl]lang-ko|외톨이야[/tpl]). Their fan club is called "Boice".

==History==

===2009: In Japan, Now or Never, and Voice===

In mid-June 2009, CNBLUE began to perform on the streets and in live clubs in Japan.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.nate.com/view/20100921n01741 |title=씨엔블루 "좀더 열심히 활동할 것" |date=September 21, 2010 |language=Korean |trans_title=CNBLUE "Further growth as a live band" |work=Nate News |accessdate=October 21, 2010[/tpl] They formally debuted on August 19, 2009 with Now or Never. The mini-album was recorded completely in English, and failed to chart on the Oricon. In late September, their original bassist Kwon Kwang Jin left the band and was replaced by Lee Jung Shin later that year. Their second extended play Voice was released in November. Recorded with both English and Japanese tracks, Voice peaked at No. 227 and stayed on the Oricon for two weeks.

===2010: Korean debut, ThankU, and other activities===

In January, FNC Entertainment unveiled new teaser pictures for CNBLUE's debut in South Korea. Bluetory was released on January 14, 2010, and stayed at the top of the Gaon Chart for two weeks. "I'm a Loner" ([tpl]lang-ko|외톨이야[/tpl]) was promoted as the title track. The song peaked at No. 2, solidifying their break into the industry. The group had their first televised live stage on KBS Music Bank on January 15. Later, on January 29, the group made their first music show chart win on Music Bank.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2010/01/cnblue-wins-first-music-bank-k-chart |title=CNBLUE wins first Music Bank K-Chart! |date=August 31, 2010 |accessdate=November 30, 2010 |work=allkpop [/tpl] They won their first Inkigayo mutizen on January 31, 2010.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2010/01/cnblue-claims-first-mutizen-award-on-inkigayo |title=CNBLUE claims first Mutizen award on Inkigayo|date=August 31, 2010 |accessdate=November 30, 2010 |work=allkpop [/tpl] CNBLUE holds the record for achieving No. 1 on South Korea's television music charts program in the shortest time since debut, grabbing their first win only 15 days after debuting with "I'm A Loner".[tpl]cite web |url=http://media.daum.net/entertain/enews/view?newsid=20120204141906597&RIGHT_ENTER=R5 |title=아이돌, 데뷔후 1위까지 얼마나 걸리나? ‘씨엔블루 15일 VS 케이윌 4년’|language= Korean |date=August 31, 2010 |accessdate=November 30, 2010 |work=Startoday |publisher= Startoday via Daum [/tpl] The group debuted their new reality show "CNBluetory" on the Korean music channel Mnet on March 10.
In March, the band started preparations for their first full-length album Thank U in Japan. It was released on March 20, 2010 with two new tracks. The album peaked at No. 90 on the Oricon charts, their highest position thus far. In April, their official fan club name was announced as BOICE, a combination of "blue" and "voice."
CNBLUE headed back to Korea in May and released their next single, "Love Light" ([tpl]lang-ko|사랑 빛[/tpl]), on May 10, 2010. The song peaked at No.10 on the Korean national chart and foreshadowed their upcoming release. On May 19, their second extended play Bluelove was released. CNBLUE held their comeback stage on M! Countdown the following day. It was promoted with the track "Love", which charted at No. 2, matching the success the band found earlier with "I'm A Loner". Bluelove peaked at No. 3 on Gaon physical chart. "Love" stayed on the digital charts for many weeks and finally won first place on M! Countdown on June 10, 2010 and Inkigayo on June 20, 2010.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2010/06/cnblue-spreads-love-and-wins-1-on-m-countdown |title=CNBLUE spreads Love and wins No. 1 on M! Countdown |date=June 10, 2010 |accessdate=November 30, 2010 |work=allkpop [/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allkpop.com/2010/06/cnblue-wins-mutizen-with-love-on-inkigayo |title=CNBLUE wins Mutizen with Love on Inkigayo |date=June 20, 2010 |accessdate=November 30, 2010 |work=allkpop [/tpl]
After their success in Korea, the band headed back to Japan. They released their first single "The Way" on June 23. For the first time, they charted in the Oricon Top 40 at No. 26. The release was followed by "I Don't Know Why" on September 16. The song reached No. 8 on the Oricon Daily Chart, marking the first time the band made it inside the Top 10. On the weekly chart, they peaked at No. 15. The band embarked on a small tour across Japan in order to promote the single.[tpl]cite web |url=http://kr.news.yahoo.com/service/news/shellview.htm?articleid=2010090211454953801&linkid=4&newssetid=1352 |title=씨엔블루, 日서 새 싱글내고 라이브 투어 |trans_title=CNBLUE, live tour for new single |date=September 2, 2010 |work=Yahoo! CNBLUE members Kang Min Hyuk and Lee Jong Hyun starred in the Korean movie "Acoustic". They released a song from the OST on October 7 entitled "High Fly".

==Controversy==

CNBLUE and its agency FNC Entertainment were sued by Korean punk band Crying Nut for unauthorized use of their song “Pilsal Off-Side” on Mnet’s “M Countdown” in 2010. Crying Nut also charged that CNBLUE had released a DVD of the concert in Japan and profited from it.[tpl]cite web|first=Hong |last=Gil-dong |url=http://www.koreaherald.com/view.php?ud=20130702000606 |title=CNBLUE, Crying Nut copyright spat gets nasty |publisher=The Korea Herald |date=July 2, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl] Crying Nut claimed that not only did CNBLUE use the song without permission, but also lip-synced along with the original Crying Nut recording.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2013/02/crying-nut-releases-an-official-statement-in-response-to-cnblue-and-fnc-entertainments-apology |title=Crying Nut releases an official statement in response to CNBLUE and FNC Entertainment's apology |publisher=allkpop |date=February 18, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl] Crying Nut allegedly posted online that CNBLUE "must pay the penalties for intellectual property rights infringement". CNBLUE later countersued Crying Nut for allegedly accusing CNBLUE of copyright infringement, filing an injunction against Crying Nut's online criticisms,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2013/07/cnblue-files-injunction-against-crying-nut-for-spreading-false-information |title=CNBLUE files injunction against Crying Nut for spreading false information |publisher=allkpop |date=July 1, 2013 |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl] claiming that the intellectual property theft was the responsibility of CJ E&M and the unspecified company responsible for the DVD sales, and that Crying Nut's allegations may have permanently damaged CNBLUE's reputation as Hallyu stars.
Earlier in 2010, CNBLUE was accused by fans of Ynot for plagiarising their song "Blue Bird" in their own song "I'm a Loner." Ultimately, the band was cleared of any wrongdoing.[tpl]cite web|url=https://en.korea.com/cnblue/news/court-rules-that-cnblue%E2%80%99s-%E2%80%9Cloner%E2%80%9D-was-not-plagiarized/ |title=Court rules that CNBLUE's "Loner" was not plagiarized |publisher=En.korea.com |date=April 13, 2011 |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl] Ynot released a statement saying they did not blame the band, but rather took issue with the composers who wrote the song and its lyrics for the band.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.allkpop.com/article/2010/01/ynot-speaks-on-cnblues-plagiarism-allegations/feed |title=Ynot speaks on CNBLUE's plagiarism allegations |publisher=allkpop |date=January 23, 2010 |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl]

==Philanthropy==

CNBLUE undertakes philanthropy work such as donating money and rice to the needy from time to time. Part of their yearly income and the income from the Bluemoon World Tour have been contributed to the construction of CNBLUE Schools.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kfhi.or.kr/campaign/2013/20130322_cnblueschool/cnblueschool_en.html |title=Cnblue School |publisher=Kfhi.or.kr |date= |accessdate=August 24, 2013[/tpl] The first CNBLUE School was built in Burkina Faso, Africa in 2012 and can accommodate up to 100 pre-schoolers and 1 000 elementary and junior high school students. CNBLUE Schools will provide meals and daycare services for the enrolled students.
As of January 2014, the second CNBLUE school is slated to be built in The Philippines.
