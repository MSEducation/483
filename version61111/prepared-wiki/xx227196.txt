[[Joan Butler, Countess of Ormond]]

CATEGORIES: 1396 births, 1430 deaths, Daughters of barons, Anglo-Normans in Ireland, Women of medieval Ireland

Joan Butler (''née'' de Beauchamp), Countess of Ormond (1396 – 3 or 5 August 1430) was the first wife of James Butler, 4th Earl of Ormond, and the mother of his five children. Their principal residence was Kilkenny Castle in Ireland.

==Life==

Joan de Beauchamp was the daughter of William de Beauchamp, 1st Baron Bergavenny and his first wife, Joan Arundel. She had a brother and sister:[tpl]sfn|Richardson I|2011|p=164[/tpl]
Joan's paternal grandparents were Thomas de Beauchamp, 11th Earl of Warwick and Katherine Mortimer, and her maternal grandparents were Richard Fitzalan, 11th Earl of Arundel and Elizabeth de Bohun.
Joan de Beauchamp died 3 or 5 August 1430, and was buried in the Mercers' Chapel, St Thomas of Acre, London.[tpl]sfn|Richardson I|2011|p=380[/tpl]

==Marriage and issue==

She married, before 28 August 1413, James Butler, 4th Earl of Ormond, by whom she had three sons and two daughters:[tpl]sfn|Richardson I|2011|p=380[/tpl]
