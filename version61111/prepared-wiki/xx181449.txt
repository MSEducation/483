[[KWWX]]

CATEGORIES: Radio stations in Washington (state), Regional Mexican radio stations

KWWX (106.7 FM, "La Super Z") is a radio station broadcasting a Regional Mexican format. Licensed to Cashmere, Washington, USA, the station serves the Wenatchee, Washington, area.  The station is currently owned by Cherry Creek Radio and licensed to CCR-Wenatchee IV, LLC.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fcc.gov/fcc-bin/fmq?call=KWWX |title=KWWX Facility Record |work=United States Federal Communications Commission, audio division[/tpl]
The station was assigned the KWWX call letters by the Federal Communications Commission on January 23, 2008.[tpl]cite web |title=Call Sign History |url=http://fjallfoss.fcc.gov/cgi-bin/ws.exe/prod/cdbs/pubacc/prod/call_hist.pl?Facility_id=5285&Callsign=KWWX |work=FCC Media Bureau CDBS Public Access Database[/tpl]

==Ownership==

In June 2006, a deal was reached for KWWX to be acquired by Cherry Creek Radio from Fisher Radio Regional Group as part of a 24 station deal with a total reported sale price of $33.3 million.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA6344749.html |work=Broadcasting & Cable |title=Deals |date=2006-06-19[/tpl]
