[[Padilla, Tamaulipas]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in Tamaulipas, Municipalities of Tamaulipas, World Digital Library related

Padilla is a municipality and former town located in the Mexican state of Tamaulipas. After the inundation the Vicente Guerrero dam in 1970, the seat was moved to the town of Nuevo Padilla, Tamaulipas. [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|title=”DIAGNÓSTICO Y PLAN MUNICIPAL DE DESARROLLO  RURAL SUSTENTABLE|url=http://www.portalestamaulipas.gob.mx/files/inicio/documents/plan_desarrollo/30.pdf|publisher=Municipio de Padilla - Estado de Tamaulipas|accessdate=24 February 2012[/tpl] Padilla served as the capital of the state of Tamaulipas from 1824 to 1825, and was home to the first Constituent Congress in the state; [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|title=Antecedentes Históricos|url=http://tamaulipas.gob.mx/tamaulipas/historia/antecedentes-historicos/|publisher=Gobierno del Estado de Tamaulipas, México|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl] it is often regarded as the "birthplace of federalism in Tamaulipas." [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|last=Guevara Castillo|first=Melitón|title=Padilla en la Historia|url=http://www.laregiontam.com.mx/?op1=notas&op2=4829|publisher=La Región Tamaulipas|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl]
Padilla is known for being the place where the Mexican liberator and Emperor Agustín de Iturbide was executed by a firing squad. [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|last=Rosas|first=Alejandro|title=Sangre en Padilla: la ejecución de Iturbide|url=http://bicentenario.com.mx/?p=15974&cp=all|publisher=Tu Bicentenario 2010|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl] It is also the place where Manuel de Mier y Terán, a Mexican general, took his life with a sword. [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|last=González Lezama|first=Raúl|title=Manuel de Mier y Terán: la intermitencia de su “eterno descanso”|url=http://www.inehrm.gob.mx/Portal/PtMain.php?pagina=exp-mier-y-teran-articulo|publisher=Secretaría de Educación Pública|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl] The town was established in 6 January 1749 by José de Escandón, the a Spanish Indian-fighter in New Spain and the founder and first governor of the colony of Nuevo Santander, and named after Maria Padilla, the wife of Juan Francisco de Güemes, the viceroy of New Spain. [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|title=PADILLA|url=http://www.e-local.gob.mx/work/templates/enciclo/tamaulipas/municipios/28030a.htm|publisher=Enciclopedia de los Municipios de México ESTADO DE TAMAULIPAS|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl] During its establishment, over 40 people from Hidalgo, Linares, and Río Blanco resided in the area. [tpl]es icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|title=Historia de Padilla|url=http://www.padilla.gob.mx/padilla/historia.htm|publisher=Municipio de Padilla, Tamaulipas|accessdate=6 March 2012[/tpl] As of 2010, the municipality of Padilla has a population of 14,020.
