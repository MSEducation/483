[[Banksia Peninsula]]

CATEGORIES: Geography of Victoria (Australia), Peninsulas of Australia

Banksia Peninsula is a peninsula in Victoria, Australia. It is located at [tpl]coord|37|56|S|147|41|E[/tpl], about 15 kilometres (9Â mi) south of Bairnsdale on the northern side of the Gippsland Lakes.
A long, narrow, sandy peninsula, it is the site of Duck Arm, a popular recreational bay with around thirty houses and a number of school camps.
The name refers to the plant genus Banksia, which grows in the area.
