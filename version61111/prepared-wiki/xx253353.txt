Reverend John Richard Bridger (8 April 1920 â€“ 14 July 1986) was an English cricketer. Bridger was a right-handed batsman and a leg break bowler.
Bridger was educated at Rugby School, where he played cricket for the school from 1935 to 1938. In one match in which he captained the Rugby side Bridger scored 153 runs in three hours, held four catches in the Marlborough first innings and took 5/54. Bridger later went to Clare College, Cambridge University, where as a Theology student he was exempt from military service in World War II.
Bridger made his first-class debut for an Under-33 side against an Over-33 side at Lord's. Bridger scored 49 in the Under-33's first innings.
In 1946 Bridger played his only game for the Marylebone Cricket Club against Cambridge University, where Bridger scored his maiden half century, making 94 runs.
Bridger made his County Championship debut for Hampshire in 1946 against local rivals Sussex, where on debut Bridger made a half century, scoring exactly fifty. In his second match of the season against Middlesex, Bridger scored his maiden first-class century with a score of 142. Bridger's second century would come in 1953 when he made 102 against Worcestershire.
Bridger made 38 first-class appearances for Hampshire between 1946 and 1954. In 1954 Bridger was awarded his County Cap by the club, in what was to be his final season. Bridger's final first-class match came against Sussex at Dean Park Cricket Ground. In his 38 first-class matches for the club, Bridger scored 1,725 runs at an Batting average of 27.82, with a high score 142. Bridger made two centuries and ten fifties and took 29 catches.
In Bridger's overall first-class career he scored 1,883 runs at an average of 28.96, and took 30 catches - Bridger had a reputation as an excellent fielder.
Bridger died in a road accident at Burley, Hampshire on 14 July 1986.
