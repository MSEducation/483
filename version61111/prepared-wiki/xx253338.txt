[[P. K. Gurudasan]]

P. K. Gurudasan (born 10 July 1935) is an Indian politician, trade unionist, and a member of Communist Party of India (Marxist). He was the Minister for Labour and Employment in the Government of Kerala headed by VS Achuthananthan between 2006 and 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.niyamasabha.org/codes/cmin.htm|title=Council of Ministers - Kerala|publisher=Kerala Legislative Assembly|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] He represents the Kollam Assembly constituency in the Kerala Legislative Assembly.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kerala.gov.in/government/mla.htm|title=Members of Legislative Assempbly|publisher=Government of Kerala|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] He is also[tpl]When|date=August 2011[/tpl] the State President of Centre of Indian Trade Unions (CITU) and a member of Central Committee of CPI(M).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kerala.gov.in/government/gurudasa.htm|title=Shri.P.K. Gurudasan|publisher=Govt. of Kerala|accessdate=10 January 2010 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20090624132440/http://www.kerala.gov.in/government/gurudasa.htm |archivedate=2009-06-24[/tpl] Gurudasan earlier served as the General Secretary of Kerala State unit of CITU.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2005/07/10/stories/2005071006230300.htm|title=P.K. ==Career==

Gurudasn was born to Krishnan and Yesodha on 10 July 1935 at Paravur in Kollam district. After completing his school and college education Gurudasan joined in the communist party at a young age. He was jailed during the Emergency period for 19 months in Poojappura Central Prison.
