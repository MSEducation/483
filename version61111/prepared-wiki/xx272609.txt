[[José Arpa]]

CATEGORIES: 1858 births, 1952 deaths, 19th-century American painters, 20th-century American painters, Landscape artists, Spanish artists, Artists from Texas

José Arpa y Perea, 1858–1952, was an artist of Spanish birth who worked in Spain, Mexico, and Texas.Mantle Fielding. 1945. Dictionary of American Painters, Sculptors and Engravers. New York: Struck, 1945; revised in 1983, Glenn B. Opitz, ed. Poughkeepsie, N.Y.: Apollo Books.Caroline Remy. 2011. "ARPA, JOSE," Handbook of Texas Online (http://www.tshaonline.org/handbook/online/articles/far17), accessed September 25, 2011. Published by the Texas State Historical Association.Jacinto Quirarte. 1973. Mexican American Artists. Austin: University of Texas Press.  Born in Carmona, Spain, he studied under Eduardo Cano de la Peña at the Museum of Fine Arts of Seville, where he won the Rome Prize three times, allowing him to study in Rome.Remy, 2011.  During his years in San Antonio, Texas he influenced many painters, most notably Xavier Gonzalez, Octavio Medellín, and Porfirio Salinas.Ruth Goddard. 1975. Porfirio Salinas. Austin: Rock House Press.  He painted in a realistic style, and was especially noted for his use of brilliant colors and his expertise in capturing the visual effects of sunlight.  His work has been widely exhibited;Frances Battaile Fisk. 1928. A History of Texas Artists and Sculptors. Abilene, Tex.: privately printed; reprinted by Morrison Books, Austin, Tex., 1986. ten of his paintings are in the collection of the San Antonio Museum of Art.
