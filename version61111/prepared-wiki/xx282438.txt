[[Patrick Robert Sydnor Log Cabin]]

CATEGORIES: African-American history of Virginia, Log cabins in the United States, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, Houses completed in 1865, Houses in Mecklenburg County, Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Mecklenburg County, Virginia

Patrick Robert Sydnor Log Cabin is a historic log cabin located near Clarksville, Mecklenburg County, Virginia. The house was built about 1865, and is a 1 1/2-story log building with a gable roof and a brick and stone chimney.  Also on the property are the contributing wood storage shed, a chicken house, and an outhouse.  The property is representative of a “home place” for several generations of African-Americans from Reconstruction onward.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Mecklenburg/058-5076_Sydnor_LogCabin_NR_FINAL_2007.pdf|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Patrick Robert Sydnor Log Cabin|author=Angelita D. Reyes | date=undated|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying four photos
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2007.
