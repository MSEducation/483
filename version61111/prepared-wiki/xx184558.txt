==History==

Hulland parish was a sub parish of the Ashbourne registration district until 1901. Hulland was also an ancient township in Ashbourne parish however became a separate, modern civil parish in December 1886.[tpl]cite web|title=UK and Ireland Genealogy|url=http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/DBY/Hulland/|publisher=Gen UK|accessdate=24 March 2014[/tpl]

==Church==

The church which is shared by Hulland and Hulland ward is an Anglican Christ church. The church was built in 1838 and seats up to 273 people. The church was in the rural deanery of Ashbourne.  The churchyard contains one commonwealth war grave from World War One and one common wealth war grave from World War Two.[tpl]cite web|title=UK and Ireland Genealogy|url=http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/DBY/Hulland/|publisher=Gen UK|accessdate=24 March 2014[/tpl]

==Housing==

In 1891 there were 50 houses in Hulland, between 1881 and 1931 the lowest recorded number of houses was 48 in the years 1891, 1921 and 1931 and the highest number of houses recorded was 52 in 1901. In the year 1951 there were 57 houses in Hulland and in 1961 there were 59.[tpl]cite web|title=Vision of Britain|url=http://www.visionofbritain.org.uk/unit/10127695/cube/HOUSES|publisher=Vision of Britain|accessdate=24 April 2014[/tpl]  This was the start of growth as in 2001 the number of houses in Hulland rose to 69,[tpl]cite web|title=2001 census data|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11124878&c=hulland&d=16&e=15&g=6413695&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1398353558427&enc=1&dsFamilyId=55|publisher=neighbourhood statistics|accessdate=24 April 2014[/tpl] and in 2011 there was 80 houses in Hulland.[tpl]cite web|title=2011 census data|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11124878&c=hulland&d=16&e=62&g=6413695&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1398353419630&enc=1&dsFamilyId=2489|publisher=neighbourhood statistics|accessdate=24 April 2014[/tpl]
