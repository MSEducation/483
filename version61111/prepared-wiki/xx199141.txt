[[Olaf Fønss]]

CATEGORIES: Danish film directors, 1882 births, Danish male actors, Danish male film actors, Danish male silent film actors, 1949 deaths, People from Aarhus

Olaf Fønss (17 October 1882–11 March 1949) was a Danish actor, director, producer, film censor and one of Denmark and Germany's biggest stars of the silent film era.

==Career==

Fønss' leading role in the 1913 Danish classic Atlantis made his face recognizable to movie fans. It was his role as the mad-scientist in the 1916 German science fiction film series, Homunculus, that earned Fønss  status as a top matinee idol.[tpl]cite book |last1=Kreimeier|first1=Klaus |last2=Kimber|first2=Robert |last3=Kimber|first3=Rita |title=The Ufa Story: A History of Germany's Greatest Film Company, 1918-1945 |year=1999 |publisher=University of California Press |isbn=0-520-22069-2|page=51[/tpl] In the 1930s, Fønss became politically active by directing two films for Denmark's Socialdemokratiske Parti (Social Democracy Party), Den Store Dag (1930) and Under Den Gamle Fane (1932).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dfi.dk/faktaomfilm/nationalfilmografien/nfperson.aspx?id=119183|title=Olaf Fønss 1882-1949|work=Fakta om Film|publisher=Det Danske Filminstitut|accessdate=21 January 2013[/tpl] He was president of the Danish Actor's Union from 1933–1947 and was a censor for the National Film Censor of Denmark for 14 years. Fønss was the older brother of actors Johannes and Aage Fønss.
