[[Joseph Ritz]]

CATEGORIES: American dramatists and playwrights, Writers from Ohio, Living people

Joseph P. Ritz is an author, playwright and retired journalist.

==Early life and education==

Ritz was raised in Canton, Ohio and graduated from grade school and Central Catholic HIgh School there. He graduated from Marquette University with a bachelor's degree in journalism in February, 1955.

==Career==

Ritz's first job out of college was as a news writer for Radio Station WFDF in Flint, Michigan. In the early 1960s he also was assistant news director for Fordham University. He also worked as a copy boy for the Milwaukee Sentinel, when it was a Hearst newspaper and for the Seattle Times. He worked as a reporter for The Buffalo Courier-Express, where he was a reporter, copy editor and editorial writer at different times from 1964 until the paper went out of business in September, 1982; The Buffalo News, where he also write a weekly column on labor unions; The Newburgh News, where he wrote part of a news series entitled The Road to Integration, that won a Pulitzer Prize for the Gannett Group of newspapers; The New Haven Journal-Courier and the Gloversville Leader-Herald.
His articles have appeared in the New York Sun, The New York Herald-Tribune, The London Express and the National Catholic Reporter.

==Awards and recognition==

Among prizes for his newspaper articles were the New York State Publishers' Award for Community Service, The New York State AFL-CIO 1986 Award "for outstanding achievements and contributions on behalf of working men and women in the state," the American Political Science Association's Award for "Distinguished Reporting of Public Affairs" and the U.S. Department of Labor Award for "outstanding efforts to advance the welfare of American's working men and women."
