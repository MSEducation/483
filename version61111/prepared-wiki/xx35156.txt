[[Kilo Ali]]

CATEGORIES: 1973 births, African-American rappers, Living people, Rappers from Miami, Florida, Rappers from Atlanta, Georgia, Ichiban Records artists

Kilo Ali (born May 1, 1973), formerly Kilo, is an American rap music Artist from Atlanta, Georgia.[tpl]cite journal |author=Charles E. Rogers |title='Organized Bass,' Kilo's debut on Interscope |journal=New York Amsterdam News |volume=88 |issue=39 |pages= |date=25 September 1997[/tpl] Kilo Ali recorded mainly bass music (described more closely to miami bass style music), but also hip hop tracks with a less distinctive southern flavor. His most well known singles include "Show Me Love",[tpl]cite journal |author=Flick, Larry; Smith, Shawnee |title=Singles:Rap |journal=Billboard |volume=109 |issue=22 |page= |date=31 May 1997[/tpl] "Baby Baby",[tpl]cite journal |title=Bubbling under hot R&B singles |journal=Billboard |volume=109 |issue=39 |page=28 |date=27 September 1997[/tpl] and "Love In Ya Mouth". 
As Kilo Ali, he released Organized Bass in 1997 on Interscope with featured artists including George Clinton, Cee-Lo from Goodie Mob, JT Money as well as Big Boi from OutKast.
