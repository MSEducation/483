[[The City Harmonic]]

CATEGORIES: Musical groups established in 2006, Musical groups from Hamilton, Ontario, Canadian Christian rock groups

The City Harmonic is a Juno Award winning Canadian Christian rock band formed in 2009 after having served together as the house worship band for an inter-denominational "worship and mission" event for students http://www.orthodoxical.org/orthodoxical/ in Hamilton, Ontario. Elias Dummer (vocals, keyboards, guitar), Eric Fusilier (bass), Aaron Powell (guitar), and Josh Vanderlaan (drums) collectively make up The City Harmonic.The City Harmonic Christian Today. Their music has been described as "nostalgic Brit-pop meets campfire sing-along mix that features raucous gang vocals along with agile, soaring anthems crafted to include the listener"  Musically, their sound is often compared to artists like Coldplay, Keane, Aqualung, The Fray, or Arcade Fire. In 2011 they won three Covenant Awards from GMA Canada for New Artist of the Year, Recorded Song of the Year and Modern Worship Song of the Year. In 2013 they won Covenant Awards for Group of the Year and Praise and Worship Song of the Year. They also won a Juno Award for the 2013 Christian/Contemporary Gospel Album of the Year.

===2013-present: Heart===

The band released their second full-length album titled Heart on September 3, 2013. In the week of release in Canada it reached No. 1 on the Inspirational Albums Charts.[tpl]cite web|author=webmaster |url=http://thecityharmonic.com/2013/09/heart-now-available-everywhere/ |title="HEART" Now Available Everywhere " The City Harmonic |publisher=Thecityharmonic.com |date= |accessdate=October 17, 2013[/tpl] For the Billboard charting week of September 21, 2013, Heart was the No. 20 most sold album in the breaking and entry chart of the United States by the Top Heatseekers and it was the No. 24 Top Christian Album as well. Critically speaking, Heart appears to be the most critically well-received of the band's releases to date. The album artwork features an adaptation of Leonardo DaVinci's Vitruvian Man, with Jesus on the cross in the center.
