[[Sex (The 1975 song)]]

CATEGORIES: 2012 singles, 2013 singles, The 1975 songs, 2012 songs

"Sex" is a song written by George Daniel, Adam Hann, Matthew Healy and Ross MacDonald of British alternative rock band The 1975, originally produced, arranged and recorded by brothers Michael and Robert Coles of Little Comets for their second extended play, Sex. It appears as the third track on the EP. The song was released as a promotional single to United Kingdom Modern Rock radio in November 2012 in promotion of the EP, and peaked at no. 35 on the Billboard Alternative Songs chart, and at number 23 on the UK Indie Chart.
"Sex" was re-recorded by The 1975 in 2013 for their debut studio album The 1975, in which it was included as the fifth track on the album. On 8 July 2013, the album version of the song premiered on Zane Lowe's BBC Radio 1 show as his 'Hottest Record in the World'.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/music/news/a496808/the-1975-confirm-new-single-sex-listen.html| title=The 1975 confirm new single 'Sex' - listen|work=Digital Spy|first=Lewis|last=Corner|date=9 July 2013|accessdate=7 August 2013[/tpl] The song was released as a single on 23 August 2013 as the band's debut single, in promotion of The 1975.[tpl]cite web| url=http://daystune.com/2013/07/the-1975-single-sex-to-be-lead-for-upcoming-debut-album/|title=The 1975 Single ‘Sex’ To Be Lead For Upcoming Debut Album|publisher=daystune.com|date=18 July 2013|accessdate=7 August 2013[/tpl] On 1 September 2013, the song debuted at no. 34 on the UK Singles Chart.
