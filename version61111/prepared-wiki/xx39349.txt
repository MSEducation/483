Some of the early Maranatha! recording groups were [[Sweet Comfort Band]], [[Love Song (band)|Love Song]], [[Chuck Girard]], [[Children of the Day]], [[The Way (band)|The Way]], [[Debby Kerner]],  [[Mustard Seed Faith]], [[Karen Lafferty]], and [[Daniel Amos]]. The label's first release was a various artists compilation entitled ''The Everlastin' Living Jesus Music Concert'', in 1971.<ref name="CCM_13_10" />
Maranatha! also branched into the children's market segment. Premier products included Psalty the singing songbook and the Kid's Praise Kids. In the early 1990s this segment represented about 40% of company revenues.[tpl]cite journal |last=Rabey |first=Steve |date=April 1991 |title=Marathana! In the 1980s, Maranatha! launched Broken Records, a label focusing on modern rock, punk and alternative music. The "Colours" series contained instrumental music in the vein of New Age artists, but the label avoided the term.[tpl]cite book |last=Powell |first= Mark Allan |title=Encyclopedia of Contemporary Christian Music |publisher=Hendrickson Publishers |year=2002 |location=Peabody, Massachusetts | page=425 |isbn=1-56563-679-1 [/tpl]
