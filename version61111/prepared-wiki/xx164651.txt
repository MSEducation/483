[[K50 Airport]]

CATEGORIES: Airports in Somalia, Lower Shebelle, Mogadishu

K50 Airport, also known as the Km N° 50 Airport, is a commercial airport in the southern Lower Shebelle region in Somalia.[tpl]Cite journal |last=Schmitz |first=Sebastain |year=2007 |title=By Ilyushin 18 to Mogadishu |journal=Airways |volume=14 |issue=7 |pages=pp. 12–17 |issn=1074-4320[/tpl]

==Overview==

Its name is derived from its location, about 50 kilometres west of Mogadishu. In the late 2000s, K50 served as Mogadishu's main airport while Aden Adde International Airport briefly shut down due to the ongoing War in Somalia. Daallo Airlines operated routes to and from K50 at one time.

==Facilities==

The airport resides at an elevation of [tpl]convert|100|ft|0[/tpl] above mean sea level. It has one runway designated 04/22 with a compacted sand surface measuring [tpl]convert|1850|x|20|m|0[/tpl].
