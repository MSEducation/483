[[Michele Columbu]]

CATEGORIES: 1914 births, 2012 deaths, People from the Province of Nuoro, Italian politicians, Italian writers

Michele Columbu (8 February 1914 – 10 July 2012Obituary) was an Italian politician[tpl]cite book|last=Stephens|first=Meic|title=Linguistic minorities in Western Europe|pages=548|isbn=9780850883626[/tpl] and writer from Ollolai, Sardinia.
For decades, between World War II and the 1970s he was the leader of Partito Sardo d'Azione (PSdAz),[tpl]cite book|last=Day|first=Alan John|title=Political parties of the world|publisher=Cartermill International|year=1988|edition=3rd|pages=309|isbn=0582026261[/tpl] Sardinian autonomist party claiming for detachment from Italy and fiercely contesting the presence of NATO military bases in Sardinia. He was elected into the European Parliament in 1984.[tpl]cite book|last=Jacobs|first=Francis|title=Western European political parties: a comprehensive guide|publisher=Longman|year=1989|pages=225–227|isbn=9780582001138[/tpl]
He wrote a collection of short stories, L'aurora è lontana (1968), and the essay collection Senza un perché.
