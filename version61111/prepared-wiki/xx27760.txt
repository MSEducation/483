[[Ananiah]]

CATEGORIES: Hebrew Bible places

Ananiah, in the Bible, is a town in the tribe of Benjamin between Nob and Hazor ([tpl]bibleverse||Nehemiah|11:32|NIV[/tpl]).  It is one of the localities inhabited by the tribe of Benjamin after the return from the Babylonian Exile.[tpl]bibleverse||Nehemiah|11:32|NIV[/tpl] Ananiah, whose name means "protected by God," was identified by the 19th century French traveler V. Guérin, author of Description de La Jude'e, with the present-day Beit Hanina, located 3 miles north of Jerusalem.Guérin, 1868, p. 394 Edward Robinson concurred, but W.F. Albright maintained that Ananiah is the village of al-Eizariya east of Jerusalem.About Beit Hanina Official Website Beit Hanina Community Center; Mohamed Shaker Sifadden[tpl]cite book | author = W.
