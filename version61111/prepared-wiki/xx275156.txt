[[Paul Hilton (footballer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1959 births, Living people, English footballers, Chadderton F.C. players, Bury F.C. players, West Ham United F.C. players, The Football League players, West Ham United F.C. non-playing staff, Gillingham F.C. non-playing staff, England youth international footballers

Paul Hilton (born 8 October 1959) is an English former professional footballer who played as a central defender making over 200 career appearances.

==Career==

Born in Oldham, Hilton represented England at under 18 level and played for Chadderton, Bury and West Ham United.[tpl]cite news|last=Scott|first=Alan|title=New Stortford FC coach is ex-Hammers ace|url=http://www.hertsandessexobserver.co.uk/Sport/New-Stortford-FC-coach-is-ex-Hammers-ace-21062012.htm|accessdate=22 July 2012|newspaper=www.hertsandessexobserver.co.uk|date=21 June 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player7/paulhilton.html|title=Profile|publisher=Post War English & Scottish Football League A - Z Player's Database[/tpl] Hilton was signed by West Ham manager, John Lyall, after he had appeared for Bury in a 10-0 League Cup defeat by West Ham. At the end of his playing career he became a coach under West Ham manager Billy Bonds and was appointed as youth team and then reserve team coach. In 2001 he was appointed coach at Ipswich Town.[tpl]cite web|title=Boys of 86 events|url=http://www.boysof86.co.uk/about.html|publisher=www.boysof86.co.uk|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl] Hilton has also spent time as youth coach at Gillingham and in 2011 he was appointed Head of Youth at Stevenage.
