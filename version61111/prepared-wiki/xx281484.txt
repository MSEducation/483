[[Xu Teli's Former Residence]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Changsha, Qing dynasty architecture

The Xu Teli's Former Residence([tpl]zh|s=徐特立故居|t=徐特立故居|p=Xú Tèlì Gùjū[/tpl]) built in the late Qing Dynasty, it is located in Wumei Township, Changsha County, Changsha City, Hunan Province, People's Republic of China. It has an area of about 1,700 square meters and a building area of about 581.7 square meters. In 1988, it was listed as a Hunan Province's most important culture and relics site ([tpl]lang|zh-hans|省级重点文物保护单位[/tpl]). In August 2005, it was rebuilt by the People's Government of Changsha County and it was opened to the pubilc. In 2013, it was listed as a China's most important culture and relics site ([tpl]lang|zh-hans|全国重点文物保护单位[/tpl]) by the State Council of China.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.sina.com.cn/o/2013-05-06/155227040004.shtml |title=徐特立故居入围第七批全国重点文物保护单位名单 |accessdate=2013 |publisher=Sina |work= |language=Simplified Chinese [/tpl]
