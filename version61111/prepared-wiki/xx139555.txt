[[Craig Morgan (footballer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1985 births, Living people, Association football defenders, Welsh footballers, Wales under-21 international footballers, Wales international footballers, Wrexham F.C. players, Milton Keynes Dons F.C. players, Peterborough United F.C. players, Preston North End F.C. players, Rotherham United F.C. players, The Football League players

Craig Morgan (born 16 June 1985 in Flint) is a Welsh international footballer who currently plays as a defender for Football League One side Rotherham United.
After beginning his career with spells at Wrexham, where he helped win the Football League Trophy in 2005, and Milton Keynes Dons, he joined Peterborough United in January 2007 after a short loan spell, helping the club reach the Football League Championship by winning consecutive promotions in the 2007–08 and 2008–09 seasons.

==Club career==

===Wrexham===

Morgan began his career as a youth player at Wrexham, making his professional debut at the end of the 2001–02 as a substitute during a 5–0 win over Cambridge United at the age of 16, a game which confirmed Wrexham's relegation to Division Three,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/1901196.stm |title=Wrexham 5–0 Cambridge |publisher=BBC Sport |date=6 April 2002 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] and was handed a second substitute appearance the following week in a 1–0 defeat to Stoke City.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/1923911.stm |title=Stoke 1–0 Wrexham |publisher=BBC Sport |date=13 April 2009 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] Shortly after, Morgan soon handed his first professional contract.[tpl]cite news|title=Dragons hand Morgan first pro deal|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11775/2262156/|publisher=Sky Sports|date=3 April 2002|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Morgan remained a bit part player the following season, although he did score his first professional goal during his first appearance of the season on 5 October 2002 in a 2–2 draw with Cambridge United.,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_3/2286242.stm |title=Cambridge 2–2 Wrexham |publisher=BBC Sport |date=5 October 2002 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] making seven appearances in all competitions as Wrexham gained promotion back into Division Two at the first attempt.
The 2003–04 season saw Morgan begin to establish himself in the Wrexham first team, making 21 appearances in all competitions as the club mostly stayed clear of a relegation battle with a 13th placed finish. During the latter part of the season, Morgan was sent off for the first time in his professional career, in a 0–0 draw with Plymouth Argyle on 27 March 2004.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/3552693.stm |title=Plymouth 0–0 Wrexham |publisher=BBC Sport |date=27 March 2004 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] The following season saw him make his most amount of appearances in a single season for Wrexham, including playing in all six of the team's matches as they won the Football League Trophy, beating Southend United 2–0 in the final at the Millennium Stadium.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/wales/north_east/4430449.stm |title=Fans jubilant at Wrexham victory |publisher=BBC News |date=11 April 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] However the season ended in disappointment for the team as they were relegated back into Division Three after being docked ten points due to entering administration.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/wales/4064257.stm |title=Wrexham FC in administration |publisher=BBC News |date=3 December 2004 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] At the end of 2003–04 season, Morgan signed a new contract[tpl]cite news|title=Morgan ends contract delay|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/wrexham/3901173.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=16 July 2004|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl]

===Milton Keynes Dons===

Following relegation, Morgan, who was out of contract with the club, entered into talks over a new contract but rejected the first offer made by Wrexham stating: "Wrexham have been a great club for me, I understand their financial problems, but I'm not happy with my offer."[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/wrexham/4569101.stm |title=Morgan upbeat on Wrexham future |publisher=BBC Sport |date=21 May 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] Two weeks later Morgan's departure from Wrexham was confirmed, signing a two-year contract with League One side Milton Keynes Dons, who had previously been warned by Wrexham boss Denis Smith over their attempts to sign him,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/wrexham/4547551.stm |title=Wrexham warns Dons over Morgan bid |publisher=BBC Sport |date=14 May 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] for an undisclosed five-figure transfer fee.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/wrexham/4642009.stm |title=Wrexham lose Llewellyn and Morgan |publisher=BBC Sport |date=4 June 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl]
He made his debut for his new club on 6 August 2005, the opening day of the 2005–06 season, in a 2–2 draw with Bournemouth,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/4730591.stm |title=MK Dons 2–2 Bournemouth |publisher=BBC Sport |date=6 August 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] and appeared in 47 during the season which ended in relegation from Division Two. After starting the next season in the first-team, Morgan fell out of favour and was allowed to return to his first club Wrexham on a one-month loan deal, one week after making his debut for Wales, becoming the first Milton Keynes Dons player to win an international cap since the team's relocation. He made two appearances during his loan spell before returning to the National Hockey Stadium where he did not make another appearance before being sent out on loan again on 23 November 2006, this time to Peterborough United, until January 2007.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/peterborough_united/6178038.stm |title=Defender Morgan seals Posh move |publisher=BBC Sport |date=23 November 2005 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl]

===Peterborough United===

He made his debut for The Posh on 2 December 2006 in a 2–1 win over Tranmere Rovers in the second round of the FA Cup as an 85th minute substitute for Shane Huke.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/fa_cup/6183108.stm |title=Tranmere 1–2 Peterborough |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 December 2006 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] After impressing during his initial loan spell, having made seven appearances, his move was made permanent on 2 January on a free transfer.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/peterborough_united/6224749.stm |title=McLean and Morgan make Posh move |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 January 2007 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] He quickly established himself in the first team and scored his first goal for the club in a 2–0 victory over Notts County on 3 March 2007.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_3/6408255.stm |title=Peterborough 2–0 Notts County |publisher=BBC Sport |date=3 March 2007 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl]
In the 2007–08 season he made 41 league appearances, as well as signing a new three-year contract in February 2008,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/peterborough_united/7434020.stm |title=Morgan committed to Peterborough |publisher=BBC Sport |date=3 June 2008 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] as the club won promotion to League One by finishing as runners-up to Milton Keynes Dons. Ahead of the clubs League One campaign in the 2008–09 season, Morgan was handed the captaincy of the team at the age of just 23, taking over from Micah Hyde.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/peterborough_united/7550255.stm |title=Morgan excited by Posh captaincy |publisher=BBC Sport |date=8 August 2008 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] However on 29 September he was replaced as captain by Russell Martin, less than two months later. Peterborough Manager Darren Ferguson put the decision down to Morgan's involvement with the Welsh national team, which often saw him miss while on international duty, stating: "Craig did fine, it's nothing about that, he was just missing games."[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/p/peterborough_united/7641583.stm |title=Peterborough make captain switch |publisher=BBC Sport |date=29 September 2008 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] Despite losing the captaincy,[tpl]cite news|title=International call-ups lead to Morgan losing captaincy|url=http://www.peterboroughtoday.co.uk/sport/international-call-ups-lead-to-morgan-losing-captaincy-1-116985|publisher=Peterborough Telegraph|date=29 September 2009|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] he remained a regular first-team player and helped the side to their second consecutive promotion after forming a defensive partnership with Gabriel Zakuani,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.theposh.com/page/ProfilesDetail/0,,10427~22204,00.html |title=Profile – Craig Morgan |publisher=theposh.com |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] finishing as runners-up to Leicester City and reaching the Football League Championship. During the summer break after the 2008–09 season, Morgan became Peterborough's all-time record international appearance maker by winning his 14th cap for Wales in a friendly against Estonia. In December 2009, Morgan revealed he nearly joined Notts County loan deadline window, but fell though in a last minute.[tpl]cite news|title=Morgan reveals Magpies approach|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/5765607/|publisher=Sky Sports|date=11 December 2009|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl]

===Preston North End===

On 6 July 2010, Morgan joined Preston North End for a fee in the region of £400,000., where he will be joined by former Peterbrough manager, Darren Ferguson[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www..net/page/NewsDetail/0,,10362~2085370,00.html |title=North End make double swoop |publisher=Preston North End F.C. |date=6 July 2010 |accessdate=2010-07-06[/tpl] On the opening day of the season, Morgan made his debut in a 2–0 loss against Doncaster Rovers. Under Ferguson, Morgan would managed to established himself in the first team, though placing unused substitute in the matches. On 19 October 2010, Morgan scored his first goal in a 3–2 loss against Scunthorpe United; several weeks later, he scored again in a 2–1 loss against Barnsley. Soon after, Ferguson was sacked after straight losses and Phil Brown succeed him. Toward the end of the season, the club was relegated to League One, finishing in the bottom three.
In his second season, with Preston now in League One, Morgan made several mistakes in the early start of the season, leading Brown to drop him out of the squad for three months.[tpl]cite news|title=Preston North End's Craig Morgan eager to take chance|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/15689955|publisher=BBC Sport|date=11 November 2011|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] On his return in a 5–2 loss against Charlton, Morgan scored his first goal. After being sent-off over professional foul in a 2–1 loss against Yeovil Town on 28 January 2012, Morgan was partly used due to injury and then falling out of favour by newly manager Graham Westley.[tpl]cite news|title=Five in line for PNE debut|url=http://www.lep.co.uk/sport/football/preston-north-end-news/five-in-line-for-pne-debut-1-4223436|publisher=Lancashire Evening Post|date=8 February 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Two months later on 31 March 2012, Morgan made his return in a 2–0 loss against Sheffield Wednesday and would be in the squad for the rest of the season.[tpl]cite news|title=Westley: ‘Players leaked my team selection’|url=http://www.lep.co.uk/sport/football/preston-north-end-news/westley-players-leaked-my-team-selection-1-4407805|publisher=Lancashire Evening Post|date=31 March 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl]
Ahead of a new season, Morgan suffered an hernia injury, ruling him out for three weeks.[tpl]cite news|title=PNE defender under the knife|url=http://www.lep.co.uk/sport/football/preston-north-end-news/pne-defender-under-the-knife-1-4770496|publisher=Lancashire Evening Post|date=25 July 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Morgan is soon making his way out at Preston, having deemed surplus to requirements.[tpl]cite news|title=Striker on the way for PNE|url=http://www.lep.co.uk/sport/football/preston-north-end-news/striker-on-the-way-for-pne-1-4832434|publisher=Lancashire Evening Post|date=15 August 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Manager Westley says his departure as a "bizarre".[tpl]cite news|title=Westley baffled by Morgan stay|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/8014214/|publisher=Sky Sports|date=24 August 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] His contract was terminated by mutual consent on 31 August 2012 (the transfer deadline).[tpl]cite news|title=Morgan exits PNE|url=http://www.lep.co.uk/sport/football/preston-north-end-news/morgan-exits-pne-1-4886913|publisher=Lancashire Evening Post|date=31 August 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl]

Since arriving at Rotherham United, Morgan established himself in the first team and was linked with League One side Coventry City and League Two side Fleetwood Town and Chesterfield.[tpl]cite news|title=Coventry, Chesterfield and Fleetwood are all chasing Rotherham defender Craig Morgan|url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11095/8235448/|publisher=Sky Sports|date=8 November 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] But the transfer speculation ended when Morgan signed a 18-month extended contract[tpl]cite news|title=Craig Morgan signs Rotherham United extension|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/20461910|publisher=BBC Sport|date=18 November 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Having signed a new contract, Morgan suffered an foot injury during a match against Wycombe.[tpl]cite news|title=ROTHERHAM UNITED: Millers rule out injured Craig Morgan|url=http://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/rotherham/rotherham-united-millers-rule-out-injured-craig-morgan-1-5122730|publisher=The Star|date=13 November 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] It reported that Morgan will be out for six weeks and even after Christmas.[tpl]cite news|title=ROTHERHAM UNITED: Not a simple answer over Craig Morgan loss|url=http://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/rotherham/rotherham-united-not-a-simple-answer-over-craig-morgan-loss-1-5148497|publisher=The Star|date=20 November 2012|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] Having been out for two months, assistant manager Paul Raynor says he is expected to come back in "two to three weeks" from full training[tpl]cite news|title=Rotherham United: How Millers are missing Morgan|url=http://www.thestar.co.uk/sport/football/rotherham/rotherham-united-how-millers-are-missing-morgan-1-5329678|publisher=The Star|date=18 January 2013|accessdate=9 February 2013[/tpl] He scored his first goal for the club in the Millers 4-1 home win over Exeter City on the 9th April 2013.

==International career==

Morgan made his debut for the Wales under-17 side on 24 September 2001 in a 2–0 friendly win over Scotland,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.faw.org.uk/fixtures-events/results/match/47 |title=Wales 2–0 Scotland |publisher=faw.org.u |date=24 September 2001 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl] going on to win 6 caps for the side before moving up to under-19 and under-21 levels.
He made his debut for the Wales senior side on 11 October 2006 in a 3–1 win over Cyprus at the Millennium Stadium during the qualifying campaign for Euro 2008.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/internationals/6031665.stm |title=Wales 3–1 Cyprus |publisher=BBC Sport |date=11 October 2006 |accessdate=2009-11-14[/tpl]
