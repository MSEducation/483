Aaron Spelling (April 22, 1923 – June 23, 2006) was an American film and television producer. As of 2009, Spelling's eponymous production company Spelling Television holds the record as the most prolific television writer, with 218 producer and executive producer credits.[tpl]IMDb name|id=0005455|name=Aaron Spelling [/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/tv--radio/the-great-escape/2005/09/17/1126750167460.html|title=The Great Escape|last=Idato|first=Michael|date=September 19, 2005|work=The Sydney Morning Herald|publisher=SMH.com|accessdate=February 25, 2009| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20090130091807/http://www.smh.com.au/news/tv--radio/the-great-escape/2005/09/17/1126750167460.html| archivedate= January 30, 2009 | deadurl= no[/tpl] Forbes ranked him the 11th top-earning deceased celebrity in 2009.[tpl]Cite news | url = http://www.forbes.com/2009/10/27/top-earning-dead-celebrities-list-dead-celebs-09-entertainment_land.html?boxes=listschannelinsidelists | title = Top-Earning Dead Celebrities |work=Forbes | first=Matthew | last=Miller | date=October 27, 2009|archiveurl=http://archive.is/VTjk|archivedate=December 5, 2012[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Spelling was born in Dallas, Texas. He was the son of Pearl (née Wald) and David Spelling, who were Jewish emigrants from Poland. His father worked as a tailor and changed his surname from Spurling to Spelling after emigrating to the United States.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.genealogymagazine.com/torispelling.html |title=A Look at Tori Spelling's Family Tree |publisher=Genealogymagazine.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-18[/tpl] Spelling had three brothers: Sam, Max, and Daniel, and a sister, Becky."AARON SPELLING BIOGRAPHY". Biography Channel.
At the age of eight, Spelling psychosomatically lost the use of his legs  due to trauma caused by constant bullying from his schoolmates, and was confined to bed for a year.[tpl]Cite news| url=http://movies.nytimes.com/person/112276/Aaron-Spelling/biography | work=The New York Times | title=Movies: Biography forAaron Spelling[/tpl] After attending Forest Avenue High School, he served in the United States Armed Forces during World War II. He later graduated from Southern Methodist University in 1949, where he was a cheerleader.[tpl]cite web|url=http://smu.edu/newsinfo/stories/aaron-spelling-dies.asp |title=Aaron Spelling and SMU - News and Communications - SMU |publisher=Smu.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-10-18[/tpl]
He married actress Carolyn Jones in 1953, in California. They divorced in 1964.[tpl]Cite book |title=A Prime-Time Life: An Autobiography |last1=Spelling |first1=Aaron |last2=Graham |first2=Jefferson |year=1996 |publisher=St. Martin's Press |location=New York |isbn=0-312-14268-4 |page=51 [/tpl] Spelling married Candy Gene (née Marer) in 1968. The couple had daughter Tori in 1973 and son Randy in 1978.[tpl]cite web|title=Aaron Spelling|work=Contemporary Authors Online|publisher=Gale|location=Detroit|year=2006[/tpl] 
In 1988, Spelling bought the [tpl]convert|6|acre|ha|adj=on[/tpl] property of Bing Crosby's former Los Angeles house.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hollywood.com/news/Spellings_Widow_Fires_Back_at_House_Sale_Reports/3528030|title=Spelling's Widow Fires Back at House Sale Reports|work=Hollywood.com|accessdate=July 7, 2006|archiveurl=http://archive.is/WuA0j|archivedate=January 25, 2013[/tpl] He demolished the property and built a 123-room home on the lot in 1991. Known as "The Manor", it has [tpl]convert|56500|sqft|m2[/tpl] of floor space and is the largest single-family home in Los Angeles.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.etonline.com/news/111552_UK_Heiress_Purchases_Aaron_Spelling_Mansion/index.html|title=UK Heiress Purchases Aaron Spelling Mega Mansion|last=Brown|first=Len|date=June 13, 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2006/07/05/aaron-spellings-widow-pu_n_24441.html|title=Aaron Spelling's Widow Puts Infamous Mansion On Market For $150 Million...|work=Huffington Post|accessdate=July 5, 2006|first=Katharine|last=Jose|date=July 5, 2006| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20060706210014/http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2006/07/05/aaron-spellings-widow-pu_n_24441.html| archivedate= July 6, 2006 | deadurl= no[/tpl] Spelling's widow Candy listed the home for sale in 2008 for $150 million; heiress Petra Ecclestone ultimately purchased the property for $85 million in 2011.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052702303714704576384013798228854.html|title=L.A. ==Career==

Spelling sold his first script to Jane Wyman Presents in 1954. That same year, he guest starred as a dogcatcher in the premiere episode of the CBS situation comedy, Willy, starring June Havoc as a young lawyer in New Hampshire, who later relocates to New York City to represent a vaudeville troupe.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0822305/|title=First Case|publisher=Internet Movie Data Base|accessdate=March 12, 2011[/tpl]
Beginning in 1968, Spelling began producing successful television shows including The Mod Squad, The Rookies, Charlie's Angels, Beverly Hills 90210 (which starred his daughter Tori), 7th Heaven, Charmed, Jane's House and Sunset Beach."Aaron Spelling". New York Times. Spelling founded Spelling Entertainment in 1972."Aaron Spelling biography". biography.com. 
In 2004, Spelling was portrayed in two television movies: Dan Castellaneta portrayed Spelling in Behind the Camera: The Unauthorized Story of Charlie's Angels,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0383915/|title=Behind the Camera: The Unauthorized Story of "Charlie's Angels"|work=IMDB.com|accessdate=March 8, 2004[/tpl] and Nicholas Hammond portrayed Spelling in television movie Dynasty: The Making of a Guilty Pleasure."Dynasty: The Making of a Guilty Pleasure". Variety. December 28, 2004.

==Death and legacy==

In 2001, Spelling was diagnosed with oral cancer.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.oralcancerfoundation.org/people/aaron_spelling.htm |title=Prime time patriarch |work=Oralcancerfoundation.org |accessdate=July 11, 2001[/tpl]
On June 18, 2006, Spelling suffered a severe stroke at The Manor, his estate in Holmby Hills, Los Angeles, California. He died at his estate on June 23, 2006 from complications of the stroke, at the age of 83.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/06/24/arts/television/24spelling.html |title=Aaron Spelling, Prolific Television Producer, Dies at 83 |work=The New York Times |accessdate=June 24, 2006 | first=Bill | last=Carter | date=June 24, 2006| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20060708100027/http://www.nytimes.com/2006/06/24/arts/television/24spelling.html| archivedate= July 8, 2006 | deadurl= no[/tpl]"TV innovator Aaron Spelling dies at 83". MSNBC. June 26, 2006. A private funeral was held several days later, and Spelling was entombed in a mausoleum in Culver City's Hillside Memorial Park Cemetery.
On August 27, 2006, Spelling was posthumously honored at the 58th Annual Primetime Emmy Awards by former employees Joan Collins, Stephen Collins, Heather Locklear, Farrah Fawcett, Kate Jackson and Jaclyn Smith.
On April 4, 2007, it was announced that 7th Heaven's  May 13, 2007 series finale would be dedicated to Aaron Spelling.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tvseriesfinale.com/2007/04/7th_heaven_will_camdens_reunite_for_last_episode.php|title=7th Heaven: Will Camdens Reunite for Last Episode? |work=TVSeriesFinale.com |accessdate=April 5, 2007 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20070410045712/http://www.tvseriesfinale.com/2007/04/7th_heaven_will_camdens_reunite_for_last_episode.php  |archivedate = April 10, 2007[/tpl] When 7th Heaven ended its run, it was touted by the network as being Spelling's longest-running series and the longest-running "family drama" in American television history.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.timewarner.com/corp/newsroom/pr/0,20812,1028153,00.html|title=10th Season Pick-Up Earns "7th Heaven" A Place In Television History|work=TimeWarner.com|accessdate=February 15, 2005| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20050216065622/http://www.timewarner.com/corp/newsroom/pr/0,20812,1028153,00.html| archivedate= February 16, 2005 | deadurl= no[/tpl]
On September 15, 1978, Spelling was honored with a star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame located at 6667 Hollywood Blvd. In 1996, he was inducted into the Television Hall of Fame.
