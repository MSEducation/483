[[Henry Reichel]]

CATEGORIES: 1856 births, 1931 deaths, Knights Bachelor, People from Belfast, Alumni of Balliol College, Oxford, Fellows of All Souls College, Oxford, People associated with the University of Wales

Sir Henry Rudolf Reichel (October 11, 1856 – 22 June, 1931), was a founder of the University of Wales. “Sir Harry Reichel” The Times  (London, England), Thursday,  Jun 25, 1931; pg. 16; Issue 45858
Born in Belfast, the son of a future Bishop of Meath, Reichel was educated at Christ’s Hospital and Balliol College, Oxford. He was a Fellow of All Souls College, Oxford from 1880 until 1894‘REICHEL, Sir Harry (Rudolf)’, Who Was Who, A & C Black, an imprint of Bloomsbury Publishing plc, 1920–2008; online edn, Oxford University Press, Dec 2007 accessed 31 Oct 2013
He was appointed the first principal of the University College of North Wales, in Bangor.Welsh Biography on-line He retained the post from the age of 28 until 1927 when he was 71.The Welsh Academy Encyclopaedia of Wales. John Davies, Nigel Jenkins, Menna Baines and Peredur Lynch (2008) pg735 ISBN 978-0-7083-1953-6
Linked to the Nationalist movement of Wales, he was the vice-chancellor of the University of Wales for six terms. Knighted in 1907, he had a quiet and reserved character, but was celebrated by many in Wales, and many tributes were given upon his death in 1931.
