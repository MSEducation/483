[[Paul Taylor (fighter)]]

CATEGORIES: 1979 births, Living people, People from Walsall, English kickboxers, English mixed martial artists, Welterweight mixed martial artists

Paul Simon Taylor (born 15 December 1979) is a retired English mixed martial artist who gained notoriety competing in the UFC's Lightweight division. He also competed for Cage Rage and fought as a Welterweight.

==Mixed martial arts career==

Paul Taylor fights for Team Kaobon of Liverpool and is a former WPKC world kickboxing champion.http://www.ufc.com/index.cfm?fa=news.detail&gid=12765 Taylor got into MMA when his coach Rob Evans entered him in the UK's first cage tournament, Cage Rage.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.sundaymercury.net/news/midlands-news/2008/10/15/mixed-martial-arts-fighter-from-walsall-faces-his-toughest-opponent-at-ufc-in-birmingham-66331-22042127|title=Mixed Martial Arts fighter from Walsall faces his toughest opponent at UFC in Birmingham|author=Anuji Varma[/tpl] Taylor had given up kickboxing after becoming disillusioned at the lack of money in the sport.
After giving up kickboxing, Taylor decided to retrain as a carpenter[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/othersports/boxingandmma/3210489/Paul-Taylors-chance-to-hammer-home-UFC-talent-Boxing-and-MMA.html | location=London | work=The Daily Telegraph | title=Paul Taylor's chance to hammer home Ultimate Fighting talent | first=Gareth A | last=Davies | date=16 October 2008[/tpl] whilst doing MMA on the side, but eventually trained MMA full-time until his retirement.

===Ultimate Fighting Championship===

Taylor sent a DVD of his fighting to the UFC to try and secure a contract with the organization and they signed him immediately.
In his UFC debut, Taylor stopped previously undefeated Edilberto de Oliveira via TKO due to strikes early in the third round. 
His second UFC fight at UFC 75 was a loss via armbar to Marcus Davis. Although Taylor lost the fight he came close to winning with the referee seconds away from stopping the action due to Taylor connecting with a high kick and landing several blows on a rocked Davis.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.sherdog.com/news/news/ufc-75-champion-vs-champion-play-by-play-8918|title=UFC 75 "Champion Vs. Champion" Play-by-Play[/tpl] Due to its high pace and excitement the fight was awarded Fight of the Night.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/2831/marcus-davis-scores-80000-in-ufc-75-bonuses-possibly-fighting-in-january.mma|title=Marcus Davis Scores $80,000 in UFC 75 Bonuses, Possibly Fighting in January|author=Dann Stupp[/tpl]
His next fight at UFC 80 was a loss via unanimous decision to fellow countryman Paul Kelly.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/3552/ufc-80-rapid-fire-live-results-and-round-by-round-updates.mma|title=UFC 80: Rapid Fire -- Live Results and Round-by-Round Updates|author=Dann Stupp[/tpl] Although he lost, Taylor along with Kelly was awarded $35,000 as a bonus for Fight of the Night.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/3557/ufc-issues-140000-in-ufc-80-bonuses.mma|title=UFC Issues $140,000 in UFC 80 "Fight Night" Bonuses|author=Dann Stupp[/tpl] This was the second time that Taylor had won the award. 
Taylor fought Jess Liaudin in a rematch at UFC 85.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.thesun.co.uk/sol/homepage/sport/ufc/article933149.ece | location=London | work=The Sun | first=Mark | last=Gilbert | title=Liaudin gets Taylor test | date=18 March 2008[/tpl] Taylor won by split decision.
Taylor met MMA veteran Chris Lytle at UFC 89 which took place at the National Indoor Arena in Birmingham, England.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.mmaontap.com/mma/category/paul-taylor|title=Report: Taylor vs. Hathaway, Hamman vs. Gustafsson Likely for UFC 105[/tpl] Lytle won a unanimous decision (29-28, 29-28, 30-27).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sherdog.com/news/news/ufc-89-play-by-play-14826|title=UFC 89 Play-by-Play[/tpl] The decision resulted in loud boos and Taylor was surprised at Lytle being awarded the judges’ decision. Sherdog, MMAWeekly and MMAJunkie thought Taylor had won the decision.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/13043/ufc-89-live-results-and-round-by-round-updates.mma|title=UFC 89 live results and round-by-round updates[/tpl]http://www.mmaweekly.com/absolutenm/templates/dailynews.asp?articleid=7369&zoneid=13 The fight was awarded Fight of the Night honors and both fighters received $40,000 following their exciting, action packed fight.http://www.mmaweekly.com/absolutenm/templates/dailynews.asp?articleid=7384&zoneid=13 Although this was Taylor's third defeat in the octagon, he had won Fight of the Night awards on three occasions when he had been defeated in the UFC.
Taylor next fought John Hathaway at UFC 105 in Manchester, England, losing via unanimous decision.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2009/09/report-paul-taylor-vs-john-hathaway-nick-osipczak-vs-tba-likely-for-ufc-105|title=Report: Paul Taylor vs John Hathaway likely for UFC 105|publisher=mmajunkie.com|date=September 10, 2009[/tpl]
Taylor was expected to make his lightweight debut against John Gunderson on 10 April 2010 at UFC 112.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmamania.com/2010/2/4/1293056/paul-taylor-drops-down-to#comments|title=Paul Taylor drops to lightweight to face John Gunderson at UFC 112|author=James Kimball[/tpl] However, due to complications after making the weight cut, Taylor was not medically cleared to fight.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmabay.co.uk/2010/04/10/ufc-112-paul-taylor-forced-to-withdraw-from-fight-with-john-gunderson-in-abu-dhabi/|title=UFC 112: Paul Taylor forced to withdraw from fight with John Gunderson in Abu Dhabi|publisher=mmabay.com|date=April 10, 2010[/tpl]
Taylor next faced Sam Stout on 23 October 2010 at UFC 121.[tpl]cite news|url= http://mmajunkie.com/news/19884/paul-taylor-vs-sam-stout-targeted-for-ufc-121.mma|title= Paul Taylor vs. Sam Stout targeted for UFC 121|publisher=mmajunkie.com |accessdate=2010-07-10|author=Steven Marrocco[/tpl] The fight saw both fighters attempt kicks. The first round saw Stout bloody Taylor's nose and successfully gain a takedown. Taylor came back in the second, catching Stout on the inside. Stout went on to win a split decision (29-28, 28-29, 30-27).
Taylor next faced Gabe Ruediger on 5 February 2011 at UFC 126.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmaweekly.com/gabe-ruediger-vs-paul-taylor-agreed-for-ufc-127-in-australia|title=Gabe Ruediger vs. Paul Taylor Agreed For UFC 127 in Australia|publisher=MMAWeekly.com|date=29 November 2010[/tpl] Taylor won via KO after landing a head kick and follow up punches in the second round.
Taylor was expected to face John Makdessi on 14 August 2011 at UFC on Versus 5.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmafrenzy.com/18742/john-makdessi-vs-paul-taylor-in-the-works-for-ufc-on-versus-5/|title=John Makdessi vs. Paul Taylor In The Works for UFC on Versus 5 |publisher=mmafrenzy.com|date=3 June 2011[/tpl] However, Makdessi was forced from the bout with an injury[tpl]cite web|url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/24135/john-makdessi-injured-forced-out-of-ufc-on-versus-5-bout-with-paul-taylor.mma|title=John Makdessi injured, forced out of UFC on Versus 5 bout with Paul Taylor|publisher=mmajunkie.com|date=25 June 2011[/tpl] and replaced by Donald Cerrone.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2011/06/with-makdessi-injured-cerrone-faces-taylor-at-ufc-on-versus-5|title=With Makdessi injured, Cerrone faces Taylor at UFC on Versus 5|publisher=mmajunkie.com|date=June 27, 2011[/tpl] However it was later revealed that Taylor had broken his foot and was forced out of his scheduled bout and replaced by Charles Oliveira.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.espn.co.uk/ufc/sport/story/98453.html|title=Paul Taylor gets Cerrone examination on August 14 |publisher=espn.co.uk.|date=27 June 2011[/tpl]
Taylor was expected to face Anthony Njokuani on 5 November 2011 at UFC 138.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/24490/anthony-njokuani-vs-paul-taylor-targeted-for-ufc-138-in-england.mma|title=Anthony Njokuani vs. Paul Taylor targeted for UFC 138 in England| work=mmajunkie.com |date=20 July 2011 |last=Morgan |first=John |accessdate=20 July 2011[/tpl]  However, just days before the event, Taylor sustained a whiplash injury during a minor traffic collision forcing him from the bout.  With no time to find a suitable replacement the bout was scrapped.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/25901/ufc-138-loses-taylor-njokuani-diabate-perosh-promoted-to-main-card.mma|title=UFC 138 loses Taylor-Njokuani; Diabate-Perosh promoted to main card| work=mmajunkie.com |date=1 November 2011 |last=Stupp |first=Dann |accessdate=1 November 2011[/tpl]
Taylor/Njokuani was expected to take place on 11 July 2012 at UFC on Fuel TV: Munoz vs. Weidman.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mmajunkie.com/news/28450/anthony-njokuani-vs-paul-taylor-added-to-ufc-on-fuel-tv-4.mma|title=Anthony Njokuani vs. Paul Taylor added to UFC on FUEL TV 4|work=mmajunkie.com |date=27 April 2012[/tpl]  However, Taylor was forced out of the bout with yet another injury, and replaced by Rafael dos Anjos.
Taylor was expected to return from the extended hiatus and the bout with Njokuani scheduled for a third time on 26 October 2013 at UFC Fight Night 30.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2013/08/paul-taylor-returns-meets-anthony-njokuani-at-ufc-fight-night-30-in-manchester|title=Paul Taylor returns, meets Anthony Njokuani at UFC Fight Night 30 in Manchester|work=mmajunkie.com |date=9 August 2013[/tpl]  However, Taylor was forced to pull out of the bout once again with another injury and was replaced this time by Al Iaquinta.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2013/09/al-iaquinta-replaces-paul-taylor-faces-anthony-njokuani-at-ufc-fight-night-30|title=Al Iaquinta replaces Paul Taylor, faces Anthony Njokuani at UFC Fight Night 30|work=mmajunkie.com |date=19 September 2013[/tpl]  After the most recent withdrawal, the UFC announced that Taylor has retired from MMA fighting.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mmajunkie.com/news/2013/09/ufc-after-injury-withdrawal-paul-taylor-announces-retirement|title=UFC: After injury withdrawal, Paul Taylor announces retirement|work=mmajunkie.com |date=20 September 2013[/tpl]
