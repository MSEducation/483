[[Shire of Kowree]]

CATEGORIES: Former local government areas of Victoria (Australia)

The Shire of Kowree was a local government area in western Victoria, Australia. The shire covered an area of [tpl]convert|5379.41|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and existed from 1862 until 1995.

==History==

Kowree was first incorporated as a road district on 24 June 1862, and became a shire on 26 January 1872. It added the South Riding of the Shire of Lowan to its North Riding on 29 May 1894.[tpl]cite book|title=Victorian Municipal Directory|year=1992|publisher=Arnall & Jackson|location=Brunswick|pages=730â€“731[/tpl] Accessed at State Library of Victoria, La Trobe Reading Room.
On 20 January 1995, the Shire was abolished, and merged with Shire of Kaniva and parts of Arapiles and Glenelg into the Shire of West Wimmera. The Black Range and Toolondo Reservoir districts joined the Rural City of Horsham, while a small unpopulated section joined the Shire of Southern Grampians.[tpl]cite book|url=http://www.ausstats.abs.gov.au/ausstats/free.nsf/0/76E601D6DB55E88ACA25722500049195/$File/12570_1994-95.pdf|title=Victorian local government amalgamations 1994-1995: Changes to the Australian Standard Geographical Classification|author=Australian Bureau of Statistics|date=1 August 1995|publisher=Commonwealth of Australia|pages=7, 11, 12|isbn=0-642-23117-6|accessdate=2008-01-05[/tpl]
