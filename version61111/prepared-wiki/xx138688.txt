==Reversal of Germanization after end of German rule over Polish territories==

After World War I ended, the Germanization of those Polish territories which were restored to Poland
was largely reversed, although significant German minorities continued to exist.
The American historian of German descent[tpl]cite web|url=http://web.ku.edu/~eceurope/hist557/bibpt1rev.htm |title=Part I: to 1914 |publisher=Web.ku.edu |date= |accessdate=2009-05-06[/tpl] Richard Blanke in his book Orphans of Versailles names several reasons for the exodus of the German population. The author has been criticised by Christian Raitz von Frentz and his book classified by him as part of a series on the subject that have an anti-Polish bias[ref]A Lesson Forgotten: Minority Protection Under the League of Nations the Case of the German Minority in Poland, 1920-1934 
Christian Raitz Von Frentz page 8[/ref] Polish professor A. Cienciala says that Blanke's views in the book are sympathetic to Germany[tpl]cite web|url=http://web.ku.edu/~eceurope/hist557/lect11.htm |title=Anna M |publisher=Web.ku.edu |date= |accessdate=2009-05-06[/tpl]
