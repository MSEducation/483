Peter Ramsden (born 9 May 1934 — died [tpl]death date and age|2002|9|1|1934|5|9|df=y[/tpl] in York) was a professional rugby league footballer of the 1950s and '60s, playing at club level for Huddersfield, and York, as a [tpl]rlp|SO-FE[/tpl], or [tpl]rlp|LF-LK[/tpl], i.e. number 6, or 13, during the era of contested scrums.

==Playing career==

===Challenge Cup final appearances===

Peter Ramsden played [tpl]rlp|SO-FE[/tpl], broke his nose after six minutes, scored two tries, and won the Lance Todd Trophy in Huddersfield's 15-10 victory over St. Helens in the 1952–53 Challenge Cup final at Wembley Stadium on Saturday 9 May 1953, and played in the 6-12 defeat to Wakefield Trinity in the 1961–62 Challenge Cup final at Wembley Stadium on Saturday 12 May 1962.Briggs, Cyril & Edwards, Barry (12 May 1962). The Rugby League Challenge Cup Competition - Final Tie - Huddersfield v Wakefield Trinity - Match Programme. Wembley Stadium Ltd. ISBN n/a
