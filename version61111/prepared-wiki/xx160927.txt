[[Jean Manga-Onguéné]]

CATEGORIES: 1946 births, Living people, Cameroonian footballers, Cameroon international footballers, Cameroon national football team managers

Jean Manga-Onguéné (born 12 June 1946http://www.national-soccer-teams.com/v2/player.php?id=35157) is a former Cameroonian footballer. He was the 1980 African Footballer of the Year.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/leagues/premierleague/arsenal/4585021/Arsenals-Emmanuel-Adebayor-named-Africa-Player-of-the-Year.html | location=London | work=The Daily Telegraph | title=Arsenal's Emmanuel Adebayor named Africa Player of the Year | date=11 February 2009[/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/football/africa/3682401.stm | work=BBC News | first=James | last=Copnall | title=Eto'o joins the greats | date=4 May 2004[/tpl]

==Club career==

He spent his whole career at Canon Yaoundé in Cameroon.http://www.fifa.com/classicfootball/clubs/club=44201/index.html

==International career==

Manga-Onguéné made several appearances for the senior Cameroon national football team, including four FIFA World Cup qualifying matches.
In 2006, he was selected by CAF as one of the best 200 African football players of the last 50 years.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Sport24.gr|title=Οι 200 κορυφαίοι Αφρικανοί|url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/article273152.ece?service=print|date=25 October 2006[/tpl]
