==Electoral history==

Pelosi's only close race so far has been the special election to succeed Sala Burton's seat after her death in February 1987. In the special election's Democratic primary, Pelosi narrowly defeated San Francisco Supervisor Harry Britt, considered the more "progressive" candidate, with 36 percent of the vote to his 32 percent.[tpl]cite news |first=Robert | last=Lindsey | title=House race in west goes to runoff |date=April 9, 1987 |work=The New York Times[/tpl] Accessed via Lexis-Nexis. In the runoff against Republican candidate Harriet Ross, Pelosi received more than a 2-to-1 majority of votes cast in a turnout that comprised about 24% of eligible voters.[tpl]cite news | title=Nancy Pelosi Wins House Seat |date=June 3, 1987 |work=The Washington Post[/tpl] Accessed via Lexis-Nexis. Since then, Pelosi has enjoyed overwhelming support in her political career, collecting 76 and 77 percent of the vote in [tpl]ushr|California|5|[/tpl] for the 1988 and 1990 Race for U.S. House of Representatives. In 1992, after the redistricting from the 1990 Census, Pelosi ran in [tpl]ushr|California|8|[/tpl], which now covered the San Francisco area. She has continued to post landslide victories since, dropping beneath 80 percent of the vote only twice.

==Personal background==

===Family===

Her husband, since 1963, is Paul Pelosi. They have five children: Nancy Corinne, Christine, Jacqueline, Paul, and Alexandra, as well as eight grandchildren. Alexandra, a journalist, covered the Republican presidential campaigns in 2000 and made a film about the experience, Journeys with George. In 2007, Christine published a book, Campaign Boot Camp: Basic Training for Future Leaders.[tpl]cite news |first=Sam |last=Whiting |title=Christine Pelosi's boot camp trains future politicians to avoid the campaign minefield |url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2008/02/03/CM1UUF1RH.DTL&feed=rss.swhiting |work=San Francisco Chronicle |date=February 3, 2008 |accessdate=October 24, 2008 [/tpl]
Pelosi lives in the Pacific Heights neighborhood of San Francisco.
