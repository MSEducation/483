Greta Scacchi (born 18 February 1960) is an Emmy Award-winning Italian-Australian actress known for her roles in the films White Mischief, Presumed Innocent and The Player.

==Early life==

Scacchi was born Greta Gracco in Milan, Italy, on 18 February 1960, the daughter of Luca Scacchi Gracco, an Italian art dealer and painter, and Pamela, an English dancer and antiques dealer.[tpl]cite news| url=http://film.guardian.co.uk/Player/Player_Page/0,,45425,00.html|work=The Guardian|location=London|title=Greta Scacchi profile|accessdate=4 May 2010[/tpl] Scacchi's parents divorced when she was four, and her mother returned to her native England with Greta and her two older brothers, first to London, then to Haywards Heath, West Sussex.[tpl]cite news |first1=Cally |last1=Law |first2=Greta |last2=Scacchi |title=Slight mischief |work=The Sunday Times |page=2 |date=17 Aug 2008 |accessdate=22 Jun 2009 |quote= |url= [/tpl] In 1975, after her mother's remarriage to Giovanni Carsaniga,[tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.yahoo.com/movie/contributor/1800019652/bio |title=Greta Scacchi Biography – Yahoo! Movies |publisher=Movies.yahoo.com |date= |accessdate=10 Nov 2012[/tpl] the family settled in Perth, Australia, where she attended Hollywood Senior High School and the University of Western Australia (UWA).[tpl]cite web | url = http://shenton.wa.edu.au/dreamgirl/filesend/225/Hollywood%20History.pdf | title = Hollywood Senior High School – A History | accessdate = 6 Jan 2011 | last = Bosworth | first = Michael | year = 2000 | format = PDF[/tpl] She made her theatrical debut at UWA's New Dolphin Theatre in Edward Bond's play Early Morning.[tpl]cite journal | title = The Octagon Takes a Bow | journal = Uniview | date = Summer 2010 | volume = 29 | issue = 1 | pages = 16–19| id = | url = http://uniview.publishing.uwa.edu.au/?f=273786 | accessdate = 6 Jan 2011[/tpl]

==Career==

In 1977, Scacchi left UWA to return to England to pursue an acting career, studying at the Bristol Old Vic Theatre School, with Miranda Richardson and Amanda Redman. In 1982, she made her film debut in the German movie, Das Zweite Gesicht (The Second Face), and gave versatile performances in films, such as Heat and Dust (1983), The Ebony Tower (1984), The Coca Cola Kid (1985), White Mischief (1987), Presumed Innocent (1990), The Player (1992) and Country Life (1994). She turned down the role of Catherine Trammell in Basic Instinct (1992).Bryce Hallett, Her world's a stage, Sydney Morning Herald, Metropolitan, 10 February 2001, p.3
In 1996, she won an Emmy Award for her work as Empress Alexandra Fyodorovna of Russia in the television film, Rasputin: Dark Servant of Destiny, and was nominated for a Golden Globe and numerous other awards. In 1999, she had a role as an Italian-Australian single mother in the Australian film Looking for Alibrandi,http://www.urbancinefile.com.au/home/view.asp?Article_ID=3540&p=y a performance for which she won the 2000 AFI award for Best Supporting Actress.http://www.screendaily.com/looking-for-alibrandi-finds-five-afi-awards/404275.article
In 2007, she received an Emmy Award nomination for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Miniseries or a Movie for Broken Trail.
Scacchi is fluent in English, French, German and Italian, which has made her a popular choice for European casting directors and has been an asset when working for European directors and producers. In May 2011, she appeared alongside Anita Dobson in the play Bette and Joan at London's Arts Theatre, directed by Bill Alexander, about the personal and professional relationship between Bette Davis and Joan Crawford.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.londontheatre.co.uk/londontheatre/reviews/betteandjoan2011.htm|title=Bette & Joan|publisher=Londontheatre.co.uk|accessdate=10 November 2012[/tpl]
In September 2013 Sir Jonathan Miller directed a Gala Performance of William Shakespeare's King Lear at The Old Vic in London. Scacchi played Regan.http://bookings.oldvictheatre.com/single/PSDetail.aspx?psn=16151

==Personal life==

Scacchi was in a relationship with New Zealand musician Tim Finn from 1983–89. A marriage to American actor Vincent D'Onofrio[tpl]cite news|first=Marianne|last=Macdonald|title=Greta Scacchi: glad to be back|date=28 September 2008|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/3561370/Greta-Scacchi-glad-to-be-back.html|work=The Telegraph|accessdate=14 May 2011[/tpl] (1989–1993) produced a daughter, Leila (born 20 March 1992). Scacchi also has a son Matteo (born 1998) by her cousin Carlo Mantegazza.[tpl]cite news|first=Kevin|last=Maher|title=Greta Scacchi takes on Miss Austen Regrets|date=26 April 2008|url=http://entertainment.timesonline.co.uk/tol/arts_and_entertainment/tv_and_radio/article3807296.ece|work=The Times|accessdate=14 May 2011[/tpl]
Scacchi applied for British citizenship after turning 18, but was turned down because her father was not a British citizen. After appealing unsuccessfully, she decided not to apply for it again, and retained her Italian citizenship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.urbancinefile.com.au/home/view.asp?a=3540|title=Scacchi, Greta – Looking for Alibrandi|accessdate=14 May 2011|last=Urban|first=Andrew L.[/tpl] In January 1995, she became an Australian citizen and has since had dual nationality.
She lived for many years in Hurstpierpoint until moving in early 2011 to a rented property in East Grinstead because of a dispute with her neighbour.[tpl]cite news|first=Emily|last=Walker|title=Greta Scacchi's "driven out" of Sussex dream home|date=5 February 2011|url=http://www.theargus.co.uk/news/8833906.Greta_Scacchi_s__driven_out__of_Sussex_dream_home|work=The Argus|accessdate=14 May 2011[/tpl]
Scacchi is an active supporter of campaigns and organisations that promote environmental causes. She has supported Greenpeace and, more recently, Christian Aid's climate change campaign.https://www.christianaid.org.uk/aboutus/celebrity-supporters/greta-scacchi.aspx In 2009, she posed nude with a Codfish to promote the documentary End of the Line, a film exposing the effects of over-fishing.http://www.theguardian.com/environment/2010/jun/20/greta-scacchi-environmental-campaigner
In October 2103 she was made a Cavaliere dell’Ordine al Merito for her services to the Arts.Cinema: conferito a Greta Scacchi Ordine a Merito
