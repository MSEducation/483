[[Eme 15]]

CATEGORIES: Eme 15, Musical groups established in 2011, Mexican pop music groups, Musical groups from Mexico City, Mexican dance musicians

Eme 15 (also stylized as EME XV and M-15) was a Mexican Latin pop band composed of the six lead actors from the 2012 Nickelodeon Latin America television series Miss XV. The band was formed for the series in Mexico City by Televisa by producer Pedro Damián in August 2011. Music for the band's album was produced and written by Carlos Lara and former pop-rock singer Lynda Thomas.
On December 18, 2013, it was officially announced to fans via the band's official Twitter account that the band would split up, following their final concert on January 5, 2014 at the Mega Feria Imperial de Acapulco in Acapulco, Mexico.http://www.radioformula.com.mx/notas.asp?Idn=377915https://twitter.com/MissXV_/status/413453419802664962

==Background==

Eme 15 was composed of the six main actors of the teen telenovela, Miss XV. Originally, the band was formed with five actors, but producers held a casting search in July 2011 to find a third female member. ==Debut Album: Eme 15 (2011-2013)==

Production on the band's debut album started in August 2011 in Mexico City. The band's self-titled album, Eme 15, was available for digital download and physical release internationally on June 26, 2012; one  bonus track was included for iTunes Mexico purchases. ==Live Album: Wonderland-Zona Preferente (2013)==

On April 30, 2013, the band released their first live album, Wonderland-Zona Preferente, a concert CD and DVD combo pack. The album and DVD were recorded in front of a live audience in Mexico City at one of two shows performed at the Auditorio Nacional on January 27, 2013. The recording also featured three new songs, including the band's third official single, Diferente.http://entretenimiento.terra.com.mx/television/paulina-goto-y-natasha-dupeyron-seran-las-mejores-amigas,3d2512d9d6c3e310VgnVCM3000009acceb0aRCRD.htmlhttp://eclecticmex.wordpress.com/2013/04/24/eme-15-presenta-wonderland-en-vivo/ The album debuted at number 6 on the Mexican album charts on May 5, 2013.http://www.centrodedesarrollodigital.com/amprofon3/Top100.pdf/ Mexican Top 100 Albums April 29 to May 5, 2013

==Tours: Miss XV promo and Wonderland tour (2012-2013)==

The band's earliest appearances to promote their debut album and television series, Miss XV were at autograph signings and showcases at MIXUP record store locations and radio venues throughout Mexico beginning in the spring of 2012. Eventually, the group embarked on a national tour, performing at venues and holding autograph signings across Mexico throughout 2012. The band performed four times at the Auditorio Nacional and sold out two shows in January 2013.http://mx.hola.com/musica/201211272753/eme-quince-banda-auditorio/ The group gained international fame following the release of their debut album. They debuted their album, performing at smaller showcases and appearing on various television and radio programs in Colombia and Argentina in October 2012. The band performed their first full concert set outside of Mexico at the famed Teatro Gran Rex theatre in Buenos Aires, Argentina on April 28, 2013.http://www.extremista.com.ar/247912/firma-de-discos-de-eme15-en-el-local-yenny-de-unicenter-shopping/http://www.eluniversal.com.mx/espectaculos/120024.html

===Wonderland Live tour (2013-2014)===

To promote their live album, Wonderland-Zona Preferente, the group made several appearances on television programs to perform their single, Diferente and gave radio interviews in Mexico.
