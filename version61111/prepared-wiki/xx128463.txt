[[Wilson Island (Queensland)]]

CATEGORIES: Islands on the Great Barrier Reef, Important Bird Areas of Queensland, Seaside resorts in Australia

Wilson Island is one of eight vegetated coral cays in the Capricornia Cays National Park.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.epa.qld.gov.au/parks_and_forests/find_a_park_or_forest/capricornia_cays_national_park/|title=Capricornia Cays National Park|publisher=EPA/QPWS|accessdate=2008-06-16 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080524094424/http://www.epa.qld.gov.au/parks_and_forests/find_a_park_or_forest/capricornia_cays_national_park/  |archivedate = 2008-05-24[/tpl] It is located north of the Tropic of Capricorn,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.derm.qld.gov.au/parks/capricornia-cays/pdf/capcays-map.pdf |title=Capricornia Cays National Park map|publisher=Department of Environment and Resource Management |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl]  approximately 72 kilometres north east off the coast of Gladstone, Queensland and 15 kilometres from nearby Heron Island.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wilsonisland.com/getting/|title=Wilson Island Getting There|publisher=Delaware North|accessdate=2008-06-16[/tpl]  It is part of the Capricornia Cays Important Bird Area.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.birdata.com.au/iba.vm |title=IBA: Capricornia Cays |accessdate=2011-06-12 |work= Birdata |first= |last= |publisher=Birds Australia |date= [/tpl]  It is also part of the Great Barrier Reef World Heritage Area.

==Wildlife==

Wilson Island is an important turtle and bird rookery fringed by a white coral beach and covered with pisonia forest.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/travel/barefoot-bliss/2007/09/13/1189276885271.html?page=fullpage#contentSwap1|title=Barefoot bliss|last=Gough Henly|first=Susan|date=2007-09-15|publisher=The Age|accessdate=2008-06-16[/tpl]  The Capricorn Silvereye, a small bird endemic to the southern Great Barrier Reef, is present.  From November to March, the island is home to wedge-tailed shearwaters and green turtles laying their eggs. The island is closed during the month of February for the bird nesting season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wilsonisland.com/|title=Wilson Island, Great Barrier Reef|publisher=Delaware North|accessdate=2008-06-16[/tpl] From January to April, baby green turtles hatch and make their way into the sea, while from June to September, humpback whales can be seen going to and from their winter feeding grounds.
