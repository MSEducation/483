[[Nuummioq]]

CATEGORIES: 2009 films, Greenlandic films, Greenlandic-language films, Danish-language films, 2000s drama films, Nuuk, Films shot in Greenland, Films set in Greenland

Nuummioq is a 2009 Greenlandic drama film directed by Otto Rosing and Torben Bech and produced by Mikisoq H. Lynge. Nuummioq means "a man from Nuuk" in the Greenlandic language. Nuummioq premiered in Nuuk on 31 October 2009.

==Plot==

Malik, a 35 year old construction worker from Nuuk discovers the love of his life at the time when he is diagnosed with cancer. He faces the choice of staying in Greenland with Nivi, the woman he has come to love − or leaving for Denmark in search of medical treatment.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Suluk |year=2010 |volume=4 |publisher=Air Greenland |first=Christian |last=Schultz-Lorsentzen |title=Acclaim to film pioneers |page=43[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dfi.dk/English/News/First-feature-from-Greenland.aspx |publisher=Danish Film Institute |title=First feature from Greenland |accessdate=31 August 2010[/tpl]

==Production==

Nuummioq is the first feature film produced entirely in Greenland. Filming began on 4 August 2008 in the area around Nuuk.[tpl]cite news|url=http://sermitsiaq.gl/kultur/article49700.ece |title="Nuummioq" støttes af rederi |work=Sermitsiaq |date=5 August 2008 |language=Danish |accessdate=31 August 2010[/tpl] The script was written by Torben Bech based on an original idea by Otto Rosing.
Torben Bech the screenwriter consulted the film in pre production and due to director Otto Rosings personal problems with depression Bech later assumed directorial duties and completed the film with producer Mikisoq H. Lynge. He is co-credited Director with Otto Rosing.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.variety.com/review/VE1117941918.html?categoryid=31&cs=1 |publisher=Variety |title=Nuummioq |first=Todd |last=McCarthy |accessdate=31 August 2010 |date=23 January 2010[/tpl]
The film was produced by Mikisoq H. Lynge of 3900 Pictures, sponsored by Royal Arctic Line, the state-owned, Greenlandic freight company.

==Cast==

The film cast included professional and amateur actors from Greenland and Denmark:[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nuummioqthemovie.com/nuummioqcast.html |publisher=3900 Pictures, Official Website |title=Cast & Crew |accessdate=31 August 2010[/tpl]

==Reception==

Nuummioq premiered at the 2010 Sundance Film Festival selected in the World Cinema Dramatic Competition.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Suluk |year=2010 |volume=2 |publisher=Air Greenland |title=Mikisoq Lynge & Otto Rosing |page=20[/tpl] The reception of the film was positive, focusing on the moody atmosphere reminiscent of the works of Ingmar Bergman.[tpl]cite news|url=http://sermitsiaq.ag/kultur/article109415.ece |title=Nuummioq triumferer i anmeldelse |work=Sermitsiaq |date=27 January 2010 |language=Danish |accessdate=31 August 2010[/tpl]
It became the first Greenlandic film to be submitted for the Academy Award for Best Foreign Language Film,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2010/20101013a.html |title=65 Countries Enter Race for 2010 Foreign Language Film Oscar |accessdate=2010-10-16 |work=oscars.org[/tpl] but it didn't make the final shortlist.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/press/pressreleases/2011/20110119.html |title=9 Foreign Language Films Continue to Oscar Race |accessdate=2011-01-19 |work=oscars.org[/tpl]
