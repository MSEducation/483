Dylan Marie Dreyer (born August 2, 1981)[tpl]citation needed|date=October 2013[/tpl] is an American television meteorologist working for NBC News. She is weather anchor on Weekend Today and MSNBC on weekends.[tpl]cite web|author=June 4, 2013 at 11:05 AM ET |url=http://www.today.com/allday/dylan-dreyer-weather-anchor-todays-weekend-editions-6C10109543 |title=Dylan Dreyer, weather anchor for TODAY's weekend editions |publisher=TODAY.com |date=2013-06-04 |accessdate=2014-01-29[/tpl]  Dreyer frequently appears on Today on weekdays as a weather correspondent and a fill-in for Al Roker, Dreyer also appears on The Weather Channel and on NBC Nightly News. Dreyer joined NBC News in September 2012 after working at NBC station WHDH in Boston, Massachusetts since 2007.[tpl]cite web|author=Kevin Eck|url=http://www.mediabistro.com/tvspy/its-official-dreyer-named-to-nbcs-weekend-today_b61177 |title=It's Official: Dreyer Named to NBC's "Weekend Today" - TVSpy |publisher=mediabistro |date=2012-09-13 |accessdate=2013-08-10[/tpl]

==Early life and education==

Dreyer was born in Manalapan, New Jersey and graduated from Rutgers University in 2003 with a bachelor's degree in meteorology.

==Career==

Dreyer has worked at WICU in Erie, Pennsylvania, WJAR in Providence, Rhode Island and WHDH in Boston, Massachusetts.
Dreyer was in a car accident while en route to cover a blizzard for The Today Show on February 9, 2013, she suffered from mild concussion and did not get to report for a while.[tpl]cite web|url=http://blog.zap2it.com/frominsidethebox/2013/02/todays-dylan-dreyer-on-her-blizzard-car-accident-it-was-so-unfortunate.html|title=‘Today’s’ Dylan Dreyer on her blizzard car accident: ‘It was so unfortunate’ - Zap2it|publisher=blog.zap2it.com|accessdate=2014-04-02[/tpl]
