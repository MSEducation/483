[[Khancoban]]

CATEGORIES: Towns in New South Wales, Snowy Mountains Scheme

Khancoban [tpl]IPAc-en|k|æ|n|.|ˈ|k|oʊ|b|ən[/tpl] is a small town in Tumbarumba Shire, New South Wales, Australia.  The town is located [tpl]convert|567|km|mi[/tpl] from the state capital, Sydney and [tpl]convert|13|km|mi|0[/tpl] from the state border with Victoria, in the foothills of the Snowy Mountains, near the upper reaches of the Murray River.  At the 2006 census, Khancoban had a population of 281.[tpl]Census 2006 AUS |id=UCL143400 |name=Khancoban (L) (Urban Centre/Locality) |accessdate=25 October 2007|quick=on[/tpl]
Khancoban Post Office opened on 7 November 1876.[tpl]Citation|last = Premier Postal History|title = Post Office List | url = https://www.premierpostal.com/cgi-bin/wsProd.sh/Viewpocdwrapper.p?SortBy=NSW&country= |accessdate = 11 June 2009[/tpl]
The town is a popular launching place for tourists exploring the Snowy Mountains area including Kosciuszko National Park.  Khancoban is linked to Jindabyne and Cooma by the Alpine Way, a scenic route that takes travellers past the ski resort of Thredbo.  The closest regional centre is Corryong, [tpl]convert|20|km|mi[/tpl] to the west in Victoria.
Khancoban was constructed to house workers involved in the Snowy Mountains Scheme, Australia's largest engineering project, designed to provide hydro-electric power and water for irrigation to vast areas of the nation.  The town is still mainly populated by workers employed by Snowy Hydro Limited working in places such as Murray 1 and Murray 2 Power Stations.
