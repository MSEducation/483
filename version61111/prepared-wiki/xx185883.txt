[[Gmina Zdzieszowice]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Opole Voivodeship, Krapkowice County

Gmina Zdzieszowice is an urban-rural gmina (administrative district) in Krapkowice County, Opole Voivodeship, in south-western Poland. Its seat is the town of Zdzieszowice, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|13|km|mi|0[/tpl] south-east of Krapkowice and [tpl]convert|31|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south-east of the regional capital Opole.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|57.85|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 17,478 (out of which the population of Zdzieszowice amounts to 13,329, and the population of the rural part of the gmina is 4,149).
The gmina contains part of the protected area called Góra Świętej Anny Landscape Park.

==Villages==

Apart from the town of Zdzieszowice, Gmina Zdzieszowice contains the villages and settlements of Januszkowice, Jasiona, Krępna, Oleszka, Rozwadza, Wielmierzowice and Żyrowa.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Zdzieszowice is bordered by the town of Kędzierzyn-Koźle and by the gminas of Gogolin, Krapkowice, Leśnica, Reńska Wieś, Strzelce Opolskie and Walce.
