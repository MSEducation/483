[[Lovette Hill]]

CATEGORIES: 1907 births, 1989 deaths, Centenary Gentlemen baseball players, Centenary Gentlemen football players, Houston Cougars baseball coaches, Houston Cougars football coaches

Lovette L. Hill (March 23, 1907 – August 28, 1989) was the fourth head coach of the Houston Cougars baseball team from 1950 to 1974.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.chron.com/CDA/archives/archive.mpl/1989_646382/former-uh-baseball-coach-dies.html |title=Former UH baseball coach dies |publisher=Houston Chronicle |date=1989-08-29 |accessdate=2011-07-18[/tpl]  Hill holds the record for the longest serving head baseball coach in University of Houston history.[tpl]cite web |url=http://uhcougars.cstv.com/genrel/hall_of_honor.html |title=UH Hall of Honor |publisher=Houston Cougars athletics |accessdate=2008-07-28[/tpl]  While at Houston, Hill compiled a 343–325–5 record with five first place finishes in the Missouri Valley Conference.  He guided the Cougars to their only two College World Series appearances, in 1953 and 1967.  In 1967, his team was national runner-up.
From 1949 to 1961, Hill also served as an assistant coach for the Houston Cougars football team.  He graduated in 1931 from Centenary College of Louisiana.
