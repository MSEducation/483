[[Debranding]]

CATEGORIES: Logos, Advertising techniques

Debranding is a term used to describe when a company removes its name from its logo for a marketing campaign. One form of this is Share a Coke, which was developed by the United Kingdom division of Coca-Cola.[tpl]cite news|last=Fisher|first=Lucy|title=Debranding: why Coca-Cola's decision to drop its name worked|url=http://www.theguardian.com/media-network/media-network-blog/2013/aug/06/coke-debranding-name-dropping|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=The Guardian|date=6 August 2013[/tpl] Another form of debranding has occurred with Starbucks, where the company has removed its names from cups, in an attempt to make them appear less corporate and more personal. Nike Inc. has been called the first company to debrand their logo, which happened in 1995[tpl]cite news|last=Handley|first=Lucy|title=Debranding: The great name-dropping gamble|url=http://www.marketingweek.co.uk/trends/debranding-the-great-name-dropping-gamble/4001018.article|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=Marketing Week|date=5 April 2012[/tpl]
