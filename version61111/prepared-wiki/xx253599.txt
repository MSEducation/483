[[Henri Lacassagne]]

CATEGORIES: France international rugby union players, French rugby union players, 1886 births, French military personnel killed in World War I, 1916 deaths

Henri André Lacassagne (19 October 1886 – 11 November 1916) was a French rugby union player. He played at scrum half for Stade Bordelais. He was capped twice for [tpl]nrut|France[/tpl].

==Career==

Henri Lacassagne's first test was on 1 January 1906, against [tpl]nrut|New Zealand[/tpl].
France played its first official match in the old Parc des Princes, against New Zealand. In their tour of the British Isles, the "All Blacks" accepted a challenge for a match in Paris. After playing their second match in Swansea on 30 December, they took the ferry to Boulogne-sur-Mer, then the train to the Gare du Nord. Fatigued by their voyage, and after a three-month tour, in which they won 31 of 32 matches, they won easily by 38–8 against France in front of 3000 spectators.[tpl]en icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web| url=http://stats.allblacks.com/asp/teamsheet.asp?level1=All_Blacks&Level2=ABC&MT_ID=1088 | title=Compte rendu France Nouvelle-Zélande de 1906 | author= | year= | editor= | work=www.stats.allblacks.com|accessdate=31 July 2009[/tpl].Collectif Midi olympique (2005), pg 11.[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] Compte rendu France Nouvelle-Zélande de 1906 sur ffr.fr. retrieved 31 July 2009.[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web| url=http://rugby-nomades.qc.ca/info-nomades/histoire_1906.php | title= 1st January1906 : Can we count on you? | author=Jean-Paul Rey | year= | editor=rugby-nomades.qc.ca | work=|accessdate=2 August 2009[/tpl].
Henri Lacassagne played his last test match on 5 January 1907, against the England national rugby union team.
He was on the champion team of France in 1904, 1905, 1906, 1907 and a finalist in 1908.
