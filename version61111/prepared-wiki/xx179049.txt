[[Gmina Nadarzyn]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Masovian Voivodeship, Pruszków County

Gmina Nadarzyn is a rural gmina (administrative district) in Pruszków County, Masovian Voivodeship, in east-central Poland. Its seat is the village of Nadarzyn, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|9|km|mi|0[/tpl] south of Pruszków and [tpl]convert|20|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south-west of Warsaw.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|73.4|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 10,362.

==Villages==

Gmina Nadarzyn contains the villages and settlements of Bieliny, Kajetany, Kostowiec, Krakowiany, Młochów, Nadarzyn, Parole, Rozalin, Rusiec, Stara Wieś, Strzeniówka, Szamoty, Urzut, Walendów, Wola Krakowiańska, Wolica and Żabieniec.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Nadarzyn is bordered by the gminas of Brwinów, Grodzisk Mazowiecki, Lesznowola, Michałowice, Raszyn, Tarczyn and Żabia Wola.
