[[Centre d'Etudes Diplomatiques et Stratégiques]]

CATEGORIES: Postgraduate schools in France

The Center for Diplomatic and Strategic Studies (French: Centre d'Etudes Diplomatiques et Stratégiques, CEDS) is an accredited postgraduate school and a think tank in diplomacy and international relations headquartered in Paris. CEDS is the graduate and doctoral school of the Écoles des hautes études internationales et politiques (HEI-HEP), founded in 1899 by Émile Durkheim, Charles Seignobos, and Romain Rolland. In 1986, Pascal Chaigneau (HEC School of Management and Paris Descartes University),[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.afdevinfo.com/htmlreports/org/org_60969.html |title=Centre d'Études Diplomatiques et Stratégiques - CEDS Paris - France |publisher=Afdevinfo.com |date=2007-09-05 |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl] initiated CEDS as a specialized graduate school for officers, diplomats, and future executives. It is accredited as a Premier College by the (Accreditation Service for International Colleges.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.asic.org.uk/collegeinfo.php?college_id=721 |title=ASIC College Info - Centre D'Ă‰tudes Diplomatiques et Strategiques (CEDS) |publisher=Asic.org.uk |date= |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.enseignement-prive.fr/ecole-specialisee/688-ceds-centre-d-etudes-diplomatiques-et-strategiques.php |title=CEDS Centre d'Etudes Diplomatiques et Stratégiques, Paris (75 - Paris) CIDE |publisher=Enseignement-prive.fr |date= |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl] It is the exclusive partner postgraduate school of the French Ecole Militaire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ecoledeguerre.defense.gouv.fr/spip.php?article648 |title=Ecole de Guerre |publisher=Ecoledeguerre.defense.gouv.fr |date= |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl]
Initially a non-academic institution reserved to serving diplomats and either generals or senior officers, it also admits commissioned officers at large, executives, and senior civil servants (Hauts fonctionnaires) linked to the diplomatic service. Among others, its Greek branch has educated the commanding officer of the Greek Navy's Frigate Command between him attending the Naval Postgraduate School and the Harvard Kennedy School of Government.[tpl]dead link|date=September 2012[/tpl]

==Specific relationship with diplomats==

Along with the Institut français, the CEDS is a de facto lever of the French diplomatic influence (Action Française à l'étranger).[tpl]dead link|date=September 2012[/tpl] It received French politician Jack Lang on September 25, 2009 for the inauguration of a Curriculum in International Relations delivered jointly with the Institut Français à Athènes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.latitudefrance.org/Jack-Lang-expose-les-Grands-enjeux.html |title=Jack Lang s'engage pour l'Europe | Grèce | Athènes |publisher=LatitudeFrance |date=2009-10-27 |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl] One of its academic advisers and professors is Carlos Antonio Carrasco, ambassador of Bolivia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://archive.gg.ca/media/doc.asp?lang=f&DocID=4170 |title=Présentation de lettres de créance et de commission à Rideau Hall |publisher=Archive.gg.ca |date=2004-04-19 |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl]

==Faculty and research==

===Research===

The CEDS edits the peer-reviewed academic journal Enjeux Diplomatiques et Stratégiques. It also publishes a limited number of its students' doctoral thesis as books either independently or via the publishing house l'Harmattan in Paris, in English, in the series "Diplomacy and Strategy".

===Faculty===

====Military====

The CEDS is formally associated to the French Joint Defence College and the ECOSOC of the United Nations. Among its faculty are antiterrorism and ICTY intelligence expert Raymond Carter,[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] http://www.wisdom-conseil.com/raymon2.html Jacques Walch of the French Army,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.franceculture.com/personne-jacques-walch.html |title=France Culture |publisher=France Culture |date= |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl] and peace operations expert and military staff committee representative Dominique Trinquand,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.operationspaix.net/TRINQUAND-Dominique |title=Banque d'experts TRINQUAND, Dominique |language=[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] |publisher=Operationspaix.net |date= |accessdate=2012-09-21[/tpl] who is also a former alumni of CEDS. Most of the military faculty of the CEDS also comes from the French Institut des hautes études de défense nationale.
