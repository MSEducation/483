CATEGORIES: 1952 births, Living people, Hong Kong democracy activists, Members of the Legislative Council of Hong Kong, Alumni of the University of Hong Kong, Hong Kong academics, Vice chancellors and presidents of universities in Hong Kong, Democratic Party (Hong Kong) politicians, Meeting Point politicians, HK LegCo Members 1995–97

Anthony Cheung Bing-leung, GBS, BBS, JP ([tpl]zh|t=張炳良[/tpl]; born 17 November 1952) is the current Secretary for Transport and Housing.
He was a member of the Legislative Council of Hong Kong and a vice-chairman of the Democratic Party of Hong Kong. He was also the 5th President of the Hong Kong Institute of Education. He is at present an official member of the Executive Council of Hong Kong. He is the only member with a pro-democracy camp background in the council.Professor the Honourable Anthony CHEUNG Bing-leung, BBS, JP
Cheung founded SynergyNet, a policy think tank, and provides public comment on policy issues. In 2011, he called for a separate regulatory code for the emerging class of political assistants and policy advisers, who are between civil servants and executive officials.The third force, SCMP. 2 Nov 2011
At present, Cheung also serves as the chairman of both the Hong Kong Consumer Council and the Subsidised Housing Committee of the Hong Kong Housing Authority.Professor the Honourable Anthony CHEUNG Bing-leung, BBS, JP

==Education==

Cheung is a graduate of Wah Yan College, Hong Kong, a prestigious all male Roman Catholic Jesuit school in Hong Kong. He has a PhD in Government from the London School of Economics, an MSc in Public Sector Management from Aston University and a BSocSc in Sociology and Economics from the University of Hong Kong (1974).
