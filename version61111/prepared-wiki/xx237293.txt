[[MV Empire Beacon]]

CATEGORIES: 1941 ships, Clyde-built ships, Empire ships, Ministry of War Transport ships, Ships sunk by mines, Shipwrecks in the Bristol Channel, Maritime incidents in 1942

Empire Beacon was an 872 GRT coaster which was built in 1941. She was owned by the Ministry of War Transport (MoWT) and managed by John Stewart & Co (Shipping) Ltd. Empire Beacon struck a mine and sank off St. Ann's Head, Pembrokeshire on 5 April 1942.

==Career==

Empire Beacon was built by Scott & Sons Ltd, Bowling, West Dunbartonshire.[tpl]cite book | first = and Sawyer, L A| last = Mitchell, W H | year = 1995| month = | title = The Empire Ships| pages = | publisher = Lloyd's of London Press Ltd| location = London, New York, Hamburg, Hong Kong|isbn=1-85044-275-4[/tpl] She was yard number 358 and was launched on 24 September 1941 and completed in November that year.[tpl]csr|register=MSI|id=1168695|accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] Empire Beacon was built for the MoWT and placed under the management of John Stewart & Co (Shipping) Ltd. Her port of registry was Glasgow.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.plimsollshipdata.org/pdffile.php?name=41b0988.pdf |title=LLOYD'S REGISTER, NAVIRES A VAPEUR ET A MOTEURS |accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl]

==Sinking==

On 5 April 1942, Empire Beacon struck a mine and sank [tpl]convert|6|nmi|km[/tpl] off St. Ann's Head ([tpl]coord|51|41|N|5|10|W[/tpl]).

==Official Numbers and Code Letters==

Official numbers were a forerunner to IMO Numbers. Empire Beacon had the UK Official Number 168695 and used the Code Letters BCSY.
