[[Museum of Brands, Packaging and Advertising]]

CATEGORIES: History museums in London, Museums in Kensington and Chelsea, Media museums in the United Kingdom, Charities based in London, Museums established in 2002, 2002 establishments in England, Advertising museums, Advertising in the United Kingdom

The Museum of Brands, Packaging and Advertising in London examines the history of consumer culture from Victorian times to the present day. The museum was set up as a registered charity in 2002.[tpl]EW charity|1093538|MUSEUM OF BRANDS, PACKAGING AND ADVERTISING[/tpl]
It is based in a mews near Portobello Road in London’s Notting Hill.

==History==

The museum is based around over 12,000 items from the Robert Opie Collection which were housed in the Museum of Advertising and Packaging in Gloucester from 1984 until its closure in October 2001.Culture 24: Lights Out At The Opie Collection, by Jon Pratty, 17 October 2001 The collection moved to Notting Hill in 2005.[tpl]cite web | url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/magazine/4504986.stm | title = Pack it in | work = BBC | author = Jonathan Duffy |date=December 2005[/tpl] Another display of the Robert Opie Collection at Opie's Museum of Memories formed part of the now-defunct Wigan Pier Experience.

==Collection==

The museum features over 12,000 original items including domestic "everyday" products, packaging, posters, toys and games.  
Set out in chronological order in the form of a “Time Tunnel” the museum gives  visitors “a trip down memory lane”, and to see how the brands around us have evolved from the naïve charm of Victorian times to the greater sophistication of today.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.thegoodword.co.uk/2010/10/26/the-museum-of-brands-packaging-and-advertising-a-quality-find/ | title = The Museum of Brands, Packaging and Advertising…a quality find! | work = The Good Word | author = Paul Sawers |date=October 2010[/tpl] It also reflects the change in shopping habits, the impact of transportation, media, the effects of two world wars and the gradual emancipation of women.

==Museum facilities==

The museum has a small tea room serving tea, coffee and biscuits. It is also available as a venue for drinks, receptions, dinners, parties and launches and has a separate conference space for meetings.
