[[Arnaud Tsamere]]

CATEGORIES: 1975 births, Living people, 21st-century French male actors, French comedians, French male film actors, French humorists, French people of Breton descent, French male stage actors, French male television actors, French television personalities, French television writers, People from Bordeaux

Arnaud Tsedri (born 11 March 1975), better known by the stage name Arnaud Tsamere ([tpl]IPA-fr|aʁ.no tsa.mɛʁ[/tpl]; sometimes written with a grave accent as Arnaud Tsamère), is a French comedian, actor and television presenter. He is primarily known for his appearances on France 2's sketch comedy show On n'demande qu'à en rire, as well as playing Captain Sport Extrême in the comedy science fiction programme Hero Corp.
After quitting his sales job, he joined the Déclic Théâtre group, where he acted in plays and participated in improvisation events. He wrote his first one-man show, Réflexions profondes sur pas mal de trucs, in 2002 with Arnaud Joyet, and his second, Chose Promise, in 2007 with Joyet and François Rollin. He has attended comedy festivals in Montreux, Avignon and Puy-Saint-Vincent, and is currently a member of the Ligue Majeure d'Improvisation.

==Early life==

Tsamere's paternal grandparents are from Guipavas, and his father is Breton.[tpl]cite web|title=Interview Arnaud Tsamère|url=http://www.saintpoldeleon.fr/?Interview-Arnaud-Tsamere|work=Saint Pol de Léon|accessdate=20 August 2013|language=French[/tpl] He was born in Bordeaux, but grew up in Versailles, Rocquencourt and Trappes, in the Yvelines departement.[tpl]cite web|last=Julien|first=Jean-François|title=La bande à Ruquier s'installe au Zénith de Limoges|url=http://www.lepopulaire.fr/limousin/actualite/2013/01/07/la-bande-a-ruquier-s-installe-au-zenith-de-limoges-1395942.html|work=Lepopulaire.fr|accessdate=20 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Ruquier's gang sets up in the Zénith de Limoges[/tpl] His father was a general in the French Air Force[tpl]cite web|last=Pasero|first=Jennifer|title='Chose promise' : Tsamère fait son show à Perpignan|url=http://www.lindependant.fr/2012/12/29/chose-promise-tsamere-fait-son-show-a-perpignan,1714443.php|work=L'Indépendant|accessdate=19 August 2013|language=French|trans_title='Chose Promise': Tsamère does his show in Perpignan[/tpl] and was awarded the Legion of Honour;[tpl]cite AV media|people=Tsamere, Arnaud|date=19 April 2012|title=On n'demande qu'à en rire|medium=Television programme|language=French|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=HuNPgpIao3s|accessdate=16 August 2013|time=6:16 (in video clip)|publisher=France 2[/tpl] his mother was a housewife, whom he has described as "the best in her domain". He has two brothers.[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamère – Chose promise|language=French|url=http://sortir.telerama.fr/evenements/spectacles/arnaud-tsamere-chose-promise,23257.php|work=Télérama|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] He describes himself as having had a "strict upbringing".[tpl]cite web|last=Bourbon|first=Nicole|title=Arnaud Tsamère|url=http://www.regarts.org/Interviews/arnaud-tsamere.htm|work=Regarts|accessdate=15 August 2013|language=French[/tpl] Une éducation rigoureuse is rendered here as "a strict upbringing".
Speaking about his early life and its relation to his surreal comic style, Tsamere said:
 I grew up in a very comfortable environment. I wanted for nothing; serious things never happened to me in life. I have never been a victim of injustice, so I have nothing to protest. I don't have any particular origins which would be interesting to talk about. ... So, I do absurd stuff because I don't want to talk about other things.[tpl]cite AV media|people=Tsamere, Arnaud (interviewee)|date=15 April 2013|title=Arnaud Tsamère en interview pour Le Mensuel 2013|trans_title=Arnaud Tsamère interviewed for Le Mensuel, 2013|medium=YouTube video|language=French|url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=OvjeMNxr6oY|accessdate=15 August 2013|work=Le Mensuel[/tpl] 
After getting his baccalauréat, he studied law at university, where he discovered an improvisational match in his first year and "fell in love" with the performance. In 1998, he obtained a master's degree in business law and later got a job working in export sales. However, he realised that he "could no longer live without the stage", and on the advice of his friend Arnaud Joyet and Déclic Théâtre co-founder Alain Degois, quit his job and launched his career as a comedian.[tpl]cite web|last=Tsamere|first=Arnaud|title=Biographie|language=French|trans_title=Biography|url=http://www.arnaudtsamere.com/biographie|work=ArnaudTsamere.com|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Gervais|first=Nicolas|title=Entretien avec Arnaud Tsamere|url=http://www.saint-quentin-en-yvelines.fr/actualites/culture/entretien-avec-arnaud-tsamere/|work=Saint-Quentin-en-Yvelines|accessdate=16 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Interview with Arnaud Tsamere[/tpl]

==Career==

===Theatre, improvisation and stand up===

After quitting his previous job, Tsamere took part in the Déclic Théâtre improvisation group under its co-founder Alain "Papy" Degois, whom he said "pushed me, gave me confidence, coddled me and got me to act in plays and improvisation matches". Between 2003 and 2005, he played the baron Christian de Neuvillette in an adaptation of Cyrano de Bergerac[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamère|url=http://www.premiere.fr/Star/Arnaud-Tsamere-2527112|work=Premiere.fr|publisher=Lagardère Group|accessdate=16 August 2013|language=French[/tpl] at the Château de Gizeux with the theatre group La Pastière, which he was introduced to by Degois.
He began writing his first one-man stand-up comedy show in 2002 with his friend Arnaud Joyet, inspired by a videotape of comedian François Rollin. The show, entitled Réflexions profondes sur pas mal de trucs ("Profound Reflections on Quite a Few Things"),[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamere – Réflexions profondes sur pas mal de trucs|language=French|url=http://www.theatreonline.com/guide/detail_piece.asp?i_Programmation=12095|work=TheatreOnline.com|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] played on theatrical "flops", a form of humour that Tsamere said "can seem easy but isn't". It was performed at the Blancs Manteaux theatre from April 2005 to January 2006.
Tsamere met Rollin at the Dinard Comedy Festival, and he joined Tsamere and Joyet to write a second show, Chose Promise ("Promised Thing"). In it, Tsamere plays a secondary school economics teacher named Patrice Valenton, performing as part of a promise made to a friend who died in a car accident.[tpl]cite web|last=Tsamere|first=Arnaud|title=One Man|language=French|url=http://www.arnaudtsamere.com/one-man|work=ArnaudTsamere.com|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Doladille|first=Elsa|title=Arnaud Tsamere : "pour On n'demande qu'à en rire, je mettais plus de coeur à l'ouvrage que pour le bac..."|url=http://www.vousnousils.fr/2013/04/23/arnaud-tsamere-dans-on-ndemande-qua-en-rire-je-mettais-plus-de-coeur-a-louvrage-que-pour-passer-le-bac-546352|work=Vousnousils|accessdate=17 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Arnaud Tsamere: "for On n'demande qu'à en rire, I put more into my work than to pass the Baccalauréat"[/tpl] Tsamere has described Valenton's profession as "not far from the style of what I am, but ... very far in substance". He chose to perform as a character under Rollin's suggestion, so that he could depict failure under the guise of somebody who is not a comedian. Regarding the performance's theme of death, he said "It's a bit strange, but it has the virtue of attracting the public's attention", and that "the most irrefutable reason to go on stage ... is to talk about something strong". Chose Promise was released on DVD on 6 March 2013 after being recorded at the Théâtre Sébastopol in Lille.[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamere – Chose promise – DVD|url=http://www.dvdfr.com/dvd/f62574-arnaud-tsamere-chose-promise.html|work=dvdfr.com|accessdate=16 August 2013[/tpl]
Tsamere has attended comedy festivals such as the Montreux Comedy Festival,[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamere|url=http://www.montreuxcomedy.ch/en/artistes/arnaud-tsamere|work=Montreux Comedy Festival|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] where he performed with his On n'demande qu'à en rire colleague Jérémy Ferrari,[tpl]cite web|title=Gala : "Jérémy Ferrari et Arnaud Tsamère : Une belle histoire de haine !"|url=http://www.montreuxcomedy.ch/evenement/gala-jeremy-ferrari-et-arnaud-tsamere-une-belle-histoire-de-haine|work=Montreux Comedy Festival|accessdate=16 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Gala: "Jérémy Ferrari and Arnaud Tsamère: A beautiful story of hatred!"[/tpl] and the Avignon Festival.[tpl]cite web|title=Arnaud Tsamere|language=French|url=http://www.avignonleoff.com/programme/2012/auteurs/T/tsamere_arnaud/|work=Avignon Festival – Off|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
He is now part of the Ligue Majeure d'Improvisation.[tpl]cite web|title=Qui sommes-nous ?|url=http://www.ligue-improvisation.com/qui.php|language=French|trans_title=Who are we?|work=Ligue Majeure d’Improvisation|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]

===Television and radio===

In 2005, with the help of Frédéric Testot, whom he met at a Puy-Saint-Vincent comedy festival, Tsamere got the job of presenting the weather forecast in Le Grand Journal on Canal+. He also appeared in Canal+'s SAV des émissions,[tpl]cite web|title=Jérémy Ferrari, Arnaud Tsamère et Baptiste Lecaplain : la tournée du trio|url=http://mag.monchval.com/jeremy-ferrari-arnaud-tsamere-baptiste-lecaplain-la-tournee-du-trio/|work=Magazine Cheval|accessdate=20 August 2013[/tpl] presented by Testot and Omar Sy.[tpl]cite web|title=Le "SAV des émissions" d'Omar et Fred ne répondra bientôt plus|url=http://www.rtl.fr/actualites/culture-loisirs/television/article/le-sav-des-emissions-d-omar-et-fred-ne-repondra-bientot-plus-7743204611|work=RTL (French radio)[tpl]![/tpl]RTL.fr|accessdate=22 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Omar and Fred's "SAV des émissions" will soon no longer answer[/tpl] After quitting the weather in June 2006, he hosted La longue nuit du pénis on the same channel that September. It was a night dedicated to the penis,[tpl]cite web|title=La longue Nuit du Pénis, ce 18 juillet sur Canal+|url=http://www.madmoizelle.com/la-longue-nuit-du-penis-ce-18-juillet-sur-canal_2006-07-18-2035|work=Madmoizelle|accessdate=20 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=La longue Nuit du Pénis, 18 July on Canal+[/tpl] which Tsamere has described as "nothing pornographic or erotic. It was a serious series of documentaries." He says he was asked after Frédérique Bel refused, because he was "probably the only one liable to accept".
In 2007 Tsamere met Simon Astier during the recording of the M6 show Off Prime. He was given the role of Captain Sport Extrême in Astier's comedy science fiction programme, Hero Corp, which is broadcast on France 4. He only appeared in one episode of the first series, but more frequently in the second.[tpl]cite web|title=Interview Arnaud Tsamère|url=http://www.herocorpfrance.com/2010/01/05/interview-arnaud-tsamre-par-herocorpfrance/|work=Hero Corp France|accessdate=17 August 2013[/tpl] Speaking about the differences between himself and his character, Tsamere said "He is a misogynist, beastly, an adventurer, unattached... I am just the opposite".[tpl]cite web|title=Interview de Captain Sport Extrême|language=French|trans_title=Interview of Captain Sport Extrême|url=http://herocorpmag.over-blog.com/article-30311148.html|work=Hero Corp Mag|accessdate=16 August 2013[/tpl] He also said "I've loved this character. I love playing crackpots."
In 2010 he participated in the sketch comedy talent show On n'demande qu'à en rire, created and presented by Laurent Ruquier and broadcast on France 2. Ruquier asked him to do so after he saw Tsamere in Monique est demandée caisse 12 and Le Comique. ===Film===

In 2011, Tsamere's first film role was Séverin in Charles Nemes' Au bistro du coin. In the same year, he appeared in Pascale Pouzadoux's La Croisière, and in the short films Le Métro (directed by Dianeïa Schaefer) and Deal (Wilfried Méance).
Tsamere played the character of Vincent in Arnaud Demanche's short film Being Homer Simpson, in which he appeared alongside Philippe Peythieu and Véronique Augereau, the voice actors of Homer and Marge respectively in the French dub of the animated series The Simpsons.[tpl]cite web|title=Being Homer Simpson : Le court-métrage avec Arnaud Tsamère et Bérengère Krief|url=http://www.meltybuzz.fr/being-homer-simpson-le-court-metrage-avec-arnaud-tsamere-et-berengere-krief-a189079.html|work=Meltybuzz|publisher=Meltygroup|accessdate=21 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=Being Homer Simpson: The short film with Arnaud Tsamère and Bérengère Krief[/tpl] He will also star in Fonzy, a remake of the film Starbuck,[tpl]cite web|last=Bori|first=Michèle|title=Premières infos sur Fonzy, le remake de Starbuck avec José Garcia|url=http://cinema.jeuxactu.com/news-cinema-fonzy-le-remake-de-starbuck-avec-jose-garcia-20066.htm|work=Filmsactu|publisher=Mixicom|accessdate=21 August 2013|language=French|trans_title=The first info on Fonzy, the remake of Starbuck with José Garcia[/tpl] in which he plays Maître Chasseigne. ==Influences==

On his website, Tsamere provides a link to that of François Rollin, as well as to that of English comedian Ricky Gervais, whom he calls "my absolute master".[tpl]cite web|last=Tsamere|first=Arnaud|title=Liens|language=French|trans_title=Links|url=http://www.arnaudtsamere.com/liens|work=ArnaudTsamere.com|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] Other role models of his are the brothers Simon and Alexandre Astier—he says of them and Gervais that he "could watch them perform anything". He has stated that his favourite director is Tim Burton, linking this to his surreal style.
