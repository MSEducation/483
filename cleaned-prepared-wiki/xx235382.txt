Anu Muhammad Anisur Rahman, better known by Anu Muhammad (; born 1956), is a Bangladeshi Economist and political activist. He is a professor at Jahangirnagar University where he has taught economics since 1982 and taught anthropology from 1991 to 2005. He was a visiting professor at University of Winnipeg and University of Manitoba in 2001 and stayed at Columbia University as a visiting Scholar in 1993. His research is focused on globalization, social transformation, gender issues, Non-governmental organizations and energy.

Political views

Besides his academic activities, Anu Muhammad is also a political activist against repression and discrimination, therefore against capitalism, imperialism, racism, patriarchy and oppression. His involvement in resistance and his appearance in TV talk shows led to a death threat in February 2008."Death threat to Anu condemned", Bangladesh News, February 27, 2008. He was injured by the police in September 2009 during a demonstration protesting exploration deals granted to two international oil companies."Attack on Anu Muhammad appalls civic leaders", Priyo News, September 4, 2009."Motia, Imam and Hanif visit Prof Anu Muhammad at hospital", bdnews24. This event led to a protest by the Bangladesh Economic Association"BEA slams police attack on Prof Anu Muhammad", The Financial Express, September 5, 2009. and to an open letter condemning the event signed by academics from around the world."Oil Companies: Hands Off Bangladesh", politicalaffairs.
