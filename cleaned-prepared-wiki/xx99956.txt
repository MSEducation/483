[[DreamWorks Classics]]

CATEGORIES: American animation studios, Film distributors, Children's television, Media companies based in New York City, Brand management, Companies established in 2000, DreamWorks Classics

DreamWorks Classics (formerly known as Classic Media), is an American animation studio and production company owned by DreamWorks Animation. It was founded in 2000 as Classic Media by former Broadway Video executives Eric Ellenbogen and John Engelman.

History

Classic Media

After its founding in May 2000, Classic Media acquired the UPA catalog from the estate of Henry Saperstein.
In April 2005, the company was recapitalized by an investment group led by Spectrum Equity Investors, Pegasus Capital Advisors, and Random House Ventures.
On December 14, 2006, it was announced that Classic Media would be acquired by UK-based rival Entertainment Rights for $210.0 million.
Entertainment Rights fell in to administration on April 1, 2009.

DreamWorks Classics

On July 23, 2012, DreamWorks Animation acquired Classic Media from Boomerang Media for $155 million; the company became a division of DreamWorks Animation and was renamed DreamWorks Classics.
