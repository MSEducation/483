[[Lolly (singer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1978 births, Living people, English female singers, English pop singers, Musicians from Birmingham, West Midlands, People from Sutton Coldfield

Lolly (born Anna Shantha Kumble, 27 June 1977, Sutton Coldfield, Birmingham) is a British pop star, TV presenter and actress.

Career

Anna Kumble worked as a model before going into a pop career.  Lolly arrived on the British pop music scene in 1999 with the release of her first single, "Viva La Radio". She released five singles and two albums over her two-year career in music. Her real name was released one letter at a time in Smash Hits magazine. After quitting her record label Polydor, Lolly reverted to her real name and began TV presenting, working for the BBC on programmes such as Xchange. In 2003, she provided backing vocals on The Fast Food Rockers album It's Never Easy Being Cheesy.

Pantomime

Kumble has starred in a number of pantomimes. In 2002, she played Tinker Bell in Peter Pan at the Theatre Royal, Brighton. and in 2013/2014 she starred in Robin Hood in the Stag, Sevenoaks.

Personal

Anna Kumble was born to Dr. Jaiker Kumble and Barbara in Sutton Coldfield. She has a brother Paul, and a sister Jodie. Kumble attended The Shrubbery School, Highclare School and Plantsbrook School in Sutton Coldfield.
