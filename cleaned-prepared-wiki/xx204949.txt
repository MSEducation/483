[[James Coughlan]]

CATEGORIES: 1980 births, Living people, Irish rugby union players, Munster Rugby players, Dolphin RFC players, Rugby union flankers, Rugby union number eights, Rugby sevens players

James Coughlan (born 6 December 1980 in Cork, Ireland) is an Irish rugby union player for Munster in the Pro 12 and Heineken Cup. He can play across the Back-Row, but primarily plays at Number 8.

Early rugby career

Born 6 December 1980 in Cork, Ireland, Coughlan started playing rugby with Old Christians at the age of 10. He continued playing when he went to CBC, and was selected for Irish Schools. He went to UCC, and played for Irish Universities. He joined Dolphin in 2001 and has played 85 times for them in AIB League/Cup, scoring 32 tries, a club record. Coughlan also played on the Irish Sevens team in 2008 that qualified for the 2009 Rugby Sevens World Cup, which was held in Dubai.

Munster

Coughlan made his Munster debut against Cardiff Blues in the Magners League in September 2006,

Ireland

Coughlan was called into the senior Ireland squad for the 2012 Six Nations Championship to replace the injured Leo Cullen but did not play.
He was called into Ireland's training squad for the 2013 Six Nations Championship on 21 January 2013.
