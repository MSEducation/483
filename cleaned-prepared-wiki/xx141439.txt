[[Allphones]]

CATEGORIES: Mobile phone companies of Australia, Privately held companies of Australia

Allphones is an Australian telecommunications retailer which specialises in offering a wide range of mobile and broadband related services across a range of carriers.
Allphones was started in 1989 with the first store in Mile End, South Australia. The company franchised a further 14 stores in South Australia before it was sold in 2000 by Brian and Helen Warner.highbeam.html Allphones has stores in every state and territory with over 170 stores nationally. 
Their products and services include mobile phones, mobile plans, broadband plans, pre-paid services and mobile phone accessories.

Class action

In 2008, the ACCC on behalf of 74 Allphones Retail franchisees, launched proceedings against Allphones Retail Pty Ltd as well as then chief executive Matthew Donnellan, director and chief operating officer Tony Baker, and former national franchising manager Ian Harkin as respondents, alleging they were "knowingly concerned in or party to the contravening conduct". Matthew Donnellan was removed from his position by the board in April 2011.theaustralian.au/business/allphones-board-terminates-chiefs-contract/story-e6frg8zx-1226039385518
The case was settled outside of court for 3 million to be shared amongst 55 franchisees represented in the action.theaustralian.au/australian-it/punishment-not-enough-for-allphones-bullying-tactics/story-e6frgakx-1225861764812

New brand image

In May 2011, Allphones changed their image with a new logo, uniforms and store design. 

Website hacking

In March 2012, Sensitive information regarding staff and a customer loyalty program were found to be unsecured on the Allphones Website. The security hole was closed quickly once the Company had been notified.
