[[Kate Kendell]]

CATEGORIES: 1960 births, Living people, LGBT rights activists from the United States, Former Latter Day Saints, University of Utah alumni, LGBT people from Utah

Kate Kendell (born Kathryn Dean Kendell, April 15, 1960) is the Executive Director of the National Center for Lesbian Rights (NCLR), a national legal organization that fights for the civil and human rights of lesbian, gay, bisexual, and transgender (LGBT) people and their families through litigation, public policy advocacy, and public education. Through direct litigation and advocacy, NCLR works to change discriminatory laws and to create new laws and policies protecting the LGBT community.

Career

Kendell has been with NCLR since 1994, when she joined the organization as its Legal Director. Two years later, she was named Executive Director.
Kendell grew up as a Latter-day Saint in Utah.
During her leadership, the issues facing the LGBT community—from homophobia in sports to immigration policy—have taken center stage in the United States' discussion of LGBT civil rights issues.

Awards

On March 23, 2010, Kendell was named a "woman who could be president" by the League of Women Voters of San Francisco at their annual "Women Who Could Be President" gala.

Personal life

Kendell turned 50 on April 15, 2010. She and her spouse, Sandy Holmes, were legally married in San Francisco in 2008, and daughter, Ariana. Their family includes Kate's daughter Emily.
