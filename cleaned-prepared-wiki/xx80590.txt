He then served as Governor of Newfoundland from 1936, throughout World War II, until 1946.

Biography

Walwyn joined the Royal Navy in 1893, spending two years training at the Dartmouth Britannia Royal Naval College before joining the battleship 
Walwyn was appointed commander of the first class protected cruiser 
On 29 February 1928 he was promoted to rear admiral.
The same year Walwyn was appointed Flag Officer Commanding and Director of the Royal Indian Marine, receiving promotion to vice-admiral on 1 November 1932,
From 1936 Walwyn served as Governor of Newfoundland and chairman of the Commission of Government. On 6 June 1939 he was appointed a Knight Commander of the Order of St. Michael and St. George (KCMG). During World War II he was active in encouraging Newfoundlanders to join the war effort. In 1946, he retired to Maiden Newton, Dorset, England, where he died in 1957.

Family

In recognition of her public and philanthropic work for the community in Newfoundland, his wife, Lady Eileen Walwyn, was created a Dame Commander of the Order of the British Empire on 1 January 1947.
