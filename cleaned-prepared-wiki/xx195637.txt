[[Mike Corey]]

CATEGORIES: American mixed martial artists, Mixed martial artists from Missouri, Featherweight mixed martial artists, Lightweight mixed martial artists, Living people, 1984 births

Mike Corey (born March 8, 1984 in O'Fallon, Missouri) is an American mixed martial artist.  He fought as a Featherweight for the MidWest Combat in the now defunct International Fight League (IFL).

Biography

A high-school wrestler, Corey joined the Marines and started MMA training while stationed in California, and won several Grapplers Quest tournaments.
Mike has made return against Tom Ahrens and which he won a clear cut decision win, winning all three rounds.
Mike now trains with Team Curran MMA in Crystal Lake, Illinois, and fighters such as Jeff Curra, Pat Curran and Bart Palaszewski.

Bellator Fighting Championships

Corey faced Chris Horodecki in his Bellator debut at Bellator 57. Corey and Horodecki fought to a majority draw (29-28, 28-28, 28-28).
Corey returned to the promotion as a replacement for Wagnney Fabiano against Ronnie Mann in the opening round of the Featherweight tournament at Bellator 60 on March 9, 2012.  After being knocked down twice in the opening round, Corey fought back to wins the next two rounds, securing a unanimous decision victory.
