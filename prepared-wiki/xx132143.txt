[[Hughes Center High School]]

CATEGORIES: Cincinnati Public Schools, High schools in Hamilton County, Ohio, Public high schools in Ohio, Magnet schools in Ohio

Hughes Center High School is a public high school located in Cincinnati, Ohio.  It is part of the Cincinnati Public Schools.

==History==

The first Hughes High School was established in 1853 on property on Fifth and Mound streets.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=gEwRAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA74#v=onepage&q&f=false | title=Illustrated Cincinnati | publisher=Stevens | accessdate=2013-05-19 | author=Kenny, Daniel | year=1875 | pages=74[/tpl]
The school owes its name to Thomas Hughes, an Englishman and shoemaker, who, by his will, dated December, 1826, left his property for a high school, which was built in 1853 at a cost of $23,375.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=US8WAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA107#v=onepage&q&f=false | title=Illustrated Guide to Cincinnati and the World's Columbian Exposition | publisher=R. Clarke | accessdate=2013-05-22 | author=Kenny, Daniel J. | year=1895 | pages=107[/tpl]

==Academics==
