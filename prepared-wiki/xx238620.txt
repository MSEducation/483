[[Arctomiaceae]]

CATEGORIES: Lecanorales, Lichens, Fungi families

The Arctomiaceae are a family of lichenized fungi in the Ascomycota, class Lecanorales.[tpl]cite journal |last=Lumbsch TH, Huhndorf SM. |date=December 2007 |title=Outline of Ascomycota – 2007 |journal=Myconet |volume=13 |issue= |pages=1–58 |id= |url=http://www.fieldmuseum.org/myconet/outline.asp |accessdate= |publisher=The Field Museum, Department of Botany, Chicago, USA [/tpl] The family was named by Theodor Magnus Fries in 1861.[tpl]cite journal |author= Fries TM.|year=1861 |title=Lichenes arctoi Europae Groenlandiaeque hactenus cogniti |journal=Nova Acta Regiae Societatis Scientiarum upsaliensis |volume=3 |issue=ser. 3 |pages=103–398 |language=Latin [/tpl] Species in this family are found in arctic and subarctic habitats, usually associated with bryophytes.[tpl]cite book |author=Cannon PF, Kirk PM. |title=Fungal Families of the World |publisher=CABI|location=Wallingford |year=2007 |page=17 |isbn=0-85199-827-5[/tpl]
