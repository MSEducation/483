[[Château de Saint-Côme-d'Olt]]

CATEGORIES: Castles in Aveyron, Official historical monuments of France

The Château de Saint-Côme-d'Olt is a medieval castle in the commune of Saint-Côme-d'Olt in the Aveyron département of France. It was the residence of the lords of Calmont and Castenau and is now the town hall.Green Guide: Languedoc, Roussillon, Tarn Gorges p95, Michelin Tyre plc, 1998, ISBN 2-06-136602-3
The castle was built in the 14th century at the heart of the fortified town and was associated with the Castelnau family, owners of the area for at least seven centuries. It has been listed since 1999 as a monument historique by the French Ministry of Culture.Ministry of Culture, accessed 22 May 2008
