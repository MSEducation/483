[[Who Killed Sgt. Pepper?]]

CATEGORIES: 2010 albums, The Brian Jonestown Massacre albums

Who Killed Sgt. Pepper? is the eleventh studio album by The Brian Jonestown Massacre. It was  
released in February 2010[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.snobsmusic.net/2010/01/brian-jonestown-massacre-ask-who-killed.html|title="Brian Jonestown Massacre ask "Who Killed Sgt Pepper?" on new album"|publisher =snobsmusic.net|accessdate=2010-01-09[/tpl] on band leader Anton Newcombe's A Records.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.brianjonestownmassacre.com/bandinfo.html|title=The Brian Jonestown Massacre official website|publisher =The Brian Jonestown Massacre|accessdate=2008-01-04 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071022153929/http://www.brianjonestownmassacre.com/bandinfo.html  |archivedate = 2007-10-22[/tpl] The album guest features, among a variety of international musicians, Spacemen 3 bassist Will Carruthers and vocalists Unnur Andrea Einarsdottir and Felix Bondareff.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.antimusic.com/news/10/jan/08The_Brian_Jonstown_Massace_Asks_Who_Killed_Sgt._Pepper.shtml|title="Brian Jonestown Massacre ask "Who Killed Sgt Pepper?" on new album"|publisher =antimusic.com|accessdate=2010-01-09[/tpl] The album was available for free streaming prior to release, and was recorded in Iceland and Berlin. Like My Bloody Underground, music videos were made for each of the album's songs. The album's title refers to The Beatles' 1967 album Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band. It may also refer to Who Killed Bambi?, an early working title for The Sex Pistols' film The Great Rock 'N' Roll Swindle, as well as a song on the film's soundtrack.
