[[Anne Fairbairn]]

CATEGORIES: 1948 births, Living people, Australian poets, Australian journalists, Members of the Order of Australia, Arab culture

Anne Fairbairn, (B´ody, née Reid, born 1948) Member of the Order of Australiahttp://blackpepperpublishing.com/fairbairn.html is a widely published Australian poet,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.manningclark.org.au/html/Paper-Fairbairn_Anne-Australian_poet_and%20bridge-builder_glimpses_behind_the_anger.html|title=Australian poet and bridge-builder: glimpses behind the anger|publisher=Manning Clark House|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl] journalist[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.arabworldbooks.com/Literature/PoetryEvening/ann.html|title=Poetry by Anne Fairbairn |publisher=Arab World Books|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl] and expert in Arab culture.[tpl]cite web|author=John Huxley |url=http://www.brisbanetimes.com.au/national/unreid-it-and-weep-taking-a-pms-name-20090821-etsp.html |title=George Houston Reid|publisher=Brisbane Times|date=22 August 2009|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl] She is the only granddaughter of Australia's fourth Prime Minister, George Reid.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/news/technology/nigerian-scam-shock/2008/08/20/1218911772460.html?page=2|title=Scammers defraud Aussies|publisher=SMH|date=20 August 2008|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl]
In 1965, she married Geoffrey Forrester Fairbairn,[tpl]cite web|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.cat-vn1867829|title=Biographical cuttings on Anne Fairbairn|publisher=National Library of Australia|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl] a Professor in the Department of History at the Australian National University. Geoffrey died in London of lung cancer on 11 September 1980.[tpl]cite web|url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.ms-ms9326|title=Papers of Geoffrey Fairbairn (1924 - 1980)|publisher=National Library of Australia|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl] Fairbairn has been known for her work in bringing together Australian and Arab cultures for over 30 years through poetry.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.austlit.edu.au/run?ex=ShowAgent&agentId=A)%2BD|title=AustLit Agent: Fairbairn, Anne |publisher=Austlit|date=3 September 2007|accessdate=16 October 2012[/tpl]
In 1995, she was awarded the Banjo Paterson Writing Award for Open Poetry.  This was followed by the Order of Australia in 1998 for services to literature and international relations between Australia and the Middle East.
