[[2010 Eureka earthquake]]

CATEGORIES: 2010 earthquakes, 2010 natural disasters in the United States, Earthquakes in California, History of Humboldt County, California

The 2010 Eureka earthquake occurred on January 9, 2010 at 4:27:38 pm PST offshore of Humboldt County, California, United States. ==Impact==

Structural damage was inflicted among older Victorian houses, power was severed for several hours, and windows were shattered. In addition, 28,000 customers of Pacific Gas and Electric Co., mostly those from Humboldt County, were left without electricity and phone services as a result.
In Eureka, the Old Town Bar and Grill building was previously believed to be severely damaged beyond repair and ordered demolished by the city, until a developer purchased and renovated it in 2011.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.times-standard.com/localnews/ci_17049428|title= Saving a piece of history: Eureka's Old Town Bar and Grill building expected to be completed by end of the year|first=Donna|last=Tam|work=The Times-Standard|date=January 9, 2011| accessdate= 9 December 2011 [/tpl] The town's high school, known as Eureka High School, and the Bayshore Mall were damaged and briefly closed, though both were later reopened with close to full services. An auditorium at Eureka High remained closed over concerns regarding its structural safety as of June 15, 2010.[tpl]cite news|title=Gov. declares state of emergency for North Coast quake|url=http://www.times-standard.com/localnews/ci_14178642|author=John Driscoll|date=January 13, 2010|newspaper=Times-Standard|accessdate=April 15, 2012[/tpl] A total of 463 buildings sustained damage as a result of the earthquake, leaving roughly $43 million in losses.[tpl]cite web|title=January 2010 Monthly Cat Recap |author=Impact Forecasting |publisher=Aon Benfield |date=January 2010 |url=http://www.aon.com/attachments/reinsurance/201002_ab_if_monthly_cat_report_Jan_2010.pdf| accessdate= 21 March 2010 [/tpl]
