[[1959 Kamchatka earthquake]]

CATEGORIES: 1959 earthquakes, Megathrust earthquakes in the Soviet Union, 1959 in the Soviet Union, Natural disasters in the Russian Far East

The 1959 Kamchatka earthquake occurred on May 4 at [tpl]Tooltip|19:15 p.m.|07:15 UTC[/tpl] with a moment magnitude of 8.0, and a surface wave magnitude of 8.2. The epicenter was near the Kamchatka Peninsula, Russia, USSR. Building damage was reported in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky.http://www.kscnet.ru/kraesc/2005/2005_6/ann17.htmlhttp://nsidc.org/rocs/archives-catalog/index.php?p=digitallibrary/getfile&id=254 The maximum intensity was VIII (Damaging) on the Medvedev–Sponheuer–Karnik scale.http://www.eeri.org/lfe/pdf/russian_federation_small_concrete_block.pdf The intensity in Petropavlovsk-Kamchatsky was about VIII MSK. 
The earthquake triggered a tsunami with [tpl]convert|.2|m|sp=us[/tpl] of runup that was recorded in Massacre Bay, Alaska, in the United States.The great Alaska earthquake of 1964, Vol. 5 by National Research Council (U.S.).
