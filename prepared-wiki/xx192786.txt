[[Shire of Leigh]]

CATEGORIES: Former local government areas of Victoria (Australia)

The Shire of Leigh was a local government area about [tpl]convert|140|km|mi|0[/tpl] west of Melbourne, the state capital of Victoria, Australia. The shire covered an area of [tpl]convert|982|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and existed from 1861 until 1994.

==History==

Leigh was incorporated as a road district on 27 August 1861, and became a shire on 22 March 1864.[tpl]cite book|title=Victorian Municipal Directory|year=1992|publisher=Arnall & Jackson|location=Brunswick|pages=736â€“737[/tpl] Accessed at State Library of Victoria, La Trobe Reading Room.
On 6 May 1994, the Shire was abolished, and merged with Shire of Grenville, Shire of Bannockburn and part of Buninyong into the Golden Plains Shire.
