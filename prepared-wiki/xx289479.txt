[[Ludwig Julius Caspar Mende]]

CATEGORIES: 1779 births, 1832 deaths, People from Greifswald, University of Greifswald faculty, University of Göttingen faculty, German obstetricians, German gynaecologists, Forensic scientists

Ludwig Julius Caspar Mende (14 September 1779, Greifswald – 23 April 1832, Göttingen) was a German obstetrician and gynecologist.
After graduation from the University of Göttingen (1801), he began work as a lecturer at the University of Greifswald. In 1814 he became an associate professor at Greifswald, followed by a full professorship during the following year. In 1823 he was appointed professor of forensic medicine and obstetrics at Göttingen, where until his death in 1832, he served as director of the department of obstetrics and gynecology.ADB: Mende, Ludwig Julius Caspar Allgemeine Deutsche Biographie @ Allgemeine Deutsche Biographie
At Göttingen, he was involved in scientific research of OB/GYN issues that included uterine retroflexion, genital prolapse, cervical cancer and postpartum hemorrhage. As an administrator, he gained a reputation for providing a regimen of high quality training in the field of midwifery.Department of Obstetrics and Gynaecology Georg-August-Universität Göttingen History of the University Hospital of GöttingenDeutsche Biographie
