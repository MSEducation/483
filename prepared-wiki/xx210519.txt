[[Zmitser Dashkevich]]

CATEGORIES: 1981 births, Amnesty International prisoners of conscience, Belarusian politicians, Belarusian prisoners and detainees, Belarusian Protestants, Living people, People from Yelsk Raion

Zmitser Dashkevich ([tpl]lang-be|Зміцер Дашкевіч[/tpl]); born July 20, 1981 in Yelsk Raion, Homiel Voblast) is a Belarusian politician and one of the leaders of the unregistered youth opposition movement Young Front.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/new-probe-launched-against-belarus-oppositionist-dashkevich/24648969.html |title=New Probe Launched Against Belarusian 'Prisoner Of Conscience' |date=18 July 2012 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |accessdate=20 July 2012|archivedate=31 October 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6BotjGXEv[/tpl]
In November 2006, Dashkevich was found guilty of "illegal political activity" by a Belarusian court and sentenced to 18 months in prison. Amnesty international (AI) declared him a prisoner of conscience, and the U.S. government condemned the verdict.[tpl]cite news |agency=Associated Press |url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/02/04/AR2007020401452_pf.html |title=Belarus Youth Group Members Detained |date=4 February 2007 |work=The Washington Post |accessdate=20 July 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6Botgh2ro|archivedate=31 October 2012[/tpl]
In March 2011, he and fellow Young Front activist Eduard Lobau were found guilty of assault one day in advance of a disputed presidential election. The Young Front activists stated that the case against them was politically motivated.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/belarus_opposition_activists_jalied_for_protest/2347885.html |title=Belarusian Activists Jailed For Holding Rally For Colleagues |date=23 March 2011 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |accessdate=20 July 2012|archivedate=October 31, 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6Botd0tyM[/tpl] Dashkevich was sentenced to two years in prison, and Lobau to four. AI again named the pair prisoners of conscience. 
According to AI, Dashkevich was offered a presidential pardon in September 2011 if he would confess his guilt, but he refused.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.amnesty.org/en/library/asset/EUR49/011/2012/en/0bf16ad9-5f56-4043-ad8b-9ebe54505091/eur490112012en.html |title=Youth leader faces longer prison sentence |date=17 July 2012 |publisher=Amnesty International |accessdate=20 July 2012|archivedate=31 October 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6Bou7eScE[/tpl] On 18 July 2012, Belarusian authorities announced that a new investigation had been opened against Dashkevich for his "systematic and aggressive refusal to follow the instructions of the penitentiary administration." On 28 August 2012, Dashkevich was found guilty in an in camera trial, and another year was added to his prison sentence.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/belarusian-orisoner-of-conscience-gets-extra-year-in-jail-dashkevich/24690960.html |title=Belarusian 'Prisoner Of Conscience' Gets Additional Year In Jail |date=28 August 2012 |archivedate=28 August 2012 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6AGF8KWKn |accessdate=28 August 2012[/tpl] On 30 October, a court ordered Dashkevich's transfer to a maximum security prison. Human Rights Watch reported that he "was being subjected to routine prison abuse, including verbal abuse, arbitrary punishments, and threats of torture, rape, and murder".[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/belarus-political-prisoner-dashkevich-sent-to-maximum-security-jail/24755730.html |title=Belarusian Political Prisoner Dashkevich Sent To Maximum-Security Jail |author=Claire Bigg |date=30 October 2012 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |archivedate=30 October 2012 |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6BotYp4s1 |accessdate=30 October 2012[/tpl]
As of September 2011, Dashkevich was engaged to another Young Front activist, Nasta Palazhanka.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/conditional_release_demanded_for_jailed_belarusian_activist/24326130.html |title='Conditional Release' Demanded For Jailed Belarusian Activist |date=12 September 2011 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |archivedate=31 October 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6BotnybOT |accessdate=20 July 2012[/tpl] The two married when Palazhanka visited him in Hrodno prison on 26 December 2012. Authorities stated that the couple would be allowed one more two-hour visit before Dashkevich's release, scheduled for August 2013.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rferl.org/content/belarusian-opposition-activist-marries-in-prison/24811094.html |title=Belarusian Opposition Activist Ties The Knot In Prison |author=Claire Bigg |date=28 December 2012 |publisher=Radio Free Europe/Radio Liberty |accessdate=2 January 2013 |archivedate=2 January 2013 |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6DNJnHxL1 |deadurl=no[/tpl]
