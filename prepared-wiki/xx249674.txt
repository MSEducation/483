[[Mastamho]]

CATEGORIES: Native American mythology of California, Deities of the indigenous peoples of North America, Mohave tribe

Mastamho, sometimes also referred to as Mustamho, is the creator deity of the first Mohave people along the Colorado River in the Mojave Desert and Colorado Desert.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pbs.org/weekendexplorer/destinations/nevada/laughlin/petroglyphs.htm|title=PBS:  Gain insight into some of the Laughlin areaâ€™s many petroglyphs|work=Laughlin|publisher=PBS|accessdate=9 January 2010[/tpl] Mastamho is the grandson of the Earth Mother (in South America referred to as Pachamama) and the Sky father.  
He was the son of Matavilya and had a sister, Frog Woman, and a brother, Kaatar. Mastamho is pictured on the Blythe Intaglios with stretched arms,[tpl]cite web|url=http://photography.nationalgeographic.com/photography/enlarge/mojave-desert-mastamho_pod_image.html|title=Mojave Desert Mastamho, California, 1998|last=Steinmetz|first=George|date=January 1998|work="Making Sense of the Millennium" |publisher=National Geographic magazine|accessdate=9 January 2010[/tpl] which indicates that he protects his people from destruction.[tpl]Citation needed|date=January 2010[/tpl]
