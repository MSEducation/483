[[Vassieux-en-Vercors]]

CATEGORIES: Vassieux-en-Vercors, Communes of Drôme, Companions of the Liberation, World War II resistance movements

Vassieux-en-Vercors is a commune in the department of Drôme in southeastern France.
The town is known for its assistance to the French Resistance during World War II, for which it was awarded Ordre de la Libération.

==Geography==

The village is built on a polje of limestone and dominated by a vast Karst landscape.[tpl]Fr icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web| url=http://www.resistance-drome.org/uk/atlas.php?e=aff&id=19| title="Cartographie de Vassieux-en-Vercors"| author=Centre Historique de la Résistance en Drôme| accessdate=2008-01-01[/tpl] This polje lies on a syncline generally known as the "Plateau de Vassieux".
The Plateau de Vassieux is the site of numerous other karst phenomena, such as limestone pavements and caves, which are studied and explored by local speleology groups.

==History==

Vassieux-en-Vercors is home to several prehistoric archaeological sites, and has a Museum of Prehistory which was created in May 1970 after the discovery of a flintknapping workshop.[tpl]Fr icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web| url=http://www.prehistoire-vercors.fr/index2.html| title="Le Musée de la Préhistoire de Vassieux en Vercors"| author=prehistoire-vercors.fr| accessdate=2008-01-01 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071215230242/http://www.prehistoire-vercors.fr/index2.html  |archivedate = 2007-12-15[/tpl]
For its great acts of resistance during the German occupation of France, the town was awarded the Ordre de la Libération. A memorial was built at the Col de la Chau [tpl]coord|44.90760|N|5.36124|E|display=inline[/tpl] to remember the grim events of the Second World War, and includes a cemetery where the victims of a July 1944 German massacre are buried.
