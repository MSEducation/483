[[Tyler Farrar]]

Tyler Farrar (born June 2, 1984) is an American professional road racing cyclist who rides with UCI ProTeam [tpl]ct|GRS[/tpl].[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.slipstreamsports.com/2012/12/28/team-garmin-sharp-barracuda-unveils-2013-roster|title=Team Garmin-Sharp-Barracuda Unveils 2013 Roster|date=December 28, 2012|accessdate=January 6, 2013|work=[tpl]ct|GRS|2012b[/tpl]|publisher=Slipstream Sports LLC|location=Boulder, Colorado[/tpl] Farrar's major career achievements include winning the Circuit Franco-Belge in 2009, and the one-day race Vattenfall Cyclassics in both the 2009 and the 2010 editions. In Grand Tours, Farrar won six individual stages, as well as assisting in two team time trial wins.

==Cycling career==

Farrar started racing at 13, and rode for [tpl]ct|JBC|2003[/tpl] in 2003, [tpl]ct|OCM|2004[/tpl] in 2004, and [tpl]ct|COF|2006[/tpl] in 2006 and 2007. In April 2006, he crashed near the finish of the Circuit de la Sarthe, which resulted in a broken collarbone, causing him to miss most of the season. For the 2008 season, he transferred to [tpl]ct|GRM|2008a[/tpl].
He has won stages in the Giro d'Italia,Giro d'Italia 2010: Tyler Farrar clinches stage as world champion Cadel Evans takes lead Telegraph, May 9, 2010 Vuelta a España,  Tour de France, Three Days of De Panne, and the USA Pro Cycling Challenge. He wore the yellow jersey on stage three of the 2008 Tour of California after winning intermediate sprint points during stage two.
Farrar is very popular in Flanders, because he is fluent in Dutch, and lives in the Belgian city of Ghent. In 2012, was made an honorary citizen of Ghent.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.bikeworldnews.com/2012/02/22/tyler-farrar-honored-city-ghent-belga/ |title=Tyler Farrar honored by the city of Ghent (Belga)|date=February 22, 2012[/tpl]
In May 2011, Farrar, together with the entire [tpl]ct|LEO|2011[/tpl] team, withdrew from the Giro d'Italia as a mark of respect for his friend and training partner Wouter Weylandt, who was killed in an accident during the race.[tpl]cite web| url=http://velonews.competitor.com/2011/05/news/giro-honors-weylandt-with-neutral-stage-peloton-vows-to-race-wednesday_172304 |title=Giro honors Weylandt with neutral stage, peloton vows to race Wednesday[/tpl]
On July 4, 2011, Farrar won his first Tour de France stage, Stage 3 from Olonne-sur-Mer to Redon, becoming the first American to win a stage of the Tour on the Fourth of July.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/sport/2011/jul/04/tour-de-france-2011-tourdefrance|title=Tyler Farrar sprints to victory on third stage|date=July 4, 2011|work=Guardian|accessdate=July 4, 2011[/tpl]
Farrar crashed four times in the 2012 Tour de France,[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.cyclingweekly.co.uk/news/tour-de-france/533760/fourth-tour-crash-for-farrar.html|title=Fourth Tour Crash for Farrar|publisher=Cycling Weekly, July 6, 2012[/tpl] including a sprint-finish crash, after which he stormed the [tpl]ct|ARG|2012b[/tpl] team bus to confront Tom Veelers, whom he blamed for the incident. At the 2012 USA Pro Cycling Challenge, Farrar took his first two wins of the season. In the opening stage, Farrar stated that he was surprised to have survived the mountainous terrain to be able to contend for the sprint. Arriving in Telluride with a field of 57 riders, he was the fastest man to the line.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.cyclingnews.com/races/usa-pro-cycling-challenge-2012/stage-1/results|title=Farrar wins opening stage in Telluride|work=Cycling News|publisher=Future Publishing Limited|date=August 21, 2012|accessdate=August 26, 2012|author=Peter Hymas[/tpl] He won again in the fifth stage of the race, dedicating the win to his former Madison partner Mike Creed, who had been raising money for the victims of the Waldo Canyon fires.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.cyclingnews.com/races/usa-pro-cycling-challenge-2012/stage-5/results|title=Farrar sprints to stage 5 victory in Colorado|work=Cycling News|publisher=Future Publishing Limited|date=August 25, 2012|accessdate=August 26, 2012|author=Pat Malach[/tpl] Due to his two wins he consolidated enough points to win the green jersey. Later in the season, Farrar crashed heavily during the first stage of the Tour of Britain, reportedly suffering a concussion.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.velonation.com/News/ID/12839/Farrars-worlds-preparation-affected-by-Tour-of-Britain-concussion.aspx|title=Farrar’s worlds’ preparation affected by Tour of Britain concussion|work=VeloNation|publisher=VeloNation LLC|date=September 10|accessdate=October 17, 2012[/tpl] In October, Farrar was given clearance to train by the team doctor, Prentice Steffen, who stated: "The health of the athlete is always our top priority."[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.velonation.com/News/ID/13085/Farrar-fully-recovered-from-concussion-and-given-green-light-to-resume-training.aspx|title=Farrar fully recovered from concussion and given green light to resume training|work=VeloNation|publisher=VeloNation LLC|date=October 17, 2012|accessdate=October 17, 2012|author=Shane Stokes[/tpl]
