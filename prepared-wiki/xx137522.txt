[[Harbor Freeway (Los Angeles Metro station)]]

CATEGORIES: Los Angeles County Metro Rail stations, Los Angeles County Metro Transitway stations, South Los Angeles

Harbor Freeway (formerly Harbor Freeway/I-105) is a transport hub located on the Harbor Transitway, within the Judge Harry Pregerson Interchange of Interstates I-105 and I-110 near Figueroa Street in the neighborhood of South Los Angeles. The station serves the Metro Green Line (light rail) and the Metro Silver Line (rapid bus transit) and other bus services. It is owned by the Los Angeles County Metropolitan Transportation Authority.

==Station layout==

The station has 3 levels: the upper level provides access to the Metro Green Line (light rail); the middle level has the connections to local bus services and the 253 space park and ride lot; the lower level provides access to the Metro Silver Line (bus rapid transit) and other express buses operating on the Harbor Transitway. It is located near the Athens district of Los Angeles.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.metro.net/around/rail/green-line/|title=Green Line station information|accessdate=2010-09-25[/tpl]

===Station improvements===

Harbor Transitway Park & Ride and transit station improvements. Enhance signage, lighting and security at Park & Ride lots were completed in June 2011. Install CCTV at Harbor Transitway stations by June 2012.

==Services==

Green Line service hours are approximately from 5:00 AM until 12:45 AM daily.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metro.net/riding_metro/bus_overview/images/803.pdf|title=Green Line timetable|accessdate=2010-09-25[/tpl] Metro Silver Line service hours are approximately from 4:15 AM until 1:45 AM Monday- Friday & 5:00 A.M.- 1:45 A.M. Saturdays/ Sundays & Holidays.
- These buses stop at the same boarding platforms of the Metro Silver Line at the freeway level.
Non-Harbor Transitway buses stop on the west side of Figueroa Street. 

==Incidents at Station==

On February 22, 2012 an incident occurred on the northbound Metro Silver Line platform during the afternoon. A vehicle struck the northbound platform of the Silver Line. The Metro Silver Line bus was not hit by the private truck when it was entering the station.There were 7 passengers who were about aboard the Silver Line bus to Downtown LA as a vehicle struck the platform.[tpl]cite web|url=http://thesource.metro.net/2012/02/22/accident-on-harbor-transitway/?utm_source%3Drss%26utm_medium%3Drss%26utm_campaign%3Daccident-on-harbor-transitway|title=The Source: Accident on The Harbor Transitway|date=2012-02-22[/tpl] The 7 passengers received critical and serious injuries.[tpl]cite web|url=http://losangeles.cbslocal.com/2012/02/22/7-injured-when-vehicle-slams-into-crowd-waiting-for-bus/y|title=7 Injured When Vehicle Slams Into Crowd Waiting For Bus|date=2012-02-22[/tpl] It took around 2 hours to clear up the scene. During the incident, the Metro Silver Line, Metro Express Lines: 450X and 550 were detoured to stop at Figueroa Street/Harbor FWY station entrance. There has never been an incident on the Harbor Transitway ever since it first opened on June 1998. As a result of the incident, Metro's CEO: Art Leahy asked Metro's safety committee to review the station layout and signage of the Silver Line stations on the Harbor Transitway portion. A report was scheduled within 60 days after the incident.[tpl]cite web|url=http://thesource.metro.net/2012/02/22/accident-on-harbor-transitway/?utm_source%3Drss%26utm_medium%3Drss%26utm_campaign%3Daccident-on-harbor-transitway|title=The Source: Accident on The Harbor Transitway|date=2012-02-22[/tpl] The report was complete during April 2012 .[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metro.net/board/Items/2012/03_March/20120322RBMItem4Handout.pdf|title=Harbor Freeway Metro Silver Line Station platform improvements|date=2012-04-20[/tpl]. Bollards were added during early August 2012 at the station. Bollards were also installed at the 37th Street/USC Metro Silver Line Station as well.
