[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metre freestyle]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The men's 200 metre freestyle event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 10–12 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl] 
After winning a bronze in Athens four years earlier, Michael Phelps blasted a new world record of 1:42.96 to claim his third straight gold, ninth career, and eleventh overall medal at the Olympics.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Michael Phelps Sets World Record in 200 Free Win|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18771.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Phelps breaks 200 free world record by nearly a second|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/oly/summer08/swimming/news/story?id=3530615|publisher=ESPN|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl] South Korea's Park Tae-Hwan added a silver to his collection, following an unprecedented triumph in the 400 m freestyle two days earlier. He established a new Asian record of 1:44.85 to clear a 1:45 barrier, and used a final-lap split of 26.17 to edge out Phelps' teammate Peter Vanderkaay, who took home a bronze medal in a personal best of 1:45.14.[tpl]cite news|last=Coffey|first=Wayne|title=Michael Phelps wins third gold medal, sets record in 200-meter freestyle|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/sports/michael-phelps-wins-gold-medal-sets-record-200-meter-freestyle-article-1.316127|publisher=New York Daily News|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl]
South Africa's Jean Basson finished fourth with a time of 1:45.97, and was followed in the fifth spot by Germany's Paul Biedermann in 1:46.00. Switzerland's Dominik Meichtry, who raced to a top seed in the prelims, earned a sixth spot in 1:46.95, while Japan's Yoshihiro Okumura (1:47.14) and Great Britain's Robbie Renwick (1:47.47) rounded out the finale.[tpl]cite news|last=Ross|first=Jack|title=Dominik Meichtry Leaves Beijing Having Accomplished The Rarest of Feats: He Beat Michael Phelps-Sort Of|url=http://archive.dailycal.org/article/102353/|publisher=The Daily Californian|date=26 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl] Renwick missed out the semifinals by 0.01 of a second from the prelims, but was offered a second shot, following a sudden withdrawal of two swimmers. Canada's Brent Hayden and France's Amaury Leveaux later scratched the event to focus on their duty in the 4×100 m freestyle relay.[tpl]cite news|title=Rebecca Adlington wins 400 metres freestyle gold for Great Britain – Beijing Olympics 2008|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/olympics/2537843/Rebecca-Adlington-wins-400-metres-freestyle-gold-for-Great-Britain-Olympics.html|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl]
