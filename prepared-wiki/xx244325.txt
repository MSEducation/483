[[1970–71 All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship]]

CATEGORIES: 1970 in hurling, 1971 in hurling

The 1970–71 All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship  was the inaugural series of the All-Ireland Senior Club Hurling Championship, an inter-county knock-out competition for Ireland's top championship clubs.  The championship was won by Roscrea who beat St. Ryangh's by 4–5 to 2–5 in the final.

==Format==

The 1970–71 club championship was played on a straight knock-out basis.  Each of the participating counties entered their respective club champions.  The format of the competition was as follows:
Provincial Championships
The Leinster, Connacht, Munster and Ulster championships were played on a straight knock-out basis. The four respective champions from these provinces advanced directly to the All-Ireland semi-finals.
All-Ireland Series
Semi-finals: (2 matches) The Munster champions played the Ulster champions while the Leinster champions played the Connacht champions.  The winners of these two games contested the All-Ireland club final.
