[[Hugo Hirst, 1st Baron Hirst]]

Hugo Hirst, 1st Baron Hirst (26 November 1863 â€“ 22 January 1943), known as Sir Hugo Hirst, Bt, between 1925 and 1934, was a German-born British industrialist.
Born near Munich, Hugo Hirsch became a naturalized British subject in 1883 and changed his surname to Hirst.Werner Eugen Mosse, Julius Carlebach, Second chance: two centuries of German-speaking Jews in the United Kingdom Mohr Siebeck, 1991 ISBN 3-16-145741-2  page 187 
He was co-founder with Gustav Binswanger of the General Electric Company plc, and in 1910 became its chairman.GEC History 
He was created a baronet, of Witton in the County of Warwick, on 2 July 1925[tpl]London Gazette |issue=33063 |date=3 July 1925 |startpage=4449[/tpl] and elevated to the peerage as Baron Hirst, of Witton in the County of Warwick, on 28 June 1934.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=34066 |date=3 July 1934 |startpage=4222[/tpl]
As both his son and his grandson died before Hirst, his baronetcy and peerage both became extinct on his death in 1943.
