[[Gary Griffin (musician)]]

CATEGORIES: American musicians, Miami University alumni, People from Cincinnati, Ohio, 1951 births, Living people

Gary Griffin (born October 26, 1951 in Cincinnati, Ohio) is an American musician, best known for performing as a  keyboardist and vocalist with The Beach Boys, Brian Wilson, Jan and Dean and the Surf City Allstars.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goldminemag.com/article/the-10-albums-that-changed-surf-musician-gary-griffins-life#sthash.fcbvffen.dpuf |title=The 10 albums that changed surf musician Gary Griffin's life |publisher=Goldminemag.com |date=2013-08-08 |accessdate=2013-10-31[/tpl]
Griffin grew up in Cincinnati and attended Miami University and the University of Cincinnati College Conservatory of Music, where he majored in piano and music theory.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.enquirer.com/columns/kiese/2000/02/27/jki_beach_boys_actors.html |title='Beach Boys' actors look authentic thanks to Bridgetown native |publisher=Enquirer.com |date=2000-02-27 |accessdate=2013-10-31[/tpl]
In 1977, Griffin moved to Los Angeles where he was hired as an organist by The Beach Boys, joining them for the recording of their Warner Bros. album MIU. 
Griffin toured and recorded with Jan and Dean throughout the 80's and 90’s, and has appeared on several television shows, most notably General Hospital and Full House.   In 2000, Griffin served as music director for the Emmy-nominated television mini-series, The Beach Boys, An American Family, for ABC. He also appeared in two roles in the film, which was produced by John Stamos. 
Griffin has produced an eclectic roster of artists, most notably Micky Dolenz of The Monkees and political satirist Harry Shearer.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goldminemag.com/article/micky-dolenz-channels-inner-crooner-with-king-for-day |title=Micky Dolenz channels inner crooner with ‘King For A Day’ |publisher=Goldminemag.com |date=2010-10-01 |accessdate=2013-10-31[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jambands.com/news/2012/07/10/dr-john-members-of-beach-boys-on-new-harry-shearer-album#.UgpsX9K1Hng |title=Need We Say More? > News > Dr. John, Members of Beach Boys on New Harry Shearer Album |publisher=Jambands.com |date=2012-07-10 |accessdate=2013-10-31[/tpl] He also co-produced the music for the PBS television series, Edens: Lost and Found, and And Thou Shalt Honor. In 2008, Griffin was featured prominently in the best-selling book, When We Get To Surf City, by Bob Greene, an account of life on the road with the Jan and Dean band in the 90’s.[tpl]Cite book | last =Greene | first = Bob| year = 2008 | title = When We Get to Surf City | publisher = John Deadline Enterprises, Inc. | location = New York, NY [/tpl]
He has continued to work with various members of The Beach Boys, most recently as a keyboardist and engineer for their 50th anniversary CD, That’s Why God Made the Radio.   Additionally, Griffin performs with The Surf City Allstars.
In July, 2013, Griffin appeared with John Stamos on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon at the host’s request.  He performed as part of a reunion of Jesse and the Rippers, the fictitious band from Full House.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.latenightwithjimmyfallon.com/video/jesse-and-the-rippers-reunite/n39152/ |title=Jesse & The Rippers Reunite | Video | Late Night | NBC |publisher=Latenightwithjimmyfallon.com |date= |accessdate=2013-10-31[/tpl]
