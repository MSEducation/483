[[Kasado Station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Mie Prefecture

[tpl]nihongo|Kasado Station|加佐登駅|Kasado-eki[/tpl] is a railway station in Suzuka, Mie Prefecture, Japan, on the Kansai Main Line. It is located 50.9 rail kilometers from Nagoya Station, and is operated by Central Japan Railway Company (JR Central).

==Station history==

Kasado Station was opened on February 6, 1892 as [tpl]nihongo|Takamiya Station|高宮駅|Takamiya-eki[/tpl] on the Kansai Railway, when the section of the Kansai Main Line connecting Yokkaichi with Suzuka was completed. It was renamed to its present name of February 1, 1902. The Kansai Railway was nationalized on October 1, 1907 becoming part of the Japanese Government Railways (JGR). The JGR became the Japan National Railways (JNR) after World War II. The station was absorbed into the JR Central network upon the privatization of the JNR on April 1, 1987. The station has been unattended since October 1, 2012.
