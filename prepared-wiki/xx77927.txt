[[Allan R. Bomhard]]

CATEGORIES: 1943 births, Living people, American linguists, Fairleigh Dickinson University alumni, Hunter College alumni, United States Army soldiers

Allan R. Bomhard (born 1943 in Brooklyn, New York) is an American linguist.[tpl]cite book|last=Rasmussen|first=Jens Elmegård|title=Selected papers on Indo-European linguistics: with a section on comparative Eskimo linguistics|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=--ssbDUSJfIC&pg=PA239|accessdate=4 April 2011|year=1999|publisher=Museum Tusculanum Press|isbn=978-87-7289-529-1|page=239[/tpl]
He was educated at Fairleigh Dickinson University, Hunter College, and the City University of New York, and served in the U.S. Army from 1964—1966. He currently resides in Charleston, SC. He has studied the controversial hypotheses about the underlying unity among the proposed Nostratic and Eurasiatic language families.
