[[Albéniz Foundation]]

CATEGORIES: Classical music in Spain, Albéniz Foundation

The Albeniz Foundation (Fundación Albeniz in Spanish), is a private non-profit organization promoting classical music in Spain[tpl]Cite web| title=Fundacion Albeniz| url=http://www.munimadrid.es/portales/munimadrid/es/Inicio/Eventos/Fundacion-Albeniz?vgnextfmt=default&vgnextoid=286da6eb4db38110VgnVCM1000000b205a0aRCRD&vgnextchannel=27dfb4f19cce3210VgnVCM10000026205a0aRCRD| publisher=Ayuntamiento de Madrid | accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] through the Queen Sofía College of Music,[tpl]Cite web| title=The Albéniz Foundation and BBVA present the inaugural concert for the new academic year of the Queen Sofía School of Music| url=http://en.sourcews.com/albeniz-foundation-bbva-present| accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] the Paloma O'Shea International Piano Competition, the Isaac Albéniz library and research centre, the International Institute of Chamber Music in Madrid.  The foundation is named after the Spanish composer and pianist Isaac Albéniz because he represents the renaissance of Spanish music at the beginning of the 20th century in Europe.[tpl]Cite web| title=Presentacion| url=http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_Presentacion.aspx| publisher=Fundación Albeniz| accessdate=10 January 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100129173951/http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_Presentacion.aspx| archivedate= 29 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl] The foundation is based in Madrid[tpl]Cite web| author=Rodriguez Emma| title=La Fundación Albéniz estrena nueva sede| url=http://www.elmundo.es/papel/2008/09/18/cultura/2498426.html| work=El Mundo | accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] and has a permanent office in Santander.
The Albeniz Foundation was founded  in 1972 by Paloma O'Shea, who is the current president of the Foundation. The Honorary President is the Infanta Margarita and the Honorary Vicepresident was Alicia de Larrocha.[tpl]Cite web| title=Trustees| url=http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_Patronato_Fundacion.aspx| publisher=Fundación Albeniz | accessdate=10 January 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100131043211/http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_Patronato_Fundacion.aspx| archivedate= 31 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl] Plácido Domingo, Montserrat Caballé, Zubin Mehta, Joaquín Soriano  and several members of the Botín family are members of the board of trustees.

==Activities of the Foundation==

===Queen Sofía College of Music===

The Queen Sofía College of Music is a private music conservatoire in Madrid.

===Isaac Albéniz library and research centre===

The centre houses the Isaac Albéniz and Federico Mompou[tpl]Cite web| title=Patrimonio cultural de la Fundación Albeniz: Rubinstein (in Spanish)| url=http://www.cervantesvirtual.com/portal/albeniz/fondos.jsp| publisher=Instituto Cervante | accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] archives and the Artur Rubinstein archive (donated in 1982 by Nela Rubinstein).[tpl]Cite web| title=Patrimonio cultural de la Fundación Albeniz (in Spanish)| url=http://www.cervantesvirtual.com/portal/albeniz/fondo.jsp?nombre=rubinstein| publisher=Instituto Cervante | accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl]

===Paloma O'Shea International Piano Competition===

The Paloma O'Shea International Piano Competition, formerly known as Concurso de Piano de Santander, is a major music competition happening in Santander every three years.

===Yehudi Menuhin Prize===

Created in 1990, the Yehudi Menuhin Prize, is an award given by the Queen Sofía College of Music to recognize and reward artists of excellence, who have contributed a great deal to the teaching of music.[tpl]Cite web| title=Yehudi Menuhin Prize| url=http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_YehudiMenuhin.aspx| publisher=Fundacion Albeniz | accessdate=10 January 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100131022534/http://www.fundacionalbeniz.com/F_YehudiMenuhin.aspx| archivedate= 31 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl] It has been given to Alfredo Kraus, Piero Farulli, Sir Colin Davis and Carlo Maria Giulini.
