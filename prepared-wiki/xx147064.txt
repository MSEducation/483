[[Audlem railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Former Great Western Railway stations, Railway stations opened in 1863, Railway stations closed in 1963, Disused railway stations in Cheshire

Audlem railway station was a station on the former Great Western Railway between Market Drayton and Nantwich, opened in 1863.
It served the village of Audlem in Cheshire, England until closure in 1963.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.audlem.org/content.php?ContID=132&SubCat=13|title=Audlem Village History website[/tpl] The station was immortalised in the song "Slow Train" by Flanders and Swann.

==History==

The station was built by the Nantwich and Market Drayton Railway (N&MDR) and opened on 20 October 1863,[tpl]harvnb|Butt|1995|p=21[/tpl] although the line was operated by the Great Western Railway from its opening, and the N&MDR eventually amalgamated with the Great Western Railway in 1897.[tpl]cite book |last=MacDermot |first=E.T. |title=History of the Great Western Railway, vol. II: 1863-1921 |year=1931 |publisher=Great Western Railway |location=Paddington |oclc=55853736 |page=7 |ref=harv [/tpl] The line passed on to the Western Region of British Railways on nationalisation in 1948, and was then closed to passengers by the British Railways Board on 9 September 1963.
