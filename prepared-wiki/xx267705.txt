[[Gayny, Perm Krai]]

CATEGORIES: Rural localities in Perm Krai, Cherdynsky Uyezd

Gayny ([tpl]lang-ru|Гайны[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-koi|Гайна[/tpl]) is a rural locality (a settlement) and the administrative center of Gaynsky District of Komi-Permyak Okrug in Perm Krai, Russia, located [tpl]convert|156|km|sp=us[/tpl] north from the town of Kudymkar on the right bank of the Kama River. Population: [tpl]ru-census|p2010=4,050|p2002=4,507|p1989=4,796[/tpl]
==History== 
It was first mentioned in 1579. This year is considered the official foundation of the settlement. It was granted urban-type settlement status on January 2, 1963, but was demoted back to a rural locality in 1998.
In 1926, it had 263 households and a population of 1,051 (including 1,008 Russians, 39 Komi-Permyaks, and 4 people of others ethnicities).
