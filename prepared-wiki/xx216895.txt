[[New Hall, Woodford]]

CATEGORIES: Houses completed in 1630, Houses in Greater Manchester, Grade II* listed buildings in Greater Manchester, Buildings and structures in Stockport

New Hall is a 17th-century cottage in Woodford, in the Metropolitan Borough of Stockport, Greater Manchester, England ([tpl]gbmapping|SJ893813[/tpl]). Above the door, there is a Tudor-arched lintel with the date '1630' along with the initials 'WDED' (William and Elizabeth Davenport) and the family shield.[tpl]cite web|title=New Hall|url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/details/default.aspx?id=440734 |publisher=Images of England|accessdate=2008-08-19[/tpl] The same Davenport family owned Bramall Hall in Bramhall, close by. The cottage, along with the adjoining farmhouse is a Grade II* listed building.
