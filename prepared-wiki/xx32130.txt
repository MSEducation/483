[[Neil Turner (British politician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1945 births, Living people, Labour Party (UK) MPs, Members of the United Kingdom Parliament for English constituencies, UK MPs 1997–2001, UK MPs 2001–05, UK MPs 2005–10, People educated at Carlisle Grammar School, People from Carlisle, Cumbria

Neil Turner (born 16 September 1945) is a British Labour Party politician and a former Member of Parliament (MP) for Wigan. He was elected in the 1999 by-election[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/uk_politics/440373.stm |title=Labour selects Wigan candidate |publisher=BBC News |date=7 September 1999[/tpl] and stood down at the 2010 general election.

==Early life==

He went to Carlisle Grammar School (became the Trinity School in 1968.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.parliamentaryrecord.com/content/profiles/mp/Neil-Turner/596 |title=WPR - Neil Turner (Ex-MP) |first= |last= |work=parliamentaryrecord.com |year=2013 |accessdate=28 November 2013[/tpl] He was a quantity surveyor for Fairclough Builders, which became AMEC, from 1967–94, then was Operations Director for North Shropshire District Council from 1995-7.

==Parliamentary career==

He represented the North West Region on the National Committee of the Labour Party Young Socialists in 1970, following Roger Stott. He contested the seat of Oswestry in 1970. He has been Parliamentary Private Secretary to Ian McCartney as:  Minister of State, Department for Work and Pensions 2001-3, Minister without Portfolio and Party Chair 2003-06 and Secretary of State for Trade and Industry 2006-.
When a dyslexic constituent, Stephen Halsall (a psychiatric nurse), sent him a letter in March 2001 complaining about a drug rehabilitation unit being built near to him, Turner returned the letter to the constituent with all the spelling and grammatical errors underlined and annotated in red ink, e,g we only have 1 Labour Party - should be Party's.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/uk/politics/mp-returned-a-dyslexics-letter--with-corrections-688442.html |title=MP returned a dyslexic's letter - with corrections |publisher=The Independent |date=2001-03-22 | location=London | first=Steve | last=Boggan | accessdate=20 May 2010[/tpl]
On 31 July 2009, Turner announced his decision to stand down at the 2010 general election.[tpl]cite web |url=http://blogs.manchestereveningnews.co.uk/politics/2009/07/post_753.html |title=Neil Turner stands down |publisher=Manchester Evening News |date=2009-07-31[/tpl]

==Personal life==

He married Susan Price on 26 March 1971 in Wigan and has one son. He follows Wigan Warriors.
