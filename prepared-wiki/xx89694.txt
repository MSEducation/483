The X-Files is an American science fiction television series that originally aired on the Fox network from September 10, 1993 to May 19, 2002.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tvguide.com/tvshows/the-x-files/episodes/100536 |title=The X-Files Episodes – The X-Files Full Episode Guides on FOX |work=TV Guide |accessdate=August 24, 2012[/tpl] The series aired for nine seasons and 202 episodes, centering on FBI special agents Fox Mulder (David Duchovny) and Dana Scully (Gillian Anderson), who work on cases linked to the paranormal, called X-Files. Mulder is a believer in the paranormal, and the skeptical Scully is assigned to make scientific analyses of Mulder's discoveries which could ultimately be used to discredit his work. Throughout the series the two develop a close friendship. Beginning with the eighth season, Duchovny's role was reduced from lead actor to an intermittent lead role.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/1337685.stm |title=Duchovny quits X-Files |work=BBC News|publisher=BBC |date=May 18, 2001 |accessdate=August 23, 2012[/tpl]
The show's premise originated with Chris Carter,[tpl]cite web|url=http://millennium-thisiswhoweare.net/cmeacg/crew_interview.php?name=Chris%20Carter&id=20|title=The Virtue of Paranoia – Interview with Chris Carter|date=February 20, 1997|author=Lipsky, David|work=Rolling Stone|publisher=Wenner Media|accessdate=August 23, 2012[/tpl] who served as an executive producer along with R. W. Goodwin, Frank Spotnitz, Howard Gordon, Vince Gilligan, John Shiban, Kim Manners, Glen Morgan, James Wong, and many others. Filming for seasons one to five took place primarily in Vancouver, British Columbia,[tpl]cite news |author=Strachan, Alex |url=http://www.mjq.net/xfiles/dd-sun-interview.htm |title= The Alienation of David Duchovny |work=The Vancouver Sun |publisher=Postmedia Network |date=February 17, 1998 |accessdate=August 23, 2012[/tpl] and for the remaining seasons in Los Angeles, California.[tpl]cite web |author=Parks, Jo-Ann |url= http://www.space.com/sciencefiction/tv/ailing_xfiles_000120.html |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20050901021930/http://www.space.com/sciencefiction/tv/ailing_xfiles_000120.html |title= What's Ailing The X-Files  |work=Space.com |publisher=TechMediaNetwork|date=January 20, 2000 |accessdate=August 24, 2012 |archivedate=September 1, 2005[/tpl] Episodes were broadcast on Fridays at 9:00 pm Eastern Standard Time (EST) for the series's first three seasons;[tpl]cite web |first=Steve |last=Johnson |url=http://articles.chicagotribune.com/1996-05-22/news/9605220114_1_x-files-new-hourlong-drama-michael-j-fox |title=Fox Moves 'X-files' To Sunday Nights |work=Chicago Tribune |publisher=Tribune Company |date=May 22, 1996 |accessdate=August 24, 2012[/tpl] the remaining six seasons aired on Sundays at 9:00 pm EST. Episodes are approximately 45 minutes in length (without including commercials) and were broadcast in standard definition. Two feature films based on the television series have been released as part of The X-Files franchise: the first premiered in summer 1998, between seasons five and six of the series, and a post-series film, The X-Files: I Want to Believe, was released in 2008.
