[[Swinburne University of Technology]]

CATEGORIES: Educational institutions established in 1908, Australian vocational education and training providers, Universities in Melbourne, TAFE Colleges in Melbourne, Astronomy institutes and departments, 1908 establishments in Australia

Swinburne University of Technology (often simply referred to as Swinburne) is an Australian public university of technology based in Melbourne, Victoria. Swinburne was founded in 1908 by the Honourable George Swinburne as the Eastern Suburbs Technical College. Its foundation campus is located in Hawthorn, a suburb of Melbourne which is located 7.5 km from the Melbourne CBD[tpl]clarify|date=May 2014[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=Hawthorn, Melbourne|url=https://maps.google.com.au/maps?ie=UTF-8&gl=au&daddr=Melbourne+VIC&saddr=Hawthorn+VIC&panel=1&f=d&fb=1&dirflg=d&geocode=KcGlcAU5QtZqMfDYjCF1VgQF%3BKfdNutK1RtZqMZDNjCF1VgQE&ei=H8t0UrS2KMeckQXRvoAw&ved=0CC4Q-A8wAA|publisher=Google Maps[/tpl]
In its first year, it enrolled 80 students in subjects including carpentry, plumbing and gas fitting. Today, Swinburne operates five campuses in two countries and has an enrolment of 60,000 students across vocational, undergraduate and postgraduate levels.Quick Facts (About Swinburne website)
In addition to its main Hawthorn campus, Swinburne has campuses in the Melbourne metropolitan area at Prahran, Wantirna and Croydon. Swinburne also has a branch campus in Sarawak, Malaysia which it has operated in partnership with the Sarawak State Government since 2000.[tpl]cite web|title=Education Malaysia website|url=http://www.educationmalaysia.gov.my/courses-education-institutes/institutes/swinburne-university-of-technology-sarawak-campus.html|publisher=Government of Malaysia|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl]
Internationally, Swinburne is ranked among the top 400 universities in the world by the Academic Ranking of World Universities[tpl]cite web|title=Academic Ranking of World Universities 2013|url=http://www.shanghairanking.com/World-University-Rankings-2013/Australia.html|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl] and the Times Higher Education World University Rankings[tpl]cite web|title=Times Higher Education World University Rankings 2013-14|url=http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/world-university-rankings/2013-14/world-ranking/institution/swinburne-university-of-technology|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl]  and in the top 500 universities in the world by the 2013 QS World University Rankings.[tpl]cite web|title=QS World University Rankings 2013|url=http://www.topuniversities.com/node/9322/ranking-details/world-university-rankings/2013|publisher=Quacquarelli Symonds Limited|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl]

==History==

thumb|right|Swinburne's original administration building, erected in 1908, still stands at the centre of the university's Hawthorn campus. Image credit: Kelvin Rowley.Swinburne University of Technology has its origins in the Eastern Suburbs Technical College, which was established in 1908 in the Melbourne suburb of Hawthorn by the Honourable George Swinburne. In 1913, the institution changed its name to Swinburne Technical College. The Institute attained university status on 1 July 1992 following the passage of the Swinburne University of Technology Act 1992.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne University of Technology Act 1992|url=http://www.austlii.edu.au/au/legis/vic/hist_act/suota1992422/|publisher=Parliament of Victoria|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl]
As a consequence of the Dawkins reforms to Australian higher education in the early 1990s, the university commenced delivery in the suburb of Prahran through a merger in 1992 with Victoria College (previously the Prahran Institute of TAFE),[tpl]cite book|last=Roche|first=Vivienne|title=Razor gang to Dawkins: a history of Victoria College, an Australian College of Advanced Education|year=2003|publisher=University of Melbourne|url=http://dtl.unimelb.edu.au/R/S4NFFN7LACUIAN7A5SN6LKEF8KQGETXPFXM45FDGD4XHEDSU37-00116?func=dbin-jump-full&object_id=65995&pds_handle=GUEST[/tpl] which stood on the site of Victoria's first training institutes, the Prahran Mechanics' Institute.
In 1997, Swinburne opened a campus at Lilydale, Victoria. In 1998, it merged with the Outer East Institute of TAFE and commenced operating from campuses at Croydon and Wantirna.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne History|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/chancellery/about/history/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl]
In 1999, Swinburne established the National Institute of Circus Arts (NICA).[tpl]cite web|title=History of NICA|url=http://web.archive.org/web/20110719162654/http://www.nica.com.au/history|work=National Library of Australia web archive|publisher=National Library of Australia|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl]
In 2000, the university opened a campus in Sarawak, Malaysia, as a partnership between the university and the Sarawak State Government.[tpl]cite web|title=Education Malaysia website|url=http://www.educationmalaysia.gov.my/courses-education-institutes/institutes/swinburne-university-of-technology-sarawak-campus.html|publisher=Government of Malaysia[/tpl]
In January 2011, Swinburne announced that it would expand its online degree offerings through a $10 million joint venture with Seek Limited.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne University to go it alone with its online courses|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/higher-education/swinburne-university-of-technology-to-go-it-alone-with-its-online-courses/story-e6frgcjx-1225990538799|publisher=The Australian|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl] Initially called Swinburne Direct, the venture subsequently became known as Swinburne Online.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne Online opens for business|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/chancellery/mediacentre/staff/news/2011/11/swinburne-online-opens-for-business|work=Swinburne University Media Centre|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl]
thumb|right|The architecturally striking Advanced Technologies Centre, at Swinburne's Hawthorn campus, opened in February 2011. Image credit: Paul Jones.In February 2011, the university opened the Advanced Technologies Centre,[tpl]cite web|title=ATC opens for business|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/chancellery/mediacentre/staff/news/2011/02/atc-opens-for-business|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|date=23 February 2011[/tpl] a 22,000 square metre building of modern architectural design at its Hawthorn campus,[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne Advanced Technologies Centre|url=http://www.kane.com.au/project/swinburne-advanced-technology-centre|publisher=Kane Constructions|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl] known locally as "the cheese grater building".[tpl]cite web|title=Largest cheese grater in the world|url=https://twitter.com/tke_rsk/status/336392641837146113|publisher=@tke_rsk|date=20 May 2013[/tpl]  The building was subsequently officially opened by the Australian Minister for Innovation, Industry, Science and Research, the Hon. Senator Kim Carr on 8 December 2011.[tpl]cite web|last=Carr|first=Kim|title=Swinburne Uni launches Advanced Technologies Centre|url=http://archive.innovation.gov.au/ministersarchive2011/Carr/MediaReleases/Pages/SWINBURNEUNILAUNCHESADVANCEDTECHNOLOGIESCENTRE.html|publisher=Australian Government|date=8 December 2011[/tpl]
Following a series of funding cuts announced by the Victorian Government to vocational education in May 2012,[tpl]cite web|last=Wheelahan|first=Leesa|title=TAFE cuts will affect everyone: state governments should think again|url=http://theconversation.com/tafe-cuts-will-affect-everyone-state-governments-should-think-again-9687|publisher=The Conversation|accessdate=31 October 2013[/tpl] Swinburne announced that it would close its Lilydale and Prahran campuses.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne to shut Lilydale campus|url=http://www.theage.com.au/national/education/swinburne-to-shut-lilydale-campus-20120706-21lga.html|publisher=The Age|author=Benjamin Preiss|date=6 July 2012[/tpl] Lilydale campus officially closed on 1 July 2013.[tpl]cite web|title=Lilydale campus officially closed|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/leader/east/swinburne8217s-lilydale-campus-officially-closed/story-fngnvlxu-1226671491594|publisher=Lilydale and Yarra Valley Leader|author=Kimberley Seedy|date=1 July 2012[/tpl]  In August 2013 the university announced that it had signed a Memorandum of Understanding with the Northern Melbourne Institute of TAFE to sell its Prahran campus to NMIT with effect from 2014.[tpl]cite web|title=NMIT to turn Swinburne’s Prahran campus into centre for creative arts|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2013-08-02/nmit-to-turn-swinburne27s-prahran-campus-into-centre-for-creat/4860982|publisher=Australian Broadcasting Corporation|date=2 August 2013[/tpl]
The university is currently completing construction of a major new building at its Hawthorn campus, the Advanced Manufacturing and Design Centre,[tpl]cite web|title=Advanced Manufacturing Centre|url=http://www.baulderstone.com.au/project-portfolio/project-details/swinburne-university-of-technology-advanced-manufacturing-centre?fp=,787,36,770,785,786,|publisher=Baulderstone Constructions|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl] part-funded by a $40 million Federal Government grant.[tpl]cite web|title=Rudd Labor invests in Victoria's students and researchers|url=http://archive.innovation.gov.au/ministersarchive2011/Carr/MediaReleases/Pages/RUDDLABORINVESTSINVICTORIASSTUDENTSANDRESEARCHERS.html|publisher=Australian Government|date=18 June 2010[/tpl]

==Academic==

===Governance===

Swinburne is governed by a 10-member Council, whose members include the Chancellor and Vice-Chancellor.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/corporate/registrar/council/memberships.html|title=Swinburne Council|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl] Bill Scales AO has been Chancellor of the University since 2005.[tpl]cite web|title=About the Chancellor|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/chancellery/chancellor/about/about.html|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology website|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl] Professor Linda Kristjanson has been Vice-Chancellor of Swinburne University since 2011 and was previously the Deputy Vice-Chancellor (Research and Development) at Curtin University.[tpl]cite web|title=Kristjanson appointed VC at Swinburne|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/higher-education/appointments/kristjanson-appointed-vc-at-swinburne/story-e6frgckf-1226018032720|publisher=The Australian|date=9 March 2011[/tpl] She was the Telstra Australian Business Woman of the Year in 2002.[tpl]cite web|title=Telstra Women's Business Awards|url=http://www.telstrabusinesswomensawards.com/award-winners/past-winners/2002/national-99.aspx|publisher=Telstra|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl]

===Rankings===

Internationally, Swinburne is ranked among the top 400 universities in the world by the Academic Ranking of World Universities and the Times Higher Education World University Rankings  and in the top 500 universities in the world by the 2013 QS World University Rankings.
Swinburne also is ranked 76-100 in the world in the field of physics by the Academic Ranking of World Universities.[tpl]cite web|title=Academic Ranking of World Universities|url=http://www.shanghairanking.com/SubjectPhysics2013.html|work=ARWU Subject rankings|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl] 
The university's computer science and information technology, sociology, psychology, and communications and media subjects were ranked 151-200 in the world by the 2013 QS World University Rankings.[tpl]cite web|title=QS World Subject Rankings 2013|url=http://www.topuniversities.com/node/9322/ranking-details/university-subject-rankings/2013|publisher=QS Quacquarelli Symonds Limited[/tpl]

===Faculties===

Swinburne comprises three "super faculties", each of which conducts both teaching and research.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne Faculty structure|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/faculties-tafe.html|accessdate=2 January 2014[/tpl]

The Faculty of Engineering and Industrial Sciences comprises the School of Science, the School of Engineering and the School of Software and Electrical Engineering and associated research centres. [tpl]cite web|title=Faculty of Science, Engineering and Technology|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/science-engineering-technology/|accessdate=2 January 2014[/tpl]

The Faculty of Health, Arts and Design comprises the School of Health Sciences, the School of Arts, Social Sciences and Humanities and the School of Design, and associated research centres.[tpl]cite web|title=Faculty of Health, Arts and Design|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/health-arts-design/|accessdate=2 January 2014[/tpl]

The Faculty of Business and Enterprise comprises the Swinburne Business School and associated research centres. [tpl]cite web|title=Faculty of Business and Enterprise|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/business-enterprise/|accessdate=2 January 2014[/tpl]

===Research===

Based on its research activity as assessed by the Australian Research Council, Swinburne is one of the most specialised of Australian universities.[tpl]cite web|last=Hare|first=Julie|title=How universities performed in ERA 2012 (Interactive Tool)|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/higher-education/how-universities-performed-in-era-2012/story-e6frgcjx-1226530828592|publisher=The Australian|date=6 December 2012[/tpl]  It is research active in 11 of the ARC’s 22 broad fields of research interest.[tpl]cite web|title=ERA 2012 outcomes, Australian Research Council|url=http://www.arc.gov.au/era/outcomes_2012/Institution/SWN|publisher=Australian Government[/tpl]
Swinburne has 14 Research Centres with a concentration in the physical and health sciences, engineering and ICT fields:[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne Research Centres|url=http://www.research.swinburne.edu.au/our-research/research-centres/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=14 March 2014[/tpl]   

===Vocational education===

Swinburne offers vocational education (TAFE) courses in a range of areas including arts and social sciences, business and management, design, digital media, engineering, English language and study skills, environment and land management, film and television, health sciences and community care, information and communication technologies, media and communications, performing and creative arts, science, apprenticeships and traineeships.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne TAFE courses|url=http://www.tafe.swinburne.edu.au/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=1 November 2013[/tpl]

===Swinburne College===

The university also operates Swinburne College, a provider of pathway education courses which prepare students for university study. Programs offered by Swinburne College include English language, foundation studies, diploma and professional year programs.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne College|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/college/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=2 January 2014[/tpl]

==Campuses==

===Hawthorn===

thumb|right|View of Swinburne's Hawthorn campus at night, as seen from the Advanced Technologies Centre. Image credit: Adrian MalecThe Hawthorn campus is Swinburne’s main campus. It hosts a range of undergraduate and vocational courses, postgraduate programs, short courses and research.

===Prahran===

In 2013, Swinburne's Prahran campus hosted the university's Faculty of Design, a range of vocational programs and the National Institute of Circus Arts (NICA). In 2012, Swinburne announced that it would cease operating vocational and higher education programs at Prahran in 2014. In August 2013, NMIT announced that it had signed a Memorandum of Understanding with Swinburne to acquire the Prahran campus.[tpl]cite web|title=NMIT and Swinburne University move forward on Prahran campus|url=http://www.nmit.edu.au/news/nmit-and-swinburne-university-move-forward-on-prahran-campus/|publisher=Northern Metropolitan Institute of TAFE|date=2 August 2013[/tpl] It has been reported that the National Institute of Circus Arts will continue to operate from Prahran.[tpl]cite web|last=Priess|first=Benjamin|title=Circus instute nets $13 million in federal funding and will stay at Prahran campus|url=http://www.smh.com.au/entertainment/circus-institute-nets-13m-in-federal-funding-and-will-stay-at-prahran-campus-20130728-2qsh6.html|publisher=Sydney Morning Herald|date=29 July 2013[/tpl]

===Croydon===

The university's Croydon campus is a TAFE specific campus, with a focus on training in trades such as building, carpentry, electrical and plumbing.[tpl]cite web|title=Croydon campus|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/campuses/croydon/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl]

===Wantirna===

Wantirna is a TAFE specific campus. It is home to the National Centre for Sustainability and a Trade Training Centre, the Knox Innovation, Opportunity and Sustainability Centre (KIOSC), which offers educational opportunities to students from schools in its local area.[tpl]cite web|title=Swinburne opens new training centre|url=http://www.educationcareer.net.au/archived-news/swinburne-opens-new-training-centre|publisher=CareerSpot|date=26 April 2013[/tpl] The campus also offers courses in areas including health and community services, visual arts, business and accounting.[tpl]cite web|title=Wantirna campus|url=http://www.swinburne.edu.au/campuses/wantirna/|publisher=Swinburne University of Technology|accessdate=2 November 2013[/tpl]

==Student life==

===Swinburne Student Amenities Association (SSAA)===

The Swinburne Student Amenities Association (SSAA) provides services and activities at Swinburne to encourage socialisation among all students across all Swinburne campuses.
The SSAA runs orientation activities; it also organises clubs and societies to represent the student body. It provides academic advice and advocacy, along with entertainment, free lunches and other activities throughout the year.MYSSAA Swinburne Student Amenities Association

===Swinburne Student Union (SSU)===

Swinburne Student Union (SSU), is the independent student representative body of Swinburne University of Technology in Melbourne, Australia. Membership is opt-in for all students. SSU offers its members free printing, free breakfasts, and academic advocacy.Swinburne Student Union services
