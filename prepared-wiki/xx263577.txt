[[Kiribati presidential election, 2012]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in Kiribati, 2012 in Kiribati, 2012 elections in Oceania

A presidential election was held in Kiribati on 13 January 2012,http://www.radioaustralianews.net.au/stories/201112/3400097.htm?desktop following a two-round parliamentary election held in October 2011.http://www.mherrera.org/elections.htm Incumbent President Anote Tong sought re-election to a third four-year term, ending months of speculation about his decision.[tpl]cite news|first=Robert|last=Matau|title=Tong confirms contesting next election |url=http://www.islandsbusiness.com/islands_business/index_dynamic/containerNameToReplace=MiddleMiddle/focusModuleID=19881/overideSkinName=issueArticle-full.tpl |work=Islands Business |publisher= |date=2011-10-12 |accessdate=2011-10-17[/tpl][tpl]cite news|first=|last=|title=Kiribati President confident he can win third term |url=http://www.rnzi.com/pages/news.php?op=read&id=63396 |work=Radio New Zealand International |publisher= |date=2011-09-27 |accessdate=2011-10-17[/tpl] 
Tong beat Tetaua Taitai and Rimeta Beniamina, who led the Kamaeuraoan Te I-Kiribati political party in the last parliament, with a little over 42% of the vote.

==Background==

The president, who is elected by popular vote from among three or four candidates chosen by legislators from within the parliament, is limited to three four-year terms under the constitution.

==Date==

The election, initially scheduled for 30 December 2011, was postponed to 13 January 2012 in order to allow citizens of the country to travel to celebrate the New Year.[ref]
</ref>

==Candidates==

The new House of Assembly of Kiribati nominated three candidates for the presidency following the 2011 parliamentary election.[tpl]cite news|first=|last=|title=Parliament Nominates 3 Candidates for Kiribati President |url=http://pidp.eastwestcenter.org/pireport/2011/November/11-28-04.htm |work=The Kiribati Independent |publisher=East–West Center's Pacific Islands Report |date=2011-11-25 |accessdate=2012-01-14[/tpl]

==Results==

Incumbent President Anote Tong was the outright winner and re-elected as president of Kiribati, with an aggregate total of 14,315 votes or 42% of the total vote.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title=Tong wins third term as Kiribati president |url=http://www.radioaustralianews.net.au/stories/201201/3408064.htm?desktop |work=Radio Australia |publisher= |date=2012-01-14 |accessdate=2012-01-15[/tpl] President Tong defeated his closest challenger, Tetaui Taitai, by more than 7%, or 2,500 votes. The third challenger, Rimeta Beniamina, only received 7,738 votes. Tong was the leading candidate in 14 out of the 23 constituencies.[tpl]cite news|first=|last=|title=Kiribati’s Tong beats challengers to win third term |url=http://www.rnzi.com/pages/news.php?op=read&id=65522 |work=Radio New Zealand International |publisher= |date=2012-01-14 |accessdate=2012-01-13[/tpl][tpl]cite news|first=|last=|title=Results of the 2012 Presidential Election in Kiribati |url=http://www.kiribationlinecommunity.com/forum/topics/results-of-the-2012-presidential-election-in-kiribati?xg_source=activity |work=Kiribati Online Community |publisher= |date=2012-01-14 |accessdate=2012-01-15[/tpl]
President Tong's percentage of the vote (42%) was much less than his 2007 re-election, when he received 64% of the popular vote.
Voter turnout in Kiribati was approximately 68% for the election. This was higher than the 2007 presidential election, when voter participation was a little over 50%.
