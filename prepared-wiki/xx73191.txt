[[Joan Riudavets]]

CATEGORIES: 1889 births, 2004 deaths, People from Minorca, Spanish supercentenarians, Male supercentenarians

Joan Riudavets Moll (15 December 1889 – 5 March 2004) was a Spanish supercentenarian and, at the time of his death, was believed to be the oldest verified person ever in the history of Spain. However, subsequent research by the International Database on Longevity has revealed that there were two anonymous women who were older than Riudavets at the time of their deaths.International Database on Longevity: List of Spaniard cases He became the oldest recognized living person in Europe following the death of Italian Maria Teresa Fumarola Ligorio in May 2003, and the oldest recognized living man in the world following the death of Japanese supercentenarian Yukichi Chuganji in September 2003.
Riudavets is the oldest man from Europe. At the time of his death, he was one of four men verified to reach 114.

==Biography==

Joan Riudavets was born in Menorca. His mother, Catalina Moll Mercadés, died at age 25 before the end of December 1889.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/3321315.stm|title=Oldest man marks 114th birthday|publisher=BBC News|date=15 December 2003|accessdate=9 October 2009[/tpl] Riudavets' wife, whom he married in 1917, was born in 1889 and died around 1979. He worked as a cobbler until retiring in 1954, and was a former village councillor of Es Migjorn Gran. He was on a Mediterranean diet, which included: olive oil, tomatoes, fish and bread. Riudavets was able to walk by himself and ride a bicycle until his death. Some of his hobbies were playing the guitar, singing, and playing football. He died in Minorca, Spain at the age of 114 years and 81 days after having a cold for a few days without being seriously ill, however due to his extraordinary age, he was unable to recover such a brief illness. .[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/3539095.stm|title=World's oldest man dies aged 114|publisher=BBC News|date=6 March 2004|accessdate=9 October 2009[/tpl]
