[[Jean Rolin (cardinal)]]

CATEGORIES: 1408 births, 1483 deaths, Duchy of Burgundy, French cardinals, Bishops of Autun, Bishops of Chalon-sur-Saône

Jean (Jehan) Rolin (1408–1483) was a Burgundian bishop and Cardinal.
His father, Nicolas Rolin, was ducal Chancellor of Burgundy, and lord of Authumes. Jean became a Cardinal in 1448, created by Pope Nicholas V, as part of diplomatic engagement between the Duchy of Burgundy and the Papacy, tending against France.Richard Vaughan, Philip the Good (2002 edition), p. 215.
He was bishop of Chalon-sur-Saône in 1431, and bishop of Autun in 1436. He was a patron of the arts, supporting the work of an anonymous illustrator known as the Master of Jean Rolin.http://wwar.com/masters/m/master_of_jean_rolin.html, http://www.getty.edu/art/gettyguide/artMakerDetails?maker=1183
