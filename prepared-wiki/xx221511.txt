[[Kent Cricket League]]

CATEGORIES: English domestic cricket competitions, Cricket in Kent, ECB Premier Leagues

The Kent Cricket League is the top level of competition for recreational club cricket in Kent, England. The league was founded in 1970, and since 1999 has been a designated ECB Premier League.List of ECB Premier Leagues From 1999 to 2002, the league experimented with matches played over two-days on consecutive Saturdays, the only ECB Premier League to do so, before reverting to a regular one-day schedule for the start of the 2003 season.
It has six divisions, and below these six divisions lies the Kent Regional Cricket League.
The teams competing in the Premier Division in 2014 are: Beckenham, Bexley, Bickley Park, Blackheath, Bromley, Canterbury, Hartley Country Club, Lordswood, Sandwich Town, and Sevenoaks Vine.
