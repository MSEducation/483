[[Wingate, Brooklyn]]

CATEGORIES: Neighborhoods in Brooklyn

Wingate is a neighborhood in the north central portion of the New York City borough of Brooklyn. The area is east of Prospect-Lefferts Gardens and south of Crown Heights. Wingate is bounded by Empire Boulevard to the north, Troy Avenue to the east, Winthrop Street to the south and New York Avenue to the west.Jackson, Kenneth T., The Encyclopedia of New York City, Yale University Press, 1995, p. 1267. The area is part of Brooklyn Community Board 9.Brooklyn Community Boards, New York City. Accessed December 31, 2007. It is sometimes considered part of Crown Heights and/or East Flatbush.[tpl]cite web|last=Holland|first=Joy|title=Pigtown|url=http://brooklynology.brooklynpubliclibrary.org/post/2009/03/05/Pigtown.aspx|work=Brooklynology|publisher=Brooklyn Public Library|accessdate=16 January 2013[/tpl] 
The area was originally known as Pig Town. It was renamed in the 1950s after the construction of the now defunct George W. Wingate High School.
