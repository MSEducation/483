[[Liberia at the 2012 Summer Olympics]]

CATEGORIES: Nations at the 2012 Summer Olympics, Liberia at the Olympics

   
Liberia competed at the 2012 Summer Olympics in London, United Kingdom from 27 July to 12 August 2012. This was the nation's eleventh appearance at the Olympics since its debut in 1956. 
Four Liberian athletes were selected to the team, competing in athletics, and in judo for the first time.  Among these athletes, American-born decathlete Jangy Addy only competed at his second consecutive Olympics. Sprinter Phobay Kutu-Akoi, a psychology graduate at the St. John's University in New York, was the nation's flag bearer at the opening ceremony.[tpl]cite web|title=Phobay Kutu-Akoi Profile – St. John's University Official Athletic Site|url=http://www.redstormsports.com/sports/w-track/mtt/kutuakoi_phobay00.html|publisher=St. John's Red Storm|accessdate=6 September 2012[/tpl] Liberia, however, has yet to win its first ever Olympic medal.

==Judo==

Liberia had 1 judoka invited, Liva Saryee.IntJudo – Invited Country to take part at the London Games However, Saryee did not compete, as he received a bye in the first round and did not start in the second round.
