[[La Dessalinienne]]

CATEGORIES: National anthems, Haitian music, National symbols of Haiti

La Dessalinienne is the national anthem of Haiti, honoring Jean-Jacques Dessalines. It was written by Justin Lhérisson and composed by Nicolas Geffrard in French and adopted in 1904.http://www.ayitihistory.com/index.htm#patriotic_songs.htm[tpl]cite book|title=Music in Latin America and the Caribbean: Volume 2 Performing the Caribbean Experience - An Encyclopedic History|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=uspTNzJ_NoYC&pg=PA256&lpg=PA256&dq=mini-jazz+haiti+history&source=bl&ots=tOsEmlF2Ra&sig=c_XH_piYT3TM5E_STd2npetYftQ&hl=en&sa=X&ei=94fYUsyNKvLTsASh6oGQBg&ved=0CDQQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q=mini-jazz%20haiti%20history&f=false|author=Malena Kuss|publisher=The Universe of Music Inc|pages=260|isbn=978-0-292-70951-5[/tpl] The Haitian Creole version was an adaptation by Raymond A. Moise. Ansy Dérose, a Haitian singer, helped popularize it. Although it became widely accepted, the 1987 Haitian Constitution makes no mention of this particular version.http://www.ayitihistory.com/index.htm#patriotic_songs.htm
