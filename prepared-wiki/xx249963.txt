[[First Presbyterian Church (Valatie, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Presbyterian churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1878, 19th-century Presbyterian church buildings, Churches in Columbia County, New York

First Presbyterian Church is a historic Presbyterian church at Church St. in Valatie, Columbia County, New York.  It was built in 1878 and is a one story, rectangular building built of face brick with limestone trim in the High Victorian Gothic style.  It features three very steep gable roofs surfaced with polychrome slate, dormer windows, a porte cochere, and an engaged brick tower capped by an open belfry.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1475|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: First Presbyterian Church|date=June 1979|accessdate=2010-07-04 |author=John F. Harwood and Larry E. Gobrecht|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1477|title=Accompanying four photos[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1979.
