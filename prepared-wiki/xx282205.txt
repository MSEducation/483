==Biography==

===Early life===

Hartgen was born in 1914 in Reading, Pennsylvania. He got master's degree in fine arts from the University of Pennsylvania where he also used to study architecture. When World War II began he joined the U.S. Army Camouflage Corps. Later on, he married Frances, a United States Weather Bureau worker.[tpl]cite news|url=http://archive.bangordailynews.com/2002/11/30/visionary-art-professor-vincent-hartgen-dies-at-88-painter-founded-um-art-department-collection/|author=Alicia Anstead|title=Visionary, art professor Vincent Hartgen dies at 88; Painter founded UM art department, collection |publisher=Bangor Daily News|date=November 30, 2002|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] 

===Teaching career===

After the war he worked as a teacher in the University of Maine where he showed his students the difficulties which Michelangelo had when he was painting the Sistine Chapel. He also showed his art in summer on the yard of his Orono home.

===Collecting===

As a collector he collected 5,000 art pieces which were painted by such famous artists as Berenice Abbott, Jasper Johns, John Marin, Diego Rivera, Giovanni Piranesi, Kathe Kollwitz, and Andrew Wyeth. He has collections in such museums as the Beaux-Arts Institute of Design, the Walker Art Center, the Brooklyn Museum, the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the De Cordova Museum, the Museum of Fine Arts, Boston, and Chase Gallery. He served as museum director for 36 years at the University of Maine Museum of Art. He died on November 27, 2002.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vincenthartgen.com/|title=Vincent Andrew Hartgen|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl] During his life he was awarded with such awards as the Black Bear Award, the Distinguished Professor Award and an even became an honorary doctor of fine arts at the University of Maine. He was also a founder of the Vincent A. Hartgen Award which was established in 1999.
