Andrew Frank Schoeppel (November 23, 1894 – January 21, 1962) was an American politician and a member of the Republican Party. He was the 29th Governor of Kansas from 1943 to 1947 and a U.S. Senator from 1949 until his death. He was born in 1894 in Claflin, Kansas and died in 1962 of abdominal cancer at the National Naval Medical Center at Bethesda, Maryland.Biographical Directory of the United States CongressFind-A-Grave profile for Frank Schoeppel

==Political life==

Schoeppel's early political life began as county attorney in Ness County, Kansas and was one of the early local officials for Ness City.Kansas Heritage-Ness County  Later he was elected mayor of Ness City and also served as chairman of the Kansas Corporation Commission.Kansas Governor's Records - Andrew F. Schoeppel Administration - Jan. 11, 1943 - Jan. 12, 1947 - Kansas State Historical Society
Schoeppel broke his allegiance to Kansas in 1952 when he supported Senator Robert Taft for president over Dwight D. Eisenhower.[tpl]citation needed|date=March 2008[/tpl]

==College football==

Schoeppel played college football from 1920 to 1922 while attending the University of Nebraska and made "honorable mention" on one of Walter Camp's first All-America football teams.Office of the Governor of Kansas  He served as the head football coach at Fort Hays State University for one season, in 1929, compiling a record of 2–5.  Schoeppel filled in as head coach while his predecessor, William D. Weidein, was on sabbatical.  Weidein did not return after his one-year sabbatical.  After Schoeppel completed his one year as head coach, the school's program was taken over by W. C. "Jack" Riley.Fort Hays Coaching Records
