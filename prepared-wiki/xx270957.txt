[[Break the Silence (van Canto album)]]

CATEGORIES: 2011 albums, Napalm Records albums, Van Canto albums

Break the Silence is the fourth studio album by the German a cappella metal band van Canto, released on September 23, 2011.  It reached number 23 in the German charts.[tpl]cite web|last=Genzel|first=Christian|title=Break The Silence (Review)|url=http://www.allmusic.com/album/break-the-silence-mw0002194271|work=AllMusic|accessdate=8 March 2013[/tpl]  It features 13 tracks, five of them being covers, and two of them featuring guest artists: Joakim Brodén (from Sabaton) guest appears in his band's cover "Primo Victoria", Marcus Siepen (from Blind Guardian) guest appears on "Spelled in Waters".News at van Canto website A Storm to Come is their longest song to date, clocking at 9:13.

==Critical reaction==

Metal.de rated it 9/10.[tpl]cite web|title=Van Canto - Break The Silence (Review)|url=http://www.metal.de/heavy-metal/review/van-canto/47561-break-the-silence/|work=Metal.de|accessdate=8 March 2013|date=2011-09-13[/tpl]  The Age of Metal called it a "jewel", praising its production and innovation.[tpl]cite web|title=Van Canto – Break The Silence (2011)|url=http://theageofmetal.com/2011/09/van-canto-break-the-silence-2011/|work=The Age of Metal|accessdate=8 March 2013|date=2011-09-20[/tpl] Metal Storm rated it 8.4/10.[tpl]cite web|title=Van Canto - Break The Silence|url=http://www.metalstorm.net/pub/review.php?review_id=9942|work=Metal Storm|date=01.12.2011[/tpl] Laut.de was less complimentary, suggesting it would be better not to break the silence and calling it "as unnecessary as a goiter".[tpl]cite web|last=Edele|first=Michael|title=Break The SIlence|url=http://www.laut.de/Van-Canto/Break-The-Silence-%28Album%29|work=Laut.de|accessdate=8 March 2013[/tpl]
