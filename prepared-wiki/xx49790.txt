[[Pilani]]

CATEGORIES: Cities and towns in Jhunjhunu district, Thikanas of Shekhawati

Pilani is a small town situated in the Shekhawati region of Rajasthan, India. Administratively, it forms a part of Jhunjhunu district.

==Geography==

Pilani is surrounded by small villages like Rayala 1km,Dhindhwa Athuna 1km,Dhindhwa bichla 3.5 km, Kherla ,2 km away,Brahmano ki dhani,10 km  Jherli 3 km away and Dhindhwa Aguna,5km Bishanpura, 6 km away on Surajgarh road.

==Demographics==

[tpl]As of|2011[/tpl] India census,[tpl]GR|India[/tpl] Pilani had a population of 26,219. Males constituted 52% of the population and females 48%. Pilani has an average literacy rate of 79%, higher than the national average of 74.04%: male literacy, according to the 2011 census, was 82.14%, and female literacy was 65.46%. In Pilani, 14% of the population was under 6 years of age.

==Climate==

Pilani is known for its extreme climate. Summer temperatures reach up to 50 degree Celsius between May and July, while winter temperatures reach sub-zero levels between December and January. Months of October and March are generally considered most pleasant. Pilani mostly suffers from low rain fall causing continuous drought for successive years.

==Notable residents==

The town is the birthplace of industrialist Ghanshyam Das Birla and the location of his ancestral residence.

==Education==

Pilani is the home to Birla Institute of Technology and Science (BITS Pilani), a technological deemed university formed in 1964.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.ugc.ac.in/inside/deemeduniv.html |title=:::Deemed University – University Grants Commission ::: |work=ugc.ac.in |accessdate=30 November 2011[/tpl] Other colleges in the town include the BK Birla Institute of Engineering & Technology (BKBIET), an engineering college formed in 2007, the Indermani Mandelia college for girls and the ShaadiLal Kataria Teacher Training college. The GD Birla Memorial Polytechnic Institute(Brla technical training institute,pilani) (BTTI) the top ranked 1st  diploma college of rajasthan, established in 1988 is also located in the town.In 2009 Shridhar University has been established under State Govt. Act as a Private University. The University provides various higher education programs up to Doctoral level.

==Travel==

Pilani is not accessible directly by rail. The nearest railway stations is Chirawa (16 km) served by Northern Western Railway and lying on the Sikar-Loharu Broad Gauge section and Loharu (25 km) in Haryana which is served by fully broad gauge sections. Jaipur is 220 km from Pilani and Delhi is 210 km and well connected by state and national highways.

==Tourism==

Pilani has some places worth visiting like the BITS, Pilani campus which includes Birla Museum, Saraswati Temple, Shivganga Canal etc. Other places of interest in Pilani are Panchwati Udhyan, Ram mandir temple & garden, Durga temple, Sai Mandir bhagina road and CEERI campus. School and college students from nearby towns and cities often visit Pilani on educational tours. Some fresco painted old Havelis are also situated in the market areas of Pilani.
