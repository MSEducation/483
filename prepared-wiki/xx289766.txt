[[Miss Shanel]]

CATEGORIES: Rwandan music

Miss Shanel, born Ruth Nirere, is a Rwandan singer and actress.[tpl]cite news | last = Umutesi | first = Doreen | title = Rwanda's Hottest Celebrities As Voted By You ! | newspaper = The New Times | location = Rwanda | date = 5 April 2013 | url = http://allafrica.com/stories/201304050040.html?page=2 | accessdate = 13 August 2013 [/tpl] 

==History==

===Early years===

She began singing as a child and released two successful singles while a secondary school student, both meant to comfort and commemorate the survivors and victims of the Rwandan Genocide. Her songs are regularly played during the annual Genocide commemoration period, and in 2004 she won the "Never Again" competition to select the best commemoration artist. 

===Musical career===

She had a major hit in 1998 at the age of 13 with the zouk tune "Ndarota!" off her debut album of the same name released earlier that year. In 2004 she released two a cappella singles to wide success, raising her to national celebrity. Her music during this period reflected a mix of genres, including R&B, soul, zouk and acoustic music.[tpl]cite news | last = Kagire | first = Edmund | title = Shanel sings to soothe hearts, souls | newspaper = Rwanda Today | location = Rwanda | date = 23 June 2012 | url = http://www.theeastafrican.co.ke/Rwanda/Lifestyle/Shanel+sings+to+soothe+hearts+souls++/-/1433242/1434180/-/o91xwoz/-/index.html | accessdate = 13 August 2013 [/tpl] 
In 2009 she released her debut album, entitled Narrow Road. This album and her subsequent music reflect more traditional Rwandan musical styles. She has collaborated on singles with numerous Rwandan, Kenyan and Ugandan artists.[tpl]cite web | title = Ruth Nirere - Comédienne - Rwanda |language = French | publisher = Festival International du Film Francophone de Namur | location = Rwanda | date = 2010 | url = http://www.fiff.be/fr/Espace-pro/Les-comediens/NIRERE-Ruth | accessdate = 13 August 2013 [/tpl] 
In 2012 she gave a series of performances for Rwandan peacekeepers stationed in Darfur, South Sudan.[tpl]cite news | last = Karuhanga | first = James | title = Rwandan Artistes Perform for Peacekeepers in Darfur| newspaper = The New Times | location = Rwanda | date = 10 July 2012 | url = http://www.irwanda.rw/news/local/story/13232-rwandan-artistes-perform-for-peacekeepers-in-darfur.html | accessdate = 13 August 2013 [/tpl]
In 2013 she moved to France to complete a two-year course in vocal performance.[tpl]cite news  | title = Miss Shanel – a movie Star | newspaper = The New Times | location = Rwanda | date =  | url = http://www.newtimes.co.rw/news/views/article_print.php?&a=12939&icon=Print | accessdate = 13 August 2013 [/tpl] 

==Awards==

Miss Shanel has been nominated three times for a Pearl of Africa Music Award in 2006, 2007 and 2008. In 2009 she won a Salax Award in the category of Best Female Artist. 

==Acting career==

In addition to her musical career, Nirere has launched a successful career as an actress. She starred in the films Avenir, Long Coat directed by Edouard Bamporiki and Matière grise directed by Kivu Ruhorahoza. She starred in Le jour ou Dieu est parti en voyage (2009), which recounts stories of the Rwandan Genocide. Nirere's performance earned her the prize for Best Actress at the Thessaloniki International Film Festival in Greece and at the International Film Festival Bratislava in Slovakia.
