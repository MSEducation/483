Paul Barden (born 1 July 1980)[tpl]cite web|url=http://hoganstand.com/Longford/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=41002|title=Barden, Paul|publisher=Hoganstand.com|date=2000-05-31|accessdate=2011-01-05[/tpl] is a Gaelic footballer from County Longford, Ireland. He has been a member of the Longford intercounty team since 1999. He won an O'Byrne Cup medal with them in 2000, he also won 2 O'Byrne Shield medals in 2006 and 2007. He was also a member of the Ireland international rules football team in 2002 and 2006[tpl]cite web|url=http://hoganstand.com/longford/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=68611|title=Bardenâ€™s faithful ambition|publisher=Hoganstand.com|date=2006-11-04|accessdate=2011-01-04[/tpl] and 2006. He also won a Railway Cup with Leinster in 2001 and 2002.[tpl]cite web|url=http://hoganstand.com/longford/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=19191|title=Barden wins second Railway Cup medal|publisher=Hoganstand.com|date=2002-11-07|accessdate=2011-01-05[/tpl] In 2011 he helped Longford overcome Roscommon in the National Football League Div 4 final in Croke Park. In 2012 Longford were back in Croke Park this time for the Div 3 final and again won beating Wexford with Barden once again captain.
He plays his club football with Clonguish[tpl]cite web|url=http://hoganstand.com/longford/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=117696|title=Longford SFC final: Barden rescues Clonguish|publisher=Hoganstand.com|date=2009-09-14|accessdate=2011-01-05[/tpl] with whom he won 3 Longford Senior Football Championships in 2003, 2004 and 2009. He also plays hurling with the club and won Longford Senior Hurling Championships in 2003, 2005 and 2006.[tpl]cite web|url=http://hoganstand.com/longford/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=67157|title=Barden stars in Clonguish hurling success|publisher=Hoganstand.com|date=2006-10-02|accessdate=2011-01-05[/tpl]
He is the son of 1960s Longford footballer Brendan Barden.
