[[Charles Chatman]]

CATEGORIES: People from Texas, Living people, 1961 births, Overturned convictions in the United States

Charles Chatman (born 1961) served 26 years in prison due to a wrongful conviction. He had been convicted in 1981 and sentenced to life in prison. He was released on 3 January 2008. Chatman became the 15th inmate from Dallas County since 2001 to be exonerated by DNA testing.[tpl]cite news |title=DNA clears man in prison for 26 years |publisher=Associated Press |date=January 3, 2008[/tpl] He stated that race was a factor in his conviction. "I was convicted because a black man committed a crime against a white woman."[tpl]cite news |title=Texas Man Freed After 26 Years in Prison |work=The Guardian |date=January 3, 2008[/tpl] Chatman was represented by Michelle Moore and Jeff Blackburn, attorneys for the Innocence Project of Texas.

==Artistic Responses to the Chatman Case==

In 2009, New York City Poet Jeanann Verlee wrote "Resurrection," a poem written in the voice of Charles Chatman.  The poem was broadcast on the August 31, 2009 Indiefeed Performance Poetry Podcast.
