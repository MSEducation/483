[[Bøler]]

CATEGORIES: Neighbourhoods of Oslo

Bøler is a south-eastern suburb of Oslo, Norway. It is one of the more picturesque areas of Oslo. Østmarka forest and lake Nøklevann in its immediately vicinity. Infrastructure-wise, it has a shopping center http://www.bolersenter.no/, two elementary schools, a public bath, a library, a church and a stop on the Østensjøbanen subway line. 

==History==

The name Bøler comes from Old Norse bæl (farm).[tpl]cite encyclopedia|year=2000|title=Bøler|encyclopedia=Oslo byleksikon|edition=4|editor=Tvedt, Knut Are|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|pages=92|isbn=82-573-0815-3[/tpl] The area was mostly built up in the late 1950s, with high-rise buildings and smaller houses.
Bøler was a separate borough of the city of Oslo, Norway from the mid-1980s up to December 31, 2003, when it became part of the borough of Østensjø.
