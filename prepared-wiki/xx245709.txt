[[Denjirō Ōkōchi]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese male film actors, 1898 births, 1962 deaths, People from Fukuoka Prefecture, Japanese male silent film actors

[tpl]nihongo|Denjirō Ōkōchi|大河内 傳次郎|Ōkōchi Denjirō|5 February 1898 – 18 July 1962[/tpl] was a Japanese film actor most famous for starring roles in jidaigeki directed by such masters as Akira Kurosawa, Daisuke Itō, Sadao Yamanaka, Teinosuke Kinugasa, Hiroshi Inagaki and Masahiro Makino. His real name was Masuo Ōbe.[tpl]cite web|url=http://kotobank.jp/word/%E5%A4%A7%E6%B2%B3%E5%86%85%E4%BC%9D%E6%AC%A1%E9%83%8E|title=Ōkōchi Denjirō|work=Nihon jinmei daijiten|publisher=Kōdansha|language=Japanese|accessdate=14 November 2010[/tpl] Training in Shinkokugeki under Shōjirō Sawada, Ōkōchi entered Nikkatsu in 1925 and soon came to fame in chanbara films playing characters such as Chūji Kunisada and Sazen Tange. At his peak, he was one of the top jidaigeki stars alongside Tsumasaburō Bandō and Chiezō Kataoka. During World War II, he also appeared in a number of war films. His house and garden in Arashiyama, Kyoto, called Ōkōchi Sansō,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.japanvisitor.com/index.php?cID=431&pID=1971|title=Okochi Sanso Villa|work=Japan Visitor|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] are still preserved and open to the public.
