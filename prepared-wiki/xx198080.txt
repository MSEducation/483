Fernando Lopes dos Santos Varela (born 26 November 1987) is a Cape Verdean professional footballer who plays for FC Steaua București in Liga I and the Cape Verde national football team, as a defender.

==Club career==

===Portugal===

Born in Cascais, Varela started playing professionally with G.D. Estoril Praia in the second division, being loaned to lowly U.D. Rio Maior for one year during his spell. In January 2009 he moved to C.D. Trofense, as the northern club was playing for the first time in the top flight; he managed to appear regularly during the season, but the club was relegated.
In the 2011 summer, after two additional division two seasons with Trofense, Varela signed for C.D. Feirense, returned to the top level after a 22-year absence. In the following campaign he played in all league games as a starter, notably scoring twice against S.L. Benfica on 28 January 2012 (one in his own net) in an eventual 1–2 loss where he also committed a penalty,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.portugoal.net/index.php/more-liga-sagres-news/30982-benfica-comeback-sees-off-feirense |title=Benfica comeback sees off Feirense|publisher=PortuGOAL|date=28 January 2012|accessdate=6 June 2012[/tpl] and his team would again drop down a level.

===Romania===

On 29 May 2012, Varela signed a three-year contract with Romanian team FC Vaslui.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.record.xl.pt/Futebol/Nacional/1a_liga/Feirense/interior.aspx?content_id=759134|title=Varela no Vaslui até 2015|trans_title=Varela in Vaslui until 2015|publisher=Record|date=29 May 2012|accessdate=1 June 2012[/tpl] On 3 September of the following year he joined fellow Liga I side FC Steaua București, after agreeing to a three-year deal for a €1.25 million transfer fee.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.steauafc.com/ro/articol/transfer_varela/|title=Bine ai venit, Fernando Varela!|trans_title=Welcome, Fernando Varela!|publisher=FC Steaua București|language=Romanian|date=3 September 2013|accessdate=3 September 2013[/tpl]

==International career==

Portuguese-born, Varela opted to represent Cape Verde internationally, his debut coming in 2008. On 24 May 2010, he appeared in a friendly in Covilhã with Portugal – which was preparing for the FIFA World Cup in South Africa – playing the entire match as the minnows (ranked 117th) managed a 0–0 draw.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.usatoday.com/sports/soccer/2010-05-24-2037303516_x.htm|title=Portugal held to 0–0 draw by Cape Verde|publisher=USA Today|date=24 May 2010|accessdate=28 June 2010[/tpl]
The Blue Sharks were disqualified from the 2014 World Cup when it was revealed that Varela had played in a qualifying match against Tunisia, despite not completing a four-match suspension for a red card he had received against Equatorial Guinea on 24 March 2013.Equatorial Guinea 0:3 Cape Verde Islands; FIFA.com, 24 March 2013 That game was later rendered invalid by FIFA as a result of Equatorial Guinea fielding ineligible Emilio Nsue, but Varela's disciplinary record stood.[tpl]cite web|url=http://msn.foxsports.com/foxsoccer/worldcup/story/cape-verde-thrown-out-of-world-cup-qualifying-after-fielding-ineligible-player-during-win-vs-tunisia-091213|title=FIFA bans Cape Verde from World Cup|publisher=FOX Soccer|date=12 September 2013|accessdate=12 September 2013[/tpl]
