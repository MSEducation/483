[[Matthew Lessner]]

CATEGORIES: 1983 births, Living people, American film directors, American music video directors, People from Roseburg, Oregon, Chapman University alumni, People from Brooklyn

Matthew Lessner is an independent award-winning filmmaker.

==Biography==

Born in Walnut Creek, California,http://www.imdb.com/name/nm2038227/ Lessner was raised in Roseburg, Oregon, where he attended Roseburg High School.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.nrtoday.com/article/20080731/NEWS/273286736/1016/NONE&parentprofile=1057 |title= RHS Grad to film locally|author=Pallone, Cara|date=July 31, 2008|publisher= ''The News-Review|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl][tpl]cite web| url=http://www.filmstew.com/ShowArticle.aspx?ContentID=17462|title=Utopia Unplugged|date=August 1, 2008|publisher=Film Stew|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl] He graduated in 2005 from Chapman University, where he studied film.Dodge College alumni make Sundance debut

==Short Films==

Lessner's directorial debut was the 2005 short film Darling Darling starring Michael Cera, which screened at over 30 film festivals worldwide [tpl]cite web| url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0481503/releaseinfo?ref_=tt_ql_9[/tpl] including the Clermont-Ferrand International Short Film Festival, Comedia, and the Ann Arbor Film Festival where Lessner won the Tom Berman Most Promising Filmmaker Award.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.badlit.com/?p=642| title=Darling Darling|author= Everleth, Mike|date=August 16, 2007|publisher=Bad Lit|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl] The film is included on the sixth issue of Wholphin DVD magazine with alternate audio versions by John Cleese and Daniel Handler.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.wholphindvd.com/issues/wholphin-no-6/| title=Wolphin No. 6|publisher= Wolphin: A DVD Magazine of Unseen Films|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl] Darling Darling was included the Clermont-Ferrand International Short Film Festival's Retrospective 2014: the twenty-first century American short films.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.clermont-filmfest.com/index.php?m=213&c=178&n1=c&n2=%20&n3=1023&annee=2014&id_prog=100000182[/tpl]
Lessner's second short film, By Modern Measure, premiered at South by Southwest[tpl]cite web| url=http://2007.sxsw.com/film/screenings/film/F6890.html| title=Film Screening - By Modern Measure|date=March 2007|publisher=South By Southwest|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] and screened at 30 film festivals [tpl]cite web| url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0939597/releaseinfo?ref_=tt_ql_9[/tpl] including the 2008 Sundance Film Festival.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.sundance.org/festival/shorts/dto.asp?genre=Comedy| title=Film Screening - By Modern Measure|date=January 2008|publisher=Sundance Film Festival|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl] In January 2011 the Sundance Institute released "By Modern Measure" on The YouTube Screening room as part of its Sundance Film Festival Classics series.http://www.sundance.org/press-center/release/sundance-institute-youtube-and-acura-to-make-12-short-films/
His third short film, Chapel Perilous featuring Sun Araw was an official selection at the 2014 Sundance Film Festival http://filmguide.sundance.org/film/14045/chapel_perilous where it won the Short Film Audience Award.http://www.sundance.org/festival/stories/award-winners/ The film premiered internationally at the Stockholm Film Festival.http://www.stockholmfilmfestival.se/en/festival/2013/film/chapel_perilous In March of 2014 Chapel Perilous was selected as a Vimeo Staff Pick.https://vimeo.com/89202310

==Feature Films==

In August 2008, Lessner began work on his first feature film The Woods, which was filmed in the woods around Lookingglass, Oregon. The Woods was invited to world premiere at the 2011 Sundance Film Festival.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.sundance.org/press-center/release/2011-sundance-film-festival-announces-films-in-next-spotlight-new-frontier/| title=Film Screening - The Woods|date=December 2010|publisher=Sundance Film Festival|accessdate=2010-12-03[/tpl] The film made history as the first film to premiere at the Sundance Film Festival that used Kickstarter for production financing.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/blogs/risky-business/sundance-woods-unique-financing-model-75710 | work=The Hollywood Reporter | first=Daniel | last=Miller | title=SUNDANCE: How 'The Woods' ' Unique Financing Model Could Shake Up the Festival[/tpl] The Woods premiered in New York at the BAMcinemaFest held at the Brooklyn Academy of Music .http://www.bam.org/view.aspx?pid=3268 The Woods premiered internationally at the Cologne Conference in Cologne, Germany.http://www.cologne-conference.de/en/reception/sections/look/the-woods/
In August 2011, Lessner was awarded a San Francisco Film Society and Kenneth Rainin Foundation Filmmaking Grant for screenwriting in support of feature film tentatively titled Terror Tuesday.http://sffs.org/content.aspx?catid=22,37&pageid=2348 In May 2012, Lessner was awarded an additional San Francisco Film Society and Kenneth Rainin Foundation Filmmaking Grant for development of the feature tentatively titled Terror Tuesday.http://sffs.org/content.aspx?catid=22,37&pageid=2927

==Music video==

Lessner directed the music video for the Dirty Projectors' single "Stillness is the Move" which was ranked one of the best music videos of 2009 by Stereogum.[tpl]cite web| url=http://pitchfork.com/news/35813-directors-cut-dirty-projectors-stillness-is-the-move/|title=Director's Cut: Dirty Projectors: "Stillness is the Move"|author=Breihan, Tom|date=July 16, 2009|publisher=Pitchfork Media|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl][tpl]cite web| url=http://stereogum.com/104541/best_music_video_2009/the-gummy-awards/[/tpl] He also directed the video Fool's Gold's "Surprise Hotel".[tpl]cite web| url=http://pitchfork.com/news/36592-video-premiere-fools-gold-surprise-hotel-new-version/| title=Video Premiere: Fool's Gold: "Surprise Hotel" (New Version)|author= Breihan, Tom|date=September 24, 2009|publisher=Pitchfork Media|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl]
