[[Cherryville, Oregon]]

CATEGORIES: Unincorporated communities in Clackamas County, Oregon, 1884 establishments in Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Oregon

Cherryville is an unincorporated community in Clackamas County, Oregon, United States.[tpl]cite gnis |id= 1139605 |name= Cherryville |entrydate= November 28, 1980 |accessdate= 2010-12-30[/tpl] It is about six miles east of Sandy on U.S. Route 26,[tpl]cite book |title= Oregon Atlas & Gazetteer |edition= 7th |year= 2008 |publisher= DeLorme |location= Yarmouth, Maine |isbn= 0-89933-347-8 |page= 29[/tpl] near the route of the Barlow Road.[tpl]cite web |url=http://ncbible.org/nwh/orhistdd.html#cherryville |work= Pioneer History to about 1900: Churches of Christ & Christian Churches in the Pacific Northwest |title= Clackamas County, Oregon |publisher= Northwest College of the Bible |accessdate=2010-12-30[/tpl]
The name of the community is said to have come from the wild cherries that grew in the area.[tpl]Cite OGN|7th|page=195[/tpl] Cherryville post office was established in 1884 and closed in 1958. In 1915 Cherryville had a population of 50, a public school, and a church.[tpl]cite book |url= http://books.google.com/books |title= In Search of Western Oregon |author= Friedman, Ralph |authorlink= Ralph Friedman |year= 1990 |edition= 2nd |page= 621 |publisher= The Caxton Printers, Ltd |location= Caldwell, Idaho |isbn= 0-87004-332-3[/tpl] The church building is now gone, but the church cemetery still exists.
