[[Marco Pezzaiuoli]]

Marco Pezzaiuoli (born 16 November 1968 in Mannheim) is a German football manager.

==Coaching career==

===Karlsruher SC===

Pezzaiuoli had two stints as interim head coach of Karlsruher SC. The first stint happened after Joachim Löw resigned on 20 April 2000.[tpl]cite news|title=Coach Joachim Löw tritt zurück - Becker ist der Wunschkandidat|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/startseite/227744/artikel_coach-joachim-loew-tritt-zurueck---becker-ist-der-wunschkandidat.html|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=20 April 2000|language=German[/tpl] Stefan Kuntz eventually took over the next season.[tpl]cite news|title=Stefan Kuntz wird neuer Trainer|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/startseite/228575/artikel_stefan-kuntz-wird-neuer-trainer.html|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=11 May 2000|language=German[/tpl] Pezzaiuoli's second stint as interim head coach happened after Kunz was sacked on 25 September 2002.[tpl]cite news|title=Stefan Kuntz muss gehen|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/startseite/272365/artikel_stefan-kuntz-muss-gehen.html|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=25 September 2002|language=German[/tpl] Lorenz-Günther Köstner was hired on 1 October 2002.[tpl]cite news|title=Köstner schon auf Spionagetour|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/2bundesliga/startseite/272681/artikel_koestner-schon-auf-spionagetour.html|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=1 October 2002|language=German[/tpl] He went to coach different teams with in the German Football Association[tpl]cite web|title=Marco Pezzaiuoli|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/1-bundesliga/2010-11/3096/trainer_marco-pezzaiuoli.html|publisher=kicker|accessdate=29 January 2014|language=German[/tpl] and was an assistant coach for Suwon Samsung Bluewings[tpl]cite web|title=Marco Pezzaiuoli|url=http://www.worldfootball.net/player_summary/marco-pezzaiuoli/|publisher=World Football|accessdate=29 January 2014[/tpl] in South Korea after leaving Karlsruhe.

===Eintracht Trier===

Pezzaiuoli was hired by Oberliga outfit Eintracht Trier on 20 September 2006 and given a contract to the end of the season.[tpl]cite news|title=Pezzaiuoli übernimmt Trier|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/amateure/amateurligen.html/355200/artikel_pezzaiuoli-uebernimmt-trier.html|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=20 September 2006|language=German[/tpl] Pezzaiuoli lost his first match in charge 6–5 to EGC Wirges.[tpl]cite web|title=Eintracht Trier|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/amateure/oberliga-suedwest-353/2006-07/eintracht-trier-116/vereinstermine.html|publisher=kicker|accessdate=29 January 2014|language=German[/tpl] Pezzaiuoli was sacked after five matches on 30 October 2006; losing three matches.[tpl]cite news|title=Pezzaiuoli nach fünf Spielen entlassen|url=http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/amateure/amateurligen.html/356944/artikel_pezzaiuoli-nach-fuenf-spielen-entlassen.html#omsearchresult|accessdate=29 January 2014|newspaper=kicker|date=30 October 2006|language=German[/tpl]  His final match was a 2–1 loss to Eintracht Bad Kreuznach.
