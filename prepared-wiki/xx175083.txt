[[Gmina Tomice]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Lesser Poland Voivodeship, Wadowice County

Gmina Tomice is a rural gmina (administrative district) in Wadowice County, Lesser Poland Voivodeship, in southern Poland. Its seat is the village of Tomice, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|3|km|mi|0[/tpl] north-west of Wadowice and [tpl]convert|37|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south-west of the regional capital Kraków.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|41.73|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 7,232.

==Villages==

Gmina Tomice contains the villages and settlements of Lgota, Radocza, Tomice, Witanowice, Woźniki and Zygodowice.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Tomice is bordered by the gminas of Brzeźnica, Spytkowice, Wadowice, Wieprz and Zator.
