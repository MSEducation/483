[[M73 motorway]]

CATEGORIES: Motorways in Scotland, Transport in North Lanarkshire

The M73 is a motorway in Glasgow and North Lanarkshire, Scotland. It is [tpl]convert|7|mi|km[/tpl] long and connects the M74 motorway with the M80 motorway, providing an eastern bypass for Glasgow. The short stretch between Junctions 1 and 2 is part of unsigned international E-road network E05, where it continues along the M8 through Glasgow.  To the south, the M74 motorway is also part of the E05.

==Route==

Starting at the M74 junction 4 (M73 junction 1) by the River Clyde, it proceeds north with Birkenshaw to the east and crosses the Whifflet railway line before meeting the M8 and A8 at a three-level interchange, west of Swinton. Immediately afterwards the North Clyde railway line and then passes alongside Woodend Loch before reaching junction 2A at Gartcosh.  There is then a further [tpl]convert|2|mi|km[/tpl] of motorway, before traffic exits onto the M80 westbound.  
The road is a dual 3 lane road between junctions 1 and 2, and a dual 2 lane road between junctions 2 and 3.  It is subject to the national speed limit.

==History and future plans==

Construction began in 1969 with the motorway opening between May 1971 and April 1972.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.iht.org/motorway/m73mamostat.htm |title=The Motorway Archive – M73 Dates Page |publisher=Iht.org |date= |accessdate=1 January 2012[/tpl]  Junction 2A was added at a later date.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cbrd.co.uk/motorway/m73/ |title=CBRD Motorway Database – M73 |publisher=Cbrd.co.uk |date= |accessdate=1 January 2012[/tpl]
In 2011, the northern end was extended as part of the M80 completion project.  This connects the motorway into the completed M80 at a new interchange.Official M80 Completion Website – Scheme Map (PDF)
