[[John Clarke (actor)]]

CATEGORIES: 1932 births, Living people, American male soap opera actors, American male television actors, Male actors from Indiana, People from South Bend, Indiana

John Clarke (born April 14, 1932 in South Bend, Indiana) is an American actor. Clarke was the first actor to play Mickey Horton on the NBC soap opera, Days of our Lives, beginning with the debut of the program in 1965 until he retired early in 2004 because of health concerns. He was replaced in the role by John Ingle, who left the program in 2006. 
In 1959, Clarke acted with Ida Lupino in an episode of CBS's The Twilight Zone titled The Sixteen-Millimeter Shrine. In the 1961-1962 television season, he was cast in  two episodes as Patrolman Joe Huddleston in Leslie Nielsen's ABC crime drama series, The New Breed.
His daughter is actress Melinda Clarke, who started out on Days of our Lives and later played Julie Cooper-Nichol on the television series, The O.C.. She currently plays Amanda on Nikita.
