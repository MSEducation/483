[[Joshua Hammer]]

CATEGORIES: 1957 births, Living people, American journalists, Writers on the Middle East

Joshua Hammer (born June 12, 1957) is an American journalist and foreign freelance correspondent  and bureau chief  for Newsweek and in Europe. While at Newsweek - he was the Nairobi Bureau Chief from 1993 to 1996, the South American Bureau Chief from 1996–1997, the Los Angeles Bureau Chief from 1997–2001, the Berlin Bureau Chief from 2000–2001, the Jerusalem Bureau Chief His articles have appeared in The New Yorker, Smithsonian, and many more.
[tpl]cite web|title=Joshua Hammer- Authors- The Atlantic|url=http://www.theatlantic.com/joshua-hammer/|publisher=The Atlantic|accessdate=5 April 2013[/tpl]

==Education==

He went to high school at Horace Mann School in Riverdale, New York.[tpl]cite web|title=Joshua Hammer - Official Site|url=http://www.joshuahammer.com|publisher=Joshua Hammer|accessdate=5 April 2013[/tpl]  He obtained his B.A  in English Literature from Princeton University in 1979 where he was Cum Laude.

==Family==

Hammer and his ex-wife have two sons together and he has one son with his girlfriend. The family currently resides in Berlin, Germany.[tpl]cite web|title=Joshua Hammer|url=http://authors.simonandschuster.com/Joshua-Hammer/19043478|publisher=Simon & Schuster|accessdate=5 April 2013[/tpl]

==Personal==

He and the photographer Gary Knight were kidnapped in the Gaza Strip in 2001.
