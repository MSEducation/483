[[The Engines of Our Ingenuity]]

CATEGORIES: NPR programs, 1988 establishments in the United States

The Engines of Our Ingenuity is a daily radio series produced jointly by KUHF-FM, Houston, TX, and the University of Houston.
The program airs nationally on many public radio stations, as well as other outlets.  The series tells the story of human invention and creativity, and the people behind it, in 3-1/2 minute essays. The series is centered upon engineering/technology, but ventures freely into mathematics, science, literature, medicine, music, art and other areas.

==History==

The Engines of Our Ingenuity was first aired on January 4, 1988.  It reached 2900 finished episodes in August 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uh.edu/engines/keywords.htm|title=Keywords for The Engines of Our Ingenuity|accessdate=2014-01-13[/tpl]
The series was founded by John H. Lienhard, retired Professor of Mechanical Engineering at the University of Houston,
[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.me.uh.edu/faculty/lienhard|title=Dr. John Lienhard, M.D. Anderson Professor of Technology and Culture, Emeritus|accessdate=2014-01-13[/tpl] and member of the National Academy of Engineering.
[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nae.edu/MembersSection/Directory20412/30769.aspx|title=Dr. John Lienhard, M.D. Anderson Professor Emeritus|accessdate=2014-01-13[/tpl]
The show is now done by Lienhard and a cadre of experts in several fields.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uh.edu/engines/contributors.html|title=Guest Episode Writers|accessdate=2014-01-15[/tpl]

==Awards==

Engines has received many awards including the prestigious Ralph Coats Roe Medal of ASME to Lienhard for developing the program[tpl]cite web|url=https://www.asme.org/about-asme/get-involved/honors-awards/achievement-awards/ralph-coats-roe-medal|title=Ralph Coats Roe Medal|accessdate=2014-01-15[/tpl] and the Ralph Coats Roe Award from the ASEE.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mechanical.asee.org/Roe_Award_page.html|title=Ralph Coats Roe Medal|accessdate=2014-01-15[/tpl]
