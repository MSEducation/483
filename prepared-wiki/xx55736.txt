[[Postal Reorganization Act]]

CATEGORIES: 1970 in law, United States federal postal legislation

The Postal Reorganization Act of 1970 was a law passed by the United States Congress that abolished the then United States Post Office Department, which was a part of the cabinet, and created the United States Postal Service, a corporation-like independent agency with an official monopoly on the delivery of mail in the United States. [tpl]USPL|91|375[/tpl] was signed into law by President Richard Nixon on August 12, 1970.[tpl]dead link|date=October 2013[/tpl]
The legislation was a direct outcome of the U.S. postal strike of 1970.
The first paragraph of the Act reads:[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.scribd.com/doc/49061423/Postal-Reorganization-Act-1970 |work=Public Law 91-375, An Act to improve and modernize the postal service, to reorganize the Post Office Department, and for other purposes |title=TITLE 39---POSTAL SERVICE |date=August 12, 1970 |publisher= |accessdate=January 28, 2012[/tpl]
The Postal Reorganization Act (at [tpl]USC|39|410(c)(2)[/tpl]) exempts the USPS from Freedom of Information Act (FOIA) disclosure of "information of a commercial nature, including trade secrets, whether or not obtained from a person outside the Postal Service, which under good business practice would not be publicly disclosed".[tpl]cite web |title=USPS: ZIP Codes are 'Commercially Sensitive' Trade Secrets |date=November 6, 2013 |work=The WebLaws.org Blog |url=http://www.weblaws.org/blog/2013/11/usps-zip-codes-are-commercially-sensitive-trade-secrets/ |accessdate=November 7, 2013[/tpl]
