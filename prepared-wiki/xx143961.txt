[[Dimitrios Rallis]]

CATEGORIES: 1844 births, 1921 deaths, People from Athens, Greek politicians, Prime Ministers of Greece, Greek people of the Greco-Turkish War (1897), 19th-century Greek people

Dimitrios Rallis (Greek: Δημήτριος Ράλλης; 1844–1921) was a Greek politician.[tpl]cite book|last=Smith|first=Michael Llewellyn|title=Ionian Vision: Greece in Asia Minor, 1919-1922|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=hgJGJHrlhwIC&pg=PA324|accessdate=29 May 2010|date=January 1999|publisher=University of Michigan Press|isbn=978-0-472-10990-6|pages=324–5[/tpl]  Rallis was elected to Parliament in 1872 and always represented the same Athenian constituency.  He became Minister in several governments and served as Prime Minister five times.[tpl]cite book|last=Clogg|first=Richard|title=Parties and elections in Greece: the search for legitimacy|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=kXm-cEJkvy4C&pg=PA4|accessdate=29 May 2010|year=1987|publisher=Duke University Press|isbn=978-0-8223-0794-5|page=4[/tpl] He last formed a government after the 1920 election and it was his cabinet that authorised the plebiscite that saw King Constantine's return to the throne. He died of cancer in Athens on August 5, 1921 at the age of 77.
Rallis was born in Athens in 1844.  He was descended from an old Greek political family. Before Greek independence, his grandfather, Alexander Rallis, was a prominent Phanariote.  His father, George Rallis, was Minister in Andreas Miaoulis' government and later Chief Justice of the Greek Supreme Court.  Dimitrios Rallis' son, Ioannis Rallis, was a Quisling Prime Minister during the war-time occupation by the German Army and his grandson, George Rallis, served as Prime Minister in the early 1980s.[tpl]cite book|last=Clogg|first=Richard|title=A concise history of Greece|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=H5pyUIY4THYC&pg=PA109|accessdate=29 May 2010|year=2002|publisher=Cambridge University Press|isbn=978-0-521-00479-4|page=109[/tpl]
