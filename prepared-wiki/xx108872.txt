[[Kevin Livingston]]

CATEGORIES: 1973 births, American cyclists, Living people, People from St. Louis, Missouri

Kevin Livingston (born in St. Louis, Missouri, 24 May 1973) was an American professional cyclist for eight years.

==Career==

Livingston rode six Tours de France, the Giro d'Italia and many of the European Classics, during a career with Motorola, Cofidis, US Postal Service and Team Telekom. He was one of Lance Armstrong's domestiques in the Tour de France and other races.[tpl]cite book|author=David Walsh|title=From Lance to Landis: Inside the American Doping Controversy at the Tour de France|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=zJ9IOdnm5mQC&pg=PA220|date=26 June 2007|publisher=Random House Publishing Group|isbn=978-0-345-50358-9[/tpl][tpl]cite book|author=Daniel Coyle|title=Lance Armstrong's War: One Man's Battle Against Fate, Fame, Love, Death, Scandal, and a Few Other Rivals on the Road to the Tour de France|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=EEtf6uAe6BUC&pg=PA154|date=17 March 2009|publisher=HarperCollins|isbn=978-0-06-174648-2|pages=154–[/tpl] His best result in the Tour de France was 17th overall, in 1998. He retired in 2002 and lives in Austin, Texas.

==Doping==

Livingston's name was on the list of doping tests published by the French Senate in 24 July 2013 that were collected during the 1998 Tour de France and found positive for EPO when re-tested in 2004.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cyclingnews.com/news/french-senate-releases-positive-epo-cases-from-1998-tour-de-france |title=French Senate Releases Positive EPO Cases From 1998 Tour De France |publisher=Cyclingnews.com |date=2013-07-24 |accessdate=2013-10-25[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://velonews.competitor.com/2013/07/news/pantini-ullrich-among-1998-tour-riders-positive-for-epo_296750 |title=Cipollini, Livingston among 1998 Tour riders positive for EPO — VeloNews.com |publisher=Velonews.competitor.com |date=2013-07-24 |accessdate=2013-10-25[/tpl]

==Post-Racing Career==

Following retirement from racing, Livingston became a spokesperson and supporter of the National Diabetes Tour de Cure. He also acts as a consultant to Medalist Sports, where he has served as Competition Director for the Amgen Tour of California and the Tour of Missouri.[tpl]cite news | title =PedalHard Training Centre Brochure | work =Kevin Livingston | work =Publicity Brochure | url =http://www.mellowjohnnys.com/pdfs/PedalHardMenu_1209.pdf | accessdate = 2013-11-21[/tpl] He set up the PedalHard Training Center, in Austin, Texas, which provides training and testing facilities.[tpl]cite news | title =PedalHard | work =Kevin Livingston Consulting LLC | work =Official Web Site | date =2006| url =http://www.pedalhard.com | accessdate = 2013-11-21[/tpl]
