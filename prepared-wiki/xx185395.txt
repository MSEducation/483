[[Just Like Eddie]]

CATEGORIES: 1963 singles, Song recordings produced by Joe Meek, Songs written by Geoff Goddard

"Just Like Eddie" is a song by recording artist Heinz. The song was his 2nd solo release after leaving the band The Tornados. 

==Background==

The song was a tribute to American rock 'n' roll pioneer Eddie Cochran.[tpl]cite web|last=Cole|first=Paul|title=Music: Bev's pick; Heinz Just Like Eddie - The Heinz Anthology (Castle)|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-159758538.html|work=Sunday Mercury|publisher=(Trinity Mirror via Highbeam Research)|accessdate=12 October 2012|coauthors=Daniels, Jack; Brookes, David|date=25 February 2007[/tpl][tpl]subscriptionrequired[/tpl] The song was produced and engineered by Joe Meek and was released via the record label Decca in 1963.Zak 2001, p.232. The guitar on the track was played by Ritchie Blackmore.Talevski 2010, p.264. The song written by Joe Meek's associate Geoff Goddard.[tpl]cite web|last=Clayson|first=Alan|title=The Joe Meek Curse; The record producer Joe Meek claimed to have learnt in a seance that Buddy Holly would die on 3 February. (He did, in 1959.) He himself died on 3 February 1967. The colour picture above, of Meek's proteges, was taken on 3 February 1999 - since when, the people in it have been dying at an alarming rate. Alan Clayson investigates|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-5133460.html|work=The Independent|publisher=(Independent Print Limited via Highbeam Research)|accessdate=12 October 2012|date=28 January 2001[/tpl][tpl]subscriptionrequired[/tpl] The song is Heinz's only successful song as a solo artist after it entered the top 20 in the UK charts.[tpl]cite web|last=Fountain|first=Nigel|title=Heinz Burt|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/news/2000/apr/11/guardianobituaries.nigelfountain|work=The Guardian|publisher=(Guardian Media Group)|accessdate=29 July 2013|date=11 April 2000[/tpl] The song peaked at number 5 on the chart. The B-side featured with the song was titled "Don't You Knock On My Door".Neely and Popoff 2009, p.309.

==Reception==

In his book, "Rock & Roll: Facts, Figures & Fun", Mike Evans said that it was a tribute record that was not opportunistic like others because it was released three years after Cochran's death.Evans 2005, p.32.

==Inclusions and covers==

"Just Like Eddie" was featured on two Decca compilation EP's following its release. These were titled "Various Artists: Thank Your Lucky Stars Vol 2" and "Various Artists: Ready Steady Go". These were respectively released in June and July 1963. It was included on Heinz's "Tribute To Eddie" collection in September 1963.Bloom 2007, p.365. The song was featured on the "Just Like Eddie" compilation CD released on 7 August 1995.[tpl]cite web|title=Just Like Eddie|url=http://www.amazon.co.uk/Just-Like-Eddie-Heinz/dp/B000024UGU|work=Amazon.co.uk|accessdate=29 July 2013[/tpl] In 2013, It was included on "Heinz - The Essential Collection".[tpl]cite web|title=Heinz - The Essential Collection: Heinz|url=http://www.amazon.co.uk/gp/product/B00CGC64HI|work=Amazon.co.uk|accessdate=29 July 2013|date=22 April 2013[/tpl] 
The song has been covered by the Finnish band Teddy and the Tigers.[tpl]cite web|title=Just Like Eddie (2007 Digital Remaster): Teddy & The Tigers|url=http://www.amazon.co.uk/Just-Like-Eddie-Digital-Remaster/dp/B001JYDTN6|work=Amazon.co.uk|date=26 September 2007|accessdate=29 July 2013[/tpl] "Just Like Eddie" was later covered by the British electro group Silicon Teens.[tpl]cite web|title=Just Like Eddie: Silicon Teens|url=http://www.amazon.co.uk/Just-Like-Eddie/dp/B001JQSEG6|work=Amazon.co.uk|accessdate=29 July 2013[/tpl]
