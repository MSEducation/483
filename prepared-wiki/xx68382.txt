[[British Chess Championship]]

CATEGORIES: Chess national championships, Women's chess national championships, Sports competitions in the United Kingdom, Chess in the United Kingdom, 1904 in chess, Recurring events established in 1904, 1904 establishments in the United Kingdom

The British Chess Championship is organised by the English Chess Federation. There are separate championships for men and women. Since 1923 there have been sections for juniors, and since 1982 there has been an over-sixty championship. The championship venue usually changes every year and has been held in different locations in England, Scotland, Wales and once on the Isle of Man. However, although the current series of the Championship has run since 1904, it has never been held in Northern Ireland.British Chess Championship Venues at chess.about.com[tpl]dead link|date=December 2011[/tpl]
The championship was originally open to citizens of any Commonwealth country and has previously been won by Mir Sultan Khan (India) and Abe Yanofsky (Canada). However in 2002 when the Indian R. B. Ramesh finished first and several other Indians took top prizes it led to many top Britons not competing in the 2003 championship and accompanied criticism that the British Championship was not serving the interests of British players. This, combined with the victory of Indian Abhijit Kunte in 2003, led to an announcement from the organisers that from 2004 only British and Irish players would be eligible to take part.

==London international tournaments (1862–1883)==

In July 1862, Adolf Anderssen won the first international tournament organized by the British Chess Association (BCF Congress), held in London. Second place went to Louis Paulsen, followed by John Owen. This was the first round-robin tournament.[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1851-99/1862London.html |title=London |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl]
In August 1872, Wilhelm Steinitz won the second British Chess Federation international tourney, held in London. Second place went to Joseph Henry Blackburne.[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1851-99/1872Londra.htm |title=Londra |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] The great London 1883 chess tournament was won convincingly by Johannes Hermann Zukertort (22 points ouf of 26) ahead of Steinitz (19/26).

==BCF Congress (1885–1899)==

In 1884, a new British Chess Association was inaugurated. In July 1885, Isidor Gunsberg won the first British Chess Federation championship in London. In August 1886, Blackburne and Amos Burn tied for first in the second British Chess Federation championship, held in London. Blackburne won the play-off. ==BCA Challenge Cup (1866–1872)==

The first British Championship was organized by the British Chess Association as an event at the 1866 London Congress.
A rule awarded the B.C.A. Challenge Cup permanently to a player who won two consecutive titles.
John Wisker accomplished this in 1872 by defeating Cecil De Vere in a play-off.
The British Championship was then discontinued until 1904.

==British Championship (1904–present)==

The current championship series was begun by the British Chess Federation in 1904.
The championship was not held in war years.
It was also not held in 1919, 1922, 1927, and 1930 as major international events were then being held in England. José Raúl Capablanca won the 12th British Chess Congress at Hastings 1919[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1900-49/1919hastings.htm |title=hastings |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] and the 15th BCC at London 1922,http://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http://www.geocities.com/siliconvalley/lab/7378/fide.htm&date=2009-10-25+09:50:00[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1900-49/1922londra.htm |title=londra |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] Alexander Alekhine won the 16th BCC at Portsmouth/Southsea 1923,http://www.webcitation.org/query?url=http://www.geocities.com/SiliconValley/Lab/7378/alekhine.htm&date=2010-01-17+12:05:40[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.anders.thulin.name/SUBJECTS/CHESS/CTCIndex.pdf |title=Name Index to Jeremy Gaige's Chess Tournament Crosstables, An Electronic Edition, Anders Thulin, Malmö, 2004-09-01 |format=PDF |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] Aron Nimzowitsch and Savielly Tartakower won at London 1927,[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1900-49/1927london.htm |title=london |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] and Edgard Colle won at Scarborough 1930.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.saund.co.uk/britbase/brit30.htm |title=BritBase: 1930-39 |publisher=Saund.co.uk |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1900-49/1930scarboro.htm |title=scarboro |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl]
In 1939 the championship was also not held as the British team was in Buenos Aires for the 8th Chess Olympiad. In that time, Max Euwe won an international tournament at Bournemouth 1939, played during the BCC.[tpl]cite web|url=http://xoomer.alice.it/cserica/scacchi/storiascacchi/tornei/1900-49/1939bournemouth.htm |title=bourne |publisher=Xoomer.alice.it |date= |accessdate=2011-12-17[/tpl] The women's championship was held in most of those years.
