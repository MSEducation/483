[[Mean down time]]

CATEGORIES: Failure, Reliability engineering

In organizational management, mean down time (MDT) is the average time that a system is non-operational. This includes all downtime associated with repair, corrective and preventive maintenance, self-imposed downtime, and any logistics or administrative delays. The inclusion of delay times distinguishes mean down time from mean time to repair (MTTR), which includes only downtime specifically attributable to repairs.[tpl]cite book |title=Reliability, Maintainability and Risk: Practical Methods for Engineers |last=Smith |first=David J. |authorlink= |coauthors= |year=2011 |publisher=Elsevier |location= |isbn= |page=22 |pages= |accessdate=June 12, 2012|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Ewh1jHEyr1AC[/tpl]
