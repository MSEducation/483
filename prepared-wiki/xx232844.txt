[[Ikkicon]]

CATEGORIES: Anime conventions, Recurring events established in 2007, 2007 establishments in Texas, Annual events in the United States, Conventions in Texas, Festivals in Texas, Culture of Austin, Texas, Festivals in Austin, Texas, Visitor attractions in Austin, Texas

IKKiCON is an annual three day anime convention held during December at the Hilton Austin in Austin, Texas. 

==Programming==

The convention typically offers live entertainment, musical performances, panels, screening rooms, vendors, video games, and workshops.[tpl]cite web | url = http://austin.ynn.com/content/top_stories/262845/-ikkicon--brings-thousands-of-anime-fans-to-austin | title = 'Ikkicon' brings thousands of anime fans to Austin | publisher = Your News Now | accessdate = 2012-05-09[/tpl]

==History==

IKKiCON was created in 2007, originally taking the date of Ushicon, and was held at the Woodward Hotel & Conference Center.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.austinchronicle.com/screens/2009-12-25/931303/ | title = Konnichiwa, Austin! Celebrating Japanese pop culture at IkkiCon IV | publisher = Austin Chronicle | accessdate = 2012-05-09[/tpl] Due to high attendance numbers, IKKiCON changed venues to the Doubletree Hotel for 2008. For 2009 the convention moved to the Hilton Austin. In 2009, voice actor Greg Ayres suffered a mild heart attack while attending the convention, and later thanked the staff for its response to the emergency.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2009-02-07/voice-actor-greg-ayres-hospitalized-after-heart-attack | title = Voice Actor Greg Ayres Hospitalized with Chest Pains | publisher = Anime News Network | accessdate = 2012-05-09[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url = http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2009-02-12/greg-ayres-releases-statement-after-mild-heart-attack | title = Greg Ayres Releases Statement after Mild Heart Attack | publisher = Anime News Network | accessdate = 2012-05-09[/tpl] For 2010 and 2010/11 the convention remained at the Hilton Austin, until for 2011/12 the convention moved to the AT&T Executive Education and Conference Center. The convention returned to the Hilton Austin for 2012 and 2013.
