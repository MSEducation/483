[[Monica Ponce de Leon]]

CATEGORIES: 1965 births, Living people, People from Caracas, Venezuelan architects, Venezuelan emigrants to the United States, Women architects, University of Miami alumni, Harvard University alumni, University of Michigan faculty

Monica Ponce de Leon (Caracas, 1965) is a Venezuelan-born architect and educator. Her interdisciplinary practice, MPdL Studio has offices in Ann Arbor, MI, New York and Boston, MA. Prior to establishing her own practice she was the founding partner with Nader Tehrani in the award-winning firm Office dA. Ponce de Leon is Dean and Eliel Saarinen Collegiate Professor at the A. Alfred Taubman College of Architecture and Urban Planning at the University of Michigan.
She became licensed as an architect in 2012.

==Early life and education==

Ponce de Leon was raised in Caracas, Venezuela, and immigrated to Miami, Florida, with her family after graduating high school. She took English classes and worked in a millwork shop before enrolling at the University of Miami, earning her Bachelor of Architecture degree in 1989. In 1991, she received a Master of Architecture in Urban Design from the Harvard Graduate School of Design, and held teaching appointments at Harvard University, Sci-Arc, Rhode Island School of Design, and Northeastern University while establishing Office dA.

==Career==

Prior to her appointment at Michigan, Ponce de Leon was Professor of Architecture, director of the digital lab, and acting architecture program director at Harvard. At Harvard she developed the first robotic fabrication lab in an architecture school in the United States. During her tenure at Harvard, she also held visiting positions at the Southern California Institute of Architecture and the Georgia Institute of Technology,http://www.graymatters.gatech.edu/tag/monica-ponce-de-leon/http:/ and has given over 60 lectures and symposia on her work, which has been published in over 200 publications worldwide. Alongside Tehrani, Ms. Ponce de Leon received both the Young Architects Award in 1997 and the Emerging Voices Award in 2003 from the Architectural League of New York. In 2002, she received an Academy Award in Architecture from the American Academy of Arts and Letters http://www.artsandletters.org/awards2_search.php and in 2008 she was named a United States Artist fellow.http://www.usafellows.org/fellow/monica_ponce_de_leon_and_nader_tehrani In 2007 Ponce de Leon received the Cooper-Hewitt National Design Award in Architecture, the first Hispanic architect to receive this honor. http://www.nationaldesignawards.org/award.asp?catID=ad&nameID=officeda Cooper Hewitt, 2007 National Design Awards in Architecture Design 
Her work has received numerous awards, including 13 Progressive Architecture awards, the Harleston Parker Medal (2002), as well as citations from the American Institute of Architects, I.D. Magazine, and the Boston Society of Architects. In 2008, the Macallen Building was named one of the Top Ten Green Projects by the American Institute of Architects Committee on the Environment.http://www.aiatopten.org/hpb/ AIA/COTE Top Ten Green Projects After the disbanding of Office dA in 2010, Monica Ponce de Leon established her own practice, MPdL Studio with offices in New York, Boston and Ann Arbor.
