[[Pettis Township, Adair County, Missouri]]

CATEGORIES: Townships in Adair County, Missouri, Kirksville, Missouri micropolitan area, Townships in Missouri

Pettis Township is one of ten townships in Adair County, Missouri, United States. As of the 2010 census, its population was 795.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/0600000US2900157224| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Pettis township, Adair County, Missouri| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=June 20, 2013[/tpl] It is named for former US Congressman Spencer Pettis, who represented Missouri from 1829 to 1831 until being killed in a duel.History of Adair County by E.M. Violette. Published 1911

==Geography==

Pettis Township covers an area of [tpl]convert|146.2|km2|disp=flip[/tpl] and contains one incorporated settlement, Millard. It contains seven cemeteries: Bragg, Canaday, Indian Hill, Indian Hill (historical), Shaver, Shoemaker and Stukey.
The streams of Elm Creek, Goose Creek, Hog Creek, Indian Creek, Sugar Creek and Turkey Run run through this township.

==Transportation==

Pettis Township contains one airport, Kirksville Regional Airport.
U.S. Highway 63 passes through the township.
