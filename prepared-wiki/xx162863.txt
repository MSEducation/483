[[Cresyl violet stain]]

CATEGORIES: Staining

The cresyl violet stain a basic dye, is a common stain used in histology. [tpl]cite journal |author=Burnett RA, Brown IL, Findlay J |title=Cresyl fast violet staining method for Campylobacter like organisms |journal=J. Clin. Pathol. |volume=40 |issue=3 |pages=353 |year=1987 |pmid=2435763 |doi=10.1136/jcp.40.3.353-b |pmc=1140916[/tpl] Free Full Text.
Cresyl violet is used to stain the neurons in the brain and spinal cord.  It is used to demonstrate the nissl substance in the neurons and cell nuclei.  In this role it is also often used as a counterstain to Luxol fast blue, which stains the myelin.
