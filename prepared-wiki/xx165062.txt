[[Jacob Tanzer]]

CATEGORIES: Oregon Supreme Court justices, Reed College alumni, University of Oregon alumni, 1935 births, Living people, University of Oregon School of Law alumni, Oregon Court of Appeals judges, People from Longview, Washington, Stanford University alumni, Grant High School (Portland, Oregon) alumni

Jacob B. Tanzer (born 1935) is an American attorney in the state of Oregon, United States. Prior to private practice Tanzer served as the 81st Associate Justice on the Oregon Supreme Court. He also served on the Oregon Court of Appeals, was a deputy district attorney for Multnomah County, Oregon, and worked for the United States Department of Justice.

==Legal career==

From 1959 until 1962 Tanzer worked in private legal practice in Portland. Tanzer then joined the U.S. Department of Justice in 1962, where he would remain until 1964. In 1964, while working for the Department of Justice he was sent to Mississippi as part of the Civil Rights Division investigating the deaths of three civil rights activists.Mahar, Ted. This incident involving the murders of Andrew Goodman, James Chaney and Michael Schwerner became the basis for the movie Mississippi Burning.
From 1965 until 1969 Jacob Tanzer worked as a deputy district attorney for Multnomah County, Oregon, the county where Portland is located. In 1969 he became Oregon’s first Solicitor General in the Oregon Department of Justice, serving until 1971 when he became the first director of the Oregon Department of Human Services. While Solicitor General he defended the state's position in the U.S. Supreme Court case of Apodaca v. Oregon.Apodaca v. Oregon, 406 U.S. 404 (1972). Tanzer remained at that agency until 1973.
