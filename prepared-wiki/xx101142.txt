[[Macewen's operation]]

CATEGORIES: Surgery

Macewen's operation is an operation for the cure of inguinal hernia,[tpl]cite book|author=Alexander Hugh Ferguson|title=The Technic of modern operations for hernia|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=qq-2eBK0dIMC&pg=PA226|accessdate=3 July 2011|year=1907|publisher=Cleveland press|pages=226–[/tpl][tpl]cite book|author=Philadelphia County Medical Society|title=Proceedings|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=s70DAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA72|accessdate=3 July 2011|year=1888|pages=72–[/tpl] developed by Scottish surgeon Sir William Macewen (1864-1924). 
It is performed by closing the internal ring with a pad made of the hernial sac.[tpl]cite book|author=Herbert William Allingham|title=Operative surgery|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=VUBQWnm6xQQC&pg=PA267|accessdate=3 July 2011|year=1904|publisher=William Wood & Company|pages=267–[/tpl]
