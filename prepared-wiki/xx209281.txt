[[Sloan Nibley]]

CATEGORIES: 1908 births, 1990 deaths, American screenwriters, Writers from Portland, Oregon, United States Navy personnel

Alexander Sloan Nibley  (June 23, 1908 - April 3, 1990) was an American screenwriter. He is the older brother of famed Latter Day Saint scholar Hugh Nibley.

==Career==

Born in Portland, Oregon to Alexander ("El") and Agnes "Sloanie" Nibley,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.koffordbooks.com/petersen/a.PDF|title=A Chronology of the Life of Hugh Winder Nibley|publisher=koffordbooks.com[/tpl] Nibely served in the Navy in New York in 1945 before beginning his career.[tpl]cite book|last=Nibley|first=Hugh |coauthors=Nibley, Alex|title=Sergeant Nibley PhD: Memories of an Unlikely Screaming Eagle|publisher=Shadow Mountain|year=2006|pages=297|isbn=1-57345-845-7[/tpl] Nibely served as screenwriter for over twenty films including Springfield Rifle, Carson City, the remake of The Golden Stallion, and Eyes of Texas (1948), a film starring Roy Rogers. He also wrote for numerous TV series, including Sea Hunt, Sky King, Wagon Train, and The Addams Family.

==Personal life and death==

Nibely married actress Linda Stirling in 1946 with whom he had two children, Chris and Tim.[tpl]cite book|last=Cline|first=William C. |title=In the Nick of Time: Motion Picture Sound Serials|publisher=McFarland|year=1997|pages=88|isbn=0-7864-0471-X[/tpl] He died on April 3, 1990 in Los Angeles, California.
