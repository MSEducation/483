[[Maurice Edwards]]

The Venerable Maurice Henry Edwards, OBE, MA (17 May 1886 – 26 April 1961) was an eminent Anglican priest in the second half of the twentieth century.

==Early life==

Edwards was born on 17 May 1886, he was educated at Ripon Grammar School and Queens' College, University of Cambridge.“Who was Who” 1897-2007 London, A & C Black, 2007  ISBN 978-0-19-954087-7

==Career==

Edwards was ordained a priest in the Church of England Crockford's clerical directory 1947-48 Oxford, OUP, 1947 in 1911. He served as Curate at Bedale, North Riding, Yorkshire, from 1911 to 1914. 
He was appointed a Royal Navy chaplain on 6 August 1914.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=28864 |date=7 August 1914 |startpage=6203 |endpage= |supp= |accessdate=2012-08-04[/tpl] In 1918, he joined the fledgling Royal Air Force Chaplaincy Service. He was granted the relative rank of squadron leader on 1 August 1919,[tpl]London Gazette |issue=32017 |date= 13 August 1920|startpage=8410 |endpage= |supp= |accessdate=2012-08-04[/tpl] the relative rank of Wing Commander on 6 August 1929,[tpl]London Gazette |issue=33531 |date=3 September 1929 |startpage=5720 |endpage= |supp= |accessdate=2012-08-05[/tpl] and the relative rank of Group Captain on 6 August 1934.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=34078 |date=14 August 1934 |startpage=5195 |endpage= |supp= |accessdate=2012-08-05[/tpl] On 10 April 1940, he granted the relative rank of Air Commodore and appointed Chaplain-in-
Chief, the most senior chaplain of the Royal Air Force.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=34831 |date=16 April 1940 |startpage=2248 |endpage= |supp= |accessdate=2012-08-05[/tpl]
From 1944 to 1947, he was based at the Rother Vale Collieries, after which he became Rector of Acton Burnell cum Pitchford, a post he held until his retirement in 1953. 
He died on 26 April 1961.The Times  Thursday, 27 Apr 1961; pg. 1; Issue 55066; col A Deaths
