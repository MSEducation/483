[[26665 Sidjena]]

CATEGORIES: Asteroids named for people, Astronomical objects discovered in 2000

26665 Sidjena (provisional designation: 2000 YF60) is a main-belt minor planet. It was discovered by the Lincoln Near-Earth Asteroid Research project in Socorro, New Mexico, on December 30, 2000. It is named after Siddhartha Jena, an American high school student whose medical research project won first place at the 2010 Intel International Science and Engineering Fair.
