[[Mark Green (Tennessee politician)]]

CATEGORIES: United States Military Academy alumni, United States Army Rangers, Tennessee State Senators, Tennessee Republicans, People from Clarksville, Tennessee, Wright State University alumni, American military personnel of the War in Afghanistan (2001–present), American military personnel of the Iraq War, Writers from Tennessee, Living people, Year of birth missing (living people)

Mark Green is an American politician from the state of Tennessee. He is a member of the Tennessee Senate and the Republican Party. Prior to running for office, Green was a member of the US Army. He served as an emergency medicine physician in Operation Red Dawn, which saw the capture of Saddam Hussein.

==Military career==

Green graduated from West Point Academy in 1986. From 1987 through 1990, Green was stationed at Fort Knox.  He later served with the 82nd Airborne Division and the 160th Special Operations Aviation Regiment.
Green decided to become a doctor after his father spent 45 days in an intensive care unit.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.lebanondemocrat.com/article/tldnews/186915?topstories=|title=State senator talks about his role in Saddam Hussein's capture|first=Laurie|last=Everett|work=Lebanon Democrat|date=July 26, 2013|accessdate=August 13, 2013[/tpl] He obtained his medical degree from the Boonshoft School of Medicine at Wright State University in 1999.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wright.edu/cgibin/news_item.cgi?730 |title=Wright State University Communications and Marketing |publisher=Wright.edu |date= |accessdate=August 13, 2013[/tpl] He completed his residency in emergency medicine in 2002. He then served as a medic in the United States Army Rangers, including tours of duty in the Afghanistan War and Iraq War. He was the special operations flight surgeon during Operation Red Dawn, the military operation that captured Saddam Hussein. Green sat with Hussein for the first 24 hours of his capture,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thechronicleofmtjuliet.com/news/9066-senator-green-to-speak-to-wilson-county-conservative-republicans |title=Senator Green to speak to Wilson County Conservative Republicans |publisher=Thechronicleofmtjuliet.com |date=July 23, 2013 |accessdate=August 13, 2013[/tpl] and interrogated him for six hours. Following his military service, he authored a book, titled A Night With Saddam, detailing the capture of Hussein and the medical care Green administered.[tpl]cite web|last=Bonecutter |first=Hank |url=http://www.clarksvilleonline.com/2011/11/22/mark-green-to-run-for-state-senate/ |title=Mark Green to run for State Senate » Clarksville, TN Online |publisher=Clarksvilleonline.com |date=November 22, 2011 |accessdate=August 13, 2013[/tpl]
Green left the military in 2006. For his service, Green won the Bronze Star, the Meritorious Service Medal, the Commendation Medal, Achievement Medal, the Air Medal, the Combat Medical Badge, the Air Assault Badge, and the Flight Surgeon Badge.

==Civilian career==

Green founded and served as chief executive officer of Align MD, a hospital staffing company.
