[[Aerial (Scottish band)]]

CATEGORIES: Scottish pop music groups, Scottish power pop groups

Aerial were a power-pop band who formed in the late 1990s in  Aberdeen, Scotland.

==History==

Aerial were a five-piece power-pop band formed in Aberdeen, Scotland in the late 1990s. In 1999, the band signed to London-label Fantastic Plastic Records[tpl]cite web|title=Fantastic Plastic Records|url=http://fpmusic.org/shop/AERIAL/back-within-reach|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]  and recorded and released two EPs, Signal[tpl]cite web|title=Discogs|url=http://www.discogs.com/Aerial-Signal-EP/release/1756295|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]  and Star of the Show[tpl]cite web|title=Star of the Show NME review|url=http://www.nme.com/reviews/aerial/2932|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]  in 2000.
Aerial played live across the UK in 2000 and 2001, making appearances at T in the Park, Glasgow’s Gig on the Green as part of a UK-tour with label-mates Astrid and Angelica.[tpl]cite web|title=Highbury Garage review, NME|url=http://www.nme.com/reviews/aerial/2718|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] Aerial’s debut album, Back Within Reach, was recorded by Duncan Cameron at Riverside Studios in Glasgow and released by Japanese label Syft Records in 2001.[tpl]cite web|title=Discogs|url=http://www.discogs.com/Aerial-Back-Within-Reach/release/3792101|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
Aerial announced via their Twitter page that they had returned to the studio to record a new album, to be released in 2013.
