[[EMD GP39DC]]

CATEGORIES: Electro-Motive Diesel locomotives, B-B locomotives, Diesel locomotives of the United States, Railway locomotives introduced in 1970

An EMD GP39DC is a 4-axle diesel-electric locomotive built by General Motors Electro-Motive Division in June 1970.  Power was provided by a turbocharged 12-cylinder EMD engine that generated |  poweroutput=[tpl]convert|2,300|hp|kW|-1|abbr=on|lk=in[/tpl].  This locomotive model was basically a GP39 with DC main generator instead of an alternator.
2 examples of this locomotive model were built for Kennecott Copper Company as 1 and 2, later sold to Copper Basin Railway as 401 and 402.
