[[Naas]]

CATEGORIES: Naas, County towns in the Republic of Ireland

Naas ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|n|eɪ|s[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-ga|Nás na Ríogh[/tpl], [tpl]IPA-ga|n̪ˠaːsˠ n̪ˠə ɾˠiː|pron[/tpl], or [tpl]lang|ga|An Nás[/tpl] [tpl]IPA|n̪ˠaːsˠ[/tpl]) is the county town of County Kildare in Ireland. With a population of 20,713,[tpl]cite web |url=http://census.cso.ie/sapmap2011/Results.aspx?Geog_Type=LT&Geog_Code=06015 |title=Naas Legal Town Results |publisher=Central Statistics Office |year=2011 |accessdate=5 August 2012[/tpl] it is also the largest town in the North Kildare Suburban region.[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2012[/tpl] Naas is a major commuter suburb, with many people residing there and working in Dublin. The nearby N7 Naas Dual Carriageway connects Naas with Dublin and the M50 Motorway. The M7 Motorway Connects Naas with the South and South West.

==History==

The Irish language name for Naas, Nás na Ríogh literally means Meeting Place of the Kings, as the place historically hosted meetings of pre-Norman Irish kings from the Kingdom of Leinster.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2010[/tpl] After the Norman invasion in 1169–71, some meetings of the Parliament of Ireland were held in the town.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2010[/tpl] Many of the earlier settlers in Kildare were Cambro-Normans from Wales, and so the medieval church was dedicated to Saint David.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2010[/tpl]
In the Middle Ages, Naas became a walled market town, occasionally raided by the O'Byrne and O'Toole clans based in the nearby area which became County Wicklow. Naas features on the 1598 map by Abraham Ortelius as "Nosse".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wdl.org/en/item/104/zoom.html |title=A Modern Depiction of Ireland, One of the British Isles |publisher=World Digital Library |year=1572 |accessdate=6 July 2012[/tpl] (It is worth noticing the "O Byrne" and "O Tolo" (O'Toole) names appearing prominently on the map).
A mayor and council were selected by the richer merchants and landowners; the mayor was titled the "Sovereign of Naas" and carried a ceremonial mace until the post was abolished in 1840.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] Because of its importance as a place for trading, public meetings and local administration, with its law courts, racecourse and the army's Devoy Barracks that closed 1998,[tpl]cite news |title=First ever class at Army Apprentice School to reunite |publisher=Leinster Leader |date=31 August 2009 |url=http://www.leinsterleader.ie/news/local/first-ever-class-at-army-apprentice-school-to-reunite-1-1937282 |accessdate=6 June 2013 [/tpl] it became known as the "county town" of County Kildare.[tpl]Cite book |last=Wright |first=George Newenham |authorlink=George Newenham Wright |title=An historical guide to the city of Dublin, illustrated by engravings, and a plan of the city |publisher=Baldwin, Cradock, and Joy |year=1825 |location=Dublin |pages=31 |url=http://books.google.fr/books?id=cGQNAAAAYAAJ&pg=PR31 [/tpl]
In the former Parliament of Ireland that was established in 1297 and ended in 1800, the constituency of Naas had 2 seats.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
One of the first battles of the rebellion of 1798 took place in Naas on 24 May 1798 when a force of about 1,000 rebels were defeated in an unsuccessful attack on the town.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] A leader of the United Irishmen, Theobald Wolfe Tone, is buried just outside Naas, at Bodenstown.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
In 1898, the Local Government Act established Naas Urban District Council (now Naas Town Council). Its jurisdiction had a circular boundary with a 2.4 km (1.5 mi) radius from the new town hall on the main street.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] Most of the rest of County Kildare is managed directly by Kildare County Council, also based in Naas since 1898, and which oversees the work of the Town Council.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
Today, Naas is the 15th largest town in Ireland.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] The town is planning new housing developments during the next five years with a projected population of 30,000.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]

==Places of interest==

Amenities in the town include: a library, tax office, a new Gaelic Athletic Association club, athletics club, a range of schools, Naas General Hospital, horse racecourse,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.naasracecourse.com |title=Naas Racecourse |publisher=Naasracecourse.com |accessdate=26 October 2008[/tpl] soccer club, tennis club, soccer club, hockey club, rugby club, two major nightclubs including the infamous 'Time', five-screen 3D cinema, several pubs, five supermarkets, county council offices, a number of hotels and the new Moat Theatre.
A large new public swimming pool and leisure centre opened on Carragh Avenue in 2009 and the old swimming pool site is now a public car park.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
The town has two Roman Catholic churches, one Church of Ireland church, and one Presbyterian church.The original parish church, St. David's Church, is used by the Church of Ireland.  The Roman Catholic parish church, the Church of Our Lady and St. David, dates from 1827.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] In 1997, the second Catholic Church opened in Ballycane on the east side of town and is dedicated to the Irish Martyrs.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
There are two racecourses near Naas. Punchestown Racecourse is just to the south east of the town, in the parish of Eadestown, and Naas Racecourse is about 1 km from the town centre. The annual Punchestown Race Festival is a major event for a whole week in April. The Oxegen music festival is also held at Punchestown in the second weekend of July.
Naas has one of the largest scouting groups in Ireland, with 2 Beaver nights, 3 cub nights, 3 Scout nights, a very large Venture group and a rover group.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]

Naas is also home to the most expensive boutique in Ireland the Emporium Kalu in the centre of the town.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] Naas boasts the largest Tesco Extra hypermarket in Ireland located on the Monread Road in the northside of the town. The Tesco Extra site will be further developed into a shopping centre with 18 more retail units including Costa Coffee, Argos and Boots stores planned for summer 2011.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]A Tesco Metro store is located on the Blessington Road. A second Aldi store is located on Monread Rd near Tesco Extra.
Recently[tpl]when|date=January 2011[/tpl] a lot of proposed stores and retail have been announced for Naas such as, Superquinn will be moving onto the Limerick Road along with plans for an Eight-Screen Cinema and leisure centre.
A new Penney's (Primark) superstore will be located on the old Superquinn site in the town centre.  Under construction is the new Naas Shopping Mall at Corbans Avenue which has seen work postponed due to the economic downturn and lack of funding.[tpl]Citation needed|date=August 2008[/tpl] Naas is considered[tpl]By whom|date=August 2010[/tpl] now to be the largest Retail Centre in County Kildare, largely as a result of its critical mass of bulky goods floorspace located in Large Modern Retail Parks in the Town's suburbs.[tpl]dead link|date=July 2012[/tpl]
The town of Naas is surrounded by commercial parks with stores such as Harvey Norman, Smyths Toy's, Clery's, PC World, Halfords and Heatons. The largest commercial park is located near the Newhall Interchange. A major business park is under construction at Osberstown.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.osberstown.com/index.htm |title=Welcome to Osberstown — Co. On 9 October 2012, the largest creation of jobs in the history of the Naas area was announced. Kerry Group is to set up a €100 million, 28-acre campus on Millennium Blvd at Millennium Park in the NW of the town. Construction started in August 2013 and hundreds of jobs are to be created during construction and when completed.[tpl]citation needed|date=February 2013[/tpl]

==Transport==

Naas railway station opened on 22 June 1855, closed for passenger traffic on 27 January 1947 and for goods traffic on 10 March 1947, and finally closed altogether on 1 April 1959.[tpl]cite web | title=Naas station | work=Railscot – Irish Railways | url=http://www.railscot.co.uk/Ireland/Irish_railways.pdf | accessdate=19 November 2007[/tpl] The railway station at Sallins is now used by many residents of Naas and the surrounding area for the daily commute to Dublin, with frequent trains throughout the day and travel times less than 30 minutes to Dublin city centre.
Naas is connected to other main towns and cities by bus services. The main carriers are Bus Éireann and JJ Kavanagh and Sons.Bus Eireann, Dublin Coach, Rapid Town Link and web site[tpl]clarify|date=November 2011[/tpl]Schedule JJ Kavanagh web site
The N7 Naas Road, which connects Naas to Dublin, was upgraded in 2006 with a six-lane carriageway with grade-separated interchanges. Plans have been laid out to construct a large interchange at the Osberstown-Millenium Park as part of the M7 upgrade.[tpl]cite web | author=ARUP Consulting Engineers | title=M7 Osbertown Interchange: Environmental Impact Statement | publisher=Kildare County Council & Naas Town Council | date=November 2008 | url=http://www.kildare.ie/CountyCouncil/NationalRoadsDesignOffice/M7OsberstownInterchangeMotorwayScheme2008/ | accessdate=27 February 2009 [/tpl] An orbital ring road is also being built; several phases have already been opened.[tpl]cite web | author=Kildare County Council | title=Naas Southern Ring Road Opens | date=March 2008 | url=http://www.kildare.ie/CountyCouncil/PressReleases/PressReleases2008/March/PressReleaseTitle,15088,en.html | accessdate=6 April 2009[/tpl]

==Education==

Naas has three secondary schools, St. Mary's, a girls convent school, the Christian Brothers School, for boys, and Pipers Hill College (formerly St. Patrick's Community College), a mixed school.[tpl]Citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl] A gaelscoil and the Irish Vocational Education Association (IVEA) headquarters are also located in the Piper's Hill campus. It also has primary schools, including the Convent of Mercy, a girl's school,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.stcorbans.com |title=St. Corban's B.N.S |publisher=Stcorbans.com |accessdate=6 July 2012[/tpl] a school for boys, Scoil Bhride, a mixed school and Ballycane, another mixed school teaching classes from Junior Infants to 2nd class and St. David's, a mixed school.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
Naas has a large public library which is located in the canal harbour area.Nass Library web site
