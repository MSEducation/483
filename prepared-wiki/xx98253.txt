[[Kona Hawaii Temple]]

CATEGORIES: 20th-century Latter Day Saint temples, Hawaiian architecture, Places of worship in Hawaii, Places of worship in Hawaii County, Hawaii, Religious buildings completed in 2000, Temples of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Oceania, Temples of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in the United States, The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints in Hawaii, 2000 establishments in Hawaii

The Kona Hawaii Temple is the 70th operating temple of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church). The temple is located in Kailua-Kona on the island of Hawaii and is the second temple built in Hawaii, along with the Laie Hawaii Temple. It is the sixth temple built in the Pacific Islands.

==Announcement==

The Kona Hawaii Temple was announced May 7, 1998,[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/31405/Locations-for-six-of-30-small-temples-given.html |title= Locations for six of 30 small temples given |date= May 23, 1998 |newspaper= Church News [/tpl][tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/35271/The-announcements-of-new-holy-edifices-bring-joy-and-tears.html |title= The announcements of new holy edifices bring joy and tears |first= Janet |last= Kruckenberg |date= Feb 20, 1999 |newspaper= Church News [/tpl] with a groundbreaking ceremony presided over by John B. Dickson of the Seventy held less than a year later on March 13, 1999.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.lds.org/ensign/2000/04/news-of-the-church/two-new-temples-dedicated-president-hinckley-visits-saints-in-pacific |title= Two New Temples Dedicated, President Hinckley Visits Saints in Pacific |date=April 2000 |journal= Ensign |accessdate= 2012-09-21 [/tpl] Approximately 1,200 people attended the groundbreaking ceremonies. Dickson, Donald L. Hallstrom, an area seventy, Hilo stake president John Sakamaki and Kona stake president Philip A. Harris each spoke at the ceremony. Dickson turned over the first shovelful of dirt, after which other onlookers, including many children, also participated.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/35392/Hawaii-edifice-to-help-establish-eternal-families.html |title= Hawaii edifice to help establish eternal families |last= Peacock |first= Joyce A. |date= March 20, 1999 |newspaper= Church News |accessdate= 2012-09-21 [/tpl]
The structure itself was constructed in concrete, white marble and some native materials. Architects used a simple classical design featuring a single spire, similar to other smaller temples constructed by the church at the same time.
Upon completion, an open house was held from January 12–15, 2000.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/36842/Open-house-dedication-dates-announced-for-temples.html |title= Open house, dedication dates announced for temples |date= Nov 27, 1999 |newspaper= Church News [/tpl]

==Dedication==

LDS Church president Gordon B. Hinckley dedicated the Kona Hawaii Temple on January 23–24, 2000.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/37099/KONA-HAWAII-Here-to-taste-the-sweet-refreshment-of-the-Holy-Spirit.html |title= Kona Hawaii: 'Here to taste the sweet refreshment of the Holy Spirit' |date= Jan 29, 2000  |newspaper= Church News [/tpl] Hinckley was accompanied to the dedication by Boyd K. Packer, Acting President of the Quorum of the Twelve Apostles, and Hallstrom.
More than 3,800 church members attended the dedicatory sessions.

==Temple facts==

The Kona Hawaii Temple has two ordinance rooms and two sealing rooms.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/37117/Facts-and-figures-Kona-Hawaii-Temple.html |title= Facts and figures: Kona, Hawaii Temple |date= Jan 29, 2000 |newspaper= Church News [/tpl]
The temple dedication was considered the first event in a yearlong commemoration of the sesquicentennial of the church in Hawaii.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/39050/150-years-in-Hawaii.html |title= 150 years in Hawaii |date= Dec 16, 2000 |newspaper= Church News [/tpl]
NFL Hall of Fame quarterback Steve Young married his wife, Barbara Graham, in the Kona Hawaii Temple on March 15, 2000.[tpl]citation |url= http://www.ldschurchnews.com/articles/46864/Steve-Young-inducted-in-NFL-Hall-of-Fame.html |title= Steve Young inducted in NFL Hall of Fame |date= February 12, 2005 |newspaper= Church News |accessdate= 2012-09-21 [/tpl]
