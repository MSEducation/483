[[Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association]]

CATEGORIES: Aviation in Maryland, Aviation organizations based in the United States, Political advocacy groups in the United States, Organizations established in 1939, 1939 establishments in Maryland

The Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association (AOPA) is a Frederick, Maryland-based  American non-profit political organization that advocates for general aviation.
The orginization started at Wings Field in Blue Bell, Pennsylvania. On 24 April 1932, The Philidelphia Aviation Country Club was founded at Wings Field. The country club was the location of meetings of members that founded AOPA.[tpl]cite journal|magazine=AOPA Pilot|title=Where it all began|author=Julie Summers|date=May 2014|page=30[/tpl] AOPA incorporated on May 15, 1939, with C. Towsend Ludington serving as the first president, AOPA's membership consists mainly of general aviation pilots in the United States.[tpl]cite Journal|magazine=Flying Magazine|date=August 1945|page=76[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|magazine=Air Progress|date=August 1989 |volume=51|page=62|first=Nick |last=Komons[/tpl] AOPA exists to serve the interests of its members as aircraft owners and pilots, and to promote the economy, safety, utility, and popularity of flight in general aviation aircraft. [tpl]cite web|url = http://www.aopa.org/About-AOPA/Governance/Mission-and-History-of-AOPA |title = Mission and History of AOPA |accessdate = 22 December 2010|publisher= Aircraft Owners and Pilots Association[/tpl] In 1971 the organization purchased Airport World Magazine, moving its operations to Bethesda, Maryland.[tpl]cite journal|magazine=Air Progress|date=September 1971|page=20[/tpl]
With 384,915 members in 2012,[ref]IAOPA Statistical Report
World Assembly, April 2012, retrieved 2012-08-01[/ref] AOPA is the largest aviation association in the world, although since 2010 it has decreased in membership from 414,224, a loss of 7% in two years. AOPA is affiliated with other similar organizations in other countries though membership in the International Council of Aircraft Owner and Pilot Associations (IAOPA).[ref]IAOPA Statistical Report
World Assembly, June 2010, retrieved 2012-08-01[/ref][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.iaopa.org/about-iaopa/index.html |title=International Council of Aircraft Owner and Pilot Associations |publisher=Iaopa.org |accessdate=2012-08-01[/tpl]
