[[France–Malaysia relations]]

CATEGORIES: Bilateral relations of France, Bilateral relations of Malaysia

France–Malaysia relations refers to a foreign relations between the two countries, France and Malaysia. France has an embassy in Kuala Lumpur, and Malaysia has an embassy in Paris.

==History==

The relations started after the Federation of Malaya achieved independence on 1957, although the first Malayan ambassador to France only arrived in Paris on 1959.[tpl]cite book|author1=Christoph Marcinkowski|author2=Constance Chevallier-Govers|author3=Ruhanas Harun|title=Malaysia and the European Union: Perspectives for the Twenty-first Century|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=VFrfBP52_1wC&pg=PA34|year=2011|publisher=LIT Verlag Münster|isbn=978-3-643-80085-5|pages=34–[/tpl] During the administration of Jacques Chirac and Mahathir Mohamad, the relations significantly improved especially in economics, politics and culturally aspects.

==Economy relations==

Many French companies has started investment in Malaysia mainly in technology sectors.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thestar.com.my/News/Nation/2013/07/30/Malaysia-continues-to-draw-French-investors-Latest-project-being-factory-to-produce-aircraft-compon.aspx/|title=Malaysia continues to draw French investors|author=Zuhrin Azam Ahmad|publisher=The Star|date=30 July 2013|accessdate=29 December 2013|archiveurl=http://archive.is/FM5ju|archivedate=29 December 2013|deadurl=yes[/tpl] Currently Malaysia has been consider by France as the second largest economic partner in ASEAN and there are 260 French companies operating which are ready to support all projects to turn Malaysia to become a fully developed country by 2020.[tpl]cite web|url=http://en.vietnamplus.vn/Home/Malaysia-France-expand-links-to-new-areas/20137/37220.vnplus|title=Malaysia, France expand links to new areas|publisher=Vietnam Plus|date=29 July 2013|accessdate=29 December 2013[/tpl]

==Education and culture==

Since 1960s, the French government has provided grants and scholarships to Malaysian students to study in France especially on science and technical fields. While in Malaysia, around 100 hundred French students go to Malaysia every year to study.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ambafrance-my.org/Franco-Malaysian-bilateral|title=Franco Malaysian bilateral relations|publisher=Embassy of France in Kuala Lumpur|accessdate=29 December 2013[/tpl]

==Security relations==

France are the one of the Malaysia main military partners in the supply of military equipment and defence services.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nst.com.my/nation/general/france-norway-offer-to-sell-arms-move-to-enhance-trade-relations-nations-keen-to-provide-malaysia-with-jet-fighters-copters-and-missiles-1.167201|title=France, Norway offer to sell arms MOVE TO ENHANCE TRADE RELATIONS: Nations keen to provide Malaysia with jet-fighters, copters and missiles|author=Nik Imran Abdullah|publisher=New Straits Times|date=6 November 2012|accessdate=29 December 2013|archiveurl=http://archive.is/SkMUU|archivedate=29 December 2013|deadurl=yes[/tpl] Since 1990s, both countries has strived to consolidate defence relations, a large numbers of French military industries has began to establish cooperation with the Malaysian partners. One of the example are the French naval shipbuilding company, the DCNS. In 2002, two Malaysian submarine are build in France and Spanish shipyards which then delivered in Malaysia in 2009 and early 2010. Other French companies also has been actively taking part on the Langkawi International Maritime and Air Show (LIMA) and in DSA exhibitions. Initially, the French-Malaysian defence relationship was based on large military programmes of arms procurement.
