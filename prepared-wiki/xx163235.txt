==Early life==

Riley was born in 1866 in Yorkville, Ontario. He owned a large sum of land that he sold off to the City of Calgary in 1904. The land was developed into the historical Hillhurst-Sunnyside community northwest of Downtown.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www3.telus.net/public/hsca1/H_S_Remembers_1978.pdf | title = Hillhurst-Sunnyside remembers | publisher = Hillhurst-Sunnyside Community Association | year= 1978 | author = Margaret Tanko | accessdate = 2007-08-01[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]

==1906 by-election==

Riley ran for the Legislative Assembly of Alberta in a by-election for the Alberta Liberal Party on December 7, 1906. He would be re-elected to a second term in the 1909 Alberta general election.

==Resignation and defeat==

After serving a year into his second term he resigned his seat to protest the leadership of Arthur Sifton who became leader of the Liberal party in the wake of the Alberta and Great Waterways Railway scandal. Riley would run in the subsequent by-election on October 3, 1910 The Conservatives did not run a candidate in that by-election instead choosing to support Riley.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.ourfutureourpast.ca/newspapr/np_page2.asp?code=n27p0353.jpg | title = Liberals Stand Back of Riley | publisher = Calgary Herald | date = October 1, 1910 | accessdate = 2007-08-01[/tpl]
