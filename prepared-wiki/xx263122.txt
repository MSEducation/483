CATEGORIES: 1950 births, Living people, New Zealand musicians, New Zealand Māori schoolteachers, Ngāi Tūhoe, New Zealand schoolteachers

'''Turuhira "Julie" Hare, known as Turuhira Hare (born c. 1957) in the Bay of Plenty region, Te Urewera Park.
She is known for her many contributions and services to Māori. Turuhira’s many contributions in song writing and kapa haka story telling have been seen widely in the country and the Māori society.    
She is decent of Ngāi Tūhoe and Te Arawa tribes.

==History==

Julie appeared in the 2008 Vincent Ward documentary Rain of the Children.[tpl]IMDb name |2754430[/tpl] and in February 2009 won the title of the best female leader of the Te Matatini Kapa Haka Nationals 2009.[tpl]cite news |url=http://whakatanebeacon.co.nz/cms/news/2009/02/art10004319.php |title=Mataatua kapa haka teams taste success at national event |publisher=Whakatane Beacon |date=2009-02-29 |accessdate=6 August 2011[/tpl]
