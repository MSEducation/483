[[Atya patya]]

CATEGORIES: Traditional sports of India

Atya patya is a traditional India sport played nine to a side, between two sides. It is more popular in rural areas of India. It is more commonly played in Maharashtra a western Indian state.[tpl]cite book|author=S.r.tiwari|title=History of Physical Education|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=NVlG1Ekw4JgC&pg=PA200|accessdate=9 August 2013|publisher=APH Publishing|isbn=978-81-313-0041-1|page=200[/tpl] Atya patya is described as a "game of feints". The playing area comprises nine trenches, coming out of either side of a central trench, points are awarded for blocking progress across the trenches made by one side, other side blocks this progress.[tpl]cite book|author=Dale Hoiberg|title=Students' Britannica India: Select essays|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=-xzljvnQ1vAC&pg=PA110|accessdate=9 August 2013|year=2000|publisher=Popular Prakashan|isbn=978-0-85229-762-9|page=110[/tpl] It has been described as a game of "militant chase".[tpl]cite book|author1=Katrin Bromber|author2=Joseph Maguire|author3=Birgit Krawietz|title=Sport Across Asia: Politics, Cultures, and Identities|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=6LYZQyDBvRIC&pg=PA135|accessdate=9 August 2013|date=15 February 2013|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-0-415-88438-9|page=135[/tpl] The sport is played in a relatively small area and requires almost no equipment like other games indigenous to India such as kabaddi, lagori, kho kho, viti-dandu, circle Kho kho and langdi.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cultural.maharashtra.gov.in/english/gazetteer/Poona/PART%20III/Chap%20%283%29/Games.htm|title=Games |work=Gazette|publisher=Government of Maharashtra|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] 
The national governed body of the sport is the "Atya Patya Federation of India".[tpl]cite book|author1=Matthew Nicholson|author2=Russell Hoye|author3=Barrie Houlihan|title=Participation in Sport: International Policy Perspectives|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=A9K5PSr_e4gC&pg=PA151|accessdate=9 August 2013|date=10 September 2010|publisher=Routledge|isbn=978-0-203-87049-5|page=151[/tpl] The federation was formed in 1982.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.punemirror.in/index.aspx?page=article&sectid=2&contentid=20120617201206170911073316416ab00|title=Atya patya is set to make a comeback|last=Thite|first=Dinesh|date=2012-06-27|work=Pune Mirror|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] The ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, government of India, lists the federation amongst its list of recognised federation for the year 2013. The federation is headquartered in Nagpur. Its president is H. B. Hallad.[tpl]cite web|url=http://yas.nic.in/writereaddata/mainlinkfile/File1004.pdf|title=LIST OF RECOGNISED NATIONAL SPORTS FEDERATIONS (As on 16-07-2013)|date=2013-07-16|publisher=Ministry of Youth Affairs and Sports, Government of India|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] 
The first south Asian Atya patya Championship was held in Bhutan  in June, 2013. It was won by India.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/todays-paper/tp-national/tp-karnataka/schoolboy-wins-south-asian-atya-patya-championship/article4842234.ece|title=Schoolboy wins South Asian Atya Patya Championship|date=2013-06-23|work=The Hindu|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]

==2013 National Championship==

The national level Atya Patya tournament for 2013 was held at Bhatkal in Karnataka. Represented by 22 states & Union Territories, the event was organized jointly by Karnataka Atya Patya association Davangere and Bhatkal Atya Patya association 

==Controversy==

The president of Pudducherry Atya patya Association, Selvan refuted accusations levelled by Anbhagan, Member of Legislative Assmebly, Pudducherry, that fake Atya patya certificates were issued to take benefit of quota available to sports persons, in seats at medical colleges. He stated that the sport was registered in 22 states of India including in Tamil Nadu.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.chennaionline.com/sports/Atya-Patiya-is-recognised-game--Selvam/84a9aea9-50ec-44a4-833d-378c4ecbe6cd.col|title= Atya Patiya is recognised game: Selvam|date=2013-08-02|work=Chennaionline.com|publisher=Chennai Interactive Business Services (P) Ltd.|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] The Orissa High Court, in 1997 observed that; "The clear and categorical case of the petitioner is that she having represented the Orissa State team is the VIth National (Senior) ATYA PATYA Championship held in New Delhi from 22nd to 24th July, 1994 which is a recognised national event is entitled to be admitted in the 1st year Diploma in Engineering under Sports quota." giving relief to a petitioner who was deined admission under sports quota.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.indiankanoon.org/doc/331424/|title=Sasmita Palei vs State Of Orissa And Ors. on 30 September, 1997|work=www.indiankanoon.org|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
