[[Žarko Vukčević]]

CATEGORIES: 1957 births, Living people, Yugoslav footballers, FK Budućnost Podgorica players, FK Mladost Podgorica players, Zonguldakspor footballers, Süper Lig players, Expatriate footballers in Turkey

Žarko Vukčević (born 20 December 1957) is a former Montenegrin football forward who played in the former Yugoslavia and Turkey.

==Career==

Born in Herceg Novi, Vukčević moved to Titograd at a young age and started playing football for the youth side of FK Budućnost Titograd. In 1975, he joined the club's senior side and competed in the Yugoslav First League for nine seasons.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Pobjeda|title=Nove obaveze bivšeg golgetera|url=http://www.pobjeda.co.me/citanje.php?datum=2005-02-27&id=55690|language=Serbian|date=27 February 2005[/tpl]
He transferred to Zonguldakspor before the 1985–86 season, playing two seasons with the club in the Süper Lig. He returned to Yugoslavia and played one season for OFK Titograd and the following half season with FK Lovćen before retiring.
After his playing career ended, Vukčević became a director of FK Budućnost.[tpl]cite web|publisher=SCMN.me|title=Opšti podaci o pravnom licu: FUDBALSKI KLUB BUDUCNOST AD|url=http://www.scmn.me/fajlovi/FK%20Buducnost%20godisnji%202008.pdf|year=2008[/tpl]
