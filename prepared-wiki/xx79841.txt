[[Hal Blaine]]

CATEGORIES: 1929 births, Living people, American drummers, Jewish American musicians, Musicians from Los Angeles, California, People from Holyoke, Massachusetts, Rock and Roll Hall of Fame inductees, American session musicians, The Wrecking Crew members, RCA Victor artists, Dunhill Records artists

Hal Blaine (born Harold Simon Belsky, February 5, 1929, Holyoke, Massachusetts) is an American drummer and session musician.Allmusic performance credits He is most known for his work with the Wrecking Crew in California. Blaine played on numerous hits by popular groups, including Nancy Sinatra, Elvis Presley, John Denver, the Ronettes, Simon & Garfunkel, the Carpenters, the Beach Boys, the 5th Dimension, The Monkees, and Steely Dan.  He has played on 50 number one hits, over 150 top ten hits and has recorded, by his own admission, on over 35,000 pieces of music over four decades of work.  Blaine is a member of the Rock & Roll Hall of Fame, Musicians Hall of Fame and Museum and the Percussive Arts Society Hall of Fame.  He is widely regarded as one of the most prolific drummers in recording music history.

==Hal Blaine Strikes Again==

Hal Blaine Strikes Again is a rubber stamp used by Blaine to mark music scores and places where he has played.  Drummer and author Max Weinberg, in his introduction to the chapter on Blaine in his book, writes:
Another drummer, Mike Botts, then with the band Bread, recalled: “Every studio I went to in the late sixties, there was a rubber stamp imprint on the wall of the drum booth that said, ‘Hal Blaine strikes again.’ Hal was getting so many studio dates he actually had a rubber stamp made. He was everywhere!”

==Legacy==

Hal Blaine's drums can be heard as part of the Wall of Sound on the Ronettes' 1963 No. 2 hit "Be My Baby",[tpl]cite book|last=Whitburn|first=Joel|title=Billboard Book of Top 40 Hits: Revised and Enlarged|publisher=Billboard Books|location=New York|year=1992[/tpl] produced by Phil Spector at Hollywood's Gold Star Studios. Max Weinberg wrote, "If Hal Blaine had played drums only on the Ronettes' "Be My Baby", his name would still be uttered with reverence and respect for the power of his big beat."[tpl]cite book|last=Weinberg|first=Max|title=The Big Beat: Conversations with Rock's Great Drummers|publisher=Billboard Books|location=New York|date=1984/1991|page=75[/tpl]  Rolling Stone magazine listed the song as No. 22 on The 500 Greatest Songs of All Time.
In March 2000, Hal Blaine was one of the first five sidemen inducted into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame. (One of the other inductees was Hal's long-time friend and drumming colleague, Earl Palmer.)[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2012[/tpl]
