[[Justin Levinson]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American singer-songwriters, Musicians from Vermont, American male singers, 1985 births

Justin Levinson is a singer/songwriter from Vermont.

==Music career==

The son of singer/songwriter Bob Levinson, Justin Levinson grew up in Vergennes, VT.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boblevinson.com/bio.cfm |title=Bio |publisher=Bob Levinson |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.7dvt.com/2008/justin-levinson-bury-your-love |title=Justin Levinson, Bury Your Love | Seven Days |publisher=7dvt.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] In high school, he was introduced to Lester Bowie and Fontella Bass, who were traveling on a grant to bring jazz to rural areas. He attended Berklee College of Music in Boston, accepted in part due to a letter of recommendation from Bowie.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hybridmagazine.com/reviews/0607/justinlevinson.shtml |title=REVIEWS | Justin Levinson - 1175 Boylston album review |publisher=Hybridmagazine.Com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url= http://www.chargerbulletin.com/2014/02/12/livewells-latest-21/ |title=The Charger Bulletin » Livewell’s Latest |first=Shannon |last=Livewell |work=chargerbulletin.com |year=2014 |quote=Lester Bowie and Motown legend Fontella Bass. They were both on a grant to bring jazz to rural areas and they happened to come to Vergennes, VT, where I was in school |accessdate=April 17, 2014[/tpl] He started out studying jazz trumpet, before switching to and getting his degree in songwriting.[tpl]cite web|url=http://upstatelive.com/2013/02/13/interview-with-burlington-vermont-based-singer-songwriter-justin-levinson-playing-the-westcott-theater-on-february-24th-3/ |title=Interview with Burlington, Vermont based singer-songwriter, Justin Levinson, playing The Westcott Theater on February 24th |publisher=Upstatelive.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]
On his earlier releases, Levinson's sound has drawn many comparisons to Ben Folds.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.myfoxboston.com/story/17768997/justin-levinson-performs-on-fox25 |title=Justin Levinson performs on FOX25 - Boston News, Weather, Sports | FOX 25 |publisher=MyFoxBoston |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boston.com/news/necn/Shows/gml/ |title=NECN Shows - News |publisher=Boston.com |date=2006-08-22 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]

===1175 Boylston===

Levinson released his first album, 1175 Boylston, in 2006, while a student at Berklee. On the album (which was named after Levinson's address at the time), he supplied the vocals and played piano, trumpet and trombone while his father played guitar, bass and drums on some tracks, with fellow Berklee student Adam Popick supplying the remaining instruments.[tpl]cite web|url=http://tech.mit.edu/V126/N31/31Levinson.html |title=CD REVIEW: ...1175 Boylston... Impressive Debut for Berklee Student - The Tech |publisher=Tech.mit.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] The single "City With Two Streetlights" spent eight weeks on the CMJ Top 25 charts.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vermontbiz.com/event/may/maritime-festival-announces-free-concert-music-series-aug-101112 |title=Maritime Festival Announces FREE Concert Music Series (Aug 10,11,12) | Vermont Business Magazine |publisher=Vermontbiz.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] Faculty members of the Berklee songwriting department also voted the album's opening track “Sunny Day” the best song of the year.[tpl]cite web|url=https://www.wacbiz.com/Justin-Levinson/artist/190 |title=Justin Levinson |publisher=Wacbiz.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] Following the album's release, Levinson played the 2006 International Pop Overthrow Festival, and was named Best Male Artist at The 4th Annual IAMA (International Acoustic Music Awards).[tpl]cite web|author=rob v |url=http://www.cheapthrillsboston.net/2006/10/international-pop-overthrow-2006.html |title=Cheap Thrills - Boston: International Pop Overthrow 2006 |publisher=Cheapthrillsboston.net |date=2006-10-31 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.inacoustic.com/artists.html |title=IAMA | International Acoustic Music Awards | Past Featured Artists |publisher=Inacoustic.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]

===Bury Your Love===

In 2007, Levinson, by then a graduate of Berklee, released his second album, Bury Your Love. Many reviews of this record noted the influence of Elton John.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.notlame.com/Browse_by_Artist/JUSTIN_LEVINSON/Page_88/CDLEVINSON2.html |title=JUSTIN LEVINSON - Bury Your Love (mini-CD) :: Not Lame Recordings - power pop and melodic rock record label, music and CDs |publisher=Notlame.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.powerpopaholic.com/2008/02/justin-levinson-bury-your-love.html |title=Justin Levinson "Bury Your Love" - Powerpopholic |publisher=Powerpopaholic.com |date=2008-02-10 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] The same year, he won the ASCAPLUS award and was a finalist in the USA Songwriting Competition.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.njfolkfest.rutgers.edu/SSwinners_2011.html |title=New Jersey Folk Festival |publisher=Njfolkfest.rutgers.edu |date=2011-12-15 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ascap.com/members/ascaplus.aspx |title=ASCAP Plus Awards Information |publisher=Ascap.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]

===Predetermined Fate===

Levinson's 2009 release, Predetermined Fate, had a more country-influenced style.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2009/11/19/music_listings/ |title=Music listings - The Boston Globe |publisher=Boston.com |date=2009-11-19 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=Name * |url=http://www.skopemag.com/2009/11/20/justin-levinson-predetermined-fate |title=Justin Levinson, Predetermined Fate | Skope Entertainment Inc |publisher=Skopemag.com |date=2009-11-20 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] One single on the album, "Waiting For Someone To Love Me", was played heavily on Sirius, and he was nominated for the Sirius singer-songwriter discovery of the year.[tpl]cite web|author=Fri 4/6 - Doors: 8:30 p.m.. ===This Side of Me, This Side of You===

===Take My Time===

In 2013, Levinson released the three-song EP Take My Time.[tpl]cite web|last=Plain |first=Higher |url=http://higherplainmusic.com/2012/12/04/justin-levinson-take-my-time-review/ |title=Justin Levinson – "Take My Time" Review |publisher=Higher Plain Music |date=2012-12-04 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] Noted in reviews as a slight departure from his work with the Valcours, the EP took a more country-influenced turn on the third and final track, “Bid The Rest Goodbye”, which employs steel guitars and fiddles.[tpl]cite web|last=Haney |first=Shawn M. |url=http://performermag.com/justin-levinson-take-my-time/ |title=Justin Levinson – "Take My Time" | Performer Magazine |publisher=Performermag.com |date=2013-03-28 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.venturemag.co.uk/2013/04/17/justin-levinson-take-my-time-ep-review/ |title=Justin Levinson – ‘Take My Time EP’ Review |publisher=Venturemag.co.uk |date=2013-04-17 |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]

==Work with Champlain Community Services==

Levinson works for Champlain Community Services, an organization dedicated to help provide services and self-advocacy to developmentally disabled adults.[tpl]cite web|url=http://ccs-vt.org/?page_id=42 |title=Champlain Community Services » » Self Advocacy |publisher=Ccs-vt.org |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] He facilitates a show called The Advocacy Team, which airs on Vermont Community Access Media.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vermontcam.org/vcam%20schedule.htm |title=vermontcam.org |publisher=vermontcam.org |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl] The show features clients of Champlain Community Services interviewing local celebrities.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wcax.com/story/22411204/the-advocacy-team |title=The Advocacy Team - WCAX.COM Local Vermont News, Weather and Sports |publisher=Wcax.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-12[/tpl]
