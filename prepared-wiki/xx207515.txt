[[Cche]]

Cche ([tpl]unicode|Ꚇ ꚇ[/tpl]; italics: [tpl]unicode|Ꚇ ꚇ[/tpl]) is a letter of the Cyrillic script. It was used in the old Abkhaz alphabets, where it represents the voiceless retroflex affricate [tpl]IPA|/tʂ/[/tpl]. The letter was invented by baron Peter von Uslar. In 1862 was published his linguistic study "Абхазский язык".Бгажба Х. С. Из истории письменности в Абхазии. — Тбилиси. 1967. С. 34 The letter is Ҽ-shaped but in 1887 Uslar's study was reprint by M. Zavadskiy who change its shape and resembles a Cyrillic Ч doubled. Later the letter returned to its initial form, created by linguist Uslar, is part of modern Abkhaz alphabet, which is depicted as Ҽ.
