[[Data Protection Commissioner]]

CATEGORIES: Data privacy, Privacy organizations

In Ireland, the Data Protection Commissioner ([tpl]lang-ga|An Coimisinéir Cosanta Sonraí[/tpl]) is the person responsible for the enforcement and monitoring of compliance with data protection legislation, including the Data Protection Act of Ireland, 1988-2003. He is also responsible for investigating breaches of the legislation and prosecuting the senders of spam emails and text messages pursuant to SI 535/2003. Only one such prosecution has occurred to date.
In the UK, this function is carried out by the Information Commissioner.
