[[Zainal Abidin Ahmad (politician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1939 births, 2010 deaths, Cancer deaths in Malaysia, Deaths from brain tumor, Malaysian politicians, Members of the Dewan Rakyat, People's Justice Party (Malaysia) politicians

Datuk Zainal Abidin Ahmad (10 October 1939 – 25 March 2010[tpl]cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/3/25/nation/20100325221353&sec=nation|title=Hulu Selangor MP Dr Zainal Abidin dies (Update)|date=25 March 2010|work=The Star (Malaysia)|publisher=Star Publications (Malaysia)|accessdate=25 March 2010[/tpl]) was a Malaysian politician. He was the Member of the Parliament of Malaysia for the Hulu Selangor constituency in Selangor until his death in 2010. He was a member of the Parti Keadilan Rakyat (PKR) party in the Pakatan Rakyat opposition coalition.
Zainal Abidin was a member of the State Assembly of Selangor from 1990 for the UMNO party before being omitted from the ticket of the governing Barisan Nasional coalition for the 2004 election.[tpl]cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/election/story.asp?file=/2008/2/16/election2008/20295502&sec=Election2008|title=Uncle may make way for nephew in Kota Damansara|date=16 February 2008|publisher=The Star (Malaysia)|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] From 1995 to 1999 he served as Deputy Menteri Besar (Chief Minister) and on the Selangor Executive Council.[tpl]cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/news/story.asp?file=/2010/1/3/nation/5405441&sec=nation|title=Hulu Selangor’s Zainal rubbishes resignation rumours|last=Habibu|first=Sira|date=3 January 2010|publisher=The Star (Malaysia)|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl]
Zainal Abidin switched to the opposition Keadilan party in 2005.[tpl]cite news|url=http://thestar.com.my/election/story.asp?file=/2008/2/25/election2008/20433305&sec=Election2008|title=Fainting spell causes excitement at KKB|date=25 February 2008|publisher=The Star (Malaysia)|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] In the 2008 election, Zainal won the federal seat of Hulu Selangor, unseating the incumbent Barisan Nasional member by a narrow margin of 198 votes.[ref]
</ref>
Zainal Abidin died on 25 March 2010 from brain cancer, triggering a by-election in Hulu Selangor which was won by YB P. Kamalanathan of (MIC) - (BN). He is survived by his wife Datin Siti Zaharah Mohd Zin and four children.
