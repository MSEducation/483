==History==

In 1987, Gordon Clemons joined with Jim Michael and Jeff Michael, investors from Minnesota and founded CorVel Corporation (originally named FORTIS). Three small vocational rehabilitation firms were consolidated to form the initial foundation of the new Company, valued at approximately $2 million with over 200 associates.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl]
Within two years the Company expanded to 51 US locations and they entered the medical bill review and medical case management business.
In 1991 the Company first issued stock to the public and began trading on the NYSE at a market capitalization value of $43 million.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl] The offering raised $3.5 million, net of the repayment of venture funding.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl] The following year the Fortis name was sold,[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl] raising $4 million[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl] and the Company changed its name to CorVel Corporation. With the funding from the name sale, CorVel began building a national preferred provider organization (PPO).
During the mid '90s CorVel doubled its office network again, and expanded its software offerings.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2010[/tpl] PPO services became an important part of most healthcare management sales. Out of network medical review was added to the portfolio of provider programs.http://www.corvel.com/about_us/our_story.aspx
