[[Proxim Wireless]]

CATEGORIES: Companies based in San Jose, California, Networking companies of the United States, Wireless networking hardware

Proxim Wireless Corporation is a Milpitas, California-based company that builds scalable broadband wireless networking systems for communities, enterprises, governments, and service providers. It offers wireless LAN, mesh, point-to-multipoint and point-to-point products through a channel network. The company is a product of many mergers and acquisitions over the years.

==History==

Proxim Corporation was founded in 1984, initially headquartered in Mountain View, California.
Starting in 1989, it began to develop  radio frequency modules using spread spectrum technology.
Its first commercial product in 1990 used 900 MHz frequency bands. End-user products called RangeLAN were introduced in 1991 and 1992, reflecting their use as network interface controllers for local area networks (LANs). 
The first adapter using the Industry Standard Architecture bus and supported Novell NetWare.[tpl]Cite news |title= Net Notes |date= February 3, 1992 |work= Network World Fusion |url= http://www.networkworld.com/archive/1992/92-02-03netn.html |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
An initial public offering (IPO) on December 15, 1993 listed the company shares on NASDAQ under symbol PROX.
In 1994 the RangLAN2 products starting using 2.4 GHz bands, and in 1995 the RangeLINK product line was introduced.[tpl]Cite web |title= Prospectus for Secondary Offering |date= June 6, 1996 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Proxim Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/914027/0000891618-96-000847.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]

===Western Multiplex===

Western Multiplex Corporation had acquired Ubiquity Communication on March 24, 2000, near the end of the dot-com bubble.[tpl]Cite web |title= Prospectus |date= July 31, 2000 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Western Multiplex Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1112263/000094018000000900/0000940180-00-000900-0001.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
Western Multiplex, founded in 1979 in Sunnyvale and acquired in 1995 by Glenayre Technologies, had their IPO on July 31, 2000, as it was listed on NASDAQ under symbol WMUX, raising about $90 million.
In addition to the Ubiquity product line, Western had lines called Tsunami and Lynx product lines.
In March 2001, Western Multiplex acquired privately held WirelessHome Corporation which had been based in Long Beach, California since 1998.[tpl]Cite news |title= Western Multiplex acquires WirelessHome |work= Press release |date= March 22, 2001 |url= http://www.wmux.com/company/news/2001/032201Wireless.html |deadurl= yes |archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20011218175409/http://www.wmux.com/company/news/2001/032201Wireless.html |archivedate= December 18, 2001 |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
In February 2002, Proxim proposed to acquire Western Multiplex and continue to use the stock symbol PROX.[tpl]Cite web |title= Proxy Statement pursuant to merger agreement |date= February 25, 2002 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Western Multiplex Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/1112263/000094018000000900/0000940180-00-000900-0001.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
The transaction was estimated at a value over $200 million. At the time, Jonathan N. Zakin led Western Multiplex and Davic C. King led Proxim.
The merger was approved on March 26, 2002.
Later in 2002, Proxim acquired the Orinoco brand of wireless products of Agere Systems group after it spun off from Lucent.
After being warned of de-listed from the NASDAQ stock market, the company filed for Chapter 11 bankruptcy in June 2005, as Moseley Associates announced it would purchase Proxim's assets for $21 million.[tpl]Cite news |title= Proxim sells assets to Moseley, files for bankruptcy |work= RCR Wireless |date= June 14, 2005 |author= Kristen Beckman |url= http://www.rcrwireless.com/article/20050614/wi-fi/proxim-sells-assets-to-moseley-files-for-bankruptcy-2/ |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
However, a few weeks later, Terabeam Incorporated announced that it had bid $28 million for the assets without any proceeds to the previous stock-holders.[tpl]Cite news |title= Terabeam Grabs Proxim's Assets |work= Light Reading |date= July 20, 2005 |author= Justin Springham |url= http://www.lightreading.com/document.asp?doc_id=615993/ |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]

===Telaxis  to Terabeam===

Millitech Corporation, founded in 1982, originally developed millimeter-wave technology, but shifted to August 1999 to focus on point-to-multipoint wireless access  products for businesses, with Newbridge Networks their largest customer.
Based in South Deerfield, Massachusetts and led by John L. Youngblood, Millitech planned their IPO in September 1999.[tpl]Cite web |title= Form S-1 Registration Statement |date= September 27, 1999 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Milltech Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/712511/000092701699003312/0000927016-99-003312.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
Milltech changed their name to Telaxis Communications, and the offering was held in February 2000 using the NASDAQ symbol TLXS, raising about $63 million.[tpl]Cite web |title= Prospectus |date=  February 1, 2000 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Telaxis Communications Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/712511/000092701600000250/0000927016-00-000250.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
Telaxis, then with the product line FiberLeap, acquired P-Com XT Corporation of Campbell, California in September 2002.[tpl]Cite web |title= Form 8K |date=  September 9, 2002 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= Telaxis Communications Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/712511/000091431702000987/0000914317-02-000987-index.htm |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
Privately held Young Design, Incorporated, had acquired Zeus Wireless, and then merged with public Telaxis in April 2003 with the name changed to YDI Wireless in June 2003. 
Located in Falls Church, Virginia, YDI acquired Phazar Corporation (holding company for Antenna Products Corporation and others and based in Fort Worth, Texas) which had traded under the symbol ANTP, announced in October 2003 and finalized in December.[tpl]Cite news |title=YDI Wireless to Acquire Phazar Corp |work= Press release |date= October 30, 2003 |publisher= YDI Wireless |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/712511/000091431703003179/formrule425_55248ydi.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl][tpl]Cite web |title= Form S-4 Registration Statement |date=  December 12, 2003 |publisher= Securities and Exchange Commission |author= YDI Wireless Corporation |url= http://www.sec.gov/Archives/edgar/data/712511/000101738603000234/ydi_s-4.txt |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
YDI acquired software maker KarlNet on May 13, 2004, merged with Terabeam Corporation (founded in 2000) effective June 22, 2004, and used the name Terabeam for the new entity.[tpl]Cite news |title= The New Proxim |date= February 27, 2007 |author= Brad Smith |work= Wireless Week |url= http://www.wirelessweek.com/news/2007/02/new-proxim |accessdate= September 6, 2013 [/tpl]
