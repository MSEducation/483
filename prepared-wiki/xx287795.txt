[[Kimberly-Clark Health Care]]

CATEGORIES: Manufacturing companies based in Georgia (U.S. state), Medical equipment manufacturers

Kimberly-Clark Health Care provides products for infection prevention, digestive health, respiratory health, surgical solutions and pain management. It sells its products through distributors in the United States and internationally. The company is headquartered in Roswell, Georgia. Kimberly-Clark Health Care Inc. operates as a subsidiary of Kimberly-Clark Corporation.[tpl]cite web |url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=5853299|title=Contact Us|author= |website=www.businessweek.com |publisher= |accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl]

==History==

On March 26, 1872, Kimberly, Clark & Co. was established in Neenah, Wisconsin through the partnership of four businessmen—John A. Kimberly, Havilah Babcock, Charles B. Clark and Frank C. Shattuck.[tpl]cite book |title=Shared Values: A History of Kimberly-Clark |last=Spector |first=Robert |last2=Wicks |first2=William W. |chapter=Kotex and Kleenex |year=1997 |publisher=Greenwich Publishing Group.|location=Lyme, CT |isbn=0944641172 |page=28[/tpl]
The first recorded healthcare product by Kimberly-Clark was absorbent wadding, intended to replace cotton. It was used as a bandage for wounded soldiers in World War I. Army nurses adapted the material for menstrual use.[tpl]cite book |title=Shared Values: A History of Kimberly-Clark |last=Spector |first=Robert |last2=Wicks |first2=William W. |chapter=Kotex and Kleenex |year=1997 |publisher=Greenwich Publishing Group.|location=Lyme, CT |isbn=0944641172 |page=55[/tpl] Kaycel fabric for surgical gowns was developed by Kimberly-Clark in 1960 for use in field hospitals during the Korean War.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.cms.kimberly-clark.com/umbracoimages/UmbracoFileMedia/K-C_Timeline_umbracoFile.pdf|title= Historical Journey in the 1960s|author= |website=Kimberly-clark.com |publisher= |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]
In 1980, Health Care began to grow as it was separated from consumer packaged goods management and products, such as Kleenex, with a move to Roswell, Georgia, its current headquarters.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.cms.kimberly-clark.com/umbracoimages/UmbracoFileMedia/K-C_Timeline_umbracoFile.pdf|title= Historical Journey in the 1980s|author= |website=Kimberly-clark.com |publisher= |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl] Kimberly-Clark Health Care currently has offices in North America, Latin America, Australia, New Zealand, China, Hong Kong, Europe, Asia and India. It also sells its products in more than 150 countries.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.kchealthcare.com/about-us/facility-addresses.aspx|title= Contact Us|author= |website=www.kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl]

===Expansion===

Kimberly-Clark Health Care opened European Health Care operations in Brussels in 1991.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.cms.kimberly-clark.com/umbracoimages/UmbracoFileMedia/K-C_Timeline_umbracoFile.pdf|title= Kimberly-Clark Heritage|author= |website=Kimberly-clark.com |publisher= |accessdate=24 July 2013[/tpl]
In 1999, Kimberly-Clark Health Care expanded into new global markets with the introduction of medical devices, including Mic-Key Enteral Feeding Systems and Trach Care Closed Suction Systems.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.cms.kimberly-clark.com/umbracoimages/UmbracoFileMedia/K-C_Timeline_umbracoFile.pdf|title= Kimberly-Clark Heritage|author= |website=Kimberly-clark.com |publisher= |accessdate=24 July 2013[/tpl]

===Company acquisitions===

In 1998, Ballard Medical Product, a maker of disposable medical devices for respiratory care, gastroenterology and cardiology, was purchased by Kimberly-Clark for $764 million.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.deseretnews.com/article/671258/Kimberly-Clark-purchases-Utahs-Ballard-Medical-Products.html?pg=all|title= Kimberly-Clark Completes Acquisition of Ballard Medical Products |author= |website=Kimberly-clark.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]
That same year, Kimberly-Clark also acquired TECNOL Medical Products, Inc., a maker of disposable face masks and patient care products.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Kimberly-Clark+to+Acquire+Tecnol+in+Tax-Free+Swap-a019723037|title= Kimberly-Clark To Acquire Tecnol in Tax-Free Swap|author= |website=freelibrary.com |publisher= |accessdate=11 July 2013[/tpl]
Kimberly-Clark acquired Safeskin Corporation in 2000, a maker of disposable gloves for healthcare, high technology and scientific industries.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.chiefsupply.com/Kimberly-Clark-Safeskin-Purple-Nitrile-Gloves-Latex-free-Powder-free-Purple.html |title=Kimberly Clark Safeskin Purple Nitrile Gloves, Latex Free, Powder-free, Purple |author= |website=chiefsupply.com |publisher= |accessdate=6 June 2013[/tpl]

Also in 2009, Kimberly-Clark acquired I-Flow Corporation, a healthcare company that develops and markets drug delivery systems and products for post-surgical pain relief and surgical site care.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.manufacturing.net/news/2009/10/kimberly-clark-buys-i-flow-for-276-million| title= Kimberly-Clark Buys I-Flow For $276 Million|author= |website=manufacturing.net |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]

==Products==

Kimberly-Clark Health Care focuses on five categories of products.

===Surgical solutions===

K-C’s surgical solutions focus on protecting the healthcare professional during procedures and keeping the surgical environment sterile.
Products include:[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kchealthcare.com/products/surgical-solutions.aspx |title= Surgical Solutions |author= |website=www.kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]
Kimberly-Clark Health Care developed the MicroCool Breathable High Performance Surgical Gown. It has the highest AAMI level of liquid barrier protection against blood and other bodily fluids. The gown has a micro porous fabric, which utilizes two layers of nonwoven fabric to enclose a protective film layer that "breathes," to dispel body heat.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.infectioncontroltoday.com/news/2011/05/microcool-surgical-gown-meets-aami-level-4-requirements.aspx|title= MicroCool Surgical Gown Meets AAMI Level 4 Requirements |author= |website=www.infectioncontroltoday.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]
In 1978, Kimberly-Clark invented Kimguard Sterilization Wrap as an affordable, high-performance alternative to traditional cloth wrapping materials and metal containers. Since creating the original wrap, Kimberly-Clark made two improvements to the product, resulting in One-Step and One-Step Quick Check sterilization wraps.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.kchealthcare.com/products/surgical-solutions/kimberly-clark-sterilization-solutions/kimguard-sterilization-wrap.aspx| title= Kimguard Sterilization Wrap|author= |website=kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]

===Digestive health===

Kimberly Clark offers digestive health products for enteral feeding, diagnostics and endoscopy procedures.
Products include:[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kchealthcare.com/products/digestive-health.aspx|title=Digestive Health |author= |website=www.kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]
Kimberly-Clark’s Mic-Key Low-Profile & Mic Feeding Tubes are used in patients who require enteral feeding, can’t tolerate oral feeding, require gastric decompression and/or medication delivery directly into either the stomach, distal to the stomach, distal duodenum or proximal jejunum.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.mic-key.com/mic-keystar.aspx|title=MIC-KEY * Enteral Feeding Tubes |author= |website=mic-key.com |publisher= |accessdate=6 June 2013[/tpl]

===Respiratory health===

KimVent Ventilator-associated pneumonia (VAP) products provide critical care clinicians with airway management products, education and compliance programs.
Products include:[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kchealthcare.com/products/respiratory-health.aspx |title=Respiratory Health |author= |website=www.kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]
Kimberly-Clark makes cuffed endotracheal tubes for both adults and pediatrics. The device was introduced by the company in 2006 and is made out of micro-thin polyurethane.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.infectioncontroltoday.com/news/2006/11/kimberly-clark-launches-microcuff-endotracheal-tu.aspx|title=Respiratory Health |author= |website=www.infectioncontroltoday.com |publisher= |accessdate=2 July 2013[/tpl]

===Pain management===

Kimberly-Clark offers pain management products for Chronic Pain and Surgical Pain.

Kimberly-Clark Cooled Radiofrequency (RF) System is used to create radiofrequency lesions in nervous tissue, which can help to treat spine anatomy.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.kchealthcare.com/products/pain-management/chronic-pain-solutions/cooled-radiofrequency-products.aspx|title=Cooled Radiofrequency Products |author= |website=www.kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl]

==HAI watch programs==

===Not on My Watch===

In 2008, Kimberly-Clark Health Care launched a global educational campaign, Not on My Watch. Its purpose is to educate healthcare workers and support their efforts to reduce the spread of Healthcare Associated Infections (HAI) such as MRSA, CLABSI and Cdiff.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.healthleadersmedia.com/content/QUA-208383/KimberlyClark-launches-antiinfection-campaign.html | title= Kimberly-Clark launches anti-infection campaign|author= |website=healthleadersmedia.com |publisher= |accessdate=11 July 2013[/tpl]
Kimberly-Clark Health Care offers a patient-focused website for HAI prevention, preventinfections.com. It is an educational website for both patients and caregivers that includes information on how they can protect themselves from the risk of HAIs during a stay in the hospital or other healthcare facility.[tpl]cite web |url= http://en.haiwatch.com/patientpage/HaiwatchPatientPage.htm|title=Prevent Infections |author= |website=preventinfections.com |publisher= |accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl]

===HAI Watchdog===

In 2010, the HAI Watchdog Awards were created to recognize the efforts of healthcare professionals working together to prevent healthcare-associated infections (HAI). It facilitates the sharing of best practices among clinicians and recognizes four exceptional participants with educational grants. The awards are broken into two categories:[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.healthcarefinancenews.com/news/four-hai-watchdog-award-winners-announced|title=4 HAI Watchdog Award winners announced |author= |website=healthcarefinancenews.com |publisher= |accessdate=6 June 2013[/tpl]

====Clinician’s Choice Award====

This category is for HAI prevention initiatives that help educate and build awareness, but may not be directly measured by specific HAI rate changes.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.haiwatchdog.com|title=Awards |author= |website=haiwatchdog.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]

====Panel-Judged Award====

This category is for successful programs yielding a measurable reduction in HAI rates over time.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.haiwatchdog.com|title=Awards |author= |website=haiwatchdog.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]

===Clean Hands Care===

In 2013, Kimberly-Clark released a campaign called “Clean Hands Care” to spread awareness about how washing and sanitizing hands can prevent Hospital-Associated Infections (HAI).[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kchealthcare.com/hai-watch/hai-threats-solutions/hand-hygiene.aspx |title=CLEAN HANDS CARE - Show You Care. Join the Watch! |author= |website=kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]

==Headquarters/Campus==

Kimberly-Clark Health Care’s campus is located in Roswell, Georgia, just north of Atlanta. It sits on 98 acres of private land with 45 acres being undeveloped natural habitat. There is a nine acre lake that provides water for irrigation and wildlife, as well as running and walking trails for employees. 
The headquarters is made up of four office buildings. Each building consists of Kimberly-Clark business sectors, including Global Health Care, Kimberly-Clark Professional and Global Non-Wovens. More than 1,200 scientists, engineers, marketers, financial analysts, sales representatives and operations employees work on the Roswell site. The campus also includes an activities center with a lake-side restaurant and a 24,000-square-foot health center. The health center offers on-site medical services, a fully equipped exercise room, exercise classes, complimentary towel/laundry services and a 25-meter indoor swimming pool.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.kchealthcare.com/about-us/roswell-campus.aspx|title=Roswell Headquarters |author= |website=kchealthcare.com |publisher= |accessdate=19 July 2013[/tpl]
