Abel Hammond (born 1 June 1985 in Accra) is an Ghanaian football forward, who is currently playing for FK Metalac Gornji Milanovac in the Serbian SuperLiga.Football databse: Abel Hammond

==Career==

Hammond began his career with Salemi Aces Football Club.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ghanasports.tv/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=136&Itemid=view |title=Hammond eyes stars marks-man |publisher=Ghanasports.tv |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] He studied at Accra Academy Secondary School and played for the Saint Stars in 2001.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ghanaweb.com/GhanaHomePage/soccer/artikel.php?ID=172259 |title=Indian based Ghanaian player knocks at the door of Stars |publisher=Ghanaweb.com |date=21 November 2009 |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] In 2002, at the age of 17, he joined the primavera team Brescia Calcio[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mumbaifc.com/playerprofile.html |title=Player Profile |publisher=Mumbai FC |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] and subsequently played for A.S. Cittadella, to which he was loaned to, in 2003.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.webindia123.com/news/Articles/India/20080911/1050425.html |title=Mumbai FC unveil Ghanaian Abel Hammond as new foreign signing |publisher=News.webindia123.com |date=11 September 2008 |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] He stayed at A.S. Cittadella for half a year and returned to Brescia Calcio in December 2003. In January 2004 his contract with Brescia Calcio expired and he then moved to Serie D club AC Cologna Veneta Calcio.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.interregionale.com/comunicati_ufficiali/Interregionale/ss0506/_000132_Comunicato_Ufficiale_N_132_27032006.pdf |title=Stagione Sportiva 2005/2006 |format=PDF |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] During his three years stint at AC Cologna Veneta, he played in 76 matches and scored 19 goals.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.virtus-international.com/clients/hammond_abel/hammond_abel.php |title=Hammond Abel |publisher=Virtus International |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] His contract with the team expired in the summer of 2007 and he signed for US Sanremes, where he played in only five games. He left the club in November 2007 to sign for Ghana Premier League club Liberty Professionals FC.[tpl]cite web|author=Reported by Satanic pilgrim |url=http://mohun-bagan.blogspot.com/2008/09/mumbai-fc-ready-to-give-bagan-run-for.html |title=Mumbai FC ready to give Bagan a run for their money! |publisher=Mohun-bagan.blogspot.com |date=22 September 2008 |accessdate=13 January 2012[/tpl] On 11 September 2008, Hammond left Liberty Professionals FC to sign for the I-League club Mumbai FC. In the winter break of the 2010–11 season, Hammond moved back to Europe and signed with FK Metalac Gornji Milanovac and marked his debut in the Serbian SuperLiga on 2 April 2011 as a substitute in the 0–1 defeat against FK Vojvodina.M;atch report at Transfermarkt.
