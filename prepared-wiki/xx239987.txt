[[Uranium Corporation of India]]

CATEGORIES: Government-owned companies of India, Companies established in 1967, Mining companies of India, Nuclear technology in India, Uranium mining, 1967 establishments in India

Uranium Corporation of India (UCIL) is a centrally owned Public Sector Undertaking (PSU), under the Department of Atomic Energy for uranium mining and uranium processing. The corporation was founded in 1967 and is responsible for the mining and milling of uranium ore in India.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hindu.com/fline/fl1618/16180650.htm|title=URANIUM PRODUCTIONFrom ore to yellow cake |date=1999-09-10|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl] The firm operates mines at Jadugora, Bhatin, Narwapahar, Turamdih and Banduhurang[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ucil.gov.in/cgi-bin/ucilpg3.html|title=Mines|publisher=UCIL|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]

==Mines==

===Jaduguda===

It is the first uranium mine of India which started its operations in 1967. This mine is located in the state of Jharkhand.http://wikimapia.org/#lat=22.6485714&lon=86.3632894&z=16&l=0&m=s&v=9 Jadugoda process plant is located close to the mine which is used for the processing of the uranium ore. The ore from Bhatin and Narwapahar mines is also processed here.

===Bhatin===

This mine is located 3 km away from Jaduguda and shares most of the infrastructure with the Jaduguda mine.

===Narwapahar tati===

This mine was commissioned in April 1995. It is known to be the most modern mine of the country.

===Turamdih===

Turamdih mine is located 24 km to the west of Jaduguda. It was commissioned in 2003. Turamdih Processing Plant has been set up to treat the ore from Turamdih, Banduharang and Mohuldin mines.

==New projects==

UCIL has taken up activities to start two underground mines in Jharkhand. Uranium reserves have been found in the state of Andhra Pradesh and the construction of an underground mine has started in Cuddapah district.

==Controversies==

There was criticism from certain sections of the local community that the mining operations of UCIL were resulting in harmful radiation to the public.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/res/web/pIe/ie/daily/19980919/26251044.html|title=Angry villagers take on uranium corporation|last=Chakrabarti|publisher=Indian Express |first=Ashis|date=1998-09-19|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl] However medical surveys later conducted showed no proof for the claim and showed that the cancer incidence in the area was less than one-third of the national average.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/res/web/pIe/ie/daily/19990822/iex22009.html|title=A hefty dose of claptrap|last= George|first=Nirmala|date=22 August 1999|publisher=Indian Express|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]

UCIL was also among the 63 Indian establishments put under sanction by USA in 1998.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.indianexpress.com/ie/daily/19980726/20750704.html|title=BARC among 63 blacklisted institutions|date=26 July 1998 |accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]

The mining activities of UCIL in the Khasi Hills were also fiercely opposed by the local tribals protesting against potential health effects.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/low/south_asia/3000991.stm|title=Tribes dig in to fight uranium|first=Subir Bhaumik |date=5 May 2003|publisher=BBC News|accessdate=2009-10-19[/tpl]
