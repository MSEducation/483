Alex Macqueen is a British actor. He has appeared on television, film and radio in the UK in productions such as Holby City, Hut 33, The Thick of It, Keeping Mum, and The Inbetweeners.

==Life and career==

Macqueen was born in Epsom, Surrey.IMDb Alex Macqueen Biography http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1276507/bio is an English actor. He is perhaps best known for his recurring role as sarcastic consultant anaesthetist Keith Greene, appearing in 75 episodes of Holby City between May 2005 and July 2010. He appeared in The Thick of It as Julius Nicholson. Macqueen appeared as Kevin Sutherland (Neil's Dad) in E4's comedy, The Inbetweeners and as 'Howard', an anti-speed bump community campaigner in two episodes [tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00s5wnn|title=Outnumbered, Series 3, Episode 3[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/programmes/b00sbjz8|title=Outnumbered, Series 3, Episode 6[/tpl] of BBC1's Outnumbered. He has been in TV advertisements for Specsavers, Dell, and Utterly Butterly.
Macqueen was educated at St John's School, Leatherhead and left to receive a first in English literature at Durham University. He went on to study for a M.Phil at Pembroke College, Cambridge, graduating in 1998.admin.cam.ac.uk Cambridge website http://www.admin.cam.ac.uk/reporter/1997-8/weekly/5737/34.html He was a member of the National Youth Theatre between 1992 and 1995, then trained as a barrister at the Middle Temple. He worked as a Business Affairs Executive for Granada International and has worked in the House of Commons with a member of the Culture & Media Select Committee, specifically working during the creation of the Broadcasting Act 1996.
Macqueen starred as Roy Tunt in The Hide, which premiered on FilmFour in February 2009. He won the award for Best Actor at the Marbella Film Festival in 2009 for this role. He appears in the film In the Loop, directed by Armando Iannucci, as Sir Jonathan Tutt, British Ambassador to the UN. This was a follow-up to Macqueen's role as Julius Nicholson, the 'blue-skies advisor' to the Prime Minister in political satire The Thick of It, also directed by Iannucci. He appeared in the Comedy Central/BBC collaboration, Kröd Mändoon and the Flaming Sword of Fire, in which he played 'Barnabus', the sidekick to the Chancellor Dongalor, played by British comedian Matt Lucas: Macqueen had previously acted alongside Lucas in the 2006 film adaptation of The Wind in the Willows.
Macqueen has appeared in a series of videos on YouTube as Clive Rudloe, a character who claims to be "the world's number one DJ". The videos are spoofs created by real-life DJs Above and Beyond.http://www.youtube.com/user/aboveandbeyondtv In late 2009, he starred in several episodes of The News at Bedtime on BBC Radio 4, in a variety of supporting roles.
In 2010, he appeared as the publisher Malcolm Dodds in Woody Allen's You Will Meet a Tall Dark Stranger. That year he also had a small role in the comedy film Four Lions.
In 2012, he appeared in a Doctor Who spin off UNIT Dominion, published by Big Finish Productions, in a role described as 'The Other Doctor', a role he reprised in the second Dark Eyes boxed set. 
He also appeared in a lead role as Edmund in Julia Davis' black period comedy Hunderby, opposite Davis and Alexandra Roach.
