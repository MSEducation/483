[[Mythimna pudorina]]

CATEGORIES: Mythimna (moth), Moths of Japan, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Estonia, Insects of Sweden, Insects of Romania, Insects of Poland, Insects of Latvia, Insects of Finland, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Insects of Hungary, Insects of Bulgaria, Insects of Italy

The Striped Wainscot (Mythimna pudorina) is a species of moth of the family Noctuidae. It is found in the Palearctic ecozone (Europe, all of Russia to Japan).
The wingspan is 35–38 mm. The moth flies from June to August depending on the location.
The larvae feed on various grasses, such as Purple Moor Grass, Phragmites and Reed canary grass.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.nhm.ac.uk/research-curation/research/projects/hostplants/  |title= Robinson, G. S., P. R. Ackery, I. J. Kitching, G. W. Beccaloni & L. M. Hernández, 2010. HOSTS - A Database of the World's Lepidopteran Hostplants. Natural History Museum, London.[/tpl]
