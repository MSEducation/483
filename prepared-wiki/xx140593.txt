[[Jared Anderson (heavy metal musician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1974 births, 2006 deaths, American heavy metal bass guitarists, American heavy metal singers, American male singers, Morbid Angel members, People from Covington, Kentucky, Place of birth missing, Place of death missing

Jared W. Anderson (December 28, 1974 – October 14, 2006) was an American death metal musician best known as the bassist and vocalist of Morbid Angel from 2001 to 2002 and Hate Eternal from 1998 to 2003.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=60425|title=Ex-Morbid Angel/Hate Eternal member Jared Anderson dead at 31|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2006-10-17|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] 
Jared Anderson formed his first band, Internecine, in December, 1997 at the age of 22, and saw his six-year career interrupted in 2003 when he was forced to leave Hate Eternal as a result of struggles with a drug dependency.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=14123|title=Hate Eternal bassist leaves the band, seeks treatment for drug problem|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2003-08-02|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] He co-wrote all of the songs for Internecine with Shannon Purdon released two demos, one in 1993 and another in 1997. Book of Lambs was released in 2001.[tpl]cite web|url=http://myspace.com/furyandflames|title=Fury and Flames|publisher=MySpace|author=Jared Anderson|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] 
Jared Anderson died in his sleep of an unspecified cause two-and-a-half months before his birthday.[tpl]cite news|url=http://dunes.cincinnati.com/classifieds/obits/obitDisplay.aspx?d=10/17/2006&st=1&id=675368|title=Jared Anderson's obituary|publisher=The Cincinnati Enquirer|date=2006-10-17|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=60568|title=Former Hate Eternal/Morbid Angel bassist/vocalist Jared Anderson died in his sleep|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2006-10-19|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] Shortly before his death, he had started a new project called As One... with Steve Tucker, also a former member of Morbid Angel.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=48327|title=Former Morbid Angel members Tucker and Anderson collaborating on new project|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2006-02-14|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] His last recording was a guest appearance in The Allknowing's cover of Motörhead's "Ace of Spades"[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=52000|title=The Allknowing debut to feature former Morbid Angel frontman|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2006-05-08|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl] which, since 2007, has been freely accessible online.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.roadrunnerrecords.com/blabbermouth.net/news.aspx?mode=Article&newsitemID=60748|title=Jared Anderson's final recording posted online|publisher=Blabbermouth.net|date=2006-10-22|accessdate=2006-10-23[/tpl]
The 2008 Hate Eternal album, Fury & Flames, was dedicated to Jared Anderson's memory.
