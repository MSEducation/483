[[Sowton]]

CATEGORIES: Areas of Exeter, Villages in Devon, Industrial parks in the United Kingdom

Sowton is a village and civil parish east of Exeter in East Devon, England.  It has a population of 639.Census 2001
Its parish council merged with that of nearby Clyst St Mary in 1976 to form Bishop's Clyst.Bishop's Clyst Parish Council : Parish Plan
St Michael's church was rebuilt in 1844–45, by John Hayward and paid for by John Garratt. He retained the original perpendicular north arcade. It is a rare example of an early Victorian Tractarian village church.[tpl]cite book|last=Cherry |first=Bridget|coauthors=Nikolaus Pevsner|title=The buildings of England: Devon| publisher = Yale University Press| year = 2004| location = New Haven and London| isbn = 0-300-09596-1|pages=755–756[/tpl] It is a grade I listed building.[tpl]cite web | url= http://www.britishlistedbuildings.co.uk/en-86189-parish-church-of-st-michael-and-all-angel|title=Parish Church of St Michael and All Angels, Sowton| work = British Listed Buildings | accessdate=29 September 2012[/tpl]
Sowton is also the name of an industrial estate at [tpl]gbmapping|SX964920[/tpl].  The area is located next to the M5 motorway  between junctions 29 and 30, the opposite side from the village and is served by Digby and Sowton railway station.
Most of the industrial estate is covered with large retailers, warehousing and distributing, as well as a small number of manufacturers.  There is also a Park and Ride location for bus services to Exeter city centre. For a number of years there has been an office development on the main road which was left incomplete as the economy took a downturn during construction.
