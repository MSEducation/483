==Early life==

George Vandeleur Fiddes was born in Great Yarmouth, the son of George Richard Fiddes and Ellen Greening. He was educated at Dulwich College, his grandmother Jane Greening being a pensioner of the Dulwich College Estate when he was 12 and with whom he was living along with his mother Ellen in 1871.1871 England Census Class: RG10; Piece: 721; Folio: 74; Page: 29; GSU roll: 823349. He was subsequently a scholar of Brasenose College, Oxford where he took a second-class in Classical Moderations in 1879.

==Career==

He entered the Colonial Office in 1881. He was promoted First Class Clerk, after long service as private Secretary, in 1896. He went on to be appointed as Imperial Secretary and Accountant to Sir Alfred Milner, High Commissioner for South Africa, in 1897. In 1900 he was made Secretary to the Transvaal Administration and he returned to the Colonial Office as Principal Clerk in 1902. In 1909 he became Assistant Under-Secretary of State and Permanent Under-Secretary in February, 1916. He was made a knight Grand Cross of the Most Distinguished Order of Saint Michael and Saint George in 1917.The Times | February 13, 1917, p. 6

==Personal life==

He married Lucia Marie Fiddes and had a daughter, Lucia Marie, in 1884 and a son, George Graham, in 1888.1891 England Census, Class: RG12; Piece: 1033; Folio 215; Page 28; GSU roll: 6096143.
