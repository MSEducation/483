[[Warburton School]]

CATEGORIES: Grade II listed buildings in Greater Manchester, School buildings completed in 1872, Houses in Greater Manchester, John Douglas buildings

Warburton School is on the south side of Dunham Road in the village of Warburton, Greater Manchester, England.  It has been converted into a house and is a Grade II listed building.[tpl]Citation |url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/details/default.aspx?id=212939 |title=Images of England: The School, Dunham Road, Warburton|publisher=English Heritage | accessdate= 9 January 2010[/tpl]
The school was built in 1871–72 for the landowner Rowland Egerton-Warburton of Arley Hall, and was designed by the Chester architect John Douglas.[tpl]cite book | last =Hubbard | first =Edward | authorlink = Edward Hubbard | title =The Work of John Douglas |edition= | publisher =The Victorian Society | year =1991 | location =London | page = 243| isbn =0-901657-16-6 [/tpl]  It is constructed in brick with stone and terracotta dressings, and has a tiled roof.  The building has three bays; from the left, the first two bays contain seven-light mullioned windows.  Above the window in the central bay is a dormer gable that contains a three-light mullioned window.  The right bay contains a modern patio window.  Between the left and central bays is a decorated brick chimney stack.  Above the main roof is a hipped bellcote.  In the interior of the building are two pairs of truncated crucks supporting the roof.
