Wayne Cashman (born June 24, 1945) is a retired Canadian professional ice hockey player and a former NHL head coach. He played 17 seasons for the Boston Bruins, helping the team win two Stanley Cups.

==Playing career==

Cashman played his junior hockey as a teammate of Bobby Orr's on the Oshawa Generals of the OHA.  He played parts of three seasons in the minor leagues for the Oklahoma City Blazers and the Hershey Bears before making the Bruins' squad for good in 1969.
Cashman played his entire NHL career with the Boston Bruins (1964–65, 1967–83). His jersey number was #12.http://www.sjjerseys.com/data/images/Cashman%20wp%20A.jpg
Cashman was a hard-grinding left winger on the era's most formidable forward line with superstar centre Phil Esposito and right wing Ken Hodge.[tpl]cite news| url=http://sportsillustrated.cnn.com/multimedia/photo_gallery/0901/nhl.classic.lines/content.11.html | work=CNN | title=Nitro Line (Bruins)[/tpl] His role was to get into the corners and battle for loose pucks, feeding them to Esposito or Hodge. Cashman was also a tenacious forechecker and he also served as an enforcer to protect Esposito and Bobby Orr.http://bruinslegends.blogspot.com/2006/05/wayne-cashman.html
Cashman was a member of Stanley Cup winning teams in 1970 and 1972, the latter where he scored twice in the deciding game six against the New York Rangers. He scored 20 goals a season eight times in his career, accomplishing this feat in four straight seasons with 100 or more penalty minutes. His best season was in 1974, when he scored 30 goals and 59 assists to finish fourth in the league in points, played in the All-Star Game, and was named to the NHL Second All-Star team.
He served as the captain of the Bruins from the 1978 season until 1983.
After his retirement as a player, Cashman served in several coaching positions, largely for his former teammate Phil Esposito; five seasons as an assistant coach for the New York Rangers, four for the Tampa Bay Lightning as an assistant coach.  After Terry Murray was dismissed, Cashman was appointed head coach of the Philadelphia Flyers for the 1997-98 season and held that post for 61 games until being replaced by Roger Neilson and served thereafter as the team's assistant coach.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/1998/03/10/sports/nhl-last-night-philadelphia-flyers-demote-cashman.html | work=The New York Times | title=N.H.L.: LAST NIGHT -- PHILADELPHIA; Flyers Demote Cashman | date=March 10, 1998[/tpl] He was also on the coaching staff of Team Canada in the 1998 Olympics in Nagano, Japan, as an assistant to Marc Crawford.http://www.collectionscanada.gc.ca/databases/olympians/001064-119.01-e.php?&photo_id_nbr=9825&&PHPSESSID=dlbghk3a8459b5l4vpsjt2nhv0http://www.kdshf.ca/index.cfm?page=inducteeDetails&id=14  He was an assistant coach with the Bruins from 2001 until 2006.
His total NHL coaching record is 32-20-9.
