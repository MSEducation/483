[[64th Indian Infantry Brigade]]

CATEGORIES: British Indian Army brigades

The 64th Indian Infantry Brigade was an Infantry formation of the Indian Army during World War II. It was formed in February 1942, at Babina in India and assigned to the 23rd Indian Infantry Division.
The brigade was transferred in March 1942, to the 19th Indian Infantry Division and fought in the Burma Campaign. The brigade remained under command 19th Division until the end of the war.[tpl]cite web|accessdate=2009-10-23|publisher=Order of Battle|title=64 Indian Brigade|url=http://www.ordersofbattle.com/UnitData.aspx?UniX=6202&Tab=Sup[/tpl]
