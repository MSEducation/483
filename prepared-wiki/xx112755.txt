[[Sam Pottle]]

CATEGORIES: American composers, American conductors (music), Yale University alumni, 1934 births, 1978 deaths, Sesame Street crew

Sam Pottle  (8 May 1934â€“4 July 1978) was an American composer, conductor, and musical director involved in many theatrical and television productions.  He is perhaps best remembered for his work on Sesame Street and The Muppet Show, having co-written the iconic theme song for the latter. However, Sam Pottle was also involved with many theatrical productions in the 1960s and 1970s.
Pottle graduated from Yale in 1955.
Sam Pottle died on 4 July 1978. His muse, partner, and fellow Sesame writer Charles Choset dedicated the 1982 plays Letters to Ben  and The Messiah to him.
