[[David Conover]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, 1983 deaths, American journalists, American photographers, Marilyn Monroe, First Motion Picture Unit personnel

David Conover (June 26, 1919 - December 21, 1983) was an author and documentary photographer who is credited with discovering Marilyn Monroe while taking photos for Yank magazine. While attached to the U.S. Army Air Forces' First Motion Picture Unit, his commanding officer was future U.S. president Ronald Reagan, who had sent Conover to the Radioplane Munitions Factory where he discovered Monroe.http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1991-01-06/news/9101010832_1_marilyn-monroe-david-conover-early-photographshttp://www.marilynmonroepages.com/conover/http://entertainment.howstuffworks.com/marilyn-monroe-early-life3.htm
