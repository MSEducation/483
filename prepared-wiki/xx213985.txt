[[Samuel Taylor (bishop)]]

CATEGORIES: 1859 births, Alumni of University College London, Archdeacons of Southwark, Bishops of Kingston, 20th-century Anglican bishops, 1929 deaths

Samuel Mumford Taylor (25 August 1859- 30 November 1929DOB/DOD) was the second Bishop of Kingston.
Taylor was educated at University College London"University College London, Dec. 3. (Official Appointments and Notices)", The Times, 5 December 1881, p7 and ordained in 1885. After a curacy at St John the Evangelist's Leeds he became the first vicar of St Aidan's (Bishop Woodford Memorial) Leeds;First Vicar of Parish He was then a canon residentiary and the precentor at Southwark Cathedral, then Archdeacon of Southwark.Who was Who 1897-1990, London, A & C Black, 1991, ISBN 0-7136-3457-X His penultimate post, until his resignation in 1921, was as a suffragan bishop. Finally he was appointed to lead the worship for St George's Chapel at Windsor Castle."New Royal Canon - Dr. Sheppard's Successor. (Official Appointments and Notices)", The Times, 5 November 1921, p10
