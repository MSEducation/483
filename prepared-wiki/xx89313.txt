[[René Laanen]]

CATEGORIES: 1952 births, Living people, Dutch classical trombonists, People from The Hague, Royal Conservatory of The Hague alumni

Antonius Philippus René Laanen (born 23 January 1952) is a Dutch bass trombonist.[tpl]Citation needed|date=May 2011[/tpl]
Born in The Hague, Laanen's father Theo was also a noted trombonist.  After training in the Royal Conservatory, he worked in the 1960s and early 1970s as a freelance trombonist in Germany and his home country.[tpl]Citation needed|date=May 2011[/tpl]
In 1972 he founded the Hague Trombone Quartet with Arthur Moore and the brothers Bart and Erik van Lier.   In 1980 he played with the Pretoria Symphony Orchestra in Johannesburg, South Africa.[tpl]Citation needed|date=May 2011[/tpl]
In the 1990s he worked as a music copyist for the famous Metropole Orchestra.[tpl]Citation needed|date=May 2011[/tpl]
