White, Senior Historian Emeritus of the Smithsonian Institution. After studying engineering and economic history at Harvard University, he received a doctorate in 1928. For the next twenty years, Hunter was engaged in research for Steamboats on the Western Rivers. In 1952, his scholarship was  acknowledged when he was awarded the Dunning Prize by the American Historical Association.
In 1937, Hunter joined the faculty of American University where he was a professor of history until his retirement in 1966.[tpl]cite news|last=Waggoner|first=Walter|title=LOUIS C. Hunter was married and the father of two daughters. Louis C. Hunter died on March 22, 1984.
