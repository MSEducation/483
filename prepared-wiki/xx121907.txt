[[Patrick Brompton]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in North Yorkshire, Civil parishes in North Yorkshire

Patrick Brompton is a village and civil parish in the Richmondshire district of North Yorkshire, England. Patrick Brompton is situated about [tpl]Convert|4|mi|km[/tpl] west of Bedale. It lies on the A684. It has a population of 167. St Patrick's Church is located in the centre of the village as well as the village pub, called The Green Tree.[tpl]cite web|title=Green Tree, Patrick Brompton|url=http://www.thegoodpubguide.co.uk/pub/view/Green-Tree-DL8-1JW|accessdate=15 March 2013[/tpl]  The village is approximately 235 miles from London and 35 miles from Middlesbrough. The nearest primary school is Crakehall C of E Primary School in Great Crakehall, 1.2 miles away.[tpl]cite web|title=Primary schools near Patrick Brompton|url=http://home.rm.com/SchoolFinder/ShowSchools.aspx?l=Patrick%20Brompton,North%20Yorkshire&t=pri|accessdate=26 March 2013[/tpl] The Brompton Beck runs through the centre of the village.

==Parish history==

In the 1880s, Patrick Brompton was described as:
The village of Patrick Brompton and other villages in the parish are mentioned in the Domesday Book which was completed in 1086. When published, there were 27 households in the village consisting of 22 villagers and 5 smallholders. The 'Hundred', Count Alan also had 195 other places including Bedale and Bellerby.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton|url=http://domesdaymap.co.uk/place/SE2190/patrick-brompton/|accessdate=26 March 2013[/tpl]
The name 'Patrick Brompton' derives from the personal name 'Patrick' and Brompton derives from broom, a thorny bush or shrub and 'tun' deriving from an enclosure; a village; an estate.[tpl]cite book|last=Ekwall|last2 =Mills|first=E|first2= A.D|title=Dictionary of English Place Names|page=68; 80–1[/tpl] Patrick Brompton is also mentioned on the 'Genuki' website http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/YKS/NRY/Patrickbrompton/ and in the 1820s, described as:
St Patrick is the one church in the village which belongs to the Church of England Diocese of Ripon and Leeds.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton: St Patrick, Patrick Brompton|url=http://www.achurchnearyou.com/patrick-brompton-st-patrick/|accessdate=15 March 2013[/tpl]
Other villages in the parish are Arrathorne, Hunton and Newton Le Willows.[tpl]cite web|title=The Ancient Parish of Patrick Brompton|url=http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/YKS/NRY/Patrickbrompton/index.html|accessdate=15 March 2013[/tpl] According to the 2011 census data, 137 people follow Christianity.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton (Parish): Key Figures for 2011 Census: Quick Statistics|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11127421&c=patrick+brompton&d=16&e=61&g=6454869&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1363363035388&enc=1&dsFamilyId=2579|work=Religion|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=15 March 2013[/tpl]

==Population and Industry==

It had a population of 167 according to the 2011 census.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadKeyFigures.do?a=7&b=11127421&c=patrick+brompton&d=16&e=62&g=6454869&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1396654703114&enc=1|title=Patrick Brompton (Parish): Key Figures for 2011 Census: Key Statistics|work=Neighbourhood Statistics|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=4 February 2013[/tpl] By looking at 2011 census data we can see the age structure of the village. The mean age of the village is 49.8 and the age variable with the highest number is 45 to 59 with 50 people. This is 29.9% of the population.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11127421&c=patrick+brompton&d=16&e=62&g=6454869&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1396654703114&enc=1&dsFamilyId=2474|title=Patrick Brompton (Parish): Key Figures for 2011 Census: Age Structure|work=Neighbourhood Statistics|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl] This suggests that the population of the village are of working ability and leave the village everyday for work purposes.
On the right you can see a population time series graph showing the population of Patrick Brompton from 1801 to 2011. It displays a sharp drop in population around 1850. From the 1881 census data, we can see that agriculture is a primary source of work or occupation with 20 people from the village working in that field.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton|url=http://www.visionofbritain.org.uk/unit/10459072/cube/OCC_ORDER1881|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl] Census data from 2011 now shows that the male population of Patrick Brompton work primarily in construction with 10 people, then 8 males working in manufacturing.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11127421&c=patrick+brompton&d=16&e=62&g=6454869&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1396654703114&enc=1&dsFamilyId=2539|title=Patrick Brompton (Parish): Key figures for 2011 Census: Key Statistics|work=Industry-males 2011|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=27 March 2013[/tpl]
The dramatic drop in population around 1850 could be due to the fact that the parish boundaries have changed over time.

==Transportation==

The A684 runs through the village and the A1 motorway is approximately 5 miles to the east of the village and 4 hours direct to London. The nearest railway station is 8.77 miles away in Northallerton which lies on the East Coast Mainline. There is also a station in Thirsk which is 12.85 miles away also lying on the East Coast Mainline.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton|url=http://www.bigreddirectory.com/north_yorkshire/patrick_brompton|accessdate=25 March 2013[/tpl] The nearest international airport is Leeds Bradford International Airport approximately an hour away.

==Housing==

According to the Office for National Statistics, there are 83 dwellings in Patrick Brompton, 48 of these being detached houses or bungalows.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do?a=7&b=11127421&c=patrick+brompton&d=16&e=62&g=6454869&i=1001x1003x1032x1004&m=0&r=1&s=1396654703114&enc=1&dsFamilyId=2481|title=Patrick Brompton (parish) Key Figures for 2011 Census: Key Statistics|work=Neighbourhood Statistics|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=26 March 2013[/tpl] The average asking house price in Patrick Brompton is £309,000. In the history of housing in the village there was a sharp decrease in homes in the village from 265 in the 1851 census. to 36 in the 1881 census.[tpl]cite web|title=Patrick Brompton Housing Statistics|url=http://www.visionofbritain.org.uk/unit/10459072/cube/HOUSES|accessdate=26 March 2013[/tpl]  This is similar, in the population figures, this could be due to the fact that parish boundaries changed over this time.

==Places of interest==
