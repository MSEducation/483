[[Ira Allen Eastman]]

CATEGORIES: 1809 births, 1881 deaths, Members of the United States House of Representatives from New Hampshire, Dartmouth College alumni

Ira Allen Eastman (January 1, 1809 – March 21, 1881) was an American manufacturer and Democratic politician in the U.S. state of New Hampshire. He served as a member of the United States House of Representatives and as a member of the New Hampshire House of Representatives in the 1800s.

==Early life==

Eastman was born in Gilmanton, New Hampshire, the son of Stephen and Hannah Eastman. He attended the local schools and Gilmanton Academy before graduating from Dartmouth College in Hanover, New Hampshire in 1829.[tpl]cite book|last=Dartmouth College|title=General catalogue of Dartmouth college and the associated institutions: including the officers of government and instruction, graduates and all others who have received honorary degrees|year=1890|publisher=Dartmouth College|page=28|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=3ElOAAAAMAAJ&pg=PA28&lpg=PA28&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+dartmouth+college&source=bl&ots=x62F65kCzb&sig=l58mrp7GukxUAZ2JHIPkdy7lO9k&hl=en&sa=X&ei=xxOhUos2hq7aBf7EgDA&ved=0CF4Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20dartmouth%20college&f=false[/tpl] He read law and was admitted to the bar in 1832. He began practicing law in Troy, New Hampshire. 

==Political career==

Eastman returned to Gilmanton in 1834 and continued the practice of law. He served as clerk of the New Hampshire House of Representatives in 1835. He was as member of the State House of Representatives from 1836-1838, and served as speaker of the State House in 1837 and 1838. He was Register of Probate for Strafford County from 1836 to 1839.[tpl]cite book|last=Lancaster|first=Daniel|title=The History of Gilmanton: Embracing the Proprietary, Civil, Literary, Ecclesiastical, Biographical, Genealogical, and Miscellaneous History, from the First Settlement to the Present Time; Including what is Now Gilford, to the Time it was Disannexed|year=1845|publisher=A. Prescott|page=226|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=utBdyz7PgXgC&pg=PA225&lpg=PA225&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+Gilmanton,+New+Hampshire&source=bl&ots=SSb059w8fi&sig=0PRYWTdolYiQivAVIvK1Lxt-BiI&hl=en&sa=X&ei=SA-hUs3GNsHF2QXj9oDAAw&ved=0CGYQ6AEwCA#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20Gilmanton%2C%20New%20Hampshire&f=false[/tpl] 
Eastman was elected as a Democrat to the Twenty-sixth and Twenty-seventh Congresses, serving from March 4, 1839 - March 3, 1843.[tpl]cite book|last=Stearns|first=Ezra S.  and Whitcher,|title=Genealogical and Family History of the State of New Hampshire: A Record of the Achievements of Her People in the Making of a Commonwealth and the Founding of a Nation, Volume 1|year=1908|publisher=Genealogical and Family History of the State of New Hampshire: A Record of the Achievements of Her People in the Making of a Commonwealth and the Founding of a Nation, Volume 1|page=200|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=M80UAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA200&lpg=PA200&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+Gilmanton,+New+Hampshire&source=bl&ots=X-3xFK-oKf&sig=bwfDciv1i0xPu-9nSWKcsasdNTU&hl=en&sa=X&ei=SA-hUs3GNsHF2QXj9oDAAw&ved=0CGkQ6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20Gilmanton%2C%20New%20Hampshire&f=false[/tpl] He served as chairman of the United States House Committee on Revisal and Unfinished Business during the Twenty-seventh Congress. He was not a candidate for renomination in 1842.
After leaving Congress, he served as judge of the New Hampshire Court of Common Pleas from 1844-1849. He served as associate justice of the New Hampshire Supreme Court from 1849-1855,[tpl]cite book|title=Proceedings of the Bar Association of the State of New Hampshire at Its Annual Meeting|year=1904|publisher=Bar Association of the State of New Hampshire, Bar Association of the State of New Hampshire. E.|title=THE NEW-HAMPSHIRE POLITICAL MANUAL, FOR 1867|page=37|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=UMYTAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA37&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+justice+of+the+New+Hampshire+Supreme+Court&hl=en&sa=X&ei=kRehUuDlNaPg2gX-1oCYAg&ved=0CHAQ6AEwBg#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20&f=false[/tpl] In 1858, he was honored with a L.L.D. degree from Dartmouth College, and in 1859 he was chosen trustee of Dartmouth.[tpl]cite book|last=Biographical Review Publishing Company|title=Biographical review: containing life sketches of leading citizens of Stafford and Belknap countries, New Hampshire|year=1897|publisher=Biographical Review|page=557|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=C2sjAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA555&lpg=PA555&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+Gilmanton,+New+Hampshire&source=bl&ots=FoOWhvftID&sig=XWMEtcQ76PtNnqnTo4eGusaPd4Q&hl=en&sa=X&ei=SA-hUs3GNsHF2QXj9oDAAw&ved=0CF0Q6AEwBg#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20Gilmanton%2C%20New%20Hampshire&f=false[/tpl]  
After resigning from judicial service, Eastman resumed the practice of law. He practiced law in Concord and Manchester. Eastman was an unsuccessful Democratic candidate for Governor of New Hampshire in 1863 and for United States Senator in 1866.[tpl]cite book|last=Bell|first=Charles Henry|title=The bench and bar of New Hampshire: including biographical notices of deceased judges of the highest court, and lawyers of the province and state, and a list of names of those now living|year=1893|publisher=Houghton, Mifflin and company|page=100|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=lAE9AAAAIAAJ&pg=PA99&lpg=PA99&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+Troy,+New+Hampshire&source=bl&ots=tAdJBr0Azu&sig=ZPpl4uU7_yU-PexI52_J0QqRZUA&hl=en&sa=X&ei=WhShUpOfAsWp2gXv44GYAQ&ved=0CFwQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20Troy%2C%20New%20Hampshire&f=false[/tpl]  
Eastman died on March 21, 1881 in Manchester and is interred in Valley Cemetery.[tpl]cite book|last=Spence|first=Thomas E.|title=Where They're Buried: A Directory Containing More Than Twenty Thousand Names of Notable Persons Buried in American Cemeteries, with Listings of Many Prominent People who Were Cremated|year=1998|publisher=Genealogical Publishing Com|page=222|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=eLWao2lIGTEC&pg=PA222&lpg=PA222&dq=Ira+Allen+Eastman+interred+in+Valley+Cemetery&source=bl&ots=1lJsKBATvd&sig=7WVDP5Q_JdyJbEDbNw9QR8SwbKM&hl=en&sa=X&ei=qhihUsvOM8Ps2wWSg4B4&ved=0CEgQ6AEwBA#v=onepage&q=Ira%20Allen%20Eastman%20interred%20in%20Valley%20Cemetery&f=false[/tpl] 

==Personal life==
