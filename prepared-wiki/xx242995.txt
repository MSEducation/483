[[Agulhas National Park]]

CATEGORIES: Protected areas of the Western Cape, National parks of South Africa

The Agulhas National Park is a South African national park located in the Agulhas Plain in the southern Overberg region of the Western Cape, about [tpl]convert|200|km[/tpl] south-east of Cape Town. The park stetches along the coastal plain between the towns of Gansbaai and Struisbaai, and includes the southern tip of Africa at Cape Agulhas. [tpl]Asof|2009|1[/tpl] it covered an area of [tpl]convert|20959|ha[/tpl].[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sanparks.org/conservation/park_man/2009/park_management_plan_stakeholder_review.pdf |title=Agulhas National Park: Park Management Plan 2009 â€“ 2013 |publisher=South African National Parks |date=24 February 2009 |page=9 |accessdate=13 February 2010[/tpl]

==Points of interest==

The primary tourist attraction in the park is Cape Agulhas, the southernmost tip of Africa and the official meeting-point of the Atlantic and Indian Oceans. Nearby is the Agulhas lighthouse, the second-oldest lighthouse in South Africa, which also includes a small museum and tearoom.
