[[Xiuyan Manchu Autonomous County]]

CATEGORIES: Manchu autonomous counties, County-level divisions of Liaoning

Xiuyan Manchu Autonomous County ([tpl]zh|s=岫岩满族自治县 |t=岫岩滿族自治縣 |p=Xiùyán Mǎnzú Zìzhìxiàn[/tpl]) is a county in the southeast of Liaoning province, People's Republic of China, and is also one of the 11 Manchu autonomous counties and one of 117 autonomous counties nationally. It is under the administration of and occupies the southernmost portion of Anshan City, the centre of which [tpl]convert|136|km[/tpl] to the north-northwest, and has a population of 500,000[tpl]when|date=July 2012[/tpl].

==Geography and climate==

Xiuyan borders Yingkou and Gaizhou to the west, Donggang and Zhuanghe to the south and Haicheng and Liaoyang to the north. Its latitude ranges from 40° 00' to 40° 39' N and longitude 122° 52' to 123° 41' E, and the area is [tpl]convert|4507|km2|abbr=on[/tpl]. The county's area is dominated by low-lying mountains part of the Changbai Mountains, with the highest peak in the county, Mount Maokui (帽盔山), at [tpl]convert|1141.5|m|abbr=on[/tpl], being the highest point in southern Liaoning.
Xiuyan has a monsoon-influenced humid continental climate (Köppen Dwa) characterised by hot, humid summers, due to the monsoon, and rather long, cold, and very dry winters, due to the Siberian anticyclone. The four seasons here are distinctive. A majority of the annual rainfall occurs in July and August. The monthly 24-hour average temperatures ranges from [tpl]convert|−9.9|°C|1[/tpl] in January to [tpl]convert|23.2|°C|1[/tpl] in July, while the annual mean is [tpl]convert|7.93|°C|1[/tpl]. Due to the mountainous location, temperatures tend to be slightly cooler than areas to the south and west, and summer rainfall is more generous.
