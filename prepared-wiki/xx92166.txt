[[James Arnold Taylor]]

CATEGORIES: 1969 births, Living people, 20th-century American male actors, 21st-century American male actors, American impressionists (entertainers), American male voice actors, American male video game actors, Male actors from Santa Barbara, California

James Arnold Taylor (born July 22, 1969) is an American voice actor. He is known for his role as Obi-Wan Kenobi in the 2003 Star Wars: Clone Wars micro-series and the 2008 Star Wars: The Clone Wars series, Johnny Test in Johnny Test, Wooldoor Sockbat from Drawn Together, Tidus from the Final Fantasy X, Ratchet from the Ratchet & Clank video game series, and the current voice of Fred Flintstone.

==Early life==

Taylor was born and raised in Santa Barbara, California.[tpl]cite web|title=James Arnold Taylor IMDB|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0852517/[/tpl]

==Voice acting career==

Taylor has been voice acting since 1995, appearing in the Japanese game "Emit". He has been an active member of the voice acting community since, appearing in numerous roles for both television, film, and video games.

==Personal life==

Taylor is a long-time comic book and Star Wars fan. He is married to Allison Taylor. Together they have one daughter named Lydia.
In 2013, his book "JAT 365: 365 Inspirations for the Pursuit of Your Dreams" was published.
