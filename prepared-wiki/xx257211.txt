Jean Bourbeau (born December 13, 1983) is a Canadian professional ice hockey player who is currently playing for the Elmira Jackals in the ECHL.

==Playing career==

Jean Bourbeau played his junior career in Grande Prairie, Alberta (2001-2004) with the Grande Prairie Storm of the AJHL Alberta Junior Hockey League, winning the league championship in 2004. Bourbeau attended St. Thomas University in Fredericton, New Brunswick (2004-2008). He played for the St. Thomas Tommies in the AUS Atlantic University Sport division of the CIS Canadian Interuniversity Sport hockey league. Bourbeau played his first three professional seasons (2008â€“11) with the Odessa Jackalopes of the CHL Central Hockey League and the Bridgeport Sound Tigers of the AHL American Hockey League. During his fourth professional season, (2011-12 season) Bourbeau served as an assistant captain with the Elmira Jackals of the ECHL and played with the Adirondack Phantoms of the AHL American Hockey League. As a fifth year pro he served as captain of the Elmira Jackals of the ECHL and earned a six game professional try out (PTO) with the Binghamton Senators of the AHL American Hockey League.
