[[Hassan Johnson]]

Hassan "Iniko" Johnson is an American actor/producer from Staten Island, NY, born November 19, 1976. His most noted performance was appearing on the HBO program The Wire as Roland "Wee-Bey" Brice.

==Career==

Johnson's first acting role was in the 1995 Spike Lee film Clockers.[tpl]cite web|url=http://bemagazine.me/2012/05/17/hassan-johnson-being-more-progressive/ |title=Hassan Johnson: BEing More Progressive |publisher=Bemagazine.me |date=2012-05-17 |accessdate=2013-03-28[/tpl] He also had a significant role in the motion picture In Too Deep, which was based on a true story. He also had a recurring role on ER as Darnell Thibeaux. Johnson has appeared in Brooklyn's Finest, Belly and The Devil's Own.[tpl]cite web|first=Mark |last=Voger |url=http://www.nj.com/entertainment/index.ssf/2010/07/brooklyns_finest_dvd_review_dr.html |title='Brooklyn's Finest' DVD review |publisher=New Jersey On-Line |work=The Star-Ledger |date=2010-07-10 |accessdate=2013-03-28[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/review?res=9B02EFDB113FF937A35752C1A96E958260 |title=FILM REVIEW; For Young Blacks, Decency vs. Crime |work=The New York Times |author=Lawrence Van Gelder |date=November 4, 1998 |accessdate=2013-03-28[/tpl]
He owns a production company called Autumn Leaves which he named after his daughter.[tpl]cite web|title=Hassan Johnson: Unconventional Means|url=http://allhiphop.com/2004/12/03/hassan-johnson-unconventional-means/|accessdate=28 March 2012[/tpl]
