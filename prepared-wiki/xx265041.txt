[[North Carolina Highway 93]]

CATEGORIES: State highways in North Carolina, Transportation in Alleghany County, North Carolina

North Carolina Highway 93 (NC 93) is a primary state highway in the U.S. state of North Carolina.  It runs from the Virginia state line to the community of Twin Oaks.

==Route description==

NC 93 begins at the Virginia state line and goes southeast for [tpl]convert|10|mi|km[/tpl] on a curvy, yet manageable road that ends at US 221.  It is overlapped with NC Bike Route 4 along the entire length of the route.

==History==

Originally established as North Carolina Highway 260 in 1935, it was renumbered in 1940 to match the older Virginia State Route 93.  In 1977, NC 93 was rerouted in Piney Creek to its current routing; abandoning Pugh Road and extending NC 113 further north to its current northern terminus.[tpl]cite web |url=http://members.cox.net/ncroads/nc093.html|title=NCRoads.com: N.C. 93|accessdate=2011-01-02[/tpl]
