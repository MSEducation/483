Otto DesslochHis name, in German, is spelled with a "sharp S"; see ß. (11 June 1889 – 13 May 1977) was a German World War II Luftwaffe general and recipient of the Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross with Oak Leaves. The Knight's Cross of the Iron Cross and its higher grade Oak Leaves was awarded to recognise extreme battlefield bravery or successful military leadership.
Dessloch was born at Bamberg. During World War II Dessloch commanded Luftwaffe units in the Eastern Front. After Paris was liberated by the Allied forces Dessloch commanded an air unit which avenged the liberation by bombing the city destroying civilian targets and killing 200 French civilians on September 1944.Mitcham 2007, pp. 185–195.
According to the Yad Vashem Memorial in Israel, Otto Dessloch is a war criminal.Yad Vashem Photo Archive, Germany, War criminal Otto Dessloch
He died in Munich in 1977.
