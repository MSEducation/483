[[Rosie Huntington-Whiteley]]

CATEGORIES: 1987 births, Living people, People from Tavistock, Devon, English people of Polish-Jewish descent, English female models, English film actresses, Actresses from Devon, 21st-century English actresses

Rosie Alice Huntington-Whiteley (born 18 April 1987) is an English model and actress. She is best known for her work for lingerie retailer Victoria's Secret and Burberry, and also for her role as Carly Spencer in the 2011 film Transformers: Dark of the Moon, the third installment in the Transformers film series.

==Career==

Whilst a 15 year old student, she undertook work experience at West End, London-based model agency Profile, before taking her GCSEs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.plymouthherald.co.uk/m-luckiest-girl-says-model-Rosie/story-12696409-detail/story.html|title=I'm the luckiest girl|publisher=This is Plymouth|date=26 July 2008|accessdate=12 November 2013[/tpl] Signed after graduating in summer 2003 aged 16, her first modelling session was for a Levi's jeans commercial. She got her first big break in January 2004 when she travelled to New York for a shoot with Teen Vogue. She later made her catwalk debut alongside Naomi Campbell in New York in Spring 2004, and was then photographed by Bruce Weber for Abercrombie & Fitch.[tpl]cite web|url=http://nymag.com/fashion/models/rhuntington/rosiehuntington/|title=Fashion: Rosie Huntington|work=New York|accessdate=27 October 2011[/tpl]
In early 2006, she was tipped to be "the next Kate Moss" by fashion experts, picked as one of five international models to look out for in the following 12 months. She was signed that year to American lingerie brand Victoria's Secret, making her debut for the brand at the brand's 2006 show in Los Angeles.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2012[/tpl]
