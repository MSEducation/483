[[Big River (Oregon)]]

CATEGORIES: Rivers of Oregon, Landforms of Lane County, Oregon, Landforms of Douglas County, Oregon

The Big River is a tributary of the Coast Fork Willamette River, approximately 12 mi (19 km) long, in western Oregon in the United States. It drains an area of the Calapooya Mountains south of Eugene.
It rises in northern Douglas County, northwest of Huckleberry Mountain, approximately 20 mi (32 km) south-southeast of Cottage Grove at [tpl]coord|43.540398|-122.897840|type:river_source:gnis-1117659_region:US-OR|name=Big River source[/tpl]. It flows northwest through Lane County to form the Coast Fork of the Willamette at its confluence with Little River, approximately 15 mi (24 km) south of Cottage Grove at [tpl]coord|43.5953987|-123.0675727|type:river_source:gnis-1117659_region:US-OR|display=inline|name=Big River mouth[/tpl]

==Course==

Flowing northwest from the northern flanks of the Calapooya Mountains in Douglas County, Big River enters Lane County about [tpl]convert|1|mi|km[/tpl] downstream from the source. Box Canyon enters from the right at about river mile (RM) 9 or river kilometer (RK) 14. Big River receives Boulder Creek from the left, then Edwards Creek enters from the right at about RM 6 (RK 10). After receiving Bar Creek from the left and Martin Creek from the right, Big River meets Little River to form the Coast Fork Willamette River about [tpl]convert|40|mi|km[/tpl] from where the larger stream meets the Middle Fork Willamette River to form the Willamette River.
