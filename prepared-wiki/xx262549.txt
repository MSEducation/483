[[Hilary Weaver]]

CATEGORIES: 1991 births, Living people, Canadian pop singers, Canadian singer-songwriters, Musicians from Toronto

Hilary Weaver (born May 2, 1991) is a singer-songwriter born in Toronto.

==Career==

In 2010, Weaver won the award in the Great American Song Contest for best pop song.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.greatamericansong.com/winners.html |title=Winners - Great American Song Contest |publisher=Greatamericansong.com |date= |accessdate=2011-01-07[/tpl] Also in 2010, Weaver's debut EPK was released on February 24, 2010 titled Tell The Next Girl.  In 2009, Weaver received a distribution deal with  Maple Music Canada.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.maplemusic.com/artists/hwv/default.asp |title=Hilary Weaver Home |publisher=Maplemusic.com |date= |accessdate=2011-01-07[/tpl] Weaver won Best Female Vocal at the Hollywood Music in Media Awards in Los Angeles, California.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hmmawards.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=169&Itemid=104 |title=2009 Winners |publisher=Hmmawards.com |date= |accessdate=2011-01-07[/tpl] Weaver also was a Top 10 finalist for British Music Week's Rising Star Award out of over 10,000 global entries in London, England in October 2009.[tpl]dead link|date=January 2011[/tpl] In 2008, Weaver began to work on her first album Tell The Next Girl with producer Mark Zubek.
In 2012, Weaver released her second album Heart Breakout.
