[[Balatonring]]

CATEGORIES: Grand Prix motorcycle circuits, Superbike World Championship circuits, Motorsport venues in Hungary, Proposed sports venues

Balatonring was to be a 4.650 km long motor racing circuit, located in Sávoly, Hungary near Central and South Europe's biggest lake Balaton. The circuit is located about 180 km away from Budapest, the capital city of Hungary. Spain's Balatonring Zrt was to operate the racing circuit with 70% share owned by Worldwide Circuit Management S.L. (WCM) and Magyar Turizmus Zrt. (Hungarian Tourism Board) owning 30%.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.motogp.com/en/news/2008/Hungarian+MotoGP+round+proposition+unveiled+in+Budapest |title=Hungarian MotoGP round proposition unveiled in Budapest |publisher=motogp.com |date=2008-07-23 |accessdate=2009-03-31[/tpl] Spanish investment group Sedesa would have been the constructor at a cost of €80 million and seating for 110,000 to 140,000 would have been constructed.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.foxsports.com.au/story/0,8659,24069281-23770,00.html |title=Hungary to build MotoGP circuit |publisher=foxsports.com.au |date=2008-07-24 |accessdate=2009-03-31[/tpl]
Construction work on the 200 million Euro project had started on 6 November 2008, and the facility was due to host its first MotoGP race on 20 September 2009. However, on 11 March 2009, motogpmatters.com reported that the Hungarian round would be canceled due to problems related to the Financial crisis of 2007–2010. The circuit would not be completed in time for the September 20th race.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.motogpmatters.com/news/2009/03/11/hungarian_motogp_round_officially_cancel.html |title=Hungarian MotoGP Round Officially Canceled |publisher=MotoGPMatters.com |date=2009-03-11 |accessdate=2009-03-31[/tpl] The first Hungarian GP was scheduled to take place in September 2010.
On 18 March 2010 the Hungarian GP was cancelled as the FIM said the construction work that needed to be done will not be done in time for the event. It was replaced by the Motorland Aragon.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.autosport.com/news/report.php/id/82220 |title=Aragon replaces Hungary on calendar |publisher=autosport.com |date=2010-03-18 |accessdate=2009-03-18[/tpl]
As of November 2012, the plot has been on auction for a month. The project has accumulated a total debt of HUF 3.5 bn (USD 16,000,000) and the plot is being sold for HUF 1.5 bn (USD 6,800,000). Nothing can be seen from the track line as weeds have grown over it.[tpl]cite news |url=http://sportgeza.hu/sport/2012/11/20/arverezik_a_meg_sem_epult_savolyi_motorpalyat/ |title=Unfinished racetrack on auction (in Hungarian)|publisher=index.hu|date=2012-11-20 |accessdate=2012-11-20[/tpl]
