[[Dry River (Northern Territory)]]

CATEGORIES: Rivers of the Northern Territory

The Dry River is a river in the Northern Territory of Australia. It is a tributary of the King River which eventually flows into the Timor Sea.

Forster |date=1978 |work= |publisher=Territory Parks and Wildlife Commission |accessdate=20 October 2011 [/tpl]  The eastern parts of the catchment are bounded by the Sturt Plateau. Upper parts of the river have been described as weakly developed.[tpl]cite web |url=http://hdl.handle.net/10070/229643 |title=Water Resources of the Sturt Plateau region, overview report |author=Des Yin Foo |date=June 2002 |publisher=Department of Infrastructure, Planning and Environment |accessdate=20 October 2011 [/tpl]
Western Creek was once a major tributary of the Dry River but it now flows into Elsey Creek. There are a number of waterholes which are permanent on the black soil plains of the river.
