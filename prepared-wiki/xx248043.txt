[[Tetrapathaea]]

CATEGORIES: Passifloraceae, Passiflora, Monotypic plant genera, Flora of New Zealand

Tetrapathaea tetrandra is a plant species endemic to New Zealand, the only species of the genus Terapathaea currently recognized. Common names include Kohia, New Zealand passionflower, New Zealand passion vine and New Zealand passionfruit.NZ Plant Conservation Network 
Tetrapathaea tetrandra is a vine climbing up to 10 m (33 feet). Leaves are alternate, broadly lanceolate, green, shiny, and untoothed. Flowers are white to yellow, in groups of 1-3 in the axils of the leaves. Fruits are lemon-shaped, orange, up to 30 mm (1.2 inches) long, inedible by humans.Raoul, Édouard Fiacre Louis. 1844. Annales des Sciences Naturelles; Botanique, sér. 3 2: 122.Candolle, Augustin Pyramus de, 1822.  Prodromus Systematis Naturalis Regni Vegetabilis 3: 323.
