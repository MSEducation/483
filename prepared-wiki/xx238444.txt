[[Pine Creek Lake]]

CATEGORIES: Lakes of Oklahoma, Geography of McCurtain County, Oklahoma, Geography of Pushmataha County, Oklahoma

Pine Creek Lake is a lake in McCurtain County and Pushmataha County, Oklahoma, USA. It is [tpl]convert|8|mi|km[/tpl] north of Valliant, Oklahoma.Oklahoma Department of Wildlife Conservation. "Pine Creek Wildlife Management Area." Retrieved December 5, 2013. It is located east of Rattan and north of Sobol.
The lake, which was built in 1969, impounds the waters of Little River, Pine Creek, and Turkey Creek.  It is managed by the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers for flood control, water supply, fish and wildlife, and recreational purposes.  The adjacent Pine Creek Wildlife Management Area extends this mission. 
Pine Creek Lake comprises [tpl]convert|3750|acre|km2[/tpl] of area and [tpl]convert|74|mi|km[/tpl] of shoreline.  Normal pool elevation is [tpl]convert|438|ft|m[/tpl] above sea level.  Its flood pool capacity is [tpl]convert|465,780|acre.ft|m3|lk=in[/tpl], at which time its flood pool is at an elevation of [tpl]convert|480|ft|m[/tpl] above sea level.  Norman pool capacity is [tpl]convert|53750|acre.ft|lk=in[/tpl].“Pine Creek Lake”, Oklahoma Water Atlas, Oklahoma Water Resources Board.
Pine Creek Lake divides the Little River into upper and lower reaches.  It is one of two impoundments on the river, the other being Millwood Lake in Howard County, Arkansas.“Little River”, Southwest Paddler, http://southwestpaddler.com/docs/littleok.html. 
The wildlife management area covers[tpl]convert|10280|acre|km2[/tpl] and is adjacent to Little River and  Pine Creek Lake.
