[[Meat slicer]]

CATEGORIES: Food preparation appliances

A meat slicer, also called a slicing machine, deli slicer or simply a slicer, is a tool used in butcher shops and delicatessens to slice meats and cheeses. The first meat slicer was invented by Wilhelm van Berkel in Rotterdam in 1898.[tpl]cite book|last=Feith|first=Jan|title=Modern Holland|publisher=Nijgh & van Ditmar's Publishing Co.,ltd|year=1922|pages=245[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.averyberkel.com/main.aspx?p=1.1.3.7|title=Berkel|publisher=Avery Berkel|accessdate=2008-10-05[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.berkelcompany.com/details.cfm?id=61|title=Company history|publisher=Berkel|accessdate=2008-10-05[/tpl] Older models of meat slicer may be operated by crank, while newer ones generally use an electric motor.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emiliomiti.com/restaurant-news/vintage-hand-cranked-meat-slicers-popular-among-green-chefs-and-restaurants/|title=Vintage Hand-cranked Meat Slicers Popular Among ‘Green’ Chefs and Restaurants|publisher=Emiliomiti LLC|accessdate=2008-10-05[/tpl]
More recently, meat slicers have become available in the home market for people wanting to slice their own meats and cheeses.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chow.com/food-news/53592/ultimate-boy-toy/|title=Ultimate Boy Toy Meat slicers come out from behind the butcher counter|last=Chu|first=Louisa|publisher=Chow|accessdate=2008-10-05[/tpl]
