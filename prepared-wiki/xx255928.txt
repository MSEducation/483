[[Saint Elian (Wales)]]

CATEGORIES: Medieval Welsh saints

Saint Elian was a Catholic saint who founded a church in North Wales around the year 450. The Parish of Llanelian is named after him. The Legend of St. Elian says he was related to Ismael and labored in the missions of Cornwall, England. His feast day is January 13. 
Tradition holds that he came by sea from Rome and landed in Anglesey at Porth yr Yehen, where he built his church. One folk tale says he forbade the keeping of greyhounds after one killed or disturbed a doe in his care.Cambrian Archaeological Association (1876). Archaeologia cambrensis. W. Pickering
Llanelian Road in Colwyn Bay, North Wales is named after him. His connection with Eglwysilan is unclear.
