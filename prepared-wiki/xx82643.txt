[[USS Boston (SSN-703)]]

CATEGORIES: Los Angeles-class submarines, Cold War submarines of the United States, Nuclear ships of the United States Navy, United States Navy Massachusetts-related ships, Ships built in Connecticut, United States Navy Buffalo-related ships, 1980 ships

USS ''Boston'' (SSN-703), a Los Angeles-class submarine, was the seventh ship of the United States Navy to be named for Boston, Massachusetts.
The contract to build Boston was awarded to the Electric Boat Division of General Dynamics Corporation in Groton, Connecticut on 10 December 1973 and her keel was laid down on 11 August 1978.  She was launched on 19 April 1980 sponsored by Mrs. Edward Hidalgo, and commissioned on 30 January 1982, with Captain Jon M. Barr in command.
In 1998 Boston participated in a UNITAS South America deployment.

==Decommissioning==

Boston was decommissioned on 19 November 1999 and stricken from the Naval Vessel Register on 19 November 1999. Ex-Boston entered the Nuclear Powered Ship and Submarine Recycling Program in Bremerton, Washington, on 1 October 2001 and on 19 September 2002 ceased to exist. Her sail and upper rudder were preserved for display at the Buffalo and Erie County Naval & Military Park in Buffalo, New York.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ussboston.org/img/SailStbd1109.jpg|title=Sub Sail Image|accessdate=18 June 2011[/tpl]

==Awards==

Boston was a much-decorated ship. The crew distinguished themselves on numerous occasions by meritorious service and outstanding performance.
