Ha-Neul "Sky" Kim (born 24 November 1982) is an Australian Olympic archer.

==Personal==

Sky's full name is Ha-Neul "Sky" Kim.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/ki/sky-kim-1.html |title=Sky Kim Biography and Olympic Results | Olympics at |publisher=Sports-reference.com |date= |accessdate=2012-06-07[/tpl] He is originally from South Korea, and was born in Daegu, Daegu, South Korea  on 24 November 1982.  He moved to Australia in 2005.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/archery/australias-archer-in-the-sky/2008/07/18/1216163203219.html |title=Archery — Sports — Olympics |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |first1=James |last2=Allen |first2=Rod |date=19 July 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl]  In 2006, he became an Australian citizen. He is [tpl]convert|183|cm[/tpl] tall and weighs [tpl]convert|95|kg[/tpl]. His father and brother were archers. He is married to a fellow archer, Hyun-Ok Jung, whom he met while training in archery in 2000.

==Archery==

Kim competes in archery. He was an Australian Institute of Sport scholarship holder.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ausport.gov.au/ais/history/achievements/olympics |title=Olympic Games : Australian Institute of Sport : Australian Sports Commission |publisher=Ausport.gov.au |date=2008-01-09 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] He was coached by Ki-Sik Lee, the head coach of the Australian Institute of Sport team in 2005.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/archery/going-for-gold-archery/2008/08/04/1217701864354.html |title=Archery — Sports — Olympics |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=4 August 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] To help with his training for archery, he has been known to do mountain climbing and play golf.
In 2004, he was the Korean national archery champion. In 2007, he competed at a Beijing based test event, where he won a gold medal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ausport.gov.au/ais/history/past_programs/archery |title=Archery : Australian Institute of Sport : Australian Sports Commission |publisher=Ausport.gov.au |date=2011-01-19 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] In 2008, he was training at the Australian Institute of Sport.  Going into the 2008 Games, he was coached by Australian Institute of Sport coach Kynmoon Oh. At the 2008 World Cup in Santo Domingo Dominican Republic, he was part of the Australian silver medal winning recurve team that.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.archery.org.au/customdata/index.cfm?fuseaction=CustomItem&ItemID=42412 |title=Archery Australia |publisher=Archery.org.au |date= |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] He returned to the Australian archery team in 2011 after an absence dating back to the 2008 Olympics.[tpl]cite web|url=http://wwos.ninemsn.com.au/article.aspx?id=8277528 |title=Archery: Search on for new coach |publisher=Wwos.ninemsn.com.au |date= |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] He attended a national team training camp in Canberra in September 2011. He attended a national team training camp in Canberra in March 2012. At the 2012 National Target Archery Championships, he finished first as a member of AACT.[tpl]cite web|url=http://210.247.205.118/assets/console/document/documents/2012nctargetfinal1.pdf|publisher=Archery Australia|accessdate=6 June 2012|title=2012 National Target Archery Championships|location=Tuggernong, Australian Capital Territory|date=March 2012[/tpl] In May 2012, he had to miss a national team in a training camp in Buderim because he had work commitments.[tpl]cite web|author=Mark Bode |url=http://www.frasercoastchronicle.com.au/story/2012/05/22/confident-tyack-all-set-for-olympic-shot/ |title=Confident Tyack set for Olympics | Shooting |publisher=Fraser Coast Chronicle |date=2012-05-22 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl]

===Olympics===

In 2004, he was the Korean national archery champion.  He aimed for Olympic selection from Korea but did not make the team. At the 2008 Summer Olympics in Beijing Kim finished his ranking round with a total of 665 points. This gave him the 14th seed for the final competition bracket in which he faced Romain Girouille in the first round, beating the French archer by 112-110. In the second round Kim was eliminated as Jacek Proć won the confrontation with 111-110.http://web.archive.org/web/20080907195827/http://results.beijing2008.cn/WRM/ENG/BIO/Athlete/1/210401.shtml  Together with Matthew Gray and Michael Naray he also took part in the team event. With his 665 score from the ranking round combined with the 654 of Gray and the 658 of Naray the Australians were in 9th position after the ranking round. In the first round they were defeated by eight seed Poland (223-218). The Polish would not come further than the quarter final. In 2008, he represented Australia at the 2008 Summer Olympics in archery. He was viewed as Australia's best medal chance in archery going into the Games.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/sport/kim-leads-archery-hopes-for-beijing-20080221-1tkw.html |title=Kim leads archery hopes for Beijing |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=21 February 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl]  He competed in both the team and individual event.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/sport/kim-leads-archery-hopes-for-beijing-20080221-1tkw.html |title=Kim leads archery hopes for Beijing |publisher=News.theage.com.au |date=2008-02-21 |accessdate=2012-03-26[/tpl]  Going into the knock out stage in the team competition, Australia was ranked ninth.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/archery/rivals-prove-poles-apart/2008/08/11/1218306780035.html |title=Archery — Sports — Olympics |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=12 August 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] In one of the early rounds of the 2008 Games, he beat Romain Giroullie of France with a score of 112-110.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2008-08-13/archers-on-target-early-for-australia/475178 |title=Archers on target early for Australia — ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation) |publisher=Abc.net.au |date=2008-08-13 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] In September 2011, he was named to the Australian archery shadow Olympic team.[tpl]cite web|url=http://archery.org.au/customdata/index.cfm?fuseaction=CustomItem&ItemID=42398 |title=Archery Australia High Performance Program Update |publisher=Archery Australia |first=Lucy |last=Warhurst |date=5 September 2011 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] In March at the 2012 Olympic Games Nomination Shoot Results, he finished first with a score of 2669.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.archery.org.au/news/index.cfm?fuseaction=NewsItem&NewsID=24579 |title=Archery Australia |publisher=Archery.org.au |date= |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl]
