[[Woodruff Hall]]

CATEGORIES: Indoor arenas in the United States, College basketball venues in the United States, Defunct sports venues in Georgia (U.S. state), Defunct college basketball venues in the United States, University of Georgia campus, Georgia Bulldogs basketball

Woodruff Hall was a 3,000 seat multi-purpose arena on the University of Georgia campus in Athens, Georgia.  It opened in 1923, replacing the Mattie Andrews Library in Founders Hall,[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=9sAKVj7_V6QC&lpg=PA62&dq=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&pg=PA62#v=onepage&q=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&f=false | title=Historic College Park | publisher=Arcadia Publishing | author=Davis, Kimberly Kennedy | year=2010 | pages=62 | isbn=9780738566788[/tpl] and was located on North Campus in the area bounded by Baldwin Street, Sanford Drive, and Hooper Street now occupied by the Psychology-Journalism Complex. It was home to the University of Georgia Bulldogs' basketball team. It was replaced when the Coliseum opened in 1964.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=EgolDRAuyHMC&lpg=PA98&dq=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&pg=PA40#v=onepage&q=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&f=false | title=A Walking Tour of the University of Georgia | publisher=University of Georgia Press | author=Boney, F. N. | year=1989 | isbn=9780820310817[/tpl] The Hall was one of the buildings that was used for the training of servicemen in the Second World War.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com.au/books?id=BipOZ3shvxUC&lpg=PA242&dq=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&pg=PA242#v=onepage&q=%22Woodruff%20Hall%22&f=false | title=The University of Georgia: A Bicentennial History, 1785-1985 | publisher=University of Georgia Press | author=Dyer, Thomas G. | year=1985 | pages=242 | isbn=9780820323985[/tpl]
