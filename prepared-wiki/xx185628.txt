[[Gmina Branice]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Opole Voivodeship, Głubczyce County

Gmina Branice is a rural gmina (administrative district) in Głubczyce County, Opole Voivodeship, in south-western Poland, on the Czech border. Its seat is the village of Branice, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|17|km|mi|0[/tpl] south of Głubczyce and [tpl]convert|69|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south of the regional capital Opole.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|121.87|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 7,728.

==Geography==

Gmina Branice is on the Głubczyce Plateau ([tpl]lang-pl|Płaskowyż Głubczycki[/tpl]; a part of the Silesian Lowlands) and partly in the Opawskie Mountains (a part of the Eastern Sudeten). Gmina Branice is located in the Oder River Basin (rivers: Opawa, Troja).

==Villages==

Gmina Branice contains the villages and settlements of Bliszczyce, Boboluszki, Branice, Dzbańce, Dzbańce-Osiedle, Dzierżkowice, Gródczany, Jabłonka, Jakubowice, Jędrychowice, Lewice, Michałkowice, Niekazanice, Posucice, Turków, Uciechowice, Włodzienin, Wódka and Wysoka.

==Partner communities==

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Branice is bordered by the gminas of Głubczyce and Kietrz. It also borders Czechia (obeces of Krnov, Úvalno, Brumovice, Holasovice and Opava).
