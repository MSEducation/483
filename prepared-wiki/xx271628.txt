[[Frank Pearl González]]

CATEGORIES: 1962 births, Living people, Colombian people of Canadian descent, People from Bogotá, University of Los Andes (Colombia) alumni, University of Western Ontario alumni, Colombian economists, Presidential advisers of Colombia, Peace Commissioners of Colombia, Ministers of Environment and Sustainable Development of Colombia

Frank Joseph Pearl González (born 1962) is a Colombian economist. He served as the 1st Minister of Environment and Sustainable Development serving in the administration of President Juan Manuel Santos Calderón, and as the 1st High Presidential Advisor for the Social and Economic Reintegration of People and Groups Up in Arms during the administration of President Álvaro Uribe Vélez between 2006 and 2010 during which time he was also entrusted the office of High Commissioner for Peace ad interim in replacement of Luis Carlos Restrepo Ramírez, until his resignation from both posts in 2010.[tpl]cite web |title=Decreto Número 2737 de 2010 |trans_title=Decree Number 2737 of 2010 |accessdate=2011-09-25 |url=http://web.presidencia.gov.co/decretoslinea/2010/julio/30/dec273730072010.pdf |date=2010-07-31 |language=Spanish |publisher=Colombia, Office of the President of Colombia |location=Bogotá[/tpl]
On 19 September 2011, President Juan Manuel Santos Calderón designated Pearl to head the process that divided the Ministry of Environment, Housing, and Territorial Development into two separate portfolios of environment and housing, and afterwards was entrusted the just created Ministry of Environment and Sustainable Development.[tpl]cite web |accessdate=2011-09-25 |language=Spanish |url=http://wsp.presidencia.gov.co/Prensa/2011/Septiembre/Paginas/20110919_04.aspx |title=Designado Frank Pearl como Ministro de Ambiente y Desarrollo Sostenible |trans_title=Frank Pearl Designated as Minister of Environment and Sustainable Development |location=Bogotá |date=2011-09-19 [/tpl]

==Personal life==

He was born in 1962 in Bogotá, D.C. to a Canadian father, John (Jackie) Pearl, and a Colombian mother, María Francisca González Gaitán.[tpl]cite journal |url=http://www.eltiempo.com/archivo/documento/MAM-2238751 |language=Spanish |title=El hombre del revolcón a la reinserción |date=2006-10-16 |accessdate=2011-09-26 |first=Francisco |last=Diaz Salazar |journal=El Tiempo [/tpl]
