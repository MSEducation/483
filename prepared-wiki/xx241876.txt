[[There Are Debts]]

CATEGORIES: 2010 albums

There Are Debts is an album by David Hopkins, released in December 2010.  It was produced by Matt Fish (musician and producer).  All songs were written by David Hopkins.  Strings and horns were arranged by David Hopkins.
To promote the album David Hopkins performed live on Today with Pat Kenny (Monday April 4, 2011).[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.rte.ie/radio1/todaywithpatkenny/2011-04-04.html | work=RTÃ‰ News | title=Monday, 4th April 2011[/tpl]  He also performed live on PBS' Vegas In Tune.

==Critical reaction==

The record was released to largely positive critical and fan reaction. Hot Press gave the record 4/5 stars, calling it "one helluva album" and stated regarding the title track that it is "a stunning track, superbly underpinned by sombre piano and brass".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hotpress.com/news/6125659.html |title=Damien Rice duets with David Hopkins |publisher=Hotpress.com |date= |accessdate=2011-02-22[/tpl]  The Sunday Times also gave the record 4/5 stars and wrote that it is "sweet and tuneful" and commented that "his harmonious duet with Laura Jansen...is a triumph" and a "stunningly beautiful ballad".[tpl]cite web|author=Post |url=http://www.thesundaytimes.co.uk/sto/news/ireland/article532658.ece |title=The Sunday Times |publisher=The Sunday Times |date= |accessdate=2011-02-22[/tpl]  The Irish Times declared that the album had "several shinning examples of nigh-on perfect songcraft here" though it only gave the album 3/5 stars.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/theticket/2011/0128/1224288435820.html |title=David Hopkins - The Irish Times - Fri, Jan 28, 2011 |publisher=The Irish Times |date=2011-01-28 |accessdate=2011-02-22 |first=Tony |last=Clayton-Lea[/tpl]  Las Vegas Weekly called Hopkins "an Ace singer-songwriter" and also gave the album 4/5 stars.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lasvegasweekly.com/news/2011/mar/30/whats-new-local-music/ |title=David Hopkins - Las Vegas Weekly - Wed, Mar 30, 2011 |publisher=Las Vegas Weekly |date=2011-03-31 |accessdate=2011-03-31[/tpl] RTE Radio 1 made it "Album of the Week".
