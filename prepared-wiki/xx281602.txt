[[Tubular sponge hydroid]]

CATEGORIES: Hydrozoa

The tubular sponge hydroid, Zyzzyzus warreni, is a species of hydroid cnidarian. It is a member of the family Tubulariidae. 

==Description==

The tubular sponge hydroid grows to about 1.5cm tall. It has a fat stem and a daisy-like polyp with sparse tentacles surrounding a group of reproductive sporosacs which in turn surround the pink and white oral tentacles. The body of the hydranths (feeding individuals) as well as the gonophores (reproductive polyps) are pinky red, while the tentacles are transparent white.Branch, G.M., Branch, M.L, Griffiths, C.L. and Beckley, L.E. 2010. Two Oceans: a guide to the marine life of southern Africa. ISBN 978-1-77007-772-0.

==Distribution==

This species is found around the South African coast from Saldanha Bay to KwaZulu-Natal as well as off the Cape Verde Islands and Trinidad. It is known from the subtidal to at least 30m underwater.

==Ecology==

These animals usually grow embedded in sponges.Millard, N.A.H. 1975. Monograph on the Hydroida of Southern Africa. Ann. S. Afr. Mus. 68:1-513
