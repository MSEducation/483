[[Conspicuous Only in Its Absence]]

CATEGORIES: The Great Society albums, 1968 debut albums, Columbia Records albums, English-language albums

Conspicuous Only in Its Absence is an album by the American psychedelic rock band The Great Society and was released in 1968 by Columbia Records.[tpl]cite web|title=Conspicuous Only in its Absence review|publisher=Allmusic|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=album|id=r39360/review|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|accessdate=2010-01-27[/tpl]  The album consists of recordings made during a live concert performance by the band at The Matrix club in San Francisco in 1966.[tpl]cite web|title=The Great Society Biography|publisher=Allmusic|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p24366|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|accessdate=2010-01-27[/tpl]  Additional recordings from the same concert were released later in 1968 on the album How It Was.[tpl]cite web|title=How It Was review|publisher=Allmusic|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=album|id=r39361|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|accessdate=2010-01-27[/tpl]  These two albums were repackaged in 1971 as a double album called Collector's Item.
Upon its initial release in 1968, Conspicuous Only in Its Absence reached #166 on the Billboard Top LPs chart.[tpl]cite web|title=Conspicuous Only in its Absence â€“ Billboard chart placing|publisher=Allmusic|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=album|id=r39360/charts-awards|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|accessdate=2010-01-27[/tpl]  A single featuring "Sally, Go 'Round The Roses" and "Didn't Think So" was released in conjunction with the album by Columbia Records but it failed to chart.[tpl]cite web|title=Conspicuous Only in its Absence|publisher=Deaddisc.com|url=http://www.deaddisc.com/ot/Conspicuous.htm|accessdate=2010-01-27[/tpl]
