[[Alafia barteri]]

CATEGORIES: Alafia, Plants described in 1891, Plants used in traditional African medicine, Flora of Liberia, Flora of Ivory Coast, Flora of Ghana, Flora of Togo, Flora of Benin, Flora of Nigeria, Flora of Cameroon, Flora of Gabon

Alafia barteri grows as a liana up to [tpl]convert|35|m[/tpl] long, with a stem diameter of up to [tpl]convert|3|cm[/tpl]. Its fragrant flowers feature a white corolla. Fruit is dark brown with paired cylindrical follicles, each up to [tpl]convert|50|cm[/tpl] in diameter. Habitat is lowland forest from sea-level to [tpl]convert|200|m[/tpl] altitude. Local medicinal uses include as a treatment for malaria and rheumatism.[tpl]cite book | title = Medicinal Plants | publisher = PROTA | year= 2008 | pages = 51â€“52 | isbn = 978-9-05782-204-9[/tpl] A. barteri is native to Liberia, Ivory Coast, Ghana, Togo, Benin, Nigeria, Cameroon and Gabon.[tpl]cite web | title = Alafia barteri | url = http://eol.org/pages/6851901/maps | work = Encyclopedia of Life | accessdate = 11 August 2013[/tpl]
