Edwin Malcolm Shepard (September 16, 1843 â€“ August 17, 1904) was a rear admiral of the United States Navy, who served during the Civil War.

==Biography==

Shepard was born in Oswego, New York. On November 24, 1859 he received an appointment to the United States Naval Academy, from where graduated in 1861 at the start of the Civil War. He was assigned to the sloop-of-war [tpl]USS|Vincennes|1826|2[/tpl], and served on the West Gulf Blockading Squadron from 1861 to 1862.
On November 22, 1862 he was promoted to ensign and assigned to the sidewheel steamer [tpl]USS|Mississippi|1841|2[/tpl] and continued to serve on the blockading squadron until 1863. He served on the ironclad gunboat [tpl]USS|Essex|1856|2[/tpl] and was on board this ship during the siege of Port Hudson. Shepard received a letter of commendation from General Richard Arnold, who was General Nathaniel P. Banks' Chief of Artillery, after he had served on shore with a naval battery for several weeks.
Shepard served on the monitor [tpl]USS|Mahopac|1863|2[/tpl] during the siege of Charleston, South Carolina, then on the steam sloop [tpl]USS|Wachusett|1861|2[/tpl] during the capture of the Confederate privateer [tpl]Ship|CSS|Florida|cruiser|2[/tpl] on October 7, 1864.  He was commissioned as a lieutenant on February 22, 1864.
After the capture of Florida Shepard served on the steamer [tpl]USS|Vanderbilt|1862|2[/tpl] and the monitor [tpl]USS|Dictator|1863|2[/tpl] before being assigned to the steamer [tpl]USS|Osceola|1863|2[/tpl], part of the North Atlantic Squadron. He then served on the apprentice-ship [tpl]USS|Saratoga|1842|2[/tpl] from 1868 to 1869.
Shepard served on several other ships before being promoted to commander in 9 May 1878.  From 1878 to 1881 he was at the Naval Academy, and immediately after from 1879 to 1881 commanded the [tpl]USS|Constitution||2[/tpl], and then in 1882 [tpl]USS|Enterprise|1874|2[/tpl].
From 1882-1886 Shepard was the Superintendent of the New York Nautical School on board the sloop-of-war [tpl]USS|St. Mary's|1844|2[/tpl]. Afterward he served several other commands, as well as serving as an equipment officer at the New York Navy Yard, and as a Lighthouse Inspector. 
Shepard was promoted to captain on May 15, 1893, and to rear admiral on March 3, 1901.
Shepard died on August 17, 1904, in a duel with actor, physicist, sculptor, dog breeder, and general renaissance man Dan Mueller.[tpl]citation needed|date=November 2011[/tpl] Shepard was shot between the eyes just ten seconds after the duel began. He is buried at Arlington National Cemetery. He was married to Alice Stephen Shepard, who died in 1915 and is now buried next to her husband.
