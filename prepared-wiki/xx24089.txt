[[Ology]]

CATEGORIES: Social networking websites, Entertainment websites

Ology is an entertainment news and social media website based in New York City, United States. The key focus of Ology is to provide a place for users to share their passions, offering a platform where they can build communities, engage with one another, and discover new content amidst a vibrant gathering of active experts and professional editors.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ologycorp.com/content/about-us|title=About Us|publisher=Ology Corp|accessdate=May 7, 2012[/tpl]

==History==

Ology was founded by Beth Haggerty and Vivian Moran in June 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ologycorp.com/content/people|title=People|publisher=Ology Corp|accessdate=May 7, 2012[/tpl] It consists of daily updated news trends, original editorial content, interviews, reviews, recaps, and user communities related to TV, film, music, celebrities, fashion, geek culture, humor, politics, and sports.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ology.com/splash|title=Ology.com Splash|publisher=Ology Corp|accessdate=May 7, 2012[/tpl] Since the social platform was released to the public in early 2012, significant communities have developed around groups for fans of Supernatural, Being Human, X-Files, Darren Criss, Carrie Underwood, Tom Felton, and Austin Mahone, among others.

==Impact==

In May 2011, Ology ranked number 1 in growth on comScore.[tpl]cite web|title=Ology Media Launches "My.Ology" The First True "Interest-Based" Social Network|url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2011/11/prweb8985917.htm|publisher=PR Web|accessdate=7 May 2012[/tpl] Ology has been cited by The American Spectator,[tpl]cite news|url=http://spectator.org/blog/2011/10/17/the-day-ahead-monday-october-1|title=The Day Ahead: Monday, October 17|last=Glass|first=Kevin|date=October 17, 2011|work=The American Spectator|publisher=American Spectator Foundation|accessdate=November 5, 2011[/tpl] The Austin Chronicle,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.austinchronicle.com/blogs/news/2011-10-19/the-romney-offensive/|title=The Romney Offensive|first=Richard|last=Whittaker|date=October 19, 2011|work=The Austin Chronicle|publisher=Austin Chronicle Corporation|accessdate=November 5, 2011[/tpl] and MTV News.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.mtv.com/news/articles/1665460/beyonce-4-reviews.jhtml|title=Beyonce's 4: Early Reviews Are In!|last=Vena|first=Jocelyn|work=MTV News|publisher=Viacom|date=June 9, 2011|accessdate=November 5, 2011[/tpl] In April 2012, Geek Sugar highlighted Ology for its "hilarious, tongue-in-cheek news stories, viral videos, and trends," and Business Insider featured the website in December for its commercial potential as a social media website. In February 2013, a shifting focus to OogLyfe was noticed by several users, but when asked for an official statement Ology declined.
