[[Chadlington]]

CATEGORIES: Civil parishes in Oxfordshire, Villages in Oxfordshire, West Oxfordshire

Chadlington is a village and civil parish in the Evenlode Valley about [tpl]convert|3|mi|0[/tpl] south of Chipping Norton, Oxfordshire. The village has five neighbourhoods: Brookend, Eastend, Greenend, Millend and Westend.

==Archaeology==

There is a bowl barrow about [tpl]convert|1.5|mi[/tpl] west of the village. Bowl barrows range from late Neolithic to late Bronze Age, i.e. 2400 to 1500 BC. The barrow is a scheduled monument.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1014562 |title=Bowl barrow 525m north of Barter's Hill Farm |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl]
Knollbury is a hill-fort [tpl]convert|1|mi[/tpl] northwest of the village. It is a scheduled monument.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1015322 |title=Knollbury camp hillfort |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl]

==Manors==

Chadlington village existed by the time of Domesday Book in 1086 and may be named after Saint Chad.[tpl]sfn|Corbett|1962|p=20[/tpl] The present Chadlington Manor House was built in the 17th century[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] and remodelled in about 1800.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1052835 |title=Manor House and Attached Walls and Outbuildings |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl] It is a Grade II* listed building.
Lower Court farmhouse was built in about 1700 as the manor house for Westend.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1368038 |title=Lower Court Farmhouse |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl] It was altered in the mid- to late-18th century and remodelled in the 19th. The house has a Stonesfield Slate roof. It is a Grade II* listed building.

==Churches and chapel==

===Church of England===

The Church of England parish church of Saint Nicholas was originally Norman,[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=524[/tpl] and the blocked head of a Norman window above the north arcade show that the building had a clerestory in Norman times.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=524[/tpl] In the 13th century Early English Gothic north and south aisles were added, with four-bay arcades linking the aisles with the nave.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|pp=524–525[/tpl] Both aisles still retain some lancet windows from this period.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl]
The bell tower was built early in the 14th century in the Decorated Gothic style.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] A chapel was added at the east end of the north aisle. The chapel's east and north windows in the Transitional style between Decorated and Perpendicular Gothic date it to of the chancel about the middle of the 14th century.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] Later pure Perpendicular Gothic additions include the windows and north door in the north aisle, the present clerestory and nave roof and the chancel arch.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] In 1870 the Gothic Revival architect Charles Buckeridge completely rebuilt the chancel.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] St. Nicholas' church is a Grade II* listed building.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1368037 |title=Church of St Nicholas, Eastend |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl]
The tower has a ring of six bells. Abraham I Rudhall of Gloucester[tpl]cite web |url= http://dove.cccbr.org.uk/founders.php |title=Bell Founders |author=Dovemaster |date=25 June 2010 |work=Dove's Guide for Church Bell Ringers |publisher= |accessdate=3 February 2011[/tpl] cast the second and third bells in 1714.[tpl]cite web |url= http://dove.cccbr.org.uk/detail.php?searchString=Chadlington&DoveID=CHADLINGTO |title=Chadlington S Nicholas |last=Davies |first=Peter |date=21 January 2007 |work=Dove's Guide for Church Bell Ringers |publisher= |accessdate=3 February 2011[/tpl] William Taylor of Loughborough cast the fifth and tenor bells in 1846 at the bellfoundry he then had in Oxford. Thomas Bond of Burford cast the fourth bell in 1911. The Whitechapel Bell Foundry cast the present tenor bell in 2006. There is also a Sanctus bell that Thomas Bond cast in 1911.
In 2001 the Church of England Benefice of Ascott-under-Wychwood, Chadlington and Spelsbury merged with that of Enstone and Heythrop to form the Chase Benefice.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.achurchnearyou.com/chadlington-st-nicholas/ |work=A Church Near You |title=Chadlington: St Nicholas, Chadlington |author=Archbishops' Council |publisher=Church of England |year=2010 |accessdate=[/tpl]
The Parsonage was designed by William Wilkinson and built in 1863.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] It is now Chadlington House.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl]

===Baptist===

Chadlington Baptist chapel was built in 1840.[tpl]sfn|Sherwood|Pevsner|1974|p=525[/tpl] It is now a private house.

===Methodist===

Chadlington has a Methodist church. It is a member of Chipping Norton and Stow on the Wold Methodist Circuit.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.methcircuit2304.org.uk/home.php?page=1&church=5 |title=Chadlington |work=Churches |publisher=Chipping Norton and Stow on the Wold Methodist Circuit 23/04 |date= |accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl]

==Social and economic history==

Chadlington used to have three public houses, the Malt Shovel, the Tite Inn, and the Sandys Arms.[tpl]cn|date=December 2013[/tpl] The Tite Inn was closed between 2009 and 2012, when it reopened with new owners.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.thetiteinn.co.uk/about/ |title=About us (and the Tite) |accessdate=15 December 2013[/tpl] and the Sandys Arms on Bull Hill closed some years earlier.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1052832 |title=Sandys House |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate= 23 January 2012[/tpl] 
Sir Henry Rawlinson (1810–95) and his brother Canon George Rawlinson (1812–1902) were born at Chadlington.[tpl]cn|date=January 2012[/tpl] Ivan Cameron, son of Conservative Party Leader and Prime Minister David Cameron is buried at Chadlington.[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/newstopics/politics/david-cameron/4933365/Ivan-Camerons-favourite-book-read-at-his-funeral.html |first=Andrew |last=Pierce |title=Ivan Cameron's favourite book read at his funeral |newspaper=The Daily Telegraph |date=3 March 2009 |accessdate=[/tpl]
