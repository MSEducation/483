[[Drew Nelson (politician)]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Ulster Unionist Party politicians, Councillors in Northern Ireland, Members of the Orange Order, Year of birth missing (living people), Irish Anglicans

Drew Nelson LLB is a solicitor,http://www.nelson-singleton.co.uk/Misc/Profiles/drew_nelson.htm former politicianhttp://www.guardian.co.uk/uk/2006/jul/14/northernireland.northernireland and Grand Secretary of the Grand Orange Lodge of Ireland.
In 1992 Nelson was selected by the South Down constituency of the Ulster Unionist Party to succeed Enoch Powell as parliamentary candidate. He lost to Eddie McGrady by 6000 votes.http://www.ark.ac.uk/elections/bsd.htm In 1989 he topped the poll in Dromore DEA and was elected to Banbridge District Council.http://www.ark.ac.uk/elections/85-89lgbanbridge.htm He retained his seat in 1993http://www.ark.ac.uk/elections/lgbanbridge.htm and went on to become Chairman of the Council before standing down in 1997. In 1996 he expressed support for the UUP ending or reforming its link with the Orange in an attempt to broaden its appeal, for which he was heckled.Feargal Cochrane, Unionist politics and the politics of Unionism since the Anglo-Irish Agreement, 2001, pp 343-344 He also supported power sharing with the Social Democratic and Labour Party in a devolved unitary legislature,Schulze, Kirsten, The Northern Ireland political process: A viable approach to conflict resolution? in Irish Political Studies, 1997, Volume 12 Issue 1 pp92-110 at pg102 however he opposed the Belfast Agreement.Kaufmann, E, The Orange Order, 2007, pg 216
He was fourth on the UUP list for election to the Northern Ireland Forum in South Belfasthttp://www.ark.ac.uk/elections/96sb.htm In 2004, he left the UUP and in 2005 became Grand Secretary of the Orange Institution.
