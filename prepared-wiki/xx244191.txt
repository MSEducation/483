[[Ethiopian general election, 1987]]

CATEGORIES: Ethiopian Civil War, 1987 elections in Africa, 1987 in Ethiopia, Elections in Ethiopia, Single-party elections

General elections were held in Ethiopia on 14 June 1987 for seats in its Shengo. This was the first election since Emperor Haile Selassie was deposed in the Ethiopian Revolution as well as the first election under the 1987 constitution, which established the People's Democratic Republic of Ethiopia (PDRE).

==Background==

The new Ethiopian Constitution, adopted by a referendum held on 1 February 1987, provided for a national parliament, the Shengo, as the supreme organ of State power. The date of the general elections was officially announced only two days in advance.
