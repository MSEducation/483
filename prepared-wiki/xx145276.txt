[[Masazumi Harada]]

CATEGORIES: People from Kagoshima Prefecture, Japanese physicians, 1934 births, 2012 deaths, Minamata disease

[tpl]nihongo|Masazumi Harada|原田 正純|Harada Masazumi|extra=September 14, 1934-June 11, 2012[/tpl] was a Japanese doctor and medical researcher. His most famous work covered the effects of Minamata disease, a type of severe mercury poisoning that occurred in the city of Minamata, Kumamoto Prefecture during the 1950s and 1960s. His publications included [tpl]nihongo|Minamata disease|水俣病|Minamata-byō[/tpl] (1972) and [tpl]nihongo|Minamata Ga Utsusu Sekai|水俣が映す世界|Minamata Ga Utsusu Sekai[/tpl] (1989).  He died June 11, 2012 of acute myelocytic leukemia at his home in Kumamoto.[tpl]cite news|title=Noted Minamata disease authority Masazumi Harada is dead at 77|url=http://www.japantimes.co.jp/text/nn20120613a8.html|accessdate=12 June 2012|newspaper=The Japan Times|date=13 June 2012[/tpl]
