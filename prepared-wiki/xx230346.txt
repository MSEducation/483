[[Grevillea pinaster]]

CATEGORIES: Grevillea, Eudicots of Western Australia, Proteales of Australia

Grevillea pinaster is a shrub in the family Proteaceae. It is endemic to south-western Western Australia. 
It  grows to between 0.5 and 3 metres in height and has a peak flowering period between May and September (late autumn to early spring) in its native range.[tpl]FloraBase|name=Grevillea pinaster |id=8838[/tpl]
The flowers are pink to red with yellowish- tipped styles . The long and narrow leaves are 20 to 55 mm long and 1 to 2 mm wide.
The species was first formally described by Carl Meissner in Hooker's Journal of Botany and Kew Garden Miscellany in 1855, from material collected by James Drummond.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.anbg.gov.au/cgi-bin/apni?TAXON_NAME=GREVILLEA+PINASTER|title=Grevillea pinaster |accessdate=2008-10-06|work= Australian Plant Name Index (APNI), IBIS database|publisher = Centre for Plant Biodiversity Research, Australian Government, Canberra[/tpl][tpl]Flora of Australia Online|name=Grevillea pinaster|id=45431 [/tpl]
The species occurs in a small area from north of Eneabba to Bindi Bindi.
Plants labelled as Grevillea stenomera in plant nurseries are often forms or hybrids of this species.[tpl]cite book|author=Olde, P. and Marriott, N.|title=The Grevillea Book Volume 3| page=97-99|publisher=Kangaroo Press|location=Australia | year=1995 | isbn=0864176112[/tpl]
