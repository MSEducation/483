[[Echis pyramidum]]

CATEGORIES: Viperinae, Animals described in 1827

Echis pyramidum is a species of venomous viper endemic to Northeast Africa and the Arabian Peninsula. Three subspecies are currently recognized, including the nominate subspecies described here.[tpl]ITIS |id=634974 |taxon=Echis pyramidum |accessdate=2 August 2006[/tpl]

==Etymology==

The specific name, pyramidum, refers to the Egyptian pyramids.Geoffroy Saint-Hilaire I.E. p. pyramidum @ Tomáš Mazuch.

==Description==

The average total length (body + tail) is [tpl]convert|30|–|60|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] with a maximum total length of [tpl]convert|85|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] (possibly slightly more).

==Common names==

Northeast African carpet viper, Egyptian saw-scaled viper, Egyptian carpet viper, Geoffroy's carpet viper.Echis pyramidum at Munich AntiVenom INdex (MAVIN). Accessed 3 August 2007.

==Geographic range==

In northeastern Africa it occurs in northern Egypt, central Sudan, Eritrea, Ethiopia, Somalia, and northern Kenya. There are also scattered populations in the southwest of the Arabian Peninsula in western Saudi Arabia (south of the 18th parallel), Yemen, South Yemen (in Hadhramaut), and in Oman. 
The type locality given is "Egypte" (Egypt).
Disjunct populations reportedly occur in Algeria, Tunisia, Libya, and northern Egypt. It is absent in southern Egypt.
