[[Avatar Land]]

CATEGORIES: Avatar (2009 film), Disney's Animal Kingdom, Walt Disney Parks and Resorts attractions, Proposed amusement rides, Themed areas in Walt Disney Parks and Resorts, Amusement rides based on film franchises

Avatar Land is a themed area currently being constructed at Disney's Animal Kingdom in Lake Buena Vista, Florida based upon the film franchise by James Cameron.[tpl]cite news|last=Cody|first=Anthony|title=Disney to build Avatar attractions at its theme parks|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/film/film-news/8782056/Disney-to-build-Avatar-attractions-at-its-theme-parks.html|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=The Telegraph|date=22 September 2011[/tpl] Construction began on January 10, 2014, with the land expected to open by early 2017.[tpl]cite web|last=Smith|first=Thomas|title=Construction Begins For AVATAR-Inspired Land At Disney’s Animal Kingdom|url=http://disneyparks.disney.go.com/blog/2014/01/construction-begins-for-avatar-inspired-land-at-disneys-animal-kingdom/|work=The Walt Disney Company|publisher=The Disney Parks Blog|accessdate=10 January 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Disney to Build 'Avatar' Attraction in Theme Parks|url=http://www.foxnews.com/travel/2011/09/21/disney-to-build-avatar-attraction-in-theme-parks/|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=Fox News|date=21 September 2011|author=Associated Press[/tpl]

==History==

In Spring 2011, James Cameron was approached by The Walt Disney Company's chief executive officer, Bob Iger, regarding the possibility of Avatar-themed attractions in Walt Disney Parks & Resorts. On September 17, 2011, a long-term, exclusivity deal was signed between Disney, James Cameron's Lightstorm Entertainment and 20th Century Fox.[tpl]cite news|title=Details emerge on Disney's plans to bring 'Avatar' to its parks|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2011/sep/21/business/la-fi-ct-disney-avatar-20110921|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=20 September 2011|author=Chmielewski, Dawn C.|author2=MacDonald, Brady[/tpl] This deal was officially announced to the public on September 20, 2011, on the Disney Parks Blog.[tpl]cite web|last=Smith|first=Thomas|title=AVATAR Coming To Disney Parks|url=http://disneyparks.disney.go.com/blog/2011/09/avatar-coming-to-disney-parks/|publisher=Disney Parks Blog|accessdate=23 September 2011|date=20 September 2011[/tpl] The highlight of the announcement was that Disney's Animal Kingdom would receive a new themed area dubbed Avatar Land. The area is set to cost approximately $500 million.[tpl]cite news|title=Disney to license rights to 'Avatar' for theme park attractions|url=http://www.latimes.com/business/la-fi-ct-disney-avatar-20110921,0,6660553.story|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=21 September 2011|author=Chmielewski, Dawn C.|author2=Keegan, Rebecca[/tpl] Construction began in January of 2014, with a planned opening date between 3 and 5 years later. The deal could see Avatar attractions added to other Disney theme parks in the future, however, there are no immediate plans to do so.[tpl]cite news|title=Coming to Disney World: Avatar Land|url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/united-states/news/article.cfm?l_id=110&objectid=10753245|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=New Zealand Herald|date=22 September 2011|author=Australian Associated Press[/tpl]

==Design==

Avatar Land is designed by Walt Disney Imagineering with Avatar producers James Cameron and Jon Landau also providing creative assistance.[tpl]cite news|last=Weinstein|first=Joshua L.|title=James Cameron on 3D, "Titanic" and all things "Avatar"|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2011/09/22/us-jamescameron-3dmovies-idUSTRE78L0HS20110922|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=Reuters|date=21 September 2011[/tpl] The area will be an immersive world based upon the fictional exoplanetary moon, Pandora and will include Pandora's floating mountains and bioluminescent plants.[tpl]cite news|last=Richwine|first=Lisa|title=Disney, Cameron to create theme-park "Avatar"|url=http://ca.reuters.com/article/entertainmentNews/idCATRE78J5U020110921?sp=true|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=Reuters Canada|date=21 September 2011[/tpl] Avatar Land is expected to span several acres and will feature multiple rides and attractions, entertainment, audio-animatronics, 3-D holograms as well as retail, food and beverage outlets.[tpl]cite web|last=Staggs|first=Tom|title=Answering Your Questions About AVATAR at Disney Parks|url=http://disneyparks.disney.go.com/blog/2011/09/answering-your-questions-about-avatar-at-disney-parks/|publisher=Disney Parks Blog|accessdate=23 September 2011|date=20 September 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Clark|first=Jayne|title='Avatar' land is coming to Disney World|url=http://travel.usatoday.com/destinations/dispatches/post/2011/09/avatar-land-is-coming-to-disney-world/548364/1|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=USA Today|date=22 September 2011[/tpl] Although exact details of the attractions have not yet been announced or even devised,[tpl]cite news|title='Avatar' attraction coming to Disney's theme parks|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/travel/news-and-advice/avatar-attraction-coming-to-disneys-theme-parks-2358543.html|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=The Independent|date=21 September 2011[/tpl] James Cameron confirmed that a flying attraction featuring "3-D projections and creature designs that were cut from the original film" was a concept on the drawing board for the area. Avatar Land will include elements from the original Avatar film as well as its three, yet-to-be-released sequels.

===Location===

Avatar Land is being built in the former location of Camp Minnie-Mickey,[tpl]cite web|title=What’s Next for Disney’s Animal Kingdom|url=https://d23.com/whats-next-for-disneys-animal-kingdom-avatar-tree-of-life-d23-expo-japan/|work=The Walt Disney Company|publisher=Disney D23|accessdate=12 October 2013|date=11 October 2013[/tpl] which was originally earmarked for the Beastly Kingdom, a never-built themed land which would have been based around mythological creatures.[tpl]cite news|title=Disney Plans Wild Animal Kingdom in Florida|url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4182/is_19950621/ai_n10084978/|accessdate=23 September 2011|newspaper=The Journal Record|date=21 June 1995|author=Associated Press[/tpl]
