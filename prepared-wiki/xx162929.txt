[[African Union – United Nations Hybrid Operation in Darfur]]

CATEGORIES: Peacekeeping missions and operations involving the African Union, United Nations operations in Africa, United Nations operations in Sudan, United Nations Security Council mandates, War in Darfur, 2007 establishments in Sudan

The African Union – United Nations Hybrid Operation in Darfur[tpl]cite web|url=http://unamid.unmissions.org/Default.aspx#|title=African Union - United Nations Hybrid Operation in Darfur[/tpl]
(UNAMID) is a joint African Union and United Nations peacekeeping mission formally approved by United Nations Security Council Resolution 1769 on July 31, 2007,[tpl]UN document |docid=S-RES-1769(2007) |type=Resolution |body=Security Council |year=2007 |resolution_number=1769 |accessdate=2008-04-10|date=31 July 2007[/tpl] to bring stability to the war-torn Darfur region of Sudan while peace talks on a final settlement continue.
Its initial 12-month mandate has been extended to July 31, 2010.[tpl]UN document |docid=S-RES-1881(2009) |type=Resolution |body=Security Council |year=2009 |resolution_number=1881 |accessdate=2009-08-16|date=31 July 2009[/tpl] Its budget is approximately $106 million per month.[tpl]UN document |docid=S-2008-443 |type=Document |body=Security Council |year=2008 |document_number=443 |highlight=rect_124,988_830,1154 |page=8 |accessdate=2008-08-18|date=7 July 2008|title=Report of the Secretary-General on the deployment of the African Union-United Nations Hybrid Operation in Darfur[/tpl] Its force of about 26,000 personnel began to deploy to the region in October 2007. The 9,000-strong  African Union Mission in Sudan (AMIS), which was previously responsible for peacekeeping, had completely merged into this new force by December 31, 2007.UN Press Release
The mandate is for a force of up to 19,555 military personnel and 3,772 police, along with a further "19 formed police units comprising up to 140 personnel each."[tpl]UN document |docid=S-RES-1769(2007) |type=Resolution |body=Security Council |year=2007 |resolution_number=1769 |page=3 |accessdate=2008-08-18|date=31 July 2007[/tpl] The peacekeepers will be allowed to use force to protect civilians and humanitarian operations. UNAMID will be the first joint UN/AU force and the largest peacekeeping mission.
As of December 2008, it has deployed 15,136 total uniformed personnel, including 12,194 troops, 175 military observers, 2,767 police officers, supported by 786 international civilian personnel, 1,405 local civilian staff and 266 United Nations Volunteers.UNAMID Mission Site
