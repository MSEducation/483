[[Codex Theodulphianus]]

CATEGORIES: Vulgate manuscripts, 10th-century biblical manuscripts, Purple parchment, Bibliothèque nationale de France collections

The Codex Theodulphianus, designated Θ, is a 10th century Latin manuscript of the Old and New Testament. The text, written on vellum, is a version of the Latin Vulgate Bible. It contains the whole Bible,Bruce M. Metzger, The Early Versions of the New Testament, Oxford University Press, 1977, p. 344-345. with some parts written on purple vellum.

==Description==

The Book of Psalms and the four Gospels are written on purple parchment in letters of silver (initial letters are in gold). The text is written in a minute minuscule hand.[ref]
