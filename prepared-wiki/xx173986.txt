[[Garendon High School]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct schools in Leicestershire, Educational institutions established in 1954, Educational institutions disestablished in 2012, 1954 establishments in England, 2012 disestablishments in England

Garendon High School was a middle school located in Thorpe Acre, Loughborough, Leicestershire, England, UK.
It contained three years (7, 8 and 9) and altogether there were 558 pupils in 2008/2009. 
The school closed over Summer 2012.

==History==

Garendon High School, which opened in 1954, was a middle school for around 560 students aged 11 to 15.
The school was recognized in its 2007 Ofsted inspection as "A good school with many outstanding features".http://www.ofsted.gov.uk/provider/files/848227/urn/120253.pdf
In September 2008, the school was awarded the status of Specialist Sports College.
It was designated as a "Lead School" by the local authority and achieved International School Status and Healthy Schools Accreditation. The school also achieved the prestigious Artsmark Gold standard.
As of September 2012, Burleigh Community College and Garendon High School merged to become Charnwood College, a through school from ages 11 to 19.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-31616861.html |title=Burleigh and Garendon to become Charnwood College |work=Loughborough Echo |date=22 June 2012 |accessdate=10 August 2012[/tpl]
