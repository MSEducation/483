[[Sarcocornia quinqueflora]]

CATEGORIES: Amaranthaceae, Flora of Queensland, Flora of New South Wales, Flora of Victoria (Australia), Flora of South Australia, Eudicots of Western Australia, Halophytes, Caryophyllales of Australia

Sarcocornia quinqueflora, commonly known as Beaded Samphire, Bead weed, Beaded Glasswort or Glasswort,[tpl]cite web|title=Beaded Glasswort|work=Victorian Resources Online|publisher=Department of Primary Industries (Victoria)|url=http://vro.dpi.vic.gov.au/dpi/vro/vrosite.nsf/pages/water_sss_beaded_glasswort|accessdate=2011-12-28[/tpl] is a species of succulent halophytic coastal shrub. It occurs in wetter coastal areas of Australia and New Zealand.

==Description==

It grows as an erect perennial up to 50 centimetres high.[tpl]cite encyclopedia | author = Wilson, Paul G. | title = Chenopodiaceae | encyclopedia = Flora of Australia | volume = IV | pages = 278[/tpl][tpl]FloraBase | name = Sarcocornia quinqueflora (Ung.-Sternb.) A.J.Scott | id = 2593[/tpl]

==Taxonomy==

It was first published as Salicornia quinqueflora in 1866, but transferred into Sarcocornia when that genus was erected in 1977.[tpl]APNI | name = Sarcocornia quinqueflora (Ung.-Sternb.) A.J.Scott | id = 9171[/tpl]

==Distribution and habitat==

It occurs in south west and south east Australia, and also in parts of the Nullarbor Plain, and part of the east coast of Cape York Peninsula.
In New Zealand it's found mostly on the shoreline in regions throughout the North Island. In the South Island S. quinqueflora is widespread on the east coast, but isn't found on the west coast.[tpl]cite web|last=Zimer|first=Eduart|title=Succulent plants from down under – Sarcocornia quinqueflora – an odd succulent halophyte|url=http://www.fileden.com/files/2008/5/20/1920751/Various/Sarcocornia%20quinqueflora.pdf|publisher=fileden.com|accessdate=2012-05-20|date=September 2007[/tpl]
