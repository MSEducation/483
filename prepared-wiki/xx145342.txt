[[Teye]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1957 births, Flamenco guitarists, Dutch guitarists

Teye (pronounced tie-ya, born 1957 as Teije Wijnterp) is a Dutch guitarist, best known for his flamenco guitar work with the Joe Ely band between 1994 and 2000. After his musical career, Teye began building exclusive handmade guitars. 

==Biography==

Teye started learning guitar in 1968, and moved to London to pursue a musical career, unsuccessfully. He returned to the Netherlands and played in a number of rock and roll bands, after which he studied classical guitar at the conservatory in Groningen.[tpl]cite web| title = Teye & Viva El Flamenco (1979 - heden)| publisher = Popinstituut Nederland| url = http://www.popinstituut.nl/encyclopedie/biografie/teye-viva-el-flamenco.9193.html| accessdate = 2010-01-26[/tpl]
Starting in the early 1980s, he begins learning to play flamenco music in Andalusia, and spent the next six years alternately studying in Spain,[tpl]cite news| last = Sculley| first = Alan| title = Joe Ely's Latest is Not the Acoustic Record He Originally Planned| newspaper = The Morning Call|| page = D12| date = 1996-07-21| url = http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/mcall/access/15336172.html?dids=15336172:15336172&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Jun+21%2C+1996&author=ALAN+SCULLEY+%28A+free-lance+story+for+The+Morning+Call%29&pub=Morning+Call&desc=%60LETTER%27+OF+INTENT+CAPTION%3A+JOE+ELY%27S+LATEST+IS+NOT+THE+ACOUSTIC+RECORD+HE+ORIGINALLY+PLANNED&pqatl=google| accessdate = 2010-01-26[/tpl] and studying and performing in the Netherlands and the United States. Teye produces a solo flamenco guitar LP, El Gitano Punky (1988), and studies modern music at the conservatory in Rotterdam from 1990 to 1994. A visit to Austin, Texas, brought him in contact with Joe Ely, who integrated Teye’s flamenco guitar into his country-rock band on the 1995 release Letter to Laredo.[tpl]cite news| last = McCall| first = Michael| title = Restless Spirit| newspaper = Nashville Scene| date = 1995-08-31| url = http://www.nashvillescene.com/1995-08-31/stories/restless-spirit/| accessdate = 2010-01-25[/tpl]
Teye moved from Sevilla, where he had been living with his soon-to-be wife Belen Oliva Bermudez (a flamenco dancer), to Austin.
In 1996, then living in Austin, Teye and Belen got married and started a flamenco ensemble in Austin, Teye & Viva El Flamenco,[tpl]cite news| last = Legaspi| first = Andre| title = Thank you Teye, viva el flamenco!| newspaper = McGill Tribune| date = 2003-01-04| url = http://media.www.mcgilltribune.com/media/storage/paper234/news/2003/04/01AE/Thank.You.Teye.Viva.El.Flamenco-404541.shtml| accessdate = 2010-01-25[/tpl]
later called Teye & Belen, releasing a CD in 1999, Viva el Flamenco and one in 2004, "FlamencObsesionArte". He worked with Joe Ely again in 1998, on Twistin' in the Wind.[tpl]cite news| last = Skanse| first = Richard| title = Texas Twister: Joe Ely goes to the movies and back to the flatlands| newspaper = Rolling Stone|date = 1998-05-18| url = http://www.rollingstone.com/artists/joeely/articles/story/5922386/texas_twister| accessdate = 2010-01-25[/tpl]

==Teye Guitars==

Teye has established himself as a builder of electric guitars. Befriended with Tony Zemaitis, he also often equipped his guitars with metal tops, achieving "stunning looks".[tpl]cite news|title=Spanish Castle Magic: The Teye El Torero/Le Guitana R Series|last=Prown|first=Pete|date=April 2012|work=Vintage Guitar|page=130|accessdate=24 May 2012[/tpl] His Electric Gypsy La Llama was praised by Guitar Player as a "dazzling piece of guitar artistry."[tpl]cite news|title=Rev. of Teye, Electric Gypsy La Llama S-Series|last=Thompson|first=Art|date=January 2012|work=Guitar Player|pages=108–110|accessdate=6 February 2012[/tpl]
