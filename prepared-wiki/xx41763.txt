[[Enrique Guzmán]]

CATEGORIES: 1943 births, Living people, People from Caracas, Venezuelan people of Mexican descent, Naturalized citizens of Mexico, Mexican male film actors, Mexican male telenovela actors, Mexican television presenters, Mexican male singers, Venezuelan emigrants to Mexico, La Academia judges

Enrique Guzmán (born 1 February 1943) is a Venezuelan-born Mexican singer. He is the father of Mexican singer Alejandra Guzmán by his former wife, actress and politician Silvia Pinal.
Enrique Guzmán was born in Venezuela to Mexican parents. They moved back to Mexico when Enrique was 12. He studied medicine at the Universidad Nacional Autónoma de México although he did not complete his studies.
Guzmán has been a rock star in Mexico throughout the last half of the 20th century, known primarily for his translation of 1950s rock standards, such as "Jailhouse Rock", for Spanish-speaking listeners.[tpl]cite web|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p33026/biography|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|title=Biography: Enrique Guzmán|last=Phares|first=Heather|publisher=AMG|accessdate=9 May 2010[/tpl] In 1958 he joined "Los Teen Tops", along with the Martínez brothers and piano player, Sergio Martel. In 1959 they debuted in the US on CBS radio, and they released their famous version of "La Plaga" (Good Golly Miss Molly). He also wrote several hits for himself "Pensaba en tí", "La Ronchita", and for other Latin stars. [tpl]Citation needed|date=December 2009[/tpl]
He also appeared in films, such as Canta Mi Corazón in 1965. His hits include Spanish covers of "Put Your Head On My Shoulder", "Rolly Polly", "Bonnie Moronie" as well as the original "Dame Felicidad" ("Give Me Joy"). During the mid-1960s Guzmán formed a motion picture company and produced some films in Ecuador. [tpl]Citation needed|date=December 2009[/tpl] In the late 1960s, Guzman and his wife, Silvia Pinal, starred in a television show called Silvia y Enrique. He has continued recording, including with his daughter Alejandra Guzmán, throughout the 1990s and 2000s.  As with his ex-wife and his daughter, he has his handprints embedded onto the Paseo de las Luminarias; he was inducted in 1983 for his work in the recording industry.

==Family life==
