[[KLM uk]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct airlines of the United Kingdom, Airlines established in 1998, Airlines disestablished in 2003, Air France–KLM, KLM Cityhopper

KLM UK was the brand name of a British airline subsidiary of KLM, which operated services within the UK and between the UK and the Netherlands using ATR-72, Fokker 50 and Fokker 100 aircraft. KLM uk had its headquarters in the Stansted House on the grounds of London Stansted Airport in Stansted Mountfitchet, Essex."World Airline Directory." Flight International. 18–24 March 1998. 82.
KLM launched the brand name "KLM uk" in 1998, when KLM acquired majority ownership of AirUK, a commuter airline founded in 1980. Air UK began to be traded as KLM uk in January 1998. The legal name changed from Air UK Limited to KLM uk Limited."www.airuk.co.uk  has moved to a new site." KLM uk. Retrieved on 28 February 2010.

==Buzz and other operations==

In 2000, KLM uk launched Buzz, a low-cost subsidiary which operated a fleet of BAe 146 and Boeing 737-300 aircraft. [tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl]
Buzz was sold to Ryanair in 2003, at which point the remainder of KLM UK operations were merged into KLM Cityhopper.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl]
