[[New Zealand and South Seas Exhibition (1889)]]

CATEGORIES: World's Fairs in New Zealand, History of New Zealand, History of Dunedin, 1889 in New Zealand, Culture in Dunedin

The New Zealand and South Seas Exhibition was a world's fair held in Dunedin, New Zealand in 1889.[ref]
</ref> It opened on 26 November 1889 and ran until 19 April 1990<ref name="FindPelp414" /> with 625000 visits made, and made a profit.<ref name="FindPelp414" />
The fair celebrated that country and the South Seas. Exhibitions included New Zealand's Eiffel Tower, a 40 metre high wooden structure based on the Eiffel Tower built by the Austral Otis Elevator Company and used to display their products.[tpl]cite web|title=New Zealand's Eiffel Tower|url=http://www.nzhistory.net.nz/media/photo/new-zealands-eiffel-tower|publisher=Ministry for Culture and Heritage|accessdate=12 November 2011[/tpl] The tower was estimated to cost £1200 and included an elevator that travelled about 30 m. A smaller Eiffel Tower, without an elevator, was situated in an adjacent garden area, near the internal courtyard of the exhibition.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nzhistory.net.nz/page/new-zealand%E2%80%99s-own-eiffel-tower-opens|accessdate = 12 November 2011|title=New Zealand’s own Eiffel Tower open|publisher=(Ministry for Culture and Heritage)[/tpl]
John Roberts was President of the Exhibition and for his services was awarded the Companionship of the Most Distinguished Order of St. Michael and St. George, in 1891.
