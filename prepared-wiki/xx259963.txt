[[Vedde]]

CATEGORIES: Cities and towns in Region Zealand

Vedde is a village located in the Sorø Municipality in Region Sjælland on the island of Zealand (Sjælland) in east Denmark. As of 1 January 2014 it has a population of 305."BEF44: Population 1st January, by urban areas" database from Statistics Denmark 
The railway station was built in 1901 as part of the private Høng-Tølløse Railway ((HTJ) Høng-Tølløse Jernbane), but since January 2009 it is operated by the Regionstog A/S.  Vedde was a junction between this and the subsequent action Sorø Vedde Railway (1903–50).
Vedde is known for the eponymous 'Vedde ash' deposits that were ejected from the Katla volcano in Iceland approximately 10,000 years ago, and spread across Scandinavia and Scotland.
