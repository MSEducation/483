[[Gu Hailiang]]

CATEGORIES: 1951 births, Living people, Presidents of universities and colleges in China, Wuhan University

Gu Hailiang (顾海良) was a president of Wuhan University, the People's Republic of China.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.moe.edu.cn/publicfiles/business/htmlfiles/moe/s179/201012/113076.html | title=顾海良同志简历 | accessdate=July 24, 2012[/tpl]
Gu was born in January 1951 in Shanghai. In 1970's, Gu was working and studying in He County, Anhui Province. From February 1978 to February 1982, Gu studied economics at Anhui University. He then enrolled in the graduate school of Chinese Academy of Social Sciences and obtained a master in economics in December 1984. From 1984 to 1996, he was teaching in Marxism–Leninism Institute at Renmin University, and became its director. Gu was promoted to associate professor in June 1991, and a full professor in 1994, and became doctorate supervisor in 1995. Since 1996, he served in the State Department and the Department of Education. In December 2001, Gu became the vice secretary of CPC committee at Wuhan University. From August 2002 to November 2008, Gu was the CPC chief at Wuhan University, with the rank of deputy minister. From 2008 to 2010, he has served as the president of Wuhan University. He was succeeded by Li Xiaohong.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.moe.edu.cn/publicfiles/business/htmlfiles/moe/moe_1485/201012/113072.html | title=李晓红任武汉大学校长 | accessdate=July 24, 2012[/tpl]
