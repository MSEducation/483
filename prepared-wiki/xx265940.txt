[[City Cross Arcade]]

CATEGORIES: Shopping centres in Adelaide

City Cross Arcade is a two-level shopping complex located on 32 Grenfell Street Adelaide South Australia with entrances to Rundle Mall, Grenfell Street, James Place and Gawler Place. The centre is owned by the Makris Group,[tpl]cite news | last = Keane | first = Anthony | date = 27 September 2007 | title = Young rich list grows as developers build fortunes | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 4 [/tpl] who purchased the site in 2001 after it was placed up for sale by the previous owners, Hawaiian Investments, in 2000.[tpl]cite news | last = Sulda | first = Marie | date = 5 April 2000 | title = Bid battle looming for rich City Cross | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 43 [/tpl][tpl]cite news | last = Gavin | first = Andrew | date = 31 May 2001 | title = $100m future for City Cross | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 13 [/tpl] The main two anchors are Harvey Norman and Amart All Sports. The complex also features smaller retailers and a large food court including KFC, Hungry Jacks, Subway, Oporto, Sumo Salad, Charlesworth Nuts, Bean Bar and many other food outlets.[tpl]cite web | title = City Cross Store Directory | work = City Cross | url = http://www.citycross.com.au/store.htm | accessdate = 19 May 2012 [/tpl] Amongst the smaller stores are Swiss Glory chocolates and, at one time, Quality 5 Crafts.[tpl]cite news | last = Anderson | first = Susan Heller | date = 26 July 2007 | title = The Two Faces Of Adelaide | work = The New York Times | page = 22 [/tpl] Australia Post Adelaide Rundle Mall Post Shop is also located on the first floor. [tpl]cite web | title = Adelaide Rundle Mall Post Shop location | work = Australia Post | url = http://auspost.com.au/pol/app/showpop/507549 | accessdate = 1 April 2013 [/tpl]
The site was redeveloped at a cost of $6m in 1984, almost doubling the number of shops in the center by expanding on to the former sites of two local buildings â€“ the Mutual Life Chambers and the Widow's Fund building.[tpl]cite news | last = Co Lyer | first = Valerie | date = 13 June 1984 | title = Big Developments Reshape Adelaide | work = The Age | location = Melbourne, Australia | page = 32 [/tpl] Among the suggestions for the site prior to the completion of the 1984 redevelopment was the construction of a Grenfell Street underpass, although these plans never eventuated.[tpl]cite news | date = 20 May 2007 | title = On This Day | work = The Sunday Mail | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 82 [/tpl] Another, smaller, redevelopment occurred in 1996 when the centre was under the management of Hawaiian Investments.
In April, 2003 a $100m redevelopment of the site was announced, to be completed in three stages.[tpl]cite news | last = Chapman | first = Jemma | date = 30 April 2003 | title = $100m plan - with an atrium | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 23 [/tpl] The first stage of the redevelopment, completed at a cost of $25m, was opened by Premier Mike Rann on 22 November 2005, and effectively doubled the retail area of the centre.[tpl]cite news | date = 22 November 2005 | title = City Cross project | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 4 [/tpl] Shortly before the completion of the first stage, Amart All Sports announced that they would be moving into the new premises, joining Harvey Norman who had previously announced their tenancy in the expanded site.[tpl]cite news | date = 25 July 2005 | title = New sports store for city | work = The Advertiser | location = Adelaide, Australia | page = 11 [/tpl] The second stage was to include a 450 car parking station above the site, with a planned third stage to incorporate a 10-storey office tower.
