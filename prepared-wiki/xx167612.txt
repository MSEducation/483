==Life and career==

Born Marybelle Luraine Ellison,Users.fortress.com in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Ellison originally sang gospel music, working in the groups the Ellison Singers and the Golden Chords in the early 1960s. She switched to the R&B genre in 1964. Her first chart entry was "I Dig You Baby" in 1965[tpl]cite web|last=Hogan |first=Ed |url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p4182|pure_url=yes[/tpl] |title=Lorraine Ellison at Allmusic |publisher=Allmusic.com |date=1985-08-17 |accessdate=2010-05-08[/tpl] on Mercury Records, which reached #22 on the U.S. Billboard R&B chart chart. 
She signed with Warner Bros. Records, and in 1966 recorded "Stay with Me" at a last minute booking, following a studio cancellation by Frank Sinatra.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.songfacts.com/detail.php?id=7536 |title=Songfacts.com |publisher=Songfacts.com |date= |accessdate=2010-05-08[/tpl] "Stay with Me" reached number 11 in the U.S. Billboard R&B chart and number 64 in the U.S. Billboard Hot 100 chart. The song was produced and  written by Jerry Ragovoy. Later releases were on the subsidiary soul music record label, Loma.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lomarecords.com/index.htm |title=Loma Records discography |publisher=Lomarecords.com |date= |accessdate=2010-05-08[/tpl] Her follow-up single was "Heart Be Still" a minor hit in 1967.[tpl]cite web|url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p4182|pure_url=yes[/tpl] |title=Allmusic.com |publisher=Allmusic.com |date=1985-08-17 |accessdate=2010-05-08[/tpl]  Ellison also recorded "Try (Just a Little Bit Harder)", a song later covered to more success by Janis Joplin.
Ellison composed many of her own songs (by herself and with manager Sam Bell) and had her own compositions recorded by several other artists, including Jerry Butler, Garnet Mimms, Howard Tate and Dee Dee Warwick.
Twice-married and using the surname Gonzalez-Keys, Lorraine Ellison gave up the music business in order to take care of her mother, before her death in January 1983 from ovarian cancer at the age of 51.[tpl]cite web|last=Moon |first=Tom |url=http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=6149527&ft=1&f=1039 |title=Npr.org/templates |publisher=Npr.org |date= |accessdate=2010-05-08[/tpl]
