[[James O. Born]]

CATEGORIES: Crime fiction writers, Living people, Barry Award winners

James O. Born is a Barry Award-winning crime fiction writer. He used the pen name James O'Neal for his science fiction novels The Human DisguiseOline Cogdill Mystery fiction review: The Human Disguise by James O'Neal May 24, 2009 Baltimore Sun and The Double Human.Oline Cogdill Mystery review: The Double Human by James O'Neal June 6, 2010 South Florida Sun-Sentinel
His earlier novels Walking Money, Shock Wave, and Escape Clause, featured Florida Department of Law Enforcement agent Bill Tasker.[tpl]cite web|title=Escape Clause (Review)|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-399-15334-1|work=Publishers Weekly|accessdate=22 October 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Field of Fire (Review)|url=http://www.bookreporter.com/reviews/field-of-fire|work=Book Reporter|accessdate=22 October 2013[/tpl]   Later books centered on clean-living ATF agent Alex Duarte, first introduced in Field of Fire (2007)[tpl]cite web|title=Field of Fire (Review)|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-399-15398-3|work=Publishers Weekly|accessdate=22 October 2013[/tpl] and later reappearing in Burn Zone.[tpl]cite web|title=Burn Zone (Review)|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-399-15454-6|work=Publishers Weekly|accessdate=22 October 2013[/tpl]
With Lou Dobbs he has written the book Border War, to be published by Forge Books in May 2014.
He won the 2009 Barry Award for best short story for "The Drought" and was shortlisted for the 2005 prize for best first novel for Walking Money.[tpl]cite web|title=Barry Awards|url=http://www.deadlypleasures.com/barry.html|work=Deadly Pleasures|accessdate=23 October 2013[/tpl]
