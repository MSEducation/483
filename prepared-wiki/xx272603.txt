==Deployment, commercial networks and devices==

[tpl]As of|2014|January[/tpl], there were 28 commercial TD-LTE networks operating in 21 countries worldwide, with an additional 45 networks planned or in deployment.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gsacom.com/downloads/pdf/gsa_status_of_the_global_lte_tdd_market_300114.php4 |title=Status of the Global LTE TDD Market |date=30 January 2014 |work=gsacom.com |publisher=Global mobile Suppliers Association |accessdate=13 February 2014[/tpl] TD-LTE deployments are especially popular in Asia and the Pacific, with 47 percent of TD-LTE contracts awarded to vendors from this region. In second place is the Middle East, with 18 percent of contracts.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.telecomlead.com/4g-lte/9-telecoms-asia-pacific-upgrade-lte-networks-lte-advanced-abi-research-73567/ |title=9 telecoms in Asia Pacific to upgrade LTE networks to LTE-Advanced: ABI Research |date=4 November 2013 |work=Telecom Lead |accessdate=10 December 2013[/tpl] According to a report conducted by research firm Ovum Ltd. in June of 2012, TD-LTE connections are expected to make up 25 percent of all LTE connections worldwide by 2016, with operators in Japan, Australia, Saudi Arabia, Sweden, and Denmark having implemented the technology, or planning to in the future.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fiercewireless.com/story/report-td-lte-power-25-lte-connections-2016/2012-06-22 |title=Report: TD-LTE to power 25% of LTE connections by 2016 |author=Phil Goldstein |date=22 June 2012 |work=FierceWireless |accessdate=10 December 2013[/tpl] 
In 2013, a total of 705 new LTE devices were launched worldwide, of which 304 supported TD-LTE, up from 180 devices in 2012.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.lightreading.in/lightreadingindia/news-analysis/287280/td-lte-device-growth-positive-india-4g |title=TD-LTE Device Growth A Positive For India’s 4G |author=Jatinder Singh |date=28 January 2014 |work=Light Reading |accessdate=11 February 2014[/tpl] By the end of the year, LG, Samsung, and Nokia had all demonstrated devices which support TD-LTE, or indicated that they plan to launch devices that support the technology in the future.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mobileworldlive.com/lg-nokia-and-samsung-detail-td-lte-plans |title=LG, Nokia and Samsung detail TD-LTE plans |author=Steve Costello |date=5 March 2013 |work=Mobile World Live |accessdate=10 December 2013[/tpl] Apple also introduced iPhone 5S and 5C models that support both TD-LTE and LTE FDD.[tpl]cite web |url=http://gigaom.com/2013/09/10/apple-shows-the-lte-love-going-for-near-global-4g-support-in-the-iphone-5s-and-5c/ |title=Apple shows the LTE love, going for near global 4G support in the iPhone 5s and 5c |author=Kevin Fitchard |date=10 September 2013 |work=GigaOM |accessdate=10 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fiercewireless.com/tech/story/apples-new-iphones-support-17-lte-bands-including-td-lte/2013-09-10 |title=Apple's new iPhones support 17 LTE bands, including TD-LTE |author=Tammy Parker |date=10 September 2013 |work=FierceWireless |accessdate=18 March 2014[/tpl]
