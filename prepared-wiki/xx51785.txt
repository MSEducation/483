Matthew Ryan Phillippe ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|f|ɪ|l|ɨ|p|iː[/tpl]; born September 10, 1974), better known as  Ryan Phillippe, is an American actor and director. After appearing on the soap opera One Life to Live, he came to fame in the late 1990s starring in a string of films, including I Know What You Did Last Summer, Cruel Intentions, and 54. In the 2000s, he appeared in several films, including 2001's Academy Award Best Picture nominee Gosford Park, 2005's Academy Award-winning ensemble film Crash, and the 2006 war drama Flags of Our Fathers. In 2007 he starred in Breach, a movie based on the true story of FBI operative Eric O'Neill, while in 2008 he headlined Kimberly Peirce's Iraq war film Stop-Loss. In 2010, he starred as Pulitzer-Prize winning photographer Greg Marinovich in The Bang-Bang Club.
He was married to actress Reese Witherspoon from 1999 to 2007; together, they have a daughter and a son. He also has a daughter from a relationship with actress Alexis Knapp.

==Career==

===Early career (1992–1999)===

Phillippe's acting career began with an appearance in ABC daytime drama One Life to Live. His character Billy Douglas, whom he played from 1992 to 1993, was the first gay teenager on a daytime soap opera.[tpl]cite web|last=Harris|first=Mark|title='One Life' Breaks One Taboo|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,310941,00.html|work=Entertainment Weekly|publisher=ew.com|accessdate=December 14, 2012|date=June 26, 1992[/tpl] After leaving the show, Phillippe moved to Los Angeles, where he appeared in a number of small parts in various television series including Matlock, Due South, the TV mini-series The Secrets of Lake Success, and movies, including the 1995 film Crimson Tide and the 1996 film White Squall.
He was cast in the 1997 horror film, I Know What You Did Last Summer, which co-starred Sarah Michelle Gellar, Freddie Prinze, Jr. and Jennifer Love Hewitt. The film was a success, and led to Phillippe gaining wider renown and being cast in a few more high-profile films, including 54 in 1998 opposite Neve Campbell, Salma Hayek and Mike Myers. In 1999 he starred in Cruel Intentions, a modern retelling of the Choderlos de Laclos novel, Les Liaisons Dangereuses, which also starred Phillippe's future wife, Reese Witherspoon, as well as his I Know What You Did Last Summer co-star, Sarah Michelle Gellar. It was a success among its intended teenage audience, cementing Phillippe's ability to play characters that require sex appeal. Phillippe can also be seen in the Marcy Playground music video "Comin' Up From Behind", which also appears on the film's soundtrack.

===Critical success and later work (2000–2009)===

In the years following, he appeared in the crime drama The Way of the Gun, starred as a famed software engineer in the thriller Antitrust, and co-starred in Robert Altman's Gosford Park, which was nominated for the Oscar for Best Picture. Subsequently, Phillippe had supporting parts in the films Igby Goes Down (2002) and Crash (2005), which won the Oscar for Best Picture. His 2003 film, The I Inside, premiered on cable.
In 2006, Phillippe played real-life Navy corpsman John Bradley in the war film Flags of Our Fathers, directed by Clint Eastwood and following the journey of the United States Marines who lifted the flag at the battle of Iwo Jima. Phillippe has said that the film was the "best experience" of his career because of its "personal meaning" to him,[tpl]cite web|title=VOA.com|work=Movie Focuses on Story Behind Famous WWII Photo|url=http://voanews.com/english/2006-10-21-voa27.cfm|accessdate=October 22, 2006[/tpl] and that he would have "given his life" to fight in World War II, noting that both of his grandfathers fought in the war.[tpl]cite web|title=Cleveland.com|work=Star of Eastwood film salutes heroes of World War II|url=http://www.cleveland.com/entertainment/plaindealer/index.ssf?/base/entertainment/1160814993295091.xml&coll=2|accessdate=October 15, 2006[/tpl] His performance was positively received by film critic Richard Roeper, who thought it was Phillippe's best performance to date.[tpl]cite web|title=Ebert & Roeper|work=Reviews for the Weekend of October 7–8, 2006|url=http://tvplex.go.com/buenavista/ebertandroeper/061009.html|accessdate=October 15, 2006[/tpl] Phillippe's next role was in the thriller Breach, in which he played FBI investigator Eric O'Neill opposite Chris Cooper. He has since commented that he believes Cooper to be "the best actor America has to offer".video interview (August 2007) He then starred in Chaos, in which he plays a police officer, Five Fingers, a drama set in Morocco, Kimberly Peirce's Iraq war film Stop-Loss, and the futuristic Franklyn.

===Newfound success and return to television (2010–2011)===

Next up for Phillippe was a rare comedic role as Lt. Dixon Piper in the unsuccessful film MacGruber, based on the SNL skit of the same name. It was released in the US and Canada on May 21, 2010.[tpl]cite web|title=First Official Images from 'MacGruber'|work=News in Film|url=http://www.newsinfilm.com/2010/01/08/first-official-images-from-macgruber|accessdate=January 11, 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=MacGruber: The Movie Really Happening!|work=Cinema Blend|url=http://www.cinemablend.com/new/MacGruber-The-Movie-Really-Happening-13375.html|accessdate=May 4, 2010[/tpl] As part of the film's promotion, Phillippe made his Saturday Night Live hosting debut on April 17, 2010, along with first-time musical performer Kesha.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tvguide.com/News/Saturday-Night-Live-1016861.aspx|title=Saturday Night Live Calls on First-Time Hosts Gabourey Sidibe and Ryan Phillippe|publisher=TVGuide.com[/tpl] Two days later, on April 19, 2010, Phillippe co-hosted WWE Raw with Jonathan Swift and Charo, also in support of MacGruber.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.wwe.com/shows/raw/special/allspecialguesthosts/upcomingguesthosts|title=Upcoming Raw Guest Hosts|publisher=World Wrestling Entertainment|accessdate=March 10, 2010[/tpl]
That same year, Phillippe starred in the Canadian/South African production The Bang-Bang Club, which tells the real-life story of the Bang-Bang Club, four South African photographers whose images documented the bloody end of apartheid.[tpl]cite web|title=The Bang Bang Club Starring Ryan Phillippe Begins Filming|work=A 'n' E Vibe|url=http://www.anevibe.com/entertainment-news/big-bang-club-starring-ryan-phillipe-announces-filming.html|accessdate=April 8, 2009[/tpl] Phillippe stars as Pulitzer Prize-winning photographer Greg Marinovich. Filming for the movie took place in South Africa, beginning in March 2009. Phillippe described the filming process as "really run-and-gun. There were no comforts, and I actually appreciate that". He noted that the experience has impacted him, explaining that "we were shooting during the day in Soweto, and it was an endless procession of funerals—death is so common. Kids there have no quality of life. It has made me want to get more involved in Africa-based charities." An early cut of the film was shown at Cannes Film Festival in May 2009,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rottentomatoes.com/m/cannes_film_festival_2009/news/1821162/cannes_2009_gambit_and_silk_spectre_go_bang_bang|title=Cannes 2009: Gambit and Silk Spectre Go Bang Bang|publisher=Rotten Tomatoes|accessdate=May 22, 2010[/tpl] with the final version shown at Cannes in May 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.filmcontact.com/south-africa/bang-bang-club-makes-its-debut-cannes|title=The Bang Bang Club makes its debut at Cannes|publisher=Film Contact|accessdate=May 22, 2010[/tpl] The film officially premiered in September 2010 at the 2010 Toronto International Film Festival. The US rights to the movie were picked up by Tribeca Film,[tpl]cite news|first=Gordon|last=Cox|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118027634|title=Tribeca goes 'Bang'|accessdate=January 2, 2011|date=November 17, 2010|work=Variety[/tpl] which premiered the movie at the 2011 Tribeca Film Festival and opened the movie in limited release on April 22, 2011.[tpl]cite news|first=John|last=Jurgenson|url=http://online.wsj.com/article/SB10001424052748703551304576261322751607398.html|title=A Virtual Night at the Movies |accessdate=April 22, 2011|date=April 15, 2011|work=Wall Street Journal[/tpl]

===Focus on directing and producing (2012–present)===

In 2012, Phillippe focused on his directorial debut, Shreveport, an upcoming indie thriller in which Phillippe will star as a fading film actor who must devise a creative escape after he is kidnapped and tortured while making a movie in Shreveport, Louisiana. In addition to directing, co-producing, and starring in the film, Phillippe also co-wrote the script with Joe Gossett.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118055769|title=Phillippe making directorial debut with 'Shreveport'|first=Jeff|last=Sneider|work=Variety|date=June 20, 2012|accessdate=August 9, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.screendaily.com/news/distribution/ryan-phillippe-to-make-directorial-debut-on-chained/5045921.article?referrer=RSS|title=Ryan Phillippe to make directorial debut on Chained|first=Jeremy|last=Kay|work=ScreenDaily|date=August 31, 2012|accessdate=September 12, 2012[/tpl] Phillippe has said that the film is based partly on the life experiences of himself and his friends (including the filming of Straight A's in Shreveport in 2011), but is partly meant to be "a fun scary movie in the vein of Misery". Filming took place on location in Shreveport in the fall of 2012, and the movie is slated for a 2013 release.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.etonline.com/movies/127489_Shreveport_Set_Visit_Ryan_Phillippe_Talks_Directorial_Debut_His_Children_Acting/index.html|title=Would Ryan Phillippe Want His Children To Act?|first=Robert|last=Pace|work=Entertainment Tonight|date=November 29, 2012|accessdate=December 5, 2012[/tpl]
In October 2013, Phillippe began filming the thriller Reclaim in Puerto Rico. The movie will feature Phillippe as an American man who travels to Puerto Rico with his wife (played by Rachelle Lefevre) to adopt an orphan from Haiti. After a confrontation with a local (played by John Cusack), the child vanishes.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/afm-john-cusacks-reclaim-inks-654235|title=AFM: John Cusack's 'Reclaim' Inks Multiple International Presales|work = Hollywood Reporter|date=November 7, 2013|accessdate=December 21, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/10/afm-thriller-reclaim-to-star-john-cusack-ryan-phillippe-and-rachelle-lefevre/|title=AFM: Thriller ‘Reclaim’ To Star John Cusack, Ryan Phillippe And Rachelle Lefevre|work = Deadline|date=October 30, 2013|accessdate=December 21, 2013[/tpl]
