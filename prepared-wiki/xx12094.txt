</ref>  The [[Franciscan Minister General]], [[Michael of Cesena]], had been summoned to Avignon, to answer charges of heresy.  A theological commission had been asked to review his ''Commentary on the Sentences'', and it was during this that Ockham found himself involved in a different debate. Michael of Cesena had asked Ockham to review arguments surrounding [[Apostolic poverty]]. The Franciscans believed that [[Jesus Christ|Jesus]] and his [[apostles]] owned no property either individually or in common, and The Rule of Saint Francis commanded members of the order to follow this practice.<ref name="mcgrade">
