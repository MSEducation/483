[[Christchurch mayoral election, 2007]]

CATEGORIES: Mayoral elections in New Zealand, 2007 elections in New Zealand, Politics of Christchurch

The Christchurch mayoral election, 2007 was part of the New Zealand local elections, 2007. On 13 October of that year, elections were held for the Mayor of Christchurch plus other local government roles. Incumbent Garry Moore retired in 2007 after nine years in the office. Bob Parker, previously mayor of Banks Peninsula, beat the Christchurch 2021 representative, Megan Woods, with a majority of 14,212 votes (13.73%).[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.nzherald.co.nz/politics/news/article.cfm?c_id=280&objectid=10469695 |title=New faces aplenty in local government shake-ups |author=Edward Gay and James Ihaka |date=13 October 2007 |newspaper=The New Zealand Herald |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] Media personality Jo Giles, who had previously contested the [tpl]NZ electorate link|Ilam[/tpl] electorate for the ACT Party for Parliament, came a distant third.[tpl]cite web |title=Official Count Results -- Ilam |url= http://2005.electionresults.govt.nz/electionresults_2005/electorate-19.html |publisher=Chief Electoral Office, Wellington |accessdate=20 November 2011 |date=1 October 2005[/tpl] A further seven candidate contested the election.
