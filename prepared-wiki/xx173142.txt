Mark Lynch ([tpl]lang-ga|Marcas Ó Loingsigh[/tpl]; born 20 February 1986[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.derrygaa.ie/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=666&Itemid=184 |title=Derry player profiles 2008 |accessdate=11 January 2009 |work=Official Derry GAA website |publisher= |date=Early 2008 [/tpl]) is an Irish Gaelic footballer who plays for Derry, with whom he has won a National League title. He plays his club football and hurling for St. Mary's Banagher.

==Football career==

===Inter-county===

Lynch was called up to the Derry Minor team in 2002 and made his debut against Antrim[tpl]cite journal |last= |first= |date=December 2003 |title=Interview with Mark Lynch |journal=Hogan Stand |pages= |url=http://www.hoganstand.com/Derry/ArticleForm.aspx?ID=6411 |accessdate=11 January 2008 [/tpl] while only 16. Lynch has been playing for the Senior team since 2004. He was instrumental in the 2008 National League, which Derry won, defeating Kerry in the final.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rte.ie/sport/gaa/2008/0427/derrykerry.html |title=Derry 2-13 Kerry 2-09 2008 National League Final report |accessdate=27 April 2008 |work=RTÉ [/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.derrygaa.ie/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=630&Itemid=56 |title=Derry 2-13 Kerry 2-09 2008 National League Final report - Derry GAA website |accessdate=27 April 2008 |format= |work=Derry GAA website [/tpl] Lynch underwent a shoulder operation in November 2008 and isn't expected to return to action until March 2009.[tpl]cite news |first=Paddy |last=Heaney |title=Bradley deserves a break from the game: Cassidy |work=The Irish News |page=60 |date=16 October 2008 |accessdate=11 November 2008|quote= |url=http://www.irishnews.com/appnews/597/5776/2008/10/16/600339_360406913922Bradleyde.html [/tpl]

===Club===

Lynch had a very successful underage football career with Banagher.

===School/college===

Lynch was part of St. Pat's Maghera's 2003 MacRory Cup and Hogan Cup winning team. He won one/two? Ulster Colleges Football All-Star with the school. In 2007 he was part of the Jordanstown side that finished runners-up to Queens University Belfast in the Sigerson Cup final.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/northern_ireland/gaelic_games/6416087.stm |title=Queen's Uni. 0-15 0-14 UUJ - Match report |accessdate=23 September 2008 |work=BBC Sport Online |publisher= |date=3 March 2007 [/tpl] Jordanstown went one step further in 2008 and won the competition, with Lynch scoring 0-06 in the final against the Garda College.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/northern_ireland/7361815.stm |title= UUJ 1-16 1-14 Garda College Match report |accessdate=26 April 2008 |format= |work=BBC Sport Online | date=22 April 2008[/tpl] He was vice-captain and stood in as captain in the early stages of the competition when regular captain Peter Donnelly was injured with a broken arm.

===International Rules===

Lynch was captain of the Ireland international rules football team that won the Under 17 International Rules Series against Australia in 2003.

==Hurling career==

===Inter-county===

Lynch represented Derry at hurling at underage level. He was part of the Derry Minor team that lost out to Antrim in the 2003 Ulster Minor Hurling Championship final.[tpl]cite news |url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/northern_ireland/gaelic_games/2990922.stm|title= Antrim outclass Derry|work=BBC Sport Online |accessdate=1 April 2008 | date=14 June 2003[/tpl]

===Club===

===School/college===

Lynch won the Mageenan Cup with St. Pat's Maghera. He was also awarded one/two? Ulster Colleges Hurling All-Star.
