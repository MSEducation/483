[[Baron Rotherham]]

CATEGORIES: Baronies in the Peerage of the United Kingdom, Extinct baronies in the Peerage of the United Kingdom

Baron Rotherham, of Broughton in the County Palatine of Lancaster, was a title in the Peerage of the United Kingdom.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=28398 |date=22 July 1910 |startpage=5269[/tpl] It was created on 18 July 1910 for the industrialist and Liberal politician Sir William Holland, 1st Baronet. He had already been created a baronet, of Queen's Gate,in the Royal Borough of Kensington in the Baronetage of the United Kingdom on 18 July 1907.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=28040 |date=16 July 1907 |startpage=4858[/tpl] Both titles became extinct on the death of his son, the second Baron, on 24 January 1950.
