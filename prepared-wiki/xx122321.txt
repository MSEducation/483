[[Kathryn Humphreys]]

Kathryn Humphreys (born 19 September 1970 in Oshawa, Ontario)[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.fullspectrumottawa.com/exposure/exposure_canadianwomen_41.html | title=Kathryn Humphreys profile | publisher=Exposure Magazine/Full Spectrum Network | accessdate=2007-11-08 | date=[/tpl] is a Canadian sports anchor for CityNews at Five, CityNews at Six and CityNews Tonight. She was previously with CityNews Weekend which she joined in March 1997.
She previously worked as an intern at 680 News in the sports department in Toronto and WBZ-TV in Boston. She returned to the Muskoka area to work at The Moose 100.9 FM in Bracebridge reporting news, sports and weather.
As the daughter of the Oshawa Generals owner, she has plenty of exposure to sports. She has played basketball, volleyball and football while sitting on Victoria College's Athletic Executive Board during university. She was also the one-time girlfriend of former NHL goalie, Craig Billington. She is married to Johnny Fay of The Tragically Hip.[tpl]cite news | url=http://jam.canoe.ca/Television/2007/11/08/4639844.html | title=City TV sportcaster leaving | first=Bill | last=Harris | publisher=Jam!/Sun Media | accessdate=2007-11-08 | date=8 November 2007 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071110171712/http://jam.canoe.ca/Television/2007/11/08/4639844.html  |archivedate = 2007-11-10[/tpl]
She earned an English degree, honours degree, and a minor in history from the University of Toronto.
The broadcaster confirmed her departure from Citytv in November 2007 after failing to resolve a new contract with station management. However, the contract dispute was resolved, and on 10 December 2007 she re-assumed her position as a CityNews sportscaster. She will also be appearing on Rogers Sportsnet and The Fan 590.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.thestar.com/Sports/article/285483 | publisher=Toronto Star | accessdate=2008-02-06 | date=14 December 2007 | title=`Home-girl' Humphreys back with a bang | first=Chris | last=Zelkovich[/tpl]
On 25 August 2009, Humphreys was nominated for a Gemini Award in the category of "Best Host or Interviewer in a Sports Program or Broadcast".[tpl]cite news | url=http://network.nationalpost.com/np/blogs/postedsports/archive/2009/08/25/number-of-sports-gemini-nominees.aspx | title=Gemini Awards recognize excellence in sports broadcasting | date=25 August 2009 | first=Sarah | last=Millar | publisher=National Post | accessdate=2010-01-22 [/tpl]
