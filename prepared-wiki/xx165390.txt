[[78th Division (People's Republic of China)]]

CATEGORIES: Infantry divisions of the People's Volunteer Army, Infantry divisions of the People's Liberation Army

The 78th Division was a military formation of the People's Volunteer Army (aka Chinese People's Volunteers (CPV) or Chinese Communist Forces (CCF)) during the Korean War. They were a component of the 26th Army, consisting of the 232nd, 233rd, and the 234th Regiments.[tpl]cite book| chapterurl = http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/ebb/ch3.htm | chapter = Chapter 3, The Enemy| last = Mossman| first = Billy C.| title = Ebb and Flow November 1950-July 1951| page = 55| url =http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/ebb/fm.htm page = 55[/tpl]
The unit was disbanded in 1985.PLA Order of Battle, Disbanded Divisions
