[[Miha Valič]]

CATEGORIES: 1978 births, 2008 deaths, Slovenian mountain climbers, Mountaineering deaths, Sport deaths in Nepal, Sportspeople from Ljubljana

Miha Valič (4 November 1978 – 5 October 2008) was a Slovenian mountaineer. He was the first person to ascend every 4000-meter peak in the Alps in one winter session.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.alpinist.com/doc/ALP19/newswire-valic-alps-4000-meter |title=4000m Winter Alps marathon completed in 102 days |publisher=Alpinist.com |date=2007-03-16 |accessdate=2008-06-10[/tpl] The Alps marathon was completed in 102 days.
Valič attended 10 expeditions, mostly in Yosemite, Patagonia and the Himalayas. In 2003 Valič spend two months in the Yosemite Valley with Matjaž Jeran, where they free climbed the El Capitan rock formation's Freerider route (1200 m, 5.12d/13a). A year later he made the first alpine-style ascent on route Eternal Flame in Trango group with Tomaž Jakofčič and Klemen Mali.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.alpinist.com/doc/ALP17/newswire-trango |title=Slovenian bonaza in the Trango group |publisher=Alpinist.com |date=2006-09-28 |accessdate=2008-06-10[/tpl] Valič was also a successful sport climber. He climbed routes up to 8b+.
On 5 October 2008, Valič died while descending Cho Oyu after a successful ascent.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.sta.si/en/vest.php?id=1326190 |title=Slovenian Climber Killed in Himalayas |publisher=Slovenian Press Agency |date=2006-10-06 |accessdate=2008-06-10[/tpl]
