[[Wei Yi]]

CATEGORIES: Chinese chess players, 1999 births, Living people, Chess grandmasters

Wei Yi ([tpl]zh|c=韦奕|p=Wéi Yì[/tpl]; 2 June 1999) is a Chinese chess grandmaster and chess prodigy. On 1 March 2013 he won his final GM norm at the Reykjavik Open, becoming, at the age of 13 years, 8 months and 23 days, the youngest grandmaster in the world at that time, and the fourth youngest in history.[tpl]cite web|url=http://en.chessbase.com/Home/TabId/211/PostId/4009007/wei-yi-has-become-the-youngest-gm-in-the-world-010313.aspx |title=Chessbase: Wei Yi has become the youngest GM in the world |publisher=chessbase.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-13[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://ratings.fide.com/apps/227816.pdf |title=Certificate of Title Result (Reykjavik) | publisher=FIDE |date= |accessdate=2013-08-23[/tpl]
Wei represents the Jiangsu Taizhou club in the China Chess League.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chessgames.com/perl/chessplayer?pid=130615 |title=Chessgames: Wei Yi  |publisher=chessgames.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-13[/tpl]

==Principal results==

In 2007, he competed in the Chinese Chess Championship B group at the age of 8, recording a draw against Grandmaster Zhou Jianchao.
In 2010, he won the Under 12s event at the Asian Youth Chess Championship, and followed this up by winning the same division at the World Youth Chess Championship.
In August 2012, he won his first GM norm at the World Junior Chess Championship in Athens, including a victory over Richárd Rapport and a draw with eventual winner Alexander Ipatov.[tpl]cite web|url=http://ratings.fide.com/apps/227818.pdf |title=Certificate of Title Result (Athens) | publisher=FIDE |date= |accessdate=2013-08-23[/tpl] The competition is open to participants under 20 at 1 January, when Wei was only 12.
In October, he took his second GM norm at the Indonesian Open, with victories over Michal Krasenkow and Sergey Fedorchuk.[tpl]cite web|url=http://ratings.fide.com/apps/227817.pdf |title=Certificate of Title Result (Indonesia) | publisher=FIDE |date= |accessdate=2013-08-23[/tpl]
In February 2013, he secured his final norm at the Reykjavik Open with a score of 7½/10, including a victory over Maxime Vachier-Lagrave, finishing 6th.
In August, he made his debut at the Chess World Cup, held that year in Tromsø. He defeated Ian Nepomniachtchi in the first round and Alexei Shirov in the second, but was knocked out by Shakhriyar Mamedyarov in the third round.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chessworldcup2013.com/games/pairings |title= Chess World Cup 2013: Pairings & Results|publisher= |date= |accessdate=2013-08-19 [/tpl]
On the November 2013 FIDE rating list, Wei, aged 14 years, four months and 30 days, reached a rating of 2604, thus becoming the youngest player in history to achieve a rating of 2600+.http://chessbase.com/Home/TabId/211/PostId/4011697/wei-yi--youngest-2600-gm-ever-011113.aspx
