[[Teleorman County]]

CATEGORIES: Teleorman County, Counties of Romania, Place names of Turkish origin in Romania

Teleorman ([tpl]IPA-ro|ˈtele.orman[/tpl]) is a county (județ) of Romania, in the historical region Muntenia, with its capital city at Alexandria.
The name Teleorman is of Cumanic (Turkic) origin. It literally means crazy forest (Deli orman) and, by extension, "thick and shadowy forest" in the Cuman language.[tpl]ro icon[/tpl] Claudiu Dumitrache, "Județul Teleorman, de la 'pădurea nebună' la grânarul țării", Adevărul, January 11, 2013; accessed July 2, 2013 It can be encountered in other toponyms, such as the Turkish name of the Ludogorie Plateau in northeastern Bulgaria.

==Demographics==

In 2011, it had a population of 360,178 and the population density was 62.2/km².

==Tourism==

The county doesn't have many spectacular attractions, but its cultural folk heritage is very rich. Many Romanian personalities have been born here, some of them later describing the life in a village in a very picturesque way. Also, the area was one of the places where the Wallachian Revolution of 1848 unfolded.
