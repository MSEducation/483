[[Pinner reaction]]

CATEGORIES: Addition reactions, Name reactions

The Pinner reaction (after Adolf Pinner) is an organic reaction of a nitrile with an alcohol using an acid catalyst, for instance, hydrochloric acid. The product formed is the hydrochloric acid salt of an imino ester or an alkyl imidate, which sometimes is called a Pinner salt. The reaction is a sequence of nucleophilic additions.[ref]
