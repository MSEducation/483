[[Seawards the Great Ships]]

CATEGORIES: 1961 films, British films, English-language films, Live Action Short Film Academy Award winners, British documentary films, British short films, 1960s documentary films, Scottish films, Documentary films about water transport, Shipbuilding / shipbreaking localities of Scotland, Films set in Glasgow

Seawards the Great Ships is a 1961 British short documentary film directed by Hilary Harris. It won an Academy Award in 1962 for Best Short Live Action Subject,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.oscars.org/awards/academyawards/legacy/ceremony/34th-winners.html |title=The 34th Academy Awards (1962) Nominees and Winners |accessdate=2011-08-22|work=oscars.org[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/300292/Seawards-the-Great-Ships/details |title=New York Times: Seawards the Great Ships|accessdate=2008-05-22|work=NY Times[/tpl] the first Scottish film to win an Oscar. The film chronicles the Shipbuilding industry of the River Clyde during the early 1960s, featuring footage from the Fairfield Shipbuilding and Engineering Company, John Brown & Company and Scotts Shipbuilding and Engineering Company. It was produced by Glasgow-based Templar Films for the Clyde Shipbuilders' Association and the Central Office of Information (COI). It was released onto Blu-ray by Panamint in 2010 as part of their 'Faces of Scotland' compilation.[tpl]cite web |url=http://homecinema.thedigitalfix.co.uk/content/id/73582/faces-of-scotland.html|title=Faces of Scotland|last=Nield|first=Anthony|date=11 Feb 2011|work=The Digital Fix|accessdate=11 February 2011[/tpl]
It includes dialogue between shipyard workers, but this is all scripted. They had hoped to use genuine dialogue between shipyard workers, but this included too many swearwords to be usable.
