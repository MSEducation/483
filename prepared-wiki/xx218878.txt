[[Idol on Parade]]

CATEGORIES: 1959 films, 1950s comedy films, British films, English-language films, Films directed by John Gilling, Films based on novels, Military humor in film

Idol on Parade also known as Idle on Parade is a 1959 youth-oriented British comedy film directed by John Gilling and starring Anthony Newly, Sid James and Lionel Jeffries. It was John Antrobus' first screenplay.[tpl]cite book | last = Antrobus | first = John | title = Surviving Spike Milligan: A Voyage Through the Mind & Mirth of the Master Goon | year = 2002 | publisher = Robson Books | location = London | isbn = 0-246-12275-7 [/tpl] p.44[tpl]cite book | last = McCann | first = Graham | title = Spike & Co. | year = 2006 | publisher = Hodder & Stoughton | location = London | isbn = 0-340-89809-7 [/tpl] The film was based on the 1958 novel Idle on Parade by William Camp that was inspired by Elvis Presley's consription into the US Army. 
The film was produced by Warwick Films and featured Newley singing five songs in a Cockney accent for the film.
One of the songs "I've Waited for So Long" became a pop hit.[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article44798411 |title=New star rises on British hit front. |newspaper=The Australian Women's Weekly (1933 - 1982) |location=1933 - 1982 |date=12 August 1959 |accessdate=19 April 2014 |page=43 Supplement: Teenagers Weekly |publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl]
