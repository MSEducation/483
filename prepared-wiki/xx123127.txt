[[Høle]]

CATEGORIES: Boroughs and neighbourhoods of Sandnes, Villages in Rogaland, Former municipalities of Norway

Høle is a borough and former municipality in Rogaland county, Norway.
The municipality was separated from Strand as Høgsfjord formannskapsdistrikt in 1842. On 1 January 1871 the district Forsand was divided from Høgsfjord to form a municipality of its own. The remaining part of Høgsfjord changed its name to Høle, and had a population of 1,122.[tpl]cite paper | author=Dag Jukvam / Statistics Norway | title=Historisk oversikt over endringer i kommune- og fylkesinndelingen | year=1999 | url=http://www.ssb.no/emner/00/90/rapp_9913/rapp_9913.pdfl[/tpl]
On 1 January 1965 the district Oltesvik with 37 became a part of the new municipality Gjesdal, whereas the rest of Høle, with 926 inhabitants, was incorporated into the neighboring municipality Sandnes.
The village Høle has a population of 335.[tpl]cite paper | author=Statistics Norway | title=Urban settlements. Population and area, by municipality. 1 January 2006 | year=2006 | url=http://www.ssb.no/english/subjects/02/01/10/beftett_en/tab-2006-07-05-01-en.html[/tpl] Presently, as a borough of Sandnes, Høle is the least populous borough with 965 inhabitants.[tpl]cite web|url=https://www.sandnes.kommune.no/Globalmeny/Om-Sandnes/Om-Sandnes/Sandnes-i-tall/|title=Sandnes i tall|publisher=Sandnes Municipality|language=Norwegian|accessdate=3 June 2011[/tpl] It is located in the eastern part of the municipality.
