</ref> Rice, maize and millet are the most commonly-eaten grains.<ref name=foodculture>[tpl]cite encyclopedia|article=Burkina Faso|encyclopedia=Food Cultures of the World|author=Liza Debrevic|pages=23-30|editor=Ken Albala|publisher=ABC-CLIO[/tpl]</ref> Grilled meat is common, particularly [[mutton]], [[goat]], [[beef]] and [[Fish (food)|fish]].<ref name="JM">Marchais, p. 99</ref> Vegetables include, besides yams and potatoes, okra, tomatoes, zucchini, carrots, leeks, onions, beets, pumpkins, cucumbers, cabbage, sorrel and spinach.<ref name=foodculture/> 

==Common dishes==

Restaurants generally serve Burkinabé dishes alongside those of neighbouring countries. Foreign dishes include a fish or meat stew called kédjénou from Côte d'Ivoire and poulet yassa, a chicken stew with lemon and onions from Senegal.
