[[Mak Pak Shee]]

CATEGORIES: Members of the Cabinet of Singapore, Labour Front politicians, Year of birth missing

Mak Pak Shee was a Singaporean politician. 

==Career==

An Indian Cantonese, Mak Pak Shee was the leader of the Singapore-based political party, the Labour Party. He left in August 1950.[tpl]cite news|url=http://newspapers.nl.sg/Digitised/Article/straitstimes19500805.2.69.aspx |title=Mak Pak Shee Quits Labour Party |date=August 5, 1950 |newspaper=The Straits Times |pages=4— [/tpl] In cabinet he held the position of Junior Minister.[tpl]cite book|author=Kim Wah Yeo|title=Political Development in Singapore, 1945-55|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=YsyvwJAsAmsC&pg=PA66|year=1973|publisher=NUS Press|isbn=978-0-8214-0486-7|pages=66–[/tpl] In his book One Man's View of the World (2013), former Prime Minister of Singapore Lee Kuan Yew described Mak as a "fixer — somebody who facilitated the fulfilment of favours for a fee".[tpl]cite book|title=One Man's View of the World |last=Lee |first=Kuan Yew |date=August 6, 2013 |pages=300—301 |location=Singapore |isbn=9789814342568 |publisher=Straits Times Press [/tpl]

==Lawsuits==

In July 1948, Mak was meted a fine of $250 for inappropriately including the honorifics "MB, BJS" in his name.[tpl]cite news|url=http://newspapers.nl.sg/Digitised/Article.aspx?articleid=straitstimes19480722-1.2.72.3 |title=False Title |date=July 22, 1948 |pages=7— |newspaper=The Straits Times [/tpl]

==Personal life==

Mak Pak Shee had at least three sons. His third son, Kok Hoe, died aged eight in February 1957 and was buried at a Muslim graveyard.[tpl]cite news|url=http://newspapers.nl.sg/Digitised/Article.aspx?articleid=straitstimes19570227-1.2.61 |newspaper=The Straits Times |title=Minister's son dies |pages=4— |date=February 27, 1957 [/tpl]
