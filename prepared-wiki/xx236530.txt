Debra Medina (born June 1, 1962) is a Republican politician in the U.S. state of Texas. She is allied with the Ron Paul and Tea Party movements. 
Medina ran unsuccessfully for the Republican nomination for Texas Comptroller of Public Accounts in the primary election held on March 4, 2014. The position is being vacated by the two-term incumbent, Susan Combs. 
In 2010, Medina ran in the Texas gubernatorial election.Texas gubernatorial election site . Retrieved 2010 January 21. After subsequently tripling her support in public opinion polls, she was invited to participate alongside her two opponents in a second debate held on January 29.

==Early life and business==

Medina was born in Beeville in Bee County in south Texas. She graduated as a registered nurse from Baptist Memorial Hospital System School of Nursing (now Baptist Health System) in San Antonio in 1984. She earned her business degree in 1995 from LeTourneau University in Longview, Texas. In 2002 she founded and managed Prudentia Inc., a medical billing company based in Wharton in southeastern Texas.Medina campaign site. Retrieved 2010 January 21. She was reared Roman Catholic but now attends a Baptist church.http://www.dallasnews.com/news/politics/state-politics/20100219-50-things-you-need-to-know-42.ece

==Political involvement==

Medina got involved in politics in the 1990s and became Wharton County GOP chairwoman in 2004. She was a high-level volunteer for Ron Paul’s 2008 presidential campaign, and served as Interim State Coordinator for the Campaign for Liberty. At the 2008 Republican Party of Texas state convention in Houston, she lost her bid for state GOP vice chairwoman. In 2008, she decided to run for governor.Debra Medina personal site. ==Controversy==

In an interview on the Glenn Beck Program on February 11, 2010, host Glenn Beck asked Medina if she thought the US government might have had a role in the 9/11 terrorist attacks. Medina said that "some very good questions have been raised in that regard ... the American people have not seen all of the evidence there, so I have not taken a position on that."[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.glennbeck.com/content/articles/article/196/36197/ |title=Interview shocker: Debra Medina||date=2010-02-11|accessdate=2010-03-01[/tpl] Soon after the interview her campaign released a press statement disavowing any 9/11 conspiracy beliefs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.medinafortexas.com/getPost?p=272|title=Medina Campaign Responds to Glenn Beck Interview|accessdate=2010-02-20[/tpl]

==Comptroller's race==

Medina finished third in the 2014 comptroller's race with 235,713 votes (19.3 percent). The leading votegetter, State Senator Glenn Hegar of Katy, polled 610,512 (49.99 percent). State Representative Harvey Hilderbran of Kerrville finished second with 317,731 votes (26 percent). Former State Representative Raul Torres of Corpus Christi ran fourth with 57,255 votes (4.7 percent).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.statesman.com/news/news/harvey-hilderbran-withdrawal-from-comptroller-runo/nd74y/|title=Republican primary election returns|publisher=team1.sos.state.tx.us|accessdate=March 6, 2014[/tpl] On March 7, 2014, Hilderbran announced that he was ending his campaign and endorsed Hegar as the Republican nominee in the fall.[tpl]cite web|url=https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Glenn_Hegar&action=edit&editintro=Template:BLP_editintro|title=Kate Alexander, "Glenn Hegar wins GOP comptroller primary after Harvey Hilderbran bows out of runoff"|publisher=Austin American-Statesman|accessdate=March 7, 2014[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Medina has three siblings. Her father worked at the telephone company, and her mother was a stay-at-home mom. Debra met Noe Medina the year that she graduated from high school. They married two years later in 1982. She and her husband have two grown children, whom they home-schooled — Jacob Medina, 20, a student at Texas A&M, and Janise Cookston, 25, an interior designer in Houston.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dallasnews.com/sharedcontent/dws/news/politics/state/stories/022110dnpol50thingsmedina.132080a0a.html|title=50 things you need to know about Debra Medina|publisher=The Dallas Morning News|date=2010-02-20|accessdate=2010-02-20[/tpl]
