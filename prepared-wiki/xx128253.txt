In 1885 Donisthorpe was co-founder of the British Chess Association and the British Chess Club.
Donisthorpe filed for a patent in 1876, for a film camera, which he named a "kinesigraph." The object of the invention was to: [tpl]cquote|facilitate the taking of a succession of photographic pictures at equal intervals of time, in order to record the changes taking place in or the movement of the object being photographed, and also by means of a succession of pictures so taken of any moving object to give to the eye a presentation of the object in continuous movement as it appeared when being photographed.[tpl]cite book|last=Burns|first= R. W.|year=1998|title=Television: An International History of the Formative Years|publisher=Institution of Engineering and Technology|location=London|isbn=0-85296-914-7[/tpl][/tpl]
