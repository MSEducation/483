[[Vallavaraiyan Vandiyadevan]]

CATEGORIES: Tamil history, Tamil novels, Indian novels, Historical novels, Novels first published in serial form, Novels set in the Chola Empire

Vallavarayar Vandhiyathevar was a feudatory of the Chola kings Raja Raja Chola I and Rajendra Chola I and chief of the Samanthas of North Arcot. ==Origins==

His origins and clan are subjects of great debate. Kalki Krishnamurthy strongly believed his clan is Vaanar Kulam (Bana Kingdom / Magadai Mandalam)  Ponniyin Selvan.

==Evidences==

His name is referred in Thanjavur Big Temple inscription in which he is referred to as the husband of Kundavai Pirattiyar.South Indian Inscriptions (Inscriptions of The Great Chola Temple at Tanjore) -Vol-II
A life sized statue depicting Vandhiyathevar controlling a horse is erected in Chennai near Anna Flyover in Mount Road as an honour to this great personality during the first tenure of Mr. Karunanidhi as Chief Minister (This was to capture the banning of horse race by the government of Tamil Nadu is a different background altogether)
