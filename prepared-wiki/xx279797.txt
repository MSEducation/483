[[Shana Feste]]

CATEGORIES: 1976 births, American women film directors, American film directors, American screenwriters, Film directors from California, People from Los Angeles, California, Women screenwriters, Writers from Los Angeles, California, University of California, Los Angeles alumni, University of Texas at Austin alumni, Living people

Shana Feste (born August 28, 1975) is an American film director and screenwriter. She has directed and written The Greatest, Country Strong, and Endless Love, and also has writing credits for You're Not You.

==Early life==

Feste was born and raised in Palos Verdes,[tpl]cite web|last=Roland|first=Tom|title=In 'Country Strong' Movie, Gwyneth Paltrow Channels Her Inner Singer|url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/473730/in-country-strong-movie-gwyneth-paltrow-channels-her-inner-singer|work=Billboard (magazine)[tpl]![/tpl]Billboard|accessdate=August 9, 2013|date=January 7, 2011[/tpl] Los Angeles, CA. She has a sister. Feste attended UCLA.[tpl]cite web|title=Shana Fest, "The Greatest": Triumph, Tragedy, and Honest Emotion|url=http://www.indiewire.com/article/shana_fest_the_greatest_triumph_tragedy_and_honest_emotion|work=IndieWire|accessdate=August 9, 2013|date=January 14, 2009[/tpl] When her parents divorced, she spent summers at her father's in Texas. She earned a master's degree in Creative Writing and Screenwriting at the University of Texas at Austin and then studied Producing at the American Film Institute,[tpl]cite web|last=Smith|first=Krista|title=Country Strong Director Shana Feste: Is Garrett Hedlund the Next Brad Pitt?|url=http://www.vanityfair.com/online/oscars/2011/01/qacountry-strong-director-shana-feste|work=Vanity Fair (magazine)[tpl]![/tpl]Vanity Fair|accessdate=August 9, 2013|date=January 7, 2011[/tpl] from where she graduated in 2003.[tpl]cite web|title=2009 AFI ALUMNI AND FELLOW AWARDS & HONORS|url=http://www.afi.com/conservatory/alumni/awards09.aspx|publisher=American Film Institute|accessdate=August 9, 2013[/tpl] She worked as a nanny, waited tables and tutored to pay for her tuition.[tpl]cite web|last=Leydon|first=Joe|title=Shana Feste’s Nanny Diaries|url=http://www.moviemaker.com/articles-directing/shana-feste-country-strong-gwyneth-paltrow-20101201/|work=MovieMaker Magazine|accessdate=August 9, 2013|date=December 1, 2010[/tpl] Following her graduation, she worked as an assistant at the Creative Artists Agency.

==Career==

Feste's directorial debut was The Greatest, which was screened at the 2009 Sundance Film Festival in the US dramatic competition section.[tpl]cite web|last=Kay|first=Jeremy|title=Sundance unveils competition lineup in four sections for 2009|url=http://www.screendaily.com/sundance-unveils-competition-lineup-in-four-sections-for-2009/4042230.article|work=Screen Daily|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=December 3, 2008[/tpl] The film is based on Feste's father who lost a son before she was born.[tpl]cite web|last=Buchanan|first=Kyle|title=Is The Greatest Director Shana Feste the Next Female Filmmaker You Need to Know?|url=http://movieline.com/2010/04/01/shana-feste-interview/|work=Movieline|accessdate=August 12, 2013|date=April 1, 2010[/tpl] It starred Pierce Brosnan and Susan Sarandon as parents dealing with the death of their son.[tpl]cite web|last=Fleming|first=Michael|title=‘Greatest’ gig for Brosnan, Sarandon|url=http://variety.com/2008/film/news/greatest-gig-for-brosnan-sarandon-1117986040/|work=Variety (magazine)[tpl]![/tpl]Variety|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=May 18, 2008[/tpl] The Greatest earned a Humanitas Prize nomination.[tpl]cite web|title=HUMANITAS Prize Announces 2009 Finalists - Press Releease|url=http://www.humanitasprize.org/PressReleases/08-17-09%20Humanitas%20Finalists%20Announced.pdf|publisher=Humanitasprize.org|accessdate=August 13, 2013|date=August 17, 2009[/tpl] 
In 2009, she wrote the script for music drama Country Strong,[tpl]cite web|title=Paltrow Tops Screen Gems' 'Love Don't Let Me Down'|url=http://www.thewrap.com/movies/article/paltrow-tops-screen-gems-love-dont-let-me-down-11081|work=The Wrap|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=December 2, 2009[/tpl] which she directed and was released in 2011. It was produced by Tobey Maguire, whom Feste came to know while she was a nanny, caring for his daughter. The film focused on recovering alcoholic country singer Kellie Canter played by Gwyneth Paltrow.
Also in 2009, Feste was hired to write a screenplay based on the novel You're Not You by Michelle Wildgen.[tpl]cite web|last=McNary|first=Dave|title=Shana Feste books ‘You’ time|url=http://variety.com/2009/film/news/shana-feste-books-you-time-1118001622/|work=Variety (magazine)[tpl]![/tpl]Variety|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=March 24, 2009[/tpl] Filming took place in December 2012.[tpl]cite web|last=Watkins|first=Jade|title=You're not you! Make-up free Emmy Rossum is dishevelled and dowdy in a mismatched outfit as she films her new movie|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-2242642/Youre-Make-free-Emmy-Rossum-dishevelled-dowdy-mismatched-outfit-films-new-movie.html|work=Daily Mail|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=December 4, 2012[/tpl] The film should be released sometime in 2013.[tpl]cite web|title=You're Not You (2013)|url=http://www.movieweb.com/movie/youre-not-you|publisher=MovieWeb.com|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
In May 2011, Feste was announced to direct an adaptation of Carolyn Turgeon's novel Mermaid: A Twist on the Classic Tale.[tpl]cite web|last=Sneider|first=Jeff|title=Sony taps Shana Feste for ‘Mermaid’|url=http://variety.com/2011/film/news/sony-taps-shana-feste-for-mermaid-1118037480/|work=Variety (magazine)[tpl]![/tpl]Variety|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=May 23, 2011[/tpl] The project is still in development.[tpl]cite web|last=Roberts|first=Katie|title='50 Shades of Grey' Screenwriter to Pen New 'Little Mermaid' Adaptation|url=http://news.moviefone.com/2013/06/11/new-little-mermaid-movie/|work=The Moviefone Blog|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=June 11, 2013[/tpl] She directed the romance film Endless Love, a remake of the 1981 film.[tpl]cite web|last=Kit|first=Borys|title='Country Strong' Director Boarding 'Endless Love' Remake|url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/country-strong-director-boarding-endless-357836|work=The Hollywood Reporter|accessdate=August 10, 2013|date=August 2, 2012[/tpl] The film was released in February 2014.[tpl]cite web|title=Universal Sets Apropos Release Date For ‘Endless Love’ Remake|url=http://www.deadline.com/2013/05/universal-sets-apropos-release-date-for-endless-love-remake/|work=Deadline.com|publisher=PMC (company)[tpl]![/tpl]PMC|accessdate=August 9, 2013|date=May 21, 2013[/tpl]
