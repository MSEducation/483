[[Kitab al-Umm]]

CATEGORIES: Sunni literature, Hadith

The Kitāb al-Umm (Arabic: كـتـاب الأم) is a book of law that is used as an authoritative guide by the Shafi'i school of fiqh (Islamic jurisprudence) within the Sunni branch of Islam.[tpl]Citation | title=Medieval Islamic Civilization, Volume 2 | author=Josef W. Meri, Jere L. Bacharach | publisher=Taylor & Francis, 2006 | isbn=978-0-415-96692-4 | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=LaV-IGZ8VKIC | quote=... His massive Kitab al-Umm (The Exemplar) covers the standard topics found in a work of Islamic law ...  ...[/tpl] The work was composed by the founder of the Shafi'i school, Imām ash-Shāfi‘ī (767-820 CE). The term "al-Umm" means "the exemplar." The Kitab al-Umm is noted for its hermeneutic approach to developing legal principles, basing them on revelation rather than traditional authority.[tpl]Citation | title=Hadith as scripture: discussions on the authority of prophetic traditions in Islam | author=Aisha Y.
