[[Mannington Mills]]

CATEGORIES: Salem, New Jersey, Manufacturing companies based in New Jersey, Companies established in 1915

Mannington Mills is an international flooring manufacturer with corporate headquarters in Salem, New Jersey. The company was founded by John Boston Campbell and his sons Neil and Kenneth in 1915.[tpl]cite web   | title = History   | work =    | publisher = Mannnington Mills   | date =    | url = http://www.mannington.com/Corporate/OurCompany/History.aspx   | accessdate = 2013-08-10[/tpl][tpl]cite web   | title = Timeline IV   | work = Timeline of Salem County   | publisher = Salem County Office of Archives and Records Management   | date =    | url = http://www.salemcountynj.gov/?wpfb_dl=607   | accessdate = 2013-08-13[/tpl]
In 2008, Mannington acquired Burke Industries which produces rubber flooring tiles, wall base, treads, and accessories. In addition to facilities at the Port of Salem and the Pureland Industrial Complex in New Jersey, the company has three additional plant locations: in Epes, Alabama; Calhoun, Georgia; and High Point, North Carolina. Burke Industries facilities are in Eustis, Florida and San Jose, California. In 2012, Mannington acquired United Kingdom-based Amtico International.[tpl]cite news   | last = Gallo, Jr.   | first = Bill   | title = Mannington Mills buys flooring manufacturer Amtico International   | newspaper = South Jersey Times   | date = March 5, 2012   | url = http://www.nj.com/salem/index.ssf/2012/03/mannington_mills_buys_united_k.html   | accessdate = 2013-08-10   | quote = [/tpl]
A privately held company, Mannington is one of the largest and oldest flooring manufacturers in the United States and is the world’s largest manufacturer of luxury vinyl tile. It also produces residential and commercial resilient, laminate, hardwood and porcelain tile floors, as well as commercial carpet and rubber. Chairman of Mannington Mills, Keith S. Campbell, serves on the board of directors of the Federal Reserve Bank of Philadelphia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.federalreserve.gov/aboutthefed/directors/ |title=Directors of Federal Reserve Banks and Branches |date= May 27, 2012 |publisher=The Federal Reserve accessdate = 2013-08-10[/tpl]
In 2009, the company initiated an environmental clean-up of its facilities adjacent to Salem in Mannington Township.[tpl]cite news   | last = Gallo, Jr   | first = Bill   | title = Mannington Mills plans major environmental cleanup at local plant   | newspaper = South Jersey Times    | date = November 7, 2009   | url = http://www.nj.com/salem/index.ssf/2009/11/mannington_mills_plans_major_e.html   | accessdate = 2013-08-13   | quote = [/tpl][tpl]cite web   | title = Mannington Mills Incorporated   | publisher = Department of Environmental Protection   | date = January 2013   | url = http://www.epa.gov/region2/waste/fsmanni.htm   | accessdate = 2013-08-13[/tpl]
