[[Albert "Sonny" Cunha]]

CATEGORIES: 1879 births, 1933 deaths, Musicians from Hawaii, Hawaii politicians, American composers, American ukulele players

Albert "Sonny" Cunha (1879 - 1933) was a Hawaiian composer, bandleader, pianist, singer, politician and entrepreneur. He was the first to popularize hapa haole music, a type of Hawaiian music with influences from popular music and with lyrics that are a combination of English and Hawaiian (or wholly English).

==Early years==

Albert Cunha was born in Honolulu, Hawaii to Angela Gilliland and Emmanuel Cunha, and later attended Yale Law School in 1898,[tpl]cite book|last=Garrett|first=Charles Hiroshi|title=Struggling to Define a Nation: American Music and the Twentieth Century|year=2008|publisher=University of California Press|isbn=978-0-520-25486-2|pages=174â€“176[/tpl] where he was a star baseball and football star, [tpl]cite book|last=Tranquada|first=Jim|title=The Ukulele: A History|year=2012|publisher=University of Hawaii Press|isbn=978-0-8248-3634-4|page=61[/tpl]although he did not graduate.[tpl]cite book|last=Yale University|authorlink=Yale University|title=Alumni Directory of Yale University and Non-graduates|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=aqrOAAAAMAAJ&lpg=PA508&ots=jHXNugVVrm&dq=Albert%20Cunha%20Yale%20-site%3Awikipedia.org%20-wiki&pg=PA508#v=onepage&q=Albert%20Cunha&f=false|accessdate=2013-04-07|year=1920|publisher=Yale University|location=New Haven, CT|oclc=657021096|page=508[/tpl]
In 1903 he composed My Waikiki Mermaid, the earliest known hapa haole song. This was followed in 1905 by his first big hit, My Honolulu Tomboy. Other notable songs followed, My Hawaiian Maid, Honolulu Hula-hula Heigh!, My Tropical Hula Girl and Honolulu Hula Girl. 
In 1905 sextette led by Cunha played the entire summer season at a California beach resort in Santa Cruz.  In 1906 he directed the Royal Hawaiian Glee Club on their tour of the West Coast of the US with the Royal Hawaiian Band.
In 1914 he published the first hapa haole songbook, Famous Hawaiian Songs.[tpl]cite book|last1=Cunha|first1=Albert R.|first2=W.H. |last2=Coney |first3=Solomon |last3=Meheula|title=Famous Hawaiian Songs|url=http://hdl.handle.net/2027/uc1.l0069685360|accessdate=2013-04-07|year=1914|publisher=Bergstrom Music Co., Ltd|location=Honolulu, HI|id=[tpl]OCLC|13403547|29479495|824689048[/tpl][/tpl] He played piano, sang and led a dance orchestra in Honolulu for many years, toured the mainland, founded the Cunha Music Company (which sold instruments and presumably sheet music), and served as a Congressman from 1923 to 1924.[tpl]dubious|Not a Congressman |reason=See Hawaii Territory's at-large congressional district |date=April 2013[/tpl]
His son was B movie director Richard E. Cunha.
