[[Burma–Denmark relations]]

CATEGORIES: Bilateral relations of Burma, Bilateral relations of Denmark

Burma–Denmark relations refer to the current and historical relationship between Burma and Denmark. Neither country has a resident embassy. ==Assistance to Burma==

Development assistance to Burma is a top priority of the DANIDAs engagement in Southeast Asia. 93 million DKK was given to education and healthcare projects.[ref][tpl]cite news |title= Activities in Burma|author= |url= http://www.ambbangkok.um.dk/en/menu/DevelopmentDanida/DanidainBurma-Myanmar/ActivitiesInBurma-Myanmar|publisher= Foreign Affairs of Denmark|date= |quote= |accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl]
[/ref]
Danish development assistance has focused on promoting democracy and human rights. Denmark was one of the first countries to respond to cyclone Nargis by providing humanitarian assistance to Burma.[tpl]cite news |title= DANIDA in Burma|author= |url= http://www.ambbangkok.um.dk/en/menu/DevelopmentDanida/DanidainBurma-Myanmar|publisher= Foreign Affairs of Denmark|date= |quote= |accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl] Three Diseases Fund was founded in 2006, and Denmark joined in 2009.[tpl]cite news |title= Three Diseases Fund|author= |url= http://www.3dfund.org/index.php|publisher= The Diseases Fund 3DF|date= |quote= |accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl] Three Diseases Fund helps Burma fight HIV and AIDS, and has assisted with 73 million dollars.[tpl]cite news |title= Three Diseases Fund (3DF)|author= |url= http://www.ambbangkok.um.dk/en/menu/DevelopmentDanida/DanidainBurma-Myanmar/ActivitiesInBurma-Myanmar/3df|publisher= Foreign Affairs of Denmark|date= |quote= |accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl]

==Burmese Consul Incident==

In 1996, the consul in Burma for Denmark, James Leander Nichols, was sentenced to three years in jail. The sentence was for illegal possession of two facsimile machines and a telephone switchboard. Two months later, he died in prison. Despite Danish insistence, Burmese authorities refused to allow an independent autopsy.[tpl]cite news|author=Anais Tamen|title=The European Union’s Sanctions Related to Human Rights: The Case of Myanmar|url=http://www.burmalibrary.org/docs09/Memoire-Anais_Tamen.pdf|quote=|date=2002–2003|page= 98|accessdate=8 December 2010[/tpl] Soon after, the European Union, with Canada, called for a United Nations gathering on the democratisation process.[tpl]cite news|title=James Leander Nichol's Death Imprisoned For Owning an Illegal Fax Machine in Burma|url=http://www.cfob.org/burmaissue/politicalPrisoners/JLNichols.shtml|publisher= CFOB Canadian Friends of Burma|quote=|date=|pages=|accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Response of the Union of Myanmar to the United Nations|url=http://www.cfob.org/Background/JLNichols.shtml|publisher= CFOB Canadian Friends of Burma|quote=|date=|pages=|accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl]

==Day's Work Day==

On 3 November 2010, students from 140 different gymnasiums in Denmark and DanChurchAid, participated in the annual Day's Work Day. The money earned by the students goes to improve education for young people in Burma.[tpl]cite news|title=21.000 gymnasieelever arbejder onsdag for bedre uddannelse i Burma |language=Danish|url=http://www.u-landsnyt.dk/nyhed/01-11-10/21000-gymnasieelever-arbejder-onsdag-bedre-uddanne|publisher= U-landsnyt.dk|quote=|date=1 November 2010|accessdate=23 January 2011[/tpl]
