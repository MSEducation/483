Metro Ciudad Deportiva ([tpl]Lang-es|Estación Ciudad Deportiva[/tpl]) is a metro station along Line 9 of the Mexico City Metro.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.metro.df.gob.mx/red/estacion.html?id=121|title=Ciudad Deportiva|language=Spanish|accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|last=Archambault|first=Richard|url=http://mexicometro.org/metro/line9/ciudad-deportiva/|title=Ciudad Deportiva »  Mexico City Metro System|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl] It is named for the nearby the Magdalena Mixhuca Sports City.
Magdalena Mixuhca Sports City was enlarged in 1967 as part of the project to create the venues needed for the 1968 Summer Olympics.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.la84foundation.org/OlympicInformationCenter/OlympicReview/1967/ore03/ore03d.pdf|title=THE IOC SECRETARY GENERAL IN CENTRAL AND SOUTH AMERICA|format=pdf|pages=4, 5, 6 |date=December 1967|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite book|url=http://www.amazon.ca/1968-Summer-Olympic-Venues-Universitario/dp/1155855914|title=1968 Summer Olympic Venues: Estadio Azteca, Magdalena Mixhuca Sports City, Arena Mexico, Estadio Olimpico Universitario, Estadio Cuauhtemoc |publisher=LLC Books|isbn=978-1-155-85591-2 |date=September 2010|accessdate=18 July 2011[/tpl]
The Sports City is home to the Autódromo Hermanos Rodríguez (racetrack), the Foro Sol (baseball stadium and concert venue),[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.ticketmaster.com.mx/Foro-Sol-boletos-Mexico/venue/163903|title=Foro Sol México, DF boletos. Como llegar, mapas, eventos. Sitio oficial de Ticketmaster.|language=Spanish|accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.elsiglodetorreon.com.mx/noticia/518933.rock-intensidad-y-decepcion-vive-latino.html|title=Rock, Intensidad y decepción Vive Latino|work=El Siglo de Torreón|date=26 April 2010|language=Spanish|accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.elporvenir.com.mx/notas.asp?nota_id=485594|title=El Porvenir : En Escena : Reventa encarece boletos para el Vive Latino|work=El Porvenir|date=10 March 2011|language=Spanish|accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl]
the Agustín Melgar Olympic Velodrome, and the Palacio de los Deportes (indoor arena).
The station is elevated and sits in the median of the Viaducto Río Piedad.  It was opened 26 August 1987.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://en.structurae.de/structures/data/index.cfm?id=s0057355|title=Ciudad Deportiva Metro Station (1987) - Structurae |accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl]
The logo for the station represents a player engaged in a Mesoamerican ballgame (a similar logo is used for Metro Deportivo 18 de Marzo on lines 3 and 6).
In December 2009 a man in his 30s was shot twice in the back at the station.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://enlamira.net/?p=8427|title=LO MATAN A BALAZOS JUNTO A ESTACION DEL METRO CIUDAD DEPORTIVA|date=5 December 2009|language=Spanish|accessdate=17 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=June 2013[/tpl]
