[[Robert Entwistle]]

CATEGORIES: 1941 births, Living people, People from Burnley, English cricketers, Lancashire cricketers, Cumberland cricketers, Minor Counties cricketers

Robert Entwistle (born 20 October 1941) is a former English cricketer. Entwistle was a right-handed batsman. He was born in Burnley, Lancashire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/29289.html|title=Player profile: Robert Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=23 September 2011[/tpl]
Entwistle made his first-class debut for Lancashire against Gloucestershire in the 1962 County Championship. He made 47 further first-class appearances for the county, the last of which came against Yorkshire in the 1966 County Championship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/First-Class_Matches.html|title=First-Class Matches played by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] In these matches, he scored a total of 1,554 runs at an average of 20.72, with a high score of 85.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/f_Batting_by_Team.html|title=First-class Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] This score came against the Marylebone Cricket Club in 1964,[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/27/27085.html|title=Lancashire v Marylebone Cricket Club, 1964|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] was one of fourteen fiftes he made, though he was not able to convert any of them to a century. His List A debut came against Leicestershire in the Preliminary round of the 1963 Gillette Cup, which was the first ever List A match to be played. He made eight further List A appearances for Lancashire, the last of which came against Middlesex in the 1966 Gillette Cup.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/List_A_Matches.html|title=List A Matches played by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] In his nine List A matches for the county, he scored 150 runs at an average of 25.00, with a high score of 48.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/a_Batting_by_Team.html|title=List A Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] He left Lancashire at the end of the 1966 season.
In 1967, Entwistle joined Cumberland, making his debut for the county in the Minor Counties Championship against Durham. He played Minor counties cricket for Cumberland from 1967 to 1984, making 91 Minor Counties Championship[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/Minor_Counties_Championship_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Championship Matches played by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] and two MCCA Knockout Trophy appearances.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/29/29289/Minor_Counties_Trophy_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Trophy Matches played by Bob Entwistle|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] During this time he also made a single first-class appearance for the Minor Counties against the touring West Indians in 1976, scoring 8 runs in the Minor Counties first-innings before being dismissed by John Holder, while in their second-innings he scored exactly 50 runs before being dismissed by Albert Padmore.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/36/36438.html|title=Minor Counties v West Indians, 1976|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] He also made a single List A appearance for Cumberland, which came in the 1984 NatWest Trophy against Derbyshire. In a match which Cumberland lost by 9 wickets, Entwistle was dismissed for a duck by Roger Finney.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/44/44924.html|title=Cumberland v Derbyshire, 1984 NatWest Trophy|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl]
