[[Moudi bint Khalid Al Saud]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, House of Saud, Saudi Arabian princesses, Saudi Arabian women in politics, Members of the Consultative Assembly of Saudi Arabia

Moudi bint Khalid Al Saud is a Saudi Arabian philanthropist and a member of the House of Saud. She is among the first female members of the Consultative Assembly of Saudi Arabia since January 2013.

==Early life and education==

Princess Moudi is the daughter of King Khalid and Sita bint Fahd Al Damir.[tpl]cite web|title=Biography of King Khalid|url=http://kke.com.sa/eng/biography|work=King Khalid Exhibition|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] She received basic education in Riyadh and studied French.

==Career==

Moudi bint Khalid is the general secretary the King Khalid Foundation and the chair of its investment committee.[tpl]cite web|title=Board of Trustees:|url=http://www.kkfeng.org/leadership.php|work=King Khalid Foundation|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] She is also the general secretary of the Al Nahda Foundation of Riyadh.[tpl]cite web|title=Princess Moudi bint Khalid|url=http://www.whoswhoarabwomen.com/profiles.asp?a=940|work=Who's Who Arab Women|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] The foundation was awarded the first Chaillot prize for human rights organisations in the Gulf in 2009.[tpl]cite web|title=‘Strong Foundations’? The Imperative for Reform in Saudi Arabia|url=http://edoc.bibliothek.uni-halle.de/servlets/MCRFileNodeServlet/HALCoRe_derivate_00003652/Strong%20Foundations.pdf|work=FRIDE|accessdate=11 August 2013|author=Ana Echagüe|coauthors=Edward Burke|pages=1–23|date=June 2009[/tpl] She is a board member of the Saut, agency of the down syndrome foundation in Saudi Arabia.[tpl]cite web|title=Board of Members|url=http://www.saut.org.sa/page-9/|work=SAUT|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] In 2011, she began to provide fellowships under the Legatum Center to Saudi Arabian students attending Massachusetts Institute of Technology.[tpl]cite web|title=Legatum Fellowship|url=http://legatum.mit.edu/our-programs/fellowships|work=MIT|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]
In January 2013, she was elected to the Consultative Assembly, being one of the first 30 Saudi Arabian women appointed to the assembly.[tpl]cite news|title=Breakthrough in Saudi Arabia: women allowed in parliament|url=http://www.alarabiya.net/articles/2013/01/11/259881.html|accessdate=11 August 2013|work=Al Arabiya|date=11 January 2013[/tpl] In addition, she is one of the two royal women appointed along with Sara bint Faisal, daughter of King Faisal.[tpl]cite journal|last=Friedman|first=Brandon|title=The Saudi Kingdom in Transition:  Women Appointed to the Majlis|journal=Telaviv Notes|date=28 January 2013|volume=7|issue=2|url=http://www.dayan.org/sites/default/files/Friedman_Brandon_CHANGE_IN_SAUDI_ARABIA_TANOTES_280113.pdf|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Royal orders amend Shura Council system and form new chamber|url=http://www.saudiembassy.net/latest_news/news01111301.aspx|work=Royal Embassy, Washington DC|accessdate=4 September 2013[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Princess Moudi is married to Abdul Rahman bin Faisal, son of King Faisal. They have three children, two daughters, Sara and Albandari, and a son, Saud.
