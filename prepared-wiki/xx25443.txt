[tpl]subscription required|via=[[British Newspaper Archive]][/tpl]</ref> a 1934 article in ''The Western Morning News'' related a trip to the cliffs at [[St Agnes, Cornwall|St Agnes]]: where after entering a narrow hole, about 150 feet in there was a "huge excavation ... the top or 'back' [of which] could not be seen, and the depth must have been about 150 to 200 feet, as we could hear the sea roaring away down there [...] This huge "gunnies" could have contained a decent sized cathedral." The writer recommended that it be made accessible so that the public could admire "the works of the men [...] whose enterprise, energy, daring and skill are unsurpassed in the history of our country".<ref>
