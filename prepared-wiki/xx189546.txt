[[Ask Foy]]

CATEGORIES: Auburn University

The Foy Information Desk, or the Foy Information Line, is a telephone and walk-in information service provided by Auburn University and hosted in the new Student Union building.  The service has been in continuous operation since the 1950s.[tpl]cite web |url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/21983239/ |title=5 phone numbers that will change your life |accessdate=2008-04-28 |date=2007-11-27 |publisher=MSNBC/Today| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20080426093445/http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/21983239/| archivedate= 26 April 2008 | deadurl= no[/tpl] At one time, the service was available 24 hours a day, but is now available from 6 am to midnight Central Standard Time The Foy Information Desk service was initially designed as a resource for Auburn students who were looking for course information, grades, or campus services, but now accepts calls from the general public.  The operators are Auburn University students, and the free service attempts to answer any questions asked by a caller or visitor.[tpl]cite episode |title=Phone Numbers That Can Change Your Life |url=http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=lI1IDHYxz0I |series= Today Show  |airdate=2007-11-27[/tpl] Auburn's Foy Information Desk service is one of the busiest information lines, receiving around 1000 calls each day.[tpl]cite web |url=http://media.www.thelantern.com/media/storage/paper333/news/2006/05/31/Campus/Fact-Lines.Help.Students.Entertain.Bar.Patrons-2014975-page3.shtml |title=Fact lines help students, entertain bar patrons |accessdate=2008-04-28 |date=2006-05-26 |author=Bryan Bullock |publisher=The Lantern: The Student Voice of Ohio State University[/tpl]
The service was featured on the Today Show on November 27, 2007.
