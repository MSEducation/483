Ann Bilansky (born Mary Ann Evards Wright) (c. 1820 Fayetteville, North Carolina â€“ March 23, 1860 Saint Paul, Minnesota) was an American housewife convicted in 1859 of poisoning her husband with arsenic.  She is the only woman to receive the death penalty and the first White person executed in Minnesota. She was executed by hanging.

==In popular culture==

Ann Bilansky's trial and execution was the basis for Jeffrey Hatcher's stage play, A Piece of Rope, which premiered in St. Paul in March 2000.City Pages, March 22, 2000.
