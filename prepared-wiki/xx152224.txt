Choe Myong-ho (Chosŏn'gŭl: 최명호, Hanja: 崔敏鎬; born 3 July 1988 in Pyongyang[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kc-camapa.ru/cgi-bin/prntxt.cgi?20060830,1,1,,6323-6324,6323-6324 |title=Северные корейцы учат русский, стоят на голове и ждут документы с родины |accessdate=29 October 2006 |language=Russian |publisher=Спорт-Экспресс |last=Эпштейн |first=Арнольд [/tpl]) is a North Korean footballer, who currently plays for Pyongyang City Sports Group.

==Career==

Choe was one of the first native North Koreans to join a club abroad, Krylia Sovetov Samara of Russian Premier League, and the first to play in Russia.[tpl]cite web|publisher=The Daily NK|author=Tertitskii, Fedor|title=North Korean Footballers in Russia|url=http://www.dailynk.com/english/read.php?cataId=nk00100&num=4510|date=2 February 2009|accessdate=14 September 2009[/tpl]
He was selected as the 2005 AFC Youth Player of the Year.[tpl]cite web |url=http://access.fifa.com/en/article/0,4151,111818,00.html |title=AFC Player of the Year Awards Al Montashari scoops top Asian award |publisher=FIFA.com |date=1 December 2006 |accessdate=29 October 2006 [/tpl]
Before moving to Russia, Choe Myong-Ho had played for the Ministry of Light Industry team (Kyonggongop) in the DPR Korea League of his native country.
Due to his remarkable skills and talent the player has been called the "North Korean Ronaldo".[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.kc-camapa.ru/cgi-bin/prntxt.cgi?20060828,40,6,,,6323-6324 |title=Северокорейский Рональдо в Самаре |date=28 August 2006 |accessdate=29 October 2006 |last=Прияткина |first=Мария |publisher=Самарский Футбол |language=Russian[/tpl]
Despite moving to the Russian club in 2006, Choe only made his full debut for the first team on 27 June 2007, in the Russian Cup, as Krylia Sovetov lost to KAMAZ Naberezhnye Chelny, by a single goal.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.kc-camapa.ru/cgi-bin/pls.cgi?n=6323 | title = Profile at the official Krylia Sovetov site | language = Russian[/tpl]
One of his teammates at Samara was Oh Beom-Seok who is from the South Korea.
Choe wasn't registered by Krylia to play in the 2009 season.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Russian Premier League|title=Крылья Советов (Самара) – Заявка клуба – Сезон 2009|url=http://www.rfpl.org/clubs.shtml?team=10&act=squad&year=2009|language=Russian|accessdate=27 September 2009[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]

==International career==

He was a member of his country's U-17 national team,[tpl]FIFA player|237149[/tpl] Choe Myong-Ho took part in the 2005 FIFA U-17 World Championship (scoring three times in four games) and the AFC U-17 Championship 2006,[tpl]cite web |title=Interview with Chief Manager and Players of DPRK U-17 |url=http://www1.korea-np.co.jp/pk/222th_issue/2005102907.htm |accessdate=29 October 2006 |year=2005 [/tpl] where his side finished runners-up.
