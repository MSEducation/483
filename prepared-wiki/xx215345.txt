[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 200 metre backstroke]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The men's 200 metre backstroke event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 13–15 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl] 
Billed as the Clash of the Titans, Ryan Lochte stormed home on the final lap to defeat his teammate Aaron Peirsol and claim his first individual Olympic gold medal. He touched the wall first in 1:53.94, faster than the world record he shared with Peirsol at 1:54.32.[tpl]cite news|title=Lochte, Peirsol take gold, silver in 200 back|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/26209853/ns/beijing_olympics-beijing_olympics_news/t/lochte-peirsol-take-gold-silver-back|publisher=NBC News|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Ryan Lochte Claims Epic Battle Against Aaron Peirsol With 200 Back World Record|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18827.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Lochte grabs 200m backstroke gold |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/swimming/7562305.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=15 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl] 
Peirsol enjoyed a head-to-head battle against Lochte in the first 150 metres, but ended up only with a silver in 1:54.33. Russia's Arkady Vyatchanin added a second bronze to his collection, finishing in a new European record of 1:54.93 to hold off Austria's Markus Rogan (1:55.49), the silver medalist in Athens four years earlier. Earlier in the semifinals, Vyatchanin edged out Germany's Helge Meeuw in a swimoff to secure a last spot for the top 8 final.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Americans Go 1-2 in Men's 200 Back, Aaron Peirsol Top Seed|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18810.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=13 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl]
Japan's Ryosuke Irie finished fifth with a time of 1:55.72, and was followed in the sixth spot by Australia's Hayden Stoeckel, who shared bronze medals with Vyatchanin in the 100 m backstroke, in an Oceanian record of 1:56.39. Romania's Răzvan Florea (1:56.52) and Great Britain's Gregor Tait (1:57.00) rounded out the finale.
