[[United States House of Representatives elections in Idaho, 2014]]

CATEGORIES: United States House of Representatives elections, 2014, United States House of Representatives elections in Idaho

Elections for both of Idaho's House seats will take place on November 4th, 2014.

==District 1==

Republican Raúl Labrador has represented Idaho's 1st congressional district since 2011.  Labrador won election to a second term in 2012, defeating former NFL player Jimmy Farris with 63% of the vote.
Labrador was rumored to be considering a run for governor in 2014, but has announced he will run for re-election instead.[tpl]cite news|title=U.S. Rep. Raul Labrador will run for reelection to Congress in 2014|url=http://www.idahostatesman.com/2013/08/14/2706207/us-rep-raul-labrador-will-run.html|accessdate=28 August 2013|work=Idaho Statesman|date=14 August 2013[/tpl]
Reed McCandless, who received 19% in the 2012 Republican primary, is challenging Labrador again.  Also running in the Republican primary are Rathdrum resident Sean Blackwell, Boise resident Lisa Marie, and Eagle resident Michael Greenway.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.spokesman.com/stories/2014/mar/15/candidates-enter-idahos-may-20-primary/|title=Candidates enter Idaho’s May 20 primary|work=Idaho Statesman|last=Russell|first=Betsy Z.|date=March 15, 2014|accessdate=March 15, 2014[/tpl]
Although Farris initially expressed interest in a rematch against Labrador, in July 2013 he told the Idaho Statesman he was leaning towards a run for a Boise-based seat in the Idaho Legislature instead.Popkey, Dan. "Awaiting dominoes, Jimmy Farris eyes Idaho Legislature" Idaho Statesman, July 22, 2013. (accessed 20 September 2013) In August 2013 State Representative Shirley Ringo of Moscow announced she is running for the Democratic nomination.[tpl]cite news|title=Idaho Democrat Shirley Ringo to run for U.S. House |url=http://www.idahostatejournal.com/news/local/idaho-democrat-shirley-ringo-to-run-for-u-s-house/article_f8971054-09fe-11e3-9b35-0019bb2963f4.html|accessdate=28 August 2013|work=Associated Press|publisher=Idaho State Journal|date=20 August 2013[/tpl]  Hayden resident Ryan Barone is also running in the Democratic primary.

==District 2==

Republican Mike Simpson has represented Idaho's 2nd congressional district since 1999. Simpson won reelection in 2012, defeating Democratic State Senator Nicole LeFavour with 65% of the vote.
In 2014 Simpson faces a primary challenge from Idaho Falls attorney Bryan Smith.[tpl]cite news|last=Rothenberg|first=Stuart|title=Incumbent Primary Challengers: Some Promising, Others Premature|url=http://blogs.rollcall.com/rothenblog/incumbent-primary-challengers-some-promising-others-premature/|accessdate=14 August 2013|newspaper=Rothenberg Report|date=5 August 2013[/tpl]
Former Congressman Richard Stallings, who represented this seat from 1985-1993, is the only Democratic candidate.[tpl]cite news|url=http://magicvalley.com/news/local/democrat-richard-stallings-mounts-congressional-bid/article_5d9531d3-f528-5817-a0c0-01bd1255116c.html|title=Democrat Richard Stallings Mounts Congressional Bid|work=Times-News|last=Kruesi|first=Kimberlee|date=March 15, 2014|accessdate=March 15, 2014[/tpl]
