[[Cummascach mac Flainn]]

CATEGORIES: 757 deaths, People from County Offaly, 8th-century Irish monarchs, Year of birth unknown

Cummascach mac Flainn (died 757) was a king of the Uí Failge, a Laigin people of County Offaly. He was one of the many sons of Fland Dá Chongal, a previous king.Mac Niocaill, pg.127 He ruled from 755 to 757.in  the Book of Leinster king list he is given a reign of 10 years. He was the first of Fland's sons by Érenach, daughter of Murchad Midi (died 715) of Uisnech to hold the throne.
Not mentioned in the Annals of Ulster, the Annals of Tigernach record that he was slain in 757 by Máel Dúin mac Áedo (died 786), King of Munster.Annals of Tigernach, AT 757.6
