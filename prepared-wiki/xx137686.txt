Lorne Allan Babiuk, [tpl]Post-nominals|country=CAN|OC|SOM|FRSC[/tpl] (born January 25, 1946) is a Canadian scientist specializing in immunology, pathogenesis, virology, molecular virology, and vaccinology. He is the Vice-President of Research at the University of Alberta and the former Director of the Vaccine and Infectious Disease Organization at the University of Saskatchewan. Dr Babiuk holds the Canada Research Chair in Vaccinology and Biotechnology[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cihr-irsc.gc.ca/e/28732.html|title=Dr. Lorne Allan Babiuk|accessdate=2009-02-03[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=November 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] and is Chair of the Board for Pan-Provincial Vaccine Enterprise (PREVENT), a vaccine development company.[tpl]cite web|url=http://announcements.usask.ca/news/archive/2008/05/new_panprovinci.html|title=New Pan-Provincial Vaccine Enterprise announces board members, vaccine targets|quote=Lorne Babiuk (Chair)|accessdate=2009-02-03[/tpl]

==Education==

Born in Canora, Saskatchewan, he received a B.S.A degree in soil science from the University of Saskatchewan's College of Agriculture in 1967, a M.Sc. degree in soil microbiology from the University of Saskatchewan, Department of Soil Science in 1969, a Ph.D. in virology from the University of British Columbia, Department of Microbiology in 1972, and a D.Sc. from the University of Saskatchewan, Department of Veterinary Microbiology in 1987.[tpl]cite web|url=http://hcr3.isiknowledge.com/author.cgi?=Browse&link2=Results&id=2011|title=ISIHighlyCited.com profile[/tpl]

==Career==

In 1973, he joined the University of Saskatchewan's Department of Veterinary Microbiology as an Assistant Professor. In 1975, he became an Associate Professor and a Professor in 1979. In 1993, he was appointed Director of the Vaccine and Infectious Disease Organization. In 2001, he was awarded the Canada Research Chair in Vaccinology and Biotechnology.
