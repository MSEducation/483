[[Gooseberry Hill National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1970, Darling Range, Gooseberry Hill, Western Australia

Gooseberry Hill National Park is a national park in Western Australia (Australia), in the locality of Gooseberry Hill, Western Australia 21Â km east of Perth. It is at the southern side of the mouth of the Helena Valley on the Darling Scarp. Statham's Quarry is located within the park boundary.
The park was named after a hill in Yorkshire by the early settlers. A walking track and the single lane bitumen zig zag drive are both found within the park. The zig zag drive follows the old zig zag railway track[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.railtrails.org.au/states/trails.php3?action=trail&trail=51|title=Total Travel - Gooseberry Hill National Park|year=2010|accessdate=10 June 2010[/tpl] which winds up the steep terrain, offering excellent views of the Swan Coastal Plain below.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.totaltravel.com.au/travel/wa/pertharea/perthhills/travel-guides/gardens/gooseberry-hill|title=Kalamunda Zig Zag Trail|year=2010|accessdate=10 June 2010[/tpl]
No entry fees apply to enter the park but no facilities are available to visitors.
