[[Struvefjella]]

CATEGORIES: Mountain ranges of Svalbard

Struvefjella is a mountain range in Sørkapp Land at Spitsbergen, Svalbard. It is located southwest of the valley Lisbetdalen, and includes the three mountains of Hohenlohefjellet, Sergeevfjellet and Lidfjellet. The mountain area is named after astronomer Friedrich Georg Wilhelm von Struve.[tpl]cite web |url=http://placenames.npolar.no/stadnamn/Struvefjella |title=Struvefjella (Svalbard) |publisher=Norwegian Polar Institute |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] 
The highest and most northern summit is the saddle shaped Hohenlohefjellet, which marks the entrance of the fjord Hornsund.[tpl]cite book |title=Arctic Pilot: Sailing directions Svalbard–Jan Mayen |year=1988 | volume =7 |publisher=The Norwegian Hydrographic Service and The Norwegian Polar Institute |location=Stavanger |language= |isbn=82-90653-06-9 |pages=192, 196 [/tpl]
