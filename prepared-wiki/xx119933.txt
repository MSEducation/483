[[Flowserve]]

CATEGORIES: Companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange, Companies based in Irving, Texas, Companies established in 1997, Manufacturing companies, Pump manufacturers

The Flowserve Corporation is an American multinational corporation and one of the largest suppliers of industrial and environmental machinery such as pumps, valves, end face mechanical seals, automation, and services to the power, oil, gas, chemical and other industries.[tpl]cite book|last=Reidy |first=R.|title=Profile of the International Pump Industry: Market Prospects to 2010|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=OVqWdjtCzbUC&pg=PA115|accessdate=26 October 2012|date=29 June 2006|publisher=Elsevier|isbn=978-1-85617-487-9|pages=115–[/tpl] Headquartered in Irving, Texas, which is a suburb of Dallas, Texas, Flowserve has over 15,000 employees in 51 countries.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl] Flowserve sells products and offers aftermarket services to engineering and construction firms, original equipment manufacturers, distributors and end users. The Flowserve brand named originated in 1997 with a merger of BW/IP and Durco International.
On August 21, 2007, Flowserve Corporation was recognized by CIO magazine as one of the 2007 CIO 100 Award Honorees.2007 Winner Profile Flowserve Corporation, CIO Magazine, 21 August 2007. ([tpl]WebCite|http://www.webcitation.org/6Bi2772gv[/tpl])

==History==

Flowserve heritage dates back to the 1790 founding of Simpson & Thompson by Thomas Simpson, later to become Worthington Simpson Pumps, one of the companies that became part of BW/IP.[tpl]cite book|last=Roberts |first=Gwilym|title=Chelsea to Cairo-- 'Taylor-Made' Water Through Eleven Reigns and in Six Continents: A History of John Taylor & Sons and Their Predecessors|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=qQhhuSvu4DwC&pg=SA8-PA9|accessdate=26 October 2012|date=1 January 2006|publisher=Thomas Telford|isbn=978-0-7277-3411-2|pages=8–[/tpl] The company was created in 1997 with the merger of two fluid motion and control companies – BW/IP and Durco International.
Acquisitions include HydroTechnik Olomouc in 2006,Flowserve.com Sealing Systems in 2007,MF Flowserve.com Calder Ag in 2009Flowserve.com and Valbart Srl in 2010.Flowserve.com In October 2011 Flowserve acquired Lawrence Pumps Inc. Also in March 2013 it acquired one plant from L&T Valves.

==Educational services==

Flowserve has five training centers for students around the world to attend.  They are in Irving, TX, Kalamazoo, MI, Houston, TX, Baton Rouge, Louisiana, and Desio, Italy.  The learning resource center (LRC) in Irving is [tpl]convert|24000|sqft|m2[/tpl] with classrooms, static labs, and power labs with complete pumping systems.
The LRC in Italy is the most recent, opening on April 20, 2010.Flowserve Opens New State-of-the-Art Learning Resource Center in Desio, Italy

==Restatements==

On Feb 3, 2004, Flowserve announced restatement of its financial statements for aggregate estimated pretax adjustments for 2003. The restatement was approximately $11 million.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Flowserve+Reports+Improved+Bookings+and+Backlog+in+Fourth+Quarter%3B...-a0112858126| title=Flowserve Announced Restatement for 2003[/tpl]
On Oct 26, 2004, Flowserve determined to restate its previously announced financial results for the second quarter of 2004, including certain post-closing adjustments and recently identified out-of-period expenses. The restatement had no impact on net cash balance.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20041026006191/en/Flowserve-Announces-Filing-Delays| title=Flowserve Announces Filing Delays[/tpl]
On Feb 7, 2005, Flowserve confirmed that it would restate its 2000 through first quarter 2004 financial statements, resulting from an ongoing comprehensive review of the company's accounts. The restatement reduced net income for the periods restated.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.businesswire.com/news/home/20050207006082/en/Flowserve-Reports-Increased-2004-Bookings-Debt-Paydown| title=Flowserve Confirms Financial Restatement[/tpl]
