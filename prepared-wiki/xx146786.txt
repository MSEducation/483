[[USS General J. H. McRae (AP-149)]]

CATEGORIES: General G. O. Squier-class transports, Cold War auxiliary ships of the United States, Transport ships of the United States Army, Ships built in Richmond, California, 1944 ships

USS ''General J. H. McRae'' (AP-149) was a [tpl]sclass-|General G. O. Squier|transport ship[/tpl] built for the United States Navy in World War II. In 1946 she was transferred to the US Army and operated as USAT ''General J. H. McRae. On 1 March 1950 she was transferred to Military Sea Transportation Service and operated as 'USNS General J. H. McRae (T-AP-149)'''. She was named for US Army Major General James H. McRae.

==Operational history==

General J. H. McRae was launched 26 April 1944 by the Kaiser Co., Kaiser Shipyards, Yard 3, Richmond, California. and commissioned 8 August 1944 at Richmond, California.
During 1944 she sailed from West Coast ports carrying troops to Honolulu, Hawaii and other Pacific Theater destinations.  On 11 January 1945 she sailed for India where she picked up troops and delivered them to various South Pacific destinations.  She sailed 19 June 1945 through the Panama Canal for Le Havre, France, where she embarked more than 4,000 troops and returned with them to Newport News, Virginia  She moved to Staten Island, N.Y. where on 26 October 1945 she picked up 2,200 "kaki clad brethen"(The "MAC" Log)" and sailed [tpl]convert|8643|nmi|abbr=on[/tpl] arriving at Iran's Shaht-El-Arab River on 19 November 1945. The MAC Log also said that up to this point the "MAC" had transported 36,000 troops over [tpl]convert|120000|nmi[/tpl]. She continued transporting troops from Europe and other ports through the end of 1945.  She was decommissioned at New York City 27 February 1946 and was returned to WSA[tpl]clarify |reason=Define this term; |date=June 2013[/tpl] for peacetime operations as an army transport.
As USAT General J. H. McRae she transported troops through 1950. One typical voyage during this period was transporting troops from Manila to San Francisco in January 1947.[ref]
[tpl]rs|date=June 2013[/tpl][/ref]
Reacquired 1 March 1950 and assigned to MSTS. Manned by civilians, she operated between New York and the United Kingdom until February 1954,McRae Souvenir Edition dated 4 January 1954[tpl]rs|date=June 2013[/tpl] then shifted her base to San Francisco, California for runs to Japan.  
On 29 October 1954 the General J. H. McRae was inactivated. She was scrapped in Taiwan in 1987.
She received four battle stars for Korean War service.
