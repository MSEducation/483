[[F.D. Crockett (log deck boat)]]

CATEGORIES: 1924 ships, Ships on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Middlesex County, Virginia

 ''F.D. Crockett''  is a Poquoson-style Chesapeake Bay log-built deck boat, built in 1924 in Dare, Virginia, by Alexander Gaines. The length of the boat is 62.8 feet. overall, 55.8 foot keel length, with a beam of 15.7 feet and draft of 4.6 feet. The gross tonnage is 28, net tonnage 16. The F.D. Crockett was built primarily for freight hauling and the buying and transporting of produce. She is presently ported at the Deltaville Maritime Museum near Deltaville, Virginia. She is on
display for educational purposes and travels the Chesapeake Bay as an ambassador for the museum.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Middlesex/059-5013_F_D_Crockett_2012_NRHP_FINAL.pdf|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: F.D. Crockett |author=David Moran| date=March 2012|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying four photos
She was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2012.
