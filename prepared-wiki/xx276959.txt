[[Jakob "Jackie" Gerlich]]

CATEGORIES: 1917 births, 1960 deaths, Male actors from Vienna, Actors with dwarfism, American male film actors, Austrian emigrants to the United States

Jakob "Jackie" E. Gerlich was a dwarf Austrian emigree most notable for his appearance in the 1939 American film The Wizard of Oz.
Gerlich was born September 21, 1917 in Vienna, Austria.[tpl]cite web|title=Jackie Gerlich|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0314417/|publisher=IMDB|accessdate=08/07/2013[/tpl] He emigrated to America when he arrived in New York City on the S.S. President Roosevelt ocean liner on March 13, 1936.[tpl]cite web|title=Jackie Jack Gerlich|url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=91174111|publisher=Find A Grave|accessdate=08/07/2013[/tpl]

==Wizard of Oz==

His first acting experience was in The Wizard of Oz, where he played a member of the Lollipop Guild.[tpl]cite web|title=The Wizard of Oz|url=http://movies.tvguide.com/wizard-oz/cast/123071|publisher=TV Guide|accessdate=08/07/2013[/tpl] Jerry Maren and Harry Doll were the other two members of the Lollipop Guild in the film.  
Leopold von Singer signed a contract with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer to provide 124 proportionately-sized little people to play Munchkins in Oz.Harmetz, Aljean. The Making of the Wizard of Oz. Hyperion, 1998. Gerlich was recruited because of his dwarfism and was paid $125 a week for his work."Mickey Carroll, A Munchkin From The 1939 Classic Film ‘The Wizard Of Oz,’ Dies At 89". Access Hollywood. Retrieved 08/07/2013. Though Gerlich is uncredited in the film, his name appears in Oz trivia articles and books.[tpl]cite book|last=Scarfone|first=Jay|title=The Wizardry of Oz: The Artistry and Magic of the 1939 M-G-M Classic|year=2004|publisher=Hal Leonard Corporation|isbn=1557836248|page=240[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Cox|first=Stephen|title=The Munchkins of Oz|year=2002|publisher=Cumberland House Publishing|isbn=1581822693|pages=11, 37, 39, 41, 77[/tpl] 
In 2007, all 124 Munchkin actors in Oz were honored with a Star on the Hollywood Walk of Fame.[tpl]cite news|title=Munchkins of 'Oz' get a star on Walk of Fame|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/movies/news/2007-11-20-munchkins-star_N.htm|accessdate=2013-08-08|newspaper=USA Today|date=2007-11-21[/tpl] For Gerlich this was a posthumous honor. Seven surviving Munchkin actors attended the ceremony, including Mickey Carroll, Ruth Duccini, Jerry Maren, Margaret Pellegrini, Meinhardt Raabe, Karl Slover and Clarence Swensen.[tpl]cite news|last=Serjeant|first=Jill|title="Oz" Munchkins get Hollywood star|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2007/11/21/us-munchkins-idUSN2062387820071121|accessdate=2013-08-08|newspaper=Reuters|date=2007-11-21[/tpl]
Maren said that due to previous engagements, the three members of the Lollipop guild did not stay in contact after Oz. In 2013 he stated "We pretty much separated because they all had previous engagements. Somebody had to go to Milwaukee, somebody had to go to Boston, New York, etc."[tpl]cite news|last=bryanenk|title='Wizard of Oz' Munchkin Star, 93, Still Singing 'Lollipop Guild'|url=https://movies.yahoo.com/blogs/movie-talk/wizard-oz-munchkin-star-93-still-singing-lollipop-001643489.html|accessdate=10 April 2014|newspaper=Yahoo|date=18 September 2013[/tpl] 

==After Oz==

Gerlich's other acting experience was also an uncredited role when he played Bobby in the 1939 film East Side of Heaven.
Besides his small movie roles, Gerlich was a circus performer in the Ringling Bros. and Barnum & Bailey Circus.[tpl]cite web|title=Ringling Bros. and Barnum & Bailey Route Book, 1948|url=http://www.circushistory.org/History/RBBB1948.htm|accessdate=9 April 2014[/tpl]
Gerlich was interviewed by Mike Wallace on May 1, 1959.[tpl]cite web|last=Wallace|first=Mike|title=Mike Wallace Papers|url=http://library.syr.edu/digital/guides/w/wallace_m.htm|publisher=Syracuse University Library|accessdate=08/07/2013[/tpl]

==Death==

On December 27, 1960, Gerlich died at the age of 43 in Sarasota, Florida.
