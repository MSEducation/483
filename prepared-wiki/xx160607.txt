Willi Reimann (born 24 December 1949 in Rheine) is a German football manager.
Reimann played in the Bundesliga for Hannover 96 and Hamburg, appearing in 287 games, in which he amounted 93 goals.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/dfbpokal/spielrunde/dfb-pokal/1973-74/hannover-96-58/12441/spieler_willi-reimann.html | title = Reimann, Willi | language = German | publisher = kicker.de | accessdate = 16 June 2011[/tpl] After one more season for the Calgary Boomers in the North American Soccer League in 1981,[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.nasljerseys.com/Players/R/Reimann.Willi.htm| title = Willi Reimann | publisher = nasljerseys.com | accessdate = 30 January 2013[/tpl] Reimann retired from playing.
As manager he worked for FC St. Pauli, HSV, VfL Wolfsburg, 1. FC Nürnberg and Eintracht Frankfurt.
After being sacked due to the relegation of Frankfurt he moved to the United Arab Emirates to manage Al-Shaab. From November 2006 until March 2007, he managed Eintracht Braunschweig.
