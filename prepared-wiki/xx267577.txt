[[Hong Ki-myung]]

Hong Ki-myung (born 1959), also known as Kim Hong, is a South Korean financier.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=newsarchive&sid=a91mSOH0RR3I&refer=home|periodical=Bloomberg|first=Cathy|last=Chan|title=Bank of America Said to Name Hong as Asia Chief as Chai Leaves|date=2009-02-03|accessdate=2011-09-23[/tpl]

==Education and career==

Hong earned his MBA from the University of Chicago's Booth School of Business.[tpl]citation|url=http://news.mk.co.kr/v3/view.php?year=2010&no=380430|periodical=Maeil Business Newspaper|date=2010-07-19|accessdate=2011-09-23|title=핌코, 아시아 헤드에 홍기명씨 임명 [tpl]bracket|Hong Ki-myung appointed PIMCO Asia head[/tpl][/tpl] He began his career in finance in the mid-1980s, working at a number of industry firms including Citigroup and Bankers Trust.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.hankyung.com/news/app/newsview.php?aid=2008061302401&sid=0106&nid=006&ltype=1|periodical=Korea Economic Daily|date=2008-06-13|accessdate=2011-09-23|title=美BOA 아시아담당 사장 홍기명씨 [tpl]bracket|Hong Ki-myung to take charge as US's BOA Asia president[/tpl]|author=이태훈 Tae-hun[/tpl] In 2001 he moved to JPMorgan Chase, to become head of their Seoul branch. He became well known in the South Korean finance industry for his role, and at one point it was thought that he would be named head of the Korea Investment Corporation, the sovereign wealth fund managing South Korea's foreign exchange reserves.[tpl]citation|url=http://news.mk.co.kr/newsRead.php?sc=30200011&cm=%EC%82%AC%EB%9E%8C%EA%B3%BC%EC%82%AC%EB%9E%8C&year=2007&no=293777&selFlag=&relatedcode=&wonNo=&sID=300|periodical=Maeil Business Newspaper|title=BOA, 아시아 마켓책임자에 JP모건의 홍기명씨 영입 [tpl]bracket|JP Morgan's Hong Ki-myung takes up post as BOA Asia markets head[/tpl][/tpl] He later relocated to Hong Kong, while remaining with JPMorgan Chase.
In 2007, Hong moved from JPMorgan Chase to Bank of America, where he took up a position as head of global markets for Asia-Pacific. He would continue to be based in Hong Kong. In June 2008, Hong was promoted to Asia-Pacific president, again based in Hong Kong, reporting to Jonathan Moulds. Hong replaced Colm McCarthy, a 30-year BofA veteran who planned to retire.[tpl]citation|periodical=MarketWatch|date=2008-06-12|accessdate=2011-09-23|url=http://www.marketwatch.com/story/bank-of-america-names-ki-myung-hong-as-asia-president|last=Gopalan|first=Nisha|title=Bank of America names Ki-Myung Hong as Asia president[/tpl] Six months later in December 2008, BofA acquired Merrill Lynch, BofA stated that former Merrill CFO Nelson Chai would relocate to Hong Kong to head the firms' newly merged Asia Pacific businesses.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2008/12/18/bankofamerica-jobs-idUSN1838985320081218|periodical=Reuters|date=2008-12-18|accessdate=2011-09-23|title=Bank of America names Merrill CFO to head Asia[/tpl] However, just two months after his appointment, Chai resigned, and BofA again named Hong as Asia-Pacific president.[tpl]citation|url=http://article.joinsmsn.com/news/article/article.asp?total_id=3481510|title=BOA appoints new Asia head|periodical=JoongAng Ilbo|date=2009-02-24|accessdate=2011-09-23[/tpl]
In 2010, PIMCO hired Hong to manage their Asia-Pacific business, with responsibility for offices in Hong Kong, Singapore, Sydney, and Tokyo. PIMCO also named him a member of their Global Operating Committee. He continued to be based in Hong Kong, reporting to COO Douglas Hodge, who previously head the position of Asia-Pacific head.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2010/07/18/pimco-asia-idUKN1813280220100718|periodical=Reuters|date=2010-07-18|accessdate=2011-09-23|periodical=Reuters|title=Pimco hires new head of Asia Pacific region[/tpl]
