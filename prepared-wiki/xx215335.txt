[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 100 metre backstroke]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The men's 100 metre backstroke event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 10–12 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl] 
Aaron Peirsol established a new world record of 52.54 to defend his Olympic title in the event. His teammate Matt Grevers earned a silver in 53.11, giving the United States a one-two finish.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Aaron Peirsol Crushes 100 Back World Record En Route To Win|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18773.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Aaron Peirsol wins gold in 100m backstroke; Matt Grevers takes silver|url=http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/olympics_blog/2008/08/piersol-wins-go.html|publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl] Meanwhile, Australia's Hayden Stoeckel and Russia's Arkady Vyatchanin, who both finished behind Grevers by 0.07 of a second, tied for the bronze medal in a matching time of 53.18.[tpl]cite news|title=Dead-heat bronze for Stoeckel, Peirsol wins backstroke gold|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/stories/2008/08/12/2332611.htm?site=olympics/2008|publisher=ABC News|date=12 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl]
Stoeckel's teammate Ashley Delaney finished fifth in 53.31, while Great Britain's Liam Tancock, who led a field in the first 50 metres, faded only to sixth place in 53.39.[tpl]cite news|title=Peirsol wins 100m backstroke gold |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/swimming/7555351.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=12 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl] Spain's Aschwin Wildeboer Faber (53.51) and Japan's Junichi Miyashita (53.99) rounded out the finale. For the first time in Olympic history, all eight swimmers went faster than a winning time of 54.09, previously set by Peirsol in Athens four years earlier.
Earlier, Grevers erased Peirsol's 2004 Olympic record of 53.45 to pick up a top seed in the prelims, until Stoeckel broke a 53-second barrier, and eventually lowered the record to 52.97 in the semifinals.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Club 52 Grows to Four, Hayden Stoeckel Wins Semifinal Round of 100 Back|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18755.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=10 August 2008|accessdate=16 May 2013[/tpl]
