[[Fabio Albergati]]

CATEGORIES: 1538 births, 1606 deaths, Italian writers, People from Bologna, 16th-century writers, 16th-century Italian people, 17th-century Italian people

Fabio Albergati (1538–1606) was an Italian diplomat and writer, known for political theory and as a moralist.http://www.filosofia.unina.it/ars/ealberg.html
He was born in Bologna,[tpl]it icon[/tpl], http://www.treccani.it/enciclopedia/fabio-albergati_(Dizionario_Biografico)/ and was in the service of Jacopo Boncompagni.J. R. Mulryne, Helen Watanabe-O'Kelly, Margaret Shewring, Europa triumphans: court and civic festivals in early modern Europe, Volume 1 (2004), p. 211 note 42; Google Books.

==Works==

He wrote against duelling in 1583, at a time when his patron was active against banditry.Gigliola Fragnito, Church, Censorship, and Culture in Early Modern Italy (2001), pp. 141–2; Google Books. He wrote a very detailed attack on Jean Bodin's theoretical dismissal of mixed constitutions. He equated reason of state with Machiavellianism.http://www.filosofia.unina.it/ars/introe.html His La Republica regia (published 1627) was a counter to Machiavelli.Frank Edward Manuel, Fritzie Prigohzy Manuel, Utopian Thought in the Western World (1979), p. 153.

==Family==

His children included Cardinal Niccolò Albergati-Ludovisi.http://documents.medici.org/people_details.cfm?personid=13857
