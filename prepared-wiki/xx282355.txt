==Biography==

Hubert was born on 1 June 1906 in Billingshurst, West Sussex. He spent his boyhood in Clevedon, Somerset where he studied art at the Reading School of Art and from 1926 to 1929 attended Slade School of Fine Art. In 1930 he held an exhibition of his painting Entombment at the New Burlington Gallery and later the same year due to his poor health he returned to Beaconsfield, Buckinghamshire from London. Between 1933 and 1937 he participated in the objective abstraction movement with Geoffrey Tibble and Rodrigo Moynihan. Between 1936 and 1947 his father and both of his brothers died and he spent time with his mother till her death somewhere in 1960. The same time his brothers died he quit the London Group, which he had joined in 1931. 
In 1942 his works were held at the Lefevre Gallery, both the London Gallery and the Anglo-French Art Centre four years later and from 1946 to 1953 were featured in the Mayor Gallery. In 1948 Lawrence Alloway suggested him to exhibit his artwork at the ICA's Forty Years of Modern Art show and the same year he held an exhibition at the La Jeune Peinture en Grande Bretagne. In 1952, his friend and neighbor, Geoffrey Tibble, have died and he himself have suffered chronic illness due to that. Later on he moved to Seaford, East Sussex where he died on 25 January 1985.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tate.org.uk/art/artists/edgar-hubert-2303|title=Edgar Hubert|accessdate=August 10, 2013[/tpl]
