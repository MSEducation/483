[[Hewick]]

CATEGORIES: Historic American Buildings Survey in Virginia, Plantations in Virginia, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, Houses completed in 1750, Houses in Middlesex County, Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Middlesex County, Virginia

Hewick is a historic home located near Urbanna, Middlesex County, Virginia.  It was constructed about 1750, and is a two-story, five-bay, "L"-shaped brick dwelling.  It was originally 1 1/2-stories, but raised to a full two stories in the mid-19th century.  The rear ell is popularly believed to have been built in the late 17th century.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Middlesex/059-0006_Hewick_1978_Final_Nomination.pdf|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Hewick |author=Virginia Historic Landmarks Commission staff| date=May 1978|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying photo
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1978.
