[[Gabbin, Western Australia]]

CATEGORIES: Wheatbelt (Western Australia)

Gabbin is a small town in the Wheatbelt region of Western Australia.
The townsite originally served as a railway station for the Wyalkatchem to Mount Marshall line that was constructed through the area in 1913. 
The townsite was gazetted in 1918 and was named after the railway station.[tpl]LandInfo WA|c|G|6 October 2008[/tpl]
In 1932 the Wheat Pool of Western Australia announced that the town would have two grain elevators, each fitted with an engine, installed at the railway siding.[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article32560140 |title=Country elevators |newspaper=The West Australian|location=Perth, Western Australia|date=6 July 1932|accessdate=6 April 2013|page=10|publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl] Work on the bulk handling depot commenced late in 1933.[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article33327893|title=Country news|newspaper=The West Australian|location=Perth, Western Australia|date=2 October 1933|accessdate=7 April 2013|page=14|publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl]
