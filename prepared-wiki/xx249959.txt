[[Emmanuel Lutheran Church of Harlemville and Cemetery]]

CATEGORIES: Lutheran churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1873, 19th-century Lutheran church buildings, Lutheran cemeteries, Cemeteries on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Cemeteries in Columbia County, New York, Churches in Columbia County, New York

Emmanuel Lutheran Church of Harlemville and Cemetery is a historic Lutheran church and cemetery at County 21 and Pheasant Lane, Harlemville Road at Ten Broeck Road in Harlemville, Columbia County, New York.  It was built 1871-1873 and is a one story, rectangular wood frame building with clapboard siding and a gable roof. It features a square bell tower and is set on a fieldstone foundation.  The cemetery hold about 200 burials and remains in use.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=14010|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Emmanuel Lutheran Church of Harlemville and Cemetery|date=May 2001|accessdate=2010-06-26 |author=Peter D. Shaver|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=14012|title=Accompanying eight photos[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2002.
