[tpl]nihongo|Hitomi Kanehara|金原 ひとみ|Kanehara Hitomi|born August 8, 1983[/tpl] is an award-winning Japanese novelist.

==Biography==

A high school dropout[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2004/06/06/world/an-aging-island-embraces-japan-s-young-dropouts.html?pagewanted=all|title=An Aging Island Embraces Japan's Young Dropouts|date=June 6, 2004|publisher=The New York Times|accessdate=January 6, 2010 | first=Norimitsu | last=Onishi[/tpl] at the age of 15, Kanehara pursued her passion for writing with the support of her father, Mizuhito Kanehara, a literary professor and translator of children's literature. She was born and currently lives in Tokyo.
She wrote her first novel Snakes and Earrings at the age of 21. The novel won the prestigious Akutagawa Prize (judged by acclaimed novelist Ryū Murakami), going on to sell a million copies.Thorne, Matt, Autofiction, By Hitomi Kanehara, trans David James Karashima, The Independent, 29 February 2008, Accessed 7 May 2008

==Awards==

In 2003, Kanehara was awarded the Subaru Literary Prize (Shueisha) for her novel Hebi ni Piasu (Snakes and Earrings); later that same year the novel won her the Akutagawa Prize, one of the most prestigious literary awards in Japan, making her and Risa Wataya the youngest people ever to receive this honor.
