[[Matt LeBlanc]]

CATEGORIES: 1967 births, Male actors from Massachusetts, American male film actors, American male television actors, American people of French-Canadian descent, American people of Italian descent, Living people, Best Musical or Comedy Actor Golden Globe (television) winners, People from Newton, Massachusetts

Matthew Steven "Matt" LeBlanc (born July 25, 1967) is an American actor and producer, known for his role as Joey Tribbiani on the NBC sitcom, Friends and its spin-off Joey. In 2011, LeBlanc began starring as a fictional version of himself in Episodes, a BBC Two/Showtime television series created by Friends co-creator David Crane and Jeffrey Klarik. He won a Golden Globe award for his work on Episodes, after being nominated for an Emmy three times for his work on Friends.

==Career==

LeBlanc first appeared in the 1987 Heinz Tomato Ketchup commercial. In 1988, he starred in the television drama TV 101 for one season. He went on to star in a spin-off of Married... with Children titled Top of the Heap, the Fox series lasted seven episodes airing in April and May 1991.
LeBlanc found success in the role of Joey Tribbiani on Friends, and went on to play this character for twelve years — ten seasons of Friends and two seasons of Joey. Friends was successful and LeBlanc, along with his co-stars, gained wide recognition among viewers. He has appeared in the films Ed (1996), Lost in Space (1998), Charlie's Angels (2000), and All the Queen's Men (2001).

==Personal life==

In May 2003, LeBlanc married Melissa McKnight, a former model.Wulff, Jennifer. "Matt Leblanc's Surprise Split", People, vol. 65, no. 15, April 17, 2006. Retrieved November 3, 2010. McKnight and LeBlanc had been introduced in 1997 by her friend Kelly Phillips, wife of actor Lou Diamond Phillips, and LeBlanc proposed to her a year later. Their daughter Marina, born in 2004, began suffering seizures at age eight months. By the time she was two years old, however, the condition, thought to be a form of dysplasia, had largely receded.
In June 2005, LeBlanc admitted to "careless and irresponsible" behavior with a stripper during a motorcycle trip to Canada. LeBlanc and McKnight separated on January 1, 2006. By that time, LeBlanc was involved in a relationship with actress Andrea Anders. In March of that year, LeBlanc filed for divorce, citing irreconcilable differences. The divorce became final on October 6, 2006.[tpl]cite web|url = http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,1180477,00.html|work= People|title = Matt LeBlanc's Other Woman| date = April 6, 2006|accessdate = March 27, 2009[/tpl]
