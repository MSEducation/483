Lennon Parham (born October 26, 1976) is an American actress and comedian best known for starring on the sitcom Accidentally on Purpose from 2009–2010 and the NBC series Best Friends Forever in 2012.

==Early life==

Parham was born in Fitzpatrick, Alabama. She attended Parkview High School in Lilburn, GA and graduated with a B.S. Degree in Theatre from the University of Evansville. Following college, she taught French for two years at T.L. Weston High School in Greenville, Mississippi as part of the Teach for America program.[tpl]cite web|url=http://losangeles.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/617|title=UCB Theater • Lennon Parham|publisher=Upright Citizens Brigade |date= |accessdate=2014-03-23[/tpl]

==Career==

After her stint as a French teacher, Parham began performing comedy with the Upright Citizens Brigade Theater. During this time, she starred in the two person show, The Adventures of Lock & Kay, and the one woman show, She Tried to be Normal. In the fall of 2009, Parham began a supporting role in the comedy Accidentally on Purpose.
Parham and Jessica St. Clair's NBC comedy series Best Friends Forever  premiered on April 4, 2012.  NBC officially canceled the series on May 11, 2012.

==Personal life==

Parham is married to educator Junior Guzman.
