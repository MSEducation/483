[[Matthew Gray (archer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1973 births, Living people, Archers at the 1996 Summer Olympics, Archers at the 2000 Summer Olympics, Archers at the 2008 Summer Olympics, Olympic archers of Australia, Australian archers, Commonwealth Games gold medallists for Australia, Australian Institute of Sport archers, Archers at the 2010 Commonwealth Games, Australian police officers

Matthew Gray is an Australian archer.  His day job is being a water policeman. He has participated in three Olympic Games and won a gold medal at the 2010 Commonwealth Games.

==Personal==

Gray is a water policeman.[tpl]cite web|url=http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/sports/news/Gray-matter-in-Aussie-archery/articleshow/6721724.cms |title=Gray matter in Aussie archery — The Times of India |publisher=Timesofindia.indiatimes.com |date=2010-10-10 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Gardiner |first=James |url=http://www.theherald.com.au/news/local/sport/other/gray-brings-home-gold/1964843.aspx |title=Gray brings home gold - Local News - Sport - Other - Newcastle Herald |publisher=Theherald.com.au |date=2010-10-11 |accessdate=2012-06-07[/tpl]  He is from Port Stephens, New South Wales.  He is married, 47, and his wife is a school teacher.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/archery/rivals-prove-poles-apart/2008/08/11/1218306780035.html |title=Archery — Sports — Olympics |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=12 August 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl]  He has two daughters. He has a nursing degree.  While training for the 2010 Commonwealth Games, he was also doing shift work at John James Hospital in Canberra.

==Archery==

Gray competes in archery. His father bought him his first bow at a garage sale, and he has been competing in archery for over thirty years.  He attributed becoming a cop with making him more aggressive as a competitor. He was an Australian Institute of Sport archery scholarship holder.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ausport.gov.au/ais/history/achievements/olympics |title=Olympic Games : Australian Institute of Sport : Australian Sports Commission |publisher=Australian Institute of Sport |date=2008-01-09 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl]
Gray has won several medals. At the 1988 World Field Championships in Bolzano, Italy, he won a bronze medal in the junior men's recurve event.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.archery.org.au/customdata/index.cfm?fuseaction=CustomItem&ItemID=42412 |title=Archery Australia |publisher=Archery Australia |date= |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl]
At the 1992 World Field Championships in Margraten, Netherlands, he was part of the Australian gold medal winning men's team alongside  Clint Freeman and Tony Pitt Lancaster. He won a bronze medal in the men's recurve event at the 1994 World Field Championships in Vertus, France. He won a gold in the men's recurve event at the 1998 European Grand Prix in Czech Republic.  His teammates included  Simon Fairweather, and Scott Hunter-Russell. In 1999, he won a team gold medal at the world indoor championships.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.channelnewsasia.com/stories/afp_sports/view/1084724/1/.html |title=Commonwealth Games English archer Danielle targets CGames gold |publisher=Channel NewsAsia |date=2010-10-02 |accessdate=2012-06-07[/tpl]  At the 2008 World Cup in Santo Domingo Dominican Republic, he was part of the Australian silver medal winning recurve team that.
In March 2003, he was ranked the fourth best male Australian archer in recurve, ahead of Robert Turner.  That month, he  had 2520 points.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Australian Archer|date=March 2003|page=29|publisher=Australian Archery|title=National Ranking List, March 2003|volume=10|id=Print Post Publication no. PP636447/001|location=Panania, New South Wales[/tpl]  In 2010, he participated in World Cup competitions in Croatia, Turkey and China. He attended a national team training camp in Canberra in September 2011. He attended a national team training camp in Canberra in March 2012. At the 2012 National Target Archery Championships, he finished fourth as a member of ANSW.[tpl]cite web|url=http://210.247.205.118/assets/console/document/documents/2012nctargetfinal1.pdf|publisher=Archery Australia|accessdate=6 June 2012|title=2012 National Target Archery Championships|location=Tuggernong, Australian Capital Territory|date=March 2012[/tpl]
In preparation for the 2010 Commonwealth Games, he trained in his garage. He represented Australia at the 2010 Commonwealth Games.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/commonwealth-games-2010/comm-games-news/australia-wins-archery-gold-20101008-16c7n.html |title=Australia wins archery gold |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=8 October 2010 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/breaking-news-sport/how-the-aussies-fared-on-oct-8-20101009-16ct7.html |title=How the Aussies fared on Oct 8 |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=9 October 2010 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2010-10-09/golden-aussies-back-archerys-future/2291242 |title=Golden Aussies back archery's future — ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation) |publisher=Abc.net.au |date=2010-10-09 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] He competed in the men's recurve archery team event where he took home a gold medal with a team score of 219, defeating Malaysia who were ranked third in the world at the time and had an event score of 212.  He also competed in the individual event on the final day of the competition.  In the quarter finals, he went up against Chu Sian Cheng.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/breaking-news-sport/archers-aim-to-end-games-on-a-high-20101010-16de0.html |title=Archers aim to end Games on a high |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=10 October 2010 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] At the Games, he was coached by Simon Fairweather.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/breaking-news-sport/elder-statesman-leads-team-to-gold-20101008-16a5z.html |title=Elder statesman leads team to gold |publisher=The Age |date=2010-10-08 |accessdate=2012-03-26[/tpl]  He was Australia's oldest competitor at the Games. In 2010, he was hoping his Commonwealth Games experiences would prepare him for Olympic selection for 2012 in London.
He has participated in three Olympic Games in the archery event.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.abc.net.au/news/2010-08-13/new-old-archers-bound-for-delhi/943546 |title=New, old archers bound for Delhi — ABC News (Australian Broadcasting Corporation) |publisher=Abc.net.au |date=2010-08-13 |accessdate=2012-06-06[/tpl] He represented Australia in archery at the 2000 Summer Olympics, the 2004 Summer Olympics and the 2008 Summer Olympics.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/breaking-news-sport/elder-statesman-leads-team-to-gold-20101008-16a5z.html |title=Elder statesman leads team to gold |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=8 October 2010 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] As a thirty-five-year-old, he represented Australia in archery at the 2008 Summer Olympics.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/archery/australias-archer-in-the-sky/2008/07/18/1216163203219.html |title=Archery — Sports — Olympics |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |first1=James |last2=Allen |first2=Rod |date=19 July 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl] He competed in both the team and individual events.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.theage.com.au/sport/kim-leads-archery-hopes-for-beijing-20080221-1tkw.html |title=Kim leads archery hopes for Beijing |publisher=The Age |location=Melbourne, Victoria |date=21 February 2008 |accessdate=26 March 2012[/tpl]  Children in his wife's class cheered for him at the Games and he kept in touch with the class during the Games.
