[[Les Woodford]]

CATEGORIES: 1897 births, Australian rules footballers from Victoria (Australia), Hawthorn Football Club players, Camberwell Football Club players, Year of death missing

Les Woodford (born 11 May 1897) was an Australian rules footballer who played with Hawthorn in the Victorian Football League (VFL).
Originally from Camberwell,[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article2088348 |title=FOOTBALL. |newspaper=The Argus (Melbourne, Vic. : 1848 - 1956) |location=Melbourne, Vic. |date=30 April 1925 |accessdate=18 June 2012 |page=15 |publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl] Woodford played for Hawthorn in their inaugural VFL season in 1925.[tpl]cite book|last=Holmesby, Russell & Main, Jim|title=The Encyclopedia Of AFL Footballers| publisher=BAS Publishing|year=2007|isbn=978-1-920910-78-5[/tpl] He was the first Hawthorn player to kick five goals in a game, doing so in their round three loss to Collingwood.AFL Tables: Les Woodford By the end of the year he had kicked 20 goals, which was enough to top their goal-kicking.
The next season he returned to Camberwell.The Argus,"Football: Movements of Players", 21 April 1926, p. 16
