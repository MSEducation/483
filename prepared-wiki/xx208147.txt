Arjun Narasingha K.C.([tpl]lang-ne|अर्जुन नरसिंह केसी[/tpl]) (born 27 September 1947) is a Nepalese politician, belonging to the Nepali Congress.  He is also the member of Consituent Assembly. He won in Nuwakot 1  with huge difference against his opponent.  http://www.nepalnews.com/index.php/news/27425-Arjun-Narsingh-wins-in-Nuwakot- He was the Joint General Secretary of Nepali Congress (NC) and the spokesperson of the party.::::: Nepali Congress Party ::::: The Nepali Congress is a Nepalese political party. Nepali Congress led the 1950 Democratic Movement which successfully ended the Rana dynasty and allowed commoners to take part in the politics.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gorkhapatra.org.np/gopa.detail.php?article_id=27979&cat_id=10 |title=::Gorkhapata::The Rising Nepal::Madhupark::Yubamanch::Muna:: |publisher=Gorkhapatra.org.np |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] It again led a democratic movement in 1990, in partnership with leftist forces, to end monarchy and reinstate parliamentary democracy. With the 12-point agreement of 22 November 2005 it worked together with the CPN-UML and the CPN-Maoist to end King Gyanendra's take over of the government.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2008/05/29/world/asia/29iht-nepal.4.13325066.html |title=The kingdom is dead, Nepal's republic is born – The New York Times |work=The New York Times  |date=29 May 2008 |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl]

==Education==

KC holds Master's Degree in Political Science from Tribhuwan University, Kathmandu, Nepal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraphnepal.com/five-questions/2009-01-07/nepalese-people-did-not-fight-to-handover-the-regime-from-one-authoritarian-to-the-other |title=nepalese-people-did-not-fight-to-handover-the-regime-from-one-authoritarian-to-the-other |publisher=Telegraphnepal.com |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] Prior to becoming a full-time politician, he was a teacher in Political Science at Tribhuvan University and also practicing  advocate.[tpl]dead link|date=October 2011[/tpl] He was also in The Thacher School of Diplomacy in United States for a month in 1982. Mr. KC has written many articles written and published in different magazines and papers on contemporary political and social issues.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ekantipur.com/np/2067/11/23/full-news/326077/ |title=मैले जानेका किसुनजी | विचार/विश्‍लेषण |publisher=ekantipur.com |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl]

==Early Political career==

KC interest in Nepalese Politics started from his early school and college days where he held several position like General Secretary at Shanti Vidhya Griha High School 1962–1963, Vice chairperson and Chairperson of National College Union 1963–1967, Chairperson in Association of Political Science from Tribhuwan University 1969–70, General Secretary of students union from Tribhuwan University 1969–1970.
KC won a seat in the 1981 Rastriya Panchayat Election, contesting as an independent.Shaha, Rishikesh. Politics in Nepal 1980–1990. New Delhi: Manohar Publications, 1990. p. 111. In 1982, he was appointed Minister of State for Health in the cabinet of Surya Bahadur Thapa.http://www.healthnet.org.np/reports/hdixit/quest/ANNEX-III.PDF He held the position until 1983.[tpl]dead link|date=October 2011[/tpl] KC was also elected as a member of parliament twice in the 1991 and 1994 parliamentary elections. He was the Minister for Health, Education and Physical Planning 1995–1999.http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-17902746.html[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.asiantribune.com/news/2005/10/23/king-gyanendra%E2%80%99s-regime-will-fall |title=King Gyanendra’s regime will fall |publisher=Asian Tribune |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl]

==Democratic movement==

Mr. KC was a Founder Member of Nepal Student Union in  1970–1971 . He was jailed for the first time at the age of 15 while he was a student, for his democratic beliefs.http://wwwbahnhof.asiantribune.com/king-gyanendra%E2%80%99s-regime-will-fall Later on, Mr. KC was put to jail  from his party office in Kathmandu on 17 February 2005. Mr. K.C. was detained on the suspicion of being part of a plan to hold a peaceful protest against King Gyanendra.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.achrweb.org/briefingpapers/Nepal-BP-0205_HTML.htm |title=Asian Centre for Human Rights |publisher=Achrweb.org |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] So far, K C has served several jail terms totaling to five years and four months.
Mr. KC was  the Coordinator of Kathmandu Valley on behalf of Nepali Congress in Seven Party Alliance for People's Movement against the direct and undemocratic rule of King Gyanendra of Nepalhttp://www.laboratoiredesfrondeurs.org/archives/Extreme_orient/Nepal/06%20NEP%201.pdf[tpl]cite web|last=Sources |first=Combined |url=http://www.politicalaffairs.net/nepal-seven-party-alliance-announces-demands/ |title=Nepal: Seven Party Alliance Announces Demands » pa |publisher=Politicalaffairs.net |date=19 April 2006 |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] which led to 19 days strike and ultimately power was given back to the people.[tpl]cite web|author=chandru |url=http://www.southasiaanalysis.org/%5Cnotes4%5Cnote312.html |title=NEPAL: The Seven Party Alliance needs to Act Together: Update No. 93 |publisher=Southasiaanalysis.org |date=16 May 2006 |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] The ensuing 2006 movement successfully reinstated the parliament and led to the formation of the Federal Republic of Nepal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gorkhapatra.org.np/detail.php?article_id=996&cat_id=5 |title=Gorkhapatra |publisher=Gorkhapatra |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] Mr KC was also the key person under the leadership of Girija Prasad Koirala to bring the Maoist to the government and to hold the Constituent Assembly election in 2008.[tpl]cite news|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2007/WORLD/asiapcf/12/23/nepal.republic/index.html |title=Nepal steps towards democracy |publisher=CNN  |date=24 December 2007 |accessdate=19 October 2011[/tpl] After the 2008 Constituent Assembly elections, Nepali Congress became the second largest party in the country in terms of the number of seats in the CA.[tpl]cite news|last=Suman |first=Journalist |url=http://articles.cnn.com/keyword/nepali-congress |title=Articles about Nepali Congress – CNN |publisher=CNN  |accessdate=19 October 2011 |date=21 July 2008[/tpl]
