[[Geography of Haiti]]

CATEGORIES: Geography of Haiti

The Republic of Haiti comprises the western three-eighths of the island of Hispaniola, west of the Dominican Republic.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/266962/Hispaniola |title=Hispaniola Article |publisher=Britannica.com |accessdate=4 January 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://cardiostart.org/blog/ |title=Dominican Republic 2014 |accessdate=24 April 2014[/tpl] It is positioned between the Caribbean Sea and the North Atlantic Ocean. Haiti's geographic coordinates are at a longitude of 72° 25′ west and a latitude of 19° 00′ north. The total area is [tpl]convert|27750|km2|0[/tpl], of which [tpl]convert|27560|km2|0[/tpl] is land and [tpl]convert|190|km2|0[/tpl] is water. Haiti is slightly smaller than the U.S. state of Maryland. Haiti has [tpl]convert|1771|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] of coastline and a [tpl]convert|360|km|0[/tpl]-border with the Dominican Republic.

==Statistics==

==Physical geography==

Haiti is a very mountainous country with more than 3/4ths of the territory above [tpl]convert|600|ft|0[/tpl]. Its climate is tropical and semiarid. Fertile valleys are interspersed between the mountain ranges forming vast areas of contrast between elevations in many areas throughout the territory. The country (and Hispaniola) is separated from Cuba by way of the Windward Passage, a [tpl]convert|45|nmi|km mi|0|abbr=on|lk=in[/tpl] wide strait that passes between the two countries. ==Islands==

Haiti also harbors several lakes. The largest lake of Haiti, and the second largest lake of Ile d'Haiti and the West Indies, is Lake Azuei. It is located in the Cul-de-Sac Depression with an area of 170 km². It is a saline lake with a higher concentration of salt than the sea water and harbors numerous fauna such as American Crocodiles and American Flamingos.
Lake Peligre is an artificial lake created by the construction of the Peligre Hydroelectric Dam.
Trou Caïman is a saltwater lake with a total area of 16.2 km². Lake Miragoâne is one of the largest natural freshwater lakes in the Caribbean, with an area of 25 km².

==Climate==

The climate is tropical, with some variation depending on altitude. Port-au-Prince ranges in January from an average minimum of [tpl]convert|23|°C|1[/tpl] to an average maximum of [tpl]convert|31|°C|1[/tpl]; in July, from [tpl]convert|25|–|35|°C|°F[/tpl]. The rainfall pattern is varied, with rain heavier in some of the lowlands and on the northern and eastern slopes of the mountains. Port-au-Prince receives an average annual rainfall of [tpl]convert|1370|mm|in|1|abbr=on[/tpl]. There are two rainy seasons, April–June and October–November. Haiti is subject to periodic droughts and floods, made more severe by deforestation. Hurricanes are also a menace.
