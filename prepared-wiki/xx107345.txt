[[Titan Airways]]

CATEGORIES: Airlines of the United Kingdom, Charter airlines of the United Kingdom, Airlines established in 1988, 3i Group companies

Titan Airways Ltd is  a British charter airline with its head office located at Enterprise House at London Stansted Airport.  The airline has a varied fleet with aircraft capable of carrying up to 265 passengers and specialises in VIP and corporate travel as well as airline sub charter and aircraft leasing. In addition, a number of the aircraft within Titan’s fleet operate as freighters on behalf of the Royal Mail. 
The company holds a United Kingdom Civil Aviation Authority Type A Operating Licence, permitting it to carry passengers, cargo and mail on aircraft with 20 or more seats.Operating Licence

==History==

Titan Airways was founded in 1988.  Today it is wholly owned by one of the original founders, Gene Willson, who completed a management buyout from private investment firm 3i at the end of 2012.http://archive.atwonline.com/airline-finance-data/news/titan-airways-management-buyout-completed-0222
Its initial fleet of aircraft included smaller airliner types such as the 33-seat Short 330 twin-engined turboprop.
Its customer base includes investment houses, aircraft manufacturers, major car companies, football teams, tour operators, cruise lines and the corporate entertainment industry.
Titan Airways is also frequently called upon by other carriers, including national flag carriers and regional airlines, when they are faced with operational difficulties.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.traveldailynews.com/news/article/55502/titan-airways-expands-airline-support |title=Titan Airways expands airline support role |publisher=Traveldailynews.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-25[/tpl] The airline has received several awards, notably a Queen’s Award for Enterprise in 2001.[tpl]cite news |title=Top honour for air charter operator |author= |url=http://www.eadt.co.uk/business/top_honour_for_air_charter_operator_1_715992 |newspaper=East Anglia Daily Times |date=November 3, 2010 |accessdate=16 July 2013[/tpl]  The carrier has been ranked in the Sunday Times Profit Track 100 league table on four occasions since the year 2000.http://www.fasttrack.co.uk/fasttrack/leagues/dbResults.asp?searchName=titan+airways&image.x=0&image.y=0
All of Titan Airways' aircraft operate under UK registrations with regular maintenance governed by the UK (CAA) Civil Aviation Authority and EASA (European Aviation Safety Agency).  The carrier is registered under the IATA Operational Safety Audit (IOSA) programme.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.iata.org/whatwedo/safety/audit/iosa/Pages/registry.aspx?Query=all |title=IOSA Registry |publisher=IATA |date=2013-04-25 |accessdate=2014-04-25[/tpl]

==Destinations==

Titan Airways operates charter flights on a seasonal basis to a number of destinations on behalf of UK-based tour operators. During the summer months the airline operates regularly to Dalaman in Turkey, to Lourdes in France and to Figari and Calvi on the island of Corsica. During the winter it operates regularly to Chambéry[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chambery-airport.com/rubrique.php3?id_rubrique=125 |title=Airlines | Chambéry Savoie Airport |publisher=Chambery-airport.com |date= |accessdate=2014-04-25[/tpl] in the French Alps.
Aside from the regular seasonal routes mentioned above, Titan Airways operates long and short haul flights on an ad hoc basis on behalf of individuals, blue chip companies, Premier League football teams, high-profile personalities and pop/rock groups.
In addition, Titan Airways operates flights worldwide on behalf of other airlines on an ACMI basis.

==UK Ministry of Defence==
