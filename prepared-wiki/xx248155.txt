[[Celtic L.F.C.]]

CATEGORIES: Celtic L.F.C., Women's football clubs in Scotland, 2007 establishments in Scotland

Celtic Football Club Women is a football team that plays in the Scottish Women's Premier League. The team is affiliated with Celtic F.C. in Glasgow and use the club's training facilities and wear the club's colours.

==History==

Celtic's women's section was established in 2007 when they took over Arsenal North L.F.C.,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.eveningtimes.co.uk/women-s-football-sfa-in-charge-as-celts-join-up-1.953392|title=Women's football: SFA in charge as Celts join up|last=Montgomery|first=Laura|date=9 August 2007|work=Glasgow Evening Times|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] founding a Girls and Women's Football Academy at the same time.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/celtic/6733935.stm|title=Celtic women's academy is hailed|date=8 June 2007|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl]
Celtic reached the Scottish Women's Cup final in their inaugural year, losing to Hibernian L.F.C. 3–1 after extra time in May 2008.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/cup-returns-to-edinburgh-1.833123|title=Cup returns to Edinburgh|date=18 May 2008|work=The Herald|accessdate=8 January 2010|location=Scotland[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://sport.scotsman.com/sport/Hibernian-Ladies-3--1.4095972.jp|title=Hibernian Ladies 3 - 1 Celtic Ladies: Women's team show male counterparts how it's done|last=Wright|first=Angus|date=19 May 2008|work=The Scotsman|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] The team's first silverware arrived two years later as Spartans were beaten 4–1 in the final of the 2010 Scottish Women's Premier League Cup.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/women/8679584.stm|title=Celtic secure women's League Cup|date=2010-05-13|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=2011-06-12[/tpl]
In October 2012 Celtic were expelled from the Scottish Women's Cup. They had tried to force the postponement of a quarter final with Glasgow City, but the SWF did not accept Celtic's reasons and instead awarded the tie to Glasgow.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/19980335|title=Celtic 'concern' after being thrown out of Scottish Cup|date=2013-10-17|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=2013-04-20[/tpl]

==Former managers==

The senior women's team was managed between 2008 and 2012 by ex–professional footballer Robert Docherty, who was also Head of the Celtic Foundation. He was succeeded by Peter Caulfield, who was appointed in January 2013, following two years as assistant manager. Caulfield departed in July 2013.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.scotzine.com/2013/07/celtic-ladies-head-coach-peter-caulfield-departs-the-club/|title=Celtic Ladies head coach Peter Caulfield departs SWPL club|date=25 July 2013|work=Scotzine|accessdate=27 July 2013[/tpl]
Former Dundee United player John Holt was the team's manager in their inaugural 2007–08 campaign.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/editors-choice/2008/02/20/girls-will-be-bhoys-at-celtic-ladies-fc-86908-20325093/|title=Girls Will Be Bhoys At Celtic Ladies FC|last=Booth|first=Samantha|date=20 February 2008|work=Daily Record|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl]
