[[Seat]]

CATEGORIES: Seats, Furniture

A seat is place to sit, often referring to the area one sits upon as opposed to other elements like armrests.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.thefreedictionary.com/seat | title=Seat | publisher=The Free Dictionary | accessdate=May 31, 2011[/tpl]
A seat is also known as a bench, a chair, a chaise longue, chesterfield, a couch, a davenport or a settee.[tpl]cite web| url=http://thesaurus.com/browse/seat?s=t|title=Seat |publisher= Thesaurus.com |accessdate=2012-05-18[/tpl]

==Etymology==

The word seat comes from Middle English sete and from Old Norse sæti; akin to Old English gesete seat, sittan to sit. The first known use of the word seat is in the 13th century.[tpl]cite web| url= http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/seat|title=Seat|publisher=Merriam Webster|accessdate=2012-05-18[/tpl]
Also public seats tend to have a lot of bird 'droppings' on them.

Frank Baum|publisher=The Literature Network|accessdate=2012-05-18[/tpl] referred to a seat in this passage of his novel Dorothy and the Wizard in Oz; “At once a little girl rose from her seat and walked to the door of the car, carrying a wicker suit-case in one hand and a round bird-cage covered up with newspapers in the other, while a parasol was tucked under her arm.”[tpl]cite web| url= http://www.thefreedictionary.com/seat|title=Seat|publisher=The Free Dictionary By Farlex| accessdate=2012-05-18[/tpl]
