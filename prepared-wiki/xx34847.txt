[[Brindabella National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of New South Wales, Protected areas established in 1996, Australian National Heritage List, 1996 establishments in Australia, Brindabella Ranges, Australian Alps National Parks and Reserves

Brindabella National Park is an [tpl]convert|18454|ha|acre[/tpl] national park in New South Wales, Australia, that is located approximately [tpl]convert|267|km[/tpl] southwest of Sydney central business district[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ga.gov.au/bin/distancedraw2?rec1=106825&placename=brindabella&placetype=0&state=0&place1=SYDNEY&place1long=151.209595&place1lat=-33.873199|title=Great Circle Distance between SYDNEY and BRINDABELLA NATIONAL PARK|accessdate=11 August 2011|publisher=Commonwealth of Australia|work=Geosciences Australia website[/tpl] in the Brindabella Range. Much of the eastern boundary of the national park forms part of the western border of the Australian Capital Territory with New South Wales.
On 7 November 2008, the park was registered on the Australian National Heritage List as one of eleven areas constituting the Australian Alps National Parks and Reserves.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/heritage/places/national/australian-alps/index.html |title=Australian Alps National Parks information |accessdate=2010-06-10 |work=Department of the Environment, Water, Heritage and the Arts [/tpl]
