==Early life==

Burnell was born on January 30, 1798 in Nantucket, Massachusetts.

==State Public service==

===Massachusetts General Court===

Burnell served as member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives in 1821-1822, and as a member of the Massachusetts Senate in 1823, from 1825 to 1833, and in 1838.[tpl]Citation |last = Starbuck  | first =Alexander  | title =Proceedings of the Nantucket Historical Association, SEVENTEENTH ANNUAL MEETING, July Twenty-sixth, Nineteen Hundred Eleven| page = 32. | publisher = Nantucket Historical Association | location = Nantucket, Massachusetts | date = July 26, 1911[/tpl]

===Massachusetts Constitutional convention of 1820===

Burnell was  a member of the Massachusetts constitutional convention of 1820.[tpl]Citation |last = Starbuck  | first =Alexander  | title =Proceedings of the Nantucket Historical Association, SEVENTEENTH ANNUAL MEETING, July Twenty-sixth, Nineteen Hundred Eleven| page = 31. | publisher = Nantucket Historical Association | location = Nantucket, Massachusetts | date = July 26, 1911[/tpl]

==Whig National Convention==

Burnell served as delegate to the Whig National Convention in 1840.

==Member of the US Congress==

Burnell was elected as a Whig to the Twenty-seventh and Twenty-eighth Congresses and served from March 4, 1841, until his death in Washington, D.C., June 15, 1843.  

==Burial==

He was interred in Congressional Cemetery.  Burnell was re-interred in Prospect Hill Cemetery, Nantucket, Massachusetts, in 1844.
