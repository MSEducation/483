[[Matt's Bar]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Minneapolis, Minnesota, Drinking establishments in Minnesota, Restaurants in Minnesota, Restaurants established in 1954

Matt's Bar is a restaurant in South Minneapolis, Minnesota.  It is known as one of two businesses that created the Jucy Lucy.[tpl]cite news|title=Matt's dispels report predicting the demise of Jucy Lucy cheeseburger|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=MN&p_theme=mn&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EFD60AA09E166A1&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|newspaper=Star Tribune|date=March 12, 1997[/tpl]
This bar was opened in 1954.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.mndaily.com/2008/03/13/burger-battle-1 |title=Burger Battle |author=Boller, Jay |date=2008-03-13 |publisher=MN Daily |accessdate=2009-11-30 [/tpl] In 1998 Scott Nelson purchased the bar from the original owner Matt Bristol.
In a 1998 City Pages article,[tpl]cite news|last=Grumdahl|first=Dara Moskowitz|title=A Tribe Called Lucy|url=http://www.citypages.com/1998-08-12/restaurants/a-tribe-called-lucy/|newspaper=City Pages|date=August 12, 1998[/tpl] Cheryl Bristol, the daughter of bar founder and namesake Matt Bristol,[tpl]cite news|title=The Names Behind the Places|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=PD&s_site=twincities&p_multi=SP&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB7399283266B4F&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|newspaper=Pioneer Press|date=March 24, 2000[/tpl] told of how one day in 1954, a customer asked a cook to put two hamburger patties together and seal up some cheese in the middle. When the customer bit into the sandwich, he was heard to exclaim, "That's one juicy Lucy!"
