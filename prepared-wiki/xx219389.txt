[[Heinz Valk]]

CATEGORIES: 1936 births, Living people, Estonian artists, Estonian politicians, Recipients of the Order of the National Coat of Arms, 3rd Class, Recipients of the Order of the White Star, 4th Class

Heinz Valk (birth name Heinrich Valk) (born March 7, 1936 in Gatchina) is an Estonian artist, caricaturist and politician.  He is credited for coining the term "Singing Revolution" [tpl]cite book  |title=Between Utopia and Disillusionment |last=Vogt |first=Henri |authorlink= |coauthors= |year=2005 |publisher=Berghahn Books |location= |isbn=978-1-57181-895-9 |pages= |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=nI73PdnqQlcC&vq[/tpl] ([tpl]lang-et| laulev revolutsioon[/tpl]) and its slogan  "One day, no matter what, we will win!" [tpl]cite web |url=http://www.president.ee/en/duties/?gid=98246 |title=President Ilves presented a commemorative stone for the restoration of independence to artist Heinz Valk |accessdate=2008-09-24 |work= |publisher=Office of the President Public Relations Department |date= [/tpl]([tpl]lang-et|Ükskord me võidame niikuinii![/tpl]) some of the most famous sentences from the end of the 1980s and beginning of the 1990s, to describe Estonia's struggle for regaining independence from the Soviet Union in the early 1990s.
