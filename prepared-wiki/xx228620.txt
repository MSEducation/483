[[Rutina Wesley]]

CATEGORIES: 1979 births, Actresses from Nevada, Juilliard School alumni, Living people, People from Las Vegas, Nevada, University of Evansville alumni, 21st-century American actresses, American voice actresses, American television actresses, African-American actresses, American film actresses

Rutina Wesley (born February 1, 1979) is an American film, stage, and television actress best known for her role as Tara Thornton on the HBO series True Blood.

==Early life and education==

Wesley was born and raised in Las Vegas, Nevada. Wesley's father, Ivery Wheeler, is a professional tap dancer and her mother, Cassandra Wesley, was a showgirl. She attended high school at the Las Vegas Academy of International Studies, Performing and Visual Arts. She studied dance at Simba Studios and the West Las Vegas Arts Center.
While at the Las Vegas Academy, Wesley missed several other auditions for college training programs and was finally accepted by the University of Evansville in Indiana. ==Career==

In December 2006, Wesley was featured in David Hare's Broadway play The Vertical Hour.[tpl]cite news | url = http://www.nytimes.com/2008/11/02/arts/television/02roch.html | title = Flesh and Blood in a Town of Vampires | last = Rochlin | first = Margy | date = October 30, 2008 | work = The New York Times | accessdate = April 14, 2009[/tpl] In 2007, Wesley also appeared in The Public Theater production of In Darfur by playwright Winter Miller, co-starring Heather Raffo, Aaron Lohr among others.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.playbill.com/news/article/107238.html | title = Public's in Darfur to Star Raffo, Lohr, Washington, Wesley, Jah | last = Pincus-Roth | first = Zachary | date = April 11, 2007 | publisher = Playbill | accessdate = April 14, 2009[/tpl]
Wesley had a minor role in 2005 film Hitch, which was edited in the final cut.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.lvrj.com/living/14688097.html|title=Las Vegas Academy grad a shooting star | last = Cling | first = Carol | date = January 29, 2008 | publisher = Las Vegas Review Journal | accessdate = April 14, 2009[/tpl] However, she made her on screen debut in the lead role in 2007 film How She Move, from British director Ian Iqbal Rashid. The character Raya Green, who enters a step dancing competition to secure funds for her education, was inspired by Tony Manero, portrayed by John Travolta in Saturday Night Fever.[tpl]cite news | url = http://www.thestar.com/Article/296879 | title = 'How She Move' shows dance as escape | last = Marchand | first = Philip | date = January 24, 2008 | work = TheStar.com | publisher = Toronto Star Inc. | accessdate = April 14, 2009[/tpl] Prior to the shooting of the film, Wesley underwent a five-week dance rehearsal period. Portraying a woman of Jamaican descent, she also took dialect coaching for the role.[tpl]cite web | url = http://newsblaze.com/story/20080120151550tsop.nb/topstory.html | title = Rutina Wesley "How She Move" Interview with Kam Williams | last = Williams | first = Kam | date = January 20, 2008 | publisher = News Blaze | accessdate = April 14, 2009[/tpl] Wesley auditioned for the role Tara Thornton in the HBO series True Blood in 2007 and secured the part after creator Alan Ball chose her because "she was the first person who showed Tara's vulnerable side".
In June 2011, it was reported that Wesley will star in the biopic Left To Tell based on Immaculée Ilibagiza's bestselling memoir about the Rwandan Genocide.[tpl]cite news | url = http://www.deadline.com/2011/06/true-bloods-rutina-wesley-lands-lead-in-harrowing-rwanda-tale-left-to-tell/ | title = True Blood's Rutina Wesley Lands Lead in Harrowing Rwanda Tale 'Left To Tell' | last = Fleming | first = Mike | date = June 9, 2011 | work = Deadline.com | accessdate = June 9, 2011[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Wesley divides her time between Los Angeles and Astoria, Queens. In 2005 she married her former Juilliard classmate Jacob Fishel, an actor. Wesley filed for divorce on August 16, 2013 citing irreconcilable differences.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.people.com/people/article/0,,20727237,00.html | title = Rutina Wesley Divorcing Jacob Fishel | publisher = People.com | date = August 20, 2013[/tpl]
