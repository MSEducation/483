[[Ray Dowker]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, 2004 deaths, People from Christchurch, New Zealand cricketers, Canterbury cricketers

Raymond Thomas Dowker (6 July 1919 – 17 December 2004) was a New Zealand cricketer who played first class cricket for Canterbury between 1949 and 1957. A right-handed batsman, he played 24 games – scoring 1,147 runs at 30.18 including one century, 122 against Auckland on 7 January 1955.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/21/21404.html|title=Auckland v Canterbury - Plunket Shield 1954/55|publisher=Cricket Archive|accessdate=2009-10-17[/tpl] Born in New Brighton, in Christchurch, Canterbury, New Zealand, he was New Zealand Cricket Almanack Player of the Year for 1955.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/21/21792/21792.html|title=Player Profile: Ray Dowker|publisher=Cricket Archive|accessdate=2009-10-17[/tpl]
Ray was also a successful Football player and won the 1945 Chatham Cup [tpl]cite web|url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1945_Chatham_Cup | title=1945 Chatham Cup | publisher=Wikipedia | accessdate=13 November 2013[/tpl] with Western AFC.[tpl]cite web | url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_A.F.C. | title=Western A.F.C | publisher=Wikipedia | accessdate=13 November 2013[/tpl] Ray went on to represent Canterbury at Football in 1948.
Ray's grandsons have also proved to be successful in the New Zealand sporting arena. Sons to daughter Catherine Wheeler (nee Dowker) Joe Wheeler [tpl]cite web | url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Wheeler_(rugby_union) | title=Joe Wheeler - wikipedia | publisher=Wikipedia | accessdate=13 November 2013[/tpl] and Ben Wheeler [tpl]cite web | url=http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ben_Wheeler_(cricketer) | title=Ben Wheeler - wikipedia | publisher=Wikipedia | accessdate=13 November 2013[/tpl] have played Rugby and Cricket respectively for regional and national teams.
