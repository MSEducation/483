[[Steve McCaffery]]

CATEGORIES: 1947 births, Living people, 20th-century Canadian poets, People from Sheffield, Language poets, York University faculty, University at Buffalo faculty, 21st-century Canadian poets

Steven McCaffery (born January 24, 1947) is a Canadian poet and scholar who was a professor at York University.  He currently holds the Gray Chair at the University at Buffalo, The State University of New York.100 Canadian Poets - Steve McCaffery - Profile  McCaffery was born in Sheffield, England and lived in the  UK for most of his youth attending University of Hull.  He moved to Toronto in 1968. In 1970, he began to collaborate with fellow poets Rafael Barreto-Rivera, Paul Dutton, and bpNichol, forming the sound-poetry group,  The Four Horsemen. McCaffery's poetry attempts to break language from the logic of syntax and structure to create a purely emotional response.::Arc Poetry::How Poems Work::David Seymour on Steven McCaffery's "Position of Sheep I"::  He has created three-dimensional structures of words and has released a number of sound and video works, often in collaboration with other poets.Project MUSE
