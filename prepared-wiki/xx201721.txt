Keirrison de Souza Carneiro (born 3 December 1988 in Dourados),[tpl]cite web | title = Keirrison | url=http://futpedia.globo.com/jogadores/keirrison| accessdate = 2009-04-15 | publisher=Futpédia|language=Portuguese[/tpl] commonly known as Keirrison, is a Brazilian footballer, currently playing as a striker for Coritiba, on loan from Barcelona.

==Club career==

===Cortiba===

Keirrison became a regular in Coritiba's main squad in 2007, helping the club win the Campeonato Brasileiro Série B and being promoted to the following year's Campeonato Brasileiro Série A.[tpl]cite web | title = Antes alvo palmeirense, Keirrison vira "mais um" no Coxa | url=http://www.correioweb.com.br/futebol2008/noticias.htm?id=4149| accessdate = 2008-08-29 | date = 15 August 2008 | publisher=Correio Braziliense|language=Portuguese[/tpl] He was the club's top goal scorer in that competition, with 12 goals.[tpl]cite web | title = Série B | url=http://esportes.terra.com.br/futebol/brasileiro2007/interna/0,,OI1556558-EI8818,00.html| accessdate = 2008-08-29 | date = 12 May 2008 | publisher=Terra|language=Portuguese[/tpl] He was the top goal scorer of the Campeonato Paranaense in 2008, with 18 goals. He played his first Campeonato Brasileiro Série A game on 11 May 2008, when his club beat Palmeiras 2–0, but he got injured in that game.[tpl]cite web | title = Keirrison se machuca, mas Coritiba supera Palmeiras | url=http://esportes.terra.com.br/futebol/brasileiro/2008/interna/0,,OI2880351-EI11421,00.html| accessdate = 2008-08-29 | date = 11 May 2008 | publisher=Terra|language=Portuguese[/tpl] He scored his first goal for the club on 9 July 2008 in a 4–0 win over Portuguesa. His first goal came less than a month later in a 0–0 win over Santos on 3 August 2003. Keirrison finished as the Campeonato Brasileiro Série A 2008 top scorer, with 23 goals, tied with Washington of Fluminense and Kléber Pereira of Santos, being the youngest top scorer in the history of Brazil.[tpl]cite web | title = Washington, Keirrison e Kléber Pereira são os artilheiros do Brasileiro com 21 gols | url=http://oglobo.globo.com/esportes/brasileiro2008/mat/2008/12/07/washington_keirrison_kleber_pereira_sao_os_artilheiros_do_brasileiro_com_21_gols-586887786.asp| accessdate = 2008-12-08 | date = 7 December 2008 | publisher=O Globo|language=Portuguese[/tpl]

===Palmeiras===

Keirrison joined Palmeiras in January 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sport.co.uk/news/Football/12959/Benitez_targets_Keirrison_as_Torres_partner_.aspx|title=Benitez targets Keirrison as Torres partner|last=Brown|first=Nigel|date=20 January 2009|work=sport.co.uk|publisher=Media Corporation plc|accessdate=2009-01-30[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/82/south-america/2009/01/15/1062517/keirrison-completes-high-profile-move-to-palmeiras|title=Keirrison Completes High-Profile Move To Palmeiras|last=Sica|first=Gregory|date=15 January 2009|publisher=Goal.com|accessdate=2009-01-30[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thesun.co.uk/sol/homepage/sport/football/article2165692.ece|title=Keirrison not on Reds' radar|last=Haigh|first=Andrew|date=23 January 2009|work=The Sun|publisher=News International|accessdate=2009-01-30[/tpl] Keirrison made his debut for Palmeiras on 24 January 2009 against Mogi Mirim, scoring twice in the 3–0 win.[tpl]cite web|url=http://soccernet-assets.espn.go.com/match?id=259837&cc=5739|title=Palmeiras 3–0 Mogi Mirim EC|work=ESPNsoccernet|publisher=ESPN|accessdate=2009-01-30[/tpl] then twice in the preliminary round of the Copa Libertadores as Palmeiras beat Real Potosí 1–1 on 29 January 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/football/feedarticle/8334209|title=Luxemburgo's Palmeiras rout Potosi in Libertadores|last=Homewood|first=Brian|date=30 January 2009|work=The Guardian|publisher=Guardian Media Group|accessdate=2009-01-30[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] On 26 June, it said on the Palmeiras Official Website that FC Barcelona had made a proposed offer for the striker. Due to this Keirrison would not be taking part in Palmeiras next game.[tpl]citation needed|date=December 2011[/tpl]

===Barcelona===

On 23 July 2009, FC Barcelona announced on their website that they had agreed to terms with Brazilian club Palmeiras to bring Keirrison to the Camp Nou. The transfer for the Brazilian striker was worth €14 million plus a bonus up to €2 million depending on the number of matches played with the first team. Keirrison signed on a five-year contract.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada09-10/07/n090723107695.html|title=Keirrison signs a five-year deal|date=23 July 2009|accessdate=2009-07-23|publisher=FC Barcelona[/tpl] On arrival at the Spanish club Keirrison expressed his adulation he has for his new club and was quoted as saying in Barcelona's official website “I hope to break into Pep Guardiola's side, and join many other famous Brazilians who have played here." Further he said, "I always followed Barcelona and the Brazilian players who played here. Now at 20-years old, I am very happy to be here, fulfilling my dream of playing at the best club in the world."[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/12/spain/2009/07/23/1399960/keirrison-barcelona-are-the-best-club-in-the-world|title=Keirrison: Barcelona Are The Best Club In The World|date=23 July 2009|work=Goal.com|accessdate=2009-07-23[/tpl] Soon after Barcelona coach Pep Guardiola stated that "in principle he'll go out on loan". Many teams throughout Europe were linked with Keirrison after they heard of Barcelona's plans of loaning the Brazilian out to get used to European football. Teams such as Roma, Monaco, and Ajax were keen on taking the Brazilian on loan.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/en-us/news/12/spain/2009/07/25/1403701/monaco-join-loan-race-for-barcelonas-keirrison-report|title=Monaco Join Loan Race For Barcelona's Keirrison|date=25 July 2009|work=Goal.com|accessdate=2009-07-25[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]

====Loan to Benfica====

On 28 July 2009 it was announced that Portuguese side Benfica agreed on terms to take the Brazilian on loan.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.slbenfica.pt/Informacao/Futebol/Noticias/noticiasfutebol_futreforcokeirrison_280709_49963.asp|title=Keirrison reforça linha avançada do Benfica|date=28 July 2009|accessdate=2009-07-28|publisher=SL Benfica|language=Portuguese[/tpl] Keirrison scored his first goal for Benfica in a friendly against Celtic.[tpl]cite news|url=http://fcbtransfers.blogspot.com/search?q=keirrison|date=3 September 2009|accessdate=2009-09-03|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl] With the abundance of strikers in form at Benfica Keirrison had found it hard to find a place in the first team. To some Keirrison was behind Óscar Cardozo, Nuno Gomes and Javier Saviola in the pecking order at the Estádio da Luz.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/12/spain/2009/10/11/1553890/barcelona-looking-to-extend-keirrisons-benfica-loan|title=Barcelona Looking To Extend Keirrison's Benfica Loan|date=11 October 2009|accessdate=2009-10-11|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl] While in Portugal, Keirrison started one game for Benfica and played a total of just 199 minutes. Benfica manager Jorge Jesus even went as far as to say that he did not plan to feature Keirrison for the rest of the season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/12/spain/2010/01/29/1767132/barcelonas-keirrison-does-not-figure-in-benfica-plans|title=Barcelona's Keirrison Does Not Figure in Benfica Plans|date=29 January 2010|accessdate=2010-01-31|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl]
He became Portuguese Champion, thanks to the 5 league games he made for Benfica.

====Loan to Fiorentina====

On 31 January 2010 Italian side Fiorentina announced on their website that they had come to terms with FC Barcelona to take the Brazilian on loan until the end of the 2011-12 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://it.violachannel.tv/dettaglio-news/items/accordo-per-keirisson.html|title=Accordo per Keirisson|date=31 January 2010|accessdate=2010-01-31|publisher=violachannel.tv[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/10/italy/2010/01/31/1769763/official-fiorentina-sign-barcelona-striker-keirrison-on-loan|title=OFFICIAL: Fiorentina Sign Barcelona Striker Keirrison On Loan|date=31 January 2010|accessdate=2010-01-31|publisher=goal.com[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.totalbarca.com/2010/news/official-keirrison-moves-to-fiorentina-on-loan/|title=Official: Keirrison moves to Fiorentina on loan|date=2 February 2010|accessdate=2010-02-02|publisher=totalbarca.com[/tpl] Barcelona also granted La viola an option to buy the player for €14 million.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.cat/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada09-10/02/01/n100201109085.html|title=Keirrison moves to Fiorentina on loan|date=1 February 2010|accessdate=2010-02-03|publisher=FC Barcelona[/tpl] On 7 February 2010, just a week after being signed, Keirrison made his official debut for La Viola coming on as a substitute for Manuel Pasqual in the 84th minute in a 1–0 loss against Roma.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/37249/fiorentina-vs-roma/play-by-play|title=Fiorentina vs Roma|date=7 February 2010|accessdate=2010-02-07|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl] On 21 February 2010 Keirrison came on as a substitute for Marco Marchionni in the 76th minute in a match against Livorno, Fiorentina went on to win that game by a score of 2–1.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/match/37265/fiorentina-vs-livorno/lineup-stats|title=Fiorentina vs Livorno|date=19 February 2010|accessdate=2010-02-19|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl] On 28 February Keirrison replaced Adem Ljajić in the 46th minute against S.S. Lazio and he later went on to score the equalizer in the 92nd minute marking his first goal for ACF Fiorentina.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/10/italy/2010/02/27/1810217/lazio-1-1-fiorentina-keirrisons-late-goal-salvages-point-for|title=Lazio 1–1 Fiorentina: Keirrison's Late Goal Salvages Point For Viola|date=27 February 2010|accessdate=2010-02-27|publisher=goal.com[/tpl]

====Return to Brazil====

On 10 July 2010 Keirrison's loan with Fiorentina was ended pre-maturely.[tpl]cite news|url=http://it.violachannel.tv/dettaglio-ultimora/items/risolto-il-prstito-di-keirrison.html|title=Risolto il prestito di Keirrison|date=10 July 2010|accessdate=12 July 2010|work=ACF Fiorentina|publisher=Viola Channel|language=Italian[/tpl] and on 12 July, he was loaned to Brazilian side Santos.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.com/web/english/noticies/futbol/temporada10-11/07/12/n100709111957.html|title=Keirrison to Santos on loan|work=FC Barcelona|date=12 July 2010|accessdate=12 July 2010[/tpl]
In Santos, Keirrison won the 2011 Copa Libertadores, but as a reserve. In August 2011 he was loaned to Cruzeiro EC until the end of 2011 Brazilian season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.goal.com/en/news/11/transfer-zone/2011/08/24/2634011/official-barcelona-loan-out-keirrison-to-cruzeiro-until-the|title=Official: Barcelona loan out Keirrison to Cruzeiro until the end of 2011|date=24 August 2011|accessdate=31 July 2012|publisher=Goal.com[/tpl]
On March 2012, Keirrison was loaned to his original club Coritiba, until the end of the 2013/2014 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.fcbarcelona.com/football/first-team/detail/article/keirrison-loaned-to-coritiba-fc-for-two-seasons|title=Keirrison loaned to Coritiba FC for two seasons|date=22 March 2012|accessdate=31 July 2012|publisher=FC Barcelona official website[/tpl]

==Personal life==

He was born in Dourados, Mato Grosso do Sul as son of Operário (MS) footballer Adir Carneiro and Alzira Rosa de Souza. His name was chosen by his father, as a tribute to rock musicians Jim Morrison from The Doors and Keith Richards from The Rolling Stones, and also an admiration for the letter K.[tpl]cite web | title = Keirrison: nome do artilheiro tem origem em lenda do rock | url=http://globoesporte.globo.com/Esportes/Noticias/Times/Coritiba/0,,MUL463662-15014,00-KEIRRISON+NOME+DO+ARTILHEIRO+TEM+ORIGEM+EM+LENDA+DO+ROCK.html| accessdate = 2008-08-29 | date = 12 May 2008 | publisher=Globo Esporte|language=Portuguese[/tpl] Keirrison and his father bought a football club from Campo Grande, the capital of Mato Grosso do Sul in April 2008, and renamed it to Centro de Futebol Keirrison, shortened as CFK9.[tpl]cite journal |title=Cartola Júnior |journal=Placar |issue=1322 |date=September 2008 |pages=24 [/tpl]
