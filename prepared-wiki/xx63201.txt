</ref> Companies listed on the Iceland Stock Exchange have also since been merged into the list. OMX also launched a pan-regional benchmark index, the [[OMX Nordic 40]], on the same date, however the individual exchanges have also retained their own national benchmark indices.

===NASDAQ takeover===

On 25 May 2007, NASDAQ agreed to buy OMX for US$3.7 billion.[tpl]cite news |title= Nasdaq bids $3.7bn for Nordic OMX |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/business/6690419.stm |work=BBC News |date=2007-05-25 |accessdate=2008-02-29[/tpl] In August 2007, however, Borse Dubai offered US$4 billion, prompting speculation of a bidding war.[tpl]cite news |title= Dubai in $4bn bid for Nordic OMX |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/business/6951878.stm |work=BBC News |date=2007-08-17 |accessdate=2008-02-29[/tpl] On 20 September 2007, Borse Dubai agreed to stop competing to buy OMX in return for a 20% stake and 5 percent of votes in NASDAQ as well as NASDAQ's then 28% stake in the London Stock Exchange.[tpl]cite news |last=Magnusson |first=Niklas |coauthors=McSheehy, Will |title= Dubai to Buy Stakes in Nasdaq, LSE; Strikes OMX Deal |url= http://www.bloomberg.com/apps/news?pid=20601087&sid=aqzGAS7oAezg |work= Bloomberg |date=2007-09-20 |accessdate=2008-02-29[/tpl] In a complex transaction, Borse Dubai acquired 97.2% of OMX's outstanding shares before selling them on to NASDAQ.[tpl]cite news |title= Borse Dubai completes planned sale of OMX to Nasdaq |url= http://www.iht.com/articles/ap/2008/02/27/business/EU-FIN-Sweden-Borse-Dubai-OMX.php |work= Associated Press |publisher= International Herald Tribune |date=2007-09-27 |accessdate=2008-02-29[/tpl] The newly merged company was renamed the NASDAQ OMX Group upon completion of the deal on 27 February 2008.
