[[Richard Raymond-Barker]]

CATEGORIES: 1894 births, 1918 deaths, Royal Flying Corps officers, British military personnel killed in World War I, Aviators killed by being shot down, Recipients of the Military Cross, British World War I flying aces

Major Richard Raymond-Barker was a World War I flying ace credited with six aerial victories. He became Manfred von Richthofen's penultimate victim.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/raymond-barker.php Retrieved 19 February 2010.

==Early service==

Raymond-Barker was commissioned on 30 November 1914. he transferred to the Royal Flying Corps on 6 August 1915. He completed pilot's training in October, and was forwarded to France on 22 November 1915.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 313 [/tpl] On 1 April 1916, he was appointed a Temporary Lieutenant with the RFC.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/29579/supplements/4812 Retrieved 19 February 2010. On 17 June 1916, Raymond-Barker was appointed a flight commander; this entailed a promotion to  Temporary Captain from Flying Officer.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/29651/pages/6602 Retrieved 19 February 2010. During this time, he was serving with 6 and 16 Squadrons, transferring out on 29 December 1916.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 313 [/tpl]

==Victories==

On 12 May 1917, Raymond-Barker was sent to 48 Squadron as a flight commander. He scored his first victories on the 20th, driving down a pair of Albatros D.IIIs out of control. By 5 June, his count was up to four.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 313 [/tpl]http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/raymond-barker.php Retrieved 19 February 2010.  On 1 July 1917, he was appointed from Temporary Second Lieutenant to Temporary Lieutenant.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/30467/supplements/666 Retrieved 19 February 2010. He scored his final two triumphs just after that, on the 8th and 17th, bringing his total to two Albatros D.IIIs destroyed and four enemy aircraft driven down out of control.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |page= 313 [/tpl]http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/raymond-barker.php Retrieved 19 February 2010.

==Command and death==

On 17 September 1917, Raymond-Barker was appointed to be a Squadron Commander, with the rank of  Temporary Major.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/30395/supplements/12123 Retrieved 19 February 2010.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/30395/supplements/12124 Retrieved 19 February 2010. He took command of 3 Squadron. On 20 April 1918, it ran into a patrol of Fokker Triplanes of the Flying Circus, and Raymond-Barker was shot down by the Red Baron.[tpl]cite book |title= Above the Trenches: A Complete Record of the Fighter Aces and Units of the British Empire Air Forces 1915-1920 |pages= 313â€“314 [/tpl] 
As Richthofen reported it: "With six planes of Jasta 11, I attacked a large enemy squadron. During the fight I observed that a Triplane was attacked and shot at from below by a Camel. I put myself behind the adversary and brought him down, burning, with only a few shots. The enemy plane crashed down near the forest of Hamel where it burned further on the ground."http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/raymond-barker.php Retrieved 19 February 2010.
Raymond-Barker's body was never recovered.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/raymond-barker.php Retrieved 19 February 2010.

==Honours and awards==

Military Cross MC
2nd Lt. (T./Capt.) Richard Raymond-Barker, Gen. List and R.F.C.
For conspicuous gallantry and devotion to duty when leading a fighting patrol. He attacked
a large hostile formation, destroying two of them. He has also done excellent work in leading distant photographic reconnaissances, notably upon two occasions when his skilful leadership enabled photographs to be taken of all the required hostile area in spite of repeated attacks from enemy aircraft. He has helped to destroy seven hostile machines, and has at all times displayed conspicuous skill and gallantry.http://www.london-gazette.co.uk/issues/1917-09-01;1917-09-30/exact=raymond-barker/start=1  Retrieved 19 February 2010.
