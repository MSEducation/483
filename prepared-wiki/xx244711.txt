[[Riverside Methodist Church and Parsonage]]

CATEGORIES: Methodist churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1859, 19th-century Methodist church buildings, Churches in Dutchess County, New York

Riverside Methodist Church and Parsonage is a historic Methodist church and parsonage on Charles and Orchard Streets in Rhinecliff, Dutchess County in the U.S. state of New York. The church was built about 1859 and the parsonage about 1888.  The church is a small, two story, rectangular stone building in the Gothic Revival style. It features a steeply pitched gable roof covered in polychrome slate. It has an open-frame bell tower and is built into the side of a hill.  The parsonage is a two story, "T" shaped frame dwelling topped by a cross-gable roof. Also on the property is a contributing garage.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=9581|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration:Riverside Methodist Church and Parsonage|date=March 1987|accessdate=2011-01-08 |author=Nancy Todd, Neil Larson|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl]
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1987.
