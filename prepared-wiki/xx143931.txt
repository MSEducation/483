[[Ana Merino]]

CATEGORIES: 1971 births, Living people, Writers from Madrid, Dartmouth College faculty, Spanish poets, Spanish writers

Ana Merino (born 1971) is a Spanish poet.

==Biography==

Ana Merino was born in Madrid in 1971, daughter of José María Merino. Ana Merino was between 2004 and 2009 an Assistant Professor of Spanish at Dartmouth College.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dartmouth.edu/~spanport/people/merino.html|title=Ana Merino - Dartmouth Faculty Website|publisher=Dartmouth College|accessdate=23 January 2010[/tpl] Since Fall 2009 she is an Associate Professor of Spanish Creative Writing and Cultural Studies at The University of Iowa where she is the director of their MFA in Spanish Creative Writing. She has published a scholarly book on comics titled El Comic Hispánico (Cátedra, 2003) a critical monograph on Chris Ware (Sinsentido 2005), a youth novel "El hombre de los dos corazones" (Anaya, 2009)  and seven books of poetry, and was a recipient of the Adonais and Fray Luis de Leon awards for poetry. She was awarded the Diario de Avisos Award for best critical short articles about comics for the Spanish literary magazine Leer. Merino is a member of the Board of Directors for the Center for Cartoon Studies and a member of the executive committee of International Comic Art Forum. Merino's articles on comics have appeared in Leer, DDLV, The Comics Journal, International Journal of Comic Art, and Hispanic Issues. She has served as curator for three comics exhibitions and is the author of the bilingual catalogue Fantagraphics creadores del canon (2003)..
