[[Aloysius Society]]

CATEGORIES: Organisations associated with the University of Oxford, Clubs and societies of the University of Oxford, Philanthropic organisations based in the United Kingdom

The Aloysius Society is a philanthropic group of current, and former, Oxford University students dedicated to supporting those in need around the globe.[tpl]cite web|author=Sharon Forrester |url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/celebrity-photos/2011/02/04/marefat-dinner-connaught-hotel-london |title=The Marefat Dinner (Vogue.com UK) |publisher=Vogue.co.uk |date=4 February 2011 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] Founded by Lawrence Barclay and Francesca Lamarque in 2008, Aloysius Society members organise large-scale events to raise money and awareness for their chosen charities. 
Past events have included the Sugarplum Ball (2009), the Oxford Red Dress Couture Ball (2010), the Marefat Dinner (2011), and the Mindful Gala Dinner (2011).

==Events==

===The Sugarplum Ball===

The first event organised by the Aloysius Society was the Sugarplum Ball, held at the Natural History Museum on 30 November 2009 in aid of SOS Children's Villages,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tatler.com/bystander/events/2010/january/the-sugarplum-ball#/1973/image/1 |title=The Sugarplum Ball |publisher=Tatler |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] an organisation that provides family-based, long-term care for children who can no longer remain with their biological families.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hellomagazine.com/celebrities-news-in-pics/30-11-2009/52741/general/ |title=Strong bond between Sarah Ferguson and Bea evident | Latest celebrity news |work=Hello!  |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] The Sugarplum Ball was attended by guests such as Sarah, Duchess of York, Princess Beatrice of York, star of the Narnia films Anna Popplewell[tpl]cite web|url=http://londonersdiary.standard.co.uk/2009/11/beatrice-is-the-belle-of-the-ball.html |title=Londoner's Diary | Evening Standard |publisher=Londonersdiary.standard.co.uk |date=30 November 2009 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] and Sir Rocco Forte.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-1231858/Princess-Beatrice-Duchess-York-dodge-torrential-rain-charity-ball.html |title=Princess Beatrice and the Duchess of York dodge torrential rain at charity ball | Mail Online |work=Daily Mail |location=UK |date=30 November 2009 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] Michelin-starred chef Tom Aikens designed the menu, Tracey Emin designed the invitations, and auction prizes included a picture of Kate Moss's bottom, owned by her. The ball's sponsors included Ivan the Terrible, and Martell.

On 18 June 2010, The Oxford Red Dress Couture Ball was a three-part event organised by the Aloysius Society[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bellanaija.com/2010/05/10/christiane-king-to-participate-at-the-oxford-red-dress-couture-ball-along-with-john-galliano-marchesa-herve-leger-and-a-line-up-of-the-industrys-greats/ |title=Christiane King to participate at the Oxford Red Dress Couture Ball along with John Galliano, Marchesa & Max Azria |publisher=Bella Naija |date=10 May 2010 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] in aid of H.E.L.P. charity. ===The Marefat Dinner===

The Society organised the Marefat Dinner with Lord Speaker Baroness D'Souza and journalist Christa D'Souza, at The Connaught on 3 February 2011. The dinner was in aid of the Marefat High School in West Kabul, Afghanistan, which champions education in one of Kabul's most devastated districts. The dinner was attended by guests including Roland Mouret, Alice Temperley, Jemima Khan, Fatima Bhutto, Edie Campbell, Vogue editor Alexandra Shulman, Tatler editor Kate Reardon, Laura Bailey, Cherie Blair,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/celebrity-photos/2011/02/04/marefat-dinner-connaught-hotel-london/gallery#/image/6 |title=The Marefat Dinner (Vogue.com UK) |publisher=Vogue.co.uk |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] Anya Hindmarch, Jeremy Clarkson,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.livelikeavip.com/2011/02/07/london-last-night-the-marefat-dinner/ |title=London Last Night: The Marefat Dinner | Zoe Griffin |publisher=Live Like A VIP |date=30 June 2010 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] Mariella Frostrup, AA Gill,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/celebrity-photos/2011/02/04/marefat-dinner-connaught-hotel-london/gallery#/image/9 |title=The Marefat Dinner (Vogue.com UK) |publisher=Vogue.co.uk |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] and Simon Cowell's Afghan fiancée Mezhgan Hussainy.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vogue.co.uk/spy/celebrity-photos/2011/02/04/marefat-dinner-connaught-hotel-london/gallery#/image/8 |title=The Marefat Dinner (Vogue.com UK) |publisher=Vogue.co.uk |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] The auction included prize experiences donated by Roland Mouret, Yotam Ottolenghi and the Dorchester Collection, with Jeremy Clarkson offering an impromptu driving lesson that fetched £19,000. An Afghan-inspired feast was designed by two-Michelin-starred chef Hélène Darroze, and drinks were sponsored by Moët & Chandon.

===The Mindful Gala Dinner===

On 21 September 2011, the Aloysius Society organised the Mindful Gala Dinner with Mind to launch the Mindful of Art exhibition curated by artist Stuart Semple.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mind.org.uk/news/5696_stephen_fry_takes_over_as_president_of_mental_health_charity_mind |title=Stephen Fry takes over as President of mental health charity |publisher=Mind |date=22 September 2011 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] The exhibition, held in the Old Vic Tunnels, included artworks from world-renowned artists such as Tracey Emin, Jake and Dinos Chapman, Sarah Lucas, Mat Collishaw and the late Sebastian Horsley. The dinner took place at the Imperial War Museum and was hosted by Lord Melvyn Bragg and Stephen Fry, who officially took up his role as President of the mental health charity during the evening.
Prizes auctioned off on the night by Sotheby's auctioneer Lord Dalmeny included a day in the recording studio with The Kooks.[tpl]cite web|author=Alice Capiaghi |url=http://www.vogue.it/en/people-are-talking-about/from-london/2011/09/lady-diana-stuart-semple |title=Lady D Underground |publisher=Vogue.it |date=28 June 2011 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] The menu was designed by Adriano Cavagnini, Executive Chef of the Four Seasons London,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.stuartsemple.com/blog/?p=501 |title=Chapman Brothers, Stephen Fry and Stuart Semple raise thousands for Mind in a [tpl]Sic|nolink=y|laba|rinth|expected=labyrinth[/tpl] under Waterloo | Stuart Semple – News |publisher=Stuartsemple.com |date=22 September 2011 |accessdate=17 October 2011[/tpl] and drinks were sponsored by Veuve Cliquot.
