[[Richard Fitz Turold]]

CATEGORIES: Anglo-Normans, Normans, People in Cornish history, 11th-century people

Richard fitz Turold (Thorold) was an Anglo-Norman landowner of the eleventh century, mentioned in the Domesday Survey.
He had a castle at Cardinham in Cornwall,Flying Past - The Historic Environment of Cornwall: The First Farms where he was a major tenant  and steward of Robert of Mortain. The holding included the manor of Penhallam.Pastscape - Detailed Result: PENHALLAM MANOR

==Family==

His son was  William Fitz Richard of Cardinham.
