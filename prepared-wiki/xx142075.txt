[[Rushton railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Disused railway stations in Staffordshire, Railway stations closed in 1960, Railway stations opened in 1849, Former North Staffordshire Railway stations

Rushton railway station was a railway station that served the village of Rushton Spencer, Staffordshire. The station was opened by the North Staffordshire Railway in 1849 as part of the Churnet Valley line.[tpl]sfn|Jeuda|1999|p=55[/tpl]
It remained open until passenger services were withdrawn from the northern end of the Churnet valley line ([tpl]stnlnk|North Rode[/tpl] â€“ [tpl]stnlnk|Leek[/tpl]) in 1960.[tpl]sfn|Christiansen|Miller|1971|p=304[/tpl]  Freight services lasted until 1964 when they too were withdrawn[tpl]sfn|Christiansen|Miller|1971|p=304[/tpl] and the track lifted.  Today the trackbed forms part of the Staffordshire Way.
