[[Ring of integers]]

CATEGORIES: Ring theory, Algebraic number theory

The ring [tpl]math|Z[/tpl] is the simplest possible ring of integers.The ring of integers, without specifying the field, refers to the ring [tpl]math|Z[/tpl] of "ordinary" integers, the prototypical object for all those rings. It is a consequence of the ambiguity of the word "integer" in abstract algebra. Namely, [tpl]math|size=120%|1=Z = OQ[/tpl] where [tpl]math|Q[/tpl] is the field of rational numbers. And indeed, in algebraic number theory the elements of [tpl]math|Z[/tpl] are often called the "rational integers" because of this.
The ring of integers  of a number field is the unique maximal order in the field.

==Properties==

The ring of integers [tpl]math|size=120%|OK[/tpl] is a [tpl]math|Z[/tpl]-module. Indeed it is a free [tpl]math|Z[/tpl]-module, and thus has an integral basis, that is a basis [tpl]math|size=120%|b1, … ,bn ∈ OK[/tpl] of the [tpl]math|Q[/tpl]-vector space [tpl]mvar|K[/tpl] such that each element [tpl]mvar|x[/tpl] in [tpl]math|size=120%|OK[/tpl] can be uniquely represented as
with [tpl]math|size=120%|ai ∈ Z[/tpl].  The rank [tpl]mvar|n[/tpl] of [tpl]math|size=120%|OK[/tpl] as a free [tpl]math|Z[/tpl]-module is equal to the degree of [tpl]mvar|K[/tpl] over [tpl]math|Q[/tpl].
The rings of integers in number ﬁelds are Dedekind domains.

==Examples==

If [tpl]mvar|p[/tpl] is a prime, ζ is a [tpl]mvar|p[/tpl]th root of unity and [tpl]math|size=120%|1=K = Q(ζ)[/tpl] is the corresponding cyclotomic field, then an integral basis of [tpl]math|size=120%|1=OK = Zζ[/tpl] is given by [tpl]math|size=120%|(1, ζ, ζ2, … , ζp−2)[/tpl].
If [tpl]mvar|d[/tpl] is a square-free integer and [tpl]math|size=120%|1=K = Q([tpl]sqrt|d[/tpl])[/tpl] is the corresponding quadratic field, then [tpl]math|size=120%|OK[/tpl] is a ring of quadratic integers and its integral basis is given by [tpl]math|size=120%|(1, (1 + [tpl]sqrt|d[/tpl])/2)[/tpl] if [tpl]math|d ≡ 1 (mod 4)[/tpl] and by [tpl]math|size=120%|(1, [tpl]sqrt|d[/tpl])[/tpl] if [tpl]math|d ≡ 2, 3 (mod 4)[/tpl].

==Generalization==

One defines the ring of integers of a non-archimedean local field F as the set of all elements of F with absolute value ≤1; this is a ring because of the strong triangle inequality. If F is the completion of an algebraic number field, its ring of integers is the completion of the latter's ring of integers.
For example, the [tpl]mvar|p[/tpl]-adic integers [tpl]math|size=120%|Zp[/tpl] are the ring of integers of the [tpl]mvar|p[/tpl]-adic numbers [tpl]math|size=120%|Qp [/tpl].
