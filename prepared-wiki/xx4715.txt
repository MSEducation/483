Iraq's dominant ethnic group are the Iraqi or Mesopotamian Arabs, who account for around [tpl]nowrap|three-quarters[/tpl] of the population. They are a fusion of old Mesopotamian, Arabian, Iranian, and other populations, who now speak [tpl]nowrap|Mesopotamian Arabic[/tpl].
Iraqi Arabs (including Palestinians) 75%, Kurds (including Yazidis) 17%, Turkmen 3%, Assyrians 2%, Persians 2%, Other 1% https://www.cia.gov/library/publications/the-world-factbook/geos/iz.html (Armenians, Circassians, Shabaks, Mandaeans).

==Languages==

Arabic is the official language of Iraq, and is spoken or understood by almost all the population. 
Kurdish is the largest minority language, and has regional language status in [tpl]nowrap|Iraqi Kurdistan[/tpl]. Aramaic, once spoken by the whole country, is now only spoken by the Assyrian minority.
Azerbaijani is spoken in pockets of northern Iraq, and Persian is spoken in pockets of southern Iraq. Numerous languages of the Caucasus are also spoken by minorities across the country.

==Religions==

Islam 97% (Shi'a 40%, Sunni 40%), Christianity, Mandaeism and other 3%.
