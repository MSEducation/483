[[Battle of Ergeme]]

CATEGORIES: Military history of Latvia, 16th century in Latvia, Military history of Estonia, 16th century in Estonia, Battles involving Russia, Battles involving the Livonian Order, Battles of the Livonian War, 1560 in Europe, Conflicts in 1560

The Battle of Ergeme (also Battle of  Ermes) ([tpl]lang-et|Härgmäe lahing[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-de|Schlacht bei Ermes[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-ru|сражение при Эрмесе[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-lv|Ērģemes kauja[/tpl]) was fought on 2 August 1560 in present-day Latvia (near Valga) as part of the Livonian War between the forces of Ivan IV of Russia and the Livonian Confederation. It was the last battle fought by the German knights in Livonia and an important Russian victory. The knights were defeated so thoroughly that the order had to be dissolved.
