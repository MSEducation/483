[[Brandon Jerwa]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1973 births

Brandon Jerwa is an American comic book writer and musician currently residing in Seattle, Washington. Jerwa is best known for his work on comic tie-ins for several prominent licensed properties, including Battlestar Galactica, Highlander, G.I. Joe and Army of Darkness.  Jerwa also collaborates frequently with fellow writer Eric S. Trautmann.
Jerwa is the lead singer and founder of Seattle-area band SD6,[tpl]cite news |title= COMIC WRITER BRANDON JERWA'S BAND SD6 SIGNS DEAL, RELEASES CD|newspaper= Comic Book Resources|date= December 12, 2006|url= http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=8839[/tpl] and was previously the lead singer and co-founder of the bands Omnibox[tpl]cite news |title= Seattle-based Brandon Jerwa leads Joe into the comic battlefield|author= Kurt Anthony Krug|newspaper= The Seattle Times|date= November 8, 2005|url= http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/entertainment/2002609952_jerwa08.html|accessdate= 2010-01-15[/tpl] and Relay.
Before he began his career in comics, Jerwa was a morning DJ at KRQT "Rocket 107" FM in Longview, Washington.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.tdn.com/lifestyles/article_4d472d13-d80b-5311-bb91-8a1a94822dcf.htm|title= Comedy shows hit local stages Saturday|author= Tom Paulu|date= November 12, 2009|publisher= TDN.com|accessdate=2010-01-16[/tpl]

==Biography==

Jerwa's comic book career began in 2003, when he submitted a script to Devil's Due Publishing that was accepted, and was eventually published in G.I. Joe: Frontline #11-14. Soon afterwards, he became the main writer on G.I. Joe: A Real American Hero and also wrote several G.I. Joe minseries.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.newsarama.com/comics/060918-Jerwa-Inferno.html|title= Embracing the Inferno: Talking With Brandon Jerwa|author= Rik Offenberger|date= June 20, 2009|publisher= Newsrama|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl]  
On May 18, 2006, Brandon was featured on episode #24 of the Game Show Network's I've Got a Secret;[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tv.com/ive-got-a-secret/show/65607/episode.html|title= I've Got A Secret Season 1 Episode Guide|author= TV Guide|date= May 18, 2006|publisher= tvguide.com|accessdate=2010-01-17[/tpl] his secret being that in honor of his work on the G.I. Joe comics, Hasbro used his face as the basis for the G.I. Joe action figure Dragonsky.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.yojoe.com/action/06/dragonsky2.shtml|title=YOJOE.COM Dragonsky[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title= King of Comics: Longview native is living his superhero dream|author= Tom Paulu|newspaper= TDN|date= March 22, 2007|url= http://www.tdn.com/lifestyles/article_357e023e-8a8f-5f89-af3d-475a8107d57b.html|accessdate=2010-01-17 [/tpl] 
In 2006 and 2007, Jerwa began writing for Dynamite Entertainment, working on the series Highlander,[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=7945|title= THERE CAN BE ONLY TWO: JERWA AND OEMING TALK "HIGHLANDER"|author= Chris Ullrich|date= August 28, 2006|publisher= Comic Book Resources|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl] cowriting the first four issues with Michael Avon Oeming before moving on to a prolific run on Battlestar Galactica.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.comicbookresources.com:8080/?page=article&id=10315|title= JERWA BACKTRACKS BATTLESTAR IN "SEASON ZERO"|author= Michael Patrick Sullivan|date= May 25, 2007|publisher= Comic Book Resources|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl]
On Free Comic Book Day 2007, Jerwa met several people affiliated with DC Comics, including Eric S. Trautmann and Greg Rucka.  Jerwa and Trautmann struck up a partnership and collaborated on a project called Wide Awake, which appeared first as a short story in Popgun Vol. 2, then as a Free Comic Book Day special,[tpl]cite web |url= http://popculturezoo.com/archives/2517|title= FCBD 2009: ‘Wide Awake’ Chapter 0|author= Joseph Dilworth, Jr.|date= May 1, 2009|publisher= Pop Culture Zoo|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl] and then later as a free webcomic.[tpl]cite web |url= http://popculturezoo.com/archives/2416|title= ECCC 09: Wacky Hi-Jinks With Brandon Jerwa And Eric Trautmann – With Exclusive Art!|author= Joseph Dilworth, Jr.|date= April 13, 2009|publisher= Comics Waiting Room|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url= http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=17865|title= NIGHT TERRORS: CREATORS TALK POPGUN'S "WIDE AWAKE"|author= Emmet Furey|date= August 27, 2008|publisher= Comic Book Resources|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl]  In 2009, the duo pitched an idea for a graphic novel to DC's imprint Vertigo, which was accepted, and soon after Jerwa was contacted about working with DC directly.
Jerwa's first work for DC Comics was in September 2009 as the writer of the Inferno co-feature in The Shield, a monthly series that introduced re-imagined versions of the Red Circle Comics superhero characters into the DC Universe.
In late 2009, Jerwa also wrote for a Dynamite series based on Stargate SG-1.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.comicbookresources.com/?page=article&id=22790 |title= JERWA & MURRAY JUMP THROUGH DYNAMITE'S "STARGATE"|author= Kiel Phegley|date= September 2, 2009|publisher= Comic Book Resources|accessdate=2010-01-15[/tpl]
In 2011 Jerwa raised $19,000 through the crowdfunding website Kickstarter for a comic book-themed documentary title  UNTOLD TALES OF THE COMIC INDUSTRY[tpl]cite web |url= https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arcmedia/untold-tales-of-the-comic-industry"|title=UNTOLD TALES OF THE COMIC INDUSTRY"[/tpl]  News of the film's progress was last updated in March 2013.https://www.kickstarter.com/projects/arcmedia/untold-tales-of-the-comic-industry/posts/419825
