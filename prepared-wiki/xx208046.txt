[[Peder Jensen Fauchald]]

CATEGORIES: 1791 births, 1856 deaths, Members of the Parliament of Norway, Mayors of places in Oppland

Peder Jensen Fauchald (15 November 1791 – 1 July 1856) was a Norwegian politician.
He was elected to the Norwegian Parliament in 1830, 1833, 1836, 1839, 1842 1845, 1848 and 1851, representing the rural constituency of Christians Amt (today named Oppland).Peder Jensen Fauchald — Norwegian Social Science Data Services (NSD)
He was born in Østre Toten. He advocated freedom of religion in general and especially the right of Jews to enter Norway (which was granted in 1851). Locally he became mayor of Østre Toten municipality in 1837, was appointed county auditor, and founded the local savings bank.Wergelands gode venn Peder Fauchald - National Archival Services of Norway [tpl]no icon[/tpl] He sat as mayor from 1837 to 1847 and 1850 to 1856, being succeeded by Ole Larsen Hammerstad.Mayors of municipalities west of Lake Mjøsa He worked as a farmer.
