[[Loch of Kirbister]]

CATEGORIES: Orkney, Lochs of Scottish islands, Freshwater lochs of Scotland

The Loch of Kirbister  is a small, shallow, somewhat triangular shaped loch on Mainland Orkney, Scotland, in the parish of Orphir. It lies [tpl]convert|5|mi|km|abbr=on[/tpl] south west of Kirkwall on cultivated land between two hills. There is a small ([tpl]convert|37|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] by [tpl]convert|19|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl]) turf covered islet known as the Groundwater of Holm just off the eastern shore of the loch.  It shows traces in stone of an oval structure and small projecting pier.[tpl]cite web |url=http://canmore.rcahms.gov.uk/en/site/1952/details/loch+of+kirbister/|title=Loch of Kirbister - Groundwater of Holm|author= |date=|website=http://canmore.rcahms.gov.uk/|publisher=RCAHMS|accessdate=2013-08-11[/tpl]  The loch is popular for trout fishing and the The Orkney Trout Fishing Association has a hatchery at the Kirbister pumphouse [tpl]cite web |url=http://www.orkneytroutfishing.co.uk/hatchery/hatchery.html|title=New trout hatchery at Kirbister|author= |date=|website=http://www.orkneytroutfishing.co.uk/|publisher=OTFA|accessdate=2013-08-11[/tpl] on the loch edge.
Mill Burn, the southerly outflow from the loch, was used to power the 18th century Kirbister Mill.[tpl]cite web |url=http://canmore.rcahms.gov.uk/en/site/1946/details/kirbister+mill/|title=Kirbister Mill|author= |date=|website=http://canmore.rcahms.gov.uk/|publisher=RCAHMS|accessdate=2013-08-11[/tpl]
The loch was surveyed in 1903 by T.N. Johnston and R.C. Marshall and later charted [tpl]cite web|url=http://maps.nls.uk/bathymetric/view/index.cfm?sid=74400508 |title=Muckle & Peerie Waters; Loch of Isbister; Loch of Sabiston; Loch of Kirbister; Loch of Tankerness (Vol. 6, Plates 91 & 92) - Bathymetrical Survey, 1897-1909 - National Library of Scotland |publisher=maps.nls.uk |date= |accessdate=2013-08-11[/tpl] as part of The Bathymetrical Survey of Fresh-Water Lochs of Scotland 1897-1909
.
