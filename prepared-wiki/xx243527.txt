[[Sudeten Creche]]

CATEGORIES: Synthpop groups, British pop music groups

Sudeten Creche was formed in 1980 as a semi Warholian experiment by Yvette Döll. The original line up featured Yvette Döll and Paul Carlin, working with associate band members over the years. The band was largely known for the minimal electronic tracks “Are Kisses out of Fashion?” and “Dance”, which featured on their first release called “Europe in the Year Zero”.S/Phonograph Ref' SPH1 This EP also included the Yazoo track “Goodbye ‘70’s” and is the first official release by Yazoo and preceded their Album Upstairs at Eric's. Also featured on the EP were Colour Me Pop, formerly known as Watch with Mother. 
Sudeten Creche’s second release was the EP “Kindergarten”Illuminated Ref' ILL 1712 in 1983 through Illuminated Records. It included the tracks “Kindergarten”, “Dance” (Instrumental and extended mix) and “Asylums in Beirut”. 
A discography of Sudeten Creche's releases can be found on the Discogs web site. See external links below.
