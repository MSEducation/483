[[Stealey (microprocessor)]]

CATEGORIES: Intel x86 microprocessors

Stealey is the codename for a low-power x86 architecture microprocessor based on a Dothan core derived from the Intel Pentium M, built on a 90 nm process with 512 KB L2 cache and 400 MT/s front side bus (FSB). It was branded as Intel A100 and Intel A110 and appeared as part of the McCaslin platform. They were replaced in 2008 by the Menlow platform, including the 45 nm Silverthorne CPU and Poulsbo SCH.[tpl]Citation |title=Intel News Disclosures From Day 2 Of The Intel Developer Forum In Beijing |date=2007-04-18 |publisher=Intel |url=http://www.intel.com/pressroom/archive/releases/20070417supp.htm |accessdate=2008-01-25[/tpl]
The A110 runs at 800 MHz, the A100 at 600 MHz, and both have a TDP of 3 watts, and a power consumption in the lowest power state of only 0.4 watts.[tpl]Citation |title=Intel Processor A100 and A110 on 90 nm Process with 512-KB L2 Cache |publisher=Intel |url=http://download.intel.com/design/mobile/datashts/31690801.pdf |accessdate=2008-01-25[/tpl]
The A100 and A110 processors are part of the Intel Ultra Mobile Platform 2007[tpl]Citation |title=Intel Ultra Mobile Platform 2007 |publisher=Intel |url=http://www.intel.com/products/mid/ultramobile2007.htm |accessdate=2008-01-25[/tpl] and were designed to be used in MIDs, UMPCs and Ultralight laptops.
