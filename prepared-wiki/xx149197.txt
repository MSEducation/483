[[Mark Lockheart]]

CATEGORIES: 1961 births, British jazz composers, Jazz tenor saxophonists, British jazz soprano saxophonists, Living people

Mark Lockheart (born Lymington, 1961) is a British jazz tenor saxophonist who came to prominence as a member of the Loose Tubes big band during the 1980s.[tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart |publisher=All About Jazz | url =http://www.allaboutjazz.com/php/musician.php?id=15233 |date=2010-03-01 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl]
Following the demise of Loose Tubes, Mark went on to form the co-led eclectic jazz/folk quartet Perfect Houseplants with Huw Warren, Dudley Phillips & Martin France.Perfect Houseplants  The band released five albums including two with the early music group The Orlando Consort.  At this time Mark also formed his own group as leader "The Scratch Band", performing his own compositions and with this group recorded two albums including "Imaginary Dances".[tpl]cite web|title =Delicate essence |publisher=The Guardian| first=John |last=Fordham |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/lifeandstyle/2002/mar/22/shopping.artsfeatures6 |date=2002-03-22 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl] In 2005 Mark put together his "Big Idea" to record the album "Moving Air".[tpl]cite web|title =CD/LP Review: Moving Air |publisher=All About Jazz |first=John|last=Kelman| url =http://www.allaboutjazz.com/php/article.php?id=19298 |date=2005-10-09 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl]
2003 saw Mark join British jazz quintet Polar Bear[tpl]cite web|title =Jazz Preview: Polar Bear |publisher=The Guardian| first=John |last=Fordham |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2009/may/28/polar-bear-vortex-london |date=2009-05-30 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl] and the group have recorded 4 albums including Held On the Tips of Fingers which saw them nominated for the Mercury Award and which was selected as one of the '100 Jazz Albums That Shook The World' by Jazzwise magazine.
A project of more of Lockheart's own compositions was released in 2009 with the "In Deep" Quintet featuring Liam Noble and Jasper Hoiby [tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart: In Deep|publisher=The Guardian| first=John |last=Fordham |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2009/jun/19/mark-lockheart-in-deep |date=2009-06-19 |accessdate =2009-06-19[/tpl] and the subsequent year Mark was awarded Jazz Musician of the Year award at the All Party Parliamentary Jazz Awards.  More recently his compositions have been recorded with the NDR Big Band.[tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart: Days Like These |publisher=The Observer| first=Dave |last=Gelly |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2010/feb/14/mark-lockheart-days-like-these |date=2010-02-14 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart: Days Like These |publisher=The Guardian| first=John |last=Fordham |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2010/feb/19/mark-lockheart-days-like-these |date=2010-02-19 |accessdate =2010-05-22[/tpl]
'Ellington In Anticipation" was released to critical acclaim in 2013[tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart: Ellington In Anticipation |publisher=The Guardian| first=John |last=Fordham |url =http://www.guardian.co.uk/music/2013/feb/14/mark-lockheart-review |date=2013-02-14 |accessdate =2013-02-14[/tpl] and as a septet featuring Sebastian Rochford, Tom Herbert, Liam Noble, Margrit Hasler, James Allsopp and Finn Peters completed a UK wide tour[tpl]cite web|title =Mark Lockheart: Kings Place - Ellington In Anticipation |publisher=The Arts Desk| first=Peter|last=Quinn|url =http://www.theartsdesk.com/new-music/mark-lockheart-kings-place |date=2013-03-24 |accessdate =2013-03-24[/tpl] supported by the Arts Council of England.
Mark has also played in sessions with Radiohead, Prefab Sprout, Stereolab amongst many others.
Lockheart currently teaches at Trinity College of Music and the Royal Academy Of Music in London, and is a tutor for the National Youth Jazz Collective.
