Ephraim "Shakes" Mashaba (born 6 August 1950) is a South African former footballer who now works as a coach.
He was previously the manager of the Swaziland national side, where he was appointed in May 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/7412759.stm|title=Mashaba takes over Swaziland|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=21 May 2008|author=Mark Gleeson|publisher=BBC[/tpl] Mashaba has previously managed the South Africa national side, but was sacked from the team on the eve of the 2004 African Cup of Nations in January 2004 after previously having been suspended.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/3374081.stm|title=Mashaba faces the sack|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=6 January 2004|author=Mark Gleeson|publisher=BBC[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/3376931.stm|title=Mashaba suspended|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=7 January 2004|publisher=BBC[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/3399807.stm|title=South Africa sack Mashaba|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=15 January 2004|publisher=BBC[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/3416443.stm|title=Mashaba bows down|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=21 January 2004|publisher=BBC[/tpl] Mashaba has also managed club sides Vaal Reef Stars and Black Leopards.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/3500801.stm|title='Shakes' lands new role|accessdate=2008-05-21|date=18 February 2004|author=Mark Gleeson|publisher=BBC[/tpl]
