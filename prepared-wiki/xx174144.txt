[[Youmex]]

CATEGORIES: Anime companies, Defunct companies of Japan, Japanese record labels, Record labels established in 1985, EMI, Toshiba

[tpl]nihongo|Youmex|ユーメックス株式会社|Yūmekkusu Kabushiki Kaisha[/tpl] was an anime production company and record label (under their label Futureland) established in 1985 as a subsidiary of Toshiba EMI and founded by Junji Fujita (formerly of King Records).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www002.upp.so-net.ne.jp/kamono/youmex.html|title=ユーメックス|accessdate=2008-01-01|language=Japanese[/tpl] The company was absorbed back into Toshiba EMI in 1998, after taking on debt defaulted on by Artmic.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ex.org/4.4/09-feature_acen6.html|title=AnimeCentral 1999|publisher=EX.org|year=1999|accessdate=2008-01-01|quote=Artmic went bankrupt; it no longer exists. AIC and others like Youmex, they were loan cosigners, so they got stuck with the bills for the loans that Artmic defaulted on.[/tpl]
Some of the more well-known works for which Youmex released soundtracks and other CDs (under its Futureland label) include Kimagure Orange Road (Sound Color 1-3, Loving Heart, etc.),[tpl]cite web|url=http://yaacc.cjas.org/series/kimagure-orange-road.html|title=Anime CD Information: Kimagure Orange Road|author=Jerry Hsu|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.usagi.org/~doi/seiyuu/cd/kor/l-heart.html|title=Kimagure Orange ☆ Road Loving Heart|author=Hitoshi Doi|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] Bubblegum Crisis (Complete Vocal Collections 1-2),[tpl]cite web|url=http://oddimals.com/ss/cd1/bgcrisis/vocal.htm|title=Bubblegum Crisis Complete Vocal Collection vol. 1|accessdate=2008-01-03 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071220183225/http://oddimals.com/ss/cd1/bgcrisis/vocal.htm  |archivedate = 2007-12-20[/tpl] and Nadia: The Secret of Blue Water (Vocal Collection).[tpl]cite web|url=http://catsspat.dyndns.org:2080/anime/Nadia/TYCY-5156/index.html|title=Nadia, Vocal Collection 1|date=2000-12-23|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] 
In 1995, Youmex worked with Adam Warren through Dark Horse Comics and Artmic to come up with the original concept for Bubblegum Crisis: Grand Mal, an original English-language manga series.
