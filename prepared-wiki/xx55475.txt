[[Dino Dini]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1965 births, People from Bristol, British video game designers, English people of Italian descent

Dino Dini (born June 5, 1965) is a British computer game developer and original creator of the successful  Kick Off series of football games.[tpl]cite web|last=Calvert|first=Justin|title=Soccer deal for Dino Dini and DC-Studios|url=http://uk.gamespot.com/news/6100674.html|publisher=GameSpot[/tpl] He is a lecturer at the NHTV Breda University of Applied Sciences in the Netherlands, teaching game programming.[tpl]cite web|title=mADE Staff >Dino Dini|url=http://made.nhtv.nl/dino-dini/|work=my Academy for Digital Entertainment|publisher=NHTV University of Applied Sciences|accessdate=9 April 2014[/tpl] 

==Career==

In 1989 Dini's football game Kick Off was published by Anil Gupta's publishing house Anco, for the Commodore Amiga. It was critically acclaimed[tpl]cite web|title=Reviews: Kick Off|url=http://amr.abime.net/review_11150|work=Amiga Magazine Rack|publisher=Amiga Magazine Team|accessdate=15 April 2014[/tpl]  and sold very well. Dini followed it with Kick Off 2, Player Manager, Goal! and Dino Dini's Soccer in the 1990s.http://www.mobygames.com/game/kick-off In 1996, Dini moved to the United States to work as Project Director for the Z-axis football game Three Lions. It was released in 1998. 
In 2001 Dini moved back to Great Britain and started to work with his new development company, Abundant Software. In 2004 Dini signed with DC-Studios to develop a new soccer game[tpl]cite web|last=Calvert|first=Justin|title=Soccer deal for Dino Dini and DC-Studios|url=http://uk.gamespot.com/news/6100674.html|publisher=GameSpot[/tpl] which was code-named Soccer 3 and later Total Control Football. The game was cancelled during the summer of 2005.[tpl]cn|date=January 2012[/tpl] 
After 2005 Dini worked as a contractor on a government funded project. In 2009, he became a lecturer for the  International Game Architecture and Design programme at NHTV Breda University of Applied Sciences in the Netherlands, teaching game programming. At the 2009 Game Developers Conference Europe (GDCE) in Cologne, Germany, Dini was guest speaker on the subject of video game design.Lecturer, Video Game Programming, NHTV Speaks at The Game Developers Conference Europe 2009.GDCE09: Dino Dini on Design, Constraints and Integrity
In 2010 Dino ran unsuccessfully for a position on the International Game Developers Association board of directors.Candidates for the IGDA board of directors In August of that year he announced via his blog that he was working on a sequel to his game Player Manager as an independent project.http://dinodini.wordpress.com/2010/08/16/the-return-of-player-manager/ In 2011 he contributed articles and the beta version of his browser-based game Letteroids 3Dto DevilsMMO, a website for MMO and MMORPG games.http://www.devilsmmo.comhttp://www.devilsmmo.com/game/letteroids-3d-beta
Dini also works as a consultant and developer through his company Igneous Entertainment Limited.http://www.igda.org/dino-dini-2010-board-statement
In 2014, Dini announced on his blog, that his debut album, title Almost There, will be released later in 2014.http://www.dinodini.com/1/post/2014/04/debut-album.html Dini has been releasing several music videos on his YouTube channel through the years.
