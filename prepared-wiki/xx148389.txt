[[Conway Smith]]

CATEGORIES: 1926 births, 1989 deaths, People from Huddersfield, English footballers, Association football forwards, Queens Park Rangers F.C. players, Huddersfield Town F.C. players, Halifax Town A.F.C. players, Nelson F.C. players, The Football League players

William Conway Smith (13 July 1926 – 1989), known as Conway Smith, was an English footballer born in Huddersfield, Yorkshire, who played as an inside forward for Huddersfield Town, Queens Park Rangers and Halifax Town in the Football League, and in non-league football for Nelson.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player4/conwaysmith.html |title=Conway Smith |work=UK A–Z Transfers |publisher=Neil Brown |accessdate=8 November 2009[/tpl]
Smith, the son of Huddersfield Town and England footballer Billy Smith, signed for Huddersfield on professional forms in 1945, and made his debut in the 1947–48 season. His progress was interrupted by a broken leg sustained in January 1949, and in March 1951, after "a lengthy spell" on the transfer list, he joined Queens Park Rangers.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.examiner.co.uk/huddersfield-town-fc/huddersfield-town-news/tm_headline=conway-smith-joins-qpr&method=full&objectid=18786920&siteid=50060-name_page.html |title=Looking Back: Conway Smith joins QPR |newspaper=Huddersfield Daily Examiner |date=20 March 2007 |accessdate=8 November 2009[/tpl] He played 174 league games for QPR, scoring 81 goals, before transferring to Halifax Town in 1956, where he achieved a similar record.
