[[Geikie Gorge]]

CATEGORIES: Watercourses of Western Australia, Kimberley (Western Australia)

Geikie Gorge (known locally as Darngku)[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.news.com.au/story/0,10117,17109972-2,00.html|title=Hunt for Lost German Tourist Intensifies|accessdate=2008-04-28|date=2005-11-02|author=Amanda Banks|work=The Australian[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] is a feature of the Napier Range and is located within the grounds of Geikie Gorge National Park, 20 km from Fitzroy Crossing, 1831 km northeast of Perth and 420 km east of Broome in the Kimberley Region of Western Australia. Believed to be one of the best-known and most easily accessed,[tpl]cite book | title=Discover Western Australia| last=Burt| first=Jocelyn| year=1996| publisher=UWA Press| isbn=187556084X[/tpl] the gorge is named in honour of Sir Archibald Geikie, the Director General of Geological Survey for Great Britain and Ireland when it was given its European name in 1883.
Along with Tunnel Creek and Windjana Gorge, Geikie Gorge is part of an ancient barrier reef that developed during the Devonian Period.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/western-australia/fitzroy-crossing/2005/02/17/1108500208437.html|title=Fitzroy Crossing|accessdate=2008-04-28|date=2004-02-08|work=The Age[/tpl] The walls of the gorge are 30 metres high.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://travel.nytimes.com/frommers/travel/guides/australia-and-pacific/australia/broome/frm_broome_1410010011.html?pagewanted=print|title=Side Trips|accessdate=2008-04-28|date=2006-11-20|work=The New York Times[/tpl] The eight kilometer gorge was created by the flowing waters of the Fitzroy River,[tpl]cite book | title=The Rough Guide to Australia| last=Daly| first=Margo| coauthors=Anne Dehne, David Leffman, Chris Scott| year=2003| pages=779| publisher=Rough Guides| isbn=1843530902[/tpl] which still flows through the region and the wildlife present in the Gorge includes: 
the freshwater crocodile, Leichhardt's sawfish and Coach-whip stingrays.[tpl]cite book | title=Kimberley: Australia's Unique North West| last=Burt| first=Jocelyn| pages=71| publisher=UWA Press| isbn=1920694153[/tpl]
