[[Acanthosicyos naudinianus]]

CATEGORIES: Cucurbitaceae, Flora of Africa

Acanthosicyos naudinianus, known as the Gemsbok cucumber, is a perennial  African melon with edible fruits and seeds.Grubben, G.J.H. & Denton, O.A. (2004) Plant Resources of Tropical Africa 2. Vegetables. PROTA Foundation, Wageningen; Backhuys, Leiden; CTA, Wageningen. The plant is green, and the flowers are yellow. The fruit is edible, but eating it before it is ripe will cause a burning sensation in one's mouth. It is not poisonous, but it can be combined with the blood of the larva of the Diamphidia beetle to produce a poison which can be used to make poison arrows.tourbrief--the gemsbok cucumber
