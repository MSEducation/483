[[Racism in Russia]]

CATEGORIES: Racism in Russia, Racism by country

Racism in Russia appears mainly in the form of negative attitudes and actions by  Russians towards people who are not considered ethnically Russian. Traditionally, this included antisemitism, as well as hostility towards various ethnicities of Caucasus and Central Asia.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.economist.com/node/6916041 |title=Russian nationalism: Playing a dangerous game |date=11 May 2006 |publisher=The Economist |accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl] The director of the Moscow Bureau for Human Rights, Alexander Brod, stated that surveys show xenophobia and other racist expressions are prevalent in 50 percent of Russians.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bumbinorn.com/2008/03/27/racist_federation.html |title=Racist Federation |date=27 March 2008 |publisher=Bumbin Orn |accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl] In 2006, Amnesty International reported that racism in Russia was “out of control” and estimated the number of Russian neo-Nazis at around 85,000 in 2008.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/europe/4969296.stm |title=Russian racism 'out of control' |date=4 May 2006 |work=BBC News |publisher=BBC |accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cjnews.com/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=20609 |title=It’s no longer just about the master’s thesis |first=Anna |last=Shternshis |date=13 January 2011 |publisher=Canadian Jewish News |accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl]

==Public sentiments and politics==

These outbursts occurred shortly after the terrorist attack in Volgograd, which left several Russians dead.
