[[DMAX (UK TV channel)]]

CATEGORIES: English-language television stations in the United Kingdom, Television channels and stations established in 2008, Discovery Communications

DMAX is a TV channel in the United Kingdom and Ireland. Discovery announced on 22 November 2007, that they would launch a version of DMAX in the UK and Ireland market on 8 January 2008 after its initial success in Germany.[tpl]cite web|last=Welsh |first=James |url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/broadcasting/a80419/discovery-to-launch-entertainment-channel.html |title=Discovery to launch entertainment channel |publisher=Digital Spy |date=2007-11-22 |accessdate=2013-04-29[/tpl]
In addition, the timeshift service DMAX +1 has also been available on Sky since the launch. A two hour timeshift channel named DMAX +2 launched on Sky on 1 April 2008. Another timeshift channel named DMAX +1.5 launched on Sky on 18 August 2008. DMAX +1.5 was closed down on 2 November 2009 when Quest +1 launched.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.agbnielsen.co.uk/agb/files/ClientServices/Data%20Information/Chan_changes.doc|title=Channel Changes implemented or notified since 01/11/2009|accessdate=2009-12-20|archivedate=2009-12-20|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5mA91u12d|publisher=AGB Nielsen Media Research[/tpl] DMAX +2 closed on 30 April 2013 on Sky when TLC launched on channel 125 and Challenge was relocated to channel 145, which had been DMAX +1.[tpl]cite web|last=Hadley |first=Paul |url=http://www.entertainment-iuk.com/2013/04/24/changes-to-discovery-channels-this-month/ |title=Changes to Discovery channels this month |publisher=Entertainment Interactive |date=2013-04-24 |accessdate=2013-04-30[/tpl] DMAX +1 moved to Sky channel 238 as TLC +1 launched on channel 195, which had been DMAX +2.
