[[2008 Saint John River Flood]]

CATEGORIES: Natural disasters in New Brunswick, Floods in Canada, 2008 in Canada, Fort Kent, Maine, Natural disasters in Maine, Madawaska, Maine

The 2008 Saint John River flood was considered the worst flooding of the Saint John River in 35 years. Flood levels reached 27 feet and 4 inches in Fredericton on May 1, almost reaching the previous record of 28 feet and 3 inches, set in 1973.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fredericton.ca/en/publicsafety/2008May1FloodUpdate.asp|title=City Officials Call for Caution As Flood Closes 50 Streets|date=May 1, 2008| accessdate= 12 May 2008 | archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20080505234157/http://www.fredericton.ca/en/publicsafety/2008May1FloodUpdate.asp| archivedate= 5 May 2008| deadurl= no[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.canada.com/vancouversun/news/story.html?id=3259e5b7-7a56-44aa-8d48-d6fc17ba5e13&k=13980|title=Rising water threatens New Brunswick legislature, homes, bridges|date=2008-05-01|publisher=The Vancouver Sun|authors=Marianne White and Bradley Bouzane| accessdate= 30 April 2008 | archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20080501180500/http://www.canada.com/vancouversun/news/story.html?id=3259e5b7-7a56-44aa-8d48-d6fc17ba5e13&k=13980| archivedate= 1 May 2008| deadurl= yes[/tpl] Flooding was attributed to the melting of record snowfall from the 2007-2008 winter and heavy rain during the melting season. Fifty streets in Fredericton area were closed as a result of the flooding. The flooding caused severe damage in areas of northern Maine and western New Brunswick including international bridges and hundreds of homes and buildings.
By May 3, the Canadian Red Cross had registered 680 residents who had been forced to evacuate from their homes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fredericton.ca/en/publicsafety/2008May3FloodRevised.asp|title=City River Level to Remain High During Weekend|date=May 3, 2008| accessdate= 12 May 2008 | archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20080505234203/http://www.fredericton.ca/en/publicsafety/2008May3FloodRevised.asp| archivedate= 5 May 2008| deadurl= no[/tpl] An addition 600 residences were also evacuated in Maine.
