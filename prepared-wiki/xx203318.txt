[[Carpocyon]]

CATEGORIES: Borophagines, Miocene carnivorans, Miocene extinctions, Prehistoric mammals of North America, Mammal genera

Carpocyon is an extinct member of the Borophaginae, and a terrestrial canine (Bear-dog) which inhabited most of North America during the Barstovian stage of the Middle Miocene through the Hemphillian stage of the Late Miocene epoch 20.4â€”3.9 Mya.PaleoBiology Database: Carpocyon Taxonomy, Species Carpocyon existed for approximately [tpl]Mya|20.4-3.9|million years[/tpl].

==Taxonomy==

Carpocyon was named by Webb in 1969 and assigned to Canidae by Webb that same year. In 1988, Carroll assigned it to Canidae with X. Wang assigning it to Borophagina in 1999. 

==Morphology==

Two fossil specimens of Carpocyon were measured by Legendre and Roth in 1988. They estimated that specimen one weighed [tpl]Convert|24.1|kg|lb|abbr=on|sigfig=2[/tpl] and the second weighed [tpl]Convert|21.8|kg|lb|abbr=on|sigfig=2[/tpl].S. Legendre and C. Roth. 1988. Correlation of carnassial tooth size and body weight in recent carnivores (Mammalia). Historical Biology: p. 85-98 Its physical size was between a jackal and a small wolf.
