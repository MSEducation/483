[[Berkshire Industrial Farm]]

CATEGORIES: Children's charities, Organizations based in New York City, Children's rights organizations in the United States, Columbia County, New York, Companies based in Columbia County, New York, Buildings and structures in Columbia County, New York

The Berkshire Industrial Farm, (previously known as the Burnham Industrial Farm) in Canaan, New York, was a rural residential facility for troubled young men from the New York area in the late 19th Century. It operated under the name Berkshire Industrial Farm from 1886-1959, after which it became known as the Berkshire Centre for Boys and, in 1974, changed its name to the modern day Berkshire Farm Center and Service for Youth.[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.berkshirefarm.org/about_us/History_of_Berkshire_Farm_Center_67_pg.htm |title=History of Berkshire Farm Center | Berkshire Farm Center and Services for Youth |publisher=Berkshirefarm.org |date= |accessdate=2014-02-18[/tpl]
Today, it is known as Berkshire Farm Center and Services for Youth (BFC)[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.p12.nysed.gov/accountability/T1/ses/ApprovedProviders/BerkshireFarmCenterandServicesforYouth.html |title=Berkshire Farm Center and Services for Youth:Approved SES Providers:NCLB::NYSED |publisher=P12.nysed.gov |date=2012-04-26 |accessdate=2014-02-18[/tpl] and is one of New York State's largest non-profit child welfare agencies, serving more than 3,000 wards. It provides "community, school, at-home and on-site services" including residential treatment center placement, juvenile detention placement, foster care placement, vocational training, and group home placement.
