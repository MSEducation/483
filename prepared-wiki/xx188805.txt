[[Charles P. de Saint-Aignan]]

CATEGORIES: 1977 births, Living people, 20th-century astronomers, American astronomers, Discoverers of asteroids, IBM employees, Scientists from Paris, Brown University alumni

Charles P. de Saint-Aignan (born 1977 in Paris) is an American software engineer who works for IBM on the IBM Watson project. He graduated from St. Paul's School (Concord, New Hampshire), in 1995,[tpl]cite web | url = http://astro.sps.edu/history/timeline.html | title = Timeline of the St. Paul's Lenore and Walter Hawley Observatory | publisher = The Hawley Observatory[/tpl] followed by Brown University (Providence, Rhode Island), in 1999.
In 1994, Charles worked for Ted Bowell at Lowell Observatory, where he discovered a number of asteroids. The first asteroid he found was named for Walter Hawley, his high school astronomy teacher.
Asteroid 5995 Saint-Aignan is named for Charles.

==Relatives==

Brother of Gregory, of CG Partners. Cousin of Stéphanie, of Stéphanie de Saint-Aignan Parfums.
