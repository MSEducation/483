[[Ashley Vincent]]

CATEGORIES: 1985 births, Living people, People from Oldbury, West Midlands, English footballers, Black English sportspeople, Association football wingers, Association football forwards, Wolverhampton Wanderers F.C. players, Cheltenham Town F.C. players, Aldershot Town F.C. players, Colchester United F.C. players, Port Vale F.C. players, The Football League players, Football Conference players

Ashley Derek Vincent (born 26 May 1985) is an English footballer who plays for League Two side Cheltenham Town. He can play on the wing or as a striker, and is renowned for his pace.
He graduated through the Wolverhampton Wanderers youth system to win a contract with Cheltenham Town in May 2004. He spent the next five years at the club, playing in the 2006 League Two play-off final victory. He was loaned out to Aldershot Town in November 2005 and to Colchester United in March 2009, before he joined Colchester permanently in May 2009. He spent three years with the club, before he moved on to Port Vale in July 2012. He helped the "Valiants" to secure promotion out of League Two in 2012–13, before he re-joined Cheltenham Town in July 2013.

==Playing career==

Oldbury, West Midlands-born Vincent started his career at Wolverhampton Wanderers, spending three years at Molineux and playing in the youth and reserve teams.

===Colchester United===

In March 2009, Vincent joined Colchester United on loan until the end of the 2008–09 season.[tpl]cite news|title=Colchester wrap up Vincent deal|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/colchester_united/7947722.stm|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=19 March 2009[/tpl] Manager Paul Lambert said that "He's got the enthusiasm and hunger and he'll be a big player for us next season. He's got pace, and when you've got that you've always got a chance."[tpl]cite news|title=Vincent to stay with Colchester|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/colchester_united/8016511.stm|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=24 April 2009[/tpl] In May 2009, Lambert signed Vincent to a three-year contract for an undisclosed fee.[tpl]cite news|title=Vincent to stay with Colchester|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/colchester_united/8074530.stm|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=29 May 2009[/tpl] However Lambert left the club after the first game of the season – a 7–1 win over Norwich City – and was replaced by new boss Aidy Boothroyd. Vincent featured just 21 times in 2009–10, scoring three goals. He went on to feature 42 times in 2010–11, scoring a total of five goals.
Vincent missed six weeks at the start of the 2011–12 season with a knee ligament injury.[tpl]cite news|title=Colchester United lose injured Ashley Vincent for six weeks|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/14530770|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=17 August 2011[/tpl] In an interview given after his recovery, he admitted that he had feared the injury could have ended his career, and also hinted at a move to a Midlands club in the summer so as to be closer to his family.[tpl]cite news|title=Ashley Vincent feared end of Colchester United career|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/15212696|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=7 October 2011[/tpl] In December, he was struck down by a hamstring injury, which kept him out of action for several weeks.[tpl]cite news|title=Ashley Vincent set for Colchester United first-team return|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/16968703|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=9 February 2012[/tpl] His injuries limited him to just 12 appearances in the campaign, as well as an appearance in the final of the Essex Senior Cup (which ended in a shock defeat to Canvey Island[tpl]cite news|title=Canvey Island win Essex Senior Cup|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/17725792|accessdate=15 July 2012|newspaper=BBC Sport|date=17 April 2012[/tpl]).
