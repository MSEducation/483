The Oklahoma City Thunder is an American professional basketball franchise based in Oklahoma City, Oklahoma. It plays in the Northwest Division of the Western Conference in the National Basketball Association (NBA). It was known as the Seattle SuperSonics from 1967 to 2008. The team plays its home games at the Chesapeake Energy Arena.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.koco.com/news/16785490/detail.html|title=City Preparing Ford Center For NBA Team|work=KOCO Oklahoma City|publisher=Hearst Television Inc|date=July 3, 2008|accessdate=July 4, 2008[/tpl] As of October 2008, the Thunder is currently owned by Professional Basketball Club LLC and coached by Scott Brooks,[tpl]cite web|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/nba/news/story?id=3718961|title=Carlesimo fired; Brooks to take over Thunder in interim|work=ESPN.com|publisher=ESPN Internet Ventures|accessdate=November 22, 2008[/tpl] with Sam Presti as its general manager. The team was formed in 1967. After spending 41 years in Seattle, Washington, the SuperSonics moved to Oklahoma City. Owner Clay Bennett, who purchased the team in 2006, sought to get public funding for a new arena in Seattle, or a major renovation of the KeyArena in 2007. After failing to do so, he decided to move the team to Oklahoma City.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.seattlepi.com/basketball/337871_arena03.html?source=mypi|title=Bennett says Sonics going to Oklahoma|work=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|publisher=Hearst Seattle Media, LLC|date=November 2, 2007|accessdate=April 2, 2008|first=Greg|last=Johns[/tpl] Seattle sued Bennett's group to enforce the lease that required the team to stay until 2010. The two sides reached a $45 million settlement to pay off the team's lease with KeyArena in July 2008.[tpl]cite web|work=ESPN.com|publisher=ESPN Internet Ventures|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/nba/news/story?id=3471503|title=SuperSonics, Seattle reach last-minute settlement| date=July 3, 2008|accessdate= July 2, 2008[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/localnews/2008030229_sonitrial02.html|title=Sonics, city reach settlement|work=The Seattle Times|publisher=The Seattle Times Company|date=July 2, 2008|accessdate=July 2, 2008[/tpl]
There have been 16 head coaches for the Thunder franchise. The franchise won its only NBA championship in the 1979 NBA Finals while coached by Hall of Famer Lenny Wilkens. Wilkens is the only member of the franchise to have been inducted into the Basketball Hall of Fame as a coach.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/coaches/wilkele01c.html|title=Lenny Wilkens Coaching Record|publisher=Sports Reference LLC|work=basketball-reference.com|accessdate=July 28, 2008[/tpl] He is also the franchise's all-time leader in regular-season games coached, regular-season games won, playoff games coached, and playoff games won. Wilkens, Nate McMillan and Bob Weiss formerly played for the team in Seattle.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/players/w/wilkele01.html|title=Lenny Wilkens Playing Record|work=basketball-reference.com|publisher=Sports Reference LLC|accessdate=October 11, 2008[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/players/m/mcmilna01.html|title=Nate McMillan Playing Record|publisher=Sports Reference LLC|work=basketball-reference.com|accessdate=October 11, 2008[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/players/w/weissbo01.html|title=Bob Weiss Playing Record|publisher=Sports Reference LLC|work=basketball-reference.com|accessdate=October 11, 2008[/tpl] Current head coach Scott Brooks is the only coach to have won the NBA Coach of the Year Award with the franchise.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nba.com/history/awards_coachofyear.html|title=Coach of the Year|work=NBA.com|publisher=Turner Sports Interactive, Inc|accessdate=April 26, 2010[/tpl]
