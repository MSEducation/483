[[Heterocarpus ensifer]]

CATEGORIES: Caridea, Animals described in 1881

[tpl]Italic title[/tpl]

Heterocarpus ensifer is a species of deep-water shrimp. The nominate subspecies is found in the Atlantic Ocean from Spain and Madeira to Angola and from North Carolina to the Gulf of Mexico and Caribbean Sea; other subspecies occur in the Pacific Ocean and around the Philippines and Indonesia.[tpl]cite book |url=http://www.gsmfc.org/seamap/picture_guide/Shrimp/heterocarpus%20ensifer%20ensifer.pdf |format=PDF |chapter=Heterocarpus ensifer ensifer A. Milne-Edwards, 1881 |title=Guide to Shelf Invertebrates, Gulf of Mexico |author=Harriet Perry and Kirsten Larsen |date=April 6, 2004 |publisher=Gulf States Marine Fisheries Commission[/tpl] Despite being one of the most abundant shrimp in some areas,[tpl]cite journal |title=Collections and Submarine Observations of Deep Benthic Fishes and Decapod Crustacea in Hawaii1 |author=Thomas A. Clarke |year=1972 |journal=Pacific Science |volume=26 |issue=3 |pages=310–317 |url=https://scholarspace.manoa.hawaii.edu/bitstream/10125/503/1/v26n3-310-317.pdf |format=PDF[/tpl] its biology is still poorly known.[tpl]cite journal |title=Relative growth and size at first maturity of the deep water shrimp, Heterocarpus ensifer (Decapoda, Pandalidae) from the southern Gulf of Mexico |author=Enrique Lozano-Álvarez, Patricia Briones-Fourzán, Adolfo Gracia & Ana Vázquez-Bader|journal=Crustaceana |volume=80 |issue=5 |year=2007 |pages=555–568 |doi=10.1163/156854007780765588[/tpl]
