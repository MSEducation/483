[[Vincetoxicum hirundinaria]]

CATEGORIES: Vincetoxicum

Vincetoxicum hirundinaria, commonly named Swallow-wort, is a long-lived herbaceous perennial of the genus Vincetoxicum in the family Apocynaceae.  It is native to cliffs and slopes, especially in calcareous soils, of continental Eurasia (including some Baltic islands).[tpl]Cite journal | doi = 10.1093/aob/mch012 |date=Jan 2004 | author = Leimu, R | title = Variation in the mating system of Vincetoxicum hirundinaria (Asclepiadaceae) in peripherial island populations | volume = 93 | issue = 1 | pages = 107â€“13 | issn = 0305-7364 | pmid = 14678943 | journal = Annals of Botany | url = http://aob.oxfordjournals.org/cgi/pmidlookup?view=long&pmid=14678943 | format = Free full text [/tpl]  There are introduced populations in North America (Ontario, Michigan, and New York).

==Iarba fiarelor==

As Iarba fiarelor the plant was mentioned by Pedanius Dioscorides as a traditional plant used by Dacians and known as "ZOO-USTE", the "herb of animals", believed to open any locked door.[tpl]cite web | url=http://epochtimes-romania.com/news/iarba-fiarelor---165220 | date=22 August 2012 | work=The Epoch Times, Romania | title=Iarba fiarelor | last=Bucurescu | first = Adrian | accessdate=24 March 2013[/tpl]
