[[AfriNIC]]

CATEGORIES: Internet in Africa, Regional Internet registries

AFRINIC (African Network Information Center) is the regional Internet registry (RIR) for Africa. Its headquarters are in Ebene City, Mauritius. Adiel Akplogan is the registry's chief executive officer. [tpl]As of|2010|11[/tpl], AFRINIC had a staff of 18.AFRINIC Staff List
Before AFRINIC was formed, IP addresses for Africa were distributed by the Asia-Pacific Network Information Centre (APNIC), the American Registry for Internet Numbers (ARIN), and the RIPE NCC.IANA.org
ICANN provisionally recognized AFRINIC on [tpl]date|11 Oct 2004[/tpl]. The registry became operational on [tpl]date|22 Feb 2005[/tpl]. ICANN gave it final recognition in [tpl]date|April 2005[/tpl].
AFRINIC has been allocated the IPv4 address blocks 41.0.0.0/8, 102.0.0.0/8, 105.0.0.0/8 and 197.0.0.0/8 and  IPv6 blocks 2c00::/12 and 2001:4200::/23. AFRINIC also administers the address space for 196.0.0.0/8 and 154.0.0.0/8.IANA.org
