[[Jan Claesz Rietschoof]]

CATEGORIES: 1651 births, 1719 deaths, Dutch Golden Age painters, People from Hoorn

Jan Claesz Rietschoof (1651–1719) was a Dutch Golden Age painter of seascapes.

==Biography==

According to Houbraken he learned painting first from  Abraham Liedts, and later from the renowned seascape painter Ludolf Bakhuizen.[tpl]Link language|nl|Middle Dutch[/tpl] Jan Klaasze Rietschoof biography in De groote schouburgh der Nederlantsche konstschilders en schilderessen (1718) by Arnold Houbraken, courtesy of the Digital library for Dutch literature

==Paintings==

His paintings today hang in various museums in the Netherlands. He taught his son Hendrik Rietschoof (1678–1747) how to paint; Hendrik's seascape and seaport drawings are online at the Amsterdam archives. In Hoorn he was a contemporary of the flower painter Jacob Rootius, who was the son of the influential schutterstuk painter Jan Albertsz Rotius.
