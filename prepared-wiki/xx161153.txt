[[Palouse Falls State Park]]

CATEGORIES: Parks in Franklin County, Washington, State parks of Washington (state), Parks in Whitman County, Washington

Palouse Falls State Park is a [tpl]convert|105|acre|km2|adj=on[/tpl] member of the Washington state park system in the Palouse region of Eastern Washington, USA.  It is named for the [tpl]convert|200|foot|m|adj=on[/tpl] Palouse Falls on the Palouse River, which are part of the park.
The park contains displays explaining the region's unique geology as well as its historical ties (the Palouse Indians and the Mullan Road both took advantage of the easy access to the plateau in the vicinity of the falls). The park provides facilities for camping.
