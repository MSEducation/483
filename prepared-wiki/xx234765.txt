[[Greg Ayers]]

CATEGORIES: Australian meteorologists, Australian public servants, Monash University alumni, Living people, Fellows of the Australian Academy of Technological Sciences and Engineering

Greg Ayers is an Australian atmospheric scientist and was Director of the Australian Bureau of Meteorology from March 2009http://www.environment.gov.au/minister/archive/env/2009/mr20090330a.html until February 2012, when he resigned due to ill health.[tpl]cite press release|url=http://www.environment.gov.au/minister/farrell/2012/mr20120220.html|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20130203124843/http://environment.gov.au/minister/farrell/2012/mr20120220.html|archivedate=3 February 2013|date=20 February 2012|title=Government thanks outgoing Bureau of Meteorology director, Dr Greg Ayers|first=Don|last=Farrell|author-link=Don Farrell[/tpl]
Prior to his working at the Bureau of Meteorology, Ayers was Chief of Marine and Atmospheric Research at the CSIRO, where he had worked since 1975.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.monash.edu.au/alumni/prominent-alumni/greg-ayers.html|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20111124222427/http://monash.edu.au/alumni/prominent-alumni/greg-ayers.html|archivedate=24 November 2011|title=Dr Greg Ayers|publisher=Monash University[/tpl] Ayers is a Fellow of the Australian Academy of Technological Sciences and Engineering.
He was educated at Monash University, where he obtained a Bachelor of Science (with honours) and a PhD.
In February 2011 he responded to Cardinal George Pell's scepticism and ignorance about climate change during a Senate Estimates hearing.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/environment/climate-change/bom-chief-lashes-pell-over-climate-stance-20110222-1b324.html|title=BoM chief lashes Pell over climate stance|newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald|publisher=Fairfax Media|date=22 February 2011|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20121107191342/http://www.smh.com.au/environment/climate-change/bom-chief-lashes-pell-over-climate-stance-20110222-1b324.html|archivedate=7 November 2012|agency=AAP[/tpl]
