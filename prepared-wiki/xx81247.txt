[[Kaori Mochida]]

CATEGORIES: 1978 births, Living people, Singers from Tokyo, Japanese female singers, Every Little Thing members

[tpl]nihongo|Kaori Mochida|持田 香織|Mochida Kaori|born March 24, 1978 in Tokyo, Japan[/tpl] is the female lead singer of Japanese pop group Every Little Thing.

==Biography==

Mochida began her career as a child actress, appearing in magazines and TV commercials. She then became a member of the second generation of idol group The Kuro Buta All-Stars, and entered the entertainment world as [tpl]nihongo2|"持田かおり"[/tpl] (Kaori Mochida, with Kaori written in hiragana).[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= |year= |url = http://www.elt.nu/database/idol.htm |title = 「持田香織」が「持田かおり」だった頃のお話|publisher = ELTファンページ | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] Shortly after, she released her first single as a solo artist, titled "Mō Ichido" in November 1993. She didn't decide to become a singer professionally until she was in high school.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.elt.nu/database/idol.htm |title = 持田香織　子供の頃: ELT story|publisher = | accessdate = May 14, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl]
In 1995, she met Mitsuru Igarashi, and signed to the Avex label. At first she was meant to debut as a solo artist, but as Max Matsuura suggested, she then formed a duo with Igarashi, and then a trio when Ichiro Ito was incorporated as a guitarist.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= June 20, 2013 |url = http://homepage2.nifty.com/keitai_mochi_ai/idol_menu/kaori_mochida_menu3.html |title = ELT物語|publisher = | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] They debuted in 1996 with "Feel My Heart". Following her debut with Every Little Thing, Mochida became a popular fashion icon in Japan, and started to appear in TV commercials for a wide variety of beauty products. The spots that she starred in usually used the songs of her band as background music. After the departure of Mitsuru Igarashi from Every Little Thing in 2000, Mochida started to take more control in the music making process, and soon started to write her own songs. The first song for Every Little Thing that she wrote lyrics for was "All along" included in the Time to Destination album, and the first song she composed was "Jump", released as a single in 2001.
She did not release any solo music until 2002, when she participated in the Avex charity project Song+Nation, where she recorded the song "In Case of Me", produced by Tetsuya Komuro. In 2004, she released "Itsunomanika Shōjo wa", a cover single of Yosui Inoue which was produced by the musician himself. In 2006, she wrote Haruka Ayase's debut single "Period" along with Takeshi Kobayashi,[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= January 6, 2006 |year= 2013|url = http://contents.oricon.co.jp/news/travel/8205/ |title = 綾瀬はるか歌手デビュー|format = |publisher = Oricon Style | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] and in 2007, she wrote "Sarai no Kaze" for May and "Going Back Home" for Mika Nakashima.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= March 14, 2007 |year= 2013|url = http://www.oricon.co.jp/music/interview/070314_03.html |title = 中島美嘉 Special Interview: 2年ぶりのオリジナルアルバムは豪華アーティストが多数参加!!|publisher = 日刊スポーツ | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] This year she also provided vocals for Dragon Ash's song "Wipe Your Eyes", song that also had a promotional video despite not being released as a single.
In 2009, Kaori Mochida's solo career officially started, and she started to work on her first solo album. She released her first official solo single, "Ame no Walts", on January 28, 2009.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= January 28, 2009 |year= 2013|url = http://news.infoseek.co.jp/article/20090128news2u43307 |title = 持田香織ソロ第一弾シングル「雨のワルツ」が遂にリリース!!!|format = |publisher = News2u.net | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] The song was credited as a collaboration between Mochida and Sakerock, a Japanese instrumental band. In March she and Takao Tajima from Original Love formed a special group called Caocao and recorded a cover of "Kojin Jugyo" by Finger 5. The song was used as theme song for the movie Oppai Volleyball.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= April 9, 2009 |year= 2013|url = http://news.infoseek.co.jp/article/20090128news2u43307 |title = 持田香織（Every Little Thing）×田島貴男（オリジナル・ラヴ）のスペシャルユニット"Caocao"のシングル「個人授業」ついにリリース！！|format = |publisher = News2u.net | accessdate = February 20, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] In July she released a double A-side single entitled "Shizuka na Yoru/Weather", and a month later, on August 12, she released her first solo album, Moka. The album featured collaborations with several artists, such as Lisa Ono, Ikuko Harada, Yuichi Ohata, Bic Runga, ohashiTrio and Sean Lennon.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= July 13, 2010 |year= 2013|url = http://www.barks.jp/news/?id=1000062647 |title = 持田香織、2ndアルバムでも多数のアーティストとコラボ|format = |publisher = Barks News | accessdate = February 20, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl] It debuted at number 9 in the Oricon charts and enjoyed moderated success.
On August 25, 2009, her second album, Niu, was released. The album had no commercial singles, except for a CD single of "Green", that was included in a special edition of fashion magazine Journal Standard. For this album she made her first domestic solo tour between September and October.
On August 1, 2012, her third solo album Manu a Manu was released.[tpl]cite web |ref=harv|date= June 20, 2013 |url = http://www.excite.co.jp/News/music/20120620/Musicman_artist18022.html |title = 持田香織 ニュー・ソロアルバムが8/1にリリース決定|publisher = Musicman-NET | accessdate = February 19, 2013 | last=| first= |quote=[/tpl]
