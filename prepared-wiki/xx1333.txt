==Religious sites==

The Church of St Andrew dates from the 14th century. It was restored in 1873 by William Butterfield. It is a Grade I listed building and contains some 15th-century stained glass and an altar table of 1631. The chest tomb in the chancel is believed to contain the remains of Sir Thomas Cheddar and is dated 1442.[tpl]cite web| title=Church of St. Andrew | work=Images of England | publisher=English Heritage | url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/details/default.aspx?id=268768 | accessdate=9 May 2006[/tpl] The tower, which rises to [tpl]convert|100|ft|m|0[/tpl],[tpl]Cite book|title=Curiosities of Somerset |last=Leete-Hodge |first=Lornie |authorlink= |year=1985 |publisher=Bossiney Books |location=Bodmin |isbn=0-906456-98-3 |page=20 [/tpl] contains a bell dating from 1759 made by Thomas Bilbie of the Bilbie family.[tpl]Cite book|last=Moore |first=James |authorlink= |url=|coauthors=Roy Rice & Ernest Hucker |title=Bilbie and the Chew Valley clock makers |year=1995 |pages= |publisher=The authors |location= |isbn= [/tpl]
There are also churches for Roman Catholic, Methodist and other denominations, including Cheddar Valley Community Church, who not only meet at The Kings of Wessex School on Sunday, but also have their own site on Tweentown for meeting during the week.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddarvalleycommunitychurch.org/|title=Cheddar Valley Community Church|publisher=Cheddar Valley Community Church|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] The Baptist chapel was built in 1831.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/Details/Default.aspx?id=268783|title=Baptist Chapel|work=Images of England|publisher=English Heritage|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl]

==Sport==

Kings Fitness & Leisure, situated on the grounds of The Kings of Wessex School, provides a venue for various sports and includes a 20-metre swimming pool, racket sport courts, a sports hall, dance studios and a gym.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddarvillage.co.uk/Sport_Leisure/index.htm|title=Sport and Leisure|publisher=Cheddar Web Site|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] A youth sports festival was held on Sharpham Road Playing Fields in 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thisissomerset.co.uk/Summer-festival-youth-sports-receives-2-000-boost/story-12312094-detail/story.html|title=Summer festival of youth sports receives £2,000 boost |publisher=This is Somerset |accessdate=6 June 2012[/tpl] In 2010 a skatepark was built in the village,[tpl]Cite news| newspaper= Cheddar Valley Gazette | url= http://www.thisissomerset.co.uk/shepton/news/Skate-park-man-appears-TV/article-1784459-detail/article.html | title= Skate park man appears on TV | date= 1 February 2010 | accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] funded by the Cheddar Local Action Team.[tpl]cite news| url= http://www.thewestonmercury.co.uk/news/lions_pledge_skate_help_1_312349| newspaper= The Weston Mercury | title= Lions pledge skate help | date= 9 July 2007|accessdate=31 January 2011[/tpl]
Cheddar Football Club, founded in 1892 and nicknamed "The Cheesemen",[tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.thisissomerset.co.uk/news/Cheddar-Football-Club-premises/article-1031461-detail/article.html|title=Cheddar Football Club could move premises|date=28 May 2009|newspaper= Cheddar Valley Gazette|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] play in the Somerset County Football League Premier Division.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddarfc.co.uk/|title=Cheddar AFC|publisher=Cheddar AFC|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] In 2009 plans were revealed to move the club from its present home at Bowdens Park on Draycott Road to a new larger site.
Cheddar Cricket Club was formed in the late 19th century and moved to Sharpham Road Playing Fields in 1964.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddarcc.co.uk/history.html|title=History|publisher=Cheddar Cricket Club|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] They now play in the West of England Premier League Somerset Division. Cheddar Rugby Club, who own part of the Sharpham playing fields, was formed in 1836. The club organises an annual Cheddar Rugby Tournament.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddar-rugby.co.uk/home.asp|title=Cheddar Rugby Club|publisher=Cheddar Rugby Club|accessdate=5 February 2011[/tpl] Cheddar Lawn Tennis Club, was formed in 1924,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cheddartennisclub.co.uk/history.html|title=History|publisher=Cheddar Tennis Club|accessdate=30 March 2010[/tpl] and play in the North Somerset League and also has social tennis and coaching. Cheddar Running Club organised an annual half marathon until 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thisissomerset.co.uk/Undefined-Headline/story-12290118-detail/story.html|title=No More Cheddar Half Marathon|publisher=This is Somerset|date= 3 December 2009 |accessdate=6 June 2012[/tpl]
