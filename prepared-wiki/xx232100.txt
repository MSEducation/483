[[Florida Scott-Maxwell]]

CATEGORIES: 1883 births, 1979 deaths, American expatriates in the United Kingdom, American writers, American suffragists, American dramatists and playwrights

Florida Pier Scott-Maxwell (14 September 1883 - 6 March 1979) was a playwright, author and psychologist. 

==Early life==

Florida Pier was born in Orange Park, Florida, and educated at home until the age of ten.[tpl]cite book|last=Harmon|first=Gary|editor=McCarthy, Kevin|title=The Book-lovers' Guide to Florida|publisher=Pineapple|location=Sarasota, FL|isbn=978-1-56164-012-6|page=54[/tpl] She grew up in Pittsburgh, then moved to New York at age 15 to become an actress. In 1910 she married John Scott Maxwell and moved to her husband's native Scotland, where she worked for women's suffrage and as a playwright. The couple divorced in 1929 and she moved to London. In 1933 she studied Jungian psychology under Carl Jung and practised as an analytical psychologist in both England and Scotland. She died in Exeter, England. Her most famous book is The Measure of My Days (1968).
