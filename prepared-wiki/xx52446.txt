[[Megumi Hayashibara]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese female singers, Japanese pop singers, Japanese singer-songwriters, Japanese voice actresses, Japanese radio personalities, Japanese nurses, Actresses from Tokyo, 1967 births, Living people, 20th-century Japanese actresses, 21st-century Japanese actresses, Japanese film actresses, Japanese television actresses, Japanese lyricists, King Records (Japan) artists, Anime musicians, Musicians from Tokyo

[tpl]nihongo|Megumi Hayashibara|林原 めぐみ|Hayashibara Megumi|born March 30, 1967[/tpl] is a Japanese voice actress, singer, radio personality, and lyricist from Tokyo. She is affiliated with Woodpark Office. She is best known for her roles in Love Hina, Saber Marionette J, Ranma ½, Neon Genesis Evangelion, Cowboy Bebop, Slayers, Detective Conan, Pokémon, All Purpose Cultural Catgirl Nuku Nuku and Shaman King. She is also a fully qualified and registered nurse.http://www.starchild.co.jp/artist/hayashi/megumi/index.html

==Biography==

Megumi Hayashibara was born on March 30, 1967 in Tokyo, Japan.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.starchild.co.jp/artist/hayashi/megumi/index.html |title=Megumi Room  — Megumi House |publisher=Starchild |accessdate=2009-04-17[/tpl] She studied at a Catholic school, and at one point was bullied in fifth grade.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page= |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] She was an active club member and participated in the Badminton, Biology, Broadcasting, Drama, and English clubs. She played the role of Alice in an English language production of Alice in Wonderland.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |pages=76–77 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] Despite qualifying as a nurse, she has never been employed in a nursing position.[tpl]cite book |title=Anime essentials |last=Poitras |first=Giles |authorlink=Giles Poitras |pages= |publisher=Stone Bridge Press |isbn=1-880656-53-1[/tpl]
On March 30, 1998, she married.http://www.ex.org/3.2/09-news.html On January 10, 2004, she announced on her radio show that she was pregnant with her first child.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.animenewsnetwork.com/news/2004-01-12/megumi-hayashibara |title=Megumi Hayashibara News  — Anime News Network |publisher=Anime News Network |accessdate=2009-04-17[/tpl] On June 28 of the same year, she gave birth to her daughter.

===Voice acting===

On the same day as submitting the application for nursing school, she went to a book store and found an advert offering free anime voice acting auditions at Arts Vision. Several months after submitting a demo tape, she received a confirmation of passing the first stage of the audition, and eventually decided to continue training as a nurse while doing voice acting.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page=18 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl][tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page=89 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] After a year of voice actor training, Hayashibara was chosen to voice small roles on Maison Ikkoku.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page=26 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] Initially, she had difficulty with her lines and had to redo many lines after the main recording sessions.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |pages=30–32 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] Hayashibara later auditioned for Ranma ½ expecting to be cast as Akane Tendo, but was cast as the female half of Ranma Saotome instead.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page=28 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] In 1993 and 1995, Hayashibara was a guest at Anime America.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.usagi.org/doi/seiyuu/hayashibara-megumi/events.html |title=Hayashibara Megumi events |publisher=Seiyuu In addition to voicing Musashi/Jessie of Team Rocket in Pokémon, Hayashibara has also voiced Ash's Pidgeotto and Pidgeot, May's Skitty, Whitney's Miltank, Clair's Dratini and Dragonair, Latios, Latias, and Anabel's Espeon in both the Japanese and English-language versions of the anime. She also provides the voice for Ai Haibara in the ongoing anime series, Detective Conan.

===DJ===

While at nursing school, she started a temporary job as a DJ at a local ice skating rink. After becoming better known as a voice actress, she was given her own Radio show, "Heartful Station".[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |pages=52–53 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl] After 17 shows, the broadcasting station cancelled the show and other anime related programming to concentrate on traditional music. However, 6 months later, Hayashibara started a new radio show at another broadcaster.[tpl]cite book |title=明日があるさ |last1=Hayashibara |first1=Megumi, |last2=Asagi |first2=Sakura |page=58 |isbn=4-04-444501-X [/tpl]

===Writing===

Hayashibara wrote a series of comics for Anime V magazine, with artwork by Sakura Asagi. The comics, known as "Megumi Toons", talked about her personal life and career. The individual chapters were collected into the book [tpl]nihongo3|"There is always Tomorrow"|明日があるさ|Ashita ga aru sa[/tpl], which has been reprinted several times.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.amazon.co.jp//404444501X/ |title=Amazon.co.jp - 明日があるさ—Sweet Time Express ちょっトク文庫版 (角川ティーンズルビー文庫) (文庫) |publisher=Amazon.co.jp[/tpl] Hayashibara has also contributed two columns to Newtype magazine; Aitakute Aitakute, and Speaking in Character. Aitakute Aitakute is a series of interviews conducted by Hayashibara with people from all walks of life. Three compilations of the column have been published. Speaking in Character has been translated into English for Newtype USA.
