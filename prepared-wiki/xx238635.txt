[[Myriangiaceae]]

CATEGORIES: Dothideomycetes, Fungi families

The Myriangiaceae are a family of fungi in the Ascomycota, class Dothideomycetes.[tpl]cite journal |last=Lumbsch TH, Huhndorf SM. |date=December 2007 |title=Outline of Ascomycota – 2007 |journal=Myconet |volume=13 |issue= |pages=1–58 |id= |url=http://www.fieldmuseum.org/myconet/outline.asp |accessdate= |publisher=The Field Museum, Department of Botany, Chicago, USA [/tpl] Species in this family have a widespread distribution (and are especially prevalent in tropical areas), and are typically found associated with scale insects.[tpl]cite book |author=Cannon PF, Kirk PM. |title=Fungal Families of the World |publisher=CABI|location=Wallingford |year=2007 |page=233 |isbn=0-85199-827-5[/tpl] The family occupies an isolated phylogenetic position within the Dothideomycetes.[tpl]cite journal |doi=10.1016/S0953-7562(08)61945-0 |author=Lumbsch HT, Lindemuth R. |year=2001 |title=Major lineages of Dothideomycetes (Ascomycota) inferred from SSU and LSU rDNA sequences |journal=Mycological Research |volume=105 |issue= 8|pages=901–908[/tpl]
