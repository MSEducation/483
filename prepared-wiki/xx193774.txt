[[Aynata]]

CATEGORIES: Bint Jbeil District, Populated places in the Nabatieh Governorate

Aynata  is a village in Lebanon. It is located in the southern portion of the country.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.boston.com/news/world/middleeast/articles/2006/08/16/reclaiming_bodies_and_shattered_lives_in_lebanon/ | work=The Boston Globe | first=Thanassis | last=Cambanis | title=Reclaiming bodies and shattered lives in Lebanon | date=16 August 2006[/tpl]  A stronghold for Hezbollah, during the war with Israel in 2006, about 60% of the homes in the town were destroyed.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2007/07/11/world/middleeast/11lebanon.html | work=The New York Times | first=Michael | last=Slackman | title=Where Outsiders, and Fear, Loom Over Daily Life | date=11 July 2007[/tpl]
The terrain consists of plateaus of varying heights, with the Aynata itself located at an elevation of 740m.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2009[/tpl]  Several valleys separate Aynata from the nearest villages.  Aynata has a moderate climate, cool summers and cold winters.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2009[/tpl]
Aynata is the birthplace of Mohammad Hussein Fadlallah.
