[[George Ewbank]]

CATEGORIES: 1839 births, 1885 deaths, English cricketers, Sussex cricketers, Cricket historians and writers, British Indian Army officers, British military personnel of the Indian Rebellion of 1857, Royal Artillery officers

George Henry Withers Ewbank (17 October 1839 – 30 April 1885) was an English cricketer. Ewbank's batting and bowling styles are unknown.
Born at Alipore in the British Raj, Ewbank made his debut in first-class cricket for a combined Gentlemen of Kent and Surrey team against the Gentlemen of England in 1855 at Lord's.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/36/36775/First-Class_Matches.html|title=First-Class Matches played by George Ewbank|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] He later played first-class cricket for Sussex, making three appearances in 1857 against the Marylebone Cricket Club, Surrey, and Kent, before making a final appearance in 1860 against the Marylebone Cricket Club. In his five appearances in first-class cricket, Ewbank scored just 10 runs from ten innings, with a high score of 6.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/36/36775/36775.html|title=Player profile: George Ewbank|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]
Ewbank served in the British Indian Army in the Raj, taking part in the Indian Mutiny (1857—58) and its aftermath. He was still serving in the Raj in 1862, as a lieutenant in the Madras Artillery.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=22621 |date=29 April 1862 |supp= |startpage=2234 |accessdate=11 August 2013 [/tpl] Between 1862 and 1868, Ewbank served in the Bengal Regiment, though by 1868 he was serving as a second—captain in the Royal Artillery.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=23379 |date=15 May 1868 |supp= |startpage=2804 |accessdate=11 August 2013 [/tpl] Ten years later he was still serving in the Royal Artillery, achieving the rank of major.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=24664 |date=31 December 1878 |supp= |startpage=7430 |accessdate=11 August 2013 [/tpl] He retired from military service in 1882.[tpl]London Gazette |issue=25145 |date=8 September 1882 |supp= |startpage=4176 |accessdate=11 August 2013 [/tpl] He died at West Norwood, Surrey on 30 April 1885. His brother, Christopher, also played first-class cricket. Ewbank's previously undiscovered biography The Ewbank Enquiry was published in 2012.[tpl]cite book|last=Allen|first=David Rayvern|title=The Ewbank Enquiry|date=25 March 2012|publisher=Sportspages UK Ltd|location=Farnham|isbn=0957189109[/tpl]
