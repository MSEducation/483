[[Sydney (ship)]]

CATEGORIES: Individual sailing vessels, Maritime incidents in 1806, Maritime history of Australia, Sailing ships, Shipwrecks of Papua New Guinea

Sydney was an East Indiaman of 900 tons that carried a crew of 130 men. The ship had been constructed in Java and was registered in Calcutta.[tpl]cite book |title=Australian Shipwrecks |volume=1: 1622-1850 |first=Charles |last=Bateson |authorlink=Charles Bateson |publisher=AH and AW Reed |location=Sydney |year=1972 |isbn=0-589-07112-2 |page=42[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Ship News |newspaper=The Sydney Gazette and New South Wales Advertiser |date=13 April 1806 |page=4 |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article627084[/tpl] Sydney sailed from Port Jackson, Australia on 12 April 1806 for Calcutta, India. On 20 May 1806, it was wrecked off the coast of New Guinea, with no crew lost. Captain Forest arrived in Calcutta on 9 October 1806 on board Varuna from Penang, having sailed from there on 4 October.[tpl]cite news |title=Ship News |newspaper=The Sydney Gazette and New South Wales Advertiser |date=15 February 1807 |page=2 |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article627394[/tpl]
