[[Enzo Moser]]

CATEGORIES: 1940 births, 2008 deaths, Italian male cyclists, People from Giovo, Sportspeople from Trentino-Alto Adige/Südtirol

Enzo Moser (5 November 1940 – 25 July 2008) was an Italian former professional 1962–1967 road bicycle racer. He was the first winner of the Giro del Trentino in 1962. He came from a famous family of cyclists, the most famous of whom is his younger brother Francesco Moser. It also includes older brother Aldo Moser, younger brother Diego and nephews Leonardo Moser and Moreno Moser, all of whom are or were professional cyclists. His achievements also include two days in the Maglia Rosa during the 1964 Giro d'Italia. He was born in Palù di Giovo, Trentino, which incidentally is also the birthplace of Gilberto Simoni. Enzo Moser was killed in an agricultural accident on 25 July 2008.
