==Career==

Kleive began to play drums in a rock and dance band, and then went to Oslo, where he studied at the Norwegian Academy of Music. He joined the jazz-rock groups Lotus (1981–83) and Oslo 13 (1981–84), and made his recording debut with the album Anti-therapy in 1983. Described as one of the "leading lights in the Oslo Nu-jazz scene",Mathieson (1 July 2008) he made his distinctive contribution to groups like Terje Rypdal's Chasers,Nicholson (May 2001) p. 69 Per Jørgensen's JøKleBa and Jon Balke's Magnetic North Orchestra as well as Marilyn Mazur & Future Song.
Kleive has released the albums, Bitt (1996–97), Generator X (2000) and Ohmagoddabl (2004) on Bugge Wesseltoft's label, Jazzland.Deutsche Nationalbibliothek
In 2010 he gave a concert as a duet with the jazz tenorist Petter Wettre. The concert was recorded and released on the album The Only Way to Travel 2 (2011), as a follow up of the 2000 volume 1 album.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.trommer.no/inlegg/trommesett/203-audun-kleive-up-close |title=Audun Kleive up-close |publisher=Trommer.no |date=2010-11-31 |accessdate=2013-10-05[/tpl])[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allaboutjazz.com/php/article.php?id=38527#.UnlQFnBWym4 |title=Petter Wettre: The Only Way To Travel 2 (2011) Track Review |first=Mark |last=Corroto |publisher=All About Jazz |date=2011-01-17 |accessdate=2013-10-05[/tpl]) In 2013 he was engaged for a set on the Music  Festival 'Punktfestivalen' in Kristiansand, where he also collaborated on recordings for the album Crime scenes (2006) with Jan Bang and Erik Honoré among others.[tpl]cite web |url=http://punktfestival.no/audun-kleive-remix-2013 |title=Audun Kleive Biography |publisher=Punktfestival.no |date=2013-07-12 |accessdate=2013-10-05[/tpl])
