[[Alfred H. Littlefield]]

Alfred H. Littlefield  (April 2, 1829 – December 21, 1893) was an American businessman and politician. He was a member of the Rhode Island House of Representatives and the Rhode Island Senate, and was the 35th Governor of Rhode Island.

==Early life==

Littlefield was born in Scituate, Providence County, Rhode Island on April 12, 1829.[tpl]cite book|last=National biographical publishing Company|title=The Biographical Cyclopedia of Representative Men of Rhode Island, Page 282, Issue 589|year=1881|publisher=National biographical publishing Company|page=518|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=rZklAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA518-IA127&dq=alfred+h+littlefield+governor+rhode+island&hl=en&sa=X&ei=GO82U5PtMKiz2gWu74CQCQ&ved=0CF4Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=alfred%20h%20littlefield%20governor%20rhode%20island&f=false[/tpl] In 1831, his family moved to Warwick, Rhode Island where he attended the common schools. He worked at a dry goods store in Central Falls, Rhode Island before working at the Littlefield Manufacturing Company. He became a partner in the company, eventually serving as president.[tpl]cite web|url=  http://archiver.rootsweb.ancestry.com/th/read/RIGENWEB/1999-08/0933653846 |title=  RIGENWEB-L Archives |publisher = Ancestry.com |accessdate= March 29, 2014[/tpl] He helped found the Pawtucket Hair Cloth Company in 1861, and served as director until his death.[tpl]cite book|last=Foster|first=E. Everton|title=Lamb's Textile Industries of the United States: Embracing Biographical Sketches of Prominent Men and a Historical Resume of the Progress of Textile Manufacture from the Earliest Records to the Present Time, Volume 1|year=1916|publisher=James H. Lamb,|page=366|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fq4yAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA366&dq=alfred+h+littlefield+Pawtucket+Street+Railway&hl=en&sa=X&ei=YfA2U5GXEYWG2wX63oHIBg&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=alfred%20h%20littlefield%20Pawtucket%20Street%20Railway&f=false[/tpl]
In 1864, he served as the division inspector of the Rhode Island Militia, and was promoted to colonel. He served in the militia for five years.[tpl]cite book|last=J.H. Beers & Company|title=Representative Men and Old Families of Rhode Island: Genealogical Records and Historical Sketches of Prominent and Representative Citizens and of Many of the Old Families|year=1908|publisher=J.H. ==Political career==

Littlefield began his political career as a member of the Lincoln Town Council in 1873, and was reelected to that office four times.[tpl]cite book|last=Bayles|first=Richard Mather|title=History of Providence County, Rhode Island, Volume 2|year=1891|publisher=W. W. Preston|page=462|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=nM84AQAAMAAJ&pg=PA462&lpg=PA462&dq=alfred+h+littlefield+governor+rhode+island&source=bl&ots=wfoTJrO7-V&sig=s-EMskTb-O4u9rhV8yBoW93ITwU&hl=en&sa=X&ei=EeQ2U6vkLo3NsQS8hYLADA&ved=0CEYQ6AEwAw#v=onepage&q=alfred%20h%20littlefield%20governor%20rhode%20island&f=false[/tpl] He was a member of the Rhode Island House of Representatives in 1876 and 1877, and served in the Rhode Island Senate in 1878 and 1879.[tpl]cite book|last=National biographical publishing Company|title=The Biographical Cyclopedia of Representative Men of Rhode Island, Page 282, Issue 589|year=1881|publisher=National biographical publishing Company|page=518|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=rZklAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA518-IA127&dq=alfred+h+littlefield+governor+rhode+island&hl=en&sa=X&ei=GO82U5PtMKiz2gWu74CQCQ&ved=0CF4Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=alfred%20h%20littlefield%20governor%20rhode%20island&f=false[/tpl]
He was elected as Republican candidate to the governor's office, serving as the 35th Governor of Rhode Island from May 25, 1880 to May 29, 1883.[tpl]cite web|url=  http://www.worldstatesmen.org/US_states_O-R.html|title=  U.S. States  O-R |publisher = World Statesman |accessdate= March 29, 2014[/tpl] He succeeded fellow Republican Charles C. Van Zandt, who did not run for re-election in 1880. Littlefield beat the Democratic candidate Horace A. Kimball by 10,224 votes against 7,440, with 5,047 votes going to the Prohibition candidate Albert C. Howard. Republican Henry H. Fay became Lieutenant Governor.Electing State Officers.; the Rhode Island Legislature Chooses a Republican Governor and Lieutenant Governor. New York Times, May 26, 1880.
During his administration, the boundary line between Rhode Island and Massachusetts was established. He was succeeded May 29, 1883, by fellow Republican Augustus O. Bourn.
After leaving the governorship. Littlefield returned to his business interests. He was president of the First National Bank of Pawtucket, the Pawtucket Gas Company and the Pawtucket Street Railway in Pawtucket.[tpl]cite book|last=Foster|first=E. Everton|title=Lamb's Textile Industries of the United States: Embracing Biographical Sketches of Prominent Men and a Historical Resume of the Progress of Textile Manufacture from the Earliest Records to the Present Time, Volume 1|year=1916|publisher=James H. Lamb,|page=366|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fq4yAQAAMAAJ&pg=PA366&dq=alfred+h+littlefield+Pawtucket+Street+Railway&hl=en&sa=X&ei=YfA2U5GXEYWG2wX63oHIBg&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=alfred%20h%20littlefield%20Pawtucket%20Street%20Railway&f=false[/tpl]

==Family life==

Littlefield was the son of John and Deborah (Himes) Littlefield.[tpl]cite web|url=  http://archiver.rootsweb.ancestry.com/th/read/RIGENWEB/1999-08/0933653846 |title=  RIGENWEB-L Archives |publisher = Ancestry.com |accessdate= March 29, 2014[/tpl] On February 9, 1853 Littlefield married Rebecca Jane Northrup. They had four children: Ebenezer N. Littlefield, Minnie J. Littlefield, George H. Littlefield and Alfred  H. Littlefield.[tpl]cite book|last=Bayles|first=Richard Mather|title=History of Providence County, Rhode Island, Volume 2|year=1891|publisher=W. W.
