[[Edward D. Libbey House]]

CATEGORIES: National Historic Landmarks in Ohio, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Ohio, Houses completed in 1895, Houses in Lucas County, Ohio, Buildings and structures in Toledo, Ohio, Shingle Style houses

Edward D. Libbey House, also known as Toledo Society for the Handicapped, is a building in Toledo, Ohio.  It is a Shingle style home designed by architect David L. Stine and built in 1895.
It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1983.[tpl]Cite document|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Register of Historic Places Inventory-Nomination: Edward D. Libbey House / Toledo Society for the Handicapped|32 KB[/tpl]|date=December, 1977 |author=Ralph J. Christian |publisher=National Park Service|postscript=[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying five photos, exterior and interior, from 1977|32 KB[/tpl]
It is located at 2008 Scottwood Avenue in Toledo, within the Old West End District, also listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
