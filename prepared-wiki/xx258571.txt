Young-ja Lee (born 4 June 1931) is a South Korean music educator and composer. She is considered the most important Korean woman composer of our time.[tpl]citation |title=Korean women's voice: The vocal music of Young-Ja Lee|author=Cho, Kyoungwha|date=2012[/tpl]

==Life and career==

Born in Wonju, she studied at Ewha Womans University, the Conservatoire de Paris, and the Royal Conservatory of Brussels. She continued her education at the Manhattan School of Music. Lee endured hardships during the Japanese occupation and Korean War, but emerged to become one of the dominant forces in Korean music in the 20th century.[tpl]citation |title=Celebrating women composers|author=University of South Florida|date=2012[/tpl] 
She was one of the six founding members of the Korean Society of Women Composers and served as the first president for the organization. She is often called the "Face and Mother of Korean Modern Music."[tpl]citation |title=Young-Ja Leeâ€™s Lyric Songs on Poems by Nam-Jo Kim (Life of Korean Women Artists)|author=Cho, Kyoung|date=2011[/tpl] Her works have been performed internationally[tpl]cite book |title=A Guide to Piano Music by Women Composers: Women born after 1900|author=Dees, Pamela Youngdahl|year=2004[/tpl][tpl]cite book |title=International who's who in classical music|publisher=Europa Publications Limited|year=2007[/tpl] and are available as recordings.[tpl]citation |title=Composers on Record: An Index to Biographical Information on 14,000 Composers Who Have Been Recorded|author=Green, Frank|date=1985[/tpl]
Young-Ja Lee's music combines elements from French, West African, Indonesian gamelan, and traditional Korean music, creating unusual intercultural compositions. She is also noted for her particular mixtures of Western and Korean music.[tpl]citation |title=The Pianist's Bookshelf: A Practical Guide to Books, Videos, and Other Resources|author=Hinson, Maurice|date=1998[/tpl]
