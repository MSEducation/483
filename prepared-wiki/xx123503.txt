[[Matthew Halliday]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1987 births, People from Norwich, English footballers, Norwich City F.C. players, Hereford United F.C. players, Torquay United F.C. players, The Football League players

Matthew Halliday (born 23 January 1987) is an English professional footballer who currently plays for Lowestoft Town. Halliday was born in Norwich and joined Norwich City as a trainee. In February 2006 he joined Hereford United on loan, although this was technically classed as work experience due to him still being a trainee.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/h/hereford_united/4739152.stm|title=Canaries teenager joins Hereford|date=22 February 2006|newspaper=BBC Sports|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl] He made the bench at Hereford, but did not make a first team appearance for the Bulls. Along with fellow trainees Andrew Cave-Brown, Robert Eagle and Andrew Fisk, Halliday was offered a professional contract in April 2006.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/4873758.stm|title=Norwich quartet offered pro deals|date=3 April 2006|newspaper=BBC Sports|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl] All four, plus a further trainee Michael Spillane signed their professional contracts on 1 July 2006.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/5136294.stm|title=Three-year pro deal for Spillane|date=1 July 2006|newspaper=BBC Sports|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl]
Halliday made his debut for the Norwich first team as a late substitute in a League Cup match against Torquay United on 23 August 2006, when he replaced injured captain Adam Drury.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.soccerbase.com/results3.sd?gameid=493028|title=Torquay 0–2 Norwich|newspaper=Soccerbase|date=23 August 2006|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl] He was unable to break into the first team again in the 2006/07 season however and joined Torquay on loan on 25 January 2007 along with teammate Rossi Jarvis.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/t/torquay_united/6298321.stm|title=Torquay set to sign Canary duo|date=26 January 2007|newspaper=BBC Sports|accessdate=16 June 2011[/tpl] He made his league debut, for Torquay, on 10 February 2007, playing in the 1–0 defeat away to Lincoln City.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.soccerbase.com/results3.sd?gameid=509820|title=Lincoln 1–0 Torquay|newspaper=Soccerbase|date=10 February 2007|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl] He played twice more for Torquay within the next ten days, in defeats at home to Hartlepool United[tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.soccerbase.com/results3.sd?gameid=513143|title=Torquay 0–1 Hartlepool|newspaper=Soccerbase|date=17 February 2007|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl] and away to Rochdale[tpl]Cite news|url=http://www.soccerbase.com/results3.sd?gameid=513132|title=Rochdale 2–0 Torquay|newspaper=Soccerbase|date=20 February 2007|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl] before returning to Norwich at the end of his one-month loan.[tpl]Cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/t/torquay_united/6397727.stm|title=Loan trio in departure from Gulls|publisher=BBC Sport|date=26 February 2007|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl]
At the end of the 2006–07 season, Halliday signed a new one-year contract,[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/6623441.stm|title=Quintet are released by Canaries|publisher=BBC Sport|date=5 May 2007|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl] however his contract was not renewed beyond the end of the 2007–08 season and he was one of nine players released by manager Glenn Roeder.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/n/norwich/7385477.stm|title=canaries release forward Huckerby|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=9 January 2009|date=6 May 2008[/tpl] He had trials in 2008 pre-season with Cambridge United and Northampton Town,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.northamptonchron.co.uk/cobblers/Triallist-earns-another-go-against.4328979.jp|title=Triallist earns another go against Baggies|publisher=Northampton Chronicle & Echo|date=28 July 2008|accessdate=9 January 2009[/tpl] but in September 2008 signed for Wroxham. In the summer of 2009 he signed for Lowestoft Town following the club's promotion to the Isthmian League Division One North.
