[[Greenpoint Reformed Church]]

CATEGORIES: Churches in Brooklyn

Greenpoint Reformed Church is a Reformed Church in America church at 136 Milton Street in the Greenpoint neighborhood of Brooklyn, New York.[tpl]cite web|title=Directory of Brooklyn Churches|url=http://www.brooklynchurches.org/directory/congregations/DQC2612.htm|publisher=Brooklyn Council of Churches|accessdate=7 May 2013[/tpl]  As of March 2012, it had a congregation of 80 people.[tpl]cite news|last=Fraser|first=Lisa A.|title=Greenpoint Reformed Church food pantry gives to record number of people|url=http://www.queensledger.com/view/full_story/17882267/article-Greenpoint-Reformed-Church-food-pantry-gives-to-record-number-of-people-|accessdate=7 May 2013|newspaper=Queens Ledger|date=15 March 2012[/tpl]
Its fireplace tilework includes historic porcelain plaques depicting Robert Fulton and Robert Livingston.  According to a curator at the Metropolitan Museum of Art, the Union Porcelain Factory in Greenpoint probably manufactured the plaques in the 1870s.  The congregation has used the current building since it purchased it in 1942.[tpl]cite web|title=Greenpoint Reformed Church|url=http://www.nycago.org/Organs/Bkln/html/GreenpointRef.html|work=The New York City Organ Project|publisher=The New York City Chapter of the American Guild of Organists|accessdate=7 May 2013[/tpl]
The Reverend Ann Kansfield has been one of the church's pastors since 2003.[tpl]cite news|last=Cohn|first=David|title=Don't Ask. Don't Tell. Just Pray.|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2006/05/28/nyregion/thecity/28past.html|accessdate=17 March 2013|newspaper=The New York Times|date=28 May 2006[/tpl] Her partner the Rev. Jennifer Aull has been co-pastor since 2006.[tpl]cite news|last=Wakefield|first=Kylie Jane|title=Greenpoint Reformed Church Co-Pastor Jennifer Aull|url=http://www.greenpointnews.com/news/3290/greenpoint-reformed-church-co-pastor-jennifer-aull|accessdate=7 May 2013|newspaper=Greenpoint Gazette|date=6 May 2011[/tpl]   Kansfield and Aull were cofounders of the group Room For All.[tpl]cite web|last=Paarlberg|first=Marilyn|title="We Have Faith" includes RfA Couple|url=http://www.roomforall.com/news/we-have-faith-includes-rfa-couple/|work=News|publisher=Room For All|accessdate=18 March 2013[/tpl]
The church hosts a dinner and soup kitchen for its local community.[tpl]cite web|title=Greenpoint Church Dinner/Pantry|url=http://greenpointchurch.org/emergency-needs/|accessdate=17 March 2013[/tpl] The church's food pantry program began in October 2007; it offers groceries to individuals and families, most of whom live in northern Brooklyn.  A homeless shelter briefly opened at Greenpoint Reformed Church in November 2012, but a few months later moved to another neighborhood church.[tpl]cite news|last=Furfaro|first=Danielle|title=Homeless shelter moves from Greenpoint Reformed Church to Church of Ascension|url=http://www.brooklynpaper.com/stories/36/7/dtg_greenpointhomelesssheltermoves_2013_02_15_bk.html|accessdate=7 May 2013|newspaper=The Brooklyn Paper|date=13 February 2013[/tpl]
