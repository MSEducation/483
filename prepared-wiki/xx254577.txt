George E. "Eck" Allen (December 31, 1911 – September 2, 1997) was an American football player and coach of football and basketball.  He served as the head football coach at the University of Maine in 1941 and from 1946 to 1948, tallying a mark of 15–11–2.  Allen was also the head basketball coach at Brown University from 1938 to 1941 and at Maine from 1945 to 1949, compiling a career college basketball record of 73–53.

==Early life and playing career==

Allen was born in Oldtown, Kentucky and attended high school in Ashland.  He played football at the West Virginia University from 1932 to 1934.[tpl]cite news |title=MAINE PICKS ALLEN AS FOOTBALL HEAD; McLaughry's Aide at Brown Succeeds Brice -- Starred at West Virginia |author= |url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive/pdf?res=F20A17F9345F167B93C1AB178AD85F458485F9 |newspaper=The New York Times |date=January 23, 1941 |accessdate=October 22, 2011[/tpl]

==Later life and death==

After retiring from coaching, Allen worked as a salesman of materials used in steel production.  He died on September 2, 1997 at the age of 85 after a long illness.  He was a resident of Kissimmee, Florida at the time of his death.[tpl]cite news |title=ALLEN |author= |url=http://archive.osceolanewsgazette.com/index.php?option=news&task=viewarticle&sid=2388&Itemid=2 |newspaper=Osceola News-Gazette |date=September 1997 |accessdate=October 23, 2011[/tpl]
