Jack McManus (born 1984) is a BRIT School educated[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-1017939/The-big-thing-JACK-McMANUS.html|title=The next big thing: Jack McManus|date=2 May 2002|publisher=Daily Mail|accessdate=24 November 2009 | location=London | first=Adrian | last=Thrills[/tpl] singer-songwriter from West Wickham, London in England.

==Music career==

A singer who has been compared to Elton John and Billy Joel,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.examiner.co.uk/leisure-and-entertainment/2008/05/12/music-jack-mcmanus-86081-20893981/|title=Music:Jack McManus|date=12 May 2008|publisher=Huddersfield Daily Examiner|accessdate=24 November 2009[/tpl] McManus' debut album Either Side Of Midnight was released on Polydor Records/UMRL on 5 May 2008, preceded by his first single "Bang on the Piano" on 28 April. His single "You Think I Don't Care" was released on 7 July. McManus co-wrote "From The Rooftops" with Groove Armada for their Soundboy Rock album. and has toured with musicians such as John Mayer, Scouting for Girls, Amy MacDonald, and Sam Sparro. Jack is co-writer of the song "Separate Cars" on Boyzone's album, Brother.
