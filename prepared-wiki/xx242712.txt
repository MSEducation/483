==Biography==

According to Houbraken he was a pupil of Jan Davidsz de Heem, whose style he successfully copied. He earned a good living as a flower painter, but died relatively young due to his "melancholy attitude".[tpl]link language|nl|Middle Dutch[/tpl] Jacob Rotius Biography in De groote schouburgh der Nederlantsche konstschilders en schilderessen (1718) by Arnold Houbraken, courtesy of the Digital library for Dutch literature
According to the RKD he was the son of Jan Albertsz Rotius, and specialized in flower and vegetable still life paintings.Jacob Rotius in the RKD
