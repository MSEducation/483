James Manning Tyler (April 27, 1835 â€“ October 13, 1926) was an American politician, lawyer and judge from Vermont.  He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Biography==

Tyler was born in Wilmington, Vermont, son of Ephraim Tyler and Mary (Bissell) Tyler. He attended the Brattleboro Academy.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.ourcampaigns.com/CandidateDetail.html?CandidateID=127092||title =TYLER, James Manning|publisher= Our Campaigns |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl] He graduated from Albany Law School and was admitted to the bar in September 1860.[tpl]cite book|last=United States Congress|title=http://books.google.com/books?id=4w09AAAAYAAJ&pg=PA1069&lpg=PA1069&dq=James+Manning+Tyler++vermont+supreme+court&source=bl&ots=ll8c-cs2jB&sig=6v6P7lBf005L4cGCSML5D43_Y54&hl=en&sa=X&ei=KU_bUOj-DfG02AWl6YCgCA&ved=0CF8Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=James%20Manning%20Tyler%20%20vermont%20supreme%20court&f=false|year=1913|publisher=Government Printing Office|pages=1069|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=4w09AAAAYAAJ&pg=PA1069&lpg=PA1069&dq=James+Manning+Tyler++vermont+supreme+court&source=bl&ots=ll8c-cs2jB&sig=6v6P7lBf005L4cGCSML5D43_Y54&hl=en&sa=X&ei=KU_bUOj-DfG02AWl6YCgCA&ved=0CF8Q6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=James%20Manning%20Tyler%20%20vermont%20supreme%20court&f=false[/tpl] Tyler began the practice of law in Wilmington.
He served as member of the Vermont House of Representatives in 1863 and 1864, and as State's attorney in 1866 and 1867. He was a trustee of the Brattleboro Retreat from 1875 until 1926, and a Trustee of the Vermont Asylum for the Insane from 1875 until 1926.
Tyler was elected as a Republican candidate to the Forty-sixth and Forty-seventh Congresses, serving from March 4, 1879 until March 3, 1883.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/james_tyler/411017||title =Rep. James Tyler|publisher= Govtrack.us |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl] He declined to be a candidate for renomination in 1882.
After serving in Congress, he resumed the practice of his profession in Brattleboro, Vermont. Tyler was appointed a judge of the Vermont Supreme Court in September 1887 and served until his resignation on December 1, 1908.[tpl]cite book|last=United States. Government Printing Office|title=Congressional serial set|year=1913|publisher=United States. Government Printing Office|pages=1069|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=9K8qAAAAYAAJ&pg=PA1069&lpg=PA1069&dq=James+Manning+Tyler++vermont+supreme+court&source=bl&ots=t80Ce5-ewK&sig=gpDHLqQFut1yP0dSudWO3-xelRs&hl=en&sa=X&ei=KU_bUOj-DfG02AWl6YCgCA&ved=0CGIQ6AEwCA#v=onepage&q=James%20Manning%20Tyler%20%20vermont%20supreme%20court&f=false[/tpl] He served as president of the Vermont National Bank from 1917 until 1923, and as resident of the Vermont-Peoples' National Bank in 1923 and 1924.[tpl]cite web|url=http://bioguide.congress.gov/scripts/biodisplay.pl?index=W000708|title=TYLER, James Manning, (1835 - 1926)|publisher= Biographical Directory of the United States Congress|accessdate= December 26, 2012 [/tpl]
Tyler died on October 13, 1926 in Brattleboro, Vermont, and is interred in the Prospect Hill Cemetery in Brattleboro.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=22657||title = James Manning Tyler|publisher= Find A Grave |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl]
