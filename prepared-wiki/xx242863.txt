[[Wesley Newcomb]]

CATEGORIES: 1818 births, 1892 deaths, American malacologists

Wesley Newcomb (1818–1892) was an American physician and a malacologist who specialized in land snails.

==Life==

Wesley Newcomb was born in New York in 1818. His father was physician Simon Newcomb.[tpl]cite news |author= Robert Edwards Carter Stearns |date= March 1892 |title= In memoriam - Dr. Wesley Newcomb |work= The Nautilus |volume=5 |number=2 |url= http://www.archive.org/stream/nautilus59192amer#page/n153/mode/2up |pages=121–124 [/tpl] portrait. He studied medicine at the Jefferson Medical College (now Thomas Jefferson University) and Castleton Medical College in Vermont.
He traveled to San Francisco in 1849, and Honolulu, Hawaii in 1850.
He went into practice with Dr. William Hillebrand[ref]
</ref> who married his stepdaughter Anna Post on November 16, 1852.<ref>[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.ulukau.org/gsdl2.7/cgi-bin/algene?e=q&a=d&d=D10-000157&gg=1 |title= Oahu marriage record 1832-1900 |work=state archives digital collections |publisher=state of Hawaii |accessdate= 2010-01-09 [/tpl]</ref> He identified over a hundred new species of snails.<ref name="Stearns 1992"/>
In 1856 he returned to New York, and then Europe in 1857. In 1858 he practiced in Oakland, California. In 1867 his collection was bought by Ezra Cornell, and he moved to Cornell University. From 1870-1888 he was curator of the Cornell Museum.[tpl]cite web |title= Wesley Newcomb |url= http://www.inhs.uiuc.edu/~ksc/Malacologists/NewcombW.html |accessdate= 2010-01-09 |publisher= University of Illinois Natural History Survey [/tpl]
He died in Ithaca, New York on January 26, 1892.
