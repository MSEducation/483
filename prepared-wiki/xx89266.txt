[[Robin Wilson (musician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1965 births, Living people, American rock singers, Gin Blossoms members, Musicians from Arizona, Musicians from Michigan, Alternative rock singers, Alternative rock guitarists, American rock guitarists, American male singer-songwriters, American alternative rock musicians

Robin Wilson (born July 12, 1965 in Detroit, Michigan) is an American musician, most notable for his work as the lead vocalist of the rock band, Gin Blossoms.

==Career==

Wilson replaced Richard Taylor as guitarist of Gin Blossoms in 1988, but switched places early on with vocalist Jesse Valenzuela. He remained a member of the band until their breakup in 1997, during which time the band came out with two albums.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.ca/newspapers?id=40AgAAAAIBAJ&sjid=cH0EAAAAIBAJ&pg=6836,383865&dq=gin-blossoms+robin-wilson&hl=en|title=Gin Blossoms confirm breakup |date=January 1, 1998|publisher=Sarasota Herald-Tribune|accessdate=May 11, 2010[/tpl]  Their hit debut album New Miserable Experience went double platinum and featured singles "Hey Jealousy", "Found Out About You", "Mrs. Rita", "Until I Fall Away", and Wilson's "Allison Road".  Their next album, Congratulations I'm Sorry, which went platinum, featured singles "Follow You Down", "As Long as it Matters", "Not Only Numb", and "Day Job", as well as Wilson's "Highwire". After the breakup of Gin Blossoms, Wilson was the lead vocalist and songwriter for the band Gas Giants,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/gin-blossoms-singer-a-giant-again-19990812|title=Gin Blossoms Singer a Giant Again|date=August 12, 1999|publisher=Rolling Stone|accessdate=May 11, 2010[/tpl] which also included guitarist Dan Henzerling and former Gin Blossoms drummer Phil Rhodes. He also contributed vocals for The Longshadows album Simple Minded Way.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.deseretnews.com/article/1,5143,660211079,00.html|title=Longshadows album melodic, introspective|date=April 13, 2007|publisher=Deseret News|accessdate=May 11, 2010[/tpl]   
Wilson runs his own studio Uranus Recording in Tempe, Arizona, which he opened in 1994.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.eastvalleytribune.com/story/70889|title=After 10 years, Gin Blossoms have new CD|date=August 2, 2006|publisher=East Valley Tribune|accessdate=May 11, 2010[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] When the Gin Blossoms reunited in 2002, Wilson began touring with the band[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/news/story/5936587/gin_blossoms_bloom_again|title=Gin Blossoms Bloom Again|date=February 14, 2003|publisher=Rolling Stone|accessdate=May 11, 2010[/tpl][tpl]deadlink|date=November 2012[/tpl] as well as recording the album, Major Lodge Victory, that was released on August 8, 2006 and featured singles "Learning the Hard Way" and "Long Time Gone".
