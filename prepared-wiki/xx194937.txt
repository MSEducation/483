[[Twenty railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations opened in 1866, Railway stations closed in 1959, Former Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway stations, Disused railway stations in Lincolnshire

Twenty railway station served the village of Twenty, Lincolnshire. It was on the route of the Spalding and Bourne Railway (opened 1866),[tpl]cite book |last=Casserley |first=H.C. |title=Britain's Joint Lines |date=April 1968 |publisher=Ian Allan |location=Shepperton |isbn=0-7110-0024-7 |chapter=Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway |page=15 [/tpl]  later part of the Midland and Eastern Railway and then part of the Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway main line between the Midlands and the Norfolk Coast.British Railways Atlas.1947. p.17[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pastscape.org.uk/hob.aspx?hob_id=507098|title=National monument record:Station[/tpl]

==History==

The station opened with the line on 1 August 1866, closed temporarily between 9 October 1880 and 1 February 1881, and closed permanently on 2 March 1959, although the line remained opened for goods until 1964.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.leytransport.i12.com/joint.htm |title=A Midland & Great Northern Miscellany |work=The Lincolnshire & East Yorkshire Transport Review |accessdate=30 April 2008 [/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=August 2013[/tpl] The three intermediate stations between [tpl]stnlnk|Spalding[/tpl] and [tpl]stnlnk|Bourne[/tpl] had unusual names, because there were few nearby settlements; in the area there was a series of drainage ditches, the twentieth of which ran close to the station, hence the name "Twenty".[tpl]dubious|Origin of name|date=March 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite book |last=Wrottesley |first=A.J. |title=The Midland & Great Northern Joint Railway |edition=2nd |year=1981 |origyear=1970 |publisher=David & Charles |location=Newton Abbot |isbn=0-7153-8173-3 |page=23 [/tpl]
Former Services
The former station buildings are still extant, unusually for this line, and in use as commercial premises[tpl]cite web|title=Page from Rex Needle's web site on Bourne|url=http://homepages.which.net/~rex/bourne/twenty.htm[/tpl] by a well-respected Double Glazing company.
