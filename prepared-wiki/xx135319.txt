[[Ajker Kagoj]]

CATEGORIES: Publications established in 1991, Bengali-language newspapers published in Bangladesh, Defunct newspapers of Bangladesh, Media in Dhaka, 1991 establishments in Bangladesh, 2007 disestablishments in Bangladesh

Ajker Kagoj ([tpl]lang-bn|আজকের কাগজ[/tpl] "Today's Paper") was a daily Bengali language newspaper published in Dhaka, Bangladesh. The newspaper was launched in 1991 under the editorship of Naimul Islam Khan. The newspaper established the modern Bengali-language newspaper. It was closed in 2007.

==Press intimidation==

On 10 July 2004, an Ajker Kagoj reporter received a death threat describing him and the newspaper as an "enemy of Islam" and warning that he would be "killed within a month."[tpl]cite news |title=2004 News Alert |url=http://www.cpj.org/news/2004/Bangla13july04na.html |accessdate=2007-12-12 |date=2004-07-13 |work=Committee to Protect Journalists[/tpl] On 22 August 2004, Kamal Hossain, another reporter from the newspaper, was murdered while collecting data for a story on organized crime in the south-eastern town of Manikchhari.[tpl]cite web|title=Kamal Hossain|publisher=Committee to Protect Journalists |date=2004-08-22 |accessdate=2013-04-07 |url=http://cpj.org/killed/2004/kamal-hossain.php[/tpl][tpl]cite web| title=Bangladesh:Annual Report 2005| publisher=Reporters Without Borders|date=January 2005| url=http://www.rsf.org/article.php3?id_article=13423| accessdate=2007-12-12| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20071030154042/http://www.rsf.org/article.php3?id_article=13423| archivedate= 30 October 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl]

==Closed==

Ajker Kagoj was shut down on 20 September 2007 as a result of continued financial difficulties.[tpl]cite news |title=Ajker Kagoj shut down |url=http://www.asiamedia.ucla.edu/article-southasia.asp?parentid=78280 |accessdate=2007-12-12 |date=2007-09-20 |publisher=The Asia Institute, University of California |work=AsiaMedia[/tpl] In a statement, editor Kazi Sahed Ahmad promised that all 225 staff and journalists would receive their pay and entitlements.[tpl]cite news |title=Bangla daily Ajker Kagoj shut down |url=http://www.thefinancialexpress-bd.com/search_index.php?page=detail_news&news_id=11791 |accessdate=2007-12-12 |date=2007-09-20 |work=The Financial Express[/tpl]
