[[27th Cavalry Regiment]]

CATEGORIES: Buffalo Soldier units and formations, Cavalry regiments of the United States Army, Black history in the United States military, Military units and formations established in 1943

The 27th Cavalry Regiment[tpl]#tag:ref|The unit's official designation was 27th Cavalry Regiment (Horse) (Colored)|group=nb[/tpl] was a short-lived African-American unit of the United States Army.  The regiment was formed as part of the 2nd Cavalry Division in 1943 and inactivated in north Africa in 1944 without seeing combat.2nd Cavalry Division

==History==

The regiment was formed at Fort Clark, Texas on 25 February 1943 and assigned to the 5th Cavalry Brigade of the 2nd Division.  The regiment shipped out on 28 February 1944 from Hampton Roads, Virginia.  A little over two weeks after arrival in Algeria, the 2nd Cavalry Division and its component regiments were inactivated.  Troops from the 27th Cavalry were given the option of transferring to service units or volunteering for combat but at the loss of all rank.27th Cavalry Some troops from the 27th Cavalry were used to activate the 6404th Port Battalion.World War II Order of Battle, p. 316.
The regiment was formally disbanded on December 12, 1951.[tpl]cite book |title=We Remember: U.S. Cavalry Association |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=qrJz6KdRJfgC&lpg=PA55&ots=JhUXSfNsQD&dq=27th%20cavalry%20disbanded%201951&pg=PA55#v=onepage&q&f=false |accessdate=3 February 2013 |year=1996 |publisher=Turner Publishing Company |isbn=9781563113185 |page=55 [/tpl]
