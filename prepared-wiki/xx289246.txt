[[J. Clarence Kind House]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Reno, Nevada, Cotswold architecture, Houses completed in 1934, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Nevada, National Register of Historic Places in Washoe County, Nevada, Tudor Revival architecture in the United States

The J. Clarence Kind House, at 751 Marsh Ave. in Reno, Nevada, United States, is a historic Tudor Revival-style house that was built in 1934.  Also known as the William Forman Home, it was listed on the National Register of Historic Places (NRHP) in 2005.
It is a large, [tpl]convert|3700|ft|adj=on[/tpl] house.  According to its NRHP nomination, it was deemed significant "for its role in Reno's community planning and development history" and "as an excellent local example of the Tudor/Cotswold Cottage style of architecture, within the broader category of Period Revival, and as representing the work of two local master architects, Russell Mills and Edward Parsons."[tpl]cite web|url=http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Text/05001121.pdf |title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: J. Clarence Kind House / William Forman Home |author=Antoinette M. Harsh |year=2005 |publisher=National Park Service[/tpl] and accompanying six photos
