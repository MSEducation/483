[[PhotoSensitive (organization)]]

CATEGORIES: Non-profit organizations based in Canada, Charities based in Canada

PhotoSensitive is a Canadian nonprofit collective with the goal of spreading social awareness through black-and-white photography. Based in Mississauga, Ontario, Canada, it was founded by Toronto Star photographer Andrew Stawicki and Toronto Star photo editor Peter Robertson.[tpl]cite journal |last1= Quinn |first1= Mark|last2= |first2= |year= 1994|title= Just what was seen|journal= Family Practice|volume= 6|issue= 29|pages=2,  45–6|publisher= |doi= |url= [/tpl] PhotoSensitive has raised funds for various charities, including donations to Daily Bread Food Bank and The Hospital for Sick Children.[tpl]cite news |first= Alfred|last= Holden| url= http://www.thestar.com/news/insight/2010/09/12/the_camera_as_a_catalyst_for_social_change.html|title= The camera as a catalyst for social change|work= Toronto Star|date=12 September 2010 |accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl] More than 100 Canadian professional and amateur photographers have participated in PhotoSensitive projects.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= | url= http://arts.nationalpost.com/2011/11/28/photo-gallery-the-fuel-of-life-exhibit-by-photosensitive/|title= Photo Gallery: The Fuel of life exhibit, by PhotoSensitive|work= Toronto Star|date=28 November 2011 |accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news |first= Francine|last= Kopun|url= http://www.thestar.com/news/2008/05/17/the_healing_lens.html|title= The healing lens|work= Toronto Star|date=17 May 2008 |pages= ID1, ID4[/tpl] In 2010, PhotoSensitive celebrated their 20 year anniversary.

==Exhibits==

===Overview===

===It's In Their Eyes===

PhotoSensitive's first project, It's In Their Eyes, started in 1990 focused on Toronto's hungry, homeless and under-housed population. Twelve photographers in partnership with Daily Bread Food Bank created a photo essay that documented the people Daily Bread served.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=10[/tpl] The exhibit launched on April 5 in 1992.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= In Search of Dignity|work= Toronto Sun|date=5 April 1992 |pages= 8–9[/tpl]

===Precious Time===

The next project, Precious Time, was a partnership between PhotoSensitive and The Hospital for Sick Children.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=18[/tpl] SickKids Hospital gave PhotoSensitive's twelve members 24-hour access to the hospital for eight months.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= Anxious moments for a father|work= Toronto Star|date=9 October 1994 |page= A1[/tpl][tpl]cite journal |first= Jeff|last= Brooke|title= PhotoSensitive|journal= News Views|volume= 21|issue= 4| page= 12|publisher= Eastern Canadian News Photographers Association[/tpl] The 120 photo exhibit was launched in 1994 with the goal to raise money for SickKids' Herbie Fund.[tpl]cite journal |first= Jeff|last= Brooke|title= PhotoSensitive|journal= News Views|volume= 21|issue= 4| pages= 11–13|publisher= Eastern Canadian News Photographers Association[/tpl]

===Them = Us===

Launched in 1997,[tpl]cite book |last= Callwood|first= June|authorlink= |title= Harmony|year= 1998|publisher= The Harmony Movement & MacMillan Canada|location= Toronto|isbn= 0-7715-7632-3|page=x[/tpl] Them = Us was organized by the Harmony Movement,[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= How we get along|work= Toronto Star|date=30 November 1997 |pages= F4–5[/tpl] and curated by Vancouver-based artist Tom Graff.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.harmony.ca/diversity-education/past-projects/them-us/|title= Them = Us Project|last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |year= 2012|website= Harmony Movement|publisher= |accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] PhotoSensitive invited twelve new photographers to the collective.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=36[/tpl] Them = Us was PhotoSensitive's first nation-wide project. The National Movement for Harmony in Canada  published a Them = Us photo book titled Harmony in 1998.
In 2008, Them = Us was exhibited at the Canadian Association of Statutory Human Rights Agencies annual conference.

===Braille = Equality===

Braille = Equality was created in 2000 in a partnership between PhotoSensitive and the CNIB (the Canadian National Institute for the Blind).[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=46[/tpl] It was launched on February 9, 2000 on Parliament Hill.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= The dots that open doors|work= Toronto Star|date=5 February 2000 |page= H5[/tpl] Since its launch, Braille = Equality has been shown across Canada, in the United States and in Melbourne, Australia.

===Child Poverty: A National Disgrace===

Starting in 2000, Child Poverty: A National Disgrace was a partnership between PhotoSensitive and Campaign 2000 in response to rising child poverty in Canada.[tpl]cite news |first= Allison|last= Dunfield|title= In 1989 we were one in seven living in poverty. Now we're at almost one in five.|url= http://www.theglobeandmail.com/news/national/in-1989-we-were-at-one-in-seven-children-living-in-poverty-now-were-at-almost-one-in-five/article1035016/ |work= Globe and Mail|date=27 November 2001 |page= [/tpl] The exhibit of 70 images by 24 photographers launched on November 26, 2001.[tpl]cite news |first= Vena|last= Eaton|title= Poverty's Children|work= Toronto Sun|date=25 November 2001 |pages= 52–3[/tpl]

===Destination Toronto===

In response to Toronto's outbreak of SARS, PhotoSensitive documented the vibrance of the city as Destination Toronto.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=76[/tpl] 
David Miller and James Bartleman launched in the Brookfield Place in February, 2004.[tpl]cite news |first= Tyler|last= Hamilton|title= Picture perfect view of Toronto|work= Toronto Star|date=2 February 2004 |page= B2[/tpl]

===Life of Water===

Life of Water was the first exhibit to be launched with a book.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=86[/tpl] PhotoSensitive sought to document the entirety of water's impact on Canadians. PhotoSensitive invited photographers from across Canada to start shooting for Life of Water in 2004.[tpl]cite news |first=Samantha |last=Grice |title= Liquid Assets|work= National Post|location= Toronto|date=7 September 2005 |pages= AL8[/tpl] The Brookfield Place was launched by David Ramsay[tpl]disambiguation needed|date=February 2014[/tpl], former Canadian Minister of Natural Resources, and saw 160 black-and-white photographs on September 7, 2005.[tpl]cite news |first=Isabel |last= Teotonio|title= Liquid assets: a portrait|work= Toronto Star|date=7 September 2005 |page= B2[/tpl] The Life of Water book contains an essay by David Suzuki.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Life of Water|url= |accessdate= |year= 2005|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 0-9687061-1-8|pages=74–5, 140[/tpl]

===Summer of Hope===

During the summer of 2005, James Bartleman, former Lieutenant Governor of Ontario, launched reading camps in five First Nations communities—North Caribou Lake, Kingfisher Lake, Muskrat Dam, Neskantaga and Fort Albany.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=104[/tpl]—to fight high youth suicide rates.[tpl]cite news |first= Louise|last= Brown|title= A boon for native schools|work= Toronto Star|date=8 April 2005 |page= B5[/tpl] James Bartleman secured a grant from the Ontario Trillium Foundation for PhotoSensitive to document the Summer of Hope

===Vibrant Communities in Focus===

Created in 2007, Vibrant Communities in Focus celebrated the 25th anniversary of the Ontario Trillium Foundation.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=134[/tpl] Twenty-seven photographers visited twenty-seven of the Trillium Foundation's most successful agencies and non-profit organizations from across Ontario.[tpl]cite book |last= |first= |authorlink= |title= Vibrant Communities|url= |accessdate= |year= 2007|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-2-1|page=5[/tpl]
Vibrant Communities in Focus exhibition was opened in Toronto and then traveled across Ontario.

===Living With===

On Tuesday 4, December 2007 seven PhotoSensitive photographers left to spend ten days in Rwanda to shed light on the state of HIV/AIDS in the country.[tpl]cite news |first= Emily|last= Mathieu|title= Photographers to focus on Rwanda|work= Toronto Star|date=2 December 2007[/tpl] Living With is the collective's third international project and was in partnership with Carleton University and the National University of Rwanda.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=144[/tpl] The 50 photo exhibit launched in Brookfield Place on July 22, 2008.[tpl]cite news |first= Paola|last= Loriggio|title= Remains of Rwanda's genocide|work= Toronto Star|date=22 July 2008 |page= AA1[/tpl]

===Cancer Connections===

Started in early 2008, Cancer Connections was shot by PhotoSensitive in partnership with the Canadian Cancer Society and dedicated to June Callwood.[tpl]cite book |last= Campbell|first= Alannah|authorlink= |title= Field of View|url= |accessdate= |year= 2010|publisher= PhotoSensitive|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-9687061-4-5|page=164[/tpl] The project called for submissions from not only photographers, but ordinary Canadians that had a story to tell.[tpl]cite book |last=Burns |first=James |authorlink= |title= Cancer Connections|url= |accessdate= |year= 2011|publisher= John Wiley & Sons Canada|location= Mississauga|isbn= 978-0-470-96449-1|page=8[/tpl] Cancer Connections contained 1,000 photos from all walks a life across Canada.[tpl]cite news |first= Antonia|last= Zerbisias|title= Cancer A thousand perspectives|work= Toronto Star|date=30 May 2008 |pages= A8, A9[/tpl] Cancer Connections launched on May 20, 2008 in Nathan Phillips Square, Toronto, Ontario. The final national show was held on June 10, 2010 in Major's Hill Park, Ottawa, Ontario.[tpl]cite news |first= Antonia|last= Zerbisias|url= |title= Cancer A thousand perspectives|work= Toronto Star|date=30 May 2008 |pages= A8, A9[/tpl]

===TIEd Together===

PhotoSensitive partnered with Prostate Cancer Canada to create TIEd Together to raise awareness for prostate cancer.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.photosensitive.com/pc/|title= TIEd Together|last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |year= 2010|website= PhotoSensitive|publisher= |accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] The exhibit featured photographs of men who were fighting or who had survived prostate cancer, including Jack Layton.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= Avenues|work= National Post|location= Toronto|date=10 January 2012 |pages= AL8[/tpl]

===Kids Who Can===

Over the summer of 2011, PhotoSensitive partnered with Easter Seals (Canada) to capture the experiences of children with disabilities.[tpl]cite news |first= Tralee|last= Pearce|title= Scenes from the 'Best Camp in the World'|work= The Globe of Mail|location= Toronto|date=27 February 2012 |page= L5[/tpl] For Kids Who Can, PhotoSensitive had twenty-five photographers on assignment to twelve Easter Seals camps.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |url= http://www.thestar.com/news/insight/2012/02/25/photosensitive_exhibit_titled_kids_who_can_aims_to_raise_public_awareness.html|title= The power of pictures|work= Toronto Star|date=25 February 2012 |page= IN4–5[/tpl] The tour was launched on June 18, 2012 at the Mic Mac Mall, Halifax, Canada.[tpl]cite web |url= http://easterseals.ca/english/kids-who-can|title= Kids Who Can|last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date= 5 June 2012|website= Easter Seals|publisher= |accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]

===Picture Change===

Launched on July 16, 2013 at the Royal Bank Plaza, Picture Change began in 2012,[tpl]cite web |url= http://npac.ca/2012/11/photosensitive-picture-change/|title= PhotoSensitive - Picture Change|last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date=14 November 2012 |website= News Photographers Association of Canada|publisher= |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] and is the collection of images chosen by the photographers that involved social change.[tpl]cite news |first= |last= |title= Picture change|work= Toronto Star|date=20 July 2013 |pages= WD7[/tpl] PhotoSensitive sent an invitation to over 100 top Canadian photographers to create the exhibit, including Doug Ball, Peter Martin and Paul Watson.[tpl]cite web |url= http://j-source.ca/article/picture-change-exhibit-features-100-best-photos-canadas-top-photographers|title= Picture Change exhibit features 100 of the best photos from Canada's top photographers|last1= Bregg|first1= Peter|last2= |first2= |date=24 July 2013 |website= J-Source|publisher= Canadian Journalism Project|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
A Picture Change book was published accompanying the opening. It is currently touring Toronto, Canada.
