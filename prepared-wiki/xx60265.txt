</ref> The original plan for a freeway along Route 29 started in the 1930s, with no progress until the 1950s, when construction began on [[John Fitch Parkway]] named after [[John Fitch (inventor)|John Fitch]]. Construction of this alignment used up much of the available waterfront in Trenton, and by 1969, after Route 29 was realigned onto the new freeway, Route 175 was designated on the former alignment.<ref name=nycroads>[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nycroads.com/roads/NJ-29/ |title=NJ 29 Freeway|author=Anderson, Steve|publisher=Eastern Roads|accessdate=September 23, 2009[/tpl]</ref><ref name="chevron">[tpl]cite map|publisher=[[Chevron Oil Company]]|title= Map of New Jersey |year=1969|cartography=[[H.M. Gousha]][/tpl]</ref> Route 175 was designated as a No Passing Zone by the [[New Jersey Department of Transportation]] on September 14, 1982.<ref name="npz">[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.state.nj.us/transportation/refdata/traffic_orders/nopass/rt175.shtm|title=No Passing Zones - Route 175|year=1982|publisher=New Jersey Department of Transportation|pages=1|accessdate=September 23, 2009[/tpl]</ref> The two highways (Route 175 and Route 29) have remained intact since.<ref name="2007map">[tpl]cite map|title=New Jersey Official Road Map|year=2007|publisher=New Jersey Department of Transportation|cartography=New Jersey Department of Transportation[/tpl]</ref>
