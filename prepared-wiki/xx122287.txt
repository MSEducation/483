[[Jack Rabbit (Seabreeze)]]

CATEGORIES: Roller coasters in New York, Roller coasters introduced in 1920

Jack Rabbit is an "out and back" wooden roller coaster located at Seabreeze Amusement Park in Irondequoit, New York.  At its opening in 1920, it was the fastest roller coaster in the world. The Giant Dipper in Santa Cruz, California, superseded it in 1924. Jack Rabbit is the fourth oldest operating roller coaster in the world[tpl]cite web |url=http://napha.org/nnn/LIBRARY/FactsFigures/WorldsOldestOperatingRollerCoasters/tabid/71/Default.aspx |accessdate=2008-06-21 |title=National Amusement Park Historic Association, World's Oldest Operating Roller Coasters[/tpl] and the second oldest in the USA. The oldest, Leap-The-Dips in Altoona, Pennsylvania, was closed from 1985 to 1999, making Jack Rabbit the oldest continuously operating coaster in the country.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cbsnews.com/stories/2010/08/07/earlyshow/saturday/main6752303.shtml?tag=cbsnewsTwoColUpperPromoArea |title=Five Best Roller Coasters in the U.S. |first=Peter |last=Greenberg |authorlink=Peter Greenberg |date=August 7, 2010 |publisher=CBS News.com |archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5roMNnYjH |archivedate=August 7, 2010 |accessdate=August 7, 2010[/tpl]
Jack Rabbit has been described as a hard ride to learn to operate; there are several brake levers, and it takes a while to learn the proper timing and sequence to accurately stop the train at the platform.[tpl]Citation needed|date=November 2007[/tpl]
