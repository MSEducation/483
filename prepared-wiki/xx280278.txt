[[Jeffrey Amestoy]]

CATEGORIES: 1946 births, People from Rutland County, Vermont, Vermont Attorneys General, Chief Justices of the Vermont Supreme Court, Living people, John F. Kennedy School of Government alumni

Jeffrey Amestoy (born July 24, 1946) is a former Chief Justice of the Vermont Supreme Court.
Born in Rutland, Vermont, Amestoy received a B.A. from Hobart College, in Geneva, New York; his Juris Doctor degree from Hastings Law School, University of California, San Francisco; and his Master of Public Administration degree from the John F. Kennedy School of Government, Harvard University. He served in the United States Army Reserve, 1968-74.  
From 1984 to 1997, Amestoy served seven terms as Attorney General of Vermont. On January 10, 1997, Governor Howard Dean appointed Amestoy to the Vermont Supreme Court, following the retirement of Chief Justice Frederic W. Allen.Office of the Vermont Secretary of State, Vermont State Archives and Records Administration, "Justices of the Supreme Court, 1778–Present, p. 12. As Chief Justice, Amestoy authored the unanimous opinion of the Vermont Supreme Court in Baker v. Vermont, 744 A.2d 864 (Vt. 1999), which held that the state's denial of marriage rights to same-sex couples violated the Vermont Constitution. The court ordered the Vermont legislature to either allow same-sex marriages or implement an alternative legal mechanism according similar rights to same-sex couples.[tpl]cite news|last=Goldberg|first=Carey|title=Vermont Panel Shies From Gay Marriage |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2000/02/10/us/vermont-panel-shies-from-gay-marriage.html |accessdate=July 13, 2013|newspaper=New York Times|date=February 10, 2000[/tpl]
Amestoy retired from the court on June 16, 2004, and was succeeded as Chief Justice by Paul L. Reiber.Office of the Vermont Secretary of State, Vermont State Archives and Records Administration, "Justices of the Supreme Court, 1778–Present, p. 13.
