[[Kesgrave]]

CATEGORIES: Towns in Suffolk, Civil parishes in Suffolk

Kesgrave is a small town in the English county of Suffolk on the eastern edge of Ipswich.

==History==

===Early history===

The town was recorded as Gressgrava in the Domesday Book, by the late 15th century its name had become Kesgrave.  Kesgrave remained a small agricultural settlement with just a church, inn and a few farmsteads for over 700 years.
In 1921 the population was only 103 housed in 20 dwellings. Since then great changes have taken place.

===Recent history===

By 1988 Kesgrave covered an area of more than [tpl]convert|800|acre|ha sqmi[/tpl].

==Schools==

Kesgrave High School is a large 11-18 comprehensive co-educational school with nearly 2000 pupils. A study for Sustrans noted that 61% of the pupils cycled to the school.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.saferoutestoschools.org.uk/index.php?p=CS1|title=Safe Routes To Schools - Case Studies - Kesgrave High School|accessdate=2008-03-01 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20070928001007/http://www.saferoutestoschools.org.uk/index.php?p=CS1  |archivedate = 2007-09-28[/tpl] This is largely due to the installation of a large cycle lane through the local housing development and along the main road. The school actively encourages walking or cycling and provides bicycle storage facilities.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.saferoutestoschools.org.uk/index.php?p=n44|title=Kesgrave High School - How we did it!|year=2005|accessdate=2008-03-01[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
