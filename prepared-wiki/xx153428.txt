He was, without doubt, one of the game's greatest ever second-row forwards and represented Great Britain, England, and Cumberland on many occasions, and also toured Australasia in 1932 and 1936 with the Great Britain tourists.
A goal-kicking [tpl]rlp|SR[/tpl]-forward, Hodgson signed for Swinton in January 1927 aged 17. He still holds the long distance penalty goal record with a kick of 77.75 yards for Swinton against Rochdale Hornets at the Athletic Grounds, Rochdale in April, 1940.
