[[Shirley Stobs]]

CATEGORIES: 1942 births, Living people, American female freestyle swimmers, Former world record holders in swimming, Olympic gold medalists for the United States, Olympic medalists in swimming, Olympic swimmers of the United States, Sportspeople from Miami, Florida, Swimmers at the 1959 Pan American Games, Swimmers at the 1960 Summer Olympics

Shirley Anne Stobs (born May 20, 1942) is an American former competition swimmer, Olympic champion, and former world record-holder.  She competed at the 1960 Olympic Games in Rome, Italy, where she received a gold medal as a member of the winning U.S. team in the women's 4×100-meter freestyle relay, together with her teammates Joan Spillane, Carolyn Wood and Chris von Saltza.  She and her relay teammates set a new world record of 4:08.9 in the event final."1960 Summer Olympics – Rome, Italy – Swimming" – databaseOlympics.com (Retrieved on April 29, 2008)
