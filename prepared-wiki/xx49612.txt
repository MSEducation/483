[[Leonidas Pyrgos]]

Leonidas Pyrgos (born 1871 in Mantineia, Arcadia) was a Greek fencer. 

==Career==

Pyrgos was the first Greek Olympic medallist in the history of the modern Olympic Games, winning his fencing event of the 1896 Summer Olympics on 7 April 1896.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/py/leon-pyrgos-1.html |title=Leonidas Pyrgos Olympic Results |accessdate=2010-03-31 |work=sports-reference.com[/tpl]David Wallechinsky (2004), The Complete Book of the Olympics 2004. Aurum Press. p.627. His competitor in the event, which consisted of a single bout of foil fencing to three touches, was one of the best fencers in the world, the Frenchman Jean Maurice Perronet. After a close contest, Pyrgos won 3-1. The crowd cheered the first Greek Olympic medallist. He was lifted onto their shoulders and carried throughout the streets of Athens.
