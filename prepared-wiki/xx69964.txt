[[Eleanor Lambert]]

CATEGORIES: 1903 births, 2003 deaths, American centenarians, American fashion designers, People from Montgomery County, Indiana, Herron School of Art and Design alumni

Eleanor Lambert Berkson (August 10, 1903 – October 7, 2003) was a central figure in the American fashion and public relations industry.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.valuewalk.com/2013/11/eleanor-lambert-demand-creator/ | title=Stahl Case Study: Eleanor Lambert Demand Creator | publisher=ValueWalk LLC | date=18 November 2013 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.britannica.com/EBchecked/topic/914905/Eleanor-Lambert | title=Eleanor Lambert | publisher=Encyclopaedia Britannica | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/10/08/nyregion/eleanor-lambert-empress-of-fashion-dies-at-100.html | title=Eleanor Lambert, Empress of Fashion, Dies at 100 | publisher=The New York Times | date=October 8, 2003 | accessdate=21 November 2013 | author=NEMY, ENID[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Lambert was born in Crawfordsville, Indiana.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl] She attended the John Herron School of Art and the Chicago Art Institute to study fashion.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl]  Lambart wanted to be a sculptor but went into advertising.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/1443712/Eleanor-Lambert.html | title=Eleanor Lambert Obituary | publisher=The Telegraph newspaper | date=10 October 2003 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl] She started at an advertising agency in Manhattan, dealing mostly with artists and art galleries.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl]
She was married twice, firstly to Wills Conner, in the 1920s,[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/10/08/nyregion/eleanor-lambert-empress-of-fashion-dies-at-100.html | title=Eleanor Lambert, Empress of Fashion, Dies at 100 | publisher=The New York Times | date=October 8, 2003 | accessdate=21 November 2013 | author=NEMY, ENID[/tpl] which ended in divorce and secondly to Seymour Berkson[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl] in 1936, which ended with his death in 1959.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/1443712/Eleanor-Lambert.html | title=Eleanor Lambert Obituary | publisher=The Telegraph newspaper | date=10 October 2003 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl] Eleanor and Seymour had one son together, the renowned poet Bill Berkson.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/obituaries/1443712/Eleanor-Lambert.html | title=Eleanor Lambert Obituary | publisher=The Telegraph newspaper | date=10 October 2003 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl] She died in Manhattan.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.nytimes.com/2003/10/08/nyregion/eleanor-lambert-empress-of-fashion-dies-at-100.html | title=Eleanor Lambert, Empress of Fashion, Dies at 100 | publisher=The New York Times | date=October 8, 2003 | accessdate=21 November 2013 | author=NEMY, ENID[/tpl]

==Career==

In the mid 1930s, Lambert was the first Press Director of the Whitney Museum of American Art and helped with the founding of the Museum of Modern Art.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.valuewalk.com/2013/11/eleanor-lambert-demand-creator/ | title=Stahl Case Study: Eleanor Lambert Demand Creator | publisher=ValueWalk LLC | date=18 November 2013 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl] Jackson Pollock, Jacob Epstein, and Isamu Noguchi were a few of the many artists she represented.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl]
In the 1940s, Lambert founded the International Best Dressed List, the Coty Fashion Critics’ Award (in 1943), and New York Fashion Week.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.valuewalk.com/2013/11/eleanor-lambert-demand-creator/ | title=Stahl Case Study: Eleanor Lambert Demand Creator | publisher=ValueWalk LLC | date=18 November 2013 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2110513_2110630_2110690,00.html | title=Eleanor Lambert | publisher=Time magazine | date=April 2, 2012 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Skarda, Erin[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/gioia-diliberto/eleanor-of-seventh-avenue_b_268619.html | title=Eleanor Of Seventh Avenue: Where Fashion Week Came From | publisher=Huffington Post | date=2009-09-09 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Diliberto, Gioia[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.loc.gov/today/pr/2012/12-004.html | title=Author to Discuss Fashion Industry Impresario Eleanor Lambert, Feb. 2 | publisher=Library of Congress | date=January 3, 2012 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl] 
In 1959 and 1967, she was asked by the US Department of State to present American fashion for the first time in Russia, Germany, Italy, Australia, Japan, Britain, and Switzerland.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.valuewalk.com/2013/11/eleanor-lambert-demand-creator/ | title=Stahl Case Study: Eleanor Lambert Demand Creator | publisher=ValueWalk LLC | date=18 November 2013 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://content.time.com/time/specials/packages/article/0,28804,2110513_2110630_2110690,00.html | title=Eleanor Lambert | publisher=Time magazine | date=April 2, 2012 | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Skarda, Erin[/tpl]
In 1965, she was appointed by President Lyndon Johnson to the National Council on the Arts of the National Endowment for the Arts.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl]  In 1962, she organized the Council of Fashion Designers of America (CFDA)  and stayed an honorary member until her death in 2003.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl]
In 2001, the CFDA created The Eleanor Lambert Award, that is presented for a “unique contribution to the world of fashion and/or deserves the industry’s special recognition.”[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl]  Months before she died, she had left her International Best Dressed List to four of Vanity Fair’s editors.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl] Shortly after her last public appearance at New York Fashion Week in September, Lambert died in 2003 at the age of 100.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cfda.com/about/history#! | title=Eleanor Lambert believed in destiny | publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America | accessdate=20 November 2013 | author=Tiffany,  John[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=7959373 | title=Eleanor Lambert (1903-2003)| publisher=Find a Grave.com | date=October 8, 2003 | accessdate=21 November 2013 | author=Moody, Ron[/tpl] Shortly after her death her grandson, Moses Berkson, completed a documentary film film about her life.

===Economic effects of career===

One source credits Lambert as "she was a factor in the gross domestic product of the U.S., and even of the world" for her influnance in the fashion industry.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.frmocorp.com/_content/essays/Eleanor_Lambert_July_2011.pdf | title=Eleanor Lambert, Demand Creator | publisher=HORIZON RESEARCH GROUP | date=July 2011 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl] Lambert's influence is described as exogenous event risk in mathematical modeling.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.frmocorp.com/_content/essays/Eleanor_Lambert_July_2011.pdf | title=Eleanor Lambert, Demand Creator | publisher=HORIZON RESEARCH GROUP | date=July 2011 | accessdate=20 November 2013[/tpl]
