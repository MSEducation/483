[[Broad chiton]]

CATEGORIES: Callochitonidae

The broad chiton, Callochiton castaneus, is a species of chitons in the family Callochitonidae. It is a marine mollusc. It is endemic to South Africa.

==Distribution==

This species is found from Port Nolloth to Kosi Bay, subtidally to around 5 m.Branch, G.M., Branch, M.L, Griffiths, C.L. and Beckley, L.E (2005): Two Oceans: a guide to the marine life of southern Africa ISBN 0-86486-672-0

==Description==

The broad chiton is a flattened chiton with granular dark-brown to orange-red valves. It can grow up to 60 mm in total length. The girdle is velvety.Kilburn, R. and Rippey, E. Sea Shells of Southern Africa MacMillan South Africa 1982 ISBN 0-86954-094-7

==Ecology==

This is a solitary animal. It is more frequently seen during night dives.
