Edward Donaghy, known as Eddy Donaghy or Ted Donaghy (born 8 January 1900) was an English association football player and coach.

==Career==

===Playing career===

Born in Grangetown, Donaghy played for the area's local club before playing with Football League teams Middlesbrough, Bradford City, Derby County and Gillingham.[tpl]cite book|first=Michael|last=Joyce|title=Football League Players' Records 1888 - 1939|publisher=Soccerdata|year=2004|isbn=1-899468-67-6|page=76[/tpl]  
Donaghy, a left half, made a total of 13 appearances in the Football League for Bradford City between May 1923 and May 1926.[tpl]cite book|first=Terry|last=Frost|title=Bradford City A Complete Record 1903-1988|publisher=Breedon Books Sport|year=1988|isbn=0-907969-38-0|page=381[/tpl]Frost, p. 395
He left Gillingham in 1928 to play in France,[tpl]cite book|first=Roger|last=Triggs|title=The Men Who Made Gillingham Football Club|publisher=Tempus Publishing Ltd|year=2001|isbn=0-7524-2243-X|page=16[/tpl] where he played for Cannes alongside fellow Englishman Stan Hillier.[tpl]cite journal|url=http://www.soccer-history.co.uk/frenchmenance.pdf|title=THE FRENCH MENACE; THE MIGRATION OF BRITISH PLAYERS TO FRANCE IN THE 1930s|issue=18|publisher=Soccer History[/tpl]

===Coaching career===

Donaghy coached Dutch side Feyenoord between 1931 and 1935, winning two league titles.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.feyenoord.nl/pages/story/s2/de+club+-+historie+-+wonderelixer.aspx|title=DE TRAINERS - WONDERELIXER|language=Dutch|accessdate=18 March 2013|publisher=Feyenoord.nl[/tpl]
