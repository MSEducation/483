[[Dorchester North Burying Ground]]

CATEGORIES: Cemeteries in Boston, Massachusetts, Historic districts in Massachusetts

The Dorchester North Burying Ground (or "First Burying Ground in Dorchester") is a historic graveyard at Stoughton Street and Columbia Road in the Dorchester neighborhood of Boston, Massachusetts.
The burial ground was established in 1634, as the front sign reads[tpl]cite web|title=Photo of Burying Ground Sign|url=http://image1.findagrave.com/photos/2008/18/CEM46889224_120076986090.jpg|publisher=Find a Grave|accessdate=27 November 2012[/tpl] and was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1974.[tpl]cite book|last=Flynn|first=Raymond L.|title=Historic Burying Grounds Report And Inventory: October, 1986|year=1986|publisher=Mayor of Boston; contained in Boston Public Library|location=Boston|url=http://archive.org/stream/reportinventoryv00bost#page/n7/mode/2up[/tpl]  The burying Ground is surrounded by a wall of concrete, with cut-out sections containing iron fencing along Columbia Road, which replaced a 19th-century decorative iron and granite fence. The original gates still provide entrance and are signified by large commemorative bronze tablets placed by the city in 1883.[tpl]cite web|title=Dorchester North Burying Ground|url=http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=cr&CRid=1963908|publisher=Find A Grave|accessdate=27 November 2012[/tpl] The site contains over 1200 markers, many of early Dorchester settlers.[tpl]cite web|title=Trees & Gardens - An Upham's Corner Photo Tour 2011  Dorchester North Burying Ground|url=http://www.uphamscornernews.com/trees-gardens-dorchester-north-burying-gd.html|publisher=Upham's Corner News Online|accessdate=27 November 2012[/tpl]
