[[Other Black]]

CATEGORIES: Black British people

Other Black is an ethnicity category used by the Office for National Statistics in the United Kingdom. It is one of three "Black or Black British" ethnicity options used in ONS statistics, alongside Black African and Black Caribbean.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.statistics.gov.uk/census2001/profiles/commentaries/ethnicity.asp|title=Census 2001 – Ethnicity and religion in England and Wales|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=2009-10-20[/tpl]
The term was used in the 1991 UK Census and the 2001 UK Census. According to the 2001 Census, 97,585 UK residents selected the Other Black category to describe their ethnicity.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.statistics.gov.uk/cci/nugget.asp?id=455|title=Population size: 7.9% from a non-White ethnic group|date=2004-01-08|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=2009-10-20[/tpl]
As of 2009, ONS estimates suggest that approximately 124,500 Other Black people are now resident in England.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.neighbourhood.statistics.gov.uk/dissemination/LeadTableView.do;jsessionid=ac1f930c30d7a9b3b4ddc44246f3a06eb5ef91b25d0e?adminCompAndTimeId=25403%3A280&a=7&b=276743&c=london&d=13&r=1&e=13&f=24438&o=254&g=325264&i=1001x1003x1004x1005&l=1809&m=0&s=1256049731939&enc=1&nsjs=true&nsck=true&nssvg=false&nswid=1276|title=Resident population estimates by ethnic group, all persons: May 2011|publisher=Office for National Statistics|accessdate=2011-05-30[/tpl]
