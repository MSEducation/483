[[Fawaz Akhras]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Syrian Muslims, People from Homs, Assad family

Fawas Akhras ([tpl]lang-ar|فواز أخرس[/tpl]) is a London, England-based, British Syrian cardiologist.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/world/bashar-al-assads-father-in-law-fawaz-akhras-cant-hide-on-houla/story-fnb64oi6-1226377816163 | title=Bashar al-Assad's father-in-law Fawaz Akhras can't hide on Houla | publisher=The Australian | date=June 1, 2012 | accessdate=July 14, 2012 | author=Fletcher, Martin[/tpl] He is a founding Director of the British Syrian Society and is involved with a number of Syrian causes.Bashar al-Assad's UK gatekeeper 'only wants to build bridges' | World news | The Guardian He is currently a consultant interventional cardiologist at the Cromwell Hospital in South Kensington, London. He also practices at his private medical clinic in Harley Street, London.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fawazakhras.com/about.asp |title=Dr. Fawaz Akhras |publisher=Fawazakhras.com |date= |accessdate=2012-05-18[/tpl] He lives in Acton, London and is married to former diplomat Sahar Otri al-Akhras. Their daughter, Asma al-Assad is married to Syrian president Bashar al-Assad.
It was reported before the Syrian civil war that he had inﬂuence on the Syrian president in domestic affairs.[tpl]cite journal|last=Bar|first=Shmuel|title=Bashar's Syria: The Regime and its Strategic Worldview|journal=Comparative Strategy|year=2006|volume=25|page=381|url=http://www.herzliyaconference.org/_Uploads/2590Bashars.pdf|accessdate=8 July 2012[/tpl] On 15 March 2012, The Guardian published allegedly intercepted emails that it claimed appeared to show that he was advising the Syrian President from the UK during the crackdown on anti-regime protestors.[tpl]cite news|author=Robert Booth |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/world/2012/mar/15/assad-emails-father-in-law-crackdown?intcmp=239 |title=Assad emails: father-in-law gave advice from UK during crackdown|publisher=The Guardian |date= 2012-03-15|accessdate=2012-05-18 |location=London[/tpl] According to The Guardian, Dr Fawas Akhras used a private email channel to the Syrian leader to offer advice on how the regime should spin its suppression of the uprising, including how best to rebut apparent video footage of Syrian forces torturing children.
