[[Kamoshika]]

CATEGORIES: Named passenger trains of Japan, East Japan Railway Company, Railway services introduced in 1986

The [tpl]nihongo|Kamoshika|かもしか||lit. "Japanese Serow"[/tpl] was a limited express train service in Japan operated by East Japan Railway Company (JR East) which ran between [tpl]ja-stalink|Akita[/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Aomori[/tpl] via the Ōu Main Line.[tpl]cite book | title = JR新幹線＆特急列車ファイル |trans_title= JR Shinkansen & Limited Express Train File| publisher = Kōtsū Shimbun | year = 2008 | location = Japan | page = 80| isbn = 978-4-330-00608-6[/tpl] The service was discontinued from December 2010.

==Service pattern==

There were three services in each direction daily, stopping at the following stations.JR Timetable, March 2010 issue, p.100

==Rolling stock==

Trains were normally formed of 3-car 485 series EMUs based at Akita depot, sometimes strengthened to 6 cars during busy seasons. Green car (first class) accommodation was included in Car 1.

==History==

The Kamoshika was first introduced on 1 November 1986 as an express service operating between [tpl]ja-stalink|Kami-Suwa[/tpl], [tpl]ja-stalink|Iida[/tpl], and [tpl]ja-stalink|Nagano[/tpl]. This service was discontinued from the start of the revised timetable on 13 March 1988.[tpl]cite book | title = 列車名鑑1995 |trans_title= Train Name Directory 1995| publisher = Railway Journal| date = August 1995 | location = Japan| page = 124| isbn = [/tpl]
The Kamoshika name was revived from March 1997 as a limited express service operating between Akita and Aomori, replacing the former Tazawa limited express service which ran between Morioka and Aomori via Akita before the Akita Shinkansen was opened.[tpl]cite book | title = こだわりの新幹線＆特急列車ガイド |trans_title= In-depth Shinkansen & Limited Express Guide| publisher = Ikaros Publishing| date = August 2000 | location = Japan| page = | isbn = 4-87149-284-2[/tpl]
From the start of the revised timetable on 4 December 2010, the Kamoshika services were discontinued, replaced by Tsugaru services, formerly operating between [tpl]ja-stalink|Hachinohe[/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Hirosaki[/tpl], which were rerouted to operate between Aomori and Akita.[tpl]cite journal| title = 12月4日ダイヤ改正後の青森地区列車運転体系|trans_title=　Operating pattern of Aomori area trains following 4 December timetable revision | journal = Tetsudō Daiya Jōhō Magazine | volume = 39| issue = 320| page = p.24–25| publisher = Kōtsū Shimbun | location = Japan | date = December 2010[/tpl]
