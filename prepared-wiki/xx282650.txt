[[Decorated kofun]]

CATEGORIES: Kofun, Japanese painting, Japanese sculpture, Kofun period

[tpl]nihongo|Decorated kofun|装飾古墳|sōshoku kofun[/tpl] is the term used for kofun or ancient Japanese tombs ornamented with painted or carved decoration. The tombs take the form of tumuli or earthen mounds piled over stone chambers as well as caves excavated from the living rock. The decoration may be on the inner walls, on stone screens, on sarcophagi or, in the case of cave tombs, around the entrance on the exterior. ==Motifs==

Decorative motifs include the chokkomon (an X-shape forming triangular zones that intersect irregular curves), circles, concentric circles, triangles, human figures, horses, birds, boats, swords, shields, and quivers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://kyuhaku.jmc.or.jp/index.php/1/-/-/-/-/-/-/m_frame.php |title=文様 |trans_title=Decorated Tomb Database: Motifs |language=Japanese |publisher=Kyushu National Museum |accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]Japanese terms: [tpl]nihongo|chokkomon|直弧文|chokkomon[/tpl]; [tpl]nihongo|circles|円文|enmon[/tpl]; [tpl]nihongo|concentric circles|同心円文|dōshin-enmon[/tpl]; [tpl]nihongo|triangles|三角文|sankaku-mon[/tpl]; [tpl]nihongo|boats|船|fune[/tpl]; [tpl]nihongo|rings with feet|双脚輪状文|sōkyakurinjō-mon[/tpl]
