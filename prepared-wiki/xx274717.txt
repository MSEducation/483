[[Trans Namib Ground]]

CATEGORIES: Cricket grounds in Namibia, Buildings and structures in Windhoek

Trans Namib Ground (also known as the Centre for Cricket Development Ground) is a cricket ground in Windhoek, Namibia.  The first recorded match on the ground was in 1998 when a Windhoek Select XI played Denmark.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/19/1341_misc.html|title=Other matches played on Trans Namib Ground, Windhoek|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl] 
The ground held its first List A match in the 2001/02 6 Nations Challenge when Canada played the Netherlands.  To date the ground has held 11 List A matches, the last of which saw Argentina play Uganda in the 2007 ICC World Cricket League Division Two.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/19/1341_a.html|title=List A Matches played on Trans Namib Ground, Windhoek|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]
