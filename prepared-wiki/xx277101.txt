[[RJ Haddy]]

CATEGORIES: Participants in American reality television series

Robert "RJ" Haddy is a special effects artist and airbrush dealer. He is best known as a contestant on season 2 of Syfy's reality television game show, Face Off in which he was a finalist and fan favorite for the season. He returned as a veteran contestant on season 5 of Face Off, but was eliminated in episode 5.

==Biography==

Haddy is a native of Charleston, West Virginia, and is a graduate of Capital High School.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.rjhaddy.com/about-rj/ | title= About RJ | publisher = RJ Haddy official site | accessdate=September 15, 2013[/tpl]  While at the Joe Blasco Make-Up Center, Haddy worked for Alterian Studios under Tony Gardner, which allowed him to work on films such as Batman & Robin, There's Something About Mary, and Contact. Haddy later returned to Charleston to Capital High School, where he taught television production, writing and performance for the camera, special effects, web and app design, and filmmaking. He also wrote and designed the curriculum.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fangoria.com/index.php/moviestv/fearful-features/7288-qface-offq-top-three-rj-haddy|title="Face Off": Top Three, R.J. Haddy|date=June 22, 2012|last=Abley|first=Sean|publisher=The Brooklyn Company, Inc.|work=Fangoria|accessdate=August 8, 2013[/tpl]
Haddy is currently working in a special effects company owned by fellow Face Off contestant Eric Fox.

===Face Off===

After the first season of Syfy's reality television game show, Face Off, Haddy auditioned for season 2 at San Diego Comic-Con International in 2011. Winning the final spot on the show after competing against two other potential contestants, he finished as one of runners-up.
