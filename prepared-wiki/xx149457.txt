[[Roma Tiburtina railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Rome, Railway stations opened in 1866

Roma Tiburtina is the second largest railway station in Rome, after Roma Termini. Located in the north-eastern part of the city, it is being redeveloped as a hub for the Italian high-speed rail services instead of Termini, which is a terminal station.

==History==

A fire broke out in the relay room on the west side of the station on 24 July 2011, at 4 am.[tpl]cite news|title=Incendio Roma Tiburtina, Fs: "limitare i viaggi"|date=24 July 2011|work=Cronaca|location=Rome|language=Italian|url=http://www.cronacalive.it/incendio-roma-tiburtina-fs-limitare-i-viaggi.html|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl] 
At 6am, water was cut from five neighbourhoods in order to facilitate efforts by the fire brigade to end the fire.[tpl]cite news|title=Dal Salario al Nomentano cinque quartieri senz'acqua|date=24 July 2011|first=Chiara|last=Piselli|work=La Repubblica|language=Italian |url=http://roma.repubblica.it/cronaca/2011/07/23/news/dal_salario_al_nomentano_cinque_quartieri_senz_acqua-19533736|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl] 
The metro line B was also temporarily closed between Castro Pretorio and Monti Tiburtini.[tpl]cite news|title=Incendio Roma Tiburtina, riaperta in parte metro B|date=24 July 2011|work=Cronaca|location=Rome|language=Italian|url=http://www.cronacalive.it/incendio-roma-tiburtina-riaperta-in-parte-metro-b.html|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl] 
As the fire broke out in the relay room, most controls for tracks and traffic signals were rendered unusable, leaving a vital part of the Italian rail network disabled with significant train delays throughout the country.[tpl]cite news|title=Spento l'incendio alla stazione Tiburtina Nel caos l'intera rete ferroviaria|date=24 July 2011|work=Corriere Della Sera|location=Rome|language=Italian|url=http://roma.corriere.it/roma/notizie/cronaca/11_luglio_24/roma-tiburtina-stazione-1901158926090.shtml|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Caos Tiburtina, ritardi in tutta Italia|date=24 July 2011|work=Corriere Della Sera|language=Italian|url=http://roma.corriere.it/roma/notizie/cronaca/11_luglio_24/tiburtina-disagi-ritardi-tutta-italia-1901160225274.shtml|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Incendio Stazione Tiburtina, caos trasporti Stop treni e metro, ipotesi dolo|date=24 July 2011|work=Il Messaggero|location=Rome|language=Italian|url=http://www.ilmessaggero.it/articolo.php?id=157290|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl] 
The damage to the structure has rendered the building in danger of collapse, with problems and delays going on for long time after the accident.
After 3 years of work, on 28 November 2011 the new station was inaugurated. The new Tiburtina is dedicated to the traditional regional trains and to the high-speed rail services on the Milan-Naples line. The new station is expected to reach a daily ridership of over 450,000 by 2015.[tpl]cite news|title=Roma Tiburtina (quasi) completa|url=http://www.cityrailways.it/home/2011/11/28/roma-tiburtina-quasi-completa.html|accessdate=28 November 2011|newspaper=iBinari CityRailways|language=Italian|date=28 November 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Tiburtina, 140 treni al giorno per l'Alta Velocità : 330 milioni di costi, 20 binari e 50mila mq|url=http://roma.corriere.it/roma/notizie/cronaca/11_novembre_28/numeri-stazione-tiburtina-1902336937919.shtml|accessdate=28 November 2011|newspaper=Corriere della Sera Roma|language=Italian|date=28 November 2011[/tpl] 
The station is served by 140 high-speed trains and 290 regional trains every day.

==Connections==

Roma Tiburtina station is served by Tiburtina, a station on Line B of the Rome metro. The station also features a large and important bus station that serves both national and international destinations, such as Kiev.[tpl]cite web|title=Bus charter da Roma Tiburtina a Kiev|date=24 October 2010|publisher=Voli Charter 24|language=Italian |url=http://www.volicharter24.it/voli/aereoporto_bus_charter_da_roma_tiburtina_a_kiev_.asp|accessdate=24 July 2011[/tpl]
