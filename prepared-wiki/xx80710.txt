[[Chenghai District]]

CATEGORIES: Chenghai District, Shantou, County-level divisions of Guangdong

Chenghai (Chinese Postal Map Romanisation: Tenghai; [tpl]zh|c=澄海[/tpl]; Hanyu Pinyin: Chénghǎi; Teochew: Thěng Hài) is a district of the city of Shantou, Guangdong Province, People's Republic of China.
It is the birthplace of Qin Mu, and Zheng Yong, the father of Taksin who was a Thai king.
It is located in the southeast part of Guangdong Province. Chenghai was a town founded since 1563 AD, under Teochew jurisdiction, controlled by Shantou City from 1983 to 1994. From 1994 onward, Chenghai was upgraded to a county-level city and was administrated by the Provincial Government directly. When in 2005, Chenghai was absorbed as a district by Shantou City, it had established its reputation as a world-class toy manufacturing hub.[tpl]Citation needed|date=September 2009[/tpl]
Major industries: toys, handicrafts, clothing.
