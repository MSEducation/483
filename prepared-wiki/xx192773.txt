[[Shire of Barrabool]]

CATEGORIES: Former local government areas of Victoria (Australia)

The Shire of Barrabool was a local government area about [tpl]convert|100|km|mi|0[/tpl] southwest of Melbourne, the state capital of Victoria, Australia. The shire covered an area of [tpl]convert|575.18|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and existed from 1853 until 1994.

==History==

Barrabool was incorporated as the second road district in the colony on 28 December 1853, and became a shire on 13 June 1865. On 31 May 1927, it annexed part of the Shire of Winchelsea. Its shire offices were located on Grossmans Road, near Surf Coast Highway, Torquay, although almost all of Torquay was within the City of South Barwon.[tpl]cite book|title=Victorian Municipal Directory|year=1992|publisher=Arnall & Jackson|location=Brunswick|pages=590â€“591[/tpl] Accessed at State Library of Victoria, La Trobe Reading Room.
On 18 May 1993, parts of the shire were annexed to the newly created City of Greater Geelong. On 9 March 1994, the Shire of Barrabool was abolished, and, along with the remainder of the City of South Barwon and parts of the Shire of Winchelsea, was merged into the newly created Surf Coast Shire.
