[[Herman Melville House (Troy, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: National Register of Historic Places in Rensselaer County, New York, Houses completed in 1786, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Herman Melville, Houses in Rensselaer County, New York

Herman Melville House is a historic home located at Lansingburgh in Troy, Rensselaer County, New York.  It was a home of author Herman Melville between 1838 and 1847.   It was originally built about 1786 and substantially remodeled in the Late Victorian style about 1872. It is a [tpl]frac|2|1|2[/tpl]-story, brick and timber frame dwelling with a gable roof.  It has a 2-story rear wing.  The building is preserved and maintained by the Lansingburgh Historical Society.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=7624|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Herman Melville House|date=May 1992|accessdate=2011-01-13 |author=Kathleen LaFrank|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=7596|title=Accompanying eight photos[/tpl] 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1992.
