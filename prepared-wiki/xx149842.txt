[[Ruth Root]]

CATEGORIES: School of the Art Institute of Chicago alumni, 20th-century American painters, 21st-century American painters, Living people, 1967 births, American women painters

Ruth Root (born 1967, Chicago, US) is an artist based in New York. She graduated from Brown University, Rhode Island, in 1990, and completed her MFA at the Art Institute of Chicago in 1993. She attended a residency at the Skowhegan School of Painting and Sculpture in 1994.Ruth Root, biography at Andrew Kreps Her work includes paintings on thin, irregularly shaped pieces of aluminiumRoberta Smith, Art in Review: Ruth Root, New York Times, May 9, 2003. and small painted paper pieces.Holland Cotter, Art in Review: Ruth Root, New York Times, March 26, 1999.
She has participated in a number of exhibitions including Abstract Redux at the Danese Gallery in New York, Kosmobiologie at the Bellwether Gallery in Brooklyn, and Son-of-a-Guston at Clementine Gallery in New York.  She has exhibited internationally at galleries such as Galerie Nikolaus Ruzicska in Austria, Galeria Marta Cervera in Spain, Seattle Art Museum, and Galleria Franco Noero, in Torino, Italy.
Her works are held in the collections of the Museum of Modern Art,moma.org Seattle Art Museum,seattleartmuseum.org Los Angeles County Museum of Art,lacma.org Walker Art Center,walkerart.org and Hirshhorn Museum and Sculpture Garden.hirshhorn.si.edu
She is represented by the Andrew Kreps Gallery in New York, Maureen Paley in London and Galerie Nikolaus Ruzicska in Salzburg.
