[[Bridgewater Railroad Station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Railway stations opened in 1901, Buildings and structures in Oneida County, New York, Stations along Delaware, Lackawanna and Western Railroad lines

Bridgewater Railroad Station is a historic train station located at Bridgewater in Oneida County, New York. It was built in 1901 and is a one story, rectangular, timber frame building 20 feet by 91 feet.  It was built by the short line Unadilla Valley Railway and also served the separate line of the Delaware, Lackawanna and Western Railroad branch to Richfield Springs, New York  It ceased use as a station in 1960 and is now home to the Bridgewater Historical Society.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=101543|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Bridgewater Railroad Station|date=December 2005|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=Raymond W. Smith|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=101545|title=Accompanying five photos[/tpl] 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2006.
