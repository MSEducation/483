[[Randy Adams]]

CATEGORIES: 1952 births, Living people, Massachusetts Institute of Technology alumni, Businesspeople from San Francisco, California, People from Bar Harbor, Maine

Randy Adams (born 1952 in Bar Harbor, Maine) is an American serial entrepreneur funded by Sequoia Capital in Menlo Park, California in the Silicon Valley, and a founder of eleven venture capital backed companies including Will Ferrell's popular comedy site, Funny or Die.AlarmClock Media ReferenceAlwaysOn Hollywood and Marlon Wayans' comedy site What The Funny, where he is currently CEO."Marlon Wayans Launching Urban Funny or Die (Exclusive)" Hollywood Reporter. Retrieved January 9, 2014
He was educated at Mount Desert Island High School in Bar Harbor before moving on to MIT, where he graduated as a Bachelor of Science in Electrical Engineering.
Adams is credited with managing the team which invented Adobe Acrobat and PDF,Boosman Blog helping Yahoo secure initial financing with Sequoia Capital,Jerry Yang and creating the first e-commerce web site, the Internet Shopping Network Internet Shopping Network in 1993.
Adams has lectured at Stanford University on the art of being an entrepreneur.Educators Corner
His former positions include software architect  at NeXT Inc.,  CEO, Emerald City Software, Director of Engineering at Adobe Systems, CEO of Appsoft, Inc., CEO of the Internet Shopping Network, CEO of Navitel, Inc., CEO of NewsNet, CEO of AuctionDrop, Inc.,American Ventures Magazine CEO of Searchme, and member of the board of directors at Yahoo.
He lives in the Malibu, California with his wife and seven children.
