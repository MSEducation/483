[[Walter Babington Thomas]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, British Army generals, Royal Hampshire Regiment officers, Companions of the Order of the Bath, Companions of the Distinguished Service Order, Recipients of the Military Cross and Bar, Living people, British military personnel of the Mau Mau Uprising

Major-General Walter Babington 'Sandy' Thomas [tpl]post-nominals|CB|DSO|MC[/tpl] (born 1919) is a former Commander of British Far East Land Forces.

==Military career==

Brought up in New Zealand, Sandy Thomas was commissioned into the New Zealand Military Forces at the outbreak of World War II.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=37669|supp=yes|startpage=3882|date=26 July 1946|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] He was wounded, became a prisoner of war in Crete, escaped from a prison hospital and then fled to Syria.Copyright Publishing After the War he obtained a commission in the Royal Hampshire Regiment and, in 1955, he was mentioned in dispatches while dealing with the Mau Mau Uprising in Kenya.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=40538|supp=yes|startpage=4154|date=15 July 1955|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] He was appointed General Officer Commanding 5th Division in 1968[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=44558|supp=yes|startpage=3863|date=29 March 1968|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] and Chief of Staff at Headquarters Far East Land Forces in April 1970[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=45078|supp=|startpage=4173|date=10 April 1970|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] before being elevated to be General Officer Commanding Far East Land Forces in October 1970.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=45219|supp=yes|startpage=11755|date=23 October 1970|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl]
He was awarded the CB in 1971[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=45384|supp=yes|startpage=5958|date=4 June 1971|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] and retired in 1972.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=45586|supp=yes|startpage=1271|date=31 January 1972|accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl]
