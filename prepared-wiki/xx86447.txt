[[Bagh-e-Jinnah, Lahore]]

CATEGORIES: Lahore, Gardens in Pakistan, Botanical gardens in Pakistan, Parks in Pakistan, Test cricket grounds in Pakistan, Visitor attractions in Lahore, Muhammad Ali Jinnah, Cricket grounds in Pakistan

Bagh-e-Jinnah (Punjabi, [tpl]lang-ur|[tpl]Nastaliq|باغ جناح[/tpl][/tpl])  (or Jinnah's Garden) is a historical park in the city of Lahore, Pakistan.[tpl]cite web|last=Google maps|title=Location of Bagh-e-Jinnah|url=https://maps.google.com/maps?f=q&source=s_q&hl=en&geocode=&q=Bagh-e-Jinnah,+Mall+Road,+Lahore,+Punjab,+Pakistan&aq=0&oq=bagh+e+&sll=31.558352,74.35564&sspn=0.089081,0.169086&vpsrc=0&ie=UTF8&hq=&hnear=&t=m&z=16&iwloc=A&cid=10005608633564160880|publisher=Google maps|accessdate=24 September 2013[/tpl] It was formerly known as Lawrence Gardens. Today, the large green space contains a botanical garden, Masjid Dar-ul-Islam,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.islamicfinder.org/getitWorld.php?id=45526 |title=Jamia Masjid Dar-ul-Islam (Bagh-e-Jinnah), Lahore, Punjab , Pakistan - Islamic Centers, Masjids Mosques, Muslim Owned Businesses, Islamic Schools and Colleges |publisher=Islamicfinder.org |date=2004-06-27 |accessdate=2013-05-21[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dailytimes.com.pk/default.asp?page=2006%5C01%5C11%5Cstory_11-1-2006_pg7_20 |title=Leading News Resource of Pakistan |publisher=Daily Times |date=2006-01-11 |accessdate=2013-05-21[/tpl] and Quaid-e-Azam Libraryhttp://www.qal.org.pk, Quaid-e-Azam Library. situated in a Victorian building.
There are also entertainment and sports facilities within the park: an open-air theatre, a restaurant, tennis courts and the Gymkhana Cricket Ground. It is located on Lawrence Road next to Lahore Zoo, directly across from the Governor's House on The Mall.

==Past – Lawrence Gardens==

Originally built as botanical garden modelled on Kew Gardens, it was named after John Lawrence, Viceroy of India from 1864 to 1869.Lawrence Gardens at Garden Visit website. (Retrieved 27 March 2007) The place used to hold his statue, which was later moved to Foyle and Londonderry College in Northern Ireland.

==Cricket Ground==

Bagh-e-Jinnah park is a famous cricket ground since 1885, built for the entertainment of government officers and civil servants.Imtiaz Sipra (2000), Bagh-e-Jinnah Cricket Ground: Where the twain shall always meet, Cricinfo, 24 November. (Retrieved 27 March 2007) Lahore Gymkhana Club 
had regular fixture here. The ground played host to friendly matches, competitive fixtures and host to Pakistan's first unofficial Test against the West Indies in 1948. A few more unofficial Tests later, Bagh-e-Jinnah became a Test venue when Pakistan took on India in 1954–55. New ZealandNew Zealand tour of Pakistan, 1955/56 2nd Test Scorecard and West IndiesWest Indies tour of Pakistan, 1958/59 3rd Test Scorecard also played a Test here before Bagh-e-Jinnah lost its Test status as it played second fiddle to Gaddafi Stadium but still hosts tour matches involving visiting nations, especially England.[tpl]cite web |title=Lawrence Gardens |url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/content/ground/58965.html |work=ESPN Cricinfo |date=17 June 2011 |accessdate=17 June 2011 [/tpl]
