[[Free Church Parsonage]]

CATEGORIES: Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1869, Churches in Dutchess County, New York, Clergy houses in the United States

Free Church Parsonage is a historic church parsonage at the junction of William and Grinnell Streets in Rhinecliff, Dutchess County, New York. It was built about 1869 and is a [tpl]frac|1|1|2[/tpl]-story, frame cottage with board-and-batten siding in the Gothic Revival style. It has a medium pitched gable roof and has a 1-story hip-roofed verandah.  Also on the property is a contributing stone wall.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=9609|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration:Free Church Parsonage|date=March 1987|accessdate=2011-01-08 |author=Nancy Todd, Neil Larson|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl]
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1987.
