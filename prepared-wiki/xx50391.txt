[[Eight Immortals of the Wine Cup]]

CATEGORIES: Cultural lists, Articles about multiple people, Tang dynasty poets, Chinese alcoholic beverages

The Eight Immortals of the Wine Cup or Eight Immortals Indulged in Wine ([tpl]zh|c=飲中八仙|p=yǐnzhōng bāxīan[/tpl]) were a group of Tang Dynasty scholars who are known for their love of alcoholic beverages. They are not deified and xian ("immortal; transcendent; fairy") is metaphorical. The term is used in a poem by Du Fu, and in the biography of Li Bai in the New Book of Tang.
