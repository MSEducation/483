[[Denis Hurley (rugby union)]]

CATEGORIES: 1984 births, Living people, Irish rugby union players, Munster Rugby players, Rugby union fullbacks, Ireland international rugby union players, People educated at Castleknock College

Denis Hurley (born 15 July 1984 in Cork, Republic of Ireland) is an Irish rugby union player for Munster in the Pro 12 and the Heineken Cup. He plays as a Fullback, Wing or Inside Centre. He attended Castleknock College, Dublin and St Munchin's College, Limerick.

==Munster==

Hurley made his Munster debut against Llanelli Scarlets in May 2006, in a Celtic League fixture.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/match_centre.php?section=lineups&fixid=5915|title=Llanelli Scarlets 18–6 Munster Rugby|date=9 May 2006|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
He sprung to notice when he was preferred to Shaun Payne at fullback in Munster's 2007–08 Heineken Cup quarter-final clash with Gloucester.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/273.php|title=Munster Through To Their 7th Heineken Semi-final|date=6 April 2008|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl] He stayed at fullback for the semi-final against Saracens,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/279.php|title=Brave Munster Battle Through|date=28 April 2008|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl] and started in the 2008 Heineken Cup Final against Toulouse, which Munster won 16–13.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/284.php|title=Munster Champions of Europe|date=24 May 2008|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
Hurley was part of the Munster team that won the Celtic League in the 2009–09 season, and again in the 2010–11 season.
He also started for Munster in their historic 15–6 victory against Australia on 16 November 2010.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/8287.php|title=Class of 2010 Excel|date=17 November 2010|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
Hurley signed a new two-year contract with Munster in March 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10236.php|title=Ryan Signs on For Two More Years|date=13 March 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=14 March 2012[/tpl] He was on the wing for Munster A in their 31–12 2011–12 British and Irish Cup Final victory against Cross Keys on 27 April 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10433.php|title=Munster Prove Too Strong|date=27 April 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 April 2012[/tpl] Hurley signed a one-year contract extension with Munster in April 2014, which will see him remain with the province until at least June 2015.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/18428.php#.U2ANzU1OWM8|title=Contract News|date=29 April 2014|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=29 April 2014[/tpl]

==Ireland==

He made his Ireland debut against USA on 31 May 2009 in Santa Clara.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/4673.php|title=Ireland Finish Tour With Four-Try Triumph|date=31 May 2009|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
He was also Ireland A's right-winger when they won the 2009 Churchill Cup.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/4855.php|title=Ireland 'A' Cap Off Stunning Season With Churchill Cup Success|date=21 June 2009|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
He played for Ireland Wolfhounds against Scotland A in January 2011,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/21984.php|title=Ireland Wolfhounds Fail To Build on Solid First Half|date=28 January 2011|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl] and against England Saxons in February 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/rugby/22066.php|title=Two-Try Wolfhounds Enjoy Ravenhill Success|date=4 February 2011|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl] His performances in these two fixtures earnt him a call-up into Ireland's 2011 Six Nations Championship squad, but he did not feature in any games.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/22121.php|title=Players Added To Ireland Squad|date=7 February 2011|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=29 November 2011[/tpl]
Hurley was selected in the Ireland Wolfhounds squad for their games against the England Saxons in January 2012, which again earned him a call-up to the full Irish squad, for the 2012 Six Nations Championship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/ireland/25402.php|title=Ireland And O2 Ireland Wolfhounds Squads Announced|date=18 January 2012|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=20 January 2012[/tpl]

==Personal life==

His father, Gerry Hurley, was on the bench for the famous Munster team that defeated the All Blacks 12–0 in October 1978.
