[[Deccan College of Engineering and Technology]]

CATEGORIES: Education in Hyderabad, India

The Deccan College of Engineering and Technology (DCET) is a technical institute in Nampally in Hyderabad, India. The college is one of the six Engineering colleges affiliated to Osmania University in Hyderabad.

==History==

The college was established in 1984 as a self-financing minority institution by the Darussalam Educational Trust chaired by Sultan Salahuddin Owaisi, a prominent politician and Member of Parliament.

==Campus==

The campus is in the heart of the city at Nampally. ==Objective==

The main objective of this group of institutions is to impart technical knowledge and skill in all aspects to the deserving candidates particularly the Muslim Minority community and promote them to the international standards. The institutions take efforts to develop every student into a well qualified Professional of the future India and also to instill confidence and courage in them to face the challenging world with new vision. Also the institutions intend to make all the students know the fundamental principles so that they can build their research career in the future. The basic objective will be developing R&D in the fundamentals of Science, Engineering & Technology disciplines.

==Admissions==

For admission to the first year of the four-year degree course in Engineering, candidates must fulfill the eligibility requirements as prescribed by the government of Andhra Pradesh and Osmania University from time to time. All admissions are made strictly in accordance with the government rules in force at the time. Since it is a Muslim minority college 75% of the seats are reserved for Muslims who appear and qualify in the state-level Engineering and Medical Common Entrance Test (EAMCET). The remaining 25% seats are filled by the College Management.
Admissions to the three-year Master of Computer Applications is made through the Integrated Common Entrance Test (I-CET).
