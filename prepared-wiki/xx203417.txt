[[1988 Hall of Fame Bowl]]

CATEGORIES: Outback Bowl, 1987–88 NCAA football bowl games, 1988 in Florida, 20th century in Tampa, Florida, Alabama Crimson Tide football bowl games, Michigan Wolverines football bowl games

The 1988 Hall of Fame Bowl, part of the 1987 bowl game season, took place on January 2, 1988, at Tampa Stadium in Tampa, Florida. The competing teams were the Alabama Crimson Tide, representing the Southeastern Conference (SEC), and the Michigan Wolverines of the Big Ten Conference (Big 10). In what was the first ever meeting between the schools, Michigan was victorious by a final score of 28–24. 

==Teams==

===Alabama===

The 1987 Alabama squad finished the regular season with losses to Florida, [tpl]cfb link|year=1987|team=Memphis Tigers|title=Memphis State[/tpl], Notre Dame and Auburn to compile an 7–4 record. Following their loss against Auburn in the Iron Bowl, the Crimson Tide accepted an invitation to play in the Hall of Fame Bowl.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=FHNhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=9aUEAAAAIBAJ&pg=2952%2C9180445 |title=Defense impresses Michigan coaches |first=David |last=Laubach |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |page=15 |date=November 28, 1987 |accessdate=January 31, 2011[/tpl] The appearance marked the first for Alabama in the Hall of Fame Bowl, and their 40th overall bowl game.

===Michigan===

The 1987 Michigan squad finished the regular season with losses to Notre Dame, Michigan State, Indiana and Ohio State to finish with a record of 7–4. Their appearance marked the first for Michigan in the Hall of Fame Bowl, and their 19th overall bowl game. In mid December 1987, head coach Bo Schembechler underwent quadruple bypass heart surgery and was unable to coach in the bowl game.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=lDkdAAAAIBAJ&sjid=6aUEAAAAIBAJ&pg=4452%2C6227820 |title=Wolverines have battled adversity |first=Cecil |last=Hurt |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |page=1B |date=December 20, 1987 |accessdate=January 31, 2011[/tpl] Due to this circumstance, Gary Moeller was given the coaching responsibilities for the game.

==Game summary==

Alabama scored first following a 51-yard field goal by kicker Philip Doyle, and led 3–0 after the first quarter.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=ebYmAAAAIBAJ&sjid=uqUEAAAAIBAJ&pg=6066%2C414802 |title=Final seconds doom the Tide |first=Cecil |last=Hurt |newspaper=The Tuscaloosa News |page=1B |date=January 3, 1988 |accessdate=January 31, 2011[/tpl] In the second quarter, Jamie Morris scored on touchdown runs of 25 and 14 yards as Michigan took a 14–3 lead to halftime. In the third quarter, Morris scored on a 77-yard touchdown run, stretching Michigan's lead to 21–3. Alabama responded with a 16 yard touchdown pass from Jeff Dunn to tight end Howard Cross, making it 21–9. In the fourth quarter, Bobby Humphrey scored on touchdown runs of 1 and 17 yards as Alabama took a 24–21 lead. Michigan scored the game winning touchdown on a 20 yard pass from Demetrius Brown to John Kolesar for a 28–24 win.
