[[Farm to Market Road 522]]

CATEGORIES: Farm to Market Roads in Texas

Farm to Market Road 522 (FM 522) is a state highway the U.S. state of Texas. Farm to Market Road 522 is a short route (4.828 miles long) that gives drivers a more scenic view from Sweeny to West Columbia, running from FM 1459 to State Highway 36 (SH 36).

==Route description==

FM 522 begins at an intersection with FM 1459 [tpl]convert|4|mi|km[/tpl] northeast of Sweeny in Brazoria County, heading east on two-lane undivided Blacksferry Road. The road heads through wooded areas with some fields and homes, crossing the San Bernard River. At an intersection with CR 353, the highway becomes unnamed and turns north and northeast passing by several riverside neighborhoods. The road then heads to the east into more rural areas, then making a sharp turn to the north, then a slight northwest to north curve. FM 522 another sharp turn to the east, coming to a T-intersection at SH 36 two miles (3Â km) south of West Columbia. FM 522 does not travel through any town but marks the northern unofficial border of unincorporated Wild Peach Village.[tpl]google maps|url=http://maps.google.com/maps?saddr=Farm-To-Market+Rd+1459%2FFM+1459+Rd&daddr=Farm-To-Market+Rd+522%2FFM+522+Rd&hl=en&sll=29.118312,-95.641994&sspn=0.018183,0.042272&geocode=FUq0uwEdIOZL-g%3BFdU8vAEdc6RM-g&vpsrc=0&mra=ls&t=h&z=14|title=overview of Farm to Market Road 522|accessdate=October 20, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite map|publisher=Texas Department of Transportation|title=County Grid Map 564|url=http://www.txdot.gov/travel/countymapbook2006/Pages/564.pdf|year=2010|accessdate=October 12, 2011[/tpl] The speed limit along the entire route is fifty miles-an-hour, and all curves have been recently given a 30-mile-an-hour suggested speed as now posted below the curve sign. 

==History==

FM 522 was initially designated in 1945 to run from SH 36 southwest to Blacks Ferry. In 1962, the highway was extended to FM 1459.
