[[Pleuron (insect anatomy)]]

CATEGORIES: Insect anatomy, Thorax (human anatomy)

The pleuron (pl. pleura, from Greek side, rib) is a lateral sclerite of  thoracic segment of an insect between the tergum and the sternum.[tpl]cite book |title=Dictionary of insect morphology, Volume 4 (Handbuch der Zoologie. Teilband 34) |last=Zombori |first= L. |coauthors= Henrik Steinmann |year=1999 |publisher=Walter de Gruyter |location= |isbn=978-3-11-014898-5 |page=177 |url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=VlAkAtcsmugC |accessdate=2010-04-02[/tpl] The terms pro-, meso- and metapleuron are used respectively for the pleura of the first, second and third thoracic segments.
