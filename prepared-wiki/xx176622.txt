[[Shinano (train)]]

CATEGORIES: Named passenger trains of Japan, Central Japan Railway Company, Railway services introduced in 1953

The [tpl]nihongo|Shinano|しなの[/tpl], branded as [tpl]nihongo|(Wide View) Shinano|（ワイドビュー）しなの[/tpl] is limited express train service in Japan operated by Central Japan Railway Company (JR Central), which runs between [tpl]ja-stalink|Nagoya[/tpl] and [tpl]ja-stalink|Nagano[/tpl] via [tpl]ja-stalink|Matsumoto[/tpl].[tpl]cite book | title = JR新幹線＆特急列車ファイル |trans_title= JR Shinkansen & Limited Express Train File| publisher = Kōtsū Shimbun | date = 2008 | location = Japan | page = 32| isbn = 978-4-330-00608-6[/tpl]

==Accommodation==

Green car (first class) and standard class accommodation is provided, with 2+2 abreast unidirectional seating in both classes. Seat pitch is [tpl]Convert|1200|mm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] in Green class and [tpl]Convert|1000|mm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] in standard class. All trains are no smoking.

==History==

The Shinano service was first introduced on 11 November 1953 as a semi-express operating between Nagoya and Nagano.[tpl]cite book | title = 列車名鑑1995 |trans_title= Train Name Directory 1995| publisher = Railway Journal| date = August 1995 | location = Japan| page = 126| isbn = [/tpl] This was upgraded to "express" status from 13 December 1959, and became a "limited express" from 1 October 1968.
