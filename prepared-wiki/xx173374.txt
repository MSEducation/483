[[Calathea roseopicta]]

CATEGORIES: Calathea

Calathea roseopicta (rose-painted calathea) is a species of plant in the family Marantaceae, native to northwest Brazil. It is a clump-forming evergreen perennial growing to [tpl]convert|50|cm|0|abbr=on[/tpl]. The large rounded leaves are dark green above, red below, marked heavily with cream or pink stripes "painted" along the veins and midriff, with feathered margins. It is tender, with a minimum temperature of [tpl]convert|16|C|0[/tpl] required, and in temperate areas is cultivated indoors as a houseplant.[tpl]cite book|title=RHS A-Z encyclopedia of garden plants|year=2008|publisher=Dorling Kindersley|location=United Kingdom|isbn=1405332964|pages=1136[/tpl]
This plant has gained the Royal Horticultural Society's Award of Garden Merit.[tpl]cite web|title=RHS Plant Selector - Calathea roseopicta|url=http://apps.rhs.org.uk/plantselector/plant?plantid=2196|accessdate=13 June 2013[/tpl]
