[[Zen (TV series)]]

CATEGORIES: 2010s British television series, 2011 British television programme debuts, 2011 British television programme endings, BBC television dramas, British crime television series, Television series by Left Bank Pictures, Television series by WGBH, Mystery!, Television series set in Italy, Television programs based on novels, English-language television programming

Zen is a British television series produced by Left Bank Pictures for the BBC, co-produced with WGBH Boston for its Masterpiece anthology series, Mediaset and ZDF.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/mar/02/zen-bbc-axed-second-series| title=Zen could survive after being sold abroad| first=Tara| last=Conlan| date=2 March 2011| accessdate=2 March 2011| newspaper=The Guardian[/tpl] It stars Rufus Sewell and Caterina Murino and is based on the Aurelio Zen detective novels by Michael Dibdin.[tpl]cite web|title=Rufus Sewell confirmed to play Aurelio Zen for BBC One|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/pressoffice/pressreleases/stories/2010/02_february/18/zen.shtml|work=BBC press releases|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2 January 2011[/tpl] The series was filmed on location in Italy, but the dialogue is in English. The series, which comprises three 90-minute films, was broadcast in the United Kingdom on Sunday evenings from 2 January 2011 on BBC One.[tpl]cite news| last=Vine| first=Richard| title=Zen and the art of Sunday night television scheduling|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/tv-and-radio/2011/jan/01/zen-bbc-sunday-drama|accessdate=2 January 2011|newspaper=The Guardian|date=1 January 2011[/tpl] The three films were based on the books Vendetta, Cabal and Ratking.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.leftbankpictures.co.uk/film/zen.html|publisher=Left Bank Pictures|accessdate=2 January 2010|title=Zen[/tpl] The series was cancelled by BBC One in February 2011; BBC One controller Danny Cohen later said there were already enough male crime-fighters on TV.[tpl]cite news|url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-13221071| title=BBC One controller to cull male crime-fighters| date= 28 April 2011| accessdate=28 April 2011|publisher=BBC[/tpl] Left Bank, the show's producer, has been in discussions with other broadcasters about continuing the series elsewhere.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/media/2011/feb/22/zen-rufus-sewell-bbc1| title=BBC1 axes Rufus Sewell detective drama Zen| first=Tara| last=Conlan| date=22 February 2011| accessdate=23 February 2011| newspaper=The Guardian[/tpl]
