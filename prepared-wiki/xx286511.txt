[[Rosana Chouteau]]

CATEGORIES: Native American women, Native American leaders, Osage people

Rosana Chouteau was the first woman to be elected chief of the Osage Beaver Band, a clan of the Native American Osage Nation in Oklahoma, in 1875, following the death of her uncle.[tpl]cite book|last=Buchanan|first=James Shannon|title=Chronicles of Oklahoma|year=1954|publisher=Oklahoma Historical Society[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Niethammer|first=Carolyn|title=Daughters of the Earth|year=2010|publisher=Simon and Schuster[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Weatherford|first=Doris L.|title=A History of Women in the United States: State-By-State Reference, 4 volume set|year=2003|publisher=Grolier|location=Danbury, CT|isbn=071725805X[/tpl] 
A year after her election, Choteau asserted, "I think my band obey me better than they would a man."[tpl]cite book|last=Foreman|first=Carolyn Thomas|title=Indian Women Chiefs|year=1975|publisher=Zenger Pub. Co.|location=Washington|isbn=0892010193|edition=Repr. Ausg. Muskogee, Okla. : Hoffman, 1954.[/tpl]
