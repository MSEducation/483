[[Richards (surname)]]

CATEGORIES: Surnames, Anglo-Cornish surnames

Richards is a common Celtic Welsh, or Cornish surname based on the English version of the parent's name ending in -S.[tpl]cite web|title=Great Britain Family Names profiling website (redirected from National Trust surname profiler site)|url=http://gbnames.publicprofiler.org/NameSelection.aspx?name=RICHARDS&year=1881&altyear=1998&country=GB&type=name|publisher=The Economic and Social Research Council|accessdate=26 April 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=British Surnames|url=http://www.britishsurnames.co.uk/surnames/RICHARDS/origin[/tpl] In 1881 people with this surname were mainly located in Wales, Cornwall and adjacent South-West counties of England.[tpl]cite web|title=Great Britain Family Names profiling website (redirected from National Trust surname profiler site)|url=http://gbnames.publicprofiler.org/Map.aspx?name=RICHARDS&year=1881&altyear=1998&country=GB&type=name|work=Richards 1881 map|publisher=The Economic and Social Research Council|accessdate=26 April 2011[/tpl] By 1998 many Welsh and Cornish people had migrated to cities in England particularly those adjacent to these areas.[tpl]cite web|title=Great Britain Family Names profiling website (redirected from National Trust surname profiler site)|url=http://gbnames.publicprofiler.org/Map.aspx?name=RICHARDS&year=1998&altyear=1881&country=GB&type=name|work=Richards 1998 map|publisher=The Economic and Social Research Council|accessdate=26 April 2011[/tpl] Originally, it was an English surname brought to England in the great wave of migration following the Norman Conquest of 1066.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2011[/tpl]
