[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's 50 metre freestyle]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The women's 50 metre freestyle event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 15–17 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl] 
Germany's Britta Steffen blasted a new Olympic record to strike a sprint freestyle double. She posted a time of 24.06, the second-fastest ever in the event, to erase Inge de Bruijn's 2000 record, and to hold off U.S. swimmer Dara Torres in a close race by a hundredth of a second (0.01).[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Britta Steffen Edges Dara Torres by .01 to Win 50 Free in Olympic Standard|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18853.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=16 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Steffen snatches gold in 50m free, Torres takes silver|url=http://sports.espn.go.com/oly/summer08/swimming/news/story?id=3539007|publisher=ESPN|date=17 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl] Returning from an eight-year absence, Torres became the first woman in Olympic history to swim past the age of 40.[tpl]cite news|title=Forty-one-year-old Olympian Torres gains pool of 40-ish fans|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/swimming/2008-07-07-torres-fans_N.htm|publisher=USA Today|date=17 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl] She established both a personal best and an American record of 24.07 to earn a silver medal and eleventh overall in her fifth Olympics since 1984.[tpl]cite news|title=Dara Torres settles for silver medal in 50-meter freestyle|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/sports/dara-torres-settles-silver-medal-50-meter-freestyle-article-1.317916|publisher=New York Daily News|date=17 August 2008|accessdate=17 May 2013[/tpl] Meanwhile, Australian teenager Cate Campbell picked up a bronze in 24.17, edging out her teammate Lisbeth Trickett (24.25) by 0.08 of a second.[tpl]cite news|last=English|first=Ben|title=Cate Campbell claims 50m bronze, Libby Trickett misses at Beijing Olympics|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/news/campbell-bronze-in-50m-freestyle/story-e6frg6n6-1111117216184|publisher=The Australian|date=17 August 2008|accessdate=17 May 2013[/tpl]
Netherlands' Marleen Veldhuis finished fifth with a time of 24.26, and was followed in the sixth spot by American Kara Lynn Joyce in 24.63. Veldhuis' teammate Hinkelien Schreuder (24.65) and Belarus' Aliaksandra Herasimenia (24.77) rounded out the finale.
Notable swimmers missed out the top 8 final, featuring Sweden's Therese Alshammar, four-time Olympian and silver medalist in Sydney eight years earlier, and Finland's Hanna-Maria Seppälä, fourth-place finalist in the 100 m freestyle.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Cate Campbell Bounces Back, Tops 50 Free Prelims|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18837.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=15 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl]
