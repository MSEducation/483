[[Joyce MacKenzie]]

CATEGORIES: 1929 births, Living people, American film actresses, People from Redwood City, California, Actresses from California, American television actresses, American schoolteachers

Joyce MacKenzie (born October 13, 1929, Redwood City, California) is an American actress who appeared in films and television from 1946 to 1961.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?nid=1350&dat=19611124&id=jZ5OAAAAIBAJ&sjid=EwEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=5907,2717470|title=Joyce victim of misleading campaign | publisher=Toledo Blade|date=November 24, 1961|accessdate=March 5, 2014[/tpl]  She is best remembered for being the eleventh actress to portray Jane.  She played the role opposite Lex Barker's Tarzan in 1953's Tarzan and the She-Devil. Her final appearance was in the role of Nancy Gilman in the 1961 Perry Mason episode, "The Case of the Duplicate Daughter". 

==Trivia==

After her acting career MacKenzie was an English teacher; one of her students was popular radio host Anthony Cumia of the Opie and Anthony Show.[tpl]cite web|last=Cumia|first=Anthony|title=Opie and Anthony Show|url=http://www.siriusxm.com/theopie&anthonychannel|publisher=SiriusXM|accessdate=2012-11-16[/tpl]
