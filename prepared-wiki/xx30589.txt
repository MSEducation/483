[[Lake Oswego, Oregon]]

CATEGORIES: Lake Oswego, Oregon, Cities in Oregon, Cities in Clackamas County, Oregon, Cities in Multnomah County, Oregon, Cities in Washington County, Oregon, Populated places established in 1847, 1847 establishments in Oregon, Populated places on the Willamette River, Portland metropolitan area

Lake Oswego [tpl]IPAc-en|ɒ|s|ˈ|w|iː|ɡ|oʊ[/tpl] is a city in the U.S. state of Oregon, primarily in Clackamas County with small portions extending into neighboring Multnomah and Washington counties.[tpl]dead link|date=September 2013[/tpl] Located south of Portland surrounding the [tpl]convert|405|acre|km2|adj=on[/tpl] Oswego Lake, the town was founded in 1847 and incorporated as Oswego in 1910. The city was the hub of Oregon's brief iron industry in the late 19th century and is today an affluent suburb of Portland.  As of the 2010 census, the city had a total population of 36,619,[tpl]cite news| title= City population is up – what will that mean?|url= http://thetribonline.net/news/story.php?story_id=130092552348458300 | newspaper= Lake Oswego Review | date= Mar 24, 2011 | accessdate= December 6, 2012[/tpl] a 3.8% increase over the 2000 population of 35,278.
