[[Sparaxis tricolor]]

CATEGORIES: Iridaceae, Flowers, Flora naturalised in Australia

Sparaxis tricolor, known by the common names Wandflower, Harlequin flower, and Sparaxis, is a bulb-forming perennial plant that grows in well-drained sunny soil. It gained its name from its colorful flowers which are bi- or tri-coloured with a golden centre and a small ring of brown surrounded by another colour.
The plant is native to southern Africa. It is present in California and Australia[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.anbg.gov.au/cgi-bin/apni?TAXON_NAME=Sparaxis+tricolor|title=Sparaxis tricolor |accessdate=18 March 2012 |work= Australian Plant Name Index (APNI), IBIS database|publisher = Centre for Plant Biodiversity Research, Australian Government, Canberra[/tpl] as an introduced species after having escaped from garden cultivation.
