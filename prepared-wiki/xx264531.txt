[[Lennart Petrell]]

Lennart Petrell (born April 13, 1984) is a Finnish professional ice hockey player, currently playing for the Genève-Servette HC of the Swiss National League A (NLA).

==Playing career==

He was originally drafted in the 6th round (190th overall) on the 2004 NHL Entry Draft by the Columbus Blue Jackets. Staying in his native Finland, Petrell played eight seasons with HIFK in the SM-liiga.
On June 15, 2011, he signed a one-year entry level contract with the Edmonton Oilers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://oilers.nhl.com/club/news.htm?id=565902&navid=DL%7cEDM%7chome |title=Oilers sign two free agents |author= |date= |work=Edmonton Oilers Press Release |publisher=Edmonton Oilers |accessdate=June 15, 2011[/tpl] During his first North American season in 2011–12 with the Oilers, Petrell scored his first NHL goal on November 3, 2011, against Jonathan Quick of the Los Angeles Kings.[tpl]Citeweb| url = http://www.nhl.com/ice/boxscore.htm?id=2011020176 | publisher = National Hockey League | date = 2011-11-03 | accessdate = 2011-11-03 | title = Kings v Oilers Game Boxscore[/tpl] On January 2, 2012, Petrell was re-assigned to the Oklahoma City Barons of the American Hockey League for a nine game stint before returning to the Oilers.
After a second season with the Oilers, Petrell was released as a free agent and returned to Europe, signing a one-year deal with Swiss club, Genève-Servette HC of the NLA on August 14, 2013.[tpl]citeweb| publisher = Genève-Servette HC | url = http://www.gshc.ch/Mop/Site/main.php?module=custom:news&selected=7074 | title = Lennart Petrell, the new finnish signing | date = 2013-08-14 | accessdate = 2013-08-14 | language = French[/tpl]
