[[Combat Zones That See]]

CATEGORIES: DARPA, Military equipment of the United States, Surveillance, Research projects

Combat Zones That See, or CTS, is a project of the United States Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency (DARPA) [tpl]cite web|url=http://en.wikisource.org/wiki/DARPA_Solicitation_Number_SN03-13:_Pre-Solicitation_Notice:_COMBAT_ZONES_THAT_SEE_%28CTS%29|title=DARPA Solicitation Number SN03-13: Pre-Solicitation Notice: COMBAT ZONES THAT SEE (CTS)|publisher=Defense Advanced Research Projects Agency|accessdate=2009-10-11[/tpl] whose goal is to "track everything that moves" in a city by linking up a massive network of surveillance cameras to a centralised computer system.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.villagevoice.com/2003-07-08/news/big-brother-gets-a-brain/1|title=Big Brother Gets a Brain|last=Schachtman|first=Noah|date=July 8, 2003|work=Village Voice|accessdate=2009-10-11[/tpl] Artificial intelligence software will then identify and track all movement throughout the city.[tpl]cite web|url=http://armed-services.senate.gov/statemnt/2005/March/Tether%2003-09-05.pdf|title=Statement by Dr. Tony Tether (DARPA) to the Senate Armed Services committee|last=Tether|first=Tony|date=March 9, 2005|accessdate=2009-10-11[/tpl]
CTS is described by DARPA as intended for use in combat zones, to deter enemy attacks on American troops and to identify and track enemy combatants who launch attacks against American soldiers.
Civil liberties activists and writers of dystopian fiction believe that such programs have great potential for privacy violations, and have openly opposed the project.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.wired.com/politics/law/news/2003/07/59471|title=Another Tool For Big Brother?|date=July 2, 2003|work=Wired Magazine|accessdate=2009-10-11[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.globalsecurity.org/org/news/2003/030801-tech01.htm|title=Spy Project Driving Fear Of Surveillance|last=Sniffen|first=Michael|date=August 1, 2003|work=GlobalSecurity.org|accessdate=2009-10-11[/tpl]
