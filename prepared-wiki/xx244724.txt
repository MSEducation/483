[[Christ Episcopal Church (Walton, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Episcopal churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, National Register of Historic Places in Delaware County, New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1834, 19th-century Episcopal church buildings, Churches in Delaware County, New York

Christ Episcopal Church is a historic Episcopal church building located at Walton in Delaware County, New York.  It was built in 1832 in the Federal Gothic style and received an overlay of Victorian period decoration in 1883.  It is a wood frame structure and characterized by a rectangular meeting house plan, including a vestibule, nave, chancel, and sacristy.  It features a steep gable roof and a central projecting entrance tower surmounted by a wooden belfry.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1688|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Christ Episcopal Church |date=January 1999|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=Kathleen LaFrank|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1689|title=Accompanying five photos[/tpl]
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1999.
