Brent Kite (born 7 March 1981 in Queanbeyan, New South Wales) is an Australian professional rugby league footballer for the Penrith Panthers of the National Rugby League (NRL). A Tonga and Australia international, and New South Wales State of Origin representative front-row forward, he has played club football for the St. George Illawarra Dragons, and Sea Eagles. Kite was named the Clive Churchill Medallist for his performance in the 2008 NRL Grand Final, in which he helped steer Manly-Warringah to a 40 - 0 thumpingSydney Morning Herald 'Manly Hand Storm record thumping' of the Melbourne Storm, scoring one try.

Kite made his NRL debut for the St George Illawarra Dragons against the Cronulla-Sutherland Sharks at Aussie Stadium on 17 March 2002.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.showroom.com.au/dragons/dragonshistory/history_players_jk.htm|title=History of St George Illawarra Dragons|publisher=History of St George|accessdate=4 August 2008[/tpl] Kite moved from the second-row into the front-row following injuries at St George in early 2004.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/articles/2004/05/03/1083436546405.html|title=Country boy and family man is high as a Kite|publisher=League Unlimited|date=4 May 2004|accessdate=4 August 2008[/tpl] After showing good form in early 2004, Kite was selected for the Country Origin side.[tpl]cite web|url=http://queanbeyan.yourguide.com.au/news/local/sport/rugby-league/state-of-origin-debut-leaves-brent-high-as-a-kite/679581.aspx|title=State of Origin debut leaves Brent high as a Kite|publisher=The Queanbeyan Age|date=7 June 2004|accessdate=4 August 2008[/tpl] Kite was also selected to represent New South Wales, playing all three games in the 2004 State of Origin series. In April 2013, Brent captained Tonga in their Pacific Rugby League International fixture against fierce Pacific rivals Samoa. He lead his team to demolish Samoa by 36-4.
In October 2013, Brent once again captained Tonga, this time in their 2013 Rugby League World Cup campaign. However, even though he was a great leader, Tonga could not advance past the group stage. He played in all 3 of Tonga's matches.
