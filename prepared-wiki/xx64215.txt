Michael Christopher Sheen, [tpl]small|OBE[/tpl] (born 5 February 1969),[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.filmreference.com/film/47/Michael-Sheen.html | title= Michael Sheen Biography (1969-) | publisher=FilmReference.com | accessdate= 2014-02-12[/tpl] is a Welsh stage and screen actor. After training at London's Royal Academy of Dramatic Art, Sheen made his professional debut in 1991, starring opposite Vanessa Redgrave in When She Danced at the Globe Theatre. He worked mainly in theatre throughout the 1990s and made notable stage appearances in Romeo and Juliet (1992), Don’t Fool With Love (1993), Peer Gynt (1994), The Seagull (1995), The Homecoming (1997) and Henry V (1997). His performances in Amadeus at the Old Vic and Look Back in Anger at the National Theatre were nominated for Olivier Awards in 1998 and 1999, respectively. In 2003, he was nominated for a third Olivier Award for his performance in Caligula at the Donmar Warehouse.
Sheen has become better known as a screen actor since the 2000s, in particular through his roles in various biopics.[tpl]cite web|last=Marshall|first=Kingsley|title=Why Great Lives Make Great Movies|url=http://www.littlewhitelies.co.uk/features/articles/why-great-lives-make-great-movies-13944|publisher=Little White Lives|date=16 February 2011 |accessdate=5 March 2013[/tpl] With writer Peter Morgan he has starred in a trilogy of films as British politician Tony Blair: the first was the television film The Deal in 2003, followed  by The Queen (2006) and The Special Relationship (2010). For playing Blair he was nominated for both a BAFTA Award and an Emmy. Sheen was also nominated for a BAFTA as the troubled comic actor Kenneth Williams in BBC Four's 2006 Fantabulosa!, and was nominated for a fourth Olivier Award nomination in 2006 for portraying the broadcaster David Frost in Frost/Nixon, a role he revisited in the 2008 film adaptation of the play. He also  starred as the outspoken football manager Brian Clough in The Damned United.
Since 2009 and into the 2010s, Sheen has become known for a wider variety of roles. In 2009, Sheen appeared in two fantasy films, Underworld: Rise of the Lycans and The Twilight Saga: New Moon, while in 2010, he made a four-episode guest appearance in the NBC comedy 30 Rock. He appeared in the science-fiction film Tron: Legacy (2010) and Woody Allen's romantic comedy Midnight in Paris (2011). At Easter 2011, Sheen directed and starred in National Theatre Wales's The Passion, a 72-hour secular passion play staged in his hometown of Port Talbot. From late 2011 until early 2012, Sheen played the title role in Hamlet at the Young Vic. In 2013, he received a Golden Globe nomination for his role in Showtime's television drama Masters of Sex.

==Early life==

Sheen was born in Newport, Wales,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/wales/arts/sites/michael-sheen/pages/biography.shtml |title=Michael Sheen biography |date=11 January 2011|publisher=BBC Cymru Wales [/tpl] the son of Irene (née Thomas), a secretary, and Meyrick, a British Steel personnel manager.[tpl]cite news |title=He's all right, Jack |author=Nigel Dempster |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1G1-76608070.html |newspaper=Daily Mail |via=Highbeam |date=18 July 2001 |accessdate=18 September 2011[/tpl] He has one younger sister, Joanne. When he was five, the family moved to Liverpool, England, but settled in his parents' hometown of Baglan in Port Talbot, Wales three years later.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/film/2009/mar/20/michael-sheen-interview-damned-united|title=That's all I play – me|author=Simon Hattenstone|newspaper=The Guardian |date=20 March 2009|accessdate=20 September 2010 [/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/moslive/article-1160026/After-40-wearing-pants-turns-sinister-Michael-Sheen-style.html|title=After 40, not wearing pants turns sinister |author=Jon Wilde| newspaper=Daily Mail |date=7 March 2009|accessdate=21 August 2011[/tpl] Port Talbot is also the hometown of actors Richard Burton and Anthony Hopkins. Director Sam Mendes has described Sheen as "a stage creature" and attributed that to the actor's Welsh roots. "I'm serious. He's Welsh in the tradition of Anthony Hopkins and Richard Burton: fiery, mercurial, unpredictable."[tpl]cite news |title=An Actor Creates A Fearful, but Still Bratty, Mozart |author=Matt Wolf |url=http://www.nytimes.com/1999/12/12/theater/theater-an-actor-creates-a-fearful-but-still-bratty-mozart.html  |newspaper=The New York Times |date=12 December 1999 |accessdate=18 December 2013[/tpl] A keen footballer, Sheen was scouted and offered a place on Arsenal F.C.'s youth team at the age of 12 but his family was unwilling to relocate to London. He later said he was "grateful" for his parents' decision as the chances of forging a professional football career were "so slim". "It would have meant moving from Wales to London as a family. It was just too big an upheaval."[tpl]cite news |title=Michael Sheen on vampires, politicians, and soccer |author=Darren Franich |url=http://popwatch.ew.com/2010/02/26/michael-sheen/ |newspaper=Entertainment Weekly |date=26 February 2010 |accessdate=18 September 2011[/tpl] He lost interest in playing football in his early teens.
Sheen was raised in a theatrical family—his parents were both involved in local amateur operatics and musicals[tpl]cite web|url=http://thisiswhoiam.empireonline.com/Celebrities/View/Michael-Sheen_ |title=Michael Sheen – This Is Who I Am |work=Empire Online |year=2009 |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20090705084423/http://thisiswhoiam.empireonline.com/Celebrities/View/Michael-Sheen_ |archivedate=2009-07-05 |accessdate=24 October 2010[/tpl] and, later in life, his father worked as a part-time professional Jack Nicholson look-alike.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.independent.co.uk/arts-entertainment/theatre-dance/features/michael-sheen-a-touch-of-frost-410387.html | newspaper=The Independent |title=Michael Sheen: A touch of Frost |author=Michael Coveney |date=3 August 2006 | accessdate=25 May 2012[/tpl] In his teenage years, Sheen was involved with the West Glamorgan Youth Theatre and, later, the National Youth Theatre of Wales.[tpl]cite web|title=Owain Arwel Hughes and Michael Sheen recognised in New Year's Honours List |date=15 January 2009 |publisher=WJEC |accessdate=21 August 2011 |url=http://www.wjec.co.uk/index.php?nav=14&news=61[/tpl] "It was a brilliant youth theatre", Sheen has said, "and it taught me not only a lot about acting, but also about work ethic; it was very disciplined."[tpl]cite news |title=British Actor Michael Sheen Fights for The Damned United |author=Brad Balfour |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/brad-balfour/qa-british-actor-michael_b_320393.html |publisher=The Huffington Post |date=14 October 2009 |accessdate=18 September 2011[/tpl] He was influenced by the performances of Laurence Olivier and the writings of theatre critic Kenneth Tynan—"the combination of those two things kind of blew my head off."[tpl]cite news |title=Profile: Michael Sheen |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7805058.stm |publisher=BBC |accessdate=21 August 2011 |date=31 December 2008[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.theartsdesk.com/index.php?option=com_k2&view=item&id=2184:theartsdesk-qa-michael-sheen&Itemid=80/ |title=theartsdesk Q&A: Actor Michael Sheen |date=11 September 2010|publisher=The Arts Desk [/tpl] Sheen was educated at Blaenbaglan Primary School, Glan Afan Comprehensive School and, finally, Neath Port Talbot College where he sat A-levels in English, Drama and Sociology.[tpl]cite news |title=Portrait of the artist as a young man |author=Gavin Allen |url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Portrait+of+the+artist+as+a+young+man%3B+Michael+Sheen+will+be+at+the...-a0194199675 |newspaper=The Western Mail |date=21 February 2009 |accessdate=20 September 2011[/tpl] He considered studying English at university but instead decided to attend drama school. He moved to London in 1988 to train as an actor at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Art (RADA), having spent the previous year working in a Welsh fast food restaurant called Burger Master to earn money. Sheen was granted the Laurence Olivier Bursary by The Society of London Theatre in his second year at RADA.[tpl]cite web |title=Laurence Olivier Bursary |url=http://www.solt.co.uk/bursaries.html  |publisher=Society of London Theatre |accessdate=21 August 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Larry, dear Larry! ===The Deal, The Queen and Fantabulosa (2002–2006)===

At this point in his career, Sheen began to devote more time to film work.[ref]
