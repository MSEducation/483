[[Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of South Australia, Protected areas established in 1985, 1985 establishments in Australia, Far North (South Australia)

Kati Thanda-Lake Eyre National Park is located in South Australia (Australia), 697Â km north of Adelaide. It contains both the North and South sections of Lake Eyre as well as sections of the Tirari Desert.[tpl]cite web|url = http://www.atn.com.au/sa/south/parks-h.htm|title = Outback National Parks South Australia (SA)|publisher = Australian Travel & Tourism Network Pty Limited| year = 2006| accessdate = 2008-06-23[/tpl]
The park protects dry desert landscapes, the nation's largest salt lake and the lowest point on the mainland.[tpl]cite book |title=Discover Australia's National Parks |last=Hema Maps |first= |authorlink= |coauthors= |year=1997 |publisher=Random House Australia |location=Milsons Point, New South Wales |isbn=1-875992-47-2 |pages=328[/tpl]
The park almost encloses Elliot Price Conservation Park, which covers the Hunt Peninsula and Brooks Island, within and around the northern section of the lake. It was established as South Australia's first arid zone conservation zone. It was named after Elliot Price, from the nearby Muloorina Station. There is no vehicle access to the park.
