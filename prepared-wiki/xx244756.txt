[[St. Peter's Episcopal Church Complex (Hobart, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Episcopal churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, National Register of Historic Places in Delaware County, New York, Federal architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1801, 19th-century Episcopal church buildings, Churches in Delaware County, New York

St. Peter's Episcopal Church Complex is a historic Episcopal church complex at the junction of Pine and Church Streets in Hobart, Delaware County, New York.  The complex includes the church, cemetery, rectory, and carriage house.  The church was built about 1801 and is a small frame building, 48 feet by 38 feet, with a stone foundation, clapboard siding, and a gable roof.  It features a central projecting square tower surmounted by a wooden balustrade and an octagonal louvered belfry with steeple.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=100964|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: St. Peter's Episcopal Church Complex|date=August 1997|accessdate=2010-02-05 |author=Kathleen LaFrank|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=100966|title=Accompanying seven photos[/tpl]
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1998.
