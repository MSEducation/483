[[Prison-Ashram Project]]

CATEGORIES: Prison-related organizations

The Prison-Ashram Project, now administered by Human Kindness Foundation, was started in 1973 by Bo and Sita Lozoff, in cooperation with Ram Dass, to encourage convicts to use meditation and other spiritual teachings, turning their prison time into an ashram-like experience. ,Yoga Journal, December 2001, pg 87 http://books.google.com/books?id=H-oDAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA87&lpg=PA87&dq=yoga+journal+lozoff&source=bl&ots=88fRghCaqS&sig=WUjanspB9zMmxhRZyQZqaU-aUhI&hl=en&sa=X&ei=oekVT6DPHsXZtwfwyfz0Dg&sqi=2&ved=0CCQQ6AEwAg#v=onepage&q&f=false "Ashram" is a Sanskrit word meaning "House of God."
Bo and Sita Lozoff were the directors of Prison-Ashram Project for decades, giving workshops in prisons throughout the world and answering up to 100 letters per day.http://www.ru.org/society/turning-prisons-into-ashrams.html Bo retired from the project in 2011; Sita now leads the project, along with a board of directors.http://www.humankindness.org/bolozoff.html[tpl]Primary source-inline|reason=Subject's webpage|date=January 2012[/tpl]
The Prison-Ashram Project has a sister project in England, The Prison Phoenix Trust, which offers yoga and meditation to prisoners in the UK and Ireland.Sunrise magazine, Theosophical University Press. http://www.theosociety.org/pasadena/sunrise/54-04-5/pu-sbd3.htm
