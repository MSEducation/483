[[Lewis Ganson]]

CATEGORIES: English magicians, 1913 births, 1980 deaths, Sleight of hand, Card magic, Coin magic

Lewis Jack Ganson (1913 West Ham, Essex) – 22 December 22, 1980 Hampshire) was an English magician who become one of the most prolific writers in magic,[tpl]cite web |url=http://geniimagazine.com/wiki/index.php/Lewis Ganson |title= Lewis Ganson MagicPedia entry[/tpl] going on to write more than sixty books on the subject.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.magicnook.com/forum/bioFG.htm |title= Biographies Page FG[/tpl]
For years, he was the editor of Harry Stanley's The Gen and contributor to Magigram magazines. He was also a vice president of the British Ring of the International Brotherhood of Magicians and founder member of the Portsmouth and district magic circle.
Ganson was a professional close-up magician, except for his time in the Royal Army Ordnance Corps 1939–1958.
