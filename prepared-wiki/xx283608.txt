[[Ali Tayebnia]]

CATEGORIES: 1960 births, Living people, People from Isfahan, University of Tehran alumni, Alumni of the London School of Economics, University of Tehran faculty, Iranian academics, Iranian economists, Government ministers of Iran

Ali Tayebnia ([tpl]lang-fa|علی طیب‌نیا[/tpl], born 5 April 1960) is an Iranian academic, economist and Iran's Minister of Economic Affairs and Finance. He was designated by President Hassan Rouhani for the position on 4 August 2013 and was confirmed by the parliament on 15 August.

==Early life and education==

Tayebnia was born in Isfahan in 1960.[tpl]cite web|last=Alfoneh|first=Ali|title=All the President's Men: Rouhani’s Cabinet|url=http://www.defenddemocracy.org/media-hit/all-the-presidents-men-rouhanis-cabinet/|work=Foundation for Defense of Democracies|accessdate=13 August 2013|format=Policy Brief|date=5 August 2013[/tpl] He holds a PhD in economics from the University of Tehran.[tpl]cite news|last=Memarian|first=Jahandad|title=New Iranian Cabinet Nominees: Building Bridges Between Factions to Yield Reform|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/jahandad-memarian/new-iranian-cabinet-nomin_b_3726826.html|accessdate=11 August 2013|newspaper=The Huffington Post|date=8 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Rouhani's proposed cabinet line-up|url=http://www.iran-daily.com/1392/5/15/MainPaper/4601/Page/3/Index.htm#|accessdate=16 August 2013|newspaper=Iran Daily|date=6 August 2013[/tpl] As part of Tayebnia's doctoral studies, he spent a year studying at the London School of Economics under the supervision of Professor Laurence Harris.[tpl]cite news|title=طیب‌نیا،‌ به وزرات اقتصاد رسید|url=http://www.isna.ir/fa/news/92052413727/%D8%B7%DB%8C%D8%A8-%D9%86%DB%8C%D8%A7-%D8%A8%D9%87-%D9%88%D8%B2%D8%B1%D8%A7%D8%AA-%D8%A7%D9%82%D8%AA%D8%B5%D8%A7%D8%AF-%D8%B1%D8%B3%DB%8C%D8%AF|accessdate=1 March 2014|newspaper=ISNA|date=17 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=LSE in print|url=http://www.lse.ac.uk/intranet/news/informationForStaff/staffStudentsAndAlumni/dailyHeadlines/2013/September/06September2013.aspx|accessdate=1 March 2014|newspaper=The London School of Economics and Political Science|date=6 September 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

Tayenia is an academic and has served in various state institutions regarding economy and finance.[tpl]cite news|title=Possible nominees for Rohani's cabinet|url=http://www.gulfinthemedia.com/index.php?m=&id=658933&lang=en|accessdate=11 August 2013|work=Gulf in the Media|date=1 August 2013|agency=Tehran Times[/tpl] He was a faculty member at his alma mater, the University of Tehran.[tpl]cite web|title=President Hassan Rouhani's pragmatic conservative, security-intelligence-oriented Cabinet nominations|url=http://www.iranpolitik.com/2013/08/06/analysis/president-hassan-rouhanis-cabinet/|work=Iran Politik|accessdate=13 August 2013|date=6 August 2013[/tpl] His field of interest is public economics
He was the secretary of the economic commission from 1997 to 2000.[tpl]cite news|title=Biography of Tayebnia, proposed as minister of Economic Affairs and Finance|url=http://www3.kermanshah.irna.ir/en/News/80763598/Politic/Biography_of_Tayebnia,_proposed_as_minister_of_Economic_Affairs_and_Finance|accessdate=11 August 2013|work=IRNA|date=5 August 2013|location=Tehran[/tpl] He served as the deputy head of the Presidential Office for planning under the former President Mohammad Khatami from 2001 to 2005. Then he was again named the secretary of the economic commission in 2005 and his tenure lasted until 2007.
He was a representative of and an advisor to Mohammad Reza Aref during the 2013 presidential elections. He was also Aref's economic advisor.[tpl]cite news|last=al Labbad|first=Mustafa|title=Rouhani's Cabinet Seeks New Balance in Iranian Policies|url=http://assafir.com/MulhakArticle.aspx?EditionId=2539&MulhakArticleId=1787820&MulhakId=6367#.Ug3dQ2TAVgs|accessdate=16 August 2013|newspaper=As Safir|date=15 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Iran politics: Rowhani takes a centre line in his cabinet nominees|url=http://viewswire.eiu.com/index.asp?layout=VWArticleVW3&article_id=1670821351&refm=vwHome&page_title=Latest%20analysis|accessdate=29 August 2013|newspaper=ViewsWire|date=8 August 2013[/tpl]
On 4 August 2013, he was proposed by President Hassan Rouhani as minister of economic affairs and finance.[tpl]cite news|title=Iran president unveils proposed Cabinet list|url=http://www.presstv.ir/detail/2013/08/04/317160/iran-president-unveils-proposed-cabinet/|accessdate=11 August 2013|work=PressTV|date=4 August 2013|agency=ILNA[/tpl] On 15 August, the Majlis approved him as minister, giving 274 votes for and 7 votes against.[tpl]cite news|title=Iranian Parliament Gives Vote of Confidence to Majority of Rouhani's Proposed Ministers|url=http://english.farsnews.com/newstext.aspx?nn=13920524000980|accessdate=15 August 2013|work=Fars News|date=15 August 2013[/tpl] He was given the highest votes for with the rate of 96.5% which was also all time record for Iranian confirmation process.[tpl]cite news|title=96.5 percent voting for a minister – all time record for Islamic Republic of Iran|url=http://www.turkishweekly.net/news/154231/96-5-percent-voting-for-a-minister-all-time-record-for-islamic-republic-of-iran.html|accessdate=22 August 2013|newspaper=The Journal of Turkish Weekly|date=16 August 2013[/tpl]
