[[Teuchezhsky District]]

CATEGORIES: Districts of the Republic of Adygea, States and territories established in 1929, States and territories disestablished in 1956, States and territories established in 1957

Teuchezhsky District ([tpl]lang-ru|Теуче́жский райо́н[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-ady|Теуцожь район[/tpl]) is an administrativeLaw #171 and a municipalLaw #284 district (raion), one of the seven in the Republic of Adygea, Russia. It is located in the west of the republic and borders with the territory of the City of Krasnodar of Krasnodar Krai, Krasnodar Reservoir, and Krasnogvardeysky District in the north, Belorechensky District of Krasnodar Krai in the east and southeast, the territory of the Town of Goryachy Klyuch of Krasnodar Krai in the south, and with Takhtamukaysky District in the west. The area of the district is [tpl]convert|700|km2|sp=us[/tpl].Official website of Teuchezhsky District. Economy of the District [tpl]ru icon[/tpl] Its administrative center is the rural locality (an aul) of Ponezhukay. District's population: [tpl]ru-census|p2010=20,643|p2002=19,951|p1989=21,929[/tpl] The population of Ponezhukay accounts for 16.7% of the district's total population.

==History==

The district was established on February 7, 1929 as Psekupsky District ([tpl]lang|ru|Псекупский район[/tpl]) and was renamed Ponezhukaysky ([tpl]lang|ru|Понежукайский район[/tpl]) in 1938 and Teuchezhsky in 1940.[tpl]Cite book|last=Азаренкова|first=А. С.|coauthors=И. Ю. Бондарь, Н. С. Вертышева|title=Основные административно-территориальные преобразования на Кубани (1793–1985 гг.)|origyear=1986|year=1986|publisher=Краснодарское книжное издательство|language=Russian|page=181[/tpl] The district was merged into Takhtamukaysky District on December 7, 1956. It was, however, restored shortly thereafter on August 5, 1957.
