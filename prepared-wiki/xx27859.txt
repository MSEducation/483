In the early 50's, The Israeli breakfast never includes meats such as ham and bacon, which are common on breakfast menus in many other countries. In accordance with the Jewish laws of Kashrut, meat and dairy ingredients are never served together in a meal. Yet the "no meat" breakfasts stick to the Israeli habit to this day. The Israeli breakfast is a dairy meal, and a variety of cheeses are offered. Fish is served, most common is herring.
