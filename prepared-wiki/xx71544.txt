[[Ansei Purge]]

CATEGORIES: Bakumatsu, Japanese historical terms, 1858 in Japan, 1859 in Japan

[tpl]nihongo|Ansei Purge|安政の大獄|Ansei no taigoku[/tpl] was a multi-year event in Japanese history of the Edo period.The name "Ansei Purge" refers to the "Ansei period", a Japanese era that followed the Kaei period and was followed by the Man'en period.  In other words, the Ansei Purge was an event that occurred during  the Ansei period, which spanned the years 1854 through 1860.  In 1858-1860, the Tokugawa shogunate got rid of those who did not support its foreign trade policies.Nussbaum, Louis-Frédéric. (2005). "Ansei no taigoku" in Japan Encyclopedia, p. 33.

==History==

The Ansei Purge was ordered by Ii Naosuke on behalf of the bakufu.Cullen, Louis. (2003). A History of Japan, 1582-1941: Internal and External Worlds,  pp. 184-188.  
The purge was carried out in an effort to quell opposition to trade treaties with the United States, Russia, Great Britain, France and the Netherlands.Nussbaum, "Ansei no Kari Jōyaku" at p. 33.
Over 100 people were victims of the crackdown.Najita, Tetsuo. (1980). Japan: The Intellectual Foundations of Modern Japanese Politics, p. 63.  Men were forced out of positions within the bakufu, or from han leadership or from the Imperial Court in Kyoto.
