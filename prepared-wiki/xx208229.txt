[[Alvin Walker]]

CATEGORIES: 1954 births, Living people, American players of Canadian football, Canadian football running backs, Texas A&M Aggies football players, Montreal Alouettes players, Ottawa Rough Riders players

Alvin "Skip" Walker (b:[tpl]Birth date|1954|09|11[/tpl]) is an American former professional football player, football coach and teacher. Walker  was a Canadian Football League (CFL) running back who played for the Montreal Alouettes and Ottawa Rough Ridersin the 1980s.[tpl]sfn|McRae|2010[/tpl] He was a CFL All-Star in 1982 and 1983. His brother Cornelius Walker also played for the Rough Riders.

==Playing career==

Walker played university football for Texas A&M. Walker was drafted by the Houston Oilers but was cut at training camp.[tpl]sfn|McRae|2010[/tpl] Walker worked as a teacher and continued to work out. In 1980, Walker moved to Canada to play for the Saskatchewan Roughriders but was traded to the Montreal Alouettes before the season began. Walker played two seasons for Montreal, but was traded to Ottawa when Montreal signed David Overstreet. Walker joined Ottawa in 1982 and played three seasons for Ottawa.  He led the CFL in  rushing yards in 1982 and 1983. In 1983, Walker set the Riders single-season rushing record with 1431 yards.[tpl]sfn|Ferguson |2005|p=461[/tpl]
After retirement, Walker returned to Texas. Walker was a coach and science teacher at Murchison Middle School (Austin, Texas) in the late 1980s and early 1990s.
Alvin "Skip" Walker is the manager of Hoover's Cooking restaurant in Austin, Texas. He is married to Kimberly James Walker.
