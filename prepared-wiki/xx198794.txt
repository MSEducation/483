==History==

Anthony Marnell III is the son of Tony Marnell (owner of Marnell Corrao Associates), who has developed resorts such as Caesars Palace, Wynn Las Vegas, and the Bellagio. Anthony Marnell III acquired the land for the resort for $240 million. MGM Mirage announced a $160 million investment in the resort on April 26, 2006.[tpl]cite news |first= Howard |last=Stutz |authorlink= |coauthors= |title= MGM Mirage invests in M Resort |url=http://www.lvrj.com/business/7219501.html |work=Las Vegas Review-Journal |publisher= |date=2007-04-27 |accessdate=2008-05-21 [/tpl] The total cost of the completed project was $1 billion.[tpl]Cite news| last = Stutz| first = Howard| title = Thousands celebrate opening of Las Vegas' newest resort, the M| newspaper = Las Vegas Review-Journal| date = March 2, 2009| url =http://www.lvrj.com/news/40525892.html| postscript = . [/tpl] Marnell Corrao Associates was responsible for development, master-planning, interior design, construction/design/program management, FFE procurement, property management, as well as being the executive design architect, architect of record, and general contractor. 
The resort opened to the public on March 1, 2009.
In October 2010, Penn National Gaming bought the resort's $860 million in debt from the Bank of Scotland for $231 million.[tpl]cite news|title=Penn National Gaming acquires M Resort|date=1 June 2011|newspaper=Bloomberg Businessweek|agency=Associated Press|accessdate=22 March 2012|url=http://www.businessweek.com/ap/financialnews/D9NJ4OU80.htm[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=M Resort officially acquired by Penn National Gaming|newspaper=Las Vegas Review-Journal|first=Howard|last=Stutz|date=1 June 2011|url=http://www.lvrj.com/business/m-resort-officially-acquired-by-penn-national-gaming-122936233.html|accessdate=22 March 2012[/tpl] They then acquired the property the following June, in exchange for canceling the debt. Marnell remained as president, but with no ownership.
