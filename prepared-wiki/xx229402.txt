==Life==

Hill was born to Thomas Hill[tpl]dn|date=January 2014[/tpl] and Mary Sophie, who was the daughter of Rev. Charles Thorold, Rector of Ludborough, Lincolnshire. He was educated at the Westminster School and subsequently went to take a degree in the Natural Science Tripos at Jesus College, Cambridge.[tpl]acad|id=HL876AG|name=Hill, Arthur George[/tpl] He took an interest in archaeology and architecture and published several books about Cambridgeshire. In 1882 he became a fellow of the Society of Antiquaries. He married Amy Williams in 1886 with whom he would have two daughters.Arthur George Hill, The Organ, Vol III:10 (October 1923), p.1 118-121.
When he left Cambridge he began work at the organ building company, William Hill & Sons, founded by his grandfather William Hill. Thomas Hill had been the head of the company since William's death in 1871, and when Thomas died in 1893 Arthur took over. He led the company until its merger with the firm of Norman and Beard in 1916.Obituary: Arthur George Hill, The Musical Times, Vol. 64, No. 965 (1 July 1923), p. 507.
He became a Docteur des lettres at the University of Lille in 1913 on a thesis called Christian Art in Spain. Arthur George Hill, The Organ, Vol III:10 (October 1923), p. 120.
In June 1923 he fell ill with pneumonia and died shortly thereafter.
