[[MGM Resorts International]]

CATEGORIES: MGM Resorts International, Companies based in Paradise, Nevada, Companies established in 2000, Companies listed on the New York Stock Exchange, Gambling companies of the United States, Hospitality companies of the United States, 2000 establishments in Nevada

MGM Resorts International ([tpl]NYSE|MGM[/tpl])  is a Paradise, Nevada based gaming and hospitality company. It is the second largest gaming company in the world by revenue—about $6 billion in 2009. It owns and operates 15 properties in Nevada, Mississippi, and Michigan, and has 50% investments in four other properties in Nevada, Illinois and Macau, China.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.reuters.com/article/topNews/idUSN2535785320080126 |title= Fire Strikes Las Vegas Hotel-casino |accessdate=2008-01-25 |last= |first= |authorlink= |coauthors= |date=2008-01-25 |format= |work= Reuters|publisher= |quote= [/tpl]MGM MIRAGE Shareholders Approve Name Change to MGM Resorts Bloomberg
The company became MGM Mirage on May 31, 2000, with the merger of MGM Grand and Mirage Resorts. In the mid-2000s, growth of its non-gaming (lodging, food, retail) revenue began to outpace gaming receipts and demand for high-rise condominiums was surging, with median property prices in Las Vegas twice the national average.[tpl]cite news|title=HAVENS; Condo Hotels Move Beyond Resort Towns| publisher=New York Times |date=2004-04-02|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2004/04/02/travel/havens-condo-hotels-move-beyond-resort-towns.html | first=Dennis | last=Rodkin[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=In Las Vegas, the house loses| publisher=Guardian |date=2009-07-29|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/cifamerica/2009/jul/29/las-vegas-housing-foreclosures-banks | location=London | first=Sadhbh | last=Walshe[/tpl]  The company shifted its business model from fully owning and operating resorts and casinos, to being more real estate focused—launching the massive Citycenter mixed-use project.[ref]
