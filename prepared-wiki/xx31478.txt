[[Aethon]]

CATEGORIES: Personification in Greek mythology, Roman mythology, Greek gods, Legendary birds, Mythological horses

The ancient Greek word aithôn means "burning", "blazing" or "shining." Less strictly, it can denote the colour red-brown, or "tawny." Il. 2.839 ; oxen at Od.18.372; and an eagle at Il. 15.690 (cf. In Greek and Roman mythology there are a number of characters known as Aethon. Most are horses, variously belonging to:
The name is twice applied to humans. In Odyssey 19.183, it is the pseudonym a disguised Odysseus assumes during his interview with Penelope upon his return to Ithaca. According to fr. 43a.5 of Hesiod's Catalogue of Women, Erysichthon was also known as Aethon due to the "burning" hunger (aithôn limos) he was made to endure by Demeter (cf.
