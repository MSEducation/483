[[François Garasse]]

CATEGORIES: 1585 births, 1631 deaths, 17th-century French Jesuits, 17th-century deaths from plague (disease)

François Garasse (1585-1631) was a French Jesuit polemicist. He was known for intemperate attacks on other theologians and thinkers, including Lucilio Vanini and Pierre Charron, whom he called athée et le patriarche des esprits forts.[tpl]CathEncy|wstitle=Pierre Charron[/tpl]
He was born at Angoulême. At the Jesuit Collège Ste. Marthe in Poitiers in 1607-8, he taught Jean-Louis Guez de Balzac. He died at Poitiers, of the plague.[tpl]CathEncy|wstitle=Poitier[/tpl]
He was himself made the target of an anti-Jesuit work of 1626 by Duvergier de Hauranne.[tpl]CathEncy|wstitle=Duvergier de Hauranne[/tpl]
