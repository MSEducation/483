[[Counter Drain railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Disused railway stations in Lincolnshire, Former Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway stations, Railway stations opened in 1866, Railway stations closed in 1880, Railway stations opened in 1881, Railway stations closed in 1959

Counter Drain railway station was a remote station in Lincolnshire serving the village of Tongue End.[tpl]cite web |url=http://homepages.which.net/~rex/bourne/tongueend.htm |last=Needle |first=Justin |title=Tongue End, Lincolnshire |accessdate=12 October 2008 [/tpl] It was on the route of the Spalding and Bourne Railway (opened 1866),[tpl]cite book |last=Casserley |first=H.C. |title=Britain's Joint Lines |date=April 1968 |publisher=Ian Allan |location=Shepperton |isbn=0-7110-0024-7 |chapter=Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway |page=15 [/tpl] later part of the Midland and Great Northern Joint Railway which ran across East Anglia to the Norfolk Coast. The station opened with the line on 1 August 1866, closed temporarily between 9 October 1880 and 1 February 1881, and closed permanently on 2 March 1959,[tpl]cite book |last=Butt |first=R.V.J. |title=The Directory of Railway Stations |year=1995 |publisher=Patrick Stephens Ltd |location=Yeovil |isbn=1-85260-508-1 |id=R508 |page=69 [/tpl] although the line remained opened for goods until 1964.British Railways Atlas.1947. p.17[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pastscape.org.uk/hob.aspx?hob_id=506996|title=National monument record[/tpl] The three intermediate stations between [tpl]stnlnk|Spalding[/tpl] and [tpl]stnlnk|Bourne[/tpl] had unusual names, because there were few nearby settlements; "Counter Drain" was the name of a drainage ditch close to the station.[tpl]cite book |last=Wrottesley |first=A.J. |title=The Midland & Great Northern Joint Railway |edition=2nd |year=1981 |origyear=1970 |publisher=David & Charles |location=Newton Abbot |isbn=0-7153-8173-3 |page=23 [/tpl]
