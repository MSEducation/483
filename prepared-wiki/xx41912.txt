[[Torey Hayden]]

Victoria Lynn Hayden, known as Torey L. Hayden (born 21 May 1951 in Livingston, Montana, U.S.), is a child psychologist, special education teacher, university lecturer and writer of non-fiction books based on her real-life experiences with teaching and counseling children with special needs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.torey-hayden.com/the_books.htm |title=The Books |publisher=Torey Hayden official website |date= |accessdate=2012-08-17[/tpl]
Subjects covered in her books include autism, Tourette syndrome, sexual abuse, fetal alcohol syndrome, and elective mutism (now called selective mutism), her specialty.

==Biography==

Hayden attended high school in Billings, Montana and graduated in 1969. She attended Whitman College in Walla Walla, Washington. She received a Master's degree in Special Education from Montana State University Billings in 1975 and moved to University of Minnesota in Minneapolis for a doctorate in educational psychology. While there, she also worked with the Department of Child and Adolescent Psychiatry in the university hospitals.
Hayden moved to Wales in 1980 and married a Scotsman named Ken in 1982. In 1985 they had a daughter (Sheena). Hayden is presently divorced.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.torey-hayden.com/biography.htm |title=Biography |publisher=Torey Hayden official website |date= |accessdate=2012-08-17[/tpl]
In Wales Hayden has worked primarily with charities associated with child neglect and abuse, including Childline, the NSPCC, the Samaritans and the Citizens' Advice Bureau.[tpl]cite web|title=Hall of Fame 2007 Torey Hayden Author|url=http://www.nspcc.org.uk/get-involved/thank-you/hall-of-fame/hall-of-fame-celebs-2007/torey-hayden/torey-hayden_wda74788.html|publisher=National Society for the Prevention of Cruelty to Children|accessdate=July 23, 2011[/tpl]
She has written four books of fiction in addition to her non-fiction books (see below).
