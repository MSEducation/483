</ref> It contains the spurious text of the [[Comma Johanneum]] after 1 John 5:8 (in the same location as in the [[Codex Cavensis]]).<ref>Nestle-Aland, ''Novum Testamentum Latine'', [[Deutsche Bibelgesellschaft]], Stuttgart 2008, p. 623.</ref> It reads ''filio'' (''for son'') in 1 John 5:10, where the majority of manuscripts have ''Deo'' (''for God'').<ref name = NA624>Nestle-Aland, ''Novum Testamentum Latine'', [[Deutsche Bibelgesellschaft]], Stuttgart 2008, p. 624.</ref> In 5:17 it reads ''iniquitas'' (''wrongdoing'') for ''iniustitia'' (''unrighteousness'').<ref name = NA624/>

==History==

IThe manuscript was written by the monk Winithar and examined and described by Gustav Scherrer and Samuel Berger.[tpl]Cite book|first=Samuel|last= Berger|title=Histoire de la Vulgate pendant les premiers si√®cles du Moyen Age|location=Paris|year=1893|pages=412|url=http://www.archive.org/stream/histoiredelavul01berggoog#page/n442/mode/2up |language=fr[/tpl] The manuscript was collated by Henry Julian White.
