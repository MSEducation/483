[[Ellison Pogo]]

CATEGORIES: 1947 births, 2013 deaths, Solomon Islands Anglicans, 20th-century Anglican archbishops, Archbishops of Melanesia, Knights Commander of the Order of the British Empire

Ellison Leslie Pogo KBE (1947 – 13 May 2013) was a Solomon Islands bishop. He was the Archbishop of Melanesia and Bishop of Central Melanesia from 1994 until December 9, 2008.Photo of Bishop Pogo He was the third Archbishop of Melanesia, following Amos Waiaru. He was married to Roslyn and had three adult children.[tpl]cite web|author=May 14, 2013 |url=http://episcopaldigitalnetwork.com/ens/2013/05/14/former-melanesian-archbishop-ellison-pogo-dies/ |title=Former Melanesian Archbishop Ellison Pogo dies |publisher=Episcopaldigitalnetwork.com |date=2013-05-14 |accessdate=2013-06-08[/tpl]
Pogo was educated at St John's College, Auckland ”Who's Who 1996 “(London, A & C Black ISBN 0-7136-4255-6) and ordained in 1979. After an early post at Anderson's Bay, Dunedin he was Bishop of Ysabel in the Solomon Islands until his translation to Melanesia.
Sir Ellison was the senior primate of the Anglican Communion until his retirement and chairman of the design group of the 2008 Lambeth Conference, and Chairman of Pacific Theological College.Visit of Archbishop of Canterbury He was also one of the few Anglican Primates to have been knighted by Elizabeth II. The Archbishop of Canterbury awarded him the Cross of St Augustine in October 2008.
