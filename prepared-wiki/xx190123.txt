[[Will Aronson]]

CATEGORIES: 1981 births, Harvard University alumni, Tisch School of the Arts alumni, American musical theatre composers, Living people, Berlin University of the Arts alumni

William Landry Aronson (born 1981, New Haven, Connecticut) is a contemporary American composer and writer for musical theater.

==Education==

Aronson holds a B.A. in music from Harvard University. As an undergraduate, Aronson was the composer of Hasty Pudding Theatricals' 154th production, Snow Place Like Home,http://www.thecrimson.com/article.aspx?ref=254138 and co-author/lyricist of its 155th production, It's a Wonderful Afterlife.http://castalbumcollector.com/recordings/14570
After graduation from Harvard, Aronson studied Music Theory as a Fulbright Scholar at Universität der Künste in Berlin.http://us.fulbrightonline.org/program_student_us.html?id=363
Aronson holds an M.F.A. from the Graduate Musical Theatre Writing Program of the Tisch School of the Arts at New York University. While studying at NYU, he received the ASCAP Frederick Loewe Scholarshiphttp://www.ascap.com/eventsawards/awards/foundation/scholarships/loewe.html and a 2006 Baryshnikov Fellowship.http://www.nyu.edu/public.affairs/releases/detail/1077 In 2007 he was named by the Dramatists Guild of America as one of "50 to Watch".

==Work==

Aronson wrote the score for a musical version of the Korean movie My Scary Girl (book and lyrics by Kyoung-ae Kang), which ran in Seoul, South Korea. The show won Best Original Musical (small theater category) at the 2009 Korea Musical Awards.http://www.koreamusicalawards.com/ An English-language version of the show, with book co-written by Mark St. Germain and additional lyrics by William Finn, ran at Barrington Stage Company's Stage II, July 10–26, 2008.http://www.playbill.com/news/article/116390.html The New York Musical Theater Festival (NYMF) presented the Korean version of My Scary Girl October 1–4, 2009. My Scary Girl was named the Outstanding New Musical at NYMF for 2009.http://www.nymf.org/Story-461.html
A reading of Aronson's musical The Trouble with Doug, co-written with Daniel Maté, was directed by Victoria Clark at the NAMT theater festival in 2010.http://www.variety.com/article/VR1118021404?refcatid=15&printerfriendly=truehttp://www.playbill.com/news/article/142223-Victoria-Clark-and-Christopher-Ashley-to-Direct-NAMT-Musicals
With William Finn as lyricist, Aronson has composed songs for Sybille Pearson’s play Next, Mary Testa’s Sleepless Variations, and Finn’s own Songs of Innocence and Experience.http://www.curtainup.com/barrington08.html Aronson was the musical arranger for the Finn-Lapine musical Little Miss Sunshine performed at La Jolla Playhouse in 2011.http://theater.nytimes.com/2011/03/09/theater/reviews/little-miss-sunshine-the-musical-review.html?pagewanted=all
In 2011 Aronson composed the score for Mormons, Mothers and Monsters (book and lyrics by Sam Salmond), which ran at Barrington Stage Company's Stage II.http://articles.boston.com/2011-05-22/ae/29571940_1_mormon-musical-theater-scene-north-shore-music-theatre
In 2012, Aronson won a Korea Musical Award for best music, based on his work as composer in Bungee Jump.
