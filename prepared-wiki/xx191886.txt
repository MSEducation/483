Emmanuel Kakoko Etepé (Born 22 November 1950) is a former Congolese football forward player[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.kicker.de/news/fussball/bundesliga/vereine/11376/nationalspieler_etepe-kakoko.html | title = Kakoko, Etepe | language = German | publisher = kicker.de | accessdate = 7 May 2011[/tpl] who represented Zaire in 1974 FIFA World Cup.[tpl]FIFA player|51768|Etepe Kakoko[/tpl]

==Career==

He played for Imana Kinshasa, VfB Stuttgart (1981–82) and 1. FC Saarbrücken.[tpl]cite web | publisher = fussballdaten.de | title = Etepe Kakoko | url = http://www.fussballdaten.de/spieler/kakokoetepe/ | language = German | accessdate = 12 August 2009[/tpl] Kakoko finished fourth in the voting for France Football's 1973 African Player of the Year.[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Pierrend, José Luis|title=African Player of the Year 1973|url=http://www.rsssf.com/miscellaneous/afrpoy73.html|date=14 February 2000|accessdate=12 August 2009[/tpl] In 2006, he was selected by CAF as one of the best 200 African football players of the last 50 years.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Sport24.gr|title=Οι 200 κορυφαίοι Αφρικανοί|url=http://www.sport24.gr/football/article273152.ece?service=print|date=25 October 2006 | language = Greek | accessdate = 15 December 2011[/tpl]

==Personal==

His son, Yannick is also a footballer, currently on the books of SV Waldhof Mannheim.
