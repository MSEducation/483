Ryan Marcus Long (born February 3, 1973) is a former Major League Baseball player. Long played for the Kansas City Royals in the [tpl]by|1997[/tpl] season. In six games, Long had twi hits in nine at-bats, with two RBIs. He played right field, and batted and threw right-handed. He was drafted by the Royals in the second round of the 1991 amateur draft. Although he played in only six games for the Royals, Long spentten seasons playing at various levels in the minor leagues. While playing baseball for Dobie High School, in Texas, Long and the Longhorns lost a playoff game against a team that featured furture New York Yankee, Andy Pettitte.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.altoonamirror.com/page/content.detail/id/542335/Giger--Pettitte-more-impressive-off-the-field-than-on.html?nav=751|title=Pettitte more impressive off the field than on|date=15 September 2010|publisher=Altoona Mirror|accessdate=7 July 2011[/tpl]
Long is currently a hitting coach for the Pittsburgh Pirates' Double-A affiliate, the Altoona Curve. He previously served as a hitting coach for the Bradenton Marauders in the Florida State League, the Pirates High-A affiliate[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.piratesprospects.com/2010/12/pirates-shuffle-minor-league-coaching-staffs.html?wpmp_switcher=mobile|title=Pirates Shuffle Minor League Coaching Staffs|date=December 23, 2010|publisher=Pirates Prospects|accessdate=7 July 2011[/tpl] and the Burlington Bees, the Midwest League affiliate for the Royals.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oursportscentral.com/services/releases/?id=3559613|title=Royals Announce Bees 2008 Field Staff|date=14 November 2007|publisher=Our Sports Central|accessdate=9 August 2010[/tpl]
