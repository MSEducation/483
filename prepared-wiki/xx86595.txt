Melody A.M. is the debut studio album by Norwegian electronic music duo Röyksopp, released on 3 September 2001 by Wall of Sound. Songs from this album have been used in television adverts and computer games; "Remind Me" was formally featured in an advertisement for GEICO Insurance in one of their popular Caveman ads, while "Eple" was licensed by Apple and used as the introduction music for the Mac OS X v10.3 setup assistant. The Silicone Soul remix of "Poor Leno" was featured in the soundtrack to the 2003 snowboarding video game SSX 3.
On 21 November 2007 the album was included on The Guardian[tpl]'[/tpl]s series of the 1000 Albums to Hear Before You Die.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.theguardian.com/music/2007/nov/21/1000tohearbeforeyoudie4 |title=1000 albums to hear before you die |work=The Guardian |date=21 November 2007 |accessdate=24 November 2009[/tpl] It is also listed in a similar selection, called 1001 Albums You Must Hear Before You Die, in a series of books by Quintessence Editions. On 24 November 2009, Melody A.M. was named the best Norwegian album of the decade by Norway's largest newspaper, Verdens Gang.[tpl]cite web |last=Talseth |first=Thomas |url=http://www.vg.no/musikk/artikkel.php?artid=588237 |title=Röyksopp åpnet dører med debuten |language=Norwegian |work=Verdens Gang |publisher=VG Multimedia AS |date=24 November 2009 |accessdate=24 November 2009[/tpl]
As of 2005, the album had sold 750,000 copies worldwide,[tpl]cite book |last=Dimery |first=Robert |title=1001 Albums You Must Hear Before You Die |year=2005 |publisher=Quintessence Editions |location=New York, NY |isbn=0-7893-1371-5 |pages=750[/tpl] including 454,271 in the United Kingdom alone.[tpl]cite web |last=Jones |first=Alan |url=http://www.musicweek.com/news/read/london-events-swing-sales-on-uk-charts/028286 |title=London events swing sales on UK charts |work=Music Week |publisher=Intent Media |date=11 July 2005 |accessdate=12 February 2014[/tpl]
