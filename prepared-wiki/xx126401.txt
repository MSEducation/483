[[Karyes]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in Laconia

Karyes ([tpl]lang-el|Καρυές[/tpl], before 1930: Αράχωβα - ArachovaName changes of settlements in Greece) is a village and a former community in Laconia, Peloponnese, Greece. Since the 2011 local government reform it is part of the municipality Sparti, of which it is a municipal unit.Kallikratis law Greece Ministry of Interior [tpl]el icon[/tpl] It is located roughly midway between Tripoli and Sparti. Population 926 (2001).

==History==

Caryae or Karyai ([tpl]lang-grc|Καρυαί[/tpl]) was a town in ancient Laconia, near the border with Arcadia. It was situated on the road from Tegea to Sparta.[tpl]cite DGRG|volume=1|title=Caryae|pages=555[/tpl] Caryae had a sanctuary of Artemis, where the Lacedaemonian maidens held chorus dances.Pausanias, Description of Greece, 3.10.7 The caryatids, sculpted female figures used in architecture, were probably named after these maidens from Caryae. The modern village Karyes was named after this ancient town.
