[[Defence Force Ground]]

CATEGORIES: Cricket grounds in Namibia, Buildings and structures in Windhoek

Defence Force Ground is a cricket ground in Windhoek, Namibia.  The first recorded match on the ground was in 1988 when the South African Defence Force played Boland in a first-class match.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/19/1342_f.html|title=First-Class Matches played on Defence Force Ground, Windhoek|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]  At the time Namibia was known as South-West Africa and was a part of South Africa, until gaining independence in 1990.  The last recorded match played on the ground came in 2001 when Namibia played the Marylebone Cricket Club.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/19/1342_misc.html|title=Other matches played on Defence Force Ground, Windhoek|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]
