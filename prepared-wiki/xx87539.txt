[[Cheltenham railway station, Melbourne]]

CATEGORIES: Premium Melbourne railway stations, Railway stations in Melbourne, Railway stations opened in 1881

Cheltenham railway station is located on the Frankston line in Victoria, Australia. It serves the south-eastern Melbourne suburb of Cheltenham opening on 19 December 1881.Cheltenham VicsigCheltenham Railway Station Kingston City Council It was upgraded to a Premium station on 8 March 1996.[tpl]cite journal|date=October 1997|title=Upgrading Eltham to a Premium Station|journal=Newsrail|publisher=Australian Railway Heritage Society|page=page 310[/tpl]
There was previously a siding on the eastern side of the station at the Melbourne end.Cheltenham Interlocking Information Vicsig A signal box is inside the station building, and was installed in 1972 to control terminating trains at Platform 1.Cheltenham Signal Box
Cheltenham station plays a key part in the Cheltenham Major Activity Centre. The station will be the subject of large future developments, including a new station forecourt, with a terraced plaza (to address the heritage listed buildings at the station) and a new taxi rank, similar to that at Mentone station.Cheltenham Structure Plan Draft Report 22 March 2010 Kingston City Council

==Platforms & services==

Cheltenham has two side platforms and one dock platform. The latter is served by one afternoon terminating service from Flinders Street that returns empty to the city. It is serviced by Metro Trains' Frankston line services.Frankston Line timetable Public Transport Victoria
