[[28th Cavalry Regiment]]

CATEGORIES: Buffalo Soldier units and formations, Cavalry regiments of the United States Army, Black history in the United States military, Military units and formations established in 1943

The 28th Cavalry Regiment (Horse) (Colored) was a short-lived African-American unit of the United States Army.  The 28th Cavalry was the last horse-mounted cavalry regiment formed by the U.S. Army. The regiment was formed as part of the 2nd Cavalry Division in 1943 and inactivated in north Africa in 1944 without seeing combat.2nd Cavalry Division
The regiment was formed at Camp Lockett, California on 25 February 1943 and assigned to the 4th Cavalry Brigade of the 2nd Division.  The regiment shipped out on 3 March 1944 from Hampton Roads, Virginia.  A little over two weeks after arrival in Algeria, the 2nd Cavalry Division and its component regiments were inactivated.  Troops from the 28th Cavalry were given the option of transferring to service units or volunteering for combat but at the loss of all rank.28th Cavalry Some troops from the 28th Cavalry were used to activate the 6487th Engineer Battalion.World War II Order of Battle, p. 316.
