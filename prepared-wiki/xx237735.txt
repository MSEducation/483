[[Uriel Feige]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Gödel Prize laureates, Theoretical computer scientists, Modern cryptographers, Public-key cryptographers, Israeli mathematicians, 20th-century mathematicians, 21st-century mathematicians, Israeli computer scientists, Israeli cryptographers, Weizmann Institute faculty

Uriel Feige ([tpl]lang-he|אוריאל פייגה[/tpl]) is an Israeli computer scientist who was a doctoral student of Adi Shamir. He is notable for co-inventing the Feige–Fiat–Shamir identification scheme along with Amos Fiat and Adi Shamir. He won the Gödel Prize in 2001 "for the PCP theorem and its applications to hardness of approximation".
Uriel Feige currently holds the post of Professor at the Department of Computer Science and Applied Mathematics, the Weizmann Institute of Science, Rehovot in Israel.Uriel Feige's profile at the Weizmann Institute
