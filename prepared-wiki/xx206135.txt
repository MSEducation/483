Rick Rembielak is an American college baseball coach, who has served as the head coach of the Akron Zips baseball program since the start of the 2012 season.[tpl]cite web|title=Rick Rembielak|url=http://www.gozips.com/sports/bsb/coaches/rembielak_rick|work=GoZips.com|accessdate=10 April 2012|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/66pTCBp5O|archivedate=2012-04-10[/tpl]  As of the end of the 2012 season, his career head coaching record is 532-432-1.

==Playing career==

Rembielak played college baseball at Miami (OH) from 1979-1981.  He was drafted in the 1981 Major League Baseball Draft by the Baltimore Orioles and played five seasons of minor league baseball in the Orioles and Chicago Cubs organizations.[tpl]cite web|title=#23 Rick Rembielak|url=http://www.ramblinwreck.com/sports/m-basebl/mtt/rick_rembielak_727770.html|work=RamblinWreck.com|publisher=Georgia Tech Sports Information|accessdate=March 19, 2013|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6FE2utaiU|archivedate=March 19, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Richard Rembielak|url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/minors/player.cgi?id=rembie001ric|work=Baseball-Reference.org|accessdate=March 19, 2013|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6FE32D5cv|archivedate=March 19, 2013[/tpl]

==Coaching career==

He served as the head baseball coach at Kent State of the Mid-American Conference (MAC) from 1994-2004, and he had a 373–251–1 (.597) record. This included a 200–100 (.667) record against MAC opponents. He won four regular season MAC championships and three MAC tournament titles. He was named the MAC Coach of the Year in 1996, 2000, and 2003.
Rembielak was the head coach of Wake Forest from 2005-2009.  In 2007, Rembielak's team finished second in the ACC Tournament, losing 1–0 to North Carolina in the championship game.  The team earned an at-large berth to the NCAA tournament.  At Wake, Rembielak posted a 95–81 record, including a 42–47 record in conference play.  Wake Forest replaced Rembielak with Tom Walter.[tpl]cite web|title=Tom Walter Named Head Baseball Coach at Wake Forest|url=http://wakeforestsports.cstv.com/sports/m-basebl/spec-rel/061609aaa.html|publisher=WAKE FOREST|accessdate=2 June 2012|date=16 June 2009[/tpl]
From 2010-2011, he was a volunteer assistant at Georgia Tech,[tpl]cite web|title=Rick Rembielak Named Georgia Tech Volunteer Assistant Coach|url=http://ramblinwreck.cstv.com/sports/m-basebl/spec-rel/091010aaa.html|publisher=CBS Interactive|accessdate=2 June 2012|date=10 September 2010[/tpl] after being let go by Wake Forest.[tpl]cite web|title=Wake Forest Will Not Renew Rembielak's Contract|url=http://wakeforestsports.cstv.com/sports/m-basebl/spec-rel/052209aaa.html|publisher=WAKE FOREST|accessdate=2 June 2012|date=22 May 2009[/tpl]
Prior to the start of the 2012 season, Rembielak was hired as the head coach at Akron.[tpl]cite web|title=Rick Rembielak|url=http://www.gozips.com/sports/bsb/coaches/rembielak_rick|work=GoZips.com|publisher=Akron Sports Information|accessdate=March 19, 2013|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/6FE3Aqmp6|archivedate=March 19, 2013[/tpl]
