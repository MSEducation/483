[[Podkraj, Ig]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in the Municipality of Ig

Podkraj ([tpl]IPA-sl|pɔtˈkɾai̯|pron[/tpl]) is a village in the Municipality of Ig in central Slovenia, on the edge of the marshland south of the capital Ljubljana. The municipality is part of the traditional region of Inner Carniola and is now included in the Central Slovenia Statistical Region.Ig municipal site

==History==

During the Second World War, Italian forces burned one house in Podkraj in October 1942.Savnik, Roman, ed. 1971. Krajevni leksikon Slovenije, vol. 2. Ljubljana: Državna založba Slovenije, p. 436. Podkraj was formally established as an independent settlement in 2007, when it was separated from the territory of Tomišelj. Prior to this it was a hamlet of Tomišelj.

==Church==

The local church is a chapel of ease dedicated to John the Baptist. It dates from the late Middle Ages and was remodeled in the Baroque style in the 17th century. The interior furnishings are Baroque.Slovenian Ministry of Culture register of national heritage reference number ešd 2569
