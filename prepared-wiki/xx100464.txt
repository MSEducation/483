[[Jnanpith Award]]

CATEGORIES: Awards established in 1961, Events of The Times Group, Indian literary awards, Indian literature, Recipients of the Jnanpith Award

The Jnanpith Award is a literary award in India. Along with the Sahitya Akademi Fellowship,Report from The Hindu, January 2007. Noted writer Manoj Das (in January 2007) "received the country's highest literary honour – Sahitya Akademi Fellowship." it is one of the two most prestigious literary honours in the country.[tpl]cite journal |url=http://www.frontline.in/navigation/?type=static&page=flonnet&rdurl=fl1603/16031170.htm |newspaper=Frontline |title=The multi-faceted playwright |author=Parvathi Menon |volume=16 |number=3 |year=1999[/tpl] The award was instituted in 1961. Any Indian citizen who writes in any of the official languages of India is eligible for the honour. It is presented by the Bharatiya Jnanpith, a trust founded by the Sahu Jain family, the publishers of the newspaper The Times of India.

==The Award==

The name of the award is taken from Sanskrit words jnāna and pīṭha (knowledge-seat). It carries a cheque for [tpl]INR[/tpl]11 lakh, a citation plaque and a bronze replica of Saraswati, the Indian goddess of knowledge, music, and the arts.[tpl]cite news|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2005-03-20/india/27865684_1_jnanpith-award-jnanpith-selection-board-bharatiya-jnanpith| title=Jnanpith award for Jayakanthan|work=The Times of India |accessdate=12 November 2007|date=20 March 2005[/tpl]
Prior to 1982, the awards were given for a single work by a writer; since then, the award has been given for a lifetime contribution to Indian literature. As of 2012 most Jananpith award winners, nine to be precise, have been writing in Hindi followed by eight in Kannada, five each in Bengali and Malayalam, four each in Oriya and Urdu and three each in Gujarati, Marathi and Telugu and two each in Assamese and Tamil.
Starting with the Bengali writer Ashapoorna Devi in 1976, seven women writers have won the award so far. The other recipients include Amrita Pritam (1981, Punjabi), Mahadevi Varma (1982, Hindi), Qurratulain Hyder (1989, Urdu), Mahasweta Devi (1996, Bengali), Indira Goswami (2000, Assamese) and Pratibha ray (2011, Oriya).
The award announcements have lately been lagging behind the award-years. The awards for the years 2005 and 2006 were announced on 22 November 2008, and were awarded to the Hindi writer Kunwar Narayan for 2005 and jointly to Konkani writer Ravindra Kelekar and Sanskrit scholar Satya Vrat Shastri for 2006.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www1.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/Delhi/Kunwar_Narayan_to_be_awarded_Jnanpith/articleshow/3752703.cms|title=Kunwar Narayan to be awarded Jnanpith|date=24 Nov 2008|work=The Times of India |accessdate=25 November 2008[/tpl] Satya Vrat Shastri is the first Sanskrit poet to be conferred the award since its inception. The awards for the 45th and 46th Jnanpith for the years 2009 and 2010 respectively, were announced on 20 September 2011. The 45th award was jointly conferred on Hindi littérateurs Amar Kant and Sri Lal Sukla, and the 46th on the Kannada littérateur Chandrashekhara Kambara. The 48th Jnanpith award for the year 2012 was announced on 17 April 2013 and was conferred to Telugu novelist, short-story writer and poet Ravuri Bharadhwaja for his overall contribution to Telugu literature.
