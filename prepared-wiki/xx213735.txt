[[Trimeresurus borneensis]]

CATEGORIES: Animals described in 1872, Endemic fauna of Borneo, Trimeresurus

Trimeresurus borneensis is a venomous pit viper species found on the island of Borneo. No subspecies are currently recognized.[tpl]ITIS|id=634905|taxon=Trimeresurus borneensis|accessdate=28 July 2008[/tpl]

==Description==

Scalation includes 19-21 rows of dorsal scales at midbody, 152-180 ventral scales, undivided anal scale, 45-58 divided subcaudal scales, and 8-11 supralabial scales.
Its coloration varies. Individuals may be brown with darker brown saddles, or light brown with a few darker markings, or even bright yellow with darker markings.
Adults may attain [tpl]convert|83|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] in snout-vent length (SVL).Das, Indraneil. 2006. A Photographic Guide to Snakes and Other Reptiles of Borneo. Ralph Curtis Books. Sanibel Island, Florida. 144 pp. ISBN 0-88359-061-1. (Trimeresurus borneensis, p. 57.)

==Geographic range==

T. borneensis is found on the island of Borneo (Brunei, Kalimantan, Sabah, Sarawak), the Malay Peninsula, Sumatra, and southern Thailand.
The type locality given is "Sarawack" (= Sarawak, Borneo).

==Habitat==

It is found in forests below an altitude of [tpl]convert|1,130|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl]. Even though the tail is prehensile, adults are usually on the forest floor. Juveniles may be in low vegetation.

==Diet==

T. borneensis preys upon small mammals.

==Reproduction==

Whether this species of Trimeresurus lays eggs or bears live young is as yet unknown.
