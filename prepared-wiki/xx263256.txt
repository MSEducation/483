[[Public Bath House No. 4]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Renaissance Revival architecture in New York, Buildings and structures completed in 1925, Buildings and structures in Yonkers, New York

Public Bath House No. 4 was a historic public bath located at Yonkers, Westchester County, New York. It was built in 1925 and was a two story, six bay wide pastel stucco building in a Second Renaissance Revival / Mission style.  It features a central pavilion flanked by slightly recessed bays containing modified Palladian windows.  The interior was in four sections: reception area, custodian's apartment, baths, and a swimming pool.  It was maintained by the Laporta family, specifically Gabrielle Laporta of Colts Neck, NJ.
