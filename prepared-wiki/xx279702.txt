[[Puthooram Aromal Chekaver]]

CATEGORIES: Hindu warriors, Malayali people, 16th-century Indian people

Aromal Chekaver (or Chevaker)[tpl]cite book|title=A Social History Of India |first=S. N. |last=Sadasivan |authorlink=S. N. Sadasivan |publisher=APH Publishing |year=2000 |isbn=9788176481700|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=Be3PCvzf-BYC[/tpl] was a legendary hero who is believed to have lived during 16th century in northern Kerala, India. He was a warrior figure of Vadakkan Pattukal (northern ballads). He is the brother of Puthooram Unniyarcha Chekaver, another legendary warrior figure of the same ballads. The songs and chronicles have to this day kept the legend alive. He was born in the famous Puthooram Veedu, a Chekavar Thiyya[tpl]cite book |title=Medieval Indian Literature: An Anthology |volume=2 |author=Ayyappappanikkar |others=Sahitya Akademi |publisher=Sahitya Akademi |isbn=9788126003655 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=KYLpvaKJIMEC&pg=PA316 |page=316[/tpl] family of northern Kerala known as Malabar. Aromal Chekaver belonged to the Thiyya community, a social group that enjoyed status and power in the society of his times and before. Through the times of Aromal Chekaverâ€™s line (14 to 16th Century AD) and despite their social segregation the Thiyyas continued excelling as swordsmen, distinguished Ayurvedic physicians, Sanskrit scholars, astrologers etc. At that time Kerala had 42 schools of martial art of which 18 were under the control of Aringodar Chekaver, 4 were under the Puthooram veedu and the rest under other Chekavers. His brother Unnikannan was also a famous warrior. He was trained in the martial arts of Kalari. He is praised for his bravery in Vadakkan Pattukal.
According to the folklore, Unniyarcha spurns Chanthu Chekaver's love for her, which leads to the murder of her brother Aromal Chekaver by Chanthu Chekaver. Later, Aromalunni, the son of Unniyarcha, takes the revenge against Chanthu Chekaver.

==Popular culture==

Oru Vadakkan Veeragatha, the Malayalam movie released in 1989, shows a different angle of the folklore.
