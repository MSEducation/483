[[John Guppy]]

John Guppy is the owner of Gilt Edge Soccer Marketing, a soccer-specific marketing agency.[tpl]cite web | title=Spotlight: John Guppy | work=sportsbusinessjournal.com |url=http://www.sportsbusinessjournal.com/article/60693[/tpl]

==Career==

Guppy was Executive VP of the Metrostars until 2005, when he was chosen by former Chicago Fire owners AEG to replace the popular Peter Wilt in Chicago—a move that resulted in substantial fan backlash and protest.[tpl]cite web | title=Fire Fire Peter Wilt, Fans Protest | work=chicagoist.com |url=http://chicagoist.com/2005/04/19/fire_fire_peter_wilt_fans_protest_at_season_opener.php| accessdate=December 31, 2007[/tpl] He successfully managed the team’s move from Soldier Field to Toyota Park in Bridgeview in 2006.[tpl]cite web | title=A chat with John Guppy | work=espn.com |url=http://soccernet.espn.go.com/columns/story?id=364407&root=mls&&cc=5901[/tpl] and was recognized by Crains Business Magazine as one of Chicago’s 40 under 40 important business leaders.[tpl]cite web | title=Focus: 40 under 40 | work=Crain's Chicago Business | date= 23 October 2006 |url=http://highbeam.com/doc/1G1-153420720.html[/tpl]
He was the president and CEO of the Fire until agreeing to part ways with new team owners, Andell Holdings, on April 11, 2008.[tpl]cite news | title=Chicago Fire president and GM John Guppy leaves team | work=usatoday.com |url=http://usatoday.com/sports/soccer/2008-04-11-1137608337_x.htm | date=April 11, 2008 | accessdate=May 6, 2010[/tpl]

==Personal==

John Guppy was born in Winchester, England and is a 1990 graduate of New Hampshire College and 1992 graduate of the University of Massachusetts Amherst. He currently lives in Naperville, Illinois with his family.
John is also the cousin of former England national team midfielder Steve Guppy.
