[[Kelley Abbey]]

CATEGORIES: Australian choreographers, Australian film actresses, Australian television actresses, Australian stage actresses, Helpmann Award winners, Living people, 1966 births, People from Brisbane

Kelley Abbey (born 28 July 1966) is an Australian actress, choreographer, singer and dancer. She has been a leading performer and choreographer in TV, film and musical theatre for more than 20 years.She remains best known for her role on soap opera E Street as Jo-Jo Adams.

==Awards==

Abbey won the Green Room Award, Mo Award[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.moawards.com/?page_id=638|title=Winners – 23rd Mo Awards 1998|year=1998|work=moawards.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] and Variety Heart awards for her performance as the lead in Sweet Charity.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.stagewhispers.com.au/news/fame-%E2%80%93-remember-my-name|title=Fame – Remember My Name|last=Pinne|first=Peter|year=2010|work=stagewhispers.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] She also won a Mo Award for playing the lead in Fame the Musical which she also choreographed.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.moawards.com/?page_id=646|title=Winners – 25th Mo Awards 2000|year=2000|work=moawards.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] She won the Green Room Award for her choreography in Fame and later restaged this show in Asia and South Africa.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.famethemusical.com.au/creatives.html|title=Kelley Abbey – Director and Choreographer|year=2010|work=famethemusical.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] 
Abbey played Sheila in the 2003 Australian cast of Hair[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/articles/2003/04/03/1048962877605.html|title=Hippie replacement|last=Low|first=Lenny Ann|date=4 April 2003|work=smh.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] and Rizzo in an arena production of Grease, and later choreographed an arena stadium tour of Grease – the Arena Spectacular.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.famenetwork.com/pastbuzz/abbeysroadtofame.php|title=Abbey's Road to Fame|date=10 October 1999|work=famenetwork.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] Other musicals Abbey has choreographed include Follies at the Sydney Opera House, Footloose and most recently Hugh Jackman's The Boy from Oz in the Arena[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/entertainment/search-on-for-boy-from-oz/2006/05/13/1146940765598.html|title=Search on for Boy from Oz|date=13 May 2006|work=theage.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] for which she won a Helpmann Award along with Kenny Ortega.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/entertainment/echo-stars-at-helpmanns/2007/08/06/1186252621272.html|title=Echo stars at Helpmanns|last=Gill|first=Raymond|date=7 August 2007|work=theage.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl]

==Choreography==

Abbey was honoured at the Choreography Media Honours in Los Angeles for her choreography in the Oscar-winning feature Happy Feet.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dancecamerawest.org/pr/2007_cmh060707.pdf|title=Choreography Media Honors|last=Hasty|first=Lynn|date=2 May 2007|work=dancecamerawest.org|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] She was also head motion capture consultant on the film, ran "penguin school" and was the principal motion capture performer.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.close-upfilm.com/features/Featuresarchive/happyfeet.html|title=The Effects Story Behind Happy Feet|work=close-upfilm.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl]
Abbey has also choreographed many music videos, pop concert tours, television commercials and awards specials. She appeared in the film In Her Skin starring Guy Pearce and Sam Neill which she choreographed and also plays a small role.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.imdb.com/title/tt0995851/fullcredits#cast|title=In Her Skin|year=2009|work=imdb.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl]
Abbey appears as a special guest judge and choreographer on the hit TV show So You Think You Can Dance[tpl]cite web|url=http://dance.ten.com.au/kelley-abbey.htm|title=Kelly Abbey – Choreographer|work=dance.ten.com.au|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl] and choreographs for the So You Think You Can Dance franchise internationally. She also was chosen to represent Australia on the reality television show Superstars of Dance on the NBC Network.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nbc.com/superstars-of-dance/about/countries/australia/|title=Superstars of Dance|work=nbc.com|accessdate=7 August 2010[/tpl]
