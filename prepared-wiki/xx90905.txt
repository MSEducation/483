Henry Ramón Blanco (born August 29, 1971) is a Venezuelan professional baseball catcher who is currently a coach with the Arizona Diamondbacks. Although working as a coach, Blanco has not officially retired.

==Professional career==

===Los Angeles Dodgers===

Blanco began his professional baseball career in 1989 when he was signed by the Los Angeles Dodgers as an amateur free agent.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/b/blanche01.shtml |title=Henry Blanco statistics |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 23, 2011 [/tpl] He didn't begin to play as a catcher until 1995.[tpl]cite book |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=4i4DAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA56&lpg=PA60&dq=sustaining+a+long+career+by+al+doyle#v=onepage&q=sustaining%20a%20long%20career%20by%20al%20doyle&f=false |title=Sustaining A Long Career |author=Doyle, Al |date=November 2002 |work=Baseball Digest |accessdate=January 23, 2011 [/tpl] After seven years in the minor leagues, he finally blossomed in 1997 when he hit for a .313 batting average in 91 games for the Albuquerque Dukes of the Pacific Coast League.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/minors/player.cgi?id=blanco001hen |title=Henry Blanco minor league statistics |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 23, 2011 [/tpl] Blanco made his major league debut with the Dodgers on July 25, 1997. He returned to play for Albuquerque in 1998 and was granted free agency in October of that year.

===Colorado Rockies===

In December 1998, Blanco signed a contract to play for the Colorado Rockies and won the starting catcher's role for the 1999 season.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/teams/COL/1999.shtml |title=1999 Colorado Rockies |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] Hitting for a .232 batting average, he embellished his defensive credentials by leading National League catchers with 39 baserunners caught stealing.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/leagues/NL/1999-fielding-leaders.shtml |title=1999 National League Fielding Leaders |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl]

===Milwaukee Brewers===

In December 1999, he was traded (along with Jamey Wright) to the Milwaukee Brewers as part of a three-team trade that sent Jeff Cirillo and Scott Karl to Colorado and Justin Miller to the Oakland Athletics.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-almanac.com/players/trades.php?p=blanche01 |title=Henry Blanco Trades and Transactions |publisher=Baseball Almanac |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] Blanco caught the majority of the Brewers' games in 2000, posting a .236 batting average with 7 home runs, 31 runs batted in.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/teams/MIL/2000.shtml |title=2000 Milwaukee Brewers |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] He led National League catchers with a 58.2% baserunners caught stealing percentage, the highest percentage since 1982 when Bob Boone also had a 58.2% success rate.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/leaders/caught_stealing_perc_leagues.shtml |title=Yearly League Leaders & Records for Caught Stealing Percentage |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] His batting average fell to .210 in the 2001 season.

===Atlanta Braves===

In March 2002, he was traded to the Atlanta Braves for Paul Bako and José Cabrera.[tpl]cite news |title=Braves land Blanco, deal Bako, Cabrera |agency=Associated Press |work=The Fayetteville Observer |page=6 |date=March 21, 2002 |accessdate=January 10, 2011 |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=Rm05AAAAIBAJ&sjid=eykMAAAAIBAJ&pg=867,18374405&dq=henry+blanco&hl=en[/tpl]
While Blanco served as a reserve catcher with the Braves working behind Javy Lopez, he also became known as Greg Maddux' personal catcher, a role he inherited from fellow Venezuelan catcher, Eddie Pérez. Although he only produced 22 runs batted in, he had 5 game-winning RBIs and provided solid defense, helping the Braves win the National League Eastern Division title.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20030117&content_id=192378&vkey=news_mlb&fext=.jsp&c_id=null |title=Maddux asks for $16 million |publisher=mlb.mlb.com |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] Blanco was the starting catcher for the Braves in Game 3 of the 2002 National League Division Series and was a late-inning replacement in Game 5 as the Braves were defeated by the San Francisco Giants.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/boxes/SFN/SFN200210050.shtml |title=2002 National League Division Series Game 3 box score |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/boxes/ATL/ATL200210070.shtml |title=2002 National League Division Series Game 5 box score |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] In January 2003, Blanco signed a $1.3 million, one-year contract with the Braves.
The Braves repeated as Eastern Division champions in 2003 however, Blanco's batting average dropped to .199 and he was left off the post-season roster in favor of catcher Johnny Estrada as, the Braves lost to the Chicago Cubs in the 2003 National League Division Series.[tpl]cite news |title=Large crowds expected at Turner Field |agency=Associated Press |work=The Albany Herald |page=3 |date=September 30, 2003 |accessdate=January 10, 2011 |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=5h5EAAAAIBAJ&sjid=9rAMAAAAIBAJ&pg=5100,5079325&dq=henry+blanco&hl=en[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/postseason/2003_NLDS2.shtml |title=2003 National League Division Series |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] When the Braves assigned him to the Triple-A Richmond Braves at the end of the season, Blanco opted for free agency.

===Minnesota Twins===

In December 2003, he signed a one year, $750,000 contract to play for the Minnesota Twins.[tpl]cite news |title=Twins sign ex-Brave Blanco |agency=Associated Press |work=Rome News-Tribune |page=2 |date=December 19, 2003 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=q_ouAAAAIBAJ&sjid=ATwDAAAAIBAJ&pg=4257,827538&dq=henry+blanco&hl=en[/tpl]
Blanco became a valuable member of the Twins when rookie catcher Joe Mauer was injured early in the 2004 season.[tpl]cite news |title=Twins no longer interested in Blanco |agency=Associated Press |publisher=ESPN |date=November 24, 2004 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/mlb/news/story?id=1931314[/tpl] He was credited for helping the Twins pitching staff with his game-calling skills, guiding them to the lowest team earned run average in the league.[tpl]cite news |title=Twins decline option on Guzman |publisher=mlb |date=October 15, 2004 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20041015&content_id=895741&vkey=news_mlb&fext=.jsp&c_id=null[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/leagues/AL/2004.shtml#teams_standard_pitching::7 |title=2004 American League Pitching Statistics |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=March 19, 2011 [/tpl] Twins pitcher, Johan Santana, gave Blanco credit for helping him win the 2004 American League Cy Young Award.[tpl]cite news |title=Back up Blanco is perfect catch for Mets |work=Newsday |location=New York |date=February 21, 2010 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://www.newsday.com/sports/baseball/mets/backup-blanco-is-the-perfect-catch-for-mets-1.1773683[/tpl] He caught the majority of the Twins' games helping them to clinch the American League Central division title.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/teams/MIN/2004.shtml |title=2004 Minnesota Twins |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] Blanco posted career-highs with 10 home runs and 37 runs batted in, while leading the American League catchers with a 49.2% caught stealing percentage. He started all four games of the 2004 American League Division Series, hitting 1 home run along with a .250 batting average as the Twins lost to the New York Yankees.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/postseason/2004_ALDS2.shtml |title=2004 American League Division Series |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl]

===Chicago Cubs===

After failing to reach a contract agreement with the Twins after the 2004 season, Blanco opted for free agency again and, in December 2004 he signed a two year, $2.7 million contract to play for the Chicago Cubs.[tpl]cite news |title=Garciaparra sticking with Cubs |agency=Associated Press |work=USA Today |date=December 7, 2004 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://www.usatoday.com/sports/baseball/nl/cubs/2004-12-07-garciaparra-signs_x.htm?POE=SPOISVA[/tpl] He worked as a back up catcher to Michael Barrett although, many of the Cubs pitchers preferred pitching to Blanco as his 2005 catcher's earned run average of 3.58 was almost 1 run lower than the 4.45 average posted by Barrett.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/b/blanche01-field.shtml#advanced_fielding_c::none |title=2005 Henry Blanco advanced fielding statistics |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/b/barremi01-field.shtml#advanced_fielding_c::none |title=2005 Michael Barrett advanced fielding statistics |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl]
In the 2006 Caribbean Series, Blanco had a walk off double off the top of the opposing shortstop's head in the championship game to give the Leones del Caracas the series championship win over the Tigres del Licey.[tpl]cite news |title=The whole country celebrates together |agency=ESPN |publisher=ESPN |date=February 14, 2006 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/mlb/worldclassic2006/columns/story?id=2323567[/tpl] In the 2006 major league season, he hit for a .266 batting average with a career-high 37 runs batted in and, in November, signed a two-year contract for $5.25 million to remain with the Cubs.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20061115&content_id=1740928&vkey=hotstove2006&fext=.jsp |title=Blanco renews deal to stay in Chicago |publisher=mlb.mlb.com |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] Blanco appeared in only 22 games for the Cubs in 2007, missing two months of the season due to a herniated disc.[tpl]cite news |title=Cubs place Blanco on DL |agency=United Press International |work=UPI.com |date=June 2, 2007 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://www.upi.com/Sports_News/2007/06/02/Cubs-place-Blanco-on-DL/UPI-39881180816378/[/tpl] The Cubs went on to win the National League Central division although, Blanco did not appear in any post-season games. In 2008, Blanco posted career-highs with a .292 batting average and a .325 on-base percentage while serving as back up and mentor to rookie catcher Geovany Soto, who went on to win the 2008 National League Rookie of the Year Award.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/awards/awards_2008.shtml#NLroy |title=2008 National League Rookie of the Year Award voting |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl]  After the 2008 season, Blanco's option was declined making him a free agent.

===San Diego Padres===

In January 2009, he signed a $750,000, one-year contract to play for the San Diego Padres.[tpl]cite news |title=Padres, Henry Blanco agree to $750K, 1-year deal |agency=Associated Press |work=USA Today |date=January 21, 2009 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://www.usatoday.com/sports/baseball/nl/2009-01-21-1615665443_x.htm[/tpl] During the 2009 season, he was a pinch-hitter, late-inning defensive replacement, and a mentor for Padres starting catcher Nick Hundley.[tpl]cite web |url=http://sandiego.padres.mlb.com/news/press_releases/press_release.jsp?ymd=20090121&content_id=3761810&vkey=pr_sd&fext=.jsp&c_id=sd |title=Padres agree to terms on a one-year contract with catcher Henry Blanco |publisher=mlb.com |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl]

===New York Mets===

Blanco signed a one year, 1.5 million dollar contract with the New York Mets on December 3, 2009. On May 8, 2010, Blanco hit a walk-off home run to help the Mets defeat the San Francisco Giants 5–4.[tpl]cite news |title=Platoon paying dividends for Mets |agency=Associated Press |work=USA Today |date=May 8, 2010 |accessdate=January 24, 2011 |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/new-york/mlb/news/story?id=5174072[/tpl]

===Arizona Diamondbacks===

Blanco signed a one-year contract on December 15, 2010 with the Arizona Diamondbacks.[tpl]cite web |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/mlb/news/story?id=5926482&campaign=rss&source=MLBHeadlines |title=D-Backs reportedly add depth to team |publisher=ESPN |accessdate=January 24, 2011 [/tpl] He played in 37 games, hitting .250 and was re-signed following the season. He played in 21 games in the 2012 season.

===Toronto Blue Jays===

Blanco signed a non-guaranteed contract on January 11, 2013 with the Toronto Blue Jays.[tpl]cite web|url=http://toronto.bluejays.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20130111&content_id=40933724&vkey=pr_tor&c_id=tor|title=Blue Jays sign Blanco|publisher=MLB.com|date=January 11, 2013|accessdate=June 14, 2013[/tpl] Blanco made the opening day roster for Toronto as the batterymate to recently acquired 2012 NL Cy Young winner R.A. Dickey. Blanco and Dickey previously played together for the New York Mets during the 2010 season, and Dickey had said Blanco was "the best Dickey's had at catching the knuckleball".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bluebirdbanter.com/2013/1/16/3883112/would-you-rather-have-henry-blanco-or-josh-thole|title=Would you rather have Henry Blanco or Josh Thole?|last=Dakers|first=Tom|publisher=BlueBirdBanter.com|date=January 16, 2013|accessdate=June 14, 2013[/tpl] Blanco was designated for assignment on June 7, 2013,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tsn.ca/mlb/story/?id=424939|title=Blue Jays designate Blanco; recall Thole, LaRoche|publisher=TSN.ca|date=June 7, 2013|accessdate=June 7, 2013[/tpl] and released on June 10. He batted .184 with the Blue Jays in 15 games played, with no home runs or RBI.

===Seattle Mariners===

Blanco was signed by the Seattle Mariners on June 14, 2013 to be a mentor to Mike Zunino.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.king5.com/sports/mariners/Mariners-sign-catcher-Blanco--211600271.html|title=Mariners sign catcher Blanco; DFA Shoppach|work=KING 5 Sports|date=June 14, 2013|accessdate=June 14, 2013[/tpl] In Blanco's first game with the Mariners on June 15 (against the Oakland Athletics), he hit a grand slam down the left field line.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.timescolonist.com/sports/grand-slam-by-henry-blanco-backs-felix-hernandez-s-third-straight-win-mariners-beat-a-s-4-0-1.322991|title=Grand slam by Henry Blanco backs Felix Hernandez's third straight win, Mariners beat A's 4-0|first=Janie|last=McCauley|work=The Associated Press|date=June 15, 2013|accessdate=June 15, 2013[/tpl] On July 25, Zunino broke his left hand, and Blanco began splitting the catching duties with recently signed Humberto Quintero.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20130726&content_id=54845976&notebook_id=54846464&vkey=notebook_sea&c_id=sea |title=Zunino placed on DL, to have surgery |work=Mariners.com |first=Greg |last=Johns |date=July 26, 2013[/tpl] On August 1, 2013, Henry Blanco hit his second grand slam since becoming a Mariner. This one also down the left field line, against the Boston Red Sox starting pitcher Ryan Dempster which gave the Mariners a 7-1 lead, but the bullpen allowed 7 runs in the final 2 innings in an 8-7 loss.
In 35 games with the Mariners, he hit .125/.215/.240 with 3 HR and 14 RBI, while throwing out 6 of 16 would-be base stealers. He was designated for assignment on October 8, 2013 after the Mariners claimed outfield prospect Travis Witherspoon off waivers from the Los Angeles Angels.[tpl]cite web |url=http://blogs.seattletimes.com/mariners/2013/10/08/mariners-do-some-housecleaning-part-ways-with-henry-blanco/ |title=Mariners do some housecleaning, part ways with Henry Blanco |work=The Seattle Times |first=Geoff |last=Baker |date=October 8, 2013[/tpl] He elected free agency the next day.

===Arizona Diamondbacks===

Blanco signed a minor-league contract, with an invitation to spring training, with the Arizona Diamondbacks on December 13, 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article/mlb/d-backs-signed-daniel-hudson-and-henry-blanco-to-minor-league-deals?ymd=20131213&content_id=64664352&vkey=news_mlb|title=D-Backs sign Hudson, Blanco to Minor League deals|last=Gilbert|first=Steve|work=MLB.com|date=December 13, 2013|accessdate=December 18, 2013[/tpl] He was released on March 31, 2014, and immediately re-signed with the Diamondbacks as a coach.[tpl]citeweb|url=http://arizona.diamondbacks.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20140331&content_id=70534652&vkey=pr_ari&c_id=ari|title=Blanco released from Minor League contract; joins D-backs coaching staff|work=MLB.com|date=March 31, 2014|accessdate=April 1, 2014[/tpl] 

==Fielding==

Blanco's .9938 career fielding percentage ranks 16th all-time among major league catchers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/leaders/fielding_perc_c_career.shtml |title=Career Leaders & Records for Fielding Percentage as Catcher |publisher=Baseball Reference |accessdate=January 3, 2013 [/tpl] Although he is a light-hitter, Blanco has had an extended major league career due to the value of his excellent defensive abilities. He has credited former catcher Mike Scioscia, who was the catching coordinator during his time with the Dodgers, with helping him develop his catching skills.[tpl]cite news |title=There’s No E in Blanco, Even After Two Years |work=The New York Times |date=July 23, 2010 |accessdate=February 16, 2011 |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2010/07/24/sports/baseball/24citifield.html[/tpl]
