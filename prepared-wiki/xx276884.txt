[[Kibatalia arborea]]

CATEGORIES: Kibatalia, Plants described in 1837, Medicinal plants of Asia, Trees of Thailand, Trees of Malaysia, Trees of Singapore, Trees of Indonesia, Flora of Borneo, Trees of the Philippines

Kibatalia arborea grows as a tree up to [tpl]Convert|45|m|-1[/tpl] tall, with a trunk diameter of up to [tpl]Convert|100|cm|-1[/tpl]. The bark is grey, grey-brown, dark brown or black. Inflorescences bear up to two flowers. The flowers feature a white or creamy corolla. The plant is found in lowland forests from sea-level to [tpl]Convert|500|m|-2[/tpl] altitude. Local medicinal uses include as a treatment for internal parasites. K. arborea is found in Thailand, Malaysia, Singapore, Indonesia and the Philippines.
