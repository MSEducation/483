Think-aloud protocol (or think-aloud protocols, or TAP; also talk-aloud protocol) is a method used to gather data in usability testing in product design and development, in psychology and a range of social sciences (e.g., reading, writing, translation research, decision making, and process tracing). The think-aloud method was introduced in the usability field by Clayton Lewis [ref]
