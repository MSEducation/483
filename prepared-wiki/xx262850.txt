[[1964 USAC Championship Car season]]

CATEGORIES: 1964 in motorsport, USAC Championship Car, 1964 in American motorsport

The 1964 USAC Championship Car season consisted of 13 races, beginning in Avondale, Arizona on March 22 and concluding at the same location on November 22. There was also one non-championship event at Pikes Peak, Colorado.  The USAC National Champion and Indianapolis 500 winner was A. J. Foyt. At Indianapolis in the 500 miles race Eddie Sachs and Dave MacDonald were killed during lap 2 of the race; Sachs was 37, MacDonald was 28 years old. In the Tony Bettenhausen Memorial at Springfield, Bill Horstmeyer died during the race; he was 34 years old. After 5 days the season ended in November Bobby Marshman testing and death at Phoenix; he was 28 years old.
