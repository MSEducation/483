[[Beauregard Parish School Board]]

CATEGORIES: School districts in Louisiana, Education in Beauregard Parish, Louisiana

Beauregard Parish School Board is a school district headquartered in DeRidder, Louisiana, United States.
The district serves Beauregard Parish.
From 1933 to 1944, Gilbert Franklin Hennigan, then of DeRidder, was the school board president. In 1943, he was elected by president of the Louisiana School Board Association. He left the board to serve from 1944 to 1956 in the Louisiana State Senate. A strong advocate of public education, Hennigan worked to make McNeese State University in Lake Charles a four-year college and to pass legislation creating the state teacher retirement system. He died at the age of seventy-seven in 1960 in Calcasieu Parish.
