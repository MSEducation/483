[[Transport in the Bahamas]]

CATEGORIES: Transport in the Bahamas

==Highways==

Approximately [tpl]convert|2718|km|mi[/tpl] of road in the Bahamas is classified as highway. Of these, approximately [tpl]convert|1560|km|mi[/tpl] are paved. As a former British colony, drivers drive on the left.

==Ports and harbours==

Marinas and harbours are plentiful on The Bahamas islands, making aquatic travel an easy way to navigate between the islands group. Boat travel can be the only way to reach some of the smaller islands. Of these, only Castaway Cay offers ships an actual pier for docking.  The others use tender boats to service ships anchored off shore.

==Merchant marine==

The Bahamas are one of the world's top three flag of convenience shipping registries.

==Airports==

The main airports on the islands are Lynden Pindling International Airport on New Providence, Grand Bahama International Airport on Grand Bahama Island, and Marsh Harbour International Airport on Abaco Island.[tpl]Citation needed|date=July 2010[/tpl] Out of 62 airports in all, 23 have paved runways, of which there are two that are over 3,047 meters long.
