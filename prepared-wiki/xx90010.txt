[[Waimea River (Hawaii)]]

CATEGORIES: Rivers of Hawaii, Surfing locations in Hawaii, Landforms of Kauai

The Waimea River is a river on the island of Kauai in the U.S. state of Hawaii. At [tpl]convert|12.1|mi[/tpl] in length,U.S. Geological Survey. National Hydrography Dataset high-resolution flowline data. The National Map, accessed April 28, 2011 it is one of the longest rivers in the Hawaiian Islands.
It rises in a wet plateau of the island's central highlands, in the Alaka'i Swamp, the largest high-elevation swamp in the world.  It flows south, passing through the spectactular [tpl]convert|3000|ft|m|adj=mid|-deep[/tpl] Waimea Canyon, known as the "Grand Canyon of the Pacific." 
The valleys of the Waimea River and its tributary, the Makaweli River, were once heavily populated.  It enters the Pacific Ocean at Waimea, near the 1778 landing place of Captain Cook on Kauai.
