[[John Trelawny (died 1680)]]

CATEGORIES: 1640s births, 1680 deaths, Cornish politicians, English army officers, Members of the pre-1707 English Parliament for constituencies in Cornwall, Gentlemen of the Privy Chamber, English MPs 1661–79, English MPs 1679, English MPs 1680–81, English military personnel killed in action

Captain John Trelawny (c.1646 – 14 May 1680) was an English army officer of Cornish descent, the eldest son of Sir Jonathan Trelawny, 2nd Baronet.
Trelawny was appointed a Gentleman of the Privy Chamber in 1674,[tpl]cite book | title=The Baronetage of England | first=William Prideaux | last=Courtney | coauthors=George Clement Boase | publisher=Longmans, Green, Reader and Dyer | year=1878 | pages=769 | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=sRYYAAAAMAAJ | accessdate=2 August 2007[/tpl] and was returned as Member of Parliament for West Looe in 1677.
He married Catherine Jenkyn and was killed, without issue, at Tangier, predeceasing his father.
