Dean Williams (born 5 January 1972) is an English former footballer who played as a goalkeeper. He made nearly 100 appearances in the Football League playing for Birmingham City, Brentford and Doncaster Rovers,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player3/deanwilliams.html |title=Dean Williams |work=UK A–Z Transfers |publisher=Neil Brown |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl] and represented Welsh Premier League clubs TNS in the UEFA Cup and Aberystwyth Town in the Intertoto Cup. Dean has made 309 appearances for non-league and Welsh premier clubs. Dean was part-time goalkeeping coach for Telford United and is now Head Coach for dwgk Tamworth http://gktamworth.co.uk/ Dean is also the founder and director for Tamworth Futsal Club C.I.C.

==Playing career==

Williams, born in Tamworth, Staffordshire, signed for Birmingham City on schoolboy forms at 14.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2009[/tpl] When he left school in 1988, he was accepted as a trainee under the Youth Training Scheme (YTS) system, and turned professional in 1990.[tpl]cite book |last=Matthews |first=Tony |title=Birmingham City: A Complete Record |year=1995 |publisher=Breedon Books |location=Derby |page=134 |isbn=978-1-85983-010-9[/tpl] He made his first-team debut aged 17 at the end of the 1989–90 season, in a 2–1 defeat at home to Notts County.Matthews, p. 225. He was one of the youngest ever Goalkeepers or Outfield Players to make his debut and play professional football for Birmingham City Football Club. A spell on loan in Republic of Ireland with Cobh Ramblers followed in 1991. Birmingham used five different goalkeepers, including Alan Miller and Kevin Dearden on loan from Arsenal and Tottenham Hotsput respectively, during the 1991–92 season, but Williams was not one of those five;Matthews, p. 50. he was released in March 1992 and joined Tamworth.
Williams played for Brentford in the 1993–94 season before joining Doncaster Rovers in August 1994. He spent three seasons with the club, making nearly 100 first-team appearances, before moving on to Gateshead in late 1997. Released by the club at the end of the 1997–98 season following their relegation from the Conference, he joined Telford United in August 1998.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-4932367.html |title=United welcome back Williams |date=15 August 1998 |work=The Independent |format=reprint; paid subscription required for full article |publisher=Highbeam Research |accessdate=1 October 2010[/tpl] He made 90 appearances for the club before joining Welsh Premier League club Total Network Solutions (TNS) in 2001.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.shropshirestar.com/2008/11/22/williams-keeps-up-bucks-links/ |title=Williams keeps up Bucks links |work=Shropshire Star |date=22 November 2008 |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl]
In nearly three seasons with TNS, Williams played 72 games in all competitions,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.saints-alive.co.uk/tpl/player.php?sort=apps&id=16 |title=Profile: Dean Williams |publisher=Saints-Alive! |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl] including representing the club in the 2003–04 UEFA Cup, conceding only twice in the second leg of the qualifying round against Manchester City at the Millennium Stadium.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.welsh-premier.com/sa_matchrep_europe.php?id=86 |title=TNS not disgraced in cup exit |publisher=Welsh Premier League |accessdate=8 October 2010[/tpl] He lost his place through injury, then was suspended illegally by the club following a dispute with the management, and left at the end of the 2003–04 season; Williams had planned to take TNS to an employment tribunal, but the parties reached a financial agreement before the hearing.[tpl]cite news |url=http://docs.newsbank.com/openurl?ctx_ver=z39.88-2004&rft_id=info:sid/iw.newsbank.com:AWNB:LDPC&rft_val_format=info:ofi/fmt:kev:mtx:ctx&rft_dat=10555739DD64BB12&svc_dat=InfoWeb:aggregated5&req_dat=0D0CB57AB53DF815 |title=Goalkeeper settles row with ex-club |work=Liverpool Daily Post |format=reprint |publisher=NewsBank |date=23 September 2004 |first=Steve |last=Bagnall |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl]
He signed for Aberystwyth Town, playing in the 2004 UEFA Intertoto Cup against Dinaburg and keeping a clean sheet in the first leg,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.welsh-premier.com/sa_matchrep_europe.php?id=97 |title=Aber fail to break down Latvians |publisher=Welsh Premier League |accessdate=8 October 2010[/tpl] and also played six games in the Welsh Premier League, before moving on in September 2004 to Forest Green Rovers,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.welsh-premier.com/player_detail_solo_europe.php?player_id=32 |title=Welsh Premier career details for Dean Williams |publisher=Welsh Premier League |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl] for whom he played 23 games in the Conference.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=39675&seasonid=134 |title=Games played by Dean Williams in 2004/2005 |work=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl] Williams then moved on to Stafford Rangers, helping them to runners-up spot in the Conference North and promotion via a man-of-the-match performance in the play-off final in which he saved Droylsden's first kick in the penalty shootout.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.staffordshirenewsletter.co.uk/staffordshirenewsletter-sport/displayarticle.asp?id=90731 |title=Cool Boro bin Bloods |work=Staffordshire Newsletter |date=11 May 2006 |first=Dave |last=McLean |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl] He played nine times for the club in the Conference before retiring at the age of 35 due to injury.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.soccerbase.com/players_details.sd?playerid=8536&seasonid=136 |title=Games played by Dean Williams in 2006/2007 |work=Soccerbase |publisher=Centurycomm |accessdate=6 March 2009[/tpl]

==Later career==

Williams joined Telford United as part-time goalkeeping coach in 2008. is a member of the Professional Footballers' Association (PFA)
Dean Williams is also a fully qualified FA Licensed Football Coach with qualifications that include, The FA Youth Award Module 1 and Module 2, The FA Goalkeeping Coaching Award Level 1 and Level 2, FA Psychology, FA Coaching Deaf Footballers, FA Coaching Disabled Footballers, FA Emergency First Aid, FA Futsal Coach, FA Coaching Children, FA Player & Match Analysis, FA Soccer Camp Coach, FA Age Appropriate Coaching, FA Equality Programme, FA Safeguarding Children plus many more. He is a highly qualified coach who has been licensed for 12 years with the FA and constantly updates his continuous professional development (CPD)with the Football Association. Dean is also FA CRB Approved and has valid FA Public & Liability Insurance for the 2013/14 season.Dean is currently Head Goalkeeping Coach at dwgk Academy with his Tamworth Venue and the surrounding area. Dean is also Head GK Coach for Birmingham County Football Associations Partially Sighted & Deaf Centre of Excellence Team and also Head GK Coach for Team Birmingham Futsal who play in the National League and play Futsal at the International Futsal Arena Dean is very passionate about coaching 5–11-year old Boys and Girls.
