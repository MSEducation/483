[[Fiachnae mac Ainbítha]]

CATEGORIES: Kings of Ulster, 9th-century Irish monarchs, 886 deaths, Medieval Gaels, Year of birth unknown

Fiachnae mac Ainbítha (died 886) or Fiachna mac Ainfítha was a Dál Fiatach king of Ulaid, which is now Ulster, Ireland. He was the son of Ainbíth mac Áedo (died 882),a previous king of Ulaid.Byrne, Table 6 He ruled briefly in 886.the Book of Leinster king list gives a reign of 1 year
In 883 he was responsible for the killing of his uncle Eochocán mac Áedo, leth-rí (half-king or co-ruler) of Ulaid jointly with his other uncle, Airemón mac Áedo (died 886).Annals of Ulster, AU 883.6 Fiachnae succeeded Airemón as sole king in 886 but was promptly killed by his own associates.AU 886.3
