[[Lorenzo Calonga]]

CATEGORIES: 2003 deaths, Paraguayan footballers, Paraguay international footballers, 1950 FIFA World Cup players, Association football forwards, Club Guaraní footballers, Deportivo Pereira footballers, Independiente Medellín footballers, Club León footballers, Irapuato FC footballers, 1926 births

Lorenzo Calonga Arce (died 20 September 2003) was a Paraguayan football striker and midfielder who was part of the Paraguay squad for the 1950 FIFA World Cup finals.1950 FIFA World Cup Brazil

==Career==

Calonga began playing football in Paraguay with Cerro Porteño, Club Olimpia and Club Guaraní, where he won the 1949 Paraguayan Primera División title.[tpl]cite web|publisher=ABC Digital|title=Se fue Ovidio Casartelli, campeón en 1949 con Guaraní|trans_title=It was Ovidio Casartelli, champion in 1949 with Guaraní|url=http://archivo.abc.com.py/2008-12-28/articulos/482511/se-fue-ovidio-casartelli-campeon-en-1949-con-guarani|language=Spanish|date=28 December 2008[/tpl] He moved to Colombia where he played for Deportivo Pereira and Independiente Medellín. He finished his playing career in Mexico with Club León and Club Irapuato.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Llanera.com|title=Se fue Pate' mula|trans_title=It was Pate' mule|url=http://www.llanera.com/index.php?pageNum_noticias=1&totalRows_noticias=2058&id=317&vn=1&categoria=Arauca&vercategoria=1&diarias=1|language=Spanish|date=23 September 2003[/tpl][tpl]cite web|publisher=El Espectador|author=Ruiz Bonilla, Guillermo|title=El baúl de los recuerdos: Estrellas de 1952|trans_title=The trunk of memories: Stars of 1952|url=http://www.elespectador.com/impreso/deportes/articuloimpreso-estrellas-1952|language=Spanish|date=19 December 2007[/tpl]
Calonga scored a goal for the Paraguay national team in a friendly against Brazil on 13 May 1950.[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Leme de Arruda, Marcelo|title=Seleção Brasileira (Brazilian National Team) 1947-1952|url=http://www.rsssfbrasil.com/sel/brazil194752.htm|date=12 June 2012[/tpl]
After he retired from playing, Calonga returned to Colombia to work as a coach.[tpl]cite web|publisher=El Colombiano|title=Muerte de "Patemula" Calonga|trans_title=Death of "Patemula" Calonga|url=http://www.elcolombiano.com/proyectos/capsulas/historico/2003/sep1/25.htm|language=Spanish|date=1 September 2003[/tpl] He managed Deportes Quindío during the 1961 season.

==Personal==

Lorenzo Calonga is from a Paraguayan family of famous athletes, including Manuel Calonga who played football and basketball for Club Guaraní.[tpl]cite web|publisher=ABC Digital|title=Brillaron en más de un deporte|trans_title=Shone in more than one sport|url=http://archivo.abc.com.py/articulos.php?fec=2005-07-04&pid=189381|language=Spanish|date=4 July 2005[/tpl]
Calonga died from pneumonia in Arauca at age 77.
