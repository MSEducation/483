[[Edward Beard]]

CATEGORIES: 1940 births, Living people, Members of the United States House of Representatives from Rhode Island, People from Providence, Rhode Island, People from Providence County, Rhode Island, Rhode Island politicians

Edward Peter Beard (born January 20, 1940) is an American businessman and politician from Rhode Island. He served in the Rhode Island House of Representatives and was a member of the United States House of Representatives.

==Early life==

Born in Providence, Rhode Island, Beard attended Assumption Elementary School and Hope High School in Providence.[tpl]cite book|last=Capace|first=Nancy|title=The Encyclopedia of Rhode Island|date=2001|publisher=North American Book Dist LLC|page=282|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=qHPYeCvnASoC&pg=PA282&dq=Edward+Beard+providence+rhode+island&hl=en&sa=X&ei=4sxSU_nlNobU8gHijIDYBA&ved=0CEMQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Edward%20Beard%20providence%20rhode%20island&f=false[/tpl] He served in the Rhode Island National Guard from 1960-1966, where he completed high school as well as a college-level course in agriculture.[tpl]cite web|url= http://store.historicimages.com/products/rsc35833|title= 1977 Press Photo Edward Beard Representative Congress Blue Collar House Painter|publisher = The Historic Images Store|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl] Beard worked as a house painter before entering politics.[tpl]cite web|url= http://history.house.gov/People/Detail/21306|title= SCHNEIDER, Claudine|publisher = History, Art & Archives United States House of Representatives|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl] 

==Political career==

Beard began his political career as a member of the Rhode Island House of Representatives, serving from 1972-1974. In 1976, he was a delegate to the Democratic National Convention.
Elected as a Democrat candidate to the Ninety-fourth Congress, he was reelected to the Ninety-fifth and Ninety-sixth Congresses.[tpl]cite web|url= http://history.house.gov/People/Listing/B/BEARD,-Edward-Peter-(B000279)/|title= BEARD, Edward Peter|publisher = History, Art & Archives United States House of Representatives|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl] Beard served in the U. S. House of Representatives from January 3, 1975 to January 3, 1981, and was chairman on the House Committee on Labor Standards.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.larouchepub.com/eiw/public/1996/eirv23n05-19960126/eirv23n05-19960126_067-edward_p_beard_sr.pdf|title= Pound Gingrichinto the ground|publisher = EIR|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl] His bid for re-election to the Ninety-seventh Congress in 1980 was unsuccessful.
After leaving Congress, he owned and operated a tavern and hosted a short lived radio talk show. He served as director of elderly affairs for the City of Providence, Rhode Island from 1986-2002.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.larouchepub.com/eiw/public/1996/eirv23n05-19960126/eirv23n05-19960126_067-edward_p_beard_sr.pdf|title= Pound Gingrichinto the ground|publisher = EIR|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl] He was an unsuccessful candidate for the Democratic nomination to the One Hundred and Second Congress in 1990.[tpl]cite web|url= http://history.house.gov/People/Listing/B/BEARD,-Edward-Peter-(B000279)/|title= BEARD, Edward Peter|publisher = History, Art & Archives United States House of Representatives|accessdate= April 19, 2014[/tpl]
He is a resident of Providence, Rhode Island.[tpl]cite book|last=Capace|first=Nancy|title=The Encyclopedia of Rhode Island|date=2001|publisher=North American Book Dist LLC|page=282|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=qHPYeCvnASoC&pg=PA282&dq=Edward+Beard+providence+rhode+island&hl=en&sa=X&ei=4sxSU_nlNobU8gHijIDYBA&ved=0CEMQ6AEwAQ#v=onepage&q=Edward%20Beard%20providence%20rhode%20island&f=false[/tpl]
