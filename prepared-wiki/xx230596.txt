[[Catocala sponsa]]

CATEGORIES: Catocala, Animals described in 1767, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Estonia, Insects of Sweden, Insects of Romania, Insects of Poland, Insects of Latvia, Insects of Finland, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Belarus, Insects of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Insects of Hungary, Insects of Greece, Insects of Bulgaria, Insects of Turkey, Insects of Italy

The Dark Crimson Underwing (Catocala sponsa) is a species of moth of the family Noctuidae. It is found in Europe, North Africa and from Anatolia up to the Caucasus. The wingspan is [tpl]convert|60|-|70|mm[/tpl]. The moth flies from July to September depending on the location. The larvae feed on oak.
