Krasnoarmeysk ([tpl]lang-ru|Красноарме́йск[/tpl]) is a town in Saratov Oblast, Russia, located [tpl]convert|75|km|sp=us[/tpl] south of Saratov. Population: [tpl]ru-census|p2010=24,364|p2002=25,411|p1989=24,055[/tpl]

==History==

It was founded in 1764—1766 as the German colony of Baltser ([tpl]lang-ru|Бальцер[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-de|Balzer[/tpl]).[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2010[/tpl] The other name of the colony was Goly Karamysh ([tpl]lang|ru|Голый Карамыш[/tpl]), after the river on which it was located. In 1914, the settlement was officially renamed Goly Karamysh. It was granted town status in 1918; the old name Baltser was restored in 1927.[tpl]citation needed|date=May 2010[/tpl] In 1942, it was renamed Krasnoarmeysk during the cleansing of German geographic names.

==Administrative and municipal status==

Within the framework of administrative divisions, Krasnoarmeysk serves as the administrative center of Krasnoarmeysky District,[tpl]OKATO reference|63 222[/tpl] even though it is not a part of it.[tpl]OKATO reference|63 418[/tpl] As an administrative division, it is incorporated separately as Krasnoarmeysk Town Under Oblast Jurisdiction—an administrative unit with the status equal to that of the districts.[tpl]citation needed|date=November 2012[/tpl] As a municipal division, Krasnoarmeysk Town Under Oblast Jurisdiction is incorporated within Krasnoarmeysky Municipal District as Krasnoarmeysk Urban Settlement.Law #110-ZSO
