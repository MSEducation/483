[[South Lakeland Council election, 2003]]

CATEGORIES: Council elections in South Lakeland, English local elections, 2003

The 2003 South Lakeland Council election took place on 1 May 2003 to elect members of South Lakeland District Council in Cumbria, England. One third of the council was up for election and the council stayed under no overall control.[tpl]cite web| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/nol/shared/bsp/hi/vote2003/locals/html/253.stm | title = Local elections | accessdate = 2009-11-10 | publisher = BBC News Online[/tpl]

==Background==

Before the election the Liberal Democrats had 21 seats, the Conservatives 19, Labour 9 and independents 3.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.southlakeland.gov.uk/main.asp?page=496|title=Local Council Elections May 2003|publisher=South Lakeland District Council|accessdate=9 August 2011|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20030606080405/http://www.southlakeland.gov.uk/main.asp?page=496|archivedate=2003-06-06  [/tpl] 17 seats were being contested with the Conservatives defending 10, the Liberal Democrats 6 and an independent 1 seat. 2 sitting Liberal Democrat councillors stood down at the election, Patricia Himsworth in Arnside and Beetham, and Claire Chorley in Burneside.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2003/04/11/The+Lake+District+Archive/6923736.Election__Lib_Dem_women_bow_out/|title=Election: Lib Dem women bow out|date=11 April 2003|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=11 August 2011[/tpl]
Major issues in the election included housing, regeneration, recycling, public toilets,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2003/04/11/The+Lake+District+Archive/6923735.Election__Battle_lines_are_drawn_in_the_fight_for_votes/|title=Election: Battle lines are drawn in the fight for votes|date=11 April 2003|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=11 August 2011[/tpl] affordable transport and refuse collection.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2003/04/17/The+Lake+District+Archive/6923654.Election__Candidates_in_the_running/|title=Election: Candidates in the running|date=17 April 2003|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=11 August 2011[/tpl]

==Election result==

The results saw the Liberal Democrats remain the largest party with 23 seats,[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thewestmorlandgazette.co.uk/archive/2003/05/02/The+Lake+District+Archive/6923211.Election__No_change_of_power_at_South_Lakeland_District_Council/|title=Election: No change of power at South Lakeland District Council|date=2 May 2003|work=The Westmorland Gazette|accessdate=11 August 2011[/tpl] but without a majority, after gaining 2 seats from the Conservatives in Cartmel and Low Furness & Swarthmoor and 1 seat from an independent in Whinfell. However the Conservatives did take 1 seat back from the Liberal Democrats in Arnside & Beetham to hold 18 seats. Labour remained on 9 seats, while independents dropped to 2 seats. Overall turnout in the election was 44%, with a low of 33.9% in Windermere Applethwaite to a high of 54.2% in Kirkby Lonsdale.
