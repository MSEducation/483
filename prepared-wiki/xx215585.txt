[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Women's 100 metre freestyle]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The women's 100 metre freestyle event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 13–15 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl] 
Germany's Britta Steffen blasted a new Olympic record to claim a gold medal in the event. Coming from eighth place in the turn, she posted a time of 53.12 to edge out Australia's world record holder and top favorite Lisbeth Trickett by 0.04 of a second. Swimming in lane eight, Trickett earned a silver with an outside record time of 53.16.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Worst to First, Britta Steffen Wins 100 Free in Olympic Record|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18830.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Steffen wins first swimming gold for Germany|url=http://www.2008.nbcolympics.com/swimming/news/newsid=215929.html|publisher=NBC Olympics|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Steffen edges Trickett for gold|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/swimming/7562354.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=13 May 2013[/tpl] She narrowly reached the final as the eighth seed, when China's Pang Jiaying was disqualified for a false start in the semifinals.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Natalie Coughlin Tops 100 Free Semis|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18809.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=13 August 2008|accessdate=18 May 2013[/tpl] Meanwhile, U.S. swimmer Natalie Coughlin powered home with a bronze in a matching American record of 53.39 for the second consecutive Olympics. It was Coughlin's fifth medal of these Games, matching her total from Athens four years earlier.[tpl]cite news|title=Coughlin claims bronze in 100 free|url=http://www.nbcnews.com/id/26210524/ns/beijing_olympics-beijing_olympics_news/t/coughlin-claims-bronze-free/|publisher=NBC News|date=14 August 2008|accessdate=18 May 2013[/tpl] 
Competing at her third straight Olympics with Steffen, Finland's Hanna-Maria Seppälä finished outside the medals in fourth place at 53.97. Earlier, she posted a top-seeded time of 53.60 from the sixth heat to lead the prelims.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Finland's Hanna-Maria Seppala Leads Qualifying in Women's 100 Free|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18801.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=13 August 2008|accessdate=18 May 2013[/tpl] She was followed in fifth place by Denmark's Jeanette Ottesen (54.06), and host nation China's Zhu Yingwen, who shared a sixth-place tie with the Netherlands' Marleen Veldhuis in 54.21. Great Britain's Francesca Halsall (54.29) closed out the field.
