Michael James Anderson (born July 30, 1966) is a former Major League Baseball player who pitched for the Cincinnati Reds in [tpl]By|1993[/tpl]. He made his major league debut on September 7 against the St. Louis Cardinals. He pitched 1[tpl]fraction|2|3[/tpl] of an inning, allowed 6 hits, 7 earned runs, and gave up 2 of 4 home runs in a single game hit by Mark Whiten. He only appeared in two more games after that. On December 10, 1993 he was traded with Darron Cox and Larry Luebbers to the Chicago Cubs for Chuck McElroy but he never made it to the majors with the Cubs. He continued to pitch in the minors until [tpl]By|1997[/tpl], when he retired.
