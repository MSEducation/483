[[Leucanthemella]]

CATEGORIES: Asteraceae genera

Leucanthemella is a genus of flowering plants in the Asteraceae family. It contains two species of herbaceous perennials found in marshy habitats. They have hairy foliage and composite daisy-like white flowers in late summer and autumn.[tpl]cite book|title=RHS A-Z encyclopedia of garden plants|year=2008|publisher=Dorling Kindersley|location=United Kingdom|isbn=1405332964|pages=1136[/tpl]
Leucanthemella serotina (autumn ox-eye), native to southeastern Europe, is a vigorous, erect perennial growing to [tpl]convert|1.5|m|ft|0|abbr=on[/tpl] tall, bearing flowerheads with white ray florets and greenish-yellow centres, throughout autumn. It is cultivated in gardens, and has gained the Royal Horticultural Society's Award of Garden Merit.[tpl]cite web|title=RHS Plant Selector - Leucanthemella serotina|url=http://apps.rhs.org.uk/plantselector/plant?plantid=1139|accessdate=21 May 2013[/tpl]
