[[John A. Kronstadt]]

CATEGORIES: 1951 births, California state court judges, Cornell University alumni, Judges of the United States District Court for the Central District of California, Living people, United States district court judges appointed by Barack Obama, Yale Law School alumni

John Arnold Kronstadt (born 1951) is a district judge on the United States District Court for the Central District of California. On April 12, 2011, the Senate voted 96-0 to confirm Kronstadt.  He received his judicial commission on April 14, 2011.

==Early life and education==

Kronstadt was born in 1951 in Washington, D.C.Senate Judiciary Committee Questionnaire: John Arnold Kronstadt, (November 15, 2010).  He graduated from Cornell University in 1973 with an A.B.President Obama Nominates Six to the United States District Court, 11/17/10, whitehouse.gov (November 17, 2010). At Cornell, he was a member of the Quill and Dagger society. He earned his J.D. from Yale Law School in 1976.  After completing law school, Kronstadt served as a law clerk for Judge William P. Gray of the United States District Court for the Central District of California.[tpl]cite news | first=Kenneth | last=Ofgang | url=http://www.metnews.com/articles/2010/kron111910.htm | title=Obama Nominates Kronstadt to U.S. District Court | work=Metropolitan News-Enterprise | date=November 19, 2010 | accessdate=December 30, 2011[/tpl]

==Judicial service==

In October 2002, California Governor Gray Davis appointed Kronstadt to the Los Angeles County Superior Court.[tpl]cite news | first=Lorelei | last=Laird | url=http://www.metnews.com/articles/supe101702.htm | title=Davis Appoints Two to Los Angeles Superior Court Bench | work=Metropolitan News-Enterprise | date=October 17, 2002 | accessdate=December 30, 2011[/tpl]  He replaced Judge Juelann Cathey, who had retired earlier that year following major heart surgery.[tpl]cite news | author=MetNews Staff Writer | url=http://www.metnews.com/articles/cath070102.htm | title=Judge Juelann Cathey Granted Disability Retirement | work=Metropolitan News-Enterprise | date=July 1, 2002 | accessdate=December 30, 2011[/tpl]
While a Superior Court judge, Kronstadt presided over the case to determine rightful ownership of the Bahia Emerald, an 840-pound gemstone that has previously been valued at $372 million.Victoria Kim, Trial begins in tussle over giant emerald, Los Angeles Times (September 25, 2010).
On November 17, 2010, President Barack Obama nominated Kronstadt to a judgeship on the United States District Court for the Central District of California to fill the vacancy created by the death of Florence-Marie Cooper. On April 12, 2011, the Senate voted 96-0 to confirm his nomination.http://judiciary.senate.gov/nominations/112thCongress.cfm  Kronstadt received his judicial commission two days later.

==Personal==

Kronstadt is married to Los Angeles County Superior Court Judge Helen Bendix.
