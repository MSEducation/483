Stan Mikawos (born May 11, 1958) is a former Canadian football player.  A native of Winnipeg, Manitoba, Mikawos played college football at North Dakota.[tpl]cite web|title=Former Sioux in the Pros|publisher=Sioux Sports|url=http://siouxsports.com/football/history/pros.htm|accessdate=2010-05-03[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Mikawos Career Goes Full Circle to the CFL|newspaper=Grand Forks Herald|date=1991-10-01|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=GF&s_site=grandforks&p_multi=GF&p_theme=realcities&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EB6E92DAA0EAF67&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM[/tpl]  He played professional football in the Canadian Football League as a defensive tackle for the Winnipeg Blue Bombers from 1982 to 1996.Winnipeg Blue Bomber Alumni  During his time with the Blue Bombers, the team won three Grey Cup championships in 1984, 1988 and 1990.[tpl]cite web|title=Grey Cup 1984|publisher=Grey Cup|url=http://greycup.cfl.ca/1984gc|accessdate=2010-05-03[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Grey Cup 1988|publisher=Grey Cup|url=http://greycup.cfl.ca/1988gc|accessdate=2010-05-03[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Grey Cup 1990|publisher=Grey Cup|url=http://greycup.cfl.ca/1990gc|accessdate=2010-05-03[/tpl] In the 1984 Grey Cup game, Mikawos recovered a fumble and ran 22 yards for a touchdown to give the Bombers a 24-17 lead.[tpl]cite news|author=Kirk Penton|title=Blue Bombers were 'bears' in '84: Grey Cup starved city feasted on win|newspaper=Winnipeg Sun|date=2005-08-13|url=http://slam.canoe.ca/Slam/Football/CFL/Winnipeg/2005/08/13/1171797-sun.html[/tpl]  He was inducted into the Winnipeg Football Club Hall of Fame in 2000.[tpl]cite web|title=WFC HALL OF FAME|publisher=Winnipeg Football Club|url=http://www.bluebombers.com/page/wfc_hall_of_fame|accessdate=2010-05-03[/tpl]  Mikawos currently resides in Los Cabos, Mexico, where he sells timeshares.
