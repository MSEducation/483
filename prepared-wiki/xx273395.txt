[[Ernest Still]]

CATEGORIES: 1852 births, 1931 deaths, English rugby union players, England international rugby union players, Rugby union forwards

Ernest Still was  a rugby union international who represented England from 1873 to 1873.

==Early life==

Ernest Still was born on 14 July 1852 in Epsom, the fifth son of Robert Still of Sutton in Surrey.Joseph Foster (ed), Alumni Oxonienses: the Members of the University of Oxford, 1715-1886: Their Parentage, Birthplace, and Year of Birth, with a Record of Their Degrees: Sabin-Zouch p1355, (Parker and co.), 1891 He attended Rugby School and went on to study Law at Brasenose College, Oxford from where he received his BA in 1874 and his MA in 1880.

==Rugby union career==

Still made his international debut on 3 March 1873 at Hamilton Crescent, Glasgow in the Scotland vs England match.

==Career and later life==

Ernest became a solicitor and later married Amy Gordon Churchill, the daughter of Charles Chrchill. Their son, Francis Churchill Still, married Margaret Burdett Money-Coutts, daughter of Francis Burdett Thomas Coutts-Nevill, 5th Lord Latymer and Edith Ellen Churchill, on 8 June 1907. He died on 14 December 1937.the peerage.com
