[[St. Monica's Church (Queens)]]

CATEGORIES: Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York City, Religious buildings completed in 1856, 19th-century Roman Catholic church buildings, Churches in Queens, New York, Jamaica, Queens

St. Monica's Church is a historic Roman Catholic church in Jamaica, Queens, New York.   It was built in 1856 and is a brick basilica type building in the Romanesque style.  It features a four story entrance tower in the center of its three bay wide front facade.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=7401|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: St. Monica's Church|date=September 1979|accessdate=2011-01-16 |author=Virginia Kurshan, Joan R. Olshansky, and Elizabeth Spencer-Ralph|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=7411|title=Accompanying six photos[/tpl] Saint Monica's Church has been refurbished on the Campus of York College as a child care center.
It was listed by the New York City Landmarks Preservation Commission in 1979 and on the National Register of Historic Places in 1980.
