[[Screaming jelly babies]]

CATEGORIES: Chemical reactions, Chemistry classroom experiments

"Screaming jelly babies" (en-UK), also known as "Growling gummy bears"[tpl]cite web|title=Growling Gummy Bear|url=http://webcache.googleusercontent.com/search?q=cache:8U1JXOm9M8QJ:wikieducator.org/images/b/b2/Chem_11_Demos.pdf+&cd=8&hl=en&ct=clnk&gl=us&client=firefox-a|work=CHEMISTRY 11 DEMONSTRATIONS|publisher=British Columbia Science Teachers' Association|accessdate=22 March 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Growling Gummy Bears|url=https://secure.chem.byu.edu/lectureprep/node/491|work=BYU Lecture Prep|publisher=Brigham Young University|accessdate=22 March 2014[/tpl]  (en-US/en-CA), is a classroom chemistry demonstration, variants of which are practised in schools around the world. It is often used at open evenings to demonstrate the more light-hearted side of secondary school science.[tpl]cite news|title=YouTube videos to ignite science|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/education/7758392.stm|accessdate=14 May 2013|newspaper=BBC news|date=1 December 2008[/tpl]
The experiment shows the amount of energy there is in a piece of candy; jelly babys or gummy bearsLubbock Christian University: Can a Gummy Bear Scream?[tpl]cite web|title=5.5 Oxidation of Sugar or Gummi Bear with Potassium Chlorate|url=http://lecturedemos.chem.umass.edu/chemReactions5_5.html|work=Chemical Reactions II: Oxidation/Reduction|publisher=University of Massachusetts Lecture Demonstrations|accessdate=22 March 2014[/tpl] are often used for theatrics. Potassium chlorate, a strong oxidising agent, rapidly oxidises the sugar in the candy,[tpl]cite web|author= |url=http://www.urn1350.net/video/screaming-jelly-baby-experiment-student-science |title=Screaming Jelly Baby Experiment - Student Science |publisher=University Radio Nottingham |date=2012-01-16 |accessdate=September 2, 2012 |accessdate=May 13, 2013[/tpl] causing it to burst into flames, producing a "screaming" sound as rapidly expanding gases are emitted from the test tube. The aroma of candy floss (cotton candy) is also given off.
Other carbohydrate or hydrocarbon containing substances can also be dropped into test tubes of molten chlorate, with similar results.
