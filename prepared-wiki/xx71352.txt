</ref> During the first lecture of the original lecture series he referred to the college as a "magical island like Atlantis" disappearing and re-emerging from the sea. This was a reference both to the [[Invisible College]] and [[Francis Bacon]]'s [[New Atlantis]].

===Bishop===

On 15 May 1992, Chartres was nominated[tpl]London Gazette|issue=52923|startpage=8409|date=15 May 1992|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] area Bishop of Stepney. He was consecrated as bishop on 22 May 1992.
In November 1995, Chartres was confirmed as the Bishop of London.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=54203|startpage=14961|date=6 November 1995|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] He also became Prelate of the Order of the British Empire[tpl]London Gazette|issue=54231|startpage=16345|date=1 December 1995|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] and Dean of the Chapels Royal.  He is a Privy Counsellor. In 1997 he was appointed a chaplain of the Most Venerable Order of Saint John (ChStJ).[tpl]London Gazette|issue=54652|startpage=595|date=16 January 1997|accessdate=2008-01-04[/tpl] He is an Honorary Bencher of the Middle Temple, a Liveryman of the Merchant Taylors' Company and of the Worshipful Company of Vintners, an Honorary Freeman of the Weavers' and the Woolmen Companies.
In 1997, Chartres was one of the executors of the will of Diana, Princess of Wales and delivered an address at her memorial service in 2007. He confirmed Prince William. On 12 September 2009 he presided at the marriage of Lord Frederick Windsor to actress Sophie Winkleman at the Chapel Royal in Hampton Court Palace.
Chartres is responsible for the Church of England's relations with the Orthodox churches, representing the Church of England at the funeral of Patriarch Alexy II of Moscow of the Russian Orthodox Church  and the enthronement of his successor, Kirill I, in Moscow.  Chartres was criticised for his promotion of Patriarch Kirill's book Freedom and Responsibility at the London Book Fair in 2011.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/commentisfree/2012/aug/19/nick-cohen-****-riot-putin?INTCMP=SRCH | location=London | deadurl=yes[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=April 2014|bot=RjwilmsiBot[/tpl]
Chartres is the founder and chairman of the trustees of the St Ethelburga's Centre for Reconciliation and Peace. He is also a trustee of Coexist, sitting on the advisory council of the Tony Blair Faith Foundation. In October 2005, he joined Marianne Suhr at St Giles in the Fields, London, to launch a new maintenance project for the capital's historic churches."Support from on high for gutter project!", www.maintainyourbuilding.org.uk, accessed 23 July 2008
Chartres was appointed Knight Commander of the Royal Victorian Order (KCVO) in the 2009 Queen's Birthday Honours.[tpl]London Gazette|issue=59090|date=13 June 2009|startpage=3|supp=yes[/tpl] In 1999 he was elected a fellow of the Society of Antiquaries of London (FSA).
In January 2006, Chartres was criticised by the media for his decision to spend Easter on a cruise ship giving lectures on theology rather than attend the services at St Paul's Cathedral. At the time, Chartres was on a two-month sabbatical, his first in 33 years.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/london/4641378.stm|date=23 January 2006|accessdate=2007-03-24|work=BBC News|title=Bishop in Easter lecture cruise[/tpl] He preached the sermon at the wedding of Prince William and Kate Middleton on 29 April 2011.

===Green issues===

Since its launch in 2006, Chartres has led the Church of England's "Shrinking the Footprint" campaign, aimed at cutting 80% of the church's carbon emissions by 2050.Church launches Shrinking The Footprint campaign, Church of England, published 2006-06-02, accessed 2007-05-01 In the launch and subsequently, Chartres criticised pollution of the planet by people going on holidays by plane. Michael O'Leary, boss of the low-cost airline Ryanair, responded that "the Bishop of London has got empty churches - presumably if no one went on holidays perhaps they might turn up and listen to his sermons. God bless the Bishop!"[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.unison.ie/irish_independent/stories.php3?ca=35&si=1660238&issue_id=14413|work=Irish Independent|title=O'Leary gives sermon to bishop on travel 'sins'|date=27 July 2006|accessdate=2007-03-24[/tpl]  Also, after criticism that his taking flights for "diocese work" as well as retaining a chauffeur-driven car were against the ideals of this campaign, he pledged not to fly for a year.The green cross code, The Guardian, 14 June 2007
In October 2008, the Independent on Sunday named Chartres as number 75 of the top 100 environmentalists in Britain on their "Green List".[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/environment/green-living/the-iiosi-green-list-britains-top-100-environmentalists-958711.html|title=The IoS Green List: Britain's top 100 environmentalists|work=The Independent on Sunday|date=12 October 2008|accessdate=2008-10-13|location=London[/tpl]
