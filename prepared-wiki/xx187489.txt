[[Kimberlee Green]]

CATEGORIES: Australian netball players, New South Wales Swifts players, Commonwealth Games competitors for Australia, Commonwealth Games silver medallists for Australia, Netball players at the 2010 Commonwealth Games, Sportswomen from Sydney, People from the Sutherland Shire, 1986 births, Living people, Australian Institute of Sport netball players

Kimberlee Green (born 5 March 1986 in Sydney, Australia) is an Australian international netball player. Green played six years in the Commonwealth Bank Trophy, for the Sydney Swifts and AIS Canberra Darters.[tpl]cite web |title=2010 New South Wales Swifts profile: Kimberlee Green |url=http://www.nswswifts.com.au/extra.asp?ID=6954 |accessdate=18 October 2010[/tpl] With the start of the ANZ Championship, Green signed with Sydney franchise the New South Wales Swifts, which won the inaugural championship in 2008. Later that year, she was selected for the Australian Netball Diamonds team.[tpl]cite news |last=Forrest |first=Brad |title=Glamour girls of netball |newspaper=St George and Sutherland Shire Leader |url=http://www.theleader.com.au/news/local/sport/netball/glamour-girls-of-netball/1241048.aspx |date=12 August 2008 |accessdate=18 October 2010[/tpl] During her international career, Green has won a bronze medal at the 2009 World Netball Series in Manchester, and a silver medal at the 2010 Commonwealth Games in Delhi.
Kimberlee Green is the daughter of South Sydney Rabbitohs footballer Michael Green and Commonwealth Games swimmer Denise Langford; her great-grandfather is former New South Wales politician Fred Green. She is also engaged to New South Wales Blues cricketer Trent Copeland.[tpl]cite news |last=Lane |first=Daniel |title=A match made in ... sand dune hell |newspaper=The Sydney Morning Herald |url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/cricket/a-match-made-in-x2026-sand-dune-hell-20101009-16cyt.html |date=10 October 2010 |accessdate=18 October 2010[/tpl]
In 2011, for the first time for Australia, Green played at the Wing Defense (WD) position in the first of three tests against Jamaica.
