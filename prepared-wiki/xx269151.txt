Shamim Azad ([tpl]lang-bn|শামীম আজাদ[/tpl]; born 11 November 1952)[tpl]cite web |url= http://bsklondon.co.uk/upcomingevents.aspx/|title=World Literature Centre, London|date=11 November 2011|accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl] Upcoming Events is a Bangladeshi-born British bilingual poet, storyteller and writer.

==Early life==

Azad was born in Mymensingh, Dhaka, East Bengal (now Bangladesh) the town where her father worked), her hometown was Sylhet. She passed her Metric from Jamalpur Girls High School in 1967 and passed her Intermediate from Tangail Kumudini College in 1969. She enrolled in Dhaka University and gained an Honours degree in 1972 and a Masters degree in 1973.[tpl]cite news |last= |first= |url=http://www.banglanews24.com/English/detailsnews.php?nssl=070151fbdb4fa874242f4d03fe75c57c&nttl=2012032833946|title=Shamim Azad's birthday to be celebrated in Dhaka|work= |location= |publisher=banglanews24|date=22 February 2012|accessdate=1 May 2012[/tpl]
In 1990, Azad came to England.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.poetrymagazines.org.uk/magazine/record.asp?id=12677|title=Biographical notes – The Poets|publisher=Poetry Magazines|year=2001|pages=293–305|accessdate=23 January 2012[/tpl] Shamim Azad She currently resides in Wanstead, Essex.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.towerhamletsarts.org.uk/?cat=29&cid=29116&guide=Artists|title=Shamim Azad – Artists directory|work=Arts & Entertainment|location= |publisher=Tower Hamlets|date= |accessdate=1 May 2012[/tpl]

==Career==

Azad's work ranges from Bangladeshi to European folktales. Her performance fuses the lines between education and entertainment and her workshops are rooted in Asian folk, oral traditions and heritage.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.poetrysociety.org.uk/content/info/npd/history/npd05/london/|title=Poetry and Translation|location=London|publisher=The Poetry Society|date= |accessdate=23 September 2011[/tpl] The Poets: Shamim Azad
Azad has published books including novels, collections of short stories, essays and poems in both English and Bengali and has been included in various anthologies including British South Asian Poetry, My Birth Was Not in Vain, Velocity, Emlit Project and Mother Tongues. She wrote two plays for Half Moon Theatre.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bbwhoswho.co.uk/whoswho_09.pdf|title=Shamim Azad|work= British Bangladeshi Who's Who 2009|location= |publisher=British Bangla Media Group|date=October 2009|page=33|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl]
Azad has performed at venues including the Museum of London, Cambridge Water Stone, Liberty Radio, Battersea Arts Centre, Lauderdale House, the Commonwealth Institute, British Library, British Council of Bangladesh, Takshila in Pakistan and New York.
Azad is a trustee of charity One World Action, Rich Mix, Bethnal Green, London, a school Governor and Chairperson of Bishwa Sahitya Kendra (World Literature Centre) London.[tpl]cite news |last=Mahboob|first=Mahdin|url=http://www.thedailystar.net/campus/2007/08/02/feature_BRAC.htm|title=Creative Writing Workshop @ BRAC University by Shamim Azad|work= |location= |publisher=The Daily Star|date=12 August 2007|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] Volume 2, Issue 31

==Awards==

Azad received the BangladeshBichitra Award in 1994, Year of the Artist 2000 Award from London Arts, Sonjojon- A Rouf Award 2004 and UK Civic Award in 2004.
