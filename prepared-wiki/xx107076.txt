[[Alex Brown (rugby union)]]

CATEGORIES: 1979 births, Living people, England international rugby union players, English rugby union players, Bath Rugby players, Pontypool RFC players, Bristol Rugby players, Gloucester Rugby players, Rugby union locks, People educated at Colston's School, Sportspeople from Bristol

Alex Brown (born on 17 May 1979 in Bristol) is a former English rugby union player, who played at lock position.
He attended the famous Colston's Collegiate School in Bristol. 
He started playing rugby at u7s level for Clifton in 1985 and worked his way through the age levels there
After a brief period with Bath and Pontypool, he joined his home city club Bristol Rugby and was a key player when they won the first Under 21 Zurich Premiership. Under the tutelage of rugby director Dean Ryan, he was voted the club’s player of the year in the 2000–01 season. Brown's father played rugby for Clifton.
Brown signed for Gloucester Rugby in the summer of 2003 rejoining Dean Ryan. The England Saxons international topped the line-out statistics charts during the 2003–04 season and was voted the players’ player of the year.
He played for England Saxons in the first Churchill Cup and was called into the England senior squad for the 2005 Autumn Internationals.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/2367214/Autumn-international-squads.html|title=Autumn international squads|publisher=Daily Telegraph|date=2005-10-25|accessdate=2009-06-24[/tpl] 
Brown was expected to make his international debut against Australia in November, but he suffered a neck injury and was ruled out of the entire Autumn programme.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/2367213/Brown-blow-for-England.html|title=Brown blow for England|publisher=Daily Telegraph|date=2005-10-25|accessdate=2009-06-24[/tpl]
He made his senior international debut for England during their 2006 summer tour, in a defeat to Australia.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/international/5060414.stm|title=Australia 34–3 England|publisher=BBC|date=2006-06-11|accessdate=2009-06-24[/tpl]
Brown then played in both tests on the 2007 summer tour, England losing both games to South Africa.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/english/6690715.stm|title=South Africa 58–10 England|publisher=BBC|date=2007-05-26|accessdate=2009-06-24[/tpl] He picked up the Gillette Power Award for the 2006–07 season. He is widely regarded as one of the most consistent performers in the Premiership and has signed a three-year contract extension with Gloucester until 2011. After proving himself one of the most consistent lock forwards in the premiership,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gloucesterrugby.co.uk/rugby/matchcentre/squads_gloucester_first_xv.php?player=4225&includeref=dynamic|title=Alex Brown: Player Biog|publisher=Gloucester Rugby|date=2012-08-01|accessdate=2012-12-20[/tpl] Brown's contract was extended until 2013. Unfortunately, following an shoulder injury sustained during the first match of the 2012-13 season, Brown was forced to announce his retirement on 20 December 2012 [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/rugby-union/20788853|title=Alex Brown: Gloucester lock forced to retire through injury|publisher=BBC|date=2012-12-20|accessdate=2012-12-20[/tpl]
He currently acts as Rugby Operations Manager at Kingsholm Stadium with Gloucester Rugby.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.gloucesterrugby.co.uk/news/8548.php#.UlQxOlCsh8F|publisher=Gloucester Rugby|title=Alex Brown named as new Rugby Operations Manager at Gloucester|date=23 April 2013[/tpl]
