[[In My Head (album)]]

CATEGORIES: Black Flag (band) albums, 1985 albums, SST Records albums

In My Head is the sixth studio album released by Black Flag. All of the music was written by guitarist Greg Ginn, who intended to record it as his first solo album.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl] After hearing the band rehearsing the album, singer Henry Rollins wrote lyrics for several of its songs.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl] Many fans[tpl]who|date=April 2013[/tpl] consider this record to be perhaps their best album musically.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl] This is a sentiment shared by Kira Roessler, who played bass on the record, according to the biography of "Spray Paint the Walls, The Story of Black Flag" written by Stevie Chick (The sentiment in question being located on pages 374-375 of the aforementioned book).[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013|reason = |Spray Paint the Walls by Stevie Chick Page 374-375 = [/tpl]  The album represents Black Flag's final stylistic change, to free jazz and hardcore punk influenced heavy metal.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl] It was released in 1985 on SST Records, and was their final album before their reunion in 2013. The CD reissue adds three of the four songs from the I Can See You EP, replicating the original 1985 cassette release which came out concurrent to the LP.
