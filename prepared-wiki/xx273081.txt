Steven Scott Clevenger (born April 5, 1986) is a Major League Baseball catcher for the Baltimore Orioles.

==Early life==

Clevenger, who was raised in Glen Burnie, Maryland, graduated from Mount Saint Joseph High School in 2004. He was the starting shortstop on the school's varsity baseball team which won the Maryland Interscholastic Athletic Association (MIAA) A conference championship in his senior year.Mills, Keith. "Clevenger Relishes Chance With Cubs," PressBox Baltimore, February 8, 2010.Mills, Keith. "Lombardozzi And Clevenger Hit Big Time," PressBox Baltimore, September 20, 2011.
He hit .347 as a first-team All-Southland Conference shortstop in his only year at Southeastern Louisiana University in 2005.Southeastern Louisiana University 2012 Baseball Media Guide. After transferring to Chipola College, he led the Indians with a .395 batting average and 77 hits.2006 Spring Baseball Roster – Chipola College Athletics.2006 Final Season Statistics – Chipola College Athletics.

==Professional career==

===Draft and Minor Leagues===

He was selected by the Chicago Cubs in the seventh round (209th overall) of the 2006 MLB Draft.2006 Major League Baseball Draft, Rounds 1–10 – Pro Sports Transactions. After signing with the Cubs on June 14,"Cubs agree to terms with 14 selections from 2006 First-Year Player Draft," Chicago Cubs press release, Wednesday, June 14, 2006. he began his professional career as the starting second baseman with the Boise Hawks. He started making the transition to catcher the following season after Hawks manager Steve McFarland convinced him that a left-handed batter at the position had a better chance of a faster promotion to the majors.
During the 2011 season Clevenger played for the Tennessee Smokies where he batted .295 and hit 5 home runs. He also played for the Iowa Cubs where he batted .407 and hit 3 home runs.http://web.minorleaguebaseball.com/milb/stats/stats.jsp?pos=C&sid=milb&t=p_pbp&pid=502182

===Chicago Cubs (2011-2013)===

Clevenger was called up to the Cubs from the expanded 40-man roster.http://chicago.cubs.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20110926&content_id=25234454&notebook_id=25239944&vkey=notebook_chc&c_id=chc He made his major league debut September 26, 2011, however he only played in two games during the 2011 season.
Clevenger made the opening day roster during the 2012 season. However, on April 28, Clevenger was placed on the 15-day disabled list with a strained right oblique. Clevenger was hitting .500 in 10 games with five doubles as the backup catcher.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mlb.mlb.com/news/article.jsp?ymd=20120428&content_id=29885688 |title=Clevenger placed on DL with oblique strain |work=MLB.com |first=Michael |last=Radano |accessdate=April 29, 2012[/tpl]
Clevenger finished the 2012 season with a .201 batting average, one home run, and 16 RBIs.http://espn.go.com/mlb/player/_/id/30135/steve-clevenger

===Baltimore Orioles (2013-present)===

On July 2, 2013, Clevenger was traded to the Baltimore Orioles with Scott Feldman for Jake Arrieta and Pedro Strop.https://twitter.com/keithlaw/status/352115008521502720 He was optioned to the Triple-A Norfolk Tides after the trade.
