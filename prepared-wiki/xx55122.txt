[[Tony Award for Best Revival of a Play]]

CATEGORIES: Tony Awards, Awards established in 1994, 1994 establishments in the United States

The Tony Award for Best Revival (Play) has only been awarded since 1994. Prior to that, plays and musicals were considered together for the Tony Award for Best Revival. The award is given to the best non-musical play that has appeared on Broadway in a previous production.

==Superlatives==

To date, five plays have been nominated for the Tony Award for Best Revival of a Play in two separate years for two separate productions: Inherit the Wind, A View from the Bridge, Death of a Salesman, The Best Man, Who's Afraid of Virginia Woolf?, A Raisin in the Sun, and Twelfth Night. Of these, only Death of a Salesman has won the award twice.
With eight nominated productions, William Shakespeare holds the record as the most represented playwrights in the category.
