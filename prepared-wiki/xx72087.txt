[[Gothenburg and Bohus County]]

CATEGORIES: Former counties of Sweden, Västra Götaland County, History of Bohuslän

Gothenburg and Bohus County ([tpl]lang-sv|Göteborgs och Bohus län[/tpl]) was a county of Sweden until 1997, when it was merged with Skaraborg County and Älvsborg County to form Västra Götaland County. 
The county was named after the city of Gothenburg and the historical province of Bohuslän. Gothenburg was the seat of residence for the governor and represented the westernmost part of the province of Västergötland.
