[[Charles W. Willard]]

CATEGORIES: 1827 births, 1880 deaths, Members of the United States House of Representatives from Vermont, Secretaries of State of Vermont, Dartmouth College alumni

Charles Wesley Willard (June 18, 1827 - June 8, 1880) was an American politician, lawyer and newspaper editor. He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Biography==

Willard was born in Lyndon, Vermont, son of Thomas Willard and Abigail (Carpenter) Willard. He attended Caledonia County Grammar School and graduated from Dartmouth College in Hanover, New Hampshire in 1851. Willard studied law and was admitted to the bar in 1853. He began the practice of law in Montpelier. In 1855 and 1856 he was Secretary of State of Vermont.[tpl]cite web|url= http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/willard.html||title = Willard, Charles Wesley (1827-1880) |publisher= The Political Graveyard |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl] He declined reelection, then served as member of the Vermont State Senate in 1860 and 1861. He became editor and publisher of the Daily Green Mountain Freeman in 1861, and served in those positions until 1873.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=22707||title = Charles Wesley Willard |publisher= Find A Grave |accessdate= December 26,  2012[/tpl]
Willard was elected as a Republican candidate to the Forty-first, Forty-second, and Forty-third Congresses, serving from March 4, 1869 until March 3, 1875.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.govtrack.us/congress/members/charles_willard/411635||title = Rep. Charles Willard |publisher= Govtrack.us |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl] He served as chairman of the Committee on Revolutionary Pensions during the Forty-first and Forty-second Congresses. He was an unsuccessful candidate for reelection in 1874 to the Forty-third Congress.
After leaving Congress, he resumed the practice of law in Montpelier and served as a member of the commission to revise the laws of Vermont in 1879 and 1880.[tpl]cite book|last=Gilman|first=Marcus Davis|title=The bibliography of Vermont: or, A list of books and pamphlets relating in any way to the state. With biographical and other notes|year=1897|publisher=Free press association|pages=331|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=T1I0AAAAMAAJ&pg=PA331&lpg=PA331&dq=charles+wesley+willard+Lyndon,+Vermont&source=bl&ots=1vUoz65oIg&sig=aXWmvAp-kYFqGgR9VBChhTUTQe8&hl=en&sa=X&ei=by3bUM3oBMry2gWq3YGoAQ&ved=0CHkQ6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=charles%20wesley%20willard%20Lyndon%2C%20Vermont&f=false[/tpl] Willard died on June 8, 1880 in Montpelier, and is interred in Green Mount Cemetery in Montpelier.[tpl]cite web|url= http://politicalgraveyard.com/bio/willard.html||title = Willard, Charles Wesley (1827-1880) |publisher= The Political Graveyard |accessdate= December 26, 2012[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Willard married Emily Doane Reed on August 24, 1855. They had four children, Mary Willard, Ashton R. Willard, Eliza M. Willard and Charles W.
