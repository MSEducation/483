[[Ligularia przewalskii]]

CATEGORIES: Ligularia przewalskii, Senecioneae, Flora of China, Flora of Mongolia, Garden plants

Ligularia przewalskii (Maxim.) Diels, also called Przewalski's golden ray, is a species of [tpl]convert|1.5|-|2|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] tall perennial herbaceous plant in the genus Ligularia and the family Asteraceae, native to damp places in Mongolia and Northern China and named after Nikolai Przhevalsky. It used to be called Senecio przewalskii Maxim. Przewalski's Ligularia is a popular ornamental plant grown for its large, deeply cut foliage and its tall spike-like inflorescences with bright yellow composite flowers blooming from July to August.
In China, Ligularia przewalskii is known as a medicinal plant. Its roots contain thirteen compounds, some with antibacterial activity.Xie, WD, Gao, X., Shen, T., Jia, ZJ (2006). Two new benzofurans and other constituents from Ligularia przewalskii. Pharmazie 61(6): 556âˆ’558

==Cultivation==

Plants grow best under cool, moist conditions, and resent hot sunny locations where they wilt extensively. They are propagated from seed or by cutting up the crowns.
