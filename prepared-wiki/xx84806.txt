Leisha Hailey (born July 11, 1971) is an American actress and musician most widely known for playing Alice Pieszecki in the Showtime Networks production The L Word.  Hailey first came to the public's attention as a musician in the pop duo The Murmurs and has continued a music career as part of the band Uh Huh Her.

==Early life==

Born in Okinawa, Japan to American parents, Hailey grew up in Bellevue, Nebraska and attended Bellevue West High School.[tpl]Citation needed|date=November 2008[/tpl] At 17, she came out as a lesbian  before moving to New York City to attend the American Academy of Dramatic Arts.L is for Leisha, a February 2004 interview by Dennis Hensley for The Advocate magazine, via Hensley's website

==Career==

===Music===

With her Academy classmate Heather Grody, she formed the pop duo The Murmurs in 1991. In the 1990s, they released several albums and toured on the Lilith Fair.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2011[/tpl] In 2001, Hailey and Grody changed their name to Gush. The band split when Hailey began working for The L Word. In 2005, Hailey founded Marfa Records.News from the Marfa Records website
In July 2007, Uh Huh Her, an electropop duo consisting of Hailey and Camila Grey, released an EP entitled I See Red; over a year later they released their first album Common Reaction.
Hailey also co-wrote the Shakira hit song "Don't Bother" for her 2005 hit album Oral Fixation Vol. 2.

===Acting===

Hailey's first major role was in the 1997 movie All Over Me, and around this time, she played a struggling musician on an episode of Boy Meets World.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2011[/tpl]  From 2003 to 2009, she starred as Alice Pieszecki, a bisexual journalist, on The L Word.
