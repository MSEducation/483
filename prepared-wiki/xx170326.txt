[[1947 Giro d'Italia]]

CATEGORIES: Giro d'Italia, 1947 in cycle racing, 1947 in Italian sport

The 1947 Giro d'Italia was the 30thÂ edition of the Giro d'Italia, a cycling race organized and sponsored by the newspaper La Gazzetta dello Sport. The race began on 24 May in Milan with a stage that stretched [tpl]convert|190|km|0|abbr=on[/tpl] to Turin, finishing back in Milan on 15 June after a [tpl]convert|278|km|0|abbr=on[/tpl] and total total distance covered of [tpl]convert|3843|km|0|abbr=on[/tpl]. The race was won by the Italian rider Fausto Coppi of the Bianchi team, with fellow Italians Gino Bartali and Giulio Bresci coming in second and third respectively.

===General classification===

There were 50 cyclists who had completed all seventeen stages. For these cyclists, the times they had needed in each stage was added up for the general classification. The cyclist with the least accumulated time was the winner.

===Mountains classification===

In the mountains classification, the Giro organizers selected different mountains that the route crossed and awarded points to the riders who crossed them first.
