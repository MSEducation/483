[[Kinfauns Castle]]

CATEGORIES: Castles in Perth and Kinross, Houses in Perth and Kinross, Category A listed buildings in Perth and Kinross, Listed castles in Scotland, Inventory of Gardens and Designed Landscapes

Kinfauns Castle was designed by Robert Smirke and built between 1822 and 1826 by Lord Gray on the site of a medieval stronghold.  It is situated in Perth and Kinross, Scotland, and is currently occupied by Scottish businesswoman Ann Gloag. The house is protected as a category A listed building,[tpl]cite web |url=http://hsewsf.sedsh.gov.uk/hslive/hsstart?P_HBNUM=11955 |title=Kinfauns Castle: Listed Building Report |publisher=Historic Scotland[/tpl] and the grounds are included in the Inventory of Gardens and Designed Landscapes in Scotland.[tpl]cite web |url=http://data.historic-scotland.gov.uk/pls/htmldb/f?p=2400:15:0::::GARDEN:GDL00240 |title=Kinfauns Castle |work=An Inventory of Gardens and Designed Landscapes in Scotland |publisher=Historic Scotland[/tpl]
The Union-Castle Line steamer RMS Kinfauns Castle was launched in 1899 and was named after this building. The vessel was painted by Charles de Lacy.http://collections.rmg.co.uk/collections/objects/13758.html Liner Kinfauns Castle painting museum entry. Retrieved 9th June 2013.
The walled garden and gardener's cottage were designed by Francis William Deas in 1910Dictionary of Scottish Architects:Deas.
