Raynor has coached at clubs Sheffield United and Boston United, where he was assistant manager for a time to Steve Evans.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bufc.co.uk/article/27286/Paul-Raynor-Assistant-Manager |title=Paul Raynor (First Team Coach) |publisher=Boston United F.C |accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl] In May 2007 Evans and Raynor left Boston to take over as manager and assistant at Crawley Town,[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/crawley_town/6700235.stm |title=Crawley appoint Evans as new boss |publisher=BBC Sport |date=29 May 2007 |accessdate=20 October 2009[/tpl] where he occasionally appeared as a player in the Conference. He now is assistant manager and first team coach at Rotherham United in the season 12–13 he achieved promotion with them alongside Steve Evans. He is known for being the one on the sidelines issuing instruction to the player, and also for his eccentric celebration (as well as Steve Evans) and protesting against decisions by the officials.
