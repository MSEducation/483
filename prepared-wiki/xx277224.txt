[[Minimalist shoe]]

CATEGORIES: Athletic shoes, Sports footwear, 20th-century fashion

Minimalist shoes are shoes intended to closely approximate barefoot running conditions.[tpl]cite web|title=What are Minimalist Running Shoes?|url=http://www.runblogger.com/2011/09/what-are-minimalist-running-shoes.html|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] They have reduced cushioning, thin soles, and are of lighter weight than other running shoes, allowing for more sensory contact for the foot on the ground. The category does not correspond to any technical or numerical specification so a claim could be made that any shoe is "minimalist".

==Types==

Generally, there are two types of minimalist shoes:[tpl]cite web|title=Barefoot/Minimalist Running Shoes: How to Choose|url=http://www.rei.com/learn/expert-advice/how-to-choose-barefoot-minimalist-running-shoes.html|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
Barefoot Running Shoes are characterized by their zero drop from heel to toe. In other words, the heel has minimal cushioning and is at the same height level as the toe. The Vibram FiveFingers belongs to this category.
Minimalist Running Shoes are in-between traditional running shoes and barefoot running shoes. They have a minimum heel height of about 4–8 mm. The Nike Free has a 17 mm heel and is marketed as minimalist running shoe, though this designation is disputed by some.[tpl]cite web|last=Hutchinson |first=Alex |url=http://www.runnersworld.com/running-shoes/do-minimalist-shoes-really-simulate-barefoot-running |title=Do Minimalist Shoes Really Simulate Barefoot Running? | Runner's World |publisher=Runnersworld.com |date=2013-01-12 |accessdate=2013-08-16[/tpl]
