Susan Ward (born April 15, 1976) is an American actress and model.

==Career==

Ward began modeling at the age of 13, leaving school and moving to New York to do so, deciding to try her hand at acting several years later.[tpl]cite web | url=http://movies.msn.com/celebrities/celebrity-biography/susan-ward.1/ | title=Susan Ward:Biography | publisher=MSN | accessdate=2013-05-22[/tpl] She appeared in soap orera All My Children in 1995, and later was cast in NBC's short-lived primetime teen drama Malibu Shores produced by Aaron Spelling.
In late 1996, Ward landed a role on Aaron Spelling's daytime soap opera Sunset Beach, where she played the virginal Meg Cummings.[tpl]cite web|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=AASB&p_theme=aasb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0EA057EAE6354209&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM|title=Ex-vixen Ward prefers new `nice girl' role|date=March 30, 1997|publisher=Austin American-Statesman|accessdate=2013-05-22[/tpl] Sunset Beach aired between 1997â€“99. In 1999 she began to pursue a movie career, and starred as the psychotic Brittany Foster in the erotic thriller The In Crowd. The movie was a commercial disappointment in theaters, but found some success on cable and home video. The following year, Ward played a supporting role in the hit movie Shallow Hal. She has also acted in numerous other direct-to-DVD movies, and has made guest appearances in various TV shows, most notably Friends.  
In 2009, she landed the role Chloe Kmetko, mother of a young gymnast, on the ABC Family drama series Make It or Break It. She was part of the main cast during the show's first two seasons.

==Personal life==

Ward was born in Monroe, Louisiana. She attended a private, Christian elementary school (Monroe Christian School), in Monroe, LA. She briefly attended Northeast Louisiana University (now University of Louisiana at Monroe). 
Ward is married to David C. Robinson, the vice-president of Morgan Creek Productions, the movie studio that produced The In Crowd.
