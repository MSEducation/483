[[Doree Lewak]]

CATEGORIES: American humorists, American women writers, Living people

Doree Lewak is an American writer and humorist. Lewak, who lives on the Upper West Side of Manhattan, has written for The New York Post, The New York Times, Entertainment Weekly, Glamour, The Jerusalem Post, The Huffington Post, Time Out New York, Newsday, and New York Daily News.Random House: Lewak bio.‘The Panic Years’ And The Singles Crisis, by Esther D. Kustanowitz,  The JewishWeek, July 28, 2008 http://www.thejewishweek.com/viewArticle/c221_a5457/Singles/First_Person_Singular_.html
Lewak is best known as the author of the 2008 book  The Panic Years: A Guide to Surviving Smug Married Friends, Bad Taffeta, and Life on the Wrong Side of 25 without a Ring.   The Panic Years offers advice on how to change one's relationship strategy to find a marriage partner.The Panic Years: A Guide to Surviving Smug Married Friends, Bad Taffeta, and Life on the Wrong Side of 25 without a Ring, Maureen Salsmon, CNN Living, July 16, 2008 http://edition.cnn.com/2008/LIVING/personal/07/16/singles.at.wedding/  Library Journal  called the book “sassy and humorous.” Library Journal Archive The success of the book turned Lewak into a quoted expert on the plight of the twenty-something single woman.Thirtysomething to thirtynothing, Sire Agrell, Globe and Mail, July 12, 2007 http://www.theglobeandmail.com/servlet/story/RTGAM.20070712.wl30nothing12/BNStory/lifeFamily/
Critics found Lewak’s insistence that women should make matrimony a goal, “repulsive,” [ref]Beyond the B.A.: a grad student perspective, By Elizabeth Brady 
May 13, 2008, Washington University Daily http://thedaily.washington.edu/2008/5/13/beyond-b-grad-student-perspective/[/ref] and criticized Lewak for “reinforc(ing) negative stereotypes.”
