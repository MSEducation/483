[[Lippitt, Rhode Island]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Kent County, Rhode Island

Lippitt, Rhode Island is a village within the town of West Warwick, Rhode Island.

==History==

It was founded by Revolutionary War General Christopher Lippitt and his brother Charles Lippitt when they and their partners constructed Lippitt Mill in 1809 and the surrounding mill village for their workers.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.pvhistorian.com/lippittmill.html|title=  LIPPITT MILL COMPLEX|publisher = Pawtuxet Valley Preservation and Historical Society |accessdate= April 27, 2014[/tpl] During the Depression following of War of 1812 the Lippitt Manufacturing Company survived by supplying yarn to convict weavers in the Vermont prison. The original mill and several of the workers row houses still survive. It is one of the oldest cotton mills in New England and has been sold to several owners since the Lippitt family sold it 1889.Rhode Island: A Guide to the Smallest State (Federal Writers Project), pg 365 http://books.google.com/books?id=bKHQOOx1eGEC&source=gbs_navlinks_s 
The Mill is on the National Register of Historic Places,[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.preservation.ri.gov/pdfs_zips_downloads/national_pdfs/west_warwick/werk_main-street-825_lippitt-mill.pdf |title=  NATIONAL REGISTER OF HISTORIC PLACESINVENTORY - NOMINATION FORM|publisher = National Register of Historic Places |accessdate= April 27, 2014[/tpl] and is one of the earliest textile mills in Rhode Island. It is one of the oldest American textile mills still used for manufacturing.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.pvhistorian.com/lippittmill.html|title=  LIPPITT MILL COMPLEX|publisher = Pawtuxet Valley Preservation and Historical Society |accessdate= April 27, 2014[/tpl]
