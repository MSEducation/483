[[Jervis Public Library]]

CATEGORIES: Libraries on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Houses completed in 1858, Georgian architecture in New York, Buildings and structures in Oneida County, New York, Rome, New York

Jervis Public Library is a historic library building located in Rome in Oneida County, New York. It was built in 1858 as the residence of John B. Jervis (1795â€“1885).  The original house is a rectangular, [tpl]frac|2|1|2[/tpl]-story brick structure with a slate-covered gable roof and cupola. The facade features a 2-story portico supported by four massive Doric order brick columns.  A 2-story library extension was added in 1926 and in 1967 a modern library facility was added.  Also on the property is a 2-story carriage house built about 1860.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6076|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Jervis Public Library|date=September 1982|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=John Harwood |publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6077|title=Accompanying seven photos[/tpl] 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.
