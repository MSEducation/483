[[Thomas McKeown (physician)]]

CATEGORIES: 1912 births, 1988 deaths, British medical historians, 20th-century British medical doctors, Academics of the University of Birmingham, Medical doctors from Northern Ireland, Canadian Rhodes Scholars

Thomas McKeown (1912-1988) was a British physician and medical historian.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oxforddnb.com/index/40/101040074/|title=Thomas McKeown|work=Oxford Dictionary of National Biography|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] McKeown argued from 1955 that the population growth of the UK post-1700 was due to economic conditions rather than improved medicine and public health. This became known as the "McKeown thesis".[tpl]cite journal|last=Cosgrove|first=James|year=2002|title=The McKeown Thesis: A Historical Controversy and Its Enduring Influence|journal=Am J Public Health|publisher=American Public Health Association|volume=92(5)|issue=5|pages=725–9|url=http://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC1447153/[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|last=Grundy|first=Emily|year=2005|title=Commentary: The McKeown debate: time for burial|journal=Int. J. Epidemiol.|publisher=Oxford Journals|volume=34|issue=3|pages=529–533|doi=10.1093/ije/dyh272|url=http://ije.oxfordjournals.org/content/34/3/529.long[/tpl]
McKeown attended university at McGill, before returning across the Atlantic to study at Oxford University as a Rhodes Scholar.[tpl]cite book|last=Bradby|first=Hannah|title=Medical Sociology: An Introduction|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=78WoLXfCUsQC&pg=PA30|year=2009|publisher=SAGE|page=30[/tpl] From 1945, he was professor of social medicine at the University of Birmingham.[tpl]cite journal|last=Bynum|first=Bill|year=2008|title=The McKeown thesis|journal=Lancet|publisher=Elsevier Ltd.|volume=371|issue=9613|pages=644–5|doi=10.1016/S0140-6736(08)60292-5|url=http://www.thelancet.com/journals/lancet/article/PIIS0140-6736%2808%2960292-5/fulltext[/tpl] He is also known for his work in geriatrics and maternal-fetal medicine.

==Personal life==

McKeown was born in Northern Ireland and then moved to Canada with his parents.
