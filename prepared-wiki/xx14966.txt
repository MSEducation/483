[[Austin Powers (character)]]

CATEGORIES: Austin Powers characters, Fictional cryonically preserved characters, Fictional secret agents and spies, Fictional people from London, Fictional British people, Fictional knights, Fictional twins, Fictional characters introduced in 1997

Sir Augustine Danger "Austin" Powers, KBE, is a fictional character from the Austin Powers series of films, and is created and portrayed by Mike Myers. He is the protagonist of Austin Powers: International Man of Mystery (1997), The Spy Who Shagged Me (1999) and Austin Powers in Goldmember (2002).[tpl]cite news|title= THE MEDIA BUSINESS: ADVERTISING; Big marketers are betting on 'Austin Powers' to endear them to young people.|work= The New York Times|date=1999-06-14|url= http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F50814F7395C0C778DDDAF0894D1494D81|accessdate=2010-11-05[/tpl]

==Personality==

Austin Powers was a character seen as a parody of James Bond and being influenced by characters played by film actor Peter Sellers.[tpl]cite book|last=Montalbano|first=Dave|title=The Adventures of Cinema Dave in the Florida Motion Picture World|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=hvfExNxOXpsC&pg=PA185|accessdate=5 August 2012|date=December 2010|publisher=Xlibris Corporation|isbn=978-1-4500-2396-2|page=185[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P2-7118916.html|title=Myers is funny, but he's no Peter Sellers|publisher=Deseret News (Salt Lake City) via HighBeam Research [tpl]subscription required[/tpl]|date=128 November 2003 |accessdate=5 August 2012[/tpl]  The character of Austin Powers represents an archetype of 1960s Swinging London, with his advocacy for free love, his use of obscure impressions and his clothing style (including crushed velvet suits and Beatle boots).

==Development==

Myers, Matthew Sweet and Susanna Hoffs  formed the faux British 1960s band Ming Tea after Myers' Saturday Night Live stint in the early 1990s.[tpl]cite web|title=Mike Myers|url=http://www.digitalhit.com/cr/mikemyers/|work=Digital Hit|publisher=Digital Hit Entertainment/ Multiplex Theatre Properties Inc|accessdate=29 July 2012|author=Digital Hit|year=1997–2012[/tpl][tpl]cite book|title=Biography Today Performing Artists|page=101|author=Cherie D. Abbey, Omnigraphics, Kevin Hillstrom|publisher=Omnigraphics|year=2004|isbn=078080709X[/tpl] The band members all performed under pseudonyms with 1960s personas. Myers adopted the pseudonym and character of Austin Powers. This group made a number of live club and television performances in character. Myers' then wife Robin Ruzan encouraged him to write a film based on Austin Powers.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.newsweek.com/sort-thing-his-bag-baby-172878|title=This Sort Of Thing Is His Bag, Baby|publisher=Newsweek|date=May 18, 1997[/tpl]
Obituaries of Simon Dee (1935–2009), the radio and television presenter, stated that his "Sixties grooviness" made him the inspiration for the character.[tpl]cite news|title= The groovy rise and sad fall of Simon Dee, the real Austin Powers|work= Daily Mail|date=2009-08-31|url= http://www.dailymail.co.uk/news/article-1210153/The-groovy-rise-sad-fall-Simon-Dee-real-Austin-Powers.html|accessdate=2010-11-05|location=London|first=Peter|last=Evans[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title= TV chat show star Simon Dee dies|work= BBC|date=2009-08-30|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/8229429.stm|accessdate=2010-11-05[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title= Dee day for the real Austin Powers|work= The Age|date=2009-08-31|url= http://www.theage.com.au/lifestyle/people/dee-day-for-the-real-austin-powers-20090831-f4y3.html|accessdate=2010-11-05|location=Melbourne[/tpl] Mike Myers has claimed his father was the inspiration behind Austin Powers.[tpl]cite news|title= Austin Powers has The Force|work= BBC|date=1999-06-14|url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/entertainment/368451.stm|accessdate=2010-11-06[/tpl]
