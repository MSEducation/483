[[Low Row]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in North Yorkshire, Swaledale

Low Row is a village in Swaledale, in the Yorkshire Dales, North Yorkshire, England. It lies about 3 miles west of Reeth and is between Healaugh and Gunnerside. It is part of the Richmondshire parish Melbecks. It is a linear village running along one road, the B6270.[tpl]cite book|last=Smith|first=Roly|title=Swaledale|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=Xvpv0gM1tqsC&pg=PA49|year=2008|publisher=Frances Lincoln Ltd|isbn=0-7112-2636-9|page=49[/tpl]
A working farm, Hazel Brow Farm, is open to visitors, and a seventeenth-century stone inn called The Punch Bowl is by the main road.

==History==

The name Low Row comes from the Norse "The Wra" (a nook).[tpl]cite book|last=Fleming|first=Andrew|title=Swaledale: valley of the wild river|year=1998|publisher=Edinburgh University Press|isbn=1-85331-197-9|page=47[/tpl] The surname "Raw" is associated with the village.[tpl]cite book|last1=Ucko|first1=Peter J.|last2=Layton|first2=Robert|title=The Archaeology and Anthropology of Landscape: Shaping Your Landscape|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=EeOGSi8x_04C&pg=PA69|year=2004|publisher=Routledge|page=0203202449[/tpl] The village was raided by Jacobites in 1745, and bodies probably from that raid are buried at the church in Low Row.[tpl]cite book|last=Scholes|first=Ron|title=Yorkshire Dales|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=tKK1tnLhInQC&pg=PA145|series=Landmark Visitor Guide|year=2006|publisher=Hunter Publishing Inc.|isbn=1-84306-209-7|page=145[/tpl]
