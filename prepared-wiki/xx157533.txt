Maximino Oliveras Gutiérrez Mako (born September 10, 1946 in Santurce, Puerto Rico) is a former Minor League Baseball player who later managed in the minors for several teams.Baseball Reference – Minor Leagues career 
Oliveras played seven seasons in the minor leagues.  He was also a coach in the Major Leagues for the California Angels and the Chicago Cubs.[tpl]cite web|title=Cubs All-Time Coaches|url=http://chicago.cubs.mlb.com/chc/history/coaches.jsp|publisher=Baseball-Reference|accessdate=2009-10-02[/tpl]
In May [tpl]By|1986[/tpl], Oliveras took over as skipper of the independent Miami Marlins after Fred Hatfield was fired. He had been "widely praised for his work in the Puerto Rican Winter League", according to Baseball America's 1987 Statistics Report, and the Marlins won the most games they had in eight years. 
The next year, Oliveras was hired by the California Angels organization and hmanaged the Midland Angels from [tpl]By|1987[/tpl] to [tpl]By|1989[/tpl]. He moved up to Triple-A, managing the Edmonton Trappers from [tpl]By|1990[/tpl] to [tpl]By|1992[/tpl] and the Vancouver Canadians in [tpl]By|1993[/tpl]. After that, he became a coach for the Angels in [tpl]mlby|1994[/tpl] and was a member of the Chicago Cubs staff from [tpl]mlby|1995[/tpl] to [tpl]mlby|1997[/tpl]. 
In [tpl]By|1998[/tpl], he managed the Kinston Indians. He took over as skipper of the Orlando Rays in [tpl]By|2002[/tpl], then moved down to the Charleston RiverDogs in [tpl]By|2003[/tpl], and then back up to the Bakersfield Blaze in [tpl]By|2004[/tpl]. In [tpl]By|2005[/tpl] and [tpl]By|2006[/tpl], he was a coach for the Montgomery Biscuits under manager Charlie Montoyo.
