[[Woodie Wilson]]

CATEGORIES: 1925 births, 1994 deaths, Sportspeople from Mobile, Alabama, Racing drivers from Alabama, NASCAR drivers

Woodrow "Woodie" Wilson (October 8, 1925-September 13, 1994) was an American stock car racing driver. One of the pioneers of NASCAR, he competed in the Grand National Division, now the Sprint Cup Series. He was awarded the series' 1961 Rookie of the Year award,[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=hHApAAAAIBAJ&sjid=iNEEAAAAIBAJ&pg=2051,2466950&dq=woodie-wilson+nascar&hl=en|title=NASCAR Honors Fast Foursome|date=February 15, 1962|work=Daytona Beach Morning Journal|page=1|location=Daytona Beach, FL[/tpl] controversially beating Wendell Scott for the honor.[tpl]cite book|last=Donovan|first=Brian|title=Hard Driving: The Wendell Scott Story|year=2009|publisher=Steerforth Press|location=Hanover, NH|isbn=978-1586421601|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=uN-JqrDKB_4C&pg=PT86&dq=%22Woodie+Wilson%22+NASCAR&hl=en&sa=X&ei=QFoKUvv3DKr02gWz44G4Cw&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=%22Woodie%20Wilson%22%20NASCAR&f=false[/tpl] Despite finishing 41st in the final standings, he won the 1961 rookie of the year award.
