[[Andrew Way]]

Andrew Greville Parry Way CMG (9 December 1909 â€“ 8 September 1974) was a British police officer in the London Metropolitan Police.
Way was the son of a clergyman. He was educated at St Edward's School and Christ Church, Oxford and joined the Metropolitan Police as a Constable in 1934. He was almost immediately selected for Hendon Police College and passed out as a Junior Station Inspector in 1936. He was promoted Sub-Divisional Inspector in 1942. In September 1943, he was commissioned into the British Army on the General List as a Provost Marshal.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=36244 |date=9 November 1943 |startpage=4963 |supp=yes[/tpl] He remained in the Army until June 1950, leaving with the rank of Major[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=38984 |date=4 August 1950 |startpage=3998 |supp=yes[/tpl] and rejoining the Metropolitan Police. He served with the Special Police Corps of the Allied Military Government of Trieste from 1947 to 1952."Scotland Yard Man Going to Montreal", The Times, 6 December 1960
From January 1961 to May 1962, as a Commander, he was seconded to the Montreal Police to assist in its reorganisation, for which he was appointed Companion of the Order of St Michael and St George (CMG) in the 1963 New Year Honours.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=42870 |date=28 December 1962 |startpage=4 |supp=yes[/tpl] Returning to London, he served as Commander in the Traffic Department and in 1963 was appointed Assistant Commissioner "B" (Traffic)."Senior Changes at Scotland Yard", The Times, 1 June 1963[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=43151 |date=5 November 1963 |startpage=9053 |supp=[/tpl] He was later moved to Assistant Commissioner "A" (Operations and Administration), probably in 1968. He commanded the British Anguilla Police Unit sent to deal with the unrest on the island in 1969"Troops and police stand by for Anguilla", The Times, 18 March 1969 and retired later that year.
