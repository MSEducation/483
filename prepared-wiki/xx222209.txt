[[Bloomsbury (Frederick, Maryland)]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Maryland, Houses in Frederick County, Maryland

Bloomsbury, also known as the Roger Johnson House, is a sandstone house in southern Frederick County, Maryland. The house was occupied by Roger Johnson, brother of Maryland governor Thomas Johnson, who established Bloomsbury Forge nearby. The property includes the remains of log slave quarters and a rare example of an early log barn.[tpl]cite web|url=http://mht.maryland.gov/nr/NRDetail.aspx?HDID=1252&COUNTY=Frederick&FROM=NRCountyList.aspx?COUNTY=Frederick|title=Maryland Historical Trust|date=2008-10-04|work= National Register of Historic Places: Properties in Frederick County|publisher=Maryland Historical Trust[/tpl]
