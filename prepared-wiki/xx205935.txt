[[Dugald C. Jackson]]

CATEGORIES: IEEE Edison Medal recipients, 1865 births, 1951 deaths, Massachusetts Institute of Technology faculty, Harvard University alumni, American electrical engineers, American educators

Dugald Caleb Jackson (February 13, 1865 - July 1, 1951) was an American electrical engineer. He received the IEEE Edison Medal for "outstanding and inspiring leadership in engineering education and in the field of generation and distribution of electric power".[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.ieeeghn.org/wiki/index.php/Dugald_C._Jackson |title=Dugald C. Jackson |work=IEEE Global History Network |publisher=IEEE |accessdate=25 July 2011|ref=harv[/tpl] Jackson headed the Department of Electrical Engineering of the Massachusetts Institute of Technology for an unprecedented time, 1907 to 1935.[tpl]cite book|first1=Karl L. |last1=Wildes|first2= Nilo A. |last2=Lindgren |year=1985|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=6ZX-GwvhcnkC |title=A century of electrical engineering and computer science at MIT, 1882-1982|publisher= MIT Press|isbn= 0-262-23119-0 |page= 32|ref=harv[/tpl]
