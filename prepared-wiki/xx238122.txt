[[Samuel R. Marshall]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Pennsylvania lobbyists, Pennsylvania lawyers, People from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, Businesspeople in insurance

Samuel R. "Sam" Marshall is a prominent lobbyist in Pennsylvania, where he is president and CEO of the Insurance Federation of Pennsylvania.[tpl]cite web| title = Lobbyist - Marshall, Samuel| work = Pennsylvania Lobbyist Disclosure Database| publisher = Pennsylvania Governor's Office of Administration| year = 2006| url = http://www.lobbyistdisclosure.state.pa.us/Lobbyist/public/EntityDetails.aspx?EntityId=492[/tpl] Prior to that, he worked as a lawyer in the insurance industry.
He was named to the PoliticsPA list of "Sy Snyder's Power 50" list of influential individuals in Pennsylvania politics in 2002 and 2003.[tpl]cite web| title = Sy Snyder's Power 50 | work = PoliticsPA | publisher = The Publius Group| year = 2002 | archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20020421165540/www.politicspa.com/features/power50.htm| url = http://www.politicspa.com/features/power50.htm| archivedate= 2002-04-21[/tpl]  In 2003, he was named to the PoliticsPA "Power 50" list, where he was described as "an influential player in Harrisburg."[tpl]cite web| title = Power 50| work = PoliticsPA| publisher =The Publius Group | year = 2003| archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20040417135307/www.politicspa.com/features/power502003.htm| url = http://www.politicspa.com/features/power502003.htm|archivedate=2004-04-17[/tpl]  In 2003, he was named to the Pennsylvania Report “Power 75” list, where it was noted that he was "at the top of his game" with the Republican takeover of the Pennsylvania General Assembly.[tpl]cite web| title =The PA Report "Power 75" List| work = Pennsylvania Report| publisher = Capital Growth, Inc.| date = January 31, 2003| url = http://www.pa-report.com/uploaded_pdf/PAReportPower75_.pdf|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20060920200116/www.pa-report.com/uploaded_pdf/PAReportPower75_.pdf|archivedate=2006-09-02|format=PDF[/tpl] In 2009, the Pennsylvania Report named him to the "The Pennsylvania Report 100" list of influential figures in Pennsylvania politics, where Marshall's "clout that few in Harrisburg can match" was felt through his "large availability of PAC funds and impressive network."[tpl]cite web| title = PA Report 100| work = Pennsylvania Report| publisher = Capital Growth, Inc.| date = January 23, 2009| url = http://www.pa-report.com/uploaded_pdf/PA%20Report%20100%20-%20Jan%2023,%202009.pdf|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5j0zKOG5a|archivedate=2009-08-14|format=PDF[/tpl]
