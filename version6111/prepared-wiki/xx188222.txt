Alessandro Pierini (born 22 March 1973) is an Italian retired footballer who played as a defender.
After playing for several clubs in his country, namely Udinese, he finished his professional career in Spain, where he represented Racing de Santander and Córdoba.

==Club career==

Born in Viareggio, Province of Lucca, Pierini began his professional career with Udinese Calcio, making his debut in 1991 and moving on loan to Fidelis Andria four years later. He joined ACF Fiorentina in 1999,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/2005/01/27/pagina-28/1355609/pdf.html|title=Pierini apre il 'Viareggio' – Fiorentina tra le favorite|trans_title=Pierini opens 'Viareggio' – Fiorentina chasing favorites|publisher=La Repubblica|language=Italian|date=27 February 2001|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl] and turned down a move to English side West Ham United in the summer of 2001 due to personal reasons,[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/w/west_ham_utd/1473676.stm|title=Hammers pull out of Pierini deal|publisher=BBC Sport|date=6 August 2001|accessdate=28 April 2008[/tpl] instead choosing to sign for Reggina Calcio after the bankruptcy of the Viola in 2002, and signed with Parma AC in January 2003.
After a second spell with Udinese, Pierini moved to Spain at already 31, joining Racing de Santander.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.fichajes.net/noticias/pierini-al-racing-de-santander|title=Pierini al Racing de Santander|trans_title=Pierini to Racing de Santander|publisher=Fichajes|language=Spanish|date=31 August 2004|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl] After only five La Liga matches he was released in the next transfer window and moved to Córdoba CF in Segunda División,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://hemeroteca.mundodeportivo.com/preview/2005/01/27/pagina-28/1355609/pdf.html|title=Fue presentado Alessandro Pierini|trans_title=Alessandro Pierini was presented|publisher=El Mundo Deportivo|language=Spanish|date=27 January 2005|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl] being relegated in that season and promoting in his third, always as an undisputed starter.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.goal.com/es/news/1910/entrevistas/2009/04/17/1214206/entrevista-alessandro-pierini-el-italiano-que-ama-la-vida-en|title=Entrevista: Alessandro Pierini, el italiano que "ama" la vida en España|trans_title=Interview: Alessandro Pierini, the Italian who "loves" living in Spain|publisher=Goal.com|language=Spanish|date=17 April 2009|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl]
In July 2009, with Córdoba consolidated in the second level, Pierini retired from playing at the age of 36, but stayed connected with his last club, immediately being named its assistant manager. In the 2012 summer, after one year with the reserves,[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.diariocordoba.com/noticias/deportes/alessandro-pierini-sera-nuevo-entrenador-cordoba-b_645151.html|title=Alessandro Pierini será el nuevo entrenador del Córdoba B|trans_title=Alessandro Pierini will be new Córdoba B manager|publisher=Diario Córdoba|language=Spanish|date=11 June 2011|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl] he had his first head coach experience away from the club, being appointed at amateurs CD Ronda.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.lavozderonda.es/2012/07/10/deportes/alessandro-pierini-presentado-como-nuevo-entrenador-del-cd-ronda/|title=Alessandro Pierini, presentado como nuevo entrenador del CD Ronda|trans_title=Alessandro Pierini, presented as new CD Ronda manager|publisher=La Voz de Ronda|language=Spanish|date=10 July 2012|accessdate=23 January 2014[/tpl]
