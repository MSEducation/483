[[Tom Stayt]]

Thomas Patrick Stayt (born 20 January 1986) is an English cricketer.  Stayt is a right-handed batsman who bowls right-arm medium-fast.  He was born in Salisbury, Wiltshire and later undertook further education at Exeter University.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/content/player/21446.html|title=Player profile: Tom Stayt|publisher=ESPNcricinfo|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
One of the first graduates of the Gloucestershire cricket academy, where he spent four years, Stayt
made his debut for Gloucestershire in a first-class match against Middlesex in the 2007 County Championship, claiming his maiden first-class wicket in the match when he dismissed Andrew Strauss.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/107/107784.html|title=Middlesex v Gloucestershire, 2007 County Championship|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  He made two further first-class appearances in the 2007 season against Leicestershire and Middlesex.  In 2008, he made his List A debut against Somerset in the 2008 Friends Provident Trophy.  The following season he made three further List A appearances for Gloucestershire in the 2009 Friends Provident Trophy.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/64/64290/List_A_Matches.html|title=List A Matches played by Tom Stayt|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  In his four List A matches, he took 3 wickets at an average of 43.33, with best figures of 2/51.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/64/64290/a_Bowling_by_Team.html|title=List A Bowling For Each Team by Tom Stayt|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  During the 2009 season, he played his last first-class match against Leicestershire in the County Championship, which was also his first since 2007.  In his total of four first-class matches, he took 6 wickets at an average of 49.16, with best figures of 3/51.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/64/64290/f_Bowling_by_Team.html|title=First-class Bowling For Each Team by Tom Stayt|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  He was released by Gloucestershire at the end of the 2009 season[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ecb.co.uk/news/domestic/counties/gloucestershire/stayt-not-staying,307895,EN.html|title=Stayt over at Gloucestershire|date=19 September 2009|work=England and Wales Cricket Board|publisher=www.ecb.co.uk|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] and currently works as a personal trainer in Bath, Somerset.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tomstayt-totalfitness.co.uk/|title=Tom Stayt Total Fitness - Bath Personal Trainer and Strength Coach|publisher=www.tomstayt-totalfitness.co.uk|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
