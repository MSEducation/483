[[Alfred Fuller]]

CATEGORIES: Canadian businesspeople, American businesspeople, 1885 births, 1973 deaths, University of Hartford

Alfred Carl Fuller (January 13, 1885 – December 4, 1973) was a Canadian-born American businessman. He was the original "Fuller Brush Man."
Fuller was born on an Annapolis Valley farm in Welsford, Kings County, Nova Scotia. He moved to Boston, Massachusetts in 1903 at the age of 18 to live with his sister. He went to work for the Somerville Brush and Mop Company, and became a successful salesman for them.Daniel H. Pink, "To Sell Is Human: The Surprising Truth About Moving Others,"   Riverhead, 2012, pp 11-13 In 1906, with a $75.00 investment, he started the Fuller Brush Company in Hartford, Connecticut, selling brushes door to door. By 1919, the company had achieved sales of more than $1 million per year.
Fuller Brush went on to be recognized throughout North America, even inspiring two comedy films, The Fuller Brush Man (1948) and The Fuller Brush Girl (1950).   In 1961 Fuller recorded the secrets to his success on Folkways Records on an album entitled, Careers in Selling: An Interview with Alfred C. Fuller.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2011[/tpl]  The company remained in the Fuller family’s hands until 1968, when it was acquired by Sara Lee Corporation.[tpl]cite web| url= http://www.nytimes.com/1989/05/18/business/fuller-brush-tries-new-approach.html?pagewanted=all&src=pm | title= Fuller Brush Tries New Approach | first= Eric N. | last= Berg| date= May 18, 1989 | publisher= The New York Times |accessdate= 2011-06-05[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Fuller divorced his wife Evelyn in 1930.[tpl]cite web| url= http://www.time.com/time/printout/0,8816,740787,00.html | title= Milestones | date= November 24, 1930| publisher= Time |accessdate= 2011-06-05[/tpl]  Fuller maintained a lifelong connection with his native Nova Scotia, buying a home in Yarmouth, where he and his family spent most summers.[tpl]Citation needed|date=June 2011[/tpl]. In 1996, his widow (who had been born in Yarmouth) donated the house, at 20 Collins Street, to the Yarmouth County Historical Society."Pelton-Fuller House," http://yarmouthcountymuseum.ednet.ns.ca/pelton.html
Fuller was a major supporter of what is now The Hartt School, University of Hartford.  The Fuller Center was built in 1963 on the college campus.
Fuller died in Hartford, Connecticut and is buried at Pleasant Valley Cemetery in Somerset, Nova Scotia.Nova Scotia's Electric Scrapbook: Alfred Carl Fuller Memorial  from ns1763.ca
