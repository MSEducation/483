[[James Gordon (bishop of Jarrow)]]

CATEGORIES: 1881 births, 1938 deaths, Church of England clergy, English Anglican priests, Alumni of Trinity College, Cambridge, People educated at Eton College, Bishops of Jarrow, Vicars of St Mary's Church, Nottingham

James Geoffrey Gordon (11 December 1881 – 28 August 1938) was a priest and bishop in the Church of England.“Who was Who” 1897-1990 London, A & C Black, 1991 ISBN 0-7136-3457-X

==Life==

James Gordon was the son of J. E. H. Gordon and Alice Mary Brandreth. He was educated at Eton College and Trinity College Cambridge.[tpl]acad|id=GRDN900JG|name=Gordon, James Geoffrey[/tpl]
He was Private Secretary to Lord President of the Council 1904 - 1906. He married Martha Sabrina Brinton in 1912. Called to the Bar 1906, Rector of Church of St John the Evangelist, Edinburgh 1919 - 1926, Vicar of St. Mary's Church, Nottingham 1926 - 1933A History of St. Mary's Church Nottingham, D.J. Peters, 1974. and Suffragan Bishop of Jarrow 1932 - 1938.Three New Bishops Suffragan Selby, Whitby, And Jarrow (News)'' The Times Saturday, Dec 24, 1938; pg. 10; Issue 48186; col E
As Bishop of Jarrow he was in attendance at the start of the Jarrow March in 1936 and gave it his blessing."The Jarrow march" Pickard,T: 1982, London, Allison and Busby ISBN 085031988
