He was born in Bristol and educated at The King's School, Canterbury.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Articles/1/1159.html|title=Brief profile of Bob Haines|last=Hignell|first=Dr. A.K.|date=December 2003|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]  He was known by his nickname of Bob.
Having played for the Kent Second XI in 1924, Haines joined Glamorgan nearly a decade later, making his first-class debut for the Welsh county against Nottinghamshire in the 1933 County Championship.  He made eleven further first-class appearances for Glamorgan, the last of which came against Somerset in the 1934 County Championship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4439/First-Class_Matches.html|title=First-Class Matches played by Bob Haines|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]  In his twelve first-class appearances, he scored a total of 350 runs at an average of 19.44, with a high score of 59.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4439/f_Batting_by_Team.html|title=First-class Batting and Fielding For Each Team by Bob Haines|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]  This score, which was one of two fifties he made, came against Sussex in 1933.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/14/14573.html|title=Glamorgan v Sussex, 1933 County Championship|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]  During World War II, Haines was the match secretary for the British Empire XI, and following the war he played three matches for Devon in the 1946 Minor Counties Championship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4439/Minor_Counties_Championship_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Championship Matches played by Bob Haines|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]
He died at Lower Cwmtwrch, Glamorgan on 28 January 1965.  His father, Alfred, played first-class cricket for Gloucestershire.
