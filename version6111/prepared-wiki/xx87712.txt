[[Westona railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Melbourne, Railway stations opened in 1985

Westona railway station is located on the Werribee line in Victoria, Australia. It serves the western Melbourne suburb of Altona opening on 21 January 1985. It initially opened as the terminus of the line from Altona until the line was extended to Laverton in April 1985.Westona VicsigWestona Station Rail Geelong
Westona was named by Joanna O'Connor, Alan Angus and Betty Angus by winning a council run contest to find a new name. As the new station was west of Altona, they suggested Westona, and won the competition.[tpl]cite news|last=Byrne|first=Bridie|title=Diamond Day|url=http://www.starnewsgroup.com.au/star/williamstown-altona-laverton/159/story/64018.html|accessdate=2013-02-15|newspaper=Williamstown, Altona, Laverton Star|date=2008-09-16[/tpl]
Westona is a crossing loop in the middle of a 10 kilometre long section of single track between Altona Junction and Laverton. The direction of trains past Westona is unusual for Melbourne with trains passing on the left.
