Her novels, in particular the Tony Hill series, are known for their graphic depictions of violence and torture.

==The McDermid Stand==

The McDermid Stand, which she sponsors, is in Stark's Park, Raith Rovers ground  in Kirkcaldy. She often invests in billboards there for her newest books. This endeavour was in honour of her father, a scout for the club.[tpl]cite web|title=Dining In The Streets Of Raith|url=http://www.valmcdermid.com/pages/archivenews2008-9.html|work=valmcdermid.com|accessdate=29 October 2011[/tpl] She has lately become a board member of the club, a year after sponsoring this stand.

==Ink attack==

On Thursday 6 December 2012 McDermid was attacked by an assailant who poured ink over the author during an event at the University of Sunderland in Northeast England.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.shieldsgazette.com/lifestyle/entertainment/author-attacked-by-oap-after-she-gave-talk-1-5216441 | title = Author attacked by OAP after she gave talk | publisher = Shields Gazette | date = 12 December 2012 | accessdate = 26 December 2012[/tpl] McDermid had completed a public lecture and was holding a book-signing event when a woman in her 60s, who was wearing a trilby hat and blonde wig,>[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.journallive.co.uk/north-east-news/todays-news/2012/12/13/ink-thrown-at-author-val-mcdermid-during-sunderland-book-signing-61634-32417502/ | title = Ink thrown at author Val McDermid during Sunderland book signing | publisher = Journal Live | date = 12 December 2012 | accessdate = 26 December 2012[/tpl] asked McDermid to sign a Top of the Pops annual which had a picture of disgraced TV presenter Jimmy Savile on the cover. When McDermid refused, the woman poured ink over her and ran out of the room.
McDermid has stated that the incident would not stop her doing future signing events, telling a Northeast local newspaper, "I have been doing this for 25 years and I’ve never had anything like this happen. It wouldn’t put me off doing book signings because the overwhelming majority of people who come to my events are readers who are intelligent people."[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/north-east-news/evening-chronicle-news/2012/12/13/author-val-mcdermid-to-carry-on-signings-after-attack-72703-32419822/ | title = Author Val McDermid to carry on signings after attack | publisher = Journal Live | date = 13 December 2012 | accessdate = 26 December 2012[/tpl] McDermid also made it clear that she did not hold the University of Sunderland responsible for the attack, stating: "They went to a lot of effort to stage the event and absolutely no blame can be attached to them.">[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.chroniclelive.co.uk/communities/sunderland/2012/12/29/author-val-mcdermid-assaulted-at-sunderland-book-signing-72703-32512651/ | title = Author Val McDermid assaulted at Sunderland book signing | publisher = Journal Live | date = 29 December 2012 | accessdate = 11 January 2012[/tpl]
On 29 December Northumbria Police announced that they had arrested a 64-year-old woman from the Hendon area of Sunderland in connection with the attack. The woman was arrested on suspicion of assault and subsequently bailed pending further investigation. In April 2013 the alleged attacker was named as Sandra Botham by the Sunderland Echo.>[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.sunderlandecho.com/news/crime/woman-denies-attack-on-crime-writer-at-sunderland-talk-1-5572819 | title = Woman denies attack on crime writer at Sunderland talk  | publisher = Sunderland Echo | date = 11 April 2013 | accessdate = 15 April 2013[/tpl] It was announced that she had been charged with common assault, denied the charges and would face a two-day trial on 8 July 2013. Botham was convicted of common assault on 10 July 2013.Liz Bury "Reader convicted for Val McDermid ink assault", guardian.co.uk, 10 July 2013 Botham received a 12-month community order with supervision and was made to pay £50 compensation and a £60 victim surcharge.[tpl]cite web|title=Widow who attacked Scottish author Val McDermid with ink is given a restraining order |url=http://www.dailyrecord.co.uk/news/scottish-news/widow-who-launched-ink-attack-2155452|accessdate=29 March 2014[/tpl] She was also given a restraining order forbidding her from contacting McDermid for a non-defined period of time.[tpl]cite web|title=Val McDermid ink attacker given restraining order |url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/entertainment-arts-23664089|accessdate=29 March 2014[/tpl] Following the trial the Northern Echo reported that Botham's actions were motivated by McDermid's 1994 non-fiction book A Suitable Job for a Woman, as Botham claimed that the book contained a passage which besmirched her and her family.Northern Echo Pensioner in court over vendetta with crime writer Val McDermid, Tuesday 9 July 2013, retrieved Wednesday 31 July 2013.
