[[Thrillville (theater event)]]

CATEGORIES: Cinemas and movie theaters in California

Thrillville is the name of a monthly theater event in Oakland, California dedicated to showcasing B-movies, Cult movies, Science Fiction films, and Exploitation films.http://www.sfweekly.com/2001-10-17/film/reel-world/  The event features a film (from one of those genres) combined with special guests and a live stage show, typically a musical or Burlesque act.  Thrillville is hosted by Will "the Thrill" Viharo (son of B-movie actor Robert Viharo) http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4176/is_20070410/ai_n18995270 and his wife, Monica "Tiki Goddess" Cortes.
In addition to one-time showings, regular themed events include "Shatfest: A Tribute to William Shatner," "Elvis D-Day," "Zombie-Rama" and "Horror Host Palooza."  In addition movie trailers, short films, and theater concession advertisements have been shown before feature presentations.

==History==

Beginning as a weekly Saturday night series in 1997 http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/chronicle/archive/2004/11/12/EBG1R9LPPG64.DTL at the Parkway Speakeasy Theater in Oakland, California. In 1999 the series changed to Thursday evenings http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/chronicle/archive/2004/11/12/EBG1R9LPPG64.DTL http://www.sfbg.com/entry.php?entry_id=4019&catid=&volume_id=254&issue_id=304&volume_num=41&issue_num=40 and has continued for eight  years http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4176/is_20070410/ai_n18995270 under the name "Thrillville."

==Live show==

In addition to the main film, the event features special guests and a live stage show, typically a musical or Burlesque act.
Thrillville's guests over the years have included Cult movie icon Ray Dennis Steckler, women from Russ Meyer films, Creature Features hosts Bob Wilkins and John Stanley, Yvonne "Batgirl" Craig, Gary Lockwood (from 2001: A Space Odyssey).
Musical and entertainment acts have included various Neo Burlesque acts and live music from the Phenomenauts, Kitten on the Keys, Pollo Del Mar, Apocalypso Now and many more.

==Ocean's 11 Remake Protest==

In December 2001, Thrillville protested the 2001 remake of the 1960 film Ocean's 11, calling the actors "impostors" http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,252871,00.html and stating that the remake was "an insult to the (Rat Pack's) legacy." This protest involved a group picketing outside Oakland's Jack London Cinemas and was featured by various nationwide news sources.  http://www.reviewjournal.com/lvrj_home/2001/Dec-02-Sun-2001/living/17544759.html http://archive.salon.com/people/feature/2001/12/11/oceans_protest/index.html http://www.sfweekly.com/2001-10-17/film/reel-world/
