[[Ministry of Foreign Affairs (South Korea)]]

CATEGORIES: 1948 establishments in Korea, Foreign affairs ministries, Foreign relations of South Korea, Government ministries of South Korea, Ministries established in 1948

South Korea's Ministry of Foreign Affairs (MOFA) is in charge of the country's foreign relations, as well as handling matters related to overseas Korean nationals. It was established on 17 July 1948.

==History==

In 1998, the ministry's name was changed to Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade (MOFAT, 외교통상부), and it was given jurisdiction over external trade.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.mofat.go.kr/english/ministry/organization/history/index.jsp|title=About the Ministry: Organization: History|publisher=Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Trade|publication-place=South Korea|accessdate=2010-03-12|year=2007[/tpl] In 2013, it reverted to its earlier name of Ministry of Foreign Affairs, and the responsibility for trade matters was handed over to the Ministry of Knowledge Economy, which was renamed the Ministry of Trade, Industry and Energy.[tpl]cite news|url=http://english.yonhapnews.co.kr/fullstory/2013/03/28/99/4500000000AEN20130328002300315F.HTML|title=English names of government ministries finalized|work=Yonhap News|date=2013-03-28|accessdate=2013-04-09[/tpl]
