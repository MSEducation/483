[[Tianlong Tunbao town]]

CATEGORIES: Visitor attractions in Guizhou

Tianlong Tunbao (also spelled Tunpu) (simplified Chinese:天龙屯堡) is an historic town and tourist attraction located near Anshun city of Guizhou province in China.[tpl]cite web|title=Tianlong Tunbao Ming Dynasty City|url=http://visitanshun.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=category&layout=blog&id=25&Itemid=26|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]  The town is home to members of the Tunbao, an officially unrecognized ethnic group of Han people originally descended from members of an army sent on an expedition to Guizhou during the Hongwu reign of the Ming Dynasty.[tpl]cite web|title=Tunpu culture in Anshun|url=http://english.cntv.cn/english/special/news/20110105/109838.shtml|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]  The traditional homes and customs of the Tunbao have been preserved, including performances of the DIxi opera or "ground opera".[tpl]cite book|title=The Rough Guide to Southwest China|year=2012|publisher=Penguin|isbn=9781405393584|page=306|url=http://books.google.ca/books?id=l5vMTs8s0n0C&lpg=PA306&dq=tiantai%20wulong&pg=PA306#v=onepage&q=tiantai%20wulong&f=false[/tpl]   Located next to the town is the Tiantaishan Wulong Temple (Simplified Chinese: 天台山伍龙寺), a former stronghold of Ming Dynasty general Wu Sangui.
