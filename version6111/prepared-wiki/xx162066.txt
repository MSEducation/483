[[Saint George Hospital University Medical Center]]

CATEGORIES: Hospitals in Lebanon, Teaching hospitals, Beirut, Eastern Orthodoxy in Lebanon, Hospitals established in 1978

Saint George Hospital University Medical Center is one of the three leading Lebanese medical centers, located in Achrafieh, Beirut, Lebanon, under the supervision of the Christian Orthodox Metropolitan of Beirut and its dependencies, Archbishop Elias Audi.

==Overview==

The hospital's medical and educational building is a T-shaped fifteen-floor building currently housing 200 beds with a modern outlook. It is affiliated with the University of Balamand, the Centre Hospitalier Universitaire de Toulouse and the Centre Hospitalier Universitaire de Poitiers.
In 2005, a new state-of-the-art building was inaugurated. The rehabilitation and reconstruction of the 1968 building is underway (Phase II)- this new extension will house an additional 200 beds by the end of 2012, thus bringing the total number of beds to 400.

==History==

Saint George Hospital was founded in 1878 as a non-profit community hospital by Panoyot Fakhoury.http://www.stgeorgehospital.org/STGHB/mission.asp A member of the Orthodox community, he donated two rooms in his Gemayze home to be used as a clinic and in-patient facility.
A new 90-bed hospital was built in 1913, followed by another 275-bed facility in 1966. The current facility is a 200-bed hospital built in June 2004, which will be supplemented by a future expansion.
