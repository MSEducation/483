[[Whitestown Town Hall]]

CATEGORIES: Government buildings on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Historic American Buildings Survey in New York, Government buildings completed in 1807, Buildings and structures in Oneida County, New York

Whitestown Town Hall, also known as Liberty Hall, is a historic town hall building located at Whitesboro in Oneida County, New York. It was built in 1807 and is a two story brick structure situated on the village green.  It features four two story pilasters which are terminated at the top by a simple wood cornice.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6079|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Whitestown Town Hall|date=December 1972|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=Virginia B. Kelly|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6081|title=Accompanying photo[/tpl] 
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1973.
