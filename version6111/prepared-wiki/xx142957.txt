Krasnogvardeysky District ([tpl]lang-ru|Красногварде́йский райо́н[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-ady|Красногвардейскэ район[/tpl]) is an administrativeLaw #171 and a municipalLaw #281 district (raion), one of the seven in the Republic of Adygea, Russia. It is located in the northwest of the republic and borders with Ust-Labinsky District of Krasnodar Krai in the north, Shovgenovsky District in the east, Belorechensky District of Krasnodar Krai in the south, and with Krasnodar Reservoir in the west. Its borders with Teuchezhsky District (in the west) and Dinskoy District of Krasnodar Krai (in the northwest) pass through the waters of Krasnodar Reservoir. The area of the district is [tpl]convert|725.5|km2|sp=us[/tpl].[tpl]citation needed|date=November 2009[/tpl] Its administrative center is the rural locality (a selo) of Krasnogvardeyskoye. District's population: [tpl]ru-census|p2010=30,868|p2002=31,536|p1989=29,953[/tpl]. The population of Krasnogvardeyskoye accounts for 30.6% of the district's total population.

==History==

The district was established on February 7, 1929.[tpl]Cite book|last=Азаренкова|first=А. С.|coauthors=И. Ю. Бондарь, Н. С. Вертышева|title=Основные административно-территориальные преобразования на Кубани (1793–1985 гг.)|origyear=1986|year=1986|publisher=Краснодарское книжное издательство|language=Russian|page=180[/tpl] It was transformed into a rural district on February 1, 1963, but this was reversed on January 12, 1965.
