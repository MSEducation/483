==Biography==

Cooledge was born in Sutter Creek, California, to Walter Scott Cooledge and Belle McFarland.  Cooledge and her mother were members of the Daughters of the American Revolution.[tpl]Citation| title=Daughters of the American Revolutionary, Sacramento Chapter History| accessdate=September 25, 2011| url=http://www.sacramentodar.org/history.htm[/tpl] Cooledge was as a mathematics professor, dean, and vice president of the Sacramento Junior College, now known as Sacramento City College.[tpl]Citation| title=It's a Woman's World at City College| publisher=Sac City Express| accessdate=September 25, 2011| url= http://saccityexpress.com/2010/03/08/its-a-womans-world-at-city-college/[/tpl] Cooledge was elected the mayor in Sacramento in 1947.
Cooledge is buried beside her parents and her mother's parents in the Lodi Memorial Park & Cemetery, in Lodi, California.[tpl]Citation| title=Find a Grave| accessdate=September 25, 2011| url= http://www.findagrave.com/cgi-bin/fg.cgi?page=gr&GRid=37409961[/tpl]

==Legacy==

A branch of the Sacramento Public Library located in the Land Park neighborhood is named in her honor.[tpl]Citation| title=Belle Cooledge Library| accessdate=September 25, 2011| url= http://www.saclibrary.org/?pageId=633[/tpl]
