[[María Teresa Torró Flor]]

CATEGORIES: 1992 births, Living people, People from Alto Vinalopó, Spanish female tennis players

María Teresa Torró Flor (born 2 May 1992 in Villena) is a professional Spanish tennis player.
Torró Flor has won one singles and one doubles title on the WTA tour as well as eleven singles and one doubles title on the ITF tour in her career. On 28 April 2014, she reached her best singles ranking of world number 50. On 6 January 2014, she peaked at world number 150 in the doubles rankings.
Torró Flor was victorious upon her debut for the Spain Fed Cup team in February 2013, defeating Ukraine's Yuliya Beygelzimer in their 2013 Fed Cup World Group II tie.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fedcup.com/en/news/articles/victorious-spain-after-torro-flor-wins-on-debut.aspx|title=Victorious Spain after Torro-Flor wins on debut|first=Mason|last=Jones|work=Fed Cup|date=10 February 2013|accessdate=10 February 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

===2012===

Torró Flor began her 2012 season by playing a $25,000 tournament in Andrezieux-Boutheon, France, where she lost in the first round of qualifying to Garbiñe Muguruza. She remained in France to play one more $25,000 event in Grenoble, for which she qualified. In the first round of the main draw, she overcame fellow qualifier and home favorite Jessica Ginier, only to be swept aside by fifth seeded Sandra Záhlavová in the second round.
Torró Flor then played her third consecutive $25,000 tournament in Rabat. She once more qualified, and defeated Cristina Dinu and Laura Thorpe en route to the quarterfinals where she lost to Jasmina Tinjić.

===2014===

In April, Torró Flor reached her first WTA final in Marrakech, Morocco. She didn't drop a single set in her four match run to the final, defeating no 4 and no 5 seeds Bojana Jovanovski and Garbiñe Muguruza in the process. In the final, she beat Romina Oprandi in three sets, winning 6-3 3-6 6-3 to claim her first WTA title. After her win, she broke into the world's top 50 for the first time, and continued her winning streak by defeating former world number 15 Peng Shuai in the first round of the Portugal Open.
