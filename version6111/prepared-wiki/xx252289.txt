[[Williamstown, Fedamore]]

CATEGORIES: Townlands of County Limerick

Williamstown is a townland in the parish of Fedamore, in County Limerick, Ireland.
Neighbouring townlands include Carnane and Arywee to the west, Ballynagarde to the east, Parkatotaun to the north, and Loughlinstown to the south.Google Maps
It has an area of approximately 40 hectares (99 acres).IreAtlas Townland Database It is located [tpl]convert|14.2|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] distance by road from Limerick city centre, in a South South East direction.Google Earth - earth.google.com
The name is a direct translation from the Irish language "Baile Liam".Placenames Database of Ireland - www.logainm.ie
Residents of Williamstown entitled to vote may do so for candidates in the Castleconnell Local Electoral Area for local elections, the Limerick (Dáil Éireann constituency) for General elections, South (European Parliament constituency) for European Parliament elections, and in referenda to change the Irish Constitution. The polling station for these elections is in nearby Caherelly National School.Limerick County Council - Register of Electors - www.lcc.ie/ereg
Williamstown Castle is included in the National Inventory of Architectural Heritage.http://www.BuildingsOfIreland.ie
