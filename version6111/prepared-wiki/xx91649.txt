[[Tea Gardens, New South Wales]]

CATEGORIES: Towns in the Hunter Region, Suburbs of Great Lakes Council

Tea Gardens is a locality in the Great Lakes Council local government area, located near the southern extremity of the Mid North Coast and the northern extremity of the Hunter Region in New South Wales, Australia.[tpl]DoL suburb image|name=Tea Gardens|codename=Tea%20Gardens|accessdate=2008-06-02[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dlg.nsw.gov.au/dlg/dlghome/dlg_Regions.asp?region=HT&regiontype=1|title=Suburb Search - Local Council Boundaries - Hunter (HT)|publisher=New South Wales Division of Local Government|accessdate=2008-06-02[/tpl]
At the 2006 census, Tea Gardens had a population of 2,094, with most of the population resident in the village of Tea Gardens at the southern end of the locality.[tpl]Census 2006 AUS|id=UCL173000|name=Tea Gardens (Urban Centre/Locality)|accessdate=2008-06-02|quick=on|map=yes[/tpl]

==Geography==

Tea Gardens extends along the west bank of the Myall River from Port Stephens approximately [tpl]Convert|14|km[/tpl] in a north-north-easterly direction. In the west it reaches the Pacific Highway. The village of Tea Gardens is located at the southernmost end of the locality, on the northern shore of Port Stephens on the west bank of the Myall River, which connects the Myall Lakes to the port. It is located directly across the river from Hawks Nest and the two lane Singing Bridge connects the two. Tea Gardens is located almost [tpl]Convert|220|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] north of Sydney, about [tpl]Convert|10|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] southeast of the Pacific Highway on the southern end of the Ramsar Convention listed wetlands of the Myall Lakes.

==Wetlands==

Due to a number of anthropogenic threats to the wetlands,[tpl]Citation needed|date=July 2008[/tpl] residents of Tea Gardens have established a website to promote the sustainable use of local wetlands.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.myallwetlands.com|title=Myall Lakes Wetlands|accessdate=2008-07-17[/tpl] These wetlands are well recognised as the foundation for the village's valuable fishing and tourism sectors.[tpl]Citation needed|date=July 2008[/tpl]
