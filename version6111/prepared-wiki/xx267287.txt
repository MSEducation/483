[[Alex Karbouris]]

CATEGORIES: 1979 births, Living people, Club DJs, Australian electronic musicians

Alex Karbouris, known professionally as Alex K, is an Australian music producer, singer, songwriter and DJ. He is known for being one of the pioneers of the music genre 'NRG',Chart-toppers set to rock the rafters in Bedford St[tpl]cite web|title=Scene Magazine – Article "Mixing it up – Kickin Hard 11" September 2011|url=http://www.scenemagazine.com.au[/tpl] and his Ultimate NRG and Kickin Hard compilations.[tpl]cite web|title=Beat Magazine – Article "Still Kickin" – Beatflipbook September 2011|url=http://www.beat.com.au/BEATFlipbook/index.php?mag=100pc[/tpl] He has sold over 600,000 albums worldwide,[tpl]cite web|title=AATW Artist Bios|url=http://www.aatw.com/artist.php?id=128294|publisher=All Around the World (AATW)|title=Alex K|date=6 November 2008|work=AATW Artist Profile|accessdate=14 October 2011[/tpl] and has remixed and written for over 200 artists across the globe.[tpl]cite web|title=Brisbane Music|url=http://www.brisbanemusic.com/2010/06/ultimate-nrg-alex-k[/tpl] Karbouris is one of Australia's highest selling hard dance exports.[tpl]cite web|title=Central Station Artist Profiles|url=http://www.centralstation.com.au/profiles/Alex%5FK/[/tpl] On 20 August 2013 he announced via his Facebook page that he has retired from producing NRG.

==Biography==

===1996–2004: Early life and career===

Originally beginning his career in 1994 at community radio station 99.3FM, he joined Australian radio station, Wild FM, and was responsible for numerous megamixes that featured on the Wild CD compilations. He signed with Central Station Records in 1998, and released the first of many compilations titled Kickin Hard, under the alias 'Alex K'.[tpl]cite web|title=Official Website of Alex K|url=http://www.alexk.com.au[/tpl]

===2004–2011: Ultimate NRG===

Karbouris moved from his hometown Sydney, Australia to Liverpool, UK in 2004, and signed a record deal with Universal/AATW.[tpl]cite web|title=Scousenation DVD Interview – Released UK 2005[/tpl] Despite the 'NRG' scene being viewed in the wider industry as a regional phenomena, largely assumed to be confined to the North West, Ultimate NRG 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 and Ultimate NRG Megamix all achieved gold status in the UK, with over 600,000 sales.[tpl]cite web|title=AATW Artist Info|url=http://www.aatw.com[/tpl] Karbouris has had several chart hits with his productions, more notably "If you were mine", "Rise Again", and "Moving too fast" – Supafly Inc (under the alias of NorthWest). He has written and worked with many writers and producers, and had writing credits alongside Annie Lennox with his track "Angel in my heart". 
His song "My Time", was featured and looped in the freeway chase scene of the movie Hancock in 2008.
He has remixed numerous mainstream and underground artists, including The Black Eyed Peas, Rihanna, Snoop Dogg, Lady Gaga, Nicole Scherzinger and David Guetta.[tpl]cite web|title=Discogs|url=http://www.discogs.com/label/Ultimate+NRG+Series[/tpl]

==Personal life==

His grandfather was Lee Gallagher, who formed the GTV-9 Showband and featured on the Graham Kennedy Show.[tpl]cite web|title=Archives|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=ZyUQAAAAIBAJ&sjid=JJUDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3163,1354166&dq=lee+gallagher[/tpl] His auntie is Greek actress Marilena Karbouri, most known from the series Xehase Me.[tpl]cite web|title=IMDB|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1742921/[/tpl] Karbouris currently resides in Sydney, Australia, but tours Europe frequently.[tpl]cite web|title=Official Website of Alex K|url=http://www.alexk.com.au[/tpl]
