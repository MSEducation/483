[[Dimona Radar Facility]]

CATEGORIES: Towers in Israel, Military installations of Israel

The Dimona Radar Facility is a facility near Dimona, Israel consisting of two 400 meter (1,300 feet) tall radar towers, designed to track ballistic missiles through space and provide ground-based missiles with the targeting data needed to intercept them.[tpl]cite news |url=http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5gJP55YHdqMPMI7rhCh3tZCGxl0Pw |accessdate=2008-10-07 |title=Israel to install radar antennae near nuclear site |date=2008-10-03 |publisher=AFP[/tpl] Using AN/TPY-2 X-band radar, it can detect missiles up to 1,500 miles / 2,400 kilometers away.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.time.com/time/world/article/0,8599,1846749,00.html?iid=sphere-inline-sidebar |first=Tim |last=McGirk |coauthors=Aaron J. Klein |title=Israelis Wary of a US Radar Base in the Negev |date=2008-10-02 |work=Time[/tpl] The towers are owned and operated by the US military, and provide only second hand intelligence to Israel. 
The towers of the facility, which are situated at [tpl]coord|30.9685905|N|35.097121|E|type:landmark|name=Dimona Radar Tower[/tpl] and at [tpl]coord|30.9756831|N|35.0986823|E|type:landmark|name=Dimona Radar Tower[/tpl], are the tallest towers in Israel, and the tallest used for radar in the world.[tpl]cite web |url=http://skyscraperpage.com/cities/?buildingID=80121 |title=Skyscraper Page: Dimona Radar Facility, Dimona Israel |accessdate=2008-10-29[/tpl]
