[[Grade I listed buildings in South Yorkshire]]

CATEGORIES: Grade I listed buildings in South Yorkshire, Buildings and structures in South Yorkshire, South Yorkshire-related lists, Lists of Grade I listed buildings in England by county

There are 62 Grade I listed buildings in South Yorkshire, England. In the United Kingdom, the term listed building refers to a building or other structure officially designated as being of special architectural, historical or cultural significance; Grade I structures are those considered to be "buildings of exceptional interest".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.manchester.gov.uk/site/scripts/documents_info.php?categoryID=514&documentID=1906|title=What is a listed building?|publisher=Manchester City Council|accessdate=2007-12-08[/tpl] In England, the authority for listing under the Planning (Listed Buildings and Conservation Areas) Act 1990[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.statutelaw.gov.uk/content.aspx?activeTextDocId=2133903|title=Planning (Listed Buildings and Conservation Areas) Act 1990 (c. 9)|publisher=Ministry of Justice|accessdate=2007-12-17[/tpl] rests with English Heritage, a non-departmental public body sponsored by the Department for Culture, Media and Sport.

==Listing by metropolitan boroughs==
