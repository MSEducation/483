[[9.3×74mmR]]

CATEGORIES: Pistol and rifle cartridges

9.3×74R is a European medium-bore cartridge invented in Germany around 1900.9.3x74mmR:  An Under-Sung Hero by Terry Wieland, African Sporting Gazette

==Design==

The 9.3×74mmR is of a rimmed, bottleneck design and uses a [tpl]convert|.366|in|mm|sing=on[/tpl] diameter bullet, usually weighing [tpl]convert|286|gr|sing=on[/tpl]. According to Hornady, at this weight the velocity is [tpl]convert|2360|ft/s|m/s|abbr=on[/tpl] and energy is [tpl]convert|3536|ft.lbf|J|abbr=on[/tpl]. This cartridge is used for CXP2 and CXP3 types of game (medium and heavy animals) and is very popular in Europe for wild boar. Initially designed for large European game like moose, brown bear, and polar bear, it started following hunters on safaris. It remains a popular cartridge in African hunting in countries with more German influence like Namibia, favored as a continental alternative to the more popular .375 H&H Magnum.The 9.3x74R by Chuck Hawks Outside Europe, Ruger still creates rifles in this cartridge, notably the Ruger No. 1 falling-block rifle.Single Shot rifles at Ruger web site
