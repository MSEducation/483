[[Bill Findlay (footballer)]]

CATEGORIES: Scottish football managers, Watford F.C. managers, 1900 births, 1949 deaths

William "Bill" Findlay (17 February 1900 – 11 June 1949) was a Scottish professional association football player and manager. Born in Wishaw, Lanarkshire, Findlay started his career in Scottish football. He played for Musselburgh Bruntonians and Third Lanark, as well as representing Scotland at junior level. In 1924 Third Lanark sold Findlay to English club Liverpool for a fee of £2,500, and he subsequently played for Leicester City and finally Watford, whom he managed between 1938 and 1947.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.watfordfc.com/page/NewsDetail/0,,10400~1410245,00.html|title=Read all about the future|accessdate=18 October 2009|date=2 October 2009|publisher=Watford F.C| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20091124175053/http://www.watfordfc.com/page/NewsDetail/0,,10400~1410245,00.html| archivedate= 24 November 2009 | deadurl= no[/tpl] During his tenure as manager Watford finished 4th in the Football League Third Division South in consecutive seasons in 1937-38 and 1938-39, before the interruption of competitive football in England due to the Second World War.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.footballsite.co.uk/Statistics/ClubbyClub/ClubHistories/Watford.htm|title=Watford:Record while in the Football League|publisher=footballsite.co.uk|accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] Although primarily the club's manager, Findlay occasionally made wartime appearances for Watford due to player shortages, playing his final game for the club at the age of 44.Jones p269 He became manager of Enfield Town in 1947, a post that he held until his death two years later.
His brother Alex was also a footballer who played for Wrexham, Bristol Rovers, Cheltenham Town and Evesham Town
