[[Regeneration (1915 film)]]

CATEGORIES: 1915 films, 1910s crime films, 1910s drama films, 1910s romance films, American biographical films, American crime drama films, American films, American romance films, American silent feature films, Black-and-white films, Films about orphans, Films based on actual events, Films based on biographies, Films based on plays, Films directed by Raoul Walsh, Films set in New York City, Films shot in New York City, Gangster films, Irish-American media, Public domain films, United States National Film Registry films

Regeneration is a 1915 American silent biographical crime drama co-written and directed by Raoul Walsh. The film, which was the first full length feature film directed by Walsh,[tpl]cite book|title=Catholics in the Movies|date=2008|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=0-195-30656-2|page=38|editor=McDannell, Colleen[/tpl] stars
Rockliffe Fellowes and Anna Q. Nilsson and was adapted for the screen by Carl Harbaugh and Walsh from the memoir My Mamie Rose, by Owen Frawley Kildare and the adapted play by Kildare and Walter C. Hackett.[tpl]cite book|last=Solomon|first=Aubrey|title=The Fox Film Corporation, 1915-1935: A History and Filmography|date=2011|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-786-48610-4|page=229[/tpl]  Cited as one of the first full length gangster films,[tpl]cite book|last=Hahn Rafter|first=Nicole|title=Shots in the Mirror: Crime Films and Society|date=2006|publisher=Oxford University Press|isbn=0-195-17506-9|page=23[/tpl]  Regeneration tells the story of a poor orphan who rises to control the mob until he meets a woman for whom he wants to change. 

==Synopsis==

The story follows the life of Owen (Rockliffe Fellowes), a young Irish American boy who is forced into a life a poverty after his mother dies.  As a result, Owen is forced to live on the street eventually turning to a life of crime.  Owen is eventually reformed, however, by the benevolent Marie Deering (Anna Q. Nilsson).

==Production notes==

Set in New York City, Regeneration was shot on location in New York City's Lower East Side and used real prostitutes, gangsters and homeless people as extras. It is the first produced by Fox Film Corporation, a forerunner of the 20th Century Fox.[tpl]cite web|title=Regeneration (1915)|url=http://www.silentera.com/PSFL/data/R/Regeneration1915.html|publisher=silentera.com[/tpl]  

==Release==

The film was originally released on September 13, 1915 to critical acclaim and was a box office hit.[tpl]cite book|last=Moss|first=Marilyn Ann|title=Raoul Walsh: The True Adventures of Hollywood's Legendary Director|date=2013|publisher=The University Press of Kentucky|isbn=0-813-14444-2|page=50[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Finler|first=Joel Waldo|title=Movie Directors Story|date=1986|publisher=Random House Value Publishing|isbn=0-517-48079-4|page=80[/tpl]  It was re-released to theaters on January 12, 1919.

==Status and preservation==

Regeneration was previously thought to be lost but was rediscovered in the 1970s. A copy of the film is preserved and held by the Museum of Modern Art Department of Film and the Film Preservation Associates.
In 2000, Regeneration was selected for preservation in the United States National Film Registry by the Library of Congress as being "culturally, historically or aesthetically significant".

==Home media==

In 2001, Regeneration was released on Region 1 DVD by Image Entertainment along with the 1915 film Young Romance in 2001. The same two-film set was released on manufactured-on-demand DVD by Image Entertainment in 2012.[tpl]cite web|title=Regeneration (1915): DVD Release Info|url=http://www.silentera.com/video/regenerationHV.html|publisher=silentera.com[/tpl] The film is currently in the public domain.
