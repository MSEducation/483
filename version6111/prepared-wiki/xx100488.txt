[[Buller Gorge]]

CATEGORIES: Buller District, Canyons and gorges of New Zealand

The Buller Gorge is a gorge located in the northwest of the South Island of New Zealand.  The Buller River flows through the deep canyon between Murchison and Westport. Land Information New Zealand lists two sections for the gorge, Upper Buller Gorge and Lower Buller Gorge.[tpl]LINZ|62458|Upper Buller Gorge|2010-04-08[/tpl][tpl]LINZ|16530|Lower Buller Gorge|2010-04-08[/tpl] State Highway 6 runs alongside, but considerably above, the river through the gorge.  The Stillwater - Westport Line railway also runs through the gorge.
New Zealand's longest swingbridge at 110 m lengthBuller Gorge Swingbridge Buller Gorge Swingbridge, Lonely Planet website. Accessed 2013-09-18. spans the Buller River 14 km west of Murchison. That area also offers rides on a zip-line across the gorgeBuller Gorge Swingbridge Adventure and Heritage Park Buller Gorge Swingbridge Adventure and Heritage Park website. Accessed 2013-09-18. as well as several short bush walks.
