[[Frankland River]]

CATEGORIES: Rivers of Western Australia, Great Southern (Western Australia), Warren (biogeographic region)

The Frankland River is a river in the great southern region of Western Australia. The Frankland River is the largest river by volume in the region and the eighth largest in the state.[tpl]cite web|url=http://portal.environment.wa.gov.au/pls/portal/docs/PAGE/DOE_ADMIN/POLICY_REPOSITORY/TAB1144270/CONSULTATIONPAPER_V21.pdf|title= Waterways WA - Strategies for Management to WA waterways|year=2001|accessdate=1 August 2007[/tpl]
The Gordon River, a tributary of the Frankland river has its source south-west of the town of Broomehill. The other two smaller tributaries are Towerlup Brook and Ornabullup Creek.
The Walpole, the Deep and the Frankland all flow into the Nornalup Inlet west of Walpole. 
Many agricultural industries are located along the frankland river Dairy farming, viticulture [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ferngrove.com.au/default.asp?V_DOC_ID=804|title=Ferngrove Wines Frankland River|year=2007|accessdate=5 October 2007[/tpl] and olive farming [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.froc.com.au/|title=Frankland River Olive Company|year=2007|accessdate=5 October 2007[/tpl]
as a result it is estimated that 85% of the catchment has been cleared for agricultural purposes.
Salinity has increased in the river system with average values of 2 ppt in 1970's to 1980's to 30 ppt since 2000.
