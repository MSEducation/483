[[Thorn in the flesh]]

CATEGORIES: New Testament words and phrases, Paul the Apostle, Christian terms

Thorn in the flesh is a colloquialism used to describe a chronic infirmity, annoyance, or trouble in one's life. It is most commonly used by Christians.  The source of this Bible expression is Paul the Apostle, in his Second Epistle to the Corinthians 12:7–9:[tpl]bibleref2|2Corinthians|12:7-9|9|2 Corinthians 12:7–9[/tpl] – multi-version compare
Other passages where "thorn" is used as a metaphor:[tpl]bibleref|Ezekiel|28:24|9[/tpl] – [tpl]bibleref|Joshua|23:13|9[/tpl]
[tpl]quote|And there shall be no more a pricking briar unto the house of Israel, nor any grieving thorn of all are round about them, that despised them; and they shall know that I am the Lord [tpl]GOD[/tpl].|Ezekiel 28:24[/tpl]
[tpl]quote|Know for a certainty that the [tpl]LORD[/tpl] your God will no more drive out of these nations from before you; but they shall be snares and traps unto you, and scourges in your sides, and thorns in your eyes, until ye perish from off this good land which the [tpl]LORD[/tpl] your God hath given you.|Joshua 23:13[/tpl]
