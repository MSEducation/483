[[Gordon Harris (cricketer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1964 births, Living people, People from Tottenham, People educated at Merchant Taylors' School, Northwood, Alumni of De Montfort University, English cricketers, Leicestershire cricketers, Bedfordshire cricketers, Hertfordshire cricketers, Middlesex cricketers

Gordon Andrew Robert Harris (born 11 January 1964) is a former English cricketer. Harris was a right-handed batsman who bowled right-arm fast-medium. He was born in Tottenham, Middlesex and educated at Merchant Taylors' School and Leicester Polytechnic.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.espncricinfo.com/ci/content/player/14069.html|title=Player profile: Gordon Harris|publisher=ESPNcricinfo|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl]
Harris made his only first-class appearance for Leicestershire in the 1986 County Championship against Worcestershire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4320/First-Class_Matches.html|title=First-Class Matches played by Gordon Harris|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] Following this match he appeared in his only List A game for the county against Worcestershire at New Road.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4320/List_A_Matches.html|title=List A Matches played by Gordon Harris|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] He joined Bedfordshire in 1988, making his debut for the county against Lincolnshire in the Minor Counties Championship. He played Minor counties cricket for Bedfordshire in 1988 and 1989, making twelve Minor Counties Championship[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4320/Minor_Counties_Championship_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Championship Matches played by Gordon Harris|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] and three MCCA Knockout Trophy appearances.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4320/Minor_Counties_Trophy_Matches.html|title=Minor Counties Trophy Matches played by Gordon Harris|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl]
In 1990, Harris joined Hertfordshire, making his debut for the county against Northumberland in the Minor Counties Championship. He played Minor counties cricket for Hertfordshire from 1990 to 1993, making 17 Minor Counties Championship and eleven MCCA Knockout Trophy appearances. He made his first List A appearance for the county in the 1990 NatWest Trophy against Warwickshire. He made two further List A appearances for Hertfordshire, against Warwickshire in the 1991 NatWest Trophy and Gloucestershire in the 1993 NatWest Trophy. He took 2 wickets in these three games, which came at an average of 58.00, with best figures of 3/32.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/4/4320/a_Bowling_by_Team.html|title=List A Bowling For Each Team by Gordon Harris|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] In 1994, he joined Middlesex, making two first-class appearances for the county against Glamorgan and Surrey. He claimed his only first-class wicket in the match against Glamorgan, that of David Hemp.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/59/59314.html|title=Middlesex v Glamorgan, 1994 County Championship|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] He also made three List A appearances for Middlesex in the 1994 AXA Equity & Law League, taking 6 wickets at an average of 16.83, with best figures of 5/26. These figures came against Northamptonshire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/59/59386.html|title=Northamptonshire v Middlesex, 1994 AXA Equity & Law League|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] His cricket career came to an end after the 1994 season.
He also played field hockey to county level.
