[[Avon Valley National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1970, Darling Range, Avon River (Western Australia)

Avon Valley is a national park in Western Australia (Australia) that is 47Â kilometres northeast of Perth. It was named after the Avon River which flows through it. The area is an undulating plateau with the sides of the valley steeply sloping back to the river approximately [tpl]convert|200|m[/tpl] below. The area contains granite outcrops and a mix of soil types including loams, gravels and lateritic sands.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.totaltravel.com.au/travel/wa/pertharea/swanvalley/travel-guides/gardens/avon-valley-national|title=Total Travel - Avon Valley National Park|year=2010|accessdate=30 April 2010[/tpl]
It was officially named on 15 October 1971.[tpl]Gazette WA | title = Naming of Avon Valley National Park (Reserve A30192) | page = 1971:4023 | file = 682/64 | date = 15 October 1971[/tpl]
Jarrah, Marri and Wandoo trees are found in the park along with 90 different species of birds making it an ideal place for bird watching.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.about-australia.com/travel-guides/western-australia/perth/attractions/natural/avon-valley-national-park/|title=About Australia - Avon Valley National Park|year=2008|accessdate=30 April 2010[/tpl] Christmas trees and grasstrees are interspersed through the woodlands.[tpl]cite AHD|9998|Avon Valley National Park|date=2010|accessdate=14 November 2010[/tpl] 
In the springtime the park is visited by wildflower enthusiasts to view the a diverse range of flowers including dryandras, donkey orchids and lechenaultias.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.experienceperth.com/en/Things_to_see_and_do/Nature_and_marine_life/National_parks/Pages/Avon_Valley_National_Park.aspx|title=Experience Perth - Avon Valley National Park|year=2009|accessdate=30 April 2010[/tpl] Other plants found in the area are Conostylis and the rare fringed lily are also found within the park.
The bushranger Moondyne Joe used the area as a hide-out with his cave and corral situated within the park boundaries. Both have since been damaged by a series of bushfires within the park.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.experienceperth.com/SiteCollectionDocuments/PDF/AVNP_Park_Guide.pdf|title= Avon Valley National Park Guide|year=2008|accessdate=30 April 2010[/tpl]
The third route of the Eastern Railway is in parts the southern border of the park, on the southern side of the Avon River, and provides - at times of bushfires and other emergencies  a track and point of access.[tpl]cite web|url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/result?q=avon+valley+western+australia|title=photo section has Avon Valley railway route photographs[/tpl]

==Facilities==

Entry and camping fess apply for visitors to the park. Toilets, water, shaded areas and wood barbecues are available for use. Trail signage and an information shelter are located within the park and a dedicated ranger is on site.
