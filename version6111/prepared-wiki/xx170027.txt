[[Molokai coffee]]

CATEGORIES: Hawaiian cuisine, Business in Hawaii, Coffee varieties, Molokai

Moloka‘i Coffee refers to coffee grown, processed and roasted on the island of Moloka'i in Maui County, Hawai[tpl]okina[/tpl]i, United States.

==Producers==

German merchant Rudolph Wilhelm Meyer (1826–1897) grew coffee on the island and also built a sugar mill.
"Coffees of Hawaii" are currently the only producer of Moloka‘i coffee, on a [tpl]convert|500|acre|km2|sing=on[/tpl] plantation located in the community of Kualapu'u.[tpl]citation needed|date=October 2010[/tpl]
A store with coffee bar and plantation tours is located at 1630 Farrington Avenue (Route 480 at Route 470, Kalae Highway) at [tpl]coord |21|9|10|N| 157|2|9|W| type:landmark_region:US-HI |display=inline[/tpl]

==Distributors==

Trader Joe's introduced this coffee in mid-2008. The packaging, a [tpl]convert|13|oz|sing=on[/tpl] can, featured two red hummingbirds on a light yellow background filled with flowers The can was titled "Moloka'i 100% One Hundred Percent Hawaiian Coffee" and labeled the coffee "Dark Roast, Robust & Earthy, 100% Arabica Whole Bean Coffee".[tpl]cite web |title= Molokai Coffee |work= Trader Joe's fan web site |date= June 4, 2008 |url= http://www.traderjoesfan.com/Trader_Joes/molokai_coffee/details/ |accessdate= October 11, 2010 [/tpl] and discontinued it in late 2009.Related by Trader Joe's employee.
