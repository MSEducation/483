[[Maureen Drake]]

CATEGORIES: 1971 births, Living people, Canadian female tennis players, People from Toronto, Racket sportspeople from Ontario, Hopman Cup competitors

Maureen Elizabeth Drake (born March 21, 1971)[tpl]cite web|url=http://sports.cbs5.com/tennis/players.asp?id=168503|title=Player Stats - Maureen Drake|publisher=CBS5.com|accessdate=October 6, 2008[/tpl] is a Canadian former professional tennis player. Her career-high WTA singles ranking is No. 47, which she reached on September 13, 1999. Her career high doubles ranking is No. 77, set on October 30, 2006.  
After Drake qualified for the round of 16 at the 1999 Australian Open, Canada did not have a Canadian reach the fourth round of a Grand Slam event until Aleksandra Wozniak at the French Open in 2009.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/wozniak-bests-clay-specialist-at-french-open-nestor-advances-1.852020|title=Wozniak bests clay specialist at French Open; Nestor advances|publisher=CBC Sports|accessdate=May 30, 2009[/tpl] Drake retired April 2011.
