[[Bass Worthington Ground]]

CATEGORIES: Derbyshire County Cricket Club, Staffordshire County Cricket Club, Defunct cricket grounds in England, Cricket grounds in Staffordshire, Burton upon Trent, Defunct sports venues in Staffordshire

The Bass Worthington Ground (also known as the Town Ground) was a cricket ground located along Derby Road in Burton upon Trent, Staffordshire. The ground was bordered to the east and north by the Pirelli tyre factory.

==History==

Originally named for the Worthington Brewery based in Burton, the first recorded match held at the ground was in 1954 when Burton-on-Trent played Derbyshire Club and Ground.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/11/364_misc.html|title=Other matches played on Bass Worthington Ground, Burton-on-Trent|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=27 January 2013[/tpl] The ground later played host to two first-class matches for Derbyshire against Oxford University in 1975 and Cambridge University in 1976, with Derbyshire winning both matches.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/11/364_f.html|title=First-Class Matches played on Bass Worthington Ground, Burton-on-Trent|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=27 January 2013[/tpl] Staffordshire also first played at the ground in 1975, in a Minor Counties Championship match against Durham. The county played a further seven Minor Counties Championship matches there, the last of which came against Hertfordshire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Grounds/11/364_minc.html|title=Minor Counties Championship Matches played on Bass Worthington Ground, Burton-on-Trent|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=27 January 2013[/tpl] The last recorded match played there was in 1991, between the Staffordshire Cricket Association and the Yorkshire Cricket Association. The ground is today abandoned, with just the pavilion remaining on the site. Directly to the west is the Pirelli Stadium, home ground of Burton Albion F.C..
