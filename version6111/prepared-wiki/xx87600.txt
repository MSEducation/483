Lennie James (born 11 October 1965) is an English actor, screenwriter, and playwright. Among his more notable roles in American television was the mysterious Mr. Hawkins in the CBS series Jericho. James also starred as Detective Joe Geddes in the AMC television series Low Winter Sun and has also been highly praised for his guest appearances as Morgan Jones in the popular AMC series The Walking Dead by fans and critics alike.

==Early life==

James was born in Nottingham, Nottinghamshire.Lennie James Biography at Film Reference. His Afro-Trinidadian parents originate from Trinidad & Tobago. He lived in South London and went to school at Ernest Bevin College. 

==Career==

James graduated from the Guildhall School of Music and Drama in 1988. He appeared in the BBC terrorism drama The State Within, the BBC spy drama Spooks, and starred as Robert Hawkins in the CBS post-apocalyptic drama series Jericho.
James appeared in a 2008 Channel 4 television drama Fallout, playing the role of a detective alongside another detective solving a teen-related murder. He also appeared in an episode of Lie to Me, as well as the AMC/ITV miniseries The Prisoner, aired in November 2009. He appeared in HBO's comedy series Hung as a pimp. In 2012, he starred as DCI Tony Gates in BBC Two's Line of Duty. In 2013, he reprised his role as Morgan Jones in an episode of the third season of The Walking Dead titled "Clear" and showrunner Scott Gimple confirmed he will not appear again until after the fourth season but that he is slated to return.
