[[Sylvia Vrethammar]]

CATEGORIES: 1945 births, Living people, Swedish female singers, Swedish jazz singers, Swedish pop singers, Easy listening music, People from Uddevalla Municipality, Melodifestivalen contestants

Eva Sylvia Vrethammar (born August 22, 1945,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.birthday.se/sok/?f=Sylvia&l=Vrethammar |title=Sök personer |publisher=Birthday.se |date= |accessdate=2013-06-01[/tpl] Uddevalla, Sweden), is a Swedish traditional pop and jazz singer. She is the daughter of Harald Vrethammar, an education official, and Britta Vrethammar, a musical education teacher, specializing in the piano.
She is best known for the 1974 song, "Y Viva España". It reached #4 in the UK Singles Chart in September 1974, spending over six months in that listing. She was known in the UK simply as Sylvia. Globally her version alone sold over one million copies, and was awarded a gold disc.
She took part in Melodifestivalen 2002, singing "Hon är en annan nu". She participated again in Melodifestivalen 2013 in the hope of representing Sweden in the Eurovision Song Contest 2013 which was held in Malmö. She sang her song "Trivialitet" in the fourth semi-final, which was also held in Malmö. She came seventh, therefore she was eliminated from the contest.
