Glance Networks is software company headquartered in Arlington, Massachusetts.  Their product, Glance, is a web-based desktop sharing and web conferencing tool.[tpl]cite web |title=Glance Networks, Inc. |url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=9246259 |work= Private company profile |publisher=Business Week |accessdate= June 9, 2013 [/tpl][tpl]cite web |title=Glance Networks |date= September 28, 2005 |author= Rich Tehrani |work= Blog |url= http://blog.tmcnet.com/blog/rich-tehrani/conferencing/glance-networks.html |publisher=TMC.net |accessdate= June 9, 2013 [/tpl]

==Overview==

The company was founded in 2000 by Taylor Kew and Rich Baker.
Glance is subscription based: businesses  subscribe on a annual, monthly or daily basis.[tpl]cite web |title=Glance Review and Rating |date= March 2, 2005 |url= http://www.pcmag.com/article2/0,2817,1771184,00.asp |publisher=PC Magazine |author=Robert P. Lipschutz |accessdate= June 9, 2013 [/tpl]
Glance eschews most traditional marketing strategies in favor of netroots-style support from blogging and social networking communities, featuring presences on Facebook, Twitter and similar sites.

==Technology==

Glance is designed as a web-based, cross-platform remote desktop application with several levels of redundancy to avoid disconnections. These are both physical and electronic; Glance's internet traffic travels through two geographically distinct colocation centers, one in Massachusetts and another in California.[tpl]cite web |title= Comments submitted to FCC Hearing at Harvard Law School |date= February 26, 2008  |url= http://blog.glancenetworks.com/2008/02/ |work= CEO Rich Baker's blog |accessdate= June 9, 2013[/tpl]
The main technologies Glance utilizes are Java and ActiveX;Glance.net FAQ the program automatically chooses which protocol to use based on the needs and capabilities of the host system.
In addition to working within Microsoft Windows and Linux, Glance supports Mac OS-based systems.[tpl]cite web |title= Glance Networks Offers Screen Sharing For the Rest of Us |date= January 17, 2007 |author= Judi Sohn |work= GigaOm blog |url= http://gigaom.com/2007/01/17/glance-networks-offers-screen-sharing-for-the-rest-of-us/ |accessdate= June 9, 2013 [/tpl][tpl]cite web  |title=Glance |author= Rob Buckley |date= May 22, 2007 |url= http://www.maclife.com/article/glance |work= MacLife |accessdate= June 9, 2013 [/tpl]NetworkWorld Community
