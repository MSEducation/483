[[Phillips-Ronald House]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, Houses completed in 1852, Houses in Montgomery County, Virginia, Blacksburg, Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Montgomery County, Virginia

Phillips-Ronald House, also known as the Carrington Lybrook House and Five Chimneys, is a historic home located at Blacksburg, Montgomery County, Virginia.  It was built in 1851-1852, and is a one-story, brick dwelling with a hipped roof and double-pile, central-passage plan. It features a late-19th century, three-bay central entrance porch with sawn brackets and spindles in the gable and slender turned posts.  Also on the property is a contributing frame garage.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Montgomery/150-0015_Phillips-Ronald_House_1989_Final_Nomination.pdf|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Phillips-Ronald House |author=Gibson Worsham| date=June 1988|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying photo
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1989.
