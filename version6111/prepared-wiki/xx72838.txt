[[K'inich Kan B'alam II]]

CATEGORIES: 635 births, 702 deaths, Rulers of Palenque, 7th-century monarchs in North America, 8th-century monarchs in North America

K'inich Kan B'alam II, (Classic Maya: K'inich Chan B'alam), also (formerly) known as Chan Bahlum II (Serpent Jaguar), (May 23, 635 – February 20, 702) was king of the pre-Columbian Maya polity of Baakal in the Classic period of Mesoamerican chronology, based around the ceremonial center and city now known as the Maya archaeological site of Palenque.Lounsbury 1976, pp. 211-224. Kan B'alam took the throne on January 10, 684, several months after the death of his father and predecessor, Pacal the Great.Lounsbury 1985, p. 45–58.  He continued the ambitious project of adorning Palenque with fine art and architecture begun by his father; his most important addition to the city of Palenque was the Temple of the Cross which is the center piece of the Temple of the Cross Complex. He was succeeded by his younger brother, K'inich K'an Joy Chitam II.Lounsbury 1985, pp. 45–58.
