[[Philip de Harcourt]]

CATEGORIES: Lord Chancellors of England, Year of birth missing, Bishops of Salisbury, 12th-century deaths, Bishops of Bayeux, House of Harcourt, Anglo-Normans

Philip de Harcourt was a medieval  Lord Chancellor of England and Bishop of Bayeux. He was also unsuccessfully elected to be Bishop of Salisbury.

==Life==

Handbook of British Chronology p. 270Spear "Norman Empire" Journal of British Studies p. 2 He was nominated by King Stephen with the advice of Waleran of Melun, but Philip's election was opposed by Henry of Blois, bishop of Winchester who was also the papal legate in England and Stephen's brother. Philip appealed to Pope Innocent II but was refused. 
In 1142 de Harcourt was named Bishop of Bayeux, an office he held until 1163.
