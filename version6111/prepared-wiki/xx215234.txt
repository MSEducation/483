[[Crawfordsville, Oregon]]

CATEGORIES: Census-designated places in Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Linn County, Oregon, 1870 establishments in Oregon, Census-designated places in Linn County, Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Oregon

Crawfordsville is a census-designated place and unincorporated community in Linn County, Oregon, United States.[tpl]cite gnis |id= 1119522 |name= Crawfordsville |entrydate= November 28, 1980 |accessdate= February 19, 2012[/tpl] It is located about [tpl]convert|8|mi|km[/tpl] southeast of Brownsville, on Oregon Route 228, near the Calapooia River.[tpl]cite book |title= Oregon Atlas & Gazetteer |edition= 7th |year= 2008 |publisher= DeLorme |location= Yarmouth, Maine |isbn= 0-89933-347-8 |page= [/tpl] Although it is unincorporated, it has a post office with a ZIP code of 97336.[tpl]cite web |url= http://zip4.usps.com/zip4/citytown.jsp |title= USPS ZIP Code Lookup |accessdate= February 19, 2012[/tpl]

==History==

Crawfordsville was founded on the land of Philemon Vawter Crawford in 1870 by Crawford and Robert Glass.[tpl]Cite OGN|7th|page=248[/tpl] When the post office was established in 1870, it was named for Crawford. Crawford was born in Madison, Indiana in 1814 and he arrived in Oregon via the Oregon Trail in 1851.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.oregonpioneers.com/1851.htm |title= Emigrants to Oregon in 1851 |first= Stephenie |last= Flora |publisher= oregonpioneers.com[/tpl] His son, Jasper V. Crawford, was the first postmaster. Philemon Crawford had previously helped establish the Boston Flour Mill near Shedd.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.bostonmill.org/Mills.html |title= The Boston/Thompson Mill |publisher= Boston Mill Society |accessdate= February 19, 2012[/tpl]
In 1915 Crawfordsville had a population of 300, two sawmills, a flouring mill, a high school, an elementary school, and three churches.[tpl]cite book |url= http://books.google.com/books |title= In Search of Western Oregon |author= Friedman, Ralph |authorlink= Ralph Friedman |year= 1990 |edition= 2nd |pages= 509–510 |publisher= The Caxton Printers, Ltd |location= Caldwell, Idaho |isbn= 0-87004-332-3[/tpl]
In the early 20th century, Crawfordsville had a population of Sikhs from Pakistan and India who worked for the Calapooya Lumber Company.[tpl]cite web |url= http://members.efn.org/~opal/indiamen.htm |title= Sikhs and Hindus from India Working as Loggers & Millworkers for the Calapooya Lumber Company |last= Williamson |first= Stephen[/tpl]

==Education==

Crawfordsville Elementary School, part of the Sweet Home School District, closed in 2011, 158 years after its founding in 1853.[tpl]cite news |url= http://special.registerguard.com/web/newslocalnews/26273425-41/hold-carnival-charter-coburg-com.html.csp |title= Celebration to mark school's closure |work= The Register-Guard |date= May 30, 2011 |accessdate= February 19, 2012[/tpl]  Area elementary students now attend Holley Elementary School in Holley.[tpl]cite news |url= http://democratherald.com/news/local/article_7a5220f6-38cf-11e0-9400-001cc4c002e0.html |title= Board to close Crawfordsville school |first= Jennifer |last= Moody |work= Albany Democrat-Herald |date= February 15, 2011 |accessdate= February 19, 2012[/tpl]

==Points of interest==

The Crawfordsville Bridge over the Calapooia River is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.
