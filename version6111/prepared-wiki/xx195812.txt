[[Jóhann Berg Guðmundsson]]

CATEGORIES: 1990 births, Living people, People from Reykjavík, Icelandic footballers, Icelandic expatriate footballers, Iceland international footballers, Eredivisie players, Breiðablik UBK players, AZ Alkmaar players, Expatriate footballers in the Netherlands, Icelandic expatriates in the Netherlands

Jóhann Berg Guðmundsson (born 27 October 1990) is an Icelandic football winger, who currently plays for AZ in the Dutch Eredivisie and the Icelandic national team.

==Club career==

===Breiðablik===

He started his career at Breiðablik, but moved to England early in his career when his parent decided to relocate there. He had spells with the youth team at Chelsea and Fulham, while studying at the International School of London. After failing to adapt to life in London, he decided to move back to Iceland in 2008, joining his boyhood club Breiðablik as he admitted missing playing football and attending school with his friends.http://fotbolti.net/fullStory.php?id=61651 After returning to Breiðablik he quickly established himself as first team regular in the 2008 Icelandic Premier League season.
He was voted best player for the second third of the Icelandic Premier League season in 2008.
On 30 September 2008, Bundesliga side Hamburger SV had an offer accepted for Jóhann,http://www.fotbolti.net/fullStory.php?action=viewStory&id=67468 the deal did however not go through. On 28 November 2008, it was reported that Jóhann was on trial at English Championship side Coventry City.http://www.coventrytelegraph.net/coventry-city-fc/coventry-city-fc-news/2008/11/28/city-look-overseas-for-winger-92746-22360463/

===AZ Alkmaar===

On 28 January 2009 Breiðablik announced that a transfer fee had been agreed with Dutch club AZ Alkmaar. Jóhann subsequently signed a 5-year contract with AZ Alkmaar.http://breidablik.is/frett.php?id_frett=4844&b=1

==International career==

Jóhann played his first U21 game for Iceland in 2008 and in August 2008, Jóhann won his first cap for Iceland in a friendly match against Azerbaijan, where he set up a goal. On 6 September 2013 Jóhann scored a hat-trick, the first person to do so for Iceland in 13 years, in a FIFA World Cup qualifying match against Switzerland, equalizing with the last goal of the match, a left-footed, curling shot from the edge of the 18-yard box in the 91st minute, with the match finishing 4-4.http://www.mbl.is/sport/efstadeild/2013/09/06/johann_bjargadi_stigi_med_thrennu/
