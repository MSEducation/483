[[Tzii-wi]]

CATEGORIES: Axes, Culture of New Mexico

The Tzii-wi (two flake or two point) is a type of axe historically used in the Jemez Plateau, New Mexico, USA.[tpl]cite book|author=United States. National Park Service|title=Publications in archeology|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=bsZGAQAAIAAJ|accessdate=26 September 2011|year=1974|publisher=U.S. Dept. of the Interior, National Park Service|page=107[/tpl] Notched and double-bitted,[tpl]cite book|last1=Reed|first1=Erik Kellerman|last2=Schroeder|first2=Albert H.|last3=Breternitz|first3=David A.|title=Collected papers in honor of Erik Kellerman Reed|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=BnN0AAAAMAAJ|accessdate=26 September 2011|year=1981|publisher=Archaeological Society of New Mexico, Albuquerque Archaeological Society Press|page=128[/tpl] it is believed to be associated with war.
