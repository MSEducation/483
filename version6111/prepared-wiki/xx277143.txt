[[Paul Korner]]

CATEGORIES: 1925 births, 2012 deaths, Australian Jews, Australian medical doctors, Australian cardiovascular researchers, Australian people of Czech-Jewish descent, Australian physiologists, People educated at Barker College, Sydney Medical School alumni, Officers of the Order of Australia

Emeritus Professor Paul Ivan Korner AO (18 November 1925[tpl]spaced ndash[/tpl]3 October 2012) was a Czech–Australian cardiac physiologist noted for his contributions to the understanding of hypertension, especially essential hypertension.

==Early life and education==

Paul Ivan Korner was born in Moravská Ostrava, Czechoslovakia as the older of two sons of Ernst Korner and Edith (née Singer).Chin-Dusting, J.; Head, G.; Wright, C.; Elghozi, J.-L.; Friberg, P.; Adams, M. A.; Courneya, C.-A.; Badoer, E.; Kingwell, B. (2013.) "Paul I Korner (1925–2012)", Clinical and Experimental Pharmacology and Physiology, 40(3):169-76.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.smh.com.au/comment/obituaries/world-authority-on-blood-pressure-20121129-2ainc.html|title=World authority on blood pressure: Paul Korner|date=30 November 2012|work=Sydney Morning Herald|publisher=Fairfax Media[/tpl]
Korner attended Barker College and the University of Sydney, where he commenced medical studies before taking a break to earn a B.Sc. (1946) and a M.Sc. (1947).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.csanz.edu.au/LinkClick.aspx?fileticket=emwYM6pvRTU%3D&tabid=101|title=On the Pulse|publisher=Cardiac Society of Australia and New Zealand[/tpl] He completed his medical education in 1951 and undertook his internship at the Royal Prince Alfred Hospital.
During his education and training, he was heavily influenced by Frank Cotton, Hugh Kingsley Ward, and, later, (C.) Ruthven (B.) Blackburn.

==Career==

Korner was the Foundation Professor and Head of the School of Physiology and Pharmacology at the University of New South Wales.Mellor, L. (2008.) "Korner, Paul Ivan", Faculty of Medicine Online Museum and Archive, University of Sydney.
Korner was a director of the Baker Medical Research Institute in Melbourne.
In 1979, the High Blood Pressure Research Council of Australia was formed, in large part due to Korner.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hbprca.com.au/about/history/|title=Title|publisher=High Blood Pressure Research Council of Australia[/tpl]
From the 1950s till his death, Korner published in excess of 330 research works.Jennings, G. (2013.) "Paul Ivan Korner", Hypertension, 61:554-5.
In 1990 he was appointed an Officer of the Order of Australia (AO).It's an Honour. Retrieved 28 January 2014

==Death==

After a brief illness, Korner died at North Shore Private Hospital in St Leonards, New South Wales, Australia. At the time, Korner resided in Woolwich, a suburb on the lower North Shore of Sydney.  He was survived by his wife, Jennifer (née Woods); his children, Nicholas, Anthony, and Harriest; and grandchildren, Mauricio, John, Brayan, Tom, Daniel, and Paul.[tpl]cite web|url=http://tributes.smh.com.au/obituaries/smh-au/obituary.aspx?pid=160319198#fbLoggedOut|title=Paul Korner Obituary|publisher=Farifax Media|date=9 October 2012|work=Sydney Morning Herald[/tpl]

===Legacy===

A weekly seminar series presented by doctoral students and postdoctoral researchers at the Victor Chang Cardiac Research Institute, located in Darlinghurst, New South Wales, Australia, has been named in honour of Korner.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.victorchang.edu.au/home/news-events/event/event-detail/?event_name=paul-korner-seminar-series|title=Paul Korner Seminar Series|publisher=Victor Chang Cardiac Research Institute[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Korner married Jennifer, with whom he had three children. Aged 19 and while a medical student on break, at Burragorang Valley, New South Wales, he met his future wife, then aged 16.[tpl]cite web|url=http://science.org.au/scientists/interviews/k/pk.html|title=Interviews with Australian Scientists: Professor Paul Korner (1925-2012)|publisher=Australian Academy of Science[/tpl]
