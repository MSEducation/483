[[Karl B. McEachron]]

CATEGORIES: IEEE Edison Medal recipients, 1889 births, 1954 deaths

Karl B. McEachron (November 17, 1889 in Hoosick Falls, New York - January 24, 1954 in Pittsfield, Massachusetts) was an American electrical engineer known for his contributions to high-voltage engineering. He received the 1949 AIEE Edison Medal (now IEEE Edison Medal) for "the advancement of electrical science in the field of lightning and other high voltage phenomena and for the application of this knowledge to the design and protection of electric apparatus systems".[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.ieeeghn.org/wiki/index.php/Karl_B._McEachron |title=Karl B. McEachron |author= |date= |work=IEEE Global History Network |publisher=IEEE |accessdate=25 July 2011[/tpl] McEachron received also the 1935 Edward Longstreth Medal of the Franklin Institute.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fi.edu/winners/show_results.faw?gs=&ln=&fn=&keyword=&subject=&award=LONG+&sy=1934&ey=1936&name=Submit |title=Franklin Laureate Database - Edward Longstreth Medal 1935 Laureates |publisher=Franklin Institute |accessdate=November 23, 2011[/tpl]
