[[Titus Andronicus (band)]]

CATEGORIES: American indie rock groups, Musical groups from New Jersey, People from Glen Rock, New Jersey, XL Recordings artists, Musical groups established in 2005

Titus Andronicus is a punk/indie rock band from Glen Rock, New Jersey, USA, formed in 2005.Titus Andronicus at Allmusic The band is composed of singer/lyricist/guitarist Patrick Stickles, drummer Eric Harm, bassist Julian Veronesi, and guitarist Adam Reich. The group takes its name from the Shakespeare play Titus Andronicus, and has cited musical and stylistic influences as diverse as Neutral Milk Hotel and Pulp.[tpl]cite web|last=Lindsay |first=Cam |url=http://www.exclaim.ca/articles/multiarticlesub.aspx?csid1=122&csid2=946&fid1=31628 |title=Titus Andronicus • Interviews • |publisher=Exclaim.ca |date= |accessdate=2012-11-13[/tpl]
Their first album, The Airing of Grievances, was released in 2008, and was described as the sound of a "violent, overblown and irreverent" indie band in a positive review in Pitchfork.Album Reviews, Pitchfork Magazine, April 2008 The band's second album, The Monitor, was released on March 9, 2010, debuting at No. 7 on the Billboard Heatseekers chart.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.billboard.com/charts/2010-03-27/heatseekers-albums|title=Heatseeking Albums: week of March 27, 2012|publisher=Billboard.com|accessdate=2012-11-13[/tpl] In March, Rolling Stone named the band one of the seven best new bands of 2010.[tpl]cite web|author=Rolling Stone |url=http://www.rollingstone.com/music/news/best-new-bands-of-2010-free-energy-grace-potter-and-the-nocturnals-and-five-more-20100317 |title=Best New Bands of 2010: Free Energy, Grace Potter and the Nocturnals and Five More | Music News |publisher=Rolling Stone |date=2010-03-17 |accessdate=2012-11-13[/tpl]  Titus Andronicus has also been featured on Late Night with Jimmy Fallon and "Last Call with Carson Daly" .
Their third album, Local Business, was released on October 23, 2012, through XL Recordings. It was listed at #38 on Rolling Stone[tpl]'[/tpl]s 50 Best Albums of 2012[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rollingstone.com/music/lists/50-best-albums-of-2012-20121205/titus-andronicus-local-business-19691231|title=50 Best Albums of 2012: Titus Andronicus - Local Business|publisher=RollingStone.com|accessdate=2013-04-30[/tpl] and gained positive reviews as well as a 7.0 from Pitchfork.[tpl]cite web|last=Cohen|first=Ian|title=Titus Andronicus Local Business|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17258-local-business/|work=Pitchfork|publisher=Pitchfork|accessdate=22 April 2013[/tpl] Local Business was Titus Andronicus' attempt to create an album that was "plug-in-and-play" ready, which allowed the studio recording to sound as close as possible to a live performance and make performing the album on tour much easier.[tpl]cite web|last=Cohen|first=Ian|title=Titus Andronicus Local Business|url=http://pitchfork.com/reviews/albums/17258-local-business/|work=Pitchfork|publisher=Pitchfork|accessdate=22 April 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=LOCAL BUSINESS: THE NEW TITUS ANDRONICUS LP|url=http://titusandronicus.net/blog/431/local-business-the-new-titus-andronicus-lp|work=Titus Andronicus|publisher=XL Recordings|accessdate=22 April 2013[/tpl] Titus Andronicus performed Nirvana's "Breed" on the tribute album Newermind.
The band has announced a fourth album, slated for the fall of 2014, in the form of a 30-song rock opera.  According to one article, Stickles describes it as a "complicated metaphor about manic depression, melding elements of philosophy, psychology, and science fiction through the plight of one troubled protagonist’s inner demons."  Stickles also announced an accompanying film element, consisting of a series of music videos.[tpl]cite web |url = http://consequenceofsound.net/2013/09/titus-andronicus-new-album-will-be-a-30-track-rock-opera-about-manic-depression/ |title = Titus Andronicus’ new album will be a 30-track rock opera about manic depression |last = Roffman |first = Michael |publisher = Consequence of Sound |date = 3 September 2013 |website = Encyclopedia of Things |accessdate= 21 January 2014 [/tpl]
