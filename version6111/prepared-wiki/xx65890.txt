[[Ashvamedha]]

CATEGORIES: Ancient India, Vedic animal sacrifice, Horses in culture and religion, Yajna

The Ashvamedha (Sanskrit: [tpl]lang|sa|अश्वमेध[/tpl] aśvamedhá; "horse sacrifice") was one of the most important royal rituals of Vedic religion, described in detail in the Yajurveda (TS 7.1-5, VSM 22–25[ref]
Ralph Thomas Hotchkin Griffith, The Texts of the White Yajurveda. Translated with a Popular Commentary (1899), 1987 reprint: Munshiram Manoharlal, New Delhi, ISBN 81-215-0047-8.[/ref]  and the pertaining commentary in the Shatapatha Brahmana ŚBM 13.1–5). The Rigveda does have descriptions of horse sacrifice, notably in hymns RV 1.162-163 (which are themselves known as [tpl]IAST|aśvamedha[/tpl]), but does not allude to the full ritual according to the Yajurveda.
