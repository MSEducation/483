[[Elvetham Heath]]

CATEGORIES: Civil parishes in Hampshire, Local Nature Reserves in Hampshire

Elvetham Heath is a residential area and civil parish, containing 1,868 residences,[tpl]Citation needed|date=September 2010[/tpl] on a [tpl]convert|311|acre|km2sing=on[/tpl] site, just outside the north western boundary of the town of Fleet in the English county of Hampshire.

==Location==

Elvetham Heath was built on reclaimed pine plantation/heathland between the M3 motorway and the London-Southampton railway line, [tpl]convert|2|mi|km[/tpl] north of Fleet town centre. Formerly known as Railroad Heath, it previously belonged to the local Calthorpe Estate. Construction finished on the development in June 2008.
Elvetham Heath serves as a commuter area to local towns; Basingstoke some [tpl]convert|12|mi|km[/tpl] to the west, Reading is [tpl]convert|16|mi|km[/tpl] to the north and London is [tpl]convert|37|mi|km[/tpl] to the northeast. The development is located precisely midway between Southampton and London. It is adjacent to the M3 motorway, [tpl]convert|12|mi|km[/tpl] from the M4, and [tpl]convert|14|mi|km[/tpl] from the M25. Fleet railway station is approximately [tpl]convert|1.2|mi|km[/tpl] from the centre of the development.

==Nature reserve==

The district has a Local Nature Reserve.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lnr.naturalengland.org.uk/Special/lnr/lnr_details.asp?C=0&N=elvetham&ID=894 |title=Elvetham Heath |series=Local Nature Reserves|publisher=Natural England|accessdate=4 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url= http://magic.defra.gov.uk/MagicMap.aspx?startTopic=Designations&activelayer=lnrIndex&query=REF_CODE%3D%271009886%27|title=Map of Elvetham Heath|series=Local Nature Reserves|publisher=Natural England|accessdate=4 August 2013[/tpl] It is managed by Hart District Council, with the intent to move it back to typical heathland, with some marsh areas. The area supports species including slow-worms, lizards, and dragonflies. Rare breed cattle graze in the nature reserve during the summer and autumn.
