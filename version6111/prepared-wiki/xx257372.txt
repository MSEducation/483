[[Richard Turpin (cricketer)]]

CATEGORIES: 1967 births, Living people, People from Solihull, People from Warwickshire, English cricketers, English wicket-keepers, Devon cricketers

Richard Charles Turpin (born 5 May 1967) is a former English cricketer.  Turpin was a right-handed batsman who played as a wicket-keeper.  He was born at Solihull, Warwickshire.
Turpin made his debut for Devon in the 1984 Minor Counties Championship against Oxfordshire.  From 1984 to 1990, he represented the county in 29 Championship matches, the last of which came against Cornwall.Minor Counties Championship Matches played by Richard Turpin  His MCCA Knockout Trophy debut for the county came against Oxfordshire in 1984.  From 1984 to 1990, he represented the county in 7 Trophy matches, the last of which came against Lincolnshire.Minor Counties Trophy Matches played by Richard Turpin  
He also represented Devon in List A cricket.  His debut List A match came against Nottinghamshire in the 1986 NatWest Trophy.  From 1986 to 1990, he represented the county in 4 List A matches, the last of which came against Somerset in the 1990 NatWest Trophy.List A Matches played by Richard Turpin  In his 4 matches, he scored 59 runs at a batting average of 19.66, with a high score of 29*.
