==Military career==

Born at Ifield in Sussex,The Peerage.com Cecil Romer was commissioned into the Royal Dublin Fusiliers[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=27051|supp=|startpage=869|date=10 February 1899|accessdate=2009-10-14[/tpl] in 1890 and served in the Second Boer War between 1899 and 1902.Liddell Hart Centre for Military Archives He went on to become a General Staff Officer in 1904.
In World War I he fought on the Western Front. He was General Officer Commanding 59th Division between 1917 and 1918.University of Birmingham
He became General Officer Commanding 1st Division at Aldershot in 1926. He was then elevated to General Officer Commanding-in-Chief Western Command in 1928 and to General Officer Commanding-in-Chief Southern Command in 1931. In 1933 he was appointed Adjutant-General to the Forces: he retired in 1935.
