[[Todd Angkasuwan]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, American music video directors, Year of birth missing (living people), People from Riverside, California

Todd Angkasuwan is a Thai-American music video and documentary film director.

==Biography==

Angkasuwan grew up in Riverside, California and has been making films since he was 9 years old."Director's Chair with Todd Angkasuwan".  AZNRaps.  July 25, 2006. Retrieved June 12, 2009. After college, he worked as a television news reporter for ABC, CBS and Fox."Serial Murder Suspect Caught".  CBS News.  January 3, 2000. Retrieved August 1, 2007. In 2005, he left his career in journalism to pursue filmmaking."Todd Angkasuwan | DXNext".  HipHopDX.  July 16, 2008. Retrieved July 27, 2008.
In the 2006 film No Sleep Til Shanghai (Raptivism/Imperial/Caroline/EMI), Angkasuwan follows rapper Jin Au-Yeung, the first Asian American signed to a major hip-hop label, on an eight-city tour of Asia. The film documents the experiences of Jin and his entourage in visiting cities like Shanghai, Tokyo, Taipei and Singapore for the first time."No Sleep Til Shanghai Review".  Variety.com.  March 28, 2006. Retrieved August 1, 2007. The film screened to a sold out audience at the 2006 San Francisco International Asian American Film Festival, where it was nominated for Best Documentary."No Sleep Til Shanghai MySpace" It was also nominated for Best Documentary at the 2005 Atlanta Hip Hop Film Festival."No Sleep Til Shanghai Wikipedia page"
Angkasuwan has written for AllHipHop"Dave Meyers: Point Of View".  AllHipHop.  January 24, 2006. Retrieved June 13, 2009. and cites Stanley Kubrick, Wong Kar-wai, Michel Gondry, Hype Williams and David Fincher as influences."Todd Angkasuwan Interview".  Dubcnn. March 2009. Retrieved June 12, 2009.
