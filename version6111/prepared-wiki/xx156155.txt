[[Midwest Millions]]

CATEGORIES: Economy of the Midwestern United States, Lottery games in the United States, Scratchcard games, Hutchinson, Kansas, Economy of Kansas, Economy of Iowa

Midwest Millions is a scratchcard game that debuted on September 7, 2007 in Iowa and Kansas.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lotterypost.com/news/156718.htm|title=Kansas, Iowa lotteries create first multi-state scratch game - Lottery Post[/tpl] It originally became available in Kansas at the State Fair in Hutchinson and then throughout Kansas on September 13. It was the nation's first multi-jurisdictional scratch game since the Powerball scratch ticket in the early 2000s.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.journalstar.com/articles/2008/01/12/news/local/doc47880cad6bb14220483720.txt | title=Lincolnite wins $500,000 in lottery | publisher=Lincoln Journal Star|date=2008-01-11|author=Laura Chapman[/tpl] In 2007, it was announced that other Midwestern lotteries may be added in a future edition.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kslottery/News/May%202007/Midwest%20Millions.htm|title=Midwest Millions[/tpl] Iowa and Kansas are members of the Multi-State Lottery Association (MUSL), best known for Powerball. MUSL also administered this instant game.
A total of 1.2 million tickets were offered, split evenly between Iowa and Kansas. The game's biggest prize, $500,000 cash, were won in both second-chance drawings. The first drawing was held in January 2008 in Topeka, Kansas;[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.kcrg.com/news/local/9708817.html | title=Iowa and Kansas Team up on Lottery Ticket | publisher=KCRG | date=2007-09-11[/tpl] Both $500,000 prizes were won on tickets bought in Iowa, although one of the winners was from Nebraska.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ialotteryblog.com/instantscratch_games/ | publisher=Iowa lottery blog | title="Midwest Millions" Test Of New Concept  [/tpl] Due to the success of the first series, another version was announced to start in April 2008[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cjonline.com/stories/011108/bre_prize.shtml | title= Nebraskan wins $500,000 prize | publisher=The Topeka Capital Journal | date=2008-01-11 [/tpl]
