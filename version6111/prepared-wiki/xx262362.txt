[[Castledawson railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Disused railway stations in County Londonderry, Railway stations opened in 1856, Railway stations closed in 1950

Castledawson railway station was on the Belfast and Ballymena Railway which ran from Cookstown Junction to Cookstown in Northern Ireland.

==History==

The station was opened by the Belfast and Ballymena Railway on 10 November 1856.[tpl]cite web|title=Castle Dawson station | work=Railscot - Irish Railways | url=http://www.railscot.co.uk/Ireland/Irish_railways.pdf | accessdate=2012-04-29[/tpl] The station buildings were designed by the architect Charles Lanyon.The Industrial Archaeology of Northern Ireland. William Alan McCutcheon, Northern Ireland. Department of the Environment, Fairleigh Dickinson University Press, 1984
The station closed to passengers on 28 August 1950.
