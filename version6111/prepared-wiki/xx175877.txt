[[WFIS (AM)]]

CATEGORIES: Radio stations in South Carolina, Defunct radio stations in the United States, Radio stations established in 1956, Radio stations disestablished in 2014

WFIS (1600 AM) was a radio station broadcasting a News Talk Information format to the Fountain Inn, South Carolina, USA area. The station was owned by Timeless Media, Inc and featured programing from Westwood One, CBS Radio and Jones Radio Network. [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fcc.gov/fcc-bin/amq?call=WFIS |title=WFIS Facility Record |work=United States Federal Communications Commission, audio division [/tpl]
WFIS was a News/Talk/Sports Radio Station in Fountain Inn/Simpsonville, South Carolina.  The Line Up included:  Rick & Bubba, Neal Boortz, Dr Joy Browne, Clark Howard, Ed Schultz, Sports Talk, Laura Ingraham, Jim Bohannon, Lovelive with Dr Drew, Rita Cosby, 1st Light with Dirk Van & When Radio Was...
Weekends: Best of Rick & Bubba, The Daisy DeadHead Show, On the House with the Carey Brothers, Best of Clark Howard, Best of Neal Boortz, Steve Dale's Pet World, Rusty Humphries, Sunday Morning Prayer & Praise with Rev Lee, Bethlehem Baptist Church, Focus on the Family Weekend Magazine, Son Rise with Kevin Peterson, Mt Zion Baptist Church, La Differncia with DJ Bronco, Unshackled, Best of Dr Joy Browne, Best of Rita Cosby.
Sports-wise: WFIS was the oldest member in South Carolina of the Braves Radio Network (MLB's Largest Radio Network), The Atlanta Hawks Radio Network, University of Georgia Bulldog Football and Hillcrest Sports.
On January 2, 2014, the station's licensee surrendered WFIS' license to the Federal Communications Commission (FCC) for cancellation. The licensee indicated that the surrender was related to their pending application to increase the broadcast power of co-owned station WTZQ.FCC CDSB Application Search for WFIS. Accessed March 5, 2014 On January 3, 2014, the FCC cancelled the station's license.

==Translators==

In addition to the main station, WFIS was relayed by an additional translator to widen its broadcast area, as well as broadcast the station on FM.
