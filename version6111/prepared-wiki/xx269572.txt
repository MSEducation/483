==Proposed==

The Governor of New South Wales and the Railways Engineer John Whitton fought a long battle in the 1860s over the introduction of horse-drawn railways and the narrow gauge, at least for lightly used branch lines; neither were never introduced. ==Gauge==

In a debate in the South Australian Parliament in 1876, it was observed that while the wider [tpl]RailGauge|1600mm[/tpl] and [tpl]RailGauge|1435mm[/tpl] gauges allowed for 2 horses side by side, the narrower [tpl]RailGauge|1067mm[/tpl] gauge did not.[tpl]cite news |url=http://nla.gov.au/nla.news-article43008886 |title=The Parliament. |newspaper=South Australian Register (Adelaide, SA : 1839–1900) |location=Adelaide, SA |date=12 July 1876 |accessdate=31 August 2012 |page=5 |publisher=National Library of Australia[/tpl]
