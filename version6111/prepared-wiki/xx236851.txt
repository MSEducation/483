[[Tschunk]]

CATEGORIES: Yerba mate, Cocktails with rum

Tschunk or chunk [tpl]IPA-de|ˈtʃʊnk|[/tpl] is the name of a German cocktail consisting of Club-Mate and white or brown rum.Philip Häusler: Tschunk. http://tschunk.org/?en. Bremen, 29.12.2013. It is usually served with limes. The edge of the glass may be covered with salt. As Club-Mate contains plenty of caffeine and sugar, the alcohol expedites the effect of the caffeine[tpl]Citation needed|date=October 2009[/tpl], thus the drink has a bracing impact.

==Pronunciation and Origin==

The vowel in tschunk is pronounced like u ("oo"); there is no connection to the English word chunk. The cocktail was originally served in the "Club Forschung" (German for research club) in Rosenthaler Straße, Berlin.[tpl]Citation needed|date=October 2009[/tpl] The club was also known as "Club for-chunk"

==Milieu==

Like Club-Mate, the chunk is a typical drink within European hacker culture and can often be found at scene typical events or locations like the Chaos Computer ClubJudith Horchert: Hacker-Kongress 30C3: Ein Fall für die Chaospatinnen. On: Spiegel online, http://www.spiegel.de/netzwelt/netzpolitik/chaospatinnen-betreuung-fuer-den-ersten-besuch-beim-hackerkongress-a-941117.html, 28.12.2013 – 18:46 Uhr or c-base. It's also a typical party-drink at German universities,[tpl]citation needed|date=August 2011[/tpl] because of the huge amount of caffeine. As a result of the international audience, today it can be found in other countries such as the United States and Canada as well.[tpl]Citation needed|date=October 2009[/tpl]
