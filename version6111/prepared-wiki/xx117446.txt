[[Bezy's night lizard]]

CATEGORIES: Night lizards, Reptiles of the United States, Fauna of the Sonoran Desert, Fauna of the Southwestern United States, Reptiles of Mexico

Bezy's night lizard (Xantusia bezyi ) is a species of lizard endemic to Arizona. 

==Etymology==

Bezy's night lizard is named after noted herpetologist Robert L. Bezy.Beolens B, Watkins M, Grayson M. 2011. The Eponym Dictionary of Reptiles. Baltimore: Johns Hopkins University Press. xiii + 296 pp. ISBN 978-1-4214-0135-5. (Xantusia bezyi, p. 25).

==Geographic range==

X. bezyi is found in central Arizona.

==Description==

Small, smooth-skinned, and gray-brown to yellow-brown, this night lizard measures [tpl]convert|1.5|to|2.75|in[/tpl] from its nose to its vent. It has a flattened head, and dark splotches on its back. The eyes lack eyelids and have vertical, linear pupils. 

==Habitat==

Desert highlands and pine woodlands are its preferred habitats.

==Diet==

This lizard's diet consists of spiders and insects.

==Behavior==

During daylight hours it shelters in rock crevices.
