[[Line of succession to the Swedish throne]]

CATEGORIES: Lines of succession, Swedish monarchy, Sweden-related lists

The line of succession to the Swedish throne is determined by the Act of Succession ([tpl]lang-sv|Successionsordningen[/tpl]), originally approved jointly by the Riksdag of the Estates assembled in Ã–rebro and King Charles XIII in 1810.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.riksdagen.se/Global/dokument/dokument/laws/the-act-of-succession-2012.pdf | title = Act of Succession | publisher = The Riksdag | accessdate = 2014-01-29 [/tpl][tpl]cite web | url = http://www.riksdagen.se/sv/Dokument-Lagar/Lagar/Svenskforfattningssamling/Successionsordning-18100926_sfs-1810-0926/ | title = Successionsordning (1810:0926) | accessdate = 2014-01-29 | publisher = The Riksdag | language = Swedish[/tpl]
In 1979, the Riksdag introduced absolute primogeniture, meaning that the eldest child of the monarch, regardless of gender, is first in the line of succession. The change entered into force on 1 January 1980, making Sweden the first country to adopt absolute primogeniture.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.kungahuset.se/royalcourt/monarchy/themonarchyinsweden/dutiesofthemonarch.4.396160511584257f2180003302.html|website=www.kungahuset.se|publisher=Swedish Royal Court|title=Duties of the Monarch|accessdate=22 December 2013|quote=By this principle, which Sweden is the first country to have introduced, the throne passes to the eldest child, whether male or female.[/tpl] The Swedish crown had previously (since 1810) descended according to agnatic primogeniture, meaning that only males could inherit it.
