[[Andreas Lavik]]

CATEGORIES: 1854 births, 1918 deaths, People from Hordaland, Revivalists, Mayors of places in Hordaland, Members of the Parliament of Norway, Moderate Liberal Party of Norway politicians, Norwegian temperance activists, Norwegian magazine editors, Norwegian educators

Andreas Johannessen Lavik (18 August 1854 – 18 December 1918) was a Norwegian revivalist, temperance advocate, magazine editor, farmer, headmaster and politician.

==Early life and family==

He was born in Eksingedalen as a son of farmer Johannes Andersson Lavik and Kari Andersdotter Kyte. He was a brother of journalist and newspaper editor Johannes Lavik and actor and theatre director Dore Lavik. In 1881 he married Margrethe Christine Matthiessen. They had eleven children. Among their sons was jurist and newspaper editor Johannes Lavik.[tpl]cite encyclopedia |title=Lavik, Johannes (1883–1952) |encyclopedia=Norsk Allkunnebok |year=1957 |volume=8 |publisher=Fonna Forlag|language=Norwegian |location=Oslo [/tpl] His wife Margrethe was elected as deputy representative to the Parliament of Norway in 1915. He was an uncle of parliamentarian Nils Lavik and journalist Herbrand Lavik.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|year=|title=Andreas Lavik |encyclopedia=Norsk biografisk leksikon|first=Arve Kjell |last=Uthaug |authorlink= |volume=|editor=Helle, Knut|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/.nbl_biografi/Andreas_Lavik/utdypning|language=Norwegian|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl]

==Career==

From 1878 to 1887 Lavik worked as a travelling lay preacher for the organization Bergens Indremisjon. In 1885 he settled in Kvinnherad. He served as mayor of Kvinnherad for eight years, and represented the Moderate Liberal Party in Søndre Bergenhus at the Parliament of Norway from 1889 to 1891.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|title=Andreas Johannesen Lavik |encyclopedia=Store norske leksikon|editor=|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/Andreas_Johannesen_Lavik |language=Norwegian|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nsd.uib.no/polsys/index.cfm?urlname=storting&lan=&MenuItem=N1_1&ChildItem=&State=collapse&UttakNr=33&person=11799|title=Andreas Johannessen Lavik|publisher=Norwegian Social Science Data Services (NSD)|language=Norwegian|accessdate=18 November 2011[/tpl]
He was a co-founder of Det Vestlandske Indremisjonsforbund in 1898,[tpl]cite web|url=http://imf.no/om-oss/historien/2/ |title=Indremisjonsforbund på Vestlandet |publisher=Indremisjonsforbundet |language=Norwegian|accessdate=16 November 2011 [/tpl] an organization still active today with about 1,500 local chapters.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|title=Indremisjonsforbundet |encyclopedia=Store norske leksikon|editor=|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/Indremisjonsforbundet |language=Norwegian|accessdate=16 November 2011 [/tpl] Lavik served as the organization's first Secretary, and editor of its magazine Sambåndet. He was a member of the Church Commission of 1908. From 1917 he was headmaster of a bible school in Bergen. He published several books, including the song book Sions sange from 1901. A Pietist, he regarded activities such as dancing and cinema as unfavourable ways of socializing.
