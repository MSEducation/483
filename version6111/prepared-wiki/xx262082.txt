[[Harry Sinkgraven]]

Harm "Harry" Sinkgraven (born 21 January 1966) is a Dutch association football coach and former professional player.
He managed Canadian soccer team FC Edmonton from December 2010 until he was released by the club in September 2012.

==Career==

===Playing career===

Born in Assen, Sinkgraven played for FC Groningen, SC Cambuur and FC Zwolle, making over 300 league career appearances.[tpl]nl icon[/tpl] Voetbal International profile

===Coaching career===

Sinkgraven spent a number of years as Assistant Manager at FC Zwolle, before taking charge of the Indonesian Olympic team in 2006. Sinkgraven left a year later, returning to the Netherlands to coach at the women's football club of SC Heerenveen, before managing FC Emmen for a year.
Sinkgraven took charge of Canadian soccer team FC Edmonton in December 2010 until he was released by the club in September 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fcedmonton.com/news/2012-09-28/Club%20Cuts%20Ties%20with%20Coaching%20Staff|title=Club Cuts Ties with Coaching Staff|date=28 September 2012|author=Andreas Morse|publisher=FC Edmonton official website[/tpl]
