[[Trimeresurus gracilis]]

CATEGORIES: Animals described in 1920, Endemic fauna of Taiwan, Reptiles of Taiwan, Trimeresurus

Trimeresurus gracilis, commonly known as the Taiwan pit viper, is a venomous pit viper species endemic to Taiwan. No subspecies are currently recognized.[tpl]ITIS |id=634914 |taxon=Trimeresurus gracilis |accessdate=28 July 2008[/tpl]

==Description==

Trimeresurus gracilis is a small snake with a total length up to [tpl]convert|60|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|authors=Hans Breuer & William Christopher Murphy|date=2009–2010|url= http://www.snakesoftaiwan.com/Trimeresurus%20gracilis/species_trimeresurus_gracilis.htm|title=Trimeresurus gracilis|work=Snakes of Taiwan|accessdate=9 October 2012[/tpl] Scalation includes 19 (or 21) rows of dorsal scales at midbody, 144-149 ventral scales, 43-53 subcaudal scales, and 7-8 supralabial scales.

==Geographic range==

It is found only in Taiwan, above [tpl]convert|2,000|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] above sea level. The type locality given is "Mt. Noko, Nanto". According to Zhao and Adler (1993), this would be "Mt. Nôkô, Nanto Co., central Formosa" (Mount Nengkaoshan, Nantou County, Taiwan).
