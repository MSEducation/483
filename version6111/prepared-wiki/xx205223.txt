==Partition of India and after==

The temple became a refugee camp in 1948.[tpl]cite web | url= http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/mag/2004/03/14/stories/2004031400190700.htm | title= City on the edge[/tpl] The original images of Lord Swaminarayan were removed and taken to India during the turbulent times of partition. One murti that was originally at this temple is now located in Khan Village, Rajasthan. People who wished to settle in India from all over Sindh (other than Muslims) awaited their departure to India by ship at this temple. The temple was visited by Muhammad Ali Jinnah, the founder of Pakistan during this period.[tpl]cite book | author= Vazira Fazila-Yacoobali Zamindar | title=The long partition and the making of modern South Asia | publisher=Columbia University Press | location= | year=2007 | url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=EfhqQLr96VgC&dq=&client=firefox-a&source=gbs_summary_s&cad=0 |oclc= |doi= |accessdate=May 22, 2009[/tpl] Page 52 In 1989, for the first time since the independence in 1947, a group of sadhus from the Shri Swaminarayan Mandir, Ahmedabad visited the temple. Since then, small groups from the Ahmedabad temple pay this temple a visit every few years in a pilgrimage.

==Festivals and events==

According to the Pakistan Hindu Council, Swaminarayan Jayanti, Ram Navami, Janmastami, Dussehra, Diwali and almost all of the main religious festivals are celebrated by Hindus in this temple.[tpl]cite web | url=http://pakistanhinducouncil.org/temples.asp | title= Temples in Pakistan: Swami Narayan Temple Opposite Kmc M.A Jinnah Road Karachi.[/tpl] Holi is celebrated with the holi bonfire lit at the centre of the temple grounds, followed by the play with colours. Janmashtami is celebrated with singing bhajans and sermons on Krishna, whiles on Diwali, devotees light lamps and candles to welcome Rama from his fourteen-year exile, at the end of which he defeated Ravana and young men burst crackers at the temple on the occasion.[tpl]cite news|title=Pak Hindu community celebrates Holi in Karachi |url=http://newsagency.thecheers.org/World-news/news_16983_Pak-Hindu-community-celebrates-Holi-in-Karachi.html|publisher=The Cheers Magazine|date= 2008-03-23|accessdate=2009-12-19[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=KARACHI: Janamashtami festival celebrated |url=http://www.dawn.com/wps/wcm/connect/dawn-content-library/dawn/the-newspaper/local/karachi-janamashtami-festival-celebrated-589 |publisher=Dawn|date= 2009-08-15|accessdate=2009-12-19[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Karachi: A place for all souls |url=http://www.globalpost.com/dispatch/pakistan/091105/diwali-all-souls-karachi-pakistan-partition|publisher=globalpost.com|date= 2009-11-07|accessdate=2009-12-19[/tpl] The Holi festival celebrations that take place at this temple are the biggest in Karachi.[tpl]cite news|title=Hindus in Pakistan set to celebrate Holi|url=http://www.gulf-times.com/site/topics/article.asp?cu_no=2&item_no=423075&version=1&template_id=41&parent_id=23|accessdate=21 March 2011|newspaper=Gulf Times|date=20 March 2011[/tpl]
The temple also doubles up as a marriage venue. In 2008, a mass wedding arrangement was made for 20 poor couples.[tpl]cite news|title=Swami Narayan Temple – Eternal bliss |url=http://www.dailytimes.com.pk/default.asp?page=2008\11\02\story_2-11-2008_pg12_10|publisher=Daily Times|date= 2008-11-02|accessdate=2009-12-19[/tpl]

==Guru Nanak Temple==

According to the Pakistan Hindu Council, there is a Guru Nanak temple within the Swaminarayan Temple complex. Here, every Moon Night and for the birthday of Guru Nanak, Baisakhi is celebrated.

==Gurdwara==

In the Swaminarayan Mandir complex in Karachi, Sindh, Pakistan where a small Hindu community lives, a Gurdwara has been created for the small Sikh community.
The Gurdwara Sahib houses three sets of Sri Guru Granth Sahib Ji in the Palki Sahib.  There are pictures of the Gurus and a small shrine devoted to Guru Nanak Dev Ji.  There is a Hindu bell in the Gurdwara Sahib as well.
The whole compound is protected by a security guard to protect the small number of Hindu families.
