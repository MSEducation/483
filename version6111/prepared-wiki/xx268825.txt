[[Melanie Sanford]]

CATEGORIES: University of Michigan faculty, Yale University alumni, California Institute of Technology alumni, 1975 births, People from Providence, Rhode Island, American chemists, MacArthur Fellows, Living people

Melanie Sanford (born June 16, 1975) is an American chemist, who currently works at the University of Michigan, where she holds the positions of Moses Gomberg Collegiate Professor of Chemistry and Arthur F. Thurnau Professor of Chemistry.https://www.chem.lsa.umich.edu/chem/faculty/facultyDetail.php?Uniqname=mssanfor Sanford is best known for her studies of high-valent organopalladium species, particularly those implicated in Pd-catalyzed C–H functionalization reactions.[tpl]cite journal|last=Dick|first=Allison R.|coauthors=Hull, Kami L.; Sanford, Melanie S.|title=A Highly Selective Catalytic Method for the Oxidative Functionalization of C–H Bonds|journal=Journal of the American Chemical Society|year=2004|volume=126|issue=8|pages=2300–2301|doi=10.1021/ja031543m[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|last=Lyons|first=Thomas W.|coauthors=Sanford, Melanie S.|title=Palladium-Catalyzed Ligand-Directed C–H Functionalization Reactions|journal=Chemical Reviews|year=2010|volume=110|issue=2|pages=1147–1169|doi=10.1021/cr900184e[/tpl] She has received numerous awards and honors, including a 2011 MacArthur Fellowshiphttp://www.macfound.org/site/c.lkLXJ8MQKrH/b.7730827/k.8FBB/Melanie_Sanford.htm and the 2013 Sackler Prize in Chemistry.[tpl]cite web|title=The Raymond and Beverly Sackler International Prize in the Physical Sciences|url=http://english.tau.ac.il/sackler_prize_in_chemistry|accessdate=June 25, 2013[/tpl]  She is also a Fellow for the American Association for the Advancement of Science. 

==Life==

Sanford was born and grew up in Providence, Rhode Island. She attended Classical High School. 
She graduated from Yale University with a BS and MS in 1996, having carried out research with Robert H. Crabtree, and from the California Institute of Technology with a Ph.D. in 2001, where she studied with Robert H. Grubbs. She did postdoctoral work at Princeton University, where she studied with John T. Groves.[tpl]cite news| url=http://sciencecareers.sciencemag.org/career_magazine/previous_issues/articles/2006_11_24/noDOI.1162095531482324995| title= Melanie Sanford: The Interview | author= Jim Austin| work=Science| date=November 24, 2006[/tpl]
