[[Dongfanghong program]]

CATEGORIES: Space program of the People's Republic of China, Satellites of China

Dongfanghong ([tpl]zh|s=东方红|p=Dōngfāng Hóng|l=The East is Red[/tpl]) was a space satellite program for the People's Republic of China. This program started in August 1965 as Project 651, a less ambitious successor of the earlier Project 581, set goal to launch a satellite that is heavier than Sputnik 1 and Explorer 1 into space, and developed the necessary technologies to do so. ==History==

In 1958, Chinese Academy of Science proposed Project 581 which included a plan to launch a satellite into space before 1 October 1959. The project was troubled due to the lack of knowledge on rocketry. On 21 January 1959, [tpl]link-zh|Zhang Jinfu|张劲夫[/tpl], who was in charge in the satellite research, postponed the research, allowing effort to be put on developing basic technologies. In December 1964, the during 3rd National People's Congress, Zhao Jiuzhang suggested to resume the work on satellites. On August 1968, the Central Special Committee approved Chinese Academy of Science's plan, which later became Project 651.  
In June 1965, Central Special Committee made the decision of researching a launch vehicle. As per the request of Commission for Science, Technology and Industry for National Defense (COSTIND), the vehicle's first stage and second stage would be based on a long range missile. A solid-fueled third stage was added to the design. [tpl]cite news | title=长征一号的研制历程 | work=People.cn | date=9 November 2007 | accessdate=25 February 2014 | first1=Guangyuan | last1=Xie | editor-first=Wenjun | editor-last=Zhang | trans_title=The development of Long March-1[/tpl]
In 24 April 1970, Dong Fang Hong 1 was launched. After reaching orbit, it transmitted  The East is Red for 28 days.
