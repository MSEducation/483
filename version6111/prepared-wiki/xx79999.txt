[[Świecino]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Puck County

Świecino [tpl]IPAc-pl|ś|f|j|e|'|ć|i|n|o[/tpl] (Kashubian: Swiecëno, German: Schwetzin) is a village in the administrative district of Gmina Krokowa, within Puck County, Pomeranian Voivodeship, in northern Poland.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.stat.gov.pl/broker/access/prefile/listPreFiles.jspa |title=Central Statistical Office (GUS) - TERYT (National Register of Territorial Land Apportionment Journal) |date=2008-06-01 |language=Polish[/tpl] It lies approximately [tpl]convert|6|km|mi|0[/tpl] south of Krokowa, [tpl]convert|16|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] west of Puck, and [tpl]convert|51|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] north-west of the regional capital Gdańsk.
Świecino is famous for a battle fought in 1462 between the Polish forces and the Teutonic Knights. See Battle of Świecino.
For more details of the history of the region, see History of Pomerania.
The village has a population of 203.
