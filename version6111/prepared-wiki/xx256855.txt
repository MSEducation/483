[[Immaculate Conception Church (Manhattan)]]

CATEGORIES: Roman Catholic churches completed in 1858, 19th-century Roman Catholic church buildings in the United States, Religious organizations established in 1855, Roman Catholic churches in New York, Churches in Manhattan, Eggers & Higgins buildings, East Village, Manhattan, 1855 establishments in New York

The Church of the Immaculate Conception is a Roman Catholic parish church in the Roman Catholic Archdiocese of New York, located at 414 East 14th Street, near First Avenue, Manhattan, New York City.The World Almanac 1892 and Book of Facts (New York: Press Publishing, 1892), p.390.

==History==

The parish was established in 1855.Remigius Lafort, S.T.D., Censor, The Catholic Church in the United States of America: Undertaken to Celebrate the Golden Jubilee of His Holiness, Pope Pius X. Volume 3: The Province of Baltimore and the Province of New York, Section 1: Comprising the Archdiocese of New York and the Diocese of Brooklyn, Buffalo and Ogdensburg Together with some Supplementary Articles on Religious Communities of Women.. (New York City: The Catholic Editing Company, 1914), p.336. A four-storey brick convent and parochial school at 415-419 East 13th Street and 414-416 East 14th Street was built in 1945 to designs by the eminent architectural firm of Eggers & Higgins of 542 Fifth Avenue for $700,000 There is another church of the same name on Staten Island established 1887.Remigius Lafort, S.T.D., Censor, The Catholic Church in the United States of America: Undertaken to Celebrate the Golden Jubilee of His Holiness, Pope Pius X.
