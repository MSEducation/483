[[Ernakulam district]]

CATEGORIES: Districts of Kerala, Ernakulam district, 1958 establishments in India

Ernakulam (also Kochi, Cochin; [tpl]lang-ml|എറണാകുളം[/tpl]) is a revenue district of Kerala situated in the central part of that state, southern India. Spanning an area of about 3,068 km2, Ernakulam district is home to over 12% of Kerala’s population. Ernakulam is known as the commercial capital of Kerala. The district is famous for its ancient temples, churches, and mosques. The district includes the largest metropolitan region of the state Greater Cochin. Ernakulam District is the highest revenue yielding district[tpl]Cite web|publisher=Govt of Kerala|url=http://www.kerala.gov.in/dept_planning/er/chapter3.pdf|title=District-wise Income[/tpl]2 in the state. It is the third most populous district in Kerala, after Malappuram and Thiruvananthapuram (out of 14).[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.census2011.co.in/census/district/278-ernakulam.html | title = District Census 2011 | accessdate = 30 September 2011 | year = 2011 | publisher = Census2011.co.in[/tpl]
Ernakulam district also hosts the highest number of international and domestic tourists in Kerala state.
Its headquarters is located at Kakkanad, a suburb of Kochi city. The language people speak in Ernakulam is Malayalam which is the mother-tongue of the State of Kerala. English is widely used in business circles.
Ernakulam became India's first 'fully literate district' in 1990 and the country's first district having 100 percent banking or full 'meaningful financial inclusion' in 2012.[tpl]cite news|title=Ernakulam to be declared first district with 100% banking|url=http://www.thehindubusinessline.com/industry-and-economy/banking/ernakulam-to-be-declared-first-district-with-100-banking/article4098347.ece|accessdate=27 February 2013|newspaper=The Hindu Businessline|date=15 November 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Soundarapandian|first=Mookkiah|title=Literacy Campaign in India|year=2000|publisher=Discovery Publishing House|location=New Delhi|page=21|url=http://books.google.co.in/books?id=KVhHEC449yoC&pg=PA21&lpg=PA21&dq=ernakulam+district+first+fully+literate+district&source=bl&ots=UY3vEmis-l&sig=TndRS_hESMi7lqOVRzpGB_HphOY&hl=en&sa=X&ei=Ot0tUeqmHsPprQfWyIGwAQ&ved=0CDwQ6AEwAw#v=onepage&q=ernakulam%20district%20first%20fully%20literate%20district&f=false[/tpl]

==Etymology==

The name Ernakulam is derived from 'Erayanarkulam' which means the abode of Lord Shiva.[tpl]cite web|title=A STUDY ON COMMUNITY TOURISM AND ITS IMPACT IN KERALA WITH SPECIAL REFERENCE TO ERNAKULAM DISTRICT|url=http://www.exclusivemba.com/jems/Images/ast.pdf|accessdate=27 February 2013[/tpl] Ernakulam was also known as "Rishinagakulam" in ancient days.

==History==

From ancient times Ernakulam District has played a part in the political history of south India. The Jews, Syrians, Arabs, Chinese, Dutch, British and Portuguese seafarers followed the sea route to Kingdom of Cochin and left their impressions in the town.
The Maharaja of Cochin, in 1896 initiated local administration by forming town council in Ernakulam. Initially Ernakulam District headquarters was at Ernakulam, which gave the district its name; the headquarters was later shifted to Kakkanad.

==Demographics==

According to the 2011 census Ernakulam district has a population of 32,82,388, roughly equal to the nation of Mauritania or the US state of Iowa.[ref]
