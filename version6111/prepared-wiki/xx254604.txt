[[Diapason (magazine)]]

CATEGORIES: Publications established in 1952, French magazines, Classical music magazines, Media in Paris, Media in Angers

Diapason (literally "through all notes") is a monthly magazine published in French by Italian media group, Mondadori. It focuses on classical music, especially classical music recordings and hi-fi.(2009) 2009 Naxos Catalog reviews and ratings Naxos Records The magazine was created by Georges Chérière in Angers, France[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] Dec. 18, 2008 Classica et Le Monde de la Musique s’allient ! Qobuz[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] (Apr. 1, 2006) Media(s) ResMusica under the title Diapason donne le ton dans l'Ouest and the first issue was published in Paris, 1956.[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] Yves Riesel (July 9, 2009) Les souvenirs de Michel Bernstein (II), Qobuz
The critics of Diapason review internationally released classical CDs and DVDs each month, and the best ten albums are awarded by the prestigious Diapason d'Or. The award is comparable with those given by the BBC Music Magazine and Gramophone.[tpl]es icon[/tpl] (Dec. 15, 2009) Diapason d’Or para el cd del Cuarteto Diotima con música de Posadas docenotas.com(March 6, 2005) Local son soars in Europe The Star online[tpl]ko icon[/tpl] Jeon, Yeong-seon (전영선) (Sep. 17, 2002) 피아니스트 임동혁군 佛 ‘황금 디아파종상’ 수상 (in Korean) Munhwa Ilbo
Diapason provides information online via two websites.
The principal French language alternative to Diapason was Le Monde de la Musique, but that magazine ceased publication in 2009. Much of its readership then transferred to Diapason, increasing the circulation there.[tpl]fr icon[/tpl] "Actualité : La mort du Monde de la musique", Diapason, March 25, 2009. Retrieved 2012-03-19. That magazine's highly regarded equivalent to the Diapason d'Or was the Choc du Monde de la Musique, often spelled CHOC.
