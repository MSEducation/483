[[Umbro Galway Cup]]

CATEGORIES: Youth association football, Sport in County Galway, Association football cup competitions in the Republic of Ireland

The Umbro Galway Cup is a National Youth Tournament which is held every year in Galway, Ireland. The tournament takes place in Salthill Devon's Drom Clubhouse venue.

==History==

The competition began in the summer of 2005 in a bid to bring the international football scene to Ireland. Over 40 teams representing 7 nations participated in the inaugural tournament. Academy teams of such football clubs as Celtic F.C., Preston North End F.C., Shelbourne F.C., Fulham F.C., Sheffield United F.C., Newcastle United F.C., Leeds United F.C., Aberdeen F.C., Leicester City F.C., Ipswich Town F.C., Derry City F.C., Glentoran F.C., Belvedere FC, MŠK Žilina, Cherry Orchard F.C. and also Football Association of Ireland youth selection  have competed. The tournament includes age groups from Under-11 to Under-17 and there is a group for a girls' Under-15 tournament. Now the youth competition has gone from strength to strength and is recognized[tpl]by whom|date=November 2010[/tpl] as one of the best youth tournaments in Europe. The tournament highlights are broadcast on Setanta Sports.
