[[Mayim Bialik]]

Mayim Hoya Bialik ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|m|aɪ|əm|_| b|i|ˈ|ɑː|l|ɨ|k[/tpl], [tpl]respell|MY|im[/tpl] [tpl]respell|bee|AH|lik[/tpl]; born December 12, 1975) is an American actress and neuroscientist. From early January 1991 to May 1995, she played the title character of NBC's Blossom. Since May 2010, she has played Dr. Amy Farrah Fowler on CBS's The Big Bang Theory. She was nominated twice for the Primetime Emmy Award for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series for her work on The Big Bang Theory.[tpl]cite doi|10.1038/nj7400-669a[/tpl]

==Early life and education==

Bialik was born in San Diego, California, to Barry Bialik and Beverly Winkelman.[tpl]cite book |url= http://books.google.com/books?id=pSafpwAACAAJ&dq=The+Mayim+Bialik+Handbook:+Everything+You+Need+to+Know+About+Mayim+Bialik&hl=en&sa=X&ei=aAw4U5THIof7rAGxloH4DA&ved=0CDEQ6AEwAQ |last=Smith|first=Emily|title=The Mayim Bialik Handbook: Everything You Need to Know About Mayim Bialik|year=2011|publisher=Tebbo|isbn=9781743334454 |deadurl=no |accessdate=March 30, 2014[/tpl] Her grandparents emigrated from Poland, Czechoslovakia, and Hungary. Israel's national poet Hayim Nahman Bialik was Barry's patrilineal great-great-granduncle. Bialik was raised as a Reform Jew.[tpl]cite news|last=Stein|first=Jason|url=http://www.jewishtelegraph.com/prof_222.html|title=Big Bang Theory star thought she was auditioning for a game show|work=Jewish Telegraph|year=c. 2011|accessdate=April 7, 2014[/tpl]
Bialik attended Walter Reed Junior High School and graduated from North Hollywood High School in North Hollywood, California as part of the class of 1993.[tpl]cite news|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1993-02-08/entertainment/ca-1113_1_north-hollywood-high-school|newspaper=Los Angeles Times|date=February 8, 1993|accessdate=April 15, 2014|title=Television|last=Kleid|first=Beth[/tpl] At the end of Blossom, she chose to attend UCLA, although she also had been accepted to both Harvard and Yale.[tpl]cite web|last=Carter|first=Alan|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,307267,00.html|title=Blossom Flowers: Mayim Bialik Grows into Adult Roles|publisher=Ew.com|date=1993-07-16|accessdate=March 12, 2011[/tpl]Mayim Bialik; Playing the Role Of an American in Paris Michael E. Hill; The Washington Post; Sep 19, 1993; Y.07; She stated that she wanted to stay close to her parents and did not want to move to the East Coast. She earned a B.S. degree in Neuroscience, Hebrew studies, and Jewish studies,Biography - official website of Mayim Bialik and went on to obtain a doctorate in neuroscience. She took a break from studies in 2005 to return to acting.[tpl]cite web|url=http://alumni.ucla.edu/share/alumni-stories/stories/mayim-bialik.aspx|title=Alumni Stories – Notable Alumni|publisher=Uclalumni.net|accessdate=March 12, 2011[/tpl] Bialik obtained her Ph.D. degree in 2007.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mayimbialik.net/2014/02/06/45/ |title=Biography |publisher=Mayim Bialik |accessdate=January 25, 2014[/tpl] Her dissertation was an investigation of hypothalamic activity in patients with Prader–Willi syndrome.[tpl]cite web|url=http://abcnews.go.com/2020/print?id=2677187|title=Life After Child Stardom – Not by the Numbers|publisher=Abcnews.go.com|date=2006-11-24|accessdate=March 12, 2011[/tpl]Bialik, Mayim C. ==Career==

Bialik started her career as a child actress in the late 1980s. Her early roles included the horror film Pumpkinhead (1988), her first acting job,[tpl]cite web|first=Leonard|last=Pierce|title=Mayim Bialik: Random Roles|url=http://www.avclub.com/articles/mayim-bialik,23149/|work=The A.V. Club|date= 2009-01-28|accessdate=July 5, 2009[/tpl] and guest appearances on The Facts of Life and Beauty and the Beast. It was for her Beauty and the Beast work, in which she played a sewer-dwelling girl named Ellie who had about 10 lines of dialogue, that Bialik obtained her Screen Actors Guild (SAG) card."How Did You Get Your SAG-AFTRA Card?" TV Guide. January 13, 2014. p. 10. She appeared in three episodes of MacGyver as Lisa Woodman. She appeared in Beaches (1988), playing Bette Midler's character as a young girl. She also appeared in the video for the song "Liberian Girl" by Michael Jackson.
In 1990, Bialik was tied to two television pilots, Fox's Molloy and NBC's Blossom. Molloy at first produced six episodes for a tryout run, followed by the shooting of the pilot special for Blossom. The latter actually aired two weeks before Bialik's Fox series and ultimately garnered higher ratings than it. When Molloy folded after its six episodes, Blossom was pursued as a regular series, airing until 1995. She had a major part in the Woody Allen comedy film "Don't Drink the Water" in 1994.
Between 1995 and 2005, Bialik mostly did voice-over work for cartoons, such as The Real Adventures of Jonny Quest and Recess.
She appeared in the feature film Kalamazoo? (2005) and appeared in three episodes of the HBO comedy series Curb Your Enthusiasm as Jodi Funkhouser, the lesbian daughter of a friend of Larry David. Bialik also made guest appearances as a fictionalized version of herself in the series Fat Actress, and made a guest appearance in an episode of Saving Grace.[tpl]cite web|url= http://celebrity-babies.com/2009/05/28/mayim-bialik-learns-what-not-to-wear/|title=Mayim Bialik Learns What Not to Wear|publisher=Celebrity-babies.com|date=2010-11-15| accessdate=March 12, 2011[/tpl] She had a recurring role as the high school guidance counselor in ABC Family's The Secret Life of the American Teenager.
In 2009, Bialik was nominated by Clinton Kelly for a makeover on TLC's What Not To Wear.http://celebritybabies.people.com/2009/05/28/mayim-bialik-learns-what-not-to-wear/, additional text.
She joined the cast of The Big Bang Theory as Dr. Amy Farrah Fowler. Her first appearance was in the season 3 finale as a potential love interest for the character of Sheldon Cooper (Jim Parsons). In Season 4, she began as a recurring character playing Sheldon's "friend that's a girl, but not a girlfriend." Beginning with Season 4, Episode 8, she became part of the main cast.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.deadline.com/2010/11/bang-theory-ups-mayim-bialik-to-regular/|title='Bang Theory' ups Mayim Bialik to Regular|publisher=deadline.com|accessdate=January 30, 2011 |first=Michael|last=Ausiello|date= 2010-11-15[/tpl] Amy is a neurobiologist, or neuroscientist, which corresponds to Bialik's real-life educational pursuits. Bialik's performance in The Big Bang Theory earned her Emmy Award nominations in 2012 and 2013 for Outstanding Supporting Actress in a Comedy Series. Bialik was one of seven actresses who had a "quirky" personality to audition for the role.[tpl]cite web|last=Perricone |first=Kathleen |url=http://omg.yahoo.com/blogs/goddess/why-mayim-bialik-returned-tv-getting-her-phd-004434573.html |title=Why Mayim Bialik returned to TV after getting her PhD | Goddess: Celebrity Moms and Dads - Yahoo Celebrity |publisher=Omg.yahoo.com |date=2012-08-28 |accessdate=January 25, 2014[/tpl]
Prior to her joining the cast, in the episode "The Bat Jar Conjecture", Raj suggests recruiting the real-life Bialik to their Physics Bowl team, saying "You know who's apparently very smart, is the girl who played TV's Blossom. She got a Ph.D. in Neuroscience or something."  She is cast as one of the guest stars on the Steve Carell 2014 improvisational sketch show Riot.[tpl]cite news|last=Kroeger|first=Jake|title=Fox Improv Show RIOT Will Feature Steve Carell, Jason Alexander, Cheryl Hines|url=http://www.nerdist.com/2014/04/fox-improv-show-riot-will-feature-steve-carell-jason-alexander-cheryl-hines/|accessdate=April 10, 2014|newspaper=Nerdist|date=April 8, 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Geddes|first=Robin|title=Steve Carell, Big Bang Theory's Mayim Bialik for new Fox series Riot |url=http://www.digitalspy.com/tv/news/a563315/steve-carell-big-bang-theorys-mayim-bialik-for-new-fox-series-riot.html#~oB0UrmhK1Bl25x|accessdate=April 10, 2014|newspaper=Digital Spy|date=April 8, 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Swift|first=Andy|title=Exclusive: Steve Carell and Cheryl Hines Among Celebrity Guests in Fox Improv Series Riot|url=http://tvline.com/2014/04/08/steve-carell-riot-fox-improv-series-full-cast/|accessdate=April 10, 2014|newspaper=TV Line|date=April 8, 2014[/tpl]
