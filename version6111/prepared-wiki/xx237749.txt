General Sir Horatius Murray GCB KBE DSO (1903â€“1989) was a British Army General during World War II.

==Military career==

Horatius Murray was commissioned into the Cameronians as a 2nd lieutenant in 1923.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=32858|supp=|startpage=5911|date=31 August 1923|accessdate=18 October 2009[/tpl] He was promoted to lieutenant in 1925.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=33080| date=1 September 1925| startpage=5767| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] In 1935 he was transferred to the Cameron Highlanders and advanced to captain.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=34181| date=19 July 1935| startpage=4683 | accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] He attended Staff College, Camberley for two years from January 1936.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=34247| date=21 January 1936| startpage=459| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] After Staff College he was given a staff posting at the War Office[tpl]London Gazette| issue=34513| date=24 May 1938| startpage=3351| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] and was promoted to major in August 1940.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=35167| date=16 May 1941| startpage=2872 | accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl]
He served in World War II being appointed Commander of 1st Battalion Gordon Highlanders in 1941. In June 1942 the battalion, forming part of 153rd Infantry Brigade (in turn part of 51st (Highland) Infantry Division), was shipped to Egypt where his unit took part in the Second battle of El Alamein.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=314[/tpl] Murray was seriously wounded in the early stages of the battle and only returned to active service again in April 1943. After a brief period as temporary GSO1 of 51st Division, Murray was given command of 153rd Infantry Brigade in the same division. After a period of rest and refit in Algeria the brigade saw action in Sicily.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=314[/tpl] after which it was shipped with the rest of the division in November 1943 to England for training and preparation for the Allied Invasion of Normandy.
Landing in Normandy on the afternoon of D-Day Murray saw nearly constant action with his brigade until August when he was ordered to Italy to take command of 6th Armoured Division.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=315[/tpl] The Division was involved in the fighting on the Gothic Line in late 1944 before being withdrawn into reserve and then joining V Corps for the Spring 1945 offensive in Italy.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=315[/tpl] Following the breaking of the Axis defences in the Argenta Gap by 56th and 78th Infantry Divisions, 6th Armoured Division was released to exploit across country.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=316[/tpl] Advancing north-west to the River Po, the division linked up with units of the U.S. Fifth Army advancing from the south to cut off Axis forces in Bologna. By 8 May the division was crossing the Austrian frontier becoming the first element of the Eighth Army to enter German territory.[tpl]sfn|Mead|2007|p=316[/tpl] He was mentioned in despatches in 1945 for his services in Italy.
At the end of the war, because of his relative youth, although he held an appointment as acting major-general, his permanent rank was still only major (war substantive lieutenant-colonel, temporary brigadier). In August 1945 he was advanced to temporary major-general, war substantive colonel.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=37239| date=24 August 1945| startpage=4319| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] His substantive rank was advanced to full colonel in December 1946[tpl]London Gazette| issue=37906| date=14 March 1947| startpage=1251| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] and again to major-general in January 1948.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=38197| date=3 February 1948| startpage=889| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl]
After the War Murray was appointed Director of Personal Services in 1946, General Officer Commanding 1st Infantry Division in 1947 and District Officer Commanding Northumbrian District and 50th Infantry Division (T.A.) in 1951.Generals.dk In 1949 he was mentioned in despatches for services in Palestine between March and September 1947.
Relinquishing command of 50th Division and the Nothumdrian District in August 1953,[tpl]London Gazette| issue=39950| date=28 August 1953 | startpage=4689 | supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] Murray saw action again as General Officer Commanding the 1st Commonwealth Division in Korea[tpl]London Gazette| issue=40006| date=3 November 1953| startpage=5903| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] relinquishing the appointment in November 1954.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=40389| date=21 January 1955 | startpage=493| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] In 1955 he was appointed General Officer Commanding-in-Chief of Scottish Command in the temporary rank of lieutenant-general[tpl]London Gazette| issue=40422| date=1 March 1955| startpage=1315| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] and Governor of Edinburgh Castle.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=40421| date=1 March 1955| startpage=1270| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] The lieutenant-general's rank was made substantive shortly afterwards, in May.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=40472| date=6 May 1955 | startpage=2691| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl]
In 1958 Murray became Commander in Chief Allied Forces Northern Europe which post he relinquished in July 1961[tpl]London Gazette| issue=42402| date=30 June 1961| startpage=4923| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] having been promoted to full general in 1959.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=41863| date=6 November 1959 | startpage=7077| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] He retired from the army in September 1961.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=42453| date=1 September 1961| startpage=6485| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl] He maintained his links with the army retaining the honorary Colonelship of The Cameronians (Scottish Rifles) until 1964.[tpl]London Gazette| issue=43283| date=127 March 1964| startpage=2836| supp=y| accessdate=12 May 2012[/tpl]
