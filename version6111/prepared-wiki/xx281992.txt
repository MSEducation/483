[[Leningradskaya (rural locality)]]

CATEGORIES: Rural localities in Krasnodar Krai, Kuban Oblast

Leningradskaya ([tpl]lang-ru|Ленингра́дская[/tpl]) is a rural locality (a stanitsa) and the administrative center of Leningradsky District in Krasnodar Krai, Russia. Population: [tpl]ru-census|p2010=36,940|p2002=38,218|p1989=34,554[/tpl]

==History==

Founded in 1794 as Umanskoye ([tpl]lang|ru|Уманское[/tpl]),Азаренкова А. С., Бондарь И. Ю., Вертышева Н. С. Основные административно-территориальные преобразования на Кубани (1793-1985 гг.). — Краснодар: Краснодарское книжное издательство, 1986. — С. 255. — 395 с. it was one of the first forty settlements by the Black Sea Cossacks in the Kuban region. It became a stanitsa in 1842.
Umanskoye survived the fall of 1932 and the winter of 1933, when hundreds of residents starved to death. In 1934, all the surviving population (1,200 families) was evicted in the northern regions of the Soviet Union and to Kazakhstan. In their stead, the stanitsa was repopulated by families from the Belarusian and Leningrad military districts[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] and its name was changed to Leningradskaya.
