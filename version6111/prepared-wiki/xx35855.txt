[[Sandringham, Norfolk]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Norfolk, King's Lynn and West Norfolk, Civil parishes in Norfolk, Post towns in the PE postcode area

Sandringham is a village and civil parish in the north of the English county of Norfolk. The village is situated some [tpl]convert|2|km[/tpl] south of the village of Dersingham, [tpl]convert|12|km|abbr=on[/tpl] north of the town of King's Lynn and [tpl]convert|60|km|abbr=on[/tpl] north-west of the city of Norwich.Ordnance Survey (2002). OS Explorer Map 250 - Norfolk Coast West. ISBN 0-319-21886-4.
The civil parish extends westwards from Sandringham itself to the shore of The Wash some [tpl]convert|6|km|abbr=on[/tpl] distant, and also includes the villages of West Newton and Wolferton. It has an area of [tpl]convert|41.91|km2|abbr=on[/tpl] and in 2001 had a population of 402 in 176 households. For the purposes of local government, the parish falls within the district of King's Lynn and West Norfolk.Office for National Statistics & Norfolk County Council, 2001. Census population and household counts for unparished urban areas and all parishes. Retrieved December 2, 2005.
Sandringham is best known as the location of Sandringham House and its associated estate. This is a favoured holiday home of Queen Elizabeth II and several of her predecessors. Near to Sandringham house is the Royal Stud, a stud farm that houses many of the royal horses. The village was also the birthplace of Lady Diana Spencer.
