[[Barbara J. Fields]]

CATEGORIES: Columbia University faculty, Harvard University alumni, Historians of the United States, Living people, Northwestern University faculty, University of Michigan faculty, University of Mississippi faculty, Winners of the Lincoln Prize, Yale University alumni, Year of birth missing (living people), MacArthur Fellows, People from Charleston, South Carolina, Writers from Washington, D.C., Women historians

Barbara Jeanne Fields (born Charleston, South Carolina) is a professor of American history at Columbia University.http://history.columbia.edu/faculty/Fields.html  Her focus is on the history of the American South, 19th century social history, and the transition to capitalism in the United States.

==Life==

She was raised in Washington, D.C., where she attended Morgan Elementary School, Banneker Junior High School, and Western High School.http://library.buffalo.edu/exhibits/ForeverFree/bio_fields.htm
She received her B.A. from Harvard University in 1968, and her Ph.D. from Yale University in 1978. At Yale, she was one of the last doctoral students of C. Vann Woodward, one of the preeminent American historians of the twentieth century.  She made a notable appearance in Ken Burns' documentary series, The Civil War.http://movies.nytimes.com/person/777329/Barbara-J-Fields
Fields was the first African American woman to receive tenure at Columbia University.  She has also taught at Northwestern University, the University of Michigan, and the University of Mississippi. She is currently at work on a book tentatively entitled Humane Letters: Writing in English about Human Affairs, as well as a study of slavery and emancipation in the Americas.http://library.buffalo.edu/exhibits/ForeverFree/bio_fields.htm
In May 2007, Bard College awarded Fields an honorary doctorate.
