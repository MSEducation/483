[[Yankin Township]]

CATEGORIES: Townships of Yangon

Yankin Township ([tpl]lang-my|ရန်ကင်း မြို့နယ်[/tpl], [tpl]IPA-my|jàɴɡɪ́ɴ mjo̰nɛ̀|pron[/tpl]) is located in the north-central part of Yangon. The township comprises 15 wards, and shares borders with Mayangon township in the north, Thingangyun township and South Okkalapa township in the east, the Inya Lake, Bahan township and Mayangon township in the west, and Tamwe township in the south. The township has 13 primary schools, seven middle schools and two high schools.
Yankin is a thriving commercial hub of Yangon. Yankin Centre is a leading shopping destination in Yangon, attracting shoppers from around the city. Saya San Road is the main thoroughfare that runs through most of the important places in the township. The Kokkine Swimming Pools, and the 18-hectare (44.5-acre) Myakyuntha Amusement Park[tpl]Cite news| title=Renovations Planned for Myakyuntha Park | url=http://www.news-eleven.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=5066:2010-09-25-21-25-22&catid=43:2009-11-10-07-39-09&Itemid=111 | language=Burmese | date=2010-09-25 | work=Bi-Weekly Eleven News[/tpl] are both located here. In 2008, the National Library of Myanmar moved to a new site in Yankin Township.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.mmtimes.com/no442/b005.htm|title=National Library moves to new location in Yankin township|last=Thein|first=Cherry|date=27 October 2011|work=Myanmar Times|accessdate=4 March 2012[/tpl] In 2011, a new children's hospital opened in the township, occupying the site of an old Ministry of Mining building.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.mmtimes.com/2011/news/567/news56713.html|title=Children’s hospital opens in Yankin|last=Thein|first=Cherry|date=21 March 2011|work=Myanmar Times|accessdate=4 March 2012[/tpl]
Yankin is a mix of upscale and middle class area with a multi-ethnic character. The township has a large Tamil community, and a rich collection of Hindu temples, (most of which are dedicated to Kali and Ganesh) as well as a large Chinese temple called Fushan Si Temple, believed to be the largest in Yangon.
The Mogaung Pagoda is the principal pagoda of the area, and the annual Mogaung Pagoda Festival attracts a number of people from around the city.

==Etymology==

Yankin means Free of Strife. The name was given by King Alaungpaya of Konbaung Dynasty in 1755 after he had conquered a small village named Thanlyin Kin as part of his reunification of the country.[tpl]Cite book| title=Beyond Ava | language=Burmese | author=Sein Myint, Aung Thein Han | page=64 | publisher=Gandama Publishing | location=Yangon | year=1967[/tpl]

==Landmarks==

Yankin was home to a number of government ministry headquarters before they were moved to Naypyidaw in 2006.
The Central Bank of Myanmar, the Ministry of Interior, the Ministry of Industries 1, the Ministry of Industries 2, the Ministry of Mines and the Ministry of Forestry all had their main offices here.
Yankin Apartments was part of one of the many public housing programs implemented by U Nu's government.
