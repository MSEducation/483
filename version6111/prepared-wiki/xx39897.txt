[[Province of Carbonia-Iglesias]]

CATEGORIES: Province of Carbonia-Iglesias, Provinces of Italy, States and territories established in 2001, 2001 establishments in Italy

Carbonia-Iglesias ([tpl]Lang-it|Provincia di Carbonia-Iglesias[/tpl], [tpl]Lang-sc|Provìntzia de Carbònia-Igrèsias[/tpl]) is a province in the autonomous region of Sardinia, Italy which includes the historical region of Sulcis-Iglesiente, the southwestern part of Sardinia.

==Overview==

The largest municipalities of the province are the two co-capitals, Carbonia (pop. 30,447 in 2001 census), and Iglesias (28,170). Another important comune is Sant'Antioco (11,730), located on the small island of the same name.
