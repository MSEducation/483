[[Gmina Głubczyce]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Opole Voivodeship, Głubczyce County

Gmina Głubczyce is an urban-rural gmina (administrative district) in Głubczyce County, Opole Voivodeship, in south-western Poland. Its seat is the town of Głubczyce, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|53|km|mi|0[/tpl] south of the regional capital Opole.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|294.33|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2007 its total population is 23,997 (of which the population of Głubczyce is 13,333, and the population of the rural part of the gmina is 10,664).

==Geography==

Gmina Głubczyce is located in the Głubczyce Hook ([tpl]lang-pl|worek głubczycki[/tpl]) partly on the Głubczyce Plateau ([tpl]lang-pl|Płaskowyż Głubczycki[/tpl]; a part of the Silesian Lowlands) and partly in the Opawskie Mountains (a part of the Eastern Sudeten). Gmina Głubczyce is located in the Oder River Basin (rivers: Cyna/Psina, Opawa, Opawica,  Stradunia, Troja).

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Głubczyce is bordered by the gminas of Baborów, Branice, Głogówek, Kietrz and Pawłowiczki and by the Czech obeces of Krnov, Město Albrechtice, Slezské Rudoltice, Rusín, Bohušov and Osoblaha.
