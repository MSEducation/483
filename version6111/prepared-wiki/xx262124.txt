Abdeslam Laghrissi (born 5 January 1962 in Ksar-el-Kebir) is a retired Moroccan footballer. He is also known as Abdeslam (or Abdelslam) El-Ghrissi.

==Club career==

Laghrissi started his professional career in Moroccan side FAR Rabat. Early in the nineties he played in Qatar.[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Josef Bobrowsky and Fran√ßois Mazet|title=African Nations Cup 1992|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tables/92a.html|date=23 September 2002 |accessdate=1 January 2011[/tpl] Then he returned to Morocco where he played for Raja Casablanca and, for the second time, for FAR Rabat. He spent two years in Oman where he played for Al-Suwaiq before retiring in 2000.

==International career==

He played for the Morocco national football team from 1983 to 1995. In 1984 he took part in 1984 Summer Olympics. In 1992 he was capped for the African Cup of Nations, and was a participant at the 1994 FIFA World Cup,[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Courtney, Barie|title=Morocco - Details of World Cup Matches|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesm/maro-intres-detwcq.html|date=14 July 2003 |accessdate=1 January 2011[/tpl] when he played a match as a used substitute against Saudi Arabia.

==Personal honours==

When playing for FAR Rabat, Laghrissi was the topscorer of the Moroccan premier division three times, in 1983, 1990 and 1995.[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Mubarak, Hassanin|title=Morocco - List of Topscorers|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablesm/marotops.html|date=29 May 2007 |accessdate=1 January 2011[/tpl]
