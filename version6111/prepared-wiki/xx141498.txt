[[Vågsfjorden, Troms]]

CATEGORIES: Fjords of Troms, Harstad, Dyrøy, Ibestad, Skånland, Tranøy

Vågsfjorden is a fjord in the southern part of Troms county in Norway.  The fjord is located between Norway's two largest islands, Hinnøya to the south and Senja to the north.  The fjord connects to the Andfjorden and the Tranøyfjorden to the north, and to the Astafjorden and Tjeldsundet to the south.  The smaller islands of Grytøya and Sandsøya lie on the western side of the fjord and the islands of Andørja and Rolla lie along the eastern side of the fjord.[tpl]cite web|url=http://snl.no/V%C3%A5gsfjorden/Troms|title=Vågsfjorden – Troms|author=Store norske leksikon|authorlink=Store norske leksikon|language=Norwegian|accessdate=2012-08-20[/tpl]
The [tpl]convert|46|km|adj=on[/tpl] long fjord flows through the municipalities of Harstad, Tranøy, Dyrøy, Ibestad, and Skånland. The city of Harstad, on the fjord's western shore, is popularly known as Vågsfjordens perle ([tpl]lang-en|pearl of Vågsfjorden[/tpl].
