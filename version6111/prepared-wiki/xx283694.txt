[[Alexander F. Andreev]]

CATEGORIES: Russian physicists

Alexander Fyodorovich Andreev ([tpl]lang-ru|Александр Фёдорович Андреев[/tpl], born 10th December 1939) is a Russian theoretical physicist known for explaining the eponymous Andreev reflection.[tpl]cite journal| author=Andreev, AF| title=THERMAL CONDUCTIVITY OF THE INTERMEDIATE STATE OF SUPERCONDUCTORS. II| journal=Soviet physics JETP| year=1965| volume=20| number=6| accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] http://www.kapitza.ras.ru/~andreev/
Since 1979, Andreev has been a professor at the Moscow Institute of Physics and Technology. He focuses on the physics of superconductivity, quantum liquids and solids, surface phenomena and magnetism.
He is currently the Vice-President of the Russian Academy of Sciences.
