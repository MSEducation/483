[[Joseph Allen Stein]]

CATEGORIES: 1912 births, 2001 deaths, University of Illinois alumni, Cranbrook Academy of Art alumni, Recipients of the Padma Shri, American architects, Indian architects, American alumni of the École des Beaux-Arts, American emigrants to India, Modernist architects, Architecture in the San Francisco Bay Area, People from the San Francisco Bay Area, Indian architecture writers

Joseph Stein, (April 10, 1912 – October 6, 2001) was an American architect and a major figure in the establishment of a regional modern architecture in the San Francisco Bay area in the 1940s and 1950s during the early days of the environmental design movement. In 1952 he moved to India. He is noted for designing several important buildings in India, most notably in Lodhi Estate in Central Delhi, nicknamed "Steinabad" after him, and where today the 'Joseph Stein Lane', is the only road in Delhi named after an architect.[tpl]cite news |title=An American in Delhi|url=http://www.livemint.com/2011/09/02203153/An-American-in-Delhi.html?h=B |publisher=Mint (newspaper) |date=September 2, 2011 [/tpl][ref]

==Biography==

Joseph Allen Stein was born on April 10, 1912, in Omaha, Nebraska. He studied architecture at the University of Illinois, the École nationale supérieure des Beaux-Arts in Paris and the Cranbrook Academy of Art.[ref]
