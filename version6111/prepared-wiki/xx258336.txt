[[Paul Gordon (swimmer)]]

CATEGORIES: Articles created via the Article Wizard, 1989 births, Living people, American swimmers, People from Sioux Falls, South Dakota

Paul Gordon born September 12, 1989 in Sioux Falls McKennan Hospital to Richard and Paula Gordon, Paul is a South Dakota native.

==Swimming==

===Sioux Falls Snow Fox===

As an age group swimmer, Paul competed for Sioux Falls Snow Fox swim team, a member of USA Swimming. Paul set a plethora of records at Snow Fox,http://www.teamunify.com/RecordsSwim.jsp?_tabid_=8321&team=czsdsfs many of those records being South Dakota records as well.  Paul first achieved his Olympic Trials cut in the 200 IM at the 2007 USA Swimming Junior National Championships,http://usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/c490f171-f451-494d-a26b-5ce4d9649c50/Results%20-%2007Jrs.html which is also one of his more notable South Dakota records. In addition to qualifying for the 2008 USA Swimming Olympic Trials, Paul qualified for the 2008 USA National Junior Team, which competed at the 2008 Victoria Championships in Melbourne, Australia.http://www.sdswimming.org/records/SD_Overall_LCM_Records.pdf

===University of Iowa===

Following a grueling recruiting process, Paul joined the University of Iowa Men's Swimming and Diving team in the fall of 2008.

====Freshman====

As a freshman Paul set personal bests at the Missouri Invite in the 200 IM with a 1:50.39 and 200 Freestyle with a 1:38.92.http://www.hawkeyesports.com/sports/c-swim/stats/2008-2009/mizzou_invite.html  At Big Tens Paul swam the 100 Freestyle setting a personal best of 45.46.  He also helped the 400 Freestyle Relay of Conor Dwyer, Ryan Phelan, and Duncan Partridge place fifth with a 2:55.19.http://www.hawkeyesports.com/sports/c-swim/stats/2008-2009/mb10saturdayfinals.html

====Sophomore====

As a sophomore Paul helped the Hawkeyes finish sixth at the Big Ten championship meet.http://grfx.cstv.com/photos/schools/iowa/sports/m-swim/auto_pdf/227103.pdf He swam the 500 Freestyle in 4:22.55, the 200 Freestyle in 1:36.01, and the 100 Freestyle in 43.88 and finished 12th, 5th and 6th respectively.  Paul had a strong duel meet season as a sophomore winning the 100 Butterfly and 400 Freestyle Relay against Minnesota in 48.96 and 2:59.59.http://grfx.cstv.com/photos/schools/iowa/sports/m-swim/auto_pdf/110609.pdf Although  he chopped off 2.91 seconds from his best 200 Free, Paul failed to qualify for the NCAA championship meet either individually or on a relay.

====Junior====

Paul changed the Hawkeye record books as a Junior by topping all five relays and the 100 Freestyle at the Big Ten championship meet. He led off the 800 Free Relay in a 1:34.85 to become the second fastest performer in school history.  That time also qualified him 13th for the 2011 Men's NCAA Swimming and Diving Championship Meet.http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/9b5d6b4c-a0bd-4d00-965c-efbc4c5f7597/DI%20Men%20Official%20Psych%20Sheet%20(after%20michigan%20scratch).pdf

====Senior====

Following a successful off-season in 2011, highlighted by a successful appearance at the 2011 Swedish National Championships in Halmstad, Sweden, and number of exciting time drops at 2011 USA Swimming National Championships, Paul continued to improve his senior year for the Hawkeyes. This was evident early in the season, when he posted a time of 56.88 at the Hawkeyes' annual Black and Gold intrasquad meet, which was faster even than homegrown Hawkeye hero, Zane Hugo, had posted in years previous.http://grfx.cstv.com/photos/schools/iowa/sports/m-swim/auto_pdf/2011-12/postgame_notes/postgame_notes_20111008aaa.pdfhttp://www.hawkeyesports.com/sports/m-swim/mtt/hugo_zane00.html
The 2011-12 Hawkeye Swimming and Diving season included a top 10 national ranking in a November CSCAA Poll,http://www.hawkeyesports.com/sports/m-swim/spec-rel/110911aab.html in which a strong freestyle squad played a large part. This was evident in the final two meets of Paul's senior year, as he and his teammates earned 2 Big Ten Championships and 4 all-american honors.http://www.hawkeyesports.com/sports/m-swim/archive/iowa-m-swim-2011.html

==Education==

In May, 2013, Paul graduated with a degree in Electrical Engineering from the University of Iowa.
