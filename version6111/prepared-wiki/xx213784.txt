[[Kokoona zeylanica]]

CATEGORIES: Flora of Sri Lanka, Kokoona

Kokoona zeylanica, known in Sinhala as Kokun is a species of plant in the Celastraceae family. The genus is classified in the family Hippocrateaceae by some authorities.Kokoona zeylanica in BoDD – Botanical Dermatology Database
It is endemic to Sri Lanka. ==Description==

The Kokun is a large tree with a rough grey bark. It flowers from March to May.

==Uses==

The Kokun is regarded as a medicinal plant traditionally used in a number of conditions.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bioversityinternational.com/Publications/Pdf/944.pdf |title=Inventory, documentation and status of medicinal plants research in Sri Lanka |accessdate=28 July 2008 |author= |last= Wijesundara |first= D S A |authorlink= |coauthors= |date= |year= |month= |format= |work=Medicinal Plants Research in Asia |publisher= International Plant Genetic Resources Institute|pages= |language= |doi= |archiveurl= |archivedate= |quote= [/tpl] The seed oil from this plant is effective as a leech repellent.
The inner bark has been used by jewellers as a polish.
