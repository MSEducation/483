[[Arnaudija Mosque]]

CATEGORIES: Religious buildings completed in 1594, Bosnia and Herzegovina architecture, Mosques in Bosnia and Herzegovina, Former mosques, Banja Luka, Buildings and structures in Republika Srpska, Attacks on places of worship during the Bosnian War, Destroyed mosques, Ottoman mosques in Bosnia and Herzegovina, 16th-century mosques

Arnaudija Mosque, built in 1594, was a large mosque in Banja Luka, Bosnia and Herzegovina before it was destroyed by the Army of Republika Srpska on 7 May 1993.Banja Luka u Snu - site visited 2006-12-04
The mosque was demolished by explosives on May 7, 1993, by the Serb militia. The demolition was organized by the authorities of the Republika Srpska which included the demolition of the entire Arnaudija mosque complex and Ferhadija mosque complex that were approximately [tpl]convert|800|m|ft|abbr=on[/tpl] distant from each other. Both mosques were destroyed in the same night just within the time frame of 15 minutes. 
One of the Serbs leaders from Banja Luka, Radoslav Brdjanin was convicted for his part in organizing the ethnic cleansing of the non-Serbs and destruction of the properties of Muslims, including mosques. Radoslav Brdjanin was sentenced to a single sentence of 32 years of imprisonment.
