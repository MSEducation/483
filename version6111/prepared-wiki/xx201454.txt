[[Johan Snyman]]

Johan 'Joe' Snyman (born 9 July 1986) is a South African rugby union player, currently playing with Welsh team Scarlets.[tpl]cite web | work=sarugby.net | url=http://www.sarugby.net/playerprofile.aspx?id=35608 | title=Player Profile Johan Snyman | accessdate=7 November 2011[/tpl]
He was born in Evander, but went to Outeniqua High School in George. He played for the [tpl]Rut SWD Eagles[/tpl] at the 2004 Craven Week, before moving to the [tpl]Rut Natal Sharks[/tpl] in 2005. He was also called up to the South Africa Under 19 squad for the 2005 Under 19 Rugby World Championship, which was won by South Africa.
He made 11 appearances for the [tpl]Rut Natal Sharks[/tpl] Vodacom Cup and Currie Cup sides. They then sent him to the International Rugby Academy in New Zealand, where he also had a short spell at New Zealand ITM Cup team Hawke's Bay Magpies.[tpl]cite web | work=sharksworld.co.za | url=http://www.sharksworld.co.za/2009/01/23/snyman-gains-experience-in-nz/ | title=Snyman gains experience in NZ | accessdate=23 November 2011[/tpl]
In 2009, he moved to the [tpl]Rut Golden Lions[/tpl], where he played in their Vodacom Cup and Currie Cup teams (including being part of the squad that won the 2011 Currie Cup Premier Division) and also played two games for [tpl]Rut UJ[/tpl] in the 2011 Varsity Cup.
He joined the [tpl]Rut Eastern Province Kings[/tpl] for the start of the  2012 Vodacom Cup season.
