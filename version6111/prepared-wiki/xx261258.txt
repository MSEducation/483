[[Hobart Upjohn]]

CATEGORIES: American architects, Architects from New York, Stevens Institute of Technology alumni, People from New York City, 1876 births, 1949 deaths, American ecclesiastical architects, American residential architects, Architects of Presbyterian churches, Architects of Anglican churches, NRHP architects

Hobart Upjohn (1876–1949) was an American architect, best known for designing a number of ecclesiastical and educational structures in New York and in North Carolina. He also designed a number of significant private homes.  His firm produced a total of about 150 projects, a third of which were in North Carolina.North Carolina Architects and Builders, A Biographical Dictionary: Hobart Brown Upjohn
He was born in New York City in 1876, a son of Richard M. Upjohn (1828–1903) and grandson of Richard Upjohn (1802–1878).  He received a degree in mechanical engineering from Stevens Institute of Technology in 1899.  He worked in his father's firm until 1903 and then opened his own practice in 1905.  He entered a partnership with George W. Conable (1866–1933) in 1908.  That partnership ended in 1914.  One of the works produced by the partnership was the 1909 Rye Town Park-Bathing Complex and Oakland Beach, added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2003.[tpl]NRISref|version=2009a[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=14072|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration:Rye Town Park-Bathing Complex and Oakland Beach|date=August 2002|accessdate=2010-12-30 |author=Peter d. Shaver|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl]  He closed his practice in 1945 and died in 1949.
A number of his works are listed on the National Register of Historic Places.[tpl]NRISref|version=2010a[/tpl]
