[[Zoutleeuw]]

CATEGORIES: Municipalities of Flemish Brabant

Zoutleeuw ([tpl]IPA-nl|ˌz̪ɔ̞u̯t̪ˈl̪eːʏ̯[/tpl], [tpl]lang-fr|Léau[/tpl]) is a municipality and city in the Hageland, in the extreme east of the Belgian province of Flemish Brabant. On 1 January 2006 the municipality had 7,947 inhabitants. The total area is 46.73 km², giving a population density of 170 inhabitants per km².
The name Leeuw means "hill", to which Zout ("salt") was added from the 16th century in recognition of the town's right to levy a salt tax.
