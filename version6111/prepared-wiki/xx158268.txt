[[Mark Irwin]]

CATEGORIES: 1950 births, Canadian cinematographers, Living people, People from Toronto

Mark Irwin, A.S.C., C.S.C. (born August 7, 1950) is a Canadian cinematographer. 
He was born in Toronto. He studied Political science at the University of Waterloo and Filmmaking at the York University.[tpl]cite web | url= http://www.cinematographers.nl/PaginasDoPh/irwin.htm | title=Mark Irwin | publisher=cinematographers.nl | accessdate=2012-07-05[/tpl]
He is famous for early David Cronenberg films such as Fast Company, Scanners, Videodrome, The Dead Zone, and The Fly. Irwin has also been a main collaborator for several directors such as Wes Craven, Todd Phillips and The Farrelly brothers.
