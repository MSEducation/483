[[Fried Coke]]

CATEGORIES: 2006 introductions, Doughnuts, Deep fried foods, Coca-Cola, American desserts

Fried Coke is a frozen Coca-Cola-flavored batter that is deep-fried and then topped with Coca-Cola syrup, whipped cream, cinnamon sugar, and a cherry.New way to get fat in Texas: Fried Coke, UPI, October 16, 2006. Retrieved July 16, 2007"Because we don't already have enough fried foods...," Reuters, October 27, 2006 It was introduced by inventor Abel Gonzales, Jr., a 36-year-old computer analyst from Texas, at the 2006 State Fair of Texas;"Gourmandising in Texas: Come fry with me", The Economist, October 9, 2009.Alice Laussade, "Abel Gonzales Jr: Dallas' Fried-Stuff Savior", Dallas Observer, September 2, 2011.[tpl]cite web|title=The Apex of American Ingenuity- Fried Soda|url=http://inventorspot.com/fried_soda|publisher=Inventor Spot|accessdate=17 February 2012[/tpl]  Gonzales is also the creator of recipes for deep-fried butter and deep-fried beer at later Texas State Fairs.[tpl]cite web|title=Hold on to your hearts, It's Deep-Fried Butter!|url=http://www.today.com/id/32665106|work=Today|accessdate=11 December 2013[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|last=Vine|first=Katy|title=I Believe I Can Fry|url=http://www.texasmonthly.com/story/i-believe-i-can-fry|work=Texas Monthly|accessdate=11 December 2013[/tpl] The concoction won the title of "Most Creative" in the second annual judged competition among food vendors.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.kirotv.com/foodnews/9789814/detail.html |title=Newest Fair Food: Deep-Fried Coca-Cola |publisher=KIRO-TV |date=2006-09-05[/tpl][tpl]Dead link|date=December 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite pressrelease |url=http://www.bigtex.com/newsroom/stories/2006/0918fairfood.html |title=Working on Labor Day pays off for State Fair concessionaires |publisher=State Fair of Texas |date=2006-09-18[/tpl][tpl]Dead link|date=December 2011[/tpl] It proved very popular in Texas, selling 10,000 cups in the first two weeks. ==Variations==

Since its introduction in 2006, several variations have appeared, using different types of soda and different toppings. A New York Times article published in 2007 about the Indiana State Fair mentions deep-fried Pepsi as being an innovation.[tpl]cite web|last=Monica|first=Davey|title=Yes, Deep-Fried Oreos, but Not in Trans Fats|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2007/08/21/us/21fat.html|work=The New York Times|accessdate=February 17, 2012|date=August 21, 2007[/tpl] In that particular variation, the balls were served with toothpicks and were described to taste "like a doughnut hole but with a noticeable Pepsi undertone."[tpl]cite web|title=Latest at the Fair: deep-fried Pepsi|url=http://heraldbulletin.com/homenews/x518788282/Latest-at-the-Fair-deep-fried-Pepsi|work=The Herald Bulletin|accessdate=17 February 2012[/tpl] They were served with either cinnamon sugar, powdered sugar or whipped cream.
