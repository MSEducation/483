[[Emil Bernstorff]]

CATEGORIES: 1993 births, Living people, People from Middlesex, British people of Danish descent, British people of German descent, Danish racing drivers, British racing drivers, Formula Ford drivers, ADAC Formel Masters drivers, Formula 3 Euro Series drivers, FIA European Formula 3 Championship drivers, German Formula Three Championship drivers, European F3 Open Championship drivers

Emil G. J. Bernstorff (born 7 June 1993) is a British racing driver of Danish and German descent.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emilbernstorff.com/racing/CV.html|title=INTRODUCTION|publisher=emilbernstorff.com|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

===Karting===

Born in Middlesex, Bernstorff began karting in 2003 and raced mostly in the United Kingdom for the majority of his karting career, working his way up from the junior ranks to progress through to the KF2 category by 2009, when he finished in eighth position in the Spanish KF2 championship.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/1500-2008/|title=Campeonato de Espana de Karting — KF2 2008|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]

===British Formula Ford===

2010 saw Bernstorff début in the British Formula Ford Championship championship with Jamun Racing.[tpl]cite news|title=Exciting international field gathers for Formula Ford|url=http://www.britishformulaford.co.uk/news/2010/190310.htm|work=British Formula Ford Championship|publisher=Max Communications|date=19 March 2010|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] He finished seventh in the championship with three podiums.

===ADAC Formel Masters===

In 2011, Bernstorff took part in the full ADAC Formel Masters season with Motopark Academy, taking thirteen podiums in twenty-four races, including five victories, to finish runner-up in the final championship standings.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.adac-formel-masters.de/news/index.php?SID&flash=&lg=d&st=165&id=313 | title = Timo Rumpfkeil: "Wir wollen Rennen gewinnen"|work=ADAC Formel Masters|publisher=ADAC|date=8 March 2011|accessdate=9 March 2011|language=German[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/1556-2011/|title=ADAC Formel Masters 2011|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]

===FIA European Formula 3 Championship===

Bernstorff stepped up to the FIA European Formula Three Championship in 2012, switching to ma-con Motorsport.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.autosport.com/news/report.php/id/98956|title=Emil Bernstorff joins European F3 series with ma-con Motorsport|first=Jamie|last=O'Leary|date=18 April 2012|work=Autosport|publisher=Haymarket Publications|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] He finished tenth, with a podium finish at the Norisring.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/195-2012/|title=FIA European Formula 3 Championship 2012|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]

===German Formula Three Championship===

Bernstorff moved to the German Formula Three Championship in 2013, reviving his collaboration with Motopark Academy, now running under the Lotus moniker.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.paddockscout.com/bernstorff-kirchhofer-and-markelov-complete-lotus-line-up/|title=Bernstorff, Kirchhöfer and Markelov complete Lotus line-up|last=Goddard|first=Stephen|date=7 April 2013|publisher=Paddock Scout|accessdate=14 April 2013[/tpl]
