[[Benton Township, Adair County, Missouri]]

CATEGORIES: Townships in Adair County, Missouri, Kirksville, Missouri micropolitan area, Townships in Missouri

Benton Township is one of ten townships in Adair County, Missouri, United States. As of the 2010 census, its population was 19,700.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/0600000US2900104546| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Benton township, Adair County, Missouri| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=June 19, 2013[/tpl] It is named for Thomas Hart Benton, US senator from Missouri.E.M. Violette, History of Adair County. Published 1911.

==Geography==

Benton Township covers an area of [tpl]convert|80.26|sqmi|km2[/tpl] and contains one incorporated settlement, Kirksville (the county seat). It contains twelve cemeteries: Bear Creek, Cater, Collett, Forest-Llewellyn, Highland Park, Maple Hills, Oak Grove, Ownbey, Park View Memorial Gardens, Prough, Sloans Point and Waddill.
The streams of Billy Creek, Dave Branch, Gill Branch and Willis Branch run through this township.

==Transportation==

Benton Township contains a heliport for medical ambulance Air Evac Lifeteam, and a heliport at Northeast Regional Medical Center also exists. Major highways in Benton Township include U.S. Highway 63, Missouri Highway 6, and Missouri Highway 11.

==Education==

Benton Township contains A.T. Still University, Truman State University, and the Kirksville campus of Moberly Area Community College. Primary and secondary education is provided by Kirksville R-3 School District for the majority of Benton Township residents. However, a portion in the western part attend Adair County R-1 in Novinger.
