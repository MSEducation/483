[[Samuel Simeon Fels]]

CATEGORIES: 1860 births, 1950 deaths, American philanthropists, Central High School (Philadelphia) alumni, University of Pennsylvania people, People from Caswell County, North Carolina, People from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, American business executives, American people of German-Jewish descent

Samuel Simeon Fels (born February 16, 1860 in Yanceyville, North Carolina - 1950) was an American businessman and philanthropist. 
Early in Samuel's life his family relocated to Philadelphia, where Samuel's older brother Joseph Fels founded a soap manufacturing company, Fels & Co. Samuel became the company's first president, a post which he held until his death. An active philanthropist, in 1936, Fels established the Samuel S. Fels Fund, which provides support to Philadelphia-area non-profits, even today. Fels also founded the University of Pennsylvaniaâ€™s Fels Institute of Government. Fels is known for commissioning Samuel Barber's Violin Concerto Op. 14 in 1939.
