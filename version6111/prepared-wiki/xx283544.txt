[[Thaha Syaifuddin]]

CATEGORIES: National Heroes of Indonesia

Thaha Syaifuddin (1816-1904) became Sultan of Jambi in 1855.[tpl]cite book |last1=Ricklefs |first1=Merle Calvin |author-link1=M. C. Ricklefs |year=2001 |title=A History of Modern Indonesia Since C. 1200 |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=0GrWCmZoEBMC&pg=PA182 |publisher=Stanford University Press |page=182 |isbn=9780804744805 |accessdate=1 November 2013 [/tpl] However, he refused to renew treaties imposed on his predecessors by the Dutch, who invaded Jambi in 1858 and imposed a series of sultans[tpl]cite book |last1=Kerlogue |first1=Fiona G |date= |year=2004 |origyear= |chapter=Jambi |editor1-last=Ooi |editor1-first=Keat Gin |title=Southeast Asia: A Historical Encyclopedia, from Angkor Wat to East Timor |trans_title= |url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=QKgraWbb7yoC&pg=PA678 |volume=1 |publisher=ABC-CLIO |page=678 |isbn=9781576077702 |accessdate=1 November 2013 [/tpl] who, under Dutch control, reigned over much of the sultanate until 1899. Thaha, however, continued to claim the sultanate and to rule over its less accessible parts until he was killed by Dutch soldiers in 1904. In 1977, he was elevated to National Hero of Indonesia, the country's highest honor.[ref]
