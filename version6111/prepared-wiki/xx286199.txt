[[Share a Coke]]

CATEGORIES: Promotional campaigns by Coca-Cola, 2013 works

Share a Coke is a debranding campaign during the summer of 2013 by Coca-Cola Great Britain where "Coca-Cola" is removed from the bottle, and replaced by a person's name. The campaign, which replaces the name with 250 of Britain's most popular names, is aims to have people go out and find a bottle with their name on it, then share it with their friends.[tpl]cite web|title=Share a Coke FAQs|url=http://www.coca-cola.co.uk/faq/products/share-a-coke.html|publisher=Coca-Cola Great Britain|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Fisher|first=Lucy|title=Debranding: why Coca-Cola's decision to drop its name worked|url=http://www.theguardian.com/media-network/media-network-blog/2013/aug/06/coke-debranding-name-dropping|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=The Guardian|date=6 August 2013[/tpl]
