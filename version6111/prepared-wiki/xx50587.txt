[[High Anxiety (album)]]

CATEGORIES: 2003 albums, Spitfire Records albums, Therapy? albums, Albums produced by Pete Bartlett

High Anxiety was the seventh full-length album by the band Therapy?,[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.therapyquestionmark.co.uk/discography/albums/highanxiety.php | title=Official Discography[/tpl] and was the first to be released on Spitfire Records. It was released on 5 May 2003. The album was recorded from December 2002 to January 2003 at Parkgate Studios in Hastings.
"High Anxiety" was the first album to feature drummer Neil Cooper, and the last recorded with guitarist/cellist Martin McCarrick, who departed in 2004. The album has a similar production value to Troublegum, and is a return to that albumsâ€™ sense of melodic punk.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.therapyquestionmark.co.uk/press/reviews/highanxiety/classicrock.php | title=Classic Rock review[/tpl]
The album title is taken from a 1977 Mel Brooks movie of the same name.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.therapyquestionmark.co.uk/biography/diary/read.php?diaryid=23 | title=High Anxiety diary entry[/tpl]
The US released limited edition CD-ROM included the video of If It Kills Me. The album reached number 113 in the UK Albums Chart.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.zobbel.de/cluk/CLUK_T.HTM | title=UK Top 200 Chart History[/tpl]
The album was released on CD and cassette.
Polish label Metal Mind Productions re-released the album on 2 November 2009. The album was remastered using 24-Bit technology, limited to 1000 copies, on a gold disk digipak CD.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.rockmymonkey.com/news/article/2009/10/Therapy_Re-issue_High_Anxiety_And_Never_Apologize_Never_Explain | title= High Anxiety and NANE re-issues[/tpl]
