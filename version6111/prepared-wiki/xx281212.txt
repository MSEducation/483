[[Honingham Hall]]

CATEGORIES: Country houses in Norfolk

Honingham Hall was a large country house at Honingham in Norfolk.

==History==

The house was commissioned by Sir Thomas Richardson, Chief Justice of the Kingâ€™s Bench in 1605.[tpl]cite web|url=http://honinghampc.norfolkparishes.gov.uk/category/village-history/history/|title=Honingham: A Brief History of the Village|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] After passing down the Richardson family it was bought by Richard Baylie, President of St John's College, Oxford, in about 1650[tpl]cite web|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=hiw2AAAAMAAJ&pg=PA343|title=A general history of the county of Norfolk, Volume 1|first=John|last=Chambers|authorlink=John Chambers (topographer)|page=343|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] and was then acquired by William Townsend, Member of Parliament for Great Yarmouth in about 1735, before passing down the Townsend family.[tpl]cite web|url=http://apling.freeservers.com/Villages/Honingham54.htm|title=Honingham|publisher=Francis White's History, Gazetteer, and Directory, of Norfolk 1854|page= 475-476|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] In 1887 it was inherited by Ailwyn Fellowes, 1st Baron Ailwyn and in 1924 by Ronald Fellowes, 2nd Baron Ailwyn who sold it in 1935. It was then bought by Sir Eric Teichman, a diplomat, who allowed it to become a Barnardo's home in 1940 during World War II.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goldonian.org/photos/photo_archive_homes/pages/honingham-hall.htm|title=Honingham Hall|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] Teichmann was murdered in the grounds of the hall by a poacher one night in December 1944.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,778290,00.html|title=Army & Navy - Murder at Honingham Hall|date=18 December 1944|publisher=Time|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] The house closed as a Barnardo's home December 1966 and was subsequently demolished.
