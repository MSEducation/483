[[Celso Golmayo Zúpide]]

CATEGORIES: 1820 births, 1898 deaths, Spanish chess players, Cuban chess players, Cuban people of Spanish descent

Celso Golmayo y Zúpide (24 April 1820, Logroño, Spain – 1 April 1898, Havana)[tpl]citation|last=Gaige|first=Jeremy|author-link=Jeremy Gaige|year=1987|title=Chess Personalia, A Biobibliography|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-7864-2353-6|page=143[/tpl] was a Spanish–Cuban chess master.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.valvanera.com/riojanos/golmayo1.htm |title=Celso Fidel Golmayo y Zupide - Riojanos Ilustres |publisher=Valvanera.com |date= |accessdate=2011-10-20[/tpl]
He had been generally accepted as Cuban champion since his 1862 match defeat of Félix Sicre.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chesshistory.com/winter/extra/corzo.html |title=Immortal but Unknown by Edward Winter |publisher=Chesshistory.com |date= |accessdate=2011-10-20[/tpl] He took part in the famous Paris 1867 tournament where he tied for 7–8th (Ignatz von Kolisch won).[tpl]cite web|url=http://www3.sympatico.ca/g.giffen/19thcent.htm |title=Major Chess Matches and Tournaments of the 19th century |publisher=.sympatico.ca |date= |accessdate=2011-10-20[/tpl]
In matches, he won against Paul Morphy 3 : 2 at Havana 1864 (blind simultan., Morphy gave odds of a knight);[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.edochess.ca/players/p235.html |title=Edo Historical Chess Ratings |publisher=Edochess.ca |date= |accessdate=2011-10-20[/tpl] lost to Gustav Neumann 0 : 3 in Paris in 1867; lost twice to Wilhelm Steinitz 2 : 9 in 1883 and 0 : 5 in 1888; won twice against Andrés Clemente Vázquez 7 : 0 in 1887 and 7 : 4 in 1890; lost thrice to George Henry Mackenzie 3 : 6 and 0.5 : 5.5 in 1887; and 4.5 : 7.5 in 1888; lost to Joseph Henry Blackburne 4 : 6 in 1891; and lost to Emanuel Lasker 0.5 : 2.5 in 1893, all in Havana.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chessmetrics.com |title=Welcome to the Chessmetrics site |publisher=Chessmetrics.com |date=2005-03-26 |accessdate=2011-10-20[/tpl]
Celso Golmayo y Zúpide was the father of Celso Golmayo y de la Torriente and Manuel Golmayo y de la Torriente.
