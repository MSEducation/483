[[Dean, Oxfordshire]]

CATEGORIES: Hamlets in Oxfordshire, West Oxfordshire

Dean is a hamlet in Spelsbury civil parish, about [tpl]convert|2|mi|0[/tpl] north of Charlbury and [tpl]convert|3.5|mi[/tpl] southeast of Chipping Norton, Oxfordshire. Its toponym derives from the fact that it is between the valleys of the Coldron Brook  and one of its tributaries.

==History==

The Hawk Stone, about [tpl]convert|0.5|mi|m[/tpl] north of Dean, is a Neolithic standing stone.
At a junction of two lanes in the centre of Dean is the base of a medieval preaching cross.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251483 |title=Wayside Cross Base Approximately 300 Metres South West of Dean Mill |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl]
Spelsburydown is a 17th-century house that was re-fronted in the 18th century.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251480 |title=Spelsburydown |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl] It is of coursed squared limestone with ashlar dressings and has a Stonesfield slate roof.
About [tpl]convert|1200|yd[/tpl] northeast of Spelsburydown is a six-bay barn built of coursed limestone rubble that dates from the late 17th or early 18th century.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1262797 |title=Barn Approximately 1 1/10 Kilometres North East of Spelsburydown, Dean |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl]
Dean Mill is a watermill on Coldron Brook. It was built probably in the 18th and altered probably in the 20th century.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251421 |title=Dean Mill |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl] The building is of coursed rubble with a Stonesfield slate roof.
Dean Manor House was built early in the 18th century, reputedly in 1702 for Thomas Rowney, MP for Oxford.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251419 |title=Manor House |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl] It is of coursed rubble with ashlar dressings.
Dean Manor Cottage is a small house built early in the 18th century.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251420 |title=Dean Manor Cottage |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl] It is of coursed rubble with a Stonesfield slate roof. Pear Tree Cottage is a mid-18th century small house built of coursed limestone rubble.[tpl]cite web |url= http://list.english-heritage.org.uk/resultsingle.aspx?uid=1251481 |title=Pear Tree Cottage |work=National Heritage List for England |publisher=English Heritage |accessdate=27 February 2012[/tpl]
