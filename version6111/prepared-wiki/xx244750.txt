[[New Kingston Presbyterian Church]]

CATEGORIES: Presbyterian churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, National Register of Historic Places in Delaware County, New York, Religious buildings completed in 1900, Churches in Delaware County, New York

New Kingston Presbyterian Church is a historic Presbyterian church on CR 6 in New Kingston, Delaware County, New York. It is a one story, rectangular wood frame building on a stone foundation built in 1900.  It features a steep gable roof with slate shingles and a broad raking cornice.  It also has a three stage, engaged corner tower.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=8516|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: New Kingston Presbyterian Church|date=November 2001|accessdate=2010-02-05 |author=Kathleen LaFrank|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=8533|title=Accompanying nine photos[/tpl]
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 2002.
