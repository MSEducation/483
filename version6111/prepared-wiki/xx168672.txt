[[You'd Be So Easy to Love]]

CATEGORIES: 1936 songs, Songs written by Cole Porter, Carmen McRae songs, Songs from Anything Goes

"(You'd Be So) Easy to Love" is a popular song written by Cole Porter for the 1936 film Born to Dance, where it was introduced by Eleanor Powell, James Stewart, and Frances Langford under its alternate title, Easy to Love. The song was cut from the original production of  Anything Goes in 1934 and was only later added to the 1987 and 2011 revivals of "Anything Goes" under the complete title “You’d Be So Easy to Love.”.Lincoln Center Theater, "Anything Goes", New Broadway Cast Recording (1987).
