[[Central Washington University]]

CATEGORIES: Central Washington University

Central Washington University, or CWU, is a publicly assisted university  in Ellensburg, Washington in the United States. The university's three chief divisions include the Office of the President, Business and Financial Affairs, and Academic and Student Life (ASL). Within ASL are four colleges: the College of Arts and Humanities, the College of Business (Ellensburg campus and centers in the Puget Sound and central regions),Supply Chain Management - BS & Certificate Programs the College of Education and Professional Studies, and College of the Sciences.
CWU is located about [tpl]convert|110|mi[/tpl] east of Seattle, Washington on Interstate 90 in Kittitas Valley.

==History==

In 1890, the state Legislature established the Washington State Normal School (WSNS) in Ellensburg for “the training and education of teachers in the art of instructing and governing in the public schools of this state.” [tpl]cite book|last1=Mohler|first1=Samuel M|title=The First 75 Years: A History of Central Washington State College|year=1967|publisher=Central Washington State College|location=Spokane, WA|url=http://www.lib.cwu.edu/sites/default/files/cwu_history001.pdf|pages=5–6|ref=harv[/tpl] WSNS originally opened on September 6, 1891, with classes held at the Washington Public School in Ellensburg.[tpl]Harvnb|Mohler|1967|pp=12[/tpl] In 1893, the school's first building was constructed and named Barge Hall, in honor of the first WSNS principal, Benjamin Franklin Barge. Barge Hall was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1976.
The school became Central Washington College of Education in 1937, Central Washington State College in 1961, and, finally, Central Washington University in 1977.  It continues to be one of the fastest growing universities in the State of Washington.[tpl]citation needed|date=June 2013[/tpl]

==Student Union and Recreation Center==

On April 26, 2006, the school opened a $58-million Student Union and Recreation Center. The Student Union and Recreation Center is home to a full-sized rock-climbing wall, fully equipped gymnasium, and an outdoor recreation office, which rents everything from rafts, to snowshoes and ice axes.
