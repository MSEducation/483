[[Tracy Hamlin]]

CATEGORIES: 1966 births, Living people, American female singers, Songwriters from Maryland, African-American female singers, People from Baltimore, Maryland

Tracy Yvette Hamlin (August 22, 1966) is an American singer and song writer.
After a successful stint as lead vocalist for smooth jazz group Pieces of a Dream (band), Hamlin now performs as a solo artist internationally.  Hamlin also writes lyrics and performs as a background vocalist for disco queen Gloria Gaynor.[tpl]cite web|title=Magic Baltimore|url=http://magicbaltimore.com/3342509/tracy-hamlins-united-states-premiere-and-interview/[/tpl]

==Solo career==

Hamlin released her first album, Seasons, in 2005 for independent label DMH Records. In 2009, Tracy released her second album, Better Days. The album's title song peaked at No. 1 UK Soul Charts.[tpl]cite web|title=Just Soul|url=http://www.justsoul.altervista.org/interviews/interview65.htm[/tpl]  In 2011, Tracy released a single entitled Drive Me Crazy along with DJ Spen and Quantize Recordings.  Drive Me Crazy made it to number 1 four different times during a four month period on Traxsource.[tpl]cite web|title=Traxsource|url=http://www.traxsource.com/genre/3/soul-funk-disco/[/tpl]
