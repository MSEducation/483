[[Rattan (film)]]

CATEGORIES: 1944 films, Hindi-language films, Indian films

Rattan  or Ratan is a 1944 Indian Bollywood film starring Swaran Lata, Karan Dewan and Amir Banu. It was the highest grossing Indian film of 1944 and is cited as an "All Time Blockbuster".[tpl]cite web|title=Top Earners 1944|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=149&catName=MTk0NA==|publisher=Box Office India|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl] The film is noted as the film which made Naushad one of the top contributors to music in Indian films and  enabled him to demand a fee of Rs 25000 a film.The heroine of the movie Swaran Lata had said in a live interview with the PTV that the tunes of the songs of Rattan were set by the lyricist Dinanath Madhok himself.[tpl]cite book|last=Magadi|first=Ranganathan|title=The Literary Works of Ranganathan Magadi|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=_-7KkouTtrcC&pg=PA657|accessdate=26 September 2011|date=11 February 2007|publisher=Ranganathan Magadi|isbn=978-1-4116-7004-4|page=657[/tpl]
Film expert and author Rajesh Subramanian opines that Kardar production spent rupees seventy five thousand in 1944 to make ‘Rattan’. The music by Naushadsaab was such a phenomenal hit that the company earned Rs 3 lacs as royalty from Gramophone sales in the first year.
