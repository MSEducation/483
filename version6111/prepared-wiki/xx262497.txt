[[Mount Penrose]]

CATEGORIES: Bridge River Country, Chilcotin Ranges, Mountains of British Columbia

Mount Penrose, known locally as Penrose, [tpl]convert|2634|m|ft|0|abbr=on[/tpl], is the easternmost summit of the Dickson Range, a subrange of the Chilcotin Ranges, near Gold Bridge, British Columbia, Canada and immediately above and to the west of Gun Lake.  It was named for Senator Boies Penrose, of Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, who was the first to climb it, during a hunting expedition led by famed guide W.G. "Bill" Manson.Tales from Seton Portage, Irene Edwards, self-publ. Lillooet, 1976]
