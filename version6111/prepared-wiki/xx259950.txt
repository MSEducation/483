[[Allied Subjects' Medal]]

CATEGORIES: Civil awards and decorations of the United Kingdom, Awards established in 1919, World War I

The Allied Subjects' Medal was a British decoration instituted in 1922,[tpl]cite web|title=War Office and Air Ministry: Service Medal and Award Rolls, First World War|url=http://www.nationalarchives.gov.uk/catalogue/DisplayCatalogueDetails.asp?CATID=13388&CATLN=3&FullDetails=True&j=1|publisher=The National Archives|accessdate=8 February 2011|quote=The Allied Subjects Medal was instituted in 1922[/tpl] and awarded to those who helped British and Commonwealth soldiers behind enemy lines between 1914-1918.
The medal was awarded in silver and bronze. 134 medals were awarded in silver and 574 in bronze, most of them to Belgian and French citizens.Zie 34199 Half of the recipients were female. 
In the Second World War the King's Medal for Courage in the Cause of Freedom took its place.
