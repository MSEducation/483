[[Lichfield District Council]]

CATEGORIES: Local government in Staffordshire

Lichfield District Council is a non metropolitan district council, which covers the district of Lichfield in Staffordshire, England. It was formed in 1974 from Lichfield City Council and Lichfield Rural District Council.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lichfield.gov.uk/cc-background.ihtml |title=Lichfield City Council Background |publisher=Lichfield.gov.uk |date= |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl] The council offices are located at the District Council House on Frog Lane ([tpl]Coord|52.681180|-1.827103|region:GB-STS_type:landmark|display=inline,title[/tpl]), in the centre of Lichfield.
Lichfield District Council is amongst the top performing councils nationally. In 2004 the audit commission informed the council that they were a good council and in 2007 they confirmed that they are getting better at managing their finances.[tpl]cite web|title=Performance|url=http://lichfielddc.gov.uk/performance|work=Lichfield District Council Website|accessdate=6 July 2011[/tpl]
Since 2011, Lichfield has formed part of the Greater Birmingham & Solihull Local Enterprise Partnership along with neighbouring authorities Birmingham, Bromsgrove, Cannock Chase, East Staffordshire, Redditch, Solihull, Tamworth and Wyre Forest.

==Political Control==

The council is majority Conservative controlled[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lichfielddc.gov.uk/site/scripts/council_democracy_index.php |title=Lichfield District Council - Councillors |publisher=Lichfielddc.gov.uk |date= |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl] and the leader since 2010 has been Mike Wilcox,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lichfielddc.gov.uk/site/custom_scripts/newsblog.php?id=291 |title=New Leader for Lichfield District Council | Lichfield District Council |publisher=Lichfielddc.gov.uk |date=2010-05-12 |accessdate=2011-10-15[/tpl] who succeeded David Smith.
Before May 2011, the second largest group was the Liberal Democrat and Independent Alliance set up after the 2007 elections. They were made up of Liberal Democrat and independent councillors.Councillors by Political Party: Liberal Democrat and Independent Alliance
At the 2011 elections the Conservatives retained overall control. The Liberal Democrats and Independent councillors lost all of their 7 seats at the election.
