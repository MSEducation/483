[[Amalveor]]

CATEGORIES: Hamlets in Cornwall

Amalveor ([tpl]lang-kw|Amal Veur[/tpl], meaning great slopePool; The Place Names of West Penwith) is a hamlet in West Penwith, Cornwall, England, United Kingdom at [tpl]gbmapping|SW 482 375[/tpl]. The hamlet is 3 miles (5 kilometres) south-west of St Ives.Ordnance Survey 1:25000 scale 'Explorer' map, sheet 102Ordnance Survey: Landranger map sheet 203 Land's End ISBN 978-0-319-23148-7
A pair of gold bracelets were discovered at Amalveor Farm (about one mile due west of the church) on 11 December 1931 and were declared to be treasure trove. The bracelets were dated as middle Bronze Age (about 1000BC) and are now in the British Museum.http://www.jstor.org/pss/4421421 JSTOR Digital Archive.
