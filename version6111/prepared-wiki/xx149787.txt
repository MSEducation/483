[[Motherwell Homestead]]

CATEGORIES: Abernethy No. 186, Saskatchewan, National Historic Sites in Saskatchewan, Museums in Saskatchewan, Open air museums in Canada, Farm museums in Canada

 
The Motherwell Homestead is a National Historic Site of Canada located just south of the community of Abernethy, Saskatchewan.[tpl]CRHP|1209|Motherwell Homestead|19 August 2012[/tpl] The site commemorates the life and achievements of William Richard Motherwell, Saskatchewan's first minister of agriculture and federal minister of agriculture for the Mackenzie King government.  
The homstead fieldstone house, called Lanark Place after Motherwell previous Ontario home and modeled after similar farmsteads built in Ontario.  The homestead is surrounded by a shelter belt. 
The 3.59 farmstead in addition to main house, includes fields defined by fences and shelter belt shrub and tree lines, and agricultural buildings. 
Visitors to the park are immersed in 1907 agriculture. The Motherwell house, barn, and outbuildings have been restored to their 1907 appearance. Employees at the park dress in period clothing. During operational months[tpl]Clarify|date=January 2010[/tpl], livestock, including hogs, cows, and chickens, are all on site.
The Friends of the Motherwell Homestead operate a restaurant and gift shop on site.
