[[Crotalus polystictus]]

CATEGORIES: Crotalus

Crotalus polystictus is a venomous pit viper species found in central Mexico. No subspecies is currently recognized.[tpl]ITIS |id=585827 |taxon=Crotalus polystictus |accessdate=1 August 2007[/tpl]

==Description==

Adults usually grow to a total length of [tpl]convert|60|to|70|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl], although exceptionally large specimens may be as much as [tpl]convert|100|cm|in|abbr=on[/tpl] long.

==Geographic range==

The species is found on the plateau of central Mexico from southern Zacatecas and northeastern Colima east to east-central Veracruz. It occurs at elevations between [tpl]convert|1450|and|2600|m|abbr=on[/tpl]. The type locality given is "Table Land, Mexico", although a restriction to "Tup√°taro, Guanajuanto, Mexico" was proposed by H.M. Smith and Taylor (1950).

==Conservation status==

This species is classified as Least Concern on the IUCN Red List of Threatened Species (v3.1, 2001).[tpl]Redlist species|no=64327|genus=Crotalus|species=polystictus|date=13 September|year=2007[/tpl] Species are listed as such due to their wide distribution, presumed large population, or because they are unlikely to be declining fast enough to qualify for listing in a more threatened category. The population trend was down when assessed in 2007.[tpl]Redlist CC2001|date=13 September|year=2007[/tpl]
