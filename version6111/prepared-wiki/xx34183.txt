[[Berney Arms]]

CATEGORIES: Public houses in Norfolk

Berney Arms is a place on the north bank of the River Yare, close to Breydon Water in the English county of Norfolk. It is part of the civil parish of Reedham, is in the district of Broadland and lies within The Broads. It comprises a railway station, a windmill, a public house and a farmhouse.Dunford. M & Lee.P (2012) The rough guide to Norfolk and Suffolk, Rough Guides UK, pp.107-109. It is the only place in mainland England to have National Rail railway access but no public road.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl]

==History==

Berney Arms takes its name from the pub, The Berney Arms, which is situated nearby and serves walkers and boaters who pass through the area.'Remote' Berney Arms pub set for new business, BBC Look East, 2013-04-13. Retrieved 2014-02-19. The pub is named after the landowner Thomas Trench Berney and gave its name to the railway station. Berney sold the land on which the railway was built only on the condition that a station was built to serve the area.McKie.D (2010) The rail to nowhere, The Guardian, 2010-07-11. Retrieved 2014-02-19.Mitchell.L (2010) Slow Norfolk and Suffolk, Bradt Travel Guides, pp.130-131.
Berney Arms Windmill was built in 1865 and is the tallest windmill in Norfolk at [tpl]convert|21.5|m|ft[/tpl] tall. The mill, which was used to grind cement clinker and then as a drainage mill, closed in 1948 and is now a Scheduled Ancient Monument in the care of English Heritage.Berney Arms Windmill, Pastscape, English Heritage. Retrieved 2014-02-19. The mill was restored in the early 20th century. The mill was the centre of the Reedham Cement Works which at one point supported a small settlement of 11 inhabited houses and a chapel.Local character area 19 - Halvergate marshes (excluding Bure loop and the west of Tunstall dyke) Broads landscape character assessment, Broads Authority, 2006. Retrieved 2014-02-20.
Berney Arms was mentioned in Arthur Ransome's popular children's book Coot Club, which is in the Swallows and Amazons series.

==Geography==

Berney Arms is in an area of marshland, much of which is at or below sea level. It lies on the River Yare just to the west of Breydon Water. The area is part of Berney Marshes RSPB reserveAbout Berney Marshes and Breydon Water, RSPB. Retrieved 2014-02-19. and within the Halvergate Marshes Site of Special Scientific Interest. These provide important habitats for a range of plant and invertebrate species as well as providing important wintering grounds for bird species such as Bewick's Swan.Halvergate Marshes, SSSI citation, Natural England. Retrieved 2014-02-20. The area is also a Ramsar Site and part of the Broadland Special Protection Area. Ashtree Farm is used by the RSPB as a series of dwellings and as its base for the marshes.
Berney Arms can be reached only by train, by boat or on foot and has no public road access, with only a private track running to it. Berney Arms railway station is a request stop on the Norwich-Great Yarmouth via Reedham line. It is served by a limited number of services each day, with a more frequent service on Sundays. Berney Arms is on the Weavers' Way and Wherryman's Way footpaths.
