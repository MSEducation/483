[[Kim Jerray-Silver]]

CATEGORIES: 1977 births, Living people, English women's footballers, England women's international footballers, FA Women's Premier League players, Arsenal L.F.C. players, Fulham L.F.C. players, Barnet F.C. Ladies players

Kimberley "Kim" Jerray-Silver (born 10th June 1977) is a former English international footballer. She was a full–time professional player with Fulham, whom she joined from Arsenal.

==Football career==

===Club===

Jerray-Silver won the 1995–96 FA Women's Premier League Cup with Wembley Ladies. In the final against Doncaster Belles, she came on as a second half substitute. The game finished 2–2 before Wembley won on penalties.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/sport/wembleys-flair-rewarded-in-shootout-1341434.html|title=Wembley's flair rewarded in shoot-out |publisher=The Independent|author=Pete Davies|date=11 March 1996|accessdate=9 August 2011[/tpl]

===International===

In May 1996 Jerray-Silver won a solitary England cap in a 3–0 Euro qualifying win over Portugal at Griffin Park.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/sport/davis-puts-portuguese-to-flight-1348221.html|title=Davis puts Portuguese to flight|author=Tom Chesshyre|publisher=The Independent|date=20 May 1996|accessdate=12 November 2009[/tpl]

==Personal life==

Jerray-Silver became a firefighter and was among the first on the scene at Edgware Road during the 7 July 2005 London bombings.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/programmes/breakfast/4395016.stm|title=Memorial for the victims of 7/7|publisher=British Broadcasting Corporation|date=1 November 2005|accessdate=28 October 2012[/tpl]
