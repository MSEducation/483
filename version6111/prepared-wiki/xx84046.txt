[[Kawakami, Nagano]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Nagano Prefecture

[tpl]nihongo|Kawakami|川上村|Kawakami-mura[/tpl] is a village located in Minamisaku District, Nagano Prefecture, Japan.
As of 2003, the village has an estimated population of 4,939 and a density of 23.56 persons per km². The total area is 209.61 km². Its village office is located at 1,185m, the highest of any municipality in Japan. Kawakami is famous for its lettuce, as well as the Kawakami-ken breed of dogs.[tpl]cite web|title=Kawakami Village Home Page|url=http://www.vill.kawakami.nagano.jp/english/index.html|accessdate=April 13, 2011[/tpl]
Although it is not widely known, the source of the Chikuma River, the longest river in Japan, is in Kawakami.[tpl]cite web|title=The Source of the Chikuma River|url=http://www.vill.kawakami.nagano.jp/english/03genryuu.html[/tpl]  They maintain this as a point of pride, as it appears in various school songs.
