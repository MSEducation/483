[[Susan Whelan]]

Susan Elizabeth Whelan, PC ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|hw|iː|l|ən[/tpl]; born May 5, 1963 in Windsor, Ontario) is a former Canadian Member of Parliament with the Liberal Party of Canada.  Whelan, a lawyer, first won a seat in the Canadian House of Commons in the 1993 election representing Essex—Windsor.  In 1997 and 2000 she was elected to represent Essex.  In 2002, Whelan was appointed by Prime Minister Jean Chrétien as Minister for International Cooperation as a cabinet minister.[tpl]CanParlbio|ID=3452c34f-d742-4572-b8c5-e2bbcb51a3d5|nolist=yes[/tpl]
Whelan unsuccessfully campaigned in the 2004 election, the 2006 election, and the 2008 election.Federal riding history from the Library of Parliament
Susan Whelan is the daughter of former Liberal Federal Minister of Agriculture, the Honourable Eugene Whelan.  Susan and her father hold the distinction of being the first father-daughter cabinet appointees.http://www.uwindsor.ca/alumni/susan-whelan-llb-88
Whelan shares her father's passionate interest in Canadian agriculture, having made Agriculture and Rural Development one of the key elements of policy during her tenure as Minister.[tpl]cite web |url=http://web4.uwindsor.ca/units/law/newschannel/alumni.nsf/inToc/AEF1A589FCF59E05852575D00052EFE4?OpenDocument |title=Susan Whelan '88 named CEO of the Canadian Cancer Society in Ontario |publisher=University of Windsor, Faculty of Law |date=May 12, 2009 |accessdate=2009-10-05[/tpl]
She has also instructed part-time at the University of Windsor, appropriately situated in the political science department.[tpl]cite web |url=http://web4.uwindsor.ca/units/pac/view.nsf/EditCategoryDocs/AE05EEF70AA6A949852572BF0056D146 |title=Three new directors join Alumni Association Board |publisher=University of Windsor |date=March 2007 |accessdate=2009-10-05[/tpl]
She previously represented the Ambassador Bridge Company on the Green Corridor Project.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.canada.com/windsorstar/news/decisioncanada/story.html?id=32530d7b-7db8-47ea-9223-50ed85fa3212 |title=Whelan's ties to Ambassador Bridge criticized |publisher=Windsor Star |date=September 17, 2008 |accessdate=2009-10-05[/tpl]
In June 2009, Whelan was named Chief Executive Officer for the Ontario division of the Canadian Cancer Society. In August the same year, she was diagnosed with breast cancer.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.cbc.ca/canada/windsor/story/2009/09/30/windsor-cancer-whelan-093009.html |title=Cancer society CEO resigns for health reasons |publisher=CBC |date=October 1, 2009 |accessdate=2009-10-05[/tpl]
She resigned from the Canadian Cancer Society in order to focus on her recovery.
Whelan authors a blog entitled Susan's Fight Back, to share her experience, strength, and hope with others.http://susansfightback.typepad.com/blog/
Whelan is currently the Executive Director of rare Charitable Research Reserve in Cambridge, Ontariohttp://www.raresites.org/  and maintains a law practice in Windsor, Ontario.
