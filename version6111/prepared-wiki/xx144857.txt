[[SN 1998bw]]

CATEGORIES: Hypernovae, Astronomical objects discovered in 1998

 
SN 1998bw was a rare type I/Ib gamma ray supernova detected on 26 April 1998 in the ESO 184-G82 spiral galaxy, which some astronomers believe may be an example of a collapsar.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.aao.gov.au/local/www/sne/98bw/98bw.html |title=Gamma-Ray Supernova 1998bw |publisher=The Anglo-Australian Observatory |accessdate=2006-10-20[/tpl] The supernova has been linked to GRB 980425, which was detected on 25 April 1998, the first time a gamma-ray burst has been linked to a supernova. The ING Newsletter, "Gamma-Ray Burst Afterglows: Surprises from the Sky", P. Vreeswijk, N. Tanvir, T. Galama, No.2 - March 2000 (accessed 11 November 2009)
