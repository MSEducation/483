[[Anja Feldmann]]

CATEGORIES: Leibniz Prize winners, 1966 births, Living people, People from Bielefeld, Berlin Institute of Technology faculty, German women academics

Anja Feldmann (born 8 March 1966 in Bielefeld)   is a German computer scientist.

==Life and Work==

She studied computer science at Universität Paderborn and received her degree in 1990. 
She continued her studies at Carnegie Mellon University, where she earned her M.Sc. in 1991 and her Ph.D. in 1995. 
Following four years of postdoctoral work at AT&T Labs Research, she held research positions at Saarland University and Technical University Munich. 
Since 2006, she has been professor of Internet Network Architectures at Telekom Innovation Laboratories at Technische Universität Berlin.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.inet.tu-berlin.de/menue/people/prof/anja/ |title=Internet Network Achitectures|accessdate=2010-12-31 |format= |work=[/tpl] 
Furthermore, since 2010, Anja Feldmann has been Dean of the Faculty of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences at Technische Universität Berlin.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.eecs.tu-berlin.de/menue/ueber_uns/dekanat/parameter/en/ | title=Technische Universität Berlin, Faculty of Electrical Engineering and computer science| accessdate=2010-12-31 |format= |work=[/tpl]
In May 2012, Anja Feldmann was elected the first woman on the employer side of the Supervisory Board of SAP.
Feldmann's area of research includes Internet traffic analysis and modeling as well as Internet routing.

==Personal life==

Anja is unmarried and has no children.
