Jon Akin is a retired American soccer forward who is the head coach of the Oglethorpe University men's soccer team.

==Player==

===Youth===

Akin graduated from Pensacola Catholic High School.  He was a four year starter on his high school soccer team and played for the North Florida Futbol Club which won the 1995 Florida Youth Soccer Championships.Soccer team signs Pensacola standout St. Petersburg Times - Wednesday, May 24, 1995  Akin attended the Saint Leo University, playing on the men's NCAA Division II soccer team from 1995 to 1998.  He is second on the Lions' career goals and points lists and holds the school record for career assists.Saint Leo Lions' soccer records  Akin was selected as a 1996 Second Team and a 1997 First Team Division II All American.1996 All Americans1997 All Americans  He was inducted into the school's Athletic Hall of Fame in 2005.Saint Leo Hall of Fame

===Professional===

Following graduation from college, Akin spent three months with the Independiente[tpl]disambiguation needed|date=October 2011[/tpl] reserve team.  In 1999, he returned to the United States when he was drafted by the Charleston Battery in the first round (twenty-fifth overall) of the USL A-League draft.1999 USL A-League Draft  In his first season with the Battery, Akin saw late game time in nine games and was sent on loan to the Carolina Dynamo of the USL D-3 Pro League.  By 2001, he was a regular first team starter.  In 2000, he went on loan to Kilkenny City.  In 2002, the Battery traded Akin to the Atlanta Silverbacks.  He spent one season with the Silverbacks.  From 2008 to 2009, Akin was a player-assistant coach with the Atlanta Eclipse of the Premier Arena Soccer League.2007/08 Atlanta Eclipse  In 2009, he played two games for the Atlanta Blackhawks of the USL Premier Development League.2009 Atlanta Blackhawks

==Coach==

In February 2003, Oglethorpe University hired Akins as the school's men's soccer coach.Professional Soccer Player Jon Akin Joins Oglethorpe University  He also served as an assistant coach with the Atlanta Eclipse of the Premier Arena Soccer League during the 2007-2008 season.
