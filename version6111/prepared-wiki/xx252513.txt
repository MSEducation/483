[[Colobochyla salicalis]]

CATEGORIES: Calpinae, Moths of Japan, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Estonia, Insects of Sweden, Insects of Romania, Insects of Poland, Insects of Latvia, Insects of Finland, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Belarus, Insects of Bosnia and Herzegovina, Insects of Hungary, Insects of Greece, Insects of Bulgaria, Insects of Turkey, Insects of Italy

The European Lesser Belle or Lesser Belle (Colobochyla salicalis) is a moth of the Noctuidae family. It is found all over Europe.
The wingspan is 26–30 mm. Adults are on wing from May to July. There is one generation per year.
The larvae feed on the young shoots and leaves of Populus tremula.
