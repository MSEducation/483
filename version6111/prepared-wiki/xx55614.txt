[[Columbia Motors]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct motor vehicle manufacturers of the United States, Companies based in Detroit, Michigan, Companies established in 1917, Columbia Motors

Columbia Motors was a Detroit, Michigan, United States based automobile manufacturer that produced automobiles from 1917 to 1924.
Columbia Motors was incorporated in 1916, with John George Bayerline as company president and William E. Metzger as vice-president.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Motor West|title=Officials of the Columbia Motors Co. of Detroit, Makers of the Columbia Six|date=Sep 15, 1917|year=1917|volume=XXVII|issue=11|page=24|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=PqYAAAAAMAAJ&pg=RA6-PA24&ci=26%2C828%2C962%2C546&source=bookclip|accessdate=28 October 2013[/tpl] Bayerline was the former president and general manager of the King Motor Car Company[tpl]cite journal|journal=Michigan Manufacturer Financial Record|title=Artemas Ward, Jr., Now Heads King Motor Car|date=June 19, 1915|volume=15|issue=25|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=nosfAQAAMAAJ&dq=Michigan%20Manufacturer%20and%20Financial%20Record%20volume%2015&pg=PT854&ci=9%2C293%2C634%2C1000&source=bookclip|accessdate=28 October 2013[/tpl] and former general manager and founder of the Warren Motor Car Company.[tpl]cite journal|journal=The Automobile|title=Detroit's Latest Warren-Detroit|date=Sep 23, 1909|year=1909|volume=21|issue=13|page=539[/tpl]  Prior to founding Columbia, Metzger was a founder of the E-M-F Company which later became the Studebaker Corporation.
Columbia Motors produced two models powered by Continental six-cylinder engines including the popular Columbia Six. In 1916, Columbia bought Argo Electric. In 1923, Columbia acquired Liberty Motor Car.
