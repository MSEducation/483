[[Kathryn Stockett]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, People from Jackson, Mississippi, People from Atlanta, Georgia, University of Alabama alumni, 1969 births

Kathryn Stockett is an American novelist. She is known for her 2009 debut novel, The Help, which is about African-American maids working in white households in Jackson, Mississippi, during the 1960s.

==Personal life==

Stockett grew up in Jackson, Mississippi. After graduating from the University of Alabama with a degree in English and Creative Writing, she moved to New York City. There she lived for 16 years and worked in magazine publishing and marketing. She is divorced, and has a daughter.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/northamerica/usa/5844739/The-maids-tale-Kathryn-Stockett-examines-slavery-and-racism-in-Americas-Deep-South.html|title=The maid's tale: Kathryn Stockett examines slavery and racism in America's Deep South|last=Calkin|first=Jessamy |date=July 16, 2009|work=The Daily Telegraph|accessdate=2009-10-20|location=London[/tpl]  
Reflective of her first novel, Stockett was very close to an African-American domestic worker.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2009/02/19/books/19masl.html|title=Racial Insults and Quiet Bravery in 1960s Mississippi|last=Maslin|first=Janet|date=February 18, 2009 |work=New York Times|accessdate=2009-10-20[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Stockett|first=Kathryn|title=This Life: Kathryn Stockett on her childhood in the Deep South|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/home/you/article-1199603/This-Life-Kathryn-Stockett-childhood-Deep-South.html|accessdate=23 June 2010|newspaper=Daily Mail|date=18 July 2009|location=London[/tpl] 
A lawsuit was filed in a Mississippi court by Abilene Cooper, a maid who used to work for Stockett's brother. It claimed that Stockett used her likeness in the book.[tpl]cite news|last=Churcher|first=Sharon|title=Her family hired me as a maid for 12 years but then she stole my life and made it a Disney movie|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/femail/article-2033369/Her-family-hired-maid-12-years-stole-life-Disney-movie.html|work=The Daily Mail|accessdate=12 September 2011|location=London|date=4 September 2011[/tpl]  A Hinds County, Mississippi judge threw the case out of court, citing the statute of limitations.[tpl]cite web|last=Mitchell|first=Jerry|title='The Help' lawsuit tossed out|url=http://www.clarionledger.com/article/20110817/NEWS/108170333/-Help-lawsuit-tossed-out|work=The Clarion Ledger|accessdate=12 September 2011[/tpl]  Stockett denies her claim of stealing her likeness, and says she only met her briefly.
