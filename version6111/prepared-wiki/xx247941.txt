[[Heartbroke]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 singles, Rodney Crowell songs, Ricky Skaggs songs, George Strait songs, The Marshall Tucker Band songs, Billboard Hot Country Songs number-one singles, RPM Country Tracks number-one singles, Songs written by Guy Clark, Epic Records singles, Song recordings produced by Ricky Skaggs, 1980 songs

"Heartbroke" is a song written by Guy Clark, and originally recorded by Rodney Crowell on his 1980 album But What Will the Neighbors Think.
It was later recorded by American country artist Ricky Skaggs.  It was released in July 1982 as the first single from the album Highways & Heartaches.  "Heartbroke" was Ricky Skaggs' third number one on the country chart.  The single stayed at number one for one week and spent a total of eleven weeks on the country chart.[tpl]cite book |title= The Billboard Book Of Top 40 Country Hits: 1944-2006, Second edition|last=Whitburn |first=Joel |authorlink=Joel Whitburn |year=2004 |publisher=Record Research |page=315[/tpl]

==Other versions==

George Strait released his version on his second album Strait from the Heart in 1982, not knowing the song had been recorded by Skaggs about two weeks earlier.
The Marshall Tucker Band also released a version on their 1982 album Tuckerized.
