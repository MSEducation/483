[[Colombo Yogurt]]

CATEGORIES: Brand name yogurts, General Mills brands

Colombo Yogurt originated from a family business run by Rose and Sarkis Colombosian, Armenian immigrants who lived in Andover, Massachusetts. Yogurt was first commercially produced and sold in the United States in 1929 by the Colombosians, whose family business later became Colombo Yogurt. The yogurt produced by the Colombosians was based on traditional Armenian cooking methods.Official Site The business saw growth in the 1960s due to the increased awareness of the health benefits of yogurt. 
In 1993, Colombo Yogurt was purchased by General Mills.Massachusettss Historical Society
In April 2008, Colombo yogurt downsized its serving package size from 8 ounces to 6 ounces, while maintaining the same price, an effective price increase of 33%.

==Termination==

On Friday, January 29, 2010 General Mills announced that it was dropping the Colombo brand to focus more on its Yoplait yogurt. It ended shipments on February 12, 2010.Eagle Tribune, "Yogurt with local roots shelved"
Colombo Yogurt is still produced locally and sold in institutional sizes in Puerto Rico.
