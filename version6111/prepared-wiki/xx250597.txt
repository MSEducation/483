[[Trevor Avery]]

CATEGORIES: English curators, Living people

Trevor Avery is an artist and curator who has been based in London, the Highlands of Scotland, and is now in the North West of England.
Currently he is a Director of  Another Space.
He has produced major touring projects including "Gernika!", a touring exhibition of Scotland and Spain,[tpl]cite news |title=Tres artistas navarros exponen en Inglaterra a través de un intercambio; José Miguel Corral, José A. Pérez y Tony Grady muestran sus obras recientes |url=http://www.euskalkultura.com/noticias/tres-artistas-navarros-exponen-en-inglaterra-a-traves-de-un-intercambio-jose-miguel-corral-jose-a-perez-y-tony-grady-muestran-sus-obras-recientes-en-diario-de-noticias?language_sync=1 |work=Diario de Noticias de Navarra |date=17 August 2006 |accessdate=12 January 2010 [/tpl] The Rock Drill and Beyond, featuring a full reconstruction of Jacob Epstein’s iconic sculpture, Asylrecht,[tpl]cite news |title=Natives on the warpath |url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/smgpubs/access/60125887.html?dids=60125887:60125887&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Jan+22%2C+1998&author=Mary+Beith&pub=The+Herald&desc=Natives+on+the+warpath&pqatl=google |work=The Herald |date=22 January 1998 |accessdate=12 January 2010 | first=Mary | last=Beith[/tpl] involving the Bundesarchiv and Yorkshire Sculpture Park, and 19.16 Memory and the Act of Remembrance at Kendal Town Hall in 2004[tpl]cite news |title=Remembering the First World War |url=http://www.cwherald.com/archive/archive/remembering-the-first-world-war-20041106195286.htm |work=Cumberland and Westmorland Herald |date=6 November 2004 |accessdate=12 January 2010 [/tpl] and the iconic Accrington Town Hall in 2008.
In 2007 Avery collaborated with artist Chris Atkins and local historian Allan King to produce Flying Boats and Fellow Travellers, an online exhibition about the World War II Short Sunderland factory at Troutbeck in Cumbria.[tpl]cite news |title=Flying Boat Builders From Lost Wartime Village Tell Their Stories |url=http://www.culture24.org.uk/history+%2526+heritage/war+%2526+conflict/art51491 |work=Culture24 |date=18 October 2007 |accessdate=12 January 2010 [/tpl]
From 2009 onwards Avery has worked with artist Mark Peter Wright on Where Once We Walked, a long term collaborative project which was premiered on 15 September 2011 at St. Mary's Church, Windermere, UK. To coincide with the performance a limited release of 150 CD copies of the work were produced. The work has also been performed and broadcast at Tate Britain (UK) and Helicotrema Festival (Italy)
His current ongoing projects are Avenue of Exile and From Auschwitz to Ambleside, two major interwoven European and international projects emanating from the arrival of three hundred child survivors of the Holocaust to the Lake District of England in 1945.[tpl]cite news |title=Artist Tells Story Of Children Taken From Auschwitz To Ambleside |url=http://www.culture24.org.uk/art/sculpture+%2526+installation/art41973 |work=Culture24 |date=5 December 2006 |accessdate=12 January 2010 [/tpl]
This work involves working with Auschwitz-Birkenau Museum and Memorial and sites in Krakow, Plaszow, Lodz, Warsaw, Budapest, London, Manchester and the Lake District.
His work from The Rock Drill and Beyond featured in Thomas Hirschhorn's U-topia Room as part of the Commonwealth exhibition at Tate Modern in 2001.
He has written for Third Text magazine, and curated exhibitions including leading Aboriginal Australian artist Dr Pam Johnston, Tanzanian artist Everlyn Nicodemus and Native American artists Linda Lomahaftewa and Jimmie Durham.[tpl]cite journal |last=Beith |first=Mary |date=Winter 1997 |title=River deep, mountain high |journal=Third Text |volume=11 |issue=41 |pages=99–100 |issn=0952-8822 |doi=10.1080/09528829708576708 [/tpl]
