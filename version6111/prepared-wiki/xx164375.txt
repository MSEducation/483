[[Shō Sei]]

CATEGORIES: 1497 births, 1555 deaths, Kings of Ryūkyū, 16th-century monarchs in Asia

Shō Sei (尚清) (1497–1555) was king of the Ryūkyū Kingdom from 1526 to 1555.Kerr, George H. (2000). [tpl]Google books|vaAKJQyzpLkC|Okinawa: The History of an Island People, pp. 115–116.|page=115[/tpl] He was the fifth son of King Shō Shin, who he succeeded.
Shō Sei suppressed a rebellion on Amami Ōshima in 1537, and took steps to improve defenses against wakō that same year.
Shō Sei died in 1555, and was succeeded by his second son Shō Gen.
