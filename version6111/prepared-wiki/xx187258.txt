[[Kita-Ayase Station]]

CATEGORIES: Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line

[tpl]nihongo|Kita-Ayase Station|北綾瀬駅|Kita-Ayase-eki[/tpl] is a railway station in Adachi, Tokyo, Japan, operated by the Tokyo subway operator Tokyo Metro.

==Lines==

Kita-Ayase Station forms the terminus of the 2.1 km Kita-Ayase branch of the Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line from Ayase Station, and is served by trains that shuttle between the two stations.

==Station layout==

The station has a single elevated side platform serving one track. The platform is just three cars long.

==History==

Kita-Ayase Station opened on 20 December 1979.[tpl]cite book | last = Terada | first = Hirokazu |title = データブック日本の私鉄 |trans_title= Databook: Japan's Private Railways | publisher = Neko Publishing | date = 19 January 2013 | location = Japan | page = 215| isbn = 978-4-7770-1336-4[/tpl]

==Passenger statistics==

In fiscal 2011, the station was used by an average of 25,225 passengers daily.

==Future developments==

The station is to undergo rebuilding work to extend the platforms approximately 135 m toward Ayase Station to allow ten-car through trains to operate to and from the main Chiyoda Line.[tpl]cite web |url= http://news.mynavi.jp/news/2014/03/04/383/|title= 東京メトロ千代田線北綾瀬駅、10両編成対応に向けたホーム延伸工事に着手|trans_title= Work to start on lengthening platform at Tokyo Metro Chiyoda Line Kita-Ayase Station to handle 10-car trains|date= 4 March 2013|work= MyNavi News|publisher= Mynavi Corporation|location= Japan|language= Japanese|archiveurl= |archivedate= |accessdate= 7 March 2014[/tpl] New station entrances will also be added. Work is scheduled to be completed by the end of fiscal 2018.
