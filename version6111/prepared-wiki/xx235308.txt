[[P.G. Vinda]]

CATEGORIES: Indian cinematographers, Living people

P.G. Vinda is an Indian cinematographer and director[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.deccanchronicle.com/headlines/akash-entertain-global-audiences-731|title=Akash to entertain global audiences|date=24 May 2009|work=Deccan Chronicle|accessdate=28 July 2010[/tpl] who works in the Telugu film industry. He is known for his work on the 2004 black-and-white film, Grahanam, which was directed by Mohan Krishna Indraganti.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2008/06/20/stories/2008062050480100.htm|title=Hyderabad : It's a game and it's animated|date=20 June 2008|work=The Hindu|accessdate=28 July 2010[/tpl] Other Vinda films include Anumanaspadam, Ashta Chemma, and Vinayakudu.

==Early life and education==

Vinda was born into a farming family in Palem, a remote village in Mehboobnagar, Andhra Pradesh. He studied mostly in state-government-sponsored residential schools through scholarships. As a child he was always fascinated with the world around and started painting right from his very early years. With time he became interested in photography and world cinema. At a point he had to choose between photography and painting. He chose photography, eventually to become a cameramen in Telugu cinema.
He graduated from the Jawaharlal Nehru Fine Arts University in Hyderabad with a degree in fine arts specializing in photography and visual communication.

==Career==

Vinda went on to assist cinematographer Madhu Ambat for Rajkumar Santoshi's Lajja and two more films after that.
Later he worked for short films Missing (2001) and Erase. He followed it up with  documentaries - The Realm of Nepal Architecture, Children of Lesser God and a travelogue, Vihari (Traveller). His chance encounter with director Indraganti Mohan Krishna led to a creative collaboration from which came Vinda's first feature Grahanam and Ashtachamma which was a major success at the box office.
Grahanam received critical acclaim and it was a pioneering effort in the history of Telugu cinema from the camera work point of view as it displayed exceptional quality of the visual although shot totally with a mini dv camera, mostly in natural light and on a meager budget.[tpl]cn|date=October 2013[/tpl]
Vinda’s work for Grahanam received attention and a National Award nomination for cinematography.
Impressed with his work in Grahanam, the offbeat director Neelakanta roped him for his thriller Nandanavanam 120 km which in turn caught the eye of director Vamsi which led to their collaboration for Anumanaspadam, another thriller.
With his recent films Key, Madhura Sreedhar’s  Snehageetam and It’s My Love Story, Vinda earned a distinction of master of digital format in Telugu cinema.[tpl]cn|date=October 2013[/tpl] Also his most successful films Ashtachamma and Vinayakudu made him the most wanted cinematographer for small budget filmmakers who dream of visual beauty.[tpl]cn|date=October 2013[/tpl]
Vinda’s first feature film as a writer-director is a children’s tale called Lotus Pond  starring Akash and Rohit. It was shot for 30 days straight in Kulu Manali, the crew trekking altitudes up to 14.000 feet at temperatures below zero degrees. Lotus Pond was screened at the Banff Mountain Film Festival and the 17th Children’s International Film Festival in India.
