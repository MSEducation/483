[[Joseph Horton House]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Houses completed in 1752, National Register of Historic Places in Dutchess County, New York, Historic house museums in New York, Museums in Dutchess County, New York, Houses in Dutchess County, New York

The Joseph Horton House is an historic structure located in New Hackensack, New York. Once part of a larger [tpl]convert|250|acre|ha|abbr=on|adj=on[/tpl] farm, the farmhouse was built between 1725 and 1752. In 1840, the farm was divided, leaving the house on a [tpl]convert|100|acre|ha|abbr=on|adj=on[/tpl] parcel, and it become known as "Old Hundred". Only a [tpl]convert|2|acre|ha|abbr=on[/tpl] lot remains intact. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places on November 2, 1988.[tpl]cite web|author=|title=National Register of Historic Places nomination, Horton, Joseph, House|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=1909|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation|date=May 24, 1988|accessdate=January 10, 2010[/tpl] The building is located on the north side of NYÂ 376, or New Hackensack Road.
The house is privately owned, but has been restored and is open for tours by appointment.
