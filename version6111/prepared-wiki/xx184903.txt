[[Al Morgan]]

CATEGORIES: 20th-century American novelists, American male novelists, 1920 births, 2011 deaths

Al Morgan (January 16, 1920 – March 3, 2011)[tpl]cite news|title=Obituaries: Al Morgan|accessdate=5 March 2011|newspaper=Brattleboro Reformer|date=5 March 2011[/tpl]  was an American producer of The Today Show during the 1960s, was a novelist best known for his trenchant look at media personalities, The Great Man (Dutton, 1955), which reviewers compared to The Hucksters and Citizen Kane. It was this novel which popularized the phrase "The Great Unwashed." As in Citizen Kane, a reporter sets out to gather information on a well-known deceased public figure.
