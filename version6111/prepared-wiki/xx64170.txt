==Early life and education==

Naidu was born in Naravari Palle, Chittoor district on April 20, 1950.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] His father, N. Kharjura Naidu, worked in agriculture and his mother Ammanamma was a housewife.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] Since his village had no school, Naidu attended primary school in Seshapuram until class five and the Chandragiri Government High School until class nine.Rediff On The NeT: The Rediff Election Profile/Andhra Pradesh Chief Minister N Chandrababu Naidu. Rediff.com (23 September 1999). Retrieved on 16 January 2012. He went to Tirupati for his higher education, studying there from class 10 until he received his master's degree. Naidu completed his BA degree in 1972 before enrolling in an MA program in economics. In 1974, he began work towards a PhD under D. L. Narayana (Andhra Pradesh Finance Commission chairman), researching the economic ideas of N. G. Ranga. ==Early political career==

Naidu entered politics as a youth leader from Chandragiri. During the Emergency he was appointed Pulicherla Youth Congress president, becoming close to Sanjay Gandhi.

===[tpl]anchor|Legislative career 1978–1983[/tpl]Legislative career, 1978–1983===

Naidu received a Congress ticket in 1978 under the 20-percent quota for youth from the Chandragiri constituency. He was elected to the Andhra Pradesh assembly in 1978 by the Congress Party and became Minister of Technical Education and Cinematography in T. Anjaiah's cabinet, at age 28 the state's youngest assembly member and minister.A High-Tech Fix for One Corner of India – Page 4 – New York Times. Nytimes.com (27 December 2002). Retrieved on 16 January 2012.
As Minister of State for Cinematography, Naidu met Telugu cinema star Nandamuri Taraka Rama Rao (NTR). The Nadamuri family, impressed with him, proposed marriage to NTR's daughter Bhuvaneswari and a large wedding was held in Madras.

==Telugu Desam Party==

In early 1982, there were rumors that NTR would enter politics by floating a regional political party. Nine months before scheduled general elections, he created the Telugu Desam Party. Naidu remained in the Congress Party, standing again in the Chandragiri constituency against TDP candidate Venkatarama Naidu. The TDP swept the election, the first defeat for the Indian National Congress in the state since independence. Naidu joined the TDP after his defeat, thinking his future would be brighter in his father-in-law's party.[tpl]cn|date=March 2014[/tpl]

===Rise in the party===

Naidu became NTR's trusted lieutenant. When Nadendla Bhaskara Rao staged a coup against NTR in August 1984, Naidu helped his father-in-law regain power by rallying the MLAs before the President of India at Rashtrapati BhavanRediff On The NeT: Naidu becomes fourth AP CM to return his party to power. Rediff.com (7 October 1999). Retrieved on 16 January 2012. and NTR appointed Naidu general secretary of the party. He began playing an important role in the TDP after Rao's coup attempt.
NTR opted for mid-term elections after regaining the chief ministry. Although Naidu did not stand, Nadendla voters gave NTR another landslide. Naidu slowly gained power in the TDP, and was believed to be building his own group in the party.[tpl]cn|date=March 2014[/tpl]

===[tpl]anchor|Legislative career 1989–1994[/tpl]Legislative career, 1989–1994===

In the 1989 Assembly elections, Naidu stood in his native constituency of Kuppam and won by a slender margin of about 5,000 votes.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] Since the Congress Party regained power in the state elections, Naidu was a member of the opposition.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl]

==2003 assassination attempt==

On 1 October 2003 Naidu survived a land-mine blast, escaping with a fractured left collarbone and hairline fractures of two right ribs. The blast occurred about 16:00 IST when Naidu was travelling in a convoy to the Lord Venkateswara temple in the Tirumala hills for the annual Brahmotsavam festival.A blast and its shock. Hindu.com. Retrieved on 24 August 2010. State Information Technology Minister B. Gopalakrishna Reddy, Telugu Desam legislators R. Rajasekhar Reddy and Ch. Krishnamurthy and driver Srinivasa Raju were also injured. After an investigation, Naidu's survival was attributed to the armoured vehicle in which he was riding and God's intervention.

==[tpl]anchor|2004 elections failure[/tpl]2004 election defeat==

The Telugu Desam Party (led by Naidu) failed to retain power after two successive wins, winning 47 of 294 seats in the state assembly and five of 42 in the Lok Sabha. While many of his ministers lost, Naidu won decisively in Kuppam.[tpl]cite news|url=http://in.rediff.com/election/2004/may/11ap7.htm|title=Naidu wins by a Huge Margin|date=20 May 2004|publisher=Rediff|accessdate=20 May 2004[/tpl]

===[tpl]anchor|Assets Case[/tpl]Assets case===

In November 2011 the Andhra Pradesh High Court ordered an investigation of Naidu, petitioned by Y. S. Vijayamma (MLA and widow of Y.S. Rajasekhara Reddy, who sought an inquiry into Naidu's alleged disproportionate assets). The petition accused Naidu of doling out favours to corporations and amassing undue wealth.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/states/andhra-pradesh/article2626821.ece|title=High Court orders probe against Chandrababu Naidu|date= 14 November 2011|publisher=Hindu|accessdate= 8 March 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.thehindu.com/news/states/andhra-pradesh/article2899722.ece|title=AP High Court dismisses petition against Naidu|date= 16 December 2012|publisher=Hindu|accessdate= 8 March 2012[/tpl]Petition against Naidu threatens to expose the former CM – Rediff.com News. Rediff.com. Retrieved on 8 May 2012.
Naidu fought to halt the investigation, challenging the high-court order before the Supreme Court; the latter refused to intervene, asking the petitioners to approach the high court for an interim order. Naidu, Ramoji Rao and others filed a vacation petition in the high court against the Central Bank of India probe. Reliance Industries impleaded in the case, and Chief Justice Madan Lokur recused himself because he owned Reliance stock. Counsel for Reliance expressed concern that the case might damage the company and its investors; allegations levelled by the petitioner against Naidu pertained to decisions about Reliance investment in the Krishna Godavari Basin and alleged kickbacks received by Naidu received through Reliance investment in Ushodaya Enterprises. On 13 December 2011 the Andhra Pradesh high court, in an interim order, stayed the investigation;[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rediff.com/news/report/hc-stays-cbi-probe-into-chandrababu-naidus-assets-case/20111213.htm|title=HC stays CBI probe into Chandrababu Naidu's assets case|date= 13 December 2011|publisher=Rediff|accessdate= 8 March 2012[/tpl] on 16 February 2012 the court dismissed the petition, ruling that it did not meet the parameters of public-interest litigation.
