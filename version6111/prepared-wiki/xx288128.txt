[[Kim Chong-hee]]

CATEGORIES: South Korean businesspeople

Kim Chong-hee (Hangul: 김종희; Hanja: 金鍾喜 1922~1981) was a Korean businessman. Born in Cheonan, South Korea under Japanese Colonial Period, he soon became a leading pioneer in explosives business when the country became independent in 1945. He established Hankook Explosives in 1952 and succeeded in self-producing dynamite in 1956. He later contributed to education as well, establishing Bugil Education Foundation in 1975. His penname is Hyeon-am (Hangul: 현암; Hanja: 玄岩).
