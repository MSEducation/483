[[Harry Ekman]]

CATEGORIES: Pin-up artists, 1923 births, 1999 deaths, People from Medford, New Jersey

Harry Ekman (born in Chicago Il. in 1923) was a significant American graphic artist.http://www.thepinupfiles.com/ekman.html His early influences were Gil Elvgren, Haddon Sundblom, Joyce Ballantyne. Ekman initially apprenticed with Sundblom http://www.coca-colacompany.com/stories/coke-lore-santa-claus#TCCC who was a close family friend and by 1951 for Elvgren.http://www.gilelvgren.com/GE/  He died in Medford, New Jersey in 1999.

==Career==

Harry Ekman studied at the American Academy of Art and the Art Institute of Chicago. He is best remembered for his pin-up and advertising work. Working with Gil Elvgren, he is thought to have penned several of the iconic images attributed to Elvgren.http://www.thepinupfiles.com/ekman.html  By 1960, Ekman was making a transition from commercial illustration and pin-up art to portraiture. The rest of his career was devoted to gallery works and portraits.
