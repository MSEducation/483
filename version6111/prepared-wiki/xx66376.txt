==Alumni==

The Alumni Association was founded by a group of alumni in 1961, and works in close partnership with INSEAD to provide services to the global INSEAD alumni community of 46,000+ members in 171 countries.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.insead.edu/Alumni/alumni_network/index.cfm |title=INSEAD - Alumni - Global Alumni Network |publisher=INSEAD |date= |accessdate=2012-06-25[/tpl] Many are members of the INSEAD International Alumni Association, which has 46 national alumni associations.[tpl]cite web|title=Welcome to the INSEAD Alumni Community|url=http://alumni.insead.edu/|accessdate=19 November 2013[/tpl]
In addition to the national associations, INSEAD alumni have formed clubs and groups dedicated to specific industries or activities.[tpl]cite web|title=Alumni Interest Groups|url=http://alumni.insead.edu/community/interest-groups.cfm|accessdate=19 November 2013[/tpl]  These include the Energy Club,[tpl]cite web|title=Energy Network|url=https://iconnect.insead.edu/IAA/inseadalumniclubs/energyclub/Pages/Homepage.aspx |publisher=INSEAD |accessdate=19 November 2013[/tpl] the INSEAD Healthcare Alumni Network[tpl]cite web|title=INSEAD Alumni Health Network|url=http://www.inseadhealthalumni.net[/tpl] the Salamander Golf Society.[tpl]cite web|title=Salamander Golf Society|url=http://salamandergolfsociety.roundtablelive.org|accessdate=19 November 2013[/tpl] and the secretive but highly influential Renaissance Club.
