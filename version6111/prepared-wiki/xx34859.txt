[[Cattai National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of New South Wales, Protected areas established in 1992, 1992 establishments in Australia, Hawkesbury River

Cattai is a national park in New South Wales (Australia), 45 km northwest of Sydney.  It is the site of a land grant to the First Fleet assistant surgeon, Thomas Arndell.  Arndell's cottage, dating from 1821, is still in the park ([tpl]coord|33.558804|S|150.892571|E|type:landmark_region:AU_scale:2500[/tpl]).  The park borders Cattai Creek, a tributary of the Hawkesbury River, and has picnic facilities.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nationalparks.nsw.gov.au/parks.nsf/ParkContent/N0079?Opendocument&ParkKey=N0079&Type=xo|title=Cattai National Park|accessdate=2006-07-23|date=25 April 2006|work=NSW NPWS website|publisher=NSW National Parks and Wildlife Service[/tpl]
