[[Circumcise Me]]

CATEGORIES: 2008 films, English-language films, Documentary films about Jews and Judaism, Stand-up comedy concert films, 2000s comedy films

Circumcise Me is a 2008 film about the American-born Israeli comedian Yisrael Campbell.[tpl]cite news|url=http://fr.jpost.com/servlet/Satellite?cid=1215330927315&pagename=JPost/JPArticle/ShowFull |title=JPost |publisher=Fr.jpost.com |date= |accessdate=November 10, 2013[/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=February 2014[/tpl]
It was produced by Matthew Kalman, foreign news correspondent in Jerusalem for USA Today, Canada's Globe & Mail and other newspapers, and David Blumenfeld, an Israel-based photographer who has photographed for Time, Newsweek and other publications.
The film, originally screened under the title It's Not in Heaven: The Comedy of Yisrael Campbell,[tpl]cite news |author=Susan Walker |url=http://www.thestar.com/article/420610 |title=A barrel of laughs with serious notes |newspaper=Toronto Star |date=May 2, 2008 |accessdate=November 10, 2013[/tpl] is about a Catholic convert to Judaism who has become a stand-up comedy star in Israel. Campbell's routine riffs on the fact that he converted to Judaism three times, once Reform, once Conservative and once Orthodox."Jewish comedy not just a punchline", Michael Posner, Globe and Mail, May 2, 2008
The Economist calls the film "hilarious and moving".[tpl]cite news |title=Belonging in Israel |date=April 24, 2008 |work=The Economist |url=http://www.economist.com/node/11081863 |accessdate=February 2, 2014[/tpl]
The film has been screened in Toronto and Jerusalem, and is scheduled to be released in the United States autumn 2008.
