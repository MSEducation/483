Paul Warwick (born 8 January 1981 in Australia) is an Australian rugby union player for English team Worcester Warriors, who play their rugby in the Aviva Premiership. He plays as a fly-half, fullback or centre.

==Connacht==

Warwick joined Irish province Connacht from Manly in 2004 on a one-year contract. A 97-point return in the 2004–05 European Challenge Cup saw Warwick awarded with a two-year extension on his contract. In a total of 19 appearances in the Challenge Cup, he scored 157 points.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/1699.php|title=Introducing – Paul Warwick|date=24 October 2007|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl]

==Munster==

Warwick joined Munster in 2007, straight from Connacht.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/1989.php|title=Warwick Joins Munster|date=6 February 2007|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] He scored 21-point on his full debut for Munster against Llanelli Scarlets on 22 September 2007.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/286.php|title=Munster See Off Scarlets|date=22 September 2007|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] He was on the bench for Munster for the 2008 Heineken Cup Final against Toulouse, but did not come on.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/284.php|title=Munster Champions of Europe|date=24 May 2008|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] In April 2009, Warwick scored two monster drop-goals and a try in Munster's 2008–09 Heineken Cup quarter-final against Ospreys, a game in which he won the Man-of-the-Match award[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/290.php|title=Heineken Cup Semi Secured|date=12 April 2009|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl]http://www.youtube.com/watch?v=Y36ucIXrvTw. On 27 January 2009, he reportedly signed a three-year deal with Aviva Premiership club London Irish, starting at the start of the 2009–10 Guinness Premiership season. However, there was speculation as to whether he would be joining the Irish Exiles or remaining with Munster for the start of the next season, and a Tug-of-War started between the two sides looking to secure his services.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rte.ie/sport/rugby/2009/0127/warwickp.html|title=Warwick leaving Munster for London Irish|date=27 January 2009|work=RTÉ Sport[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.breakingnews.ie/sport/rugby/mhsngbgbmhkf/|title=Munster want to keep Warwick|date=28 January 2009|work=breakingnews.ie[/tpl] On 9 April 2009, Munster and London Irish announced that the issue over Warwick has been resolved to the satisfaction of all parties and that Warwick would be remaining with Munster for 2010–11.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/439.php|title=Warwick Situation Sorted|date=9 April 2009|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] He was part of the Munster team that won the 2008–09 Magners League.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/392.php|title=Magners is Munster's|date=30 April 2009|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/307.php|title=Munster End in Style|date=15 May 2009|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] Warwick scored all of Munster's points during their historic 15–6 win over Australia in November 2010, kicking 3 penalties and 2 drop goals.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/8287.php|title=Class of 2010 Excel|date=17 November 2010|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=11 December 2011[/tpl] On 20 December 2010, Munster confirmed that Warwick would leave at the end of the 2010–11 season. He attracted the interest of several European clubs, who offered terms that Munster could not match.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/8431.php|title=Warwick Move Confirmed|date=20 December 2010|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl] Warwick's last appearance for Munster was when he came off the bench during the 2011 Celtic League Grand Final. Munster won the match, against newly crowned Heineken Cup champions and fierce rivals Leinster, 19–9 in Thomond Park.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/9094.php|title=Munster Crowned Magners League Champions|date=28 May 2011|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=15 December 2011[/tpl]
As Warwick has played Rugby sevens with the Australia Sevens, he will never be eligible to play for Ireland, despite the fact that he would now qualify through marriage and residency.

He joined Stade Français in 2011 on a two-year contract.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishtimes.com/sports/rugby/2011/0204/1224289017090.html|title=Warwick bound for Stade Français|date=4 February 2011|work=irishtimes.com[/tpl]

==Worcester Warriors==

It was announced on 25 January 2013 that Warwick will join Aviva Premiership side Worcester Warriors at the beginning of the 2013–14 season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/rugby-union/21198819|title=Worcester Warriors: Paul Warwick to join in the summer|date=25 January 2013|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=25 January 2013[/tpl] He made his debut for the club on 5 October 2013, coming off the bench against Newcastle Falcons.
