[[Thomas Holzer]]

CATEGORIES: 1985 births, Living people, People from Augsburg, German racing drivers, Formula BMW ADAC drivers, German Formula Three Championship drivers, Formula 3 Euro Series drivers, 24 Hours of Le Mans drivers, FIA World Endurance Championship drivers, European Le Mans Series drivers, ADAC GT Masters drivers

Thomas Holzer (born 27 December 1985) is a German racing driver.

==Career==

===Formula BMW===

Born in Augsburg, Holzer began his karting career in 1997, before moving into the Formula BMW ADAC championship in 2001, with ADAC e.V. Motorsport.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cmv-sportmedia.de/holzer/results?lang=en|title=Results|publisher=cmv-sportmedia.de|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] Holzer finished seventh in the championship.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/201-2001/|title=Formula BMW Junior 2001|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] He switched teams and went to the main class, joining MÃ¼cke Motorsport for 2002 and finished the season on the sixth place with two wins at EuroSpeedway Lausitz.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/96-2002/|title=Formula BMW ADAC 2002|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]

===Formula Three===

Holzer graduated to the Formula 3 class in 2003, joining the German Formula Three Championship and Trella Motorsport. He finished seventh with one podium.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/17-2003/|title=Deutsche Formel 3 Meisterschaft 2003|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] He stayed in the series for the next year, but switched to his family team AM-Holzer Rennsport. He improved to fifth place in the standings and won race at EuroSpeedway Lausitz.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/17-2004/|title=Deutsche Formel 3 Meisterschaft 2004|work=driverdb.com|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2005, Holzer moved to the Formula 3 Euro Series. He finished 22nd in the standings without scoring a point.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/15-2005/|title=Formula 3 Euro Series 2005|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]

===ADAC GT Masters===

After six-year absence Holzer returned in racing, participating in local series ADAC GT Masters with Heico Motorsport.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.driverdb.com/standings/1898-2011/|title=ADAC GT Masters 2011|publisher=Driver Database|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]

===FIA World Endurance Championship===

In 2012 Holzer moved to the endurance racing, joining FIA World Endurance Championship and Lotus.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.autosport.com/news/report.php/id/97675|title=Thomas Holzer to drive Lotus-backed LMP2 Kodewa Lola in World Endurance Championship|last=O'Leary|first=Jamie|date=24 February 2012|work=Autosport|publisher=Haymarket|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl] He continued his LMP2 campaign with Lotus-backed Kodewa in 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.endurance-info.com/version2/news-focus-15593.html|title=Lotus LMP2 in FIA WEC and 24H of Le Mans|publisher=Endurance-Info|work=endurance-info.com|date=1 February 2013|accessdate=8 August 2013[/tpl]
