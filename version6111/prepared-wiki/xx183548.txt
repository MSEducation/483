[[Dr. Fisk Holbrook Day House]]

CATEGORIES: Houses in Milwaukee County, Wisconsin, Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Wisconsin, National Historic Landmarks in Wisconsin

Dr. Fisk Holbrook Day House, also known as Sunnyhill Home,
is an interesting house on its own, that is further significant for its association with geologist Fisk Holbrook Day.
It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1997.[tpl]Cite document|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Historic Landmark Nomination: Day, Dr. Fisk Holbrook, House / Sunnyhill Home|203 KB[/tpl]|date=September 25, 1994 |author=Joanne Kluessendorf and Donald G. Mikulic |publisher=National Park Service|postscript=[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying 5 photos, exterior and interior, from 1996.|573 KB[/tpl]
Day collaborated with Thomas Greene whose former home, the Thomas A. Green Memorial Museum and Collection is now a museum and is also designated an NHL.
The pianist Liberace once sought to purchase this home as the location for his museum in 1978.
Liberace Planning Tour of Mansion. The Milwaukee Journal, 30 July 1978.
