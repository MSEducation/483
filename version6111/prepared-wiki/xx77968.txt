[[Fashion and Textile Museum]]

CATEGORIES: Textile museums in the United Kingdom, Fashion museums in the United Kingdom, Museums in Southwark, Museums established in 2003, 2003 establishments in England

The Fashion and Textiles Museum is a museum based in Bermondsey dedicated to all aspects of fashion and textile design.O'Ceallaigh, John, London 2012: 50 of the best unusual museums in London - part two. The Telegraph. 7 June 2012. Accessed 8 June 2012 Founded in 2003 by the designer Zandra Rhodes, the museum is now operated by Newham College and rather than running a permanent exhibition, offers an ongoing programme of temporary displays.Maddocks, Fiona, Zandra goes to the opera. Evening Standard. 7 November 2007. Accessed 8 June 2012

==Building==

The museum is housed in a converted warehouse which was redesigned by the Mexican architect Ricardo Legorreta in collaboration with Rhodes.Davison, Phil, Obituary: Ricardo Legorreta: Architect whose work included the Fashion and Textile Museum. The Independent. 13 January 2012. Accessed 8 June 2012Legorreta + Legorreta. Fashion and Textile Museum, London, UK. Accessed 8 June 2012 It was Legorreta's first and only building in Europe.Barton, Laura, Rhodes' scholars, The Guardian, 26 November 2002. Accessed 8 June 2012Rhodes pays tribute to Ricardo Legorreta, Building Design, January 6, 2012. Accessed 8 June 2012 In addition to the exhibition space, the building contains a textile studio and printing workshop and private residential quarters. The building has become a tourist attraction in its own right due to its outrageous colour scheme of hot pink, burnt orange, yellow, and bright blue.
