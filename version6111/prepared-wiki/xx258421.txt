George Dickson (born September 27, 1923 in Boston, Massachusetts) is a retired American football player and coach was the head coach of the Hamilton Tiger-Cats of the Canadian Football League for the first two games of the 1976 season.

==Early life==

Dickson was born in Boston and grew up in South Pasadena, California. He was a star quarterback at South Pasadena High School and in 1940 joined the Notre Dame Fighting Irish football team.

===Military===

After his freshman season, Dickson enlisted as a Paratrooper. He saw extensive action during the war, including combat in Normandy on D-Day. Following the fighting in Normandy, Sgt. Dickson and his unit moved to the Ardennes. There, they were battered by constant bombing by German artillery during the Battle of the Bulge.
He returned to Notre Dame in 1946, but dropped out in order to work full time. He returned to Notre Dame again in 1948 and spent his final two seasons of eligibility as a reserve quarterback behind Frank Tripucka and Bob Williams.[tpl]cite news |title=Grid Staff Fill at Notre Dame as Dickson Hired |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=xJ8pAAAAIBAJ&sjid=M-YDAAAAIBAJ&pg=1344,4598405&dq |quote= |work=The Spokesman-Review |date=February 14, 1954 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl]
Sgt. George Dickson received the Admiral Thomas J. Hamilton Award from the All-American Football Foundation in 2006.

==Coaching career==

Dickson began his coaching career in 1952 as an assistant at NYU. The football program was disbanded after his first season there and after spending the 1953 season as an assistant at Mount Carmel High School in Chicago, Dickson rejoined his NYU boss Hugh Devore at Dayton. Dickson's time at Dayton was short-lived as one month later he left Dayton to become the backfield coach at his alma mater, Notre Dame. After only one season, Dickson left Notre Dame and returned to Dayton.[tpl]cite news |title=Irish's Dickson Goes to Dayton |url= |quote= |work=Los Angeles Times  |date=August 24, 1955 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl]
After stints at Marquette, USC, Pacific, and Tulane, Dickson joined the pro ranks as an assistant with the Oakland Raiders of the American Football League.[tpl]cite news |title=Oakland Raiders Fire Eddie Erdelatz |url= |quote= |work=The Hartford Courant |date=September 19, 1961 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl] He returned to college football in 1962 as an assistant at Oklahoma.[tpl]cite news |author=AP |title=Sports In Brief |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=CWUhAAAAIBAJ&sjid=K4cFAAAAIBAJ&pg=563,3418907&dq |quote= |work=Tri-City Herald |date=August 26, 1962 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl]
In 1976, Dickson received his first head coaching position when he was hired by the CFL's Hamilton Tiger-Cats. After the Tiger-Cats lost all four of their preseason games and their first two regular season games under Dickson, General Manager Bob Shaw fired him and succeeded him as head coach.[tpl]cite news |author=Bob Hughes |title=CFL coaching survivors honor Bob Shaw |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=G05VAAAAIBAJ&sjid=dT4NAAAAIBAJ&pg=2732,1016308&dq |quote= |work=The Leader-Post |date=January 20, 1977 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl]
After his firing, Dickson served as an assistant coach at San Bernardino Valley College.[tpl]cite news |title=Names in the News |url= |quote= |work=Los Angeles Times |date=July 22, 1977 |accessdate=2010-12-31 [/tpl]
