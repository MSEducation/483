[[Calathea makoyana]]

CATEGORIES: Calathea, Flora of Brazil, House plants, garden plants, plants described in 1872

Calathea makoyana (peacock plant, cathedral windows) is a species of plant belonging to the genus Calathea in the family Marantaceae, native to eastern Brazil.Morren, Charles Jacques Ã‰douard. 1872. La Belgique Horticole 22: 321, Calathea makoyana 
Calathea makoyana is an evergreen perennial, growing to [tpl]convert|45|cm|0|abbr=on[/tpl], with round pale green leaves, the upper surfaces marked with dark green blotches along the veins, and the lower surfaces coloured deep purple. It requires a minimum temperature of [tpl]convert|16|C[/tpl], and in temperate areas is often cultivated as a houseplant.[tpl]cite book|title=RHS A-Z encyclopedia of garden plants|year=2008|publisher=Dorling Kindersley|location=United Kingdom|isbn=1405332964|pages=1136[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.desert-tropicals.com/Plants/Marantaceae/Calathea_makoyana.html|title=Peacock Plant (Calathea makoyana)|accessdate=2008-01-02|author=Philippe Faucon|year=2005[/tpl]
