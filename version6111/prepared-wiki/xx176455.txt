[[2009 IIHF World U18 Championships]]

CATEGORIES: 2009 in ice hockey, Fargo–Moorhead, IIHF World U18 Championships

The 2009 IIHF World U18 Championships were held in Fargo, North Dakota and Moorhead, Minnesota, United States. The championships ran from April 9 to April 19, 2009.[tpl]cite press release|title=2009 IIHF Under-18 Worlds hosts named|url=http://www.usahockey.com/Template_Usahockey.aspx?NAV=TU_04_02_04&id=220040|publisher=USA Hockey|date=13 February 2008|accessdate=2008-02-28[/tpl] Games were played at the Urban Plains Center in Fargo and the Moorhead Sports Center in Moorhead. Fargo-Moorhead defeated Providence, Rhode Island and St. Cloud, Minnesota for the rights to host the event.
The United States, as the host country, won their third gold medal in five years, defeating Russia 5–0 in the final.  Finland rounded out the podium with a 5–4 shootout win over Canada in the bronze medal game.
All 17 games played at the Urban Plains Center in Fargo were broadcast live by USA Hockey partner FASTHockey.com.  The gold medal game featuring the United States versus Russia was broadcast for free.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2009[/tpl]
