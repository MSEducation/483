==Biography==

Kristin Halbrook is an American author of young adult fiction, known for her novel Nobody But Us. She was born in Anaheim, California and moved to the Pacific Northwest with her family as a child. She attended the University of Washington in Seattle, studying English literature and Women's studies as an undergraduate and Secondary Education in graduate school. Halbrook has worked with Title 1 students from elementary to high school ages teaching writing and nutrition education, as a tutor, and as a freelance writer.[tpl]cite web|title=Kristin Halbrook: Bio|url=http://www.kristinhalbrook.com/p/bio.html|accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl] She is a cofounder of the young adult reading and writing website YA Highway.[tpl]cite web|title=YA Highway: who we are|url=http://www.yahighway.com/2009/05/who-we-are.html|accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Welcome Friends!|url=http://www.yahighway.com/2009/05/welcome-friends.html|work=YA Highway|accessdate=August 12, 2013|date=May 26, 2009[/tpl]
Halbrook currently resides in Seattle with her husband and three children. She enjoys playing sports, travel, food culture and attending music events.[tpl]cite web|last=Schick|first=Michal|title=Hypable Exclusive Author Interview: Kristin Halbrook|url=http://www.hypable.com/2012/12/18/hypable-exclusive-author-interview-kristin-halbrook/|accessdate=August 12, 2013|date=December 18, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Kristin Halbrook: Bio|url=http://www.kristinhalbrook.com/p/bio.html|accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Abraham|first=May|title=Author Interview: Kristin Halbrook|url=http://www.wovenmyst.com/2013/03/13/author-interview-kristin-halbrook-author-of-nobody-but-us/|work=Wovenmyst Magazine|accessdate=August 12, 2013|date=March 13, 2013[/tpl]

==Reception==

ALA Booklist writes that Nobody But Us is "raw, immediate, and utterly unflinching. Halbrook's debut features complex characters, and attempting to puzzle them out makes for a thought-provoking read."[tpl]cite journal|last=Kelley|first=Ann|title=Nobody but Us|journal=The Booklist|date=February 1, 2013|volume=109|issue=11|page=61|url=http://search.proquest.com.ezproxy.cul.columbia.edu/docview/1283338446/13FD9B358D3E88CF68/1?accountid=10226|accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl] Publishers Weekly calls it "a dark, romantic wild ride reminiscent of Thelma and Louise."[tpl]cite web|title=Nobody but Us|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-0-06-212126-4|work=Publishers Weekly|accessdate=August 12, 2013|date=December 24, 2012[/tpl]
In August 2013, it was announced that the novel has been optioned by Haven Entertainment for development as a feature film, under the title Will and Zoe. Short film director Leanne Welham has signed on to adapt the novel and direct the film, and Jesse Hara, Kevin Mann, and Pouya Shahbazian of New Leaf Literary will serve as executive producers.[tpl]cite news|last=McNarry|first=Dave|title=Kristin Halbrook's 'Nobody But Us' In Development as Movie|url=http://variety.com/2013/film/news/kristin-halbrooks-nobody-but-us-movie-1200576951/|accessdate=August 12, 2013|newspaper=Variety|date=August 12, 2013[/tpl]
