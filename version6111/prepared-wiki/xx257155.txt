</ref>  He met [[Nanasaheb Peshwe]] and [[Tatya Tope]] and started building armed organizations in [[Satara]], [[Kolhapur]], [[Sangli]], and [[Belgaon]].<ref>"Diamond Maharashtra Sankritikosh ([tpl]lang-mr|डायमंड महाराष्ट्र संस्कृतीकोश[/tpl])," Durga Dixit, Pune, India, Diamond Publications, 2009, ISBN 978-81-8483-080-4. </ref> However when his plan was exposed, many of the fighters he had recruited were killed <ref>
</ref>
and Gupte went underground. In 1857, he went to Thane to attend a religious ceremony at his relative Prabhakar Viththal Gupte's residence near Jambhali Naka. When British police arrived to arrest him, Gupte escaped in the disguise of an old woman and was never found again. In his memory the Jambhali Naka has been named as Rango Bapuji Chowk.[ref]
