[[Alexander Garnet Brown]]

CATEGORIES: Nova Scotia Liberal Party MLAs, Members of the Executive Council of Nova Scotia, 1930 births, 2010 deaths

Alexander Garnet Brown (May 2, 1930[tpl]cite web |title =Nova Scotia Government Hansard |url=http://www.gov.ns.ca/legislature/hansard/han59-1/house_05may19.htm|date=May 19, 2005 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl] – January 7, 2010[tpl]cite web |last=Jackson |first=David |title=Former Liberal MLA Garnet Brown dead at 79  |publisher=The Chronicle Herald |date=January 7, 2010 |url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/Front/9014733.html |accessdate=January 7, 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100111095934/http://thechronicleherald.ca/Front/9014733.html| archivedate= 11 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]), known as Garnet Brown, was a Canadian businessman and politician who served in the Nova Scotia House of Assembly from 1969 to 1978. He represented the electoral district of Halifax Eastern Shore as a Liberal.
Brown was an athlete in his youth, and was signed by the Brooklyn Dodgers, playing in their farm system for two years.[tpl]cite web |title=Obituary: BROWN - A. Garnet - Businessman, Community Activist |publisher=The Chronicle Herald |date=January 11, 2010 |url=http://jobspress.com/announcements/obituaries/brown-garnet-businessman-community-activist |accessdate=January 11, 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100114043058/http://jobspress.com/announcements/obituaries/brown-garnet-businessman-community-activist| archivedate= 14 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl]
In the 1950s, Brown and his father founded Atlantic Canada's largest food brokerage, A.G. Brown and Sons Ltd.[tpl]cite web |last=Jackson |first=David |title=Garnet Brown remembered for generosity, business acumen, work in Regan cabinet |publisher=The Chronicle Herald |date=January 9, 2010 |url=http://thechronicleherald.ca/NovaScotia/1161442.html |accessdate=January 11, 2010| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20100112131217/http://thechronicleherald.ca/NovaScotia/1161442.html| archivedate= 12 January 2010 | deadurl= no[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] He was also co-founder of Halifax Cablevision, and a founder and director of Dartmouth Cable Television.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.smu.ca/administration/archives/brown_g.html |title=Saint Mary's University bio |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
First elected in a by-election on July 8, 1969,[tpl]cite news |first=|last=|title=Election Return 1969|url=http://www.electionsnovascotia.ns.ca/results/byElections/By%20Election%201969.pdf|work=|publisher=Elections Nova Scotia|year=1970 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl] Brown was re-elected in 1970[tpl]cite news |first=|last=|title=Election Return 1970|url=http://electionsnovascotia.ns.ca/results/Elections/General%20Election%201970.pdf|work=|publisher=Elections Nova Scotia|year=1970 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl] and 1974.[tpl]cite news |first=|last=|title=Election Return 1974|url=http://electionsnovascotia.ns.ca/results/Elections/General%20Election%201974.pdf|work=|publisher=Elections Nova Scotia|year=1974 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl] He served until he was defeated in the 1978 general election.[tpl]cite news |first=|last=|title=Election Return 1978|url=http://electionsnovascotia.ns.ca/results/Elections/General%20Election%201978.pdf|work=|publisher=Elections Nova Scotia|year=1978 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl]
Brown served in the Executive Council of Nova Scotia for Premier Gerald Regan. His cabinet duties included Highways (1970–1972), Public Works (1970–1971), Recreation (1973–1978), and the Residential Tenancies Act (1972–1975).
In 2001, Brown was awarded a Doctorate of Commerce (honoris causa) by Saint Mary's University.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gov.ns.ca/legislature/hansard/han58-2/h01may14.htm |title=Nova Scotia Legislature Hansard |date=May 14, 2001 |accessdate=January 7, 2010[/tpl]
Brown died on January 7, 2010.
