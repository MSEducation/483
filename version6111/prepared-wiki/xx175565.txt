[[Turner Hall (Milwaukee, Wisconsin)]]

CATEGORIES: German-American culture in Wisconsin, National Historic Landmarks in Wisconsin, Cultural infrastructure completed in 1882, Buildings and structures in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, Music venues in Wisconsin, Visitor attractions in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, Clubhouses on the National Register of Historic Places in Wisconsin

Turner Hall is a site in Milwaukee, Wisconsin, significant for its association with the American Turners, a German-American association. The four story building was designed by architect Henry C. Koch. The interior includes a ballroom with balcony level, restaurant and beer hall and meeting rooms.
In 1994 the Governor proclaimed Turner Hall one of the "Ten Most Endangered Historic Sites" in Wisconsin. It was declared a National Historic Landmark in 1996.[tpl]citation|title=[tpl]PDFlink|National Historic Landmark Nomination: Turner Hall|401 KB[/tpl]|date=May, 1995 |author=Dawn Schumann and James H. Charleton |publisher=National Park Service[/tpl] and [tpl]PDFlink|Accompanying 10 photos, exterior and interior, from 1994.|1.26 MB[/tpl] In 2000, The Milwaukee Turners established the Turner Ballroom Preservation Trust to renovate and maintain the building.
It now hosts a wide variety of concerts. The Turner Hall Ballroom was awarded the "Venue of the Year" at the 2010 Wisconsin Area Music Industry (WAMI) awards.[tpl]cite news|title=First time event held outside Milwaukee|date=April 13, 2010|publisher=The Post Crescent|pages=A-3, A-6|accessdate=19 April 2010[/tpl]
