[[Armor Holdings]]

CATEGORIES: Defense companies of the United States, BAE Systems subsidiaries and divisions, Companies based in Jacksonville, Florida, Companies established in 1969, 1969 establishments in Florida

Armor Holdings, Inc. was a manufacturer of military, law enforcement and personnel safety equipment. It was acquired by BAE Systems on July 31, 2007[tpl]cite web |url= http://production.investis.com/armorholdings/aboutus/aboutarmor/ |title=About Armor Holdings â€“ BAE Systems|work=production.investis.com |year=2010 |accessdate=June 9, 2011| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20110713052651/http://production.investis.com/armorholdings/aboutus/aboutarmor/| archivedate= 13 July 2011 | deadurl= no[/tpl] and renamed BAE Systems Mobility & Protection Systems. The divisions have been reorganised within BAE Systems Land and Armaments.

==History==

Armor Holdings was founded in 1969 as American Body Armor and Equipment, Inc.Armor Holdings, Inc. -- Company Profile at Jacksonville, Florida. In January 1996 the company underwent a change in control;  Kanders Florida Holdings, Inc. and others purchased the stock held by the company's two largest shareholders. The Armor Holdings, Inc. name was adopted on August 21, 1996.
It acquired Fairfield, Ohio-based O'Gara-Hess & Eisenhardt in 2001.[tpl]cite news  |title = British company agrees to buy Armor Holdings |work = The Associated Press |date = 2007-05-08 |accessdate = 2007-05-22 | url = http://news.enquirer.com/apps/pbcs.dll/article?AID=/20070508/BIZ/705080340/1001/BIZ[/tpl]  and renamed it Centigon.
In 2003, it acquired Simula, Inc. a developer and producer of military seating systems, the Cockpit Air Bag System (CABS) for US Army helicopters, the Small Arms Protective Insert (SAPI) armor system, and parachutes.  Simula developed and produced the first crashworthy armored crew seats for the US Army UH-60 Black Hawk and AH-64 Apache helicopters.DoD SBIR Success Stories (Simula)Hoover's Profiles (Simula)Answers.com (Simula)
On August 2, 2005, Armor Holdings acquired Second Chance, a body armor manufacturing company, for $45 million.
In 2006, Armor Holdings obtained a $649 million order for the U.S. Army to manufacture tactical vehicle trucks.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.upi.com/Business_News/Security-Industry/2006/02/28/Armor-Holdings-gets-32M-contract/UPI-76271141153840/ |title=Armor Holdings gets $32M contract - UPI.com|work=upi.com |year=2011 |accessdate=June 28, 2011[/tpl]

===BAE acquisition===

On May 7, 2007 BAE Systems announced its subsidiary BAE Systems Inc. was to purchase Armor Holdings for US$4.1 billion (US$4.5 billion including net debt).[tpl]cite press release| title =BAE Systems plc announces proposed acquisition of Armor Holdings Inc.| publisher = BAE Systems plc| date = 2007-05-07| url = http://www.baesystems.com/BAEProd/groups/public/documents/bae_publication/bae_pdf_armor.pdf| format = PDF| accessdate = 2007-05-07| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20070616024943/http://www.baesystems.com/BAEProd/groups/public/documents/bae_publication/bae_pdf_armor.pdf| archivedate= 16 June 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl]  The acquisition was completed on July 31, 2007.[tpl]cite press release| title =BAE Systems completes acquisition of Armor Holdings Inc.| publisher = BAE Systems plc| date = 2007-07-31| url =http://www.baesystems.com/Newsroom/NewsReleases/autoGen_107631191035.html| format = PDF| accessdate = 2007-09-07| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20070930154544/http://www.baesystems.com/Newsroom/NewsReleases/autoGen_107631191035.html| archivedate= 30 September 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl]
Armor Holdings' three business units; Aerospace & Defense Group, the Products Group and the Mobile Security Division were merged into BAE Systems as Mobility & Protection Systems, BAE Systems Products Group, and BAE Systems Mobile Security respectively.About Armor Holdings

==Zylon Body Armor Settlement==

On October 7, 2008, the US Department of Justice announced a $30 million settlement with Armor Holdings Products LLC over claims that Armor Holdings knowingly violated the Federal False Claims Act by selling defective Zylon body armor to the government. This is part of a larger investigation of Zylon armor by the DOJ and the military.http://www.usdoj.gov/opa/pr/2008/October/08-civ-901.html
