[[Peak Charles National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1979, Goldfields-Esperance, 1979 establishments in Australia

Peak Charles National Park is a national park in Western Australia (Australia), [tpl]convert|507|km[/tpl] east of Perth and [tpl]convert|100|km|abbr=on[/tpl] north west of Esperance.[tpl]cite web|url=http://rac.com.au/Travel/Self-drive-holidays/Caravans-and-camping/National-park-camping-areas.aspx|title=RAC Travel - National park camping areas|year=2010|accessdate=29 May 2010[/tpl]
The park is named for one of the main features, Peak Charles, which is an ancient granite peak with an elevation of [tpl]convert|651|m|abbr=on[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.exploreaustralia.net.au/Western-Australia/Goldfields|title=Explore Australia - Goldfields|year=2009|accessdate=29 May 2010[/tpl]
that dominates the park along with its neighbour Peak Eleanora. Both afford excellent view over the park which is primarily composed of dry sand plain heaths and salt lake systems.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.about-australia.com/travel-guides/western-australia/australias-golden-outback/attractions/natural/peak-charles-national-park/|title=About Australia - Peak Charles National Park|year=2009|accessdate=29 May 2010[/tpl]
No entry fee applies to enter the park but there are few facilities available to tourists. A car park, campground and toilets are available.
