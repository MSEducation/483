[[Peter Greer]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1975 births, Messiah College alumni, John F. Kennedy School of Government alumni

Peter Keith Greer (born 1975) is a Christian advocate for the poor, an author, and the president of HOPE International (HOPE), a global faith-based microfinance organization based in Lancaster, Pennsylvania, serving entrepreneurs throughout Africa, Asia, the Caribbean and Eastern Europe.  Greer and his wife, Laurel, have two boys and a girl and live in Landisville, Pennsylvania."About HOPE Leadership: Peter Greer." HOPE International. HOPE International. Web. 20 Oct. 2009.

==Biography==

Greer was raised in Carlisle, Massachusetts, graduating from Concord-Carlisle High School (1993).  He is a graduate of Messiah College (B.S., 1997) and Harvard University’s Kennedy School of Government (M.P.P., 2004).  While traveling to Russia through Messiah’s International Business Institute, Greer was first introduced to microfinance and "quickly recognized it as a perfect opportunity to combine my interests in business and missions."[tpl]cite web|url=http://storylink.messiah.edu/?p=190|title=Alum uses microfinance to battle poverty.|work=Story Link|publisher=Messiah College|date=September 2, 2008|accessdate=March 14, 2010[/tpl]
Upon graduation, Greer spent two years in the business sector before World Relief hired him to work as a microfinance adviser in Phnom Penh, Cambodia. He also served as a technical adviser for Self-Help Development Foundation (CARE Zimbabwe) in Bulawayo, Zimbabwe.Greer, Peter; Smith, Phil. The Poor Will Be Glad: Joining the Revolution to Lift the World out of Poverty. Grand Rapids, Michigan: Zondervan, 2009. In 1999, he became the managing director for URWEGO Community Bank in Kigali, Rwanda, managing there for three years. After gaining experience in microfinance, he attended Harvard University, focusing one of his research projects on HOPE International’s work in the Democratic Republic of Congo.Strausbaugh, Judy A. "Greer brings his passion for economic aid to HOPE." Lancaster Sunday News. December 5, 2004: B5.
In 2004, Jeff Rutt, founder of HOPE International, invited Greer to take leadership of the non-profit.[tpl]cite web|title=The Keystone Difference|url=http://www.keystonecustomhome.com/Jeff-Rutt|publisher=Keystone Custom Homes|accessdate=26 August 2013[/tpl]  Under Greer’s leadership and Jeff Rutt's help, HOPE has expanded from having a presence in two countries, with an operating budget of $1.2 million to being in 14 countries, with an operating budget of $6.2 million in 2008.HOPE International. He has emphasized HOPE's partnerships with the local and global church as well as initiated HOPE's four distinctives: focus on microfinance, intentional witness for Christ, commitment to stewardship, and dedication to the hard places around the world. During his presidency, he co-founded the HOPE Global-Investment Fund in September 2008 with the intention of raising $6 million in private investments.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.christianitytoday.com/ct/2009/january/5.15.html|title=Microfinance, Now More Micro|date=December 17, 2008|accessdate=March 14, 2010|work=Christianity Today|first=Ken|last=Walker[/tpl]

==Role in international development==

As an advocate for the Church’s role in reaching the poor, Greer has been a featured speaker at leading Christian conferences, such as Catalyst, Urbana, Passion, and Jubilee, and he has been a frequent speaker for Harvard’s International Development Conference and Southern New Hampshire University’s School of Community Economic Development.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thepoorwillbeglad.com/about.html|title=About The Poor Will Be Glad|work=The Poor Will Be Glad|accessdate=March 12, 2009[/tpl]
