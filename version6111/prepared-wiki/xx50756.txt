Ramadi ([tpl]lang-ar|الرمادي[/tpl]; BGN: Ar Ramādī) is a city in central Iraq, about [tpl]convert|110|km|mi|sp=us[/tpl] west of Baghdad.  It is the capital of Al Anbar Governorate.http://www.globalsecurity.org/military/world/iraq/ramadiyah.htmjj. The city extends more than 60 kilometers along the Euphrates and is the largest city in Al-Anbar.

==History==

Ramadi is located in a fertile, irrigated, alluvial plain, within Iraq's Sunni Triangle.Henry Field, and Richard A. (Richard Arthur) Martin, The anthropology of Iraq, Chicago: Field Museum, 1940, p. 17. It was founded in 1869 under the Ottoman Empire. The main purpose of the city was to give the Ottomans a base for communications with and control of the Dulaim tribe of the region. 
During the Mesopotamian Campaign of World War I, British forces under Lieutenant General Frederick Stanley Maude took Ramadi.  In November 1917, British forces fought what was left of the Ottoman forces there.  Sir Maude died soon after Ramadi was taken.
During the Anglo-Iraqi War during World War II, Ramadi was held by a brigade-sized unit loyal to Rashid Ali.
Ramadi was a base of resistance to the U.S. occupation of Iraq between 2003 and 2006.
