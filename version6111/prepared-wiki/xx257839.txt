[[Clavelina robusta]]

CATEGORIES: Ascidiacea, Animals described in 1990

[tpl]italic title[/tpl][tpl]italictitle[/tpl]

Clavelina robusta is a species of tunicate (sea squirt), in the genus Clavelina (the "little bottles"). Like all ascidians, these sessile animals are filter feeders.

==Description==

This species frequently occurs in dense clusters. It has large 2-4 cm cylindrical zooids. They generally range from black to dark blue to gray in colour, with individual zooids having fluorescent green, yellow, or white rings around both siphons.Almost Us!? ==Distribution==

Clavelina robusta is found, often in large colonies,Allen, Gerald (2001), Marine Life of the Pacific and Indian Oceans, Periplus (Singapore), p. 62 in the Western Pacific, Australia, Indonesia, the Philippines, Japan, and the Solomon Islands.
