[[William A. Clark (soldier)]]

CATEGORIES: 1828 births, 1916 deaths, People of Pennsylvania in the American Civil War, Union Army soldiers, American Civil War recipients of the Medal of Honor

Corporal William A. Clark (July 24, 1828 - January 9, 1916) was an American soldier of the Civil War who, during a battle at Nolensville, Tennessee on 15 February 1863, successfully defended a wagon train. For his actions he earned the Medal of Honor. He served with the 2nd Minnesota Volunteer Infantry Division.[tpl]cite web | url=http://projects.militarytimes.com/citations-medals-awards/recipient.php?recipientid=3209 | title=William A. Clark | publisher=Military Times: Hall of Valor | accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl] He was born in Pennsylvania, lived in Shelbyville and is buried in Nicollet, Minnesota.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.cmohs.org/recipient-detail/259/clark-william-a.php | title=CLARK, WILLIAM A | publisher=Congressional Medal of Honor Society | accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]
