[[Weaver House (Cowie Corner, Virginia)]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Virginia, Houses completed in 1840, Buildings and structures in Greensville County, Virginia, National Register of Historic Places in Greensville County, Virginia

The Weaver House, also known as Waller, is a historic home located at Cowie Corner, Greensville County, Virginia. It was built between 1838 and 1840 for Jarrad Weaver on land which had belonged to the Waller family of Williamsburg.  It is a two-story, single-pile, wood frame residence.  It sits on a brick basement and has a standing-seam sheet metal gable roof.  The front facade features a hipped-roof porch with square columns.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dhr.virginia.gov/registers/Counties/Greensville/040-0006_Weaver_House_1982_Final_Nomination.pdf|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Weaver House|author=Virginia Historic Landmarks Commission Staff | date=June 1981|publisher=Virginia Department of Historic Resources[/tpl] and Accompanying photo
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.
