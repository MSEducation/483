[[USS Natalie Mae (SP-1005)]]

CATEGORIES: Patrol vessels of the United States Navy, World War I patrol vessels of the United States

USS ''Natalie Mae'' (SP-1005) was a United States Navy patrol vessel in commission during World War I.
Natalie Mae is known to have been assigned the section patrol number 1005SP-1005 ''Natalie Mae at NavSource Online: Section Patrol Craft (SP) and Civilian Vessels (ID) Index and to have operated on patrol duties in the 12th Naval District at San Diego, California, ca. December 1917 as USS Natalie Mae (SP-1005), occasionally in rotation with the patrol boats [tpl]USS|Albacore|SP-751[/tpl], [tpl]USS|Normannia|SP-756[/tpl], [tpl]USS|Rosa|SP-757[/tpl], and [tpl]USS|Nomad|SP-1046[/tpl].See the Dictionary of American Naval Fighting Ships entry for [tpl]USS|Nomad|SP-1046[/tpl] at http://www.history.navy.mil/danfs/n6/nomad.htm. However, no other records of Natalie Mae''[tpl]'[/tpl]s operational history have been found, nor have any records of her characteristics.
