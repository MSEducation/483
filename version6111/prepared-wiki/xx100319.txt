[[Department of the Environment (Australia)]]

CATEGORIES: Government departments of Australia, Conservation in Australia, Environment of Australia, Environment ministries, Water ministries, Water in Australia, Ministries established in 2013, Public policy in Australia, 2013 establishments in Australia

The Australian Department of the Environment is a department of the Government of Australia charged with the responsibility of developing and implementing national policy, programs and legislation to protect and conserve Australia's environment and heritage.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/index.html|title=Home Page|work=Department of the Environment|publisher=Government of Australia|date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl]

==Structure==

The department is an Australian Public Service Department of State in the environment portfolio, under the Public Service Act 1999.[tpl]citation|url=http://www.finance.gov.au/sites/default/files/FMACACFlipchart_0.pdf|publisher=Department of Finance|title=Chart of 109 Agencies under the FMA Act|date=1 January 2014|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20140304225331/http://www.finance.gov.au/sites/default/files/FMACACFlipchart_0.pdf|archivedate=4 March 2014|deadurl=no[/tpl]
The head of the department is the Secretary of the Department of the Environment, currently Dr Gordon de Brouwer [tpl]post-nominals|country=AUS|PSM[/tpl], who is responsible to the Minister for the Environment, currently the Hon. Greg Hunt [tpl]post-nominals |country=AUS|MP[/tpl] and to the Parliamentary Secretary to the Minister for the Environment, currently Senator the Hon. Simon Birmingham.

The department was formed by way of an Administrative Arrangements Order issued on 18 September 2013[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dpmc.gov.au/parliamentary/docs/aao_20130918.pdf|date=18 September 2013|title=Administrative Arrangements Order|date=18 September 2013|publisher=Commonwealth of Australia|accessdate=27 October 2013|work=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet|format=PDF[/tpl] and absorbed the responsibilities of the former Department of Sustainability, Environment, Water, Population and Communities (DSWEPaC)[tpl]cite web |url=http://australia.gov.au/directories/australia/environment |title=Department of the Environment |work=australia.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl] and climate change from the former Department of Industry, Innovation, Climate Change, Science, Research and Tertiary Education.[tpl]cite web |url=http://australia.gov.au/directories/australia/industry |title=Department of Industry |work=australia.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/national-affairs/tony-abbott-puts-broom-through-bureaucracy/story-fn59niix-1226721922686|title=Tony Abbott puts broom through bureaucracy |author=Packham, Ben|date=18 September 2013|newspaper=The Australian|accessdate=24 September 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite press release|title=The Coalition will restore strong, stable and accountable government|url=http://www.pm.gov.au/media/2013-09-18/coalition-will-restore-strong-stable-and-accountable-government|first=Tony|last=Abbott|author-link=Tony Abbott|date=18 September 2013|publisher=Commonwealth of Australia|work=Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet|location=Canberra, Australia|accessdate=20 September 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|author=Wilson, Lauren|newspaper=The Australian|title=Coalition carves up the public service|url=http://www.theaustralian.com.au/national-affairs/coalition-carves-up-public-service/story-fn59niix-1226722363408|date=19 September 2013|accessdate=24 September 2013[/tpl]

==Mission==

The stated aims of the department are to achieve the protection and conservation of the environment; to ensure that Australia benefits from meteorological and related sciences and services; and to see that Australia's interests in Antarctica are advanced. The department develops and implements national policy, programs and legislation to protect and conserve Australia's environment and heritage.
==Operational activities== 
The functions of the department are broadly classified into the following matters:

===Agencies===

The department and the following executive agencies and statutory authorities form the Australian Government's environment portfolio.

===Programs===

The department manages a number of major programs. The most significant of those dealing with natural resource management come under the umbrella of the Natural Heritage Trust and the National Action Plan for Salinity and Water Quality. Both the Trust and National Action Plan are administered jointly with the Department of Agriculture.

===Divisions===

Divisions of the department include the Australian Antarctic Division, Supervising Scientist Division, Heritage Division, Parks Australia, Policy and Communications, Australian Wildlife, Sydney Harbour Federation Trust, Australian Land and Coasts plus a number of executive agencies and statutory authorities.

===Environmental protection===

The Department of the Environment administers environmental laws, including the Environment Protection and Biodiversity Conservation Act 1999 and a range of other Acts. It is also responsible for Australia's participation in a number of international environmental agreements.

===Islands administration===

The department also administers areas of the Coral Sea Islands,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/marinereserves/coralsea/management.html |title=Coral Sea Commonwealth Marine Reserve - Management |work=environment.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl] Heard Island and the McDonald Islands,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/heritage/places/world/heard-mcdonald/information.html |title=Heard and McDonald Islands (HIMI) - World Heritage |work=environment.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl] and oversees certain policy areas in Norfolk Island[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/parks/norfolk/management/index.html |title=Norfolk Island National Park - Park Management |work=environment.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl] and Christmas Island.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.environment.gov.au/parks/christmas/management/index.html |title=Christmas Island National Park - Park Management |work=environment.gov.au |date= |accessdate=12 October 2013[/tpl]
