[[In Strict Confidence]]

CATEGORIES: Electro-industrial music groups, German musical groups, Musical groups established in 1989

In Strict Confidence is a German electro-industrial band. Formed in 1989/1990, they were known as Seal of Secrecy until 1992.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl]  In Strict Confidence has been signed to several record labels, including Metropolis Records and WTII Records.WTII Records Official Website - In Strict ConfidenceWTII Records Official Facebook page

==History==

In Strict Confidence started out as a quartet, but stripped down to a duo (Dennis Ostermann and JÃ¶rg Schelte) in 1992,[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl] releasing two demos tapes on the way to their debut release 'Cryogenix' on Zoth Ommog in 1996 (the initial pressing of this album sold out in one week), shortly followed by an EP 'Collapse'.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl] The band left the label after releasing follow-up effort 'Face The Fear' in 1998, releasing their third album 'Love Kills!' on Bloodline two years later.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl]
Founding member Stefan Vesper then re-joined the group in time for 'Mistrust the Angels' in 2002 with subsequent albums 'Holy' and 'Exile Paradise' appearing at two-year intervals.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl] These albums saw the project's sound diversify further, notably with increasing use of female vocals from Antje Schulz, vocalist of the band Chandeen[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl], and the addition of female vocalist Nina de Lianin[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.instrictconfidence.de/english/neues/index.shtml|title=In Strict Confidence - NEW FEMALE SINGER "NINA DE LIANIN" |publisher=In Strict Confidence|accessdate=6 March 2010[/tpl]
Dennis Ostermann has a side project called Controlled Fusion.  Stefan Vesper has produced material under his own name and also as Steve Dragon.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2010[/tpl]
