[[Flemming Jørgensen]]

CATEGORIES: 1947 births, 2011 deaths, People from Randers, Danish male singers, Danish actors, Deaths from heart failure, Deaths from myocardial infarction

Flemming "Bamse" Duun Jørgensen (7 February 1947[tpl]spaced ndash[/tpl]1 January 2011) was a Danish pop singer and actor, best known as lead singer of the band Bamses Venner ([tpl]lang-en|Teddy (Bear)'s Friends[/tpl]). During the recent years he also released some solo albums, the latest being Tæt på ([tpl]lang-en|Close-up[/tpl]) from 2010. Bamse was part of the Danish music scene for more than 35 years, and sold more than 3.5 million albums.[tpl]cite web |last=Blak |first=Poul |title=Portræt: Vennernes Bamse |url=http://kpn.dk/popmusik/article2292484.ece |publisher=Jyllands-Posten |language=Danish |accessdate=1 January 2011 |date=1 January 2011[/tpl]
Flemming "Bamse" Jørgensen occasionally worked as an actor and in 1986 he received a Robert Award for best male supporting actor of the year in the movie Ofelia kommer til byen ([tpl]lang-en|Ophelia comes to town[/tpl]).[tpl]cite web |last=Christensen|first=Uffe |title=En musikkens arbejdsmand er død |url=http://kpn.dk/popmusik/article2292472.ece |publisher=Jyllands-Posten |language=Danish |accessdate=1 January 2011 |date=1 January 2011[/tpl] Flemming "Bamse" Jørgensen died a month before his 64th birthday in the early hours of New Year's Day 2011 of a cardiac arrest in his home in Egå, a suburb to Aarhus.[tpl]cite web |title=Musikeren Flemming Bamse er død - Danmark |url=http://www.berlingske.dk/danmark/musikeren-flemming-bamse-er-doed |work=Ritzau |publisher=Berlingske Tidende |language=Danish |accessdate=1 January 2011 |date=1 January 2011[/tpl]
