[[Ntambanana Local Municipality]]

CATEGORIES: Local Municipalities of uThungulu

Ntambanana is an administrative area in the uThungulu District of KwaZulu-Natal in South Africa.
The municipality is named after the Ntambanana River, which passes through the municipality.South African Languages - Place names
The Ingonyama Trust owns 85% of the land in the municipal area, with the remaining 15% being privately owned by commercial farmers.[tpl]cite web|title=Home|url=http://www.ntambanana.org.za/|publisher=Ntambanana Municipal Website|accessdate=12 September 2013[/tpl]

==Politics==

The municipal council consists of sixteen members elected by mixed-member proportional representation. Eight councillors are elected by first-past-the-post voting in eight wards, while the remaining eight are chosen from party lists so that the total number of party representatives is proportional to the number of votes received. In the election of 18 May 2011 no party obtained a majority, and the African National Congress (ANC) and the National Freedom Party (NFP) formed a coalition to govern the municipality.
