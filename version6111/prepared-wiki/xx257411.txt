[[Jeffrey L. Wilson]]

CATEGORIES: 1974 births, American bloggers, American non-fiction writers, Living people, Video game critics

Jeffrey L. Wilson (born May 12, 1974, Fort Bragg, North Carolina) is a consumer technology/video game editor and speaker who has written for several publications in the past decade, including 1UP.com, AOL, E-Gear, LifeStyler, LAPTOP, Parenting, PC Mag, Sync, Wise Bread, and WWE Magazine.
Wilson is currently a resident of New York City, New York, where he works as a software analyst for PC Mag, specializing in mobile apps, video games, streaming music services, digital comics, and desktop utilities.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.pcmag.com/author_bio/0,1908,a%253D5045,00.asp|title=PC Mag | work=PC Magazine[/tpl] He graduated from CUNY Brooklyn College in May 2002, receiving a BA in English Literature.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.jeffreylwilson.net|title=Jeffrey L. Wilson's homepage[/tpl] 
Wilson founded 2D-X, an independent Web site devoted to video games and video game culture.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.2d-x.com/author/admin/|title=2D-X[/tpl] The site was birthed from his love of 2D video games and his frustrations with overly complicated control schemes of some modern video games.[tpl]cite web|url=http://heybrooklyn.com/444/jeffrey-wilson/|title=Hey Brooklyn[/tpl]
Wilson spoke at New York Comic Con 2013's "Geeks of Color Assemble: Minorities in Fandom" panel, addressing minority representation in video games and comics.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.racialicious.com/2013/10/17/nycc-panel-recap-geeks-of-color-assemble-minorities-in-fandom/|title=Racialicious[/tpl]
