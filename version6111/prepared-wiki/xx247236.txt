[[Nettleton, Wiltshire]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Wiltshire, Roman sites in England, Archaeological sites in Wiltshire

Nettleton is a village and civil parish about [tpl]convert|6.5|mi|km[/tpl] northwest of Chippenham in Wiltshire, England. To the southeast is Nettleton Mill, which was part of the Castle Combe estate.

==Lugbury long barrow==

Lugbury is a chambered long barrow about [tpl]convert|0.75|mi|km[/tpl] east of the village.Pevsner & Cherry, 1975, page356 Excavations in the 19th century found 28 human skeletons in its chambers.

==Shrine of Apollo Cunomaglus==

South-west of Brotton Hill wood, where the Fosse Way crosses the Broadmead Brook, in the north-west of the parish, remains of a Romano-British settlement of about 30 buildings have been found.[tpl]cite web | title = Wiltshire Community History: Nettleton | url= http://history.wiltshire.gov.uk/community/getcom2.php?id=168[/tpl] This was excavated between 1956 and 1971; it is now field and woodland again. It was interpreted as a religious site including an octagonal shrine some 10 metres in diameter. Apollo Cunomaglus may have been its overall patron; dedications to other deities, probably including Diana, Mercury, and Mars with his Gaulish consort Rosmerta, were also found.Wedlake, 1982
