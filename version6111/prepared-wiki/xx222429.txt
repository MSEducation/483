[[Sir Charles Mills, 1st Baronet]]

CATEGORIES: 1792 births, 1872 deaths, Baronets in the Baronetage of the United Kingdom, Members of the Council of India, Directors of the British East India Company

Sir Charles Mills, 1st Baronet (23 January 1792 – 4 October 1872) was a British banker and member of the Council of India.
Born at Popes, Hatfield, he was the third son of William Mills, a director of the Honourable East India Company, and the younger brother of John Mills.
[tpl]cite book | title=Modern English Biography v. 2 | first=Frederic | last=Boase | publisher=Netherton & Worth | year=1897 | page=890 | url=http://books.google.com/?id=_IVmAAAAMAAJ | accessdate=2008-10-04[/tpl]
Like his father, he was connected with the banking firm of Glyn, Mills and Company, in conjunction with Sir Richard Glyn, 1st Baronet and later his son Lord Wolverton and grandson George Grenfell Glyn.[tpl]cite journal | title=Banking Obituary | journal=The Bankers' magazine and journal of the money market | publisher=Richard Groombridge | year=1872 | pages=972–973 | url=http://books.google.com/?id=j5EEAAAAQAAJ&pg=PA972 | accessdate=2008-10-04[/tpl]
On 28 August 1822, he was appointed a director of the East India Company, retaining the post until 1858. Upon the liquidation of the Company by the Government of India Act 1858, he was appointed to the Council of India, acting as a financial adviser to the Secretary of State for India until resigning in 1868. He was created a baronet, of Hillingdon Court, Middlesex, on 17 November 1868,[tpl]London Gazette |issue=23439 |date=10 November 1868 |startpage=5812[/tpl] for his services on the council.
In 1825, he married Emily Cox, daughter of the banker Richard Henry Cox, of Hillingdon House, Middlesex. Sir Charles had Hillingdon Court built nearby to serve as the Mills family home.Pearce 2007, p.44 Their son Charles Henry followed his father into banking and was later raised to the Peerage as Baron Hillingdon.  He died in 1872 at Hillingdon Court, having acquired a large estate there.
