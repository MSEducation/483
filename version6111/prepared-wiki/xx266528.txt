[[Chris Russell]]

CATEGORIES: 1989 births, Living people, People from Newport, Isle of Wight, English cricketers, Worcestershire cricketers

Christopher James Russell (born 16 February 1989) is an English cricketer.  Russell is a right-handed batsman who bowls right-arm medium-fast.  He was born in Newport on the Isle of Wight.
Having played for the Worcestershire Second XI since 2008,[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/354/354438/all_teams.html|title=Teams Chris Russell played for|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] Russell made his full debut for Worcestershire in a List A match against the Unicorns in the 2010 Clydesdale Bank 40, claiming his maiden List A wicket when he dismissed Chris Murtagh.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Scorecards/263/263000.html|title=Worcestershire v Unicorns, 2010 Clydesdale Bank 40|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  He made a further appearance in that competition against Surrey.[tpl]cite web|url=http://cricketarchive.com/Archive/Players/354/354438/List_A_Matches.html|title=List A Matches played by Chris Russell|publisher=CricketArchive|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]  Russell didn't feature for Worcestershire in the 2011 season. A notable appearance in the 2012 season saw Russell gain match figures of 6-72 in a drawn two-day tour match against South Africa - only days after England had only managed to take two wickets in a five day test match.
