[[Burmese general election, 1951–1952]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in Burma, 1952 in Burma, 1951 in Burma, 1952 elections in Asia, 1951 elections in Asia

Burma's first general elections held as an independent state took place over several months between June 1951 and April 1952, due to internal conflict within the country.[tpl]cite book|last=Taylor|first=Robert H.|title=The Politics of elections in Southeast Asia|publisher=Cambridge University Press|location=Cambridge, UK|year=1996|pages=173|isbn=978-0-521-56443-4[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Hoffmann|first=Mark S|title=World almanac and book of facts, Volume 69|publisher=Newspaper Enterprise Association|year=1954|page=338[/tpl] 
The Anti-Fascist People's Freedom League (AFPFL) won 60% of the vote and 147 out of 250 seats. Turnout for the election was low at 20%, as only 1.5 million voters out of an eligible 8 million participated.[tpl]cite book|last=Rotberg|first=Robert I|title=Burma: prospects for a democratic future|publisher=Brookings Institution Press|year=1998|page=43|isbn=978-0-8157-7581-2[/tpl] It was the lowest turnout for a Burmese election since the 1920s boycotts in colonial Burma.
