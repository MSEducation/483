[[Chollar Mansion]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in Nevada, Italianate architecture in Nevada, Houses completed in 1861, Storey County, Nevada

The Chollar Mansion, at 565 S. D St. in Virginia City, Nevada, is a historic Italianate-style house that was built in 1861.  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1993.
It was designed by N. J. Coleman and was built when Virginia City was a mining boom town.  It was moved about [tpl]convert|.25|mi[/tpl] in 1870 due to mining needs.  In 1993, the house was used as a bed and breakfast inn.[tpl]cite web|url=http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Text/93000689.pdf |title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Chollar Mansion |author=Bernadette Francke |date=March 1, 1993 |publisher=National Park Service[/tpl] and accompanying four photos from 1993
It was deemed significant for its association with William "Billy" Chollar, miner whose Chollar Mine, later merged with Potosi Mine to form the Chollar-Potosi Mine, tapped part of the Comstock Lode, and yielded enormous amounts of silver ore.  It is also significant for its fine Italianate architecture.
