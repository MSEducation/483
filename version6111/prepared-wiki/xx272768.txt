[[Cophecheilus bamen]]

CATEGORIES: Cyprinidae, Monotypic fish genera, Freshwater fish of China, Animals described in 2011

Cophecheilus bamen is a species of fish in the family Cyprinidae, the carps and minnows. It is the only member of the monotypic genus Cophecheilus bamen. It was described in 2011 from a tributary of the Zou-Jiang, a river in the Pearl River drainage in Jingxi County, Guangxi, China.Zhu, Y., et al. (2011). Cophecheilus bamen, a new genus and species of labeonine fishes (Teleostei: Cyprinidae) from South China. Zootaxa 2881, 39-50. The species name bamen is from Ba Men, the local common name for this and similar fish.
This fish is distinguished from related species by the morphology of its mouth, including its lips and jaws. Specimens examined so far were an average of about 7.8 centimeters long. The species has an elongated, laterally compressed body, two pairs of long barbels, and a forked caudal fin.
