[[Farali potatoes]]

CATEGORIES: Potato dishes, Indian cuisine

Farali dry potatoes or farali potatoes ([tpl]lang-hi|फराली आलू की सब्जी|[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-gu|ફરાળી બટાટા ની સબ્જી[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-mr|फराळी आलू कि सब्जी[/tpl]) is a dish in Indian cuisine. It is a dish which can be eaten during fasting ("farali" refers to fasting). It is based on potatoes (called aloo in Hindi and Urdu) and contains cumin seeds, groundnut powder, and also includes oil and sugar. Salt is typically not used,[tpl]citation needed|date=August 2013[/tpl] and cumin may be used in preparing the dish.

==Variations==

Sabudana vadas is a type of deep fried farali potato dish that is prepared with green chili peppers, potatoes and sabudanas.[tpl]cite web | url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2012-08-13/food-reviews/33181415_1_janmastami-buff-wada-farali-food | title=A farali food fiesta on Janmastami | publisher=The Times of India | date=August 13, 2012 | accessdate=14 August 2013 | author=Trivedi, Tanvi[/tpl]
Buff wada is a variant of the farali potato dish consisting of potato balls, stuffed with masala (mixed spices),  are deep-fried.
