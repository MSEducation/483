[[You Jin]]

CATEGORIES: 1950 births, Singaporean writers, Living people

Tham Yew Chin, known by her pseudonym You Jin, (born 1950) is a Singaporean writer. She received the Cultural Medallion Award in 2009 for her contributions to Singapore's literary arts scene.

==Early life and family==

Tham was born 1950 in Ipoh, Malaysia. Her family relocated to Singapore when she was eight years old. Moving to Singapore, she suffered from a language barrier as she only spoke Cantonese. Her father was a construction worker, and her paternal grandfather was an immigrant from then-poverty-torn China.[tpl]cite book|pages=349—352 |title=My Lifelong Challenge: Singapore's Bilingual Journey |last=Lee |first=Kuan Yew |isbn=9789814342032 |publisher=Straits Times Press |year=2012 [/tpl]

==Career==

Tham has published close to 160 literary works under the pseudonym of You Jin.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.epigrambooks.sg/?p=5272 |title=Teaching Cats to Jump Hoops |publisher=Epigram Books |accessdate=August 10, 2013 [/tpl] In 2009, she received the Cultural Medallion for literary arts.[tpl]cite book|author=Editions Didier Millet|title=Singapore at Random|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=NhVXH9N1GcgC&pg=PA141|year=2011|publisher=Editions Didier Millet|isbn=978-981-4260-37-4|pages=141–[/tpl] Tham contributed an essay titled A Fish in Water for former Prime Minister of Singapore Lee Kuan Yew's 2012 book, My Lifelong Challenge: Singapore's Bilingual Journey.
