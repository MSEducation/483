[[Uzeir]]

CATEGORIES: Arab villages in Israel, Al-Batuf Regional Council, Populated places in North District (Israel)

Uzeir ([tpl]lang-ar|عزير[/tpl]; [tpl]lang-he-n|עֻזֵיר[/tpl]) is an Arab village in northern Israel. Located near Nazareth Illit in the Lower Galilee, it falls under the jurisdiction of al-Batuf Regional Council. In 2006 it had a population of 2,600.Population of localities numbering above 1,000 residents Central Bureau of Statistics

==History==

Findings from the Roman, Byzantine and Early Islamic periods have been found in the village.Cinamon, 2012, ‘Uzeir, West
In the late Ottoman period, in 1875, the French explorer Victor Guérin reached the village and described it as consisting of about 20 houses on the side of a hill. A few old columns were remains after an ancient site which had preceded the present village. A Muslim maquam with two small domes, dedicated to "Neby A'ouzeir"  was close by.Guérin, 1880, pp. 364 ff In the 1881 "Survey of Western Palestine", the village (called El Azeir) was described as:  A stone village at the foot of the hill. The plain to the north is cultivated with cotton, barley, etc. There are about 150 Moslems in the village. Water is supplied by cisterns in the village, and a tank.Conder and Kitchener, 1881, vol. 1, p. 362 
In a 1922 census conducted by the British authorities, 'Uzair had a population of 70, all Muslims,[tpl]cite book | editor = J. B. Barron | title = Palestine: Report and General Abstracts of the Census of 1922 | publisher = Government of Palestine | year = 1923 | at = Table XI[/tpl] while in the 1931 census, 'Uzeir had 15 occupied houses and population of 88, still all Muslim.1931 British Mandate Census. p. 76. In 1945 the population was 150 while the total land area was 766 dunams, according to an official land and population survey.Government of Palestine, Department of Statistics. Village Statistics, April, 1945. Quoted in S. Hadawi, Village Statistics, 1945. PLO Research Center, 1970, p. 63 Of this, 737 dunams were allocated for cereals,Government of Palestine, Department of Statistics. Village Statistics, April, 1945. Quoted in S. Hadawi, Village Statistics, 1945. PLO Research Center, 1970, p. 110 while 7 dunams were classified as built-up areas.Government of Palestine, Department of Statistics. Village Statistics, April, 1945. Quoted in S. Hadawi, Village Statistics, 1945. PLO Research Center, 1970, p. 160
In 1948, Uzeir was captured by the Israeli army during the second part of Operation Dekel, 15-18 July. The village remained under Martial Law until 1966.
