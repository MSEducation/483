Christopher Kevin Boyle (23 May 1943 – 25 December 2010) was a Northern Ireland-born human rights activist, barrister and educator. He was among the first in the academic law community to engage in human rights activism.[tpl]cite news|first=Carol |last=Coulter |title=Tributes to human rights lawyer Kevin Boyle |url=http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/ireland/2010/1229/1224286423017.html |publisher=The Irish Times |type=obituary |date=29 December 2010 |accessdate=26 January 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|publisher=Times Higher Education |url=http://www.timeshighereducation.co.uk/story.asp?sectioncode=26&storycode=414808&c=2 |title=Kevin Boyle, 1943–2010 |type=obituary |date=31 January 2011 |accessdate=26 January 2012[/tpl]
Born and brought up in Newry, Boyle studied law at Queen's University Belfast. He was a lecturer in law at Queen's when he took part in the 1969 People's Democracy march from Belfast to Derry which was attacked by loyalists at Burntollet.[tpl]cite news|first=Alf |last=McCreary |url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/news/local-national/northern-ireland/obituary-kevin-boyle-inspirational-figure-behind-original-civil-rights-movement-15042748.html#ixzz1A05e19K9 |title=Obituary: Kevin Boyle – Inspirational figure behind original civil rights movement |publisher=Belfast Telegraph |type=obituary |date=31 December 2010 |accessdate=26 January 2012[/tpl] He was later involved in the Northern Ireland Civil Rights Association. Boyle's brother, Louis Boyle, also an alumnus of Queen's, was at the time active as a Catholic Unionist, and unsuccessfully sought to be the Unionist and Conservative candidate for South Down in the 1969 Stormont elections.
In the 1970s Boyle took up a post at University College, Galway (UCG, now the National University of Ireland, Galway). He became dean of the Faculty of Law in 1978, and in 1980 established the UCG human rights centre. In the 1980s he helped to develop the Essex Human Rights Law Centre, founded by Professor Malcolm Shaw, at the University of Essex in Colchester, England.[tpl]cite news|title=Founder of Colchester's Human Rights Centre dies |publisher=Essex County Standard |first=James |last=Calnan |url=http://www.essexcountystandard.co.uk/news/ecsnews/8760754.Founder_of_Colchester_s_Human_Rights_Centre_dies/?ref=rss |date=29 December 2010 |accessdate=26 January 2013[/tpl]
Boyle was involved in several missions on behalf of Amnesty International in the 1980s. Later, he served as the first director of the human rights NGO Article 19 from 1986–1989.[tpl]cite news|first=Caroline |last=Moorehead |title=New body set up to attack censorship / Launch of Article 19 organization |publisher=The Times (London) |date=31 October 1986 |accessdate=26 January 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Human rights academic Kevin Boyle dies |publisher=RTÉ News |url=http://www.rte.ie/news/2010/1228/boylek.html |date=28 December 2010 |accessdate=26 January 2013[/tpl] In 1990 he became director of the Human Rights Centre at Essex, holding that position until 2001.
In 1998, Boyle was named Liberty's Lawyer of the Year, alongside Françoise Hampson, for their work advancing human rights claims before the European Court of Human Rights.Geoff Gilbert, et al. "The Common Introduction", The Delivery of Human Rights: Essays in Honour of Professor Sir Nigel Rodley, pg. xxvi (2010) He was based in Geneva from 2001–2002 as a special advisor to Mary Robinson during her time as UN High Commissioner for Human Rights, returning to Essex University Human Rights Centre, where he again served as director in 2006–07.
Boyle was called to the bar in Northern Ireland, in the Republic of Ireland, and in England and Wales.
