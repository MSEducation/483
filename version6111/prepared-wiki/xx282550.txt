[[Evelyn Cusack]]

CATEGORIES: 1964 births, Living people, People from County Laois, RTÉ television presenters

 

Evelyn Cusack is Deputy Head of Forecasting at Met Éireann, Secretary of the Irish Meteorological Society, and a well known RTÉ weather presenter.

==Personal Life==

Cusack was born in Clonaslee, County Laois, the middle child of a family of seven.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/tv/failtetowers/evelyncusack.html|title=RTÉ Television - Fáilte Towers|accessdate=10 August 2013 | work=RTÉ News[/tpl] She has a bachelor's degree in physics and mathematics from University College Dublin, and also holds a master's degree in physics.
Cusack's driving license was suspended for two years in 2004 following her conviction for drink driving.

==Career==

Cusack has been a weather presenter on RTÉ since 1988.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/weather/presenters/cusacke.html|title=The Weather Team - Evelyn Cusack|accessdate=10 August 2013 | work=RTÉ News[/tpl] In 1999, Cusack and other well-known weather presenters such as Gerry Fleming, who were all professional meteorologists, were temporarily let go from their jobs at RTÉ in favour of younger, less qualified presenters. However, after protest from viewers, Cusack and her colleagues were swiftly rehired.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.independent.ie/irish-news/met-storm-forced-tv-bosses-to-change-tack-and-save-face-26136704.html|title=Met storm forced TV bosses to change tack and save face|date=28 October 1999|work=Irish Independent|accessdate=10 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2008, she also participated in RTÉ reality TV show Fáilte Towers. The show involved thirteen celebrities running a hotel for sixteen days and nights in order to win money for their designated charities. Cusack was the fifth to be eliminated.
