==Location==

Sveti Srdj emerged near the Benedictine Shirgj Monastery, an abbey dedicated to Sergius and Bacchus, and built in the 11th century. Due to its favorable geographical position near the mouth of river Bojana this new market soon became a small populated place which significance surpassed many bigger towns of the region.[tpl]cite book|author=Zarij M. Bešić|title=Istorija Črne Gore: Od kraja XII do kraqja XV vijeka. 2 pts|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=t2QNAQAAIAAJ|accessdate=10 August 2013|year=1970|publisher=Red. za istoriju Črne Gore|page=31|quote=Свети Срђ је чак, кад се мјери улога у привредном животу, оставио далеко за собом неке од мањих старих градова.[/tpl]

==History==

Since the reign of Stefan Nemanja in the Serbian Grand Principality[tpl]cite book|author=Ivo Smoljan|title=Neretva|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=RS1pAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=11 August 2013|year=1988|publisher=Klek|page=216|quote=To ograničenje postoji od Nemanjina vremena, kad su bila samo četiri trgovišta i to: Drijeva (Gabela), Dubrovnik, Kotor i Sveti Srđ na Bojani.[/tpl] Sveti Srdj was one of four markets allowed to trade salt (the other three being Kotor and Drijeva while Dubrovnik joined them after it was established as Republic in mid 14th century[tpl]cite book|author1=Gertrud Krallert|author2=Institut zur Erforschung des Deutschen Volkstums im Süden und Südosten in München|author3=Südost-Institut München|coauthors=Universität Passau. In 1330 near Sveti Srdj, the King of Serbia Stefan Dečanski met with envoys of Dubrovnik who congratulated him for his victory in the Battle of Velbazhd. On that occasion Dečanski asked them to support his military campaigns with 6 galleys.[tpl]cite book|last=Maletić|first=Mihailo|title=Crna Gora|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=03sBAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=21 December 2013|year=1976|publisher=Književne novine|page=139[/tpl] After the collapse of the Serbian Empire in 1371 Sveti Srdj belonged to Zeta until 1392 when Ottomans captured Zeta's lord Đurađ II Balšić. That way Venetian takeover ended centuries long monopolistic status of Sveti Srdj in salt trading in wider region around Bojana which it had for centuries while it was in Serbia.[tpl]cite book|title=Balcanica|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=v3EvAQAAMAAJ|accessdate=11 August 2013|year=1970|publisher=Srpska Akademija Nauka i Umetnosti, Balkanolos̆ki Institut|page=250[/tpl]
A peace treaty signed in Sveti Srdj in 1423 ended the Second Scutari War waged between Serbian Despotate (initially Zeta) and Venetian Republic over Scutari and other former possessions of Zeta controlled by Venice. This treaty is known as the Peace of Sveti Srdj.[tpl]citation | first = John Van Antwerp | last = Fine |title = The Late Medieval Balkans: A Critical Survey from the Late Twelfth Century to the Ottoman Conquest | publisher = University of Michigan Press | year = 1994 |page=519|quote= |isbn = 978-0-472-08260-5 | url=http://books.google.rs/books?id=LvVbRrH1QBgC&pg=PA519&lpg=PA519&dq=%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B0%D0%BD+%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B7%D0%B0%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%9B+1422&source=bl&ots=9gPXVyz0re&sig=De9Cdlm8qyLhoAj6Q4pNuH6XlCQ&hl=sr&sa=X&ei=do4ZUMKMCciGswb60IGICg&ved=0CFkQ6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B5%D1%84%D0%B0%D0%BD%20%D0%BB%D0%B0%D0%B7%D0%B0%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%B2%D0%B8%D1%9B%201422&f=false[/tpl] While it was in Venetian hands the salt traded in Sveti Srdj had to be transported from, also Venetian controlled, Corfu.[tpl]cite book|title=Balcanica|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=v3EvAQAAMAAJ|accessdate=11 August 2013|year=1970|publisher=Srpska Akademija Nauka i Umetnosti, Balkanolos̆ki Institut|page=259|quote=Kao i deceniju ranije, za Sveti Srđ i Kotor, morala se s0 dovoziti sa Krfa.[/tpl]
In 1479 Ottomans captured the remaining part of the region of northern Albania and this market soon became deserted.
