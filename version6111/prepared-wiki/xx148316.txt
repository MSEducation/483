[[Patchewollock]]

CATEGORIES: Towns in Victoria (Australia), Mallee (Victoria), Australian soldier settlements

Patchewollock is a town in north-west Victoria, Australia. At the 2006 census, Patchewollock and the surrounding area had a population of 322.[tpl]Census 2006 AUS | id = SSC26417 | name = Patchewollock (State Suburb) | accessdate = 2007-10-01 | quick = on[/tpl]  
The name Patchewollock originated from two Aboriginal words: putje, plenty, and wallah, porcupine grass.[tpl]cite web|title=Patchewollock|publisher=Yarriambiack Shire Council|url=http://www.yarriambiack.vic.gov.au/tourism/patchewollock|accessdate=1 September 2012[/tpl]
The town was first established after the First World War when soldier settlement blocks were appearing in the area. A Post Office (originally a Receiving Office) opened on 27 July 1920  [tpl]Citation| last = Premier Postal History  | title = Post Office List | url = https://www.premierpostal.com/cgi-bin/wsProd.sh/Viewpocdwrapper.p?SortBy=VIC&country= | accessdate = 2008-04-11 [/tpl] Previous localities of Baring [tpl]coord|35|24|S|142|04|E|region:AU-VIC_type:city|name=Baring[/tpl] with a post office open from 1926 to 1948, and Dering [tpl]coord|35|22|S|142|19|E|region:AU-VIC_type:city|name=Dering[/tpl] with a post office open from 1923 to 1949, lie within that part of the Patchewollock locality in the Rural City of Mildura.
As at 2011, the township maintained a pub and a post office.
