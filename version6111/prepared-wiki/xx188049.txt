William Charles Wentworth[/tpl] In 1870 he purchased a Cornetcy in the Royal Horse Guards.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=23581 |date=28 January 1870 |startpage=536[/tpl] He was promoted Lieutenant in 1871[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=23876 |date=16 July 1872 |startpage=3197[/tpl] and Captain in 1878.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=24634 |date=18 October 1878 |startpage=5609[/tpl]

He retired from the Army in 1883.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=25223 |date=20 April 1883 |startpage=2111[/tpl] An expert on horses, in 1901 he was appointed Master of the Stables and Extra Equerry to the Prince of Wales.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=27378 |date=19 November 1901 |startpage=7472[/tpl][tpl]LondonGazette |issue=27384 |date=6 December 1901 |startpage=8711 |supp=yes[/tpl] On the latter's accession as King George V in 1910 he was promoted to Crown Equerry and Secretary to the Master of the Horse,[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=28402 |date=29 July 1910 |startpage=5498[/tpl] a post he held until his retirement in 1924.
