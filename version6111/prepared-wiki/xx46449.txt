[[Bertrix]]

CATEGORIES: Municipalities of Luxembourg (Belgium)

Bertrix (Walloon: Bietris) is a Walloon municipality of Belgium located in the province of Luxembourg. 
On 1 January 2007 the municipality, which covers 137.7 km², had 8,164 inhabitants, giving a population density of 59.3 inhabitants per km².
 

==Climate==

 
 

==Twin towns==

Bertrix is twinned with Charmes, France (since 1968), Rusca Montana (Romania) (since 1990) and Church Point, Louisiana (United States) (since 1992).

==Transport==

Bertrix railway station, in the east of the town, has regular train services to Libramont, Dinant and Virton. On week-days trains run via Virton and Aubange to Arlon.

==Air Base==

Bertrix hosts the NATO reserve Jehonville Air Base.
