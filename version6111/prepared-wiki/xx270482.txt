[[Scratchgravel Hills]]

CATEGORIES: Landforms of Lewis and Clark County, Montana

The Scratchgravel Hills, el. [tpl]convert|5233|ft|m[/tpl] are a small summit of hills northwest of Helena in Lewis and Clark County, Montana. The area has seen increased development and a drop in water level in recent years. The Scratchgravel Hills have alluvial deposits on top of faulted granitic bedrock.[tpl]cite web|title=GWIP Project Area: Scratchgravel Hills, Lewis and Clark County|format=PDF|work=Ground-Water Investigations Program|publisher=Montana Bureau of Mines and Geology, Montana Tech of the University of Montana|url=http://www.mbmg.mtech.edu/gwip/gwip_pdf/gwip_scratchgravelHillsFactSheet13.pdf|accessdate=September 24, 2011[/tpl] There was extensive mining in the area in late 1800s through the 1930s. The northern part of the region has folding shale, sandstone and limestone from the Algonkian (Belt) age. Adjacent granite has altered them into quartz-mica schist and related rocks. The southern and central portions of the region contains quartz monzonite.[tpl]cite web|title=Scratchgravel Hills Historical Context|publisher=Montana Department of Environmental Quality|url=http://deq.mt.gov/abandonedmines/linkdocs/104tech.mcpx|accessdate=September 24, 2011[/tpl] Bitterroot and conifers are common in the area.[tpl]cite web|title=Botanical Survey of the Scratchgravel Hills, Lewis and Clark County, Montana|format=PDF|publisher=Montana Natural Heritage Program|url=http://mtnhp.org/plants/reports/Scratchgravel.pdf|accessdate=September 24, 2011[/tpl]
