[[Michael Wager (Ohio)]]

CATEGORIES: Living people

Michael Wager is an attorney for Taft Stettinius & Hollister in Cleveland, Ohio.  Wager is currently running as a Democrat in Ohio's 14th congressional district. 

==Personal life and education==

Wager earned his B.A. from American University in 1973 and his J.D. from New York University School of Law in 1981, where he was Editor-in-Chief of the Journal of International Law and Politics. He also earned his MA from Columbia University in 1976.[tpl]cite web|title=Michael Wager (bio)|url=http://www.taftlaw.com/attorneys/616-michael-wager|publisher=Taft Stettinius & Hollister LLP|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] Wager has taught courses on urban policy and the U.S. judicial system at Case Western Reserve University in the Department of Political Science.[tpl]cite news|last=Eaton|first=Sabrina|title=Attorney Michael Wager to seek 14th District congressional seat|url=http://www.cleveland.com/open/index.ssf/2012/12/attorney_michael_wager_to_seek.html|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Plain Dealer|date=12 December 2012[/tpl]

==Career==

During the 1970s, Wager worked as a staff member for Representatives Charles Vanik and Mary Rose Oakar of Ohio,[tpl]cite news|last=Eaton|first=Sabrina|title=Attorney Michael Wager to seek 14th District congressional seat|url=http://www.cleveland.com/open/index.ssf/2012/12/attorney_michael_wager_to_seek.html|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Plain Dealer|date=12 December 2012[/tpl] as well as Senator Lawton Chiles.[tpl]cite news|last=Giroux|first=Gregory|title=Sherrod Brown Financial Backer Plans Own Race in 2014|url=http://go.bloomberg.com/political-capital/2012-12-06/sherrod-brown-financial-backer-plans-own-race-in-2014/|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=Bloomberg|date=6 December 2012[/tpl] More recently, Wager served as Senator Sherrod Brown's campaign finance chair.
Wager is an attorney with Taft Stettinius & Hollister, where he specializes in corporate governance, strategic growth, and corporate compliance and FCPA investigations. Before joining Taft, Wager was a partner at Squire Sanders & Dempsey LLP.[tpl]cite web|title=Taft Welcomes Accomplished Business & Finance Partner, Michael Wager, to its Team|url=http://www.taftlaw.com/news/press_room/story/789-taft-welcomes-accomplished-business-finance-partner-michael-wager-to-its-team|publisher=Taft|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] Wager has also worked at Weil Gotshal & Manges LLP in New York and Benesch, Friedlander, Coplan & Aronoff LLP in Cleveland. Wager has served on the boards of numerous organizations including Clean Ohio Council, Northeast Ohio Development Fund, Cuyahoga County Renewable Energy Task Force, the White House Business Council, Cleveland Jewish Federation, Jewish National Fund, University Hospitals Seidman Cancer Center, West Side Ecumenical Ministry, the Gateway Economic Development Corporation of Greater Cleveland, the Quicken Loans Arena (formerly Gund Arena)), Michael Anthony Holdings Inc., Reynard American Partners, L.P., American Speedy Printing Centers and Sembcorp Utilities (formerly Cascal N.V.).[tpl]cite web|title=Michael Keith Wager J.D.|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/people/person.asp?personId=607816&ticker=MAJJ&previousCapId=287126&previousTitle=MICHAEL%20ANTHONY%20HOLDINGS%20INC|publisher=Bloomberg Businessweek|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Michael Wager|url=http://www.taftlaw.com/attorneys/616-michael-wager|publisher=Taft|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]
Wager served on the Cleveland-Cuyahoga County Port Authority from 2002 to 2009, serving as chairman by the end of his tenure.[tpl]cite news|last=Breckenridge|first=Tom|title=Outgoing Port Authority Chairman Michael Wager sees opportunities for Cleveland waterfront|url=http://www.cleveland.com/business/plaindealer/index.ssf?/base/business-11/123281102364110.xml&coll=2|accessdate=14 December 2013|newspaper=The Plain Dealer|date=24 January 2009[/tpl] Wager had supported an aggressive plan to redevelop the Cuyahoga River waterfront, but was not reappointed by Cleveland Mayor Frank G. Jackson.[tpl]cite news|last=Miller|first=Jay|title=Jackson makes waves at Cleveland-Cuyahoga County Port Authority|url=http://www.crainscleveland.com/article/20081208/FREE/812089967|accessdate=14 December 2013|newspaper=Crain's Cleveland Business|date=8 December 2008[/tpl]

==2014 Congressional Candidacy==

Michael Wager announced his candidacy for Congress in Ohio's 14th congressional district in December 2012. The seat is currently held by Republican Congressman David Joyce who assumed office in 2013, and faces State Representative Matt Lynch in the May 6, 2014 Republican primary. .[tpl]cite news|last=Eaton|first=Sabrina|title=Attorney Michael Wager to seek 14th District congressional seat|url=http://www.cleveland.com/open/index.ssf/2012/12/attorney_michael_wager_to_seek.html|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Plain Dealer|date=12 December 2012[/tpl] On March 3,2014, Wager, who is unopposed in the Democratic primary, was designated as an Emerging Race in the "Red-to-Blue" program of the Democratic Congressional Campaign Committee.
