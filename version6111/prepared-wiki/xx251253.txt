[[Western Town Hall]]

CATEGORIES: Government buildings on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Government buildings completed in 1911, Buildings and structures in Oneida County, New York

Western Town Hall, also known as Liberty Hall, is a historic town hall building located at Westernville in Oneida County, New York. It was built in 1911 and is a two story, gable roofed timber frame structure.  It was constructed from dismantled components of the ca. 1850 Empire Hotel in Delta.  It was rebuilt as "Liberty Hall," a social gathering hall, and named in honor of General William Floyd.  The second floor has an auditorium.  It has been used as the town hall since 1962.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6127|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Western Town Hall|date=September 1995|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=Raymond W. It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1995.
