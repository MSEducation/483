[[Huang Xing's Former Residence]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Changsha

Huang Xing's Former Residence ([tpl]zh|s=黄兴故居|t=黃興故居|p=Huáng Xīng Gùjū[/tpl]) is the house where Chinese statesman and military leader Huang Xing (1874–1916) was born. It is now an important cultural site.
Built in the late Qing Dynasty, it is located in Yangtuo Village, Huangxing Town, Changsha County, Changsha City, Hunan Province, People's Republic of China.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.xinhuanet.com/yzyd/local/20130806/c_116834636.htm |title=台湾大学魏启林博士一行到长沙县参观黄兴故居  |accessdate=2013 |publisher=Xinhuanet |work= |language=Simplified Chinese)[/tpl] It has a building area of about 2 mu, embodies buildings such as the old houses.

==History==

In 1864, in the third year of the age of the Tongzhi Emperor, it was built by Huang Xing's family. Huang Xing was born in here on 25 October 1894. In 1903, Huang Xing sold it.
In 1981, it was listed as one of Hunan Province's most important culture and relics site ([tpl]lang|zh-hans|省级重点文物保护单位[/tpl]). The People's Government of Changsha County rebuilt it and it was opened to the public.
In 1988, it was listed as one of China's most important culture and relics site ([tpl]lang|zh-hans|全国重点文物保护单位[/tpl]) by the State Council of China.
