[[Quebrada de los Cuervos]]

CATEGORIES: Treinta y Tres Department, Visitor attractions in Uruguay, Protected areas of Uruguay, Birdwatching sites in Uruguay, National parks and nature reserves of Uruguay

The Quebrada de los Cuervos (Spanish for Crows Ravine) is an important landform in Treinta y Tres Department, Uruguay.
Crossed by the Yerbal Chico creek, it constitutes a natural protected area.Crows Ravine [tpl]es icon[/tpl]Law No. 17234 - Natural Protected Areas System [tpl]es icon[/tpl] This Important Bird Area (IBA)Áreas de Importancia para la Conservación de las Aves en el Uruguay [tpl]es icon[/tpl] is inhabited by several endangered species, such as Agelaius flavus, Xolmis dominicanus, Limnoctites rectirostris, Sporophila cinnamomea, Sporophila ruficollis.Species at the Quebrada de los Cuervos [tpl]es icon[/tpl] The very name of this place refers to crow-like Cathartidae which breed on its cliffs: Cathartes burrovianus, Cathartes aura, and Coragyps atratus.Quebrada de los Cuervos [tpl]es icon[/tpl]
