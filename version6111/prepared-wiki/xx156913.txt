[[British Double Reed Society]]

CATEGORIES: Double reed organizations, Music societies, Music organisations based in the United Kingdom, Charities based in the United Kingdom, 1988 establishments in the United Kingdom, Organizations established in 1988

The British Double Reed Society (BDRS) is a society for players of double reed instruments in the United Kingdom and around the world.  It was founded in 1988 to "promote and enhance the interests of all double reed players, whether students, amateurs, professionals or teachers".[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.bdrs.org.uk/membership.html | title=Join the BDRS | publisher=British Double Reed Society | accessdate=9 January 2013[/tpl]  Primarily, the Society's members are British players of the oboe and bassoon.  Some specialise in the cor anglais, contrabasson or French bassoon.

==About the society==

The Society was originally established by George Caird, William Waterhouse and others to host the International Double Reed Society convention at the Royal Northern College of Music in Manchester in 1989[tpl]cite journal |last=Newman | first=Gareth |date=Fall 1989 | title=The 1989 IDRS Conference - A Personal View | journal=The Double Reed | volume=12 | issue=2 | url=http://www.idrs.org/publications/DR/DR12.2/DR12.2.Newman.Conf.html | accessdate=2 September 2008 | publisher=International Double Reed Society[/tpl] and has since flourished as a society in its own right.
The current chair of the Society is Robert Codd and the joint presidents are Roger Birnstingl and Karl Jenkins.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.bdrs.org.uk | title=Welcome to the BDRS | publisher=British Double Reed Society | accessdate=9 January 2013[/tpl]
The BDRS is a registered charity.[tpl]cite web | title=1080461 - The British Double Reed Society | url=http://www.charity-commission.gov.uk/Showcharity/RegisterOfCharities/SearchResultHandler.aspx?RegisteredCharityNumber=1080461 | publisher=Charity Commission for England and Wales | accessdate=9 January 2013[/tpl]

==Main activities==

The Society offers offering a wide range of services as well as providing a forum for the interchange of expertise, knowledge and opinion on all matters relating to double reed instruments.
