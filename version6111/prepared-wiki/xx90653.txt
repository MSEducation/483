Maureen Elizabeth Reagan (January 4, 1941 – August 8, 2001) was the first child of Ronald Reagan and his first wife, Jane Wyman.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jane-wyman.com/biography.html |title=Biography|publisher=Oliver Del Signore|accessdate=2011-01-16[/tpl] Her siblings were Michael Reagan (adopted); a sister, Christine, who died shortly after birth, and—from her father's second marriage to Nancy Davis—Patti Davis and Ron Reagan.

==Biography==

===Early life===

Reagan graduated from Immaculate Heart High School in Los Angeles and briefly attended Marymount University.[tpl]cite news|first=Jane|last=Allen|url=http://articles.latimes.com/2001/aug/09/news/mn-32208|title=Maureen Reagan, 60, Dies of Cancer|work=Los Angeles Times|date=August 9, 2001|accessdate=2013-03-30[/tpl]

===Acting career===

Reagan pursued a career in acting in her youth, appearing in films such as Kissin' Cousins (1964) with Elvis Presley.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0713975|title=Maureen Reagan |publisher=Internet Movie Database|accessdate=2009-01-23 [/tpl] She also sang and performed in sketches on a January 28, 1971 episode of The Dean Martin Show.

===Political activities===

Reagan was involved with the Republican Party and worked as a political activist, radio talk show host, commentator and health care advocate. Her most active political years were in the 1980s, during her father's presidency, when she was a member of the California World Trade Commission, chairwoman of the United States delegation to the United Nations' Decade for Women Conference in Kenya, in 1985; co-chairwoman of the Republican National Committee, and chairwoman of the Republican Women's Political Action League. [tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl]
She was the first daughter of a president to run for political office,Doug Wead, All the Presidents' Children: Triumph and Tragedy in the Lives of America's First Families. Atria Books (2003). p. 155. but both of her attempts at election to a public office ended in defeat. She ran unsuccessfully for the United States Senate from California in 1982, and in 1992 for California's 36th congressional district.[ref]
[tpl]cite book|last=Foerstel|first=Karen|coauthor=Herbert N. Foerstel|title=Climbing the Hill: Gender Conflict in Congress|publisher=Greenwood Publishing Group|year=1996|chapter=The Decade of the Woman: An Uncertain Promise|pages=77–78|isbn=9780275949143[/tpl][/ref]
Although they maintained a united front, Maureen Reagan differed from her father on several key issues. Although reared Roman Catholic following her mother's conversion, she was pro-choice on abortion.Foerstel 1996, p. 77. She also held the belief that Oliver North should have been court-martialed.[tpl]cite book|last=Reagan|first=Maureen|title=First Father, First Daughter: A Memoir|publisher=Little, Brown and Company|year=2001|chapter=Iran-Contra|page=374|isbn=9780316736367[/tpl]
After her father announced his diagnosis of Alzheimer's disease in 1994, Maureen Reagan became a member of the Alzheimer's Association board of directors and served as the group’s spokeswoman. During her hospitalization for melanoma cancer, Maureen was only floors away from her father, who had suffered a severe fall.[tpl]cite news|title=Family Misfortune|url=http://www.people.com/people/archive/article/0,,20133520,00.html|publisher=People (magazine)|id=Vol. 55 No. 4|quote=A Fall Lands Ronald Reagan in the Same Hospital as His Cancer-Stricken Daughter|date=2001-01-29|accessdate=2009-01-23[/tpl]
Reagan served on the Board of Trustees of her father's alma mater, Eureka College, in Eureka, Illinois, from March  2000 until her death. On May 9, 2000, she was the speaker at the dedication of the Ronald Reagan Peace Garden on campus. The Reagan Peace Garden recognized her father's contribution to the end of the Cold War. It had been raining all day during several other speeches, but when she stepped to the lectern the sun broke through. She opened her remarks to the crowd of more than 1,000 by saying, "the sun always shines on Ronald Reagan". [tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl]

===Death===

Maureen Reagan died in Granite Bay, California, on August 8, 2001, aged 60, from melanoma. She was survived by her husband, her daughter, her parents and her siblings.
Reagan volunteered with actor David Hyde Pierce, of TV's Frasier, at the Alzheimer’s Association. At her funeral on August 19, 2001, Pierce spoke to the gathering at Cathedral of Blessed Sacrament in Sacramento, California and recalled his friend's tireless devotion to fighting the mind-robbing illness. "When she was given lemons, she did not make lemonade. She took the lemons, threw them back and said, 'Oh, no you don't.'"[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nydailynews.com/archives/news/2001/08/19/2001-08-19_reagan_s_daughter_mourned.html |title=Reagan's Daughter Mourned |publisher=nydailynews.com |date=2001-08-19 |accessdate=2010-01-14[/tpl]
