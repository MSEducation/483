Princess Maria Carolina Augusta of Bourbon-Two Sicilies[tpl]cite web | url=http://thepeerage.com/p11237.htm#i112363| title=Maria Carolina Auguste di Borbone, Principessa di Borbone delle Due Sicilie| date=10 March 2007 | accessdate=6 October 2008 | publisher=thePeerage.com | author=Darryl Lundy[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.angelfire.com/realm/gotha/gotha/2sicilies.html| title=TWO SICILIES | date= | accessdate=5 October 2008 | publisher=Paul Theroff's Royal Genealogy Site | author=Paul Theroff[/tpl] (born 26 April 1822 in Vienna, Austrian Empire; died 6 December 1869 in Twickenham, London, England,) was a Princess of Bourbon-Two Sicilies by birth and a princess of the House of Orléans through her marriage to Prince Henry of Orléans, Duke of Aumale. Maria Carolina was the daughter of Prince Leopold of Bourbon-Two Sicilies, Prince of Salerno and his wife (and niece) Archduchess Maria Clementina of Austria, daughter of Francis II, Holy Roman Emperor.

==Marriage and issue==

Maria Carolina married Prince Henry, Duke of Aumale, fifth and second youngest son of Louis-Philippe of France and his wife Maria Amalia of the Two Sicilies on 25 November 1844 in Naples. Maria Carolina and Henry had four children:

==Later life==

Maria Carolina remained inconsolable over the death of her eldest son, Louis, and died in exile at Twickenham, England, in 1869 from infectious tuberculosis. She and her husband had a loving marriage and after the death of his wife, Henry preferred to remain a widower until his own death in 1897.
