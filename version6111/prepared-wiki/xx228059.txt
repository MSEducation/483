[[Mexico–Turkey relations]]

CATEGORIES: Bilateral relations of Mexico, Bilateral relations of Turkey

Mexican–Turkish relations refers to the diplomatic relations between Mexico and Turkey.

==History==

Diplomatic relations between the two nations were established in 1928 after the transformation of Turkey from the Ottoman Empire by Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, founder of the modern Turkish republic in 1923; and after the end of the Mexican revolution in 1920. Since then, both nations have opened embassies in each other's capitals respectively.Diplomatic Relations Mexico-Turkey

==Present day==

Today relations between Mexico and Turkey are based on friendship and on similarities. Both nations are developing countries with major economies which both sit on enormous cultural cross-roads between developed and developing countries; Turkey bordering both Asia and Europe (including the European Union) and Mexico bordering the United States and Latin America. In 2009, Recep Tayyip Erdogan became the second Turkish Prime Minister to visit Mexico.Mexico and Turkey: Working together to meet global challenges In December 2013, President Enrique Peña Nieto became the first Mexican president to pay an official state visit to Turkey.President Enrique Peña Nieto pays a state visit to Turkey from 16-19 December, 2013 (Spanish)

==Trade==

Today, Mexico is the fourteenth biggest economy and Turkey is seventeenth biggest economy in the world.The World Bank Although both nations are huge economic world players, trade between the two nations, unfortunately, is minuscule with two-way trade between the two nations amounting to approximately eight hundred million dollars (USD) in 2011.Turquía está interesado en un TLC con México (Spanish) Currently both nations are seeking to establish a free trade agreement before the end of 2013.ibid

==Common memberships==

Both nations are members of the G-20, OECD and the United Nations.
