[[Waris Ahluwalia]]

CATEGORIES: 1975 births, American male film actors, American male television actors, American jewelry designers, American people of Punjabi descent, American Sikhs, Indian emigrants to the United States, Living people

Waris Ahluwalia (born 1974 in Amritsar, Punjab) is an Indian American designer and actor based in New York City. His company, House of Waris, has collaborated with a number of other designers and artists throughout the years.[tpl]cite web|title=House of Waris|url=http://houseofwaris.com/|accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl] House of Waris came into being after the owners of Maxfield's in Los Angeles noticed Ahluwalia's elaborate diamond rings and placed an order, which sold out.Interview, SicChic (2007).

==Early life==

Ahluwalia was born in Amritsar, Punjab, India, and moved to New York at the age of five.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2011/03/18/fashion/18iht-acaj-sikh-18.html |title=Traditional Jewelry Turned on Its Head |last1=Pfeiffer |first1=Alice |date=20 March 2011 |accessdate=9 March 2014 |work=The New York Times[/tpl]

==Career==

===Designer===

House of Waris, founded by Ahluwalia, is strongly influenced by the romance and history of ancient kingdoms.[tpl]cite web|title=Waris Ahluwalia|url=http://www.hall-london.com/waris_ahluwalia/biography|publisher=Hall London|accessdate=20 July 2013[/tpl]
Ahluwalia, who is now based in New York City, travels frequently to Rome and Rajasthan, working with craftsmen in each city, from goldsmiths to diamond setters who once made the crests for royal families.[tpl]cite web|title=CFDA Member [tpl]![/tpl] Waris Ahluwalia|url=http://cfda.com/members#!waris-ahluwalia|publisher=Council of Fashion Designers of America|accessdate=23 July 2013[/tpl] He was a CFDA/Vogue Fashion Fund Finalist in 2009, and in 2010 he was inducted as an official member of the CFDA.[tpl]cite web|title=Waris Ahluwalia|url=http://showstudio.com/contributor/waris_ahluwalia|publisher=Showstudio|accessdate=28 July 2013[/tpl] The same year, Ahluwalia was placed on Vanity Fair's Best Dressed List, anointed British GQ's second best dressed man internationally and included in Vogue's 10 Most Impactful people list.[tpl]cite web|last=Little|first=Jaclyn|title=A Year in the Best Dressed Life of Waris Ahluwalia|url=http://www.vanityfair.com/style/features/2010/09/best-dressed-list-walis-ahluwalia-slide-show-201009|publisher=Vanity Fair|accessdate=6 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Best Dressed International Men|url=http://www.gq-magazine.co.uk/style/articles/2011-01/19/gq-style-news-ten-best-dressed-international-men/viewgallery/9|publisher=British GQ|accessdate=6 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=The Impact List of 2010|url=http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/the-impact-list-of-2010/#1|publisher=Vogue|accessdate=6 August 2013[/tpl] He also pens the column 'Love & Waris' on Style.com.[tpl]cite web|title=Love and Waris|url=http://www.style.com/trendsshopping/stylenotes/111312_Ask_Waris/|publisher=Style.com|accessdate=6 August 2013[/tpl]
In October 2010, House of Waris opened a pop up tea room under the High Line (New York City).[tpl]cite web|last=Nerenberg|first=Jenara|title=Wes Anderson's Pal Waris Ahluwalia Pops Tea Room Under NYC Highline|url=http://www.fastcompany.com/1694988/wes-andersons-pal-waris-ahluwalia-pops-tea-room-under-nyc-high-line|publisher=Fast Company|accessdate=6 August 2013[/tpl] The pavilion of the pop up space housed apparel and accessories from designers like Rodarte, A.P.C., book publisher Assouline and jewelry by Ahluwalia himself. The garden area served tea from the Himalayas and food from NYC restaurants like Café Cluny and Bar Pitti.[tpl]cite news|title=House of Waris Opens Pop-Up Tea Shop|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2010/10/06/house-of-waris-opens-popu_n_752985.html|publisher=Huffington post|accessdate=6 August 2013|first=Grace|last=Green|date=6 October 2010[/tpl]
In February 2011, Ahluwalia held his first New York Fashion Week presentation at the Museum of Arts & Design, launching a line of scarves made in India: block printed, hand dyed, silk screened, made of the finest cashmere and silks.[tpl]cite news|last=Whitworth|first=Melissa|title=Waris Ahluwalia: jewellery designer, style icon and Hollywood actor|url=http://fashion.telegraph.co.uk/news-features/TMG8316754/Introducing-Waris-Ahluwalia-New-Yorks-chicest-Sikh.html|publisher=Fashion Telegraph|accessdate=6 August 2013|location=London|date=11 February 2011[/tpl]

====Collaborations/special projects====

Using House of Waris as a platform for collaborations, Waris has worked with A.P.C., Forevermark of the De Beers Group of Companies, and Tilda Swinton for Pringle of Scotland.[tpl]cite web|title=HOUSE of WARIS for Forevermark|url=http://www.forevermarkdiamond.com/us/jewelry/house-waris-forevermark-light-emanating-heart|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Holgate|first=Mark|title=House of Waris and Tilda Swinton Work Together on a Pringle of Scotland Project|url=http://www.vogue.com/vogue-daily/article/vd-house-of-waris-and-tilda-swinton-work-together-on-a-pringle-of-scotland-project-/#1[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=House of Waris Collaborations|url=http://houseofwaris.com|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] Ahluwalia has also collaborated with Benjami Cho, yoox.com, Lookmatic, the Webster Miami, Colette in Paris, Elliott Puckette for NewbarK, and the World Gold Council.[tpl]cite web|last=Parr|first=Christopher|title=Waris Ahluwalia's House of Waris|publisher=The Pursuitist|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Whitworth|first=Melissa|title=Waris Ahluwalia: jewellery designer, style icon and Hollywood actor|url=http://fashion.telegraph.co.uk/news-features/TMG8316754/Introducing-Waris-Ahluwalia-New-Yorks-chicest-Sikh.html|publisher=Fashion Telegraph|location=London|date=11 February 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Sowray|first=Bibby|title=Moda Operandi unveil exclusive Punk collection|url=http://fashion.telegraph.co.uk/news-features/TMG10033260/Moda-Operandi-unveil-exclusive-Punk-collection.html|publisher=Fashion Telegraph|accessdate=9 August 2013|location=London|date=2 May 2013[/tpl]
In response to the attacks in Mumbai in November 2008, Ahluwalia, along with Mortimer Singer and Tina Bhojwani, created To India, with Love, a book aiming to raise funds, spirits, and awareness for the victims of the attacks.[tpl]cite web|title=To India with Love|url=http://www.amazon.com/To-India-With-Love-Mumbai/dp/2759404218|publisher=Amazon|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] All of the proceeds went directly to Taj Public Service Welfare Trust in conjunction with Mumbai: We Got Your Back! (WGYB!), an organization founded by the three editors.[tpl]cite web|title=To India with Love|url=http://www.assouline.com/9782759404216.html|publisher=Assouline|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Grieve|first=Cayte|title=Society Says: Mumbai, We Got Your Back|url=http://www.blackbookmag.com/nightlife/society-says-mumbai-we-got-your-back-1.32783|publisher=BlackBook Mag|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2012, Ahluwalia contributed to the summer issue of The Paris Review, writing an essay on art featuring the work of Walton Ford and Ryan McGinley.[tpl]cite web|last=Wunsch|first=Noah|title=Watch: Issue 201 in Action!|url=http://www.theparisreview.org/blog/tag/waris-ahluwalia/|publisher=The Paris Review|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] Ahluwalia also collaborated with the world-renowned restaurant from New Delhi, BUKHARA.[tpl]cite web|title=BUKHARA Pop Up Restaurant in London|url=http://cheriecity.co.uk/2012/04/02/bukhara-pop-up-restaurant-in-london/|publisher=Cherie City|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] The restaurant opened an exclusive pop up in London at the Sheraton Park Tower in which Ahluwalia served as Creative Director.[tpl]cite web|title=The Luxury Collection Hotels & Resorts and ITC Hotels Bring BUKHARA, India's Most Coveted Culinary Experience, to London for Two Weeks|url=http://development.starwoodhotels.com/news/5/407-the_luxury_collection_hotels_resorts_and_itc_hotels_bring_bukhara_indias_most_coveted_culinary_experience_to_london_for_two_weeks|publisher=Starwood Hotels and Resorts|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] The pop up ran for two weeks and donated a portion of the proceeds obtained to support the Elephant Family non-profit organization.[tpl]cite web|title=Indian high cuisine restaurant Bukhara pops up in London for just two weeks|url=http://www.eastwind.es/newsletter_detalle.php?id=4045&idioma=2|publisher=Eastwind|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
As a part of his role as Global Ambassador for Starwood's Luxury Collection Hotels & Resorts, Ahluwalia wrote and produced the original short film, "HERE: a Film" by Luca Guadagnino. The film's team included Tilda Swinton (co-writer), Heidi Bivens (stylist), Jason Schwartzman (music) and Agyness Deyn (actress).[tpl]cite web|last=Mau|first=Dhani|title=Watch: Agyness Deyn Plays a Hitchcock Heroine in Waris Ahluwalia's New Short Film|url=http://fashionista.com/2012/01/watch-agyness-deyn-as-a-hitchcock-heroine-in-waris-ahluwalias-new-short-film/|publisher=Fashionista|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=The Luxury Collection Hotels & Resorts Premieres 'HERE', an Original Short Film Capturing an Unexpected Journey Across America|url=http://development.starwoodhotels.com/news/7/345-the_luxury_collection_hotels_resorts_premieres_here_an_original_short_film_capturing_an_unexpected_journey_across_america|publisher=Starwood Hotels and Resorts|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2013, House of Waris launched a line for Valentines Day, called 'Boo', referring to it as a term of endearment. To promote the line, Ahluwalia commissioned 9 artists and filmmakers to make short films titled "You’re my Boo." The list of artists included Phillip Andelman, Casey Neistat, Brett Stabler, Ben Watts, and Hailey Gates; as well as John Forté, Mick Rock, Matthew Frost.[tpl]cite web|title=Hey Boo, Love Your New House of Waris Necklace|url=http://www.standardculture.com/posts/6068-Hey-Boo-Love-Your-New-House-of-Waris-Necklace|publisher=The Standard|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=House of Waris channel|url=https://www.youtube.com/user/HouseOfWaris|publisher=Youtube|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
Ahluwalia has also collaborated with Illesteva Eyewear on a line of sunglasses that launched April 2013. [tpl]cite news|last=Hartman|first=Eviana|title=Shades On [tpl]![/tpl] House of Waris and Illesteva|url=http://tmagazine.blogs.nytimes.com/2013/04/02/shades-on-house-of-waris-and-illesteva/?_r=0|publisher=T Magazine|accessdate=9 August 2013|date=2 April 2013[/tpl] The sunglasses are made in France and are available in four colors.[tpl]cite web|title=Don't Look Us in the Eye|url=http://www.thezoereport.com/house-of-waris-for-illesteva-sunglasses/|publisher=Zoe Report|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl]
In 2013, Ahluwalia was featured in a Gap "Make Love" campaign ad that has gotten attention from consumers and the media alike.

===Actor===

Ahluwalia's first film was The Life Aquatic with Steve Zissou (2004) by Wes Anderson, as Vikram Ray, with a cast that included Bill Murray, Owen Wilson, Angelica Huston, and Cate Blanchett.[tpl]cite web|title=Waris Ahluwalia|url=http://www.imdb.com/name/nm1753302/|publisher=IMDB|accessdate=2 August 2013[/tpl]
