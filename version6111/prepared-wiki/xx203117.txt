[[West Virginia Folklore Society]]

CATEGORIES: Organizations based in West Virginia, West Virginia folklore

The West Virginia Folklore Society was an organization devoted to studying and collecting folklore in the United States, founded in 1913. It was among the most prominent such organizations in the early 20th century.Musical Traditions
John Harrington Cox, archivist and editor for the West Virginia Folklore Society, published an influential collection of folk songs in 1925, called Folk-Songs of the South: Collected Under the Auspices of the West Virginia Folk-Lore Society.Crawford, pg. 604 Cox had founded the society with WVU vice-president Robert Armstrong[tpl]dn|date=August 2012[/tpl] and Walter Barnes of Fairmont Normal School.Tribe, pgs. 9-10
