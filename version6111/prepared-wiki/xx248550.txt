[[Liz Mikel]]

CATEGORIES: Actresses from Texas, African-American actresses, Living people, 1963 births, American television actresses, American film actresses, American stage actresses, American musical theatre actresses

Elizabeth Juliene "Liz" Mikel (née Lacy) (born November 7, 1963) is an American actress and jazz vocalist from Dallas, Texas.  She is best known for her role as Corinna "Mama" Williams, mother of star running back Brian "Smash" Williams, on the NBC television series Friday Night Lights.[tpl]cite news |first=Amy |last=Amatangelo |work=Boston Herald |title=NBC's bright 'Lights' |url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/bostonherald/access/1252980141.html?dids=1252980141:1252980141&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Apr+11%2C+2007&author=AMY+AMATANGELO&pub=Boston+Herald&desc=TELEVISION+REVIEW+%3B+NBC's+bright+`Lights'&pqatl=google |date=April 11, 2007[/tpl]

==Career==

Mikel has been performing on stage for more than twenty years, including extended runs with the Dallas Theater Center and Theater Three.[tpl]cite news |first=Lawson |last=Taitte |work=Dallas Morning News |title=Liz Mikel brings stature and star power to 'Caroline' at Theatre Three |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=DM&p_theme=dm&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=119951803A9A4220&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |date=June 4, 2007[/tpl] In 2007, the Dallas Morning News described Mikel as "a fixture on the Dallas theater scene".[tpl]cite news |work=Dallas Morning News |title=Her old stomping grounds |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=DM&p_theme=dm&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=119920D5B84A6298&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |date=June 4, 2007[/tpl] She stepped in for Oprah Winfrey in a special 2008 performance of The Vagina Monologues.[tpl]cite pressrelease |publisher=Billboard Publicity Wire |title=Friday Night Lights Actress Liz Mikel Steps Into Oprah's Role For V-Day |url=http://www.prweb.com/releases/2008/04/prweb866774.htm |date=April 16, 2008[/tpl]
Her film credits include roles in The Quest for Freedom, It's in the Water, Detention (aka Learning Curve), Seventy-8, Carried Away, and as Ruthie Jenkins in Welcome Home, Roscoe Jenkins. Besides Friday Night Lights, television roles have included guest appearances on LAX, Prison Break, and Past Life plus a recurring role as Maggie on Sordid Lives: The Series.
Dallas Morning News theater critic Jerome Weeks once described Mikel's voice as "an immense, joyful force" and stated, "If a mountain range could belt a bawdy song, it would sound like Mikel."[tpl]cite news |first=Jerome |last=Weeks |work=Dallas Morning News |title='Empress' of all she surveyed |url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=DM&p_theme=dm&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=0ED3D5B6AEE9CC9A&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |date=April 2, 1995[/tpl]
Liz starred on Broadway in Lysistrata Jones, for which the New York Times praised her performance as "commanding."[tpl]cite web |last=Brantley |first=Ben |url=http://theater.nytimes.com/2011/12/15/theater/reviews/lysistrata-jones-at-walter-kerr-theater-review.html |title=‘Lysistrata Jones’ at Walter Kerr Theater - Review |publisher=NYTimes.com |deadurl=no |accessdate=July 30, 2012[/tpl]

==Awards and honors==

In 1998, Mikel received the Leon Rabin Award for "Outstanding Performance by an Actress in a Musical" from the Dallas Theatre League.[tpl]cite news |first=Cindy |last=Chaffin |publisher=KERA |title=Art&Seek Q&A: Liz Mikel |url=http://www.kera.org/artandseek/content/2010/01/08/artseek-qa-liz-mikel/ |date=January 8, 2010[/tpl] She has also been the recipient of the Sankofa Award for her "dedication to the Arts in the Community" and the Dallas Theater Critics Forum Award 2004 for her role in Ain’t Misbehavin. D Magazine named Mikel "Best Actress" in their 2004 "Best of Big D" issue.[tpl]cite news |work=D Magazine |title=Best Actress: Liz Mikel |url=http://directory.dmagazine.com/best-of-big-d/Best-Actress-Liz-Mikel |year=2004[/tpl] The Dallas Weekly featured her as "Queen of the Arts: The Face of Black Theater in Dallas" in March 2006.  Mikel was awarded The 2008 Column Theater Award for "Best Actress in a Musical-Equity" for Caroline, or Change.

==Fire==

On January 7, 2010, the Holly Hills apartment complex in Dallas where Mikel lived burned to the ground, destroying all of Mikel's clothing, possessions, and memorabilia. The local arts community responded with a series of benefit concerts and the Dallas Theater Center announced they would donate "100 percent of all ticket sales" from the world-premiere performance of the musical, Give It Up!, in which Mikel appears.[tpl]cite news |first=Robert |last=Wilonsky |work=Dallas Observer |title=Give It Up For Liz Mikel |url=http://blogs.dallasobserver.com/unfairpark/2010/01/give_it_up_for_liz_mikel.php |date=January 8, 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news |first=Nancy |last=Visser |work=Dallas Morning News |title=Tuckers' Blues is hosting fundraiser for diva Liz Mikel, whose home burned this week |url=http://eastdallasblog.dallasnews.com/archives/2010/01/tuckers-blues-is-hosting-fundr.html |date=January 8, 2010[/tpl]
