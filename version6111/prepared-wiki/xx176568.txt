[[Michael Langham]]

CATEGORIES: 1919 births, 2011 deaths, Alumni of the University of London, British Army personnel of World War II, British theatre directors, Juilliard School faculty, People educated at Radley College, People from Bridgwater, Stratford Shakespeare Festival

Michael Langham (22 August 1919 – 15 January 2011) was an English actor and director, who  spent much of his career living and working in Canada and the United States.
He was educated at Radley College and  studied law at the University of London before enlisting in the British Army in 1939. After spending five years as a prisoner of war, Langham set his sights on the theatre and led several repertory theatres in the U.K. including Coventry (1946–48), Birmingham (1948–50) and Glasgow (1953–54).Canadianencyclopedia.ca: Langham, Michael
He was the second Artistic Director at the Stratford Festival in Canada from 1956 to 1967,and the third Artistic Director of the Guthrie Theater from 1971 to 1977.Michael Langham: 1971-1977 | Guthrie Theater He was also director of the Juilliard School from 1979 to 1982, and again from 1987 to 1992. Langham's association with the Stratford Festival lasted over a half century as he directed Love's Labours Lost and There Reigns Love for the 2008 season.Stratfordfestival.ca  In 1995 he directed two plays for the inaugural season of the Atlantic Theatre Festival in Wolfville, Nova Scotia.
Langham was married to actress Helen Burns. Their son is the writer, actor and comedian Chris Langham.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/tvshowbiz/article-385913/Profile-Chris-Langham.html|title=Profile: Chris Langham|publisher=Daily Mail|date=12 May 2006|accessdate=28 December 2009 | location=London[/tpl]
Langham died on 15 January 2011 in Cranbrook, Kent, England after a short illness.Michael Langham: Former Stratford Artistic Director dies at 91
