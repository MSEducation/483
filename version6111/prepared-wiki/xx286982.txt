[[How You Like Me Now? (song)]]

CATEGORIES: 2009 songs, 2009 singles, Soul songs

"How You Like Me Now?" is a song by British rock band The Heavy from their 2009 album The House That Dirt Built.Rolling Stone
The song samples "Let a Woman Be a Woman" by Dyke and the Blazers.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.whosampled.com/sample/view/22643/The%20Heavy-How%20You%20Like%20Me%20Now%3F_Dyke%20%26%20the%20Blazers-Let%20a%20Woman%20Be%20a%20Woman%20-%20Let%20a%20Man%20Be%20a%20Man |title=Who Sampled Who, The Heavy How You Like Me Now |date= |accessdate=2010-04-16[/tpl]
The song was used by Republican presidential candidate Newt Gingrich in 2012 until he was legally ordered to stop using the song for his campaign.Rolling Stone
The song has since featured in several trailers and movies. It was used in the trailer for the 2012 movie Ted, during the closing credits for Horrible Bosses and G.I. Joe: Retaliation in 2013, the closing credits for role-playing first-person shooter, Borderlands 2, the closing credits for the platform Knack (video game) on the Playstation 4, and then again in a TV spot for the 2013 animated movie Turbo.http://www.theshiznit.co.uk/trailer/official-all-mark-wahlberg-trailers-to-feature-how-you-like-me-now.phphttp://www.imdb.com/name/nm3337593/[tpl]YouTube|oBZvcxC38CQ[/tpl]http://www.justisntmusic.com/news/030713-the-heavy-how-you-like-me-now-in-turbo-trailer/
The song is also used as the theme song for Intentional Talk on MLB Network. A variant of this song called "How Do You Like Me Now (Beats Mix)" was performed by The Heavy and was prominently featured in the 2010 installment of Sony's venerable baseball video game franchise MLB The Show.[tpl]YouTube|nk28o5AFAHQ[/tpl]
