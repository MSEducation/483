William Glyndwr "Wil" Edmunds OBE (born 1947) is a retired Welsh educationalist. He was Principal of Coleg Ceredigion and then Principal/Chief Executive of Deeside College 1997-2004,[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.deeside.ac.uk/about-us/history.php| author=Deeside College|title=History of Deeside College: Principals at the college|accessdate=2008-04-28[/tpl] during which time the college was the first Further Education college to be awarded the Queen's Anniversary Prize.[tpl]cite web| url=http://www.deeside.ac.uk/about-us/news/index.php?subaction=showfull&id=1082645851&archive=&start_from=&ucat=1|author=Deeside College|title=Deeside College goes to Buckingham Palace|date=2003-02-18|accessdate=2008-04-28[/tpl] In 2004 he was appointed chair of ACCAC, the Qualifications, Curriculum and Assessment Authority for Wales.[tpl]cite web|author=Welsh Assembly Government|title=Jane Davidson appoints new chair of ACCAC |url=http://new.wales.gov.uk/news/archivepress/educationpress/edpress04/706480/?lang=en|date=2004-07-19| accessdate=2008-04-28[/tpl]
Edmunds has a degree in music from Bretton Hall College, University of Leeds, and a masters degree in social psychology from the University of Liverpool.  He received the OBE in 2001 for "services to Further Education".
