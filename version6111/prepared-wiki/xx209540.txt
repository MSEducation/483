[[Domnall mac Flaíthnia]]

CATEGORIES: 783 deaths, People from County Offaly, 8th-century Irish monarchs, Year of birth unknown

Domnall mac Flaíthnia (died 783) was a king of the Uí Failge, a Laigin people of County Offaly. He was the son of Flaithnia mac Flainn (died 755), a previous king.Mac Niocaill, pg.127 He ruled from 782 to 783.in  the Book of Leinster king list he is given a reign of 3 years. 
His predecessor and uncle Mugrón mac Flainn (died 782) had been slain in battle versus his overlord the King of Leinster. In 783 Domnall was killed in captivity in Cloncurry, County Kildare.Annals of Ulster, AU 783.1 The exact circumstances are unknown.
