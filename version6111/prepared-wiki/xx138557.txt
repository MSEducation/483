[[Celine Dion singles discography]]

CATEGORIES: Celine Dion, Discographies of Canadian artists, Pop music discographies

The singles discography of Canadian recording artist Celine Dion consists of 140 singles and 5 charity records. At age twelve, Dion collaborated with her mother and brother, Jacques to compose her first song, "Ce n'était qu'un rêve", which was released as a single in Quebec in June 1981.[tpl]cite web | title=Did You Know? | work=celinedion.com | url=http://www.celinedion.com/celinedion/english/whatsgoinon_archive.cgi?page=2 | accessdate=2010-07-06[/tpl][tpl]cite book | author=Beauregard, Sylvain| title=Passion Celine Dion the Book |language=French| publisher=Trafford Publishing | year=2002 | isbn=1-55369-212-8[/tpl] By 1983, she became the first Canadian artist to receive a Gold record in France for the single "D'amour ou d'amitié".[tpl]cite web | title=The Journey so Far | work=celinedion.com | url=http://www.celinedion.com/celinedion/english/journey.html | accessdate=2010-09-09[/tpl] Further success in Europe came when she represented Switzerland in the Eurovision Song Contest 1988 with the song "Ne partez pas sans moi", which later won the contest.[tpl]cite book|last=O'Connor |first=John Kennedy |title=The Eurovision Song Contest — The Official History |authorlink=John Kennedy O'Connor |publisher=Carlton Books |location=UK |date=2007-04-02 |isbn=1-84442-240-2[/tpl] In 1990, Dion made her debut into the American market with Unison. The album included "Where Does My Heart Beat Now", which became her first single to reach the top ten on the United States Billboard Hot 100, peaking at number four. In 1991, Dion sang with Peabo Bryson in the song "Beauty and the Beast".[tpl]cite news|last=Bliss |first=Karen |title=25 Years of Canadian Artists |work=Canadian Musician |date=2004-03-01 |page=34 |issn=0708-9635[/tpl] It became her second top ten Billboard Hot 100 single, and won the Academy Award for Best Original Song, as well the Grammy Award for Best Pop Performance by a Duo or Group with Vocals.Alexander, Charles P. Released in late 1993, The Colour of My Love spawned Dion's first US, Canadian, and Australian number-one single "The Power of Love".Alexander, Charles P. "The Arts & Media/Music: At Age Five She Belted Out French pop tunes standing atop tables." Time International. 1994-02-28. pg 44. In the United Kingdom, both the album and the single "Think Twice" simultaneously occupied the top of the British charts for five consecutive weeks. "Think Twice", which remained at number one for seven weeks, became the fourth single by a female artist to sell in excess of one million copies in the UK.[tpl]cite web | title=The Journey so Far | work=celinedion.com| url=http://www.celinedion.com/celinedion/english/journey3.html | accessdate=2005-08-16[/tpl] Keeping to her French roots, Dion continued to release French language recordings between each English album. D'eux was released in 1995 and amassed huge success with the single, "Pour que tu m'aimes encore". The song reached number one in France, stayed at the top position for twelve weeks, and sold over 955,000 copies in France, alone.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.infodisc.fr/S_ToutTemps.php?debut=100 |title=Les meilleures ventes tout temps de 45 T. / singles |work=infodisc.fr|language=French|accessdate=2007-12-20[/tpl] "Pour que tu m'aimes encore" topped the charts in Francophone countries and also peaked inside top ten in the UK, Ireland, Netherlands and Sweden.

After selling 1.48 million copies of "My Heart Will Go On" and 1.30 million copies of "Think Twice" in the UK, Dion became the only female artist to have two million-selling singles in this country.[tpl]cite web|url=http://music.guardian.co.uk/pop/story/0,,2225054,00.html|title=People Are Jealous|publisher=The Guardian|date=2007-12-10|accessdate=2008-01-01[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-million-sellers-the-uks-biggest-ever-selling-singles-revealed-1685/|title=The Million Sellers – the UK's biggest ever selling singles revealed!|publisher=UK Charts Company|date=2012-11-04|accessdate=2012-11-04[/tpl] She's also one of the biggest selling female singles artists of all time in the UK.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.officialcharts.com/chart-news/the-official-top-10-biggest-selling-female-singles-artists-of-all-time-revealed-1670/|title=The Official Top 10 biggest selling female singles artists of all time revealed!|publisher=UK Charts Company|date=2012-10-27|accessdate=2012-10-27[/tpl]

It became Dion's fourth Billboard Hot 100 number-one single and her second song, after "My Heart Will Go On" to debut at the chart's top position. Released in 1999, All the Way... A Decade of Song included the successful lead single "That's the Way It Is", which reached number six on the Billboard Hot 100. After a two-year hiatus, Dion released the album, A New Day Has Come in March 2002.
