[[Childline South Africa]]

CATEGORIES: Children's charities, 1986 establishments in South Africa, Helplines

Childline South Africa is a non-profit organisation which works to protect children from violence and further the culture of children's rights in South Africa.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za|title=Childline South Africa[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl] Childline runs a national, 24-hour, toll-free telephone counselling service for children and adults, handling over 1 million calls annually.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.info.gov.za/issues/hotline/|title=South African government call centres and help lines[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/how-we-help/63-uncategorised/116-crisisline|title=Crisisline[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl] In addition to the Crisis Line telephone counseling service, Childline also offers services such as online counseling, training programs for continuous professional development, training on court preparation, and workshops on child law.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/how-we-help|title=How we help[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/how-we-help/63-uncategorised/117-training|title=Childline Training[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl]

==Crisis line telephone counselling==

The 24-hour national telephone counselling service is available to children up to 21 years of age, as well as adults with concerns about children. Telephone calls are routed to the Childline office closest to the caller, where trained counsellors are available to provide assistance. Calls to the Crisis Line are free from South African telephone networks Telkom, Vodacom and CellC, with the exception of MTN.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.looklocal.co.za/looklocal/content/en/polokwane/polokwane-news-general?oid=7676037&sn=Detail&pid=4730437&Teach-your-children-about-Childline-s-helpful-services|title=Teach your children about Childline's helpful services[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mm3admin.co.za/documents/docmanager/D43F9813-E6C9-4927-8342-D16FB6ECCFA8/00005442.pdf|title=Childline Gauteng 2008-2009 Annual Report (page 10)[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://telkom.investoreports.com/telkom_ar_2004/downloads/corporate_social_investment.pdf|title=Telkom Foundation 2004 Annual Report (page 3)[/tpl] Counsellors are trained to deal with a wide variety of issues.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.myschool.co.za/schools/villages/105-childline|title=MySchool[/tpl]

==Online counselling==

Childline offers a free online counselling service through the Mxit instant messaging application, available to youth under the age of 21 years, as well as adults with concerns about children.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mxitreach.org/partners/childline/|title=MXit Partners[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/how-we-help/63-uncategorised/115-online-counselling|title=Online Counselling[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl] Initially trialled as a pilot project in 2009, the service quickly saw thousands of young people making use of the service to seek help.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.news24.com/SouthAfrica/News/MXit-survey-highlights-abuse-20091209|title=MXit survey highlights abuse[/tpl] Chat rooms are staffed by trained counselors based at the Childline national office in Durban, and are operational Monday to Saturday, from 2pm to 6pm. Conversations between children and counsellors are strictly confidential and are conducted on a one-on-one basis, allowing children to seek assistance on topics they would ordinarily find difficult to discuss.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.parent24.com/Teen_13-18/health_safety/MXit-basics-for-parents-20100604|title=MXit basics for parents[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.witness.co.za/index.php?showcontent&global%5B_id%5D=42024|title=Parent's guide to how MXit works[/tpl] Children requiring face-to-face services may be referred to their closest regional office for further counselling.

==Partnerships==

===Google===

Google is partnered with Childline in promoting online safety for children through its online child safety campaign, launched on 1 June 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://themediaonline.co.za/2012/08/parents-corner-welcomes-google-sa-campaign-to-drive-online-child-safety/|title=The Media Online: Parents' Corner welcomes Google SA campaign to drive online child safety[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.google.co.za/intl/en/goodtoknow/web/partners/|title=Google Partners[/tpl] The partnership is intended to facilitate the coordination of work done by government, industry and civil society.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.itweb.co.za/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=57064|title=ITWeb: Parents' Corner welcomes Google SA campaign to drive online child safety[/tpl] The online child safety campaign saw the launch of a South African version of the Google Family Safety Centre, available in English, isiZulu and Afrikaans, and provides parents with information on how to prevent children from being exposed to inappropriate content through Google services.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.parentscorner.org.za/blog/google-launches-family-safety-centre-south-africa|title=Google launches Family Safety Centre for South Africa[/tpl]

===International Society for Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect (ISPCAN)===

International Society for Prevention of Child Abuse and Neglect (ISPCAN) focuses on training and education in the child protection field at a regional and international level. Childline is a member organisation of ISPCAN[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ispcan.org/?page=Resource_Centers|title=ISPCAN Resource Centers[/tpl] and is associated with ISPCAN training and international congresses, having assisted in the organization of the ISPCAN International Congress held at the Inkosi Albert Luthuli International Convention Centre in 2008 in Durban.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ispcan.org/?page=Partner_Program|title=ISPCAN Partner Program[/tpl] Childline has also had numerous papers accepted for presentation at the ISPCAN International Congresses, held every two years.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/partnerships-aboutmenu-56|title=Childline Partnerships[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl] The national coordinator of Childline is listed as a member of the expert faculty of ISPCAN, and also an elected member of the executive council of the organization.[tpl]cite web|url=http://c.ymcdn.com/sites/www.ispcan.org/resource/resmgr/docs/ispcan_membership_brochure3.pdf|title=ISPCAN Membership Brochure (page 1)[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ispcan.org/?page=Council|title=ISPCAN Executive Council[/tpl]

===Child Helpline International (CHI)===

Child Helpline International (CHI) is a global network of 173 child helplines in 142 countries.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childhelplineinternational.org/about/|title=About Child Helpline International[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childhelplineinternational.org/what-we-do/|title=Child Helpline International: What We Do[/tpl] Its functions are to provide a forum for information sharing and mutual support, assistance with advocacy and lobbying, promoting the rights of children and child helplines as a medium of assistance to children, and to support the initiation and development of child helplines in countries which do not have such services. Childline is partnered with Child Helpline International, and hosted the CHI Sixth International Consultation in 2012 in Durban.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childhelplineinternational.org/conference-2012/|title=CHI International Consultation 2012[/tpl]

==Funding==

Childline is primarily funded by ad-hoc donations from corporations and government parastatals.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.childlinesa.org.za/index.php/funding-supportmenu-71|title=Childline funding[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl] The lack of a reliable source of recurring corporate and governmental donations has resulted in the threat of imminent closure of some Childline offices on several occasions.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.iol.co.za/news/south-africa/kwazulu-natal/childline-may-close-its-doors-1.1308922#.Uga4pKBCBKk|title=Childline may close its doors[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=https://www.facebook.com/ChildlineKzn/posts/400662010038395|title=It's Sink or Swim for Childline[/tpl][tpl]official[/tpl]
