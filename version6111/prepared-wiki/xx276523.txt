[[Richard Schelp]]

Richard H. Schelp (April 21, 1936, Kansas City, Missouri, USA – November 29, 2010, Memphis, Tennessee, USA) was an American mathematician.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.memorialparkfuneralandcemetery.com/obituary/user/show/template?id=7654 | title=Richard Schelp: In Celebration of a life well lived | publisher=Memorial Park Funeral Home & Cemetery | accessdate=September 27, 2011[/tpl]
Schelp received his Bachelors degree in mathematics and physics from the University of Central Missouri and his Masters degree and doctorate in mathematics from Kansas State University. The adviser from his thesis was Richard Joseph Greechie.[tpl]cite web | url=http://genealogy.math.ndsu.nodak.edu/id.php?id=11245 | title=Richard Schelp | publisher=Mathematics Genealogy Project | accessdate=September 27, 2011[/tpl]
He was an associate mathematician and missile scientist at Johns Hopkins University for five years. He then became an instructor of mathematics at Kansas State University for four years. Finally in 1970 he became a professor of mathematics in the Department of Mathematical Sciences at the University of Memphis. He retired in 2001.
Schelp, an Erdős number one mathematician, was the fourth most frequent scholarly collaborator with Paul Erdős.https://files.oakland.edu/users/grossman/enp/Erdos0p.html[tpl]DBLP|id=Schelp:Richard_H=[/tpl] He also collaborated on research with another top ten most frequent Erdős collaborator, Ralph Faudree, who is based at the University of Memphis as well.https://files.oakland.edu/users/grossman/enp/Erdos0p.htmlhttp://www.msci.memphis.edu/faculty/rousseauc.html
