[[Kit Smythe]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, 1940 births, American actresses

Kit Smythe (born 1940) is an American Broadway and television actress.

==Career==

Smythe's first acting job was when she was noticed by Sherwood Schwartz, and assigned to play the role of "Ginger" in the pilot episode of Gilligan's Island.[tpl]cite news |title=A DIFFERENT CAST OF CASTAWAYS The Original `Gilligan's Island' |author=Andy Edelstein |url=http://pqasb.pqarchiver.com/newsday/access/77568911.html?dids=77568911:77568911&FMT=ABS&FMTS=ABS:FT&type=current&date=Oct+15%2C+1992&author=By+Andy+Edelstein.+STAFF+WRITER&pub=Newsday+%28Combined+editions%29&desc=A+DIFFERENT+CAST+OF+CASTAWAYS+The+Original+%60Gilligan%27s+Island%27&pqatl=google |newspaper=Newsday |date=15 October 1992 |accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl] Smythe's character of Ginger was a secretary, as was another character "Bunny" played by Nancy McCarthy. After Gilligan's Island's pilot, Smythe did not continue with the series, and was instead replaced by Tina Louise who plays Ginger Grant. 
She would go on to appear in TV series such as Bewitched (1964), The Farmer's Daughter (1965), Burke's Law (1965), and Gunsmoke (1966). Smythe's final television acting performance was in an episode of Police Woman in 1976.
As of 2005, Smythe was still performing as a showgirl in The Fabulous Palm Springs Follies in Palm Springs, California.[tpl]cite news |title=So Very Alive and Kicking |author=Mireya Navarro  |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2005/01/02/fashion/02FOLL.html |newspaper=New York Times |date=2 January 2005 |accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
