[[Kōnan Railway Kōnan Line]]

CATEGORIES: Railway lines in Japan, Rail transport in Aomori Prefecture, 1067 mm gauge railways in Japan, Railway lines opened in 1927

The [tpl]Nihongo|Kōnan Line|弘南線|Kōnan-sen[/tpl] is a railway route operated by the Japanese private railway operator Kōnan Railway} in Aomori Prefecture, from Hirosaki Station in Hirosaki to Kuroishi Station in Kuroishi.

==Station list==

==Rolling stock==

Rolling stock on the line consists of former Tokyu 6000 and 7000 series EMUs. Ex-Nankai Railway 1521 series 4-door EMUs were also previously used, but these were withdrawn in 2008 following the discontinuation of limited-stop "Rapid" services. An electric locomotive is also available for use on winter snow-clearing duties.

==History==

The Kōnan Railway was founded on March 27, 1926, and began operations between Hirosaki and Tsugaru-Onoe Station on September 7, 1927.[tpl]cite book | last = Terada | first = Hirokazu |title = データブック日本の私鉄 |trans_title= Databook: Japan's Private Railways | publisher = Neko Publishing | date = 19 January 2013 | location = Japan | page = 13| isbn = 978-4-7770-1336-4[/tpl] By July 1, 1948, the entire line was electrified at 600 volts DC. The line was extended to Kōnan-Kuroishi Station (present-day Kuroishi Station) by July 1, 1950. Voltage on the line was raised to 750 volts by April 1, 1954, and to 1,500 volts by September 1, 1961.[tpl]Citation needed|date=November 2013[/tpl]

==Accidents==

A train derailed at Hiraka Station on June 12, 2007, but no injuries were reported.[tpl]Citation needed|date=October 2013[/tpl]
