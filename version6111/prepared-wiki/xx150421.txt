[[Loch Carron]]

CATEGORIES: Sea lochs of Scotland, Lochs of Highland (council area)

Loch Carron (Scottish Gaelic: "Loch Carrann") is a sea loch on the west coast of Ross and Cromarty in the Scottish Highlands.  It is the point at which the River Carron enters the North Atlantic Ocean.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl]
According to the marine charts, the tidal currents reach [tpl]convert|3|knot[/tpl] in the narrows, although not much water disturbance is visible in the flow.  At the narrows, the depth of water is less than 20 metres, but in the basins on either side, it extends to a depth of more than 100 metres.Retrieved from http://www.divemagazine.co.uk/news/article.asp?UAN=2294&v=5&sp=[tpl]Dead link|date=April 2013[/tpl].  Beneath the cliffs at Strome Castle is a colony of flame shells.

==Tourism==

Tourism is a significant industry in the Highlands of Scotland and one that generates important local economic activity. It provides employment for local people and attracts many visitors to Wester Ross in general and Lochcarron in particular because of its traditional seaside location.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl]
The Kyle of Lochalsh Line runs along the south side of the loch, with railway stations at Attadale, Stromeferry, Duncraig, and Plockton.[tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2013[/tpl]
