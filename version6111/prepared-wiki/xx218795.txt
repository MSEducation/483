David Lindsey Hulse (February 25, 1968 in San Angelo, Texas), is a retired Major League Baseball player who played outfielder from [tpl]by|1992[/tpl]-[tpl]by|1996[/tpl] for the Texas Rangers and Milwaukee Brewers.
Hulse played collegiate baseball at Schreiner University and was drafted by the Rangers in the thirteenth round of the 1990 amateur draft.[tpl]cite web|title=David Hulse Statistics and History|url=http://www.baseball-reference.com/players/h/hulseda01.shtml|accessdate=20 March 2012[/tpl] 
Hulse is best known for an at-bat that he had against the Angels.  He fouled four pitches in a row into the Angels' dugout. By the time he fouled off his third pitch, the entire Angels' dugout had concentrated themselves away from the edge of the dugout. Hulse ended the AB by grounding out, after which the Angels players went back to the end of dugout they had emptied.
