[[HMS Eskimo (F119)]]

CATEGORIES: Tribal-class frigates, 1960 ships

HMS ''Eskimo'' (F119) was a Tribal-class frigate of the Royal Navy in service from 1963 to 1980. She was scrapped in 1992.

==Service==

Eskimo began her third commission in October 1966. In 1967, Eskimo transferred to the Mediterranean Fleet, with which the ship took part in a number of port visits and exercises.[tpl]citation needed|date=March 2012[/tpl] In June, Eskimo  replaced her sistership Ashanti off Aden in response to the start of the Six-Day War.British warships redeployed in Mediterranean. The Times (56960): Col A, p. 10. 6 June 1967. In 1968 she took part in Portsmouth 'Navy Days'.Programme, Navy Days at Portsmouth August 31st-September 2nd 1968, p.19.
In 1976, Eskimo served as the West Indies Guardship. She escorted Queen Elizabeth II aboard Britannia as part of America's Bi-Centennial celebrations. Eskimo returned to the Caribbean in 1977.[tpl]citation needed|date=March 2012[/tpl]
