[[Bryotropha basaltinella]]

CATEGORIES: Animals described in 1839, Gelechiidae, Moths of Europe, Insects of Germany, Insects of Poland, Insects of Belgium, Insects of Hungary

 
Bryotropha basaltinella is a moth of the Gelechiidae family. It is found in Great Britain, the Benelux, Germany, France, Spain, Switzerland, Austria, Hungary, Slovakia, the Czech Republic and Poland.
The wingspan is 11–12 mm.microlepidoptera.nl The forewings are dark grey-brown with a distinct ochreous basal spot followed by blackisk blotches on the costa and tornus. The hindwings are pale fuscous, but darker towards the apex.[tpl]aut|Karsholt, Ole & Twan Rutten[/tpl], 2005, the genus Bryotropha Heinemann in the western palaearctic (Lepidoptera: Gelechiidae), Tijdschrift voor Entomologie 148: 77-207. The larvae feed on various mosses. They live in a densely spun silken tube beneath the surface of the host plant. The larvae have a dull purplish brown body. They can be found in spring.
