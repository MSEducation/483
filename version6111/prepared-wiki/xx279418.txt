[[James T. Rosenbaum]]

CATEGORIES: 1949 births, Living people, American physicians, American academics

James T. Rosenbaum (born September 29, 1949) is an American physician-scientist who is Chief of Ophthalmology at the Legacy Devers Eye Institute, Portland, Oregon, where he holds the Richard Chenoweth Chair,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.legacyhealth.org/for-health-professionals/legacy-research-institute/our-scientists/james-t-rosenbaum-md.aspx |title=James T. Rosenbaum MD |publisher=Legacyhealth.org |date= |accessdate=2013-11-06[/tpl] and Chief of Arthritis and Rheumatic Diseases at the Oregon Health & Science University where he holds the Edward E Rosenbaum Professorship in Inflammation Research.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ohsu.edu/xd/health/services/providers/index.cfm?personid=rosenbaj |title=James T. Rosenbaum, M.D. | Arthritis & Rheumatic Diseases (Adult) | OHSU |publisher=Ohsu.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-11-06[/tpl] Rosenbaum is the only practicing rheumatologist/non-ophthalmologist in the world to serve as a chief of ophthalmology. He is recognized for his description of an animal model of uveitis (inflammation inside the eye) resulting from injection of bacterial endotoxin (Nature, 286:611, 1980) and for more than 400 scholarly publications, mostly related to the intersection between rheumatology and ophthalmology.[tpl]cite web|author=Nature Reviews Rheumatology |url=http://www.nature.com/nrrheum/journal/v8/n5/authors/nrrheum.2012.43.html |title=About the authors : Spondyloarthritis: The eyes have it: uveitis in patients with spondyloarthritis : Nature Reviews Rheumatology |publisher=Nature.com |date= |accessdate=2013-11-06[/tpl] He is a co-author of book, The Clinical Neurology of Rheumatic Diseases.[tpl]cite journal| doi=10.1056/NEJM199705083361922 | title=Book Review Clinical Neurology of Rheumatic Diseases by Richard B. ==Education==

Rosenbaum is a National Merit Scholar who graduated from Harvard College in 1971, magna cum laude. He graduated from Yale Medical School with honors in 1975. He did an internship and residency in internal medicine at Stanford Medical Center from 1975 to 1978. He was a fellow of the Arthritis Foundation under the supervision of Hugh O. McDevitt at Stanford from 1978 to 1981.[tpl]cite web|author=Friederike Mackensen |url=http://www.iovs.org/content/52/10/7711.full |title=Introducing James T. Rosenbaum, the 2011 Recipient of the Friedenwald Award |publisher=Iovs.org |date=2011-09-28 |accessdate=2013-11-06[/tpl]

==Family==

Rosenbaum is part of a notable family that includes ten physicians including his father, Edward E Rosenbaum, author of The Doctor.[tpl]cite web|author=Steve Beaven, The Oregonian |url=http://www.oregonlive.com/news/index.ssf/2009/05/prominent_portland_doctor_and.html |title=Prominent Portland doctor and author dies |publisher=OregonLive.com |date= |accessdate=2013-11-06[/tpl]
