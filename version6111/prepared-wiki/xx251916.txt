[[Robert Grosvenor (aviator)]]

CATEGORIES: 1895 births, 1953 deaths, Royal Air Force personnel of World War I

Captain Robert Arthur Grosvenor was a World War I flying ace credited with 16 aerial victories.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/grosvenor.php  Retrieved 9 January 2010. 
He was the son of Helen Sheffield and Lieutenant Colonel Arthur Hugh Grosvenor.[tpl]cite web |last=Lundy |first=Darryl |url=http://www.thepeerage.com/p1026.htm |title= Retrieved 9 January 2010. |publisher=The Peerage[/tpl][tpl]Verify credibility|failed=y |date=September 2012[/tpl]
Robert Arthur Grosvenor scored his first triumph on 6 December 1917 while flying a RAF SE.5a for No. 84 Squadron. He would not score again until 18 February, but during the next three months after that scored 15 more victories. His victory tally was split evenly between enemy aircraft destroyed and those driven down, at eight each. He shared a victory with another pilot in both categories.http://www.theaerodrome.com/aces/england/grosvenor.php  Retrieved 9 January 2010.
Postwar, Grosvenor married Doris May Wignall on 9 December 1925.[tpl]cite web |last=Lundy |first=Darryl |url=http://www.thepeerage.com/p1026.htm |title= p. 1026 |publisher=The Peerage[/tpl][tpl]Verify credibility|failed=y |date=September 2012[/tpl] He was elected as a member of the Royal Aero Club on 2 May 1929.http://www.flightglobal.com/pdfarchive/view/1929/1929%20-%200931.html Retrieved 9 January 2010.

==Honors and awards==

Military Cross (MC)
Lt. (T./Capt.) Robert Arthur Grosvenor, Dn. Gds., and R.A.F.
For conspicuous gallantry and devotion to duty as a leader of offensive patrols against hostile aircraft and troops on the ground. On one occasion his patrol was attacked from the rear by five enemy scouts. By skilful piloting he manoeuvred his machine into position behind one of the scouts, which he shot down in flames. He was attacked again, and drove the scout to destruction. On other occasions he has destroyed and driven down out of control at least eight hostile machines.http://www.flightglobal.com/pdfarchive/view/1918/1918%20-%200852.html Retrieved 9 January 2010.http://www.gazettes-online.co.uk/issues/30813/supplements/8804/page.pdf Retrieved 9 January 2010.
Military Cross (MC) Bar
Lt. (T./Capt.) Robert Arthur Grosvenor, M.C., Dgn. Gds. and R.A.F.
For conspicuous gallantry and devotion to duty in attacking enemy aircraft. He has lately destroyed two machines and driven down four others out of control. He has shown brilliant leadership and dash against heavy odds.Supplement to the London Gazette, 16 September 1918 (30901/10891)
