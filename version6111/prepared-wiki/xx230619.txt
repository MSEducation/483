[[Clearing Corporation]]

CATEGORIES: Financial services companies of the United States

The Clearing Corporation (TCC, former CCorp) is "a Delaware corporation owned by 17 stockholders (which include banks Goldman Sachs, Deutsche Bank and Morgan Stanley, as well as inter-dealer brokers ICAP and GFI Group and German derivatives exchange Eurex), many of whom represent the world-wide derivatives marketplace participants and market makers." http://www.efinancialnews.com/tradingandtechnology/derivatives/content/2451991530http://www.clearingcorp.com/about/who-are-we.html
In 2008, the Clearing Corporation and the Depository Trust & Clearing Corporation (DTCC) announced a credit default swap (CDS) public clearing facility http://www.clearingcorp.com/press/pressreleases/20080528-dtcc-cds.html that will be linked to DTCC's Trade Information Warehouse.  On September 29 of 2008, CCorp affirmed that the CDS clearing counterparty will launch by year-end.http://www.clearingcorp.com/press/pressreleases/20080929-nybank.htmlhttp://www.finextra.com/fullstory.asp?id=19050
