[[Celso Golmayo Torriente]]

CATEGORIES: 1879 births, 1924 deaths, Cuban chess players, Spanish chess players, Cuban people of Spanish descent

Celso (Celsito) Golmayo y de la Torriente (1879, Havana – 22 January 1924, Seville)[tpl]citation|last=Gaige|first=Jeremy|author-link=Jeremy Gaige|year=1987|title=Chess Personalia, A Biobibliography|publisher=McFarland|isbn=0-7864-2353-6|page=143[/tpl] was a Cuban–Spanish chess master.
He was the son of Celso Golmayo y Zúpide and the brother of Manuel Golmayo y de la Torriente.
Celsito Golmayo won the 1897 Cuban championship in Havana after a play-off against Andrés Clemente Vázquez. In that tournament Enrique Ostolaza came third, Juan Corzo fourth and Manuel Golmayo fifth.  Golmayo's victory was unusual because national championship were rarely won by teenagers in the 19th century.Chess Notes by Edward Winter
