[[Kent District Library]]

CATEGORIES: Education in Kent County, Michigan, Public libraries in Michigan, Libraries established in 1927

The Kent District Library is a public library system located in Kent County, Michigan. With eighteen branch locations and an annual circulation of more than 5 million items (2007 circulation data), the Kent District Library is one of the largest and busiest library systems in all of Michigan.[tpl]cite web |url=http://woodlands.lib.mi.us/stat/ |title=Michigan Public Library Statistics 2009 |year=2009 |work=http://woodlands.lib.mi.us/stat/ |publisher=Library of Michigan |accessdate=10 November 2010[/tpl]

==History==

The beginnings of the library are in 1927 as a library extension work project of the Kent County Federation of Women's Clubs.  The Kent County Library Association was established in 1936 and functioned as a department of Kent County until 1994 when it became Kent District Library.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kdl.org/about |title=About KDL |year=2010 |work=KDL.org |publisher=Kent District Library |accessdate=18 September 2010[/tpl] 
In summer 2013, Kent District Library invited local Wikipedians to use library space and resources for Meetups, and offered Wikipedia editing classes to the community.

==Branch locations==

The library has 18 branches throughout the county.
