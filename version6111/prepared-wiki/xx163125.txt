[[The Sister Dominica Manor]]

CATEGORIES: Skyscrapers in Providence, Rhode Island, Buildings and structures completed in 1966, Residential buildings in Rhode Island

The Sister Dominica Manor, also known as Federal Hill Manor and originally called Bradford House, is a high-rise building located in the Federal Hill district of Providence, Rhode Island. Standing at 175 feet (53 meters), it is currently the 15th-tallest building in the city.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/bu/?id=streetsisterdominicamanor-providence-ri-usa|title=The Sister Dominica Manor|accessdate=2007-08-02|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl] The Sister Domininca Manor has 16 floors, and was completed in 1966. 
The Sister Dominica Manor was constructed as the tallest of three low-income elderly housing projects built by Rhode Island Housing and the city of Providence in the 1960s.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.providenceri.com/history/centuries2.html|title=History & Facts; America's Renaissance City: THREE AND ONE-HALF CENTURIES AT A GLANCE: The Age of Optimism: 1900-1929|accessdate=2007-07-04|publisher=ProvidenceRI.com[/tpl] It was the tallest all-residential building in Providence until late 2007, when the Waterplace Towers were completed.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/cx/?id=waterplace-providence|title=Waterplace|accessdate=2007-08-02|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl]
