Paul Michael Valley (born September 24, 1966) is an American television and stage actor.

==Early life and education==

Valley was born in Whitefish Bay, Wisconsin.[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2012[/tpl] When he was eight years old he moved with his family to Greenwich, Connecticut.[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2005/10/21/AR2005102102041.html | title = Another World, Indeed | work = The Washington Post | first = Chip | last = Crews | date = October 22, 2005[/tpl] In 1984 he moved to Washington, D.C., where he attended American University for two years, followed by a year as an apprentice to Michael Kahn, the artistic director of the Shakespeare Theatre Company. Valley then attended the Juilliard School in New York City, where he was a member of the drama division's Group 20 (1987â€“1991).[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2011-2012/1111/index.php | title = Alumni News | publisher = The Juilliard School | date = November 2011 | archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20120718161936/http://juilliard.edu/alumni/news/news_decades/2011-2012/1111/index.php | archivedate = 2012-07-18[/tpl] After his third year at Juilliard, he left to take on roles in television soap operas.

==Career==

Among his numerous television credits, his most memorable and long-lasting role was his portrayal of Ryan Harrison on the soap opera Another World from 1990 to 1995.  He left the show when executive producer Jill Farren Phelps fired him and had his character shot to death by his brother Grant (Mark Pinter).
Valley then took on stage roles, including Bertram in All's Well That Ends Well at the Shakespeare Theatre and as Thomas Jefferson in 1776 at the Roundabout Theatre, which moved to Broadway in 1997.[tpl]IBDB name|id=71499|name=Paul Michael Valley[/tpl] He followed that with work in regional theater and also appeared in guest roles on the television series Third Watch, Ed, and Law and Order: SVU.
In 2011, he starred in the off-Broadway comedy Any Given Monday at the 59E59 Theaters.[tpl]cite web | url = http://offoffbroadway.broadwayworld.com/article/Paul-Michael-Valley-Joins-Cast-of-ANY-GIVEN-MONDAY-at-59E59-20110916 | title = Paul Michael Valley Joins Cast of ANY GIVEN MONDAY at 59E59 | work = BroadwayWorld.com | date = September 16, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url = http://theater.nytimes.com/2011/10/18/theater/reviews/any-given-monday-at-59e59-theaters-review.html | title = Review: 'Any Given Monday' | work = The New York Times | first = Daniel M. | last = Gold | date = October 17, 2011[/tpl]
