[[Anti-Bases Campaign]]

CATEGORIES: Organisations based in Christchurch, Peace organizations

Anti-Bases Campaign is an organisation campaigning to remove foreign military bases and intelligence installations from New Zealand, and to dismantle the Government Communications Security Bureau.[tpl]cite news | last = Bromby | first = Jocelyn| coauthors =| title =  Group hopes for end to Waihopai base| work =| pages =| publisher = The Press| date = 2000-01-11| url =| accessdate =  [/tpl]
The group organises regular protests at the Government Communications Security Bureau base in the Waihopai valley have published the Peace Researcher (ISSN 1173-2679) since its inception in 1983.
The group was founded by Owen Wilkes.[tpl]cite journal|last=Horton|first=Murray|date=July 2010|title=Owen Wilkes' SIS file - Spies Play Hard To Get|journal=Peace Researcher|issue=40|issn=1173-2679|url=http://www.converge.org.nz/abc/pr40-199[/tpl] According to the New Zealand Herald, "Claims by Wilkes in the early 1980s that the communications centre at Tangimoana in the lower North Island was actually an electronic spy station and part of an American worldwide network (Echelon) were immediately denied by the Government. Activists to this day are calling for the closure of Tangimoana and Waihopai base near Blenheim, another suspected spy base".[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10125391 |title=Obituary: Owen Wilkes |author=Falconer, Phoebe |date=14 May 2005 |work=The New Zealand Herald |accessdate=16 September 2011[/tpl]
