[[901 Fifth Avenue]]

CATEGORIES: Leadership in Energy and Environmental Design basic silver certified buildings, Office buildings in Seattle, Washington, Office buildings completed in 1973, Skyscrapers in Seattle, Washington, Skyscrapers between 150 and 199 meters

901 Fifth Avenue is a [tpl]convert|163.38|m|abbr=on[/tpl] skyscraper in downtown Seattle, Washington. It was completed in 1973 and has 42 floors. It is the 11th tallest building in Seattle, and was designed by John Graham and Associates. The building was opened as the Bank of California Building. Flood lights illuminate all sides of the tower at night. It was renovated in 2007 and achieved LEED Certified Silver status, which is rare for preexisting buildings.
Formerly the Union Bank of California Building, the name was changed to 901 Fifth Avenue following the sale by Beacon Capital Partners to Kennedy Wilson Inc. of Beverly Hills. From its opening in 1973 to July 2007 the building address was 900 Fourth Avenue.
