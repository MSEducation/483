[[Helen Rose]]

CATEGORIES: 1904 births, 1985 deaths, American people of German descent, American people of Russian descent, American costume designers, Artists from Chicago, Illinois, People from Palm Springs, California, Wedding dress designers, Best Costume Design Academy Award winners

Helen Rose (February 2, 1904 – November 9, 1985) was an American costume designer and clothing designer who spent the bulk of her career with Metro-Goldwyn-Mayer.

==Career==

Helen Rose was born on February 2, 1904 to William Bromberg and Ray Bobbs in Chicago, Illinois of German and Russian descent.  She attended the Chicago Academy of Fine Arts and then designed nightclub and stage costumes for various acts.[tpl]cite book|author=H. Kristina Haugland|title=Grace kelly: icon of style to royal bride|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=JE5ITOBYBJUC&pg=PT14&lpg=PT14#v=onepage&q&f=false|publisher=Yale University Press|year=2006|accessdate=May 2, 2011[/tpl]  She moved to Los Angeles in 1929, where she designed outfits for the Ice Follies.  In the early 40s she spent two years working for 20th Century Fox, where she designed wardrobes for musical selections.  In 1943 MGM hired her in the wake of Adrian's departure and by the late '40s Rose was promoted to chief designer at the studio.
Rose won two Academy Awards for Best Costume Design, for The Bad and the Beautiful in 1952 and for I'll Cry Tomorrow in 1955. She was nominated a further eight times and was also very well known for designing famous wedding dresses of the era.  She designed the famous wedding dress of Grace Kelly  [tpl]cite news|title='Iconic royal wedding gowns |url=http://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/fashion-articles/iconic-royal-wedding-gowns#slide-3|newspaper=Harpers Bazaar[/tpl] when she married Prince Rainier of Monaco in 1956.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.slantmagazine.com/house/2011/03/designing-woman-helen-rose/|title=Designing Woman: Helen Rose|author=Ronald Bergan|work=Slant Magazine|date=March 31, 2011|accessdate=May 2, 2011[/tpl]  She also designed clothing for Elizabeth Taylor in the movies Father of the Bride and Cat on a Hot Tin Roof as well as Elizabeth Taylor's wedding dress when she married Conrad "Nicky" Hilton.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thesun.co.uk/sol/homepage/woman/fashion/3488981/Elizabeth-Taylor-the-fashion-icon.html|title=Elizabeth Taylor: the fashion icon|author=Toni Jones|coauthors=Bella Battle|work=The Sun|date=March 24, 2011|accessdate=May 2, 2011[/tpl]
In the late 60s, Rose left the studio to open her own design business and continued to provide fancy attire for the famed and the wealthy. She also wrote a fashion column.  She wrote two books - her autobiography Just Make Them Beautiful in 1976 and "The Glamorous World of Helen Rose".  In the '70s Rose also staged a traveling fashion show featuring some of her MGM-designed costumes that was called "The Helen Rose Show".[tpl]cite web|url=http://vintagefashionguild.org/fashion-history/helen-rose/|title= Helen Rose|work = Vintage Fashion Guild|accessdate=May 2, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1985-11-12/local/me-5029_1_helen-rose|author=Burt A. Folkart|title=Film Costume Designer Helen Rose Dies|work=LA Times|date=November 12, 1985|accessdate=May 2, 2011[/tpl]
Helen was married to Harry V. Rose, whose birth name was Harry Rosenstein (1902–1993), and they had a daughter. She died in Palm Springs, California in 1985."Rose, Helen (1904–1985)."
