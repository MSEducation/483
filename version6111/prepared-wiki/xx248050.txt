[[Hans Ramberg]]

CATEGORIES: 1917 births, 1998 deaths, Norwegian geologists, Swedish geologists, University of Oslo alumni, University of Chicago faculty, Uppsala University faculty, Members of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences, Wollaston Medal winners, Norwegian expatriates in Sweden, Norwegian expatriates in the United States

Hans Ramberg (15 March 1917 – 7 June 1998) was a Norwegian-Swedish geologist. The mineral rambergite was named after him.

==Life and work==

He received his Ph.D from the University of Oslo in 1946. He subsequently worked at the University of Chicago (1948–1961) and at the Geophysical Laboratory  of the Carnegie Institution for Science (1952–1955) at the Universidade Federal de Ouro Preto (1960–1961) and for the rest of his career at the University of Uppsala (1961–1982).[tpl]cite book | url = http://books.google.com/?id=cyJJniAWICQC | isbn = 978-0-8137-1193-5 | editor = Hemin A. Koyi and Neil S. In 1967, he was elected a member of the Royal Swedish Academy of Sciences, and in 1972 he was awarded the Wollaston Medal.
