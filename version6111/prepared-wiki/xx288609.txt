[[United States House of Representatives elections in Washington, 2014]]

CATEGORIES: United States House of Representatives elections, 2014, United States House of Representatives elections in Washington (state)

The 2014 United States House of Representatives elections in Washington will be held on Tuesday, November 4, 2014 to elect the ten U.S. Representatives from the state of Washington, one from each of the state's ten congressional districts.
==District 1== 
The first district is represented by Democrat Suzan DelBene who initially won her seat in 2012. DelBene is running for reelection.
For the Republicans, former Microsoft software engineer Pedro Celis and former Snohomish County Council legislative aide Ed Moats are running.[tpl]cite web|url=http://seattletimes.com/html/localnews/2023001350_delbenechallengerxml.html |title=Fellow Microsoft alum to run against DelBene for Congress |publisher=seattletimes.com |date=February 26, 2014 |accessdate=February 28, 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.heraldnet.com/article/20140227/BLOG13/140229241/Two-Republicans-emerge-to-challenge-DelBene |title=Two Republicans emerge to challenge DelBene |publisher=HeraldNet.com |date=February 27, 2014 |accessdate=March 3, 2014[/tpl]

==District 2==

The second district is represented by Democrat Rick Larsen who initially won his seat since 2000. Larsen is running for reelection.

==District 3==

The third district is represented by Republican Jaime Herrera Beutler who initially won her seat in 2010. Herrera Beutler is running for reelection. 
Businessman and former Maria Cantwell aide Bob Dingethal is running for the Democratic nomination.[tpl]cite news|last=Connelly|first=Joel|title=Rep. Herrera-Beutler gets credible challenger|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/seattlepolitics/2013/12/10/rep-herrera-beutler-gets-credible-challenger/|accessdate=10 December 2013|newspaper=SeattlePi|date=10 December 2013[/tpl] Herrera Beutler is also opposed by Republican Michael Delavar, who ran against Brian Baird in 2008 and served as a councilman for Washougal from 2009 to 2011.[tpl]cite news|last=Mathieu|first=Stevie|title=Herrera Beutler draws Republican challenger in 2014|url=http://www.columbian.com/news/2013/oct/28/herrera-beutler-draws-republican-challenger-2014/|accessdate=13 January 2014|newspaper=The Columbian|date=October 28, 2013[/tpl]

==District 4==

The 4th district is a large and predominantly rural district in Central Washington that encompasses numerous counties and is dominated by the Tri-Cities and Yakima areas. Republican Doc Hastings, who has represented the 4th district since 1995, is retiring.[tpl]cite web|title=Rep. Doc Hastings (R-Wash.) to retire|publisher=The Washington Post|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/blogs/post-politics/wp/2014/02/13/rep-doc-hastings-r-wash-to-retire/|accessdate=February 13, 2014|date=February 13, 2014[/tpl]
The district is not considered to be competitive. The last time any Democrat running for any partisan office carried it was when State Auditor Brian Sonntag was re-elected in 2004.[tpl]cite web|title=Last time a Dem carried|publisher=Twitter|author=Stephen Wolf|url=https://twitter.com/StephenWolfUNC/status/434023259193876480|accessdate=February 14, 2014|date=February 13, 2014[/tpl]
