On 9 August 2003, an open air stage located 350 ft from the sea[ref]

</ref> which was used by religious groups and political parties to address gatherings, was demolished by the state government in order to modernise the beach. ==Incidents==

In 1966, a tramp ship SS Damatis sank near the Marina due to a cyclone in the region.[ref]
</ref>
The beachfront was severely damaged by the 2004 Indian Ocean Tsunami. The tsunami waves, caused due to an M 9.0 magnitude earthquake at about 257 km south-southeast of Banda Aceh, Sumatra, Indonesia, on the Indian Ocean floor on 26 December 2004 at 6.20 am IST, struck the beach, which is about 2028 km North West from the epicentre, at 8.40 am IST. The reported height of the tsunami waves at the beach was 6 m[ref]
</ref> which washed away about 206 persons on the beach,<ref>
</ref><ref name="BeachClosedFollowingTsunami">
</ref><ref>
</ref> most of whom were morning joggers and children playing cricket on the beach, including a few tourists.<ref>
