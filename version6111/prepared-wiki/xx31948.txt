[[Antheia]]

CATEGORIES: Greek goddesses, Epithets of Aphrodite, Epithets of Hera

Antheia ([tpl]lang-grc|Ἀνθεία[/tpl]) was one of the Charites, or Graces, of Greek mythology and was the goddess of flowers and flowery wreaths. She was depicted in Athenian vase painting as one of the attendants of Aphrodite.[tpl]cite web| title=Kharities | url=http://www.theoi.com/Ouranios/Kharites.html | publisher=Theoi Project | accessdate=2013-12-29[/tpl]
Her name which is derived from the ancient Greek word [tpl]lang|grc|ανθος[/tpl] anthos means 'flower' or 'blossom'.  Her symbols are gold colored items.  She was known to the Romans as Anthea. Her center of worship was on the island of Crete. The name Antheia was also given to Hera and connected to the Horae,[tpl]cite book | author=Joan V. O'Brien | year=1993 | title=The Transformation of Hera | url=http://books.google.com/books?isbn=0847678083 | page=139 [/tpl] under which she had a temple at Argos.Pausanias, Description of Greece ii. 22. § 1. It was also an epithet of Aphrodite at Knossos.[tpl]cite book| author=R. F. Willetts | year=1977 | title=The Civilization of Ancient Crete |url=http://books.google.com/books?isbn=0520034066 |page=209[/tpl][tpl]EB1911|wstitle=Aphrodite[/tpl] She was the goddess of vegetation, gardens, blossoms, especially worshipped in spring and near lowlands and marshlands, favorable to the growth of vegetation. She was also the goddess of human love.[tpl]cite book | author=Ramesh Chopra | year=2005 | title=Academic Dictionary Of Mythology | url=http://books.google.com/books?isbn=8182052327 | page=28 [/tpl]
Antheia is also the Greek name of Ancient Sozopolis in modern Bulgaria, and another Antheia was a village which was later adopted into Patras around 1000 BC.
