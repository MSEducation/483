[[Eastern Lower Carniolan subdialect]]

CATEGORIES: Slovene dialects

The Eastern Lower Carniolan subdialect (vzhodnodolenjski govor,Smole, Vera. 1998. "Slovenska narečja." Enciklopedija Slovenije vol. 12, pp. 1–5. Ljubljana: Mladinska knjiga, p. 2. vzhodna dolenjščinaLogar, Tine. 1996. Dialektološke in jezikovnozgodovinske razprave. Ljubljana: SAZU, p. 42.) is a Slovene subdialect in the Lower Carniolan dialect group. It is spoken south of the Lower Sava Valley dialect in the watersheds of the Mirna and Temenica rivers, east of a line running from west of Trebnje and west of Novo Mesto to the lower Krka Valley. The dialect includes the settlements of Novo Mesto, Šentjernej, Kostanjevica na Krki, Trebnje, Šentrupert, Krmelj, Mokronog, Raka, and Škocjan.Toporišič, Jože. 1992. Enciklopedija slovenskega jezika. Ljubljana: Cankarjeva založba, p. 360.

==Phonological and morphological characteristics==

The Eastern Lower Carniolan subdialect has a pitch accent.
