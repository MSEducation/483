[[Sherwood House]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Houses completed in 1740, Buildings and structures in Yonkers, New York, Houses in Westchester County, New York, Museums in Westchester County, New York, Historic house museums in New York

Sherwood House is a historic home located at Yonkers, Westchester County, New York. It was built in 1740 and is a 3-story frame structure on an exposed basement built into a hillside.  Additions and modifications took place about 1810, 1850, and 1880.  It features a 2-story porch spanning the entire facade and 2 dutch doors. The house was extensively remodeled in 1955.  Also on the property is a [tpl]frac|1|1|2[/tpl]2-story, frame caretaker's cottage and a barn.  Both were built about 1840.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=10825|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Sherwood House|date=January 1984|accessdate=2011-01-01 |author=Austin O'Brien|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=10794|title=Accompanying nine photos[/tpl] 
It was added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1984.  It is now owned by the Yonkers Historical Society, which offers tours.
