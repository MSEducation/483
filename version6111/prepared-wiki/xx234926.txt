Richard Lee "Dick" Hoover (December 15, 1929 â€“ September 17, 2009) was an American professional bowler. He won two American Bowling Congress Masters titles in 1956 and 1957, and helped start the PBA in 1958 with founder Eddie Elias.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bowlingdigital.com/bowl/node/7107 |title=Professional Bowlers Association Pioneer Dick Hoover dies |publisher=bowlingdigital.com |accessdate=October 17, 2009[/tpl]
Born in Roseville, Ohio, Hoover grew up in Akron. He gained his first fame as a 16-year-old schoolboy by rolling an 847 series, the highest in the nation during 1946. Five years later, at 21, he became the youngest bowler to win the All-Star tournament. A four-time All-American, he was inducted into the American Bowling Congress Hall of Fame in 1974.[tpl]cite web |url=http://bowling.teamusa.org/news/article/16095 |title=USBC Hall of Famer Dick Hoover dies at age 79 |date=September 18, 2009 |publisher=bowling.teamusa.org |accessdate=October 17, 2009[/tpl]
Hoover owned and operated Dick Hoover's Lanes in Brunswick, Ohio.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dickhooverslanes.com/ |title=Dick Hoover's Lanes |publisher=dickhooverslanes.com |accessdate=October 17, 2009[/tpl]
