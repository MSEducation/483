[[Simon Morgan]]

CATEGORIES: 1966 births, Living people, Sportspeople from Birmingham, West Midlands, Association football defenders, English footballers, Leicester City F.C. players, Fulham F.C. players, Brighton & Hove Albion F.C. players, The Football League players, England under-21 international footballers

Simon Morgan (born 5 September 1966 in Birmingham) is a former professional footballer, who most famously played for Fulham and Leicester. He also represented England at Under 21 level, playing two matches in 1986.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rsssf.com/tablese/eng-u21-intres86.html |title=England - U-21 International Results 1986-1995 - Details |publisher=RSSSF|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl]
Morgan began his football career with Leicester City, representing them in the First and Second Divisions of The Football League,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.foxeshistory.co.uk/playerdetails.php?pid=356 |title=Leicester City career stats for Simon Morgan |publisher=Foxes History|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] before being bought to then Third Division[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1990-1991/table |title=Fulham 1990-1991 : English Division Three (old) Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] Fulham in October 1990 by Alan Dicks. The Cottagers finished 21st in Morgan's first season there (avoiding relegation by two points), and 9th in his second season (during which the club replaced Dicks with Don Mackay[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/history/managers/alan-dicks |title=Alan Dicks 1990-1991 |publisher=Fulham Football Club|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/history/managers/don-mackay |title=Don Mackay 1991-1994 |publisher=Fulham Football Club|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] and missed out on the play-offs by four points)[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1991-1992/table |title=Fulham 1991-1992 : English Division Three (old) Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] with a squad which Morgan felt "should have got promoted but for one reason or another we blew it". However, a 12th-placed finish in 1992/93[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1992-1993/table |title=Fulham 1992-1993 : English Division Two Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] was followed by the club slipping to 18th with nine matches remaining of the 1993/94 season,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1993-1994/table/1994-03-26 |title=English Division Two Table as of 26 March 1994 |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] as a result of which Mackay was replaced by Ray Lewington,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/history/managers/ray-lewington |title=Ray Lewington 1986-1990, 1991, 1994 & 2007 |publisher=Fulham Football Club|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] and after going through these managerial changes, Fulham were relegated to the Fourth Division.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1993-1994/table |title=Fulham 1993-1994 : English Division Two Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl]
After finishing within three points of the play-offs in 1994/95 under Ian Branfoot,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1994-1995/table |title=Fulham 1994-1995 : English Division Three Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] the club had slipped to 23rd by February 1996,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1995-1996/table/1996-02-17 |title=English Division Three Table as of 17 February 1996 |publisher=Statto|accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl] and eventually appointed Micky Adams as manager, who helped them retain their Football League status.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1995-1996/table |title=Fulham 1995-1996 : English Division Three Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] The team was promoted to the Second Division in 1997.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/1996-1997/table |title=Fulham 1996-1997 : English Division Three Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] The club was then bought by Mohamed Al Fayed,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/history/mohamed-al-fayed |title=Mohamed Al Fayed|publisher=Fulham F.C.|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] which was followed by Fulham's rise into the Premier League in 2001.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.statto.com/football/teams/fulham/2000-2001/table |title=Fulham 2000-2001 : English Division One Table |publisher=Statto|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] Due to injury, Morgan only played once in Fulham's victorious 2000-01 Division One campaign, coming on as a substitute against Wolverhampton Wanderers, which earned him the Man of the Match award despite playing only 13 minutes.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2008/dec/03/football-trivia-knowledge-premier-league |title=What's the shortest time a man of the match has been on the pitch? | first=Howard | last=Swains|last2=Fanning|first2= Evan |publisher=Guardian |date=3 December 2008|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl]
Morgan was granted a testimonial year in the 2000-01 season.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.theguardian.com/football/2000/apr/01/sport.comment |title=Diary |publisher=Guardian |date=1 April 2000|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] This included a special edition of Fulham fanzine TOOFIF, called "There's Only One Simon Morgan" (TOOSM),[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sport.co.uk/football/three-fulham-cult-heroes/35295/ |title=Three Fulham Cult Heroes|publisher=sport.co.uk|date=10 March 2010|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] and culminated in a friendly match against Spurs,[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/f/fulham/863921.stm |title=Spurs lack flair |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2 August 2000|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] supported by a Fulham vs. Chelsea veterans' game (in which his son played). Morgan was unable to participate in the Spurs match due to injury, but kicked the match off before being shown a pre-arranged red card by referee Graham Poll.
Morgan then went to play at Brighton & Hove Albion for a season, scoring once against Northampton, before bringing his playing career to an end in 2002.[tpl]cite web |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/brighton/1978560.stm |title=Morgan forced to retire |publisher=BBC Sport |date=9 May 2002|accessdate=6 December 2013[/tpl] After retiring he rejoined Fulham as head of the club's community scheme. He left the club in 2007 to become the Premier League's Head of Community Development.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fulhamfc.com/Club/News/NewsArticles/2007/June/MorganDeparts.aspx |archiveurl=http://archive.is/vjtH |archivedate=30 July 2012 |title=Simon Morgan Departs|publisher= Fulham Football Club.|date=11 June 2007 |accessdate=5 December 2013[/tpl]
