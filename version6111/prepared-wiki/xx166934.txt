[[88th Division (People's Republic of China)]]

CATEGORIES: Infantry divisions of the People's Volunteer Army, Infantry divisions of the People's Liberation Army

The 88th Division was a military formation of the People's Volunteer Army (Chinese People's Volunteers (CPV) or Chinese Communist Forces (CCF)) during the Korean War. They were a component of the 30th Army. The 88th Division was actually assigned though as a reinforcing Division to the 26th Army.[tpl]cite book| chapterurl = http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/20-2-1/sn39.htm| chapter = Chapter XXXIX The Big Question| last =  Appleman| first =  Roy E. | title = South to the Naktong, North to the Yalu| page = 769| id = CMH Pub 20-2-1| url = http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/20-2-1/toc.htm| publisher = United States Army Center of Military History[/tpl][tpl]Cite book| chapterurl = http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/ebb/ch3.htm| chapter = Chapter III The Enemy| url = http://www.history.army.mil/books/korea/ebb/fm.htm| title =Ebb and Flow November 1950-July 1951| page = 55| series = United States Army in the Korean War | year = 1990| first = Billy C.| last = Mossman[/tpl]
