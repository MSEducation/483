[[Formally étale morphism]]

CATEGORIES: Morphisms of schemes

In commutative algebra and algebraic geometry, a morphism is called formally étale if has a lifting property that is analogous to being a local diffeomorphism.

==Formally étale homomorphisms of rings==

Let A be a topological ring, and let B be a topological A-algebra.  B is formally étale if for all discrete A-algebras C, all nilpotent ideals J of C, and all continuous A-homomorphisms [tpl]nowrap|u : B → C/J[/tpl], there exists a unique continuous A-algebra map [tpl]nowrap|v : B → C[/tpl] such that [tpl]nowrap|u [tpl]=[/tpl] pv[/tpl], where [tpl]nowrap|p : C → C/J[/tpl] is the canonical projection.EGA 0IV, Définition 19.10.2.
Formally étale is equivalent to formally smooth plus formally unramified.EGA 0IV, Définition 19.10.2.

==Formally étale morphisms of schemes==

Since the structure sheaf of a scheme naturally carries only the discrete topology, the notion of formally étale for schemes is analogous to formally étale for the discrete topology for rings.  That is, let [tpl]nowrap|f : X → Y[/tpl] be a morphism of schemes, Z be an affine Y-scheme, J be a nilpotent sheaf of ideals on Z, and [tpl]nowrap|i : Z0 → Z[/tpl] be the closed immersion determined by J.  Then f is formally étale if for every Y-morphism [tpl]nowrap|g : Z0 → X[/tpl], there exists a unique Y-morphism [tpl]nowrap|s : Z → X[/tpl] such that [tpl]nowrap|g [tpl]=[/tpl] si[/tpl].EGA IV4, Définition 17.1.1.
It is equivalent to let Z be any Y-scheme and let J be a locally nilpotent sheaf of ideals on Z.EGA IV4, Remarques 17.1.2 (iv).
