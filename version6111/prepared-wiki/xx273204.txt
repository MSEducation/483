[[Tajh Boyd]]

CATEGORIES: 1990 births, Living people, American football quarterbacks, Clemson Tigers football players

Tajh Boyd (born September 25, 1990) is an American football quarterback. He played college football at Clemson. He was the Tigers starting quarterback from 2011 to 2013.

==Early years==

Boyd attended Phoebus High School in Hampton, Virginia. He was awarded the Pete Dawkins Trophy for being the co-MVP of the 2009 U.S. Army All-American Bowl after completing seven of nine passes for 179 yards and three touchdowns.Army All-American Bowl notebook: Boyd faces yet another decision. ==College career==

After being redshirted in 2009, Boyd spent 2010 as the backup quarterback to Kyle Parker. He finished the season completing 33 of 63 passes for 329 yards with four touchdowns and three interceptions. 
Boyd took over as the starter in 2011.Tigers name Boyd starter. The Augusta Chronicle. April 21, 2011. Retrieved April 15, 2014. Boyd led the Tigers to a 10-4 record, winning the ACC Championship and setting up a game against West Virginia in the Orange Bowl. Although the Tigers lost the Orange Bowl in record fashion, 70-33, Boyd had a highly successful year. He finished the year completing 298 of 499 passes for 3,828 yards and 33 passing touchdowns.
