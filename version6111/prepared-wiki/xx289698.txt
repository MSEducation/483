[[Poston Butte]]

CATEGORIES: Landforms of Pinal County, Arizona, Mountains of Arizona

Poston Butte is a hill located along Hunt Highway in Florence, Pinal County, Arizona, United States,[tpl]cite gnis |id=9682 |name=Poston Butte |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl] near the eastern end of the San Tan Mountain Range.[tpl]cite web |url=http://repository.azgs.az.gov/category/place-keywords/poston-butte |title=Poston Butte |publisher=AZGS Document Repository |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl] Geologically, it is characterized by basalts overlying altered granite.[tpl]cite web |url=http://archives.arizonageologicalsoc.org/guidebooks/AGS_1973-11_Poston_Mineral_S.pdf |title=Poston, Butte-Mineral, Eutte-Sacaton: Fall Field Trip |publisher=Arizona Geological Society |date=November 11, 1973 |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl]
This hill is noted for having the pyramidal tomb of Charles Debrille Poston at its summit. Poston lobbied President Lincoln and Congress to create the Territory of Arizona[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gutenberg.org/catalog/world/readfile?fk_files=1479850&pageno=31 |title=Building a State in Apache Land |last=Poston |first=Charles D. |page=31 |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl] and was appointed Superintendent of Indian Affairs because of his familiarity with the Native populations of the area.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gutenberg.org/catalog/world/readfile?fk_files=1479850&pageno=31 |title=Building a State in Apache Land |last=Poston |first=Charles D. |page=32 |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl] The tomb can be accessed by a [tpl]convert|0.6|mi|adj=on[/tpl] trail starting from the trailhead near Hunt Highway.[tpl]cite web |url=http://hikearizona.com/decoder.php?ZTN=1977 |title=Poston's Butte â€“ Florence, AZ |publisher=HikeArizona.com |accessdate=2014-02-08[/tpl]
