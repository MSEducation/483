[[2007 Wilkes-Barre/Scranton Pioneers season]]

CATEGORIES: Wilkes-Barre/Scranton Pioneers seasons, 2007 Arena Football League season

The 2007 Wilkes-Barre/Scranton Pioneers season was the team's sixth season as a member of the af2.  Under coach Rich Ingold and led by quarterback Ryan Vena, the Pioneers advanced to their fourth straight playoff appearance, winning three games at home to earn a spot in the ArenaCup.  The Pioneers lost the championship to the Tulsa Talons 73–66 after the Pioneers' final pass play in the waning seconds of the game fell short.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.af2.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=3525&ATCLID=1178090&ISWIDE=1 | title=Tulsa claims ArenaCup8 title in epic battle, 73–66 | date=2007-08-25[/tpl]  Several members of the team were given season awards, including David Davis as 2007 Havoc Kicker of the year,[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.af2.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=3525&ATCLID=1151235&ISWIDE=1 | title=David Davis named 2007 Havoc Kicker of the Year | date=2007-08-14[/tpl] Ryan Vena as Schutt Offensive Player of the Year,[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.af2.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=3525&ATCLID=1157668&ISWIDE=1 | title=Ryan Vena named Schutt Offensive Player of the Year | date=2007-08-20[/tpl] and Coach Ingold as Sportexe Coach of the Year.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.af2.com/ViewArticle.dbml?DB_OEM_ID=3525&ATCLID=1153740&ISWIDE=1 | title=Rich Ingold named Sportexe Coach of the Year | date=2007-08-17[/tpl]
