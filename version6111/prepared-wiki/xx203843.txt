[[Mladen Šubarić]]

CATEGORIES: 1920 births, Living people, Croatian chess players, Yugoslav chess players, Sportspeople from Zagreb

Mladen Šubarić (born 1920) is a Croatian chess master.
In 1939 he tied for 3rd-4th in Zagreb (Svetozar Gligorić won).[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.vjesnik.com/pdf/2001%5C03%5C25%5C31A31.PDF | title=Kronologija hrvatskog šaha (356): Kolo iznenađenja | date=March 25, 2001 | work=Vjesnik | language=Croatian | format=PDF | accessdate=2009-05-10[/tpl]  In December 1941, he played at fourth board against dr Pazman (2 : 0) in a match Croatia – Slovakia in Zagreb. Šubarić took 9th at Munich 1942 (European Individual Chess Championship, Wertungsturnier – Qualification Tournament, Gösta Danielsson won).1942Gillam, Anthony J. & Swift, A J (2001), 1st European championship Munich 1942, Nottingham: The Chess Player, ISBN 1-901034-46-1
After World War II, he played several times in Yugoslav Chess Championship. In 1945 he tied for 12-16th in Novi Sad (Petar Trifunović won).
In 1946 he tied for 14-16th in Zagreb (Trifunović won).1946 In 1948, he tied for 17-18th in Belgrade (Gligorić and Vasja Pirc won).1948
In other tournaments, he won ahead of Mijo Udovčić at Zagreb 1948, and took 2nd, behind Braslav Rabar, at Zagreb 1950 (CRO-ch).
