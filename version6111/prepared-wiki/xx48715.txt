[[Johnny Reb]]

CATEGORIES: American culture, American folklore, Confederate States of America, Cultural history of the American Civil War, DC Comics characters, Mascots, Military slang and jargon, National personifications

Johnny Reb or Johnny Rebel is the national personification of the Southern states of the United States, or less generally, the Confederacy during the American Civil War.Bell Irvin Wiley. 1943. The Life of Johnny Reb: The Common Soldier of the Confederacy. LSU Press. 444 pages. The latter part of his name is derived from Rebellion. Patriots used Johnny Reb and his Union counterpart Billy Yank to symbolize the common soldiers in the American Civil War of the 1860s.
Johnny Reb is usually pictured in gray wool uniform with the typical kepi-style forage cap made of wool broadcloth with a rounded, flat top, cotton lining, and leather visor.  He is often shown with his weapons and with the Confederate flag.
