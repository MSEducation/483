[[William Henry Hadow]]

CATEGORIES: Academics of Durham University, Academics of the University of Sheffield, Alumni of Worcester College, Oxford, Commanders of the Order of the British Empire, English educationists, English musicologists, English composers, Knights Bachelor, Composers awarded knighthoods, People educated at Malvern College, Vice-Chancellors and Wardens of Durham University, 1859 births, 1937 deaths

Sir William Henry Hadow CBE (27 December 1859 – 8 April 1937) was a leading educational reformer in Great Britain and a musicologist.
Hadow was born at Ebrington, Gloucester, England. He studied at Malvern College,The Malvern Register (1865-1904), 1905 followed by Worcester College, Oxford, where he taught and became Dean (1889).[tpl]cite news | title = W.H. Hadow's Visit | url = http://query.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=9C00EED81739E433A25756C1A96E9C946297D6CF | publisher = The New York Times | date=15 August 1903[/tpl] In 1905, Hadow was elected the first Old Malvernian member of the Council of Malvern College.The Council, The Malvern Register (1865-1904), 1904 In 1909, he was appointed principal of Armstrong College in the Newcastle Division of Durham University before succeeding, as Warden & Vice-Chancellor of the University of Durham in 1916. In 1919, he was appointed the Vice-Chancellor of Sheffield University (1919–30).
As chairman of several committees, he published a series of reports on education, notably The Education of the Adolescent (1926) which called for the re-organization of elementary education, the abandonment of all-age schools, and the creation of secondary modern schools.  These became known as the Hadow Reports. He was a leading influence in English education at all levels in the 1920s and 1930s.
Hadow wrote a number of publications on music and music theory, including the Oxford History of Music which he wrote and edited. He was a composer. He was also a Member of the Council of the Royal College of Music.
He was awarded a Knight Bachelor in 1918[tpl]cite news | title = New Year Honours. The Official Lists., New Peers And Baronets., Long Roll Of Soldiers. He died at Westminster, London.
