[[Francisco Núñez]]

CATEGORIES: MacArthur Fellows, American conductors (music), People from New York City, Living people, 1965 births

Francisco J. Núñez (born 1965 New York City) is an American conductor, composer, and the Director of the Young People’s Chorus of New York City.http://www.ypc.org/aboutypc/fnunez.html[tpl]cite news| url=http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/09/21/in-a-city-of-reinvention-genius-finds-a-home/#more-351751| date=September 21, 2011| title=In a City of Reinvention, Genius Finds a Home| author=Clyde Haberman| work=The New York Times [/tpl]
Núñez founded the Young People's Chorus in 1988 "to provide children of all ethnic, religious, and economic backgrounds with a safe haven for personal and artistic growth."http://www.ypc.org/aboutypc/fnunez.html  He is a 2011 MacArthur Fellow.http://www.macfound.org/site/c.lkLXJ8MQKrH/b.7730991/k.781D/Francisco_Nuacutentildeez.htm

==Life==

Núñez was born in New York City, and grew up in the Dominican Republic and New York's Washington Heights neighborhood.[tpl]cite news| url=http://cityroom.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/09/21/in-a-city-of-reinvention-genius-finds-a-home/#more-351751| date=September 21, 2011| title=In a City of Reinvention, Genius Finds a Home| author=Clyde Haberman| work=The New York Times [/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/2011/10/04/arts/music/classical-musics-genius-grant-recipients-pause-to-reflect.html?pagewanted=all| date=October 3, 2011| title=Two Chosen 'Geniuses' Reflect on a Label| author=Anthony Tommasini| work=The New York Times [/tpl]  He graduated from New York University in 1988 with a B.S. in piano performance.http://www.ypc.org/aboutypc/fnunez.html
Núñez is the recipient of numerous awards in addition to the MacArthur Fellowship, including a 2009 ASCAP Concert Music Award, the 2009 New York Choral Society’s Choral Excellence Award, and the 2005 Liberty Award from the New York Post.http://www.ypc.org/aboutypc/fnunez.html  He is a member of ASCAP.http://www.ascap.com/playback/2011/09/faces_places/concert/Francisco_Nunez.aspx?rss=1
Nunez founded an annual festival called Transient Glory in 2001.  Transient Glory features original compositions created expressly for YPC by award-winning composers such as Paquito D'Rivera.  The Transient Glory festival has been performed at leading New York City music venues such as Carnegie Hall.http://www.ypc.org/transientglory/#list
Núñez also leads the University Glee Club of New York City, its fifth conductor since the all-men’s choir was established in 1894.  He served as the director of choral activities at New York University from 2003 to 2010.http://www.ypc.org/aboutypc/fnunez.html
Nunez served as conductor of the 2011 North Carolina Middle School Honors Chorus. Núñez wrote a piece entitled 'Forever Is My Song' for the event.
