[[Yan Yu (poetry theorist)]]

CATEGORIES: Song dynasty poets, 1191 births, 1241 deaths, People from Nanping, Writers from Fujian

Yan Yu (Chinese:严羽, 1191 – 1241p. 11, The Cambridge History of Chinese Literature, vol. 2, Cambridge University Press, 2010 http://books.google.com/books?id=1jGz0qXPgM0C&lpg=PA11&ots=Qo2LQpwceu&pg=PA11&redir_esc=y#v=onepage&q&f=false) was a Chinese poetry theorist and poet of the southern Song Dynasty. He was born in Shaowu and was active during the reign of Lizong. Yan never became an officer all through his life, and he spent most of his lifetime at home. But he was very concerned about national affairs, and wrote many poems to impugn those affairs of state. He was most famous for his poetic theory book Canglang Poetry Talks (《沧浪诗话》), in which he first proposed that High Tang poetry be taken as the model for all poetry.[tpl]zh icon[/tpl]Dong Naibin. Yan Yu, Encyclopedia of China (Chinese Literature Edition), 1st ed.
