[[Betsy Hart]]

CATEGORIES: University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign alumni, 1960s births, Living people, American bloggers, American columnists, Commentators, E. W. Scripps Company people

Betsy Hart (born c. 1963) is a syndicated columnist  for the Scripps Howard News and conservative commentator who is a frequent contributor to CNN and the Fox News Channel.  She also appeared frequently on ABC's show Politically Incorrect. 

==Works==

Hart wrote a column on  family and cultural issues titled, “From the Hart”. The column is distributed each week to hundreds of newspapers all over the country. It appears regularly in mainstream newspapers such as The Chicago Sun-Times, The Denver Rocky Mountain News, The Boston Herald, The Washington Times and among many others. Betsy’s first book, "It Takes a Parent: How the Culture of Pushover Parenting is Hurting out Kids – and What to do About It," was released in September, 2005 and was a top seller. The book received an incredible amount of media attention and was endorsed by top public figures which include: Bill Bennett, Laura Ingraham, Kate O'Beirne.  When Hart appeared on FOX's show Hannity, Sean Hannity told his audience “I love this book!” Betsy has her own radio program which focuses on the understandings of parenting and family life. Her radio radio program is titled, "It Takes a Parent," and is featured on Chicago’s WYLL/AM1160 radio.[tpl]cite web|title=Betsy Hart Conservative Columnist|url=http://townhall.com/columnists/betsyhart/|publisher=Town Hall|accessdate=25 July 2012[/tpl]

She is currently working on her next book Let's Look at Things a Little Differently. She is now divorced from her husband after 17 years of marriage. Betsy is raising her four young children in the suburbs of Chicago.[tpl]cite web|title=Betsy Hart Conservative Columnist|url=http://townhall.com/columnists/betsyhart/|publisher=Town Hall|accessdate=25 July 2012[/tpl]  

==Thoughts==

Ms. Hart believes that production and consumption of organic food is less safe and more expensive than conventionally produced food, although she admits that if "people want to spend more on the stuff, that’s their deal." She does not believe that global warming is primarily a man-made condition.  

==Education==

In 1985 she graduated from the University of Illinois with a B.A. in Russian Studies.
