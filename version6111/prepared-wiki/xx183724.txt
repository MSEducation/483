[[Morrow Township, Adair County, Missouri]]

CATEGORIES: Townships in Adair County, Missouri, Kirksville, Missouri micropolitan area, Townships in Missouri

Morrow Township is one of ten townships in Adair County, Missouri, United States. As of the 2010 census, its population was 431.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/0600000US2900150096| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Morrow township, Adair County, Missouri| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=June 20, 2013[/tpl] It is named for John Morrow, one of Adair County's original judges.History of Adair County by E.M. Violette. Published 1911

==Geography==

Morrow Township covers an area of [tpl]convert|138.27|km2|disp=flip[/tpl] and contains no incorporated settlements.  It contains eight cemeteries: Branstutter, Broyles, Campbell, Cox, Daniels, Megrew, Morelock and Shibleys Point.
The streams of Pleasant Creek, Plum Branch and Turkey Creek run through this township.
