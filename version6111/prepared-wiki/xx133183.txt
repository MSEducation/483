[[Steve Redmond]]

CATEGORIES: 1967 births, Living people, English footballers, Premier League players, Manchester City F.C. players, Oldham Athletic A.F.C. players, Bury F.C. players, Burscough F.C. players, England under-21 international footballers, Association football defenders, Bury F.C. non-playing staff, Sportspeople from Liverpool

Stephen 'Steve' Redmond (born 2 November 1967) is an English former professional footballer, who played as a central defender for Manchester City, Oldham Athletic and Bury. Captain of the Manchester City youth team that won the 1986 FA Youth Cup, Redmond made his first-team debut at 18. He quickly established himself in the side, and was named the club's Player of the Year in 1988. The same year, he became the youngest ever Manchester City captain. Between 1987 and 1990 he played every single game in three straight seasons.
Redmond transferred to Oldham Athletic in 1992, where he spent six years. He then played for Bury for five seasons, and also had a period as caretaker-manager in the 1999–2000 season. He left Bury in 2003, and spent a season in non-league football before retiring.

==Career==

Born in Liverpool, Redmond signed schoolboy forms with Manchester City in October 1982,[tpl]cite book |last=Penney |first=Ian |title=The Maine Road Encyclopedia |publisher=Mainstream |location=Edinburgh |year=1995 |isbn= 1-85158-710-1|pages=164–5[/tpl] choosing City ahead of his hometown club Liverpool.[tpl]cite book |last=Clayton |first=David |title=Everything under the blue moon: the complete book of Manchester City FC – and more! |publisher=Mainstream publishing |location=Edinburgh |year=2002 |isbn=1-84018-687-9|pages=169–70[/tpl] As a junior he played for Blue Star, a Manchester City affiliated team based in Cheadle. His Blue Star team-mates included Paul Lake, Andy Hinchcliffe and Ian Brightwell.[tpl]cite book |last=James|first= Gary| title= The Official Manchester City Hall of Fame |publisher= Hamlyn |year= 2005 |isbn= 0-600-61282-1|page=58[/tpl] He signed his first professional contract in November 1984.
Redmond was part of a promising group of young players from the same intake, of whom he and Paul Moulden were the first to play for the first team.[tpl]cite book |last=Lake |first=Paul |coauthor=& Lake, Joanne |title=I'm Not Really Here: A Life of Two Halves |publisher=Century|location=London |year=2011 |isbn=978-1-84605-824-0|page=51[/tpl] His debut came in February 1986, against Queen's Park Rangers. In total he made eight senior appearances that season,James, The Official Manchester City Hall of Fame, p. 64. including the final of the 1986 Full Members Cup. At the same time, Redmond was also captain of the youth team, who progressed to the final of the FA Youth Cup. In the final, City beat local rivals Manchester United over two legs, and as captain it was Redmond who lifted the trophy.
In the 1986–87 season, Redmond made 32 first team appearances,[tpl]cite book |last=James |first=Gary |title=Manchester City – The Complete Record |publisher=Breedon |location=Derby |year=2006 |isbn=1-85983-512-0 |page=441[/tpl] mainly at centre-half, but also sometimes as a sweeper.[tpl]cite news|title=Sweeping up after Europe |author=Frank McGhee |work=The Observer| date=30 November 1986[/tpl] He also played enough reserve matches to claim a Central League winner's medal. The first team fared less well, and were relegated to the Second Division at the end of the season. As the club season came to a close, Redmond received a call-up to the England under-19 team for a tour of South America.[tpl]cite news|title=Machin backs his minors for a major surprise |author=Cynthia Bateman |work=The Guardian| date=12 March 1988 |page=14[/tpl]
In the Second Division, Redmond was first-choice centre-half, and started every game.James, Manchester City – The Complete Record, p. 443. This included a 10–1 victory against Huddersfield Town that was the club's biggest win in the 20th Century, and a goalscoring tally that has not been equalled in English professional football since.[tpl]cite web| author=Ian Farrell |work=When Saturday Comes |title=Caught in a landslide |url=http://www.wsc.co.uk/the-archive/30-Clubs/4564-caught-in-a-landslide--huddersfield |date=February 2008 |accessdate=9 November 2012[/tpl] At the end of the season he was voted the club's Player of the Year. Club captain Kenny Clements left Manchester City in the 1988 close season, and Redmond was appointed as his successor. At 20, he became the club's youngest ever captain. He again started every single match, as Manchester City gained promotion to the First Division. A third season as an ever-present followed in 1989–90. He continued to play regularly until March 1992, when he lost his place to Michel Vonk,James, Manchester City – The Complete Record, p. 185. and was transfer-listed shortly thereafter.[tpl]cite news|title=Wegerle joins the Balckburn bonanza |author=Cynthia Bateman |work=The Guardian| date=6 March 1992 |page=16[/tpl]
Redmond received his first call-up to the England under-21 team in 1988, and later captained the side. Redmond was the first player from Manchester City's 1986 Youth Cup winning squad to gain under-21 honours,[tpl]cite news|title=Rocastle comes in as crowd puller – England v Denmark |author=Dennis Signy |work=The Times |date=13 October 1988[/tpl] though several others soon followed. In October 1988 five of them were called up at the same time: Redmond, Ian Brightwell, Andy Hinchcliffe, Paul Lake and David White.[tpl]cite news|title=Five City youngsters called up by Sexton |work=The Times |date=21 October 1988[/tpl]
In total Redmond played for Manchester City 287 times and scored seven goals. In August 1992 he transferred to Oldham Athletic. Redmond and Neil Pointon went from City to Oldham in exchange for Rick Holden and £300,000.[tpl]cite news|title=United wait on Dublin as Everton join chase |author=Russell Thomas |work=The Guardian| date=6 August 1992 |page=14[/tpl] Redmond was part of the Oldham team that reached the FA Cup semi-final in 1994, but Redmond missed the semi-final itself through suspension.[tpl]cite news|title=Royle happy to stay on for Oldham's great adventure |author=Cynthia Bateman |work=The Guardian| date=9 April 1994 |page=21[/tpl] He played 239 times for Oldham between 1992 and 1998, and scored four goals. In summer 1998 he joined former Oldham manager Neil Warnock at Bury, on a Bosman transfer.[tpl]cite news|title=Warnock lines up a triple swoop |author=Neil Bonnar |work=Lancashire County Publications| date=20 June 1998[/tpl] When Warnock left the club in December 1999, Redmond and Andy Preece became joint caretaker-managers.[tpl]cite web|title=The Sharp end: Newcastle, Scunthorpe, Bury |date= March 2000 |accessdate=10 November 2012 |work=When Saturday Comes | url=http://www.wsc.co.uk/the-archive/25-Sharp-end/3743-newcastle-scunthorpe-bury [/tpl] Preece was appointed manager on a permanent basis at the end of the season, and Redmond became player-assistant manager. After five years at Bury, Redmond ended his career with a spell in non-league football. He played a single game for Burscough,[tpl]cite news|title=Linnets defender makes swift exit after attracting Conference outfit |author=David Bassett|work=Liverpool Echo| date=22 August 2003[/tpl] and then joined Leigh RMI of the Football Conference in August 2003,[tpl]cite news|title=Hardy pair play on |author=Nicholas Harling |work=Daily Telegraph| date=22 August 2003[/tpl] and spent a season at the club.
At the time of an interview in June 2011, Redmond was working for a builder's merchant.[tpl]cite web|title=Where are they now? Steve Redmond |date= June 2011 |accessdate=10 November 2012 |work=Manchester City FC official website | url=http://www.mcfc.co.uk/citytv/features/2011/june/where-are-they-now-steve-redmond [/tpl] Redmond has three children.[tpl]cite web|title=Steve Redmond's wife tells it how it is |date= 5 January 2002 |accessdate=10 November 2012 |work=Manchester Evening News | url=http://menmedia.co.uk/manchestereveningnews/news/s/39/39975_steve_redmonds_wife_tells_it_how_it_is.html [/tpl] His second child, Daniel, is a Wigan Athletic player, who played on loan for Hamilton Academical during the second half of the 2011–12 season.
