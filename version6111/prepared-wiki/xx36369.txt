[[The Dove (1927 film)]]

CATEGORIES: 1927 films, Black-and-white films, American silent feature films, Films based on plays, 1920s romance films, Films whose art director won the Best Art Direction Academy Award, Films directed by Roland West

The Dove (1927) is an American silent film directed by Roland West and starring Norma Talmadge, Noah Beery, and Gilbert Roland, and based on a 1925 Broadway play by Willard Mack.[tpl]cite book|last=Reid|first=John|title=Award-Winning Films of the 1930s|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Vaq_ClRbFf0C&pg=PA69&dq=%22The+Dove%22+%2B%22Norma+Talmadge%22#v=onepage&q=%22The%20Dove%22%20%2B%22Norma%20Talmadge%22&f=false|year=2004|isbn= 9781411614321|page=69[/tpl]

==Background==

The original story is about a Mexican despot (played by Beery), who falls in love with a dancing girl (played by Talmadge), who rejects him. Due to the political repercussions of condemning Mexico, it was decided to relocate the plot to some anonymous Mediterranean country.The Dove at the silentera.com database  the film was Norma Talmadge's first feature for United Artists.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=6LcKAAAAIBAJ&sjid=CU4DAAAAIBAJ&pg=2537,490021&dq=the-dove+norma-talmadge&hl=en|title=Norma Talmadge in The Dove|date=April 1, 1928|work=St. Petersberg Times|publisher=Google archives|accessdate=22 November 2010[/tpl]

==Recognition==

Though the film was not well received, William Cameron Menzies won the first Academy Award for Best Art Direction in 1928 for this film and Tempest, though the award was then called "Interior Decoration."[tpl]cite web |url=http://movies.nytimes.com/movie/89874/The-Dove/details |title=NY Times: The Dove |accessdate=2008-12-07|work=NY Times[/tpl]
At the Library of Congress are reels 1, 3, 4, and 8. The film is missing reels 2, 5, 6, 7, and 9.Anna May Wong: A Complete guide to Her Film, Stage, Radio and Television Work by Philip Leibfried and Chei Mi Lane page 59 c.2004
In 1932, Herbert Brenon directed a new talkie version named Girl of the Rio, starred by Dolores del Rio for RKO Radio Pictures.

==Awards and nominations==

The Dove won the 1929 Academy Award for 'Best Art Direction' for William Cameron Menzies.
