[[Live Gamer]]

CATEGORIES: Companies based in New York, 2007 establishments in New York, Cloud applications

Live Gamer is an e-commerce and monetization SaaS company headquartered in New York. It facilitates transactions in digital goods for online gaming, digital entertainment and technology providers globally.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://tech.fortune.cnn.com/2010/08/05/zynga_farmville_media_micropayments/ |title=How Mafia Wars can fix the media |date=5 August 2010 |last=Pullen |first=John Patrick |publisher=CNN Money |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]

==History==

Live Gamer was founded by Mitch Davis and Andrew Schneider in 2007.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://techcrunch.com/2009/08/24/virtual-gaming-marketplace-live-gamer-acquires-twofish-to-boost-micropayments-platform/ |title=Virtual Gaming Marketplace Live Gamer Acquires Twofish To Boost Micropayments Platform |date=24 August 2009 |last=Rao |first=Leena |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] Schneider currently serves as the company's president while Davis is CEO. The company provides monetization services to businesses in the interactive entertainment industry via its Elements platform.
Live Gamer also handles advertising through its GamerDNA network.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www.nytimes.com/2012/03/07/business/media/self-magazine-creates-social-game-for-its-annual-workout-in-the-park.html?_r=0 |title=Taking a Page From the Web, Magazine Pitches a Social Game |publisher=The New York Times |date=6 March 2012 |last=Elliot |first=Stuart |accessdate=26 July 2013[/tpl]
As of June 2013, Live Gamer has raised more than $35 million in venture capital funding.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.xconomy.com/new-york/2013/07/17/live-gamer-lands-2789672-new-funding/ |title=Live Gamer Lands $2,789,672 New Funding |accessdate=1 August 2013[/tpl]

===Acquisitions===

Live Gamer ventured into e-commerce in 2009 when it acquired Korean micro-transaction company, N-Cash. The N-Cash was employed by 100 games in 23 different countries.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://venturebeat.com/2009/07/28/live-gamer-acquires-koreas-n-cash-in-bid-to-expand-into-primary-virtual-goods-market/ |title=Live Gamer acquires N-Cash to deliver "one-stop shop for virtual goods" |date=28 July 2009 |first=Dean |last=Takahashi |accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl] N-Cash was later rebranded to Live Gamer Asia. That same year Live Gamer also acquired Twofish, another micro-transaction platform. Both acquisitions resulted in considerable expansion of Live Gamer's commerce solutions.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.gamasutra.com/view/news/115893/Interview_Live_Gamer_Beefs_Virtual_Economies_Offering_With_Twofish_Acquisition.php |title=Interview: Live Gamer Beefs Virtual Economies Offering With Twofish Acquisition |date=24 August 2009 |first=Leigh |last=Alexander |accessdate=11 August 2011[/tpl]
Live Gamer subsequently acquired BrandPort and gamerDNA in 2011,[tpl]Cite web |url=http://venturebeat.com/2011/07/19/live-gamer-acquires-brandport-and-gamerdna-as-it-moves-into-games-ads/ |title=Live Gamer acquires Brandport and GamerDNA as it moves into games ads |date=July 19, 2011 |first=Dean |last=Takahashi |accessdate=28 July 2013[/tpl] acquisitions which also led to the formation of Live Gamer Media.[tpl]Cite news |url=http://www.adweek.com/news/technology/live-gamer-gets-ad-business-133506 |title=Live Gamer Gets Into The Ad Business Virtual goods startup buys into the market with new acquisitions |first=Anthony |last=Ha |date=19 July 2011 |accessdate=27 July 2013[/tpl] BrandPort was later rebranded as Ad Elements, an advertising unit through which Live Gamer permits gamers to earn virtual currency. GamerDNA is a social media gaming platform. In 2011 comScore rated gamerDNA as the fifth largest gamer ad network in the United States and Europe.
