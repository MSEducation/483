[[Esy Morales]]

CATEGORIES: 1917 births, 1950 deaths, Puerto Rican musicians, Manor Records artists, Deaths from myocardial infarction

Ismael "Esy" Morales (March 3, 1917 - November 2, 1950), was a musician born in Puerto Rico. His brothers, Hunberto and Noro, were also musicians. Before the age of ten, Esy was performing on tenor sax, clarinet and flute. In 1930 he moved to New York City, and is credited on Xavier Cugat's album Cugat On Film, as well as other Cugat records. In the late 1930s he formed a band with his brothers Humberto and Noro.
Morales went on to form his own band, The Esy Morales Orchestra and had a cameo appearance in the 1949 motion picture Criss Cross performing the song "Jungle Fantasy".  His name is also listed in the credits of this movie. ==Death==

Morales died in 1950 at the age of 33 from a heart attack which was brought on by diabetes. He also became blind due to diabetes. He was about to resign with Rainbow Records, which had recorded his "Jungle Fantasy" a few years earlier. Morales recorded for Artistic, Manor, and Decca under his own name.
