Juan Nicolas Jarvis (born December 6, 1950 in Guantanamo, Cuba[tpl]Citation needed|date=September 2008[/tpl]) is a former Cuban athlete. He is the fourth son of Esther Ocana Nolasco and  Teodoro Jarvis.
Competing as a javelin thrower for more than 10 years, he participated in many international competitions as a part of the Cuban national team. At the 1971 Central American and Caribbean Championships in Athletics he won the gold medal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gbrathletics.com/ic/cacc.htm|title=Central American and Caribbean Championships results|publisher=Athletics Weekly|accessdate=2008-09-17[/tpl] He won silver at both the 1974 Central American and Caribbean Games and the 1975 Pan American Games,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gbrathletics.com/ic/pag.htm|title=Pan American Games results|publisher=Athletics Weekly|accessdate=2008-09-17[/tpl] and gold once again at the 1977 Central American and Caribbean Championships in Athletics.
