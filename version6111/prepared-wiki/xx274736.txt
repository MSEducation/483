[[Chitralekha (1941 film)]]

CATEGORIES: 1941 films, Hindi-language films, Indian films, Films based on novels, Films set in the 3rd century BC, Works about the Maurya Empire

Chitralekha is a 1941 Indian Bollywood film, directed by Kidar Sharma, and was based on 1934 Hindi novel by the same name by Bhagwati Charan Verma.  Music of film was given by noted classical musician Ustad Jhande Khan, giving popular songs like, Sun sun Neelkamal Muskaye, Saiyyan Saware Bhaye Baware, and Tum Jao Bde Bhagwan Bane, Insaan BanoGulzar, p. 282It was the second grossing Indian film of 1941.[tpl]cite web|title=Top Earners 1941|url=http://www.boxofficeindia.com/showProd.php?itemCat=146&catName=MTk1MQ==|publisher=Box Office India|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl] Khan used he used classical Ragas like Bhairavi and Asavari to film score making it an influential film for classical based Hindi film songs., p. 270 This was the debut film of actor Bharat Bhushan, who later achieved fame with Bhaiju Bawra (1952). Gulzar, p. 533 
The film was remade by Sharma in 1964, also titled Chitralekha, starring Meena Kumari and Ashok Kumar.  [tpl]imdb title|0138360|Chitralekha[/tpl]
