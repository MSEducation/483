[[Martin Opic]]

CATEGORIES: Czech footballers, 1977 births, Living people, Gambrinus liga players, Bohemians 1905 players, FBK Kaunas footballers, FK Ústí nad Labem players

Martin Opic (born 28 September 1977) is a Czech football player who currently plays for FC Hlučín.
Opic played in the first qualifying round of the 2003–04 UEFA Champions League, scoring as a substitute in a 1–0 first leg win for FBK Kaunas away to HB Tórshavn.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2003/matches/round=1709/match=71273/postmatch/report/index.html#kaunas+delight+opic+intervention |title=Kaunas delight in Opic intervention|publisher=UEFA.com |date=16 July 2003 |accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl] He started the second leg, scoring again in a 4–1 home win.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.uefa.com/uefachampionsleague/season=2003/matches/round=1709/match=71274/postmatch/report/index.html#kaunas+celtic+meeting |title=Kaunas set up Celtic meeting|publisher=UEFA.com |date=23 July 2003 |accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl]
Opic signed for Hlučín in 2011, arriving from Karviná.[tpl]cite news |url=http://opavsky.denik.cz/fotbal_region/fotbaliste-hlucina-maji-dve-posily20110728.html |title=Fotbalisté Hlučína mají dvě posily |language=Czech |publisher=denik.cz |date=28 July 2011 |accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl]
Being originally a professional firefighter, Opic travelled to the World Police and Fire Games in August 2011 to represent the Czech Republic.[tpl]cite news |url=http://opavsky.denik.cz/fotbal_region/fotbaliste-hlucina-se-musi-obejit-bez-martina-opic.html |title=Fotbalisté Hlučína se musí obejít bez Martina Opice |language=Czech |publisher=denik.cz |date=27 August 2011 |accessdate=25 September 2011[/tpl]
