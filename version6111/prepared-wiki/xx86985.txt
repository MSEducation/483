[[Lisa Peluso]]

CATEGORIES: 1964 births, American soap opera actresses, Living people

Lisa Peluso (born July 29, 1964) is an American soap opera actress.

==Biography==

Peluso was born in Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, the daughter of Mary Peluso.http://www.filmreference.com/film/14/Lisa-Peluso.html Her first big break came at the age of nine, when she starred in the Broadway production of Gypsy with Angela Lansbury. At age 14, she played "Linda," the younger sister of John Travolta's "Tony," in the classic Saturday Night Fever.
Peluso had a nine-year stint in the soap world as teenager Wendy Wilkins on Search for Tomorrow (1977–1986). She was offered the role of "Wendy" after Andrea McArdle vacated the role to star in the Broadway musical "Annie".  While playing "Wendy," a sixteen-year old Peluso received her first on-screen kiss from Kevin Bacon.[tpl]citation needed|date=October 2013[/tpl]
Other important soap credentials include her portrayal of "Ava Rescott Masters" on Loving (1988–1995), as well as her portrayal of Lila Hart Roberts Cory Winthrop on Another World (1997–1999) and continued the character on As the World Turns (1999).  She has also appeared twice on the ABC soap opera One Life to Live, first as "Billie Giordano"  from 1987–1988, then as "Gina Russo" in 2001.
She also made a guest star appearance on Designing Women, playing "Shannon," a lawyer who irritates Julia Sugarbaker (Dixie Carter) by dating her beau, Reese Watson (Hal Holbrook).
Peluso was featured on a 2002 episode of the TLC Network series A Baby Story, chronicling her pregnancy and the birth of her second child, Parker. She and her husband, photographer Brad Guice, also have a daughter, Phoebe.
