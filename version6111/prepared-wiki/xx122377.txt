[[International reactions to the Qana airstrike]]

CATEGORIES: 2006 Lebanon War, 2006 in international relations, International reactions

thumb|right|250px|New York City: Union Square protest of Israeli bombing of Lebanon, August 3, 2006International reactions to the 2006 Qana airstrike, which saw the greatest loss of civilian life in the 2006 Israel-Lebanon conflict, twenty eight deaths with thirteen missing, largely involved the condemnation of Israel by many countries around the globe, bringing about a supposed 48 hours cessation of air operations by the Israeli Air Force.  Bombings resumed only a few hours after the start of the cessation of air operations.
