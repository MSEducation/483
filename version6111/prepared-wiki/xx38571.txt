[[Vic Fleming]]

CATEGORIES: 1951 births, Living people, Arkansas state court judges, Puzzle designers, Davidson College alumni, William H. Bowen School of Law alumni

Victor Anson "Vic" Fleming (born December 26, 1951) is an American judge, teacher and author residing in Little Rock, Arkansas. 
He was born in Jackson, Mississippi, and educated at Davidson College and the University of Arkansas at Little Rock School of Law, where he has taught Law and Literature as an adjunct faulty member since 2003.UALR William H. Bowen School of Law Adjunct Faculty He was elected as a district judge for the City of Little Rock in 1996, and then reelected in 2000, 2004 and 2008. 
Fleming has written two books of legal humor, including Real Lawyers Do Change Their Briefs.Fleming, Victor A., and John Deering. Real Lawyers Do Change Their Briefs. Little Rock, Ark: Rose Pub. Co, 1989. ISBN 0-914546-82-1. He also constructs crossword puzzles as a hobby: His puzzles have been published in several papers, including the New York Times; several magazines, including Games Magazine; and in several books. He appeared in the 2006 documentary Wordplay, which also featured a song that he wrote.
He has been married since 1973 and has two grown children.
