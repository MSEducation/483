[[Shawn Estrada]]

CATEGORIES: American boxers of Mexican descent, Boxers at the 2008 Summer Olympics, Middleweight boxers, 1985 births, Living people, Olympic boxers of the United States

Shawn Estrada (born April 1, 1985 in East Los Angeles, California) is an undefeated Mexican American[tpl]cite journal | title = A Proud Mexican Fighter | journal = Fan House | date = 2009-09-10 | first = Elie | last = Seckbach| id = | url = https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=siR1UuNDuV0 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] professional boxer in the Super Middleweight division and was a U.S. Olympian at the 2008 Summer Olympics.[tpl]cite journal | title = U.S. Olympian | journal = Box Rec | date = 2008-05-28 | first = Fransico | last = Mier| id = | url = http://boxrec.com/media/index.php/Shawn_Estrada | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] Shawn was a candidate running for the city council in Commerce, California.http://sports.yahoo.com/box/news;_ylt=Atd6PuxfxnOpBR8NMRgzejiUxLYF?slug=ki-estrada021111

==Early life==

Raised in East Los Angeles, Estrada is the son of former 1968 Mexican Olympic team boxer Juan Estrada and the youngest of 16 siblings. He credits the sport of boxing as the force that kept him out of gangs growing up,[tpl]cite journal | title = Growing up around L.A. Gangs | journal = Los Angeles Times | date = 2008-08-07 | first = Kevin | last = Baxter| id = | url = http://articles.latimes.com/2008/aug/07/sports/sp-olyestrada7 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] His father had hoped to travel to the Beijing Olympics with his son, but his failing health kept him home in Maywood. On August 17, 2008 his father died at St. Vincent Medical Center.[tpl]cite journal | title = Shawn's Father Dies | journal = Los Angeles Times | date = 2008-08-18 | first = Kevin | last = Baxter| id = | url = http://articles.latimes.com/2008/aug/18/local/me-father18 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl]

==Amateur career==

Estrada had an outstanding amateur record of 110-7. He won the 2000 Junior Olympic Championship at age 14 and the 2001 Pan-American Games Gold Medal, beating the top Cuban and Puerto Rican boxers in his weight class and age bracket. However, Estrada’s junior amateur career ended abruptly after his brother was killed in a gang-related shooting.[tpl]cite journal | title = Shawn's Brother Dies | journal = Ring Magazine | date = 2009-05-24 | first = Doug | last = Fischer| id = | url = http://www.ringtv.com/blog/483/new_faces_shawn_estrada/ | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] In 2007 he lost to Shawn Porter but won the Olympic qualification beating highly touted Danny Jacobs twice.[tpl]cite journal | title = Estrada's Olympic Qualifying | journal = Associated Press ESPN | date = 2007-08-26 | first = Saul | last = Mier| id = | url = http://sports.espn.go.com/oly/news/story?id=2991322 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] He lost at the first American Olympic qualifier 3:13 to Carlos Góngora but won three bouts at the second qualifier.[tpl]cite journal | title = Estrada's Olympics in his Hands | journal = Associated Press ESPN | date = 2008-04-30 | first = Alessandro | last = Mulei| id = | url = http://sports.espn.go.com/sports/boxing/news/story?id=3374366 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] At the World Championships 2007 he Beat Israel's Artur Zlatopolski and then Japan's Ryota Murata, but was upset by Konstantin Buga of Germany 11-11.[tpl]cite journal | title = 2007 World Championships | journal = Yidio | date = 2010-03-06 | first = Blanca | last = Escobedo| id = | url = http://www.yidio.com/shawn-estrada-us-olympian-promointro-by-bluemercury-for-goossen-tutor/id/2991524935 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl]

===Olympic results===

At the 2008 Olympics Estrada won against Argentina's Ezequiel Maderna[tpl]cite journal | title = East L.A. gets a win in the Olympics | journal = USA Boxing | date = 2008-10-06 | first = Fernando | last = Luevanos| id = | url = http://usaboxing.org/news/2008/10/06/middleweight-shawn-estrada-opens-the-2008-olympic-games-with-a-first-round-victory/3838 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] but lost to the eventual gold medalist England's James DeGale 5-11 in his second bout.

==Pro career==

On November 29, 2008[tpl]cite journal | title = Pro Debut a K.O. | journal = Boxing Scene | date = 2008-11-30 | first = Blanca | last = Escobedo| id = | url = http://www.boxingscene.com/?m=show&id=17224 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] Shawn made his Pro debut with a first round K.O. of veteran boxer Lawrence Jones.[tpl]cite journal | title = East Los Angeles a K.O. | journal = Liver Puncher | date = 2008-11-30 | first = Boxing | last = Harris| id = | url = http://www.liverpuncher.com/articles/ringside-report-nightmares-punishment.html | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] He's had layoffs because of back injuries[tpl]cite journal | title = Back Injuries | journal = Fight Hype | date = 2009-02-02 | first = Blanca | last = Escobedo| id = | url = http://www.fighthype.com/pages/content4233.html | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] and then had to undergo surgery for a ruptured tendon in his hand.[tpl]cite journal | title = Ruptured Tendon for Estrada | journal = Boxing Scene | date = 2009-12-15 | first = Alessandro | last = Mulei| id = | url = http://www.boxingscene.com/?m=show&id=24154 | accessdate = 2010-06-23[/tpl] He is also a member of Team Fight to Walk, which supports America's first clinical stem cell trial, along with other notable fighters such as Boyd Melson, Demetrius Andrade, Steve Cunningham, Denis Douglin and Deandre Latimore.
