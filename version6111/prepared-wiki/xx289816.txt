==History==

===Formation and early years===

Rodeo Ruby Love was founded in 2005 by lead singer Zack Melton. Their music is inspired by the church hyms and pop-country radio of Zack Melton's childhood, and the energy of the scene in central Indiana. The band's name Rodeo Ruby Love was inspired by the name of Zack Melton's grandmother, Ruby, and his second favorite Garth brooks song, Rodeo.Allis, Greg. "Interview with Zack from Rodeo Ruby Love." LIVE HIGH FIVE. N.p., 20 June 2013. Web. 14 Aug. 2013. . Rodeo Ruby Love recorded its first album, Your Love Has Made Everything Beautiful (2006), in a basement in Bloomington Indiana. They followed the album by simultaneously releasing two EP's, What Loneliness Can Do To You and Honest To God (2007), and touring through the midwest and southeast. Their second album, Vs. The Great American Cities (2008), was released under Crossroads of America Records."Rodeo Ruby Love." MFT:Spreading Indiana Music. N.p., n.d. Web. 14 Aug. 2013..

===Rise to prominence===

The band's popularity started to spread beyond the midwest in 2010. The band released their third album, This Is Why We Don't Have Nice Things (2010), under Crossroads of America Records, and went on their first self-booked national tour. Rodeo Ruby Love they signed onto the Pentimento Music Company, and kicked off the switch with a re-release of This Is Why We Don't Have Nice Things and by going on a national tour with two prominent ska bands, Streetlight Manifesto, and Reel Big Fish, performing to sold out crowds at nationally recognized venues."Rodeo Ruby Love." MFT:Spreading Indiana Music. N.p., n.d. Web. 14 Aug. 2013..

===Fourth album===

Rodeo Ruby Love released its fourth album, The Pits, on June 11, 2013, and went on a month long national tour with Streetlight Manifesto and Empty Orchestra. The album was engineered and mixed by Wes DeBoy and mastered by Carl Saff. "The Pits - Rodeo Ruby Love | Credits" All Music. N.p., 11 June 2013. Web. 13 Oct. 2013 .
