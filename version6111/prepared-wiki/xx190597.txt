[[Jun Murai]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese computer scientists, 1955 births, Living people, Keio University alumni, Keio University faculty, Internet Hall of Fame

[tpl]nihongo|Jun Murai|村井 純|Murai Jun|extra=born March 29, 1955 in Tokyo[/tpl] is a Japanese computer scientist, known as "the father of Japan's Internet".[tpl]cite web |url=http://d.hatena.ne.jp/keyword/%C2%BC%B0%E6%BD%E3 |title=村井純とは - はてなダイアリー |publisher=Hatena |language=Japanese |accessdate=2008-04-29[/tpl] A professor at Keio University, Murai is the founder of JUNET and founder of the WIDE Project. Murai graduated from Keio University in 1979 and received a Ph.D. from the same school in 1984.
On October 1, 2009, Murai became the Dean of the Faculty of Environment and Information Studies.[tpl]cite web | url=http://vu9.sfc.keio.ac.jp/faculty_profile/cgi/f_profile_e.cgi?id=859452959bdc256a| title= Employee profile at Keio University website | work=SFC Clip | language=English| accessdate=2010-03-11[/tpl]  
Murai was awarded the 2011 IEEE Internet Award "For his leadership in the development and deployment of the global Internet, especially across the Asia-Pacific region.” Murai entered the Internet Hall of Fame in 2013.http://www.internethalloffame.org/inductees/jun-murai
