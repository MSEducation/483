Jesse Rae is a Scottish singer and composer from St Boswells in Scotland.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.alba.org.uk/scot07constit/s08.html|title=Candidates and Constituency Assessments: Roxburgh & Berwickshire|work=Scottish Politics|publisher=Alba Publishing|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl]

==Singing and songwriting==

Jesse Rae's single "Over the Sea", reached number 65 in the UK Singles Chart in 1985.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chartstats.com/songinfo.php?id=12476|title=Jesse Rae – Over The Sea|work=Chart Stats|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Warwick|first=Neil|coauthors=Kutner, Jon; Brown, Tony|title=The Complete Book Of The British Charts|publisher=Omnibus Press|year=2004|page=888|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=ib4MyAIpe3MC&pg=PA888&dq=%22jesse+rae%22&as_brr=3&sig=ACfU3U3JpwyQEPozrxt8IGgc07ljtxNfzg | isbn=978-1-84449-058-5[/tpl] The video for the single – which features a kilted, helmeted, claymore-brandishing Rae in both New York City and the Scottish Highlands – won a Vira award.[tpl]cite news|url=http://findarticles.com/p/articles/mi_qn4161/is_20040919/ai_n12906436|title=The chop for Rae's claymore|last=Fairburn|first=Robert|date=2004-09-19|work=Sunday Mirror|publisher=Mirror Group Newspapers|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl] His début album, entitled The Thistle, was released in 1987 on WEA but failed to chart.
A second studio album, Compression was eventually released in 1996,[tpl]cite web|author=David Belcher |url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/spl/aberdeen/new-world-warrior-takes-on-the-globe-1.431912 |title=New world warrior takes on the globe |publisher=Herald Scotland |date=1996-10-11 |accessdate=2012-10-12[/tpl] notable for being recorded via BT's Integrated Services Digital Network (ISDN) system.
As a songwriter, he is best known for the 1982 Odyssey hit single, "Inside Out".[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/celtic/1754844.stm|title=Townsend eyes Borders return|date=2002-01-11|work=BBC Sport|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/3672706.stm|title=Sword taken from singer at court|date=2004-09-20|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl] Rae's own version being included on The Thistle album.
On 21 October 2010, Rae opened for Adam Ant at The Union Chapel in London, playing a half hour set. Also earlier in the year, The Thistle album saw a digital reissue on Savage Hospitality Records.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.savage-hospitality.com/jesse-rae/ |title=Jesse Rae | Savage Hospitality Records |publisher=Savage-hospitality.com |date=2010-10-21 |accessdate=2012-01-01[/tpl] On 23 May 2011, he was one of the support acts for Adam Ant at the O2 Academy in Glasgow and played two "live music video shows" at Edinburgh's 'Voodoo Lounge' bar.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thevoodoorooms.com/event-detail-nu.php?guest-name=Jesse+Rae&date_id=1320 |title=Jesse Rae at The Voodoo Rooms in Edinburgh |publisher=Thevoodoorooms.com |accessdate=2012-01-01[/tpl]
2012 saw the release of Rae's compilation album,  The Best O' ,http://www.therocktologist.com/jesse-rae---the-best-o.html on CD with a companion CD release of rare tracks, Funk Warrior - A Collection, released in July. Both are limited editions of 500 copies each.
Rae's 1987 album The Thistle is scheduled for an expanded double CD reissue in April 2014.

==Politics==

In 2007, Rae stood for the Scottish Parliament as an independent in the Scottish Borders electoral constituency of Roxburgh and Berwickshire.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/scotland/south_of_scotland/6549377.stm|title=Tartan rocker seeks election role|last=Rinaldi|first=Giancarlo |date=2007-04-12|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl] He gained 318 votes for a 1.2% share of the vote.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/vote2007/scottish_parliment/html/490.stm|title=Scottish elections 2007: Roxburgh & Berwickshire.|first=2007-05-04|work=BBC News|publisher=BBC|accessdate=2008-07-29[/tpl] He stood again in 2011 as an independent candidate in the expanded seat of Ettrick, Roxburgh and Berwickshire this time polling 308 votes for a 1.1% share.
