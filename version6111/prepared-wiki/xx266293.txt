[[Sione Kalamafoni]]

CATEGORIES: 1988 births, Living people, Tongan rugby union players, Tongan expatriate rugby union players, Expatriate rugby union players in England, Tongan expatriates in the United Kingdom

Sione Kalamafoni (born 18 May 1988) is a rugby union footballer who plays at number 8. Kalamafoni played for Tonga at the 2011 Rugby World Cup.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.rugbyworldcup.com/home/teams/team=47/player=28802/index.html|title=Profile|date=|work=2011 Rugby World Cup Official Site|accessdate=22 September 2011[/tpl]
Kalamafoni first signed to Nottingham in the RFU Championship from the 2010/11 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/rugby_union/8978009.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|title=Nottingham sign Tonga International forward Sione Kalamafoni|date=7 September 2010[/tpl] 
On 15 March 2012, Kalamafoni would leave Nottingham to sign for Gloucester Rugby in the Aviva Premiership on a two-year contract from 2012/13 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/rugby-union/17380106|publisher=BBC Sport|title=Gloucester sign Tonga International Sione Kalamafoni from Nottingham|date=15 March 2012[/tpl] However on 14 November 2013, Kalamafoni signed a two-year contract extension with Gloucester until the end of 2015/16 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/rugby-union/24939433|publisher=BBC Sport|title=Kalamafoni contract extension with Gloucester|date=14 November 2013[/tpl]
Sione Kalamofoni's notable achievements off the field include saving 27 children from a Tongan orphanage that was burning down.
