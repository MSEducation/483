[[Munn v. Illinois]]

CATEGORIES: United States Supreme Court cases, United States substantive due process case law, 1876 in United States case law, Legal history of Illinois

Munn v. Illinois, 94 U.S. 113 (1877), was a United States Supreme Court case dealing with corporate rates and agriculture. The Munn case allowed states to regulate certain businesses within their borders, including railroads, and is commonly regarded as a milestone in the growth of federal government regulation.[tpl]citation needed|reason=Case involved state regulation, not federal|date=July 2012[/tpl] Munn was one of six cases, the so-called Granger cases, all decided in the United States Supreme Court during the same term, all bearing on the same point, and all decided on the same principles.[tpl]Cite Americana|wstitle=Granger Cases[/tpl]
