[[Pennsylvania Route 331]]

CATEGORIES: State highways in Pennsylvania, Transportation in Washington County, Pennsylvania

Pennsylvania Route 331 (PA 331) is a [tpl]convert|14.5|mi|km|adj=mid|-long[/tpl], east–west state highway located in Washington County, Pennsylvania.  The western terminus is at the West Virginia state line in Independence Township where the road continues as West Virginia Route 67.  The eastern terminus is at PA 844 in Canton Township. PA 331 was initially established by 1940s. The route was rerouted in the 2000s following the closure of a bridge over Buffalo Creek. 

==Route description==

PA 331 begins at the West Virginia state border, at the eastern terminus of WV 67.  The route heads east in Independence Township paralleling Buffalo Creek. After [tpl]convert|1.8|mi|km[/tpl], the route makes a left turn onto Mount Hope Ridge Road.[tpl]cite map|url=ftp://ftp.dot.state.pa.us/public/pdf/BPR_pdf_files/Maps/GHS/Roadnames/washington_GHSN.PDF |title=General Highway Map - Washington County, Pennsylvania |publisher=Pennsylvania Department of Transportation |year=2012 |accessdate=March 31, 2013[/tpl] PA 231 meets PA 331 in the community of Mount Hope and together head south towards the community of Acheson. After turning east, PA 231 exits the concurrency and PA 331 alone heads east following the course of Brush Run. After passing through the southernmost portion of Hopewell Township, it reaches its eastern terminus at PA 844 in Canton Township.[tpl]google maps|url=https://maps.google.com/maps?saddr=PA-331+E%2FBrush+Run+Rd&daddr=40.1912624,-80.4865789+to:PA-331+E%2FBrush+Run+Rd&hl=en&ll=40.20405,-80.430565&spn=0.107245,0.222988&sll=40.201035,-80.420952&sspn=0.10725,0.222988&geocode=FWpYZQIdMmEz-w%3BFR5FZQIdTt8z-ykhynScT8o1iDF2H4b4eASktg%3BFWKQZQIdujQ2-w&mra=dpe&mrsp=1&sz=13&via=1&t=m&z=13 |accessdate=March 31, 2013[/tpl]

==History==

The route was established by 1940 on much of the same alignment it runs today.[tpl]cite map |url=ftp://ftp.dot.state.pa.us/public/pdf/BPR_pdf_files/Maps/Statewide/Historic_OTMs/1940fr.pdf |title=Official Road Map of Pennsylvania |year=1940 |publisher=Pennsylvania Department of Highways |accessdate=February 26, 2014[/tpl] Prior to 2005, PA 331 followed a more direct route between the state line and Acheson by following Buffalo Creek in Independence and Donegal Townships.ftp://ftp.dot.state.pa.us/public/pdf/BPR_PDF_FILES/Maps/Type_10_GHS_Historical_Scans/Washington_1941_Sheet_2.pdf On August 30, 2005, the Pennsylvania Department of Transportation (PennDOT) closed and later removed one of the bridges over Buffalo Creek due to safety concerns.[tpl]cite news|url=http://nl.newsbank.com/nl-search/we/Archives?p_product=WORB&p_theme=worb&p_action=search&p_maxdocs=200&p_topdoc=1&p_text_direct-0=10C562174125F200&p_field_direct-0=document_id&p_perpage=10&p_sort=YMD_date:D&s_trackval=GooglePM |title=PENNDOT closes bridge in Independence |newspaper=Observer–Reporter |location=Washington, Pennsylvania |date=August 31, 2005 |accessdate=March 31, 2013[/tpl] By 2007, PA 331 was routed onto then-SR 4065 (Mount Hope Ridge Road) and PA 231 and the decommissioned sections of PA 331 became SR 3001, SR 4034, and SR 4036.ftp://ftp.dot.state.pa.us/public/pdf/BPR_PDF_FILES/MAPS/Statewide/otm/2007/otmsw_2007.PDF
