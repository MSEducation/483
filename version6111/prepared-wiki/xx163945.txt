[[Anne D. Neal]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing (living people), Harvard Law School alumni, Living people, Spouses of members of the United States House of Representatives

Anne deHayden Neal is the president of the American Council of Trustees and Alumni (ACTA), a non-profit organization committed to advancing academic quality, accountability and affordability at colleges and universities in the United States.[tpl]cite web|url=http://voices.washingtonpost.com/college-inc/2011/01/guest_post_academically_adrift.html|title=Guest post: 'Academically Adrift,' indeed |publisher=Washington Post |date=January 19, 2011|accessdate=February 10, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://washingtonexaminer.com/local/people/2011/09/3-minute-interview-anne-neal/118746|title=The 3-Minute Interview: Anne Neal|publisher=Washington Examiner|accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl]

==Early life==

Neal spent her childhood in Indiana, where her father was the editor of the Noblesville Daily Ledger, a small-town newspaper.[tpl]cite web|url=http://indianajournalismhof.org/1990/01/james-t-neal/ |title=James T. Neal · 1990|publisher=Indiana Journalism Hall of Fame| accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl] She graduated magna cum laude as a member of Phi Beta Kappa from Harvard College in 1977 with an A.B. in American history and literature. Neal earned her J.D. from Harvard Law School in 1980.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.goacta.org/about/staff.cfm |title=Anne D. Neal, President|publisher=American Council of Trustees and Alumni | accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] Neal served as the first female editor of the Harvard Journal on Legislation.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.philanthropyroundtable.org/events/speaker_bios/anne_d_neal |title=Anne D. Neal: President, American Council of Trustees and Alumni|publisher=Philanthropy Roundtable | accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl]

==Career==

Neal began her career as an attorney at the firm of Rogers & Wells in New York City, where she specialized in First Amendment law. She also served as an associate at Wiley Rein in Washington, D.C. Neal later served as senior vice president of corporate legal affairs for the Recording Industry Association of America where she was involved in the RIAA's campaign to oppose mandatory lyrics labeling.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.popecenter.org/about/author.html?id=514|title=Pope Center Authors: Anne Neal|publisher=The John William Pope Center for Higher Education Policy | accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://articles.latimes.com/1990-04-03/entertainment/ca-828_1_recording-industry|title=Record Industry Sees Tide Turning in Labeling Issue| publisher=Los Angeles Times|date=April 3, 1990|accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.law.umn.edu/news/calendar.html?cal=details&my_db=rooms&event_id=9693&my_date=2008-04-03&StartTime=12:15:00|title=Anne Neal, President of the American Council of Trustees and Alumni| publisher=University of Minnesota Law School|date=April 3, 2008|accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl]
In the early 1990s, Neal served as general counsel and congressional liaison for the National Endowment for the Humanities under Lynne V. Cheney, where she addressed a range of issues including academic freedom, intellectual diversity, and historical literacy.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nationalreview.com/author/40505/bio |title=Anne D. Neal Biography|publisher=National Review Online | accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] Along with Lynne Cheney, U.S. Senator Joe Lieberman, former Colorado governor Richard Lamm, former University of Colorado at Boulder president and U.S. Senator Hank Brown, Nobel Laureate Saul Bellow, and sociologist David Riesman, Neal co-founded the American Council of Trustees and Alumni (then known as the National Alumni Forum) in 1995.[tpl]cite book |title=Protecting Intellectual Freedom in Your Academic Library |last=Jones|first=Barbara |year=2009 |publisher=American Library Association|isbn=978-0-8389-3580-4 |page=20|url=http://books.google.com/?id=GKD6G7Ta-oAC&pg=PA20&lpg=PA20&dq=Saul+Bellow+ACTA#v=onepage&q=Saul%20Bellow%20ACTA&f=false |accessdate=2012-03-06[/tpl] Neal served as vice president and general counsel of the organization from 1995–2003 before succeeding Jerry L. Martin as president in 2003.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.philanthropydaily.com/?page_id=3262|title=Anne Neal|publisher=Philanthropy Daily|accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl] 
In both 2007 and 2010, Neal was appointed to the National Advisory Committee on Institutional Quality and Integrity, an advisory body that makes recommendations to the United States Secretary of Education.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.chea.org/Government/FedUpdate/CHEA_FU10.html|title=Appointments To National Advisory Committee on Institutional Quality and Integrity Now Complete|publisher=Council for Higher Education Accreditation|date=May 28, 2010|accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl]
Neal’s writing has been published in the Washington Examiner,[tpl]cite web|url=http://washingtonexaminer.com/opinion/op-eds/2010/07/anne-d-neal-college-tax-credit-shouldnt-require-community-service/31932|title=Anne D. Neal: College tax credit shouldn't require community service|publisher=Washington Examiner|accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] Inside Higher Ed,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.insidehighered.com/views/2010/11/12/neal |title=Asking Too Much (and Too Little) of Accreditors|publisher=Inside Higher Ed |date=November 12, 2012| accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] the Washington Post, and National Review Online.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nationalreview.com/author/40505|title=Anne D. Neal|publisher=National Review Online|accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] Neal has appeared on Fox Business News, CNN, Fox News, WGN-TV, and NPR.[tpl]cite book|url=http://books.google.com/books/about/The_Intelligent_Donor_s_Guide_to_College.html?id=GUTWAQAACAAJ|title=The Intelligent Donor's Guide to College Giving|publisher=American Council of Trustees and Alumni|date=September 1, 2011|accessdate=February 14, 2012[/tpl]
Neal has testified before the Commission on the Future of Higher Education, the United States Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor, and Pensions, the Louisiana Postsecondary Education Commission, and the California Advisory Committee to the U.S. Civil Rights Commission. She has presented at conferences sponsored by the American Enterprise Institute, the American Legislative Exchange Council, the University of Notre Dame, the Foreign Policy Association, the Association of American Colleges and Universities, Montana State University, the American Association of University Professors and the Council for Higher Education Accreditation.
Neal serves on the boards of the Mount Vernon Ladies’ Association, the United States Capitol Historical Society, and Casey Trees.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uschs.org/Content/70.htm|title=U.S. Capitol Historical Society Board of Trustees|publisher=United States Capitol Historical Society|accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://caseytrees.org/about/board/|title=Casey Trees Board of Directors|publisher=Casey Trees|accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl]
