[[Conley]]

CATEGORIES: Anglicised Irish-language surnames, Surnames of Irish origin

Conley (from O′Conghaile,[tpl]cite book |editor=Michael C. O'Laughlin |title=The Book of Irish Families, Great & Small |edition= 3rd |year=2002 |publisher=Irish Genealogical Foundation |location= Kansas City, MO |isbn=0-940134-09-8 |page=61 [/tpl] Ó Conghalaigh) is a surname of Irish origin. It is a variant spelling of several more common names like Conly, Connelly, and Connolly. “Conly” is listed in the census of 1659 as coming from the city of Dublin. “O′Connoly” was a principal name of Monaghan in 1679. 1911 60 people with the surname Conley are listed in the digitized census records of Ireland.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.census.nationalarchives.ie |title=1901/1911 Census, Ireland |publisher= The National Archives of Ireland |accessdate=9 November 2012[/tpl]
Today 1,048 people have the surname Conley in the United Kingdom, a few less (-36) than in the 1881 census.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.britishsurnames.co.uk/surnames/CONLEY |title=British surnames |accessdate=10 September 2012[/tpl] As of the 2000 U.S. Census, 54,194 people had the surname Conley, making it the 562nd most common surname in America.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.census.gov/genealogy/www/data/2000surnames/index.html |title=Genealogy Data: Frequently Occurring Surnames from Census 2000 - U.S. Census Bureau |publisher=U.S. Census Bureau |accessdate=9 November 2012[/tpl]
