[[Gmina Nowe Brzesko]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Lesser Poland Voivodeship, Proszowice County

Gmina Nowe Brzesko is an urban-rural gmina (administrative district) in Proszowice County, Lesser Poland Voivodeship, in southern Poland. Its seat is the town of Nowe Brzesko, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|10|km|mi|0[/tpl] south-east of Proszowice and [tpl]convert|33|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] east of the regional capital Kraków.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|54.53|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 5,769. It was formerly classed as a rural gmina, becoming urban-rural when Nowe Brzesko became a town on 1 January 2011.

==Villages==

Apart from the town of Nowe Brzesko, Gmina Nowe Brzesko contains the villages and settlements of Grębocin, Gruszów, Hebdów, Kuchary, Majkowice, Mniszów, Mniszów-Kolonia, Pławowice, Przybysławice, Rudno Dolne, Sierosławice, Śmiłowice and Szpitary.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Nowe Brzesko is bordered by the gminas of Drwinia, Igołomia-Wawrzeńczyce, Koszyce and Proszowice.
