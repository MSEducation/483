Cottbus Air Base ([tpl]lang-de|Flugplatz Cottbus-Nord[/tpl] – "Airport Cottbus-North") [tpl]Airport codes||ETHT[/tpl] is a former military airport [tpl]convert|2|km|abbr=in[/tpl] north-west of Cottbus in Brandenburg, Germany.

==History==

During World War II the air base in Cottbus was used by the Luftwaffe and during the 1970s and '80s by the National People's Army. Following German reunification until 2004, the air base was also used by the Bundeswehr.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mil-airfields.de/de/flugplatz-cottbus.htm|title=Flugplatz Cottbus|work=Military Airfield Directory|accessdate=10 January 2010|language=German[/tpl]

==Present==

The airport is currently closed to air-traffic, although the concrete paved runway has been designated for use by cargo flights in the event of an emergency. There is an aerospace museum on site.

==Future==

Having been bought by the local government in 2007,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bundesimmobilien.de/Dateien/Artikel_FP_Nord.pdf|title=Flugplatz Cottbus-Nord im Süden Brandenburgs verkauft|work=Bundesanstalt für Immobilienaufgaben|accessdate=10 January 2010|language=German[/tpl] the city of Cottbus plans to turn the former air base into an industrial park, creating around 1,000 jobs.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cottbus.de/unternehmer/tip/the_property,255030420.en.html|title=The Property|work=Cottbus.de|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web|first=Jürgen|last=Becker|url=http://www.lr-online.de/regionen/cottbus/Auf-Ex-Flugplatz-Cottbus-Nord-sollen-1000-Jobs-entstehen;art1049,1719722|title=Auf Ex-Flugplatz Cottbus-Nord sollen 1000 Jobs entstehen|work=Lausitzer Rundschau|date=25 July 2007|accessdate=10 January 2010|language=German[/tpl]
