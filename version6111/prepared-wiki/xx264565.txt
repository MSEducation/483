[[Sado, Amarapura]]

CATEGORIES: Populated places in Mandalay District, Amarapura Township

 
Sado or Sartoe is a river village on the Myitnge River in Amarapura Township, Mandalay District, in the Mandalay Region of central Burma. It is located just to the southeast of Myitnge.[tpl]cite map|title=Google Maps|publisher=Google|accessdate=2 January 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite map|title=Bing Maps|publisher= Microsoft and Harris Corporation Earthstar Geographics LLC|accessdate=2 January 2011[/tpl]

==History==

During World War II, on March 18, 1944, the Indian 19th Infantry Division, 4th Brigade passed through Sado in which they reportedly faced opposition, before later capturing Amarapura and Mandalay.[tpl]cite book|last=Crew|first=Francis Albert Eley|title=The Army Medical Services: campaigns|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=fM-xAAAAIAAJ|accessdate=2 January 2011|publisher=H.M.S.O.|page=442[/tpl]
In campaigning for the 2010 elections in Burma, Democratic Party executive secretary Daw Than Than Nu visited the village in late October 2010 in which she said about the villagers, “What they mostly want from the government is good education for their children and good healthcare for every citizen.”[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mmtimes.com/2010/news/548/news54804.html|title=‘Presents’ won’t win over voters: Than Than Nu|author= Soe Than Lynn|date=November 8–12, 2010|accessdate=2 January 2011[/tpl]
