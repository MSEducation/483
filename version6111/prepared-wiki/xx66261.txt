[[USS Pittsburgh (SSN-720)]]

CATEGORIES: Los Angeles-class submarines, Cold War submarines of the United States, Nuclear ships of the United States Navy, United States Navy Pennsylvania-related ships, Ships built in Connecticut, 1984 ships, Active submarines of the United States

USS ''Pittsburgh'' (SSN-720) is a [tpl]sclass|Los Angeles|submarine[/tpl] and was the fourth ship of the United States Navy to be named for Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania.

==History==

The contract to build Pittsburgh was awarded to the Electric Boat Division of General Dynamics Corporation in Groton, Connecticut on 16 April 1979 and her keel was laid down on 15 April 1983. She was launched on 8 December 1984 sponsored by Dr. Carol Sawyer, and commissioned on 23 November 1985, with Commander Raymond Setser in command.
On 2 April 1991 Pittsburgh and [tpl]USS|Louisville|SSN-724|2[/tpl] conducted submarine-launched Tomahawk missile attacks against Iraq during Operation Desert Storm. "Gulf War: April 1991." US Navy.
Pittsburgh departed in October 2002 to deploy in the Mediterranean Sea. There, she again fired Tomahawk missiles into Iraq during Operation Iraqi Freedom.[tpl]fact|date=August 2012[/tpl] She returned from that deployment on 27 April 2003.
On 3 August 2012, Commander Michael Ward II was relieved of command of the ship after "allegations of personal misconduct" surfaced in the media."Sub commander relieved of duty after woman alleges he faked death to end affair."
