==Biography==

Stratton was born in Nowganj, Bundelkhand, Madhya Pradesh, India on 5th April 1867. Her birth/baptism is recorded in the India Office Collection at the British Library and has been indexed on the IGI (familysearch.org/pal:/MM9.1.1/FGQ2-BZ6) She lived and worked in London and for many years in Bath where she died age 95. She attended Art Nouveau school in Glasgow where she studied child clothes. Between 1896 and 1908 she illustrated five books of Hans Christian Andersen and from 1903 to 1929 she illustrated Grimms' Fairy Tales and One Thousand and One Nights the later of which came out in 1925. She was also a well-known watercolour illustrator for a book called The Princess and the Goblin which was written by George MacDonald and its sequel The Princess and Curdie in 1912.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.answers.com/topic/helen-stratton|title=Helen Stratton|accessdate=August 12, 2013[/tpl]
