[[Charlie Austin]]

CATEGORIES: 1987 births, Living people, People from Hungerford, English footballers, Association football forwards, Thatcham Town F.C. players, Poole Town F.C. players, Swindon Town F.C. players, Burnley F.C. players, Queens Park Rangers F.C. players, The Football League players

Charles "Charlie" Austin (born 5 July 1989) is an English footballer who plays as a striker for Championship side Queens Park Rangers.

==Career==

===Non-league===

Born in Hungerford, Austin trained with Reading's youth teams as a youngster but was released for being too small.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/football/article-1263141/Charlie-Austin-proving-real-brick-Swindon-Town.html|title=Charlie Austin proving a real brick for Swindon Town|newspaper=Daily Mail|date=3 April 2010|accessdate=13 March 2010 | location=London | first=Ivan | last=Speck[/tpl] He subsequently played for local team Kintbury Rangers and his hometown side Hungerford Town.Town in top form ahead of Fulham Cup tie Wilts and Gloucestershire Standard Moving with his family to Bournemouth, he then switched to nearby semi-professional Wessex League Premier Division team Poole Town while also working as a bricklayer.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/football/2010/mar/07/charlie-austin-swindon-town-bricklayer|title=Charlie Austin pinches himself on road from bricklayer to football hero|newspaper=The Guardian|date=7 March 2010|accessdate=13 March 2010 | location=London | first=Stuart | last=James[/tpl] In the season of 2008–09, Austin scored 46 goals in 48 appearances for Poole in all competitions.[tpl]cite web |title=Poole Town Player Statistics 2008/09 |url= http://www.poole-townfc.co.uk/Player%20Stats/player_stats_0809.html |publisher = Poole Town F.C.|year=2009| accessdate=19 October 2009[/tpl]
Austin spent the pre-season of the 2009–10 season on trial with League Two club Bournemouth. Although Bournemouth's manager, Eddie Howe, was keen to sign the striker on a permanent deal, Bournemouth were unable to make a move for the striker due to a transfer embargo imposed on the club by the Football League.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bournemouthecho.co.uk/sport/football/4649747.Austin_s_Robins_move_is_on_the_cards/|title = Austin's Robins move is on the cards|newspaper= Bournemouth Daily Echo|date=26 September 2009|accessdate=18 January 2010[/tpl] Austin continued to play for Poole Town, scoring 18 goals in 11 games at the beginning of the season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.poole-townfc.co.uk/Player%20Stats/player_stats_0910.html|title= Poole Town Player Statistics 2009/10|publisher = Poole Town F.C.|year=2010|accessdate=13 April 2010[/tpl]

===Swindon Town===

Austin was offered a trial at Swindon Town in September 2009 after Swindon Town's then chief scout Ken Ryder "stumbled across Charlie playing for Poole Town...and he scored four goals...and then the following week he scored three or four".[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.calameo.com/read/001705068f3bd4655d3dd| title=interview with Ken Ryder in The Dugout Magazine|date=11 November 2012|accessdate=11 November 2012[/tpl] Austin was then after being watched for three games by the Swindon Town scouting team asked to go on a week long trial at Swindon Town. Austin impressed in his first reserve game against Swansea and scored a hat trick, immediately after he was signed by Danny Wilson for an undisclosed fee and given a contract until the end of the season.[tpl]cite web | url =http://www.swindontownfc.co.uk/page/NewsDetail/0,,10341~1816187,00.html | title = Charlie Austin Deal Complete |date=2 October 2009 |publisher = Swindon Town F.C. | accessdate = 20 October 2009[/tpl] He made his debut appearance for Swindon Town on 6 October in a 1–1 draw with Exeter in the 2009–10 Football League Trophy, replacing Ben Hutchinson as a substitute in the 88th minute.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/8284815.stm|title=Exeter 1–1 Swindon|publisher=BBC Sport|date=6 October 2010|accessdate=15 April 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.swindontownfc.co.uk/page/MatchReport/0,,10341~50867,00.html|title= Exeter City vs Swindon Town|date=6 October 2009 |publisher = Swindon Town F.C.|accessdate=15 April 2010[/tpl] Austin made his Football League debut coming on in the 70th minute in the league match away at Norwich City on 24 October 2009 and on 21 November 2009, he scored his first professional goal in only the third minute of his full debut away at Carlisle United.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/eng_div_2/8353322.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|title=Carlisle 0–1 Swindon|date=21 November 2009|accessdate=15 April 2010[/tpl] He scored again on his full home debut three days later against Huddersfield Town, hitting the match-winner in the 59th minute for a 2–1 victory.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.swindonadvertiser.co.uk/sport/4757921.FULL_TIME__Town_2_Huddersfield_1/?action=complain&cid=8121793|title=FULL TIME: Town 2 Huddersfield 1|newspaper=Swindon Advertiser|date=24 November 2009|accessdate=13 March 2010[/tpl]
After forming a successful strike partnership with Billy Paynter and scoring 10 goals in 11 starts, Austin was rewarded with an extended two-and-a-half-year contract with the club in February 2010.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/swindon_town/8494085.stm|title=Striker Charlie Austin commits future to Swindon Town|publisher=BBC Sport|date=2 February 2010|accessdate=13 March 2010[/tpl] On 14 May 2010, Austin scored the first goal as Swindon took a narrow 2–1 advantage in the first leg of the League One play-off semi-final against Charlton Athletic. In the second leg at Charlton, Swindon progressed to the final at Wembley after a penalty shoot-out in which Austin scored. Swindon lost the final 1–0 against Millwall; Austin found himself through on goal with an opportunity to equalise in the 72nd minute but shot wide after the ball bobbled on the Wembley turf as he struck the ball.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.timesonline.co.uk/tol/sport/football/football_league/article7140002.ece|title=Paul Robinson breaks deadlock for Millwall|newspaper=The Times |date=29 May 2010| accessdate=30 May 2010 | location=London | first=Nick | last=Szczepanik[/tpl] At the end of his first season as a professional, Austin had scored 20 goals in 32 starts.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/swindon_town/8730367.stm|title=Swindon Town rubbish reports of Austin departure |publisher=BBC Sport| date=9 June 2010| accessdate=2 February 2011[/tpl]
Despite suffering a dislocated shoulder in September, Austin scored another 17 goals in 27 games at the start of the 2010–11 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11758_6704911,00.html|publisher=Sky Sports|title=Town see Austin bid accepted|date=27 January 2011|accessdate=1 February 2011[/tpl] His form prompted several Championship clubs to make offers for him during the January transfer window – all of which were rejected by Swindon.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/s/swindon_town/9365140.stm|title=Swindon Town's Charlie Austin hands in transfer request|publisher=BBC Sport|date=18 January 2011|accessdate=1 February 2011[/tpl] Disappointed at Swindon's reluctance to sell, Austin subsequently stated he was eager to play at a higher level and handed in a transfer request.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.skysports.com/story/0,19528,11095_6660457,00.html|publisher=Sky Sports|title=Austin open to Swindon exit|date=13 January 2011|accessdate=1 February 2011[/tpl] Swindon accepted a bid from Ipswich Town just over a week later but they failed to agree personal terms with Austin and Burnley subsequently stepped in with an undisclosed fee which was also accepted.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/b/burnley/9378506.stm|title=Swindon Town striker Charlie Austin joins Burnley| date=28 January 2011| accessdate=28 January 2011| publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl]

===Burnley===

On 28 January 2011, Austin signed for Burnley on a three-and-a-half-year contract. He made his Burnley debut on 1 February in a 1–0 loss away to Doncaster Rovers.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.lancashiretelegraph.co.uk/sport/8826050.FULL_TIME__Doncaster_Rovers_1_Burnley_0/|title=Full Time: Doncaster Rovers 1 Burnley 0 |newspaper=Lancashire Telegraph|date=1 February 2011| accessdate=2 February 2011[/tpl] In his first full season with Burnley, Austin scored 16 goals in the Championship and finished the campaign as the club's top goalscorer in the league.
On 23 October 2012, Austin scored two goals in a 3–4 win over Bristol City, and subsequently equalled Ray Pointer's club record of scoring in eight consecutive appearances, which was achieved back during the 1958–59 season.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/20066554|title=Charlie Austin equals Burnley goalscoring record at Bristol City|work=BBC Sport|date=24 October 2012|accessdate=24 October 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.burnleyfootballclub.com/news/article/austin-on-matching-rays-record-241012-444887.aspx?pageView=full#anchored|title=Austin's Pride at Matching Ray Pointer|work=Burnley Football Club|date=24 October 2012|accessdate=26 October 2012[/tpl]
Two weeks later, on 6 November 2012, Austin broke another Burnley record as he became the quickest-ever player to reach 20 goals in a season after scoring in a 1–0 win over Leeds United — his 17th appearance of the season. The record was previously held by Bert Freeman, who scored 20 goals in his first 19 matches for the club during the 1911–12 season. He also became only the second-ever Burnley player to score 20 goals before the end of November after Andy Lochhead, who reached 20 goals in 25 games on 26 November 1966.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.burnleyfootballclub.com/news/article/charlie-sets-new-record-061112-470376.aspx|title=Charlie Sets New Record|work=Burnley Football Club|date=6 November 2012|accessdate=7 November 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/20237543|title=Burnley striker Charlie Austin reaches 20-goal landmark|work=BBC Sport|date=7 November 2012|accessdate=14 November 2012[/tpl]
Austin managed to get his goal tally up to 23 goals by the end of the calendar year.
On 8 July 2013, a fee was agreed between Burnley and Premier League club Hull City for Austin.http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/22984455 However, on 9 July 2013, Austin failed his medical and Hull pulled out of the deal.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www1.skysports.com/football/news/11714/8814422/Transfer-news-Burnley-striker-Charlie-Austin-fails-medical-at-Hull-City | work=Sky Sports[/tpl]
===Queens Park Rangers=== 
Austin moved to Queens Park Rangers, on a three-year deal, on 1 August 2013. QPR paid Burnley an undisclosed fee,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23533841|title=Charlie Austin: QPR sign Burnley striker for undisclosed fee|publisher=BBC Sport|date=1 August 2013|accessdate=1 August 2013[/tpl] believed to be £4 million.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.dailymail.co.uk/sport/football/article-2382194/QPR-confirm-signing-Charlie-Austin-Burnley-year-deal.html|title=QPR confirm the signing of Charlie Austin from Burnley on three-year deal|publisher=Mail Online|date=1 August 2013|accessdate=1 August 2013|location=London[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dailystar.co.uk/sport/football/329967/Hotshot-Charlie-Austin-secures-move-to-Championship-favourites-QPR|title=Hotshot Charlie Austin secures move to Championship favourites QPR |publisher=Daily Star|date=1 August 2013|accessdate=1 August 2013[/tpl] He scored his first goal for QPR in a League Cup tie against Exeter City on 6 August 2013.[tpl]cite news|title=Exeter 0 – 2 QPR|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23499499|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=6 August 2013|date=6 August 2013[/tpl] On 14 September 2013, Austin scored his first league goal for QPR in a 1–0 win over Birmingham City at Loftus Road.[tpl]cite news|title=QPR 1–0 Birmingham|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/23999394|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=14 September 2013|date=14 September 2013[/tpl] On 28 September 2013, he scored a penalty in a 2–0 win at home to Middlesbrough. On 5 October 2013, he scored 2 goals including a penalty in a 2–0 win at home to Barnsley which increased his goal tally in the home and away season to 6.[tpl]cite news|title=QPR 2 – 0 Barnsley|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/sport/0/football/24349212|publisher=BBC Sport|accessdate=5 October 2013|date=5 October 2013[/tpl]
