==Early career==

A graduate of the University of Missouri, Rapp served as the Tigers End coach from 1967-71.[tpl]cite news |author= |coauthors= |title=Browns' Mack Pleads Guilty To Cocaine Use |url= |quote= |work=St. Louis Post-Dispatch |date=August 31, 1989 |accessdate=2011-01-01 [/tpl] In 1972, he became the offensive coordinator of the Canadian Football League's Edmonton Eskimos. During his tenure in Edmonton, the Eskimos appeared in the Grey Cup four times; winning in 1975. Rapp left Edmonton in 1977 to become the receivers coach at Miami.[tpl]cite news |author= |coauthors= |title=Lou Saban completes his coaching staff |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=w9wzAAAAIBAJ&sjid=7IwDAAAAIBAJ&pg=5910,893321&dq |quote= |work=Boca Raton News |date=January 7, 1977 |accessdate=2011-01-01 [/tpl] Less than three weeks after accepting the Miami job, BC Lions' general manager Bob Ackles hired Rapp to replace Cal Murphy as Lions head coach.

==BC Lions==

In his first season as Lions coach, Rapp lead the Lions to a 10-6 record and was named the Canadian Football League's Coach of the Year.[tpl]cite news |author=The Associated Press |coauthors= |title=Sports Shorts |url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=_DcvAAAAIBAJ&sjid=AqwFAAAAIBAJ&pg=1277,2308953&dq |quote= |work=The Argus-Press |date=January 28, 1978 |accessdate=2011-01-01 [/tpl] The Lions made the playoffs three times under Rapp, but never advanced past the Western Final. After six season in Vancouver without a championship, Ackles felt that Rapp would not be able to lead the Lions to a championship and fired him.[tpl]cite news |author=Lyndon Little |coauthors= |title=Former Lions coach Vic Rapp reflects upon Wall of Fame induction |url=http://www.vancouversun.com/sports/Former+Lions+coach+Rapp+reflects+upon+Wall+Fame+induction/3288937/story.html |quote= |work=Vancouver Sun |date=July 16, 2010 |accessdate=2011-01-01 [/tpl]

==Later career==

After his firing, Rapp served as an assistant with the Houston Oilers, Los Angeles Rams, Tampa Bay Buccaneers, Detroit Lions, Chicago Bears, and Arizona Cardinals. He spent one season as a scout for the Philadelphia Eagles.[tpl]cite news |author= |coauthors= |title=Cardinals Hire Fassel as Offensive Coordinator |url= |quote= |work=St. Louis Post-Dispatch |date=February 12, 1996 |accessdate=2011-01-01 [/tpl]
Rapp is currently retired and resides in Orlando, Florida.
