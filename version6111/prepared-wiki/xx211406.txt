[[Cristiano Michelena]]

Cristiano Rositoano Michelena (born March 9, 1971 in Rio Grande do Sul[tpl]cite web|title = ASU, a team with Brazilian tradition|work=Best Swimming|date = May 19, 2008|url = http://www.bestswimming.com.br/beijing2008/conteudo.php?id=8899|accessdate =May 4, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl] [tpl]cite web|title = Placar Sports Magazine|work=Placar|date = October 26, 1987|url = http://books.google.com.br/books?id=NQXBU2En3tEC&pg=PA56&lpg=PA56&dq=cristiano+michelena+ga%C3%BAcho&source=bl&ots=_mcQ4zIAu_&sig=yWkDwDe2n5XRsypudkVaRMv3E7I&hl=pt-BR&sa=X&ei=4SmFUbDaLMeR0QHt8YGABQ&ved=0CC0Q6AEwAA#v=onepage&q=cristiano%20michelena%20ga%C3%BAcho&f=false|accessdate =May 4, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl] ) is a former international freestyle swimmer from Brazil. He participated in two consecutive Summer Olympics for his native country, starting in 1988. His best result was the sixth place in the Men's 4×100-metre freestyle Relay in Barcelona, Spain.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/mi/cristiano-michelena-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
He was at the 1987 Pan American Games, in Indianapolis. He won silver in the 400-metre freestyle, and bronze in the 4×100-metre freestyle and 4×200-metre freestyle.  [tpl]cite web | title = Brazil medals at 1987 Pan | work = UOL | year = 2007 | url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/historia/1987/nopodio.jhtm | accessdate = April 20, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl] He also finished 4th in the 1500-metre freestyle, and 6th in the 200-metre freestyle. [tpl]cite web|title = Results at 1987 Pan Am Games in Indianapolis|work=USA Swimming|year = 2013|url = http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/ca27f27a-3d91-4248-bdab-1ab0dc7880e1/87_panam%5B1%5D.pdf|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
At the 1988 Summer Olympics, in Seoul, he finished 10th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, 12th in the 4×100-metre freestyle, 23rd in the 200-metre freestyle, 23rd in the 400-metre freestyle, and 26th in the 1500-metre freestyle.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/mi/cristiano-michelena-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
On April 17, 1989, he broke the short-course South American record in the 400-metre freestyle, with a time of 3:46.39. [tpl]cite web | title = Final Results | work = CBDA | year = November 16, 2001 | url = http://www.cbda.org.br/resultados/final-results-id-789 | accessdate = May 11, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl] The record was broken only in the end of 2005, by Armando Negreiros.[tpl]cite web|title = Kaio, on top of the world in his three races|work=CBDA|date = December 18, 2005|url = http://www.cbda.org.br/noticias/kaio-no-topo-do-mundo-em-suas-tres-provas-id-6508|accessdate =May 11, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title = Negreiros' UOL profile|work=UOL|year = 2007|url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/modalidades/natacao/brasileiros/armandonegreiros.jhtm|accessdate =May 11, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl]
At the 1992 Summer Olympics, in Barcelona, he finished 6th in the 4×100-metre freestyle, 7th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, and 21st in the 200-metre freestyle.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|date = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/mi/cristiano-michelena-1.html|accessdate =April 20, 2013[/tpl]
