[[Eric Ortner]]

CATEGORIES: American television producers, American television journalists, Year of birth missing (living people), Living people

Eric Ortner is a Senior Producer for ABC News 20/20, Primetime, and non-fiction programming.  He was also Senior Producer of ABC Television Network's Six Degrees of Martina McBride.
In December 2005, he departed NBC News Today show, where he first began in high school, and was named Senior Producer of ABC's Good Morning America.http://www.variety.com/article/VR1117935981.html In 2006, when broadcasting and cable covered new technology, Ortner integrated into Good Morning America.http://www.broadcastingcable.com/article/CA6387646.html
Prior to joining ABC, Eric Ortner worked in the position of senior tape producer for NBC News Weekend Today and also as producer in charge of consumer and investigative stories for NBC News Today.http://www.mediabistro.com/tvnewser/abc/today_prod_moving_to_gma_weekend_30115.
In September 2001, Eric Ortner was profiled by Bob Dotson on Today and in a PoynterOnline article chronicling Ortner's experiences as a volunteer EMS during and following the September 11 attacks.http://www.poynter.org/content/content_view.asp?id=5972
In September 2009, Ortner admiringly referred to Diane Sawyer as a "Super Nova" in The New York Times. http://mediadecoder.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/09/03/at-abc-news-attention-now-turns-to-gma/?hp
On September 30, 2009, Ortner announced with his company Eric Ortner Productions
http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/hr/content_display/news/e3ie41d1967dbc1d0964549480c356a07bb a production partnership with Flavor Flav and ISH Entertainment to produce an unscripted show with the working title: "Flavor Flav Goes Back to High School". http://www.accesshollywood.com/flavor-flav-gets-ready-to-head-back-to-school_article_23587. 
Additionally, on April 12, 2010, an announcement revealed that Eric Ortner Productions acquired the rights to produce and develop the Golden Raspberry Awards Awards, commonly called the Razzies.http://popwatch.ew.com/2010/04/12/razzies-may-be-televised/ Retrieved May 12, 2010.
On April 12, 2010, it was announced that Ortner acquired the rights to produce and develop the Golden Raspberry Awards ( Razzie ) Awards. http://popwatch.ew.com/2010/04/12/razzies-may-be-televised/
Ortner and Eric Ortner Productions will produce future awards telecasts, along with Razzie branded television shows and other entertainment properties.  http://www.accesshollywood.com/razzie-awards-coming-to-tv-in-2011_article_31052
Ortner helped with the Obama re-election campaign in 2012 and was seen at the DNC.
