[[Loyalty Day]]

CATEGORIES: Observances in the United States by presidential proclamation, May observances, American culture, Fixed holidays

Loyalty Day is observed on May 1 in the United States.  It is a day set aside for the reaffirmation of loyalty to the United States and for the recognition of the heritage of American freedom.
Loyalty Day is celebrated with parades and ceremonies in several U.S. communities, like Batavia, Illinois, although many people in the United States remain unaware of it.[tpl]citation needed|date = May 2013[/tpl] Although a legal holiday, it is not a federal holiday, and is not commonly observed.[tpl]citation needed|date = May 2013[/tpl]
