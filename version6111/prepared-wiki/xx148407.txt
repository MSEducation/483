[[Ernie Shepherd]]

Ernest "Ernie" Shepherd (14 August 1919 – 2001) was an English footballer who played in the Football League as an outside left for Fulham, West Bromwich Albion, Hull City and Queens Park Rangers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/player1/ernieshepherd.html |title=Ernie Shepherd |work=UK A–Z Transfers |publisher=Neil Brown |accessdate=7 October 2009[/tpl] He went on to manage Southend United.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.southendunited.co.uk/page/HistoryDetail/0,,10444~1028927,00.html |title=Former managers |publisher=Southend United F.C |accessdate=7 October 2009[/tpl]
Shepherd was born in Wombwell, near Barnsley, in Yorkshire. He joined Fulham in 1934 before signing for West Bromwich Albion in December 1948, moving on again only after only three months to Hull City.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.11v11.co.uk/index.php?pageID=537&playerID=39151&teamID=60&sqlID=97&divID=7&seasonend=1948 |title=Ernie Shepherd |publisher=Association of Football Statisticians |accessdate=7 October 2009[/tpl] He joined Queens Park Rangers in 1950, and made his debut in August that year against Chesterfield.[tpl]cite web |url=http://qprnet.com/history/seasons/excels/QPR1950.xls |format=Excel spreadsheet |title=Queens Park Rangers 1950–51 |first=Kenneth |last=Westerberg |publisher=QPRnet |accessdate=7 October 2009[/tpl] Shepherd played 219 league games for QPR scoring 51 goals before retiring from playing in 1957. He went on to coach both in England and abroad, and from 1967 to 1969 was manager of Southend United. Shepherd died in Eastwood, Essex, in 2001.
