[[Clifton Daniel (bishop)]]

CATEGORIES: 1947 births, Living people, Bishops of the Episcopal Church in the United States of America

Clifton Daniel 3rd (born July 4, 1947, Goldsboro, North Carolina), was the Seventh Bishop Diocesan of the  Episcopal Diocese of East Carolina.  He served the diocese as Bishop Diocesan from 1997 until 2013, when he became Bishop Provisional of The Episcopal Diocese of Pennsylvania.

==Biography==

===Education===

Bishop Daniel attended the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, having graduated in 1969 with a Bachelor of Arts in Spanish Education.  He earned his Master of Divinity from Virginia Theological Seminary in Alexandria, Virginia.  He was granted honorary Doctor of Divinity degrees from both Virginia Theological Seminary in 1997 and the University of the South in Sewanee, Tennessee in 1998.

===Vocational Milestones===

Bishop Daniel was ordained to the diaconate in June 1972, and then to the priesthood in April 1973.  In 1996 he was elected and consecrated Bishop Coadjutor of the Episcopal Diocese of East Carolina.  With the death of The Rt. Rev. B. Sidney Sanders, he became Bishop Diocesan on June 5, 1997. On February 28, 2013, Bishop Daniel resigned as Bishop Diocesan of East Carolina in order to accept a call to serve as the Bishop Provisional of The Episcopal Diocese of Pennsylvania.
