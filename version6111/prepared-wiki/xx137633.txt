Metro San Joaquín ([tpl]Lang-es|Estación San Joaquín[/tpl]) is a station along Line 7 of the metro of Mexico City.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.metro.df.gob.mx/red/estacion.html?id=93|title=San Joaquín|language=Spanish|accessdate=2 August 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|last=Archambault|first=Richard|url=http://mexicometro.org/metro/line7/san-joaquin/|title=San Joaquín »  Mexico City Metro System|accessdate=2 August 2011[/tpl]
Its logo represents the silhouette of a bridge of the radial Viaducto Río San Joaquín.
It was opened on 20 December 1984.[tpl]Cite web|last=Schwandl|first=Robert|url=http://www.urbanrail.net/am/mexi/mex-history.htm|title=Opening Dates for Mexico City's Subway|accessdate=1 August 2011[/tpl]
It is located in the Colonia Pencil Sur neighnorhood and nearby points of interest include the Panteón Francés.
