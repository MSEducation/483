Luca Franchini (born 31 December 1983) is a retired Italian football player. He is currently a youth coach at Brentford.

==Career==

Franchini started his career at Internazionale at age of 8.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=en&N=8763|title= FRANCHINI: "THANKS INTER. A DREAM HAS COME TRUE." |date=4 May 2003|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it[/tpl] He started to play as a left back and turned to centre back in 2001, as the team had Giovanni Pasquale. He won the Primavera League title in 2002.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=en&N=3056|title= ITALIAN YOUTH CHAMPIONSHIP FINAL – SCUDETTO FOR INTER |date=6 June 2002|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it[/tpl] In 2002–03 season, he succeed Nicola Beati as Primavera team captain which Beati was promoted to first team.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=en&N=6160|title= YOUTH TEAMERS IN THE FIRST TEAM – LUCA FRANCHINI |date=3 December 2002|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it[/tpl] Franchini led the team entered the League playoffs final, but lost to Lecce in 2–3.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=en&N=9188|title= INTER 2 LECCE 3 – NERAZZURRINI MISS OUT ON SECOND CONSECUTIVE TITLE |date=6 June 2003|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it[/tpl]
He also made 2 official appearances for Inter first team in 2002–03 season, one at Serie A, one at Coppa Italia.
In summer 2003, he graduated from youth team and loaned to Padova of Serie C1.
In August 2004, he was on loan to Ascoli along with Isah Eliakwu.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=it&N=23714|title= MERCATO: DUE PRESTITI ALL'ASCOLI |date=19 August 2004|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it|language=Italian[/tpl]
Due to fail to enter the first team, in January 2005, he was on loan to Vis Pesaro 1898, but changed to Ancona of Serie C2 on 31 January 2005, the last day of the transfer window.
In summer 2005, he was sold to Pro Patria of Serie C1.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.inter.it/aas/news/reader?L=en&N=19406|title= NEW ITALIAN CLUBS FOR THREE PLAYERS |date=22 July 2005|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=inter.it[/tpl] A year later, Mantova of Serie B bought him from Pro Patria and signed a 3-year contract[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.acmantova.org/news_estesa.asp?idnews=1200|title= ACMantova News |date=7 July 2006|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=AC Mantova|language=Italian[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] In the first season he just played 9 starts in 10 league appearances. In the next season, he played more regular with 19 starts.
He joined Gallipoli Calcio on August 2009.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.tuttomercatoweb.com/?action=read&id=169271|title=UFFICIALE: Luca Franchini è del Gallipoli|date=31 August 2009|accessdate=16 March 2010|work=Tutto Mercato Web|language=Italian[/tpl] On 26 January 2011 signed with FC Südtirol.FC Südtirol verpflichtet Abwehrspieler Luca Franchini
