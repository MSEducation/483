[[Mitchell Kutney]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Canadian bloggers, Businesspeople from Toronto, 1985 births

Mitchell Kutney (born May 27, 1985) is an Ottawa based nonprofit entrepreneur and blogger.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mitchell-kutney/ | title=Huffington Post Biography: Mitchell Kutney |work=Huffington Post[/tpl]

==Background==

Mitchell Kutney was born in Burlington, Ontario and completed his undergraduate degree in Psychology at the University of Ottawa and his Masters in Public Policy and Administration at Carleton University.

==Career==

Mitchell Kutney began his career by spearheading an innovative immigrant youth program in partnership with the University of Ottawa and the City of Ottawa known as Youth Futures. Citizens Academy |publisher=Citizensacademy.ca |date=2012-07-09 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite news | url=http://metronews.ca/news/ottawa/343413/ottawans-need-help-to-navigate-scary-black-hole-of-city-politics-group/ | title=Ottawans need help to navigate scary black hole of city politics group |work=Metro[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://awesomeottawa.ca/2012/08/citizens-academy-ottawa/ |title=Citizens Academy Ottawa | Awesome Ottawa |publisher=Awesomeottawa.ca |date=2012-08-19 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.knowledgemobilization.net/archives/1553 |title=Mitchell Kutney will be speaking about Citizens Academy Ottawa at Volunteer Canada Event — Institute for Knowledge Mobilization |publisher=Knowledgemobilization.net |date=2012-09-21 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl] and co-founded JustChange Inc.,[tpl]cite news | url=http://metronews.ca/news/ottawa/541456/new-microgrant-group-invites-ottawa-to-justchange/ | title=New Microgrant Group Invites Ottawa to JustChange |work=Metro[/tpl][tpl]cite news | url=http://www.ottawastar.com/small-change-makes-a-big-difference/ | title=Small change makes a big difference |work=Ottawa Star[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.justchange.ca/the-board/ |title=The Board | Just Change |publisher=Justchange.ca |date= |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=November 29, 2012 |url=http://cised.ca/justchange-ottawas-own-social-enterprise-seed-funds/ |title=JustChange: Ottawa’s own social enterprise seed funds | CISED |publisher=Cised.ca |date=2012-11-29 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl] a micro-granting agency that supports high-risk social enterprises.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.communityforwardfund.ca/newsroom/blog/justchange-venture-philanthropy-with-shallow-pockets-big-dreams-for-social-change/ |title=CFF Social Finance Blog | JustChange's Venture Philanthropy | CFF |publisher=Communityforwardfund.ca |date=2013-06-18 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl]

==Blogging==

Mitchell Kutney is an established blogger on topics related to social enterprise, philanthropy and charity.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.mitchellkutney.com/what-people-are-saying-about-mitchellkutney-com/ |title=What people are saying about |publisher=Mitchellkutney.com |date= |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.policyplay.com/contributors |title=Contributors |publisher=PolicyPlay |date= |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://blueandgreentomorrow.com/author/mitchell-kutney/ |title=Blue and Green Tomorrow |publisher=Blue and Green Tomorrow |date=2013-08-08 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gotomoe.com/ |title=We search daily entrepreneurial news so you don't have to |publisher=gotoMoe |date= |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl] He is best known for his original article titled: "Philanthropy is what sustains the charitable sector, not money" where he explores what it means to be a philanthropist. This article was the editors pick for the United Kingdom's Blue & Green Tomorrow[tpl]cite news | url=http://blueandgreentomorrow.com/features/philanthropy-is-what-sustains-the-charitable-sector-not-money/ | title=Philanthropy is what sustains the charitable sector, not money |work=Blue & Green Tomorrow[/tpl] magazine on August 8, 2013 and was featured in The Guide to Giving and Philanthropy. He has also written on the topic of social finance and impact investing.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.communityforwardfund.ca/newsroom/blog/social-finance-news-for-april/ |title=Social Finance News for April | CFF |publisher=Communityforwardfund.ca |date=2013-04-29 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl]http://www.nexii.com/blog/industry-news-round-up-25-january/
His debut with the Huffington Post brought into question the structures of the charitable sector and how these might be influencing the long-term outcomes of charity,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/mitchell-kutney/the-end-game-of-charity_b_3750301.html |title=The End Game of Charity |publisher=The Huffington Post |date=2013-08-13 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl] which subsequently led to an interview with the Globe and Mail on the future of sustainable business models.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.theglobeandmail.com/report-on-business/careers/career-advice/life-at-work/good-vibes-for-social-responsibility/article14575293/ |title=Is doing good a sustainable business model? |publisher=The Globe and Mail |date=2013-08-28 |accessdate=2013-08-28[/tpl] He has also been interviewed by the Ottawa Citizen with regards to social enterprise in Ottawa and how it can contribute to making the city a more fun and exciting place to live.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ottawacitizen.com/Ottawa+town+that+forgot/9606300/story.html |title=Is Ottawa the town that fun forgot? |publisher=Ottawa Citizen |date=2014-03-12 |accessdate=2014-03-12[/tpl] Mitchell is also a guest writer for many leaders in the field such as the Impact Investing Policy Collaborative (IIPC)[tpl]cite web|url=http://iipcollaborative.org/authors/mitchell-kutney/ |title=Lessons Learned from Microfinance for the Impact Investing Sector |publisher=IIPCollaborative |date= |accessdate=2013-12-14[/tpl] and he was the first blogger to reveal Visa's smallenfreuden viral campaign in Toronto.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.marketingmag.ca/news/marketer-news/visa-reveals-its-the-brand-behind-smallenfreuden-78554 |title=Visa reveals it’s the brand behind smallenfreuden | Marketing Magazine |publisher=Marketingmag.ca |date=2013-05-10 |accessdate=2013-08-14[/tpl] On March 3, 2014, Mitchell made his debut with the Toronto Star writing about the provincial National Democratic Party's private member’s bill to cap public sector salaries, which included charitable organizations like hospitals and universities.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thestar.com/opinion/commentary/2014/03/03/charities_are_powerhouses_of_canadas_economy.html |title=Charities are powerhouses of Canada’s economy |publisher=Toronto Star |date=2014-03-03 |accessdate=2014-03-31[/tpl] The article was met with equal criticism and praise after being shared over 250 times across social media networks, with one comment stating, Mitchell "is one of the few willing to speak out against misguided popular thinking."
