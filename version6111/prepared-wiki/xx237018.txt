[[Irving Glickman]]

CATEGORIES: 1914 births, 1972 deaths, Periodontists

Irving Glickman (b 1914 in Williamsburg, Brooklyn - d 1972) was an American clinical researcher in the field of periodontology and author. He was one of the first to classify furcation defects and the role of occlusal trauma on periodontal disease.Jan Lindhe: Trauma from Occlusion: Periodontal Tissues. In Jan Lindhe, editor: Clinical Periodontology and Implant Dentistry, 5th Edition, Blackwell Munksgaard 2008, page 350. Glickman attended dental school at Tufts University. In 1941, he joined the faculty and became chair of Department of Periodontology [tpl]cite book|last1=Newman|first1=Michael G.|last2=Takei|first2=Henry|last3=Klokkevold|first3=Perry R.|coauthors=Fermin A. In the 1950s, he developed the bone factor concept about the factors that determine the severity of periodontal destruction,[tpl]cite book|last1=T Siji Jacob|first1=|last2=P Arunmozhi|first2=|title=Viva Voce in Periodontics|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=gWEymJtmAx0C&pg=PA74|year=2008|publisher=Jaypee Brothers Publishers|isbn=9788184483772|pages=74–[/tpl] and developed a classification system for furcation involvement[tpl]cite book|last1=Reddy|first1=Shantipriya|last2=Reddy|first2=|title=Essentials of Clinical Periodontology and Periodontics|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=WM67jyzXrAUC&pg=PA367|accessdate=15 December 2013|year=2008|publisher=Jaypee Brothers Publishers|isbn=9788184481488|pages=367–[/tpl] In 1965 Glickman proposed a theory involving the relation of occlusal trauma to periodontal disease which led to further research in animal models.
