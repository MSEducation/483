Italian-Lebanese relations are relations between Italy and Lebanon. Both countries are members of the Union for the Mediterranean. Their relationship spans many centuries. 

==History==

In 64 B.C., the Roman general Pompey added both Lebanon and Syria to the Roman Republic.[tpl]Citation needed|date=February 2009[/tpl]  During and before this time, Phoenicians and Romans exchanged knowledge, habits, and customs.

===Modern history===

Lebanon opened an legation in 1946, which was transformed into an embassy in 1955.[tpl]Citation needed|date=February 2009[/tpl]  Both countries signed a Treaty of Friendship, Cooperation and Navigation in 1949.[tpl]Citation needed|date=February 2009[/tpl]

==Italian assistance==

The Italian Government supported the reconstruction of Lebanon after the Taef Agreement.[tpl]Citation needed|date=February 2009[/tpl]
In December 2013, the Italian Government announced increased funding to Lebanon to assist Lebanon in dealing with refugees fleeing the Syrian Civil War. Italy also announced a rescue mission with five to seven ships to rescue thousands of migrants, mostly Syrians, in the Mediterranean Sea http://www.dailystar.com.lb/News/Lebanon-News/2013/Dec-14/241078-italy-to-organize-conference-to-support-lebanese-army.ashx#axzz2yFshKIbs
