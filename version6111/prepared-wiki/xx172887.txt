[[Sir John Sinclair, 3rd Baronet]]

Sir John George Tollemache Sinclair, 3rd Baronet (8 November 1825 – 30 September 1912) was a Scottish landowner and Liberal politician who sat in the House of Commons from 1869 to 1885.

==Early life and education==

Born in Edinburgh in 1825, he was the son of Sir George Sinclair, 2nd Baronet, and Lady Catherine Camilla Tollemache, daughter of William Talmash, Lord Huntingtower. He was a Page of Honour for Queen Adelaide.
He was educated at Cheam School and the University of Durham.[tpl]cite book |url=http://www.archive.org/stream/debrettshouseo1881londuoft#page/210/mode/2up/search/durham |title=Debrett's House of Commons |page=210 |publisher=Dean |location=London |lccn=JN671 |year=1867[/tpl]

==Career==

He served as a Lieutenant in the Scots Fusilier Guards. 
In 1861 he was made Vice-Lieutenant for Caithness.[tpl]LondonGazette |issue=22504 |date=23 April 1861 |startpage= 1754 |endpage=1754 |accessdate=18 February 2013[/tpl]
Sinclair was elected Member of Parliament for Caithness in 1869 and held the seat until 1885.[tpl]Rayment-hc|c|1|date=March 2012[/tpl] His majority of 13 over the Conservative candidate at the 1874 election is one of the smallest on record.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.alba.org.uk/westminster/smallest1832.html |title=Smallest majorities at Westminster |accessdate= 1 January 2008[/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=February 2013[/tpl] At the 1885 General election, his son Clarence succeeded him as Liberal candidate, but was defeated by Gavin Brown Clark, the Crofters' Party candidate.

==Title and family==

Sinclair married Emma Standish, daughter of William Standish, Duxbury Park, Lancashire, and Cocken Hall, Durham in 1853.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.freebmd.org.uk/cgi/information.pl?cite=naSrSl8encNEUlIxyK5Beg&scan=1|title=Index entry|accessdate=19 February 2013|work=FreeBMD|publisher=ONS[/tpl] The couple had two sons and two daughters: 
In 1868 he succeeded his father to the baronetcy.
The marriage was dissolved in 1878.
He was succeeded in the baronetcy by his grandson Archibald Henry Macdonald Sinclair, later Viscount Thurso.

==Miscellaneous==

Sinclair was the earliest born person to have made a gramophone disc recording. He made titles for Columbia, Gramophone and Typewriter Ltd. and Odeon, all in 1906.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.sol.co.uk/c/chrishamilton/DOCUMENTARY78S.HTM |title=A Gallery of Documentary and Speech 78s |accessdate=1 January 2008 [/tpl] He also commissioned a statue of Mary, Queen of Scots, at 143–144 Fleet Street, London.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.ludgatecircus.com/mary_queen_of_scots.htm |title=Statue commissioned by Sinclair |accessdate=1 January 2008 [/tpl]
