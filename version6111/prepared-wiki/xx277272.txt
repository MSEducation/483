[[John Blumsky]]

John Patrick Cyril Blumsky [tpl]post-nominals|country=NZL|QSM[/tpl] (13 November 1928 – 1 August 2013) was a New Zealand journalist and broadcaster, best known for his coverage of the Erebus disaster in 1979.

==Biography==

Blumsky was educated at Nelson College from 1943 to 1945.Nelson College Old Boys' Register, 1856–2006, 6th edition Before moving into broadcast journalism, he worked as an insurance clerk.[tpl]cite book |title=Electoral district of Nelson: supplementary roll No. 2 of persons entitled to vote for the Members of Parliament in New Zealand |year=1951 |page=6[/tpl]
In the early 1960s, Blumsky was a broadcaster with station 2XN in Nelson.[tpl]cite news | url=http://photonews.org.nz/nelson/issue/NPN3_19610204/t1-body-d30.html  | title=Roving camera | date=4 February 1961 | work= Nelson Photo News | accessdate=7 August 2013 | page=42 [/tpl] He was also an active member of the Nelson Operatic Society at that time.[tpl]cite news | url=http://photonews.org.nz/nelson/issue/NPN23_19620915/t1-body-d7.html  | title="Where's Charley?" | date=5 September 1962 | work= Nelson Photo News | accessdate=7 August 2013 | page=9 [/tpl] He was a member of Johnny Shearer's skiffle group, alongside Cliff Whiting, in 1961.[tpl]cite news | url=http://photonews.org.nz/nelson/issue/NPN3_19610204/t1-body-d36.html  | title=Plenty of talent in Nelson | date=4 February 1961 | work= Nelson Photo News | accessdate=7 August 2013 | page=52 [/tpl]
In late 1962, Blumsky was transferred by the New Zealand Broadcasting Corporation to Dunedin, where he worked on both radio and the newly established television station DNTV2.[tpl]Cite episode |title= |episodelink= |url=http://www.radionz.co.nz/national/programmes/soundshistorical/20130804 |accessdate=7 August 2013 |series=Sounds Historical  |serieslink= |credits= |network=Radio New Zealand National |station= |city= |date=4 August 2013 |season= |seriesno= |number=560 |minutes= |time= |transcript= |transcripturl= |quote= |language=[/tpl] In Dunedin, Blumsky continued his involvement in amateur dramatics, playing Dr Lomas in the Southern Comedy Players' 1963 production of The Pohutukawa Tree, directed by the play's author, Bruce Mason.[tpl]cite book |last=Mason |first=Bruce |title=The Pohutukawa Tree |year=2004 |edition=revised |publisher=Victoria University Press |location=Wellington |isbn=0-86473-073-X [/tpl]
Blumsky ran for the New Zealand Parliament in the 1972 general election, standing unsuccessfully for the National Party against Tom McGuigan in the Lyttelton electorate. His son, Mark Blumsky, later became Mayor of Wellington and a National Party Member of Parliament.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.parliament.nz/en-nz/pb/debates/debates/speeches/48HansS_20051115_00001027/blumsky-mark-address-in-reply |title=Blumsky, Mark: Address in Reply |date=15 November 2005 |website=New Zealand Parliament |accessdate=7 August 2013[/tpl]
Following the crash of an Air New Zealand DC-10 into Mt Erebus on 28 November 1979, Blumsky gained prominence as the only broadcast journalist sent to Antarctica to cover the disaster for the world's media. He was also one of the pioneers of talkback radio in New Zealand.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.nzherald.co.nz/nz/news/article.cfm?c_id=1&objectid=10907792  | title=Broadcaster John Blumsky dies | date=3 August 2013 | work= New Zealand Herald | accessdate=7 August 2013 [/tpl] In December 1997, Newstalk ZB cut Blumsky's Sunday radio show, which he co-hosted with Mike Minehan.[tpl]cite news | title=Radio duo switched off | date=13 December 1997 | work= The Press | page=4 [/tpl]
In the 2001 Queen's Birthday Honours he was awarded the Queen's Service Medal for public services.Queen's Birthday Honours List 2001. Department of the Prime Minister and Cabinet. Retrieved 7 August 2013.
He died in Christchurch in 2013.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.stuff.co.nz/the-press/news/8999367/Erebus-reporter-John-Blumsky-dies-in-Christchurch  | title=Erebus reporter John Blumsky dies in Christchurch | date=3 August 2013 | work= The Press | accessdate=7 August 2013 [/tpl]
