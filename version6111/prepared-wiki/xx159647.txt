[[Governador Jorge Teixeira de Oliveira International Airport]]

CATEGORIES: Airports in Rondônia, Airports established in 1969

Porto Velho-Governador Jorge Teixeira de Oliveira International Airport [tpl]airport codes|PVH|SBPV[/tpl] also called Belmonte Airport referring to the neighborhood where it is located, is the airport serving Porto Velho, Brazil. Since July 3, 2002 the airport is named after Jorge Teixeira de Oliveira (1922-1987), first governor of the state of Rondônia.[tpl]Cite web | url=http://www.leidireto.com.br/lei-10481.html | title=Lei n˚10.481, de 3 de julho de 2002 | publisher=Lei Direto | language=Portuguese | date=July 3, 2002 | accessdate=April 13, 2011[/tpl]
The airport is operated by Infraero.
Some of its facilities are shared with the Porto Velho Air Force Base of the Brazilian Air Force.

==History==

The airport was opened on April 16, 1969 as a replacement to Caiari Airport, which was then closed. The airport is operated by Infraero since 1979 and in 2002 it gained international status.
