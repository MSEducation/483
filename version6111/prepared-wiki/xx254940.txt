[[John Mercanti]]

CATEGORIES: American sculptors, United States Mint engravers, Living people, University of the Arts (Philadelphia) alumni, 1943 births

John M. Mercanti (born April 27, 1943)Marquis Who's Who on the Web is an American sculptor and engraver. He was the twelfth Chief Engraver of the United States Mint until his retirement in late 2010.

==Biography==

Mercanti was born in Philadelphia. There, he attended the Pennsylvania Academy of Fine Arts, the Philadelphia College of Art and the Fleisher Art Memorial School. He also served in the Pennsylvania Army National Guard for six years.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.usmint.gov/about_the_mint/sculptor_engravers/index.cfm?action=mercanti |title=United States Mint Sculptors and Engravers: John M. Mercanti |publisher=United States Mint |accessdate=January 10, 2010[/tpl]
In 1974, Mercanti joined the United States Mint as a sculptor-engraver after working as an illustrator.[tpl]cite web |url=http://money.cnn.com/magazines/fortune/fortune_archive/2006/12/25/8396759/index.htm |last=Serdyukova |first=Anastasia |title=Money Maker: John Mercanti 63, Senior Engraver, U.S. Mint, Philadelphia |publisher=Fortune Magazine (December 25, 2006) via CNNMoney.com |date=January 19, 2007 |accessdate=January 10, 2010[/tpl] On May 19, 2006, he was appointed Chief Engraver of the U.S. Mint (also known as Supervisor of Design and Master Tooling Development Specialist).[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.coinbooks.org/esylum_v09n22a02.html |title=John Mercanti Elevated to Head of U.S. Mint Engraving Department |work=The E-Sylum |publisher=Numismatic Bibliomania Society |date=May 28, 2006 |accessdate=January 10, 2010[/tpl] The position had been officially vacant for 15 years following the retirement of Elizabeth Jones, the Mint's eleventh Chief Engraver, in 1991.
In June 2011, Mercanti became a paid spokesperson for Goldline International and appeared in a television commercial for the company.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.goldline.com/content-retired-us-mint-chief-engraver-be-spokesperson |title=Retired U.S. Mint Chief Engraver to be Spokesperson |publisher=Goldline International |date=June 28, 2011 |accessdate=June 29, 2011[/tpl]

==Work==

Mercanti has produced more coin and medal designs than any employee in United States Mint history (more than 100 as of 2006). Among these are the 1984 Olympic gold ten-dollar coin, the 1986 Statue of Liberty dollar coin, the 1989 Congress Bicentennial gold five-dollar coin, the obverse of the 1990 Eisenhower Centennial silver dollar, the obverse of the 1991 Mount Rushmore five-dollar coin, the obverse of the 1991 Korean War Memorial silver dollar, and the obverse of the 2005 John Marshall commemorative dollar. In addition to designing and sculpting a number of Congressional Gold Medals, Mercanti worked on quarters for the states of Arkansas, Iowa, North Carolina, Pennsylvania, and West Virginia for the 50 State Quarters Program.
