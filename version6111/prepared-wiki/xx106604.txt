Ed Sikov (born 1957, Pennsylvania) is an openly gay[tpl]citation |title=Ed Sikov: About |url=http://www.edsikov.com/about.html |accessdate=2007-08-24 |periodical=EdSikov.com[/tpl][tpl]cite book|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=C2QEAAAAMBAJ&pg=PA67&lpg=PA67&dq=ed+sikov+gay&source=bl&ots=5Vak_fWkOX&sig=0l_ZClT8xcKo3aR7kvDuq4d8Llc&hl=en&ei=lVHpTJjWEsH88AahlYnBDA&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5&ved=0CC4Q6AEwBDgK#v=onepage&q=ed%20sikov%20gay&f=false |title=The Advocate, Mar 2, 1999 |publisher= |date=1999-03-02 |accessdate=2013-12-04[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/11/08/AR2007110801971.html |title=The Washington Post, November 11, 2007 |publisher=Washingtonpost.com |date=2007-11-11 |accessdate=2013-12-04[/tpl] American film scholar and author. His books include Mr. Strangelove: A Biography of Peter Sellers (published in 2002), On Sunset Boulevard: The Life and Times of Billy Wilder (published in 1998), and Laughing Hysterically: American Screen Comedies of the 1950s'' (published in 1994).
Sikov was born in Natrona Heights, Pennsylvania, majored in English at Haverford College, and has a PhD from Columbia University; Sikov has since written eloquently of the difficulties he experienced as a gay student in the 1970s.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.haverford.edu/publications/fall99/lambda3.htm |title=Gay Life at Haverford, or the Virtues and Limitations of the Shoulder Check. ''The Campus Closet' |publisher=Haverford.edu |date= |accessdate=2013-12-04[/tpl] He has lived in New York City since 1979, and currently resides with his husband, Bruce Schackman.
Sikov's most recent book is a textbook – Film Studies: An Introduction; before that was Dark Victory: The Life of Bette Davis (Henry Holt, 2007). He recorded a commentary track for the Special Collector's Edition DVD of Billy Wilder's Sunset Boulevard, and is often called upon as a film expert for articles and documentaries, such as the 10-part American Cinema series produced by the New York Center for Visual History in association with the BBC and PBS. He has also written for the British and US editions of Premiere magazine as well as for Out, The Advocate, Connoisseur, Spy, Village Voice and Film Quarterly.
Critical reception to Sikov's books has been appreciative: film historian Jeanine Basinger finds him "a caring biographer" who is exhaustive in his research;The Real Margo Channing’s Fasten-Your-Seatbelts Life.
