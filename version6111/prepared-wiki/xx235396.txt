[[Ralph Eastwood]]

Lieutenant General Sir Thomas Ralph Eastwood KCB DSO MC (1890–1959) was a British Army General during World War II.

==Early life==

Thomas Ralph Eastwood was born on 10 May 1890 at Canterbury in the county of Kent in England.[tpl]cite web |url=http://muse.aucklandmuseum.com/databases/cenotaph/36267.detail |title=Cenotaph Record - Thomas Ralph Eastwood |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date= |website=www.aucklandmuseum.com |publisher=The Auckland War Memorial Museum  |accessdate=12 April 2014[/tpl] He was the second son of Captain (later Colonel) Hugh de Crespigny Eastwood of the King's Dragoon Guards who went on to distinguish himself in the Second Boer War, earned the Distinguished Service Order in 1902 and finished his military career as Inspector of Cyclist Units in 1918. Ralph's mother was Elinor, who married Hugh in 1887 and was the daughter of General John Hall Smyth.[tpl]cite web |url=http://angloboerwar.com/index.php/medals-and-awards/non-boer-war/80-awards/boer-war-period-medals/1884-distinguished-service-order?option=com_grid&gid=22_uw_0&p=15 |title=Medals and awards - Non-Boer War |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date= |website=angloboerwar.com |publisher=Anglo Boer War Website |accessdate=12 April 2014[/tpl] Elinor's sister was Ethel Smyth, the composer and militant suffragette.[tpl]cite web |url=http://home.zipworld.com.au/~lnbdds/home/smythethelgen.htm |title=Voyages In Time ~ The Family Vault - Ethel Smyth |last1= |first1= |last2= |first2= |date= |website= |publisher= |accessdate=12 April 2014[/tpl] Ralph's older brother Hugh became a Lieutenant Commander in the Royal Navy. Ralph was educated at Eton College from 1904 to 1908.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.unithistories.com/officers/army_officers_e01.html |title=British Army Officers 1939-1945 - Eagger, A.A. to Exham, R.K. |last1=Houterman |first1=Hans |last2=Koppes |first2=Jeroen |date=August 2005 |website=www.unithistories.com |publisher= |accessdate=12 April 2014[/tpl]

==Military career==

After leaving Eton, Ralph Eastwood was accepted into the Royal Military College, Sandhurst. He was commissioned into the Rifle Brigade (Prince Consort's Own) in October 1910.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=28346|supp=|startpage=1684|date=8 March 1910|accessdate=17 October 2009[/tpl] In November 1912, he was appointed Aide-de-Camp to the Governor of New Zealand, Lord Liverpool. He was released from this role on the outbreak of World War I, when he was commissioned into the New Zealand Expeditionary Force, later serving as a Captain in the New Zealand Rifle Brigade (Earl of Liverpool's Own). After participating in the Occupation of German Samoa, Eastwood left New Zealand with the Third Reinforcement in February 1915, arriving in Suez 40 days later. In April 1915, his battalion deployed to Gallipoli where he was awarded the Military Cross for his leadership of a column during a night assault on  6–7 August 1915. After service with the Egyptian Expeditionary Force his brigade was transferred to France, becoming a General Staff Officer with the rank of Major in October 1917.
Eastwood rejoined the British Army on 17 October 1918. and in 1919, served in the ill-fated North Russia Intervention as Brigade Major on Lord Rawlinson's staff. After staff duties at Aldershot, Cork and the War Office in London, Eastwood became an instructor at the Staff College, Camberley in 1928. Following a spell as Commanding Officer of the 2nd Battalion, The King's Royal Rifle Corps, he was appointed Commandant of the Royal Military College Sandhurst with the rank of Major General in 1938.Farahardupre
Eastwood served in World War II becoming General Officer Commanding of the 59th (Staffordshire) Infantry Division in the British Expeditionary Force until 31 May 1940, then taking command of the 4th Division. In October 1940, he was appointed to the new post of Inspector-General of the Home Guard, becoming Director-General of the Home Guard with the rank of Lieutenant General in November. In June 1941, Eastwood was appointed General Officer Commanding-in-Chief of Northern Command. He went on to be Governor of Gibraltar in 1944 and retired from the British Army in 1947.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=37969|supp=yes|startpage=2475|date=30 May 1947|accessdate=17 October 2009[/tpl] He accepted the largely honorary post of Colonel Commandant of 1st Bn the Rifle Brigade.[tpl]LondonGazette|issue=36973|supp=yes|startpage=1309|date=6 March 1945|accessdate=17 October 2009[/tpl] until 1951, when he became a Justice of the Peace in Wiltshire.

==Family life==

Ralph Eastwood married Mabel Vivian Prideaux on 21 April 1921; they had one son, Thomas Hugh Eastwood (12 March 1922– 25 October 1999), who was a composer.
