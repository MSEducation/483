[[Johnny Carson (American football)]]

CATEGORIES: 1930 births, 2009 deaths, Sportspeople from Atlanta, Georgia, American football tight ends, Georgia Bulldogs football players, Washington Redskins players, Houston Oilers (AFL) players, Eastern Conference Pro Bowl players, Players of American football from Georgia (U.S. state)

Johnny Richard Carson, Sr. (January 31, 1930[tpl]spaced ndash[/tpl]April 1, 2009) was a professional American football tight end for the Washington Redskins of the National Football League from 1953 to 1959.  He also played for the Houston Oilers of the American Football League during their inaugural season in 1960.  He played college football at the University of Georgia, where he was an All-American.  Carson was drafted in the fifteenth round of the 1953 NFL Draft by the Cleveland Browns.
