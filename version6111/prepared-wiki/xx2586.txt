Jacques-Louis David ([tpl]IPAc-en|ʒ|ɑː|k|'|l|w|i|_|'|d|ɑː|v|iː|d[/tpl]; [tpl]IPA-fr|ʒak lwi david|lang[/tpl]) (30 August 1748 – 29 December 1825) was an influential French painter in the Neoclassical style, considered to be the preeminent painter of the era. In the 1780s his cerebral brand of history painting marked a change in taste away from  Rococo frivolity toward a classical austerity and severity, heightened feeling[ref]Matthew Collings.
