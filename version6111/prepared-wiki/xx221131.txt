[[Here Lies Love]]

CATEGORIES: 2006 musicals, 2007 musicals, 2010 soundtracks, 2010 video albums, Albums produced by David Byrne, Art rock albums, Collaborative albums, Concept albums, David Byrne soundtracks, David Byrne video albums, Disco albums, English-language compilation albums, Fatboy Slim albums, Imelda Marcos, Off-Broadway musicals, Philippine culture, Rock musicals, Nonesuch Records soundtracks, Nonesuch Records video albums, Theatre soundtracks, Todo Mundo soundtracks, Todo Mundo video albums

Here Lies Love is a concept album and rock musical made in collaboration between David Byrne and Fatboy Slim, about the life of the former First Lady of the Philippines Imelda Marcos along with the woman who raised her—Estrella Cumpas—and follows Marcos until she and her family were forced to leave the Philippines.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.davidbyrne.com/here_lies_love/about.php |title=About "Here Lies Love"; Here Lies Love |publisher=David Byrne |date= |accessdate=2011-02-20[/tpl] The album features 22 guest vocalists and was released on April 6, 2010, under Nonesuch Records and Todomundo in several formats, including a deluxe double-Compact Disc set with a DVD of music videos from the album and a 120-page book.  The album was adapted as a rock musical for a 2013 premiere at The Public Theater.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.playbill.com/news/article/177074-Public-Theater-Announces-Second-Extension-of-David-Byrne-Fatboy-Slim-Musical-Here-Lies-Love|title=Public Theater Announces Second Extension of David Byrne-Fatboy Slim Musical Here Lies Love|date=2013-04-18|first=Adam|last=Hetrick|publisher=Playbill.com|accessdate=2013-04-20[/tpl] The musical was first produces at the Public Theatre in 2013.

==Background==

The title of the album is taken from a comment made by Imelda Marcos during a visit to her husband Ferdinand Marcos's embalmed body. Imelda Marcos expressed that she would like the phrase "Here Lies Love" to be inscribed on her tombstone.

==Recording and release==

The album features mostly guest female singers, including Florence Welch, Allison Moorer, Nellie McKay, Cyndi Lauper, Tori Amos, Martha Wainwright, Natalie Merchant, Sia Furler, Santigold, Charmaine Clamor, Nicole Atkins, and Kate Pierson among many others, alternately playing the roles of Imelda Marcos and Estrella Cumpas.
Byrne described Cyndi Lauper's performance on the album as "amazingly fine-tuned" and "very impressive." The only male vocalists are Steve Earle on "A Perfect Hand", and Byrne himself on "American Troglodyte" and "Seven Years", the latter being a duet with Shara Worden from My Brightest Diamond.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.spinner.com/2009/12/14/david-byrne-announces-upcoming-album-with-fatboy-slim/|title=David Byrne Announces Upcoming Album With Fatboy Slim|date=2009-12-14|first=Michael D.|last=Ayers|publisher=Spinner|accessdate=2010-04-01[/tpl]
The first promotional single from Here Lies Love is "Please Don't" featuring Santigold on vocals. The single became available on Byrne's website as a free high-quality MP3 download on January 19, 2010.

==Live performances==

Here Lies Love was performed live four times before the album was released. It was first presented as a song cycle at the Adelaide Festival of Arts in Adelaide, Australia, on March 10, 2006 with additional dates on March 13 and March 14, and was also performed live at the Carnegie Hall in New York City on February 3, 2007, as part of the Carnegie Hall Perspectives Series.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.davidbyrne.com/tours/index.php |title=David Byrne Tour Index |publisher=Davidbyrne.com |date= |accessdate=2011-02-20[/tpl]
