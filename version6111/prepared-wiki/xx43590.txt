[[George Swede]]

CATEGORIES: 1940 births, Living people, Canadian poets, English-language haiku poets, People from Riga, Canadian children's writers, Latvian emigrants to Canada

George Swede ([tpl]lang-lv|Juris Švēde[/tpl]), (born as Juris Puriņš, November 20, 1940 in Riga, Latvia) is a Canadian psychologist, poet and children's writer who lives in Toronto, Ontario. He is a major figure in English-language haiku, known for his wry, poignant observations.

==Life==

In 1947, Swede arrived with his mother and stepfather from post-WW II Europe to live with his maternal grandparents on a fruit farm in Oyama, British Columbia and, when his stepfather died in 1950, Swede moved with his mother to Vancouver where he finished junior high and high school.http://www.themercurypress.ca/?q=books/imagination_action, Swede, G. Tracks in the Sand: Why Do We Write? pp. 56-61 Then he studied at the University of British Columbia, where he graduated with a B.A. in Psychology in 1964. After that, he worked briefly as a psychologist at B.C. Penitentiary in New Westminster. In 1965, he got an M.A. at Dalhousie University.[tpl]fact|date=June 2012[/tpl] 
From 1966 to 1967, Swede was a psychology instructor at Vancouver City College, after which he worked as a school psychologist at the Scarborough Board of Education in Toronto until 1968. 
He resumed his academic career at Ryerson University, where he stayed as member of the psychology department from 1968 to 2006 (as chair from 1998 to 2003). From 1970 to 1975 he served as Director for Developmental Psychology at Ryerson Open College, a virtual university which broadcast lectures by radio (on CJRT-FM) and TV (CBC and CTV) from 1970 to 1975; and from 1993 to 2000 he was engaged in Ryerson University Now (RUN),Outreach initiatives build bridges and make university education accessible an initiative to get bright but disadvantaged students interested in going to university. This was achieved by enrolling Vaughan Road Academy studentsGood News At Vaughan Road Academy in a university level introductory psychology course that Swede taught. Most graduated and many received scholarships to attend university.
Swede retired in 2006 and was awarded Honorary Life Membership by the Canadian Psychological Association in 2007. For the 2008-2009 term, he was named the Honorary Curator of the American Haiku ArchivesHonorary Curator George Swede at the California State Library in Sacramento, California.
