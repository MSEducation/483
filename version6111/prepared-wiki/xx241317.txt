[[Sahrawi Football Federation]]

CATEGORIES: Football in Western Sahara

The Sahrawi Football Federation (FSF) ([tpl]lang-ar|الصحراوية لكرة القدم[/tpl], [tpl]lang-es|Federación Saharaui de Fútbol[/tpl]) is the governing body of football for Western Sahara, a territory that is disputed between Morocco and the Sahrawi Arab Democratic Republic. It was created on exile in 1989,[tpl]cite web|title=Team presentation -Group A- Western Sahara|url=http://www.vivaworldcup2012.com/team-presentation.htm|publisher=www.vivaworldcup2012.com|accessdate=2012-06-05[/tpl] and is based on the 27 February school, quarter 4, at the Sahrawi refugee camps in Tindouf province, Algeria.El-Area.com - Sahara Occidental [tpl]es icon[/tpl]

==International affiliation==

The Sahrawi Football Federation is not affiliated neither to the CAF neither to the FIFA. They were the first federation on being provisionally affiliated to the NF-Board in December 2003.Nouvelle Fédération-Board List Federations Affiliated to N.F.-BoardFedefutbol.net - Sahara Occidental [tpl]es icon[/tpl]
On March 25, 2012, Mohamed Moulud Mohamed Fadel, SADR Minister for Youth and Sports, announced the creation of the Sahrawi national football team, by decision of the own team players, stating that "the reason behind its absence of the African football competitions is due to its non-membership in the Confederation of African Football.".[tpl]cite news|title=Establishment of Saharawi national football team (Minister of Youth and Sport)|url=http://www.spsrasd.info/en/content/establishment-saharawi-national-football-team-minister-youth-and-sport|publisher=SPS|date=2012-03-25|accessdate=2012-03-27[/tpl]
