Christian Martin (born 1967) is a Vice President of Broadband Strategy and Development at A+E Networks.

==Early life==

Christian was born in New York City in 1967. His mother, Nancy Fales Garrett[tpl]dead link|date=September 2011[/tpl] is a playwright and school teacher. His father, Jared Martin,http://www.imdb.com/name/nm0552474/ is an actor, photographer and past creative director of the Big Picture Alliance.http://bigpicturealliance.org/who-we-are/history/ His paternal grandfather was Charles E. Martin,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pressherald.com/life/audience/cover-boy_2011-07-03.html |title=Cover boy | The Portland Press Herald / Maine Sunday Telegram |publisher=Pressherald.com |date= |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] the New Yorker Magazine cartoonist and cover artist. His maternal grandfather is the attorney Haliburton Fales II,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.amazon.com/Trying-Cases-Haliburton-Fales-II/dp/0814726712 |title=Trying Cases: A Life in the Law (9780814726716): Haliburton Fales II: Books |publisher=Amazon.com |date= |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] former President of The Morgan Library[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nytimes.com/1981/03/22/arts/haliburton-fales-named-head-of-morgan-library.html |title=Haliburton Fales Named Head of Morgan Library |publisher=NYTimes.com |date=1981-03-22 |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] in New York.

===Education===

He attended Saint Ann's School in Brooklyn, New York. He went to college at University of Michigan where he received a BA in 1989 and then to New York University where he received an MA in 1993.

==Career==

Martin has held the post of Vice President Broadband Strategy and Development for A+E Networks also known as Lifetime Networks since June 2008. At A+E, Christian is responsible for all Broadband, loosely described as video, where he and his team created all of the Project Runway[tpl]cite web|last=Emmrich|first=Stuart|url=http://runway.blogs.nytimes.com/2011/08/19/on-project-runway-judges-steal-the-show/ |title=On 'Project Runway,' Judges Steal the Show — NYTimes.com |publisher=Runway.blogs.nytimes.com|date=2011-08-19|accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] video extras including the Tim Gunn's Workroom and the Project Runway Buzz Room[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.digiday.com/stories/lifetime-digital-works-it-girl/|title=Lifetime Digital Works It, Girl|publisher=Digiday|date=2009-09-08|accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] an aggregation of all the internet chatter around Project Runway.
He worked for NBC News, mostly for Dateline NBC, from 1993 till 2006. He has also covered the 2000, 2002, 2004 and 2006 Olympics for NBC Sports — he won Sports Emmys for his coverage of the 2000, 2002http://www.emmyonline.tv/emmy/sportsceremony.html and 2004[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emmyonline.org/mediacenter/sports_26th_winners.html |title=The Emmy Awards - 26th Annual Sports Emmy Awards Winners |publisher=Emmyonline.org |date= |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] Olympics.
From 2003 to 2006 he was primarily Ann Curry's producer for NBC. Together they covered earthquakes in Pakistan,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/9979343/#.Tnyq6q6S57c |title=Inside Dateline: What we saw in Pakistan — Dateline NBC — msnbc.com |publisher=MSNBC |date= |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] the Tsunami in Sri Lanka, interviewed First Lady Laura Bush, Brad Pitt and Angelina Jolie,[tpl]cite web|last=Curry |first=Ann |url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/18833893/ns/dateline_nbc-straight_from_the_heart/t/straight-heart/#.TnysWK6S57c |title=Angelina Jolie on film, family, and the future — Dateline NBC - 'Straight from the Heart' - msnbc.com |publisher=MSNBC |date=2007-05-24 |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl] and the American hostage Thomas Hamill.[tpl]cite web|last=Curry |first=Ann |url=http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/5059171/ns/dateline_nbc/t/long-journey-home/#.Tnys1a6S57c |title=American hostage tells story of escape — Dateline NBC — msnbc.com |publisher=MSNBC |date=2004-05-26 |accessdate=2011-09-30[/tpl]
