==Early life==

Carey was born in Boise, Idaho. She went to Jackson Elementary, West Junior High, and graduated from Borah High School in Boise in 1993.[tpl]cite news|last=Moeller|first=Katy|title=Borah grad makes big-screen debut with 'The To Do List'|url=http://www.idahostatesman.com/2013/07/15/2656043/borah-grad-makes-big-screen-debut.html|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=Idaho Statesman|date=July 15, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Oland|first=Dana|title=Boise native Carey enjoys success with ‘The To Do List’ |url=http://www.idahostatesman.com/2013/08/10/2698485/boise-nativecarey-enjoys-success.html|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=Idaho Statesman|date=August 10, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Cohn|first=Gretta|title=For 'The To Do List,' Director Maggie Carey Goes Back To The Summer Of '93|url=http://soundcheck.wnyc.org/story/307033-do-list-maggie-carey/|work=The Soundcheck Blog|publisher=WNYC (New York Public Radio)|accessdate=15 November 2013|date=July 17, 2013[/tpl]  
In high school, Carey said, "I was in every AP class possible. I played a ton of sports. I was in student council. I was an all-American (soccer player)." Carey was also a lifeguard at the Borah pool.[tpl]cite news|last=McNary|first=Dave|title=‘The To-Do List’: Director Maggie Carey Goes Home in Aubrey Plaza Comedy|url=http://variety.com/2013/film/news/aubrey-plaza-to-do-list-maggie-carey-1200385759/|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=Variety|date=April 18, 2013[/tpl] 
Carey went to the University of Montana and graduated with a bachelor's degree in English literature. Carey played Division I soccer at Montana, and was co-captain of the team. She has a master's of fine arts in film production from the University of Texas, Austin.[tpl]cite web|title=Maggie Carey|url=http://www.ucbtheatre.com/talent/view/5683|work=UCB Theatre|accessdate=15 November 2013[/tpl] 
Carey was a member of New York’s Upright Citizens Brigade improv troupe.[tpl]cite news|last=Hall|first=Tim|title=Q&A with The To Do List’s Director/Writer Maggie Carey|url=http://blog.seattlepi.com/peoplescritic/2013/07/21/qa-with-the-to-do-lists-directorwriter-maggie-carey/|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=Seattle Post-Intelligencer|date=July 21, 2013[/tpl] Carey performed with both the Upright Citizens Brigade Theatre in New York and Improv Olympics West in Los Angeles.[tpl]cite news|last=Fallon|first=Kevin|title=Growing Up Maggie Carey: The writer-director and recovering overachiever checks 'debut film' off her to-do list.|url=http://mag.newsweek.com/2013/07/24/maggie-carey-on-making-the-to-do-list-hand-jobs-being-an-overacheiver.html|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=Newsweek|date=July 24, 2013[/tpl]

==Personal life==

She is married to comedian and former Saturday Night Live cast member Bill Hader.[tpl]cite news|last=Freydkin|first=Donna|title=On Bill Hader and Maggie Carey's to-do list: Togetherness|url=http://www.usatoday.com/story/life/people/2013/07/25/to-do-list-hader-carey/2517457/|accessdate=15 November 2013|newspaper=USA Today|date=July 31, 2013[/tpl] They met in Los Angeles, California. Carey said she went to college "with a guy in his sketch group. They didn't actually have a theater they were affiliated with - they put on shows in their friends' backyard. In one of the sketches, Bill was wearing overalls and no shirt. "I'm from Idaho, and that's a total turn-on. After the show, I was, 'Who is that funny guy in the overalls?" 
They have two children together, Hannah Kathryn, born October 6, 2009, and Harper, born July 28, 2012.[tpl]citation needed|date=November 2013[/tpl]
