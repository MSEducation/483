Harvard ethicist Louis M. Guenin describes the "kernel" of intellectual honesty to be "a virtuous disposition to eschew deception when given an incentive for deception.""Candor in Science", Synthese, Vol. 145, No. 2 (June 2005), p. 179.
Intentionally committed fallacies in debates and reasoning are sometimes called intellectual dishonesty.
