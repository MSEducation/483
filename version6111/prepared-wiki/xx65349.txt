[[Dee Dee Myers]]

CATEGORIES: 1961 births, Living people, White House Press Secretaries, Santa Clara University alumni, Clinton Administration personnel, People from North Kingstown, Rhode Island, CNBC people

Dee Dee Myers (born Margaret Jane Myers, September 1, 1961), a political analyst, was the White House Press Secretary during the first two years of the Clinton administration, from January 1993 to December 1994. She was the first woman and the second-youngest person to hold that position.
Myers later co-hosted the news program Equal Time on CNBC, and was a consultant on The West Wing. She was the inspiration for fictional White House Press Secretary C.J. Cregg. She is also the author of the 2008 New York Times best-selling book, Why Women Should Rule The World. In 2010, she became a managing director of public affairs at The Glover Park Group.

==Personal life==

Margaret Jane “Dee Dee” Myers was born in 1961 in Quonset Point, Rhode Island, to Stephen and Judy Myers. She spent most of her formative years in Valencia, California. Her father was a Navy pilot who completed two tours of duty in the Vietnam War, and her mother worked as a real estate agent.[tpl]cite news |title=Weddings; Dee Dee Myers, Todd S. Purdum |newspaper=The New York Times |date=May 25, 1997 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] Myers is a graduate of William S. Hart High School and Santa Clara University (1983).[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/dee-dee-myers |title=Dee Dee Myers |work=The Huffington Post |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl]
She met her husband, Todd Purdum, the national editor for Vanity Fair, former White House correspondent, and Los Angeles bureau chief for the New York Times, during the 1992 presidential campaign. The couple married in 1997, have two children and live in Washington, D.C.

==Political career==

Myers' career in political communications began shortly after graduating college, taking a full-time role on the 1984 presidential campaign of Walter Mondale.[tpl]cite news |title=In The Cage With The Beasts |first=Kim |last=Masters |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=April 1, 1993 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] She later served as a field representative for California State Senator Art Torres, and as assistant press secretary in the office of Los Angeles Mayor Tom Bradley and on his 1986 campaign for California governor. She was also a spokesperson for Michael Dukakis' 1988 presidential bid and Dianne Feinstein's 1990 California gubernatorial campaign, and served as campaign manager for Frank Jordan's 1991 run for San Francisco mayor.[tpl]cite news |title='Niceness' Issue May Be the Theme of SF Mayoral Race |author=Jerry Roberts |newspaper=The San Francisco Chronicle |date=November 9, 1991 |page=A4[/tpl]

===Clinton years===

Myers joined Bill Clinton's presidential campaign team in 1991[tpl]cite news |title=Departing Myers Recalls Jubilation, Tribulations; |first=Ann |last=Devroy |url= |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=December 23, 1994 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] and, following the 1992 presidential election, was named Clinton's first White House Press Secretary, a role she held from January 20, 1993, to December 22, 1994. She was the first woman to serve in that position, as well as being the second-youngest overall, having taken the job at the age of 31.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fashionweekdaily.com/the-fix/article/dispatch-jones-new-york-empowerment-breakfast |title=Dispatch: Jones New York Empowerment Breakfast' |first1=Alexandra |last1=Ilyashov |date=May 11, 2011 |work=Daily Front Row |accessdate=August 11, 2011[/tpl] Myers is sometimes cited as the youngest-ever White House Press Secretary, however Ron Ziegler, Richard Nixon's press secretary, was 29 when he assumed the role in January 1969.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.independent.co.uk/news/obituaries/ron-ziegler-730032.html |title=Ron Ziegler |first1=Rupert |last1=Cornwell |date=February 12, 2003 |work=The Independent |location=UK |accessdate=August 12, 2011[/tpl]
According to the Detroit Free Press, Myers earned a reputation for her "humour and patience" in handling the White House press corps, often delivering "quick one-liners" while issuing daily press briefings.[tpl]cite news |title=The Answer Woman Dee Dee Myers Is On The Front Lines |first=Angie |last=Cannon |newspaper=Detroit Free Press |date=June 21, 1994 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] Until September 1994, when she was given the rank of Assistant to the President,[tpl]cite news |title=Not Exactly All Shook Up; Myers Stays On in White House Restructuring |author=Ruth Marcus |coauthors=Ann Devroy |newspaper=The Washington Post |date=September 24, 1994  |page=A1[/tpl] she held the lesser title of "deputy assistant" and had a lower salary than previous White House Press Secretaries.[tpl]cite news |title=First woman to be press secretary gets title, little else |author=Joyce Price |newspaper=The Washington Times |date=January 23, 1993 |page=A4[/tpl] She also lacked consistent access to Clinton's inner circle.[tpl]cite news |title=Dee Dee Myers is a hero for our time |first=Christopher |last=Matthews |newspaper=Houston Chronicle |date=October 6, 1994 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] This was frequently noted in news coverage about Myers throughout her term, and is addressed in her 2008 book, Why Women Should Rule The World.[tpl]cite news |title=Pretty Complicated In Pink |first=Siri |last=Agrell |newspaper=The Globe and Mail |date=May 8, 2008 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl]

==Post-White House career==

After the Clinton administration, she became a political analyst and commentator, appearing on television and speaking at events on politics and the media.

===Television and popular culture===

In May 1995, Myers became the co-host of political talk show Equal Time on CNBC. She hosted the show opposite Mary Matalin initially,[tpl]cite news |title=Ex-Clinton staffer Myers to co-host Equal Time |first=Peter |last=Johnson |newspaper=USA Today |date= May 26, 1995 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] and later opposite Bay Buchanan.[tpl]cite news |title=Bay Buchanan To Voice Opinions On `Equal Time' |first=Tom |last=Jicha |url=http://articles.sun-sentinel.com/1996-07-11/news/9607110084_1_pat-buchanan-bay-buchanan-equal-time |newspaper=The Sun-Sentinal |date= July 11, 1996 |accessdate=August 12, 2011[/tpl] In 1997, she was the winning contestant on an episode of Celebrity Jeopardy!, beating Robert F. Kennedy, Jr. and Representative Jesse Jackson, Jr.[tpl]cite news |title=Dee Dee Myers wins celebrity Jeopardy! in Washington |newspaper=The Baltimore Sun |date=November 3, 1997 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] Myers has also appeared as a guest on other television programs, including The Today Show,[tpl]cite news |title=Dee Dee Myers Named to CSU Board |first=Pamela |last=Burdman |newspaper=The San Francisco Chronicle |date=March 27, 1999 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] Charlie Rose,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.charlierose.com/guest/view/4374 |title=Guest: Dee Dee Myers |work=CharlieRose.com |accessdate=August 13, 2011[/tpl] and ABC's Nightline.
Writer Aaron Sorkin recruited Myers to serve as a consultant and script advisor on the Emmy Award-winning TV series The West Wing, beginning in 1999. The character of C. J. Cregg (played by Allison Janney), the White House Press Secretary on the series, was partially inspired by Myers.[tpl]cite web |url=http://web.archive.org/web/20070927225407/http://www.austin360.com/tv/content/tv/stories/2006/05/14westwing.html |title='West Wing' |first1=Diane |last1=Holloway |date=May 14, 2006 |work=Austin American-Statesman |publisher=Cox Newspapers |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://politicalticker.blogs.cnn.com/2011/02/08/dee-dee-myers-talks-about-helping-aaron-sorkin-with-the-west-wing/ |title=Dee Dee Myers talks about helping Aaron Sorkin with the 'West Wing' |first1=Cody |last1=Combs |date=February 8, 2011 |work=CNN |publisher=Turner Broadcasting System |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] News articles have also reported that she may have served as the basis for the character of Daisy Green in the novel and film Primary Colors, played by Maura Tierney.[tpl]cite web |url=http://washingtonscene.thehill.com/party-events-pictures/archive/1449-reception-and-discussion-of-washington-post-white-house-correspondent-anne-kornbluts-new-book |title=Reception and discussion of Washington Post White House Correspondent Anne Kornblut's new book |first1=Kate |last1=Oczypok |work=The Hill |publisher=News Communications Inc. |accessdate=August 11, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Critic's Choice |first=Henrietta |last=Walmark |url= |newspaper=The Globe and Mail |date=June 6, 2008 |page=R28[/tpl]

===Commentator===

Myers has contributed to Vanity Fair since July 1995, originally as a Washington editor, and since 1997 as a contributing editor.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.vanityfair.com/magazine/bios/bio_myers|title=Dee Dee Myers  |work=Vanity Fair |publisher=News Communications Inc. |date=July 7, 2011 |accessdate=August 12, 2011[/tpl] She is the author of the 2008 New York Times best-selling book Why Women Should Rule The World, about women taking on positions of leadership and her own experiences in the White House.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=88404299 |title=Dee Dee Myers |first1=Michele |last1=Norris |work=NPR |date=March 17, 2098 |accessdate=August 13, 2011[/tpl] Her writings have also appeared in a number of other publications, including the Los Angeles Times,[tpl]cite news |title=In Pursuit of Traditional Power |author=Dee Dee Myers |newspaper=Los Angeles Times |date=June 6, 1999 |page=1M[/tpl] The New York Times, Time, The Washington Post, O: the Oprah Magazine, Politico and The Huffington Post. Since leaving the White House, Myers has also been invited to speak at numerous events, usually on the topics of women's issues, the media and politics.[tpl]cite news |title=Dee Dee Myers to be keynote speaker at Women of Influence event |newspaper=The Kansas City Star |date=April 21, 2006 |accessdate=August 9, 2011[/tpl] In 2011, she became a brand ambassador for Jones New York's Empower your Confidence campaign, and appeared as a model in advertisements for the brand.

===Public affairs consulting===

Myers joined the Glover Park Group in September 2010, as a managing director of public affairs.[tpl]cite news |title=Dee Dee Joins Glover Park Group |first=Betsy |last=Rothstein |url=http://www.mediabistro.com/fishbowldc/dee-dee-joins-glover-park-group_b20445 |work=Mediabistro |date=September 7, 2010 |accessdate=August 14, 2011[/tpl] Her role at the firm involves consulting with clients on communications strategy, marketing and reputation management.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.gloverparkgroup.com/our-team/dee-dee-myers/ |title=Dee Dee Myers |work=Glover Park Group.com |accessdate=August 14, 2011[/tpl] In January 2013 she was the spokeswoman for Paula Broadwell during the Petraeus scandal.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2012/11/20/us-usa-generals-broadwell-idUSBRE8AJ02620121120 |title=Broadwell hires Washington PR firm to deal with Petraeus scandal |publisher=Reuters |date=20 November 2012[/tpl]
