[[No Shouts, No Calls]]

CATEGORIES: 2007 albums, Electrelane albums, Too Pure albums

No Shouts, No Calls is the fourth album by English rock group Electrelane. It was released on CD and LP in 2007 by Too Pure.
No Shouts, No Calls is also their first album to be entirely digitally recorded and mixed.[tpl]cite web | last = Pollock | first = Jesse | title = Electrelane Interview | publisher = FecalFace.com | date = 8 March 2007 | url = http://www.fecalface.com/SF/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=534&Itemid=92 | accessdate = 8 February 2008 [/tpl] The group began writing material in Berlin's Planet Roc studios in the summer of 2006, at the height of the World Cup. During that period, the band became fans of the sport, going so far as to rearrange their recording schedules around the event and including a sample of a match between Hertha BSC Berlin and FC Moskva in the song "Five" of their new album.
In September and October, they were at Key Recording in Benton Harbor recording and mixing their album. In November, the band announced on their official website that they had finished recording and had titled their album No Shouts, No Calls. The album was released in on 23 April 2007 in Japan, 3 May 2007 in the USA and 30 April 2007 elsewhere. The first single, titled "To the East," was released on 12 March 2007. The album received generally positive reviews, with Heather Phares of Allmusic calling some songs "among the band's finest work";[tpl]cite web | last = Phares | first = Heather | title = No Shouts No Calls | publisher = Allmusic | year = 2004 | url = [tpl]Allmusic|class=album|id=r1030967|pure_url=yes[/tpl] | accessdate = 6 February 2008 [/tpl] detractors included
Leonie Cooper of The Guardian, who mentioned the band had a "penchant for turning every tune into a proggy wig-out."[tpl]cite news | last = Cooper | first = Leonie | title = Electrelane, No Shouts No Calls | publisher = The Guardian | date = 27 April 2007 | url = http://arts.guardian.co.uk/filmandmusic/story/0,,2066338,00.html | accessdate = 6 February 2008 [/tpl] James Reed of the Boston Globe picked the album as one of the best of 2007.[tpl]cite news | last = Reed | first = James | title = The Best CDs of 2007 | publisher = The Boston Globe | date = 16 December 2007 | url = http://www.boston.com/ae/music/articles/2007/12/16/james_reed/ | accessdate = 6 February 2008 [/tpl]
