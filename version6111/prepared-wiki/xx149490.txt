[[Pepe Pearson]]

CATEGORIES: 1975 births, Living people, American football running backs, Ohio State Buckeyes football players, San Francisco 49ers players, Jacksonville Jaguars players, Chicago Bears players, Cleveland Browns players, Detroit Lions players, Rhein Fire players, Tampa Bay Buccaneers players, Las Vegas Outlaws players

Pepe Pearson (born December 11, 1975) is a former American football tailback, starting in college for the Ohio State Buckeyes.

==Playing career==

A four-year letterwinner at Ohio State, Pearson is the Buckeye’s sixth all-time leading rusher (3,121 yards) as of 2008,[tpl]cite news|title=Ohio State–Illinois postgame notes|work=The Columbus Dispatch|date=2008-11-15|url=http://www.dispatch.com/live/content/sports/stories/2008/11/15/1115postgamenotes.html|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl] trailing only Archie Griffin, Eddie George, Tim Spencer, Keith Byars, and Chris Wells.  His 1,484 rushing yards as a junior is the sixth highest season total in team history.  With 17 touchdowns, Pearson is eleventh on the Buckeyes' scoring list.  Pearson's had a 173-yard rushing performance in Ohio State's 1996 victory over Notre Dame.[tpl]cite web|last=Helwagen|first=Steve|title=OSU's 1996 Win Evened Series With Notre Dame|publisher=Scout.com|date=2005-12-28|url=http://ohiostate.scout.com/2/483132.html|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl]
After completing his college career, Pearson spent time in the National Football League with the Chicago Bears and Pittsburgh Steelers.[tpl]cite news|title=Browns include former Ohio State standout Pearson among latest free agents signed|work=Sunday Times-Sentinel|agency=Associated Press|date=1999-01-10|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=kuxDAAAAIBAJ&sjid=4a8MAAAAIBAJ&pg=5304,374429|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Flynn|first=Jim|title=Better Later Than Never|publisher=Scout.com|date=2001-07-25|url=http://tam.scout.com/2/11915.html|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl]  His greatest professional success came in NFL Europe where he scored the game-winning touchdown for the Rhein Fire to win the 2000 World Bowl Championship.[tpl]cite news|last=Carlson|first=Mike|title=Fire Downs Claymores in World Bowl|work=International Herald-Tribune|date=2000-06-27|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2000/06/27/sports/27iht-bowl.2.t.html|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl]  He later spent time with the Dayton Warbirds, an expansion team in the National Indoor Football League.

==Coaching career==

After his playing career ended, Pearson began a career in coaching.  Since 2004 he has been the running backs coach for Ohio Dominican University.  In 2005 he was also the head coach of the Daytona Beach Hawgs of the NIFL,[tpl]cite news|title=Uf Women Continue Roll|work=Orlando Sentinel|date=2005-05-25|url=http://articles.orlandosentinel.com/2005-05-25/sports/0505250297_1_stetson-magley-ucf|accessdate=2011-02-18[/tpl] leading them to a playoff berth just before the team was disbanded. He was also the Head Coach for the now defunct Marion Mayhem of the Continental Indoor Football League (CIFL).
