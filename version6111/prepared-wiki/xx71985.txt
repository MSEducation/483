[[Józef Ignacy Kraszewski]]

CATEGORIES: 1812 births, 1887 deaths, Writers from Warsaw, Polish novelists, Polish writers, Polish historical novelists, Polish opinion journalists

Józef Ignacy Kraszewski (28 July 1812 – 19 March 1887)  was a Polish writer, historian and journalist who produced more than 200 novels and 150 novellas, short stories, and art reviews (including reviews of painters, e.g., Michał Kulesza[tpl]cite journal | title = Pejzaż, Michał Kulesza | journal = Tygodnik Petersburski | year = 1847 | first = Józef Ignacy | last = Kraszewski | volume = 18 [/tpl]). He is best known for his epic series on the history of Poland, comprising twenty-nine novels in seventy-nine parts.
As a novelist writing about Polish history, Kraszewski is generally regarded as second only to Henryk Sienkiewicz.

==Life==

Kraszewski was born in Warsaw and brought up in Romanów, Gmina Sosnówka, by his grandmother.http://univ.gda.pl/~literat/autors/kraszew.htm|biography 
He attended schools in Biała Podlaska (1822–26), Lublin (1826–27) and Świsłocz (1827–29).http://univ.gda.pl/~literat/autors/kraszew.htm|biography 
After 1829 he studied medicine and literature. From 1859 he was an editor at Gazeta Warszawska (The Warsaw Gazette) and in 1870 an editor at Tydzień (The Week).http://univ.gda.pl/~literat/autors/kraszew.htm|biography 
In 1883 he was arrested in Berlin, charged with collaborating with French military intelligence, and sentenced to three and a half years of imprisonment at Magdeburg Fortress.http://univ.gda.pl/~literat/autors/kraszew.htm|biography 
He died at Geneva in 1886.http://univ.gda.pl/~literat/autors/kraszew.htm|biography

==Adaptation==

In 2003 the first book in the series, Stara Baśń (An Ancient Tale, 1876), was made into a feature film by Jerzy Hoffman.
