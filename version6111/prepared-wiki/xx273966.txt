[[The Kegs Drive-In]]

CATEGORIES: Commercial buildings on the National Register of Historic Places in North Dakota, Commercial buildings completed in 1946, Novelty buildings in the United States, Root beer stands, Greater Grand Forks, Drive-in restaurants, Restaurants in North Dakota, Restaurants established in 1946, 1946 establishments in North Dakota

The Kegs is a historic drive-in located in Grand Forks, North Dakota.  The restaurant takes the form of two large wooden barrels.  The barrel structures were originally built in 1935 as part of a chain of seven walk-up root beer stands called The Barrel[tpl]cite news|last=Schuster|first=Ryan|title=The Kegs placed on national historic register|url=http://www.grandforksherald.com/event/article/id/215470/group/homepage/|accessdate=25 September 2011|newspaper=The Grand Forks Herald|date=13 September 2011[/tpl] by William Harry Muzzy. In 1946 the current drive-in came into being when the barrels were moved to their current location and joined by rectangular section housing the front counter.  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2011.[tpl]cite news|title=ND restaurant makes list of historic places|url=http://www.therepublic.com/view/story/4ab4ae84848044cbac5e8eba33714167/ND--Historic-Places-GF-Drive-In/|accessdate=25 September 2011|newspaper=The Republic|date=14 September 2011|agency=Associated Press|location=Columbus, OH[/tpl]
