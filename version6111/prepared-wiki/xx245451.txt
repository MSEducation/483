[[Sigbjørn Bernhoft Osa]]

CATEGORIES: 1910 births, 1990 deaths, People from Ulvik, Norwegian fiddlers, Spellemannprisen winners

Sigbjørn Bernhoft Osa (3 May 1910[tpl]spnd[/tpl] 2 February 1990) was a Norwegian fiddler and traditional folk musician. He was one of the best known Norwegian performers of folk music in the 1900s. 

==Personal life==

Osa was born in Ulvik, Hardanger as the son of fiddler and painter Lars Osa and children's writer Augusta Sophie Hermine Bernhoft. He spent his first years in Valle, Setesdal, and moved to Voss when he was six years old. He was married to Kersti Alice Grambo from 1937, and to Anne Heggtveit from 1950. He died in Voss in 1990.[tpl]cite encyclopedia|year=|title=Sigbjørn Bernhoft Osa |encyclopedia=Norsk biografisk leksikon|first=Bjørn |last=Aksdal |editor=Helle, Knut|publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget|location=Oslo|url=http://www.snl.no/.nbl_biografi/Sigbj%C3%B8rn_Bernhoft_Osa/utdypning |language=Norwegian |accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] 

==Career==

Osa's father was a skilled violin and Hardingfele player, and the boy started to play both instruments. He studied violin with Bjarne Brustad in Oslo, then in Bergen with the academy of Musikselskabet Harmoniens orkester from 1929 to 1930, and in Berlin from 1931 to 1932.
He made his début as violinist in 1937.[tpl]cite encyclopedia |title=Sigbjørn Bernhoft Osa |encyclopedia=Store norske leksikon |publisher=Kunnskapsforlaget |location=Oslo |url=http://www.snl.no/Sigbj%C3%B8rn_Bernhoft_Osa |language=Norwegian |accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] He started playing for the radio (Norwegian Broadcasting Corporation) in the 1940s, and recorded more than 350 traditional airs ([tpl]lang-no|slått[/tpl]), either on hardingfele or violin, between 1948 and 1978. His airs were often based on the Voss tradition, from elder fiddlers such as Sjur Helgeland, Ola Mosafinn or his own father. He often toured in Norway, Europe and the United States, with concerts or causeries.
He composed a concert for hardingfele, Three fjords, in cooperation with Geirr Tveitt. In the 1970s he cooperated with the rock group "Saft". He published the text book Hardingfela in 1952.
He became Horary Citizen of the U.S. state of Washington in 1968. He received the Arts Council Norway Honorary Award in 1976.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.kulturrad.no/om_oss/aeresprisen/ |title=Æresprisen  |publisher=Norsk Kulturråd (Arts Council Norway) |date= |language=Norwegian |accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl]  He also received the Spellemannprisen Honorary Prize.
