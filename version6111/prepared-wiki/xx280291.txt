[[Rubus aboriginum]]

CATEGORIES: Rubus

Rubus aboriginum is a species of dewberry, known as Aboriginal dewberry and Garden dewberry. Like other dewberries, it is a species of flowering plant in the rose family, related to the blackberry. It is found throughout the Southeastern United States and Northeastern Mexico.

==Description==

Rubus aboriginum is a bushy, viny bramble, up to [tpl]convert|6|ft|m[/tpl] in height and breadth, but often smaller. Branches appear 'hairy' when young, and become smooth as they mature, with infrequent, short, hooked thorns. Leaves are ovate, with serrated edges; flowers are white, have five petals, and are about [tpl]convert|1|in|mm[/tpl] in diameter. Fruits resemble other dewberries or small blackberries. 
It is very closely related to the northern dewberry, Rubus flagellaris,[tpl]cite web|title=Rubus aboriginum|url=http://www.biosurvey.ou.edu/shrub/rubu-abo.htm|work=University of Oklahoma Biological Survey|publisher=University of Oklahoma|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] and is sometimes treated as a subspecies.

==Distribution and habitat==

Rubus aboriginum is found throughout the Southeastern United States,  California, and Northeastern Mexico. It typically inhabits areas of rocky soil and partial shade, such as open woodlands and abandoned fields.
