[[Votkinsk Reservoir]]

CATEGORIES: Reservoirs in Russia, Reservoirs built in the Soviet Union, Geography of Perm Krai, Geography of the Udmurt Republic

The Votkinsk Reservoir ([tpl]lang-ru|Воткинское водохранилище[/tpl]), is a reservoir formed by the dam of the Votkinsk Hydroelectric Station on the Kama River in Perm Krai and Udmurtia, Russia. The reservoir filling took place in 1962-1964. The Votkinsk Reservoir has a surface area of 1,120 km² and a water volume of 9,4 cubic km. Its length is 365 km, major width - 9 km, average depth - 8,4 m. The Votkinsk Reservoir was created for the benefit of transportation and energetics. It also performs seasonal flow regulation. The cities of Perm, Krasnokamsk, Nytva, Okhansk, Osa, and Chaykovsky are located on the shores of the Votkinsk Reservoir.
