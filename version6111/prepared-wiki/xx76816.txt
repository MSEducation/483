[[Microsoft Studios]]

CATEGORIES: 2002 establishments in Washington (state), Academy of Interactive Arts & Sciences members, Companies based in Redmond, Washington, Companies established in 2002, First-party video game developers, Microsoft divisions, Video game companies of the United States, Video game development companies

Microsoft Studios is the video game production wing for Microsoft, responsible for the development and publishing of games for the Xbox, Xbox 360, Xbox One, Games for Windows, Steam, Windows RT and Windows Phone platforms. They were established in 2002 as Microsoft Game Studios to coincide with the release of the Xbox, before being re-branded in 2011. Microsoft Studios develops and publishes games in conjunction with first and third party development studios under their publishing label.

==Owned franchises and properties==

Also, despite having entered into the console gaming industry with the Xbox, they have nevertheless allowed their own franchises to appear on Nintendo's handhelds, including the Game Boy Advance and the Nintendo DS, since Microsoft has not currently entered the video game handheld industry. So far there are no games announced for the Nintendo 3DS.

===Game quality===

For 2010, the review aggregation site Metacritic gave the average score of Microsoft games as 73.4 (out of 100), making them the fourth highest-scoring major publisher, ahead of rival console manufacturer Sony but behind Nintendo.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/feature/game-publisher-rankings-for-2010-releases|title=Metacritic's 1st Annual Game Publisher Rankings|publisher=Metacritic|date=2011-02-09|accessdate=2014-02-13[/tpl] For 2011, their average score rose to 77.2, the highest for any major publisher, including Nintendo and Sony.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/feature/game-publisher-rankings-for-2011-releases |title=Metacritic's 2nd Annual Game Publisher Rankings |publisher=Metacritic|accessdate=June 21, 2012[/tpl] For 2012, they had dropped to second with an average of 73.0, just behind Electronic Arts.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/feature/game-publisher-rankings-for-2012-releases |title=Metacritic's 3rd Annual Game Publisher Rankings |publisher=Metacritic|accessdate=June 15, 2013[/tpl] For 2013, they were ranked sixth (behind both Sony and Nintendo) largely due to their failure to publish any games scoring 90 or above for the first time in four years.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.metacritic.com/feature/game-publisher-rankings-for-2013-releases|title=Metacritic's 4th Annual Game Publisher Rankings|publisher=Metacritic|date=2014-01-30|accessdate=2014-02-13[/tpl]
