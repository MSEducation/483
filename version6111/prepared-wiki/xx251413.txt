[[Kureepuzha Sreekumar]]

CATEGORIES: 1955 births, Living people, Malayali people, People from Kollam, Writers from Kerala, Indian poets, Malayalam-language writers, Malayalam poets, Recipients of the Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award

Kureepuzha Sreekumar (b. 10 April 1955 in Kureepuzha, Kollam, Kerala[tpl]cite book|last=Dutt|first=Kartik Chandra|coauthors=Sahitya Akademi|title=Who's who of Indian Writers, 1999: A-M|publisher=Sahitya Akademi|location=India|year=2001|isbn=978-81-260-0873-5|url=http://books.google.co.uk/books?id=QA1V7sICaIwC&pg=PA652&dq=Kureepuzha+Sreekumar&as_brr=3&cd=1#v=onepage&q=Kureepuzha%20Sreekumar&f=false|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl]) is a noted Malayalam poet of the post modern era. His books of poetry include Habibinte Dinakkurippukal, his first, published in 1984, Sreekumarinte Dukkangal, Rahulan Urangunnilla, Amma Malayalam,Keezhaalan and Suicide Point.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2008/06/15/stories/2008061553300300.htm|title= Kureepuzha has enriched poetry: Chullikkad  |date=15 June 2008|work=The Hindu|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl]
He won the Best Poet Award in 1975 from Kerala University, the 1987 Vyloppilli Award for Malayalam poetry, and the Kerala Sahithya Akademi Sree Padmanabhaswamy Award for 2003 for the best work in children's literature for his work Penangunni.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2004/02/10/stories/2004021001890500.htm|title=Award for Kakkanadan  |date=10 February 2004|work=The Hindu|quote=The following are the winners of the Akademi endowments for 2003: Sree Padmanabhaswamy Award, Rs. 2,500 — Kureepuzha Sreekumar (for the work, `Penangunni'); I. C. Chacko Award, Rs. 2,000 — E. V. N. Namboodiri (`Kerala Bhasha Charithram'); ....|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] He refused to accept the Sree Padmanabhaswamy Award as it was named after a Hindu God.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.hindu.com/2004/02/11/stories/2004021108600400.htm|title=Briefly: Poet rejects Akademi award |date=11 February 2004|work=The Hindu|quote=The noted poet, Kureepuzha Sreekumar, who won the Kerala Sahithya Akademi award for the best work in children's literature for his work Penangunni, has rejected the award. In a brief statement here today, Mr. Sreekumar said he was rejecting the award because it was named after a Hindu God, Sree Padmanabha.|accessdate=10 January 2010[/tpl] In 2011 he won the Kerala Sahitya Akademi Award (Poetry) for his work Keezhalan.
