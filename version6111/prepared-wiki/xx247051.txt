Jason Mark Rees (born 22 December 1969 in Aberdare) is a Welsh former professional footballer and Wales international.

==Club career==

Rees began his career with Luton Town before moving to Portsmouth in 1994 on a free transfer,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.independent.co.uk/sport/football-ins-and-outs-in-the-first-division-who-went-where-and-for-how-much-1383059.html |title=Ins and outs in the First Division: Who went where and for how much |publisher=The Independent |date=1994-08-12 |accessdate=2010-01-08 | location=London[/tpl] having spent the majority of the 1993–94 season on loan at Mansfield Town.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.independent.co.uk/sport/sporting-digest-football-1469670.html |title=Transfers |publisher=The Independent |date=1993-12-28 |accessdate=2010-01-08 | location=London[/tpl] He struggled to hold down a first team place at Fratton Park and was allowed to join Exeter City on loan during the 1996–97 season but a permanent move never materialised and he returned to Portsmouth until his release at the end of the season. After a spell with Cambridge United, he returned to Exeter City on a permanent deal in 1998.
Following his release by Exeter, Rees spent time on trial with Cheltenham Town,[tpl]cite news |url= http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/cheltenham_town/855411.stm |title=Rees still searching |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2000-07-28 |accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] eventually joining Tiverton Town.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://www.tivertontownfc.com/historypage.htm |title=Club history |publisher=tivertontownfc.com |accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl] He returned to The Football League several months later in December 2000 with Torquay United,[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/sport/football/2997985/December-Transfers.html |title=December transfers |publisher=The Daily Telegraph |date=2001-01-15 |accessdate=2010-01-08 | location=London | first=John | last=Ley[/tpl] where he spent two seasons before rejoining Tiverton in 2002. One year later, he returned to Plainmoor, captaining Tiverton Town as they won Devon Bowl for a record eighth time against his former club Torquay United.[tpl]cite news |url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/t/torquay_united/3113437.stm |title=Tivvy stun Torquay |publisher=BBC Sport |date=2003-07-31 |accessdate=2010-01-08[/tpl]
