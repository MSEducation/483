[[Nick Casey]]

CATEGORIES: 1953 births, Living people, West Virginia lawyers, West Virginia Democrats, American accountants, University of Kentucky alumni, West Virginia University College of Law alumni

Nick Casey is an West Virginian attorney and accountant. Casey is the former chairman of the West Virginia Democratic Party. He is running for Congress in 2014.

==Personal life and education==

Casey earned a B.S. in accounting from the University of Kentucky and a J.D. from West Virginia University.[tpl]cite web|title=G. Nicholas Casey, Jr.|url=http://www.lgcr.com/attorney_profiles/Casey,7.aspx|publisher=Lewis Glasser Casey and Rollins|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl] Casey is married to Mary Panzera Casey.[tpl]cite web|title=Conference Staff|url=http://www.catholicconferencewv.org/conference_staff.php|publisher=Catholic Conference of West Virginia|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

Casey has served as treasurer for Senator and former Governor Joe Manchin's campaigns, and also served as campaign treasurer for Mario Palumbo.[tpl]cite news|last=Kercheval|first=Hoppy|title=Nick Casey prepares for run in WV-2|url=http://wvmetronews.com/2013/04/30/nick-casey-prepares-for-run-in-wv-2/|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=MetroNews|date=30 April 2013[/tpl] Casey served as chairman of the West Virginia Democratic Party from 2004 to 2010. In 2008, Casey was a Democratic superdelegate.[tpl]cite news|last=Searls|first=Tom|title=W.Va. superdelegates hold firm|url=http://www.wvgazette.com/News/ElectionCentral/200805140862|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=Charleston Gazette|date=15 May 2008[/tpl] In 2009, Casey was recommended for a federal judgeship by West Virginia Senators Robert Byrd and Jay Rockefeller; Casey ultimately was not nominated.[tpl]cite news|last=Porterfield|first=Mannix|title=Nick Casey recommended for federal judgeship|url=http://www.register-herald.com/local/x546330689/Nick-Casey-recommended-for-federal-judgeship|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Register Herald|date=20 October 2009[/tpl] Casey was also a candidate to succeed Robert Byrd after the latter's death, but Governor Joe Manchin appointed Carte Goodwin instead.[tpl]cite news|last=Graham|first=David|title=Who Will Succeed Robert Byrd as West Virginia's Senator?|url=http://www.thedailybeast.com/newsweek/blogs/the-gaggle/2010/06/28/who-will-succeed-robert-byrd-as-west-virginia-s-senator.html|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Daily Beast|date=28 June 2010[/tpl]
Casey is the managing partner of Lewis, Glasser, Casey, & Rollins, a Charleston-based law firm.  Casey was elected treasurer of the American Bar Association in 2013, and previously served as president of the West Virginia Bar Association. Casey is also a certified public accountant.[tpl]cite news|last=Boucher|first=Dave|title=Nick Casey launches 2014 US House run|url=http://www.dailymail.com/News/statenews/201304300080|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=Daily Mail Capitol Reporter|date=30 April 2013[/tpl] Casey has served as the chairman of the board of St. Francis Hospital,[tpl]cite news|last=AP|title=Former Democrat chairman running for Congress|url=http://www.wvgazette.com/News/201304300017|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The Charleston Gazette|date=30 April 2013[/tpl] and has volunteered for Habitat for Humanity, Project HOPE, and Appalachian Service Project.

==2014 congressional election==

Casey is running in West Virginia's 2nd congressional district. The seat is open after Republican Shelley Moore Capito decided to run for the Senate.[tpl]cite news|last=Ali|first=Ann|title=Q&A: Nick Casey Prepares for Congressional Campaign Trail|url=http://www.statejournal.com/story/22988423/qa-nick-casey-prepares-for-congressional-campaign-trail|accessdate=13 August 2013|newspaper=The State Journal|date=1 August 2013[/tpl]
