[[Trimeresurus karanshahi]]

CATEGORIES: Animals described in 1997, Endemic fauna of Nepal, Reptiles of Asia, Trimeresurus

Trimeresurus karanshahi is a venomous pit viper species described from central Nepal in the Himalayas. No subspecies are currently recognized.[tpl]ITIS|id=635005|taxon=Trimeresurus karanshahi|accessdate=28 July 2008[/tpl]

==Etymology==

The specific name, karanshahi, is in honor of Mr. Karan Bahadur Shah, Professor of Herpetology and Mammalogy at Tribhuvan University in Nepal.Natural History Museum Tribhuvan University. nhmnepal.org/members.php.

==Geographic range==

Known only from the type locality given, which is "Central Nepal, Bagmati Province, Godavari village" (in the Himalayas).
