[[Temples (band)]]

CATEGORIES: English indie rock groups, Musical groups established in 2012, British psychedelic rock music groups, People from Kettering, 2012 establishments in England

Temples are an English psychedelic rock band, formed in Kettering, Northamptonshire in 2012, by singer-guitarist James Edward Bagshaw and bassist Thomas Edison Warmsley. They have received considerable press attention during their short existence and have been cited by Johnny Marr and Noel Gallagher as the best new band in Britain.[tpl]cite web|last=Gourlay |first=Dom |url=http://drownedinsound.com/in_depth/4146260-dis-meets-temples |title=DiS meets Temples |work=Drowned in Sound |date=2013-04-05 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl]

==History==

The band was initially formed as a home studio project[tpl]cite web|url=http://thequietus.com/articles/12841-temples-interview |title=Ascending The Golden Throne: Temples Interviewed |publisher=The Quietus |date=2013-07-16 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] in mid 2012. The duo had previously worked together in The Moons,[tpl]cite web|author=Martin |url=https://www.orangeamps.com/the-moons-bassist-tom-warmsley/ |title=The Moons - bassist: Tom Warmsley |publisher=Orange Amplification |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] with Bagshaw also having co-fronted another earlier Kettering band Sukie who formed in 2006 and charted at No 1 in the UK Indie Chart with their debut single "Pink-A-Pade".[tpl]cite web|url=http://sukie.musikear.com/ |title=Discography of Sukie |publisher=Sukie.musikear.com |date=2013-08-14 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] Sukie were featured in the fanzine "Siren", edited and created by Warmsley to document Kettering's music scene.http://www.bbc.co.uk/northamptonshire/content/articles/2007/02/16/sounds_siren_feature.shtml
The duo uploaded four self produced tracks to YouTube which caught the attention of Heavenly Recordings founder Jeff Barrett who agreed to release the band's debut single in the form of "Shelter Song" in November 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thisisfakediy.co.uk/articles/features/temples-you-never-know-where-a-song-is-destined-to-go/ |title=Temples: 'You Never Know Where A Song Is Destined To Go'|publisher=Thisisfakediy.co.uk |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl][tpl]cite press release|url=http://heavenlyrecordings.com/news/2012/10/temples-announce-debut-single-release-with-heavenly/ |title=Temples announce debut single 'Shelter Song' with Heavenly |publisher=Heavenly Recordings |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] They then enlisted fellow Kettering residents drummer Sam Toms (Secret Fix and ex-Koolaid Electric Company http://amusicblogyea.com/2013/06/29/gimme-your-answers-an-interview-w-secret-fix/) and keyboard player Adam Smith and started rehearsing as a band, so they could play their songs live, having never played live as a band before. The band released their second single "Colours To Life" in June 2013.
Having played major UK and European festival and supported the likes of Suede, Mystery Jets, Kasabian and The Vaccines[tpl]cite press release|url=http://heavenlyrecordings.com/news/2013/03/temples-announce-uk-dates-including-support-for-suede-kasabian-and-the-vaccines/ |title=Temples announce UK dates including support for Suede, Kasabian and The Vaccines |publisher=Heavenly Recordings |date=2013-03-14 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] alongside select headline dates, the band announced their first headline UK tour to take place in October 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.pressparty.com/pg/newsdesk/bigmouth/view/79104/ |title=TEMPLES announce UK headline tour, Autumn 2013 |publisher=Pressparty |date=2013-06-19 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] In November 2013, Temples announced their self-produced debut album, Sun Structures, which was released on 5 February 2014.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/temples/73909 |title=Temples announce details of debut album 'Sun Structures' |work=NME |date=2013-11-18 |accessdate=2013-11-29[/tpl] The band embarked on a headlining tour around Europe, North America and Australia."AU Review" Gemma Bastiani, The AU Interview: James Bagshaw of Temples (UK), The AU Review, February 10, 2014.
Their relative lack of airplay on major radio stations in the UK, especially BBC Radio 1, has been criticized by fellow musician and fan Noel Gallagher.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/noel-gallagher/73245 |title=Noel Gallagher criticises BBC Radio 1 for ignoring Temples and Jagwar Ma |work=NME |date=2013-10-16 |accessdate=2013-12-01[/tpl]
