[[Fred von Lohmann]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing (living people), Living people, Copyright activists, American lawyers, Stanford Law School alumni

Fred von Lohmann is an American lawyer practicing as a senior copyright counsel at Google.
Before joining Google in July 2010,[tpl]cite news| title= Tech champion, watchdog heads to Google |url=http://www.cnet.com/news/tech-champion-watchdog-heads-to-google/ |accessdate=4 April 2014 |publisher=CNET.com |date=23 June 2010 [/tpl] Fred was a senior staff attorney with the Electronic Frontier Foundation, specializing in intellectual property matters.  In that role, he has represented programmers, technology innovators, and individuals in a variety of copyright and trademark litigation. He was also involved in EFF's efforts to educate policy-makers regarding the proper balance between intellectual property protection and the public interest in fair use, free expression, and innovation.
Before joining EFF, Fred was a visiting researcher with the Berkeley Center for Law and Technology and an associate with the international law firm of Morrison & Foerster LLP. He has appeared on CNN, CNBC, ABC's Good Morning America, The NewsHour with Jim Lehrer, and Fox News O'Reilly Factor and has been widely quoted in a variety of national publications. Fred has received the California Lawyer of the Year Award,[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] the American Library Association's 2010 L. Ray Patterson Copyright Award[tpl]cite news| title= ALA announces 2010 winner of L. Ray Patterson Copyright Award |url=http://www.districtdispatch.org/2010/03/ala-announces-2010-winner-of-l-ray-patterson-copyright-award/ |accessdate=4 April 2014 |publisher=districtdispatch.org |date=30 March 2010[/tpl] and recognition as one of 2010's "25 Most Influential People in IP" by both The American Lawyer[tpl]cite journal |title=Most Influential People in IP |url=http://www.americanlawyer-digital.com/americanlawyer/ipfall2010/?pg=20#pg20 |journal= Intellectual Property |location=New York, New York |publisher=The American Lawyer |date=Fall 2010 |accessdate= 4 April 2014[/tpl] and Billboard[tpl]citation needed|date=April 2014[/tpl] magazines. Fred has an A.B. from Stanford University and a J.D. from Stanford Law School.
