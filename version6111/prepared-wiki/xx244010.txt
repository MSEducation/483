Joseph J. "Joe" Mullery (born June 1944) is a Minnesota politician and member of the Minnesota House of Representatives. A member of the Minnesota Democratic–Farmer–Labor Party (DFL), he represents District 59A, which includes portions of the city of Minneapolis in Hennepin County, which is part of the Twin Cities metropolitan area. He is an attorney by profession.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.leg.state.mn.us/legdb/fulldetail.asp?ID=10442 |title=Minnesota Legislators Past & Present – Legislator Record – Mullery, Joe |publisher=Leg.state.mn.us |date= |accessdate=July 20, 2010[/tpl]

==Early life, education, and career==

Mullery graduated from the University of Minnesota in Minneapolis with a B.A., and from the University of Minnesota Law School, where he earned his J.D. He has been a member of the board of governors of the Minnesota State Bar Association, of the governing council of the Hennepin County Bar Association, and of the legal panel for the Minnesota Senior Foundation. He has also been a community leader and organizer in the Camden Neighborhood of north Minneapolis for many years.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.votesmart.org/bio.php?can_id=13696 |title=Project Vote Smart – Representative Joe Mullery – Biography |publisher=Votesmart.org |date= |accessdate=July 20, 2010[/tpl]

==Minnesota House of Representatives==

Mullery was first elected in 1996 and has been re-elected every two years since. He previously served as chair of the Economic Development and International Trade subcommittees for Economic Development and for the Economic Development Finance Division for Government Efficiency and Reform during the 1997–1998 biennium.
