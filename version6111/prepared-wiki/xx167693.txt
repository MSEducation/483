[[Daisy Pearce]]

CATEGORIES: Women's Australian rules football, Women's Australian rules footballers, Living people, Year of birth missing (living people)

Daisy Pearce is an Australian rules football player in the Victorian Women's Football League.  She is current captain at the Darebin football club in the VWFL Premier Division, and in 2007 named in the All-Australian team.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.diamondvalleyleader.com.au/article/2007/07/25/18792_dvv_sports.html|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20070928203239/http://www.diamondvalleyleader.com.au/article/2007/07/25/18792_dvv_sports.html|date=25 July 2007|archivedate=28 September 2007|title=Daisy is starting to bloom|first=Eliza |last=Sewell[/tpl]  Daisy has been hailed a legend[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.leadernews.com.au/article/2007/06/13/16289_vwfl_story.html|date=13 June 2007|title=Not too young to be a legend|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20070928021110/http://www.leadernews.com.au/article/2007/06/13/16289_vwfl_story.html|archivedate=28 September 2007|first=Eliza|last=Sewell[/tpl] by locals as she leads the way for female football athletes in a sport dominated by males.
The Victorian Women's Football League player captained Victoria's under-19 side at the  2007 AFL Women's National Championships in July 2007.  The 19-year-old was named in the All-Australian team and was also applauded for her efforts during the week-long carnival with the joint-player of the tournament award. Daisy led the team to the Premiership in a 47 point win against South Australia.[tpl]cite web|title=AFL National Women’s Championships – Day Four|url=http://prod.www.gfc.cfour.com.au/Season2007/News/NewsArticle/tabid/3933/Default.aspx?newsId=47251|publisher=Australian Football League|accessdate=13 August 2011|date=16 July 2007|archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20070928042514/http://prod.www.gfc.cfour.com.au/Season2007/News/NewsArticle/tabid/3933/Default.aspx?newsId=47251|archivedate=28 September 2007[/tpl]
In June 2007 Daisy was one of two Victorian Women's Football League representatives in the E J Whitten Legends Game where she played again such football legends as Scott Cummings, Nick Holland, Mick Martyn and Nicky Winmar.[tpl]cite news|last=McClure|first=Geoff|title=Men v women: ratings winner|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/sport/men-v-women-ratings-winner/2007/06/07/1181089237042.html?page=fullpage#contentSwap1|accessdate=13 August 2011|newspaper=The Age|date=8 June 2007[/tpl]
On 15 May 2013 Daisy Pearce was the number one draft pick chosen by the Melbourne Demons Football Club in the first ever Women's AFL Draft Pick. Pearce will be one of 50 women to make history on 29 June when the Melbourne Demons Women's team take on The Western Bulldogs Women's team at the MCG as a pre game curtain raiser to the men's matches.[tpl]cite news|last=Pellizzeri|first=Teo|title=Pearce tops women's draft|url=http://www.theage.com.au/afl/afl-news/pearce-tops-womens-draft-20130515-2jn0c.html#ixzz2TScAxb10|accessdate=16 May 2013|newspaper=The Age|date=15 May 2013[/tpl]
