[[Dromia personata]]

CATEGORIES: Dromiacea, Crustaceans of the Atlantic Ocean, British Isles coastal fauna, Animals described in 1758

[tpl]Italic title[/tpl]

Dromia personata is a species of crab found in the North Sea, the Mediterranean Sea, and connecting parts of the northeastern Atlantic Ocean.[tpl]cite web |author=Cédric d'Udekem d'Acoz |date=February 20, 2003 |url=http://www.tmu.uit.no/crustikon/Decapoda/Decapoda2/Species_index/Dromia_personata.htm  |publisher=Tromsø Museum |accessdate=January 9, 2010 |title=Dromia personata (Linnaeus, 1758) |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20070625161731/http://www.tmu.uit.no/crustikon/Decapoda/Decapoda2/Species_index/Dromia_personata.htm |archivedate = June 25, 2007[/tpl] It grows up to a carapace length of [tpl]convert|53|mm[/tpl], and lives mainly from the lower shore to a depth of [tpl]convert|8|m[/tpl],[tpl]cite book |url=http://ip30.eti.uva.nl/bis/crustacea.php?menuentry=soorten&id=185 |title=Macrobenthos of the North Sea: Crustacea |author=M. J. de Kluijver & S. S. Ingalsuo |chapter=Dromia personata |publisher=Universiteit van Amsterdam[/tpl] or occasionally to [tpl]convert|100|m[/tpl], and often in caves. The last two pairs of legs are positioned dorsally, and are used by young crabs to hold a sponge in place as camouflage.
