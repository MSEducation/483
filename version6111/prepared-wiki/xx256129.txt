[[Ntabankulu Local Municipality]]

CATEGORIES: Local Municipalities of OR Tambo

Ntabankulu Local Municipality is an administrative area in the OR Tambo District of Eastern Cape in South Africa.
Ntabankulu is an isiXhosa name meaning “great or big mountain”, since the municipal area is mountainous in character.South African Languages - Place names
It was formerly part of the OR Tambo District Municipality, but was transferred to the Alfred Nzo District Municipality  after the 2011 municipal election.[tpl]cite web |url=http://mfma.treasury.gov.za/MFMA/Circulars/MFMA%20Circular%20No%2054%20-%20MFMA%20Circular%202011_12%20MTREF_FINAL.pdf |title=MFMA Circular No. 54: Municipal Budget Circular for the 2011/12 MTREF |publisher=National Treasury |date=10 December 2010 |page=5 |accessdate=23 March 2011[/tpl]

==Politics==

The municipal council consists of thirty-six members elected by mixed-member proportional representation. Eighteen councillors are elected by first-past-the-post voting in eighteen wards, while the remaining eighteen are chosen from party lists so that the total number of party representatives is proportional to the number of votes received. In the election of 18 May 2011 the African National Congress (ANC) won a majority of thirty-one seats on the council.
