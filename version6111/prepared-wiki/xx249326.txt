[[Amar Upadhyay]]

CATEGORIES: Indian male film actors, Actors in Hindi cinema, Indian television actors, Film and Television Institute of India alumni, 1976 births, Living people, People from Mumbai, Bigg Boss participants

Amar Upadhyay is an Indian model, film and television actor. The role which gave Amar his major breakthrough and is well known for is of Mihir Virani from the popular serial Kyunki Saas Bhi Kabhi Bahu Thi on Star Plus channel.[tpl]cite news |title= AMAR UPADHYAY Even today I'm called Mihir |url=http://www.screenindia.com/old/fullstory.php?content_id=8581 |work=Screen |date=16 July 2004 [/tpl]

==Early life==

Amar grew in Malad suburb of Mumbai, and studied chemical engineering after completing his schooling.He is a Black belt holder in Taekwondo.[tpl]citation needed|date=October 2011[/tpl]

==Career==

In 1994, some of his photographs appeared in Stardust magazine, which lead to Jaya Bachchan offering him the TV series Dekh Bhai Dekh. This was followed by numerous TV series like, Arzoo, Suhana Safar, Tulsi, Kalash and Aruna Irani's Mehndi Tere Naam Ki. Eventually, he became famous in 2000 with mega TV daily soap, Kyunki Saas Bhi Kabhi Bahu Thi (2000). In fact, when his character Mihir 'died' in 2001, it was followed by protest marches, the TV channel's head office was flooded with phone-calls and letters, eventually his character came back to life, and he is still recounted amongst TV superstars.[tpl]cite news |title=Who's the biggest superstar on TV?|url= http://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/Entertainment/Whos_the_biggest_superstar_on_TV/rssarticleshow/2294971.cms|work=The Times of India |date=  21 August 2007|first1=Deepika|last1=Sahu[/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Dead man walking: Amar Upadhyay|url=http://articles.timesofindia.indiatimes.com/2001-07-13/mumbai/27229406_1_mihir-virani-television-serial-kyunki-saas-amar-upadhyay |work=The Times of India |date=13 July 2001 [/tpl]
After his success in television he ventured into film with Dahshat 2003, followed by a big launch, Dhund – The Fog (2003), along with Aditi Govitrikar, directed by Sham Ramsay, though later he also appeared in J.P. Dutta's war movie, LOC Kargil (2003), his film career did not turn out as expected.
Subsequently he returned to television after a two-year hiatus, playing a key role in the serial Chand Ke Paar Chalo  on NDTV Imagine.[tpl]cite news |title=  Mystery in the mist: `Dhund – The Fog', a musical murder mystery..|url=http://www.hindu.com/thehindu/mp/2003/02/20/stories/2003022001380400.htm |work=The Hindu |date=20 February 2003 |location=Chennai, India[/tpl][tpl]cite web |title=Playing safe with Amar Upadhyay |url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2002/mar/15amar.htm |date=15 March 2002|publisher=Rediff.com Movies [/tpl][tpl]cite news |title=Part Deux |url=http://www.indianexpress.com/news/part-deux/396118/ |work=The Indian Express |date=9 December 2008 [/tpl]
In October 2011 he joined the fifth season of the reality TV series Bigg Boss and finished in fifth place.[tpl]cite web |url= http://biggboss.in.com/season-5-housemates/amar-upadhyay-63.html#top |title=Amar Upadhyay Bigg Boss Season 5 |work=biggboss.in.com |accessdate=11 October 2011[/tpl]
