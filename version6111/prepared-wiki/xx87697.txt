[[Fawkner railway station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations in Melbourne, Railway stations opened in 1889

Fawkner railway station is located on the Upfield line in Victoria, Australia. It serves the northern Melbourne suburb of Fawkner opening on 8 October 1889 as Fawkner. It closed on 13 July 1903, reopening on 12 December 1906 as Fawkner Cemetery, resuming the Fawkner name in 1914.Fawkner Vicsig
It is located adjacent to the Fawkner Crematorium and Memorial Park which opened in 1906, and from then until 1939 special mortuary trains ran.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fcmp.com.au/histrain.asp|title=Fawkner Crematorium & Memorial Park : Restored Mortuary Carriage|publisher=www.fcmp.com.au|accessdate=2008-08-24[/tpl] From October 1914 only seven trains daily ran to Fawkner, as well as the daily mortuary train to the cemetery.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.moreland.vic.gov.au/about-moreland/history-of-moreland/history-of-fawkner-moreland.html|title=History of Fawkner - Moreland City Council, Victoria, Australia|publisher=www.moreland.vic.gov.au|accessdate=2008-08-24[/tpl] A former mortuary van is situated next to Platform 2.
Until 1959 Fawkner was the extent of suburban electrified services, with an AEC railmotor used from 1928 until 1959 to provide a connecting service north to Somerton station.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.victorianrailways.net/photogallery/suburb/aecfawk.html|title=Fawkner|publisher=www.victorianrailways.net|accessdate=2008-08-24[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Somerton|work=Victorian Signalling Histories|publisher=Andrew Waugh|url=http://www.vrhistory.com/Locations/Somerton.pdf|accessdate=2008-01-22 [/tpl]
Until 1998 the station had one platform (current Platform 2). The second track and Platform 1 were added as part of the duplication of the line between Fawkner and Gowrie.
