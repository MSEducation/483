Mohammad-Ali Karimi Pashaki ([tpl]lang-fa|محمدعلی کریمی پاشاکی[/tpl], born on 8 November 1978), known as Ali Karimi,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.persianfootball.com/forums/showthread.php?108352-Ali-Karimi-Tractor-Sazi-2013-14|title=Tractor Sazi Fan Thread |publisher=PersianFootball.com|date= |accessdate=1 February 2014[/tpl] is an Iranian football player, who currently plays for Tractor Sazi in Iran's Premier Football League and played for 15 years for the Iranian national team. He was awarded the Asian Footballer of the Year in 2004 and made the second place in 2012 at the age of 34.  He is capable of playing on the wings, as an attacking midfielder or as a deep-lying forward. He is known for ball skill and dribbling runs. He is often referred to as the Asian Maradona[tpl]cite web | url = http://espnfc.com/columns/story/_/id/911809/john-duerden:-karimi-could-rise-to-the-top?cc=5901 | title = Karimi could rise to the top | publisher = ESPN FC | date = 26 April 2011 | accessdate = 17 June 2013 | first = John | last = Duerden[/tpl] and is believed to be one of Asia's greatest players.
Karimi began his career with Fath Tehran youth club and joined Persepolis in 1998. Internationally, Karimi has been capped 127 times since making his debut in October 1998, and has scored 38 goals. He has participated in five major tournaments including 1998 Asian Games, 2000, 2004, and 2007 Asian Cups and 2006 FIFA World Cup.

==Club career==

Karimi started his professional career with second division side Fath Football Club in 1997 where training was conducted on the streets with a plastic ball.

===Persepolis===

In 1998, Karimi joined Persepolis football club, which won the domestic double in 1999 and the championship again in 2000. He attended a trial with Perugia Calcio, but the transfer was aborted when Perugia deemed the transfer fee too high.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.worldsoccer.com/news/ali_karimi_news_55745.html |title=Ali Karimi |date=2 September 2003 |publisher=World Soccer |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] There was also reportedly an offer from Spain's Atlético Madrid who were willing to pay Karimi $5 million in a six-year contract,[tpl]citation needed|date=February 2012[/tpl] but Karimi remained at Al Ahli because he wanted to stay close to home.

===Al-Ahli===

Karimi signed with Al Ahli in the United Arab Emirates in 2001. In that season Al-Ahli managed to win its first trophy, when they became the Winners of the President Cup in 2001–02 season.
He scored a league-high 14 goals for Al Ahli in 2004.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/worldcup2006/stars/bios/karimi-ali.html |title=Ali Karimi – Iran |publisher=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl]

===Bayern Munich===

On 3 May 2005, Bundesliga champions Bayern Munich's website announced that they signed a one-year contract with Karimi who became the third Iranian after Ali Daei and Vahid Hashemian to try his luck with Bayern.[tpl]cite web|title=Bayern swoop for Iran star Karimi|url=http://www.fcbayern.t-com.de/en/news/news/2005/03939.php?fcb_sid=ca324b72903047818b273eabeb3a6f26|publisher=The official FC Bayern Munich website|accessdate=18 September 2006[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Iran star Karimi seals Bayern move|url=http://edition.cnn.com/2005/SPORT/football/05/03/germany.bayern/|publisher=CNN|accessdate=18 September 2006 | date=3 May 2005[/tpl] In October 2004, Iran hosted Germany in a friendly game in which Karimi played. The magazine kicker picked Karimi as the man of the match. FC Bayern's scouts studied Karimi in Dubai closer and came back with a recommendation not to sign the player due to doubts over his fitness. Felix Magath decided to go ahead with the signing despite the negative recommendations.http://www.iransportspress.com/news/127/ARTICLE/1933/2005-07-12.html[tpl]Dead link|date=February 2012[/tpl]
On 13 August 2005, Karimi made his starting debut for Bayern when Felix Magath put him on the starting line-up of a 5–2 victory against Bayer Leverkusen on the second day of Bundesliga, in which he played on the left side of the midfield. Karimi made an assist to Roy Makaay for Bayern's second goal followed by his first goal in the Bundesliga.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fcbayern.t-com.de/en/news/matchreport/2005/05137.php?fcb_sid=1216ecbab41ccabd667ddb9e537d8570 |title=Rampant Reds crush sorry Bayer |author=Chris Hamley |date=13 August 2005 |publisher=FC Bayern.com |accessdate=18 July 2010[/tpl] He came off in the 58th minute for Brazilian international Zé Roberto and received a warm ovation from the traveling Munich fans.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.dw-world.de/dw/article/0,,1685846,00.html |title=Bayern's Karimi Bids to Reverse Iran Trend |date=20 August 2005 |publisher=Deutsche Welle |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] Karimi scored on his first UEFA Champions League appearance against Rapid Vienna on 22 November 2005.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fcbayern.t-com.de/en/news/matchreport/2005/06294.php?fcb_sid=48f8bf83e376cb4c91f36a57891e2a43 |title=Rampant Reds book place in last sixteen |author=Chris Hamley |date=22 November 2005 |publisher=FC Bayern.com |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] On 17 December 2005, Karimi played for 88 minutes and scored a goal in Bayern's 2–1 win over Borussia Dortmund.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.fcbayern.t-com.de/en/news/matchreport/2005/06562.php?fcb_sid=48f8bf83e376cb4c91f36a57891e2a43 |title=Battling Bayern hold on for record win |author=Chris Hamley |date=17 December 2005 |publisher=FC Bayern.com |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl]
Karimi made his 25th appearance for Bayern on 4 March 2006 against Hamburg, which resulted in an automatic 12-month extension to his one-year contract that would expire on 30 June 2007. He unfortunately injured his ankle in the same game, which sidelined him for six weeks.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.the-afc.com/en/member-association-news/central-a-south-asia-news/5268 |title=Karimi injured, contract with Bayern renewed |date=10 March 2006 |publisher=Asian Football Confederation |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl]
In the 2006–07 season, Karimi was often kept on the bench. Karimi expressed his belief that it would be his last season with Bayern and his contract would not be renewed. It was confirmed that Red Bull Salzburg offered Karimi a contract, which he rejected.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iransportspress.com/?c=127&a=3808 |title=Ali Karimi: Contract Unlikely to be Renewed |date=14 November 2006 |publisher=ISP |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] Al-Ahli also made an official bid for him, which was eventually vetoed by Felix Magath as Munich's midfield was depleted by the sudden retirement of Sebastian Deisler.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iransportspress.com/?c=127&a=4040 |title=Karimi to fulfill Bayern contract after all as Al-Ahli withdraws offer |date=25 January 2007 |publisher=ISP |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] On 29 May 2007, Bayern Munich announced Karimi would be leaving the club, though his new club was not yet known.

==International career==

Karimi made his debut for Iran national football team in October 1998 in a match against Kuwait.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.teammelli.com/Roster/KarimiRoster.htm |title=Roster Ali Karimi |accessdate=8 July 2010 | publisher = teammelli.com[/tpl] That same year, he won the Gold Medal with Iran at the 1998 Asian Games. Karimi scored one of Iran's two goals in the final against Kuwait.
He was also one of Iran's main players in its 2002 FIFA World Cup qualification campaign. In 2001, after losing to Bahrain and drawing with Thailand, Iran finished second in their qualifying group and had to play a home-and-away play-off with the Republic of Ireland for a spot at the 2002 World Cup. Karimi started as an attacking midfielder in the first game in Dublin, and used his acceleration on the break and his dribbling to attack Ireland's defence. However, he was also off target on a number of occasions, and Iran lost the match 2–0. They won the return leg 1–0 but missed out on the final.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.persianfootball.com/news/sticky/EpAkyFFukFbHRFCqSe.shtml |title=Iran’s Karimi "on a high"  after finding scoring touch |date=16 August 2004|publisher=Persian Football |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl]
At the 2004 Asian Cup, Karimi scored five goals that made him joint top scorer. Iran finished third in the overall tournament, behind Japan and China. In the quarter final, Karimi scored a hat trick in Iran's 4–3 win over South Korea. His first two goals were headers and the third a finish on a low cross from Mehdi Mahdavikia. Karimi was given the prestigious Asian Player of the Year award in 2004, joining his fellow Iranians, Mahdavikia in 2003, Ali Daei in 1999, and Khodadad Azizi in 1996.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iransportspress.com/?c=118&a=1265 |title=Ali Karimi Voted Asian Player of the Year |date=7 December 2004 |publisher=ISP |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] He also won the 2004 West Asian Football Federation Championship with Team Melli.
His performance in the Iran-Germany friendly game for Bam victims held in Azadi Stadium in Tehran in October 2004 was said to be the reason behind Bayern's interest in the player.
Coming off a serious ankle injury, Karimi had a poor World Cup finals at Germany 2006.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iransportspress.com/?c=126&a=1574 |title=Ivankovic: "national team players deserve to be praised" |date=6 April 2005 |publisher=ISP |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] He was unable to exert much influence on proceedings as the Iranians lost 1–3 to Mexico and 0–2 to Portugal. He was substituted in the 65th minute of the Portugal game and kicked the team's sports bags by the dugout. Iran's then manager Branko Ivankovic said he may drop Karimi in the upcoming game due to disciplinary reasons.[tpl]cite web |url=http://sports.espn.go.com/espn/wire?section=soccer&id=2492515 |title=World-Karimi may be dropped for disciplinary reasons | first = Iain | last = Rogers |date=20 June 2006 |publisher=ESPN soccernet US |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] Karimi did not play in the 1–1 draw against Angola, but Ivankovic attributed Karimi's omission to his refusal to play because of an injury.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.thestandard.com.hk/news_detail.asp?pp_cat=19&art_id=21326&sid=8535057&con_type=1&d_str=20060623 |title=`Professor' studies his options after Iranians' fall | first = Annika | last = Breidthardt |date=23 June 2006  |work=The Standard |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl]
Karimi played in the 2007 Asian Cup. In May 2008, Karimi was banned from playing in the national team after he made criticisms towards the IRIFF in the Ardebil training ground,[tpl]cite web | url = http://www.iranproleague.net/news/Morenews.cfm?News=4024 | title = National team bans Ali Karimi | accessdate =26 May 2008 | date =  26 May 2008 | publisher = iranproleague.net[/tpl][tpl]Dead link|date=September 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] but has been reinstated in the national squad after intervention from Iranian President Mahmoud Ahmadinejad and Hassan Khomeini, the grandson of revolutionary leader Ayatollah Ruhollah Khomeini.[tpl]cite web | url = http://afp.google.com/article/ALeqM5isDSzbZv3Y-IEf0XM83EkJlPWS_g | title = Iran's Karimi reinstated after Ahmadinejad intervenes | date = 1 June 2008 | accessdate =26 February 2012 | agency = Agence France-Presse[/tpl]
In October 2008, Karimi retired from Team Melli. Ali Daei, the then Iran football coach, said Karimi will never be called up again.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tehrantimes.com/index_View.asp?code=179917 |title=Ali Karimi out for good, says Daei |date=13 October 2008 |work=Tehran Times |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] In February 2009, Karimi announced that he was ready again to serve his country, if he was to be picked by Team Melli coach, Afshin Ghotbi.
He played in 2010 FIFA World Cup qualification for Team Melli and on 10 June 2009 Karimi scored in a 1–0 victory over U.A.E. to keep Iran's 2010 World Cup qualifying hopes alive. On 17 June 2009, he played in Iran's 1–1 draw with South Korea, a result that eliminated Iran from the 2010 World Cup finals. During the match, a number of Iranian players, including Karimi, wore green wristbands, which were interpreted to be a show of support for Iranian opposition leader Hossein Mousavi, who accused the Iranian government of vote rigging the election on 12 June. Western media reported after the game that the players who wore the green wrsitbands have been banned from the team for life, while a newspaper in Iran reported that the players concerned have retired.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.nydailynews.com/sports/2009/06/24/2009-06-24_iran_soccer_players_banned.html |title=Iran soccer players banned from national team after wearing green pro-opposition wristbands |first = Andy | last = Clayton |date=24 June 2009 |work=Daily News|location=New York |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] On 24 June 2009, FIFA wrote to Iran's football federation asking for clarification on the situation. The Iranian football federation replied that no disciplinary action has been taken against any player.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.cbc.ca/sports/soccer/story/2009/06/26/sp-iran-green.html |title=Iran says it didn't punish soccer players |date=26 June 2009 |publisher=Canadian Broadcasting Corporation |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] On 3 March 2010, he returned for the 2011 AFC Asian Cup qualification match against Thailand.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.iransportspress.com/news/170/ARTICLE/9877/2010-03-03.html |title=Iran win to seal Asian Cup fate of Robson's Thailand [VIDEO |date=3 March 2010 |publisher=ISP |accessdate=8 July 2010[/tpl] Karimi was not picked to be part of Iran's 2011 AFC Asian Cup squad as Ghotbi decided to use younger players in his position.
He was invited to the national football team again by Carlos Queiroz in 2011 to play in the 2014 FIFA World Cup qualification and played for the team until his retirement in 2013. Queiroz later announced that Karimi is one of his best players during his coaching times. He is currently the third most capped player and the third highest scorer of Iran.
On 27 March 2014, Carlos Queiroz announced in an interview that Karimi can return to the national team and will be using Karimi in the 2014 FIFA World Cup, if he accepts.
