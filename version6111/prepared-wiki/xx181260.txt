[[Best of Intentions]]

CATEGORIES: 2000 singles, Travis Tritt songs, Billboard Hot Country Songs number-one singles, Songs written by Travis Tritt, Song recordings produced by Billy Joe Walker, Jr., Columbia Records singles

"Best of Intentions" is a song written and recorded by American country music singer Travis Tritt. It was released in June 2000 as the first single from his album, Down the Road I Go. The song reached the top of the Billboard Hot Country Singles & Tracks chart and peaked at number 27 on the U.S. Billboard Hot 100 chart. It also became Tritt's first Number One single since 1994, and the last Number One hit of his career.

==Content==

The song is a ballad in which the narrator talks about his best intentions which never materialized into the life he'd always planned to build for the woman he loves.

==Critical reception==

Deborah Evans Price, of Billboard magazine reviewed the song favorably saying that it is a "gorgeous ballad" and that "the song boasts a sweet melody and tender lyric." Price goes on to say that it is a "stirring anthem of devotion that will likely strike a chord with country listeners."Billboard, July 1, 2000

==Music video==

The music video for "Best of Intentions" was filmed at Tennessee State Penitentiary, where movies such as Marie, Ernest Goes to Jail, Last Dance and The Green Mile were filmed.A Prison Break

==Chart positions==

"Best of Intentions" debuted at number 62 on the U.S. Billboard Hot Country Singles & Tracks for the week of July 1, 2000.
