[[Klamath Agency, Oregon]]

CATEGORIES: Unincorporated communities in Klamath County, Oregon, Klamath, 1866 establishments in Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Oregon

Klamath Agency is an unincorporated community in Klamath County, Oregon, United States, on Oregon Route 62 where Agency Creek enters Crooked Creek.[tpl]cite book|title=Oregon Atlas & Gazetteer|publisher=DeLorme|year=1991|edition=1st|location=Freeport, Maine|page=30|isbn=0-89933-235-8[/tpl]
Klamath Agency was an Indian agency for the Klamath Indian Reservation established May 12, 1866, on the shore of Agency Lake.[tpl]Cite OGN|7th|pages= 540–541[/tpl] The current site of the former agency is [tpl]convert|3|mi|km|0[/tpl] north of that location. The Klamath Reservation was terminated in 1961, but the community at Klamath Agency still exists. Klamath Agency post office was established in 1878 and ran until 1965, when the mail was instead routed to Chiloquin.
In 1870 there was a sawmill at the agency; it burned down in 1911.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.ohs.org/education/oregonhistory/narratives/subtopic.cfm?subtopic_ID=278 |title= Subtopic : Putting Nature to Work: Running the Mill |author= Most, Steven |year= 2003 |accessdate= 2010-01-10[/tpl] In the 1890s, Klamath Agency was the site of two Indian boarding schools—one for boys and one for girls.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.wsulibs.wsu.edu/MASC/finders/pc124.htm |title= J. O. Lewis Photographs, PC 124 |work= J. O. Lewis Collection of Photographs of Native American Schools, ca. 1895-1900 |publisher= Washington State University |accessdate= 2010-01-10[/tpl]
In 1945, Ray Enouf Field was dedicated at the agency. The airfield was named in honor of the only Klamath Indian to die in World War II.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.history.navy.mil/library/online/indians.htm |title= Indians in the War: 1945 |publisher= United States Navy Historical Center |accessdate= 2010-01-10[/tpl] Raymond L. Enouf was a Marine private first class, who was killed while acting as a medic in the front lines during the Battle of Iwo Jima.
