Hunter Hart (born 11 March 1897, date of death unknown) was a footballer who played for Everton in the Football League for most of the 1920s. He was born in Glasgow and played mainly as a wing half.[tpl]Cite book |title=Football League Players' Records 1888 to 1939 |first=Michael |last=Joyce |publisher=Soccerdata |year=2004 |isbn=1-899468-67-6[/tpl] 
He started as a left-half but switched to the centre of defence before the end of his career. He was transferred from Airdrieonians for Â£4000 in 1922.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.evertonfc.com/history/everton-legends-e-h.html|title=Everton Heroes E - H|publisher=Everton FC|accessdate=27 July 2013[/tpl] Hart was made captain for a while[tpl]cite web|title=Hunter Hart - 1921 -1929|url=http://www.efchistory.co.uk/Player%20Gallery%201920-1939.htm|work=Gallery of Players Signed Between 1920 - 1939|publisher=The History of Everton Football Club|accessdate=18 May 2011[/tpl]  and helped the club to avoid relegation, and later was part of the Everton team that won the 1927-28 league championship. He retired just after Christmas during the 1929-30 season, when Everton were ultimately relegated.
In total he made 300 senior appearances for Everton (scoring on 5 occasions).
