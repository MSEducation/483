[[Miroslav Janů]]

Miroslav Janů (8 November 1959 – 24 January 2013) was a Czech football defender and later manager. As a player he played a total of 240 matches in the Czechoslovak First League, scoring five times.

==Playing career==

Janů started his football career as a youth player at TJ Dynamo České Budějovice, before moving to Slavia Prague at the age of 17.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.jihoceskyfotbal.cz/index.php?stranka=osobnost&id=16|title=Miroslav Janů|language=Czech|work=jihoceskyfotbal.cz|publisher=|date=|accessdate=8 February 2012[/tpl] He played 201 matches for Slavia in an 11-year spell. He went on to play for Bohemians Prague for two years before heading to Malaysia. In the 1992–93 season he returned to Czechoslovakia, playing four more games for Bohemians. He finished his career with a total of 240 games in the Czechoslovak First League, having scored five goals.
He played for the Czechoslovakia Olympic football team in some qualification matches for the 1988 Summer Olympics but the team didn't qualify for the tournament.

==Management career==

In May 2002, Janů was appointed as manager of Czech 2. Liga side Most, replacing Luboš Urban. He was given the position until the end of the season, with five games remaining.[tpl]cite news |url=http://fotbal.idnes.cz/fotbalovy-most-odvolal-trenera-urbana-dho-/fot_dsouteze.aspx?c=A020507_100631_fot_dsouteze_vis|title=Fotbalový Most odvolal trenéra Urbana|language=Czech|work=idnes.cz|publisher=Mladá fronta DNES|date=7 May 2002|accessdate=8 February 2012[/tpl]
Janů was appointed as assistant manager to Karel Jarolím at Slavia Prague in April 2005.[tpl]cite news |url=http://fotbal.idnes.cz/csaplar-skoncil-do-slavie-jde-jarolim-dwi-/fotbal.aspx?c=A050412_161532_fotbal_min|title=Csaplár skončil, do Slavie jde Jarolím|language=Czech|work=idnes.cz|publisher=Mladá fronta DNES|date=12 April 2005|accessdate=8 February 2012[/tpl] He left Slavia and moved to Indonesia in October 2006 to become the manager of Arema FC.[tpl]cite news |url=http://fotbal.idnes.cz/kouc-janu-miri-na-asijskou-misi-dmr-/fotbal.aspx?c=A061006_073836_fotbal_ot|title=Kouč Janů míří na asijskou misi|language=Czech|work=idnes.cz|publisher=Mladá fronta DNES|date=6 October 2006|accessdate=8 February 2012[/tpl]
His coaching adventure in Indonesia started in Persigo and then PSM.[tpl]Citation needed|date=February 2012[/tpl] He led PSM to second place in the 2004 Liga Indonesia Premier Division.
He joined Arema for a second time in the 2010–11 season, replacing Dutch winning manager, Robert Alberts. Arema finished the season in second place.
Retire at Persebaya Surabaya

==Death==

He died in Surabaya (Indonesia) on January 24, 2013 suffering a suspected heart attack.
