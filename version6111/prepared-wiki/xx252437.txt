[[Rome Arsenal]]

CATEGORIES: Houses on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Federal architecture in New York, Houses completed in 1813, Houses in Oneida County, New York, Rome, New York

The Rome Arsenal was a [tpl]convert|3|acre|m2[/tpl] fortification complex near Rome, New York. It was built in 1813, and used until 1873.
Rome Arsenal included barracks, arsenal, magazine, workshops, and other buildings, built to support American forces waging the War of 1812.  Major James Dalleba of Ordnance Department of United States Army supervised constriction[tpl]clarify|date=February 2014[/tpl] (not construction) of Rome Arsenal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6069|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Arsenal House|date=April 1973|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=E. Stevens Wright|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6070|title=Accompanying two photos[/tpl] 
Arsenal House, also known as Commandant's House, is a historic home located at Rome in Oneida County, New York. It is a [tpl]frac|2|1|2[/tpl]-story, brick structure on a stone foundation, [tpl]convert|30|by|36|ft[/tpl], in the Federal style. At a later date, 1-story wings were added plus front and rear porches.  It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1974.
