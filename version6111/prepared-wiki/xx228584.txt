[[Haliacmon (mythology)]]

CATEGORIES: Sea and river gods, Greek mythology

Haliacmon (or Aliacmon, Ancient Greek: [tpl]lang|grc|Ἁλιάκμων[/tpl]) was in Greek mythology a son of Oceanus and Thetys.[tpl]cite encyclopedia | last = Schmitz | first = Leonhard | authorlink = | title = Haliacmon | editor = William Smith | encyclopedia = Dictionary of Greek and Roman Biography and Mythology | volume = 2 | pages = 325 | publisher = Little, Brown and Company | location = Boston | year = 1867 | url = http://quod.lib.umich.edu/cgi/t/text/pageviewer-idx?c=moa;cc=moa;idno=acl3129.0002.001;q1=haliacmon;size=l;frm=frameset;seq=335[/tpl]  He was a minor river god in his own right, of the eponymous Haliacmon in Macedonia.Hesiod, Theogony 341Strabo, vii. p. 330  In other mythological traditions he was the son of Palaestinus and grandson of Poseidon.Plutarch, De Fluv. 11
