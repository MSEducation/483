[[Félix Pissarro]]

CATEGORIES: Pissarro family, 1874 births, 1897 deaths, People from Pontoise, French Jews, 19th-century French painters, French etchers, French caricaturists

  
Félix Pissarro (24 July 1874 – 29 November 1897), born in Pontoise, Paris, in the year of the first Impressionist exhibition, was a nineteenth-century French painter, etcher and caricaturist. Under the adopted pseudonym of Jean Roch,[tpl]cite book|last=Roth|first=Cecil|title=Jewish art: an illustrated history|year=1961|publisher=McGraw-Hill|isbn=|pages=581|url=http://books.google.cz/books?id=lEVAAAAAIAAJ&q=F%C3%A9lix+Pissarro&dq=F%C3%A9lix+Pissarro&hl=cs&ei=JUXKTqGAMZDy8QPZhoRw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=9&ved=0CE8Q6AEwCDgK[/tpl][tpl]OCLC|832856[/tpl] also known as Titi in his family circle, he was the third son of the painter Camille and Julie Pissarro.[tpl]cite news|last=Jones|first=Jonathon|title=Portrait of Félix Pissarro, Pablo Picasso (1881) |work=The Guardian| date=26 January 2002| issue=92| pages=4 |url= http://www.guardian.co.uk/culture/2002/jan/26/art|accessdate=21 November 2011[/tpl]
Félix's works very early demonstrated great strength and originality. His father regarded him as the most promising of his sons[tpl]cite book | title=Pissarro in West London (Kew, Chiswick and Richmond)| publisher=Lilburne Press | author=Reed, Nicholas | year=1997|edition=Fourth | page=46|isbn=1 901167 02 X[/tpl] but before he was able to realise his full potential, he contracted tuberculosis and died in a sanatorium at 262 Kew Road, Kew (which is now in the London Borough of Richmond upon Thames), at the age of 23. He is buried at the cemetery at Lower Grove Road, Richmond.
