[[The Fat of the Land]]

CATEGORIES: 1997 albums, English-language albums, Maverick Records albums, The Prodigy albums, XL Recordings albums, Warner Bros. Records albums

The Fat of the Land is the third studio album by British Electronic group The Prodigy. The album was released by XL Recordings on 30 June 1997 and on 1 July 1997 in the United States by Maverick Records. The album title comes from the old English phrase 'living off the fat of the land', which means living well or being wealthy.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.phrases.org.uk/meanings/235000.html|title=Living off the fat of the land|work=phrases.org.uk|accessdate=11 September 2009[/tpl] The album has sold over 10 million copies worldwide as of 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nme.com/news/the-prodigy/66719 |title=The Prodigy to release 15th anniversary edition of 'The Fat of the Land' | News |publisher=Nme.Com |date=19 October 2012 |accessdate=10 December 2012[/tpl]http://bloody-disgusting.com/news/3198375/prodigys-the-fat-of-the-land-to-get-15th-anniversary-rerelease/

==Reception==

The Fat of the Land has received critical acclaim. The album debuted at number one on the US Billboard 200 chart.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.allmusic.com/artist/p26871/charts-awards |title=The Prodigy |work=Allmusic |accessdate=12 September 2011[/tpl] The album has since gone double platinum, selling over 2 million copies in the USRIAA Certifications – Search 'Prodigy' In 1999, the album entered the Guinness World Records as the fastest-selling UK album and was also nominated for a Grammy Award.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.shahinbadar.com/ |title=Shahin Badar |work=shahinbadar.com |accessdate=12 September 2011[/tpl]
It has been featured in a number of music publication lists:
The album is included in the book 1001 Albums You Must Hear Before You Die. The album was also nominated for the 1997 Mercury Music Prize.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
In 2004, KISS co-founder Gene Simmons covered "Firestarter" on his second solo album, Asshole. "Firestarter" was also covered by Sepultura on their album Kairos, and by Jimmy Eat World on their Firestarter (EP).
The album also caused some controversy. The National Organization for Women objected to the seeming misogyny of "Smack My Bitch Up", though the band maintains that its true interpretation is "doing anything intensely".[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.rockonthenet.com/artists-p/prodigy_main.htm |title=Prodigy |work=Rock on the Net |accessdate=12 September 2011[/tpl]
