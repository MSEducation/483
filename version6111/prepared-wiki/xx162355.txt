Raymond Simon Lamanno (November 17, 1919 – February 9, 1994) was an American professional baseball player. A catcher, he appeared in 442 games played in the Major Leagues for the Cincinnati Reds (1941–1942; 1946–1948). The native of Oakland, California, stood [tpl]convert|6|ft[/tpl] tall and weighed [tpl]convert|185|lb[/tpl]. He threw and batted right-handed.
Lamanno's career was interrupted by World War II service in the United States Navy in the Pacific Theater of Operations.Baseball in Wartime.com
In [tpl]by|1946[/tpl], Lamanno was selected as a National League All-Star.
