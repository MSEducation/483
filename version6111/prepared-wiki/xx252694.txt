[[Cambarellus puer]]

CATEGORIES: Cambaridae, Endemic fauna of the United States, Freshwater crustaceans of North America, Animals described in 1884

[tpl]Italic title[/tpl]

Cambarellus puer, sometimes known as the swamp dwarf crayfish, is a species of crayfish native to Oklahoma, Texas, Louisiana, Illinois, Arkansas, Mississippi, Tennessee, and Kentucky.[tpl]cite web |url=http://iz.carnegiemnh.org/crayfish/NewAstacidea/species.asp?g=Cambarellus&s=puer&ssp= |accessdate=February 19, 2011 |publisher=Carnegie Museum of Natural History |work=Crayfish & Lobster Taxonomy Browser |title=Cambarellus (Pandicambarus) puer Hobbs, 1941 |author=James W. Fetzner, Jr. |date=December 1, 2010[/tpl] This species is often found in swamps, and also occurs in ditches, bayous, ponds, and slow-moving streams. It is listed as Least Concern on the IUCN Red List.[tpl]IUCN2010 |assessors=F. Alvarez, M. López-Mejía & C. Pedraza Lara |year=2010 |version=3.1 |id=153931 |title=Cambarellus puer |downloaded=February 19, 2011[/tpl]
