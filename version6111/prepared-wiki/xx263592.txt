[[Gambian presidential election, 2011]]

CATEGORIES: Elections in the Gambia, 2011 elections in Africa

Presidential elections were held in the Gambia on 24 November 2011.http://www.mherrera.org/elections.htm The incumbent Yahya Jammeh (in office since seizing power in a coup d'état in 1994) faced Ousainou Darboe of the United Democratic Party and Hamat Bah of the National Alliance for Democracy and Development.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-15851706 | work=BBC News | title=Gambia: Ecowas observers boycott 'unfair poll' | date=23 November 2011[/tpl]
Voting took place using marbles dropped into coloured containers each containing a gong.

==Monitoring and criticism==

The election was monitored by the African Union who praised the process,[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.voanews.com/english/news/africa/African-Union-Observers-Impressed-With-Gambia-Election--134467893.html |title=African Union Observers Impressed With Gambia Election | Africa | English |first= |last= |work=voanews.com |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl] European Union, Organisation of Islamic Cooperation and Commonwealth. The Economic Community of West African States (ECOWAS) did not send any monitors because of "an unacceptable level of control of the electronic media by the party in power... and an opposition and electorate cowed by repression and intimidation".[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.aljazeera.com/news/africa/2011/11/2011112574417929393.html |title=Gambia's Jammeh wins disputed elections - Africa - Al Jazeera English |first= |last= |work=aljazeera.com |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl]
Before the election Jammeh had claimed "I will never compromise peace and stability at the altar of so-called democracy",[tpl]cite news |url= http://latimesblogs.latimes.com/world_now/2011/11/gambians-vote-in-election-condemned-by-regional-body-ecowas.html |title=Gambians vote in election condemned as unfair - latimes.com |first= |last= |work=latimesblogs.latimes.com |year=2011 |quote='I will never compromise peace and stability at the altar of so-called democracy,' |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl] that "there is no way I can lose unless you tell me that all Gambian people are mad" and in response to press criticism said "The journalists are less than 1% of the population and if anybody expects me to allow less than 1% of the population to destroy 99% of the population, you are in the wrong place."
On the election day Bah claimed that he had not heard of any intimidation of his supporters[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.monitor.co.ug/News/World/-/688340/1278978/-/11kjbun/-/ |title=Ecowas on the spot over Gambia election - World |monitor.co.ug |first= |last= |work=monitor.co.ug |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl] although Darboe claimed the vote was fraudulent, rejected the result[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/world-africa-15897134 |title=BBC News - Gambia's Yahya Jammeh wins fourth presidential term |first= |last= |work=bbc.co.uk |accessdate=26 November 2011 |date=25 November 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url= http://www.freedomnewspaper.com/Homepage/tabid/36/mid/367/newsid367/7091/Gambia--Breaking-News-GAMBIAS-OPPOSITION-LEADER-REJECTS-POLL-RESULTS/Default.aspx |title=Welcome to Freedom Newspaper Online |first= |last= |work=freedomnewspaper.com |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url= http://news.xinhuanet.com/english2010/world/2011-11/26/c_122338427.htm |title=Jammeh re-elected president of the Republic of Gambia |first= |last= |work=news.xinhuanet.com |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl] and complained of intimidation from the presence of military vehicles on the streets.[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.telegraph.co.uk/news/worldnews/africaandindianocean/gambia/8913150/Gambians-go-to-the-polls-with-marbles.html |title=Gambians go to the polls with marbles - Telegraph |first= Aislinn|last= Laing|work=The Daily Telegraph |date= 24 Nov 2011 |publisher=TMG |location=London |issn=0307-1235 |oclc=49632006 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl]
The Independent Electoral Commission also said there was no intimidation[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.voanews.com/english/news/africa/west/Early-Results-Show-Gambian-President-in-the-Lead-134490253.html |title=Gambian President Wins Re-Election | West Africa | English |first= |last= |work=voanews.com |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl] and that "it is impossible to rig elections in Gambia".
There was also criticism of the election organisation as many voters went to the wrong polling station.

==Results==

The election was won by a huge majority by Yahya Jammeh[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.bloomberg.com/news/2011-11-25/gambia-s-jammeh-wins-fourth-term-with-71-of-votes-cast-1-.html |title=Gambia’s Jammeh Wins Fourth Term With 71% of Votes Cast - Bloomberg |first= |last= |work=bloomberg.com |accessdate=26 November 2011 |first1=Emily |last1=Bowers |first2=Suwaibou |last2=Touray |date=25 November 2011[/tpl] on an 83% turnout.[tpl]cite web |url= http://www.iec.gm/sites/default/files/pdfs/November_%2024th_%20Presidential_%20Results_2011_.pdf |title=Independent Electoral Commission The Gambia |first= |last= |work=iec.gm |year=2011 |accessdate=26 November 2011[/tpl]
