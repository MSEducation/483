[[Gmina Ojrzeń]]

CATEGORIES: Gminas in Masovian Voivodeship, Ciechanów County

Gmina Ojrzeń is a rural gmina (administrative district) in Ciechanów County, Masovian Voivodeship, in east-central Poland. Its seat is the village of Ojrzeń, which lies approximately [tpl]convert|13|km|mi|0[/tpl] south-west of Ciechanów and [tpl]convert|69|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] north-west of Warsaw.
The gmina covers an area of [tpl]convert|123.11|km2|sqmi|1[/tpl], and as of 2006 its total population is 4,393.

==Villages==

Gmina Ojrzeń contains the villages and settlements of Baraniec, Brodzięcin, Bronisławie, Dąbrowa, Gostomin, Grabówiec, Halinin, Kałki, Kicin, Kownaty-Borowe, Kraszewo, Łebki Wielkie, Lipówiec, Luberadz, Luberadzyk, Młock, Młock-Kopacze, Nowa Wieś, Obrąb, Ojrzeń, Osada-Wola, Przyrowa, Radziwie, Rzeszotko, Skarżynek, Trzpioły, Wojtkowa Wieś, Wola Wodzyńska, Zielona and Żochy.

==Neighbouring gminas==

Gmina Ojrzeń is bordered by the gminas of Ciechanów, Glinojeck, Sochocin and Sońsk.
