[[Pin Point, Georgia]]

CATEGORIES: Populated coastal places in Georgia (U.S. state), Unincorporated communities in Chatham County, Georgia, Unincorporated communities in Georgia (U.S. state), Savannah metropolitan area

Pin Point is an unincorporated community in Chatham County, Georgia, in the United States; it is located [tpl]convert|18|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] southeast of Savannah, Georgia. Pin Point is part of the Savannah Metropolitan Statistical Area.Pin Point, Georgia
The town is best known as the birthplace of Supreme Court Justice Clarence Thomas on 23 June 1948.[tpl]cite journal|journal=Washington Post|url=http://www.washingtonpost.com/wp-dyn/content/article/2007/04/21/AR2007042101475.html?hpid=moreheadlines |title=Justice Thomas's Life A Tangle of Poverty, Privilege and Race|first1=Kevin |last1=Merida |first2=Michael A.|last2= Fletcher|date=22 April 2007|accessdate=30 October 2011[/tpl]
A rural settlement founded by freed slaves after the American Civil War,[tpl]cite book|last1=Mayer|first1=Jane|last2=Abramson|first2=Jill|title=Strange Justice: The Selling of Clarence Thomas|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=uJfWAAAAMAAJ|accessdate=30 October 2011|date=6 November 1995|publisher=Plume|isbn=978-0-452-27499-0|page=33[/tpl] Pin Point is [tpl]convert|1.6|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] wide and [tpl]convert|2.5|km|mi|abbr=on[/tpl] long.  
Pin Point is a small, predominantly African American community that has a well-established group of Gullah speakers. The historic creole language of the Low Country was drawn extensively from West African languages, and it continues to grow, change and develop today.
