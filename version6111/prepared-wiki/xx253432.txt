[[A.D. Ovarense]]

CATEGORIES: Football clubs in Portugal, Association football clubs established in 1921, 1921 establishments in Portugal

Associação Desportiva Ovarense commonly known as simply as Ovarense is a Portuguese sports club from the city of Ovar, Aveiro.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.footballzz.co.uk/local.php?id=276 |title=Ovar|trans_title=|work=ZeroZero|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl] The club was founded on 19 December 1921. It currently plays at the Estádio Marques da Silva which they have played in since 1954. The stadium also plays host to the club's reserve and youth teams.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.footballzz.co.uk/estadio.php?id=45 |title=Marques da Silva|trans_title=|work=ZeroZero|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl] As a club it is well known for its basketball team who have won nineteen major trophies since there establishment in 1970.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.ovarensedolcevita.com/ |title=Notícias|trans_title=News|work=ovarensedolcevita.com|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl] The club currently competes in the AF Aveiro Second Division following a seventh place finish in the 2011–12 AF Aveiro Second Division season.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.footballzz.co.uk/edicao.php?id_edicao=25444 |title=AF Aveiro 2ª Divisão Série B 2011/12|trans_title=|work=ZeroZero|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl]
Ovarense was one of the founding members of the Aveiro Football Association along with Anadia, Beira-Mar, Bustelo, Clube dos Galitos, Espinho, Fogueirese, Paços Brandão, Sanjoanense, SC Oliveirense, Sociedade Recreio Artístico and UD Oliveirense.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.afaveiro.pt/?page_id=57 |title=HISTÓRIA|trans_title=History|work=afaveiro.pt|language=Portuguese| date=14 July 2012 |accessdate=14 July 2012 [/tpl] The association was founded on 22 September 1924. The club has played on various occasions in the Liga de Honra, Portuguese Second Division and the Portuguese Third Division.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.adovarense.net/index.php/historial |title=Histórico no Futebol|trans_title=Football history|work=adovarense.net|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl] There most recent Liga de Honra appearance came in the 2005–06 Liga de Honra season.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.footballzz.co.uk/edicao.php?id_edicao=1139 |title=II Liga 2005/2006|trans_title=|work=ZeroZero|language=Portuguese | date=18 July 2012 |accessdate=18 July 2012 [/tpl] Shortly after, serious financial problems led to the team's demotion to the fifth level of national football.
