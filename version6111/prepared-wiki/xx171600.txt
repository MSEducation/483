[[Lek Nana]]

CATEGORIES: 1924 births, 2010 deaths, Thai businesspeople, Thai Muslims, Thai people of Indian descent, Democrat Party (Thailand) politicians, Government ministers of Thailand, Alumni of Assumption College (Thailand)

Lek Nana (เล็ก นานา in Thai;  1924 – April 1, 2010[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bangkokpost.com/news/local/35431/|title=Lek Nana dies, 85|date=2 April 2010|work=Bangkok Post|accessdate=4 April 2010[/tpl]) was a Thai businessman and politician. One of the founders of Thailand's Democrat Party at the end of World War II, he served as Deputy Foreign Minister and as Minister of Science, Technology, and Energy."New Thai Minister of Science, Technology, and Energy appointed", Xinhua News Agency, 29 August 1985  A Muslim of GujaratiDubey (1990), p. 76 ancestry, he was a senior member of the Central Islamic Committee of Thailand. The Nana area on Sukhumvit Road derives its name from him.
Nana became Deputy Foreign Minister in 1975 under Prime Minister Seni Pramoj, losing his office in the military coup that followed the October 1976 massacre of leftist protesters at Thammasat University.
He served as an honorary consul-general for Iraq in Thailand until 1981. In December 1982, a powerful bomb exploded in his office building in Bangkok's Chinatown, killing a police bomb disposal expert, injuring 20 other people, and causing a fire that damaged five buildings. Nana was not in the office at the time."Thai bomb disposal expert killed in blast", United Press International, His office had formerly been the Iraqi consulate, and a connection to the Iran-Iraq War was suspected to have been the reason.The Associated Press, 2 December 1982
In 1982 Nana became secretary general of the Democrat Party under party leader Bhichai Rattakul. He also served on the party's executive board repeatedly."Sixth leader in the party's 57-yr history", The Nation, 20 April 2003
In 1985, Prime Minister Prem Tinsulanonda appointed Nana as Minister for Science and Technology, following the suicide of the previous minister, Damrong Latthapipat, said to have been displeased by the inadequate funding he had received.
Nana was nicknamed the "landlord of Bangkok" and  owned much property along Bangkok's Sukhumvit Road, especially in the area is now known as "Soi Nana" (Sukhumvit Soi 4). The name Nana is also used for the nearby Skytrain station, the Nana Hotel and the Nana Plaza entertainment complex.//www.nationmultimedia.com/home/2010/04/02/national/King-gives-soil-for-democrat-founder&039;s-burial-30126150.html
A philanthropist, Nana donated land for the headquarters of the Democrat Party, for the Princess Mother Memorial Park, and for a hospital.Princess Mother Memorial Park, accessed 7 August 2007 [tpl]th icon[/tpl]
Lek Nana died of a heart attack in a Bangkok hospital in 2010 at the age of 85. He was buried at Ban Somdej Mosque in Thonburi, with royal representation from HM King Bhumibol Adulyadej.
