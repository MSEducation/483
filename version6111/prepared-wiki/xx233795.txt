J.R. Celski ([tpl]IPAc-en|ˈ|s|ɛ|l|s|k|i[/tpl], born July 17, 1990) is an American short track speed skater and three-time medalist in the Winter Olympics. Celski holds the World Record in the 500 m (39.937) and is the first person to skate under 40 seconds in this distance.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.usatoday.com/story/sports/olympics/2012/10/21/jr-celski-sets-short-track-speedskating-world-record/1648375/|title=J.R. Celski Sets Short Track World Record|publisher=The Associated Press|accessdate=2012-10-21[/tpl] Celski also holds the Junior World Record in the 1000 m with a time of 1:25.304.[tpl]cite web|url=http://shorttrack.sportresult.com/Records.aspx?evt=11213100000008|title=Short Track Speed Skating Records|publisher=ISU[/tpl]
At the 2010 Winter Olympics, Celski won bronze in the 1500 m and the 5000 m relay. Five months before the Olympics, Celski suffered a gruesome injury when he fell during a race and his right skate blade gashed his left thigh and was uncertain if he would compete at the Olympics.[tpl]cite news|title = J.R. Celski poised for return after gruesome injury|last=Borzilleri|first=Meri-Jo|url=http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/olympics/2011004461_celski07.html|newspaper =The Seattle Times|publisher =|date = 2010-02-07|archiveurl = http://www.webcitation.org/5sP2awi50| archivedate = 2010-08-31| accessdate =2010-08-31[/tpl]
Celski's breakthrough performance was at the 2009 World Short Track Speed Skating Championships where he won five medals (two gold, one silver, two bronze).  Overall, Celski has won three gold medals, four silver medals, and four bronze medals at the World Championships.  He also has one bronze medal at the World Team Championships and four medals (two gold, two bronze) at the World Junior Championships.

==Appearances==

On Tuesday, February 9, 2010 J.R. appeared on the TV show The Biggest Loser where he introduced a pop-challenge to the contestants of the show. During the segment J.R. offered words of encouragement, and showed the contestants how to use a slide board.[tpl]cite news|title=J.R. ==Career==

===2009 World Junior Championships===

In the 1500 m, Celski won silver behind Um Cheon-Ho with a time of 2:16.780.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000026&gen=m&ref=5411&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Junior Championships, Canada – 1500 M Super Final – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] In his second event, the 500 m, Celski won with a time of 41.462 breaking the previous Junior World Record.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000026&gen=m&ref=5409&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Junior Championships, Canada – 500 M – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski finished third overall with 58 points.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000026&gen=m&ref=5407&view=ocl|title=2009 ISU World Junior Championships, Canada – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski then combined with Eduardo Alvarez, Jonathan Sermeno, and Robert Lawrence in the 3000 m relay to finish first with a time of 4:06.032.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000026&gen=m&ref=5412&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Junior Championships, Canada – 3000 M Relay – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl]

===2009 World Championships===

In the 1500 m, Celski placed third behind Lee Ho-Suk and Kwak Yoon-Gy with a time of 2:14.974.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000030&gen=m&ref=5459&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Championships, Austria – 1500 M – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] In the 1000 m, Celski placed third behind Lee Ho-Suk and Apolo Ohno with a time of 1:26.348.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000030&gen=m&ref=5451&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Championships, Austria – 1000 M – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] In his third event, the 3000 m, Celski won with a time of 4:48.444.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000030&gen=m&ref=5457&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Championships, Austria – 3000 M Super Final – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski finished second overall with 65 points.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000030&gen=m&ref=5449&view=ocl|title=2009 ISU World Championships, Austria – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski then combined with Ryan Bedford, Jordan Malone, and Apolo Ohno in the 5000 m relay to finish first with a time of 6:51.400.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210100000030&gen=m&ref=5452&view=dcl|title=2009 ISU World Championships, Austria – 5000 M Relay – Men |publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl]

===2010 Winter Olympics===

====Trials====

The U.S. Short Track Speed Skating Olympic Trials were held September 8–12, 2009 in Marquette, Michigan.  Celski finished second overall and was leading in points after the first two nights of the trials, but was injured during a crash in the semifinals of the 500 m race where his right skate sliced into his left leg; he did not skate in the second 1000 m races.  His injury required emergency surgery, 60 stitches, and five months of rehabilitation with the help of Eric Heiden.  Celski was unsure if he would ever compete again.[tpl]cite news|title = Speed skater J.R. Celski overcomes bloody injury to capture bronze|last=| first=|url =http://www.cnn.com/2010/SPORT/02/14/celski.bronze/index.html|newspaper =|publisher =CNN|date = 2010-02-14|archiveurl = http://www.webcitation.org/5t1BBZIzE| archivedate = 2010-09-25| accessdate =2010-09-25[/tpl] During the 1000 m time trial, Apolo Ohno, with a personal best of 1:24.500, came in second to Celski, who skated a personal best of 1:23.981.  Celski, along with Ohno, Jordan Malone, Travis Jayner and Simon Cho were the top five finishers at the trials. Afterwards, Ohno said about the nominated team: "This is the strongest team we've ever had.  I feel really good about how we will do in the next Olympics".[tpl]cite news|title = Skater Celski injured in crash at short track Olympic trials|last=| first=|url =http://www.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/2009-09-12-short-track-speedskating_N.htm|newspaper =USA Today|publisher =|date = 2009-09-16|archiveurl = http://www.webcitation.org/5t1BbsdJO| archivedate = 2010-09-25| accessdate =2010-09-25[/tpl]

====Games====

At the 2010 Winter Olympics in Vancouver, Celski won a pair of bronze medals.  One in the 1500 m and another in the 5000 m relay.  In the 1500 m final, Celski won the bronze medal after 2006 silver medalist Lee Ho-Suk crashed into fellow Korean skater Sung Si-Bak during the final turn of the last lap.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/winter/2010/STK/mens-1500-metres-final-round.html|title=Short Track Speed Skating at the 2010 Vancouver Winter Games: Men's 1,500 metres Final Round|publisher = Sports Reference LLC|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski was in fifth place leading into the crash and as a result moved into third place.  Apolo Ohno moved into second place.[tpl]cite news|title=JR Celski Wins Bronze Medal in Men's 1500 Short Track Olympics Skating|url=http://media-newswire.com/release_1112382.html|publisher=Media-Newswire.com|author=|date=2010-02-13|accessdate=2010-02-13[/tpl] Celski was disqualified in the 1000 m semifinals after knocking Canadian skater Francois Hamelin to the ground.[tpl]cite news|title=Fil-Am speed skater Celski disqualified in Winter Olympics event|url=http://www.abs-cbnnews.com/sports/02/22/10/fil-am-speed-skater-celski-disqualified-winter-olympics-event|publisher=ABS–CBN Corporation|author=|date=2010-02-22|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] The 5000 m relay team for the United States finished with the bronze medal.  The team, consisting of Apolo Ohno, Simon Cho, Travis Jayner, Jordan Malone and Celski, was in the fourth position for the majority of the race.[tpl]cite news | first=Steve | last=Kelley | title=Ohno helps U.S. take bronze in 5,000 relay | date=2010-02-27 | url =http://seattletimes.nwsource.com/html/stevekelley/2011204889_kelley27.html | work =The Seattle Times | accessdate = 2010-02-28[/tpl] With a strong push from Celski with two laps to go, Ohno as the anchor leg was able to pass the Chinese team for third place; Canada won the gold and South Korea took silver.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/winter/2010/STK/mens-5000-metres-relay-final-round.html|title=Short Track Speed Skating at the 2010 Vancouver Winter Games: Men's 5,000 metres Relay Final Round|publisher = Sports Reference LLC|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl]

===2010 World Championships===

Celski advanced to finals of the 1500 m after winning 1st place in the heats, quarterfinals, and semifinals. During the finals, Celski was in the lead with about five laps to go, but in the last few laps, the three Korean skaters managed to overtake him, sweeping the podium with Kwak Yoon-Gy in 1st. Celski finished in 4th place, in front of teammate Travis Jayner, who came in 6th.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6407&view=dcl|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – 1500 m – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] The next day, Celski competed in the 500 m. During the semifinals, Celski attempted a pass from fourth to third place, but during his pass, François-Louis Tremblay of Canada, in second place at the time, fell, tripping Celski on his way down. Celski finished the race in 3rd, but was then disqualified, while Tremblay advanced.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6849&view=rou|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – 500 m – Men (semifinals)|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] In the 1000 m, Celski finished third behind Lee Ho-Suk and Kwak Yoon-Gy with a time of 1:27.515.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6399&view=dcl|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – 1000 m – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] In the 3000 m, Celski again placed third behind Lee Ho-Suk and Kwak Yoon-Gy.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6872&view=rou|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – 3000 m – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski finished fourth overall with 39 points.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6397&view=ocl|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl] Celski then combined with Jordan Malone, Travis Jayner, and Simon Cho in the 5000 m relay to finish second behind South Korea with a time of 6:46.205.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportresult.com/federations/ISU/ShortTrack/Results.aspx?evt=11210200000007&gen=m&ref=6400&view=dcl|title=2010 ISU World Championships, BUL, Sofia – 5000 m Relay – Men|publisher = ISU|accessdate=2010-06-27[/tpl]
