[[Wind power in Belgium]]

CATEGORIES: Wind power in Belgium

Wind power in Belgium depends partially on regional governments (Brussels-Capital Region, Flemish Region, Walloon Region)  and partially on the Belgian federal government. Wind energy producers in both the Flemish and Walloon regions get green certificates but not with the same  conditions.
On February 8, 2014, due to high winds, 16% of the consumed electricity was generated by wind turbines.[tpl]cite news|title=Nieuwe records voor windenergie door winderige winter|url=http://www.standaard.be/cnt/dmf20140210_00972781|accessdate=9 April 2014|newspaper=De Standaard|date=10-2-2014[/tpl] At the start of 2012, there were 498 operational wind turbines in Belgium, with a capacity of 1080 MW.Environ 500 éoliennes en Belgique, Lalibre.be, 11 janvier 2012 The amount of electricity generated from wind energy has surpassed 2 TWh per year.

==Background==

The Belgian State committed to generate 13% of its energy (i.e. electricity and heating) from sustainable sources by 2020. The annual wind energy potential in Belgium is estimated at 8.2 TWh (5.4 TWh offshore, 2.8 TWh onshore). This requires a production capacity of 2.1 GW offshore and 1.1 GW onshore. Wind energy makes up 47% of the 17 TWh renewable energy potential by 2020, according to the GEMIX report. In 2008, Belgium consumed 88 TWh of electricity (excluding electricity that was not transported on the high voltage grid).
Wind turbines are mainly installed offshore and in the Flemish and Walloon Regions. Brussels-Capital Region is an urban area which is not particularly suited for large wind turbines. Smaller turbines more appropriate for urban environments are being studied but until today no technology is deemed sufficiently efficient.[tpl]cite web|title=Windturbines|url=http://documentatie.leefmilieubrussel.be/documents/IF_Energie_ER11_part_NL.PDF|publisher=Brussels Instituut voor Milieubeheer[/tpl]
The expanding capacity and the intermittent nature of wind power creates challenges for grid operator Elia. Requiring accurate wind forecasts[tpl]cite web|title=Forecast and actual wind power generation|url=http://www.elia.be/en/grid-data/power-generation/~/media/files/Elia/Grid-data/Power%20Generation/WindForecasting_EN.pdf|publisher=Elia[/tpl] and infrastructure changes. In particular transporting the large amounts of electricity generated offshore inland required work. Currently the construction of various 150 kV connections has prepared the electricity grid to connect up 900 MW of offshore wind turbines. By 2016 the Stevin Project aims to expand this further by constructing new high voltage substations in Zeebrugge and Zomergem and a double 380 kV connection between them.[tpl]cite web|title=Stevin|url=http://www.elia.be/en/projects/grid-projects/stevin|publisher=elia|accessdate=25 December 2012[/tpl]
In September 2010, a survey was held by the Hogeschool West-Vlaanderen among more than 800 families who live very close to wind turbines in West Flanders. The results were better than expected. Before the turbines were constructed 59.3% were satisfied, this percentage rose to 68.3 after they were constructed. In addition 83.9% didn't oppose the construction of additional wind turbines.[tpl]cite news|title=Studie Howest: windmolens beter onthaald dan gedacht|url=http://kw.knack.be/west-vlaanderen/nieuws/algemeen/studie-howest-windmolens-beter-onthaald-dan-gedacht/article-1194825656513.htm|accessdate=25 December 2012|newspaper=Krant van West-Vlaanderen|date=2010-09-27[/tpl] There has however also been fierce protest against some projects for example plans to construct turbines in Mol and Hannut have stalled.[tpl]cite web|title=Le "non" aux éoliennes|url=http://www.dhnet.be/dhjournal/archives_det.phtml?id=1298614|publisher=DHnet|accessdate=25 December 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Ecopower mag geen windmolens bouwen in Mol|url=http://www.deredactie.be/permalink/1.1451201|publisher=vrtnieuws|accessdate=25 December 2012[/tpl]

===Belwind===

The Bligh Bank Offshore Wind Farm will have an installed capacity of 330 MW and is expected to produce 1.1 TWh annually.[tpl]cite web|url=http://meewind.nl/belwind/ |title=Belwind en Northwind versterkt door nieuwe Japanse partner - Meewind |publisher=Meewind.nl |date=2013-09-30 |accessdate=2013-10-15[/tpl] The first half of the farm (165 MW) was completed on 9 December 2010.Toespraak van Eerste Minister Yves Leterme bij de inhuldiging van het Belwind windturbinepark op de Blighbank (Noordzee), 9 December 2010. Accessed: 10 December 2010. The construction of the second phase is planned for 2014.

===C-Power===

The first two construction phases of the Thorntonbank Wind Farm were completed in 2012. Currently the wind farm has a capacity of 215 MW. 
[tpl]cite web|title=REpower: C-Power’s offshore wind farm Thornton Bank II installed|url=http://www.repower.de/fileadmin/press_release/2012_07_27_Thornton_Bank_II_installed.pdf|publisher=REpower|accessdate=6 November 2012|date=2012-07-27[/tpl] The third and final phase, planned for 2015, will extend this capacity further to about 325 MW. When completed the turbines will produce an estimated 1 TWh a year.[tpl]dead link|date=October 2013[/tpl]

===Planned projects===

===Energy island===

In order to use the generation capacity more efficiently plans are being made to construct an artificial island 3 km from Wenduine. On the island a lake would function as the reservoir of a pumped storage power station, estimated to produce 300 MW for 3 hours a day.[tpl]cite news|title=Binnen vijf jaar eiland voor onze kust|url=http://www.standaard.be/artikel/detail.aspx?artikelid=DMF20130116_00436413&word=eiland|newspaper=De Standaard|date=17 January 2013|language=nl[/tpl] The project is currently only in the concept phase.
