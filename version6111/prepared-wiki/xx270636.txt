[[Two-striped grass frog]]

CATEGORIES: Hylarana, Amphibians of Bangladesh, Amphibians of Bhutan, Amphibians of Burma, Amphibians of Cambodia, Amphibians of China, Fauna of Hong Kong, Amphibians of Laos, Amphibians of Taiwan, Amphibians of Thailand, Amphibians of Vietnam

The two-striped grass frog, Hylarana taipehensis, is a species of true frog in the genus Hylarana found in northeastern India, Bangladesh, Cambodia, southern China, Hong Kong, Laos, Myanmar, Taiwan, Thailand, and Vietnam.[tpl]cite web |url=http://research.amnh.org/vz/herpetology/amphibia/?action=names&taxon=Hylarana+taipehensis |title=Hylarana taipehensis (Van Denburgh, 1909) |author=Frost, Darrel R. |year=2013 |work=Amphibian Species of the World 5.6, an Online Reference |publisher=American Museum of Natural History |accessdate=24 November 2013[/tpl] It has recently been reported also from Bhutan.[tpl]cite doi |10.11609/JoTT.o3539.4774-83[/tpl]
This small frog may grow up to [tpl]convert|4|cm|abbr=on[/tpl] in snout-vent length (males are slightly smaller).[tpl]cite web |url=http://amphibia.biota.biodiv.tw/pages/424 |title=Hylarana taipehensis |author=Lue, Kuang-Yang |date= |work=BiotaTaiwanica |publisher= |accessdate=15 December 2012[/tpl]
