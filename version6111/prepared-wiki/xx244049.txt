[[Lookingglass, Oregon]]

CATEGORIES: 1870s establishments in Oregon, Census-designated places in Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Douglas County, Oregon, Census-designated places in Douglas County, Oregon, Unincorporated communities in Oregon

Lookingglass is an unincorporated community and census-designated place in the Lookingglass Valley of Douglas County, Oregon, United States, about [tpl]convert|9|mi|km[/tpl] southwest of Roseburg. As of the 2010 census it had a population of 855.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov| title=Profile of General Population and Housing Characteristics: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (DP-1): Lookingglass CDP, Oregon| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=February 13, 2012[/tpl] Lookingglass is considered a suburb of Roseburg.

==History==

The valley was named in 1846 by surveyor Hoy Flournoy, who said the beautiful green grass of the valley reflected light almost as well as a mirror.[tpl]cite book |url= http://books.google.com/books?id=4i1grNoMcWgC&lpg=PA227&ots=EqXxJwgjFS&dq=%22Hoy%20Flournoy%22%20Oregon&pg=PA227#v=onepage&q=&f=false |title= In Search of Western Oregon |author= Friedman, Ralph |year= 1992 |edition=2nd |location= Caldwell, Idaho |publisher=The Caxton Printers, Ltd. |pages= 226–227[/tpl][tpl]cite book |last= McArthur |first= Lewis A. |authorlink= Lewis A. McArthur |coauthors= Lewis L. McArthur |title= Oregon Geographic Names |origyear= 1928 |edition= 7th |year= 2003 |publisher= Oregon Historical Society Press |location= Portland, Oregon |isbn= 0-87595-277-1 |page= 590[/tpl] Flournoy later returned to settle in the area.
The Lookingglass Store, built circa 1875, was once the terminus for the Oakland to Lookingglass stage and freight road.[tpl]cite news |url= http://www.nrtoday.com/article/20080913/NEWS/809123186 |title= Lookingglass Store still functions as center of community |date= September 12, 2008 |location= Roseburg, Oregon |newspaper= The News-Review |author= Levings, Debbie |accessdate= 2013-05-12[/tpl] It was also the beginning of the Coos Bay Wagon Road. Today the store continues to serve as the hub of the community. Lookingglass also has a school, a grange hall, a church and a fire station. Lookingglass post office closed in 1942.
In the 1970s, Lookingglass, population 40 at the time, received national media attention for installing a two-horse parking meter, a telephone booth, and a fire hydrant.[tpl]cite news |url= http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=TP0LAAAAIBAJ&sjid=hlcDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3797,3078478&dq=lookingglass+oregon&hl=en |title= Town Celebrates Phone Booth |date= July 13, 1971 |agency= Associated Press |newspaper= The Evening Independent |location= St. Petersburg, Florida |accessdate=2009-10-20[/tpl][tpl]cite news |url= http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=3sIUAAAAIBAJ&sjid=GuEDAAAAIBAJ&pg=3227,5701981&dq=lookingglass+oregon&hl=en |title= Lookingglass decorates for Christmas |date= December 22, 1971 |newspaper= The Register-Guard |location= Eugene, Oregon |accessdate= 2009-10-20[/tpl] Lookingglass became a minor tourist attraction.[tpl]cite news |url= http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F60C13F6355F11738DDDAB0A94D9415B808BF1D3 |title= Lone Parking Meter Is Top Tourist Lure In an Oregon Town |newspaper= The New York Times |date= November 22, 1970 |accessdate= 2009-10-20[/tpl] When the fire hydrant was dedicated in 1971, it was accompanied by two manhole covers, which covered nothing, donated by a Eugene, Oregon iron company and the mayor of Cedar Rapids, Iowa.[tpl]cite news |url= http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=lPQLAAAAIBAJ&sjid=jVcDAAAAIBAJ&pg=1845,6702175&dq=lookingglass+oregon&hl=en |title= Two-Horse Parking Meter Gets Company |date=  September 27, 1971 |agency= Associated Press |publisher= The Evening Independent |location= St. Petersburg, Florida |accessdate= 2009-10-20[/tpl]
David Brinkley anchored his segment of the NBC Nightly News from the steps of the Lookingglass Store in about 1970. Chet Huntley, David Brinkley's co-anchor of the Huntley-Brinkley Report, was the great-grandson of the Lookingglass area's first settler, Daniel Huntley, who arrived in 1851.[tpl]cite book |url= http://www.archive.org/stream/oregonendoftrail00writrich/oregonendoftrail00writrich_djvu.txt |title= Oregon: The End of the Trail |author= Work Projects Administration |publisher= Binfords & Mort | location= Portland, Oregon |year= 1940 |series= American Guide Series[/tpl]
The James Wimer Octagonal Barn near Lookingglass was built in 1892 and is listed on the National Register of Historic Places.[tpl]cite web|title=Oregon National Register List|url=http://www.oregon.gov/OPRD/HCD/NATREG/docs/oregon_nr_list.pdf?ga=t|publisher=Oregon Parks & Recreation Department|accessdate=2011-12-02|page=11|format=PDF|date=2011-06-06[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory—Nomination|url=http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Text/85003039.pdf|publisher=National Park Service|accessdate=2011-12-02|page=2|format=PDF|date=1985-10-31[/tpl]

==Education==

Lookingglass Elementary School is part of the Winston-Dillard School District.[tpl]cite web |url= http://les.wdsd.org/ | archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20080617101403/http://les.wdsd.org/ |title= Lookingglass School |archivedate= 2008-06-17[/tpl] It was founded in 1898 and currently serves grades kindergarten through six.
