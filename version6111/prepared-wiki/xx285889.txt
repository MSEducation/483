[[Dream Boys]]

CATEGORIES: Rwandan music

Dream Boys is a Rwandan R&B group composed of Nemeye Platini (known as Platini) and Claude Mujyanama (called T.M.C.). The group was formed in 2009 and has toured throughout Rwanda as well as regionally to member countries of the East African Community, including Uganda.[tpl]cite news | last = Ssenyonga  | first = Jumah  | title = Dream Boys to perform in Uganda | newspaper = New Times | location = Rwanda | date = 8 March 2013 | url = http://www.newtimes.co.rw/news/index.php?i=15290&a=64679 | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl] 

==History==

Platini and Mujyanama are friends since childhood and are both university graduates. The duo compose their own songs, which are produced by studio professionals in Uganda and Rwanda. They are managed under the label Kina Music.[tpl]cite news | last = Kwesiga   | first =Brian  | title = Interview: Tom Close Promises Fire At Rwanda Nite | newspaper = Chimp Reports| location = Rwanda | date = 28 June 2013 | url = http://chimpreports.com/index.php/entertainment/12109-riderman-wins-rwanda-talent-search.html | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]
Their first album, Sinzika, was released in 2009 to strong success in Rwanda. The first single off the album, Magorwa, achieved widespread popularity across the country. Their second release,  Isaano, was well received but garnered less popular attention than their debut. A third album, Uzambariza Mama, was released in 2012. The duo has also collaborated with other local and regional artists on numerous singles, including No one like me with Ugandan Eddie Kenzo, Nzagaruka with Burundian T-Max, and Rwanda uri Nziza with Rwandans Kitoko, Urban Boyz, Riderman and Ugandaâ€™s Radio and Weasel.[tpl]cite news | last = Ndaka | first = Olivia  | title = Dream Boys to Thrill Rwandans in Uganda | newspaper = Rwanda Express | location = Rwanda | date = 8 March 2013 | url = http://www.rwandaexpress.rw/sports-entertainment/961/dream-boys-to-thrill-rwandans-in-uganda/ | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl] The band has named Rwandan artists Miss Jojo and Tom Close as inspirations, as well as Tanzanian Ali Kuba.[tpl]cite news | last = Asiimwe | first = Sarah  | title = Rwanda: 'Music Is Business' - Dream Boys | newspaper = Rwanda Focus | location = Rwanda | date = 12 November 2012 | url = http://allafrica.com/stories/201211120046.html | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]

==Themes==

The music of Dream Boys addresses themes relating to the challenges of their daily lives in Rwanda. The band has publicly advocated for social causes, including a male circumcision campaign in Ruhango District to reduce prevalence of HIV infection.[tpl]cite news | title = Rwanda: Dream boys call for youth circumcision | newspaper = RwandaShow | location = Rwanda | date = 16 March 2012 | url = http://www.rwandashow.com/index.php/2012/03/rwanda-dream-boys-call-for-youth-circumcision/ | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]
