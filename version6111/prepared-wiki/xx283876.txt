[[Omar Khalid]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Pashtun people, Tehrik-i-Taliban Pakistan

Omar Khalid (Urdu:عمر خالد), also known as Omar Khalid Khorasani is the commander of Tehrik-e-Taliban Pakistan (TTP) in Mohmand Agency. He is considered one of TTP's most effective and powerful leaders in the Federally Administered Tribal Areas.[tpl]cite web|last=Roggio|first=Bill|title=Pakistan claims 25 Taliban fighters killed in Mohmand airstrikes|url=http://www.longwarjournal.org/threat-matrix/archives/2011/06/pakistan_claims_25_taliban_fig.php|work=Long War Journal|date=19 June 2011|accessdate=11 August 2013[/tpl]  His real name is Abdul Wali[tpl]cite news|last=Mahmood|first=Javed|title=TTP leader readies deadly suicide squad of ‘Jannati Khawateen’|url=http://pakobserver.net/detailnews.asp?id=185553|accessdate=11 August 2013|newspaper=Pakistan Observer|date=5 December 2012[/tpl] and belongs to the Safi tribe.
Omar Khalid gained prominence in Mohmand during the summer of 2007 after seizing the shrine of a famous anti-colonialist fighter in the village of Ghazi Abad and renaming it Lal Masjid (Red Mosque), after the radical mosque in Islamabad whose followers had attempted to impose sharia in the Pakistani capital.[tpl]cite book|title=Talibanistan: Negotiating the Borders Between Terror, Politics, and Religion|year=2013|publisher=Oxford University Press|authors=Peter Bergen & Katherine Tiedemann|isbn=9780199893096|page=371[/tpl] In July 2008, Khalid became the dominant Taliban commander in Mohmand after defeating the Shah Khalid group (also known as Shah Sahib group), a rival pro-Taliban militant group associated with Lashkar-e-Toiba.[tpl]cite web|last=Roggio|first=Bill|title=The Pakistani Taliban's top leaders|url=http://www.longwarjournal.org/archives/2010/05/the_pakistani_taliba_1.php|work=Long War Journal|date=17 May 2010|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
