[[Saleh Hosseini]]

CATEGORIES: 1946 births, Iranian translators, Iranian literary critics, Living people, People from Sonqor

Saleh Hosseini (born in 1325 Hijri, 1946 A.D.), from Songhor, Kermanshah Province, Iran is a translator and critic.[tpl]cite news |title=صالح حسینی و معیارهای نقد شعر |url=http://www.aftabir.com/news/view/2006/dec/05/c5c1165321225_art_culture_literature_verse.php/%D8%B5%D8%A7%D9%84%D8%AD-%D8%AD%D8%B3%DB%8C%D9%86%DB%8C-%D9%88-%D9%85%D8%B9%DB%8C%D8%A7%D8%B1%D9%87%D8%A7%DB%8C-%D9%86%D9%82%D8%AF-%D8%B4%D8%B9%D8%B1 |work=Aftab |date=5 December 2006 |accessdate=25 September 2011 [/tpl] 
He has a PhD in English of George Washington University in  America. He is currently teaching in Shahid Chamran University of Ahvaz. Having finished the introductory education in his hometown, he continued his education in Shiraz University of Iran where he graduated with an M.A. Then he went to the U.S., got his PhD in George Washington University and came back to Iran in the same year (1979). He was acclaimed by Iranian Ministry of Culture and Guidance as the leading translator and critic of the Iranian year 1376 (1997).
Of his great works of art are the translation of 1984 by George Orwell[tpl]cite news |title=اورول سیاست را از زبان كنار گذاشت |url=http://www.ibna.ir/vdce.n8ebjh8vp9bij.html |work=IBNA |date=6 July 2007 |accessdate=25 September 2011 [/tpl] and the introduction of Brave New World translated into Persian by Sa’id Hamidian. He also translated To the Lighthouse (Virginia Woolf)[tpl]cite journal |last=Hori |first=A. A. F. |date=Summer 2009 |title=A study of translator's style in translated narrative texts: translation universals in Persian translation of Woolf's To the lighthouse |journal=Translation Studies |volume=7 |issue=26 |pages=5–26 [/tpl][tpl]cite journal |last=Somayeh |first=Delzendehrooy |date=Summer 2010 |title=Adequacy or acceptability? Toward making the style of the author known |journal=Translation Studies |volume=8 |issue=30 |pages=27–42 [/tpl] and Heart of Darkness (Joseph Conrad) which are both great in source language and translated target language. Nazari-be-Tarjome (نظری به ترجمه) Look to Translation and Farhang-i-Nabarabarhaye Adabi (فرهنگ نابرابرهای ادبی) Dictionary of Literary Inequalities are among the books written by Dr. Saleh Hosseini.
