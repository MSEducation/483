[[Golden Hat Foundation]]

CATEGORIES: Organizations established in 2010, Autism-related organizations

The Golden Hat Foundation is a non-profit organisation founded by actress Kate Winslet and Margret Ericsdottir in 2010.[tpl]cite news|last=Clark|first=Cindy|title=Kate Winslet wears a new hat: Charity coordinator|url=http://usatoday30.usatoday.com/life/people/story/2012-03-26/Kate-Winslet-Golden-Hat-Foundation/53793266/1|publisher=USA Today|accessdate=12 August 2013|date=26 March 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Film moves Winslet to start charity|url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/entertainment/film-tv/news/film-moves-winslet-to-start-charity-28560940.html|publisher=Belfast Telegraph|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] The two met while Winslet was filming the documentary A Mother's Courage: Talking Back to Autism, which focused on Ericsdottir's son Keli Thorsteinsson, who has non-verbal autism. The organization centers on raising strengths-focused autism awareness and states that their mission is to "Change the way people on the autism spectrum are perceived, by shining a light on their abilities and emphasizing their great potential."[tpl]cite web|title=The Golden Hat Foundation|url=http://www.goldenhatfoundation.org/about-us/mission-statement|publisher=The Golden Hat Foundation |accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
The organization takes its name from a poem written by Thorsteinsson, in which he described a magical golden hat that had the ability to speak for its protagonist, who was unable to speak.[tpl]cite web|last=Seidman|first=Ellen|title=The Gift Of Communication For Kids With Autism And Other Special Needs|url=http://www.parents.com/blogs/to-the-max/2012/02/10/autism/the-gift-of-communication-for-kids-with-autism-and-other-special-needs/|publisher=Parents.com|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] In 2011 the Golden Hat Foundation paired with the cosmetics company Lancôme to create a golden hat themed make-up collection,[tpl]cite news|title=Kate Winslet launches makeup range with Lancôme|url=http://www.independent.co.uk/life-style/fashion/kate-winslet-launches-makeup-range-with-lancme-2340145.html|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=Independent|date=18 August 2011|location=London[/tpl] with the intent for part of the proceeds to benefit the organization.[tpl]cite news|title=Kate Winslet and Lancôme team up for charity|url=http://www.belfasttelegraph.co.uk/woman/fashion-beauty/kate-winslet-and-lancme-team-up-for-charity-28662572.html|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=Belfast Telegraph|date=27 September 2011[/tpl]

==Documentary==

A Mother's Courage: Talking Back to Autism released in 2009 and centered upon Ericsdottir's attempts to find a way for her son Keli to communicate.[tpl]cite web|last=Monder|first=Eric|title=Film Review: A Mother's Courage: Talking Back to Autism |url=http://www.filmjournal.com/filmjournal/content_display/reviews/specialty-releases/e3i386774579db7c20313e4507fd8b9273f|publisher=Film Journal|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] The documentary was directed by Friðrik Þór Friðriksson and featured Winslet as the film's narrator. A Mother's Courage was met with positive reviews.[tpl]cite web|last=Taylor|first=Ella|title=Overcoming Autism With 'A Mother's Courage'|url=http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=129992514|publisher=NPR|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Webster|first=Andy|title=Confronting Autism|url=http://www.nytimes.com/2010/09/24/movies/24mother.html?_r=0|publisher=New York Times|accessdate=12 August 2013|date=23 September 2010[/tpl] The documentary introduced Ericsdottir and Winslet to one another, and Winslet came up with the idea of creating the Golden Hat Foundation after her daughter Mia asked Winslet about what it would be like to be unable to communicate with her mother.[tpl]cite news|title=Winslet launches autism charity|url=http://www.lfpress.com/life/2010/09/24/15463346-wenn-story.html|accessdate=12 August 2013|newspaper=WENN.com|date=September 24, 2010[/tpl]

==Book==

On March 27, 2012, Winslet and the Golden Hat Foundation published The Golden Hat: Talking Back to Autism through Simon & Schuster.[tpl]cite web|last=Corridan|first=Kara|title=A Mom You Won’t Forget|url=http://www.parents.com/blogs/goodyblog/tag/the-golden-hat-talking-back-to-autism/|publisher=Parents.com, Goodyblog|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl] The book compiled correspondence between Winslet and Ericsdottir, personal statements from various celebrities, and several contributions from Ericsdottir's son Keli. Publishers Weekly gave a positive review for The Golden Hat, praising it for its "warmth and sincerity".[tpl]cite web|title=Review: The Golden Hat:  Talking Back to Autism|url=http://www.publishersweekly.com/978-1-4516-4543-9|publisher=Publishers Weekly|accessdate=12 August 2013[/tpl]
