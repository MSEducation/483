[[Love and Mr Lewisham]]

CATEGORIES: British novels, 1899 novels, Novels by H. G. Wells, Novels set in London, 1899 in London

Love and Mr Lewisham is an 1899 novel set in the 1880s by H. G. Wells.  It was among his first outside the science fiction genre.  Wells took considerable pains over the manuscript and said of it that "the writing was an altogether more serious undertaking than I have ever done before."David C. Smith, H.G. Wells: Desperately Mortal: A Biography (New Haven and London: Yale University Press, 1986), p. 208.  He later included it in a 1933 anthology entitled Stories of Men and Women in Love.

==Plot summary==

At the beginning of the novel, Mr. Lewisham is an 18-year-old teacher at a boys' school in Sussex, earning forty pounds a year.  He meets and falls in love with Ethel Henderson, who is paying a visit to relatives.  His involvement with her causes him to lose his position, but he is unable to find her when he moves to London.  
After a two-and-one-half-year break in the action, Mr. Lewisham is in his third year of study at the Normal School of Science in South Kensington.  He has becomes a socialist, declaring his politics with a red tie, and is an object of interest to Alice Heydinger, an older student. ==Reception==

Love and Mr Lewisham was well received, and C. F. G. Masterman told Wells that he believed that along with Kipps it was the novel most likely to endure.David C. Smith, H.G. Wells: Desperately Mortal: A Biography (New Haven and London: Yale University Press, 1986), p. 202.  Richard Arman Gregory compared the novel to Thomas Hardy's Jude the Obscure.David C. Smith, H.G. Wells: Desperately Mortal: A Biography (New Haven and London: Yale University Press, 1986), p. 208.
