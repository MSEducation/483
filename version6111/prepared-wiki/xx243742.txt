[[Cherwell Council election, 2002]]

CATEGORIES: English local elections, 2002, Cherwell Council elections

The 2002 Cherwell Council election took place on 2 May 2002 to elect members of  Cherwell District Council in Oxfordshire, England. The whole council was up for election with boundary changes since the last election in 2000 reducing the number of seats by 2.[tpl]Cite web| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/hi/english/static/vote2002/local_elections/30.stm | title = Cherwell | accessdate = 2009-10-20 | publisher = BBC News Online[/tpl] The Conservative party stayed in overall control of the council.[tpl]cite news | title = Local Elections: Results | publisher = The Guardian | page = 9 | date = 3 May 2002 | accessdate = 2009-10-20[/tpl]
The election saw the two independent councillors and one independent Socialist councillor stand down from the council after deciding not to seek re-election.[tpl]Cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2002/3/30/42071.html | title = Independents decide not to seek re-election | accessdate = 2009-10-20 | date = 30 March 2002 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl] When the candidates were announced, five Conservatives including the leader of the council, George Reynolds, were unopposed in the election.[tpl]Cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2002/4/12/41353.html | title = Council leaders escape poll tests | accessdate = 2009-10-20 | date = 12 April 2002 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl] The results saw the Conservative party consolidate their control of the council after winning 37 of the 50 seats on the council including all of the seats in Bicester.[tpl]Cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2002/5/3/40412.html | title = Tories strengthen grip | accessdate = 2009-10-20 | date = 3 May 2002 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl][tpl]Cite web| url = http://archive.oxfordmail.net/2002/5/3/40392.html | title = Conservatives take control with 37 seats | accessdate = 2009-10-20 | date = 3 May 2002 | publisher = Oxford Mail[/tpl]
