[[PS Earl of Ulster (1878)]]

CATEGORIES: 1878 ships, Passenger ships of the United Kingdom, Steamships, Barrow-built ships, Ships of the London and North Western Railway, Ships of the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway

PS ''Earl of Ulster'' was a paddle steamer passenger vessel operated by the London and North Western Railway and the Lancashire and Yorkshire Railway from 1878 to 1894.Railway and Other Steamers, Duckworth. 1962

==History==

She operated on services from Fleetwood to Belfast.
On 30 June 1883 she was involved in a collision off the Isle of Man with the schooner Susanna.Manchester Evening News - Friday 17 August 1883
On 12 March 1889 she collided with the Holywood Lighthouse in Belfast Lough and destroyed it.Belfast News-Letter - Wednesday 13 March 1889
In 1894 she was sold to A.M. Carlisle and a few years later passed to Mr. McCausland, Portaferry.
