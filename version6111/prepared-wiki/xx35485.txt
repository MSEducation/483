[[The Lakes National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Victoria (Australia), Protected areas established in 1927, 1927 establishments in Australia, East Gippsland

The Lakes National Park is a national park in the East Gippsland region of Victoria, Australia. The park is located approximately [tpl]convert|245|km|miles|abbr=on[/tpl] east of the capital city of Melbourne.

==Location and features==

The [tpl]convert|2390|ha|acre|adj=on[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://parkweb.vic.gov.au/__data/assets/pdf_file/0020/315650/Park-note-The-Lakes-National-Park.pdf|format=PDF|title=The Lakes National Park: Park notes|work=Parks Victoria|publisher=Government of Victoria|date=August 2012|accessdate=15 January 2014 [/tpl] park is set on the east shore of the Gippsland Lakes, fringed by the waters of Lake Victoria and Lake Reeve.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.bonzle.com/c/a?a=p&p=59786&cmd=sp|title=The Lakes National Park|work=Bonzle Digital Atlas of Australia|year= |accessdate=15 January 2014 [/tpl] The park includes Sperm Whale Head peninsula and Rotamah and Little Rotamah islands. The park is managed by Parks Victoria jointly with the Gunaikurnai Traditional Owners'.[tpl]cite web |url=http://parkweb.vic.gov.au/explore/parks/the-lakes-national-park/joint-management |title=Joint management |work=The Lakes National Park |publisher=Parks Victoria |date= |accessdate=15 January 2014 [/tpl]
An area of [tpl]convert|1451|ha|acre[/tpl] was initially set aside as a nature reserve in 1927, with the park proclaimed in 1956. Rotamah Island and Little Rotamah Island were added to the park in 1978.
