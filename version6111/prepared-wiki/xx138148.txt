[[Metro UAM-I]]

CATEGORIES: Mexico City metro stations, Railway stations opened in 1994

Metro UAM-I is a station along Line 8 of the Mexico City Metro.[tpl]Cite web|url=http://www.metro.df.gob.mx/red/estacion.html?id=114|title=UAM-I|language=Spanish|accessdate=26 July 2011[/tpl][tpl]Cite web|last=Archambault|first=Richard|url=http://mexicometro.org/metro/line8/uam-i/|title=UAM-I »  Mexico City Metro System|accessdate=26 July 2011[/tpl]
The station's logo is the logo of the Universidad Autónoma Metropolitana campus Iztapalapa, which is close to the station. The station was opened on 20 July 1994.[tpl]Cite web|last=Monroy|first=Marco|editor-last=Schwandl|editor-first=Robert|url=http://www.urbanrail.net/am/mexi/mex-history.htm|title=Opening Dates for Mexico City's Subway|accessdate=20 August 2011[/tpl]  Until September 1996 this station was known as La Purísima, the name of the street where it is located. The previous logo was the silhouette of the Virgin of Guadalupe.

==Curiosities==

Although the name of the station has changed, you can appreciate on the platforms the murals depicting La Purísima. That is, as the station was opened with that name, and until being renamed UAM-I, still keeps design iconography and original name.
