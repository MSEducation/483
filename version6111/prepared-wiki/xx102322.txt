Richard Helmut Grathoff was a phenomenologist and Professor Emeritus in the Department of Sociology at Bielefeld University, Germany.[tpl]cite book|last=Srubar|first=Ilja|title=Development of sociology in Japan|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Em-gp7fdpxEC&pg=PA160|accessdate=16 April 2011|date=2005-01-17|publisher=VS Verlag|isbn=978-3-531-14513-6|page=160[/tpl][tpl]cite book|last=Ritzer|first=George|title=Classical sociological theory|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=_yZFAAAAYAAJ|accessdate=16 April 2011|year=1996|publisher=McGraw-Hill|isbn=978-0-07-053017-1|page=389[/tpl]
Born on August 30, 1934 in Unna, Westphalia, Germany, he received his Ph.D. from the New School for Social Research in 1969 after studying under Aron Gurwitsch, Thomas Luckmann and Peter L. Berger. Influenced by Charles Sanders Peirce, Edmund Husserl and Alfred Schutz, his work has included research in the topics of milieu and life-world ("Lebenswelt") and contemporary social theory. He died in Oerlinghausen, Germany on November 10, 2013.http://www.nw-trauer.de/Traueranzeige/Richard-Grathoff
