[[David Jay]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Asexual men, 1982 births, Wesleyan University alumni, American activists

David Jay (born April 24, 1982) is an American asexual activist. Jay is the founder and webmaster of the Asexual Visibility and Education Network (AVEN). 

==Activism==

Frustrated with the lack of resources available regarding asexuality, Jay launched AVEN's website in 2002.[tpl]cite web|url=http://nymag.com/nymetro/nightlife/sex/columns/mating/11243/| title=Shifting to Neutral: No interest in sex is nothing to get worked up about| author=Amy Sohn| date=February 28, 2005| publisher=New York Times| accessdate=2009-03-04[/tpl] Since then, he has taken a leading role in the asexuality movement, appearing on multiple television shows, and being featured heavily in Arts Engine's 2011 documentary (A)sexual.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/lifeandstyle/2012/feb/26/among-the-asexuals|title=Among the asexuals|author= Rosie Swash|publisher=The Guardian|date= 26 February 2012|accessdate=2012-02-27[/tpl] 
AVEN, which Salon.com referred to as the "unofficial online headquarters" of the asexuality movement,[tpl]cite web|url=http://dir.salon.com/story/mwt/feature/2005/05/26/asexual/print.html| title=Asexual and Proud!| author=Lynn Harris| date=May 26, 2005|publisher=Salon.com| accessdate=2009-03-04[/tpl] is widely recognised as the largest online asexual community.[tpl]cite web|url=http://xpress.sfsu.edu/archives/magazine/007109.html| title=Just Don't Do It| author=Asiana Ponciano| date=October 9, 2006|publisher=Xpress Magazine| accessdate=2009-03-04[/tpl] Its two main goals are to create public acceptance and discussion about asexuality and to facilitate the growth of a large online asexual community.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.asexuality.org/home/about.html| title=About AVEN|accessdate=2009-03-04[/tpl] As of June 17, 2013, AVEN has nearly 70,000 registered members.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.huffingtonpost.com/2013/06/17/what-is-asexuality_n_3360424.html?1371476978|title=What Is Asexuality? A Community's Coming Of Age|author=Dominique Mosbergen|publisher=Huffington Post|date=June 17, 2013|accessdate=June 20, 2013[/tpl]

==Personal life==

At the age of 15, Jay began considering himself asexual, and he came out as asexual while a student at Wesleyan University in Connecticut. As of November 2012, Jay is in a romantic relationship with an asexual girlfriend and hopes to adopt a child.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/women/9651265/The-moment-I-realised-I-was-asexual.html|title='The moment I realised I was asexual'|author=Olivia Gordon|date=November 12, 2012|publisher=The Daily Telegraph|accessdate=December 10, 2012[/tpl]
