[[Eugene Katchalov]]

CATEGORIES: 1981 births, American poker players, World Poker Tour winners, World Series of Poker bracelet winners, Living people

Eugene Katchalov (born February 21, 1981 in Kiev, Ukraine) is an American professional poker player raised in Brooklyn, New York since age 10. He has been ranked as high as number two by the Global Poker Index,GPIas well as being the all-time leading money winner from the Ukraine.Ukraine All Time Money List 
Katchalov, a graduate with a business degree from NYU, is the winner of the largest payout for a non-championship event in the World Poker Tour (WPT), earning $2,482,605 with a win at the 2007 Doyle Brunson Five Diamond World Poker Classic.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.cardplayer.com/tournaments/live_updates/13903?view_all=true |title=Live Update Log - Player Bio: Eugene Katchalov |accessdate=2008-05-23 |date=2007-12-18 |publisher=CardPlayer.com [/tpl]

==Poker==

Katchalov has been playing poker professionally since 2003 and in March 2011, he joined Team PokerStars Pro http://www.pokerstarsblog.com/2011/team-pokerstars-signs-eugene-katchalov-079562.html and now plays under the screen name ‘E.Katchalov’. However, Katchalov was previously well known online under his former non-Team PokerStars Pro screen name 'MyRabbiFoo'.
In addition to his 2007 WPT win, Katchalov finished 9th in the 2009 WPT Championship event.  He has cashed nine times on the WPT tour for a total of $2,759,761.WPT Statistics
Katchalov has had 30 cashes in World Series of Poker events, including a win in the 2011 $1,500 Seven-Card Stud tournament.  He also finished 39th at the 2009 WSOP Main Event.WSOP Statistics
Katchalov has also succeeded on the European Poker Tour.  He has cashed nine times in Main Events including third place finishes in London, 2009, and Barcelona, 2011 and a second place finish in Deauville, 2014.  At the PokerStars Caribbean Adventure in 2011, Katchalov bested 37 other players in the $100,000 Super High Roller tournament, defeating Team Pokerstars Pro Daniel Negreanu heads-up, to win $1.5million.http://www.pokerstarsblog.com/pca/2011/eugene-katchalov-defeats-daniel-negreanu-077285.html 10 days later he came in 2nd place in the PCA $10,000 High Roller - 6 Max event winning $131,920 http://www.pokerpages.com/player-profile/eugene-katchalov.htm.
