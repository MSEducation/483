[[Terramatta]]

CATEGORIES: Italian documentary films, 2012 films, 2013 film awards

Terramatta: Il Novecento italiano di Vincenzo Rabito analfabeta siciliano, in English, Terramatta - The Italian Twentieth Century of Vincenzo Rabito Sicilian illiterate) is a 2012 documentary film directed by Costanza Quatriglio, based on the memoir Terra Matta by Vincenzo Rabito. Winner of several awards, including the Nastro d'Argento 2013, it was shown in all the major Italian cities.[tpl]cite web|title=Progetto Terramatta|url=http://www.progettoterramatta.it/?cat=1|accessdate=10 August 2013|language=it[/tpl][tpl]cite journal|title=Nastro a Terramatta miglior documentario|journal=La Sicilia|date=8 May 2013|url=http://palermo.repubblica.it/cronaca/2013/05/08/news/nastro_a_terramatta_miglior_documentario-58374779/|accessdate=10 August 2013|language=it[/tpl]

==Plot==

The story of The Twentieth Century told by a last, and is inspired by the Terra Matta, a memoir published by Einaudi in 2007, written in approximate Italian by Vincenzo Rabito (class 1899), a former laborer and Sicilian worker semi-literate but of great narrative ability, who attended the world War I and African adventure (in Ogaden). Then went to work in German mines, at the beginning of world War II, and in 1943 has witnessed the Allied invasion of Sicily. After the end of World War II has achieved the goal of sending their children to school and after to university.
