[[Tourism in Iceland]]

CATEGORIES: Tourism in Iceland, Tourism in Europe

Tourism in Iceland has become a more significant part of the national economy[tpl]Clarify timeframe[/tpl]. The tourism industry was estimated to contribute to 5.9% of the country's GDP as of 2009.Statistics Iceland In 2012, an estimated 672,000 tourists visited Iceland,Icelandic Tourist Board and tourism contributed 23.5% in export revenue.Icelandic Tourism Board

==History==

Tourism to Iceland has grown steadily since the 1990s. In  2000, the annual number of visitors exceeded the total resident population for the first time. Since then,  tourism has grown by about 7.3% on average each year.[tpl]cite web|title=Tourism in Iceland in Figures, April 2013|url=http://www.ferdamalastofa.is/static/files/ferdamalastofa/talnaefni/tourism-in-iceland-in-figures-april-2013.pdf|accessdate=2014-03-08[/tpl] Some 360,000 people visited Iceland in 2004,[tpl]cite web|title=Tourism in Iceland|url=http://www.visiticeland.com/displayer.asp?cat_id=599|accessdate=2008-03-21[/tpl] compared with 485,000 in 2007,[tpl]cite web|title=Tourism in Iceland|url=http://www.ferdamalastofa.is/file.asp?id=2328|accessdate=2009-01-26[/tpl] 500,000 in 2008 and 672,000 in 2012. The number of nights spent was 0.585 million in 2000 and 3.7 million in 2012.
Jobs in the tourism industry were estimated at 7,385 in 2000 and at 9,241 in 2009 (5.1% of the total workforce).Statistics Iceland
