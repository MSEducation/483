[[Jonathan Tyler and the Northern Lights]]

CATEGORIES: American hard rock musical groups

Jonathan Tyler & the Northern Lights a.k.a. "JTNL" are an American rock band from Dallas, Texas.[tpl]cite web|last=Mueller |first=Chelsea |url=http://blogs.dallasobserver.com/dc9/2007/12/were_so_ready_for_blues_with_j.php |title=We're So Ready for Blues with Jonathan Tyler – Dallas Music – DC9 At Night |publisher=Blogs.dallasobserver.com |date=December 27, 2007 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl]

==History==

The band was formed in January 2007 in Dallas, Texas by Jonathan Tyler, Brandon Pinckard, along with Oklahoma natives Nick Jay, and Jordan Cain.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.jonathantylermusic.com/bio/ |title=Jonathan Tyler Music | Biography | Info & Bio |publisher=JonathanTylerMusic.com |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] The band immediately went into the studio with local producer Chris Bell to record their first independent record Hot Trottin[tpl]'[/tpl]. After playing nearly every venue in Deep Ellum the band began to venture outside of Dallas into Austin, Houston, and other surrounding cities. Emotion Brown joined the band in May 2007.
By 2008, the band began garnering regional and national attention by supporting major national acts including Erykah Badu, Leon Russell, Deep Purple, The Black Crowes, Kool & the Gang, Chicago, Heart, Cross Canadian Ragweed, among others.  The band was discovered at SXSW 2008 by an A&R representative from Atlantic Records, and was soon signed to F-Stop Music/Atlantic Records.  The band was awarded "Best Blues Act" by the 2008 Dallas Observer Music Awards.[tpl]cite web|author=Robert Wilonsky |url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/2008-07-24/news/2008-dallas-observer-music-awards/3/ |title=Our 20th Music Awards – Page 3 – News – Dallas |publisher=Dallas Observer |date=July 24, 2008 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl]
In 2009, JTNL continued touring extensively across the United States, most notably alongside Lynyrd Skynyrd, Kid Rock,[tpl]cite web|author=Pete Freedman |url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/2009-06-26/music/kid-rock-lynyrd-skynyrd-jonathan-tyler-the-northern-lights/ |title=Kid Rock, Lynyrd Skynyrd, Jonathan Tyler & The Northern Lights – Page 1 – Music – Dallas |publisher=Dallas Observer |date=June 26, 2009 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] O.A.R., & AC/DC.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.spinner.com/2010/03/01/sxsw-2010-jonathan-tyler-and-the-northern-lights/ |title=Jonathan Tyler and the Northern Lights Interview: SXSW 2010 |publisher=Spinner |date=March 1, 2010 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl]  The band also played the Austin City Limits Music Festival,[tpl]cite web|author=Author: Lynne Margolis |url=http://www.txmusic.com/articles/2010/01/spotlight-jonathan-tyler-northern-lights |title=Spotlight: Jonathan Tyler & the Northern Lights | Texas Music Magazine |publisher=Txmusic.com |date=January 1, 2010 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] Forecastle Festival, Summerfest, Wakarusa, and SXSW. In August, JTNL recorded their first album Pardon Me for F-Stop Music/Atlantic Records.  Jay Joyce was selected by the band to produce the record from his Nashville studio. The band was awarded "Best Group", "Best Male Vocalist", and "Best Blues Act" by the 2009 Dallas Observer Music Awards.[tpl]cite web|last=Freedman |first=Pete |url=http://blogs.dallasobserver.com/dc9/2009/07/domaxxi_the_winners_announced.php |title=DOMAXXI: The Winners! Announced! – Dallas Music – DC9 At Night |publisher=Blogs.dallasobserver.com |date=July 22, 2009 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] Rave reviews from outlets across the country began pouring in for the band and their live show, including praise from critics in USA Today, American Songwriter, Chicago Sun Times, Orlando Weekly, Austin American Statesman, Arizona Daily Star,  Urban Tulsa Weekly, and Nuvo Weekly (Indianapolis) among numerous others.
On April 27, 2010, Pardon Me was released nationwide.  It was awarded "Reader's Pick Best Local CD Release" in the Dallas Observer,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.dallasobserver.com/bestof/2010/award/readers-pick-best-local-cd-release-1931646/ |title=Dallas READERS' PICK BEST LOCAL CD RELEASE – Pardon Me, Jonathan Tyler And the Northern Lights – Best Of Dallas |publisher=Dallas Observer |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] the "fourth best release of 2010" by the Dallas Morning News, and received favorable reviews nationwide.
In 2010, JTNL most notably toured alongside ZZ Top,[tpl]cite web|author=Show Time:8:00 pm |url=http://www.jambase.com/shows/event.aspx?eventid=1181923 |title=ZZ Top and Jonathan Tyler & The Northern Lights on 5/8/2010 at Chastain Park Amphitheater in Atlanta, GA on JamBase |publisher=Jambase.com |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl] JJ Grey & MOFRO, American Bang, Robert Randolph and the Family Band, and others.  They played Bonnaroo Music Festival, Voodoo Fest, Summerfest, BamaJam, and SXSW.
Blender Magazine exclusively debuted the video for "Gypsy Woman" November 15, 2010 on Blender.com.[tpl]dead link|date=November 2011[/tpl]

Jonathan Tyler was named "Best Male Vocalist" by the 2010 Dallas Observer Music Awards.[tpl]cite web|last=Freedman |first=Pete |url=http://blogs.dallasobserver.com/dc9/2010/07/domaxxii_announcing_your_2010.php |title=DOMAXXII: Announcing Your 2010 Dallas Observer Music Awards Winners! – Dallas Music – DC9 At Night |publisher=Blogs.dallasobserver.com |date=July 21, 2010 |accessdate=November 10, 2011[/tpl]
