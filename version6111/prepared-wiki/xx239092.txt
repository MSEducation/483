[[Gerald Campbell]]

CATEGORIES: British diplomats, High Commissioners of the United Kingdom to Canada, 1879 births, Year of death missing

Sir Gerald Campbell (born 1879) was a British diplomat.
He served as British High Commissioner to Canada from 16 May 1938 to 1941.[tpl]cite web |url=http://ukincanada.fco.gov.uk/en/our-offices-in-canada/our-high-commissioner/previous-high-commissioners |title=Previous High Commissioners |archiveurl=http://web.archive.org/web/20091202022951/http://ukincanada.fco.gov.uk/en/our-offices-in-canada/our-high-commissioner/previous-high-commissioners |archivedate=2 December 2009[/tpl] He then was transferred in 1941 by Winston Churchill to serve as British Consul General to the United States, in order to improve relations between Britain and the United States during World War II. He had previously held this position in New York from 1931-1938.[tpl]Cite book |first=Auriol |last=Weigold |title=Churchill, Roosevelt and India: Propaganda During World War II |url=http://books.google.ca/books?id=f-KPH1i56VAC&pg=PA71&lpg=PA71&dq=Sir+Gerald+Campbell&source=bl&ots=aUTLQJYxvn&sig=qHYExeaJK4W8szYPcTVC-YdQIyo&hl=en&ei=PZDbStvbB5XENuet2OoC&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=8&ved=0CBkQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=Sir%20Gerald%20Campbell&f=false [/tpl] He became very popular in the United States, especially for his "after-dinner stories".[tpl]cite web |url= http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,801212,00.html |title=Great Britain: The Campbell Is Coming |work=TIME |date=27 January 1941 |accessdate=14 February 2014[/tpl]
He wrote his autobiography, Of True Experience, published in 1948.[tpl]Cite book |first=Gerald |last=Campbell |title=Of True Experience |location=London |publisher=Hutchinson |year=1948 |url=http://catalogue.nla.gov.au/Record/2442534 [/tpl]
