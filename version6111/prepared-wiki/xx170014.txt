[[Ave Perez Jacob]]

CATEGORIES: Filipino writers, Tagalog-language writers, Living people, Year of birth missing (living people), Manuel L. Quezon University alumni

Ave Perez Jacob is a Filipino writer,[tpl]cite news|last=Trinidad|first=Recah|title=After Clottey, Raging Bore, what to make out of Hopkins-Jones?|url=http://newsinfo.inquirer.net/inquirerheadlines/metro/view/20100406-262597/After-Clottey-Raging-Bore-what-to-make-out-of-Hopkins-Jones|accessdate=29 December 2010|newspaper=Philippine Daily Inquirer|date=6 April 2010[/tpl] columnist, literary critic and novelist popular for his socio-political works. He was one of few Tagalog writers who popularized short stories with theme of injustice and tragedy set on the realistic portrayal of poverty during the 1960s. His short story "Guwardiya" won a Palanca Awards in 1975.[tpl]cite book|title=Book of the Philippines|year=1976|pages=31|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=9gZvAAAAMAAJ&q=Ave+Perez+Jacob+palanca&dq=Ave+Perez+Jacob+palanca&hl=en&ei=wZgaTbCsA5GosAOYxZycDw&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=5&ved=0CDYQ6AEwBA|author=Research and Analysis Center for Communications|accessdate=29 December 2010[/tpl] He is also the author of the novel "Sibol sa mga Guho."
Prof. Ave Perez Jacob was honored by the Unyon ng mga Manunulat sa Pilipinas (UMPIL) with “2001 Gawad Pambansang Alagad ni Balagtas” for his socio-realist novels and short stories.
Jacob, who graduated from Manuel L. Quezon University,  is currently teaching literature and Filipino at the Polytechnic University of the Philippines.
