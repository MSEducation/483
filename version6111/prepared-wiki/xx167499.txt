[[Złoty Potok, Silesian Voivodeship]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Częstochowa County

Złoty Potok [tpl]IPAc-pl|'|z|ł|o|t|y|-|'|p|o|t|o|k[/tpl] is a village in the administrative district of Gmina Janów, within Częstochowa County, Silesian Voivodeship, in southern Poland.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.stat.gov.pl/broker/access/prefile/listPreFiles.jspa |title=Central Statistical Office (GUS) - TERYT (National Register of Territorial Land Apportionment Journal) |date=2008-06-01 |language=Polish[/tpl] It lies approximately [tpl]convert|2|km|mi|0[/tpl] south of Janów, [tpl]convert|25|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south-east of Częstochowa, and [tpl]convert|60|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] north-east of the regional capital Katowice. The village has a population of 1,101.
The history of the village dates to at least the 12th century. In 19th century a palace by Raczyński family, and a mansion of Krasiński family, were built here. In the Krasińscy mansion there is a museum dedicated to the Polish poet Zygmunt Krasiński.
