[[List of tallest buildings in Minneapolis]]

CATEGORIES: Lists of tallest buildings in the United States by city, Skyscrapers in Minneapolis, Minnesota, Buildings and structures in Minnesota, Lists of buildings and structures in Minnesota

Minneapolis, the largest city in the U.S. state of Minnesota, is home to 245 completed high-rises,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/city/minneapolis-mn-usa|title=About: Minneapolis|accessdate=2013-02-08|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl] 34 of which stand taller than [tpl]convert|300|ft|m|0[/tpl]. The tallest building in Minneapolis is the 57-story IDS Tower, which rises [tpl]convert|792|ft|m|0[/tpl] and was designed by architect Philip Johnson.[tpl]Cite web|title=IDS Tower|url=http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/bu/?id=122742|accessdate=2008-03-29|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl] The tower has been the tallest building in the state of Minnesota since its completion in 1973, and it also stands as the 47th-tallest building in the United States. The second-tallest skyscraper in the city and the state is Capella Tower, which rises [tpl]convert|775|ft|m|0[/tpl] and was completed in 1992.[tpl]Cite web|title=225 South Sixth|url=http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/bu/?id=225south6-minneapolis-mn-usa|accessdate=2008-03-28|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl] Overall, seventeen of the twenty tallest buildings in Minnesota are located in Minneapolis. Additionally, most of the tallest buildings in Downtown Minneapolis are linked via the Minneapolis Skyway System, the largest pedestrian skywalk system in the world.[tpl]cite book |last=Millett |first=Larry |authorlink=Larry Millett |title=AIA Guide to the Twin Cities: The Essential Source on the Architecture of Minneapolis and St. Paul |year=2007 |publisher=Minnesota Historical Society Press |location= |isbn=0-87351-540-4 |pages=32–33[/tpl]
The history of skyscrapers in the city began with the construction of the Lumber Exchange Building, now also known as the Edison Building, in 1886; this structure, rising [tpl]convert|165|ft|m|0[/tpl] and 12 floors,[tpl]cite web|url=http://skyscraperpage.com/cities/?buildingID=27074|title=Lumber Exchange|accessdate=2008-03-30|publisher=SkyscraperPage.com[/tpl] is often regarded as the first skyscraper in Minnesota and one of the first fire-proof buildings in the country.[tpl]cite web|url=http://usa.archiseek.com/minnesota/minneapolis/Lumber_Exchange_Building.html|title=Lumber Exchange Building|accessdate=2008-03-30|publisher=Archiseek.com |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20071117144633/http://usa.archiseek.com/minnesota/minneapolis/Lumber_Exchange_Building.html  |archivedate = 2007-11-17[/tpl] The Lumber Exchange Building also stands as the oldest structure outside of New York City with at least 12 floors.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/en/wm/bu/?id=lumberexchangeedisonbuilding-minneapolis-mn-usa|title=Lumber Exchange / Edison Building|accessdate=2008-03-30|publisher=Emporis.com[/tpl] Minneapolis went through a small building boom in the early 1920s, and then experienced a much larger boom lasting from 1960 to the early 1990s. During this time, 24 of the city's 36 tallest buildings were constructed, including the IDS Tower, Capella Tower and Wells Fargo Center. The city is the site of ten skyscrapers at least [tpl]convert|492|ft|m|0[/tpl] in height, including three which rank among the tallest in the United States. [tpl]as of|2013[/tpl], the skyline of Minneapolis is ranked 16th in the United States and 66th in the world with 28 buildings rising at least [tpl]convert|330|ft|m[/tpl] in height.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.emporis.com/statistics/most-skyscraper-cities-worldwide|title=Cities with the most skyscrapers|publisher=Emporis.com|accessdate=2013-02-08[/tpl]
Minneapolis entered into another high-rise construction boom in 2000, and has since seen the completion of six buildings rising over [tpl]convert|300|ft|m|0[/tpl] tall. The latest, IVY Hotel + Residences, was completed in late 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://skyscraperpage.com/cities/?buildingID=29232|title=IVY Hotel + Residences|accessdate=2013-02-08|publisher=SkyscraperPage.com[/tpl] The tallest building under construction in the city is the [tpl]convert|376|ft|m|0|adj=on[/tpl] 1368 LaSalle Avenue, which is expected to be complete by 2014. Overall, [tpl]As of|2013|02|alt=as of February 2013[/tpl], there were nine high-rise buildings under construction or proposed for construction in Minneapolis.

==Tallest buildings==

This list ranks Minneapolis skyscrapers that stand at least [tpl]convert|300|ft|m|0[/tpl] tall, based on standard height measurement. This includes spires and architectural details but does not include antenna masts. The "Year" column indicates the year in which a building was completed.

==Tallest buildings by pinnacle height==

This list ranks Minneapolis skyscrapers based on their pinnacle height, which includes radio masts and antennas. As architectural features and spires can be regarded as subjective, some skyscraper enthusiasts prefer this method of measurement. Standard architectural height measurement, which excludes antennas in building height, is included for comparative purposes. 

==Tallest under construction or proposed==

This lists skyscrapers that are under construction or proposed in Minneapolis and planned to be at least [tpl]convert|300|ft|m|0[/tpl] tall, but are not yet completed structures. A floor count of 20 stories is used as the cutoff for buildings whose heights have not yet been released by their developers. 
 Table entries with dashes (—) indicate that information regarding building heights or dates of completion has not yet been released.
