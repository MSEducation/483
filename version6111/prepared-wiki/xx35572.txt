[[Karlamilyi National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Pilbara, Protected areas established in 1977, Great Sandy Desert

Karlamilyi National Park (formerly Rudall River National Park) is a national park in the Pilbara region of Western Australia (Australia), [tpl]convert|1252|km[/tpl] northeast of Perth. It is the second largest national park in Australia and one of the largest in the world. Kakadu National Park in the Northern Territory is the largest national park in Australia with an area of [tpl]convert|1980400|ha[/tpl].
The area was traditionally Martu country, which they call Karlamilyi.[tpl]Citation | author1=Western Desert Working Group | author2=Western Australia. Dept. of Conservation and Land Management | title=The significance of the Karlamilyi Region to the Martujarra people of the Western Desert | publication-date=1989 | publisher=Dept. of Conservation and Land Management | url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/23309101 | accessdate=1 November 2012 [/tpl]
The park is situated between the Little Sandy Desert and the Great Sandy Desert[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.environment.gov.au/biodiversity/publications/series/paper4/gsd.html|title=Department of the Environment WA - Refugia fro Biodiversity|year=2009|accessdate=30 August 2010[/tpl] and includes the catchment area of the Rudall River.
Rudall River was originally named by Frank Hann who was one of the first Europeans to explore the area. He named the river after another explorer and surveyor, William Frederick Rudall.[tpl]Citation | author1=Rudall, W. F. (William Frederick) | title=Account of expedition in search of missing members of Calvert Expedition exploring party | publication-date=1982 | url=http://trove.nla.gov.au/work/16396726 | accessdate=1 November 2012 [/tpl] 
The name of the park was changed in 2008 to Karlamilyi National Park to acknowledge the traditional owners of the area.
