Ignacio David Fideleff (born 4 July 1989 in Rosario) is an Argentine-Israeli footballer of Russian-Jewish descent. He plays for Primera División side Tigre on loan from Serie A team Napoli.

==Career==

Fideleff was born in Rosario, Santa Fe and grown up as a footballer in Club Atlético Newell's Old Boys's youths. He made his debut for the Newell's first team on 24 March 2008 against Lanús,[tpl]cite news | title = Fideleff to the Napoli. De Laurentiis: "A purchase that satisfies us" | url = http://www.sscnapoli.it/client/render_e.aspx?Lang=en-US&root=707&fwd=4277&content=0 | publisher = Napoli official website |accessdate = 2011-09-24[/tpl] scoring a goal.[tpl]cite news | title = Fideleff tuvo su estreno y fue el autor del gol de Newell’s | url = http://www.lacapital.com.ar/contenidos/2008/03/25/noticia_1053.html | publisher = La Capital | language = Spanish |accessdate = 2011-09-24[/tpl] He played for the club for five seasons, collecting 35 appearances in Argentine Primera División, scoring 3 goals.
At the start of the 2011–12 season, Fideleff was purchased by Serie A club Napoli, for a reported fee of around €3m, believed to be a substitute for Spaniard Víctor Ruiz who was sold to Valencia.[tpl]cite news | title = Napoli complete deal for Newell’s Old Boys defender Ignacio Fideleff | url = http://www.tribalfootball.com/articles/napoli-complete-deal-newell-s-old-boys-defender-ignacio-fideleff-1854431 | publisher = tribalfootball.com |accessdate = 2011-09-24[/tpl] He debuted in Serie A on 21 September 2011, featuring in the starting line-up against Veronese club Chievo.
On 14 July 2012, Fideleff joined Parma on a loan deal for the 2012-13 season.[tpl]cite news | title = Santana and Fideleff transferred on loan to Torino and Parma | url = http://www.sscnapoli.it/client/render_e.aspx?root=707&fwd=4924&content=0 | publisher = Napoli |work=official website |accessdate = 2012-07-14[/tpl]
On 30 January 2013, Fideleff joined Maccabi Tel Aviv on a loan deal for the remainder of the season. The deal came through after Fideleff received an Israeli citizenship due to his Jewish origins.
