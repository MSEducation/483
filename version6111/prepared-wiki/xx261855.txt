[[Dorian Marin]]

Dorian Marin (born 18 June 1960) is a Romanian football coach who is currently in charge of Ghana Premier League club King Faisal Babes.

==Coaching career==

In 1991, Marin attended the federal coaching school of the Romanian Football Federation which he graduated in 1992.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.9am.ro/stiri-revista-presei/2007-09-03/peripetiile-unui-roman-in-eritreea.html | title=Peripetiile unui roman in Eritreea|language=Romanian| work=9am.ro | date=3 September 2007 | accessdate=26 February 2009| author=Viorel Dobran[/tpl]
He began his coaching career in his native Romania where he managed lower league sides Minerul Comănești, Baia Mare and Midia Năvodari.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.impartialdebacau.ro/marin-dorian-vrea-sa-scape-de-razboiul-din-siria/|title=Marin Dorian vrea să scape de războiul din Siria |accessdate=18 August 2012|date=16 August 2012|language=Romanian|publisher=Impartialdebacau.ro[/tpl]
Between 2001 and 2004, Marin was in charge of Syrian clubs Hutteen and Qardaha before moving to Lebanon where he coached Racing Beirut in the 2004–05 season.
After quiting his job in Beirut, Marin was named coach of both the Eritrea national football team and the  Eritrean under-17 simultaneously.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.fifa.com/tournaments/archive/u17worldcup/korea2007/news/newsid=108448/index.html|title=Eritrea & Burkinabe join African elite|work=FIFA.com|date=11 December 2006|accessdate=4 December 2012[/tpl]
Marin managed to qualify his Eritrea U17 side at the 2007 African U-17 Championship in Togo after defeating in the qualifiers Libya, Egypt and Zambia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://english.people.com.cn/200612/11/eng20061211_330972.html|title=Zambia Under-17 lose to Eritrea in Togo Championship qualifier|accessdate=5 December 2012|date=11 December 2006|publisher=People's Daily Online[/tpl]
In qualification for the 2008 Africa Cup of Nations, under the guidance of Marin, Eritrea finished second behind Angola, failing to qualify for the final tournament. However they beat Kenya twice and drew at home to Angola. The Eritrea side were primarily made up of local players compared to Kenya who had at least seven foreign-based footballers playing their trade in Europe. Eritrea registered massive improvement in the FIFA rankings during his tenure climbing 56 positions, from position 177 in June 2006 to 121 in July 2007.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.futaa.com/football/article/romanian-coach-expresses-interest-in-sofa-job|title=Romanian coach expresses interest in Sofa job|accessdate=15 March 2013|date=22 August 2012|author=Jeff Kinyanjui|publisher=Futaa.com[/tpl]
In December 2007, Marin signed with newly promoted Ugandan Super League side Nalubaale FC.[tpl]cite web|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/200712060052.html|title=Uganda: Nalubaale's Romanian Coach in Saturday|accessdate=19 August 2008|date=5 December 2007|publisher=AllAfrica.com[/tpl] His contract was cancelled on 30 September 2008 due to the financial difficulties of the club. Marin then took control of Uganda Revenue Authority in January 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://allafrica.com/stories/200801150191.html|title=I Can't Wait for URA - Dorian Marin|accessdate=1 January 2011|date=14 January 2008|publisher=AllAfrica.com[/tpl]
After Samson Siasia left Nigerian club Heartland to manage the Nigerian national team in 2010, Marin was one of four European coaches named on the shortlist to replace him.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/africa/9328477.stm|title=Heartland move to replace Siasia|accessdate=1 January 2011|date=30 December 2010|author=Oluwashina Okeleji|publisher=BBC Sport[/tpl]
In January 2014, it was announced that Marin took over as coach of Ghanaian side King Faisal Babes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ghananation.com/news/40022-alhaji-grunsa-signs-romanian-coach-dorian-for-king-faisal.html|title=Alhaji Grunsa Signs Romanian Coach Dorian For King Faisal|accessdate=30 January 2014|date=29 January 2014|author=Maureen Abotsi|publisher=Ghananation.com[/tpl]
