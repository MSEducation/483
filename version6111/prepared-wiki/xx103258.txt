[[Richard Ellis (biologist)]]

CATEGORIES: Cetologists, Cryptozoologists, American marine biologists, American science writers, Living people, 20th-century American painters, 21st-century American painters, American muralists, People associated with the American Museum of Natural History, 1938 births

Richard Ellis (born April 2, 1938) is an American marine biologist, author, and illustrator. He is a research associate in the American Museum of Natural History's division of paleontology,http://www.msnbc.msn.com/id/35584261/ns/technology_and_science-science/ special adviser to the American Cetacean Society, and a member of the Explorers Club. He was U.S. delegate to International Whaling Commission from 1980 to 1990.[tpl]citation needed|date=September 2011[/tpl]
His paintings have been exhibited in galleries and museums around the world, and his murals can be seen in the Denver Museum of Natural History, the New Bedford Whaling Museum in Massachusetts, and Whaleworld, a museum in Albany, Western Australia. He is the author of more than 100 magazine articles, which have appeared in National Geographic, Natural History, Audubon, Curator, National Wildlife, Geo, Australian Geographic, and Reader's Digest. He has written 23 books, including The Book of Sharks, The Book of Whales, Dolphins and Porpoises, Men and Whales, Great White Shark (with John McCosker), Encyclopedia of the Sea, Aquagenesis: The Origin and Evolution of Life in the Sea, Deep Atlantic, Monsters of the Sea, Imagining Atlantis, Tiger Bone & Rhino Horn, No Turning Back: The Life and Death of Animal Species, Sea Dragons: Predators of Prehistoric Seas, Tuna,[tpl]cite news| url=http://articles.sfgate.com/2008-07-22/entertainment/17173126_1_bluefin-canned-tuna-richard-ellis | work=The San Francisco Chronicle | first=Jason | last=Warshof | date=July 22, 2008 | title=Enjoy the tour, and please don't lick the tuna[/tpl] The Empty Ocean, and Swordfish: A Biography of the Ocean Gladiator. On Thin Ice looks into the changing world of polar bears and highlights their problems caused by global warming and disappearing Arctic ice.http://www.npr.org/templates/story/story.php?storyId=120668816&ft=1&f=1032 In 2011 the University Press of Kansas published The Great Sperm Whale: A Natural History of the Ocean's Most Magnificent and Mysterious Creature. Richard Ellis has been named Curator for a show on sharks in art for the Fort Lauderdale Art Museum, scheduled to open in May 2012.http://www.moafl.org/museum#/current-exhibitions/shark[tpl]update inline|date=July 2013[/tpl]
