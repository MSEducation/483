[[Duckhorn Vineyards]]

CATEGORIES: Wineries in Napa Valley, Companies based in Napa County, California

Duckhorn Vineyards is a boutique winery in St. Helena, California, specializing in premium Merlot wines.

==History==

Founder Dan Duckhorn, a Santa Rosa native and Berkeley graduate, toured the Pomerol and Saint-Émilion in the Bordeaux region of France in the mid-1970s with Ric Forman,[tpl]cite news |work=Wine Spectator |url=http://www.winespectator.com/Wine/Features/0,1197,3922,00.html |title=New investor takes control of Duckhorn Wine Co. |date=July 31, 2007 |accessdate=2007-07-06 |author=James Laube & Daniel Sogg[/tpl] who as winemaker of Sterling Vineyards had introduced vintage-dated Merlot wine to California.  He returned to California with his then-wife Margaret (the couple divorced in 2000) to promote a new market for Merlot.  They founded Duckhorn Vineyards in 1976.
Production grew from 1,600 cases of Cabernet Sauvignon and Merlot in 1978 to more than 20,000 cases in 2006. Beginning in 1988, Duckhorn purchased seven estate vineyards on Howell Mountain and the valley floor,[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.duckhornvineyards.com/ |accessdate=2007-07-06 |title=Continuing the Tradition[/tpl] for a total of [tpl]convert|168|acre|ha|1[/tpl] in Napa Valley, and four vineyards totaling [tpl]convert|153|acre|ha|1[/tpl] in the Anderson Valley, Mendocino County.
In July, 2007, GI Partners, a private equity firm, purchased a controlling interest in Duckhorn Wine Company and with it Duckhorn's various brands and operations[tpl]cite news |date=July 2007 |work=Wine Enthusiast |title=Duckhorn Wine Company sells controlling share |url=http://www.winemag.com/ME2/dirmod.asp?sid=&nm=&type=Publishing&mod=Publications%3A%3AArticle&mid=8F3A7027421841978F18BE895F87F791&tier=4&id=1C2856E51D3E4D67A1ACD94E6CA8F4FC |author=Steve Heimoff |accessdate=2007-08-01[/tpl] at a valuation believed to exceed $250 million.[tpl]cite news |work=San Francisco Chronicle |accessdate=2007-08-01 |date=2007-08-01 |title=Napa Valley stunner - famed winery sold |url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?file=/c/a/2007/08/01/MNFBRAN0I3.DTL |author =Jon Bonné[/tpl]

==Production==

Duckhorn Vineyards produces several single-vineyard Merlots and Cabernet Sauvignons, as well as a highly regarded but less-expensive Sauvignon blanc.  In 2005, the San Francisco Chronicle proclaimed Dan Duckhorn its "Winemaker of the Year" and named four of his wines to its list of "Top 100 Wines of 2005."  In addition to Duckhorn, Duckhorn Wine Company also operates Goldeneye, a maker of Pinot noir in Anderson Valley, and Paraduxx, a blend of Zinfandel and Cabernet Sauvignon made at a winery on the Silverado Trail between Yountville and Oakville, California.[tpl]cite news |work=San Francisco Chronicle |accessdate=2007-08-01 |url=http://www.sfgate.com/cgi-bin/article.cgi?f=/c/a/2005/12/08/WIGHOG1BSB1.DTL |date=2005-12-08 |author=Linda Murphy |title=Winemaker of the Year: Duckhorn takes wing[/tpl]  The company also labels under the names "Decoy" and "Migration."
Some of Duckhorn's Napa Vineyards in the Pope Valley region are managed by Oscar Renteria.Alan Goldfarb "Tending the Vines of the Napa Valley: An interview with Salvador and Oscar Renteria" Appellation America, November 1st, 2006
