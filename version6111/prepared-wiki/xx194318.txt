[[La Boîte à merveilles]]

CATEGORIES: Moroccan novels, 1954 novels, French-language novels, Autobiographical novels, Novels set in Morocco

La Boîte à merveilles is an autobiographical 1954 novel by Moroccan writer Ahmed Sefrioui. 
It was written in 1952, and was long thought to be the first Moroccan novel written in the French language.[tpl]efn|In fact the first Moroccan novel written in French was Mosaïques ternies (Tarnished Mosaics) by the writer Abdelkader Chatt of Tangier, published in France in 1930 under the pseudonym Benazous of Chatt.[/tpl]  
The novel presents a slice of life within the context of North African Francophone literature of the century.
The book follows the Maghreb tradition of an autobiographical style, written in the first person.[tpl]sfn|Bonn|Rothe|1995|p=40[/tpl]
It explores, through an adult's memories of commonplace events in his childhood, 
his perception and emotional relationship with his parents.[tpl]sfn|Bonn|Baumstimler|1991|p=51[/tpl][tpl]sfn|Bonn|Baumstimler|1991|p=55[/tpl]
Surrounded by the love, passion and drama of life, in quiet times the boy can always return to his secret box of marvels.[tpl]sfn|Gaudio|1982|p=236[/tpl]
The novel has been recognized by critics as exceptional in style and content.[tpl]sfn|Alami|Martini-Valat|2005|p=40[/tpl]
It has been described as a classic work.[tpl]sfn|Benchama|Groux|2011|p=190[/tpl]
The novel has been used as a vehicle for teaching French to students of Maghreb origin, since they can more easily relate to the experiences of the author than they would to books written by and about French people.[tpl]sfn|Benchama|Groux|2011|p=197[/tpl]
Extracts from the book has been used for the first year Baccalauréat examinations by the French Ministry of Education.[tpl]sfn|Omar|2012[/tpl]
