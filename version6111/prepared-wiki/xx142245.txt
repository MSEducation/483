[[Benjamín Noval]]

CATEGORIES: 1979 births, Living people, People from Mieres, Asturias, Spanish male cyclists, Tour de France cyclists

Benjamín Noval González (born 23 January 1979) is a former Spanish professional road bicycle racer, who competed as a professional between 2001 and 2013.[tpl]cite news|last=Hood|first=Andrew|title=Benjamin Noval, Contador's longtime lieutenant, retires from the peloton|url=http://velonews.competitor.com/2013/10/news/benjamin-noval-contadors-longtime-lieutenant-retires-from-the-peloton_305874|work=VeloNews|publisher=Competitor Group, Inc.|date=15 October 2013|accessdate=15 October 2013[/tpl]
Born in Mieres, Asturias, Noval turned professional in 2001, riding for Relax-Gam for three years before switching to [tpl]ct|USP|2004[/tpl] (later Discovery Channel), where he stayed for four years. Noval switched to Astana for 2008, and for the 2011 season joined compatriots and team-mates Alberto Contador, Jesús Hernández and Daniel Navarro in signing for [tpl]ct|SAX|2011[/tpl].http://www.cyclingnews.com/news/saxo-bank-sungard-signs-contadors-top-spaniards At the end of the 2013 racing season Noval announced his retirement from professional road racing, despite being offered a contract by [tpl]ct|SAX|2014|nolink=yes[/tpl] for the following season.
