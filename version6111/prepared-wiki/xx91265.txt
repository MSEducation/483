==Life and career==

Geary was born in Coalville, Utah, the son of a homemaker and a contractor.May 29th Anthony Geary Biography (1947-)  attended the University of Utah in Salt Lake City, Utah, and made his television debut in the episode of ABC comedy series Room 222. He later  guest-starred on an early episode of All in the Family with fellow future soap star Phil Carey, and well in The Mod Squad, Mannix, Marcus Welby, M.D., The Streets of San Francisco and Barnaby Jones.
Geary's first daytime role was in NBC soap opera Bright Promise from 1971 to 1972.[tpl]cite web|url=http://movies.yahoo.com/person/anthony-geary/biography.html |title=Anthony Geary Biography - Yahoo! Movies |publisher=Movies.yahoo.com |date= |accessdate=2013-07-08[/tpl] He later played the rapist George Curtis in The Young and the Restless, and in 1978 was cast as Luke Spencer in ABC soap General Hospital. His character in General Hospital began as a hit man and later as a rapist who fell in love and subsequently married his victim, Laura Webber (played by Genie Francis).
Geary was originally hired for a 13-week in General Hospital, which grew into a full contract role.[tpl]cite web|last=West|first=Abby|title=Tony Geary Reflects on 30 Years of GH|url=http://www.ew.com/ew/article/0,,20187225,00.html|work=Entertainment Weekly|publisher=ew.com|accessdate=September 8, 2012|date=2008-03-31[/tpl] Geary received Daytime Emmy nominations for Outstanding Lead Actor in a Drama Series in 1980, 1981, and had his first win in 1982.[tpl]cite news|title=Lucci Gets Her First Emmy|url=http://community.seattletimes.nwsource.com/archive/?date=19990522&slug=lucc22|work=The Seattle Times|publisher=seattletimes.com|accessdate=September 8, 2012|agency=Associated Press|date=1999-05-22[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=DeGeneres, ‘General Hospital’ win Emmys|url=http://today.msnbc.msn.com/id/12533996/ns/today-entertainment/t/degeneres-general-hospital-win-emmys/#.UEt4yxjA37g|work=MSNBC|publisher=msnbc.com|accessdate=September 8, 2012|agency=Associated Press|date=2006-05-03[/tpl] Geary was nominated again in 1983, before leaving the show in 1984.
In 1991, Geary returned to General Hospital as Luke's cousin and look-alike Bill Eckert, due to the actor's desire to play something other than Luke. However, due to audience feedback and demand, the character was killed off and Geary resumed the role of Luke in 1993. Geary was nominated in 1997 and 1998 for Daytime Emmys for Outstanding Lead Actor, and had his second win in 1999, and his third in 2000. He received another nomination in 2003, and had his fourth win in 2004.
