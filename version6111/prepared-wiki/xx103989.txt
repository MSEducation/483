[[Clash of the Wolves]]

CATEGORIES: 1925 films, United States National Film Registry films, Black-and-white films, American films, American silent feature films, 1920s adventure films, Films about dogs, Warner Bros. films, 1920s Western (genre) films, American Western (genre) films

Clash of the Wolves is a 1925 silent film produced by and distributed through Warner Brothers. It is an extant film and stars canine actor Rin Tin Tin Clash of the Wolves at silentera.com
It was filmed on location in Chatsworth, California and at what would, decades later, become the Joshua Tree National Park. 
Rin Tin Tin featured as the half-breed leader of a wolf pack, named 'Lobo' (despite Rin Tin Tin bearing little resemblance to a wolf).  At one point, 'Lobo' is forced to wear false chin whiskers and leather booties in order to elude the law. 
The leading man is Charles Farrell.
In 2004, Clash of the Wolves was deemed "culturally, historically, or aesthetically significant" by the United States Library of Congress and selected for preservation in the National Film Registry.
