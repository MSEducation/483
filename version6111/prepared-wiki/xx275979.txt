[[Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral (Indianapolis)]]

CATEGORIES: Religious organizations established in 1892, Roman Catholic churches completed in 1907, Roman Catholic cathedrals in the United States, Roman Catholic Archdiocese of Indianapolis, Roman Catholic churches in Indiana, Neoclassical architecture in Indiana, Churches in Indianapolis, Indiana, 1892 establishments in the United States

Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral is a Catholic cathedral located in Indianapolis, Indiana, United States. It is the seat of the Archdiocese of Indianapolis.  St. John the Evangelist Church in Indianapolis served as the Proto-cathedral from 1871-1907.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.stjohnsindy.org/who-we-are/our-history|title=Our History|publisher=St. John the Evangelist Church|accessdate=2011-09-26|last=|first= [/tpl]  Saints Peter and Paul parish was established in 1892.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.archindy.org/parishes/listings/001.html |title=Saints Peter and Paul Cathedral |publisher= Archdiocese of Indianapolis |accessdate=2011-09-26|last=|first=[/tpl]  The cathedral itself was built between 1905 and 1907.  The Diocese of Vincennes was officially renamed the Diocese of Indianapolis by Pope Leo XIII on March 28, 1898.[tpl]cite web|url= http://www.catholic-hierarchy.org/diocese/dindi.html|title=Archdiocese of Indianapolis|publisher=Catholic-Hierarchy|accessdate=2011-09-26|last=|first=[/tpl]
