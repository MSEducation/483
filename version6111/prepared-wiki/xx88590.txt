John Balance (first name also spelled Jhon and Jhonn; born Geoffrey Laurence Burton, later taking his stepfather's surname of Rushton; 16 February 1962 – 13 November 2004), was an English[tpl]cite web|title=JOHN BALANCE|url=http://www.brainwashed.com/coil/info/john.html|publisher=http://www.brainwashed.com|accessdate=27 April 2012[/tpl] musician and a co-founder of the experimental music group Coil, in collaboration with his partner Peter 'Sleazy' Christopherson.[tpl]citation |title= Obituary: John Balance |first=Richard |last=Smith |date=11 December 2004 |periodical=The Guardian |accessdate=22 August 2007 |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/obituaries/story/0,3604,1371372,00.html[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=The First Five Minutes After Death: Various Artists Remember Peter "Sleazy" Christopherson (UPDATE: Genesis P-Orridge eulogy)|url=e First Five Minutes After Death: Various Artists Remember Peter “Sleazy” Christopherson|work=Steriogum|publisher=BUZZMedia|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Brandon|author2=Genesis P-Orridge|date=29 November 2010[/tpl]  He was responsible for vocals, lyrics, chants, synthetics and various esoteric sound-making instruments and devices.  Outside of Coil he collaborated with Nurse with Wound, Death in June, Psychic TV, Current 93, Chris & Cosey,[tpl]cite web|title=Coil|url=http://old.disinfo.com/archive/pages/dossier/id344/pg1/index.html|publisher=disinformation|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Scott Treleaven|date=11 March 2001[/tpl] Thighpaulsandra and produced several Nine Inch Nails remixes.[tpl]cite web|title=Coil and the Occult|url=http://www.nachtkabarett.com/coil|publisher=http://www.nachtkabarett.com|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Nick Kushner|format=Article|year=2004–2012[/tpl] His early work and wide-ranging collaborations made him one of the most influential figures in the industrial, experimental minimalist and neofolk music scenes.
Balance died on 13 November 2004, after falling from a two story balcony at his home.[tpl]cite web|title=John Balance 1962–2004|url=http://www.heathenharvest.com/staticpages/index.php?page=20041209134726123|publisher=http://www.heathenharvest.com|accessdate=27 April 2012[/tpl]  Peter Christopherson announced Balance's death on the Threshold House website, and provided details surrounding the tragedy. Balance's memorial service was held near Bristol on 23 November, and was attended by approximately 100 people.[tpl]cite web|title=John Balance|url=http://www.thresholdhouse.com/2004/11/30/john-balance/|publisher=Threshold House|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Peter 'Sleazy' Christopherson|date=30 November 2004[/tpl] Ossian Brown, a long term collaborator and member of Coil, dedicated his first book Haunted Air to Balance.[tpl]cite web|title=Ossian Brown, "Haunted Air"|url=http://brainwashed.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=9011:ossian-brown-qhaunted-airq&catid=17:books&Itemid=134|publisher=http://brainwashed.com|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Justin Patrick|format=Review|date=13 November 2011[/tpl]

==Discography==

Balance first recorded using the alias "Merderwerkers". The Merderwerkers track, "Blue Funk (Scars for E)", was included on the Sterile Records cassette compilation Standard Response. Balance also published an underground zine, Stabmental,[tpl]cite web|title=Stabmental|url=http://culturalamnesia.com/misc.php?id=36|publisher=http://culturalamnesia.com|accessdate=27 April 2012[/tpl] and released a track, "A Thin Veil of Blood", also using the nom du guerre Stabmental. "A Thin Veil of Blood" was included on the cassette compilation Deleted Funtime – Various Tunes for Various Loons. Balance then joined up with Peter Christopherson and Boyd Rice to record Nightmare Culture under the moniker "The Sickness of Snakes". Balance subsequently joined Psychic TV and performed alongside Christopherson; however in 1984, Balance and Christopherson left the group to develop Coil.[tpl]cite web|title=They Are Going To Take Me Away Ha Ha, 1984|url=http://www.brainwashed.com/coil/text.php|work=Coil Text Archive|publisher=Brainwashed Inc.|accessdate=27 April 2012|format=Interview from 'zine (PDF)|year=1984[/tpl]  A short collaboration with Zos Kia produced the split tape Transparent.[tpl]cite web|title=Introduction: COIL – Interview from 1986|url=http://datacide.c8.com/coil-interview-from-1986-plus-introduction/|publisher=Datacide|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Christoph Fringeli|format=Interview|year=1986[/tpl]  Credit for the album was shared, and marked Coil's first release. The original Coil / Zos Kia tape, Transparent, was released as a "His-Storical" CD reissue in 1997.[tpl]cite web|title=Coil: news 1997|url=http://www.brainwashed.com/coil/news1997.php|publisher=Brainwashed Inc.|accessdate=27 April 2012|author=Coil|date=14 July 1997[/tpl]
During Coil's 23 year career, Balance collaborated with a number of his peers, including Jim Thirlwell/Clint Ruin (Foetus), Marc Almond, Thighpaulsandra, NON, Current 93, and CoH; appearing on many of these artists' albums.
