[[André Teixeira]]

CATEGORIES: 1974 births, Living people, Brazilian swimmers, Swimmers at the 1992 Summer Olympics, Swimmers at the 1995 Pan American Games, Swimmers at the 1996 Summer Olympics, Olympic swimmers of Brazil, People from Rio de Janeiro (city), Male freestyle swimmers, World Aquatics Championships medalists in swimming

André Luiz Castro Teixeira (born April 24, 1974 in Rio de Janeiro) is a former international butterfly and freestyle swimmer from Brazil. He participated in two consecutive Summer Olympics for his native country, starting in 1992. His best result was in Atlanta, Georgia where he got the 10th place in the men's 4×200-metre freestyle.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|year = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/te/andre-luiz-teixeira-1.html|accessdate =April 23, 2013[/tpl]
At the 1992 Summer Olympics, in Barcelona, Teixeira finished 19th in the 200-metre butterfly.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|year = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/te/andre-luiz-teixeira-1.html|accessdate =April 23, 2013[/tpl]
Teixeira participated at the 1993 FINA World Swimming Championships (25 m), in Palma de Mallorca, where he finished 6th in the 200-metre butterfly,Resultsswimrankings breaking the South American record, with a time of 1:57.06. He also finished 10th in the 100-metre butterfly.
At the 1994 World Aquatics Championships, held in September in Rome, Italy, the Brazilian got the bronze in the 4×100-metre freestyle, and finished 20th in the 100-metre butterfly.[tpl]cite web|title = Results at 1994 Rome|work=USA Swimming|year = 2013|url = http://www.usaswimming.org/_Rainbow/Documents/26cf958f-cf83-4b68-8c90-349d985a5a1e/94_worlds.pdf|accessdate =April 23, 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web | title = Brazil in the World Championships | work = CBDA | date = March 26, 2006 | url = http://www.cbda.org.br/especiais/o-brasil-nos-mundiais-id-6785 | accessdate = April 23, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl]
Teixeira swam at the 1995 Pan American Games held in March in Argentina, where he won a silver medal in the 4×100-metre medley, and a bronze medal in the 200-metre butterfly.[tpl]cite web | title = Brazil medals at 1995 Pan | work = UOL | year = 2007 | url = http://pan.uol.com.br/pan/2007/historia/1995/nopodio.jhtm | accessdate = April 23, 2013[/tpl] [tpl]pt icon[/tpl]
At the 1996 Summer Olympics, in Atlanta, Teixeira finished 10th in the 4×200-metre freestyle, and 32nd in the 100-metre butterfly.[tpl]cite web|title = Sports Reference Profile|work=Sports Reference|year = 2013|url = http://www.sports-reference.com/olympics/athletes/te/andre-luiz-teixeira-1.html|accessdate =April 23, 2013[/tpl]
