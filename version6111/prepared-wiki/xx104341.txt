==Recordings==

Latin bandleader Xavier Cugat recorded a version of "Mary Ann" in the late 1940s. During the 1956-57 American calypso craze, the Easy Riders, Burl Ives and other interpreters of folk music further popularized the calypso, generally under the title of "Marianne".  The song continued to be a favorite with steelbands and calypso entertainers at Caribbean tourist hotels for many years.
The most popular version was recorded by Terry Gilkyson and The Easy Riders (#4 on the Billboard Top 100);[tpl]citation |url=http://books.google.com/books?id=PgGqNrqfrsoC&lpg=PT19&dq=marianne%20%22Terry%20Gilkyson%22%20billboard&pg=PT19#v=onepage&q=marianne&f=false |title=The Billboard Book of Number 1 Hits |page=19 |year=2003 |publisher=Random House (retrieved via Google Books) |author=Bronson, Fred[/tpl] another version was recorded by The Hilltoppers in 1957 (#3 on the Billboard Top 100).
Trini Lopez included "Marianne" on his album Trini Lopez at PJ's on Reprise Records RS-6093.
