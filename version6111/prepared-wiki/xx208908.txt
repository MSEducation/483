[[Levoamphetamine]]

CATEGORIES: Amphetamines, Drugs acting on the cardiovascular system, Enantiopure drugs, TAAR1 agonists, Amphetamine

Levoamphetamine (also levamfetamine, (R)-amphetamine or (âˆ’)-amphethamine) is a psychostimulant known to increase wakefulness and concentration in association with decreased appetite and fatigue. Levoamphetamine is the levorotatory stereoisomer of the amphetamine molecule.

==Chemistry==

Levoamphetamine is the levorotary stereoisomer of the amphetamine molecule. The amphetamine racemate contains two stereoisomers, dextroamphetamine and levoamphetamine, both exhibiting amphetaminergic (amphetamine-like) effects.

==Formulations==

===Adderall===

The drug Adderall contains 25% levoamphetamine salts and 75% dextroamphetamine salts.[ref]

===Benzedrine===

Benzedrine was a racemic (1:1 racemate) mixture of dextroamphetamine and levoamphetamine introduced in the United States as an inhaler in 1933http://www.merriam-webster.com/dictionary/benzedrine for decongestant uses. Benzedrine was converted into tablet form in 1938 after it was discovered that amphetamine could treat narcolepsy and behavioral disorders.
