==Early life==

Gugu Mbatha-Raw was born Gugulethu Sophia Mbatha[tpl]IMDb name|1813221|Gugu Mbatha-Raw[/tpl] in Oxford, England. Her name, "Gugulethu", is a contraction of igugu lethu, means "our pride" (depending on context) in Zulu, like the township of the same name.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-features/5395153/Hamlet-with-Jude-Law-at-Wyndhams-Theatre-Gugu-Mbatha-Raw-is-mad-for-it.html | location=London | work=The Daily Telegraph | first=Georgia | last=Dehn | title=Hamlet with Jude Law at Wyndham's Theatre: Gugu Mbatha-Raw is mad for it | date=28 May 2009[/tpl][tpl]cite news| url=http://www.hollywoodreporter.com/news/jj-abrams-finds-undercovers-female-19431 | work=The Hollywood Reporter | first=Nellie | last=Andreeva | title=J.J. Abrams finds 'Undercovers' female lead | date=13 October 2010[/tpl] Her mother, Anne Raw, is an English nurse, and her father, Patrick Mbatha, is an African doctor, originally from the Republic of South Africa.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.complexmag.ca/pop-culture/2012/06/the-50-hottest-biracial-women/gugu-mbatha-raw |title=Gugu Mbatha-Raw — The 50 Hottest Biracial Women |publisher=Complexmag.ca |date=12 June 2012 |accessdate=28 February 2014[/tpl] Mbatha-Raw grew up in the small town of Witney in Oxfordshire, and attended The Henry Box School, before moving to London to train at the Royal Academy of Dramatic Arts in 2001.

==Career==

Mbatha-Raw played minor roles on television series such as Bad Girls (2006), Doctor Who (2007) and Marple (2007). One of her earliest breakthroughs in drama was in Romeo and Juliet at the Royal Exchange Theatre, Manchester in 2005.[tpl]Cite news |title=Romeo and Juliet - Royal Exchange, Manchester |last=Gardner |first=Lyn |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/stage/2005/sep/15/theatre |newspaper=The Guardian |date=15 September 2005 |accessdate=2012-04-07[/tpl] Mbatha-Raw was nominated for best actress in the Manchester Evening News Theatre Awards for her portrayal of Juliet and also played Cleopatra in the Anthony and Cleopatra at the same theatre in 2005.[tpl]Cite news |title=Hamlet with Jude Law at Wyndham's Theatre: Gugu Mbatha-Raw is mad for it |last=Dehn |first=Georgia |url=http://www.telegraph.co.uk/culture/theatre/theatre-features/5395153/Hamlet-with-Jude-Law-at-Wyndhams-Theatre-Gugu-Mbatha-Raw-is-mad-for-it.html |newspaper=The Telegraph |date=28 May 2009 |accessdate=2012-04-07[/tpl]
In September 2010, she starred in the J. J. Abrams TV series Undercovers, which was cancelled two months later.[tpl]cite news|title=NBC cancels 'Undercovers' after 13 episodes |url=http://content.usatoday.com/communities/entertainment/post/2010/11/nbc-cancels-undercovers-after-13-episodes-/1|accessdate=4 November 2010|last=Oldenburg|first=Ann|newspaper=USA Today|date=4 November 2010[/tpl][tpl]cite news|last=Carter|first=Bill|title=NBC Cancels ‘Undercovers’|url=http://artsbeat.blogs.nytimes.com/2010/11/04/nbc-cancels-undercovers/|accessdate=4 November 2010|newspaper=New York Times|date=4 November 2010[/tpl]
In June 2011, Mbatha-Raw was cast as the female lead in the Fox pilot Touch opposite Kiefer Sutherland.[tpl]cite news | url = http://www.deadline.com/2011/06/undercovers-star-gugu-mbatha-raw-set-as-female-lead-in-foxs-pilot-touch/ | title = 'Undercovers' Star Gugu Mbatha-Raw Set As Female Lead In Fox's Pilot 'Touch' | last = Andreeva | first = Nellie |date = 10 June 2011 | work = Deadline.com | accessdate = 10 June 2011 [/tpl]

In 2014 she appeared in Amma Asante's Belle, playing the eponymous character, Dido Elizabeth Belle, a mixed-race aristocrat in 18th century England.[tpl]cite web|last=Obenson|first=Tambay A.|title=Gugu Mbatha-Raw Will Be "Belle" In Slavery Pic Based On Mixed-Race Woman Raised In Aristocratic Family|url=http://blogs.indiewire.com/shadowandact/0ae00390-9ec3-11e1-bcc4-123138165f92#|work=Shadow and Act|publisher=Indiewire|accessdate=28 February 2014[/tpl] The same year she had a supporting role in Jupiter Ascending and played the lead character in Gina Prince-Bythewood's film Blackbird.
