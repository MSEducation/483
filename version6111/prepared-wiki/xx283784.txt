[[Mario Dubsky]]

CATEGORIES: 1939 births, 1985 deaths, Alumni of the Slade School of Art, 20th-century British painters, British muralists

Mario Dubsky (1939 - 1985) was an artist born in London to Viennese Jewish parents who had converted to Christianity.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oxforddnb.com/index/101064268/Mario-Dubsky |title=Mario Dubsky |publisher=Oxforddnb.com |date= |accessdate=2013-11-19[/tpl]
Accepted at the Slade School of Fine Art aged 17, Dubsky's early work was influenced by the work of the Anglo-Jewish artist David Bomberg, a Slade School alumni of 40 years earlier.Patrick Proctor; Self-Portrait, London, 1991, pp.34-35 ISBN 0297811665
Dubsky was included in the New Generation show at the Whitechapel Gallery in 1966 and 1968. Awarded a Harkness Fellowship he traveled to New York, where he lived from 1969 until 1971  In New York, Dubsky and John Button co-created a large muralRich Wandel, Photo of Agit-Prop Photo Montage Mural by Mario Dubsky and John Button, 1971, 2.4m x 10m, National Archives of Lesbian and Gay History, New York. in paint and collage at the then-headquarters of the Gay Activists Alliance. The mural was lost in the arson attack that destroyed the building.
In the late 1960s, Dubsky developed a more abstract colour field manner of painting with figuration, as in the large-scale Laocconesehttp://www.bbc.co.uk/arts/your_paintings/paintings/laocoonese-42053 [tpl]dead link|date=November 2013[/tpl] of 1968 at University Collece London named after the classical sculpture, the Laocoon.
From the 1970s, Dubsky liked to sketched prehistoric bone and skeleton forms at the Natural History MuseumX Factor, Exhibition Catalogue, intro by Peter de Francia, South London Art Gallery, London, May 1984, p.20 and returned to expressionist figuration. His last solo exhibition X Factor at South London Gallery in 1983 contained Cabaret Valhalla[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.tate.org.uk/art/artworks/dubsky-cabaret-valhalla-t04908 |title='Cabaret Valhalla', Mario Dubsky |publisher=Tate |date= |accessdate=2013-11-19[/tpl] now held by the Tate Gallery. His poems and illustrations in Tom Pilgrim's Progress Among The Consequences of Christianity, London, 1981.Gay Men's Press London 1981, ISN 0907040098 with an introduction by Edward Lucie-Smith, was claimed by the artist as his angry response to the 1977 Blasphemy Trial of Gay News.X Factor, Exhibition Catalogue, South London Art Gallery, London, May 1984 pp.26-30 Dubsky died following a period of illness caused by HIV infection and is buried in Highgate Cemetery.
Dubsky's work is held in a number of public collections.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/arts/yourpaintings/artists/mario-dubsky |title=Watch a slideshow of 12 paintings by Mario Dubsky |publisher=Bbc.co.uk |accessdate=2013-11-19[/tpl]
