[[Piranthus]]

CATEGORIES: Salticidae

Piranthus is a spider genus of the Salticidae family (jumping spiders).
Simon compares this genus with Padilla.Murphy & Murphy 2000: 286
According to Thorell, the genus name is taken from Greek mythology.

==Description==

The female P. decorus is about 9Â mm long. The spider is mostly black, with some white pubescence on the sides of the cephalorthorax, and a dark median stripe in the middle of a wide light median band on the longish abdomen. The female palps are yellowish white. The legs are very short, with the first pair much more robust. They are reddish-brown with black rings.
