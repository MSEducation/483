[[Kristinestad]]

CATEGORIES: Cities and towns in Finland, Populated coastal places in Finland, Municipalities of Ostrobothnia (region), Grand Duchy of Finland, Populated places established in 1649

Kristinestad  ([tpl]lang-fi|Kristiinankaupunki[/tpl]) is a town and a municipality in Finland. It is located in the western part of Finland on the shore of the Bothnian Sea. The population of Kristinestad is [tpl]Formatnum: [tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/population count|Kristinestad[/tpl][/tpl] ([tpl]#time: j F Y|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/population count|sourcedate[/tpl] [/tpl])  and the municipality covers an area of [tpl]convert|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/land area|Kristinestad[/tpl]|km2|abbr=on[/tpl] (excluding sea areas) of which [tpl]convert|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/inland waters area|Kristinestad[/tpl]|km2|abbr=on[/tpl] is inland water ([tpl]#time: j F Y|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/total area|sourcedate[/tpl] [/tpl]). The population density is [tpl]convert|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/population density|Kristinestad|round=2[/tpl]|PD/km2|abbr=on[/tpl]. The population is bilingual with a majority speaking Swedish ([tpl]Formatnum:[tpl]pct|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/native language Swedish|Kristinestad[/tpl]|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/native language total|Kristinestad[/tpl]|0[/tpl][/tpl]) and the minority Finnish ([tpl]Formatnum:[tpl]pct|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/native language Finnish|Kristinestad[/tpl]|[tpl]Infobox Finnish Municipality/native language total|Kristinestad[/tpl]|0[/tpl][/tpl]). The town was chartered in 1649 at Koppö island and is named for Queen Christina of Sweden. Kristinestad is known for its old town with low wooden houses and narrow alleys.
In April, 2011, Kristinestad became Finland's first Cittaslow community.
