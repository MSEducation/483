[[Vishal (actor)]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, Male actors from Tamil Nadu, Indian male film actors, Male actors in Tamil cinema, Telugu people, Don Bosco schools alumni, Loyola College, Chennai alumni, 21st-century Indian male actors

Vishal Krishna Reddy, known by his stage name Vishal, is an Indian film actor who appears in Tamil cinema. Often starring in his home productions, Vishal became an actor after a stint as an assistant director in films.
He played his first role in the romantic thriller Chellamae, before going on to secure a run of three consecutive successes with roles as action hero in the 2005 film, Sandakozhi, and with Thimiru in 2006.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.hindu.com/2006/11/26/stories/2006112614460200.htm|title= Actor Vishal riding on success  |date=26 November 2006|work=The Hindu|accessdate=8 February 2010[/tpl] After appearing in two further successful films thamirabarani and malaikottai, the failure of his hyped home production, Sathyam halted his streak. Following further commercial failure thoranai and average grosser theeratha vilayattu pillai, he enjoyed success again with Avan Ivan (2011), which became a significant film in his career.

==Personal life==

Vishal Krishna Reddy was born  to G. K. Reddy and Janaki Devi on the 25th December. His mother tongue is Telugu.[tpl]citation needed|daie=April 2013|date=August 2013[/tpl] His father was a film producer for Telugu and Tamil films and his family settled in Chennai. His elder brother, Vikram Krishna has produced several of Vishal's films and had previously appeared as an actor in small budget films under the screen name of Ajay. vishal's brother is married to Sriya Reddy, who played the love interest of Vishal in his home production, Vishal has also been an assistant director for shankarThimiru.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sify.com/movies/Vikram-Krishna-weds-Shreya-Reddy-imagegallery-Movies-jdwmP9fahjg.html?html=5|title=Vikram Krishna weds Shriya reddy|last=|first=|date=8 March 2008|work=Sify|accessdate=9 January 2011[/tpl] Vishal also has a younger sister, Aishwarya, who is pursuing a degree at college. Vishal obtained his secondary education from Don Bosco Matriculation Higher Secondary School before securing a degree in visual communications from Loyola College, Chennai.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.vishalblog.com/biography/|title=Biography|last=|first=|year=2008|work=Vishalblog.com|accessdate=9 January 2011[/tpl]

==Career==

After completing his degree in Visual Communication at Loyola College, Vishal was able to secure an apprenticeship with film-maker Ram Gopal Varma but his parents refused and wanted him to work in South Indian films. Vishal subsequently went on to assist actor-director Arjun in his venture Vedham.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.telugucinema.com/c/publish/stars/interview_vishal_2006.php |title=Star Interviews : Hero Vishal: Interview and profile |publisher=Telugucinema.com |date= |accessdate=2013-05-10[/tpl] Whilst on the sets of Arjun film, a producer spotted Vishal on the sets and signed him to appear in Gandhi Krishna's directorial debut, Chellamae.[tpl]cite web|author=|date=20 September 2004|title=Interview with Chellamae Vishal|publisher=IndiaGlitz|accessdate=2011-01-07|url=http://www.indiaglitz.com/channels/tamil/interview/6154.html[/tpl] Accepting the role, Vishal prepared by joining Koothu-P-Pattarai to hone his acting skills and played the protagonist role of Raghunandan whose wife, played by Reemma Sen is kidnapped by her childhood friend, portrayed by Bharath. The film opened to rave reviews and subsequently went on to become a commercial success, but Vishal's performance was overshadowed by Reemma Sen's and Bharath's with a critic claiming his performance is "apt" but "there was plenty left to hone".[tpl]cite web|author=Rangarajan, Malathi|date=17 September 2004|title=Chellamae|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.hinduonnet.com/thehindu/fr/2004/09/17/stories/2004091702810300.htm[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=|date=20 September 2004|title=Chellamey|publisher=Sify|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.sify.com/movies/chellamay-review-tamil-13563830.html[/tpl] His next film was Sandakozhi with Linguswamy who had previously worked with his father's production house as an assistant director. The action film co-starring Meera Jasmine and Rajkiran, upon release became a blockbuster and its Telugu dubbing also created an impact.[tpl]cite web|author= Prasad, G.|date=11 March 2006|title=Riding high on crest of success|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.hindu.com/mp/2006/03/11/stories/2006031100490100.htm[/tpl] The film also gained critical acclaim and Vishal was described to have "improved from his Chellamay days" and "clicked as an action hero".[tpl]cite web|author=|date=14 November 2005|title=Sandakozhi|publisher=Sify|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.sify.com/movies/sandakozhi-review-tamil-14049504.html[/tpl] The success of Sandakozhi saw further opportunities arise for Vishal, with film journalists claiming that he was "the fastest rising action hero" of the time.[tpl]cite web|author=|date=9 May 2006|title=Vishal-The dark horse!|publisher=Sify|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.sify.com/movies/vishal-the-dark-horse-news-tamil-kkfvlxaideb.html[/tpl] After a cameo as himself in Sasi's Dishyum, he went on to appear alongside Reemma Sen for the second time and Sriya Reddy in Tarun Gopi's action film, Thimiru. The film opened to mixed reviews with Vishal being praised for his intense performance with a critic claiming his performance "is the film's only strength and, to an extent, making up for the weak script and poor characterisation".[tpl]cite web|author=Sudha, S|date=4 August 2006|title=Thimiru is a time-pass flick|publisher=Rediff|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2006/aug/04thimuru.htm[/tpl] The film eventually went on to become Vishal's third consecutive commercial success, and he began to emerge as a new leading actor in Tamil films.[tpl]cite web|author=|date=8 August 2006|title=Vishal alters the list of top heroes in Kollywood!!|publisher=Behindwoods|accessdate=2011-01-09|url=http://www.behindwoods.com/tamil-movie-articles/movies-03/08-08-06-vishal.html[/tpl]
His next film, the political drama Sivappathigaram directed by Karu Pazhaniappan and featuring Mamta Mohandas and Raghuvaran became a failure financially despite favourable reviews.[tpl]cite web|author=Rajaraman, R|date=25 November 2006|title=Sivapathigaram Review|publisher=Nowrunning.com|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.nowrunning.com/movie/3341/tamil/sivapathigaram/958/review.htm[/tpl] His next released in January 2008, Hari's multi-starring family drama Thaamirabharani and the film went on to gain considerable box-office success, carrying good reports amidst other big budget releases.[tpl]cite web|author=Iyer, Sriram|date=14 January 2007|title=Thamirabharani is worth a watch|publisher=Rediff|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/jan/17ssth.htm[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=|date=18 January 2007|title=Box-office analysis|publisher=IndiaGlitz|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.indiaglitz.com/channels/tamil/article/28555.html[/tpl] Later in the year he appeared Boopathy Pandian's Malaikottai, a comedy entertainer starring Priyamani. In his first full-length comic role, Vishal received mixed feedback for his portrayal from critics, with Rediff stating that he is "a far cry from the rather endearing young man in Sandakozhi", whilst describing his performance as "cringeable", but another critic cited that he had utilised the opportunity well.[tpl]cite web|author=Srinivasan, Pavithra|date=28 September 2007|title=Malaikottai is avoidable|publisher=Rediff|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.rediff.com/movies/2007/sep/28ssmalai.htm[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=|date=28 September 2007|title=Malaikottai – Masala comedy, no strings attached|publisher=Behindwoods|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.behindwoods.com/tamil-movie-articles/movies-06/malaikottai-review.html[/tpl] Despite mixed reviews, the film took a large opening and became another big success for Vishal.[tpl]cite web|author=S, Aishwarya|date=4 November 2007|title=Fans, frenzy at 50th day celebration of ‘Malaikottai’|publisher=The Hindu|accessdate=2011-01-13|url=http://www.hindu.com/2007/11/04/stories/2007110459380200.htm[/tpl]
