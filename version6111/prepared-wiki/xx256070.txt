[[SS Trondhjemsfjord]]

CATEGORIES: 1911 ships, Tyne-built ships, Ocean liners, Passenger ships of Norway, Steamships of Norway, Norwegian America Line, World War I merchant ships of Norway, Ships sunk by German submarines in World War I, Maritime incidents in 1915

The vessel was launched 21 December 1911 by Northumberland Shipbuilding Company of Newcastle upon Tyne, England, and delivered in March 1912 as SS Cotswold Range for Neptune Steam Navigation Company of Sunderland[tpl]cite web |editor-first=Børge |editor-last=Solem  |editor2-first=Trond |editor2-last=Austheim |url=http://www.norwayheritage.com/p_ship.asp?sh=trofj |title=S/S Trondhjemsfjord (1), NAL - The Norwegian-America Line |website=Norway-Heritage |accessdate=2010-12-29[/tpl] – owned since 1906 by Furness Withy in Hartlepool, UK. The ship was sold to the Norwegian America Line on 23 December 1914 and renamed Trondhjemsfjord.[tpl]cite web |editor-first=S. |editor-last=Swiggum |editor2-first=M. |editor2-last=Kohli |url=http://www.theshipslist.com/ships/lines/neptune.html |title=Neptune Steam Navigation Company |department=The Fleets |website=The Ships List |accessdate=2010-12-29[/tpl]
While sailing from New York to Bergen, Norway, Trondhjemsfjord was torpedoed by German submarine U-41, captained by Claus Hansen, northwest of the Shetland Islands on 28 July 1915. The passengers and crew of the sunken ship were rescued by the sailing vessel Glance; the submarine that sank Trondhjemsfjord was itself sunk on 24 September of the same year.
