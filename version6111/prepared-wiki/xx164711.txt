[[Hugh Brophy]]

CATEGORIES: Republic of Ireland association footballers, Shamrock Rovers F.C. players, League of Ireland players, Bohemian F.C. players, Crystal Palace F.C. players, The Football League players, 1948 births, Living people

Hugh Brophy (born 2 September 1948 in Dublin) was an Irish football player during the 1960s and 1970s.
Brophy was a midfielder who played for Shamrock Rovers and Bohemians amongst others during his career in the League of Ireland.Bohemian FC match programme, Vol. 44, no. 20 Brophy played alongside the likes of Tommy Kelly and Turlough O'Connor during his time at Bohs.
He made his Rovers debut on 20 March 1966 against Drumcondra at Milltown. He signed for Crystal Palace in July 1966 http://www.irishtimes.com/newspaper/archive/1966/0720/Pg003.html#Ar00302:3BAE533F1E6D2CCEC6305EE035EE55372E6F373E543ADE6E3A5EB13FAECB but after only one league appearance http://www.neilbrown.newcastlefans.com/crystalpalace/crystalpalace.html re-signed for Rovers in November 1968 winning the FAI Cup in 1969.
He played for the Republic of Ireland national football team amateur team in the qualifiers for the 1967 UEFA Amateur Cup.
