[[Niall Ronan]]

CATEGORIES: 1982 births, Living people, Sportspeople from County Meath, Irish rugby union players, Leinster Rugby players, Munster Rugby players, Shannon RFC players, Rugby union flankers, Meath Gaelic footballers, Gaelic footballers who switched code, Ireland international rugby union players

Niall Ronan (born 14 September 1982 in Pilltown, Meath) is a retired Irish rugby union player, who played for Leinster, Munster and Ireland during his career. He plays as a Flanker.

==Early years==

Ronan grew up playing Gaelic football and represented Meath GAA in the All-Ireland Minor Football Championship. He won the Senior Schools Leinster Championships in 2000 with St. Mary's, Drogheda.

==Leinster==

Switching from football to rugby, he played at underage level for Leinster and Ireland, and spent four seasons with Leinster.

==Munster==

He moved to Munster in 2007, and made his debut against Scarlets in September of that year.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/286.php|title=Munster See Off Scarlets|date=22 September 2007|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=9 September 2011[/tpl] He was in the Munster team that beat Leinster to win the 2011 Magners League Grand Final.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/rugby/9094.php|title=Munster Crowned Magners League Champions|date=28 May 2011|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=9 September 2011[/tpl]
A knee injury sustained in Munster's Heineken Cup Round 5 clash with Castres Olympique ruled Ronan out for the rest of the 2011–12 season.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10001.php|title=Niall Ronan Gone For The Season|date=18 January 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=20 January 2012[/tpl] He signed a new two-year contract with Munster in March 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/10236.php|title=Ryan Signs On For Two More Years|date=13 March 2012|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=14 March 2012[/tpl]
Ronan earnt his 100th cap for Munster on 3 May 2013, coming on against Zebre in a Pro 12 fixture. On 30 April 2014, it was announced that Ronan was retiring from rugby due to a knee injury sustained in October 2013.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.munsterrugby.ie/news/18431.php|title=Ronan Forced To Retire|date=30 April 2014|work=munsterrugby.ie|accessdate=30 April 2014[/tpl]

==Ireland==

He made his debut for Ireland against Canada on 23 May 2009,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/14596.php|title=Kidney Takes Positives From Winning Start|date=24 May 2009|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=9 September 2011[/tpl] starting and finishing the match in a 25–6 win. He earnt his second cap a week later against USA. He was called up to the squad for the 2010 Summer Tour as a replacement for Kevin McLaughlin and played against the Barbarians, New Zealand Māori and Australia.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/19601.php|title=Ireland Team To Play Australia|date=22 June 2010|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=9 September 2011[/tpl]
Ronan also played for Ireland against Scotland in a 2011 Rugby World Cup warm-up on 6 August 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.irishrugby.ie/news/23473.php|title=Ireland Team To Play Scotland|date=4 August 2011|work=irishrugby.ie|accessdate=9 September 2011[/tpl]
