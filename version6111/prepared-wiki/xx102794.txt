[[Rufus Hollis Gause]]

CATEGORIES: Pentecostal theologians, American theologians, American Pentecostals, 1925 births, Living people, Pentecostal writers

Rufus Hollis Gause (born 1925), known as Dr. Hollis Gause,[tpl]cite journal|last=Land|first=Steven J.|title=TRIBUTE TO DR. R. HOLLIS GAUSE|journal=Wesleyan Theological Journal|date=April 1999|volume=34|series=1|pages=Pg. 261|url=http://wesley.nnu.edu/fileadmin/imported_site/wesleyjournal/1999-wtj-34-1.pdf|accessdate=5 April 2011[/tpl] is a leading Pentecostal theologian in the Church of God (Cleveland). Gause began his teaching career in 1947 at Lee College (Cleveland, Tennessee).  He became the first dean and director of the Church of God School of Theology in 1975.  Gause served as president of the Society for Pentecostal Studies from 1971 to 1972.
Gause has authored books of both a popular and scholarly nature.[tpl]cite web|last=Gause|first=R. Hollis|title=Barnes & Noble|url=http://productsearch.barnesandnoble.com/search/results.aspx?store=book&ATH=R.+Hollis+Gause|publisher=BN.com|accessdate=5 April 2011[/tpl]  In 2006 he co-authored a book with Dr. Kimberly Ervin Alexander entitled Women in Leadership:  A Pentecostal Perspective published by the Church of God Theological Seminary's Center for Pentecostal Leadership and Care.[tpl]cite book |last1=Gause |first1=Hollis|title=Women In Leadership: A Pentecostal Perspective (Pentecostal Leadership Series) |year=2006 |location=Cleveland, TN |isbn=978-1-59684-170-3 |url=http://www.amazon.com/dp/1596841702 |author2=Kimberly Alexander[/tpl]
