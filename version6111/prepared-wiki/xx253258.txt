[[Holland Patent Railroad Station]]

CATEGORIES: Railway stations on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Buildings and structures in Oneida County, New York, Transportation in Oneida County, New York, Railway stations opened in 1890, Stations along New York Central Railroad lines

Holland Patent Railroad Station is a historic train station located at Holland Patent in Oneida County, New York. It was built in 1890 and is a one story, rectangular, wood frame building sheathed in board-and-batten siding.  It was built by the Utica and Black River Railroad, later acquired by New York Central.  It was used until 1960 and now the line is used by the Adirondack Scenic Railroad.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6168|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: Holland Patent Railroad Station|date=December 1999|accessdate=2010-01-08 |author=Raymond W. Smith|publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=6166|title=Accompanying three photos[/tpl]
The building was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 2000, and according to a plaque on the building exterior, was renovated in 2003.
