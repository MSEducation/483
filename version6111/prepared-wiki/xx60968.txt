[[Rich Skrenta]]

CATEGORIES: American computer programmers, MUD developers, 1967 births, Living people, Northwestern University alumni, People from Mt. Lebanon, Pennsylvania, DMOZ

Richard "Rich" Skrenta (born 1967 in Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania) is a computer programmer and Silicon Valley entrepreneur who created the search engine blekko."The Next Google Search Challenger: Blekko"
In 1982, as a high school student at Mt. Lebanon High School, Skrenta wrote the Elk Cloner virus that infected Apple II machines.  It is widely believed to be the first large-scale self-spreading personal computer virus ever created.[tpl]cite news|author=Associated Press|publisher=CTV|url=http://www.ctv.ca/servlet/ArticleNews/story/CTVNews/20070831/virus_prank_070831/20070831?hub=SciTech|title=Prank starts 25 years of computer security woes[/tpl]
Skrenta graduated from Northwestern University. Between 1989 and 1991 he worked at Commodore Business Machines with Amiga Unix. Between 1991 and 1995 he worked at Unix System Labs and from 1996 to 1998 with IP-level encryption at Sun Microsystems. He later left Sun and became one of the founders of DMOZ. He stayed onboard after the Netscape acquisition, and continued to work on the directory as well as Netscape Search, AOL Music and AOL Shopping. After his stint at AOL he went on to cofound Topix LLC, a Web 2.0 company in the news aggregation & forums market.[tpl]cite news|author=USA Today|url=http://www.usatoday.com/tech/webguide/2007-04-01-topix-site_N.htm|title=Interview with Topix founder Rich Skrenta | date=2007-04-01 | accessdate=2010-05-22[/tpl]  In 2005, he and his fellow cofounders sold a 75% share of Topix to a newspaper consortium made up of Tribune, Gannett, and Knight Ridder. Currently, he heads the startup company Blekko Inc, an internet search engine,Start-Up Blekko Tries to Take On Google The Wall Street Journal, October 31, 2010 which began public beta testing on November 1, 2010[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.news-record.com/content/2010/11/02/article/blekko_launches_human_driven_search_engine|title=Blekko launches human-driven search engine|last=Wollman|first=Dana|work=News & Record|agency=Associated Press|date=2010-11-02|accessdate=2010-11-03[/tpl] and
recently gained Marc Andreessen as an investor.Stealth Search Engine Blekko Gets Money From Marc Andreessen, SoftTech TechCrunch, May 14, 2008
He was involved in the development of VMS Monster an old MUD for VMS. VMS Monster was part of the inspiration for TinyMUD. He is also known for his role in developing TASS, an ancestor of tin,Tass threaded newsreader Rich Skrenta's Official Tass Page the popular threaded Usenet newsreader for Unix systems.  In 1989 he started working on a multiplayer simulation game. In 1994 it was launched under the name Olympia as a pay-for-play PBEM game by Shadow Island Games.Olympia homepage
