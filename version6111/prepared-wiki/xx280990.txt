[[Thomas Condon (politician)]]

CATEGORIES: Year of birth missing, Year of death missing, Independent politicians in Ireland, Members of the 2nd Seanad, Members of the 5th Seanad

Thomas Condon was an Irish independent politician. He was a member of Seanad Éireann in 1938 and again in 1944. He was first elected to the 2nd Seanad in April 1938 by the Administrative Panel.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oireachtas.ie/members-hist/default.asp?housetype=1&HouseNum=2&MemberID=1211&ConstID=208|title=Mr. Thomas Condon|work=Oireachtas Members Database|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] 
He lost his seat in the August 1938 Seanad election. He was re-elected by the Industrial and Commercial Panel at the 1944 Seanad election. He was disqualified from the Seanad on 25 October 1944 by virtue of his membership of the Irish Tourist Board.[tpl]cite web|url=http://debates.oireachtas.ie/seanad/1944/10/25/00007.asp|title=Official Report (Seanad Éireann), Volume 29: Vacancy in Seanad – Motion|publisher=Houses of the Oireachtas|date=25 October 1944|accessdate=9 August 2013[/tpl] He was replaced by Frederick Summerfield.[tpl]cite web|url=http://debates.oireachtas.ie/seanad/1945/03/07/00003.asp|title=Official Report (Seanad Éireann), Volume 29: New Senator Takes His Seat|publisher=Houses of the Oireachtas|date=7 March 1945|accessdate=25 August 2013[/tpl]
