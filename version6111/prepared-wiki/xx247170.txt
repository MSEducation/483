[[Knut Brautaset]]

CATEGORIES: 1939 births, Living people, Norwegian engineers, Norwegian expatriates in the United States, University of Agder faculty, Rectors of universities and colleges in Norway, People from Grimstad

Knut Brautaset (born 16 September 1939) is a Norwegian engineer.
He worked as a civil engineer in Detroit, Michigan from 1965 to 1970. When moving back to Norway in 1970, he was hired at the Southern Norway Technical School,[tpl]cite news|title=70 år 16. september: Tidligere rektor Knut Brautaset|agency=Norwegian News Agency|date=1 September 2009|language=Norwegian[/tpl] which changed its name to Agder Regional College of Engineering ([tpl]lang-no|Agder ingeniør- og distriktshøgskole[/tpl]). From 1982 to 1989 he was the rector of this Grimstad-based college.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uia.no/no/portaler/om_universitetet/organisasjonen/historia/hoegskolene_foer_hia|title=De regionale høgskolene|publisher=University of Agder|language=Norwegian|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] When Agder University College was created through a merger of the region's colleges in 1994, Brautaset became the first rector there, and served until 2000.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.uia.no/no/portaler/om_universitetet/organisasjonen/historia/hoegskolen_i_agder|title=De regionale høgskolene|publisher=University of Agder|language=Norwegian|accessdate=8 January 2010[/tpl] He has also been the board chairman of the Norwegian Centre for International Cooperation in Higher Education for six years.
He resides in Grimstad.
