[[Walsall Council election, 2011]]

CATEGORIES: English local elections, 2011, Walsall Council elections

The 2011 Walsall Council election took place on 5 May 2011 to elect members of Walsall Metropolitan Borough Council in the West Midlands, England. One third of the council was up for election and the Conservative party lost overall control of the council to no overall control.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/special/election2011/council/html/cu.stm|title=England council elections|date=10 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl]

==Election result==

The results saw the Conservatives lose their majority on the council after Labour gained 8 seats, including 5 from the Conservatives.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-13285267|title=Labour gains seats on Birmingham City Council|date=6 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl] This meant the Conservatives had 28 councillors compared to 26 for Labour, leaving the Liberal Democrats, down one on five seats, holding the balance.
Following the election the Conservatives continued to run the council as a minority administration.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.bbc.co.uk/news/uk-england-birmingham-13556842|title=Walsall Council opts for minority administration|date=26 May 2011|publisher=BBC News Online|accessdate=24 September 2011[/tpl]
