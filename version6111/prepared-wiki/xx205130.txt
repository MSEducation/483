[[Dennis Tedlock]]

CATEGORIES: American anthropologists, American Mesoamericanists, Mayanists, Translators from Mayan, Tulane University alumni, University at Buffalo faculty, 20th-century Mesoamericanists, American academics of English literature, 1939 births, Living people

Dennis Tedlock (b. June 19, 1939)[tpl]cite linked authority file|id=n82-66635|accessdate=2008-05-26[/tpl] is the McNulty Professor of English and Research Professor of Anthropology at the State University of New York at Buffalo.[tpl]cite web |url=http://epc.buffalo.edu/authors/tedlock/RESUME.htm |title = Dennis Tedlock - resume | accessdate=2008-05-24[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://anthropology.buffalo.edu/webstuff/people/people.htm|title=Department of Anthropology, University of Buffalo, Faculty listing|accessdate=2008-05-24 |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080402125514/http://anthropology.buffalo.edu/webstuff/people/people.htm  |archivedate = 2008-04-02[/tpl] He received his Ph.D. in 1968 from Tulane University. In 1986, he won the 
PEN Translation Prize for his book Popul Vuh: The Mayan Book of the Dawn of Life,[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.pen.org/page.php/prmID/591 | title = PEN translation prize winners | accessdate=2008-05-24[/tpl] and in 1997 was the joint recipient of the American Anthropological Association President's Award, along with his wife, Barbara Tedlock.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.aaanet.org/about/prizes-awards/#president%27s |title=AAA Prizes and Awards |accessdate=2008-05-24 |publisher=American Anthropological Association[/tpl]
Proponent of dialogical anthropology.
