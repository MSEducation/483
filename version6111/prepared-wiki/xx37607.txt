[[Dango]]

CATEGORIES: Japanese words and phrases, Japanese cuisine, Wagashi, Dumplings, Skewered foods

[tpl]nihongo|Dango|団子|[/tpl] is a Japanese dumpling and sweet made from mochiko (rice flour), related to mochi. It is often served with green tea.
Dango is eaten year-round, but the different varieties are traditionally eaten in given seasons. Three to four dango are often served on a skewer.

==Types==

There are many different varieties of dango which are usually named after the various seasonings served on or with it. http://theanimeblog.com/japanese-recipes/dango-digest-a-thorough-look-at-japanese-dumplings-part-two/

==Derived terms==

A common Japanese proverb [tpl]nihongo|“Hana yori dango”|花より団子|extra= literally, “dumplings rather than flowers”[/tpl] refers to a preference for practical things rather than aesthetics.
A hairstyle consisting of dango-like buns on either side of the head is sometimes known as odango.
