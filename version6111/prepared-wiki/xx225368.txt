[[Gymnasium Carolinum (Osnabrück)]]

CATEGORIES: High schools in Germany, Schools in Lower Saxony, Osnabrück, Educational institutions established in the 9th century, 804 establishments

The Gymnasium Carolinum in Osnabrück, Germany, was founded in 804 by Charlemagne, king of the Franks. It is reputedly the oldest school in GermanyThe building of the Gymnasium Carolinum, Osnabrück accessed October 2008 and is also one of the oldest surviving schools in the world.

==History==

===Twentieth Century===

In 1933 the boys' school of the Gymnasium Carolinum had twenty-two teachers, all of whom were Catholics and thirteen of whom had served in the First World War.  Only one had up to that point joined the Nazi party.Panayi, P. Life and Death in a German Town: Osnabrück from the Weimar Republic to World War II and Beyond. New York: Tauris Academic Studies, 2007. p 73-74. Yet Nazi educational policy changed both the curriculum and the views of students. The number of schools hours devoted to physical exercise, history, and geography increased, while those involving foreign languages and religion decreased.[tpl]sfn|Panayi|2007|p=74-75[/tpl] In 1939 essays written by students at the school reflected the new policies and referred frequently to works by Hitler and other Nazi leaders.[tpl]sfn|Panayi|2007|p=76[/tpl]
