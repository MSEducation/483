[[Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics – Men's 400 metre freestyle]]

CATEGORIES: Swimming at the 2008 Summer Olympics

The men's 400 metre freestyle event at the 2008 Olympic Games took place on 9–10 August at the Beijing National Aquatics Center in Beijing, China.[tpl]cite web|url=http://content.usatoday.com/sports/olympics/beijing/schedule.aspx?dc=SW|title=Olympic Swimming Schedule|publisher=USA Today|date=9 August 2008|accessdate=14 May 2013[/tpl]
Reigning world champion Park Tae-Hwan made an Olympic milestone to become South Korea's first ever gold medalist in swimming. He powered past the field to an unexpected triumph in an Asian record of 3:41.86.[tpl]cite news|last=Lohn|first=John|title=Olympics, Swimming: Tae Hwan Park Captures Gold Medal in Men's 400 Free|url=http://www.swimmingworldmagazine.com/lane9/news/18731.asp|publisher=Swimming World Magazine|date=10 August 2008|accessdate=15 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Park secures 400m freestyle gold|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport2/hi/olympics/swimming/7552057.stm|publisher=BBC Sport|date=10 August 2008|accessdate=15 May 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=S. Korean Park wins men's 400m freestyle Olympic gold|url=http://www.chinadaily.com.cn/olympics/2008-08/10/content_6922562.htm|work=Xinhua|publisher=China Daily|date=10 August 2008|accessdate=15 May 2013[/tpl] Coming from sixth place in the final lap, China's Zhang Lin delighted the home crowd with a silver-medal effort in 3:42.44. Meanwhile, U.S. swimmer Larsen Jensen set a new American record of 3:42.78 to take the bronze.[tpl]cite news|title=Park Tae-hwan wins swim event|url=http://www.news24.com/xArchive/Sport/Olympics2008/Park-Tae-hwan-wins-swim-event-20080810|publisher=News24|date=11 August 2008|accessdate=15 May 2013[/tpl]
American Peter Vanderkaay delivered a strong swim with a fourth-place effort in 3:43.11, just 0.35 of a second ahead of Tunisia's Oussama Mellouli, who established a new African record of 3:43.45. Leading the field in the first 100 metres, Australia's Grant Hackett, the silver medalist in the event from Athens, could not keep the pace over the final laps, and settled only for sixth place with a time of 3:43.84. Russian duo Yuri Prilukov (3:43.97) and Nikita Lobintsev (3:48.29) rounded out the finale.
