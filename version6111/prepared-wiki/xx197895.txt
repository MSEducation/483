[[Randi Flesland]]

Randi Runa Svenkerud Flesland (born 17 November 1955 in Oslo) is a Norwegian civil servant.
Flesland was educated at Trinity College, Dublin, with education in business administration, psychology and pedagogy. She has had several leading positions in Norges Statsbaner,[tpl]cite news|first=Henrik D.|last=Meyer|title=- En seier for flysikkerheten|url=http://www.dn.no/forsiden/naringsliv/article675830.ece|work=Dagens NÃ¦ringsliv|language=Norwegian|date=14 December 2005|accessdate=5 October 2008[/tpl] until 2000, when she became acting president. This was against her will, as she originally resigned in protest due to her support of the fired president Osmund Ueland.[tpl]cite news|title=Leder NSB mot sin vilje|date=30 June 2000|agency=Norwegian News Agency|accessdate=23 November 2009[/tpl] Arne Wam took over some days later.[tpl]cite news|title=Siste arbeidsdag i NSB for Randi Flesland|date=3 July 2000|agency=Norwegian News Agency|accessdate=23 November 2009[/tpl]
Later in 2000 she became director of the Norwegian National Airport Administration; later the agency became Avinor. She got fired in December 2005 after a long conflict with the trade unions. She was instead hired in IBM. In 2008 she became the new director of the Norwegian Consumer Council.[tpl]cite press release |title=Flesland i ny sjefsjobb |url=http://www.nrk.no/nyheter/1.6284565 |publisher=Norwegian Consumer Council |date=28 October 2008 |accessdate=16 January 2009[/tpl]
She shares the name Flesland with Norway's second largest airport.
