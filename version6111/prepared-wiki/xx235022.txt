[[St. John's Episcopal Church (Honeoye Falls, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Episcopal churches in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1841, 19th-century Episcopal church buildings, Churches in Monroe County, New York

St. John's Episcopal Church is a historic Episcopal church located at Honeoye Falls in Monroe County, New York.  It is a one story, stone structure built in 1841-1842, with a bell tower added in 1855.  The building features Greek Revival massing and a Doric order portico with Gothic Revival arched windows and doorways.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=4197|title=National Register of Historic Places Registration: St. John's Episcopal Church|date=May 1988|accessdate=2009-10-01 |author=Alicia A. Jettner |publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl] See also: [tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=4198|title=Accompanying seven photos[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1988.
