[[Alton Bay, New Hampshire]]

CATEGORIES: Unincorporated communities in New Hampshire, Unincorporated communities in Belknap County, New Hampshire

Alton Bay is an unincorporated community in the town of Alton, New Hampshire, United States, located on Alton Bay, a [tpl]convert|4|mi|km|adj=mid|-long|0[/tpl] cove of Lake Winnipesaukee which forms the southernmost point on the lake. The village is part of the Lakes Region, a popular resort area of New Hampshire.
Alton Bay is located at the intersection of New Hampshire Route 11 and Route 28A, [tpl]convert|1|mi[/tpl] north of the center of Alton.  Route 11 is an east-west highway that crosses the entire state of New Hampshire, connecting Laconia to the west and Rochester to the east.  Route 28A is a local highway that joins Route 28 [tpl]convert|4|mi|0[/tpl] north of Alton Bay.
In 1851 the village became the terminus of the Cochecho Railroad from Dover, New Hampshire.Report of the Directors of the Boston & Maine Railroad to the Stockholders, September 12, 1849 The rail line became the Lake Shore Railroad in 1890, continuing west to Lakeport in Laconia.  It was in operation until 1935.  The MS Mount Washington and its predecessor ships on Lake Winnipesaukee have made Alton Bay a stop since 1872.
Alton Bay is home to the Alton Bay Campmeeting Association, a Christian campground organized in 1863 and continuing a year round ministry. The campground is home to retreat groups and summer residents who live there and participate in the services of the Association.
The village has a separate ZIP code (03810) from the rest of the town of Alton.
