==Biography==

He graduated from The Roxbury Latin School, where he is celebrated as one of its greatest athletes. Whitman was American intercollegiate singles tennis champion in 1896[tpl]cite web|title=Former U.S. Davis Cup stars meet for Centennial Celebration|url=http://onlineathens.com/stories/071499/spo_0714990101.shtml|publisher=Athens Banner-Herald|accessdate=April 14, 2012|date=July 14, 1999[/tpl]  and doubles champion in 1897 and 1898[tpl]cite web|title=NCAA Championships (1883-1956)|url=http://www.ivyleaguesports.com/history/championships/NCAA/1883-1956|publisher=Council of Ivy League Presidents|accessdate=April 19, 2012[/tpl] as a student at Harvard University. He graduated from Harvard Law School in 1899 and received his bachelor in law degree in 1902.[tpl]cite news|title=Jumps To Death From Penthouse|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=lfxPAAAAIBAJ&sjid=6lQDAAAAIBAJ&pg=6024%2C2186216|newspaper=The Evening Independent|date=December 28, 1932[/tpl]
In 1896 Whitman entered his first U.S. National Championships at the Newport Casino and lost in the quarterfinals to Bill Larned. The following year, 1897, he again lost in the quarterfinals, this time against Harold Nisbet.[tpl]cite web|title=Lawn Tennis At Newport|url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=F50F12FF3E5913738DDDAB0A94D0405B8785F0D3|publisher=The New York Times|accessdate=April 15, 2012|date=August 22, 1897[/tpl] Whitman is best known for this hat-trick of singles titles at the U.S. National Championships. Between 1898 and 1900, he stayed undefeated there. In 1901 he did not compete and in the 1902 Championships he lost in the All-Comers final to Englishman Reginald Doherty. According to the Doherty brothers Malcolm Whitman and Bill Larned were at the time the best American singles players. 
He played on the inaugural American Davis Cup squad in 1900 and beat Englishman Arthur Gore in Boston, MA to help his US team win the trophy.[tpl]cite web|title=Davis Cup - Results 1900|url=http://www.daviscup.com/en/draws-results/tie/details.aspx?tieId=10003699|publisher=ITF|accessdate=April 14, 2012[/tpl] In the 1902 Davis Cup final against Great Britain in Brooklyn, NY he again contributed to his team's win by defeating Joshua Pim and Reginald Doherty in the singles.[tpl]cite web|title=Davis Cup - Results 1902|url=http://www.daviscup.com/en/draws-results/tie/details.aspx?tieId=10003701|publisher=ITF|accessdate=April 14, 2012[/tpl] 
Whitman retired from tennis in 1902 at the age of 25. He was a member of the Executive Committee of the U.S. National Lawn Tennis Association (USNLTA) and held management positions in several companies.
In 1932 he wrote a book on the origin of tennis titled "Tennis - Origins and Mysteries".

==Personal life==

Whitman married his first wife, Janet McCook in 1907. She died in December 1909 after the birth of their second child.[tpl]cite web|title=Mrs. Malcolm Whitman|url=http://query.nytimes.com/mem/archive-free/pdf?res=F20D14FA3F5A15738DDDA00994DA415B898CF1D3|publisher=The New York Times|accessdate=April 14, 2012|date=December 19, 1909[/tpl] In July 1912 Whitman married Jennie Adeline Crocker but they divorced in 1924. In 1926, Whitman married Lucilla Mara de Vescovi, known as the Countess Mara. In December 1931 his daughter Mary, 16, from his second marriage, died of pneumonia.[tpl]cite web|title=Young Heiress Dies|url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=9m8bAAAAIBAJ&sjid=S0sEAAAAIBAJ&pg=4314,3487910&dq=malcolm+whitman&hl=en|publisher=The Pittsburgh Press|accessdate=April 14, 2012|page=6|date=December 31, 1931[/tpl] On December 28, 1932 Whitman committed suicide by jumping off an apartment building after a nervous breakdown.[tpl]cite web|title=M.D. Whitman Ends His Life In Leap|url=http://select.nytimes.com/gst/abstract.html?res=F00C10F9345516738DDDA00A94DA415B828FF1D3|publisher=The New York Times|accessdate=April 14, 2012|date=December 29, 1932[/tpl]
