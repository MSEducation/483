[[Uncial 096]]

CATEGORIES: 7th-century biblical manuscripts, Palimpsests, Greek New Testament uncials, National Library of Russia collection

Uncial 096 (in the Gregory-Aland numbering), α 1004 (Soden),[tpl]cite book|last=Gregory|first=Caspar René|authorlink=Caspar René Gregory|title=Die griechischen Handschriften des Neuen Testament|url=http://www.archive.org/stream/diegriechischen00greggoog#page/n51/mode/2up|year=1908|publisher=J. C. Hinrichs'sche Buchhandlung|location=Leipzig|page=40[/tpl] is a Greek uncial manuscript of the New Testament, dated paleographically to the 7th-century. 

==Descritpion==

The codex contains a small part of the Acts of the Apostles 2:6-17; 26:7-18, on two parchment leaves (29 cm by 22 cm). The text is written in one column per page, 26 lines per page, in uncial letters. It is a palimpsest, the upper text was written in Georgian, in the 10th-century.[ref]
 </ref>
The Greek text of this codex is a mixture of text-types. Aland placed it in Category III.
Currently it is dated by the INTF to the 7th century.[tpl]Cite web |url=http://intf.uni-muenster.de/vmr/NTVMR/ListeHandschriften.php?ObjID=20096 |title=Liste Handschriften |publisher=Institute for New Testament Textual Research |accessdate=21 April 2011 |location=Münster[/tpl]
The codex is located now at the Russian National Library (Gr. 19)Uncial 095 has catalogue number 17 and Uncial 097 has catalogue number Gr. 18 in the same library. in Saint Petersburg.
