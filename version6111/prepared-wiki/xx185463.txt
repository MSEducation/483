[[All Saints' Church, Urmston]]

CATEGORIES: Churches completed in 1868, 19th-century Roman Catholic church buildings in the United Kingdom, Grade I listed churches, Grade I listed buildings in Greater Manchester, Gothic Revival architecture in Greater Manchester, Buildings and structures in Trafford, Roman Catholic churches in Greater Manchester, Listed Roman Catholic churches in England and Wales

All Saints' Church is a Roman Catholic church in Urmston, Greater Manchester, England, and is located on Redclyffe Road ([tpl]gbmapping|SJ76649741[/tpl]). The church was constructed between 1867 and 1868 and was designed by E. W. Pugin in the Gothic Revival style for Sir Humphrey de Trafford.[tpl]cite web|title=All Saints’ Church|url=http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/details/default.aspx?pid=1&id=212996|publisher=Images of England|accessdate=2008-01-03| archiveurl= http://web.archive.org/web/20071226014249/http://www.imagesofengland.org.uk/details/default.aspx?pid=1&id=212996| archivedate= 26 December 2007 | deadurl= no[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=All Saints’ Church|url=http://www.genuki.org.uk/big/eng/LAN/BartononIrwell/AllSaints.shtml |publisher=GENUKI.org.uk |accessdate=2008-01-03[/tpl] The church is a Grade I listed building and considered to be an example of Pugin’s best work.

==Fittings and furniture==

The church has timber pews, stained glass, and gargoyles. There is a carved stone altar and reredos. The church also contains a painting depicting E. W. Pugin with a plan of the church.
