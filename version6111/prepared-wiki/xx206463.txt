[[Peekskill Seaplane Base]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct airports in New York

Peekskill Seaplane Base [tpl]Airport codes|||7N2[/tpl] was a public use seaplane base located three nautical miles (5.5Â km) southwest of the central business district of Peekskill, a city in Westchester County, New York, United States. It was privately owned by James M. W. Martin.
The seaplane base was situated on the Hudson River in Verplanck, a hamlet in the Town of Cortlandt.
Also located at this site is the Riveredge Trailer Park, founded and operated by James Martin since 1950. In 1991 he donated the seaplane base, trailer park and waterfront area to the Town of Cortlandt. The [tpl]convert|26|acre|m2[/tpl] of land was given with an agreement that the trailer park would remain open until 10 years after his death, at which time the town will it into a park. Martin had been offered $5.5 million by a developer looking to build condominiums on the property.[tpl]cite news |url=http://query.nytimes.com/gst/fullpage.html?res=9907E2DB133BF930A25756C0A9679C8B63&sec=&spon=&partner=permalink&exprod=permalink |title=A Trailer Park With a View Worth Millions |last=West |first=Debra |publisher=The New York Times |date=2001-05-13[/tpl]
Martin died on July 3, 2006 in his Greenwich, Connecticut home at the age of 87.[tpl]cite news |url=http://www.northcountynews.com/archives/archives_2006/7-19-06/obits.htm |title=Obituary: James Marsh Woodrow Martin |publisher=North County News |date=2006-07-19 [/tpl]
The seaplane base was removed from FAA records in 2007.

==Facilities and Aircraft==

Peekskill Seaplane Base covered an area of [tpl]convert|6|acre|ha|lk=on[/tpl] and had a 15,000 by [tpl]convert|500|ft|m|sing=on[/tpl] seaplane landing area designated 16W/34W. For the 12-month period ending April 29, 2005, it had 1,020 aircraft operations, an average of 85 per month, of which 98% was general aviation and 2% was military. At that time 9 aircraft were based there, including 89% single-engine and 11% multi-engine.
