[[Catherine Deveny]]

CATEGORIES: 1968 births, Living people, Australian atheists, Australian women comedians, Australian columnists, Australian television writers

Catherine Deveny (born 1968) is an Australian comedy writer and stand-up comedian who was a regular columnist in The Age newspaper between 2001 and 2010. She has performed on Australian television networks, in Australian comedy venues and on Australian radio. Deveny has been described as "a serial pest and professional pain in the arse".[tpl]cite web|title=Catherine Deveny|url=http://www.mwf.com.au/2008/content/mwf_2008_standard.asp?name=DevenyC|work=Melbourne Writers Festival|publisher=Melbourne Writers Festival|accessdate=26 March 2013|date=August 2008[/tpl]

===Writer===

Deveny has written for events such as the Logie Awards and the Australian Recording Industry Association (ARIA) Music Awards, and co-wrote the 2005 Australian Film Institute (AFI) Awards with Russell Crowe.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2013[/tpl]
From 2001 to 2010, Deveny was a regular columnist for The Age newspaper, published in Victoria, Australia. McNicoll[/tpl] Deveney's column was then removed from the newspaper after the writer/comedian caused controversy with Twitter posts that were in relation to the 2010 Logies awards ceremony.[tpl]cite news|title=Deveny dropped as columnist for The Age|url=http://www.theage.com.au/entertainment/tv-and-radio/deveny-dropped-as-columnist-for-the-age-20100504-u6si.html|accessdate=26 March 2013|newspaper=The Age|date=4 May 2010|author=Daniel Sankey[/tpl]
In a radio interview following her sacking, Deveny claimed that her employers did not understand the nature of new media and how it is used.[tpl]cite web|last=Davies|first=Rebecca|title=Logies organisers 'ban guests from Twitter'|url=http://www.digitalspy.co.uk/showbiz/news/a314062/logies-organisers-ban-guests-from-twitter.html|work=Digital Spy|publisher=(Hachette Filipacchi UK)|accessdate=19 September 2011|date=12 April 2011[/tpl] On 18 March 2009, during an ABC 774 radio interview between Jon Faine and the editor of The Age Paul Ramadge, angry supporters ambushed the show calling for Deveny's return as a regular columnist;

===Live performance===

The 2009 Festival of Dangerous Ideas, held at the Sydney Opera House, featured Deveny in a live debate with Cardinal George Pell, the Roman Catholic Archbishop of Sydney.[tpl]cite news|title=By George, that floored them|url=http://www.theage.com.au/news/entertainment/tv--radio/by-george-that-floored-them/2009/10/15/1255195864751.html|accessdate=26 March 2013|newspaper=The Age|date=17 October 2009|author=Catherine Deveney[/tpl] In April of the same year, Deveny returned to stand-up comedy in the Melbourne International Comedy Festival with the show Mother of The Year.
Deveny appeared with Daniel Burt at the Butterfly Club venue in "An Evening Of Insight And Filth"—due to a high level of popularity, the show was extended by six shows. Deveny then appeared with Richard Dawkins, Peter Singer, Phillip Adams and PZ Myersat at the 2010 Global Atheist Convention in Melbourne, Australia.[tpl]cite web|title=Program|url=http://www.atheistconvention.org.au/program/|work=2012 Global Atheist Convention|publisher=2012 Global Atheist Convention|accessdate=26 March 2013|date=April 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Cyber attacks smite atheist websites|url=http://www.theage.com.au/technology/security/cyber-attacks-smite-atheist-websites-20091021-h7ul.html|accessdate=26 March 2013|newspaper=The Age|date=21 October 2009|author=Asher Moses[/tpl] Also in 2010, Deveny appeared in a one-woman show, entitled God Is Bullshit, That's The Good News, as part of the 2010 Melbourne Comedy Festival.
Deveny has also regularly acted as a substitute broadcaster on Australian radio station 774 ABC Melbourne.

===Twitter comments controversy===

In April 2010 Deveny caused controversy by posting comments on Twitter, such as "Anzac Day. Men only enlisted to fight for the money, for the adventure or because they were racist." and "An Australian Flag in your front yard tells everyone you're only a couple of Bundy and Cokes away from lynching a wog, slope or Arab."[tpl]citation needed|date=August 2012[/tpl]
In May 2010 a similar controversy arose when Deveny posted a number of Twitter comments during the Logies Awards ceremony. Deveny's comments caused controversy as they were in relation to public figures, such as Bindi Irwin ("I do so hope Bindi Irwin gets laid"); Rove McManus and wife Tasma Walton ("Rove and Tasma look so cute ... hope she doesn't die, too"—Rove's first wife Belinda Emmett died after being diagnosed with breast cancer).[tpl]cite news|title=Catherine Deveny's vile twitter at Bindi Irwin, Rove McManus|url=http://www.heraldsun.com.au/entertainment/confidential/catherine-devenys-vile-twitter-at-bindi-rove/story-e6frf96x-1225861776586|accessdate=26 March 2013|newspaper=The Herald-Sun|date=4 May 2010[/tpl] The Age fired Deveny two days after the event and a "Twitter ban" was consequently enforced during the 2011 Logies Awards event.[tpl]Citation needed|date=March 2013[/tpl]
