[[James M. Swift (American football)]]

CATEGORIES: Eastern Michigan Eagles football coaches

James M. Swift was the first college football head coach at Michigan State Normal School (now Eastern Michigan University), and is credited for introducing the sport of American football to the school.

==Early years==

==Coaching career==

James M. Swift was the first head college football coach of the Michigan State Normal School football team (now Eastern Michigan University), located in Ypsilanti, Michigan, and the school's 1893 yearbook credits him with introducing "scientific football" to the school. He held the position of head football coach for the 1891 season.[tpl]Cite web|last=Shafer|first=Ian|title=Eastern Michigan University (All seasons results)|publisher=College Football Reference|url=http://cfreference.net/cfr/school.s?m=viewAllSeasons&id=202|accessdate=December 30, 2010[/tpl] His coaching record at Michigan State Normal was 0 wins and 2 losses.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.sportsnetwork.com/merge/tsnform.aspx?c=sportsnetwork&page=cfoot/teams/direct495.htm|publisher=The Sports Network|accessdate=April 13, 2011|title=Football Bowl Subdivision: Eastern Michigan University Directory[/tpl]  As of the conclusion of the 2010 season, this ranks him No.37 at Eastern Michigan in total wins and No.38 at the school in winning percentage ([tpl]Winning percentage|0|2|0[/tpl]).
