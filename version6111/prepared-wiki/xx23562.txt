[[Mimosoideae]]

CATEGORIES: Mimosoideae, Plant subfamilies

Mimosoideae are a subfamily of the flowering plant family Fabaceae (Leguminosae)[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.ars-grin.gov/cgi-bin/npgs/html/family.pl?1573 |title=Fabaceae Lindl., nom. cons. subfam. Mimosoideae |work=Germplasm Resource Information Network |publisher=United States Department of Agriculture |accessdate=2009-07-13[/tpl]  characterized by flowers with small petals and numerous prominent stamens. This subfamily is subdivided into four tribes: Acacieae, Ingeae, Mimoseae, and Mimozygantheae.
Some classification systems, for example the Cronquist system, treat Fabaceae in a narrow sense, raising Mimisoideae to the rank of family as Mimosaceae.  The Angiosperm Phylogeny Group treats Fabaceae in the broad sense.
