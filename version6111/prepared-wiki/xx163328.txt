Rivaldo González Kiese (born 23 December 1987) is a Paraguayan footballer who last played for Italian Serie B club Bari. Rivaldo also holds Spanish nationality.Swiss Football League Profile, require manual search He is an attacking midfielder.

==Biography==

Born in Asunción, the capitol of Paraguay, Rivaldo transferred to Italy at young age. Despite being young, he played for the first team of Venezia.After the club was bankrupted, he left for Genoa. That season, he played 9 times in the 2005–06 Serie C1. The club finished as runner-up, winning the promotion playoffs. In August 2006 he left, along with Michele Bacis and Stefano De Angelis.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.genoacfc.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=265&Itemid=31|title=Il Grifo sfoltisce la rosa: cinque sono partiti |date=29 August 2006|accessdate=10 August 2011|work=Genoa CFC|language=Italian[/tpl]
In 2007 he left for Challenge League club Lugano, from the Italian speaking region of Switzerland. Genoan team-mates Tiago Pires, Alessandro Di Maio and Diogo Tavares also joined this club. 
In January 2008 he returned to Italy to play for Potenza.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.genoacfc.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=3784&Itemid=31|title=Rivaldo al Potenza, Di Maio-Valenzana |date=17 January 2008|accessdate=13 November 2011|work=Genoa CFC|language=Italian[/tpl]
In August 2008 he left Potenza for SPAL along with Mirko Martucci in a co-ownership deal for a peppercorn fee of €500 each (€1,000 in total).[tpl]cite book|title=Genoa CFC 2009 bilancio|date=28 April 2010|publisher=Genoa CFC & C.C.I.A.A.|language=Italian[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.genoacfc.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=5439&Itemid=31|title=ULTIME FASI DEL CALCIO-MERCATO ESTIVO |date=29 August 2008|accessdate=13 November 2011|work=Genoa CFC|language=Italian[/tpl] He played 7 games before Genoa gave up the remaining 50% registration rights to SPAL,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.lega-calcio.it/media/Compartecipazioni.pdf|title=Official Document "Resolutions of co-ownership agreements"|date=26 June 2009|accessdate=13 June 2011|archiveurl=http://www.webcitation.org/5qosKsLQx|archivedate=28 June 2010|language=Italian|work=Lega Calcio[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.genoacfc.it/index.php?option=com_content&task=view&id=7468&Itemid=31 |title=ULTIME DAL CALCIO-MERCATO |date=26 June 2009|accessdate=13 November 2011|work=Genoa CFC|language=Italian[/tpl] which caused Genoa to register a financial loss of €500 in 2009 financial year,In page 35 (hand write p.44 or p. 43 CCIAA version), item oneri da compartecipazioni ex art.102-bis N.O.I.F. as the cost listed as with other players along Giacomo Cotellessa and, a total of €1,000 as the revenue of the other half was already booked into the 2008 financial year.
In 2009 he left for Lega Pro Seconda Divisione club Gubbio, rejoining former Venice team-mate Rodrigue Boisfer. The club won the promotion playoffs and was promoted.
He returned to South America and played 6 times for Patronato in 2010–11 Clausura season of Primera B Nacional.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.unoentrerios.com.ar/deportes/Rivaldo-Gonzales-Kiesse-es-la-quinta-incorporacion-de-Patronato-20110131-0040.html|title=Rivaldo Gonzales Kiesse es la quinta incorporación de Patronato|date=31 January 2011|accessdate=13 November 2011|work=UNO de Entre Ríos|language=Spanish[/tpl]
Rivaldo subsequently returned to Italy for the second division club; Bari in July 2011, signing a 2-year contract.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.asbari.it/societa/altre-news/3505-rivaldo-firma-per-due-anni.html|title=Rivaldo firma per due anni |date=21 July 2011|accessdate=13 November 2011|work=AS Bari|language=Italian[/tpl] He also re-joined former Gubbio coach Vincenzo Torrente.
