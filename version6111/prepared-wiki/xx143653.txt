[[Blacksmith Institute]]

CATEGORIES: Environmental organizations based in the United States, Organizations established in 1999

Founded in 1999, Blacksmith Institute is an international non-for-profit organization dedicated to eliminating life-threatening pollution in the developing world. Blacksmith identifies and cleans up the world's worst polluted places, focusing on communities where children are most at risk. Based in New York, Blacksmith has completed over 50 cleanup projects around the world. Blacksmith is currently engaged in over 30 projects in 15 countries.
In 2011 Blacksmith was recognized with the UN-backed Green Star Award for its work in environmental emergencies.[tpl]cite web|title=Six UN-backed green awards handed out for work in disasters|url=http://www.un.org/apps/news/story.asp?NewsID=38428&Cr=disaster&Cr1=risk|work=UN News Centre|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=18 May 2011[/tpl]  In 2010, Blacksmith's work was profiled in Time magazine's "Power of One" column[tpl]cite web|last=Walsh|first=Bryan|title=Power of One|url=http://content.time.com/time/magazine/article/0,9171,2024212,00.html|work=TIME Magazine|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=18 October 2010[/tpl] and in April 2012, nationally syndicated columnist Georgie Anne Geyer wrote about Blacksmith in "Heroic NGO Takes on Big Job of Global Toxic Cleanup."[tpl]cite web|last=Geyer|first=Georgie Anne|title=Heroic NGO Takes On Big Job of Global Toxic Cleanup|url=http://www.uexpress.com/georgieannegeyer/?uc_full_date=20120416|work=uExpress|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=16 April 2012[/tpl] 
Blacksmith Institute has been recognized as one of the country’s top performing nonprofits, earning a 4 star charity rating,[tpl]cite web|title=Charity Navigator Awards Blacksmith Institute Its Highest Rating|url=http://www.blacksmithinstitute.org/charity-navigator-awards-blacksmith-its-highest-rating.html|work=Blacksmith Institute|accessdate=21 November 2013[/tpl] the highest rating from Charity Navigator, the country’s leading independent charity evaluator.  
Blacksmith is known for its annual World's Worst Polluted Places Reports,[tpl]cite web|title=Worst Polluted|url=http://www.worstpolluted.org/|accessdate=21 November 2013[/tpl] for the creation of the Blacksmith Index (used around the world to rate levels of health risk from pollution), and for the Blacksmith database, the only resource of its kind, which currently documents over 600 of the world's worst polluted places. This Polluted Places Initiative identifies polluted sites throughout the world by means of an online nomination process.
Blacksmith is currently expanding their database with the Global Toxic Sites Identification Program, formerly known as Global Inventory Project.[tpl]cite web|title=Blacksmith Institute Leads International Partnership To Build World's First Global Inventory Of Polluted Sites|url=http://www.blacksmithinstitute.org/blacksmith-institute-to-lead-global-inventory-of-polluted-sites.html|work=Blacksmith Institute|accessdate=21 November 2013[/tpl] Blacksmith investigators are assessing polluted hotspots in over 60 countries to build the first comprehensive global inventory of the world's worst polluted places with human health impact. The sites identified in this global inventory will be targeted for cleanup under a global alliance.
Blacksmith serves as Secretariat for the Global Alliance on Health and Pollution. 

==The GAHP (Global Alliance on Health and Pollution)==

In  July 2012, Blacksmith convened a third meeting of world leaders[tpl]cite web|title=Incubating Ideas for Change at the Bellagio Center|url=http://www.blacksmithinstitute.org/blog/?p=1025|work=Pollution Blog|publisher=Blacksmith Institute|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=18 April 2012[/tpl] and experts on pollution at the Rockefeller Foundation's Bellagio Center in Italy to develop an action plan to tackle toxic pollution in low- and middle-income countries.  The newly formed Global Alliance on Health and Pollution (GAHP)[tpl]cite web|title=New Global Alliance Seeks To Tackle Toxic Pollution Hotspots|url=http://www.blacksmithinstitute.org/new-global-alliance-seeks-to-tackle-toxic-pollution-hotspots.html|work=Blacksmith Institute|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=30 July 2012[/tpl] is the first alliance of its kind to respond to the threat of toxic pollution on a worldwide scale. Blacksmith serves as Secretariat for the GAHP, which is supported by the World Bank, the Asian Development Bank, and UNIDO, among other agencies. Blacksmith began coordinating an international effort to create a global alliance in 2008.  The effort was formerly called the Health and Pollution Fund.[tpl]cite web|title=Health and Pollution Fund|url=http://www.hpfund.org/bp.php|accessdate=21 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|title=Global Alliance on Health and Pollution|url=http://www.gahp.net/new/|accessdate=21 November 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|last=Dolan|first=David|title=Toxic hotspots affect 600 million in developing world|url=http://www.reuters.com/article/2009/05/05/us-development-pollution-interview-idUSTRE5442C520090505|work=Reuters|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=5 May 2009[/tpl]

==World's Worst Polluted Places Reports==

Since 2006, Blacksmith Institute's yearly reports have been instrumental in increasing public understanding of the health impacts posed by the world's worst polluted places, and in some cases, have compelled cleanup work at these sites.  Previous reports have identified the top ten world's worst polluted places or pollution problems. The 2009 report focuses on 12 Cases of Cleanup and Success. Blacksmith reports have been issued jointly with Green Cross Switzerland since 2007.[tpl]cite web|last=Rudolf|first=John Collins|title=Report Notes Few Toxic Cleanup Successes|url=http://green.blogs.nytimes.com/2009/10/29/report-notes-few-toxic-cleanup-successes/|work=The New York Times|accessdate=21 November 2013|date=29 October 2009[/tpl]

==How Blacksmith Works==

Blacksmith works cooperatively around the world in partnerships that include governments, the international community, NGOs and local agencies to design and implement innovative, low-cost pollution solutions that save lives.  Blacksmith provides strategic, technical, and financial support to local champions working for the betterment of their communities. [tpl]Citation needed|date=April 2012[/tpl]
