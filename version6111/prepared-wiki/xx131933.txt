[[Luis Moreno (footballer)]]

Luis Moreno (born 19 March 1981) is a football defender who plays for Tauro. He gained notoriety after violently kicking an injured owl that had landed on the pitch during a league match between Junior de Barranquilla and Deportivo Pereira. The owl died due to the kick.

==Club career==

Born in Panama City, Moreno began playing football with local side Deportivo Italia. He was signed by first division club Tauro F.C. in 2001. He had a brief spell with Colombian side Envigado Fútbol Club during 2005 and 2006, before returning to Tauro. Peruvian side Universitario de Deportes attempted to sign him in 2006,[tpl]cite web|publisher=Peru.com|title=Un panameño a la "U"|url=http://www.peru.com/futbol/AutoNoticias/FutbolPortada/2006/08/19/DetalleNoticia78457.asp|language=Spanish|date=19 August 2006[/tpl] but he stayed with Tauro.
In January 2007, Moreno returned to the Colombian league, joining Independiente Santa Fe.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Prensa.com|title=Moreno es uno de los cinco refuerzos|url=http://mensual.prensa.com/mensual/contenido/2006/12/15/hoy/deportes/830187.html|language=Spanish|date=15 December 2006[/tpl] The club terminated his contract in May 2007, after a disciplinary incident.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Prensa.com|title=Luis Moreno separado del equipo Santa Fe|url=http://mensual.prensa.com/mensual/contenido/2007/05/09/hoy/deportes/976788.html|language=Spanish|date=9 May 2007[/tpl] He returned to Tauro, but shortly after joined Mexican Primera División A side Tiburones Rojos de Coatzacoalcos.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Panamá America|author=Figueroa, Carlos|title=Luis Moreno y Edwin fueron presentados|url=http://www.pa-digital.com.pa/periodico/buscador/resultado.php?story_id=617201&texto=uat|language=Spanish|date=18 August 2007[/tpl]
Moreno was loaned to Deportivo Pereira, but returned to Tauro in 2011, where he would help the club win its tenth league title in 2013.[tpl]cite web|publisher=CONCACAF.com|title=Luis 'Lucho' Moreno cree en Tauro|url=http://www.fepafut.com/noticias_detail.asp?id=3775|language=Spanish|date=13 February 2014[/tpl]

==International career==

Moreno made his debut for the Panama national football team in 2001. He has been a key member of the side, participating in the 2005, 2007 and 2009 CONCACAF Gold Cup finals.[tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Courtney, Barrie and Saaid, Hamdan|title=CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2005 - Full Details|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tables/05gc-full.html|date=30 July 2005[/tpl][tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Saaid, Hamdan|title=CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2007 - Full Details|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tables/07gc-full.html|date=19 July 2007[/tpl][tpl]cite web|publisher=RSSSF|author=Lugo, Erik Francisco|title=CONCACAF Championship, Gold Cup 2009 - Full Details|url=http://www.rsssf.com/tables/09gc-full.html|date=12 August 2009[/tpl]

==Owl kicking incident==

During a February 2011 league match between Junior de Barranquilla and Deportivo Pereira, Junior's unofficial mascot owl landed on the field during play and was struck by a pass. As the owl lay stunned on the field, Moreno, from the opposing team, kicked it approximately 3 meters (~10 feet) to the touchline.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.smh.com.au/sport/football/owl-kicked-by-player-dies-from-shock-20110302-1bdnt.html |title=Owl kicked by player dies from shock |publisher=Smh.com.au |date=2011-03-02 |accessdate=2012-06-12[/tpl] Shortly after, he claimed he was prompting it to fly away"Football player faces sanctions for kicking injured owl in Colombia", The Canadian Press, February 28, 2011.[tpl]cite web|url=http://southamerican-futbol.blogspot.com/2011/02/luis-moreno-owl-abuse.html |title=Luis Moreno Owl Abuse |publisher=Southamerican-futbol.blogspot.com |date=2011-02-27 |accessdate=2012-06-12[/tpl] but in a later interview he admitted "The kick was a product of tension on the field at the time." The owl was treated for its injuries at a local veterinary clinic, but later died of stress related to the incident. Moreno received a two-match ban, a $560 fine from Colombian football's governing body in addition to veterinary costs for the owl's treatment and was ordered to do community service at a zoo.[tpl]cite news|author=Reuters |url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/football/2011/apr/28/owl-killing-footballer-community-zoo |title=Owl-killing footballer ordered to do community service at a zoo | Football | guardian.co.uk |publisher=Guardian |date= 28 April 2011|accessdate=2012-06-12 |location=London[/tpl]
