[[Alan McHugh]]

CATEGORIES: Scottish male television actors, Scottish male stage actors, Scottish male comedians, Living people

Alan McHugh is a Scottish actor and playwright. He is best known for his roles in television shows such as Taggart, Take the High Road, Limmy's Show, and Rab C. Nesbitt.

==Early life==

McHugh graduated from the Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama in 1991 and went on to write and star in a catalogue of successful plays, pantomimes, and programmes.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.eveningtimes.co.uk/mobile/times-out|title=Panto King Alan takes time out to act|date=29 January 2013|work=Evening Times|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]dead link|date=August 2013[/tpl]

==Career==

McHugh has vast writing credits with I Dreamed a Dream and numerous pantomimes among them.[tpl]cite web|url=http://m.imdb.com/name/nm0570423/|title=Alan McHugh|date=3 August 2013|accessdate=13 August 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://www.eveningtimes.co.uk/mobile/times-out/panto-king-alan-takes-time-out-to-act-113938n.20014786 |title= Panto king Alan takes time out to act |date= 29 January 2013 |accessdate=23 August 2013[/tpl] He starred in the hit Scottish soap opera Take the High Road as local policeman Tony Piacentini. His theatre work includes Aladdin, Jack and the Beanstalk, and Cinderella.
