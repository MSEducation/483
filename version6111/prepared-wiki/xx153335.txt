==Career==

===Early career===

Lawwell started his career as a trainee accountant with Babcock Energy in the 1970s.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.heraldscotland.com/sport/spl/celtic/lawwell-logic-remains-paramount-at-parkhead-1.1127918|title=Lawwell logic remains paramount at Parkhead|work=The Herald|publisher=Herald & Times Group|first=Richard|last=Wilson|date=7 October 2011|accessdate=9 October 2011[/tpl] After a career working as an executive for a number of companies, including ICI and Hoffman-LaRoche, Lawwell joined Celtic as financial controller in 1990. He left the club and held senior positions in Mining Scotland and Clydeport.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/sport1/hi/football/teams/c/celtic/7619610.stm|title=Lawwell rejects Celtic exit talk|work=BBC Sport|accessdate=21 February 2012[/tpl]

===Celtic===

In September 2003, he returned to Celtic succeeding Ian McLeod as Chief Executive. The job title was changed for Lawwell to Executive Director, Head of Operations as many of the previous Chief Executives had performed poorly in that role.[ref]
