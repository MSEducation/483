[[Sheep Ranch Fortified House]]

CATEGORIES: Basque-American culture in Oregon, National Register of Historic Places in Oregon, Buildings and structures completed in 1863, Malheur County, Oregon

The Sheep Ranch Fortified House, near Arock, Oregon, is a historic structure that was built in approximately 1863.  Also known as McWilliams House, it was built by a man with last name McWilliams.  It served as a ranch house, as a stagecoach stop, and as a fort.
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1974.[tpl]cite web|title=Oregon National Register List|publisher=Oregon Parks and Recreation Department|url=http://www.oregon.gov/OPRD/HCD/NATREG/docs/oregon_nr_list.pdf|page=26|format=PDF|date=June 6, 2011|accessdate=September 28, 2013[/tpl]
It was bought in 1914 by Pascual Eiguren, a Basque immigrant, whose family continued to own it in 1974, and it is partly notable for its Basque association.[tpl]cite web|url=http://pdfhost.focus.nps.gov/docs/NRHP/Text/74001695.pdf |title=National Register of Historic Places Inventory/Nomination: Sheep Ranch Fortified House |author=Paul Hartwig and D. W. Powers III |date=July 16, 1974 |publisher=National Park Service[/tpl] and accompanying two photos from 1972
