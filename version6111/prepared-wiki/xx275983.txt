Tom Norris (born 1971) is an English musician, composer, ensemble leader and songwriter who plays classical violin with the London Symphony Orchestra and also manages a solo pop music career.

==Life==

Tom Norris was born in Kent and attended Chetham's School of Music as a child.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.christchurch-westwimbledon.org/jigsaw/jigsaw_players1.htm#Violins|title=The Jigsaw Players|accessdate=29 September 2011[/tpl] Later he studied at the Guildhall School of Music and Drama in London, and began singing and playing in local clubs as a jazz violinist with musicians including Lionel Grigson. After finishing school, he played as an extra with the London Symphony Orchestra, began to write songs and toured in a piano trio with Norbert Zehm and Kate Shortt. He continued his studies at Banff Centre for the Arts in Alberta, Canada, and then took a position for two years as Principal Second Violinist with the Winnipeg Symphony Orchestra. He returned to London for a position with the London Symphony Orchestra, where he plays Co-Principal Second Violin.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tomnorris.net/index.php?option=com_content&view=category&layout=blog&id=35&Itemid=54|title=Biography|accessdate=29 September 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web |url=http://lso.co.uk/page/3128/Players|title=Players|accessdate=29 September 2011[/tpl]
Norris has worked with a broad range of musical acts including The Who, Eric Clapton, Antony and the Johnsons, John Adams, Deep Purple, Manu Delago, Seal, Dave Brubeck, Elvis Costello, Daniel Barenboim, Bernard Haitink and Pierre Boulez.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.musolife.com/tom-norris.html|title=Going solo: Tom Norris|date=30 July 2010|author=Strain, Lauren|accessdate=26 September 2011[/tpl] In March 2010, Norris played violin for The Who's production of Quadrophenia at the Royal Albert Hall in London as a tenth anniversary charity benefit for the Teenage Cancer Trust. On 3 July 2010, he performed as the opening act for Eric Clapton at DTE Energy music theatre.
Norris leads the Vuillaume Quartet, formed in 2001 for the London Symphony Orchestra's Discovery program. In August 2010, his classical ensemble The Puertas Quartet reached the semi-finals of the Bordeaux International String Quartet Competition.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.tomnorris.net/index.php?option=com_myblog&Itemid=58|title=Back to Classics|accessdate=29 September 2011[/tpl]

==Discography==

Norris maintains a broad discography, having appeared on numerous CDs as a musician, composer and/or ensemble leader. In 2010 he debuted as a solo recording artist, issuing his first popular music CD, Edge of the World.
