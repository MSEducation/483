[[Christ Church (Bronx, New York)]]

CATEGORIES: Richard Michell Upjohn buildings, Episcopal churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York City, Second Empire architecture in New York, Gothic Revival architecture in New York, Churches completed in 1866, 19th-century Episcopal church buildings, Churches in the Bronx

The Christ Church Complex (Christ Church, Riverdale) is a historic Episcopal parish church and related structures at 5030 Riverdale Avenue in the Riverdale area of The Bronx in New York City.
The church was founded in 1866 and its building, made from brick and local stone with a simple pierced-wall belfry; the church building was built 1866 to designs by architect Richard M. Upjohn. It was built in 1866 and added to the National Register of Historic Places in 1983.
