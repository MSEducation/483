[[Oloplotosus]]

CATEGORIES: Plotosidae, Fish of New Guinea

Oloplotosus is a genus of eeltail catfishes endemic to the island of New Guinea.

==Species==

There are currently three recognized species in this genus: [tpl]FishBase genus | genus = Oloplotosus| month = February | year = 2012[/tpl]
O. luteus originates from the upper Fly River in New Guinea, O. mariae is known from Western New Guinea, and O. torobo is known from the Soro River of Papua New Guinea.[tpl]cite journal|url=http://silurus.acnatsci.org/ACSI/library/biblios/2007_Ferraris_Catfish_Checklist.pdf|title=Checklist of catfishes, recent and fossil (Osteichthyes: Siluriformes), and catalogue of siluriform primary types|first=Carl J., Jr.|last=Ferraris|journal=Zootaxa|volume=1418|pages=1–628|year=2007|format=PDF[/tpl] Otoplotosus species grow to about 15.0–20.0 centimetres (5.9–7.9 in) SL.[tpl]FishBase species|genus=Otoplotosus|species=luteus|year=2007|month=Aug[/tpl][tpl]FishBase species|genus=Otoplotosus|species=mariae|year=2007|month=Aug[/tpl][tpl]FishBase species|genus=Otoplotosus|species=torobo|year=2007|month=Aug[/tpl] O. luteus occurs mostly from moderate to fast-flowing, gravel-boulder bottom creeks in mountainous tributaries of the Ok Tedi River. O. mariae occurs in clear, rocky streams in deeper pools, usually in hilly or mountainous terrain. O. torobo is known only from the type locality; specimens were captured in 1983 from shallow water with a soft, muddy bottom and abundant aquatic vegetation. It is considered a vulnerable species.
