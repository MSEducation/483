[[Brian Ó Domhnaill]]

Brian Ó Domhnaill (born 18 October 1977) is an Irish Fianna Fáil politician and member of Seanad Éireann since 2007.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.oireachtas.ie/members-hist/default.asp?housetype=1&HouseNum=23&MemberID=2261&ConstID=210|title=Mr. Brian Ó Domhnaill|work=Oireachtas Members Database|accessdate=13 March 2010[/tpl]
He attended the University of Ulster where he obtained a Bachelors Degree in Food Technology Management.[tpl]cite web|url=http://brianodomhnaill.ie/background/|title=About me|work=Brian Ó Domhnaill's website|accessdate=14 June 2013[/tpl] He was a sports scholarship student at the university and is a former Irish international athlete.
He is from Cloughaneely in County Donegal. He was a member of Donegal County Council for the Glenties area from 2004 to 2007. Ó Domhnaill topped the poll on his first occasion running as the Fianna Fáil candidate in the Glenties local elecoral area.[tpl]cite web|title=Glenties – Local Elections 2004|url=http://electionsireland.org/result.cfm?election=2004L&cons=357|work=ElectionsIreland.org|accessdate=14 June 2013[/tpl] He was an unsuccessful candidate at the Seanad election for the Industrial and Commercial Panel in 2007.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.electionsireland.org/candidate.cfm?ID=175|title=Brian Ó Domhnaill|work=ElectionsIreland.org|accessdate=13 March 2010[/tpl]
He was elected to Údarás na Gaeltachta board in 1999 and re-elected in 2005. He was nominated by the Taoiseach, Bertie Ahern to the Seanad on 3 August 2007. He was the Fianna Fáil Seanad spokesperson on Lifelong Learning, Youth Work and School Transport in the 23rd Seanad.
He is the Fianna Fáil Seanad spokesperson on Agriculture, Marine and Fisheries.
He was the Fianna Fáil candidate in the 2010 by-election in the Donegal South–West constituency, which was held on 25 November 2010. He received 7,344 first preference votes (21.3%) and finished in third place on the final count. The seat was won by Pearse Doherty of Sinn Féin.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/news/2010/1126/donegaltracker.html|title=Live updates: Donegal by-election count|work=RTÉ News|date=26 November 2010[/tpl] He was an unsuccessful candidate at the 2011 general election for Donegal South–West.
He was elected to the Seanad on the Agricultural Panel in April 2011.
On 20 November 2012 the Standards in Public Office Commission announced a formal investigation into allegations relating to Ó Domhnaill's travel and subsistence expenses claims.[tpl]cite news|url=http://www.rte.ie/news/2012/1120/brian-o-domhnaill-fianna-fail-sipo-expenses.html|title=FF Senator Brian Ó Domhnaill probed over expenses|work=RTÉ News|date=20 November 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite news|url=http://www.donegaldaily.com/2012/11/20/o-domhnaill-to-be-formally-investigated-over-expenses-allegations/|title=Ó Domhnaill to be formally investigated over expenses allegations|work=Donegal Daily|date=20 November 2012[/tpl] On the 6 June 2013, the commission announced that it was postponing its investigation into Ó Domhnaill until a later date.[tpl]cite web|publisher=Standards in Public Office Commission|title=Standards Commission Investigation Postponed|url=http://www.sipo.gov.ie/en/About-Us/News/Press-Releases/2013-Press-Releases/Standards-Commission-Investigation-Postponed.html|accessdate=14 June 2013[/tpl]
In July 2013, during the Protection of Life During Pregnancy Bill 2013 Seanad debate, Ó Domhnaill claimed that allowing abortions in the case of foetal anomalies would deprive Ireland of future Special Olympics athletes. He also remarked that Down Syndrome babies could be "left to die on sterilised trays".[tpl]cite web|publisher=Irish Examiner|date=18 July 2013|title=Senator: Abortions would deprive future Special Olympians of life|url=http://www.irishexaminer.com/ireland/senator-abortions-would-deprive-future-special-olympians-of-life-237179.html|accessdate=18 July 2013[/tpl]
