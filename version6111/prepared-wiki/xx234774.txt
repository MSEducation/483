Leah Janine Marville[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] (born 13 November 1985) is a model, TV host and former Miss Barbados World.
Born and raised in Barbados, Marville started her international career after being spotted at Barbados Fashion Week (2006) by Dwight Peters.[tpl]cite web|url=http://dredecarlo.wordpress.com/2009/03/06/leah-marville-interview/ |title=Leah Marville Interview!!!!! « Dre DeCarlo- Music & FASHION |publisher=Dredecarlo.wordpress.com |date=6 March 2009 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Marville was encouraged to participate in the Fashion Face of the Caribbean competition in Jamaica, where she came third.[tpl]cite web|url=http://bimchat.wordpress.com/2009/01/02/barbadian-model-on-international-circuit/ |title=Barbadian Model On International Circuit « Bajan Global Report |publisher=Bimchat.wordpress.com |date=2 January 2009 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Recommended by international pageant scouts, Leah was flown to New York to develop her portfolio. She was first signed internationally to South African agency Base Model Management[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basemodelagency.com |title=Cape Town, South Africa |publisher=Base Model Agency |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]
[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.basemodelagency.com/details.asp?subid=2313&sexid=2&careerid=1&i=1&cboModelID=296360 |title=Base Model Agency Cape Town – Leah Marville Women |publisher=Basemodelagency.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] and German model agency East West Models.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.eastwestmodels.de/html/index.php |title=EastWestModels |publisher=Eastwestmodels.de |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Leah is signed to RED model management in New York.
Dubbed the ""Sexiest Woman Alive"" in 2009 by Global Beauties,[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] Leah is a former host on Splash, a Caribbean lifestyle series on Black Entertainment Television (BET) sister network Centric.

==Biography==

===Childhood and education===

Leah lost her father, Don Marville, at age eleven to brain cancer and was subsequently raised by her mother, Heather Gibson. She had an active childhood and was part of the Dancing Africa dance troupe.
Marville attended Erdiston Primary school and Queen's College. She went on to study law at the University of the West Indies, Cave Hill campus, Barbados. She received her Bachelor of Laws with honours in 2007.[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] She completed her Legal Education Certificate at the Hugh Wooding Law School in Trinidad in August 2012. She has interned at a prominent entertainment law firm, Gordon-McGrath in Jamaica, and had oversight responsibilities for high profile clients such as Beenie Man and family members of the late Reggae icon Bob Marley. Marville was also employed at the Jamaica Human Rights Council, where she was responsible for overseeing the human rights of death row inmates throughout prisons in Kingston, Jamaica.
While at Queens college she briefly served on her school's Cadet programme where her cousin Shontelle, who is signed to Street Records Corporation/Universal Motown Records[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nationnews.com/story/310904951872867.php |title=Shontelle on Universal roll |publisher=Nationnews.com |date=15 May 2010 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] was her drill sergeant.[tpl]cite news|url=http://news.bbc.co.uk/2/hi/entertainment/7880732.stm|title=Talking Shop: Shontelle|date=5 March 2009|work=BBC News|accessdate=5 March 2009[/tpl]

===Career===

Marville started modeling professionally in 2008 in Cape Town, South Africa for Base Model Management. She has appeared in several magazine publications such as Cosmopolitan Elle and Marie Claire and in 2009, after being titled the Sexiest Woman Alive, shot her first cover for SHE Caribbean[tpl]cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=UMFiPG24gpM&feature=player_embedded#! |title=Behind the Cover Vol. 55 |publisher=YouTube |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] and her second cover for ZING LIAT Magazine.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.ta-emags.com/V1/Zing/Z9/ |title=Z9 |publisher=Ta-emags.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]
Her clients have included: Dunhill Cigarettes, American Swiss, and Nivea-Visage. Marville has participated in several Caribbean fashion weeks: Barbados Fashion Week, Style Week Jamaica,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.styleweekjamaica.com/ |title=Style Jamaica | Cash Advance | Debt Consolidation | Insurance at |publisher=Styleweekjamaica.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Saint Kitts fashion weekend, Guyana Fashion week, Dominicana Moda, and Fashion Week Trinidad and Tobago. On 24 October 2010, Marville was given the award for Top Caribbean Female model, at the Caribbean fashion awards. Her tenth anniversary She magazine cover, was also awarded best Caribbean magazine cover for 2010.
In 2011, Marville signed on as the face and spokesmodel for the inaugural staging of Caribbean International Fashion Week.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.caribbeaninternationalfashionweek.com |title=CIF Week |publisher=Caribbeaninternationalfashionweek.com |date=25 May 2011 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]

==Miss Barbados World==

After attending  Miss World in 2008, and watching fellow country-woman Natalie Griffith[tpl]cite web|url=http://misscontest.blogspot.com/2008/04/natalie-olivia-griffith-miss-barbados.html |title=Global Miss Contest: Natalie Olivia Griffith, Miss Barbados World 2007 |publisher=Misscontest.blogspot.com |date=19 April 2008 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] place in the top fifteen, Leah was inspired to enter her local pageant. On 3 October 2009, she was crowned Miss Barbados World 2009,[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] and represented her country at the 59th staging of the Miss World pageant, hosted in Johannesburg South Africa where Miss World Kaiane Aldorino was the winner. At the Miss Barbados pageant Marville won two fast track events: "Most Photogenic" and "Beauty With A Purpose".
At Miss World 2009 Marville won the "Continental Queen of Beauty" Award for being the highest placed Caribbean delegate. Her Love Campaign won second place for the Miss World Beauty with a Purpose fast track category[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.missworld.com/Miss-World-News/julia-morleys-beauty-with-a-purpose.html |title=Beauty with a Purpose project |publisher=Missworld.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Marville also went on to win the Miss World "People's Choice" award.Missology[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] The Love Campaign was entered as Leah's "Beauty With A Purpose" project for the Miss Barbados World competition and received an award from the HIV Commission of Barbados and the HIV Food Bank.
In April 2011, Stephanie Chase-Yates, former Miss Barbados World 2001 and CEO of SFC International,  handed over the Miss Barbados World franchise to  Marville Management, which is owned and operated by Leah Marville.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.barbadostoday.bb/barticlenew.php?ptitle=Marville+now+in+charge&article=4856&pdate=2011-04-21 |title=Marville now in charge |publisher=News.barbadostoday.bb |date=21 April 2011 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] The new Miss Barbados World, 2011 competition was launched  1 July, at the Limegrove lifestyle centre[tpl]cite web|author=Written by Jennifer |url=http://beautypageantnews.com/miss-barbados-world-2011-press-launch/ |title=Miss Barbados World 2011 – Press Launch at BEAUTY PAGEANT NEWS |publisher=Beautypageantnews.com |date=8 July 2011 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]

==Philanthropy==

Marville's humanitarian efforts began in 2007, when she hosted a charity walkathon dubbed "Fashion with a Mission".[tpl]cite web|author=NationNews |url=http://archive.nationnews.com/archive_detail.php?archiveFile=2007/September/15/Entertainment/45330.xml&start=0&numPer=20&keyword=fashion+on+a+mission&sectionSearch=&begindate=1%2F1%2F1994&enddate=12%2F31%2F2009&authorSearch=&IncludeStories=1&pubsection=&page=&IncludePages=&IncludeImages=&mode=allwords&archive_pubname=Daily+Nation%0A%09%09%09 |title=NationNews – Fashion with a mission |publisher=Archive.nationnews.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] The charity benefited several children who had lost their families in accidents. The funds were donated to the Barbados Media Disaster Relief Fund. Through corporate and public donations, the fund went on to generate over five hundred thousand Barbadian dollars.[tpl]cite web|author=NationNews |url=http://archive.nationnews.com/archive_detail.php?archiveFile=2007/September/10/LocalNews/45068.xml&start=0&numPer=20&keyword=barbados+relief+fund&sectionSearch=&begindate=1%2F1%2F1994&enddate=12%2F31%2F2009&authorSearch=&IncludeStories=1&pubsection=&page=&IncludePages=&IncludeImages=&mode=allwords&archive_pubname=Daily+Nation%0A%09%09%09 |title=NationNews – $1/2M PLEDGE |publisher=Archive.nationnews.com |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]
Founded in March 2009,[tpl]dead link|date=May 2012[/tpl] The Love Campaign, as conceived by Marville, aimed to regenerate a culture of giving and awareness in Barbados and to encourage people to abandon the HIV social stigma. The campaign slogan is: "Don't judge, just love: Abandon the stigma".
The campaign promotion included several multi-media adverts,[tpl]cite web |url=https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=JcBBwMZxAWw |title=Leah One Love Campaign- Miss Barbados World 2009 |publisher=YouTube |date=10 July 2009 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] and featured celebrities such as Wyclef Jean, Marlon Wayans, Eve, Beenie Man, and Kelly Rowland. Reggae-Soul singer Cezar Cunningham sang the campaign theme song "These Three Letters". The campaign was officially endorsed by the former Prime Minister of Barbados David Thompson,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.barbadosadvocate.com/newsitem.asp?more=local&NewsID=5361 |title=Trinidadian and Bajan flavours combine at breakfast party |publisher=The Barbados Advocate |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] the Ministry of  Health, and the Ministry of Youth, Sports and Culture.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.gisbarbados.gov.bb/index.php?categoryid=15&p2_articleid=1732 |title=BGIS Media – Ministry News – Love Campaign Has Ministry Of Youth’s Approval |publisher=Gisbarbados.gov.bb |date=14 May 2009 |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl]
The Love Campaign partnered with Barbadian designer Toni Thorne in 2009, to launch the "Martha's Smile" campaign in Jamaica.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.missbarbadosworld.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=97:miss-barbados-world-partners-with-bouik-designs&catid=36:our-articles&Itemid=2 |title=Just another WordPress site |publisher=Miss Barbados World |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] Donations from the partnership went towards the rebuilding fund for Martha's Home, a hospice for abandoned children living with HIV/AIDS, after it had been destroyed by an electrical fire By October 2010, the campaign has raised more than US$70,000.
In 2010, Marville launched The Love Campaign School Tour, which delivered the message of healthy lifestyles to children at several secondary schools under the banner "respect yaself".http://www.entertainment.southcentralbarbados.com/index.php?option=com_content&view=article&id=68&Itemid=67 The Love Campaign won second place at the 2009 Miss World "Beauty with a Purpose" competition. The campaign has earned Marville several local awards, including two national youth awards: the Bayan Award and the Humanitarian Award; and the Phenomenal Woman award[tpl]cite web|url=http://edition.pagesuite-professional.co.uk/launch.aspx?referral=other&pnum=&refresh=8k0Y12PftW15&EID=18457092-9049-4b28-bfad-489b1328eb3d&skip=true |title=Barbados TODAY News – 05/04/2011 digital edition |publisher=Edition.pagesuite-professional.co.uk |accessdate=5 May 2012[/tpl] presented by the Girlfriends Foundation in the category of philanthropy.
