[[Żółkiewka, Lublin Voivodeship]]

CATEGORIES: Villages in Krasnystaw County

Żółkiewka [tpl]IPAc-pl|ż|u|U|'|k|J|e|f|k|a[/tpl] is a village in Krasnystaw County, Lublin Voivodeship, in eastern Poland. It is the seat of the gmina (administrative district) called Gmina Żółkiewka. It lies approximately [tpl]convert|26|km|mi|0[/tpl] south-west of Krasnystaw and [tpl]convert|42|km|mi|0|abbr=on[/tpl] south-east of the regional capital Lublin.
The village has a population of 790.

==History==

The earliest mention of Żółkiewka (then named Żółkiew) occurs in historical documents from 1359. In 1702 the village received town status, later it acquired its current name. In 1868 it lost its town status and reverted to being a village.  According to the 1921 census the village had a Jewish community consisting of 1308 people, or 64.6 percent of its total population.Jewish Historical Institute community database The Jewish community perished in the Holocaust, and was not reconstituted afterwards.
