[[Don Hennon]]

CATEGORIES: Basketball players from Pennsylvania, Cincinnati Royals draft picks, Pittsburgh Panthers men's basketball players, University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine alumni, Living people, 1930s births

Don Leroy Hennon (born c. 1938) is a surgeon and a former basketball player for the University of Pittsburgh Panthers basketball team, where he was a two-time Consensus All-American. A [tpl]convert|5|ft|8|in|m[/tpl] tall guard, Hennon was noted for his prolific scoring ability and is a member of the Helms Athletic Foundation Basketball Hall of Fame.[tpl]cite book | url=http://books.google.com/books?id=Bn_WIdKtD68C&lpg=PT42&ots=qF10jH96cC&dq=don%20hennon%20helms&pg=PT42#v=onepage&q=&f=false | title=Pitt: 100 Years of Pitt Basketball | first=Sam | last=Sciullo | page=38 | year=2005 | publisher=Sports Publishing LLC | location = Champaign, IL | isbn = 1-59670-081-5| accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl]
In high school, Hennon led Wampum High to an undefeated 31-0 record and a state championship in 1955. During his high school days he set a Western Pennsylvania Interscholastic Athletic League four-year scoring record (1951â€“55) of 2,376 points that endured until 1993.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.lawrencechs.com/basketball.html | title=Basketball: Don Hennon | publisher=Lawrence County Historical Society | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl]
Hennon played in college at the University of Pittsburgh from 1956 to 1959, where he led the Panthers to the 1957 and 1958  NCAA Basketball Tournament. While there he became a First Team Consensus All-American selection in 1958 and a Second Team Consensus All-American in 1959, while being named to the United Press International and Helms Foundation first teams that season. Hennon's basketball career was highlighted by a 1957 contest where he scored a school record 45 points (scoring on 20 of 42 field goals and 4 of 5 free throws), leading Pitt to an 87-84 double-overtime victory over Duke University.[tpl]cite news | url=http://www.pittsburghlive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/sports/college/pitt/s_543629.html | title=Humble Hennon reflects on prolific night | first=John | last=Grupp | newspaper=Pittsburgh Tribune-Review | date=2007-12-20 | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl]  He finished his career at Pitt, an era without the three point shot and before freshman could play, as the school's all-time leading scorer with 1,841 points, and currently remains fifth on the school's all-time scoring list.
Hennon was named to the East team, coached by Adolph Rupp, of the 1959 East-West All-Star Contest.[tpl]cite news | url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=CYUuAAAAIBAJ&sjid=aDEDAAAAIBAJ&dq=don%20hennon&pg=4219%2C2835281 | title=Three More Named For East-West All-Star Contest | last=Associated Press | newspaper=Rome News Tribune |  location=Rome, GA | date=1959-05-25 | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl]
Hennon was picked 41st by the Cincinnati Royals in the sixth-round 1959 NBA Draft, but turned down professional basketball in order to study medicine.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.basketball-reference.com/draft/NBA_1959.html | title=1959 NBA Draft | publisher=Basketball-Reference.com | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl] He earned his MD in 1963 from the University of Pittsburgh School of Medicine and went on to become a surgeon. Hennon spent time as a medical surgeon in the Army and later continued his medical career in the Pittsburgh area, where he still resides. Hennon's number 10 jersey was retired by the University of Pittsburgh in 1968.  He was named to the Helms Foundation Basketball Hall of Fame in 1970 and inducted into the Lawrence County Hall of Fame in 1984.[tpl]cite news | url=http://news.google.com/newspapers?id=uxkNAAAAIBAJ&sjid=2mwDAAAAIBAJ&pg=5513%2C4091527 | title=Pitt's Hennon Named to Helms Hall | date=1970-01-28 | newspaper=Pittsburgh Post-Gazette | location=Pittsburgh, PA | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.lawrencechs.com/hall_of_fame_inductees.html | title=Hall of Fame Inductees | publisher=Lawrence County Historical Society | accessdate=2009-01-08[/tpl]
