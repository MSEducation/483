[[Worcester Council election, 2008]]

CATEGORIES: Worcester City Council elections, English local elections, 2008

The 2008 Worcester Council election took place on 1 May 2008 to elect members of Worcester District Council in Worcestershire, England. One third of the council was up for election and the council stayed under no overall control.[tpl]cite news| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/shared/bsp/hi/elections/local_council/08/html/47ue.stm | title = Worcester | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | publisher = BBC News Online | date=2008-04-19[/tpl]

==Campaign==

Before the election the Conservatives ran the council as a minority administration,[tpl]cite news | first = Nicholas | last = Watt | title = Local elections: From south to north, seats fall to Cameron: Prediction that Tories will claim 44% of council vote, with Labour down to 24% | publisher = The Guardian | page = 6 | date = 2008-05-02 | accessdate = 2010-01-08[/tpl] and required one extra seat in order to win a majority.[tpl]cite news | title = Cameron to claim a Tory renaissance | publisher = Financial Times | page = 2 | date = 2008-05-02 | accessdate = 2010-01-08[/tpl] They had lost their majority after Labour gained a seat from them in a by-election in 2007.[tpl]cite news| url = http://news.bbc.co.uk/1/hi/england/7367619.stm | title = Key battles for local councils | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | date = 2008-04-25 | publisher = BBC News Online[/tpl] 12 seats were up for election with 5 Conservative, 4 Labour, 2 independent and 1 Liberal Democrat seats being contested.[tpl]cite web| url = http://www.stourbridgenews.co.uk/news/worcestershire/2237701.politicians_wait_for_the_verdict_from_the_voters/ | title = Politicians wait for the verdict from the voters | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | date = 2008-05-01 | publisher = Stourbridge News[/tpl] The Conservatives only contested 10 of the 12 seats after they decided not to oppose the two independents who were up for re-election in Nunnery and St John wards, however both they and the independent councillors denied that any deal had been done.[tpl]cite web| url = http://archive.malverngazette.co.uk/2008/4/8/517101.html | title = Tories deny 'dirty deals' to hang on to council seats | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | date = 2008-04-08 | publisher = Malvern Gazette[/tpl]
Three members of the Shadow Cabinet including the Shadow Chancellor George Osborne visited Worcester to campaign for the Conservatives.[tpl]cite web| url = http://www.stourbridgenews.co.uk/news/worcestershire/2209414.conservative_top_gun_visits_city/ | title = Conservative top gun visits city | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | date = 2008-04-18 | publisher = Stourbridge News[/tpl]

==Election result==

The results saw the council remain without any party having a majority with the Conservatives continuing to run the administration.[tpl]cite news | first = Jonathan | last = Walker | title = My blue Brum | publisher = Birmingham Post | page = 1 | date = 2008-05-02 | accessdate = 2010-01-08[/tpl] They had come within 28 votes of winning in Arboretum ward but Labour's Joy Squires held the seat.[tpl]cite web| url = http://www.stourbridgenews.co.uk/news/worcestershire/2242026.tories_stay_in_control/ | title = Tories stay in control | accessdate = 2010-01-08 | date = 2008-05-02 | publisher = Stourbridge News[/tpl]
The Green Party contested ten of the twelve seats, falling back slightly overall from 9.2 to 8.5% of the vote. Its claim of 10% related to the seats it contested. While it failed to win a seat, its best results were in the Rainbow Hill and Cathedral wards, where it scored 23.3 and 18.6% respectively.[tpl]cite web| url = http://www.worcestershiregreenparty.com/worcester_city_council_results_2008.htm | title = Worcester City Council results May 2008 | accessdate = 2010-01-09 | publisher = Worcestershire Green Party[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl]
