[[Symbian Ltd.]]

CATEGORIES: Software companies of the United Kingdom, Companies established in 1998, Nokia assets, Symbian OS

Symbian Ltd. was a software development and licensing company, known for the Symbian OS, a smartphone operating system, and other related technologies.[tpl]cite web|url=http://investing.businessweek.com/research/stocks/private/snapshot.asp?privcapId=1546771 |title=Symbian Ltd |accessdate=26 July 2009 |publisher=BusinessWeek [/tpl]  Its headquarters were in Southwark, London, England, with additional offices in the United Kingdom, China, India, Japan, Korea, and the United States.
It was established on 24 June 1998 as a partnership between Ericsson, Nokia, Motorola, and Psion to exploit the convergence between PDAs and mobile phones. The acquisition of Symbian Ltd. by Nokia was completed on 2 December 2008,[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.nokia.com/press/press-releases/showpressrelease?newsid=1274570 |title=Nokia acquires Symbian Limited |accessdate=26 July 2009 |publisher=Nokia [/tpl] at which point all Symbian employees became Nokia employees. ==Symbian OS==

Symbian Ltd formerly developed and licensed Symbian OS, an operating system for advanced 2.5G and 3G mobile phones. User interface layers were provided by third parties. These included Series 60, Series 80 and Series 90 by Nokia, UIQ from UIQ Technology and MOAP(S) for NTT DoCoMo.
All three interfaces, as well as the OS, are now owned and maintained by the Symbian Foundation.

==Shareholding==

Before its outright purchase by Nokia in December 2008, Symbian Ltd. was owned by Nokia (56.3%), Ericsson (15.6%), Sony Ericsson (13.1%), Matsushita (10.5%), and Samsung (4.5%).
The company's founder shareholders were Psion, Nokia, Ericsson and Motorola. Motorola sold its stake in the company to Psion and Nokia in September 2003.[tpl]cite web|url=http://news.cnet.com/Motorola-to-sell-off-its-Symbian-stake/2100-1036_3-5070006.html |title=Motorola to sell off its Symbian stake |accessdate=26 July 2009 |last=Kawamoto |first=Dawn |date=23 August 2003 |work=CNet News |publisher=CNet [/tpl] Psion's stake was bought by Nokia, Matsushita, Siemens AG and Sony Ericsson in July 2004.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thefreelibrary.com/Symbian%27s+Autonomy+Assured+as+Owners+Split+Psion+Stake.-a0119040949 |title=Symbian's Autonomy Assured as Owners Split Psion Stake |accessdate=26 July 2009 |last=Cripps |first=Tony |date=8 July 2004 |work=Computergram International [/tpl]
Much of Symbian's initial intellectual property came from the software arm of Psion PLC.
