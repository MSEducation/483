[[Martin Narey]]

Sir Martin James Narey (born 1955, Middlesbrough) is an advisor to the British Government, and a former civil servant and charity executive. He served as Director General of the Prison Service of England and Wales between 1998 and 2003, and Chief Executive of the National Offender Management Service from 2004 to 2005. He was as Chief Executive Officer of the charity Barnardo's from 2005 to 2011. He is currently a special advisor to the education secretary  Michael Gove.

==Early life==

Narey was born in 1955 in Middlesbrough, England.[tpl]cite web|title=Sir Martin Narey|url=http://www.portmangroup.org.uk/about/meet-the-team/sir-martin-narey|publisher=Portman Group|accessdate=13 February 2014[/tpl] He was educated at St Mary's College, a Catholic comprehensive school in Middlesbrough.[tpl]cite news|last=Vickers|first=Anthony|title=Sir Martin Narey: 'No-one thought it odd an 11-year-old should be out late, at a Boro match, in a crowd'|url=http://www.gazettelive.co.uk/sport/sir-martin-narey-boro-first-6695870|accessdate=14 February 2014|newspaper=Evening Gazette|date=11 February 2014[/tpl] He studied at Sheffield Polytechnic.

==Career==

Narey joined the Her Majesty's Prison Service in 1982 and began prison governor training. He was the Director General of the Prison Service of England and Wales between 1998 and 2003 before becoming the first Chief Executive of the National Offender Management Service (NOMS). In 2005, he left the Civil Service to become Chief Executive Officer of Barnardo's before stepping down in January 2011.[tpl]cite news|author=Amelia Hill|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/society/2011/jan/21/adoption-barnardos-chief-martin-narey|title=Adoptions need to quadruple, says outgoing Barnardo's chief|publisher=The Guardian|date=21 January 2011|accessdate=9 June 2013[/tpl] As Director General of Prisons he has been credited with "invoking moral principles rather than security concerns when articulating the Service's priorities".[tpl]cite book|last1=Cavadino|first1=Michael|last2=Dignan|first2=James|title=The Penal System: An Introduction|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=CTbLquL1bYYC&pg=PA202|accessdate=9 June 2013|date=17 September 2007|publisher=SAGE Publications|isbn=9781446238301|pages=202–[/tpl]
He is a Visiting Professor in Applied Social Sciences at Durham University,[tpl]cite web|title=Congratulations, Winter Congregation and Honorary Degrees, Northern Ireland Peace Prizes, IAS, Professorial Inaugural Lectures, Data Protection, Records Management and Information Security, EthOS, Bhudda's Birthplace, Riverside Café and Bar Launch|url=https://www.dur.ac.uk/vice.chancellor/local/bulletin/archive/?itemno=16488|publisher=Durham University|accessdate=14 February 2014|date=22 January 2013[/tpl] and a Visiting Professor at Sheffield Hallam University. He is Chair of The Portman Group Complaints Panel and a Board Member of the Advertising Standards Authority. From 2001–13 he was the Government's Advisor on Adoption and his advice, based on an Independent Report commissioned by The Times[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.thetimes.co.uk/tto/life/families/article3083832.ece|title=THE NAREY REPORT: A blueprint for the nation's lost children|last=Narey|first=Martin|date=5 July 2011|work=The Times|accessdate=9 June 2013[/tpl] led to adoption becoming one of the UK Government's domestic priorities. [tpl]cn|date=September 2013[/tpl] He summarised the reforms for the Guardian in July 2012.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.guardian.co.uk/social-care-network/2012/jul/30/children-adoption-martin-narey?INTCMP=SRCH|title=Too many children are missing out on adoption|last=Narey|first=Martin|date=30 July 2012|publisher=The Guardian|accessdate=9 June 2013[/tpl]
In February 2013 it was announced that he was taking on a wider role, advising Michael Gove, Secretary of State for Education on children's social care.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.communitycare.co.uk/articles/15/02/2013/118921/childrens-minister-announces-wider-social-care-brief-for-martin-narey.htm|title=Children's minister announces wider social care brief for Sir Martin Narey|date=15 February 2013|publisher=Community Care|accessdate=9 June 2013[/tpl]
