Michael Angelis (born 18 January 1952)IMDB entry is a Greek-English actor and voice artist.

==Career==

He trained at the College of Dramatic Art, Royal Scottish Academy of Music and Drama, Glasgow, where, inter alia, he played roles in Brendan Behan's The Hostage and The Zykovs by Maxim Gorky.
Michael Angelis featured in the BBC drama serial Boys from the Black Stuff (1982) and another Alan Bleasedale drama G.B.H.. He has also appeared in comedies such as The Liver Birds (1975–78), as Lucien Boswell, and much later in Luv (1993–94). In 1983, he appeared at the Manchester Royal Exchange in Harold Pinter's 'The Caretaker'. He was a villain in the revived television series Auf Wiedersehen, Pet (2002), alongside former fellow Black Stuff star Alan Igbon.
Angelis narrated the Thomas & Friends TV series (UK version) from 1991 to 2012, when he took over from Ringo Starr. He also narrated John Peel's autobiography, Margrave of the Marshes,http://www.audiogo.com/uk/john-peel-remembered-margrave-of-the-marshes-john-peel-gid-21154b which also broadcast on BBC Radio 4 in 2005.
In 2006, he starred in the film Fated, set in his hometown of Liverpool, as well as in episodes of Midsomer Murders and The Bill in 2007.
Angelis was married to the Coronation Street actress Helen Worth, but the couple divorced in 2001.[tpl]cite news|last=Crawford|first=Sue|title=Coronation Street's Gail in Jail: Helen Worth on soap's hottest story|url=http://www.mirror.co.uk/celebs/news/2010/03/07/coronation-street-s-gail-in-jail-helen-worth-on-soap-s-hottest-story-115875-22090711/|accessdate=16 June 2010|newspaper=Daily Mirror|date=7 March 2010[/tpl]
In September 2011, he participated in the BBC Radio 4 programme The Reunion talking with others cast members about his roles in the Boys from the Blackstuff.BBC
He is the younger brother of fellow actor Paul Angelis.
