[[Hamilton Union Presbyterian Church]]

CATEGORIES: Presbyterian churches in New York, Properties of religious function on the National Register of Historic Places in New York, Queen Anne architecture in New York, Religious buildings completed in 1886, 19th-century Presbyterian church buildings, Churches in Albany County, New York

Hamilton Union Presbyterian Church is a historic Presbyterian church at 2291 Western Turnpike in Guilderland, Albany County, New York.  It was built in 1886 and is in the Eastlake / Stick style.  It features a large, open bell tower on the south side.  The structure incorporates buttresses to compensate for the lack of roof trusses.[tpl]cite web|author=Town of Guilderland |title=Building-Structure Inventory: Hamilton Union Presbyterian Church (Building #5) |url=http://www.oprhp.state.ny.us/hpimaging/hp_view.asp?GroupView=286|date=June 1979 |accessdate=2010-10-12 |publisher=New York State Office of Parks, Recreation and Historic Preservation[/tpl]
It was listed on the National Register of Historic Places in 1982.
