[[Kalamunda National Park]]

CATEGORIES: National parks of Western Australia, Protected areas established in 1964, Darling Range, Important Bird Areas of Western Australia, Kalamunda, Western Australia

Kalamunda National Park is a national park in Western Australia (Australia), [tpl]convert|23|km|mi|0[/tpl] east of Perth, near the town of Kalamunda.

==Description==

The park is composed of typical Darling Scarp woodland including species of Marri, Jarrah and Wandoo with a diverse understorey including a range of wildflowers.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.totaltravel.com.au/travel/wa/pertharea/perthhills/travel-guides/gardens/kalamunda-national|title=Total Travel - Kalamunda National Park|year=2010|accessdate=10 June 2010[/tpl]
Piesse Brook flows through the park before joining the Helena River, making the park and important catchment area for both the Helena and the Swan River.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.swanrivertrust.wa.gov.au/science/catchment/Documents/Report%20Cards%20by%20area/Helena%20River%20Report%20Card.pdf|title=Swan River Trust - Helena River|year=2010|accessdate=10 June 2010[/tpl]
No fees apply to enter the park but facilities exist within the park for visitors apart from several walk trails including the northern end of the Bibbulmun Track.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.australia.com/itineraries/wa_bibbulmun_track.aspx|title=The bibbulmun Track Western Australia|year=2010|accessdate=10 June 2010[/tpl]

==Important Bird Area==

The park lies within the Mundaring-Kalamunda Important Bird Area, so identified by BirdLife International because of its importance as a non-breeding season roost site and foraging base  for Long-billed Black Cockatoos.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.birdata.com.au/iba.vm |title=IBA: Mundaring-Kalamunda |accessdate=23 August 2011 |work= Birdata |first= |last= |publisher=Birds Australia |date= [/tpl]
