[[Dorothy Thomas]]

Dorothy Quincy Thomas (born 1960) is an American human rights activist.[tpl]cite news| url=http://www.nytimes.com/1998/06/04/nyregion/public-lives-245000-richer-activist-looks-for-trouble.html| title=Public Lives; $245,000 Richer, Activist Looks for Trouble| author= Joyce Wadler| date=June 4, 1998| work=The New York Times[/tpl] She was a 1998 MacArthur Fellow, and a 1995 Fellow of the Radcliffe Institute for Advanced Study.

==Life==

She graduated from Georgetown University with an M.A. in 1984.http://college.georgetown.edu/43708.html
She is senior program advisor to the US Human Rights Fund. She was founding director of the Human Rights Watch, Women's Rights Division, from 1990 to 1998.
She was a visiting fellow at the London School of Economics, from 2007 to 2008.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www2.lse.ac.uk/humanRights/whosWho/Dorothy_Thomas.aspx|title= Dorothy Q Thomas |publisher=London School of Economics|accessdate=10 October 2013[/tpl]
She is a research associate at the School of Oriental and African Studies at the University of London.
She is a director of the Ms. Foundation for Women.[tpl]cite web|url=http://forwomen.org/content/34/en/Leadership|title=Leadership|publisher=Ms. Foundation for Women|accessdate=10 October 2013[/tpl]
