[[Andrija Fuderer]]

CATEGORIES: 1931 births, 2011 deaths, Chess grandmasters, Croatian chess players, Yugoslav chess players, Belgian chess players

Andrija Fuderer (13 May 1931, Subotica, the Autonomous Province of Vojvodina, Yugoslavia – 2 October 2011, Palamós, Cataloniahttp://www.chess.co.uk/twic/chessnews/obituaries/andrija-fuderer-1931-2011) was a Croatian–Belgian chess master.
At the beginning of his career, he won the Yugoslav Junior Chess Championship in 1947. He was the 1951 Croatian champion,http://sport.hrt.hr/htvsport/htvsport/povijest/tekstovi/20060921_fuderer.html and was a common participant in the Yugoslav Chess Championship tying for 2nd in 1951 (Braslav Rabar won), took 2nd, behind Petar Trifunović, in 1952, and won (jointly) in 1953.
In other tournaments, he took 4th at Bled 1950 (Miguel Najdorf won),bled shared 2nd, behind Albéric O'Kelly de Galway, at Dortmund 1951,dortmund took 5th at Beverwijk 1952 (Max Euwe won).beverwijk

After that last failure, he left chess for a University career in chemistry.1955 Gothenburg Interzonal
