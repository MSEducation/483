[[Lingua (Sculpture)]]

CATEGORIES: Sculptures by Jim Sanborn, 2002 sculptures

The Lingua is a sculpture by American artist Jim Sanborn located at the Walter E. Washington Convention Center.

==Sculpture==

Lingua is composed of Two 16' tall cylinders, with text waterjet cut in Russian, Mandarin Chinese, Ethiopian, French, Spanish, Latin, Greek, and Iroquois.  It is said to contain historical text ranging from as far back as 1400 BC. These include the recalling of both historic meetings and conventions.[tpl]cite web|url=http://web.archive.org/web/20080210100326/http://www.dcconvention.com/artcollection.aspx|title=Walter E. Washington Convention Center Art Collection|date=2009-09-06|publisher=Washington Convention Center Authority |accessdate=2009-10-20[/tpl]

==History==

==Artist==

Jim Sanborn was born in 1945 in Washington, D.C. He grew up in Alexandra, Virginia and attended JEB Stuart High School in Fairfax, Virginia where he studied archaeology and Romanesque cathedrals. Sanborn later studied archaeology at Oxford University, and in 1968, he received a Bachelor of Arts degree in paleontology, fine arts, and social anthropology from Randolph-Macon College. In 1971, he received a Master of Fine Arts degree in sculpture from Pratt Institute in Brooklyn.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.elonka.com/kryptos/sanborn.html|title=Jim Sanborn: Sculptor, Photographer, Artist|publisher=Elonka|accessdate=2009-10-18[/tpl]
