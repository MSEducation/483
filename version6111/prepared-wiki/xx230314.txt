[[Isopogon sphaerocephalus]]

CATEGORIES: Eudicots of Western Australia, Isopogon

Isopogon sphaerocephalus (Drumstick Isopogon) is a small shrub that is endemic to the south-west of Western Australia.
It is usually between 0.5 and 1.5 metres high and produces cream to yellow flowers between July and January in the species native range.[tpl]FloraBase|name=Isopogon sphaerocephalus|id=2237[/tpl]
The species was first formally described by botanist John Lindley in A sketch of the vegetation of the Swan River Colony in 1839.
