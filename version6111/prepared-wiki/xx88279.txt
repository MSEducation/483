[[Ziff (Book of Mormon)]]

CATEGORIES: Metals, Book of Mormon words and phrases

Ziff ([tpl]IPAc-en|z|ɪ|f[/tpl]LDS.org: "Book of Mormon Pronunciation Guide" (retrieved 2012-02-25), IPA-ified from «zĭf»)  is an unknown metal mentioned in the Book of Mormon (Mosiah 11:3,8).
In Hebrew, the word zîw means "splendor" or  "brightness".[tpl]cite journal|title=What's in a Name? A Look at the Book of Mormon. Onomasticon|author=John A. Tvedtnes|journal= FARMS Review|volume = 8|issue= 2|pages=34–42|publisher=Provo, Utah: Maxwell Institute|year= 1996|url=http://maxwellinstitute.byu.edu/publications/review/?vol=8&num=2&id=222[/tpl]
John L. Sorenson, a Latter-Day Saint scholar of Mesoamerican archaeology has opined that the most likely possibility for ziff is an alloy called tumbaga composed of gold and copper.Sorenson, John L.. (1970-04-03) Digging into the Book of Mormon: Our Changing Understanding of Ancient America and Its Scripture – Ensign Sept. 1984 – ensign. Lds.org. Retrieved on 2012-01-25.
