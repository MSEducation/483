[[Sadni Falls]]

CATEGORIES: Waterfalls of Jharkhand

Sadni Falls (also called Sadnighagh Falls) is a waterfall located about [tpl]convert|3|km[/tpl] from Rajadera village in Gumla district in the Indian state of Jharkhand.[tpl]cite web|url = http://www.india9.com/i9show/Rajadera-15124.htm| title = Rajadera  |publisher = india9 |accessdate = 2010-04-20 [/tpl]

==The Falls==

The [tpl]convert|60|m[/tpl] falls on the Sankh River is a scarp fall. It is referred to as a snake type falls and is a popular picnic spot.[tpl]cite web|url =http://jharkhand.nic.in/access.htm | title =Jharkhand |publisher =Jharkhand Govt.  |accessdate = 2010-04-20 [/tpl][tpl]cite book|url = http://books.google.co.in/books?id=T1Y_Ytx9wp4C&pg=PA161&lpg=PA161&dq=hundru+falls&source=bl&ots=9rdY5mtPac&sig=nNchjTZDYWy2hxCxVK5PYiqHS6A&hl=en&ei=ME7NS5yeE4KmrQez6rFq&sa=X&oi=book_result&ct=result&resnum=6&ved=0CBEQ6AEwBThk#v=onepage&q=hundru%20falls&f=false | title = Physical Geography: Hydrosphere By K. Bharatdwaj |publisher = Google Books |accessdate = 2010-04-20 [/tpl] Its surroundings are spectacular with hillocks, forests and streams.[tpl]cite web|url =http://www.india9.com/i9show/-Jharkhand/Gumla/Sadani-Waterfalls-15105.htm | title = Sadani Falls  |publisher = india9 |accessdate = 2010-04-20 [/tpl]

==History==

Once upon a time, active diamond mines existed at Sadni Falls. These mines yielded many large and fine stones in the sixteenth and the seventeenth centuries.[tpl]cite web|url = http://nitishpriyadarshi.blogspot.com/2007_08_01_archive.html| title = History of Diamond mining in Jharkhand and Chhattisgarh|publisher = Environment and Geology |accessdate = 2010-04-20 [/tpl]
