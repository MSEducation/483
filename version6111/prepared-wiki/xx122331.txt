[[Alexios Xiphias]]

CATEGORIES: Byzantine generals, Catepans of Italy, 11th-century Byzantine people, 11th-century Italian people

Alexios Xiphias ([tpl]lang-el|[tpl]lang|grc|Ἀλέξιος Ξιφίας[/tpl][/tpl], [tpl]lang-it|Alessio Xifea[/tpl]) was a Byzantine protospatharios and catepan of Italy from 1006 to 1008, following the long term of office of Gregory Tarchaneiotes. In March 1007, he promulgated a diploma in favour of Alexander, abbot of S. Giovanni in Lamis.
