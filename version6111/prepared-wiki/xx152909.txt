[[St. Vital Centre]]

CATEGORIES: Buildings and structures in Winnipeg, Shopping malls in Manitoba, Eaton's

St. Vital Shopping Centre is an retail shopping mall located in southeast Winnipeg, Manitoba. 
The mall opened 17 October 1979 and is located by the intersection of St. Mary's Road and Bishop Grandin Boulevard.[tpl]cite web|title=S T. V I TA L C E N T R E|url=http://www.20vic.com/propertyPDFs/1112/st-vital-centre.pdf|publisher=20 vic|accessdate=4 November 2012[/tpl] It opened with Eaton's, The Bay, and Woolco as anchor tenants.[tpl]cite web|last=Lorch|first=Brian J|title=The Evolution of a Big-Box Landscape: A Case Study of the Winnipeg Market|url=http://www.cjrs-rcsr.org/archives/29-3/4-Lorch.pdf|publisher=Lakehead University|accessdate=4 November 2012|pages=379, 383|date=Autumn 2006[/tpl] The mall was renovated in 1986, 1998, and 2012.
The building currently has seven anchor tenants and 160 smaller stores, and has a gross leasable area of [tpl]convert|926310|sqft|m2[/tpl] including the freestanding Safeway store, CIBC, Montana's Cookhouse, McDonald's, Old Navy, and Earls.[tpl]cite web|title=Tenant Area|url=http://www.stvitalcentre.com/info/tenant-login|publisher=St Vital Centre|accessdate=4 November 2012[/tpl]  St. Vital Centre is owned by the Ontario Pension Board and managed by 20 VIC Management Inc.
The mall has 4,661 parking spaces, as well as a city transit bus depot positioned close to an entrance.[tpl]cite web|last=Lorch|first=Brian J.|title=Big Boxes, Power Centres and the Evolving Retail Landscape of Winnipeg: A Geographical Perspective|url=http://www.uwinnipeg.ca/faculty/ius/iusweb_backup/pdf/bigboxes_lorch.pdf|publisher=Institute of Urban Studies The University of Winnipeg|accessdate=4 November 2012|page=18|year=2004[/tpl] As of 2001, the mall serves approximately 53,825 households within a 5 kilometre radius, with an average household income of $56,925. The location sees approximately 178,000 pedestrians per week.
The mall recycles plastics and aluminum in their food court, and paper in their offices, and recycles [tpl]convert|4315|MT[/tpl] of cardboard every year.[tpl]cite web|last=SEREBRIN|first=JACOB|title=Recycling Christmas|url=http://uniter.ca/pdf/uniter-58-16.pdf|publisher=The Uniter|accessdate=4 November 2012|page=3|date=15 January 2004[/tpl]
In 2012, the mall underwent a $10 million renovation, which was completed in November 2012. The renovation included new flooring, ceilings, wall coverings and energy efficient lighting.[tpl]cite news|last=McNeill|first=Murray|title=Mall makeover a thing of beauty St. Vital spruces up after 14 years|url=http://www.winnipegfreepress.com/business/mall-makeover-a-thing-of-beauty-143775306.html|accessdate=4 November 2012|newspaper=Winnipeg Free Press|date=22 March 2012[/tpl]
