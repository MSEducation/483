[[Knowles (singer)]]

CATEGORIES: Rwandan music, 1990 births, Living people

'''
Knowles, born Jeanne Ingabire Buteru, is a Rwandan singer. Her stage name is taken from American R&B vocalist Beyoncé Knowles. Butera writes many of her own songs and is known as a strong entertainer famous for her dancing and beauty. Her songs touch on such themes as romantic relationships, societal issues and daily life.

==History==

===Early years===

Butera was born in Ruhango District on 1 October 1990, the only child of Jean-Marie Vianney Butera and Marie Claire Uyambaje, who are both deceased. Her mother was formerly the lead singer in a local Seventh-day Adventist church choir. Butera attended ESCAF primary school in Kacyiru, APARUDE secondary school in Ruhango and APACE secondary school in Kigali with a focus on computer sciences and management. As a teenager she sang in the local choir.  In 2012 she began to pursue a university degree at Kigali Institute of Science and Technology.[tpl]cite web | last = Baike | first = Prisca | title = Knowles: Rwanda's True Music Diva! | newspaper = Tukabyine | location = Rwanda | date = 2 January 2012 | url = http://www.tukabyine.com/index.php/articles/knowles-rwandas-true-music-diva/ | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]

===Musical career===

Knowles released her debut album, Komeza, in December 2011. Her second album, Uwo Ndiwe, was released in March 2013.[tpl]cite news | last = Asiimwe | first = Peninah | title = Action packed: Female stars take over Rwanda| newspaper = The Rwanda Focus | location = Rwanda | date = 14 April 2013 | url = http://focus.rw/wp/2013/04/action-packed-female-stars-take-over-rwanda/ | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]  Knowles is managed under the label Kina Music.[tpl]cite news | last = Kwesiga   | first =Brian  | title = Interview: Tom Close Promises Fire At Rwanda Nite | newspaper = Chimp Reports| location = Rwanda | date = 28 June 2013 | url = http://chimpreports.com/index.php/entertainment/12109-riderman-wins-rwanda-talent-search.html | accessdate = 12 August 2013 [/tpl]
She has performed throughout Rwanda and in neighboring countries, including Uganda, and has collaborated with numerous artists, including Rwandans Danny, Paccy, Ciney, Jay Polly, Kamichi and Urban Boyz, and Ugandan group Vampos. She has named Belgium-based Rwandan musician Cecile Kayirebwa and American R&B singer-songwriter Brandy Norwood as inspirations.
