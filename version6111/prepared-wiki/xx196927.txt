[[Kern County Fire Department]]

CATEGORIES: County government agencies in California, Fire departments in California, Government of Kern County, California

The Kern County Fire Department (KCFD) provides fire protection and emergency response services for the unincorporated areas of Kern County, California, and for multiple cities within the county. The department covers an area which spans over [tpl]convert|8000|mi2|km2[/tpl].
In addition to the unincorporated areas of Kern County, the department also services the cities of [tpl]city link|Arvin|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Delano|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Maricopa|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|McFarland|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Ridgecrest|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Shafter|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Taft|California[/tpl], [tpl]city link|Tehachapi|California[/tpl] and [tpl]city link|Wasco|California[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=Stations List|url=http://www.kerncountyfire.org/index.php?option=com_webmapplus&view=webmapplus&category=1&Itemid=17|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=17 January 2014[/tpl]

==Equipment==

===Helicopters===

The KCFD has two helicopters in their air operations unit, H-407 and H-408. Both helos are Bell UH-1H "Huey"s and are fitted for both hoisting of personnel for a rescue as well as for dropping of water during a fire.[tpl]cite web|url=http://registry.faa.gov/aircraftinquiry/NNum_Results.aspx?NNumbertxt=407KC|title=N Number N407KC|publisher=FAA|accessdate=12 January 2014[/tpl][tpl]cite web|url=http://registry.faa.gov/aircraftinquiry/NNum_Results.aspx?NNumbertxt=408KC|title=N Number N408KC|publisher=FAA|accessdate=12 January 2014[/tpl]  The underside can also be fitted with water dropping tanks that carry 320 gallons of water and have the ability to pump water from a reservoir or other water source or they can be filled on the ground with fire 
hose.

===Fixed-wing===

In addition to their helicopters, Kern County also has a fixed-wing Aero Commander 690A at their disposal.[tpl]cite web|url=http://registry.faa.gov/aircraftinquiry/NNum_Results.aspx?NNumbertxt=490KC|title=N Number N490KC|publisher=FAA|accessdate=12 January 2014[/tpl] The Air Attack 490 is used on brush fires to help coordinate the air attack as well as to organize ground forces.[tpl]cite press release| title = Antelope Fire Update #1 | url = http://www.cdinfo.fire.ca.gov/pub/cdf/images/incidentfile498_743.pdf| publisher = Kern County Fire Department| date = 20 June 2011| accessdate   = 12 January 2014[/tpl]

==Emergency Operations==

===Battalion 1 - Tehachapi===

Battalion 1 comprises the southeastern portion of Kern County. It is divided by Highway 58 that runs east and west and by Highway 14 that runs north and south. A portion of the California Aqueduct, which runs north and south, serves as the eastern boundary of the State Responsibility Area (SRA) for Battalion 1. The western edge of the SRA in Battalion 1 is established by the southeastern edge of the San Joaquin Valley. Battalion one provides fire protection services for the incorporated cities of [tpl]city link|Tehachapi|California[/tpl], [tpl]City link|Arvin|California[/tpl] and [tpl]City link|Rosamond|California[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=Unit Strategic Fire Plan Kern County Fire Department - Battalion 1|url=http://cdfdata.fire.ca.gov/pub/fireplan/fpupload/fpppdf716.pdf|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=14 January 2014|page=5|date=23 June 2011[/tpl]

===Battalion 2 - Western Kern===

Battalion 2 makes up most of the west side of the County of Kern, specifically areas west of Highway 33 and the area surrounding Highway 166. There are no large incorporated cities within Battalion 2.[tpl]cite web|title=Unit Strategic Fire Plan Kern County Fire Department - Battalion 2|url=http://cdfdata.fire.ca.gov/pub/fireplan/fpupload/fpppdf716.pdf|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=14 January 2014|page=6|date=23 June 2011[/tpl]

===Battalion 3 - North Kern===

Battalion 3 includes the north central portion of the county with Highway 65 serving as the western boundary.[tpl]cite web|title=Unit Strategic Fire Plan Kern County Fire Department - Battalion 3|url=http://cdfdata.fire.ca.gov/pub/fireplan/fpupload/fpppdf716.pdf|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=14 January 2014|page=6|date=23 June 2011[/tpl]

===Battalion 4 - Valley===

Battalion 4 comprises the unincorporated parts of Bakersfield to the West and South.[tpl]cite web|title=Kern County Fire Department Wildland Fire Management Plan|url=http://cdfdata.fire.ca.gov/pub/fireplan/fpupload/fpppdf205.pdf|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=17 January 2014[/tpl] 

===Battalion 5 - Mt. Pinos===

Battalion 5 is located in the South and South West portion of the County bordering it is bordering Los Angeles County and Ventura County. Included in Battalion 5's coverage area are the cities of [tpl]City link|Lebec|California[/tpl], [tpl]City link|Frazier Park|California[/tpl], [tpl]City link|Lake of the Woods|California[/tpl], Pinion Pines and [tpl]City link|Pine Mountain Club|California[/tpl].[tpl]cite web|title=Unit Strategic Fire Plan Kern County Fire Department - Battalion 5|url=http://cdfdata.fire.ca.gov/pub/fireplan/fpupload/fpppdf716.pdf|publisher=Kern County Fire Department|accessdate=14 January 2014|page=6|date=23 June 2011[/tpl]
