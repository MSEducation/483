[[Russia in the Eurovision Song Contest 2008]]

CATEGORIES: Russia in the Eurovision Song Contest, Countries in the Eurovision Song Contest 2008, 2008 in Russia

Russia participated in the Eurovision Song Contest 2008 in Belgrade, Serbia. The Russian entry was selected through a national final, Evrovidenie 2008 (Евровидение 2008) organised by the Russian broadcaster Rossiya Channel (RTR). Dima Bilan represented Russia with the song "Believe", which qualified from the first semi-final of the competition and won the contest, placing 1st in the final with 272 points.[tpl]cite web|title=Eurovision Song Contest 2008 Final|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/history/by-year/contest?event=1469|work=Eurovision.tv|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl]

==Evrovidenie 2008==

For the 2008 Contest, Rossiya Channel (RTR) was responsible for the selection of the Russian entry, opting to organize a national final. On 8 December 2007, RTR announced a submission period for interested artists and composers to submit their entries from 15 December 2007 until the deadline on 1 February 2008.[tpl]cite web|first=Michael|last=Kuipers|title=Russian national final on March 9th|url=http://www.esctoday.com/9840|work=ESCToday.com|date=9 December 2007|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl][tpl]cite web|first=Marcus|last=Klier|title=Russia: more details on national final|url=http://www.esctoday.com/9846|work=ESCToday.com|date=10 December 2007|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl]
On 16 February 2008, RTR held a press conference to confirm details regarding the national selection. A jury panel was assembled to select 25 entries from the submissions to compete in the national final. RTR announced it would produce a documentary about the national final and that Muz-TV and Love Radio would follow the preparations of the participants through special diary presentations.[tpl]cite web|first=Stella|last=Floras|title=Russia: Details of the 2008 preselection announced|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10538|work=ESCToday.com|date=17 February 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] On 25 February 2008, RTR revealed the list of 25 entries that had been selected from 167 submissions.[tpl]cite web|first=Stella|last=Floras|title=Russia: List of finalists announced|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10660|work=ESCToday.com|date=25 February 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] Among the competitors were former Russian entrants Dima Bilan (2006) and Premyer-Ministr (2002). On 4 March 2008, an additional two entries were added to the 25 entries previously announced, with former Belarusian entrant Polina Smolova (2006) among them.[tpl]cite web|first=Anna|last=Krasilnikova|title=Russia: Was 25, now 27 national finalists|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10760|work=ESCToday.com|date=4 March 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl]

===Final===

The Russian national final took place on 9 March 2008 at the Akademicheskiy Concert Hall in Moscow, hosted by Oxana Fedorova and Oskar Kuchera.[tpl]cite web|first=Andy|last=Mikheev|title=Russia 2008|url=http://www.esckaz.com/2008/rus.htm|work=ESCKaz|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] 27 songs competed and the winner was selected through a combination of televoting (50%) and jury voting (50%). 2007 Serbian entrant Marija Šerifović, 2008 Ukrainian entrant Ani Lorak and  2008 Belarusian entrant Ruslan Alekhno were the interval acts during the show. Dima Bilan was the winner with the song "Believe", earning top marks from both the public and the jury panel.[tpl]cite web|first=Anna|last=Krasilnikova|title=Russia: Dima Bilan returns to Eurovision|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10828|work=ESCToday.com|date=9 March 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] Bilan previously represented Russia at the Eurovision Song Contest 2006.
The jury panel was composed of Igor Krutoy, Sergey Arhipov, Maxim Fadeev, Gennady Gohstein and Vladimir Matetsky.

==At Eurovision==

With changes to the format of the Eurovision Song Contest coming into effect in 2008, Russia was required to compete in one of two semi-finals in order to qualify to compete in the final. In the allocation draw on 28 January 2008, Russia was drawn to compete in the first semi-final on 20 May 2008.[tpl]cite web|first=Barry|last=Viniker|title=The Eurovision Song Contest semi final draw|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10310|work=ESCToday.com|date=28 January 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] On 17 March 2008 during the draw for running order, Russia was drawn to receive the third of three wildcards for the first semi-final, allowing the Russian head of delegation to select the starting position for the nation.[tpl]cite web|first=Marcus|last=Klier|title=Live: Eurovision 2008 - Draw of the running order|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10890|work=ESCToday.com|date=17 March 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] Russia chose to perform 18th in the first semifinal, following Romania and preceding Greece.
Bilan was joined on stage by figure skater Evgeni Plushenko, who performed on a mobile ice rink placed on stage specifically for the Russian performance, and violinist Edvin Marton, who performed with a Stradivarius.[tpl]cite web|first=Stella|last=Floras|title=Russia: Bilan's staging plans for Belgrade|url=http://www.esctoday.com/10920|work=ESCToday.com|date=21 March 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] Russia qualified from the first semi-final, placing 3rd and scoring 135 points.[tpl]cite web|title=Eurovision Song Contest 2008 Semi-Final (1)|url=http://www.eurovision.tv/page/history/by-year/contest?event=1468|work=Eurovision.tv|accessdate=23 July 2013[/tpl] During the winners' press conference following the conclusion of the first semi-final broadcast, Russia was drawn to perform 24th in the final following Serbia and preceding Norway.[tpl]cite web|first=Marcus|last=Klier|title=UPD Eurovision 2008: the results of the first semi final|url=http://www.esctoday.com/11873|work=ESCToday.com|date=21 March 2008|accessdate=25 July 2013[/tpl] Russia won the 2008 Contest placing 1st and scoring 272 points. As the winning nation of the 2008 Contest and the host of the forthcoming contest, Russia pre-qualified to compete in the final of the Eurovision Song Contest 2009.
The first semi-final and final were broadcast on Rossiya 1, with commentary by Dmitriy Guberniyev and Olga Shelest. The voting spokesperson for Russia was Oxana Fedorova.
