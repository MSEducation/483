[[Billabong Koala and Wildlife Park]]

CATEGORIES: Zoos in Australia, 1989 establishments in Australia, Parks in New South Wales, Buildings and structures in New South Wales, Mid North Coast

Billabong Koala and Wildlife Park is a [tpl]Convert|5|acre|sing=on[/tpl] wildlife park and koala breeding center located in Port Macquarie, New South Wales, Australia. It was opened in 1989, and features a wide variety of Australian and exotic animals. It is known world wide as a breeding centre for koalas.

==History==

The park was opened in 1989, and was a major breeding centre for Koalas. Starting in 2005, new ownership of the park has seen it move from simply breeding Koalas to a more common wildlife park.

==Exhibits==

The park contains a number of native and exotic animals.[tpl]cite web|last=Billabong Koala Park|title=Attractions|url=http://www.billabongkoala.com.au/attractions.php|accessdate=26 December 2010[/tpl]
The park also contains picnic barbecues, gardens, and billabongs (small lakes) covered with water lilies and stocked with koi carp. It also features a cafe in the main entrance building.

==Education==

The park has a number of education talks through the day including a Koala photo session. 

==Conservation==

The park breeds Koalas to send to other zoos in the Australasian breeding program.[tpl]cite web|last=Zoo Aquarium Association|title=Taxon Advisory Group Koalas|url=http://www.arazpa.org|publisher=ZAA|accessdate=26 December 2010[/tpl]
In 2011 the park joined the Zoo and Aquarium Association (ZAA) and has participated in a number of species management programs including Koalas and Snow Leopards.
