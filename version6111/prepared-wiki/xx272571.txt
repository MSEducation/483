[[Hylarana leptoglossa]]

CATEGORIES: Hylarana, Amphibians of Bangladesh, Amphibians of Bhutan, Amphibians of Burma, Frogs of India, Amphibians of Thailand

Hylarana leptoglossa, commonly known as the long-tongued frog, is a species of true frog in the genus Hylarana. It is native to Bangladesh, northeastern India, Myanmar, and western Thailand. It is also known under the common names Cope's frog, Cope's Assam frog, palebrown small frog,  and Assam forest frog.[tpl]cite web |url=http://research.amnh.org/vz/herpetology/amphibia/?action=names&taxon=Hylarana+leptoglossa |title=Hylarana leptoglossa (Cope, 1868) |author=Frost, Darrel R. |year=2013 |work=Amphibian Species of the World 5.6, an Online Reference |publisher=American Museum of Natural History |accessdate=24 November 2013[/tpl] It has recently been reported also from Bhutan.[tpl]cite doi |10.11609/JoTT.o3539.4774-83[/tpl]
Hylarana leptoglossa live near streams in evergreen forests. They are generally found at moderate elevations, below [tpl]convert|500|m|abbr=on[/tpl] in India and between [tpl]convert|600|-|700|m|abbr=on[/tpl] in Thailand. Deforestation, fires, and agricultural encroachment can pose threats to this species.
