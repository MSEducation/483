[[Dan Hirschhorn]]

CATEGORIES: Living people, People from Philadelphia, Pennsylvania, American newspaper reporters and correspondents, Pennsylvania political journalists, Brandeis University alumni

Dan Hirschhorn is a national political journalist based in New York City where he currently serves as the Continuous News Editor of Time.Com.http://www.time.com/time/http://observer.com/2013/09/time-com-is-staffing-up/?utm_content=buffer13798&utm_source=buffer&utm_medium=twitter&utm_campaign=Buffer He was previously a contributing political reporter for The Wall Street Journal.http://stream.wsj.com/story/campaign-2012-continuous-coverage/SS-2-9156/SS-2-238572/ Prior to this position he worked for The Daily before it was shut down,http://www.politico.com/blogs/media/2012/12/murdochs-the-daily-to-shut-down-150872.html, and before that, for
Politico in Washington D.C.  At Politico he was the Deputy 2012 Editor, as well as a reporter.
He attended Brandeis University.[tpl]cite web| title = Dan Hirschhorn :: Reporter - Writer - Journalist| work = phillyreporter.net| date = | url = http://www.phillyreporter.net/[/tpl] While at Brandeis, Dan Hirschhorn was editor in chief of the Brandeis independent student newspaper the Justice.[tpl]cite web| title = The Justice| work = thejusticeonline.com| date = | url = http://www.thejusticeonline.com/[/tpl] Following graduation, he worked as a journalist for The Bulletin in Philadelphia. During his tenure at The Bulletin, Dan Hirschhorn won an award for enterprise reporting from the Pennsylvania Associated Press Managing Editors for a series of pieces concerning the Philadelphia Family Court.[tpl]cite web| title= Pennsylvania Associated Press Managing Editors awardees| work = papme.com| date = | url = http://www.papme.com/contestwinners.aspx[/tpl] He was the "Philly-based half" of PolitickerPA, a political news Web site operated by The New York Observer media company.[tpl]cite news | last = Micek | first = John | authorlink =John Micek| title = Check It Out | newspaper = The Morning Call| location =Allentown, Pennsylvania| publisher = | date = April 26, 2009| url = |quote="Dan Hirschorn, last seen as the Philly-based half of the late PolitickerPa.com, has re-emerged with a new Web site, pa2010.com -- a one-stop shop for the latest campaign news. In addition to news and analysis, Hirschorn said the site will include social-networking facets for campaign operatives and junkies.[/tpl]
After the PolitickerPA Web site was folded into the national Politicker Network, Hirschhorn founded pa2010.com, a political news Web site dedicated to the 2010 election cycle in Pennsylvania.  News items first appearing on pa2010.com have appeared in other media, including stories about Arlen Specter returning $100,000 in campaign contributions from Republicans after he changed parties,[tpl]cite news | title = Fast Eddie owns up to slots haul | newspaper = Pittsburgh Tribune Review  | date = May 17, 2009 | url = http://www.pittsburghlive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/opinion/columnists/whispers/s_625407.html[/tpl] that Jack Wagner would run for Pennsylvania governor,[tpl]cite news | title = Toomey up by 21| newspaper = Pittsburgh Tribune Review| date = April 26, 2009 | url = http://www.pittsburghlive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/opinion/s_622234.html[/tpl] and that Joe Hoeffel was "mulling" a run for Governor.[tpl]cite news  | title = Hey, I May Run Too | newspaper = The Morning Call|date = July 12, 2009 | url = | quote="And Democrat Hoeffel's friends told Pa2010.com the former U.S. House member is mulling a run for governor."[/tpl]
