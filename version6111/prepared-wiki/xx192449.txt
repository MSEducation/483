[[J'ai tout oublié]]

CATEGORIES: 2001 singles, Cristina Marocco songs, Marc Lavoine songs, Songs written by Marc Lavoine, Number-one singles in France, Vocal duets, Singles certified gold by the Syndicat National de l'Édition Phonographique

"J'ai tout oublié" is a 2001 song recorded as a duet by the French artist Marc Lavoine and the Italian singer Cristina Marocco. The song was released on November 19, 2001, as the second single from Lavoine's eighth album, just entitled Marc Lavoine. It reached number one on the French Singles Chart, thus becoming to date the most successful single of the singer in France.

==Background and cover versions==

The song was written by Marc Lavoine and the music composed by Georges Lunghini, the father of Elsa Lunghini. This song allowed Lavoine to take up with the success, because its previous single failed to reach Top 50. ==Chart performances==

In France, the single went straight to number eight on 27 November 2001, and gained a few places almost every week, until reaching number-one in the 14th week, and stayed there for two weeks. Then, the single almost did not stop to drop on the chart and totaled 18 weeks in the top ten, 27 weeks in the top 50 and 30 weeks in the top 100."J'ai tout oublié", in French Singles Chart Lescharts.com (Retrieved April 29, 2008) It achieved Gold statusFrench certifications Disqueenfrance.com (Retrieved April 29, 2008) and was ranked respectively number 692001 French Singles Chart Disqueenfrance.com (Retrieved April 29, 2008) and number 202002 French Singles Chart Disqueenfrance.com (Retrieved April 29, 2008) on 2001 and 2002 End of the Year Charts.
