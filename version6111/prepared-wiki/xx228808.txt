[[List of number-one Billboard Top Latin Albums of 2008]]

CATEGORIES: Lists of number-one Latin albums in the United States, 2008 record charts

The Billboard Top Latin albums chart, published in Billboard magazine, is a record chart that features Latin music sales information. This data are compiled by Nielsen SoundScan from a sample that includes music stores, music departments at electronics and department stores, Internet sales (both physical and digital) and verifiable sales from concert venues in the United States.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/about_us/bbmethodology.jsp |title=Billboard Methodology |accessdate=2008-07-28 |publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|work=Billboard |archiveurl = http://web.archive.org/web/20080722111707/http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/about_us/bbmethodology.jsp  |archivedate = 2008-07-22[/tpl]
There were 23 number-one albums in 2008; one of these, La Vida... Es un Ratico by Colombian singer-songwriter Juanes, won five Latin Grammy Awards, including Album of the Year.[tpl]cite web |url=http://theenvelope.latimes.com/awards/la-grammy_listx_send-2008sep11,0,6719342.htmlstory|title=Latin Grammy Awards scorecard|date=2008-09-10|publisher=Los Angeles Times |work=The Envelope |accessdate=2008-11-18[/tpl] One month after the death of their lead vocalist Sergio Gómez in late December 2007,[tpl]cite web |url=[tpl]Allmusic|class=artist|id=p561057|pure_url=yes[/tpl]|author=Gutiérrez, Evan|publisher=Macromedia Corporation |title=K-Paz de la Sierra - Biography|work=Allmusic |accessdate=2008-11-18[/tpl] the Chicago-based group K-Paz de la Sierra returned to the top of the chart with Capaz de Todo Por Tí, which was number one for six consecutive weeks. Cómplices, the eighth number-one album by Mexican singer Luis Miguel, debuted at number 10 on the Billboard 200, the highest peak for the singer on that chart, with 32,000 album units sold.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1045402/diamond-dominates-billboard-200-with-first-no-1|title=Diamond Dominates Billboard 200 With First No. 1|author=Hasty, Katie|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc |work=Billboard |date=2008-05-14|accessdate=2008-11-18[/tpl] This album also gave the singer the record for the most number-one albums on this chart for one month, until it was tied by fellow Mexican singer-songwriters Marco Antonio Solís and Los Temerarios. This record was broken on October 25, 2008 by Solís with the debut of his ninth number-one album No Molestar.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.billboardenespanol.com/musica/content_display/noticias/ultimas-noticias/e3id5cad753451dc1bf878926449aadbf29|title=Marco Antonio Solís bate récord en Top Latin Albums|language=Spanish |date=2008-10-16|author=Brito, Joel|accessdate=2008-10-20 |publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc|work=Billboard en Español[/tpl]
Vicente Fernández's Para Siempre won the Latin Grammy Award for Best Ranchero Album;[tpl]cite web|title=9th Annual Latin Grammy Awards Winners List|url=http://www.grammy.com/latin/9_latin/#05|accessdate=2008-11-14|date=2007-11-13 |work=Latin Grammy Awards |publisher=The Recording Academy[/tpl] it spent three non-consecutive weeks at the top of the chart. Primera Fila, a live album by Fernández, was the last number-one album of 2008. With 26,000 album units sold, Daddy Yankee debuted at number 13 on the Billboard 200 with Talento de Barrio, a soundtrack for the movie that starred the reggaeton performer, which also debuted at number one on this chart.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1044400/jonas-bros-score-third-best-debut-week-of-08|title=Jonas Bros. Score Third-Best Debut Week Of '08|author=Hasty, Katie|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc |work=Billboard |date=2008-08-20|accessdate=2008-11-18[/tpl] Wisin & Yandel returned to number one with Los Extraterrestres, an album that was awarded with the Latin Grammy Award for Best Urban Music Album. and ended the year as the best selling Latin album of 2008.[tpl]cite web|title=Top Latin Albums |url=http://www.billboard.com/bbcom/yearend/2008/charts/top-latin-albums.shtml|accessdate=2008-12-18|work=Billboard |publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc[/tpl] [tpl]Dead link|date=October 2010|bot=H3llBot[/tpl] On November 29, another Wisin & Yandel recording, a collaboration with DJ Nesty, titled La Mente Maestra debuted at number one. R.K.M & Ken-Y, Flex, Jenni Rivera and Los Pikadientes de Caborca peaked at number one for the first time in their careers. Luis Fonsi's Palabras del Silencio, earned his best sales week ever with 30,000 album copies sold and the second-largest sales week of the year for a Latin performer; only Luis Miguel's Cómplices sold more (32,000 albums in May). This album became the second number-one album on this chart for Fonsi.[tpl]cite web |url=http://www.billboard.com/articles/news/1044279/slipknot-edges-the-game-atop-billboard-200|title=Slipknot Edges The Game Atop Billboard 200|author=Hasty, Katie|publisher=Nielsen Business Media, Inc |work=Billboard |date=2008-09-03|accessdate=2008-11-18[/tpl]
