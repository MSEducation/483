[[Irvin Morris]]

CATEGORIES: Native American writers, Cornell University alumni, Living people

Irvin Morris (1958) is a Navajo Nation author from the Tobaahi clan.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.ipl.org/div/natam/bin/browse.pl/A423 | title=Irvin Morris, 1958- | publisher=IPL2 | work=Native American Authors | accessdate=December 6, 2012[/tpl] He has taught at Cornell University, the State University of New York, the University of Arizona, and Dine College.  He received his MFA at Cornell University, and a PhD in American Indian Studies from the State University of New York at Buffalo. His work, From the Glittering World: A Navajo Story (1997) is a blend of Navajo creation narrative, history, fictionalized memoir, and Navajo stories.[tpl]cite web | url=http://www.goodreads.com/book/show/1104571.From_the_Glittering_World | title=From the Glittering World: A Navajo Story by Irvin Morris | work=2012 | accessdate=December 6, 2012[/tpl][tpl]cite web | url=http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P1-79515909.html | title=From the Glittering World: A Navajo Story | publisher=News From Indian Country | date=October 31, 2001 | accessdate=December 6, 2012[/tpl] The title is taken from the Navajo creation story about the last of five existing worlds, our own, which is called the glittering world.
