[[Morehead-Rowan County Airport]]

CATEGORIES: Airports in Kentucky, Buildings and structures in Rowan County, Kentucky

Morehead-Rowan County Airport [tpl]airport codes|||I32[/tpl] was a public airport located six nautical miles (11 km) southwest of the central business district of Morehead, a city in Rowan County, Kentucky, United States. It was owned by the Morehead-Rowan County Airport Board.
In 2003, the airport board began construction on the Morehead-Rowan County Clyde A. Thomas Regional Airport [tpl]airport codes|||M97[/tpl]. The new airport, built to accommodate a longer runway, is located seven nautical miles (13 km) northwest of the city at [tpl]coord|38|12|54|N|83|35|15|W|region:US_type:airport|name=Morehead-Rowan County Clyde A. Thomas Regional Airport (M97)[/tpl].[tpl]FAA-airport|ID=M97|use=PU|own=PU|site=07286.*A[/tpl], effective 2007-12-20 The new airport opened in 2007, which resulted in the closure of the old airport.Morehead-Rowan County Airport: Services

==Facilities and aircraft==

Morehead-Rowan County Airport covered an area of [tpl]convert|45|acre|ha|lk=on[/tpl]. It contained one runway designated 5/23 with an asphalt surface] measuring 2,600 x 75 ft (792 x 23 m). For the 12-month period ending August 28, 2002, the airport had 5,300 aircraft operations, an average of 14 per day: 92% general aviation, 4% air taxi and 4% military.
