[[Hayden, Alabama]]

CATEGORIES: Towns in Blount County, Alabama, Towns in Alabama, Birmingham–Hoover metropolitan area

Hayden is a town in Blount County, Alabama, United States. At the 2010 census the population was 444.[tpl]cite web| url=http://factfinder2.census.gov/bkmk/table/1.0/en/DEC/10_DP/G001/1600000US0133640| title=Geographic Identifiers: 2010 Demographic Profile Data (G001): Hayden town, Alabama| publisher=U.S. Census Bureau, American Factfinder| accessdate=September 17, 2013[/tpl]

==Geography==

Hayden is located in southwestern Blount County at 33°53'36.236" North, 86°45'25.139" West (33.893399, -86.756983)[tpl]GR|1[/tpl]. Hayden Mountain is a broad, low ridge that rises to the northwest above the town. Alabama State Route 160 passes through the center of the town, leading [tpl]convert|5|mi|0[/tpl] southwest to Interstate 65 north of Warrior, and northeast [tpl]convert|13|mi[/tpl] to Cleveland, Alabama.
According to the U.S. Census Bureau, the town of Hayden has a total area of [tpl]convert|2.9|km2|disp=flip[/tpl], all of it land.

==Demographics==

At the 2000 census[tpl]GR|2[/tpl], there were 470 people, 191 households and 136 families residing in the town. The population density was 499.1 per square mile (193.1/km²). There were 207 housing units at an average density of 219.8 per square mile (85.0/km²). The racial makeup of the town was 91.91% White, 6.60% Black or African American, 0.64% Native American, 0.43% Asian, and 0.43% from two or more races. 0.21% of the population were Hispanic or Latino of any race.
There were 191 households of which 35.6% had children under the age of 18 living with them, 59.2% were married couples living together, 10.5% had a female householder with no husband present, and 28.3% were non-families. 26.7% of all households were made up of individuals and 8.9% had someone living alone who was 65 years of age or older. The average household size was 2.46 and the average family size was 2.98.
Age distribution was 26.8% under the age of 18, 9.6% from 18 to 24, 28.3% from 25 to 44, 25.3% from 45 to 64, and 10.0% who were 65 years of age or older. The median age was 36 years. For every 100 females there were 93.4 males. For every 100 females age 18 and over, there were 88.0 males.
The median household income was $31,484, and the median family income was $36,667. Males had a median income of $30,667 versus $17,375 for females. The per capita income for the town was $14,435. About 10.7% of families and 11.0% of the population were below the poverty line, including 6.9% of those under age 18 and 20.3% of those age 65 or over.
