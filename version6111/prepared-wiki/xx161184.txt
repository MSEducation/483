[[Eliakim Persons Walton]]

Eliakim "E. P. Walton" Persons Walton (February 17, 1812 â€“ December 19, 1890) was an American journalist, editor and politician. He served as a U.S. Representative from Vermont.

==Biography==

Walton was born in Montpelier, Vermont to Ezekiel Parker Walton and Prussia Persons. He attended the common schools and the Washington County, Vermont grammar school. He was apprenticed to a printer (publishing). He studied law under United States Senator Samuel Prentiss, a distant relative.[tpl]cite book|last=Duffy|first=John J.|title=The Vermont Encyclopedia|year=2003|publisher=UPNE|pages=310|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=uTBCXqOou0YC&pg=PA310&lpg=PA310&dq=Eliakim+Persons+Walton+delegate+to+the+Republican+National+Convention&source=bl&ots=AKSK3pkCX_&sig=huhj_u7FhrJ6z7FiTfp9ZTJIWLY&hl=en&sa=X&ei=6_W4UMylIMnLqQHJoYDwCw&ved=0CF8Q6AEwCQ#v=onepage&q=Eliakim%20Persons%20Walton%20delegate%20to%20the%20Republican%20National%20Convention&f=false[/tpl] Walton was admitted to the bar,[tpl]cite book|last=United States Government Printing Office|title=Congressional serial set|year=1918|publisher=United States Government Printing Office|pages=1087|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=aMlTAAAAIAAJ&pg=PA1087&lpg=PA1087&dq=Eliakim+Persons+Walton+delegate+to+the+Republican+National+Convention&source=bl&ots=Bq0lwZBihx&sig=HbezQ-eapqf5-8oHb-hy5uCZXGM&hl=en&sa=X&ei=6_W4UMylIMnLqQHJoYDwCw&ved=0CFsQ6AEwBw#v=onepage&q=Eliakim%20Persons%20Walton%20delegate%20to%20the%20Republican%20National%20Convention&f=false[/tpl] but did not engage in the practice of law.

Walton was a member of the Vermont House of Representatives in 1853. He was elected as a Republican candidate to the Thirty-fifth, Thirty-sixth, and Thirty-seventh Congresses, serving from March 4, 1857 until March 3, 1863. He declined to be a candidate for reelection and returned to his editorial and literary labors.
In 1864, he was a delegate to the Republican National Convention,[tpl]cite book|last=United States Congress|title=A Biographical congressional directory: with an outline history of the national congress, 1774-1911 : the Continental Congress, September 5, 1774 - October 21, 1788, the United States Congress , from the first to the sixty-second congress, March 4, 1789 - March 3, 1911|year=1913|publisher=Government Printing Office|pages=1087|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=4w09AAAAYAAJ&pg=PA1087&lpg=PA1087&dq=Eliakim+Persons+Walton+delegate+to+the+Republican+National+Convention&source=bl&ots=ll8aYhr-pt&sig=mpthyxRQbq6gR0MI84H9cFktoeg&hl=en&sa=X&ei=OfW4UPPjMMGmqgG_5oCYBA&ved=0CFkQ6AEwBg#v=onepage&q=Eliakim%20Persons%20Walton%20delegate%20to%20the%20Republican%20National%20Convention&f=false[/tpl] and served as a member of the State constitutional convention in 1870.[tpl]cite book|last=Hemenway|first=Abby Maria|title=The History of the Town of Montpelier, Including that of the Town of East Montpelier, for the First One Hundred and Two Years|year=1882|publisher=Abby Maria Hemenway|pages=273|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=aYoQ5EssbR0C&q=eliakim+walton#v=snippet&q=eliakim%20walton&f=false[/tpl] He served in the Vermont State Senate in 1875 and 1877,[tpl]cite book|last=Hemenway|first=Abby Maria|title=The History of the Town of Montpelier, Including that of the Town of East Montpelier, for the First One Hundred and Two Years|year=1882|publisher=Abby Maria Hemenway|pages=273|url=http://books.google.com/books?id=aYoQ5EssbR0C&q=eliakim+walton#v=snippet&q=eliakim%20walton&f=false[/tpl] and was a trustee of the University of Vermont and of the Vermont State Agricultural College from 1875 until 1887. He served as president of the Vermont Historical Society from 1876 until 1890. He edited Volume II of the "Collections of the Vermont Historical Society", including the Haldimand Negotiations papers, and edited eight volumes of "Records of the Governor and Council."

==Personal life==

Walton married Sarah Sophia Howes. Following Sarah's death, he married Clara P. Snell Field.[tpl]cite web|url=http://records.ancestry.com/Eliakim_Persons_Walton_records.ashx?pid=115037607/| title = Eliakim Persons Walton (1812 - 1890)| publisher= Ancestry.com |accessdate= November 26, 2012[/tpl]

==Death==

Walton died on December 19, 1890 in Montpelier, Vermont. He is interred in Green Mount Cemetery in Montpelier.
