[[Henry E. Bothfeld]]

CATEGORIES: 1859 births, Massachusetts Republicans, Members of the Massachusetts House of Representatives, People from Newton, Massachusetts, Massachusetts city council members, Mayors of places in Massachusetts, 1953 deaths

Henry Edmund Bothfeld (March 4, 1859-May, 1953) was an American businessman, and politician who  served as the mayor of Newton, Massachusetts and as a member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives.[tpl]Citation|  title =Who's Who in State Politics, 1911 | page =91. | publisher=Practical Politics | location = Boston, MA | year = 1911[/tpl]

==Early life==

Bothfeld was born to Herman F. and Julia (Ferguson) Bothfeld in New York, New York on March 4, 1859.

==Family life==

Bothfeld married Haldee Soule in Newton, Massachusetts on June 18, 1885,[tpl]Citation| last=Marquis|first= Albert Nelson|  title =Who's Who in New England, First Edition | page =126. | publisher=A.N. Marquis | location = Chicago, Illinois |year =1909[/tpl] they had four children Theodore, Haldee, Helen and Henry Soule.[tpl]Citation| last=Leonard|first= John William | title =Who's Who in in Finance, Banking, and Insurance, 1920-1922, Vol. 2 | page =77. | publisher=Who's Who in in Finance, Incorporated | location = Brooklyn, New York |year =1922 [/tpl]

==Public service==

===Newton, Massachusetts city government===

Bothfeld was active in the civic affairs of the city of Newton, Massachusetts, serving on the School Committee, Common Council, as a member of and the President of the Board of Aldermen, and in 1895 he served as the  mayor of Newton.

===Massachusetts House of Representatives===

From 1910 to 1915[tpl]Citation|  title =Who's Who in State Politics, 1916 | page =210. | publisher=Practical Politics | location = Boston, MA | year = 1916[/tpl]  Bothfeld served as a member of the Massachusetts House of Representatives from the Fourth  Middlesex District.
