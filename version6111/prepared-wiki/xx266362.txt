[[Tabula Rasa (Pittsburgh band)]]

CATEGORIES: American post-hardcore musical groups, Musical groups established in 2000, Musical groups from Pittsburgh, Pennsylvania, Rock music groups from Pennsylvania, A-F Records artists

Tabula Rasa was a post-hardcore & math-rock band from Pittsburgh, PA. They were featured on Deep Elm’s Me Against the World: The Emo Diaries Chapter 7 and released a self-titled EP on One Day Savior Recordings as well as a full length album The Role of Smith on A-F Records.

==History==

The band was formed in the fall of 2000 when bassist Chris Miskis[tpl]cite web|url=http://findarticles.com/p/news-articles/pittsburgh-city-paper/mi_8166/is_20050216/origen-chris-miskis-multi-instrumentalist/ai_n50518819/ |title=Origen of Chris Miskis; On Sixty Forty, a multi-instrumentalist rediscovers his roots, The | Pittsburgh City Paper |publisher=Find Articles |date=February 23, 2005 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl] brought drummer Jeff Kopanic together with guitarists Andrew Grossmann and Rob Spagiare. Kopanic had never met either guitarist before the band’s first practice at the Mr. Roboto Project.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/ae/20030628afrecord0628fnp5.asp |title=Touring with Anti-Flag pays off for Tabula Rasa |publisher=Post-gazette.com |date=June 28, 2003 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl] Within a few months, the band had finished recording seven songs at +/- Studio in Pittsburgh with Andy Wright. One of those songs, “The Effects that Try,” appeared as the first track on Me Against the World: The Emo Diaries Chapter 7. The other six were released by One Day Savior Recordings as a self-titled EP.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.bandtoband.com/band/tabula-rasa/s-t |title=Tabula Rasa "s/t" |publisher=BandToBand.com |date= |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl] Miskis left the band in 2001[tpl]cite web|author=Sunday, March 26, 2006 |url=http://www.pittsburghlive.com/x/pittsburghtrib/ae/music/s_436588.html |title=CD Reviews: Modey singer takes an acoustic turn - Pittsburgh Tribune-Review |publisher=Pittsburghlive.com |date=March 26, 2006 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl] and was replaced by Keith Smallwood. Smallwood left before recording with the band and was replaced by Justin Campbell, who played on The Role of Smith, which was produced by J. Robbins and released by A-F Records in 2003.http://store.anti-flag.com/index.php/a-f-records-1/tabula-rasa.html The album was recorded in just seven days at Phase Recording Studios in College Park, MD.http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-507776331.html Kopanic left the band in 2004[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/04261/380477-42.stm |title=Music Preview: Voice in the Wire perfects the punk assault of Teddy Duchamp's Army |publisher=Post-gazette.com |date=September 17, 2004 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl]http://www.highbeam.com/doc/1P3-761067341.html and was replaced by Brian Garbark, who left the band less than a year later. Dennis Kern played drums for the band's last few shows. Spagiare and Grossmann now play in Knot Feeder.[tpl]cite web|url=http://www.post-gazette.com/pg/09022/943526-42.stm |title=Knotfeeder expands on Don Caballero's math-rock legacy preview |publisher=Post-gazette.com |date=January 22, 2009 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl][tpl]cite web|author=POSTED ON JANUARY 22, 2009: |url=http://www.pittsburghcitypaper.ws/gyrobase/PrintFriendly?oid=oid%3A57886 |title=Knot Feeder draws on strong local pedigree for debut full-length, Light Flares - Music - Music Features - Pittsburgh City Paper |publisher=Pittsburghcitypaper.ws |date=January 22, 2009 |accessdate=September 30, 2011[/tpl]
