[[Portland (minor league baseball team)]]

CATEGORIES: Defunct minor league baseball teams, Defunct baseball teams in Oregon, Sports teams in Portland, Oregon, 1893 establishments in Oregon, 1893 disestablishments in Oregon

Portland was the name of a professional baseball team that a member of the fleeting Oregon State League in 1893. Portland represented Portland, Oregon and played against teams from Albany, Independence, and Oregon City. In the early stages of the team, H. Hammond oversaw the club's operations. Later, W. H. Kennedy led the team financially and Edward Stapleton bore the managerial duties. Portland used City View Park as their home until switching to the West End Grounds after June 25. In all league games, Portland played 18 games compiling seven wins and 11 losses.

==Formation==

A Portland club was one of the four planned team to be a member of the newly formed Oregon State League.[tpl]cite news|title=In fields of sport|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=16 April 1893|page=3|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] In March, there was a report that Henry W. Hammond of Portland was working to get a baseball league in the State of Oregon.[tpl]cite news|title=Other kinds of sport|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=13 March 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] Hammond announced he was looking for financial backers for teams in Portland, Salem, Astoria, Albany, Eugene, The Dalles, and Oregon City. The name "Oregon State League" was later devised and expected to start sometime in late-May with a four-team circuit.[tpl]cite news|title=In fields of sport|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=23 April 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=The Oregon State League|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=12 May 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] 
At a league convention on May 11, 1893 in Salem, Oregon, Hammond and Watts released the league constitution, and by-laws. Both stepped down as leaders of the league and in their place league owners voted W. M. Hunt of the Salem club as president. A few weeks later, Hunt announced the league's schedule would feature 36 games.[tpl]cite news|title=State ball league|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=14 May 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The original league schedule had Albany traveling to Portland on May 27, however, the league's opening day was pushed back to May 30 due to bad weather.[tpl]cite news|title=Oregon's baseball league|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=23 May 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=3[/tpl] Before their first game, Portland worked deals with the local electric streetcar operators to give fans a discount of fare to City View Park, the club's home field. Admission into the park would cost twenty-five cents a person. Women, however, had the privileged of being admitted into the grandstands for free while their male counterparts were faced with an additional twenty-five cent charge.

==Regular season==

Portland's official first game started at 2:45 p.m. on May 30, 1893.[tpl]cite news|title=The opening game|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=30 May 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] Their opponent was changed from Albany to Oregon City. The crowd was described as "large". Albert Collet of the Independence club was allowed to play for Portland since their newly signed second baseman had yet made it to town. Both teams played sloppy according The Oregonian as Portland lost 13–19. Portland's second game was played against the Albany club at City View Park in front of a "small crowd".[tpl]cite news|title=First of season|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=4 June 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] Albany took the game 11–5.
On June 4, Portland faced Albany for the second time in as many days, losing the them by a score of 6–11.[tpl]cite news|title=Albany wins again|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=5 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] On their first road trip, Portland traveled to Albany where they were beat twice in one weekend (June 10–11).[tpl]cite news|title=Portland defeated in Albany|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=11 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl][tpl]cite news|title=Portland loses at Albany|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=12 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The final game of the series was in front of a crowd of 1,000 people. Portland's sixth straight loss since the opening of the season came on June 17 in Independence. It was noted that not many people turned out to see Portland fall to the home team 11–5.[tpl]cite news|title=Portland again defeated|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=18 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl]
Portland's first win of the season came on June 18 in Independence. Portland toppled the home team, 5–1.[tpl]cite news|title=Won a game at last|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=19 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] During their scheduled home game against Independence on June 24, Portland was forced to switch venues at the last minute since their ballpark was being occupied by an amateur team.[tpl]cite news|title=The state league|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=25 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The two clubs instead used the Oregon City ballpark where Portland defeated Independence 10–9. On June 25, Portland compiled 17 hits, and stole 26 bases in their victory over Independence.[tpl]cite news|title=Three in a row|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=26 June 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The two clubs instead used the Oregon City ballpark where Portland defeated Independence 10–9. Five-hundred spectators were present as Portland marked their first game at their new home, West End Grounds.
In their next four games, Portland faced Oregon City. The first two games were played on the weekend of July 2–3 in Oregon City. During the second game, Portland's Rankin hit two home runs.[tpl]cite news|title=Portland lost again|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=3 July 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] Both ended in loses for Portland.[tpl]cite news|title=The state league|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=2 July 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The teams traveled back to Portland for the next weekend's series.[tpl]cite news|title=In second place|accessdate=26 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=9 July 1893|page=2|location=Portland, Oregon[/tpl] The two games took place at West End Grounds. Portland won both match-ups.[tpl]cite news|title=Nearing the top|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=10 July 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=4[/tpl] During their next match-up in Albany on July, 15, Portland out scored the home team 19–12.[tpl]cite news|title=The state league|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=16 July 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] Albany was defeated by Portland again the next day.[tpl]cite news|title=Seven in the first|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=17 July 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] The score was 7–4. Portland was two wins away from a .500 winning percentage with their 7–9 record going into their game against Albany which was scheduled to be played in Astoria, Oregon on July 22.[tpl]cite news|title=The state league|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=23 July 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] Albany won the closely contested game, 10–9. Portland's last recorded game came on July 30 at Oregon City.[tpl]cite news|title=Portland lost to Oregon City|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=31 July 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=2[/tpl] Portland was edged 6–5 in that contest. The league folded after the game and play was canceled.
It was announced on August 12, 1893 that the Portland club was going to travel to Boise, Idaho to play their team.[tpl]cite news|title=Portland beaten twice|accessdate=27 September 2011|newspaper=The Oregonian|date=15 August 1893|location=Portland, Oregon|page=3[/tpl] In the first game, Portland sent Youngy Johnson to start. Boise edged Portland 7–6. Portland also lost the second game, 10–8.
